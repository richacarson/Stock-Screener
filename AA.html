Company Profile
Basic Materials ·Aluminum ·NYSE ·United States
Alcoa Corporation is a global leader in bauxite mining, alumina refining, and aluminum production, operating across Australia, Brazil, Canada, Iceland, Norway, Spain, and the United States. The company sells commodity-grade and value-add aluminum ingot to customers in transportation, construction, packaging, and industrial markets, and is pivoting surplus industrial sites toward AI data center infrastructure.

Investment Thesis
Alcoa is a fundamentally transformed company following the 2024 Alumina Limited acquisition and dramatic earnings recovery — net income surged from $60M ($0.26 EPS) in 2024 to $1.2B ($4.42 EPS) in 2025 on higher aluminum prices and volume growth [1]. At 10.2x forward P/E with D/E of just 44.5%, the stock is deeply discounted relative to its improved earnings power. The data center brownfield sales targeting $500M–$1B in proceeds represent an innovative unlocking of stranded asset value as AI infrastructure demand surges [2]. Aluminum production guidance of 2.4–2.6M metric tons for 2026 (up from 2025) with smelter restarts provides volume tailwinds, and the ELYSIS zero-carbon technology positions Alcoa for regulatory-advantaged production long-term [1].
The bear case centers on Alcoa's inescapable commodity cyclicality — aluminum prices have been volatile and a global recession could send prices below $2,500/ton, compressing margins on a business with only 9% profit margins [1]. Revenue declined 1.1% YoY in the most recent period despite higher prices, suggesting volume headwinds. The San Ciprián smelter restart will create $70M in sequential cost headwinds in Q1 2026 [1]. Environmental liabilities remain material — 101 water pollution violations at the Warrick County smelter and multiple Superfund cleanup settlements represent both financial and reputational risk [3]. The $0.40 annual dividend (0.68% yield) provides minimal income support, and the flat dividend since the 2016 spin-off shows no commitment to dividend growth despite improved earnings.
Key Catalysts
  1. Data center brownfield site sales targeting $500M–$1B in proceeds, with first transaction expected to close by June 2026, unlocking stranded asset value from AI infrastructure demand [2]
  2. Smelter restart program increasing aluminum production to 2.4–2.6M metric tons in 2026, driving volume growth and operating leverage on higher aluminum prices [1]
  3. ELYSIS zero-carbon smelting technology development with Rio Tinto, positioning Alcoa for regulatory-advantaged premium aluminum production as carbon pricing expands globally [3]
Key Risks
  1. Aluminum price cyclicality — a global recession or demand slowdown could push prices below $2,500/ton, compressing Alcoa's thin 9% profit margins and reversing the 2025 earnings recovery [1]
  2. Environmental litigation exposure — 101 water pollution violations at Warrick County, Superfund cleanup obligations at multiple sites, and air quality lawsuits create material financial and reputational liability [3]
  3. San Ciprián smelter restart execution risk — $70M sequential cost headwind in Q1 2026 with uncertain timeline to full production and profitability in a high-energy-cost European operating environment [1]
