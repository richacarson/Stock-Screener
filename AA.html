Investment Thesis
Alcoa is positioned to benefit from structural tailwinds in aluminum demand driven by AI data center construction, EV adoption, and green energy infrastructure. With strong 2025 profitability ($1.17B net income), reasonable valuation (forward P/E ~10), and emerging revenue streams from idle site monetization and gallium production, the company offers cyclical upside with sustainability credentials including 87% renewable electricity usage.
Key risks include aluminum price volatility, the San Ciprian refinery expected to generate $75-100M EBITDA loss in 2026, and exposure to global trade tensions. The stock trades below its 50-day SMA of $61.54, and the company's sub-1% dividend yield provides minimal income support. Cyclical earnings swings and commodity dependence limit visibility.
