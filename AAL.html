Investment Thesis
American Airlines offers deep value with a forward P/E of ~3.9x and management guidance for $1.70-$2.70 adjusted EPS in 2026, implying significant earnings recovery. The company is aggressively deleveraging ($2.1B debt reduction in 2025), deploying AI across operations for efficiency gains, and expects 7-10% Q1 2026 revenue growth. At $10.43, the stock is priced for distress despite record revenue.
The bear case centers on the industry's largest debt burden at $36.5B, razor-thin 0.2% profit margins, and extreme cyclicality. A government shutdown cost $325M in Q4 revenue, highlighting macro vulnerability. The stock has fallen from $13.43 (50-day SMA) and faces headwinds from potential recession, fuel price spikes, and labor cost inflation. No dividend and negative equity make this a speculative turnaround bet.
