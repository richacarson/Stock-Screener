Company Profile
Technology ·Communication Equipment ·NASDAQ ·United States
Applied Optoelectronics designs, manufactures, and sells fiber-optic networking products including optical transceivers, lasers, and laser components for data center, CATV, and telecom markets. The company is vertically integrated, fabricating its own laser chips in Sugar Land, TX and Taipei, Taiwan, with a focus on 800G and 1.6T transceivers for AI data center infrastructure. Market cap ~$11.8B.

Investment Thesis
AOI is at the epicenter of the AI infrastructure buildout — its 800G and 1.6T optical transceivers are essential components connecting GPUs in hyperscale AI data centers [1]. Q1 2026 revenue surged 51% to $151.1M with data center revenue up 154% to $81.4M, and management raised full-year guidance to $1.1B with $140M non-GAAP operating income, representing a transformative growth trajectory from $456M in 2025 [1]. 800G shipments of $4.6M in Q1 are expected to quadruple in Q2, with 60-80% sequential growth in Q3/Q4 as the new Texas facility comes online. The $382M equity raise provides $440M cash to fund expansion, and the $20.9M Texas state grant validates the U.S. onshoring strategy [2]. CEO Dr. Lin's 29-year tenure and personal insider buying of 100,000+ shares during the company's darkest period demonstrate rare founder-CEO alignment [3]. Gross margins are guided to improve from 29% to 35% by year-end and 40%+ in 2027.
The bear case centers on extreme valuation, dilution, and customer concentration risk. At 81x forward earnings, 23x TTM revenue, and with negative GAAP profitability (-8.6% margins, -6.1% ROE), the stock prices in substantial future growth that must be executed flawlessly [4]. Shares outstanding surged 50% YoY from the $382M equity raise, creating massive dilution that offsets per-share economics. Free cash flow was -$449M as heavy capacity investment continues. AOI's 2017-2022 collapse was caused by losing a single customer (Amazon) — the same structural concentration risk exists today with Microsoft and Digicomm representing the majority of revenue [1]. Competition from Coherent, Lumentum, and Chinese manufacturers could pressure pricing as 800G supply catches demand. The 60-80% sequential Q3/Q4 growth guidance requires the new facility to ramp on schedule — any construction or production delays would create a significant earnings miss [1].
