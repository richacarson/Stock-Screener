Investment Thesis
AAT offers a 7.42% dividend yield from high-quality coastal West Coast properties in barriers-to-entry markets. The diversified portfolio across retail, office, multifamily, and mixed-use provides income diversification, and 14 consecutive years of dividend payments demonstrate commitment to distributions. Trading below its 50-day SMA, the stock may offer value if office market concerns are overdone.
The bear case is substantial: earnings declined 65.1%, revenue fell 2.9%, dividend growth CAGR of ~1% fails to keep pace with inflation, and the 148% GAAP payout ratio raises sustainability concerns. Office exposure in an AI-enabled remote work world is a structural headwind. Elevated debt (D/E 156%) limits financial flexibility, and the high yield may signal a value trap rather than a genuine opportunity.
