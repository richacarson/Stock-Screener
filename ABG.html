Investment Thesis
Asbury Automotive trades at a compelling valuation (7.4x earnings, 6.2x forward) for a company generating ~$18B in annual revenue with clear digital transformation catalysts. The Tekion DMS rollout completing in Q3 2026 should drive meaningful cost savings in H2 2026 and beyond, while the $500M buyback authorization provides downside support. The $30B revenue target by 2030 offers substantial upside if the consolidation strategy succeeds.
Significant risks include high debt-to-equity of 157%, declining earnings (-52% YoY in Q4), and exposure to cyclical auto demand and potential tariff impacts. The CEO transition in mid-2026 introduces execution risk at a critical time. Autonomous vehicles and evolving car ownership models (ride-sharing, subscriptions) could structurally challenge the dealership model over the next decade.
