Investment Thesis
Abbott is a diversified healthcare powerhouse with exceptional momentum in Medical Devices, which delivered 12 consecutive quarters of double-digit organic growth led by FreeStyle Libre targeting $10B by 2028 [1]. Full-year 2025 sales reached $44.3B with 6.7% organic growth, and 2026 guidance calls for $5.55-$5.80 adjusted EPS (10% growth at midpoint) with 6.5-7.5% organic sales growth [1]. The Exact Sciences acquisition positions Abbott to enter the fast-growing cancer diagnostics market, while AI integration across Lingo, LibreView, and surgical systems creates a defensible data ecosystem [3]. The ultra-conservative D/E of 27% and 43+ year dividend history provide exceptional safety [5].
The primary risk is the Exact Sciences integration — large healthcare M&A carries execution risk, potential regulatory hurdles, and could dilute Abbott's return profile in the near term [1]. GAAP EPS of $3.72 versus adjusted EPS of $5.15 reveals a significant gap, with GAAP earnings impacted by $1.43 of adjustments including restructuring and deal costs. Profit margin of 14.7% and ROE of 13.0% are below healthcare peer benchmarks, partly reflecting Abbott's diversified model with lower-margin segments like Nutritionals and Established Pharmaceuticals [5]. The baby formula crisis resulted in a 'Significant' controversy level from Sustainalytics and ongoing DOJ/FDA oversight that creates reputational and operational tail risk [4]. At 28.4x trailing P/E, the stock requires continued double-digit device growth to justify the premium.
