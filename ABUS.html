Investment Thesis
Arbutus presents a unique value situation: the $2.25B Moderna settlement ($950M upfront July 2026 + $1.3B contingent) substantially exceeds the current ~$815M market cap, suggesting the stock trades at a significant discount to settlement value alone — before any HBV pipeline value. The company's plan to return capital to shareholders in Q3 2026 provides a clear near-term catalyst. Additionally, imdusiran's functional cure data and AB-101's promising PD-L1 results provide pipeline upside.
Key risks include the $1.3B contingent payment tied to an appellate ruling on Section 1498, which introduces legal uncertainty. The company's strategic direction post-settlement is unclear — whether it will invest in HBV development, return capital, or pursue a combination. Revenue declined 60.5% YoY as the company shifted focus from licensing to internal development, and quarterly losses continue. HBV clinical programs face standard biotech development risk, and the competitive landscape includes larger, better-resourced pharma companies.
