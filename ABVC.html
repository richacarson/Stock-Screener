Investment Thesis
ABVC's diversified Phase II pipeline sourced from top academic institutions provides multiple shots on goal, with licensing revenue projected to reach $7M for 2025. The licensing model de-risks the costly Phase III process. Total assets nearly tripled to $21M, and the company has articulated a path to commercialization within five years. Vitargus (ABV-1701) for retinal detachment addresses a clear clinical need.
ABVC is a micro-cap ($31M) with deeply negative ROE (-119%), a CEO serving dual-role as Interim CFO for over a year, and a subsidiary structure under YuanGene that limits strategic independence. The purchase of Asian land assets with biotech capital is concerning. No pipeline asset has advanced beyond Phase II in the company's 35-year history. The low stock price ($1.22) risks Nasdaq compliance, and daily trading volume of ~58K shares limits institutional interest. The fragmented pipeline across six drugs suggests a lack of focus.
