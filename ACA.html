Investment Thesis
Arcosa is a well-positioned infrastructure play benefiting from secular tailwinds in grid modernization, renewable energy, and construction spending. Record FY2025 results ($2.88B revenue, 20.2% EBITDA margin) and constructive 2026 guidance validate the strategy. The $450M barge divestiture provides capital for debt reduction and high-return investments. Analyst consensus of Strong Buy with $126.67 target (26% upside) reflects confidence. The company's Engineered Structures segment directly benefits from AI data center power infrastructure demand.
At 23.8x trailing earnings and 18.8x forward, Arcosa is not cheap for an industrial company. Revenue growth of 7.6% is solid but not exceptional, and ROE of 8.2% is modest. The stock has declined 13% below its 50-day SMA, suggesting near-term momentum challenges. Cyclical infrastructure spending risk, integration challenges from acquisitions, and commodity price volatility in aggregates could pressure results. The minimal $0.05/quarter dividend (0.16% yield) with no growth provides negligible income.
