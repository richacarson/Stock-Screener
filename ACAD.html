Company Profile
Healthcare ·Biotechnology ·NASDAQ ·United States
ACADIA Pharmaceuticals is a biopharmaceutical company focused on developing and commercializing medicines for neurological and rare diseases. Its two commercial products are NUPLAZID (pimavanserin) for Parkinson's disease psychosis and DAYBUE (trofinetide) for Rett syndrome. Pipeline includes remlifanserin for Alzheimer's disease psychosis and ACP-211 for major depressive disorder.
796 Employees ·www.acadia.com

Investment Thesis
ACADIA crossed the $1B revenue milestone in 2025, growing 12% YoY with two monopoly-position products: NUPLAZID ($760-$790M 2026 guidance) for Parkinson's disease psychosis and DAYBUE for Rett syndrome [1]. The company boasts exceptional profitability with 36.5% profit margins, 39.9% ROE, and a near-debt-free balance sheet (D/E 4.3%), rare for a mid-cap biotech [5]. Management projects combined 2028 net sales of approximately $1.7B, implying a ~17% revenue CAGR from 2025 [1]. New CEO Catherine Owen Adams brings deep commercial expertise from BMS and J&J, and the Phase 2 pipeline (remlifanserin for Alzheimer's psychosis, ACP-211 for depression) could further broaden the franchise.
The bear case centers on concentrated product risk and decelerating growth. NUPLAZID faces potential generic competition as patents expire, and a 2023 investor lawsuit alleged the company misled investors about the drug's expanded-use prospects [4]. Forward PEG of 50.9x signals Wall Street expects sharp earnings growth deceleration, and the trailing P/E of 9.6x versus forward P/E of 24.1x reflects a one-time earnings spike from DAYBUE launch that may not recur [5]. Revenue growth slowed from 32% (2023) to 12% (2025) as DAYBUE's launch ramp normalizes. ACADIA has only 796 employees and remains dependent on licensing (Neuren for trofinetide) rather than fully internal innovation. Pipeline risk is significant — failure in the Alzheimer's psychosis or depression programs would narrow the growth runway. The $3.8B market cap provides limited margin of safety if growth disappoints.
Key Catalysts
  1. NUPLAZID volume acceleration with Q4 2025 volume up 13% sequentially, guided to $760-$790M in 2026 net sales as physician awareness of Parkinson's psychosis treatment grows [1]
  2. Phase 2 data readouts for remlifanserin (Alzheimer's disease psychosis) and ACP-211 (major depressive disorder) could significantly expand ACADIA's addressable market [2]
  3. Management's $1.7B combined 2028 revenue target provides a clear growth roadmap, with new CEO Owen Adams bringing BMS/J&J commercial execution capability [1][3]
Key Risks
  1. Concentrated product risk — NUPLAZID and DAYBUE represent nearly all revenue, and patent expirations or competitive entries in Parkinson's psychosis could compress the franchise [1]
  2. Forward PEG of 50.9x signals Wall Street expects significant earnings growth deceleration, creating downside risk if revenue growth slows below the guided $1.22-$1.28B for 2026 [5]
  3. Pipeline binary risk — Phase 2 failures in Alzheimer's psychosis or depression programs would narrow the growth runway for a company with only two commercial products [2]
