Company Profile
Healthcare ·Drug Manufacturers - Specialty & Generic ·NASDAQ ·Canada
Aurora Cannabis Inc. produces, distributes, and sells cannabis and cannabis-derivative products in Canada and internationally. The company operates through Canadian Cannabis and Plant Propagation segments, offering pharmaceutical-grade and consumer cannabis products including dried flower, oils, concentrates, edibles, and topicals. Brand portfolio includes San Rafael '71, Greybeard, Daily Special, MedReleaf, and international brands Pedanios and Bidiol. Founded in 2013 and headquartered in Edmonton, Canada.

Investment Thesis
Aurora Cannabis has successfully pivoted to become an international medical cannabis leader, delivering record medical revenue of $76.2M in Q3 FY2026 (12% YoY growth) with guidance for $269-281M annually [1]. Adjusted EBITDA of $52-57M expected for FY2026 demonstrates operational improvement under CEO Martin's restructuring. The company holds $108M cash against $101M debt and consolidated adjusted gross margin of 62% reflects a higher-quality medical-focused business model compared to consumer cannabis peers [1]. Potential US federal rescheduling or banking reform could catalyze a re-rating of the entire cannabis sector, and Aurora's medical focus positions it well for any regulatory shift toward pharmaceutical-grade standards.
The bear case remains severe: Aurora trades at a $206M market cap after destroying ~$10B+ in shareholder value through dilutive acquisitions, impairments, and reverse stock splits [4]. GAAP net income remains deeply negative (-22.5% profit margin) despite positive adjusted EBITDA, meaning the 'profitability' is an adjusted metric that excludes real cash costs. ROE of -15.5% reflects ongoing value destruction. The Canadian cannabis market suffers from structural oversupply and pricing compression that shows no sign of abating. US federal legalization remains uncertain and could benefit US-based MSOs more than Canadian LPs like Aurora. Forward P/E is negative (-31.6x), meaning no near-term GAAP profitability is expected. Revenue growth of 6.8% is below the cannabis sector's growth potential, suggesting Aurora is taking share slowly in a challenged market. The history of shareholder dilution creates persistent discount risk [3][4].
Key Catalysts
  1. US federal cannabis rescheduling or banking reform (SAFE Banking Act) could catalyze sector-wide re-rating and enable Aurora's international medical cannabis infrastructure to access the US market [1]
  2. International medical cannabis expansion — 12% growth in global medical revenue could accelerate as Germany, Australia, and new markets liberalize medical cannabis regulations [1]
  3. Continued adjusted EBITDA improvement toward $52-57M FY2026 guidance could eventually convert to GAAP profitability, removing a key investor objection [1]
Key Risks
  1. Persistent GAAP losses despite positive adjusted EBITDA — -22.5% profit margin and -15.5% ROE mean real value destruction continues even as operational metrics improve [4]
  2. Canadian cannabis market structural oversupply and pricing compression — consumer cannabis remains commoditized with no pricing power, limiting upside even as medical grows [3]
  3. History of catastrophic shareholder dilution — market cap declined from ~$10B+ to $206M through acquisitions, impairments, and reverse splits; further dilution risk remains if GAAP losses persist [4]
