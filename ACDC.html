Investment Thesis
ProFrac's vertical integration into sand production and chemical services creates a differentiated cost structure in the fragmented frac services market. The Q4 2025 recovery with EBITDA up 49% sequentially shows operating leverage when activity rebounds. Haynesville basin wins and potential supply-demand tightening in 2026 could drive meaningful upside from depressed levels, with the stock trading well below book value.
ProFrac carries $1.03B in net debt against just $17M cash and generated only $25M in free cash flow in 2025 — a dangerously leveraged position for a cyclical business. Analysts have a consensus 'Sell' rating with a $6.13 average target near the current price. The company is forecast to remain unprofitable for the next 3 years, and the fossil fuel services sector faces long-term secular decline. Family control limits governance quality and shareholder rights.
