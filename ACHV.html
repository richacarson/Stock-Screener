Investment Thesis
Achieve is approaching a transformative FDA decision on June 20, 2026 for cytisinicline, which would be the first new smoking cessation drug in nearly 20 years. The Phase 3 data from 2,000+ participants shows significantly higher abstinence rates with a favorable safety profile. Breakthrough Therapy designation for vaping cessation expands the addressable market to the youth vaping epidemic. The plant-derived mechanism differentiates from synthetic alternatives and strong faith alignment adds social value.
This is an ultra-binary investment — FDA approval or rejection will determine the company's fate. With only 25 employees, zero revenue, and deeply negative ROE, Achieve lacks the infrastructure for commercial launch and will require a partner or significant capital raise. Even if approved, competition from generic varenicline and established nicotine replacement therapy creates commercial uncertainty. The $213M market cap reflects speculative premium that could evaporate on FDA rejection.
