Investment Thesis
ACIC trades at an exceptionally low P/E of 5.1x with a 38.6% ROE and 60.1% combined ratio that significantly outperforms its 65% target. Net income of $106.8M beat the $70-90M guidance range, and Q4 earnings surged 437%. The apartment program expansion to $100-200M in premiums by 2029 provides a growth runway. Florida Hurricane Catastrophe Fund access creates a structural cost advantage over non-admitted competitors.
Florida is the most catastrophe-exposed insurance market in the US, and a single major hurricane could cause significant losses despite reinsurance. The Florida commercial property market is softening with premiums down 9% since year-end and pricing declining 13% in Q3 2025. Revenue declined 23.5% YoY, partially due to reinsurance changes. The 68-person company is heavily dependent on a single geography and product line, creating concentration risk. The stock's $539M market cap and moderate liquidity limit institutional access.
