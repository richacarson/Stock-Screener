Investment Thesis
Arcellx is being acquired by Gilead for $115/share cash plus $5/share CVR, providing a clear floor valuation. Anito-cel's 97% response rate and 93.1% MRD-negative rate with a superior safety profile position it as a potential best-in-class CAR-T for multiple myeloma. The FDA PDUFA date of December 23, 2026 provides a clear regulatory timeline. The $7.8B valuation reflects Gilead's confidence in anito-cel's blockbuster potential against Carvykti's $1.8B run-rate.
The stock trades at $114.77 against the $115 base acquisition price, leaving minimal upside beyond the $5 CVR. The acquisition is expected to close Q2 2026 but faces regulatory approval risk. Pre-acquisition, the company is pre-commercial with negative ROE. If the deal breaks, the stock could decline significantly. The CVR is contingent on specific milestones that may not be achieved. For IOWN purposes, the merger arbitrage spread is thin and the stock offers limited upside from current levels.
