Company Profile
Technology ·Semiconductor Equipment & Materials ·NASDAQ ·United States
ACM Research develops, manufactures, and sells single-wafer wet cleaning equipment for the semiconductor industry. The company's products include the SAPS, TEBO, and Ultra C cleaning tools used in advanced semiconductor manufacturing for wafer cleaning, electrochemical plating, and advanced packaging. Principal operations are conducted through subsidiary ACM Research (Shanghai) in China.
2,513 Employees ·www.acmr.com

Investment Thesis
ACM Research is a high-growth semiconductor equipment company riding the dual tailwinds of China's domestic chip localization push and global AI-driven semiconductor capacity expansion. Revenue grew from $133M in 2020 to $901M in 2025 (46.6% 5yr CAGR), and management guided 2026 revenue of $1.08-$1.175B (21-30% growth) driven by new product launches (ACM Planetary furnace, Ultra C Tahoe) and production capacity expansion [1]. The balance sheet is pristine with D/E of just 15.4% and $1.16B cash against $298M debt, providing substantial financial flexibility [5]. At 24.6x forward P/E and ~1.09 PEG, the stock is reasonably valued for 20%+ revenue growth. Gross margin of 44.4% is healthy for semiconductor equipment, and founder-CEO Dr. Wang's 100+ patents and 26-year tenure provide deep technical leadership. The company's environmentally innovative products (75% chemical reduction, ~100% solvent recovery) create additional competitive differentiation [3].
The bear case centers on significant geopolitical risk and insider selling. ACM Research generates the majority of its revenue through its Shanghai subsidiary serving Chinese semiconductor fabs, creating concentrated exposure to U.S.-China trade tensions and export controls [1]. Escalating restrictions could limit the company's ability to serve advanced-node customers in China or restrict component sourcing from Western suppliers. Insider selling is a material concern — CEO Wang sold $5.15M, CFO McKechnie sold $4.49M, and Director Liu sold $2.85M in March 2026, totaling $12.5M in insider sales in a single month [4]. Trailing EPS declined 77.7% YoY to $1.37 due to one-time charges and margin pressure, and profit margin of 10.4% and ROE of 8.1% remain below target thresholds. Free cash flow is negative (-$112M) as the company invests in capacity expansion. Competition from re-entering Western incumbents (as geopolitical dynamics evolve) and emerging Chinese competitors could pressure ACM's domestic market share gains. The stock's 40.9x trailing P/E prices in significant growth that must materialize to justify current valuation.

AI Resilience LOW RISK
