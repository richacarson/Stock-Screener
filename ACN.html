Investment Thesis
Accenture is the world's largest IT services company trading at its cheapest forward P/E (13.4x) in years due to the 'AI Disruption Discount,' which has driven the stock nearly 50% below its 52-week high. Meanwhile, GenAI bookings tripled to $5.9B and the company generated $9.4B in FCF. The 3.26% dividend yield with a 13.65% 5-year growth CAGR provides attractive income. Q1 FY26 beat estimates on both revenue and EPS. The market is pricing Accenture as an AI loser when the data shows it is an AI winner — this disconnect creates a compelling value opportunity for patient investors.
The 50% stock decline reflects real concerns that AI will reduce the human hours needed for consulting engagements, potentially compressing revenue growth. Q2 FY26 revenue guidance of $17.35-18B was below analyst expectations, validating near-term growth concerns. The 786,000-person workforce creates massive operating leverage risk if project volumes decline. Revenue growth of 8.3% is modest for the current valuation, and the company's positioning as an AI integrator could face margin pressure as AI tools become more commoditized and enterprises build internal capabilities.
