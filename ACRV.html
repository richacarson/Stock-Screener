Company Profile
Healthcare ·Biotechnology ·NASDAQ ·United States
Acrivon Therapeutics is a clinical-stage biopharmaceutical company discovering and developing precision oncology medicines using its proprietary Acrivon Predictive Precision Proteomics (AP3) platform. The lead candidate ACR-368 targets CHK1/CHK2 for endometrial and ovarian cancers. Founded in 2018 and headquartered in Watertown, Massachusetts.

Investment Thesis
Acrivon Therapeutics offers a differentiated precision oncology platform with compelling clinical data. The AP3 proteomics platform — which uses machine learning to identify which patients' tumors will respond to specific drugs — produced a 52% confirmed overall response rate for ACR-368 in serous endometrial cancer, a strong signal in a difficult-to-treat population [1]. The company is preparing for Phase 3 readiness by mid-2026 and plans to initiate an all-comer registrational arm in H1 2026 [1]. The pipeline extends beyond ACR-368 to ACR-2316 (WEE1/PKMYT1) and preclinical programs, suggesting platform optionality. At $76M market cap, a successful Phase 3 would represent significant upside given that endometrial cancer alone represents a multi-billion-dollar market opportunity.
The bear case is severe and binary. Acrivon is a pre-revenue company burning $64M annually with $119M cash — roughly 18 months of runway — creating near-certainty of dilutive financing in 2027 if not sooner [4]. Clinical-stage biotechs have over 90% failure rates from Phase 2 to approval, and while 52% cORR is encouraging, Phase 3 outcomes are unpredictable. The $76M market cap means a failed Phase 3 trial would likely result in >80% share price destruction. The stock trades at $1.97 with forward EPS of -$1.86, reflecting deep losses with no revenue visibility until commercialization (earliest 2028-2029). The AP3 platform's competitive moat is unproven at commercial scale — larger pharma companies could replicate proteomics-based patient selection approaches with greater resources. Institutional interest in micro-cap biotech is limited, creating liquidity risk for investors [4].
Key Catalysts
  1. Phase 3 readiness for ACR-368 in combination with PD-1 therapy by mid-2026 — successful initiation would de-risk the program and potentially trigger partnership interest from large pharma [1]
  2. Registrational intent serous EC all-comer Arm 4 enrollment in US in H1 2026 — broader patient population data could significantly expand the addressable market [1]
  3. ACR-2316 clinical development milestones — advancement of the second pipeline asset would validate AP3 platform utility beyond ACR-368 [2]
Key Risks
  1. Binary clinical risk — Phase 3 failure for ACR-368 would likely destroy 80%+ of the company's value, with no commercial revenue to cushion the blow [1]
  2. Cash runway exhaustion — $119M cash against $64M annual burn implies dilutive financing by H2 2027, potentially at depressed micro-cap valuations [4]
  3. Micro-cap liquidity and institutional neglect — $76M market cap and $1.97 share price limit institutional ownership and create adverse price dynamics during any future capital raise [4]
