Investment Thesis
ACV is transitioning from a high-growth, loss-making marketplace to sustainable profitability, with 2026 guidance for $31-35M non-GAAP net income. Revenue of $760M growing 11-13% with adjusted EBITDA expected to grow 24-31% demonstrates improving unit economics. At 35% franchise rooftop penetration, significant market share remains untapped. ACV Capital (48% growth) and AI-driven products provide additional growth vectors.
The stock dropped 14.7% post-Q4 earnings as the market digested slowing growth guidance (11-13% vs. 19% in 2025). GAAP net losses of $66M show the company is still not truly profitable, and forward P/E of 14x on uncertain non-GAAP earnings is aggressive. The used car market is cyclical and competitive, investment in inspectors will pressure margins, and the 30% decline from 50-day SMA suggests serious momentum problems.
