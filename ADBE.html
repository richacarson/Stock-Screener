Investment Thesis
Adobe trades at a compelling 9.4x forward P/E for a company generating $26B ARR growing 11%, 59% ROE, and $23.50 non-GAAP EPS guidance. Firefly AI adoption (29B generations, 3x QoQ growth) positions Adobe as the leader in commercially-safe creative AI. The cloud subscription model provides predictable recurring revenue, and the company's 40-year track record of reinvention provides confidence in navigating the AI transition. At $248, the stock is 37% below its 2024 highs.
The 36.6% one-year stock decline reflects genuine concern that AI is democratizing creative tools, potentially compressing Adobe's seat-based pricing power. AI-native competitors (Midjourney, Canva, Runway) offer comparable capabilities at lower price points. Revenue growth has decelerated from 20%+ to 11%, and the Zacks Rank #3 (Hold) reflects moderate analyst sentiment. The company faces an identity crisis: is Firefly a feature enhancement or a new revenue stream? If AI tools commoditize creative workflows, Adobe's premium pricing comes under pressure.
