Company Profile

Adobe Inc. operates as a diversified software company worldwide through three segments: Creative & Marketing Professionals (Photoshop, Illustrator, Premiere Pro, After Effects, Lightroom, Creative Cloud), Business Professionals & Consumers (Acrobat, Adobe Sign, Adobe Express), and Document Cloud. Investment Thesis
Adobe delivered record Q2 2026 revenue of $6.62B (up 13%) with non-GAAP EPS of $5.96, and raised full-year guidance to $20.5-20.6B revenue and $24.35-24.45 non-GAAP EPS [1]. At $195 per share, the stock trades at just 7.53x forward non-GAAP earnings and 0.55x PEG — the cheapest valuation in Adobe's history as a public company, reflecting a 49% decline from 52-week highs. Despite the stock collapse, fundamentals are improving: ARR reached $27.10B (up 12.5%), remaining performance obligations climbed to $22.27B, operating cash flow grew 10% to $5.12B in H1 2026, and the Semrush acquisition creates a content-to-marketing analytics pipeline [1]. Firefly AI with 100+ creative skills and agentic capabilities positions Adobe to lead the AI transformation of creative workflows. ROE of 62.95%, profit margins of 28.69%, and D/E of 61% demonstrate a financially elite software franchise trading at deep-value multiples.
The bear case centers on the existential question: can Adobe defend its creative software monopoly against AI-native competitors? The 49% stock decline reflects genuine investor fear that Canva, Midjourney, DALL-E, and other AI tools will commoditize creative production, enabling non-professionals to produce work that previously required Adobe expertise and expensive subscriptions [2]. CEO Shantanu Narayen's March 2026 departure announcement after 18 years introduces leadership uncertainty at the most critical moment since the subscription transition — the successor must navigate AI disruption without the institutional knowledge and credibility Narayen brought. The failed $20B Figma acquisition (2023) left Adobe without its most important competitive moat acquisition, and the $1.87B Semrush deal is a much smaller strategic bet. Adobe's premium pricing ($55/month for Creative Cloud) is increasingly challenged by free or low-cost AI alternatives that deliver 80% of the functionality for casual users.
Key Catalysts
  1. Firefly AI Assistant with agentic capabilities driving higher Creative Cloud ARPU as AI features become premium tier differentiators, with subscription revenue already growing 13% in Q2 2026
  2. FY2026 raised guidance to $20.5-20.6B revenue and $24.35-24.45 non-GAAP EPS, with forward P/E of 7.53 creating significant multiple expansion potential if AI monetization concerns abate
  3. Semrush acquisition integration creating a unified content creation-to-marketing analytics platform, opening new enterprise cross-sell opportunities across Adobe's $27.10B ARR base
Key Risks
  1. AI-native creative tools (Canva AI, Midjourney, DALL-E, Runway) threaten to commoditize creative production, potentially compressing Adobe's pricing power and TAM as non-professional users shift to cheaper AI alternatives
  2. CEO Shantanu Narayen's departure after 18 years introduces leadership uncertainty during Adobe's most critical strategic transition — the unnamed successor must execute the AI pivot without institutional credibility
  3. 49% stock decline reflects structural investor concern about Adobe's long-term competitive position in an AI-disrupted creative market, and recovery requires demonstrating Firefly AI monetization at scale — a thesis the market has been unwilling to buy
