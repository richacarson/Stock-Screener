Company Profile
Real Estate ·REIT - Retail ·NYSE ·United States
Agree Realty Corporation is a publicly traded net lease REIT that acquires and develops properties net leased to industry-leading, omni-channel retail tenants. As of December 31, 2025, the Company owned and operated a portfolio of 2,674 properties located in all 50 states containing approximately 55.5 million square feet of gross leasable area.

Investment Thesis
Agree Realty is a best-in-class net lease REIT with a 4.2% dividend yield, 13 consecutive years of dividend growth, and exceptional portfolio quality — 99.7% occupancy across 2,674 properties in all 50 states leased to omni-channel, necessity-based retail tenants [1]. Q1 2026 results beat expectations with revenue of $200.8M (+18.7% YoY), AFFO per share up 7.9%, and net income rising 33.4% to $60.2M [1]. The company's $2.3B liquidity position enables continued accretive acquisitions, and AFFO guidance of $4.54-$4.58/share implies 5.4% growth for FY2026. D/E of 59.6% provides balance sheet safety rare among REITs.
The bear case centers on valuation and structural REIT challenges: at 38.4x forward P/E (17x AFFO is more appropriate for REITs), Agree Realty is richly valued relative to net asset value, and rising interest rates compress the spread between acquisition cap rates and the cost of capital [6]. The 168% GAAP payout ratio (though covered by AFFO) leaves limited margin if property-level cash flows deteriorate. Glassdoor reviews describing 'toxic culture,' 'nepotism,' and below-market pay suggest organizational risk in a 90-person company where employee quality is critical to acquisition underwriting [4]. Revenue growth of 18.7% is driven by share issuance-funded acquisitions rather than organic same-store growth, diluting per-share economics. Concentration in retail real estate creates sector-specific risk if consumer spending declines.
Key Catalysts
  1. Continued accretive property acquisitions funded by $2.3B liquidity position, with 2026 AFFO guidance of $4.54-$4.58/share implying 5.4% growth at the midpoint
  2. Omni-channel retail thesis validation as necessity-based tenants (grocery, convenience, auto parts) prove resilient to both e-commerce disruption and economic cycles
  3. Potential inclusion in larger REIT indices as the portfolio scales, driving passive fund inflows and multiple expansion from the current $9.2B market cap
Key Risks
  1. Rich valuation at 38.4x forward P/E (17x AFFO) creates downside risk if interest rates rise further or acquisition cap rates compress, reducing the spread that drives accretive growth
  2. Organizational culture concerns (Glassdoor: 'toxic culture,' 'nepotism,' below-market pay) in a 90-person company where underwriting talent retention is critical to portfolio quality
  3. Equity issuance-driven growth diluting per-share economics — revenue growth of 18.7% significantly exceeds AFFO/share growth of 5.4%, reflecting the cost of share-funded acquisitions
