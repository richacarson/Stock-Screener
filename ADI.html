Company Profile
Technology ·Semiconductors ·NASDAQ ·United States
Analog Devices, Inc. designs, manufactures, tests, and markets integrated circuits (ICs), software, and subsystems for signal processing, data conversion, power management, and digital signal processing. The company serves industrial, automotive, communications, and consumer end markets. Founded in 1965 and headquartered in Wilmington, Massachusetts, ADI is the global leader in high-performance analog, mixed-signal, and digital signal processing semiconductors, bridging the physical and digital worlds.

Investment Thesis
Analog Devices delivered a record Q2 FY2026 with $3.62B revenue (up 37% YoY), adjusted EPS of $3.09 (up 67% YoY), and adjusted gross margin of 73.0% [1]. The company is riding multiple secular tailwinds: AI data center demand (segment revenue up 90%), automotive electrification, industrial automation, and 5G communications infrastructure. Q3 guidance of $3.9B implies an annualized run rate approaching $16B, with adjusted operating margin expanding to ~49% [1]. The Empower Semiconductor acquisition positions ADI for the next wave of AI power delivery demand [1][3]. With a conservative balance sheet (D/E of 26%), a 22-year dividend payment streak with 21 consecutive years of growth recently extended with an 11.1% increase, and category leadership in high-performance analog semiconductors, ADI combines growth momentum with income reliability. The 5yr revenue CAGR of 15.17% and current 29.75% YoY growth demonstrate the structural demand expansion for analog components in an increasingly digital world [5].
The primary concern is valuation relative to normalized earnings. At 58.33x trailing P/E and 28.30x forward P/E, ADI is priced for continued exceptional growth — any deceleration in AI data center demand or broader semiconductor cycle softening could trigger meaningful multiple compression [5]. ROE of just 6.57% reflects the goodwill-heavy balance sheet from the $21B Maxim acquisition, and while improving as integration synergies materialize, it remains well below analog industry norms. The payout ratio of 61.65% is sustainable but leaves limited room for aggressive dividend increases if earnings growth slows. ADI's cyclical exposure to industrial and automotive end markets (which together represent the majority of revenue) creates vulnerability to macroeconomic slowdowns — the 2023-2024 downturn saw significant revenue contraction before the current recovery. Concentration risk in AI data center growth (90% segment increase) could create tough year-over-year comparisons in future quarters if hyperscaler capital spending normalizes [1][2].
Key Catalysts
  1. AI data center demand acceleration with data center revenue up 90% YoY and the Empower Semiconductor acquisition closing H2 FY2026 expanding ADI's addressable market in AI power delivery [1][3]
  2. Q3 FY2026 guidance of $3.9B ±$100M with ~49% adjusted operating margin implies continued revenue acceleration and margin expansion as operating leverage takes hold [1]
  3. Automotive electrification and industrial automation secular growth driving sustained demand for ADI's high-performance analog, power management, and battery management system ICs across multiple end markets [2][5]
Key Risks
  1. Forward P/E of 28.30x and trailing P/E of 58.33x reflect a premium valuation that requires sustained above-trend growth — any normalization in AI data center capital spending or semiconductor cycle deceleration could trigger significant multiple compression [5]
  2. ROE of 6.57% remains depressed by Maxim acquisition goodwill, and while improving, signals that the $21B deal has not yet delivered returns commensurate with its cost — full integration synergies are still being realized [1][2]
  3. Cyclical exposure to industrial and automotive end markets (majority of revenue) creates vulnerability to macroeconomic slowdowns, as demonstrated by the 2023-2024 revenue contraction that preceded the current recovery [5]
