Investment Thesis
ADP is a best-in-class human capital management platform with an unassailable data moat — 42 million wage earners across 1.1 million clients in 140 countries. Q2 FY2026 delivered 6% revenue growth to $5.4B and 11% adjusted EPS growth to $2.62, both beating estimates. The ADP Assist generative AI suite transforms ADP from a payroll processor into an intelligent workforce advisor, leveraging its data scale advantage. CEO Maria Black's #1 CEO ranking and 19 consecutive years as a Fortune Most Admired Company signal exceptional leadership quality. The 3.3% yield with 51 consecutive years of dividend increases and 12% 5-year CAGR makes ADP a premier compounding dividend machine, with a +51 Inspire score providing faith-alignment appeal.
The bear case is valuation-driven. ADP stock has declined 27.5% over the past year as investors rotated toward AI-native companies, questioning whether ADP can sustain its premium in a world where Workday and Paylocity offer cloud-native alternatives. Revenue growth of 6% is pedestrian for a tech company commanding a 20x P/E, and the 60.7% payout ratio is elevated relative to target, potentially limiting future dividend acceleration. The PEO segment's 5-7% growth outlook includes zero-margin pass-throughs that inflate revenue without improving profitability. Employment market softening — ADP's own data shows cooling job creation — could slow new business bookings and pressure ES segment growth below guidance.

Key Catalysts
  1. ADP Assist agentic AI adoption driving higher ARPC (average revenue per client) as existing clients upgrade to AI-powered payroll and HR capabilities, with monetization beginning in FY2026
  2. Employer Services new business bookings hitting record Q1 volumes, suggesting market share gains despite macro headwinds — full-year guidance of 4-7% bookings growth provides upside optionality
  3. Margin expansion of 50-70 bps guided for FY2026 as AI automation reduces service delivery costs while enabling premium pricing on ADP Assist features
Key Risks
  1. Employment market deterioration — ADP's revenue is directly tied to payroll volumes, and any meaningful recession-driven reduction in the employed workforce would compress both ES and PEO segment revenues simultaneously
  2. AI-native HCM competition — Workday, Rippling, and Deel are building AI-first platforms without ADP's legacy architecture constraints, and could capture disproportionate share of new small business formation
  3. Multiple compression continuation — if investors continue to favor high-growth AI stocks, ADP's 6% revenue growth profile may not support re-rating back to its historical 30x+ P/E, leaving the stock range-bound despite strong fundamentals
