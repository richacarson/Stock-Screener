Investment Thesis
ADP is an essential employment infrastructure business — the company pays 42 million workers across 140 countries and serves 1.1M+ clients, processing approximately 25% of US payrolls [1][2]. Q2 FY2026 delivered 6% revenue growth with 11% adjusted EPS growth and 80bps of margin expansion, while full-year guidance was raised to ~6% revenue growth and 9-10% adjusted EPS growth [1]. At 16.9x forward P/E with 73.8% ROE, the stock is attractively valued for a Dividend King with 50 consecutive years of dividend growth, a 3.35% yield, and 11.5% five-year dividend growth CAGR [4]. ADP's regulatory compliance moat (tax codes across 140 countries), massive data advantages, and high switching costs create durable competitive positioning. The Responsible AI framework and new AI agents in ADP Marketplace demonstrate the company is proactively embracing AI rather than being disrupted by it [2]. The $6B buyback authorization and margin expansion trajectory provide additional shareholder return upside.
The bear case centers squarely on AI disruption risk. Payroll processing, HR administration, and compliance management are rule-based, data-intensive workflows that AI is increasingly capable of automating — Seeking Alpha analysis has explicitly warned investors not to underestimate AI's threat to ADP's business model [2]. The PEO segment faces particular pressure, with margin compression and slower growth suggesting AI-driven HR platforms may reduce demand for outsourced services. Revenue growth of 6.2% YoY is decelerating from the post-pandemic acceleration, and U.S. pays per control is projected flat for FY2026, indicating limited organic employment growth [1]. The 60.7% payout ratio, while sustainable, limits financial flexibility for aggressive AI investment. Insider selling has been noted in analyst coverage, suggesting leadership may be reducing personal exposure [2]. At $82B market cap, any acceleration in AI-driven competitive displacement of ADP's core payroll and HR functions could compress the multiple and threaten the dividend growth trajectory. Client revenue retention showed a slight decline in Q2, a concerning leading indicator for a business built on recurring relationships [1].
