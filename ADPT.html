Company Profile
Healthcare ·Biotechnology ·NASDAQ ·United States
Adaptive Biotechnologies develops an immune medicine platform combining proprietary chemistry, computational biology, and machine learning to decode the adaptive immune system. Its primary commercial product, clonoSEQ, is the first FDA-cleared assay for Minimal Residual Disease (MRD) detection in blood cancers. The company also operates life sciences research and drug discovery businesses, leveraging its immunosequencing platform and partnerships with Microsoft and Genentech.
624 Employees ·www.adaptivebiotech.com

Investment Thesis
Adaptive Biotechnologies holds a monopoly position in FDA-cleared MRD testing with clonoSEQ, the only approved assay for detecting residual cancer cells in blood cancer patients. Full-year 2025 revenue grew 55% to $277M with 74.2% gross margins, and management projects 22-30% MRD revenue growth in 2026 with 30%+ test volume increases [1]. The company expects to reach positive adjusted EBITDA and free cash flow by end of 2026, marking a critical inflection point for this platform business. The Microsoft partnership for AI-powered immune system decoding and the broader immunoSEQ Dx platform represent significant optionality beyond MRD — a universal blood test for disease detection would be transformative [2]. At $2.2B market cap with $277M revenue growing 20%+, the valuation is reasonable for a clinical monopoly approaching profitability.
The bear case centers on profitability risk and competitive dynamics. Despite 55% revenue growth, Adaptive still posted a -21.5% profit margin with -$46M operating cash flow in 2025 [4]. The path to profitability requires operating expense discipline ($350-360M in 2026) while sustaining revenue growth — a narrow margin for error. Genentech exited its drug discovery partnership, removing a revenue source and validating concerns about Adaptive's drug development optionality [3]. The clonoSEQ monopoly could face competition from next-generation sequencing competitors (Invivoscribe, Hematologics) or liquid biopsy companies (Guardant Health, Foundation Medicine) entering the MRD space. Beta of 2.3 reflects high stock volatility, and the $14.47 share price is well below the $20.76 52-week high. The company's single-product dependence on clonoSEQ creates concentration risk if adoption plateaus or reimbursement rates compress [1].
Key Catalysts
  1. Expected inflection to positive adjusted EBITDA and free cash flow by end of 2026, validating the commercial model and potentially triggering multiple re-rating [1]
  2. clonoSEQ test volume growth projected at 30%+ in 2026, with expansion into additional cancer indications and growing clinical standard-of-care adoption [1]
  3. Microsoft AI partnership for universal immune system-based blood test could open a massive TAM beyond oncology into autoimmune and infectious disease detection [2]
Key Risks
  1. Persistent unprofitability with -21.5% profit margin and -$46M operating cash flow despite strong revenue growth — profitability path requires tight expense management [4]
  2. Genentech partnership exit removed drug discovery revenue and signals challenges in expanding beyond diagnostics into therapeutics [3]
  3. Single-product concentration on clonoSEQ MRD testing creates vulnerability to emerging competition from next-gen sequencing and liquid biopsy companies [1]
