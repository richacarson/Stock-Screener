Adaptive Biotechnologies is a commercial-stage biotechnology company that harnesses the power of the adaptive immune system to develop products for disease diagnostics and therapeutics. Its flagship product clonoSEQ is the first FDA-cleared minimal residual disease (MRD) test, and the company is developing T-cell receptor (TCR) based immune medicine platforms.

Investment Thesis
Adaptive Biotechnologies offers transformative potential as the only FDA-cleared MRD diagnostic provider with 55% revenue growth and rapidly narrowing losses. The Pfizer partnerships (potentially $890M in milestones) validate the immune medicine platform, while MRD revenue of $212M with segment-level profitability demonstrates a viable commercial foundation. The path to company-wide profitability appears achievable with 2026 MRD guidance of $255-$265M.
The stock faces significant risks with continued GAAP losses, an elevated 9.9x P/S ratio (3x industry average), and dependency on the MRD business scaling to cover immune medicine investment costs. The Genentech agreement revenue ($41.3M) was a one-time recognition that inflated 2025 results. Competition in MRD testing is emerging, and the therapeutic pipeline remains early-stage with uncertain commercial outcomes.
