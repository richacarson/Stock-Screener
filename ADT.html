Company Profile
Industrials ·Security & Protection Services ·NYSE ·United States
ADT Inc. provides security, interactive, and smart home solutions to residential and small business customers in the United States. The company offers burglar and life safety alarms, smart security cameras, smart home automation, video surveillance, and personal emergency response systems. ADT serves over 6 million customers through its monitoring centers, field service network, and the ADT+ technology platform developed in partnership with Google. Founded in 1874, headquartered in Boca Raton, Florida.
12,200 Employees ·www.adt.com

Investment Thesis
ADT is the dominant U.S. home security provider with over 6 million customers, generating $5.1B in revenue with 80.8% gross margins and nearly $1B in annual free cash flow [1]. The company trades at just 6.7x forward earnings — a deep value multiple for a business with high recurring revenue, strong brand moats, and a 3.27% dividend yield. The Google partnership is transforming ADT from a legacy alarm company into a smart home platform, and management's multi-year framework targets 5% revenue CAGR, 10% EPS CAGR, and 20% adjusted FCF growth in 2026 [1]. Q1 2026 results showed continued execution with $1.3B revenue (beating consensus) and EPS of $0.23 (beating estimates by 9.5%), and the 152-year brand history provides durable competitive advantage in a market where trust matters enormously.
The primary risk is ADT's leveraged balance sheet — $7.8B in total debt from its Apollo Global Management buyout history yields a D/E ratio of 204.4%, limiting financial flexibility and consuming significant cash flow in interest payments [1]. Revenue growth of just 0.9% YoY and flat 2026 guidance suggest the mature security market offers limited organic expansion. The dividend was cut from $0.84 to $0.14 during the pandemic, and at $0.22 today remains well below pre-cut levels, raising questions about management's commitment to income investors. DIY security competitors (Ring, SimpliSafe) continue to pressure the professionally-installed market with lower-cost AI-powered alternatives, and the stock's decline to $6.48 reflects market skepticism about whether the Google partnership can reignite meaningful growth against these structural headwinds [2].
Key Catalysts
  1. ADT+ platform adoption driving higher ARPU through premium smart home features and Google-powered camera analytics, targeting 5% revenue CAGR over the multi-year framework
  2. Adjusted free cash flow growth of approximately 20% in 2026 enabling accelerated debt repayment and potential dividend increases that could rerate the equity from current deep-value levels
  3. Continued Google partnership evolution delivering differentiated AI-powered security and home automation features that widen the competitive moat against DIY alternatives
Key Risks
  1. Heavy debt burden at 204.4% D/E ($7.8B total debt) constrains financial flexibility, consumes cash flow in interest payments, and creates vulnerability to interest rate increases
  2. Flat revenue and EPS guidance for 2026 in a mature security market suggests limited organic growth potential, with DIY competitors like Ring and SimpliSafe continuing to pressure the professionally-installed segment
  3. Dividend history includes a dramatic cut from $0.84 to $0.14 in 2020, and the current $0.22 annual payout remains well below pre-pandemic levels — only 2 consecutive years of growth undermines the income investment thesis
