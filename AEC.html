Investment Thesis
Anfield is uniquely positioned in the U.S. uranium sector with one of only three licensed conventional mills (Shootaring Canyon), two mines advancing toward production in 2026, and strong macro tailwinds from AI-driven electricity demand and the U.S. nuclear renaissance. Uranium's designation as a critical mineral could unlock federal funding and accelerated permitting.
The company has zero revenue, no feasibility studies, and development decisions are not backed by economic viability assessments. The stock has declined 26% from its 50-day SMA, and debt-to-equity of 22.4% with negative ROE suggests financial strain. Production timelines targeting H2 2026 face permitting and construction risks, and uranium price volatility could undermine the entire business case.
