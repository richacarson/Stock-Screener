Company Profile
Utilities ·Utilities - Regulated Electric ·NYSE ·United States
Ameren Corporation operates as a regulated electric and gas utility holding company serving 2.4 million customers across Missouri and Illinois. The company engages in rate-regulated electric generation, transmission, and distribution, as well as natural gas transmission and distribution, with a generation mix including coal, nuclear, natural gas, and growing renewable energy assets.

Investment Thesis
Ameren is a well-managed regulated utility delivering predictable earnings growth underpinned by a massive $31.8B infrastructure capital plan driving 10.6% rate base CAGR through 2030 [1]. FY2025 revenue grew 15.4% to $8.80B, EPS guidance for 2026 is $5.25-$5.45 with 6-8% long-term CAGR expected through 2030, and the dividend has grown 12 consecutive years at a 7.3% 5yr CAGR with a manageable 53% payout ratio [1][5]. At 19.0x forward P/E, the stock is reasonably valued for a utility with above-peer growth. The AI-driven data center boom provides a secular demand tailwind for electricity, positioning Ameren to benefit from load growth in its regulated territory. Inspire Impact score of +43 (ALIGNED) with zero negative attributions makes this a clean faith-aligned holding.
The bear case centers on execution risk and balance sheet leverage. D/E of 147% reflects the capital-intensive nature of the $31.8B build-out, and negative free cash flow of -$1.5B indicates Ameren is spending far more than it earns — requiring continuous access to capital markets through equity issuances that dilute existing shareholders [5]. Regulatory risk is ever-present: if Missouri or Illinois commissions deny rate recovery on infrastructure investments, returns could compress. Revenue growth has been lumpy (quarterly decline of -8.8% in the most recent quarter) and the 3.4% 5yr CAGR is modest [5]. The 3.4/5 Glassdoor rating and reports of a hierarchical, change-resistant culture raise questions about whether Ameren can execute its ambitious transformation from coal to renewables effectively [2]. Data center load growth may ramp slower than expected, leaving the company investing ahead of demand.
Key Catalysts
  1. $31.8B infrastructure capital plan driving 10.6% rate base CAGR through 2030, with 6-8% EPS growth guidance providing clear earnings visibility [1]
  2. AI data center demand creating incremental electricity load growth in Ameren's regulated service territory, converting AI capex into recurring utility revenue [2]
  3. Dividend growth trajectory — 12 consecutive years of increases with 7.3% 5yr CAGR and a sustainable 53% payout ratio, appealing to income-oriented investors [5]
Key Risks
  1. Regulatory risk — Missouri and Illinois commission decisions on rate recovery for the $31.8B capital plan could compress returns if cost recovery is denied or delayed [1]
  2. Balance sheet leverage at 147% D/E with negative free cash flow requires continuous capital market access; rising interest rates or credit tightening could increase funding costs [5]
  3. Data center demand uncertainty — if AI-driven electricity load growth materializes slower than projected, Ameren could be investing billions ahead of confirmed demand [2]
