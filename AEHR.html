Company Profile
Technology ·Semiconductor Equipment & Materials ·NASDAQ ·United States
Aehr Test Systems provides semiconductor test and burn-in solutions for wafer-level, singulated die, and packaged part applications. The company's FOX-XP, FOX-NP, and Sonoma systems serve customers testing silicon carbide power devices for EVs, AI accelerator processors, gallium nitride power semiconductors, silicon photonics, and data storage devices. Aehr is the only provider of wafer-level turnkey test and burn-in solutions for AI processors.
136 Employees ·www.aehr.com

Investment Thesis
Aehr Test Systems is the only provider of wafer-level turnkey test and burn-in solutions for AI processors, positioning it at the critical bottleneck of AI semiconductor reliability. Second-half FY2026 bookings exceeded $92M — far surpassing the $60-$80M guided range — anchored by a record $41M production order from a lead hyperscale AI customer [1]. The pivot from SiC/EV concentration to multi-vertical AI infrastructure (AI processors, silicon photonics, GaN, flash memory) diversifies the revenue base and opens a multi-year growth runway as AI data center buildout accelerates. The PEG ratio of 0.9 signals analysts expect dramatic earnings recovery, and management anticipates returning to non-GAAP profitability in Q4 FY2026 with significant FY2027 revenue growth visibility [1][3]. A pristine balance sheet ($37M cash, $10M debt, D/E of 7.2%) provides zero financial risk during the transition [5].
The bear case centers on extreme valuation disconnect, current losses, and execution risk. At $95.91 (up ~12x from the $8.02 52-week low), AEHR trades at 639x forward earnings on just $0.15 in projected EPS — virtually all future growth is priced in with no margin of safety [5]. Current-year revenue of $45-50M is declining 43.7% YoY with -25.2% profit margins, meaning the company is actively losing money while the stock prices in a massive recovery [5]. The $92M booking surge must convert to recognized revenue and then to profits, a process that involves manufacturing execution, supply chain management, and customer acceptance risk for a 136-person company. Customer concentration in hyperscale AI creates binary risk — a single customer delay or cancellation could materially impair guidance. Significant insider selling in April 2026 ($4.2M+ in director sales) signals that insiders view current prices as an opportunity to take profits [4]. The semiconductor equipment industry is cyclical, and Aehr's history of revenue volatility (from $80M peak to $45M trough) suggests the current AI boom could reverse.
