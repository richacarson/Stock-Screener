Company Profile
Healthcare ·Medical Devices ·NASDAQ ·United States
Aethlon Medical is a clinical-stage medical device company developing the Hemopurifier, an extracorporeal affinity device designed to remove circulating viruses and immunosuppressive exosomes from the bloodstream. The device has FDA Breakthrough Device Designation for cancer and life-threatening viruses.

Investment Thesis
The Hemopurifier is a scientifically compelling technology with FDA Breakthrough Device Designation addressing a massive unmet need — two-thirds of cancer patients fail immunotherapy. Preclinical data is encouraging (98.5% EV removal, Long COVID binding), the oncology trial Cohort 1 showed no safety concerns, and the 'pipeline within a device' concept could address cancer, Long COVID, transplant, autoimmune diseases, and sepsis. At a $2.8M market cap, any positive clinical readout could generate 10x+ returns.
After 20+ years of development, the Hemopurifier has not reached commercialization. The $2.8M market cap and $137K daily trading volume make this stock effectively uninvestable at any meaningful scale. ROE of -208.6% signals severe financial distress, and the company has no revenue and no clear timeline to FDA approval. Competing against major pharmaceutical companies with billions in resources, Aethlon's path to market is extremely uncertain. Dilutive financing is virtually guaranteed given the capital needs for ongoing clinical trials.
