Company Profile
Utilities ·Utilities - Regulated Electric ·NASDAQ ·United States
American Electric Power is one of the largest electric utilities in the United States, serving approximately 5.6 million customers across 11 states through regulated subsidiaries. AEP owns and operates approximately 40,000 miles of transmission lines — the largest in the US — and generates, transmits, and distributes electricity from a diversified generation fleet including coal, natural gas, nuclear, wind, and solar.

Investment Thesis
AEP is a premier regulated electric utility positioned at the intersection of two secular tailwinds: the energy transition and AI-driven data center demand. Full-year 2025 GAAP EPS of $6.70 (up 19.6% YoY) on revenue of $21.88B (up 10.9%) demonstrates the earnings power of a growing rate base, while 2026 operating EPS guidance of $6.15-$6.45 and a long-term 7-9% EPS growth rate provide multi-year visibility [1]. The $72 billion five-year capital plan driving 10% annual rate base growth is the largest in AEP's history, funded by constructive regulatory relationships across 11 states. The 2.86% dividend yield with 14+ consecutive years of growth and a manageable 56% payout ratio makes AEP a reliable income compounder [4]. AEP's 40,000-mile transmission network — the largest in the US — is irreplaceable infrastructure that benefits directly from AI data center electricity demand.
The bear case centers on execution risk and capital market dependency. The $72 billion capital plan requires sustained access to debt and equity markets at reasonable rates — rising interest rates would compress AEP's regulated return spreads and increase financing costs on a balance sheet already carrying 154% D/E [4]. Regulatory risk is ever-present: AEP operates across 11 states with varying political and regulatory environments, and any adverse rate case outcomes could impair return on invested capital. The coal transition creates stranded asset risk — 42% of generation capacity remains coal-fired, and accelerated retirement timelines could trigger write-downs [3]. Earnings growth of 7-9% may not compensate for the forward P/E of 19.3x if interest rates remain elevated, as utility valuations are inversely correlated with treasury yields. Additionally, AEP's historical environmental controversies, including the $4.6B Clean Air Act settlement, demonstrate the ongoing regulatory and reputational costs of operating legacy fossil fuel infrastructure [3].
Key Catalysts
  1. AI data center demand driving unprecedented electricity load growth across AEP's 11-state service territory, with new large load interconnection requests accelerating rate base growth beyond historical trends [1][2]
  2. $72 billion five-year capital plan driving 10% annual rate base growth and supporting 7-9% long-term EPS growth guidance, the largest investment program in AEP's history [1]
  3. Energy transition investments in renewable generation and grid modernization creating long-duration regulated assets with constructive return profiles and potential for premium rate recovery [1]
Key Risks
  1. Rising interest rates compressing utility valuations and increasing financing costs on $72B capital plan, with D/E already at 154% creating sensitivity to credit market conditions [4]
  2. Adverse regulatory outcomes across any of AEP's 11-state jurisdictions could impair returns on the massive capital deployment program and reduce earned ROE below authorized levels
  3. Coal stranded asset risk — 42% of generation capacity remains coal-fired, and accelerated regulatory or economic retirement timelines could trigger material write-downs and earnings charges [3]
