AerCap Holdings N.V. is the world's largest aircraft leasing company, managing a portfolio of approximately 3,500 owned, managed, or on-order aircraft, engines, and helicopters. Investment Thesis
AerCap is the world's largest aircraft lessor trading at a deeply discounted 8.4x forward earnings and 0.8x PEG ratio despite generating record 2025 GAAP net income of $3.8B ($21.30/share) and 21.1% ROE [1][4]. The company manages approximately 3,500 aircraft representing critical aviation infrastructure that airlines cannot operate without, creating a durable competitive moat with structural immunity from AI disruption. The 44% profit margin reflects the capital-efficient leasing model, and $60B invested in new technology aircraft since 2015 positions AerCap for decades of fleet management revenue. CEO Aengus Kelly's 15-year tenure and the lean 668-person team managing this portfolio demonstrate exceptional operational efficiency. Named a 2026 Industry ESG Leader by Sustainalytics with 'AA' MSCI rating [3].
The bear case is anchored in AerCap's 238% debt-to-equity ratio, which is structural for aircraft leasing but creates significant vulnerability to interest rate increases and credit market disruptions — the company guided $2.0B in interest expense for 2026 [1]. Conservative 2026 EPS guidance of $12-13 disappointed analysts expecting $14.76, signaling near-term headwinds from Spirit Airlines restructuring, maintenance timing, and lower other income [1]. The aviation industry remains cyclical and exposed to geopolitical disruption (war, pandemics, sanctions), fuel price spikes, and airline bankruptcies that could impair lease collections and aircraft values. The Russia-Ukraine conflict already required significant fleet write-downs. Additionally, the stock's 1.17% dividend yield and 3-year dividend history offer minimal income protection for long-term holders.
Key Catalysts
  1. Resolution of Spirit Airlines restructuring and normalization of maintenance timing could unlock the earnings bridge from 2025's $15.37 adjusted EPS to consensus estimates above $14 for 2026
  2. Continued new technology fleet investment targeting ~85% composition, reducing operating costs for lessees and supporting premium lease rates on newer aircraft
  3. Expansion of shareholder returns through the $4B+ share repurchase program and growing dividend (increased from $0.27 to $0.40 quarterly) as cash generation remains robust
Key Risks
  1. Rising interest rates could compress net interest margins on the $238% leveraged balance sheet, with $2.0B guided interest expense representing a significant earnings sensitivity
  2. Geopolitical disruption (conflict, sanctions, pandemic) could impair aircraft values and lease collections — the Russia-Ukraine precedent demonstrated material write-down risk
  3. Airline industry cyclicality including potential recession-driven traffic decline or fuel price spikes could trigger lessee defaults and aircraft oversupply, pressuring lease rates

AI Resilience (25%)
AI Resilience LOW RISK
9
AI Risk Protection (9/9 — IMMUNE): AerCap's business of owning, leasing, and managing physical aircraft is fundamentally immune to AI disruption. Commercial aviation requires physical aircraft that must be manufactured, maintained, and operated according to stringent regulatory standards — AI cannot substitute for physical planes carrying passengers and cargo [1]. AerCap sits at the nexus of aviation infrastructure: airlines need aircraft to operate, aircraft need financing to be acquired, and the global fleet requires continuous management. If anything, AI acceleration increases demand for air travel (business development, data center construction, supply chain complexity) and thereby aircraft [3]. The aviation leasing industry's $60B+ asset base, deep regulatory moats (FAA, EASA), and multi-decade aircraft useful lives create structural immunity from technological disruption. AI Opportunity (0/1): AerCap does not generate AI-related revenue; the company's value creation is entirely through aircraft asset management, leasing, and capital allocation.
