Company Profile
Energy ·Oil & Gas Equipment & Services ·NYSE ·United States
Atlas Energy Solutions operates 12 proppant production facilities across the Permian Basin with 28 million tons of annual frac sand capacity and the Dune Express conveyor logistics system. Founded in 2017 by Bud Brigham, the company provides frac sand, logistics, and distributed power services to oil and gas producers in West Texas.

Investment Thesis
Atlas Energy Solutions is the largest Permian Basin frac sand producer pivoting into distributed power for AI data centers and oil field applications. The Dune Express conveyor system targets 10M+ tons in 2026, and power contributions are explicitly called out as a growth vector in 2026 guidance [1]. Founder Bud Brigham's track record of building and selling exploration businesses across multiple cycles provides operator credibility. The suspended dividend frees capital for high-return power investments at a moment when AI infrastructure demand for on-site generation is accelerating. 5-year revenue CAGR of ~40% reflects rapid post-IPO scale-up, and D/E of 48% provides flexibility.
The bear case centers on commodity pricing, governance, and capital structure. Q1 2026 EBITDA guidance was revised to $26-30M due to maintenance costs, Q4 2025 revenue fell 8.1% YoY, and the full-year 2025 net loss was $50.3M with a -4.6% net margin [1][3]. The 2024 Up-C to C-corp reorganization is being challenged in court for allegedly transferring tax benefits to founder Bud Brigham and insiders without compensating minority shareholders — a meaningful governance red flag [3]. The $450M senior notes offering and $390M convertible private placement in April 2026 add leverage during a pricing trough [3]. Frac sand is commoditizing and the power business is still early-stage with minimal revenue contribution.
Key Catalysts
  1. Power business monetization — AI data center demand for distributed generation could materially expand Atlas's power revenue contribution in 2026-2027 [1]
  2. Dune Express volume ramp to 10M+ tons in 2026, improving logistics margins and capturing share as oil and gas producers consolidate around lowest-cost suppliers [1]
  3. Frac sand pricing recovery if oil and gas capex accelerates in H2 2026 — Atlas is positioned to capture operating leverage given the 28M ton capacity [1]
Key Risks
  1. Up-C to C-corp restructuring lawsuit — adverse legal outcome could result in material payouts to minority shareholders and governance overhang [3]
  2. Frac sand pricing pressure — Q4 2025 revenue down 8.1% YoY with margin compression, 2026 first-half expected to remain depressed before volume recovery [1]
  3. Leverage expansion from $450M senior notes + $390M convertible during earnings trough elevates refinancing risk if oil and gas capex weakens further [3]
