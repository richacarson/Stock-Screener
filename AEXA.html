Investment Thesis
AEXA offers downside-protected exposure to a potential AI, energy, defense, or DeFi acquisition through an improved SPAC structure with no speculative warrants and sponsor vesting tied to 50%+ stock appreciation. The $345M trust provides a floor near $10/share, and institutional interest from Allianz ($9.8M stake) suggests smart money sees value. If Palihapitiya identifies a high-quality target, the upside could be meaningful given his network and access.
Palihapitiya's SPAC track record is objectively poor: Virgin Galactic (-98%), Akili Interactive (acquired at $0.43/share), with only SoFi as a clear success. SPACs as an asset class have delivered negative average returns post-merger. The stock trades at only 11% premium to trust value, reflecting market skepticism. With no target announced and a finite deadline to find one, investors are essentially betting on Palihapitiya's deal-making ability—a bet the market has increasingly priced at near-zero premium.
