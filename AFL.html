Investment Thesis
Aflac is a best-in-class supplemental insurer and Dividend Aristocrat with 43 consecutive years of dividend increases at a 15.68% 5-year CAGR, supported by a conservative 34% payout ratio. The stock trades at 13.7x forward earnings with a 2.3% yield that grows at 5x the rate of inflation. Market-leading positions in both US and Japan, 21% net margins, and AI-driven operational improvements create durable competitive advantages. Wells Fargo recently raised its target to $118, and the low 0.67 beta provides portfolio stability.
Revenue declined 9.9% and EPS fell 22.9%, partly due to yen depreciation against the dollar. The Japan business (the larger segment) creates persistent currency risk that impacts reported results. Mizuho maintains a Sell rating, suggesting limited upside. The stock has pulled back from its 52-week high of $119.32. The supplemental insurance model could face pressure if employer-sponsored benefits evolve or healthcare costs moderate. Japan's aging population and shrinking workforce pose structural headwinds for the company's largest market.
