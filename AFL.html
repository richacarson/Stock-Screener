Investment Thesis
Aflac is a Dividend King supplemental insurance franchise with a durable just cause and exceptional capital return discipline. Q4 2025 net earnings of $1.4B and the 43rd consecutive annual dividend increase (5.2% for Q1 2026) demonstrate a multi-decade track record [1]. Aflac has been recognized as a World's Most Ethical Company for 19 consecutive years and its executive team ranks in the Top 5% on Comparably with 88% employee approval [2][3]. Founder-family CEO Dan Amos has led for 35+ years — the longest-serving CEO in the FORTUNE 250 [2]. AI is deployed conservatively via the Code Based Processing platform for claims automation while the CIO has explicitly chosen a 'never first' stance on generative AI to protect policyholders [2]. Inspire Impact score of +51 (ALIGNED) with 14 positive attributions and zero negatives.
The bear case is growth. Q4 2025 revenue of $4.9B came in below the $5.4B prior-year figure, 2026 guidance calls for Japan earned premium to decline 1-2% and U.S. growth at the low end of the 3-6% range, and adjusted diluted EPS was up only 0.6% YoY [1]. AFL is a capital-return story, not a growth story. Yen/FX exposure creates earnings volatility given Japan's outsized contribution. Supplemental insurance is a mature category with limited total-addressable-market expansion, and reserving assumptions in a long-duration insurance book carry asymmetric downside if claims experience worsens. For investors seeking compounding yield and ethical alignment, AFL is attractive; for growth-focused portfolios, it will underperform.
