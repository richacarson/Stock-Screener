Company Profile
Financial Services ·Credit Services ·NASDAQ ·United States
Affirm Holdings operates a buy now, pay later (BNPL) payment network enabling consumers to split purchases into installments with no hidden fees or late charges. The platform serves merchants across e-commerce and retail through partnerships with Amazon, Shopify, and others, while the Affirm Card extends BNPL to in-store purchases.

Investment Thesis
Affirm Holdings is the leading U.S. BNPL platform executing a compelling growth story: 30% revenue growth to $4.1B guided for FY2026, GMV surging 36% with the Affirm Card breakout product (159% GMV growth, 3.7M active users), and a clear path to sustained GAAP profitability after achieving its first quarterly operating profit. CEO Max Levchin's AI-driven underwriting engine is a genuine technology moat that outperforms traditional credit scoring.
The forward P/E of 11.2x on expected $3.82 EPS represents a reasonable entry point for a 30% grower, though the trailing P/E of 53x reflects the early profitability stage. Key concerns include elevated debt-to-equity of 259% (structural for a lending business), cultural deterioration post-layoffs per Glassdoor, the loss of Walmart as a major merchant, and regulatory risk around AI-driven lending decisions and BNPL oversight. International expansion (UK) and new verticals (rent, B2B) provide long-term optionality, while the Stripe partnership deepens embedded finance distribution.
Key Catalysts
  1. Affirm Card GMV surging 159% YoY to $2.19B with 3.7M users, extending BNPL from online checkout to everyday in-store spending
  2. First sustained GAAP profitability with operating margins guided to 8.5-10.5% by Q4 FY2026, validating the long-term business model
  3. International expansion into the UK market and new verticals (rent, B2B) to be detailed at May 2026 investor forum, opening incremental TAM
Key Risks
  1. Consumer credit deterioration in a slowing economy could drive delinquency spikes, pressuring Affirm's AI-based underwriting model and credit reserves
  2. BNPL regulatory scrutiny intensifying globally, with potential rules around ability-to-pay assessments, fee disclosures, and AI lending bias
  3. Loss of Walmart merchant partnership and post-layoff cultural decay (Glassdoor complaints of toxicity) could erode competitive positioning and talent retention

AI Resilience (25%)
AI Resilience LOW RISK
7
AI is a core enabler rather than a threat for Affirm — the company's entire underwriting model is built on machine learning that outperforms traditional FICO-based credit scoring [3]. Adapt AI optimizes merchant financing offers in real-time, and AI-driven fraud detection strengthens the credit risk model [3]. The Stripe AI-powered BNPL integration validates Affirm's approach rather than threatening it [3]. Primary AI risks include potential regulatory scrutiny of AI-driven lending decisions (bias, transparency), cybersecurity vulnerabilities from AI attack surfaces, and competition from traditional banks deploying their own AI-powered installment products [3]. Affirm has disclosed AI risk factors including potential for 'inaccurate or biased outputs' — a mature acknowledgment of the challenge [3].
