Company Profile
Basic Materials ·Silver ·NYSE ·Canada
First Majestic Silver Corp. is a silver and gold mining company with operations in Mexico including the San Dimas, Santa Elena, Los Gatos, and La Encantada mines. Founded in 2002 by CEO Keith Neumeyer, the company is one of the world's largest primary silver producers with over 71,000 hectares of mineral concessions. Incorporated in 1979 and headquartered in Vancouver, Canada.

Investment Thesis
First Majestic Silver is a premier pure-play silver miner entering 2026 with powerful tailwinds. The company delivered $1.27B in revenue (up 124% YoY) and $165M in net income after three consecutive years of losses, driven by record production and silver prices above $80/oz [1]. With $974M cash, minimal debt (D/E 9.7%), and a forward P/E of 17.5x, the valuation is attractive for a commodity producer in a secular bull market. Analysts project 30% revenue growth and 52% earnings growth in 2026, with the margin-focused strategy likely to expand profitability further [1]. The doubling of the dividend signals management confidence in sustained cash flow.
The bear case centers on silver price dependency — if silver retreats from $80+/oz toward $20-30/oz historical norms, revenue and profitability would collapse, as demonstrated by three consecutive years of losses from 2022-2024 when prices were lower [1]. Mexico concentration risk is material, with all four operating mines subject to Mexican regulatory, tax, and security risks. The trailing P/E of 64x reflects the rapid earnings recovery and could contract sharply on any price weakness. ROE of 9.3% remains below the 15% target despite favorable conditions, suggesting structural cost challenges. Free cash flow of $503M is strong but historically volatile — the company consumed cash during the downturn years.
Key Catalysts
  1. Sustained silver prices above $80/oz driven by AI-related electronics demand, solar panel growth, and monetary policy uncertainty supporting precious metals
  2. Margin optimization strategy in 2026 with maintained production levels could expand profit margins from 13% toward 20%+ as cost controls take effect
  3. Dividend policy doubling to 2% of net quarterly revenues attracts income-oriented precious metals investors and signals management confidence in sustainable cash flow
Key Risks
  1. Silver price collapse below $30/oz would recreate the 2022-2024 loss scenario, as the company's all-in sustaining costs leave limited margin at lower prices
  2. Mexico operational concentration exposes all four mines to regulatory changes, labor disputes, security concerns, and Mexican government mining tax policy shifts
  3. Cyclical earnings volatility makes forward P/E unreliable — the trailing P/E of 64x reflects the trough-to-peak earnings inflection rather than sustainable profitability
