Investment Thesis
First Majestic delivered record Q1 2026 results with $476.7M revenue (+95% YoY), $128.1M net income, and $223.5M free cash flow, building a war chest of $1.13B in treasury [1]. With D/E of just 9.7%, forward P/E of 18.9x, and analysts projecting 52% earnings growth in 2026, the valuation is reasonable for a commodity producer in an upcycle. Silver demand is structurally supported by AI chip manufacturing, solar energy, and EV growth, while persistent global supply deficits provide a favorable price backdrop [2]. The 'Margin First' strategy and nearly quadrupled dividend signal disciplined capital allocation under founder-CEO Neumeyer's 24-year leadership.
The bear case centers on silver price dependency — a reversion below $24/oz would compress margins toward breakeven. The trailing P/E of 69x reflects the cyclical nature of earnings, and ROE of 9.3% remains below the 15% target as the expanded asset base is still scaling. Operations concentrated in Mexico face political and regulatory risk, including ongoing tax disputes with the Mexican government and the Wirikuta sacred site controversy that could threaten social license [3]. Cost inflation in all-in sustaining costs could erode the margin advantage that the 'Margin First' strategy aims to protect. The stock at $23.58 has appreciated significantly from $18 in March, pricing in much of the Q1 upside.
Key Catalysts
  1. Sustained silver prices above $30/oz driven by AI semiconductor demand, solar energy expansion, and persistent global supply deficits creating further operating leverage [2]
  2. Santa Elena plant expansion to 3,500 tpd in H2 2026 driving production growth toward the upper end of 13-14M oz guidance [1]
  3. Jerritt Canyon gold mine restart in H2 2027 adding a domestic US production asset and reducing Mexico concentration risk [1]
Key Risks
  1. Silver price decline below $24/oz would compress margins to near-breakeven given projected AISC, and the trailing P/E of 69x amplifies downside in a commodity downturn
  2. Mexico operational risk — Wirikuta sacred site controversy, government tax disputes, and potential regulatory tightening in the mining sector could impair key assets [3]
  3. ROE of 9.3% remains below the 15% target, indicating the expanded asset base (including Gatos acquisition) has not yet reached full return potential
