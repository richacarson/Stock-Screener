Company Profile
Healthcare ·Healthcare Plans ·NYSE ·United States
agilon health, inc. operates a healthcare platform that empowers community-based physicians to transition to a total-care, value-based delivery model for senior patients. Through long-term partnerships with primary care physician groups, agilon assumes global risk capitation contracts with Medicare Advantage payers and ACO models, providing technology, analytics, capital investment, and clinical program support. Founded in 2016, headquartered in Long Beach, California, the company serves approximately 536,000 members across 26 markets.

Investment Thesis
agilon health's Q1 2026 results mark a potential inflection point in the company's turnaround. Revenue of $1.42B beat estimates by 3.2%, medical margin surged 16.4% to $149M, adjusted EBITDA swung to $54M (up 157% YoY), and net income turned positive at $49M versus $12M a year ago [3]. Management raised full-year 2026 guidance to $5.74B revenue and $25M EBITDA at the midpoint, signaling confidence that the exit of unprofitable contracts and improved medical cost management are producing durable results [3]. The balance sheet provides a safety net with $303M cash against just $32M debt, and the 0.3x price-to-sales ratio implies the market prices in significant execution risk [4]. New CEO Tim O'Rourke brings 25+ years of payor and value-based care expertise, and the appointment of a permanent leader after 10 months of transition provides needed stability [5].
The bear case is substantial. agilon lost $296M in adjusted EBITDA in FY2025 after medical costs spiraled out of control, and the turnaround requires sustained execution on contracting discipline, clinical program effectiveness, and medical cost management — none of which has been proven over multiple quarters yet [1]. Revenue is declining (FY2025 -2%, Q1 2026 -7.2% YoY) as unprofitable contracts roll off, and the guided $25M full-year EBITDA on $5.7B revenue implies razor-thin margins of ~0.4% even in a 'recovery' year [3]. The stock required a 1-for-25 reverse stock split to maintain NYSE compliance, reflecting the severity of the prior decline [10]. Leadership instability — three leaders in 10 months — creates execution risk during a critical turnaround window. The value-based care model faces structural challenges including unpredictable Medicare Advantage rate changes, physician group retention risk, and competition from well-capitalized entrants like CVS/Oak Street Health and Optum.
Key Catalysts
  1. Q2-Q4 2026 earnings demonstrating sustained medical margin improvement above $350M full-year guidance, validating that contract exits and clinical programs are producing durable unit economics
  2. New CEO Tim O'Rourke establishing strategic credibility with investors through a clear multi-year plan for returning to membership growth once profitability is established
  3. Expansion of ACO REACH and traditional Medicare models (currently 110,000 beneficiaries) as a diversification lever beyond Medicare Advantage dependence
Key Risks
  1. FY2025's $296M EBITDA loss demonstrates the fragility of the global risk capitation model — a single year of elevated medical costs or adverse coding adjustments can produce catastrophic losses on thin margins
  2. Leadership instability with three CEOs in 10 months (Sell, Williams interim, O'Rourke) during a critical turnaround creates execution risk and potential strategic inconsistency
  3. Declining membership (536K, down 11% YoY) and revenue ($5.7B guided, down from $6.06B in FY2024) could accelerate if physician partners lose confidence in the platform during the transition period
