Company Profile
Healthcare ·Medical Devices ·NASDAQ ·United States
AdaptHealth distributes home medical equipment (HME), medical supplies, and home healthcare services across the United States through four segments: Sleep Health, Respiratory Health, Diabetes Health, and Wellness at Home. The company services Medicare, Medicaid, and commercial insurance beneficiaries with CPAP machines, oxygen concentrators, insulin pumps, CGMs, wheelchairs, and other HME devices.

Investment Thesis
AdaptHealth trades at an attractive 10.4x forward P/E with a new CEO executing a strategic pivot from M&A-driven growth to operational efficiency and capitated care models. FY2026 guidance of $3.44-3.51B revenue (6-8% growth) and $680-730M EBITDA represents meaningful improvement from the FY2025 trough of $616.7M EBITDA [1]. The capitated contract covering ~50,000 members in three Mid-Atlantic states — scalable to >10M patients — could fundamentally reshape the business model toward recurring, predictable revenue. Significant insider buying ($24.4M by OEP VII and multiple executives in early 2026) signals conviction at current prices [4]. The stock has rallied from $7.95 to $13.08, but at 1.2x forward EBITDA, it remains deeply discounted to healthcare peers.
The bear case centers on execution risk and financial fragility. AdaptHealth posted a $70.8M net loss in FY2025 vs. $90.4M income in 2024, driven by Adjusted EBITDA declining 10.5% to $616.7M [1]. D/E of 124.7% constrains financial flexibility during a transformation period. The company remains dependent on Medicare/Medicaid reimbursement rates, which face ongoing political pressure. CEO Foster has been in the role less than two years, and the cultural challenges (3.3 Glassdoor rating, complaints about overwork and understaffing) could undermine the operational improvements she is targeting [2]. Revenue growth has been essentially flat (-0.5% YoY), and the capitated care model is unproven at scale. Negative profit margins must turn positive for the turnaround thesis to work.
Key Catalysts
  1. Capitated care contract scaling from 50,000 members to potentially 10M+ patients, driving 5-6% organic growth in 2026 and establishing a recurring revenue model [1]
  2. Operational efficiency improvements under CEO Foster including AI-powered self-scheduling and automation, targeting EBITDA recovery to $680-730M in FY2026 [1][2]
  3. Potential return to profitability as cost restructuring and revenue growth converge — forward EPS of $1.26 implies a dramatic earnings inflection from FY2025 losses [5]
Key Risks
  1. Medicare/Medicaid reimbursement rate pressure — federal budget constraints could reduce payment rates for HME, directly compressing margins on the majority of revenue [1]
  2. Execution risk on the capitated care model — this is an unproven business model for AdaptHealth and early go-live disruptions could impact near-term results while the model matures [1]
  3. Leverage and liquidity concerns — D/E of 124.7% with negative net income limits financial flexibility if the turnaround stalls or macro conditions deteriorate [5]
