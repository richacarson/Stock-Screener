Investment Thesis
C3.ai operates in the structurally growing enterprise AI market with a platform serving marquee clients including Shell, Bank of America, the U.S. Air Force, and Koch Industries. Q2 FY2026 showed promising signals: $75.1M revenue (7% sequential growth), 93% subscription revenue mix, 49% QoQ bookings growth, and 89% YoY federal bookings growth [1]. The near-zero debt (D/E 8.4%) provides balance sheet safety, and the company has guided FY2026 revenue of $289.5M-$309.5M [1]. New CEO Stephen Ehikian's restructuring focuses on sales execution, federal/defense expansion, and a path to non-GAAP profitability. At $7.76 per share, the $1.1B market cap prices in significant distress.
The bear case is overwhelming and near-term. Q3 FY2026 revenue collapsed 46% YoY to $53.3M, erasing the Q2 recovery narrative and triggering a 26% workforce reduction [1][2]. Profit margin of -141.4% and ROE of -55% demonstrate the company is burning cash at an unsustainable rate. Short sellers (Kerrisdale Capital, Spruce Point) have flagged accounting red flags including inflated unbilled receivables, reliance on Baker Hughes revenue, and opaque customer counting [3]. A class-action lawsuit alleges misleading guidance tied to founder Siebel's health. The stock has declined from $36+ post-IPO to $7.76 (79% decline) as the enterprise AI market explodes around C3.ai — competitors Palantir, Microsoft, and Google are gaining share while C3.ai shrinks. The 26% workforce cut risks brain drain to OpenAI, Anthropic, and other well-funded AI companies competing for the same engineering talent [2][3].
Key Catalysts
  1. Federal and defense bookings acceleration — 89% YoY growth in Q2 FY2026 driven by government AI mandates and C3.ai's FedRAMP certification, potentially offsetting commercial weakness [1]
  2. New CEO restructuring execution — Ehikian's sales reorganization, IPD-focused growth model, and cost cuts could stabilize the business if Q4 FY2026 shows sequential revenue recovery [1]
  3. Enterprise AI market tailwind — the structural demand for AI applications in government and large enterprises could eventually lift C3.ai if the platform remains competitive and sales execution improves [1]
Key Risks
  1. Revenue collapse trajectory — Q3 FY2026 revenue fell 46% YoY to $53.3M; if the decline continues, the company faces existential viability questions within 12-18 months given ongoing losses [1]
  2. Competitive displacement — Microsoft Azure AI, Google Vertex AI, Palantir, and Databricks offer more integrated enterprise AI platforms, and C3.ai's standalone model may be structurally uncompetitive as hyperscalers bundle AI tools [2]
  3. Governance and litigation risk — class-action lawsuit over misleading guidance, short-seller accounting allegations (Kerrisdale, Spruce Point), revolving-door CFOs, and founder key-person dependency create persistent investor distrust [2][3]
