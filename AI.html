C3.ai, Inc. operates an enterprise AI software platform that enables organizations to develop, deploy, and operate AI applications at scale. Products include the C3 Agentic AI Platform, industry-specific SaaS applications for manufacturing, energy, defense, and financial services, and C3 Generative AI. Founded by Tom Siebel (founder of Siebel Systems/Oracle CRM), headquartered in Redwood City, California.

Investment Thesis
C3.ai sits at the epicenter of the enterprise AI megatrend with a pure-play platform (C3 Agentic AI, C3 Generative AI) generating $227.1M in subscription revenue (91% of total), a fortress balance sheet with $673M in cash against minimal $60M debt, and the return of founder Tom Siebel as CEO bringing the vision and urgency the company needs [1][2]. The NVIDIA Connect partnership positions C3.ai alongside the leading AI infrastructure provider, and at $9.68/share with $673M cash ($4.83/share in cash), the market is valuing the operating business at less than 1x revenue — a potential deep value entry if enterprise AI adoption accelerates as projected [4]. The enterprise AI market is forecast to grow at 30%+ CAGR through 2030, and C3.ai's platform approach could capture meaningful share as enterprises move beyond proof-of-concept to production deployment.
C3.ai's FY2026 revenue collapsed 35.67% to $250.3M with net losses of $498.5M, burning through cash at an unsustainable rate while competing against Microsoft, Google, AWS, Salesforce, and Palantir — companies with 100-1000x C3.ai's resources and existing enterprise relationships [1][4]. The 14 analysts covering the stock have a consensus 'Sell' rating with an $8.82 price target implying further downside, and the CEO transition back to 73-year-old founder Tom Siebel after health issues raises serious succession and governance concerns rather than inspiring confidence [2][4]. The repeated business model pivots (subscription to consumption to agentic) suggest strategic confusion rather than vision, and the company has never achieved profitability despite 15+ years of operation and $2B+ in cumulative losses [1].
Key Catalysts
  1. Stabilization of revenue decline and return to growth under Siebel's direct leadership, particularly if enterprise AI adoption accelerates with the Agentic AI platform [1][2]
  2. NVIDIA Connect partnership could provide distribution leverage and technical credibility for the foundational brain model and enterprise AI applications [5]
  3. $673M cash position provides 3+ years of runway at current burn rate, giving time for the turnaround thesis to play out without dilutive financing [1]
Key Risks
  1. Revenue declined 35.67% in FY2026 to $250.3M with $498.5M in net losses — the company is destroying shareholder value at an accelerating rate with no clear path to profitability [1]
  2. Competing against Microsoft Azure AI, Google Vertex AI, AWS SageMaker, and Palantir with 869 employees vs. their hundreds of thousands — a structural resource disadvantage that no platform architecture can overcome [4]
  3. Tom Siebel's return as CEO at age 73 after health-related absence creates governance and succession risk — the company's strategy depends entirely on one person with no clear successor identified [2]
