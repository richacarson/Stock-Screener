Company Profile
Healthcare ·Medical Devices ·NASDAQ ·United States
Firefly Neuroscience, Inc. is an AI-powered medical technology company developing neuroscientific solutions for brain health assessment and neurological disorder diagnosis. Its FDA-510(k) cleared Brain Network Analytics (BNA) technology analyzes EEG data using machine learning to objectively assess cognitive function across conditions including depression, dementia, anxiety, concussions, and ADHD. The company has performed over 77,000 brain scans on 17,000+ patients and maintains a growing proprietary brain data repository. Incorporated through a reverse merger with WaveDancer, Inc. and expanded via the 2025 acquisition of Evoke Neuroscience. Headquartered in Kenmore, NY with operations in Toronto, Canada.

Investment Thesis
Firefly Neuroscience operates at the intersection of AI and brain health with an FDA-510(k) cleared BNA platform that has performed 77,000+ brain scans on 17,000+ patients, supported by a proprietary neural data repository and 23 US patents [3]. The NVIDIA Connect partnership to build a foundational model of the human brain positions the company at the frontier of AI-powered neuroscience [3]. The Evoke Neuroscience acquisition expanded commercial capabilities with integrated EEG hardware, and Q1 2026 revenue of $485K (up from $43K YoY) shows nascent commercial traction [2][4]. The COO's pedigree as co-founder of ClosedLoop.ai (#1 AI Healthcare Software 2022-2023) brings credible AI healthcare experience to the leadership team [6]. At $1.80/share with a $28M market cap, success in commercializing BNA at scale or delivering the NVIDIA-powered brain model could generate significant multiples of the current valuation.
The bull case is overwhelmed by existential risks. Firefly has $1.1M in TTM revenue against $19.9M in net losses with going concern risk flagged by auditors — the company may not survive without additional dilutive capital raises [4][5]. ROE of -319% and a net margin of approximately -2,706% reflect a business burning cash at an unsustainable rate relative to revenue generation. Daily dollar volume of approximately $270K ($1.80 x ~150K shares) fails the IOWN $1M liquidity floor, making the stock uninvestable for any meaningful portfolio allocation [5]. Revenue growth from Q1's $485K is encouraging but built on a near-zero base and driven by acquisition rather than organic demand [4].
