Investment Thesis
AIG is a transformed commercial insurer trading at a deep discount — 9.90x forward P/E with PEG of 0.60, implying significant undervaluation against the 20%+ forward EPS CAGR target [1][4]. Q1 2026 delivered an 80% EPS increase to $2.11 with underwriting income tripling to $774M, net premiums written up 24% YoY, and adjusted pretax income surging 65% to $1.5B [1]. Capital return is aggressive: $760M returned in Q1 alone ($519M buybacks + $241M dividends), with an 11% dividend increase to $0.50/quarter announced [1]. New CEO Eric Andersen brings 30 years of Aon experience, where he helped grow market value from $35B to $85B [2]. D/E of 22.7% provides a conservative balance sheet, and book value per share of $75.82 (+6% YoY) nearly matches the stock price [1].
The bear case centers on execution risk through the leadership transition and structural headwinds. The 5-year revenue CAGR of -9.3% — while distorted by Corebridge separation — reflects a company that has been shrinking, not growing, at the top line [4]. ROE of 7.72% remains below peer averages despite the 10%+ core operating ROE target. Glassdoor's 3.6/5 rating with Culture & Values at 3.4/5 and only 64% recommending the company suggests deep cultural challenges that AI tools alone cannot fix [2]. CEO Andersen's June 1, 2026 transition from Aon creates integration risk at a critical moment. AIG remains a significant insurer of oil and gas operations, creating ESG and regulatory risk as climate policies tighten [3]. The legacy of the 2008 financial crisis and historical SEC fraud settlements ($1.6B+) still weigh on brand perception and institutional investor trust. Catastrophe exposure in an era of increasing climate-related losses could impair underwriting results.
