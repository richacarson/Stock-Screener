Company Profile
Healthcare ·Biotechnology ·NYSE American ·United States
AIM ImmunoTech Inc. is a clinical-stage immuno-pharma company focused on developing Ampligen (rintatolimod) for treatment of pancreatic cancer, other solid tumors, and immune-deficiency disorders. The company also markets Alferon N Injection for genital warts. Founded in 1966, AIM has been in perpetual clinical development with minimal commercial revenue.

Investment Thesis
AIM ImmunoTech represents a speculative lottery ticket on Ampligen's clinical success. The Phase 2 pancreatic cancer trial with AstraZeneca's Imfinzi showed positive interim results, and the PPD collaboration adds credibility to the clinical program [1]. The stock trades at just $0.585 with a $2.5M market cap, meaning any positive clinical readout or partnership announcement could generate outsized percentage returns. The Inspire Insight score of 40 (ALIGNED) reflects the company's healthcare mission and ethical practices.
The bear case is overwhelming: AIM has $88K in annual revenue, a $9.8M stockholders' deficit, going concern doubt, and faces NYSE delisting by June 2026 if it cannot raise equity above $6M [3]. The company has burned through capital for 60 years without bringing Ampligen to commercial approval. Net losses of $14M annually against negligible revenue create existential funding risk. The 1-for-100 reverse split in 2025 and serial dilutive financings have destroyed shareholder value — the stock is down 95% in the past year [1]. With a current ratio of 0.53 and only $3M in cash, the company may not survive to see its clinical trials complete. This is not an investment; it is a speculation with a high probability of total loss.
