Company Profile
Healthcare ·Biotechnology ·NYSE American ·United States
AIM ImmunoTech Inc. is a clinical-stage immuno-pharma company focused on developing Ampligen (rintatolimod), a first-in-class investigational dsRNA and TLR3 agonist, for treatment of pancreatic cancer, ovarian cancer, and immune-deficiency disorders. The company also markets Alferon N Injection for genital warts. Founded in 1966, AIM regained NYSE American listing compliance in June 2026 after temporary OTC suspension. Generates negligible commercial revenue (~$88K TTM).

Investment Thesis
AIM ImmunoTech is a speculative binary-outcome biotech with genuinely promising recent clinical data. The Phase 2 ovarian cancer trial with Merck reported a 50% objective response rate with a 21% complete response rate and 79% clinical benefit rate — strong results for recurrent ovarian cancer [3]. The Phase 2 DURIPANC pancreatic cancer trial enrolled its final patient with primary endpoint evaluation expected December 2026, and PPD (Thermo Fisher) has been signed to design a potential Phase 3 trial [1]. The stock at $0.32 with ~$8-10M market cap prices in extreme pessimism — any positive Phase 2/3 readout or partnership announcement could generate outsized returns. AIM regained NYSE American listing compliance in June 2026 after temporary OTC suspension [4]. The Inspire score of 40 (ALIGNED) reflects the healthcare mission.
The bear case is existential. AIM generates approximately $88K in annual revenue, burns ~$11M+ in annual operating cash flow, and holds only $2.4M in cash against $2.3M in debt — requiring constant dilutive financings to survive [4][5]. The company raised $6.25M in May-June 2026 alone through registered direct offerings. Ampligen has been in development for 60 years without FDA approval, and the stock has declined 94% in the last 52 weeks [5]. The proxy fight with activists led by convicted criminals (Franz Tudor, Michael Xirinachs) created governance chaos, though the board prevailed [4]. With 21 employees, a P/E of -0.24, and EPS of -$8.62, this is not an investment but a speculation with a high probability of total loss. The only scenario for returns is a successful Phase 3 trial and eventual FDA approval — an outcome that has eluded the company for six decades.
