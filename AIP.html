Investment Thesis
Arteris is uniquely positioned as an essential enabler of AI chip design, with its Network-on-Chip IP embedded in next-generation semiconductors across automotive, AI, and enterprise computing. Revenue grew 22% to $70.6M in 2025 with ACV plus royalties reaching $83.6M. The company expects to reach non-GAAP operating profitability as early as 2026, with guidance for $89-93M revenue. Strong RPO growth of 32% and expanding royalty streams provide recurring revenue visibility.
However, Arteris remains unprofitable with a $34.7M net loss in 2025, and the path to GAAP profitability is unclear. The Cycuity acquisition adds near-term margin pressure. Revenue concentration in a few large semiconductor customers creates key-man risk. The company faces competition from much larger EDA players like Synopsys and Cadence, and geopolitical risks in the semiconductor supply chain could impact growth. At a forward P/E of 147x, the stock prices in substantial future growth.
