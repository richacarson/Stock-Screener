Investment Thesis
AAR is well-positioned in the aerospace aftermarket, benefiting from an aging global fleet, supply chain constraints, and growing defense spending. FY2025 revenue grew 20% to $2.78B with Q1 FY2026 showing 17% organic growth and expanding margins. Analyst targets averaging $119.60 imply meaningful upside. The company's MRO expansion and Eaton partnership strengthen its competitive moat in a structurally growing market.
The stock trades at an elevated trailing P/E of 39.6x despite a forward P/E of 18.4x, suggesting near-term earnings headwinds. FY2025 earnings declined 73% despite strong revenue growth, reflecting cost pressures. The recent share offering dilutes existing shareholders. ROE of just 6.9% is low for the valuation. Defense spending could face budget pressure, and any aviation downturn would impact demand across all segments.
