Investment Thesis
reAlpha Tech Corp. is building an AI-powered end-to-end homebuying platform integrating brokerage, mortgage, title, and conversational AI across 30+ US states. FY2025 revenue surged 376% to $4.5M with $116M in total transaction volume, and Q1 2026 transaction volume grew 119% to $131.3M while gross margins expanded to 66% [1]. Claire AI operates as a zero-commission smart buyer's agent — a genuine AI product with differentiated positioning. The pending InstaMortgage acquisition would deepen vertical integration into mortgage lending. At ~$10.5M market cap, extreme pessimism is priced in, and the $3T+ US real estate services TAM provides massive headroom if the platform scales [1][2].
The bear case is existential. Q1 2026 revenue declined 9% to $841K with a $4.4M net loss, cash fell from $7.8M to $4.7M in one quarter, and management issued a going concern warning [1][3]. The 1-for-25 reverse stock split in April 2026 reduced shares from 134M to 5.36M to regain Nasdaq compliance — but the stock at $1.96 remains at risk of reversion below $1.00. A 25% workforce reduction in May 2026 signals financial distress, and two board resignations in February created audit committee non-compliance [3]. Average daily dollar volume of ~$103K post-split is far below the $1M IOWN liquidity floor, making the stock effectively untradeable for institutional investors. The company competes against Zillow ($12B revenue), Redfin, and Rocket Mortgage with vastly greater resources. Serial dilution through capital raises has destroyed shareholder value. With no path to profitability visible and cash burn of ~$3M per quarter, additional dilutive financing is inevitable [1][3].
