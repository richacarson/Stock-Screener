Investment Thesis
AirSculpt Technologies offers exposure to the branded minimally-invasive body-contouring category with a 63.97% gross margin business model at scale and FY2026 guidance of $151–$157M revenue with $15–$17M EBITDA [1]. Same-store sales turned positive in February 2026 after a very weak 2025, suggesting the demand environment is stabilizing under new CEO Yogesh Jashnani's fiscal-discipline strategy [1]. Major shareholder Jorey Chernett's 90,000-share purchase in April 2026 at a distressed $3.12 level reflects insider conviction that the recovery is real [3]. The core surgical body-contouring procedure is structurally AI-resilient — AI cannot replace a licensed clinician performing a minimally-invasive surgery [2]. At ~$219M market cap trading at ~13–14x forward EV/EBITDA on guided 2026 numbers, the stock prices in considerable pessimism.
The bear case is severe. FY2025 revenue collapsed 15.8% to $151.8M with case volume down 15.6% and net loss widening to $11.7M, and FY2026 guidance of $151–$157M implies essentially flat revenue — no recovery [1]. ROE of -21.85% and net margin of -11.41% mean the business is unprofitable at current scale. Glassdoor ratings of 2.5/5 with cited 'dysfunctional and demoralizing' reviews and 'over 11 employees quit in a single week' after CEO transition signal serious organizational stress that typically precedes further service-quality and revenue deterioration [2]. The emergence of GLP-1 weight-loss pharmaceuticals (Ozempic, Mounjaro, Zepbound) is a structural demand threat to aesthetic body-contouring surgery — weight reduction via pharmacology could compress the total addressable market for surgical fat removal even if AI itself is not the substitute [1]. The CEO turnover from Dennis Dean (interim) to Yogesh Jashnani in January 2025 is the latest in a series of leadership transitions [2].

AI Resilience (25%)
AI Resilience MODERATE
7
AI Risk Protection (7/9): Body-contouring medical procedures require physical surgical work by licensed clinicians at clinic locations — AI cannot substitute for a minimally-invasive liposuction surgery. Regulatory licensing, physical infrastructure (surgical suites), and patient-physician trust all create structural defensive moats against AI substitution [2]. Potential AI enhancements (patient consultation chatbots, marketing optimization, scheduling AI) could reduce costs but do not displace the core revenue activity. Risk: Consumer AI-driven GLP-1 awareness and weight-loss-drug access could shift aesthetic demand away from surgical body contouring to pharmaceutical approaches, although this is a pharmacology substitution rather than an AI substitution [1]. AI Opportunity (0/1): AIRS does not monetize AI directly; no AI revenue line [1].
