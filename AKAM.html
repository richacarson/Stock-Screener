Company Profile

Akamai Technologies operates the world's most distributed cloud and security platform, providing cybersecurity solutions (web application protection, API security, zero trust), cloud computing services (compute, storage, AI inference), and content delivery across a global edge network with 4,100+ points of presence in 130 countries. The company signed a landmark 7-year, $1.8B AI inference contract and is deploying NVIDIA Blackwell/RTX Pro GPUs across its distributed platform. Founded in 1998 from MIT research, co-founder/CEO Tom Leighton has led the company for 28 years.

Investment Thesis
Akamai is successfully transforming from a legacy CDN company into a cloud/security/AI inference platform, validated by a landmark 7-year, $1.8B committed AI inference contract — the largest customer deal in company history [1]. Q1 2026 delivered $1.074B revenue (+6% YoY) powered by CIS at 40% growth (raised to 50%+ outlook), security at 11% growth, and API Security exceeding $100M run rate [1]. At 16.92x forward P/E with PEG of 1.34 and $1.52B operating cash flow, AKAM offers compelling value for a company with accelerating AI/cloud adoption and a 28-year co-founder/CEO providing strategic continuity [2][4]. The Glassdoor rating of 4.3/5 (90% recommend) and Inspire score of 67 (ALIGNED) reflect strong culture and ESG positioning. Management expects double-digit total revenue growth by 2027 as the AI contract ramps and CIS accelerates [1].
The bear case centers on the legacy CDN drag and competitive intensity. Delivery revenue is declining mid-single digits and will pressure overall growth until CIS fully offsets it — total revenue grew only 6% in Q1 2026 despite 40% CIS growth [1]. D/E of 119.53% from Linode and other acquisitions constrains financial flexibility as $250M is invested in AI infrastructure. A $200M memory cost headwind and CapEx at 40-42% of revenue compress margins — profit margin is only 10.2% and 5-year EPS CAGR is negative [4]. Competition from Cloudflare (faster-growing, purpose-built), hyperscalers (AWS CloudFront, Azure CDN with unlimited capital), and pure-play AI inference providers (CoreWeave, Lambda) threatens market share in cloud and AI [2]. The $1.8B contract doesn't ramp fully until late 2026, creating near-term execution risk. Investors who still perceive Akamai as a CDN company may not re-rate the stock until AI revenue becomes a larger portion of the mix.
