Company Profile
Industrials ·Rental & Leasing Services ·NYSE ·United States
Air Lease Corporation purchases new commercial jet aircraft and leases them to airlines worldwide. Founded in 2010 by industry pioneer Steven Udvar-Házy, the company owns 490 aircraft, manages 45, and has 218 on order with manufacturers. AL operates a buy-new, lease, sell strategy focused on modern, fuel-efficient aircraft.

Investment Thesis
Air Lease offers compelling value at 7.6x forward P/E with 36.1% profit margins, 10.3% revenue growth, and a 490-aircraft fleet that is 100% placed through 2026 [1][2]. Net income surged 154% to $1.09B in 2025 as the company recovered from prior-year Russian sanctions impairments, and consensus expects a further 14.1% earnings growth in 2026 with upward estimate revisions [2]. The 218-aircraft order backlog with Boeing and Airbus provides years of embedded revenue growth, while the chronic aircraft supply shortage (manufacturer backlogs extending 5+ years) gives lessors unprecedented pricing power on lease rates. The company trades at 0.86x book value despite generating $163M in free cash flow and maintaining a 13-year dividend growth streak.
The bear case centers on leverage and cyclical exposure. D/E of 232.9% with $19.7B in total debt exposes Air Lease to rising interest rates — each 100bp increase in funding costs compresses margins on the existing fleet [1]. The airline industry remains cyclical; a deep recession could trigger lease deferrals or airline bankruptcies, as seen during COVID when Air Lease wrote off $800M+ in Russian-sanctioned aircraft. The stock's 52-week high of $65.00 (current price) may limit near-term upside. Boeing's ongoing production challenges could delay aircraft deliveries, disrupting the order backlog placement schedule. The 1.35% dividend yield provides minimal income support, and insider ownership at 6.1% is modest. A Sustainalytics review flagged the highest controversy level in the past three years, though the specific issue was not disclosed [5].
Key Catalysts
  1. Aircraft supply shortage — Boeing and Airbus backlogs extending 5+ years give lessors unprecedented pricing power on lease extensions and new placements, supporting revenue growth above 10% [2]
  2. Earnings normalization — 2025 net income of $1.09B (up 154%) reflects recovery from Russian write-offs, with consensus expecting another 14.1% earnings growth in 2026 as the fleet generates clean returns [2]
  3. Below-book valuation — trading at 0.86x book value ($75.63) provides margin of safety for a fleet of new-technology aircraft that should hold residual value in a supply-constrained market [1]
Key Risks
  1. Interest rate exposure — $19.7B in debt at 232.9% D/E means rising funding costs directly compress lease rate margins, and the company's profitability is sensitive to the credit spread environment [1]
  2. Airline credit risk — cyclical airline industry creates counterparty risk; a deep recession could trigger lease payment deferrals, airline bankruptcies, or aircraft repossessions that require costly remarketing [2]
  3. Boeing delivery delays — production challenges at Boeing could push back aircraft deliveries from the 218-unit order backlog, disrupting the placed-through-2026 schedule and delaying revenue recognition [2]

AI Resilience (25%)
AI Resilience LOW RISK
8
AI Risk Protection (8/9): Air Lease's core business — owning and leasing physical aircraft to airlines — is structurally immune to AI disruption. Aircraft are tangible assets that must be manufactured, maintained, and physically operated; AI cannot substitute for a 737 MAX or A321neo [2]. The company's competitive moat rests on fleet strategy, airline relationships, and access to manufacturer order slots — none of which AI can replicate. If anything, AI-driven efficiency gains at airlines could stimulate demand for air travel and thus aircraft leasing. The aircraft supply chain remains constrained with Boeing and Airbus backlogs extending years out, further protecting lessors [2]. AI Opportunity (0/1): While industry reports suggest GenAI could enhance residual value forecasting and lease structuring, Air Lease has not disclosed specific AI-generated revenue or quantified AI-driven operational savings [3].
