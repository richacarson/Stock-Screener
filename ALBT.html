Investment Thesis
Avalon GloboCare is a micro-cap ($3.4M market cap) company attempting to pivot into AI through the December 2025 acquisition of RPM Interactive and a pending YOOV Group merger. The KetoAir breathalyzer device begins marketing in April 2026, and the Catch-Up SaaS AI video platform represents a potentially scalable product [1][2]. The company regained Nasdaq compliance after the hearing process, avoiding immediate delisting. At $0.50/share, the stock is priced for failure, meaning any successful execution on AI products or the YOOV merger could generate outsized returns from a very low base.
The bear case is overwhelming. ALBT has $1.3M TTM revenue, -$5.5M operating cash flow, only $334K cash, negative equity of -$4.5M, and just 4 employees [1][4]. The company has pivoted business models multiple times without achieving profitability in any of them. Three board members resigned in recent months, the CEO is interim, and the company narrowly avoided Nasdaq delisting [3]. The February 2026 private placement at $0.51/share diluted shareholders to raise a modest $3.25M. Revenue has declined over 5 years while operating losses persist at -370% margin [1]. The RPM Interactive AI acquisition and YOOV merger are speculative bets by a company with no demonstrated ability to execute in AI, competing against companies with billions in resources. This is a distressed micro-cap with extreme binary risk [1][3].
