Investment Thesis
Alkermes has a compelling CNS franchise with three growing products ($1.2B proprietary sales +9%), $1.3B cash, near-zero debt, and an expanding pipeline including alixorexton with FDA breakthrough designation. Forward P/E of 13.6x is reasonable for a profitable pharma with multiple growth drivers. The Avadel acquisition and pipeline catalysts in 2026 provide near-term upside.
The bear case includes VIVITROL generic competition beginning in 2027 ($468M at risk), CEO transition uncertainty, Q4 EPS miss, and the one-time gross-to-net adjustments that inflated 2025 results ($27M VIVITROL, $14M ARISTADA). Revenue declined 10.6% YoY in Q4 and R&D expense jumped 32%, pressuring near-term margins. Integration risk from the Avadel acquisition and increasing SG&A ($702M) could weigh on profitability.
