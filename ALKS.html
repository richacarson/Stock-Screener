Company Profile
Healthcare ·Drug Manufacturers - Specialty & Generic ·NASDAQ ·Ireland
Alkermes is a global biopharmaceutical company focused on developing and commercializing treatments for central nervous system disorders including schizophrenia, bipolar I disorder, alcohol and opioid dependence, and narcolepsy. Key marketed products include ARISTADA, VIVITROL, LYBALVI, and the recently acquired LUMRYZ. The company also licenses its proprietary drug delivery technology platforms to third parties.

Investment Thesis
Alkermes is a specialty biopharmaceutical company at an inflection point following the transformative Avadel acquisition in February 2026, which adds LUMRYZ (projected $350-370M 2026 revenue) and expands the company's CNS therapeutic portfolio into narcolepsy/sleep disorders [1]. The combined entity is guided to $1.73-1.84B in 2026 total revenue (17-25% growth over FY2025's $1.48B), with adjusted EBITDA of $370-410M [1]. The balance sheet remains conservative with only $70M total debt against $588M cash, and the stock trades at an undemanding 16.9x forward P/E for a specialty pharma with accelerating top-line growth and expanding therapeutic reach [4]. Core products ARISTADA, VIVITROL, and LYBALVI provide a durable CNS franchise with high switching costs in mental health treatment.
The bear case centers on integration execution risk and revenue concentration. Alkermes is absorbing its largest acquisition while simultaneously managing a CEO succession from 30-year veteran Richard Pops to Blair Jackson, creating dual leadership transitions [2]. FY2025 revenue declined 10.6% YoY, and 2026 GAAP earnings will be depressed by $150M noncash inventory step-up charges and $50M transaction costs from the Avadel deal [1]. LUMRYZ faces competitive pressure from Jazz Pharmaceuticals' established Xyrem/Xywav franchise in narcolepsy, and generic competition could erode VIVITROL's market position. Free cash flow was negative $363M in FY2025, raising questions about near-term cash generation capacity during the integration period [4]. The company's 1.4% insider ownership and persistent insider selling pattern suggest limited management conviction at current prices [4].

  3. Negative free cash flow of -$363M in FY2025 and persistent insider selling raise concerns about near-term capital allocation and management confidence [4]
