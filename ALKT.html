Company Profile
Technology ·Software - Application ·NASDAQ ·United States
Alkami Technology provides a cloud-based digital banking platform for community, regional, and super-regional financial institutions in the United States. The platform includes digital banking, onboarding and account opening, and data and marketing modules that enable banks and credit unions to digitally engage customers, accelerate revenues, and enhance operational efficiency.

Investment Thesis
Alkami is the fastest-growing cloud-native digital banking platform serving 250+ community and regional financial institutions with 16M+ digital banking users, delivering 35% Q4 2025 revenue growth to $120.8M and guiding for $525.5-$530.5M in 2026 revenue (18.5-19.6% growth) [1]. The MANTL acquisition expanded account-opening capabilities, and 35% ARR growth demonstrates strong recurring revenue momentum. With forward P/E of 16.2x on accelerating profitability (2026 adjusted EBITDA guidance of $93.5-$97.5M at 18.1% margins), Alkami offers compelling growth at a reasonable valuation in the $50B+ digital banking TAM [1]. General Atlantic's $59.7M share purchase in March 2026 signals institutional confidence in the platform's trajectory.
The bear case centers on Alkami's persistent unprofitability — negative 10.7% profit margins and -13.3% ROE reflect a company still investing ahead of profitability despite 15 years of operations [4]. D/E of 101.8% and $368M in debt against only $99M in cash creates balance sheet risk if growth decelerates. The Glassdoor rating of 3.2/5 with management quality concerns raises execution risk at a critical inflection point. Competition from incumbent core banking providers (FIS, Fiserv, Jack Henry) who are building their own cloud capabilities could pressure Alkami's growth, and AI-powered fintech tools may commoditize some platform features. Revenue growth is decelerating from 35% to 19% YoY guidance, and the path to sustained GAAP profitability remains unproven at scale [1][4].
Key Catalysts
  1. Path to profitability inflection — 2026 adjusted EBITDA guidance of $93.5-$97.5M at 18.1% margins represents the company's first significant profitability year, potentially driving re-rating from growth to profitable-growth multiple [1]
  2. MANTL integration expanding account-opening capabilities across 250+ existing financial institution clients, driving upsell revenue and increasing platform stickiness [1]
  3. General Atlantic's $59.7M share purchase and continued insider buying signal institutional conviction in the platform's long-term competitive position [4]
Key Risks
  1. Revenue growth deceleration from 35% to 19% YoY guidance raises concerns about market penetration ceiling as Alkami moves beyond early-adopter community banks [1]
  2. Management quality concerns reflected in 3.2/5 Glassdoor rating and 43% employee recommendation rate create execution risk during critical scaling phase [2]
  3. Incumbent competitive response — FIS, Fiserv, and Jack Henry collectively serve thousands of banks and are investing in cloud-native capabilities that could neutralize Alkami's architectural advantage [2]
