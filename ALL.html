Company Profile
Financial Services ·Insurance - Property & Casualty ·NYSE ·United States
The Allstate Corporation is one of the largest publicly held personal lines property and casualty insurers in the United States, providing auto, homeowners, and other insurance through exclusive agents, independent agents, and direct channels under the Allstate, National General, and Esurance brands. The company also offers protection services including identity protection and roadside assistance.

Investment Thesis
Allstate is a deeply undervalued insurance franchise trading at 8.3x forward earnings and 5.6x trailing P/E with a 39.5% ROE, reflecting the dramatic earnings recovery from the 2022-2023 catastrophe loss cycle [1][5]. Full-year 2025 EPS of $38.06 exceeded estimates every quarter (average 50%+ beats), with Q4 EPS of $14.31 crushing the $9.85 consensus by 45% [1]. Revenue of $67.7B (up 5.6%) combined with a 10.5% payout ratio means the $4.32 annual dividend has massive room to grow — the 13.0% 5yr dividend CAGR should accelerate given the low payout and earnings power [5]. The PEG ratio of 0.47 suggests significant value relative to growth, and the $55.6B market cap provides institutional-grade liquidity [5].
The bear case is catastrophe risk — Allstate's earnings are structurally volatile because P&C insurance profitability is driven by weather events, wildfire seasons, and natural disaster frequency that are worsening due to climate change [1]. The 2022-2023 period saw Allstate post significant underwriting losses, and a severe hurricane season or wildfire event could quickly reverse the current earnings recovery. The insurance industry faces regulatory pressure on rate adequacy in key states like California and Florida. AI-enabled insurtech competitors (Progressive's telematics, Lemonade's AI underwriting) threaten to structurally disrupt traditional agent-based distribution [2]. Revenue growth of 5.1% is primarily driven by rate increases rather than organic policy growth, and the sustainability of $38+ EPS is uncertain as rate adequacy cycles normalize. Sustainalytics assigns a 'Moderate' controversy level of 4, suggesting some ESG governance concerns [3].
Key Catalysts
  1. Q1 2026 consensus expects $7.46 EPS (+111.3% YoY), suggesting the earnings recovery cycle has further room to run with continued rate adequacy across auto and home lines [1]
  2. 10.5% payout ratio with 13.0% 5yr dividend CAGR provides massive headroom for accelerated dividend growth from a 2.02% yield — potential to double the dividend within 5 years at current earnings power [5]

Key Risks
  1. Catastrophe loss volatility — climate change is increasing hurricane, wildfire, and severe weather frequency, creating structural risk to P&C insurer earnings that cannot be fully priced into premiums [1]
  2. Rate adequacy normalization — the 20-30% rate increases that drove the earnings recovery may face regulatory pushback and competitive pressure, compressing margins as the hard market softens [1]
  3. Insurtech disruption from Progressive's telematics pricing, Lemonade's AI underwriting, and direct-to-consumer platforms threatening Allstate's traditional agent-based distribution model [2]
