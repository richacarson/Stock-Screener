Company Profile
Healthcare ·Biotechnology ·NASDAQ ·United States
Alumis Inc. is a clinical-stage biopharmaceutical company focused on developing precision medicines for autoimmune disorders. Its lead candidate envudeucitinib is a TYK2 inhibitor in Phase 3 for plaque psoriasis with NDA planned for H2 2026. The pipeline includes A-005 (CNS-penetrant TYK2 inhibitor), lonigutamab (IGF-1R antibody for thyroid eye disease), and an IRF5 program targeting immune dysfunction.
221 Employees ·www.alumis.com

Investment Thesis
Alumis is a clinical-stage biotech with a potentially best-in-class oral TYK2 inhibitor (envudeucitinib) that achieved leading skin clearance in Phase 3 — 65% PASI 90 and >40% PASI 100 at Week 24 — surpassing Bristol-Myers Squibb's Sotyktu in cross-trial comparisons [1]. The NDA submission is targeted for H2 2026 in plaque psoriasis (an $8B+ market), with SLE Phase 2b data expected Q3 2026 and additional pipeline assets (A-005, lonigutamab, IRF5) extending the platform. The $345M offering in early 2026 provides cash runway into Q4 2027, sufficient to reach key regulatory milestones [1]. At $2.9B market cap, the stock prices in meaningful probability of approval but significant upside remains if the psoriasis launch captures even mid-single-digit market share in a large and growing oral therapy segment [5].
The bear case centers on binary regulatory and commercial risk. Alumis has never commercialized a product and must build a sales force from scratch to compete against BMS' established Sotyktu franchise and potential TYK2 competitors from other large pharma companies [1]. The $386M annual R&D burn against $24M in licensing revenue ($202M negative FCF) means the company is entirely dependent on capital markets or a partnership deal for survival beyond Q4 2027 [1]. FDA rejection or a clinical hold — standard risks for any late-stage biotech — would devastate the $2.9B market cap. The TYK2 class faces lingering safety monitoring after BMS' experience, and any unexpected adverse events in long-term extension studies could delay or derail approval. With 221 employees, Alumis lacks the commercial infrastructure of established pharma and would need to execute a flawless launch while simultaneously advancing four pipeline programs [5].
Key Catalysts
  1. Envudeucitinib NDA submission to FDA in H2 2026 for moderate-to-severe plaque psoriasis, with best-in-class oral efficacy data from ONWARD1 and ONWARD2 Phase 3 trials [1]
  2. SLE Phase 2b topline data expected Q3 2026, potentially opening a second large indication (systemic lupus) for envudeucitinib [1]
  3. Potential partnership or acquisition interest from large pharma seeking next-generation TYK2 assets, following precedent of Sanofi's Principia acquisition ($3.7B) [2]
Key Risks
  1. FDA regulatory rejection or Complete Response Letter for envudeucitinib NDA would collapse the valuation thesis with no near-term revenue offset [1]
  2. Cash runway only extends to Q4 2027 — any clinical delay or failed trial would force dilutive financing or a fire-sale partnership at depressed terms [1]
  3. Commercial execution risk as a 221-person company building a de novo salesforce to compete against BMS and established dermatology franchises in a market requiring significant payer access and physician education [1][5]
