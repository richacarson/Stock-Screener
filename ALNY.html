Company Profile
Healthcare ·Biotechnology ·NASDAQ ·United States
Alnylam Pharmaceuticals discovers, develops, manufactures, and commercializes therapeutics based on ribonucleic acid interference (RNAi). The company's commercial portfolio includes ONPATTRO and AMVUTTRA for hereditary transthyretin-mediated amyloidosis, GIVLAARI for acute hepatic porphyria, and OXLUMO for primary hyperoxaluria type 1. Alnylam is a pioneer in RNAi therapeutics and is expanding into cardiometabolic disease.

R&D Intensity (1/1): Alnylam consistently invests heavily in R&D, spending approximately 40-50% of revenue on research, well above biotech peer median [1].

Investment Thesis
Alnylam is executing one of the most impressive commercial inflections in biotech history, with 2025 revenue of $3.71B (up 85% YoY) and 2026 guidance of $4.9-$5.3B representing 71% growth at midpoint [1]. The company just achieved GAAP profitability after two decades of losses, and the 'Alnylam 2030' strategy targets >25% revenue CAGR through 2030 with ~30% non-GAAP operating margins [1]. AMVUTTRA's dominance in ATTR cardiomyopathy — validated by the landmark HELIOS-B trial showing 28% reduction in cardiovascular death and 33% reduction in cardiovascular events — positions Alnylam as the leader in a market that could exceed $10B globally [1]. At a forward PEG of 0.61 and forward P/E of 21.9x, the stock is attractively valued for a company with this growth profile and expanding margins [4].
The bear case centers on Alnylam's concentrated revenue base and emerging competition. Over 80% of revenue comes from the TTR franchise (AMVUTTRA/ONPATTRO), creating significant single-product-line risk [1]. AstraZeneca's Wainua (eplontersen) could show competitive or superior data in late 2026, potentially challenging Alnylam's best-in-class status in ATTR [2]. D/E of 376% reflects the massive debt accumulated during pre-profitability years, and while the company is now cash-flow positive, deleveraging will take time and limits financial flexibility [4]. Profit margin of just 8.4% remains thin for a company at this revenue scale, reflecting the ongoing heavy investment in commercialization infrastructure and pipeline R&D [4]. The trailing P/E of 132x means any deceleration in growth or competitive setback could result in significant multiple compression. Pipeline risk remains — ziltivekimab and fitusiran must succeed to diversify beyond TTR and justify the 'Alnylam 2030' revenue trajectory.

  3. High leverage at 376% D/E from pre-profitability debt accumulation constrains financial flexibility and increases refinancing risk if growth decelerates or interest rates remain elevated [4]

AI Resilience (25%)
AI Resilience MODERATE
7
AI Risk Protection (6/9): Alnylam's core business — discovering, manufacturing, and commercializing RNAi therapeutics — is highly protected from AI disruption. The company's competitive moat rests on proprietary RNAi chemistry platforms (GalNAc conjugation, lipid nanoparticle delivery), a deep patent estate, FDA-approved manufacturing processes, and complex clinical trial expertise that AI cannot substitute [1]. Drug manufacturing and regulatory approval processes are physical-world operations with enormous switching costs. However, AI could accelerate competitor drug discovery timelines, potentially enabling rivals like Arrowhead Pharmaceuticals or AstraZeneca (Wainua/eplontersen) to develop competing ATTR therapies faster [2]. AI Opportunity (1/1): Alnylam generates indirect AI value through its Viz.ai partnership deploying FDA-cleared AI echocardiography for earlier cardiac amyloidosis diagnosis — which directly expands the addressable patient population for AMVUTTRA — and uses Amazon Bedrock generative AI for internal operational efficiencies [2].
