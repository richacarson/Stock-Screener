Company Profile
Technology ·Software - Application ·NASDAQ ·United States
ALT5 Sigma Corporation (formerly JanOne Inc.) operates through Fintech and Biotechnology segments. The Fintech segment provides blockchain-powered technologies including ALT 5 Prime (digital asset OTC trading platform) and ALT 5 Pay (crypto payment gateway). The company bills itself as a WLFI (World Liberty Financial) treasury company linked to Trump family crypto ventures. The Biotechnology segment develops non-opioid pain therapies including JAN 101 (sodium nitrite for peripheral arterial disease). Based in Las Vegas, Nevada with only 10 employees.

Investment Thesis
ALT5 Sigma offers speculative exposure to crypto fintech infrastructure and a dormant biotech pipeline. Q3 2025 fintech revenue of $7.6M at 35% gross margin shows the ALT 5 Prime trading platform is generating real, growing revenue, with YoY growth of 53.3% [1]. The WLFI digital asset holdings generated $57M in Q3 net income, and the analyst consensus target of $18 (vs. current $1.16) implies extreme upside if the WLFI relationship delivers sustained value [1]. Low debt ($17.8M) and minimal D/E of 1.14% provide balance sheet flexibility. The biotech pipeline, with JAN 101 in Phase 2a for peripheral arterial disease, represents a hidden option value in a non-opioid pain market with significant unmet medical need.
The risks are extraordinary and multi-dimensional. Leadership instability is the most acute concern — three C-suite departures in six weeks (CEO suspended, acting CEO removed, COO departed) signals governance breakdown [2]. The Trump-linked WLFI connection has attracted Congressional investigations into potential conflicts of interest and national security concerns, creating regulatory overhang [2]. The $57M Q3 net income is almost entirely unrealized gains on volatile digital assets that could reverse sharply. Operating margins of -218% indicate the core business is deeply unprofitable, and with only 10 employees, operational capacity is severely constrained [3]. The stock has lost 89% from its 52-week high of $10.95 to $1.16, reflecting market judgment on the company's viability. The biotech pipeline has no clear funding path or development timeline. The $146M market cap with $24.9M annual revenue and persistent operating losses suggests the company may require dilutive capital raises [3].
