Company Profile
Energy ·Oil & Gas Midstream ·NYSE ·United States
Antero Midstream Corporation owns, operates, and develops midstream energy infrastructure assets in the Appalachian Basin. The company operates gathering pipelines, compressor stations, and processing facilities that collect and process natural gas and NGLs from Antero Resources' wells in West Virginia and Ohio. Its Water Handling segment delivers freshwater and manages wastewater for Antero Resources' drilling operations, including the largest water pipeline system in Appalachia. Revenue is primarily secured through long-term, fixed-fee, take-or-pay contracts with Antero Resources.

Investment Thesis
Antero Midstream offers a 4.1% dividend yield supported by long-term, fixed-fee take-or-pay contracts with Antero Resources, providing predictable cash flows from essential energy infrastructure in the Appalachian Basin [1][7]. The $1.1B Marcellus acquisition is expected to boost FY2026 EBITDA by 8% and free cash flow by 11%, and the AI-driven surge in natural gas demand (Goldman Sachs estimates 6 Bcf/d of incremental pipeline capacity needed) creates a structural tailwind for midstream volumes [1][2]. At 14.8x forward earnings with a 32.8% profit margin, the stock offers attractive income-oriented value in an energy infrastructure asset with multi-decade useful life [7].
The bear case is driven by dividend quality concerns and structural limitations. The payout ratio of 104.7% means the company is paying more in dividends than it earns in net income, and the dividend has been flat at $0.90/year since 2020 — following a 27% cut from $1.23 in 2019 — representing zero growth for 6 consecutive years [7]. D/E of 163% is elevated, partly reflecting the $1.1B acquisition debt, and customer concentration risk is extreme with virtually all revenue tied to a single customer (Antero Resources) under contracts that will eventually expire or renegotiate [1]. Secular risk from the energy transition — while decades away for natural gas — creates long-term uncertainty for infrastructure with 30+ year asset lives. The Glassdoor culture score of 2.9/5 for the Antero enterprise is concerning for long-term organizational health [5].
