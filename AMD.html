Investment Thesis
AMD is the most compelling AI semiconductor investment outside of Nvidia, with data center GPU revenue forecast to surge 114% to $15B in 2026 and total data center revenue projected at $28.7B (up 73%). The OpenAI partnership (6 GW of AMD GPUs) and Oracle Cloud deployment validate AMD as the credible #2 AI infrastructure provider. At 18.7x forward P/E with guided >35% revenue CAGR and forward PEG of ~0.53, AMD trades at a significant discount to its growth trajectory. Lisa Su's track record—46.3% average annual TSR since taking over—and the company's near-zero leverage (6.4% D/E) provide both leadership confidence and financial flexibility.
The bear case acknowledges that GAAP ROE of 7.1% significantly trails semiconductor peers due to the massive Xilinx goodwill on the balance sheet, and GAAP profit margins of 12.5% lag Nvidia's 55%+ margins. AMD remains firmly the #2 AI GPU supplier, and any market share losses to Nvidia's next-generation Blackwell/Rubin platforms or to custom ASICs from Google (TPU), Amazon (Trainium), and Microsoft (Maia) could significantly compress growth expectations. Export restrictions barring additional AI GPU sales to China after Q1 2026 remove a significant TAM, and HBM supply constraints could limit production volumes.
