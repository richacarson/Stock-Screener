Company Profile
Industrials ·Specialty Industrial Machinery ·NYSE ·United States
AMETEK is a global manufacturer of electronic instruments and electromechanical devices operating through two segments: Electronic Instruments Group (EIG) and Electromechanical Group (EMG). Products include process and analytical instrumentation, aerospace sensors, power quality equipment, precision motion control, engineered medical devices, specialty metals, and thermal management systems. Founded in 1930, headquartered in Berwyn, Pennsylvania.
22,500 Employees ·www.ametek.com

Investment Thesis
AMETEK is a best-in-class diversified industrial compounder — record 2025 revenue of $7.4B, record adjusted operating income of $1.94B, and record margins demonstrate the AMETEK Growth Model's consistent execution across economic cycles [1][2]. Operating margins of 27.5% and profit margins of 20% are exceptional for an industrial company, while FCF of $1.35B provides acquisition firepower to sustain the compounding formula. CEO Zapico's top-10% leadership rating and 96-year operating heritage create organizational stability [3]. At 26.7x forward P/E with 2026 EPS guidance of $7.87-$8.07 (+6-9%), AMETEK offers quality industrial exposure with Q1 2026 revenue expected up ~10% [2]. The portfolio of 170+ niche technology businesses provides diversification across aerospace, healthcare, semiconductor, and energy end markets.
The bear case centers on valuation and growth deceleration. At 36.3x trailing P/E and PEG of 2.92, AMETEK trades at a premium to the S&P 500 despite mid-single-digit organic growth guidance — the stock requires continued execution and acquisitions to justify the multiple [5]. Revenue growth deceleration from 13.4% in 2025 to mid-to-high single digits in 2026 may disappoint momentum investors. The acquisitive model creates integration risk and goodwill accumulation (P/B of 5.0x). Some Glassdoor reviews note a financial-performance-over-culture orientation that could challenge talent retention in competitive markets [3]. Tariff and trade policy uncertainty creates risk for AMETEK's international operations. The 0.58% dividend yield offers minimal income return, and while the 19.4% payout ratio provides growth reinvestment capacity, income-focused investors may look elsewhere. Cyclical industrial exposure means any economic downturn would pressure the aerospace, semiconductor, and manufacturing end markets simultaneously [5].
