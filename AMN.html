Company Profile
Healthcare ·Medical Care Facilities ·NYSE ·United States
AMN Healthcare Services is a technology-enabled healthcare workforce solutions and staffing company serving acute and sub-acute care hospitals and other healthcare facilities in the United States. Operating through Nurse and Allied Solutions, Physician and Leadership Solutions, and Technology and Workforce Solutions segments, the company provides travel nurse staffing, locum tenens, healthcare IT solutions, vendor management systems, and workforce optimization services. Founded in 1985 and headquartered in Dallas, Texas.
2,664 Employees ·www.amnhealthcare.com

Investment Thesis
AMN Healthcare is positioned for dramatic near-term revenue acceleration, with Q1 2026 guided at $1.225-$1.24B representing 78-80% YoY growth driven by approximately $600M in labor disruption revenue from healthcare facility strikes and staffing crises [1]. At 18.5x forward P/E and 1.22x PEG, the stock is attractively valued for a company with this growth trajectory and AI-powered technology platform differentiating it from pure staffing competitors [4]. CDIO Hagan's investments in AI, predictive analytics, and scheduling optimization position AMN to capture structural workforce shortage demand as the U.S. healthcare system faces persistent nurse and physician deficits [2]. The company's scale — largest healthcare staffing provider in the U.S. — provides leverage in vendor management and rapid crisis deployment that smaller competitors cannot match. Q4 2025 revenue of $748M already showed a 2% YoY inflection point after years of post-COVID decline [1].
The bear case centers on revenue cyclicality, AI disruption risk, and the sustainability of labor disruption revenue. The $600M in projected Q1 2026 labor disruption revenue is inherently volatile and unpredictable — it depends on healthcare worker strikes and acute staffing crises that can resolve quickly, as demonstrated by the 76% revenue decline from the 2022 COVID-driven peak ($5.24B) to 2025 ($2.73B) [1][4]. Trailing ROE of -14.2% and profit margin of -3.5% reflect genuine earnings weakness, not just one-time items. D/E of 125% is elevated for a services company. The Technology and Workforce Solutions segment — which should be AMN's growth engine — is projected to decline 16-18% YoY in Q1 2026, suggesting technology adoption is not offsetting core staffing headwinds [1]. AI disruption risk is material: AI-native workforce matching platforms could disintermediate traditional staffing agencies by enabling direct facility-to-worker connections, potentially compressing the staffing margins that AMN depends on. With only 2,664 corporate employees and an $811M market cap, the company has limited resources to outspend AI-native competitors on technology development [4].

AI Resilience (25%)
AI Resilience HIGH RISK
4
AI Risk Protection (3/9): AMN Healthcare faces significant AI disruption risk across its core business. Healthcare staffing — matching nurses, therapists, and physicians with facility needs — is fundamentally an information-matching and credential-verification process that AI platforms can increasingly automate [2]. AI-powered scheduling and workforce optimization tools could reduce the need for intermediary staffing agencies by enabling hospitals to better manage their own workforce internally. The Technology and Workforce Solutions segment (vendor management systems, workforce optimization) is directly exposed to AI-native competitors building more capable platforms. Physician and leadership search is vulnerable to AI-enhanced matching algorithms. The Nurse and Allied segment retains some protection because physical healthcare workers performing clinical care cannot be replaced by AI, and crisis staffing (labor disruptions, travel nursing) requires rapid mobilization of human professionals that benefits from AMN's scale. However, the intermediary matching function is AI's sweet spot, and management's own emphasis on AI deployment [2] implicitly acknowledges the need to stay ahead of disruption. AI Opportunity (1/1): AMN generates meaningful AI-adjacent value through its AI-powered predictive analytics for staffing matching, scheduling optimization, and credential management [2]. CDIO Hagan has specifically cited AI and ML as enablers of organizational goals, and the Televate teletherapy platform represents technology-enabled service delivery.
