Investment Thesis
Alpha Modus is a ~$18M micro-cap vertical AI company pursuing a two-track strategy: patent enforcement against retail technology users (Lowe's, Broadcom, Sensormatic/Johnson Controls, Navori, A2Z, meldCX) and commercial deployment of the AlphaCash financial service kiosk network [1][3]. The Q1 2026 pilot with ~100 AlphaCash kiosks across a Texas retail chain, and an identified opportunity of 4,000+ additional U.S. retail locations, offers operational upside if the pilot converts at management's conservative $2,000/month per-kiosk baseline [1]. Recent IP enforcement settlements (Mood Media dismissed with prejudice January 2026; additional settlements announced March 2026) provide episodic cash flows [3]. One analyst 12-month price target of $1.50 implies material upside from $0.36.
The bear case is decisive. Alpha Modus FAILS the IOWN liquidity floor: $0.36 price x 818k avg daily volume = $295k of daily dollar liquidity, well below the $1M threshold, which forces Infrastructure to 0 [4]. TTM losses of ~$4.3M and no product revenue visibility beyond early-stage AlphaCash pilots mean the company depends on patent settlements and dilutive capital raises (a $250M S-3 shelf is filed) to fund operations [3]. The patent-enforcement business model is inherently adversarial, and governance concerns exist around funding agreements with CEO-controlled entities (Alpha Modus Ventures) [3]. Retail AI is a crowded space where Amazon, Google, and better-funded startups own meaningful technology. The combination of micro-cap illiquidity, going-concern-level losses, litigation-dependent cash flow, and dilution risk makes AMOD unsuitable for IOWN. SELL.
