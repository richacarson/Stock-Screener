Company Profile
Industrials ·Electrical Equipment & Parts ·NYSE ·United States
Amprius Technologies, Inc. develops, manufactures, and markets lithium-ion batteries with silicon anode technology for mobility applications. The company provides batteries under the SiCore and SiMaxx product platforms, achieving industry-leading energy density for applications in defense (drones, unmanned aerial systems), light electric vehicles, and aerospace. Amprius is scaling production through partnerships including the Amprius Korea Battery Alliance. Founded in 2008, headquartered in Fremont, California.

Investment Thesis
Amprius Technologies has achieved a genuine technological breakthrough in silicon anode batteries, delivering industry-leading energy density of 450+ Wh/kg and rapidly scaling revenue from $4.2M in 2022 to $71.9M in 2025 [1][2]. Q1 2026 revenue of $28.5M (up 153% YoY) and raised full-year guidance of $130M+ demonstrate accelerating commercial traction, particularly in defense applications where the U.S. military's increasing drone and autonomous systems programs provide a growing addressable market. The company achieved 20% gross margin in Q1 2026 (up from negative) and is approaching Adjusted EBITDA breakeven at $4M+ for the full year [1]. At $2.1B market cap, the stock is priced for significant growth but the underlying technology and defense demand provide credible support.
The investment case is severely undermined by corporate governance concerns. Short seller Manatee Research published a report in May 2026 alleging exaggerated orders, hollow manufacturing partners (including Eurocell, whose Korean factory was seized by prosecutors), and bill-and-hold revenue recognition that inflates reported revenue [4]. Insiders sold over $79M of stock between November 2025 and May 2026, and Block & Leviton is investigating the company for potential securities law violations. The stock has declined from $18.67 (50-day SMA) to $15.00, reflecting growing investor skepticism. Even setting aside the fraud allegations, Amprius remains deeply unprofitable with -44% profit margins, -44% ROE, and a Forward P/E of 258.6x [1]. The CEO transition from founder Dr. Kang Sun to Tom Stepien adds execution risk at a critical scaling moment, and competition from deep-pocketed incumbents (CATL, LG, Panasonic) developing their own silicon anode technologies could compress Amprius' technological lead [3].
