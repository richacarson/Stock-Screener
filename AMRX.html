Investment Thesis
Amneal Pharmaceuticals is a turnaround specialty-generics story that delivered its sixth consecutive year of revenue growth in 2025, reaching $3.02B (+8%) with Q4 revenue of $814M (+11%) and adjusted EBITDA of $688M for the full year [1]. The Specialty segment grew 19% led by CREXONT for Parkinson's disease and UNITHROID, with Q4 Specialty up 38% — a genuine pivot from commodity generics into higher-margin branded therapeutics [1]. 2026 guidance calls for revenue of $3.05-$3.15B, adjusted EBITDA of $720-$760M, and adjusted diluted EPS of $0.93-$1.03 (15-20% EPS growth) [1]. The stock trades at high GAAP P/E (~60x) but forward P/E near 10x on adjusted earnings, giving the business optionality if the Specialty growth continues and deleveraging progresses.
The bear case is substantial. In August 2025, the FDA issued Amneal a warning letter tied to manufacturing deficiencies underlying recent recalls of vancomycin, ropivacaine, magnesium sulfate, and sulfamethoxazole/trimethoprim products — signaling persistent quality-system issues and potential facility action [3]. Legacy debt from the 2018 Impax combination keeps debt/equity elevated well above the Infrastructure floor. 2026 revenue guidance of only 1-4% growth is modest and suggests Specialty acceleration is needed to re-rate the stock. Governance concerns persist around the Patel-family dual-CEO 'cult of personality' culture cited in Glassdoor reviews [2]. Faith alignment is MISALIGNED (-62) per Inspire Insight due to Abortifacients attribution (oral contraceptives/emergency contraception in generic portfolio). Overall 51 = WATCH — potential turnaround but real operational, governance, and faith-alignment concerns.
