Investment Thesis
Amrize is a well-positioned building materials leader with $11.8B revenue, $3.0B adjusted EBITDA, and improving margins — targeting 70 bps expansion in 2026 with 4-6% revenue growth and 8-11% EBITDA growth guidance [1]. Forward P/E of 16.88 is attractive for a capital-intensive company with strong free cash flow generation. The PB Materials acquisition strengthens the aggregates business in high-growth West Texas, and the ASPIRE program targets $250M in synergies by 2028 [1].
The bear case includes cyclicality risk in construction spending, which is sensitive to interest rates and housing starts. Revenue growth of just 0.9% in 2025 shows the company is volume-constrained in a flat construction environment [1]. D/E of 47.9% is manageable but rising CapEx ($900M in 2026) could pressure free cash flow. The cement industry faces increasing ESG scrutiny over carbon emissions, which could drive regulatory costs. ROE of 10.2% is below the 15% target, suggesting returns on capital are modest for the risk profile [1].
Key Catalysts
  1. ASPIRE efficiency program targeting $250M in synergies by 2028, with 70 bps margin expansion expected in 2026 [1]
  2. PB Materials acquisition contributing $180M+ revenue with synergies expected to be EPS accretive in 2026 [1]
  3. Infrastructure spending tailwinds from government programs and AI data center construction driving building materials demand [1]
Key Risks
  1. Construction cyclicality — revenue is sensitive to housing starts, commercial construction, and infrastructure spending which correlate with interest rates [1]
  2. ESG/carbon regulation — cement production is carbon-intensive and faces increasing regulatory scrutiny that could drive compliance costs [1]
  3. Integration risk — PB Materials acquisition and ASPIRE synergy targets may underperform, pressuring margins and returns [1]
