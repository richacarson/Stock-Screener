Company Profile
Industrials ·Specialty Business Services ·NYSE ·United States
Amentum Holdings is a global leader in advanced engineering, technology, and mission-critical government services. Formed in late 2024 through the merger of legacy Amentum (a Jacobs Engineering spinoff) and AECOM's Management Services, the company operates through Digital Solutions (intelligence analytics, cybersecurity, space systems) and Global Engineering Solutions (environmental remediation, nuclear power, platform sustainment, supply chain management) segments serving U.S. government and allied nations.

Investment Thesis
Amentum is a newly formed $14B+ revenue government services platform with a record $47.2B backlog and $23B in pending awards, created through the late-2024 merger of legacy Amentum and AECOM Management Services [1]. The forward P/E of just 10.0x against FY2026 adjusted EPS guidance of $2.25-2.45 suggests the market is undervaluing the earnings power of the combined entity, particularly as merger integration drives margin expansion toward the 8%+ adjusted EBITDA margin target [1]. Key growth vectors include nuclear energy (over $1B in Q1 awards), digital solutions (intelligence analytics, cybersecurity), and space systems. Free cash flow guidance of $525-575M provides meaningful capital allocation flexibility for a $6.7B market cap company. The massive backlog provides multi-year revenue visibility unusual for a company of this valuation [1].
The bear case focuses on merger integration risk and razor-thin profitability. GAAP profit margin of 0.69% and ROE of 1.8% reflect a business that has not yet demonstrated the profitability expected of a mature services company — net income was just $66M on $14.4B revenue in FY2025 [4]. Total debt of $3.94B against only $247M cash creates financial fragility if integration synergies fail to materialize or if government spending is cut under DOGE or sequestration scenarios [4]. Revenue declined 5.2% YoY due to contract transitions, and organic growth of only ~3% is modest for a company that needs to demonstrate the merger's strategic rationale [1]. The government services sector faces secular headwinds from the current administration's cost-cutting posture, and Amentum's heavy dependence on U.S. government contracts (defense, intelligence, energy) creates concentration risk. Insider activity shows no open-market buying, suggesting executives may be waiting for clearer integration progress [4].
Key Catalysts
  1. Merger integration synergies driving adjusted EBITDA margins from 8.1% toward the $1.1-1.14B target, demonstrating the strategic rationale of the combined entity [1]
  2. Nuclear energy contract wins accelerating — over $1B in Q1 FY2026 alone — as energy transition creates secular demand for remediation and new plant services [1]
  3. Pending awards of $23B converting to contract wins, driving book-to-bill above 1.0x and providing confidence in multi-year revenue growth trajectory [1]
Key Risks
  1. GAAP profitability risk — 0.69% net margin and $3.94B debt create vulnerability if merger synergies underperform or government contract margins compress [4]
  2. U.S. government spending cuts under DOGE or budget sequestration could directly impact Amentum's $14B revenue base concentrated in federal contracts [1]
  3. Integration execution risk — combining 50,000 employees from two distinct organizational cultures while maintaining contract performance and client relationships [2]
