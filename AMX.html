Investment Thesis
América Móvil is the dominant Latin American telecommunications leader, operating wireless, fiber, and broadband across 23 countries with 1M+ km of fiber, 5 satellites, and 38 data centers under Slim-family stewardship [3]. Q4 2025 delivered revenue growth of 3.4% in pesos (6.2% constant FX), service revenue up 5.3%, net profit quadrupled YoY, and EBITDA up 4.2% in pesos (6.9% constant FX) as mobile postpaid and broadband verticals accelerated [1][2]. Balance sheet discipline is exemplary — net debt/EBITDA fell to 1.52x, free cash flow grew ~40% to MXN 82B, and 2026 CapEx is guided to 14-15% of revenue ($6.8-7.0B) [1]. At $27.15 and 17.3x trailing P/E with a ~2.1% dividend yield, AMX offers stable emerging-market telco cash flow with real 5G and fiber scale benefits. Data-center infrastructure positions AMX as a beneficiary of Latin American AI hyperscaler buildout.
The bear case centers on Inspire misalignment, regulatory exposure, and OTT disintermediation. The Inspire score of -62 with a 'Sexually Explicit' negative attribution tied to content carried on AMX platforms materially disqualifies the stock for many faith-aligned investors [6]. Mexican and Brazilian regulators have historically pressured AMX with sharing mandates and spectrum obligations, and political populism in Latin America could tighten telecom regulation further [3]. OTT services (WhatsApp, Netflix, YouTube) have long eroded voice, SMS, and Pay TV revenue, and AI-native content creation could accelerate cord-cutting. FX translation risk on the Mexican peso and Brazilian real adds earnings volatility for U.S. ADR holders. Q4 2025 EPS of $0.37 missed consensus by $0.06 despite the revenue beat, showing margin and FX headwinds [1]. Leverage at 1.52x net debt/EBITDA is well-managed but constrains flexibility. At 17.3x trailing P/E the stock is fairly rather than cheaply valued.
