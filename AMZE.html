Company Profile

Amaze Holdings, Inc. (formerly Fresh Vine Wine, Inc., renamed March 2025) operates an end-to-end, creator-powered commerce platform offering tools for product creation, e-commerce storefronts, subscription offerings, digital content delivery, and managed services. The platform targets independent digital entrepreneurs and small businesses, integrating storefront customization, payment processing, merchandising, and performance analytics. The company also retains a wine products segment selling across the United States and Puerto Rico. Headquartered in Costa Mesa, California, it trades on the NYSE American (AMEX) with a micro-cap market capitalization of approximately $7 million.

Investment Thesis
Amaze Holdings is a micro-cap speculative turnaround built on the premise that a creator-powered commerce platform — operating through an LA Times Studios distribution partnership and an AI-driven Moments product — can achieve escape velocity in the $100B+ creator economy [1][2]. The bull case is narrow but real: management has secured a distribution partnership with one of the largest media properties in the United States (100M+ monthly unique users), disclosed $12M year-two revenue targets for modular vertical launches, and is building a data monetization strategy around creator and consumer behavioral data — a high-margin business model if GMV scales [1][2]. The Inspire Insight ALIGNED rating (+33) with positive attributions across customer welfare, sustainability, and ethical supply chain suggests the company's creator-welfare orientation may attract mission-aligned entrepreneurs and partners who have alternative platform choices [4].
The bear case is overwhelming. Full-year 2025 revenue was just $2.0M while operating losses reached $54.4M (including a $34.3M goodwill impairment), and the company lists zero employees — providing no operational foundation for the ambitious vertical launch strategy [1][3]. The stock declined from a 52-week high of $12.42 to $0.173, triggering the IOWN liquidity floor and reflecting near-total market skepticism [3]. The forward P/E of 0.60 implies either an extraordinary earnings recovery or speculative distortion — neither validates near-term investment. Amaze competes directly against Shopify, Patreon, and Spring with no disclosed technology differentiation, no R&D budget, and a market cap smaller than most Series A funding rounds. All revenue targets are management projections with no third-party validation [1][2]. This stock is appropriate only for investors with very high risk tolerance and full expectation of potential total loss.
