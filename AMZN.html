Investment Thesis
Amazon is a compelling BUY as a scaled AI infrastructure platform with AWS generating $142B annualized revenue (24% growth) at 35% operating margins [1]. The $244B performance obligations backlog (up 40% YoY) provides multi-year revenue visibility, and Bedrock's 60% QoQ customer spend growth validates Amazon's AI platform strategy [1]. At 22.0x forward P/E, the stock is reasonably valued for a company with 14% revenue growth, improving margins, and massive AI optionality. The advertising business ($85B+ annually, growing 22%) is a high-margin revenue stream that is still in early innings. Net income of $77.7B and operating cash flow exceeding $100B provide firepower for the $200B CapEx plan without compromising financial health [1].
The bear case centers on the $200B CapEx plan creating margin and FCF pressure through 2026-2027. The 8% stock drop on the CapEx announcement signals market concern about capital returns declining [1]. AWS growth of 24% trails Azure (39%) and Google Cloud (48%), raising questions about competitive position despite the larger base. Depreciation and ramp costs will increase significantly as $200B in assets come online, potentially compressing margins. The e-commerce and North America retail segments face competitive pressure from Temu, Shein, and Walmart. Regulatory risk from antitrust scrutiny (FTC lawsuit, EU investigations) threatens the platform model. Additionally, if AI spending proves cyclical rather than structural, the massive CapEx commitment could become a structural ROIC drag [1].
Key Catalysts
  1. Trainium 3 launch with nearly full supply commitment by mid-2026, establishing Amazon as a top-tier AI chip provider alongside NVIDIA [1]
  2. AWS re-acceleration above 24% growth as AI workloads scale and $244B backlog converts to revenue [1]
  3. Advertising revenue trajectory toward $100B+ as AI-powered targeting and Prime Video ad tier gain traction [1]
Key Risks
  1. $200B CapEx commitment creating margin and free cash flow pressure — if AI demand moderates, Amazon faces a structural over-investment problem [1]
  2. AWS competitive pressure from Azure (39% growth) and Google Cloud (48% growth) potentially eroding market share in the fastest-growing cloud segments [1]
  3. Antitrust and regulatory risk — FTC lawsuit, EU investigations, and potential breakup scenarios could constrain Amazon's platform integration advantages
