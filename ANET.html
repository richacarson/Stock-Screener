Courage to Lead

Raising 2026 revenue guidance to 25% growth ($11.25B) while acknowledging supply chain inflation shows confidence to invest through uncertainty.

Investment Thesis
Arista Networks is the dominant provider of AI data center networking infrastructure, delivering Q4 2025 revenue of $2.49B (up 28.9% YoY) with net income exceeding $1B quarterly for the first time [1]. The company raised its 2026 revenue guidance to $11.25B (25% growth) with AI networking revenue targeted to double to $3.25B, driven by unprecedented hyperscaler capital expenditure on AI infrastructure [1]. With a virtually debt-free balance sheet ($10.74B cash, D/E 0.73%), 39% net margins, and 48.2% non-GAAP operating margins, Arista combines hypergrowth with best-in-class profitability [1][4]. Under Jayshree Ullal's 18-year founding leadership, the company has consistently disrupted legacy networking and is now positioned as essential AI infrastructure alongside NVIDIA and Broadcom. Q1 2026 guidance of $2.6B topped consensus by $150M, signaling sustained momentum [1].
The bear case centers on customer concentration, valuation, and supply chain risk. Microsoft and Meta represent approximately 30-40% of revenue, creating significant concentration risk if either hyperscaler slows AI capex spending [1]. At 28.3x forward P/E and 43.9x trailing P/E, the stock prices in sustained 25%+ growth that is not guaranteed beyond the current AI infrastructure buildout cycle [4]. Memory shortages are expected to persist for multiple years, and Arista has signaled price increases on memory-intensive SKUs that could pressure customer relationships [1]. The stock declined 3.96% after the Q4 earnings beat, suggesting the market already discounts strong results. Competition from Broadcom's custom networking ASICs and NVIDIA's Spectrum-X Ethernet platform could erode Arista's AI networking share if hyperscalers shift to vertically integrated solutions. Revenue per employee of $1.76M implies lean operations but also limited operational redundancy.
Key Catalysts
  1. AI networking revenue doubling to $3.25B in 2026 as hyperscaler AI infrastructure buildout accelerates — raised from prior $2.75B target on surging demand [1]
  2. Customer diversification with one or two additional 10% customers expected in 2026 beyond Microsoft and Meta, reducing concentration risk [1]
  3. Q1 2026 revenue guidance of $2.6B exceeded consensus by $150M, with full-year 2026 target of $11.25B representing 25% growth [1]
Key Risks
  1. Customer concentration — Microsoft and Meta represent ~35% of revenue; any slowdown in hyperscaler AI capex would disproportionately impact Arista [1]
  2. Memory supply chain constraints expected to persist for years with price increases on memory-intensive SKUs, potentially compressing gross margins from the 64.6% FY2025 level [1]
  3. Competitive threat from NVIDIA Spectrum-X and Broadcom custom ASICs that could shift AI networking share toward vertically integrated solutions [2]
