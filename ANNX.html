Lead programs include tanruprubart for Guillain-Barré syndrome and vonaprument (ANX007) for geographic atrophy.

Investment Thesis
Annexon is a clinical-stage biotech with a differentiated complement-targeting platform approaching transformative milestones in 2026: a potential BLA filing for tanruprubart in GBS (first approved treatment for the disease) and Phase 3 topline data for vonaprument in geographic atrophy (659-patient ARCHER II study) [1][3]. With $238.3M in cash funding operations into H2 2027, the company has runway through these catalysts.
The $939M market cap reflects significant optionality on the pipeline — GBS approval alone could generate meaningful peak sales in an uncontested market, while GA represents a multi-billion-dollar opportunity competing against Apellis and Iveric Bio's approved complement inhibitors [3]. However, clinical-stage risk is extreme: binary trial outcomes could destroy 50%+ of market value, the Glassdoor feedback about aggressive management culture raises execution concerns, and the zero-revenue profile means Infrastructure scores are inherently weak [4]. For IOWN, the WATCH rating reflects strong Innovation and AI resilience offset by pre-revenue financial fragility and cultural red flags.
Key Catalysts
  1. Tanruprubart BLA filing with FDA in 2026 for GBS, with MAA already filed in Europe — potential first approved treatment for the disease [1][3]
  2. Vonaprument Phase 3 ARCHER II topline data expected H2 2026 in geographic atrophy with 659 patients enrolled [3]
  3. ANX1502 oral small molecule proof-of-concept in cold agglutinin disease expected 2026, validating the next-gen platform [3]

  2. Cash runway extends only to H2 2027, requiring additional capital raises that would dilute existing shareholders if milestones slip [3]
  3. Glassdoor reports of micromanagement and aggressive culture could impact execution quality and talent retention at a critical juncture [4]
