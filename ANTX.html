Company Profile
Healthcare ·Biotechnology ·NASDAQ ·United States
AN2 Therapeutics, Inc. is a clinical-stage biopharmaceutical company focused on discovering and developing novel small molecule therapeutics derived from its proprietary boron chemistry platform. The company's lead candidate is epetraborole, a once-daily oral treatment in Phase 3 clinical trials for non-tuberculous mycobacterial (NTM) lung disease, as well as in Phase 2 for acute melioidosis. The pipeline also includes AN2-502998 (AN153) in development for Chagas disease.
21 Employees ·www.an2therapeutics.com

Investment Thesis
AN2 Therapeutics offers speculative exposure to a differentiated boron chemistry anti-infective platform targeting rare and neglected infectious diseases with significant unmet need. Epetraborole is in Phase 3 for NTM lung disease — a condition with limited approved therapies and a growing patient population — and Phase 2 for acute melioidosis, while AN153 targets Chagas disease [1]. CEO Eric Easom's 22-year track record in infectious disease drug development, including the Anacor platform acquired by Pfizer for $5.2B, provides credibility for the boron chemistry approach [2]. The $40M private placement and improving net loss trajectory (-$35M in 2025 vs. -$64M in 2023) suggest better capital discipline, and the $173M market cap provides meaningful upside if Phase 3 data is positive [1][4].
The bear case centers on clinical and financial risk inherent to a 21-person pre-revenue biotech. Cash of $58M against a ~$35M annual burn rate provides limited runway without additional capital raises, which would be dilutive to existing shareholders [4]. Phase 3 NTM data carries binary risk — failure would be devastating for a single-platform company, and the FDA pathway for NTM treatments is uncertain given the small patient population and complex trial design. Competition from Insmed's established NTM franchise and potential future entrants could limit commercial opportunity even with approval. The company's focus on rare and neglected diseases, while socially admirable, limits total addressable market size and may constrain peak revenue potential. At -$0.93 consensus EPS for 2026, AN2 remains deeply unprofitable with no near-term revenue visibility [1].
