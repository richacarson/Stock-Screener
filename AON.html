Investment Thesis
Aon is a best-in-class global insurance broker and professional services firm trading at 15.0x forward earnings with a 21.5% profit margin, 46.9% ROE, and $3.06B in annual free cash flow [4]. Full-year 2025 revenue of $17.18B grew 9.45% with 6% organic growth for the second consecutive year, and 2026 guidance calls for continued mid-single-digit organic growth with 70-80 bps of margin expansion [1]. The asset-light brokerage model generates high returns on invested capital, and the NFP acquisition in 2024 expanded the addressable market into the mid-market segment. Greg Case's 21-year leadership provides strategic continuity, and the firm's proprietary data and analytics capabilities create sustainable competitive advantages in risk pricing and human capital advisory [2].
The bear case centers on valuation, leverage, and macro sensitivity. D/E of 168.3% with $16.1B in debt reflects aggressive financial engineering — while serviceable given $3B+ FCF, rising rates increase carry costs [4]. The PEG of 2.55 suggests mid-single-digit organic growth is already priced in, limiting upside unless Aon accelerates. Revenue growth of 3.7% in the most recent quarter was below the full-year 9.45% rate, suggesting possible deceleration [1]. Insurance brokerage revenue is cyclical and correlated with premium rates — a soft insurance market could compress organic growth. Integration risk from the $13B NFP acquisition remains, and the prior failed Willis merger highlights execution uncertainty. The 1.0% dividend yield provides minimal income support for a $69B market cap company [4].
Key Catalysts
  1. 2026 guidance for mid-single-digit organic growth with 70-80 bps margin expansion and double-digit FCF growth signals continued operational momentum [1]
  2. NFP acquisition integration creating mid-market cross-selling opportunities and expanding the addressable market for Aon's data analytics platform [1]
  3. AI-driven risk analytics and ESG advisory capabilities positioning Aon as an indispensable advisor in an increasingly complex risk landscape [2][3]
Key Risks
  1. Elevated leverage (D/E 168.3%, $16.1B debt) with rising interest rates increasing carry costs on the acquisition-driven capital structure [4]
  2. Insurance market cyclicality — a soft premium environment could compress organic revenue growth below mid-single-digit guidance [1]
  3. Integration execution risk from the $13B NFP acquisition and potential for margin dilution during the multi-year integration period [1]
