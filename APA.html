Company Profile
Energy ·Oil & Gas E&P ·NASDAQ ·United States
APA Corporation is an independent energy company that explores for, develops, and produces natural gas, crude oil, and natural gas liquids. The company operates in the United States (Permian Basin), Egypt, and the North Sea, with exploration activities in Suriname. Founded in 1954, APA completed its acquisition of Callon Petroleum in 2024 and has paid dividends since 1965.

Investment Thesis
APA delivered Q1 2026 net income of $446M ($1.26/share) with adjusted EPS of $1.38, beating the $1.08 consensus by 28%, and generated nearly $500M in free cash flow [1]. At 5.34x forward earnings with 3.03% dividend yield, APA is among the most attractively valued E&P companies. The Callon integration is delivering $350M+ in cost savings, U.S. oil production of 124K bopd exceeded guidance from Permian efficiency gains, and net debt reduction continues with $4.54B against $293M cash [5]. Egypt operations provide international diversification with strong gas production growth, and the Suriname GranMorgu development with TotalEnergies offers exploration optionality.
The bear case centers on commodity price vulnerability and declining revenue trajectories. Revenue fell 15.2% YoY in Q1 2026 and 5.3% for FY2025 as oil prices weakened, and the 5-year revenue CAGR of just 3.6% reflects the cyclical nature of E&P earnings [1][5]. The DOJ/EPA enforcement action for Clean Air Act violations at 422 well pads ($4M penalty plus $5.5M remediation) creates ongoing regulatory and reputational risk [4]. The 2020 dividend cut from $1.00 to $0.10 (now restored to $1.00) eliminated dividend growth credibility — the stock has been a dead-money investment over 5 years despite the current deep value metrics. APA's lack of diversification into renewables leaves it structurally exposed to long-term energy transition risk, and the stock has dropped even after Q1 2026 earnings beats, suggesting the market sees limited re-rating catalysts [1].
Key Catalysts
  1. Suriname GranMorgu development progress with TotalEnergies — successful appraisal could unlock a world-class offshore resource and materially rerate the stock
  2. Continued debt reduction toward sub-$4B target and potential dividend increase as free cash flow generation sustains at ~$500M quarterly
  3. Palantir AI partnership delivering measurable operational cost savings across supply chain, predictive maintenance, and production optimization driving margin expansion
Key Risks
  1. Commodity price sensitivity — a sustained oil price below $60/bbl would compress free cash flow and force capital allocation trade-offs between dividends, debt reduction, and development spending
  2. Revenue declined 15.2% YoY in Q1 2026 reflecting commodity weakness; the 5-year revenue CAGR of 3.6% suggests structural low-growth characteristics masked by commodity price volatility
  3. Regulatory and environmental liability — DOJ/EPA enforcement for Clean Air Act violations at 422 well pads in NM/TX, with escalating emissions monitoring and potential for additional enforcement actions
