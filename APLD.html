Investment Thesis
Applied Digital is a high-conviction AI infrastructure play with $16B in prospective lease revenue across 600 MW of committed capacity. Q3 FY2026 revenue of $126.6M massively beat the $76.6M consensus estimate, and the company's existing Jamestown (106 MW) and Ellendale (180 MW) facilities are operating at full capacity [1]. Two Polaris Forge campuses under simultaneous construction will bring 600 MW online through calendar 2026-2027, with lease revenues already ramping from the first 100 MW data center after $1B+ investment.
The bear case is execution risk at scale — building two 300 MW campuses simultaneously with 205 employees and $687M+ in debt creates vulnerability to construction delays, supply chain bottlenecks (transformers, cooling components), and cost overruns [1]. The -59.5% profit margin and negative forward P/E of -63.7 reflect a company years from self-sustaining profitability. CoreWeave customer concentration creates cascading risk if that customer faces its own financial stress. At $44.59 per share ($12.7B market cap), the stock prices in significant future success — any deceleration in AI infrastructure spending would amplify losses given APLD's heavy fixed-cost structure [1][3].
Key Catalysts
  1. Polaris Forge 1 and Forge 2 campus completions through calendar 2026-2027, with lease revenue recognition accelerating as capacity comes online [1]
  2. Potential announcement of additional 900 MW across three sites in advanced discussions, which would more than double the company's committed capacity [1]
  3. Transition to positive EPS as construction phase matures into operational recurring revenue, with analysts projecting profitability inflection in FY2027 [1]
Key Risks
  1. Construction delays or supply chain bottlenecks for transformers, cooling, and GPUs could defer revenue recognition and increase cash burn beyond current $687M+ debt capacity [1]
  2. CoreWeave customer concentration — majority of contracted revenue depends on a single counterparty that itself carries extreme leverage [1]
  3. Negative profit margins (-59.5%) and negative forward P/E (-63.7) mean any slowdown in AI infrastructure spending would amplify losses given the heavy fixed-cost structure [3]
