Applied Digital Corporation designs, develops, and operates next-generation AI data center infrastructure ('AI Factories') with purpose-built high-performance computing (HPC) facilities featuring large-scale liquid cooling systems. Investment Thesis
Applied Digital is positioned at the epicenter of the AI infrastructure buildout, with $11B+ in committed long-term lease value across CoreWeave (400MW, ~$11B over 15 years) and an investment-grade hyperscaler ($5B over 15 years for 200MW at Polaris Forge 2) [1][2]. Revenue surged 250% YoY to $126.6M in fiscal Q2 2026, with HPC hosting revenue of $85M as the first 100MW at Polaris Forge 1 came online [1]. The pipeline of 830MW+ across three campuses, backed by Macquarie development financing, positions APLD to generate $1B+ in annual rental revenue at full build-out. At $11.8B market cap, the stock trades at roughly 1x committed lifetime lease value, offering significant upside if execution continues on schedule.
The bear case is substantial: Applied Digital is deeply unprofitable with a -59.5% profit margin, -$1.5B in free cash flow, and $2.83B in total debt against $1.73B cash [8]. Construction execution risk is acute — any delays at Polaris Forge 2 or the third campus could trigger covenant violations on the Macquarie facility and jeopardize the revenue trajectory. Tenant concentration is extreme, with CoreWeave representing the vast majority of committed capacity; CoreWeave's own financial health and ability to pay depends on continued AI spending by its own customers (primarily NVIDIA and Microsoft). The GAAP net loss of $100.9M in fiscal Q3 2026 (-$0.36/share) missed estimates by $0.20, highlighting ongoing execution variability [1]. Share dilution has been persistent, with the share count rising from ~170M to ~285M over the past year.
Key Catalysts
  1. Polaris Forge 2 initial operations in calendar 2026 with 200MW leased to an investment-grade hyperscaler, projected to generate ~$333M annually at full capacity
  2. Transition from construction-phase losses to recurring rental revenue as Polaris Forge 1 reaches full 400MW utilization, targeting cash flow positivity by late FY2027
  3. Potential additional hyperscaler lease announcements for the third campus (150MW, anticipated calendar 2027) could derisk the growth trajectory and support re-rating
Key Risks
  1. Extreme tenant concentration — CoreWeave accounts for the majority of committed lease value, and CoreWeave's own financial stability depends on sustained AI compute demand from a small number of hyperscaler customers
  2. Construction execution risk across three simultaneous campus projects, with any material delays threatening debt covenants on the $2.4B Macquarie development facility and revenue timelines
  3. Persistent share dilution (share count up ~68% in the past year) and deep unprofitability (-$0.36/share GAAP loss in Q3 FY2026) create overhang and could pressure the stock if AI sentiment cools
