Investment Thesis
Apple Hospitality REIT is a defensively positioned select-service hotel REIT with 221 upscale properties (~29,900 rooms across 85 markets) under premium brands (Residence Inn, Hampton, Hilton Garden Inn, Courtyard)[2]. The stock yields 7.96% via a monthly $0.08 dividend ($0.96 annualized), providing reliable income for IOWN dividend sleeve investors[3]. 2025 comparable hotel EBITDA of $474M with 34.3% margin is described as 'industry-leading,' and the lean 65-employee corporate structure with 12+ year CEO tenure (Justin Knight) offers governance stability. Insider buying by Executive Chairman Glade Knight ($95,899 in November 2025) signals leadership conviction at current prices[3]. Forward P/E of 17.6 is reasonable for a high-yielding hotel REIT.
However, fundamentals are softening: 2025 comparable RevPAR was $118 (-1.6% YoY), Q4 2025 RevPAR was $107 (-2.6%), and 2026 guidance calls for flat RevPAR in the -1% to +1% range[1]. EBITDA margins compressed 190-210 bps versus 2024. Government room nights were down 12% for full-year 2025 due to policy uncertainty and reduced government travel, and the dividend payout ratio is elevated relative to GAAP earnings. The dividend was cut/paused during COVID so consecutive years of growth is 0. Bear case: a recession compresses leisure travel as well, forcing further dividend pressure. The faith alignment score of +51 is positive.
