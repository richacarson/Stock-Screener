Company Profile
Technology ·Software - Application ·NASDAQ ·United States
Digital Turbine operates a mobile growth platform serving advertisers, publishers, carriers, and device OEMs through two segments: On Device Solutions (app preloads, content delivery, programmatic advertising on mobile devices) and App Growth Platform (DSP, Offer Wall, ad monetization). The company's products enable brand discovery, user acquisition, and operational efficiency across the mobile application ecosystem. Headquartered in Austin, Texas.
647 Employees ·www.digitalturbine.com

Investment Thesis
Digital Turbine is a deeply discounted mobile ad tech turnaround trading at just 4.8x forward earnings with 76% EBITDA growth in the most recent quarter [1]. FQ3 2026 revenue of $151.4M grew 12% YoY, with international business surging 60%+ and exceeding 25% of ODS revenue for the first time. Adjusted EBITDA guidance of $100-105M for FY2026 implies continued margin expansion, and the DT iQ AI/ML platform positions the company to improve targeting and campaign ROI for advertisers [2]. The appointment of AI-focused CTO Ben John (ex-Microsoft, Xandr) signals serious commitment to AI-driven product development. At $3.01 per share with $34.2M free cash flow, the stock prices in significant risk and offers asymmetric upside if the revenue recovery continues and margins normalize.
The bear case is substantial and explains the deep discount. Revenue has declined 34% from the $748M FY2022 peak to $491M, with a 3-year CAGR of -13.1% — the turnaround is not yet proven over a full cycle [4]. D/E of 186% with $362.7M debt against just $40.2M cash creates real financial risk if revenue recovery stalls. Trailing ROE of -27.5% and profit margin of -9.1% mean the company is still losing money on a GAAP basis [4]. Platform risk is existential: Apple's ATT privacy framework and Google's Privacy Sandbox are constraining the data that powers Digital Turbine's targeting, and both companies could build native app discovery features that bypass on-device third-party solutions entirely. The $361M market cap and $3.01 share price create liquidity risk — daily trading volume of $8.9M is adequate but could thin in a downturn. CEO Stone acknowledged that alternate app discovery channels could erode on-device relevance [2].
Key Catalysts
  1. International revenue inflection — 60%+ growth with ODS international revenue exceeding 25% of total for the first time, providing a new growth vector independent of the mature U.S. carrier market [1]
  2. AI-powered product evolution through DT iQ platform and new CTO Ben John, potentially improving targeting ROI enough to reverse the revenue decline and expand advertiser wallet share [2]
  3. Margin expansion trajectory — FQ3 2026 adjusted EBITDA of $38.8M (76% YoY growth) on 12% revenue growth demonstrates significant operating leverage as the business scales back toward peak revenue levels [1]
Key Risks
  1. Platform dependency risk — Apple ATT and Google Privacy Sandbox are constraining targeting data, and both platforms could build native app discovery features that bypass Digital Turbine's on-device model entirely [2]
  2. Revenue recovery uncertainty — 34% decline from $748M peak to $491M with -13.1% 3-year CAGR; the turnaround must be sustained over multiple quarters to prove structural rather than cyclical [4]
  3. Balance sheet fragility — D/E of 186% with $362.7M debt against $40.2M cash, creating refinancing risk and limiting strategic flexibility if the mobile ad market deteriorates further [4]

AI Resilience MODERATE

AI Risk Protection (4/9): Digital Turbine operates in mobile advertising technology, a space where AI creates both opportunity and significant disruption risk [2]. AI Opportunity (1/1): The company's DT iQ platform leverages AI/ML for targeting and campaign optimization, and CEO Stone reports AI is deployed across the entire business including QA, coding, automation, and campaign setup — generating measurable efficiency gains for a 647-person company [2].
