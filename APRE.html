Company Profile
Healthcare ·Biotechnology ·NASDAQ ·United States
Aprea Therapeutics is a clinical-stage biopharmaceutical company developing novel synthetic lethality-based cancer therapeutics targeting DNA damage response (DDR) pathways. Lead candidate APR-1051 is an oral WEE1 kinase inhibitor in Phase 1 trials for advanced solid tumors, and ATRN-119 is an ATR inhibitor in Phase 1/2a trials. The company generates essentially zero revenue and is funded through private placements.

Investment Thesis
Aprea Therapeutics offers high-risk/high-reward exposure to the DDR-targeted oncology space. APR-1051 (WEE1 inhibitor) showed a confirmed partial response in Phase 1 with 50% tumor reduction in a PPP2R1A-mutated endometrial cancer patient, and 2 partial responses plus 5 stable disease out of 24 patients enrolled at doses up to 220mg QD [1]. The $30M private placement closed in March 2026 provides approximately 2-3 years of runway at current burn rates, extending the cash runway through critical Phase 1 dose-escalation milestones. At a $10.8M market cap — barely above cash on hand post-raise — the stock is priced for failure, meaning any positive clinical data could drive significant revaluation. The DDR-targeted oncology market is large and growing, with synthetic lethality representing a scientifically validated approach to precision oncology.
The bear case is that Aprea is a borderline non-viable company. Revenue is $285K (essentially zero), the company had going-concern warnings as of September 2025, and the stock has declined from a 52-week high of $2.22 to $0.94 [3]. With only 8 employees, the company lacks the infrastructure to advance a two-asset clinical pipeline through registration trials and commercialization without significant additional funding or a partnership. The Phase 1 data, while showing signals of activity, involves only 24 patients — far too early to assess clinical viability. The previous p53 platform (eprenetapopt) failed its pivotal Phase 3 trial, demonstrating that early clinical promise does not guarantee success. Each future financing will be massively dilutive at the current share price, and NASDAQ minimum bid compliance risk is real with shares at $0.94. This is a speculative lottery ticket, not an investment.

  2. ATRN-119 Phase 1/2a clinical data in solid tumors could establish a second DDR asset, diversifying the pipeline beyond a single-candidate risk [1]

  3. NASDAQ delisting risk — stock price of $0.94 is below the $1.00 minimum bid requirement, and continued non-compliance could trigger delisting proceedings [3]
