Company Profile
Healthcare ·Drug Delivery / Specialty Pharmaceuticals ·NASDAQ ·United States
Aquestive Therapeutics is a specialty pharmaceutical company developing and commercializing differentiated products using its proprietary PharmFilm drug delivery technology. Products include Sympazan (clobazam oral film for seizures) and Libervant (diazepam buccal film). Lead pipeline candidate Anaphylm is a needle-free epinephrine sublingual film for anaphylaxis. The company also develops AdrenaVerse prodrug technology for sustained epinephrine delivery and generates royalty revenue from a Zevra Therapeutics partnership.
130 Employees ·aquestive.com

Investment Thesis
Aquestive Therapeutics offers speculative upside driven by its proprietary PharmFilm drug delivery platform and the potential approval of Anaphylm, a needle-free epinephrine sublingual film targeting the $2B+ anaphylaxis market [7]. Q1 2026 revenue surged 66% YoY to $14.4M with net losses narrowing from -$22.9M to -$8.1M, demonstrating accelerating commercial traction from Sympazan and Libervant alongside Zevra royalties [1][3]. The company's $110.7M cash position against $43.6M total debt provides approximately two years of runway at current burn rates, and 2026 guidance of up to $50M revenue signals continued momentum. The PharmFilm platform is structurally immune to AI disruption, and Inspire Insight rates the company ALIGNED with a score of 28, recognizing positive contributions in Business Ethics, Customer Welfare, and Product Access and Affordability.
The bear case is significant. Aquestive remains deeply unprofitable with a -137% profit margin, negative stockholders' equity, and analyst consensus forecasting a -$72.8M EPS loss for 2026 [6]. The FDA flagged deficiencies in the Anaphylm NDA, creating meaningful regulatory risk for the company's most important growth catalyst — a complete response letter or extended review could push approval well beyond current timelines and require additional capital [7]. Persistent insider selling across the CEO and multiple board members in 2026, with zero insider purchases, signals limited leadership conviction at current valuations [4]. The 5-year revenue CAGR of just 1.4% reveals that the recent revenue acceleration is unproven over longer periods, and the company's ability to sustain this trajectory depends heavily on continued Libervant uptake, Zevra royalties, and eventual Anaphylm commercialization. At $4.70 per share, the stock carries the binary risk profile typical of small-cap specialty pharma.
