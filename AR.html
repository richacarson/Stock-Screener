Company Profile
Energy ·Oil & Gas E&P ·NYSE ·United States
Antero Resources Corporation is an independent natural gas and natural gas liquids (NGL) company engaged in the exploration, development, and production of unconventional resources in the Appalachian Basin. The company holds approximately 520,000 net acres in West Virginia and Ohio, primarily targeting the Marcellus and Utica shale formations. Antero is one of the largest natural gas producers in the United States with net production of 3.9 Bcfe/d.

Investment Thesis
Antero Resources delivered exceptional Q1 2026 results with revenue up 44% to $1.95 billion, net income surging 157% to $535.2 million, and adjusted free cash flow nearly tripling to $657.5 million, driven by the transformative HG Energy acquisition and record net production of 3.9 Bcfe/d [1]. The company trades at just 8.3x forward earnings with a pristine balance sheet (D/E of 19%), offering compelling value relative to its structural position as a top-5 U.S. natural gas producer benefiting from secular demand growth driven by AI data centers, LNG exports, and industrial electrification. Full-year 2026 production guidance of 4.1 Bcfe/d represents approximately 20% YoY growth, with analysts setting an average price target of $50.40, implying 46% upside from current levels [5].
The primary risk is Antero's leverage to natural gas commodity prices, which have historically exhibited extreme volatility — the 5-year revenue CAGR of just 5.8% masks a boom-bust cycle from $7.1B (2022) to $4.3B (2024) and back to $5.9B TTM [5]. The company's lack of diversification beyond Appalachian Basin natural gas and NGLs concentrates risk, and the recent leadership transition from co-founder Paul Rady to Michael Kennedy introduces execution uncertainty during a period of rapid growth. Net insider selling of $4.2 million in the last 12 months, including the incoming CEO's intent to sell $7.3 million in shares, raises questions about management's conviction in the stock at current levels despite the compelling valuation [4].
