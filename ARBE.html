Company Profile
Technology ·Scientific & Technical Instruments ·NASDAQ ·Israel
Arbe Robotics develops 4D imaging radar solutions for autonomous vehicles, ADAS, robotaxi, defense, and maritime applications. Its chipset delivers up to 100x higher resolution than competing radar systems with 20,000+ detections per frame at 300+ meters range, enabling real-time perception for L2+ through full autonomy. Founded in 2015, headquartered in Tel Aviv with offices in the US, Germany, and China.

Investment Thesis
Arbe Robotics possesses genuinely differentiated 4D imaging radar technology offering 100x resolution improvement and 20,000+ detections per frame at 300+ meters, positioning it as critical sensor infrastructure for autonomous vehicles, robotaxi, and defense [2]. The NVIDIA DRIVE AGX partnership (CES 2026), Level 4 Chinese automaker design win (SOP December 2026), and European OEM data collection orders provide a credible pathway to revenue scaling in 2026-2027 [1][2]. New CEO Ram Machness brings 30+ years of semiconductor commercial experience (12 years at Texas Instruments) and 8 years at Arbe, offering execution focus while co-founder Marenko remains as President [2]. Revenue guidance of $4-6M for 2026 (vs. $1M in 2025) would represent transformative growth, and $53.6M in liquidity provides approximately 6.5 quarters of runway at current burn [1].
The bear case is substantial: Arbe generated only $0.5M in Q1 2026 revenue against $11.3M operating expenses, with management guiding $4-6M for the full year — still negligible for a $92M market cap company [1]. Nasdaq issued a minimum bid price compliance notice in April 2026, giving Arbe until October 2026 to maintain $1.00+ for 10 consecutive trading days or face potential delisting [4]. The CFO is departing in August 2026, creating governance uncertainty. Shares outstanding increased 30.4% in one year through dilutive financing, and further dilution is likely before profitability. Forward P/E is meaningless (negative), and the company competes against well-funded lidar companies and camera-first approaches (Tesla, Mobileye) that could marginalize radar-only solutions. The sub-$1 stock price and $92M market cap create significant liquidity and execution risk. CTO Arkind sold shares in June 2026, and no significant insider buying was identified [3].
