Company Profile
Financial Services ·Asset Management ·NASDAQ ·United States
Ares Capital Corporation is the largest publicly traded business development company (BDC) in the United States with $31.2B in total assets. Externally managed by Ares Management ($622B AUM), ARCC provides direct loans and investments to private middle market companies across diverse sectors including software, healthcare, consumer, and financial services.

Investment Thesis
Ares Capital is the gold standard in BDC investing — the largest publicly traded BDC ($31.2B total assets) backed by Ares Management's $622B platform, trading at 0.88x book value ($19.94 NAV) with an 11% dividend yield and 9.1x forward P/E [1][4]. Record gross originations of $15.8B in 2025 with 100+ new borrowers added demonstrate the platform's competitive advantages in deal sourcing. Credit quality remains solid with non-accruals at 1.8% of cost, average LTV of 44%, and improving interest coverage (~2.2x) [1]. Spillover taxable income of $988M ($1.38/share) provides a substantial distribution cushion even if core EPS compresses. Multiple insider purchases in February 2026 — CEO, COO, CFO, and a director collectively buying $943K — signal strong management conviction at current levels [4]. The $2.2B pipeline backlog and $1.4B year-to-date 2026 commitments suggest continued origination momentum.
The bear case centers on rate sensitivity and structural BDC limitations. Management guided a ~$0.10/share core EPS headwind in 2026 from declining base rates, and further Fed cuts could compress net investment income meaningfully — ARCC's floating-rate loan portfolio (~90%) is directly rate-sensitive [1]. GAAP EPS declined 24.9% YoY in Q4 2025 as the rate tailwind faded. The 24% software portfolio exposure faces AI disruption uncertainty despite management's dismissal of concerns; Seeking Alpha analysts have flagged 'SaaSpocalypse' risk in ARCC's tech lending book [3]. The external management structure with 20% incentive fees creates ongoing fee drag that competitors like internally-managed BDCs avoid. Dividend growth has stalled at $1.92/share for three consecutive years, and the 3.7% 5yr CAGR trails inflation. Trading at 0.88x book suggests the market is pricing in NAV erosion risk from potential credit deterioration in a slowing economy. The 103% GAAP payout ratio leaves minimal margin for error if credit losses spike [4].
Key Catalysts
  1. Origination momentum with $2.2B pipeline backlog and $1.4B year-to-date 2026 commitments, with Ares Management's $622B platform providing proprietary deal flow advantages unavailable to smaller BDCs [1]
  2. Trading at 0.88x book value with 11% yield while multiple C-suite insiders purchased $943K in shares in February 2026, suggesting meaningful upside to NAV and distribution sustainability [4]
  3. Expansion into specialized verticals (sports media, energy, digital infrastructure) and non-sponsored originations (50%+ growth) diversifies revenue sources beyond traditional sponsored middle-market lending [1]
Key Risks
  1. Base rate sensitivity — ~$0.10/share core EPS headwind guided for 2026 from rate declines, with further Fed cuts potentially compressing net investment income on the 90%+ floating-rate portfolio [1]
  2. Software portfolio concentration (24% of assets) faces AI-driven disruption risk, where borrower revenue compression could increase non-accruals above the current 1.8% level [2][3]
  3. Stalled dividend growth at $1.92/share for three consecutive years with 103% GAAP payout ratio, limiting the income growth that BDC investors depend on for total return [4]
