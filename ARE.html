Investment Thesis
Alexandria Real Estate Equities is the leading life-science REIT and one of the highest faith-aligned holdings in the IOWN universe (Inspire score +80, ALIGNED, with positive attributions across 16 ESG dimensions) [3]. Founder Joel Marcus's three-decade mission to 'advance human health' through clustered life-science real estate is a genuinely durable just cause, and the 35.9 million RSF portfolio in Cambridge, SSF, San Diego, NYC, and RTP cannot be easily replicated [3]. After a brutal 2025 — $(8.44) net loss, 45% dividend cut to $2.88 annual, and stock crash to $42.61 — ARE now trades at 6.8x forward P/E with a 6.76% yield on the reset dividend, $5.30B of liquidity, and $2.9B of planned 2026 dispositions to delever [1][4][5]. Recommendation is WATCH given execution risk, with potential upgrade to HOLD/BUY if 2026 dispositions execute and life-science demand stabilizes.
The bear case is that ARE is a value trap. 2026 FFO guide of $6.25-$6.55 represents a ~30% YoY decline, occupancy is heading into the high-80s, and the 45% dividend cut suggests management has limited visibility into 2027 cash flows [1][4]. Net debt plus preferred to adjusted EBITDA of 5.7x is elevated for a REIT in a declining-FFO environment, and rising interest rates plus weakening lease spreads compress refinancing economics. Life-science tenant demand has softened with biotech funding pullback and U.S. administration policy uncertainty around NIH funding and drug pricing [1][4]. The disposition plan ($2.9B at 2026 midpoint) requires successful execution in a weak commercial real estate market — fire-sale pricing risk is real. Founder Marcus's transition to Executive Chairman (since 2018) has not produced a clear succession answer for operating leadership.
Key Catalysts
  1. $2.9B of 2026 dispositions executing at reasonable prices, demonstrating delevering capability and capital recycling [4]
  2. Life-science funding stabilization — if biotech IPO market and NIH funding clarify in H2 2026, tenant demand could rebound supporting H2 occupancy growth [4]
  3. Reset dividend of $2.88 annual at 6.76% yield is now well-covered (46% payout ratio on 2026 FFO guide), creating a more sustainable income profile [5][6]
Key Risks
  1. Further FFO declines in 2027 if life-science tenant demand and lease spreads continue weakening, potentially triggering additional dividend cuts [1][4]
  2. Forced disposition pricing risk — $2.9B of asset sales in a weak commercial real estate market could realize materially below book values [4]
  3. Net debt + preferred / adj EBITDA at 5.7x is elevated; rising rates plus declining FFO could create refinancing stress [4]
