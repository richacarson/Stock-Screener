Company Profile
Real Estate ·REIT - Mortgage ·NYSE ·United States
Apollo Commercial Real Estate Finance, Inc. is an externally managed commercial mortgage REIT that originates, acquires, invests in, and manages commercial first mortgage loans, subordinate financings, and other commercial real estate-related debt investments. The company is managed by an affiliate of Apollo Global Management.

Investment Thesis
ARI offers a 9.0% dividend yield at a 12.7x forward P/E, supported by Apollo Global Management's $700B+ asset management platform providing superior deal flow and credit analytics for commercial real estate lending [1]. Q1 2026 results showed stabilization with $0.22 distributable EPS covering the $0.25 quarterly dividend, no realized losses, and net income of $26.2M essentially flat year-over-year [1]. The $150M stock repurchase authorization at a significant discount to book value signals management confidence, and the 47% profit margin reflects the attractive spread-based economics of the lending model.
The bear case is significant: D/E of 428% exposes ARI to interest rate and credit risk in a commercial real estate market still adjusting to higher rates, with FY2024 net income of negative $119.6M demonstrating the portfolio's vulnerability to credit losses [6]. The dividend has been cut five times since 2019, falling from $1.84 to $1.00/share annually — a 46% reduction that erodes investor confidence in the income thesis. Revenue declined 9.6% on a 3-year CAGR basis as commercial real estate transaction volumes contracted, and the 123% payout ratio suggests the current dividend may still exceed sustainable distributable earnings. The externally managed structure creates inherent fee conflicts with Apollo Global Management.
Key Catalysts
  1. Commercial real estate market stabilization and potential Fed rate cuts improving loan origination volume and reducing credit stress on the existing portfolio
  2. $150M stock repurchase program execution at a significant discount to book value, accretive to remaining shareholders if ARI trades below NAV
  3. Apollo Global Management's institutional platform providing access to higher-quality deal flow and credit analytics capabilities as commercial real estate lending normalizes
Key Risks
  1. Further commercial real estate credit deterioration forcing additional loan loss provisions and potential sixth dividend cut, with the 123% payout ratio leaving no margin of safety
  2. 428% D/E leverage amplifying losses in a prolonged commercial real estate downturn — FY2024's -$119.6M net loss demonstrates the portfolio's downside vulnerability
  3. External management fee structure creating misaligned incentives between Apollo's fee income and ARI shareholders' total return, particularly during periods of portfolio stress
