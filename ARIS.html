Company Profile
Basic Materials ·Gold ·NYSE ·Canada
Aris Mining Corporation is a Canadian gold mining company focused on South America, operating the Segovia and Marmato underground gold mines in Colombia. Formerly GCM Mining Corp., the company produced 257,000 ounces of gold in 2025 and is executing expansion projects targeting 500,000 ounces annually. Aris also holds the Toroparu project in Guyana and the Juby project in Ontario, Canada.

Investment Thesis
Aris Mining is a high-growth gold producer at an inflection point, delivering 82% revenue growth to $909M and 265% adjusted EPS growth to $1.28 in 2025 on the combination of 22% production growth and 48% higher gold prices [1]. The growth trajectory is clearly defined: 2026 guidance of 300-350K oz (vs. 257K in 2025), with Segovia's second mill ramping and Marmato's CIP plant commissioning in Q4 2026, targeting 500K oz annually at full capacity [1]. Q1 2026 production of 74,300 oz (36% YoY increase) at >$4,860/oz realized price confirms the ramp is on track, with gold revenue exceeding $360M in the quarter [1]. At $20.33/share with a $4.2B market cap, Aris trades at a significant discount to major gold producers on a production-growth-adjusted basis. The conservative D/E of 36% and $322M operating cash flow provide financial flexibility to self-fund expansion [1]. The ICSID arbitration settlement with Colombia removes a major legal overhang and establishes a long-term government partnership framework [4].
The bear case centers on jurisdiction risk, gold price dependence, and execution challenges. Colombia presents ongoing security, regulatory, and political risks — the Soto Norte project's environmental license suspension demonstrates how quickly government policy can change [4]. Revenue is entirely dependent on gold prices; the current $2,800+/oz environment has driven record profitability, but a reversion to $1,800-2,000/oz would compress margins significantly given underground mining's high cost structure. Profit margin of 8.4% and ROE of 6.3% are below peers, reflecting the capital-intensive growth phase — if expansion projects face cost overruns or delays, returns may disappoint [5]. The Marmato CIP plant commissioning in Q4 2026 and pathway to 500K oz requires flawless execution across two complex underground mines. Currency risk (Colombian peso exposure) and energy cost inflation could pressure all-in sustaining costs. The trailing P/E of 49.6x suggests the market has priced in significant future production growth, limiting upside if guidance is merely met rather than exceeded.
