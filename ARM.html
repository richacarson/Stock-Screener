Company Profile
Technology ·Semiconductors ·NASDAQ ·United Kingdom
Arm Holdings plc designs and licenses central processing unit (CPU) architectures, systems, and related technologies to semiconductor companies and OEMs worldwide. Arm's architecture powers over 99% of the world's smartphones, a significant share of IoT devices, and is rapidly expanding into data center, automotive, and AI infrastructure markets. The company operates on a licensing and royalty model, earning revenue from both upfront technology licenses and per-unit royalties on chips shipped by partners.

Investment Thesis
Arm Holdings delivered record FY2026 results with $4.92B revenue (up 23% YoY), marking the third consecutive year of 20%+ growth, driven by licensing revenue surging 25% to $2.31B and royalty revenue growing 21% to a record $2.61B [1]. The company sits at the architectural foundation of the AI computing revolution — its CPU designs power chips from NVIDIA, Qualcomm, Apple, Google, and Amazon, making Arm a structural beneficiary of every dollar spent on AI infrastructure. The data center business doubled YoY, the Arm AGI CPU attracted 50+ ecosystem partners for Agentic AI workloads, and the near-monopoly in mobile (99%+ market share) provides a royalty base that funds expansion into new markets. Non-GAAP EPS of $1.77 for the full year demonstrates improving profitability alongside growth.
The overwhelming concern is valuation. At 158.7x forward earnings and a PEG of 3.02, Arm is priced for extraordinary growth that must not only continue but accelerate to justify the $371B market cap [5]. The trailing P/E of 409x means investors are paying for a future that assumes Arm captures a dramatically larger share of AI computing economics than its current royalty model suggests. SoftBank's controlling 90%+ stake creates governance risk — CEO Rene Haas's dual role as SoftBank Group International CEO raises questions about whether strategic decisions serve minority shareholders. The stock's 3.79 beta means it will amplify any AI sentiment correction, and the 52-week range of $100-$453 illustrates the extreme volatility investors must accept. RISC-V represents a long-term architectural alternative that could pressure Arm's licensing pricing power.
Key Catalysts
  1. AI infrastructure buildout driving continued doubling of data center revenue, with the Arm AGI CPU and 50+ ecosystem partners creating a new high-value licensing revenue stream for Agentic AI workloads
  2. Expansion from pure IP licensing toward full-chip production ambitions, signaled by hiring AWS's former AI chip director Rami Sinno, potentially transforming Arm's economics from royalty-based to product-based
  3. Third consecutive year of 20%+ revenue growth with royalty revenue reaching record $2.61B, demonstrating that expanding end-market penetration is translating into sustainable per-unit economics growth
Key Risks
  1. Forward P/E of 158.7x and PEG of 3.02 leave zero margin of safety — any deceleration in AI-driven growth or broader tech sentiment correction could trigger severe multiple compression given the stock's 3.79 beta
  2. SoftBank's 90%+ controlling stake and CEO Haas's dual role as SoftBank Group International CEO create governance risk, with potential conflicts between minority shareholder interests and SoftBank portfolio optimization
  3. RISC-V open-source architecture is gaining momentum as a royalty-free alternative, threatening Arm's pricing power in cost-sensitive IoT and embedded markets and potentially expanding into data center applications
