Company Profile
Energy ·Oil & Gas Equipment & Services ·NYSE ·United States
Archrock is the largest owner and operator of natural gas compression equipment in the United States. The company provides contract compression services and aftermarket parts/maintenance to oil and gas producers, processors, gatherers, and transporters through its fleet of compression equipment. Founded in 1990 and headquartered in Houston, Texas.
1,350 Employees ·www.archrock.com

Investment Thesis
Archrock is the dominant U.S. natural gas compression provider riding a structural growth cycle fueled by rising natural gas demand from AI data centers, LNG exports, and domestic power generation. Full-year 2025 revenue of $1.49B (up 15.5% YoY) with adjusted EBITDA up 51% and adjusted EPS up 68% demonstrates exceptional operational leverage [1]. 2026 guidance of $865-$915M adjusted EBITDA with ~170,000 HP deliveries planned reflects continued momentum. At 15.8x forward P/E with 22.9% ROE and 21.6% profit margin, AROC offers compelling growth at a reasonable valuation with a 2.29% dividend yield that has been paid for 12 consecutive years and growing at 6.6% CAGR [5]. CEO Brad Childers' 14-year tenure and a 4.5/5 Glassdoor rating suggest stable, culture-driven execution [2].
The bear case centers on energy cycle dependence and leverage. D/E of 162.6% with $2.43B in total debt creates interest rate sensitivity and limits financial flexibility during an energy downturn — natural gas prices and drilling activity directly drive compression demand [5]. The TOPS acquisition inflated revenue growth rates that will normalize; organic growth is more modest. Net income guidance of $306-$356M for 2026 is potentially below 2025's $322M (which included $47M in asset sale gains and $33M in tax settlements), so underlying earnings growth may be slower than headline EBITDA suggests [1]. Archrock's business is tied to fossil fuel infrastructure — a long-duration energy transition could eventually reduce demand, though this risk is decades away. The 1,350-employee company has limited diversification beyond U.S. compression and is vulnerable to regulatory changes affecting natural gas drilling on federal lands [3].
Key Catalysts
  1. AI data center buildout driving structural natural gas demand growth — CEO Childers has cited AI onshoring as a significant catalyst for compression services demand [2]
  2. 2026 adjusted EBITDA guidance of $865-$915M with ~170,000 HP of fleet deliveries, 60% expected in H1 2026, driving near-term revenue and earnings growth [1]
  3. Dividend growth trajectory — 6.6% five-year CAGR with recent increases, supported by strong operating cash flow of $622M and manageable 43.7% payout ratio [5]
Key Risks
  1. Natural gas price and drilling activity cyclicality — a sustained gas price decline below $2/MMBtu would reduce compression demand and pricing power [1]
  2. Acquisition-related leverage at 162.6% D/E with $2.43B total debt creates interest expense headwinds and limits financial flexibility during an energy downturn [5]
  3. Regulatory risk from potential restrictions on federal lands drilling or methane emission regulations increasing compliance costs for compression operations [3]
