Company Profile
Healthcare ·Biotechnology ·NASDAQ ·United States
Arcutis Biotherapeutics is a commercial-stage biopharmaceutical company focused on developing and commercializing treatments for dermatological diseases. Its lead product ZORYVE (roflumilast) is FDA-approved as a topical cream for plaque psoriasis and atopic dermatitis, and as a foam for seborrheic dermatitis and scalp/body psoriasis. The company also develops ARQ-234, a fusion protein checkpoint agonist. Founded in 2016, headquartered in Westlake Village, California.

Investment Thesis
Arcutis is a rare commercial-stage biotech that has achieved category-creating market position with ZORYVE, the only FDA-approved topical PDE4 inhibitor across four dermatological indications [1]. Revenue grew 91% to $376M in FY2025 with Q4 representing the first profitable quarter ($17.4M net income), and raised 2026 guidance of $480-$495M implies 29-33% growth with sustained profitability [1]. At 19.3x forward P/E with 30%+ revenue growth, 90% gross margins, and a clean balance sheet ($221M cash, D/E of 60%), the risk/reward is compelling. The multi-indication ZORYVE platform addressing a $10B+ topical dermatology market, combined with the ARQ-234 pipeline and a lean 354-person team generating exceptional revenue per employee, creates a durable growth story with significant upside if execution continues [2].
The bear case centers on single-product concentration and competitive dynamics. ZORYVE accounts for substantially all revenue, and any safety signal, formulary exclusion, or competitive launch could materially impact growth [1]. The dermatology market is attracting increased investment from larger pharma companies with deeper commercial and R&D resources. Profit margin remains negative (-4.3%) and FCF was negative for FY2025, meaning the profitability transition is still early and fragile [4]. Insider selling by CEO Watanabe ($941K), Director Leonard ($997K), and multiple officers in March 2026 suggests leadership may be de-risking personal exposure [4]. The 91% revenue growth rate will inevitably decelerate as ZORYVE penetration matures, and the 2026 guidance of 29-33% growth already shows this trajectory. ARQ-234 is early-stage with uncertain clinical and commercial outcomes, and biotech pipeline risk remains a constant overhang.
