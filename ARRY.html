Investment Thesis
Array Technologies is a solar tracker manufacturer riding the long-term energy transition tailwind, with FY2025 revenue up 40% to $1.3B and a record $2.2B order book signaling robust demand. New products (OmniTrack, SkyLink, Hail XP) now represent nearly 40% of orders, and international expansion diversifies beyond U.S. policy dependency.
However, the investment case is clouded by severe margin compression (gross margins falling from 32.5% to 23.2%), a $112M net loss, $103M goodwill impairment, and 294% debt-to-equity. Array lost its #2 U.S. market position to Gamechange Solar while facing tariff headwinds and Glassdoor-documented leadership dysfunction. The 2026 guidance of $1.4-1.5B revenue and $200-230M adjusted EBITDA suggests a path to profitability, but execution risk is elevated given competitive intensity and policy uncertainty around IRA credits and tariffs.
Key Catalysts
  1. Record $2.2B order book converting to $1.4-1.5B in 2026 revenue with improving adjusted EBITDA margins to $200-230M
  2. AI datacenter electricity demand boom driving accelerated utility-scale solar deployment, benefiting tracker manufacturers
  3. Next-generation OmniTrack product launch and international expansion into EMEA and Latin America opening new growth vectors
Key Risks
  1. Gross margin compression from 32.5% to 23.2% may persist as competition from Nextracker, Gamechange, and Chinese manufacturers intensifies pricing pressure
  2. Tariff exposure and IRA/45X credit uncertainty create policy-dependent revenue risk, with potential changes to domestic content requirements
  3. Debt-to-equity of 294% and $112M net loss limit financial flexibility, with $103M goodwill impairment signaling potential further asset writedowns

AI Resilience (25%)
AI Resilience NEUTRAL
6
AI is a moderate enabler for Array Technologies with limited direct threat. The company's SmarTrack software already leverages algorithmic optimization for solar tracking efficiency, and management has committed to an AI roadmap spanning operations, design, and customer service [3]. AI-driven datacenter growth is a significant tailwind, driving increased demand for utility-scale solar installations [3]. However, competitors like Nextracker also leverage AI/ML for panel optimization, and AI-driven design tools could commoditize tracker engineering over time [1].
