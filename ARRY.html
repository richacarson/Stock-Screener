Company Profile
Industrials ·Solar Equipment & Services ·NASDAQ ·United States
Array Technologies, Inc. is a leading manufacturer of ground-mounting systems used in solar energy projects, including tracker systems that optimize energy production by automatically adjusting panel orientation. The company serves utility-scale solar installations globally and has achieved over 100 GW in cumulative tracker installations. Array recently acquired APA Solar to expand its fixed-tilt and carport product lines.

Investment Thesis
Array Technologies is executing a strong revenue recovery with FY2025 revenue up 40% to $1.28B and a record $2.4B orderbook as of Q1 2026, driven by surging utility-scale solar demand from AI data center buildout, IRA tax incentives, and global decarbonization trends [1]. The company beat Q1 2026 revenue estimates ($223.4M vs. $205.9M consensus) and reaffirmed full-year guidance of $1.4-1.5B revenue with $200-230M adjusted EBITDA. At 10.9x forward earnings and a PEG of 0.91, Array offers compelling value relative to its growth trajectory, and the 100 GW installation milestone validates its market leadership position in solar trackers. The APA Solar acquisition diversifies the product portfolio into fixed-tilt and carport segments.
The bear case centers on Array's current unprofitability — TTM net loss, negative 5.6% profit margin, negative ROE of 22.65%, and D/E of 294% reflect a balance sheet under strain from acquisition-related debt and restructuring costs [4]. The solar tracker market is cyclical and policy-dependent; the 42% revenue decline from 2024 to 2025 demonstrated how quickly demand can contract. Management instability (1.9-year average tenure, cultural concerns on Glassdoor) raises execution risk during a critical growth phase. IRA incentive uncertainty under potential policy changes could dampen the domestic solar installation pipeline, and commodity steel price volatility directly impacts Array's gross margins. The stock's 52-week range of $5.39-$12.23 reflects the market's uncertainty about whether the earnings recovery will be sustained.
Key Catalysts
  1. Record $2.4B orderbook provides 1.6x revenue coverage for 2026 guidance, offering unusually strong visibility into near-term revenue and reducing execution risk relative to historical periods
  2. AI data center power demand driving unprecedented utility-scale solar procurement, with hyperscaler renewable energy commitments creating a structural demand tailwind for Array's tracker systems
  3. Earnings inflection from GAAP losses to profitability expected in 2026, with adjusted EPS guidance of $0.65-0.75 representing a potential catalyst for multiple re-rating from the current 10.9x forward P/E
Key Risks
  1. Balance sheet strain with D/E of 294% and negative shareholder equity limits financial flexibility, and any delay in the earnings recovery could pressure the company's ability to service acquisition-related debt
  2. IRA incentive policy uncertainty under potential political changes could reduce domestic utility-scale solar installations, directly impacting Array's orderbook conversion and revenue growth trajectory
  3. Management instability with 1.9-year average tenure and ongoing leadership rebuilding creates execution risk during a period requiring operational excellence to convert the record orderbook into profitable revenue
