Company Profile

Amer Sports designs, manufactures, and distributes premium sports equipment, apparel, and footwear through iconic brands including Arc'teryx (technical apparel), Salomon (outdoor performance), and Wilson (ball and racquet sports). The company operates across three segments with global distribution through owned retail, wholesale, and e-commerce channels. Majority-owned by ANTA Sports.

Investment Thesis
Amer Sports is a premium brand growth story with exceptional momentum. Revenue grew 27% in 2025 to $6.6B, net income surged 489% to $427M, and 2026 guidance of 16-18% revenue growth is supported by Arc'teryx (18-20% guided growth) and Salomon crossing $2B in sales [1]. Gross margin of 57.6% reflects pricing power in technical outdoor apparel and footwear — categories with secular tailwinds from outdoor recreation trends. At 22.1x forward P/E with an estimated PEG of ~0.88, the stock offers reasonable value for a high-growth consumer brand platform. The $400M capex investment in 2026 and SAP cloud migration position the company for margin expansion as Salomon investments scale [1][2]. Analyst consensus price target of $49 represents 49% upside from current levels [4].
The bear case centers on valuation risk and execution. The stock trades at 43.3x trailing P/E, and Q1 2026 EPS guidance of $0.28-0.30 missed consensus of $0.33, causing the stock to fall from its $42.76 all-time high to $32.92 — a 23% decline [1][4]. Near-term margin compression from elevated Salomon SG&A investment means profitability improvement will take quarters to materialize. ANTA Sports' 62.6% controlling ownership limits float and raises governance concerns about minority shareholder interests. The premium outdoor apparel market faces saturation risk as Arc'teryx's luxury positioning attracts fashion-driven consumers who may be less loyal than core outdoor enthusiasts [1]. Tariff uncertainty and supply chain concentration in Asia add macro risk, though management expects minimal tariff impact. Beta of 2.8 means significant volatility in risk-off environments [4].
Key Catalysts
  1. Arc'teryx continuing 18-20% revenue growth with expanding margins as the brand scales its global DTC retail network [1]
  2. Salomon crossing $2B in sales with investments in brand awareness, trail running, and lifestyle footwear driving sustained growth [1]
  3. Margin expansion inflection as Salomon SG&A investments normalize and economies of scale from $400M capex program materialize [1][2]
Key Risks
  1. Near-term margin compression from Salomon investment — Q1 2026 EPS guidance of $0.28-0.30 missed consensus $0.33, and elevated SG&A may persist through 2026 [1]
  2. Premium outdoor apparel market saturation — Arc'teryx's luxury positioning attracts fashion-driven consumers who may prove less loyal in a downturn, creating cyclical risk [1]
  3. ANTA Sports controlling ownership (62.6%) limits minority shareholder governance rights and concentrates strategic decision-making in a Chinese parent company [4]

AI Resilience (25%)
AI Resilience MODERATE

AI Risk Protection (5/9): Amer Sports' core business — designing and manufacturing physical sporting goods, apparel, and footwear — has moderate AI exposure. Physical products cannot be digitized, and brand equity in Arc'teryx and Salomon is built on decades of performance credibility that AI cannot replicate [2]. However, the retail and e-commerce channels face disruption risk as AI-powered shopping assistants could disintermediate brand relationships with consumers, and CDO Donghai Chen acknowledged 'how exactly [AI] will be transformative and on what timeline is still genuinely uncertain' [2]. The consumer discretionary nature of premium outdoor gear means demand is sensitive to economic cycles that AI-driven market shifts could exacerbate. AI Opportunity (1/1): Amer Sports generates emerging AI revenue through AI-powered sporting goods (25 patent applications planned), smart technology R&D, and AI-embedded workflow tools that improve content creation, supply chain optimization, and customer analytics [2].
