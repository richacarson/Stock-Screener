Company Profile
Technology ·Software - Application ·NYSE ·United States
Asana, Inc. operates a work management platform that enables organizations to orchestrate work from daily tasks to strategic initiatives. Products include AI Teammates and AI Studio for AI-powered workflow automation. Founded in 2008 by Dustin Moskovitz (Facebook co-founder) and Justin Rosenstein, the company transitioned CEO to Dan Rogers in mid-2025, with Moskovitz becoming Executive Chair focused on AI strategy. FY2026 revenue of $790.8M (+9.2% YoY) and market cap ~$1.5B.

Investment Thesis
Asana at 17.05x forward earnings with an 88.5% gross margin and $424.6M cash represents a discounted option on the AI-powered work management category, trading at just 1.94x trailing revenue ($1.53B market cap / $790.8M revenue) [1][7]. The company achieved its first non-GAAP profitability in Q4 FY2026 with $18.2M operating income (9% margin), demonstrating the operating leverage inherent in the SaaS model as growth moderates. AI Teammates and AI Studio position Asana at the intersection of two massive trends — enterprise AI adoption and agentic workflow automation — with Moskovitz's full-time focus as Executive Chair providing strategic continuity through the CEO transition to Dan Rogers [3][4]. The 31.44% 5-year revenue CAGR and 88.5% gross margin reflect a business with strong unit economics that has yet to translate into sustained profitability.
The bear case centers on competitive moat erosion and growth deceleration. Revenue growth has slowed dramatically from 31% 5-year CAGR to 9.5% YoY, and Q1 FY2027 showed only 9.52% growth to $205.1M, suggesting the deceleration has not yet bottomed [2]. Microsoft's integration of work management and AI copilot features into its dominant 365 ecosystem poses an existential competitive threat — enterprises may consolidate on platforms they already own rather than maintain standalone work management subscriptions. ROE of -87.55% and net profit margin of -20.21% reflect a company that remains GAAP unprofitable after 18 years of operation, with persistent stock-based compensation masking the true cost structure [7]. Net insider selling of $1.96M over 90 days, including sales by both the new CEO and CFO, does not signal management conviction at current prices [5]. The CEO succession announced in March 2026 triggered a stock decline and introduced execution risk during a critical competitive inflection.

  3. CEO succession from founder Moskovitz to professional manager Dan Rogers during a critical AI competitive transition introduces execution risk, with net insider selling of $1.96M and stock declines following the March 2026 announcement suggesting market skepticism [3][5]
