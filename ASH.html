Investment Thesis
Ashland is emerging from a transformative portfolio restructuring with focus on high-margin pharma and personal care additives. The $60M manufacturing optimization, $50-55M in cost savings, and forward P/E of 10.3 suggest meaningful upside if execution succeeds. The 3.4% dividend yield and 25 years of consecutive payments provide income support.
However, the FY2025 net loss of $845M (though largely non-cash), declining revenue, negative ROE, and uncertain restructuring timeline create significant near-term risk. Fiscal 2026 guidance of only 1-5% organic growth is modest. The company must demonstrate improved profitability to justify current valuation.
