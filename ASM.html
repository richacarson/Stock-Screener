Company Profile
Basic Materials ·Other Precious Metals & Mining ·NYSE American ·Canada
Avino Silver & Gold Mines is a silver, gold, and copper mining company operating 100%-owned properties in Durango, Mexico: the Avino Mine (flagship), La Preciosa Mine (expansion), and Oxide Tailings Project. The company produced 1.1M oz silver, 7,544 oz gold, and 5.7M lbs copper in FY2025. Founded in 1968, Avino is included in the S&P/TSX Global Gold Mining Index and VanEck Junior Gold Miners ETF (GDXJ).

Investment Thesis
Avino Silver & Gold Mines offers compelling value as a debt-free silver-primary producer with exceptional financial metrics: 28.9% profit margin, 53% mine operating margin, $57.3M cash, D/E of just 2.6%, and forward P/E of 6.5x [1][4]. Revenue growth of 25.3% YoY and 82.9% EPS growth reflect both La Preciosa production contribution and silver price tailwinds. The 2026 outlook is strong: La Preciosa scaling to 500 tonnes/day from 200 tonnes/day, 15,000 meters of exploration drilling, and AI-integrated geological modeling for resource expansion [2]. Silver's structural demand growth from solar, electronics, and AI infrastructure provides a secular tailwind. Q4 2025 revenue of $30.5M exceeded expectations by 6.2%, and the company ranked 5th on the TSX 30 with +610% three-year share price performance [1].
The bear case centers on commodity price dependence and Mexico operating risk. Silver prices have driven much of Avino's financial improvement — a sustained silver price decline below $25/oz would compress margins significantly given AISC of $24.06/oz [1]. The company operates exclusively in Durango, Mexico, creating geographic concentration risk including potential regulatory changes, tax increases, or security concerns. Despite strong growth, Avino remains a junior miner ($964M market cap) with limited diversification and dependence on two mines. Q4 2025 EPS of $0.06 missed the $0.08 forecast despite revenue beating estimates, suggesting cost pressures from La Preciosa scaling [1]. The stock has fallen from ~$8.38 (50-day SMA) to $5.94, a 29% decline that may reflect market concerns about peak silver prices or execution risk on La Preciosa throughput expansion.
Key Catalysts
  1. La Preciosa scaling to 500 tonnes/day in 2026 from 200 tonnes/day in Q4 2025, potentially tripling silver production from that mine [2]
  2. 15,000 meters of budgeted exploration drilling at both properties with updated mineral reserve/resource estimates expected H1 2026, potentially unlocking significant resource expansion [2]
  3. Structural silver demand growth from solar panels, electronics, and AI data center infrastructure supporting sustained high silver prices above $30/oz [1]
Key Risks
  1. Commodity price dependence — AISC of $24.06/oz silver equivalent leaves limited margin if silver prices decline below $25/oz, compressing profitability from current 53% mine operating margins [1]
  2. Geographic concentration in Durango, Mexico creates single-jurisdiction risk including regulatory changes, tax increases, and security concerns in a region with historical instability [2]
  3. La Preciosa execution risk — scaling from 200 to 500 tonnes/day is operationally complex and Q4 2025 EPS miss suggests costs are rising faster than production gains [1]
