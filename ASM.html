Company Profile
Technology ·Semiconductor Equipment & Materials ·Euronext Amsterdam ·Netherlands
ASM International N.V. is a leading supplier of semiconductor process equipment, specializing in atomic layer deposition (ALD), epitaxy, PECVD, and vertical furnace systems. ALD accounts for over half of equipment revenue and is the fastest-growing segment in wafer fab equipment, critical for next-gen GAA transistors and advanced memory. Founded in 1968, headquartered in Almere, Netherlands, with a market cap of approximately EUR 52 billion.

ASM International demonstrates strong infinite game characteristics through 58 years of continuous operation, deep investment in ALD technology leadership spanning multiple semiconductor cycles, and commitment to Net Zero by 2035. The appointment of 25-year veteran M'Saad as CEO preserves institutional knowledge. However, the company operates in a capex-cycle-dependent industry where quarter-to-quarter order fluctuations can dominate narrative, and competitive positioning against Applied Materials and Lam Research sometimes tilts toward finite market share language.

Courage to Lead

Investment Thesis
ASM International delivered Q1 2026 revenue of EUR 862.5M at the top of guidance, with record adjusted operating margin of 33.1% and gross margin of 53.3%, driven by AI-led demand acceleration in leading-edge logic and memory [1]. Equipment sales rose 14% YoY in constant currency led by ALD, and management guided Q2 2026 revenue of EUR 980M (±5%) with H2 exceeding H1. ASM's monopoly-like position in ALD — the fastest-growing and most critical deposition technology for GAA transistors at 2nm and below — creates structural content growth per wafer as node transitions increase ALD step counts. The debt-free balance sheet, 23% ROE, and 15.2% 5-year revenue CAGR demonstrate sustained compounding. Full-year 2026 is expected to outpace the broader WFE market, with gross margins at the upper end of the 47-51% range [1].
The primary risk is valuation and cyclicality. At 39x forward earnings and an estimated PEG of 2.6, ASM is priced for sustained double-digit growth, leaving limited margin of safety in a semiconductor downcycle [6]. Year-end 2025 backlog of EUR 1,247M declined from EUR 1,566M a year earlier, and the TTM book-to-bill of approximately 0.90 suggests orders are trailing revenue shipments — a potential leading indicator of deceleration [7]. ASM discontinued quarterly order disclosure starting Q1 2026, reducing investor visibility into demand trends. Customer concentration with a handful of leading-edge foundries (TSMC, Samsung, Intel) creates significant single-client risk. Geopolitical tensions and US-China export restrictions could limit ASM's addressable market in China, which contributed meaningfully to 2024-2025 revenue through mature logic/foundry demand. The stock price of EUR 1,066 trades near its 52-week high (EUR 1,080), suggesting limited near-term upside.
Key Catalysts
  1. Gate-all-around (GAA) transistor adoption at 2nm and below structurally increasing ALD steps per wafer, expanding ASM's content per wafer across all leading-edge customers
  2. Q2 2026 revenue guidance of EUR 980M implying acceleration, with H2 expected to exceed H1 and full-year growth outpacing the WFE market
  3. Net Zero 2035 ambition and 35% reduction in thermal energy per wafer target positioning ASM as the sustainability leader in semiconductor equipment
Key Risks
  1. Premium valuation at 39x forward P/E and PEG of 2.6 requires sustained double-digit growth; any semiconductor capex cycle downturn could trigger significant multiple compression
  2. Year-end 2025 backlog declined to EUR 1,247M from EUR 1,566M with TTM book-to-bill of ~0.90, potentially signaling order deceleration despite strong revenue execution
  3. Geopolitical risks from US-China export restrictions could limit ASM's addressable market in China, where mature logic/foundry demand has been a meaningful revenue contributor

AI Resilience (25%)
AI Resilience LOW RISK
9
AI Risk Protection (8/9): ASM International IS AI infrastructure — the company's ALD equipment deposits the atomic-scale thin films required for every advanced semiconductor chip, including AI accelerators (GPUs, TPUs, NPUs) [5]. AI cannot displace ASM; every AI chip fabricated at leading-edge nodes requires ASM's deposition technology. The transition to gate-all-around (GAA) transistor architectures at 2nm and below structurally increases the number of ALD steps per wafer, directly expanding ASM's content per wafer. Hyperscaler AI capex expansion drives chip demand, which drives wafer fab equipment demand. No analyst has flagged AI as a disruption risk; AI is purely an accelerant for ASM's business. AI Opportunity (1/1): ASM generates direct revenue from AI chip manufacturing — every leading-edge AI chip requires ASM's ALD tools, making AI a quantifiable and rapidly growing revenue driver as foundry customers expand AI chip capacity [1][5].
