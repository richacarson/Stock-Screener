Company Profile
Technology ·Semiconductor Equipment & Materials ·NASDAQ ·Netherlands
ASML is the world's sole manufacturer of extreme ultraviolet (EUV) lithography systems, the critical technology enabling the most advanced semiconductor chips at 5nm and below. The company also provides deep ultraviolet (DUV) lithography, metrology and inspection systems, and computational lithography software. ASML's customers include TSMC, Samsung, Intel, and other leading chipmakers driving the AI computing revolution.

ASML exemplifies infinite game thinking through its 40+ year commitment to advancing lithography technology. The company invested billions over two decades in EUV development when success was uncertain, demonstrating extraordinary courage to lead and existential flexibility. The transition from basic optical to DUV to EUV to now High-NA EUV represents one of the most sustained technological reinvention stories in industrial history.

Worthy Rivals

Existential Flexibility
10
Transformative flexibility (2/2 points). ASML has reinvented its core technology multiple times: from basic optical lithography to DUV immersion to EUV, each requiring fundamentally new physics and engineering. The current transition to High-NA EUV represents yet another transformative leap. The EUV development program — 20+ years, billions invested, with uncertain commercial viability for much of that period — is one of the most remarkable technology bets in industrial history [1][2].
Courage to Lead
10
Pattern of extraordinary courage (2/2 points). ASML invested billions in EUV development over two decades when many industry observers believed the technology would never work [1][2]. The company persisted through repeated delays, technical setbacks, and skepticism. This is the quintessential example of sacrificing near-term results for long-term capability. The current investment in High-NA EUV continues this pattern of courageous long-term capital allocation.

Investment Thesis
ASML is the irreplaceable monopoly at the foundation of the AI revolution. Q1 2026 revenue grew 13% to EUR8.8B with 53% gross margin, and management raised full-year 2026 guidance to EUR36-40B (up to 22% growth over 2025), driven by AI-related semiconductor capacity expansion [1]. The company holds a 100% monopoly in EUV lithography — the only technology capable of manufacturing chips at 5nm and below — making it structurally impossible for AI to exist without ASML's machines. ROE of 52.2%, net cash balance sheet (EUR8.4B cash vs EUR2.7B debt), profit margin of 29.7%, and 15.6% 5-year revenue CAGR demonstrate financial excellence befitting a monopolist [4]. High-NA EUV systems are now shipping to lead customers, extending the roadmap to 2nm and beyond. Record order intake and markedly stepped-up customer capacity plans provide multi-year revenue visibility [1].
The bear case centers on valuation and geopolitics. At 30.5x forward P/E and 48x trailing P/E, ASML trades at a significant premium that requires sustained growth to justify the $573B market cap [4]. China revenue is expected to drop sharply in 2026 as export control restrictions on advanced lithography equipment take full effect — China represented a significant pre-buying tailwind in 2024-2025 that is now reversing [1]. Geopolitical risk extends beyond China: if U.S.-China tensions escalate further or if Taiwan contingencies materialize, ASML's key customers (TSMC) face existential disruption. The CEO has warned about 'risks related to customers and geopolitics' [2]. The semiconductor industry is cyclical, and the current AI-driven supercycle could decelerate if hyperscaler capital spending moderates. PEG of 2.26 suggests the market already prices in robust growth, limiting upside if execution falters [4].
Key Catalysts
  1. Full-year 2026 guidance raised to EUR36-40B with 51-53% gross margin, driven by AI chip capacity expansion and record customer order intake [1]
  2. High-NA EUV system ramp to additional customers, extending ASML's technology lead and ASP per system at 2nm nodes and below [1]
  3. Services revenue growing 25% as the installed base of EUV systems expands, creating recurring high-margin revenue streams [1]
Key Risks
  1. China revenue cliff — export controls are expected to sharply reduce China sales in 2026 after a pre-buying surge, creating a year-over-year headwind [1]
  2. Geopolitical concentration risk — TSMC (ASML's largest customer) and the broader semiconductor supply chain are concentrated in Taiwan, creating tail risk from U.S.-China tensions [2]
  3. Valuation compression risk — at 30.5x forward P/E and PEG of 2.26, any deceleration in AI-driven semiconductor capex could trigger multiple contraction from the current $573B market cap [4]
