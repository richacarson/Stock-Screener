Company Profile
Technology ·Communication Equipment ·NASDAQ ·United States
Actelis Networks, Inc. designs, develops, manufactures, and markets cyber-hardened and hybrid fiber networking solutions for IoT and telecommunication applications. The company serves governmental agencies and commercial customers in North America, Europe, the Middle East, and Asia Pacific with products for intelligent traffic systems, utilities, rail, telecom, defense, and campus networking. Founded in 1998 and headquartered in Sunnyvale, California, the company is transitioning to OTC markets following Nasdaq delisting.

Investment Thesis
Actelis Networks occupies a niche at the intersection of cyber-hardened networking, critical infrastructure modernization, and AI-enabled edge computing — three themes benefiting from increased government and defense spending [3]. The Washington, D.C. Department of Transportation deployment provides a reference customer for intelligent traffic systems, and the planned acquisition entry into AI-driven data center networking could expand the addressable market significantly. Q4 2025 showed sequential improvement (revenue up 113% QoQ to $1.37M, gross margin improving to 35%), and the company's low D/E of 21% with $4M cash plus $7.3M ATM raise provides near-term liquidity [1].
The bear case is severe and multifaceted. Revenue collapsed 52.7% from $7.8M (2024) to $3.7M (2025), the net loss widened to $8.3M, and the company was delisted from Nasdaq in April 2026 for failing minimum bid price requirements — the stock trades at $0.10 with a $2.6M market cap that is below one year of revenue [1][4]. With 39 employees and an operating loss of $7.2M, the company burns more cash than it generates in revenue. The 28-year-old company has never achieved sustainable profitability despite multiple technology pivots. The transition to OTC markets severely limits institutional investor access and could trigger further selling. CEO Barlev's track record includes Superfish Inc., which was embroiled in a major adware controversy when pre-installed on Lenovo laptops. Competitive risks from Cisco, Juniper, and other established networking vendors are existential for a company of this scale.
Key Catalysts
  1. Successful execution of planned acquisition into AI-driven data center networking could significantly expand addressable market and provide revenue acceleration beyond the current $3.7M base
  2. Expanding Washington D.C. Department of Transportation deployment and additional government/defense contracts could drive revenue recovery, leveraging cyber-hardened infrastructure positioning amid rising federal modernization spending
  3. Q4 2025 sequential improvement (113% QoQ revenue growth, 35% gross margin) may signal a revenue inflection point if government budget constraints from the 2025 shutdown are resolved
Key Risks
  1. Nasdaq delisting to OTC markets severely restricts institutional access, reduces liquidity, and may trigger forced selling by funds with exchange-listing requirements — the $0.10 stock price and $2.6M market cap signal distress-level valuation
  2. 28-year history of failing to achieve sustainable profitability across multiple technology cycles raises fundamental questions about the company's ability to compete — revenue has declined in 2 of the last 3 years with no numeric 2026 guidance
  3. Cash burn of approximately $2M per quarter against limited cash reserves creates existential liquidity risk — the $7.3M ATM raise was dilutive and additional capital raises at $0.10/share would be catastrophically dilutive to existing shareholders
