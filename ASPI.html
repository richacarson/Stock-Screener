Company Profile

ASP Isotopes is an advanced materials company developing proprietary Aerodynamic Separation Process (ASP) technology to produce and enrich isotopes for nuclear medicine, green energy, and quantum computing. The company operates enrichment plants in South Africa and acquired Renergen Limited, a helium and LNG producer, in January 2026.

Excellence Evaluation — Think Like an Owner
Innovation STRONG
8
ASP Isotopes holds proprietary ASP enrichment technology that represents a fundamentally different approach to isotope separation — laser-free, lower-cost, and scalable. The company is simultaneously developing five isotope production lines (Silicon-28 for quantum computing, Ytterbium-176, Carbon-14, Molybdenum-100 for nuclear medicine, and HALEU for nuclear fuels). First commercial batches of Si-28 shipped Q1 2026, with Yb-176 and C-14 expected H1 2026. The Renergen acquisition adds helium (critical for quantum/semiconductor cooling) and LNG production. The UK subsidiary Quantum Leap Energy is engaging regulators for HALEU production. Innovation breadth and technical differentiation are exceptional for a company this size.

AI Resilience DEVELOPING

AI is largely neutral to ASP Isotopes' core business — isotope enrichment is a physical-chemical process that AI cannot replicate. However, AI-driven demand is a tailwind: Silicon-28 is critical for quantum computing (which intersects with AI), and the semiconductor industry's AI buildout drives demand for ultra-pure materials. AI could optimize enrichment processes and quality control over time. The primary risk is that AI-driven advances in alternative enrichment methods (laser-based) could undermine ASP's cost advantage, though the capital intensity of the field creates barriers.

Investment Thesis
ASP Isotopes controls differentiated isotope enrichment technology with applications across nuclear medicine, clean energy, and quantum computing — three secular growth markets. First commercial revenues arrived in 2025, and 2026 targets of $50-70M would validate the technology at scale. The Renergen acquisition adds near-term revenue and strategic helium assets.
The company trades at an extreme revenue multiple, has posted $96.5M in losses over nine months, and is executing across five simultaneous production lines — a strategy that risks dilution and overextension. Regulatory delays, government-funded competitors, and execution risk across multiple geographies could push breakeven into 2027 or beyond.
