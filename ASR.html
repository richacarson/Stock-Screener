Company Profile
Industrials ·Airports & Air Services ·NYSE ·Mexico
Grupo Aeroportuario del Sureste (ASUR) operates and develops airports across Mexico, Colombia, Puerto Rico, and the United States. Its flagship Cancun International Airport is one of Latin America's busiest tourist destinations. In 2025, ASUR expanded into U.S. airport commercial operations (LAX, O'Hare, JFK) and agreed to acquire CPC's Brazilian airport portfolio.

AI Resilience LOW RISK

Airport concessions are among the most AI-resilient businesses — physical infrastructure with regulated monopoly characteristics. AI cannot replicate runways, terminals, or landing rights. AI may modestly improve airport operations (passenger flow optimization, predictive maintenance, dynamic pricing for commercial spaces) but poses no competitive threat. The growth of AI-driven e-commerce could indirectly benefit air cargo volumes. Tourism demand is driven by human experiences that AI enhances (trip planning) rather than replaces.

Investment Thesis
ASUR is transforming from a Mexican regional airport operator into a pan-American infrastructure platform with operations across six countries. The stock trades at just 12.6x earnings with an 8.8% dividend yield, conservative leverage (0.8x Net Debt/EBITDA), and growing passenger traffic. The CPC acquisition adds 45M+ passengers and significant EBITDA accretion.
FX risk is material — the MXN dividend creates currency exposure for USD investors. The CPC acquisition (~$936M) significantly increases leverage. Mexican regulatory risk persists with government control over concession terms. Q4 2025 showed declining earnings (net income down 22% YoY) due to FX losses and rising expenses. Geopolitical tensions between the U.S. and Mexico could affect tourism.
