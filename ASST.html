Company Profile
Financial Services ·Asset Management ·NASDAQ ·United States
Strive, Inc. (formerly Asset Entities) is a publicly traded Bitcoin treasury and asset management company. The company operates an SEC-registered investment advisory with ~$1.9B in AUM across ETFs and direct indexing, while accumulating Bitcoin on its balance sheet to increase Bitcoin per share. Founded by Vivek Ramaswamy and Anson Frericks in 2022, now led by CEO Matt Cole following the 2025 merger between Strive Asset Management and Asset Entities.
28 Employees ·www.strive.com

Investment Thesis
Strive has carved a unique niche as the first publicly traded asset management Bitcoin treasury company, combining ~$1.9B AUM in anti-ESG ETFs with a Bitcoin accumulation strategy designed to increase BTC per share [1]. Revenue grew 57% in 2025 to $5.73M, and the stock's ~$950M market cap reflects meaningful Bitcoin holdings on the balance sheet with a clean D/E of 0.57%. The anti-ESG/pro-Bitcoin positioning aligns with current political tailwinds, and CEO Matt Cole's strategic advocacy has generated outsized brand awareness relative to the company's scale. If Bitcoin appreciates significantly, the treasury strategy provides asymmetric upside.
The bear case is severe. Strive reported a $424.9M net loss in 2025 on just $5.73M in revenue, with TTM losses exceeding $697M as the Bitcoin treasury strategy introduces extreme balance sheet volatility tied to BTC price [1]. ROE of -125% and negative profit margin reflect a company that is operationally unprofitable at any meaningful scale. The 28-person team has limited capacity to execute across asset management, Bitcoin treasury, and media operations simultaneously. The founder's departure for politics and two major strategic pivots in three years raise governance and strategic consistency concerns. A sustained Bitcoin downturn could erode book value rapidly, and the company's $7M annual revenue provides no meaningful operating cushion against crypto volatility [2]. The stock's beta of 17.4 indicates extreme price volatility that is inappropriate for most portfolio allocations.
Key Catalysts
  1. Bitcoin price appreciation directly increases book value and NAV per share, providing leveraged upside to BTC through the treasury accumulation strategy [1]
  2. Fidelity and Schwab direct indexing distribution partnerships could accelerate AUM growth beyond the current $1.9B base, generating meaningful fee revenue [1]
  3. SATA digital credit product launch and True North media acquisition diversifying revenue beyond pure Bitcoin treasury and ETF management [2]
Key Risks
  1. Bitcoin price decline could rapidly erode book value — with $697M TTM losses already reported, any sustained crypto downturn could threaten solvency given only $5.73M annual operating revenue [1]
  2. Beta of 17.4 and extreme stock price volatility make position sizing impractical for institutional portfolios; the stock has declined dramatically from its peak [1]
  3. Founder departure, two strategic pivots in three years, and only 28 employees create execution and governance risk — CEO Matt Cole has been in role since September 2025 with limited public company track record [2]
