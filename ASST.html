Company Profile
Financial Services ·Asset Management ·NASDAQ ·United States
Strive Asset Management is an SEC-registered investment adviser managing ~$1.9B in AUM across approximately 13 ETFs, collective investment trusts, and a direct indexing platform. Founded in 2022 by Vivek Ramaswamy and Anson Frericks, Strive has pivoted to a Bitcoin treasury strategy under CEO Matt Cole, positioning itself as a Bitcoin treasury company seeking to outperform Bitcoin over the long run.

Investment Thesis
Strive Asset Management has carved a unique niche as the largest anti-ESG asset manager with ~$1.9B AUM and distribution partnerships with Fidelity and Schwab [3]. Revenue grew 57% in 2025 to $5.73M, and the Bitcoin treasury pivot under CEO Matt Cole offers asymmetric upside if Bitcoin appreciates significantly — the company holds meaningful BTC on its balance sheet and trades at just 0.77x book value [1]. The Semler Scientific acquisition adds a revenue-generating healthcare business. Analyst consensus is 'Strong Buy' with a $16 mean price target (60% upside), and the anti-ESG/pro-Bitcoin positioning aligns with current political tailwinds [1].
The bear case is substantial. Strive lost $424.9M in 2025 on just $5.73M in revenue, with the Bitcoin treasury strategy introducing extreme balance sheet volatility tied to BTC price [1]. The stock has collapsed 85.5% YTD and 96% from its 52-week high of $268 (pre-split adjusted), reflecting deep market skepticism. Short interest at 22.3% of float signals active bearish positioning. The company has only 28 employees and negligible operating revenue relative to its ambitions. The Bitcoin treasury playbook (following MicroStrategy) concentrates risk in a single volatile asset, and the founder's departure for politics raises governance concerns. A sustained Bitcoin downturn could render the company insolvent given its cash burn rate of $132M in free cash flow [1].
Key Catalysts
  1. Bitcoin price appreciation directly increases book value and NAV per share, providing leveraged upside given the current 0.77x P/B discount [1]
  2. Fidelity and Schwab direct indexing distribution partnership could accelerate AUM growth beyond the current $1.9B base [3]
  3. Semler Scientific acquisition integration adds healthcare revenue stream and diversifies beyond pure Bitcoin/asset management exposure [1]
Key Risks
  1. Bitcoin price decline could rapidly erode book value and trigger solvency concerns given -$132M annual free cash flow burn rate and only $67.5M cash [1]
  2. 22.3% short interest and 85.5% YTD stock decline reflect deep market skepticism about the Bitcoin treasury pivot's viability [1]
  3. Founder Vivek Ramaswamy's departure and leadership instability — CEO Matt Cole has been in role only since September 2025, and the company has pivoted strategy twice in three years [2]
