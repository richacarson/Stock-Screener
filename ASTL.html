Company Profile
Basic Materials ·Steel ·NASDAQ ·Canada
Algoma Steel produces and sells flat/sheet and plate steel products in Canada, the United States, and internationally. Founded in 1901, the company serves automotive, construction, railcar, bridge, military, pipeline, and wind energy markets. Algoma recently completed a transformative transition from blast furnace to Electric Arc Furnace (EAF) steelmaking to reduce carbon emissions by up to 70%.

Existential Flexibility

This was a multi-billion-dollar bet that caused enormous near-term financial pain ($985M net loss) but positions the company for long-term viability in a carbon-constrained world. Sinek would view this as a company willing to destroy its present to serve its future [2 pts].

Investment Thesis
Algoma Steel is in the midst of a potentially transformative EAF transition that, if successful, could structurally reduce operating costs and position the company as a leading low-carbon steel producer in North America [1][3]. The stock trades at just $4.73 with a $499M market cap, offering significant upside if EAF ramp-up delivers the expected cost improvements and demand normalizes. The completion of blast furnace wind-down removes legacy environmental liabilities and up to 3 million tonnes of annual CO2 emissions. A new defense partnership with Roshel and potential benefits from North American trade protection add near-term catalysts [2].
The bear case is severe and well-supported by current financials. Algoma posted a $985M net loss in 2025 on $2.1B revenue (negative 47% margin), with operating margins of negative 90% and free cash flow of negative $421M [6]. D/E stands at 186% with only $78M cash against $916M debt, creating genuine solvency risk if the EAF ramp-up is delayed or steel demand remains soft [6]. Q1 2026 EBITDA guidance is negative $25-35M including $90-95M in EAF capacity utilization adjustments, meaning profitability remains elusive in the near term [1]. Revenue has declined 18.2% annually over three years, reflecting both the operational disruption and a weak North American steel market. The CEO transition to Rajat Marwah adds leadership uncertainty during the most critical phase of the transformation [2].
Key Catalysts
