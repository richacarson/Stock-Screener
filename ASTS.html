Company Profile
Technology ·Communication Equipment ·NASDAQ ·United States
AST SpaceMobile, Inc. designs, develops, and deploys the BlueBird constellation of satellites to provide cellular broadband network access directly to unmodified smartphones. The SpaceMobile service targets the estimated 5.5 billion mobile subscribers globally who lack reliable connectivity, partnering with nearly 60 mobile network operators covering over 3 billion subscribers.

Investment Thesis
AST SpaceMobile is creating an entirely new market — direct-to-smartphone satellite broadband — with proven technology (98.9 Mbps peak speed), FCC authorization, 60 MNO partners covering 3B+ subscribers, and full-year 2026 revenue guidance of $150-200M as commercial services begin [1]. The $3.5B cash position provides multi-year runway, and the founder-CEO's equity-only compensation signals maximum alignment with long-term shareholder value creation [5].
This remains a high-risk, high-conviction investment: the company lost $191M in Q1 2026 alone, carries $112% D/E, and faces execution risk on deploying 45 satellites while scaling commercial operations from near-zero [1]. Competition from SpaceX/T-Mobile's Starlink Direct-to-Cell partnership creates credible competitive threat, and the shareholder securities class-action lawsuit regarding production timeline misrepresentations highlights governance risk. At $25B market cap for a company with $85M TTM revenue, the stock prices in substantial future success that must be validated by consistent satellite deployments and MNO revenue ramp throughout 2026-2027.
Key Catalysts
  1. Successful deployment of ~45 BlueBird satellites in orbit during 2026, validating manufacturing scale-up and enabling commercial service coverage expansion [1]
  2. Revenue ramp toward $150-200M full-year 2026 guidance from MNO partnerships and US government contracts, demonstrating commercial viability of the direct-to-smartphone model [1]
  3. Additional MNO partnership announcements and geographic expansion beyond the current 60-partner, 3B-subscriber footprint, particularly in high-ARPU developed markets
Key Risks
  1. Satellite deployment execution risk: any launch failures, manufacturing delays, or on-orbit performance issues could derail the constellation buildout timeline and revenue guidance [1]
  2. Competition from SpaceX/T-Mobile Starlink Direct-to-Cell partnership, which has the backing of the world's most prolific rocket launcher and largest US carrier, potentially commoditizing the market before ASTS achieves scale
  3. Cash burn sustainability: $191M net loss in Q1 2026 with $379M capex means the $3.5B cash position could be consumed within 2-3 years if revenue ramp disappoints, potentially requiring dilutive fundraising [1]
