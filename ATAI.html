Company Profile
Healthcare ·Biotechnology ·NASDAQ ·United States
AtaiBeckley is a clinical-stage biopharmaceutical company developing psychedelic and neuroplastogen-based therapeutics for mental health disorders. Its pipeline includes BPL-003 (intranasal mebufotenin for treatment-resistant depression), VLS-01 (buccal DMT film for TRD), and EMP-01 (oral R-MDMA for social anxiety disorder). Formed through the merger of atai Life Sciences and Beckley Psytech in November 2025.
99 Employees ·www.ataibeckley.com

Investment Thesis
AtaiBeckley represents a high-risk, high-reward opportunity in psychedelic therapeutics addressing the massive unmet need in treatment-resistant depression (affecting ~100M patients globally). The company's BPL-003 (intranasal mebufotenin) has FDA Breakthrough Therapy designation with Phase 3 initiation on track for Q2 2026, while Phase 2a data showed sustained antidepressant effects through Week 12 with a 19-point MADRS reduction [1]. The diversified pipeline across three distinct mechanisms (5-MeO-DMT, DMT, R-MDMA) with AI-driven drug discovery reduces single-program dependency risk [2]. Recent CRSP index inclusion (March 2026) triggers mandatory buying from $3T+ in passive index assets, providing technical support [1]. Cash of $220.7M with runway into early 2029 eliminates near-term dilution risk, and the Beckley Psytech merger consolidated the two strongest psychedelic pipelines [1].
The bear case is primarily clinical and regulatory. AtaiBeckley has experienced multiple trial failures — arketamine (PCN-101) failed its primary endpoint, and the Recognify schizophrenia program was abandoned after 250-patient study failure [4]. The FDA's refusal to approve Lykos Therapeutics' MDMA for PTSD creates precedent risk for the entire psychedelic therapeutics category, potentially raising the bar for AtaiBeckley's EMP-01 program. Revenue of $4.1M TTM against $103M annual cash burn means the company is years from profitability, and additional dilutive financing is likely before any product reaches commercial launch [5]. Schedule I classification of psychedelic compounds creates manufacturing, distribution, and insurance reimbursement challenges even after potential FDA approval. The $1.29B market cap prices in substantial clinical success that binary trial outcomes may not deliver. Peter Thiel's involvement as a major investor has drawn ESG-focused scrutiny [4].
