Investment Thesis
Atour offers a compelling growth story as China's largest upper midscale hotel chain with a differentiated lifestyle retail platform driving 35% total revenue growth and 67% retail growth in 2025. The forward P/E of 14.3x significantly undervalues a company with 49.7% ROE, 25.3% EBITDA margins, and RMB 5.9B in cash. The asset-light franchise model generates strong free cash flow, and 2026 guidance of 20-24% revenue growth with 25-30% retail growth provides visibility into continued expansion.
The bear case centers on China macro risk — consumer confidence, property sector contagion, and potential geopolitical escalation could compress hotel RevPAR and retail spending. The Cayman Islands VIE structure exposes investors to regulatory risk if China tightens rules on offshore listings. ESG management rated 'Weak' by Sustainalytics, the limited dividend track record (only 2 years), and the 2026 growth deceleration from 35% to 20-24% may disappoint momentum investors. As a Chinese ADR, ATAT carries delisting and capital controls risk.
