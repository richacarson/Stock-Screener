Company Profile
Healthcare ·Medical Devices ·NASDAQ ·United States
Alphatec Holdings is a medical technology company dedicated to revolutionizing spine surgery. The company designs, develops, and markets spinal implants, instruments, biologics, and imaging/informatics systems including the EOS full-body imaging platform, SafeOp neural monitoring, and Valence navigation/robotics system. ATEC focuses exclusively on surgical treatment of spinal disorders.

Investment Thesis
Alphatec Holdings is a high-growth spine surgery disruptor approaching its profitability inflection point, with 2025 revenue of $764M (up 25%), surgical volume growth of 24%, and 2026 guidance of $890M (17% growth) with $134M adjusted EBITDA and $20M+ free cash flow [1]. The company's differentiated platform — combining EOS AI-powered imaging, SafeOp neural monitoring, and Valence navigation/robotics under the AlphaInformatiX umbrella — creates a procedural ecosystem that larger competitors have not replicated [2]. Gross margins of 69.6% demonstrate strong pricing power, and CEO Pat Miles' 7+ year track record of transforming ATEC from a struggling small-cap into the fastest-growing pure-play spine company provides execution credibility. At $11.66/share (21.2x forward P/E on $0.55 EPS), the stock offers asymmetric upside if profitability scales as guided.
The bear case focuses on financial fragility and insider selling. D/E of 1,654.5% reflects near-zero book value from accumulated losses, and the $596M debt load against $161M cash requires continued revenue growth to service [5]. GAAP profit margin of -18.8% and ROE of -630.7% mean the company has never been profitable on a GAAP basis, and the profitability inflection ($20M+ FCF in 2026) is still forward guidance, not proven performance. Insider selling of $7.7M+ by the CFO, COO, and multiple officers in March 2026 is concerning for a company guiding toward its first profitable year [4]. The stock has declined from $23.29 52-week high to $11.66, reflecting either execution doubt or the broad market selloff. Sustainalytics flags a 'Highest Controversy' level for ESG risk, and the company's heavy reliance on independent sales agents creates distribution fragility [3]. Spine surgery is highly competitive with Medtronic, Globus, and Stryker having vastly greater resources.

AI Resilience MODERATE

AI Risk Protection (6/9): Spine surgery is a physical-world medical procedure requiring hands-on surgical skill, FDA-approved implants, and hospital infrastructure that AI cannot replace [1]. Spine implants, biologics, and surgical instruments are tangible products that must be physically manufactured, sterilized, and implanted. The regulatory framework (FDA 510(k) and PMA) creates significant barriers to disruption. However, AI could disrupt portions of surgical planning and navigation, potentially commoditizing ATEC's informatics differentiation. AI Opportunity (1/1): ATEC generates meaningful AI-enabled revenue through EOS Insight, its AI-powered end-to-end spine surgery platform that integrates imaging, navigation, and robotic assistance [2]. The AlphaInformatiX platform uses AI for surgical planning and intraoperative decision support, making AI a core competitive differentiator rather than an add-on.
