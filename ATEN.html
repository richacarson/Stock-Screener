Investment Thesis
A10 Networks delivered a record 2025 with $290.6M revenue, 79.3% gross margins, and strong profitability. The company is well-positioned to benefit from AI infrastructure buildouts and rising cybersecurity spending. The forward P/E of 18.9x is reasonable for a growing, profitable tech company with $377.8M in cash. Management's 10-12% revenue growth guidance for 2026 suggests continued momentum.
The bear case includes elevated trailing P/E of 37.6x, a YoY earnings decline of 47.5%, and competition from much larger players like F5, Cloudflare, and Palo Alto Networks. The dividend has shown zero growth since initiation, and the company's relatively small scale ($290M revenue) limits pricing power and R&D investment capacity relative to peers. Customer concentration risk is a factor in the enterprise security market.
