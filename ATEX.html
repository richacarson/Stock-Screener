Innovation STRONG

Anterix has created a unique business model around enabling private LTE broadband networks for utilities using its exclusive 900 MHz spectrum portfolio. The January 2026 FCC vote to expand the 900 MHz broadband allocation from 6 MHz to 10 MHz was a transformative regulatory achievement that Anterix petitioned for. The November 2025 launch of TowerX with Crown Castle offers a turnkey tower service to accelerate 900 MHz deployments. Eight utilities now leverage the spectrum across 15 states, and the $3 billion pipeline of prospective contracts demonstrates strong market demand for the company's innovative spectrum-as-a-service model.

Investment Thesis
Anterix owns an irreplaceable asset — the largest licensed 900 MHz spectrum portfolio in the U.S. — positioned at the intersection of utility modernization and private wireless broadband. The FCC's January 2026 expansion of the broadband allocation from 6 to 10 MHz significantly enhances the spectrum's capabilities. With $123M in contracted proceeds, a $3B pipeline, zero debt, and the TowerX partnership accelerating deployments, the catalysts for value realization are accumulating.
The bear case centers on lumpy revenue recognition, deeply negative forward P/E suggesting expected losses, and execution risk in converting the $3B pipeline into binding contracts. Revenue growth has been essentially flat (0.4% YoY) despite the compelling narrative, and fiscal 2027 estimates point to a return to losses after a one-time positive year. The market-by-market clearing process is slow, and spectrum clearing obligations of $43.6M can compress economics.
