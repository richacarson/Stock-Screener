Company Profile
Industrials ·Metal Fabrication ·NYSE ·United States
ATI Inc. produces and sells specialty materials and complex components for aerospace, defense, medical, and energy markets worldwide. The company operates through High Performance Materials & Components and Advanced Alloys & Solutions segments, specializing in titanium alloys, nickel-based superalloys, and advanced stainless steels used in jet engines, airframes, missiles, and medical devices.

Investment Thesis
ATI is a rare pure-play on the aerospace and defense materials supercycle, with an effective duopoly in domestic titanium production and deepening strategic positioning as A&D grows to 70%+ of revenue in 2026. The company's 2025 results were exceptional — adjusted EBITDA up 18% to $859M, free cash flow up 53% to $380M, and jet engine sales up 21% — with 2026 guidance calling for $975M-$1B EBITDA (another 16% growth) [1][2]. The $1.2B in long-term aerospace sales commitments provides unusual revenue visibility for a materials company.
The HOLD rating reflects strong fundamentals tempered by a forward P/E of 32x and trailing P/E of 58x that prices in significant continued growth. The near-term earnings dip (Q4 2025 EPS down 29.5% YoY) was driven by timing of shipments and mix, creating noise in the trailing metrics. The key risk is ATI's concentration in aerospace — if commercial aviation build rates disappoint or defense spending faces budget pressure, the premium valuation could compress. The specialty metals business is inherently cyclical despite the structural tailwinds, and the company's cultural challenges (Glassdoor 3.3/5) warrant monitoring as it scales capacity [1][6].
Key Catalysts
  1. 2026 EBITDA guidance of $975M-$1B represents 16% growth with mid-teens jet engine sales growth and missile revenue continuing to surge, supported by $1.2B in long-term commitments [1][2]
  2. A&D mix exceeding 70% of revenue in 2026 reduces cyclical commodity exposure and supports margin expansion toward specialty materials peer levels [2]
  3. Capacity expansion in isothermal forging and specialty alloys positions ATI to capture growing demand from next-generation engine programs (LEAP, GE9X) and defense platforms [1][4]
Key Risks
  1. Forward P/E of 32x and trailing P/E of 58x price in continued strong execution — any disruption to commercial aviation build rates (Boeing/Airbus delays) or defense budget cuts could trigger significant de-rating [1][2]
  2. Heavy concentration in aerospace and defense (68-70%+ of revenue) creates single-sector dependency, amplifying cyclical risk if the aerospace supercycle peaks earlier than expected [2]
  3. Glassdoor reviews (3.3/5) cite cultural concerns including leadership focus on revenue over people; scaling manufacturing capacity requires workforce engagement that cultural challenges could undermine [6]

AI Resilience (25%)
AI Resilience LOW RISK
9
AI Risk Protection (8/9): ATI's core business — melting, forging, and machining titanium and nickel superalloys for jet engines, missiles, and medical devices — is fundamentally physical-world manufacturing that AI cannot replace [1][4]. The company operates in a heavily regulated, capital-intensive industry with multi-year qualification cycles; switching costs are enormous because aerospace alloy specifications take years to certify. AI actually increases demand for ATI's materials as autonomous systems, drones, and AI-enabled defense platforms all require the same specialty alloys. The domestic titanium oligopoly position is structurally immune to AI disruption [4]. AI Opportunity (1/1): ATI deploys AI internally for predictive maintenance and yield optimization across its manufacturing operations, and the broader AI infrastructure buildout drives incremental demand for precision components in data center cooling systems and power generation equipment [3].
