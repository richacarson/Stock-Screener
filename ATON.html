Company Profile
Financial Services ·Asset Management ·NASDAQ ·United States
AlphaTON Capital Corp. (renamed Alpha Compute Corp. in April 2026) is a NASDAQ-listed technology and digital asset treasury company formerly known as Portage Biotech Inc. The company pivoted from oncology drug development to AI compute infrastructure and TON blockchain treasury management in September 2025. Operations focus on GPU-as-a-service via NVIDIA B300 clusters, the Cocoon AI inference platform for Telegram, and TON token treasury management. Incorporated in the British Virgin Islands with principal offices in Dover, Delaware.

Investment Thesis
AlphaTON Capital (now Alpha Compute) is a $6.4M micro-cap that pivoted from failed oncology biotech to AI compute infrastructure and TON blockchain treasury management in September 2025 [4]. The bull case rests on early revenue generation from Cocoon AI inference processing on NVIDIA B300 clusters since December 2025, a $46M GPU expansion commitment, and the $44M private placement demonstrating capital access [1][3]. If the company can scale GPU-as-a-service revenue to its ~$23M annual projection and TON treasury holdings appreciate, the stock's 94% decline from highs could represent a speculative entry point.
The bear case is overwhelming. A 4-person company with a $6.4M market cap and going concern disclosure is committing to $46M in GPU infrastructure — a funding gap that requires massive additional dilution or debt [3][5]. The stock has lost 94.1% of its value over twelve months, trading at $0.27 with daily liquidity below $1M. The serial rebranding (Portage Biotech to AlphaTON to Alpha Compute in under a year) and the connection to promotional figures like Anthony Scaramucci raise serious credibility concerns [4]. The GPU-as-a-service market is dominated by AWS, Azure, and GCP with effectively infinite balance sheets, making sustainable competitive advantage nearly impossible for a 4-person startup. The $123.7M reported net income is from non-recurring fair value adjustments on crypto holdings, masking an operating loss of approximately -$14.89M [5]. This is a highly speculative situation with significant risk of permanent capital loss.
