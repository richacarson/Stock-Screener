Company Profile

Mission Produce is a global leader in the sourcing, farming, packaging, marketing, and distribution of avocados, mangoes, and blueberries. The company operates through Marketing and Distribution, International Farming, and Blueberries segments. In May 2026, Mission completed its $430M acquisition of Calavo Growers, creating the dominant North American avocado platform with expanded prepared foods capabilities. Founded in 1983 and headquartered in Oxnard, California.

Investment Thesis
Mission Produce is the world's largest avocado distributor, now consolidating the industry through its $430M Calavo Growers acquisition completed in May 2026. The combined platform creates dominant scale in North American avocado sourcing, ripening, and distribution with $25M+ in expected synergies and expansion into prepared foods [1][2]. D/E of 35.6% is conservative, Forward P/E of 13.8x is attractive, and the company's vertically integrated farming operations in Peru and Colombia provide supply security and margin upside as trees mature. The Inspire Insight score of 62 with 11 positive ESG attributions including Ethical Labor Practices and Ethical Supply Chain Management reflects responsible agricultural practices [3]. The business is structurally immune to AI disruption — avocados require physical growing, harvesting, ripening, and distribution that no technology can replace.
The bear case centers on commodity pricing exposure, thin margins, and integration risk. Q2 FY2026 revenue declined 24% YoY driven by a 36% drop in per-unit avocado prices from higher Mexican fruit supply, highlighting Mission's sensitivity to commodity cycles [1]. Profit margin of 1.83% is structurally thin for food distribution, and ROE of 3.98% indicates modest capital efficiency. The Calavo integration adds execution complexity — $430M in acquisition spending during a revenue downturn increases balance sheet strain. Trailing P/E of 35.9x is elevated relative to depressed current earnings. The avocado industry faces secular risks from water scarcity in growing regions, tariff uncertainty on Mexican imports, and potential consumer shifts. Revenue growth 5yr CAGR data is unavailable, limiting visibility into long-term growth trajectory. Zacks consensus expects a 15.2% earnings decline in FY2026 before 5.9% recovery in FY2027 [1].
Key Catalysts
  1. Calavo Growers integration delivering $25M+ in synergies through combined sourcing, distribution, and overhead elimination, plus new prepared foods revenue stream from guacamole and fresh-cut products [2]
  2. Avocado pricing recovery — current 36% price decline reflects cyclically high Mexican supply; normalization would drive significant revenue and margin expansion on higher volume base (+15% volume growth already achieved) [1]
  3. International farming operations in Peru and Colombia reaching maturity, providing owned-source fruit with better margins and supply chain control versus third-party sourcing [3]
Key Risks
  1. Commodity pricing exposure — avocado prices declined 36% YoY in Q2 FY2026 from higher Mexican supply, and Mission lacks pricing power in a commodity market; further price weakness would compress already-thin 1.83% profit margins [1]
  2. Calavo integration execution risk — $430M acquisition during a revenue downturn increases leverage and operational complexity; failure to achieve synergy targets would strain the balance sheet and delay margin improvement [2]
  3. Water scarcity and climate risk in key growing regions (Peru, Colombia, Mexico) could disrupt avocado supply; tariff uncertainty on Mexican fruit imports adds regulatory risk to the core business [3]

AI Resilience LOW RISK

AI Risk Protection (8/9): Mission Produce's core business — growing, sourcing, ripening, packing, and distributing avocados and other fresh produce — is fundamentally physical and cannot be replicated or replaced by AI. Agriculture requires land, water, climate, labor, and physical logistics infrastructure. Avocado ripening is a biological process requiring controlled environments and physical handling. The global supply chain from farms in Peru, Colombia, and Mexico to retailers in the US involves customs, cold chain logistics, and perishable goods management that AI cannot substitute. There is no analyst concern about AI disruption to the fresh produce distribution industry. AI may modestly improve demand forecasting and supply chain optimization, but these are efficiency gains, not business model threats. AI Opportunity (0/1): Mission Produce does not generate revenue from AI products or services. Any AI adoption would be for internal efficiency rather than revenue generation.
