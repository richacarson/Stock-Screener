Company Profile
Technology ·Semiconductor Equipment & Materials ·NASDAQ ·United States
AXT, Inc. develops and manufactures high-performance compound semiconductor substrate wafers comprising indium phosphide (InP), gallium arsenide (GaAs), and germanium (Ge). These substrates are used in applications where standard silicon wafers cannot meet performance requirements, including AI/data center optical interconnects, 5G infrastructure, passive optical networks, LED lighting, lasers, and satellite solar cells. Headquartered in Fremont, California, with manufacturing facilities in China through its subsidiary Tongmei.

Investment Thesis
AXT holds a dominant 40% global market share in indium phosphide substrates — the critical material enabling high-speed optical interconnects in AI data centers [2][3]. Q1 2026 revenue of $26.9M jumped 39% YoY with the company carrying $100M+ in InP backlog and targeting capacity doubling in H2 2026 funded by a $100M capital raise. Analysts project 60%+ revenue growth for 2026 and a path toward profitability as scale benefits materialize. The structural thesis is compelling: every new AI data center requires thousands of InP-based optical transceivers, and AXT is the dominant supplier in an oligopolistic market with multi-year customer qualification barriers.
The bear case centers on extreme valuation disconnect: a $4.97B market cap on $96M TTM revenue (52x sales) for a company that is currently unprofitable with -15.3% profit margins and -5% ROE [1]. The stock has rallied from $1.80 to $78 in 12 months on AI hype, creating a setup where any execution miss could trigger severe multiple compression. Chinese export permit dependency introduces binary event risk — a single permit denial cycle could crater quarterly revenue as seen in mid-2025 when FY2025 revenue declined 11% to $88.3M [1]. CEO Morris Young's $22M+ in insider sales during 2026 raises questions about whether management believes the current valuation is sustainable [6]. If AI capex spending decelerates or alternative optical interconnect technologies like silicon photonics gain traction, AXT's concentrated product thesis faces structural risk.
Key Catalysts
  1. InP capacity doubling in H2 2026 enabling AXT to convert its $100M+ backlog into revenue, potentially driving 60%+ annual revenue growth and a path toward profitability
  2. Continued hyperscale AI data center buildout by Microsoft, Google, Amazon, and Meta driving sustained multi-year demand for optical interconnect substrates
  3. Resolution of Chinese export permit bottleneck could unlock pent-up demand and normalize quarterly revenue volatility
Key Risks
  1. Extreme valuation at 52x TTM revenue for a loss-making company creates significant downside risk if AI capex sentiment shifts or quarterly results miss expectations
  2. Chinese export permit dependency introduces binary regulatory risk — tightening of controls on indium phosphide exports could materially impair revenue without warning
  3. CEO Morris Young's $22M+ in share sales during 2026 signals potential insider concern about valuation sustainability despite publicly bullish guidance
