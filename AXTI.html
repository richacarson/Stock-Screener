Company Profile
Technology ·Semiconductor Equipment & Materials ·NASDAQ ·United States
AXT designs, develops, manufactures, and distributes compound semiconductor substrates — indium phosphide (InP), gallium arsenide (GaAs), and germanium (Ge) — used in high-speed data center connectivity, 5G communications, fiber optic lasers, silicon photonics, lidar, and AI infrastructure. The company holds approximately 40% global market share in InP substrates and operates primarily through its Beijing-based Tongmei subsidiary.

Investment Thesis
AXT holds a dominant 40% global market share in indium phosphide substrates — the critical material enabling high-speed optical interconnects in AI data centers [1]. As hyperscalers build out AI infrastructure, demand for InP is surging — AXT expects scale-out optics demand to double in 2026 and again in 2027, and the company is doubling production capacity by year-end 2026 funded by a $100M capital raise [1]. Analysts forecast 41% revenue growth in 2026 to ~$100M and a doubling to $200M in 2027 as AI infrastructure spending accelerates. Co-founder CEO Dr. Morris Young brings 40 years of compound semiconductor expertise, and the proprietary VGF technology provides a quality advantage over competitors [2]. The $3B+ market cap reflects investor conviction in the AI infrastructure thesis.
The bear case is dominated by geopolitical risk and extreme valuation. AXT manufactures primarily in Beijing through its Tongmei subsidiary, and Chinese government export controls on InP (imposed February 2025) require per-customer export permits that have already caused revenue guidance misses — FY2025 revenue declined 11% to $88.3M [1]. At 130x forward P/E and a P/S ratio of 22x, the stock prices in flawless execution of capacity doubling, export permit approvals, and sustained AI demand — any disruption would trigger severe multiple compression. The 445% stock appreciation since November 2025 has been almost entirely P/S expansion rather than fundamental improvement [1]. Insider selling by the CEO and CFO during this run-up is a concerning signal. US-China tensions could escalate further, potentially restricting AXT's ability to export from its Chinese facilities or forcing costly reshoring of manufacturing [3].
Key Catalysts
  1. InP capacity doubling by end of 2026 funded by $100M capital raise, enabling AXT to capture surging AI data center interconnect demand at 40% market share [1]
  2. Scale-out optics demand expected to roughly double in 2026 and again in 2027, with scale-up/CPO demand reaching an inflection point around 2028-2029 [1]
  3. Revenue inflection from $88.3M (FY2025) to analyst-estimated $100M (2026) and $200M (2027) as China export permits normalize and AI infrastructure spending accelerates [1]
Key Risks
  1. China export control dependency — InP export permits from China's Ministry of Commerce are required for every customer order, and delays have already caused revenue misses and 11% annual revenue decline [1]
  2. Extreme valuation at 130x forward P/E and 22x P/S — the 445% stock appreciation is almost entirely multiple expansion, leaving no margin of safety if growth disappoints [1]
  3. US-China geopolitical escalation risk — potential restrictions on AXT's Beijing-based Tongmei subsidiary could force costly manufacturing reshoring or loss of competitive cost advantages [3]
