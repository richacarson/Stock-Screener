Company Profile
Industrials ·Aerospace & Defense Components ·NYSE ·United States
Barnes Group Inc. was a global provider of highly engineered precision components and industrial technologies, operating through Industrial and Aerospace segments. In January 2025, the company was acquired by Apollo Funds in a $3.6 billion transaction and subsequently delisted from NYSE in March 2025, separating into Barnes Aerospace and The Industrial Solutions Group. The Aerospace segment had achieved a $1.80B backlog prior to acquisition, with OEM backlog growing 19% sequentially to a record $1.46B.

Investment Thesis
Barnes Group's Aerospace segment — with a $1.80B backlog, 49% YoY sales growth in Q3 2024, and a projected $242M EBITDA run-rate — represented a high-quality aerospace manufacturing business with long-term tailwinds from commercial aviation recovery and defense spending growth [1]. The Apollo acquisition at $3.6B validated this thesis, separating the Aerospace and Industrial businesses to unlock individual value. Barnes Aerospace, now independent, has a clean balance sheet and record backlog entering 2026.
The stock no longer trades publicly, having been acquired by Apollo Funds and delisted from NYSE in March 2025 [1]. From a public equity standpoint, there is no actionable investment opportunity. The industrial segment faces secular headwinds from automation and EV transition impacts on traditional drivetrain component demand. Any future public market opportunity would require a Barnes Aerospace IPO or SPAC transaction, with no current timeline disclosed.
Key Catalysts
  1. Barnes Aerospace IPO or re-listing under Apollo ownership would create a public investment opportunity in a high-quality aerospace manufacturing business
  2. Commercial aviation OEM production ramp (Boeing 737 MAX and 787 return to full production) would drive organic growth in the $1.46B backlog
  3. Defense spending increases under the current U.S. government budget would accelerate the military aerospace manufacturing revenue line
Key Risks
  1. No public equity exists — the stock has been delisted and the company is now private, making this report informational only
  2. Apollo's typical 5-7 year hold period means limited public market opportunity for several years
  3. Integration of MB Aerospace and management of the post-separation industrial entity carries execution risk under private ownership
