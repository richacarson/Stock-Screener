Company Profile
Energy ·Oil & Gas E&P ·NYSE American ·United States
Battalion Oil Corporation is an independent energy company focused on acquiring, producing, exploring, and developing onshore oil and natural gas assets in the Delaware Basin of West Texas. Operating approximately 27,097 net acres in Ward County (Monument Draw position), the company produced 12,578 Boe/d in Q1 2026. Formerly known as Halcon Resources Corporation, founded in 1987, headquartered in Houston, Texas.

Investment Thesis
Battalion Oil's speculative bull case rests on asset value and production momentum: Q1 2026 production increased to 12,578 Boe/d (up from 11,900 in Q1 2025), positive equity reached $157.1 million, net debt fell to $108.3 million, and lease operating costs improved to $9.82/Boe from $11.01/Boe [1]. The Ward County acquisition expanded the Monument Draw acreage position to 27,097 net acres with development upside, and the forward P/E of 9.5x implies meaningful upside if the company can sustain profitability. At a $48 million market cap, the stock trades at a steep discount to proved reserve value.
The bear case is severe and multidimensional: Q1 2026 revenue fell to $39.2 million from $47.5 million despite production growth, driven by a $9.73/Boe decline in realized prices [1]. The $64.8 million Q1 net loss, -32% profit margin, -29.4% ROE, and negative book value of -$3.12 per share demonstrate fundamental financial distress. Battalion is operating under NYSE American compliance review with a November 30, 2026 deadline — failure means delisting. With only 40 employees managing significant acreage and $159 million in debt, the company has extreme operational and financial leverage. Revenue has declined from $359 million in 2022 to a $157 million trailing rate, a 3-year CAGR of -22.7%. This is a survival situation, not an investment opportunity aligned with IOWN's disciplined framework.
