Company Profile
Energy ·Oil & Gas E&P ·AMEX ·United States
Battalion Oil Corporation is a micro-cap independent energy company focused on the acquisition, production, exploration, and development of onshore oil and natural gas assets in the Delaware Basin (Pecos, Reeves, Ward, and Winkler counties, Texas). Formerly Halcon Resources Corporation, it renamed in January 2020 after a management overhaul following bankruptcy. Headquartered in Houston, Texas with approximately 40 employees and a market cap of ~$28M.

Investment Thesis
Battalion offers a speculative deep-value proposition — the Fury Resources merger agreement values shares at $9.80 versus the current $1.25 price, representing a theoretical 684% upside if the deal closes [2]. Operationally, the company achieved record drilling results in the Delaware Basin and completed the Monument Draw midstream project 8% under budget, increasing production throughput 20.3% to 12,578 Boe/d [1][2]. The $62.5M West Quito Draw divestiture provides near-term liquidity, and the acreage position of 27,097 acres in the Delaware Basin represents tangible asset value that may exceed the current $28M market cap.
The bear case is overwhelming. Q1 2026 net loss of $64.81M on $39.17M revenue represents catastrophic value destruction, driven by a $9.73/Boe decline in realized prices and a shift in oil mix from 53% to 47% [1]. The NYSE American compliance notice requires remediation by November 2026 or delisting ensues, the balance sheet carries $108.3M net debt against negative book equity of ($3.12/share), and extreme short interest with $375M dilution overhang threatens further equity erosion [2]. The Fury Resources merger status remains uncertain — the merger agreement was amended, and at $1.25 versus a $9.80 deal price, the market is pricing significant closure risk. Sustainalytics rates ESG management as 'Weak,' and the Halcon Resources bankruptcy legacy creates governance concerns [3].
Key Catalysts

  1. NYSE American delisting if the company fails to meet compliance requirements by November 2026 — negative stockholders' equity and accumulated losses currently violate listing standards
