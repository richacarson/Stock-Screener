Company Profile
Financial Services ·Banks - Regional ·NYSE ·Brazil
Banco Bradesco S.A. is one of Brazil's largest private banks, operating through Banking and Insurance segments. The company provides investment, national, international, and private banking services, along with insurance, pension plans, and asset management. Bradesco serves millions of customers across Brazil with a branch network and digital platforms. Q1 2026 recurring net income reached R$6.8B (+16.1% YoY) on R$36.9B total revenue.

Investment Thesis
Banco Bradesco is one of Brazil's largest private banks trading at 6.6x forward earnings with a 4.5% dividend yield, delivering Q1 2026 recurring net income of R$6.8B (+16.1% YoY) on R$36.9B total revenue (+14.0% YoY) [1]. The cultural transformation under CEO Noronha is showing results with ROAE improving to 15.8%, the loan portfolio reaching R$1.09T (+8.4% YoY), and the cost-to-income ratio falling to 46.9% [1][2]. The bank's ninth consecutive quarterly earnings increase signals sustainable operational momentum.
The bear case centers on Bradesco's structural challenges: intense competition from Nubank and fintechs is pressuring traditional banking margins, the bank's cultural revolution is early-stage and unproven, and BRL/USD currency risk creates volatility for ADR holders [4]. The WATCH recommendation at 48 reflects attractive valuations and improving fundamentals offset by weak organizational culture (Euromoney's 'cultural revolution' assessment), a generic banking mission, finite competitive mindset, and emerging market execution risks. The bank needs to demonstrate sustained ROE improvement and successful digital transformation to warrant a higher rating [3][4].
Key Catalysts
  1. Continued ROAE improvement toward management's target — Q1 2026 ROAE of 15.8% is trending in the right direction from 13.4% trailing [1]
  2. Cultural transformation under CEO Noronha bringing external talent and customer-centric operations could unlock long-term competitiveness [4]
  3. R$1.09T loan portfolio growth driven by higher-quality secured lending (payroll, auto, real estate) improving credit quality and margins [1]
Key Risks
  1. Intense fintech competition from Nubank (100M+ customers) and other digital banks pressuring traditional banking margins in Brazil [4]
  2. BRL/USD currency risk creates significant volatility for ADR holders — Bradesco's fundamentals in BRL may not translate to USD returns [3]
  3. Cultural transformation is early-stage — decades of insular leadership practices cannot be reversed quickly, and execution risk on digital initiatives remains high [4]
