Consumer Discretionary ·Specialty Retail ·NYSE ·United States
Bath & Body Works, Inc. is a specialty retailer of home fragrance, body care, and soap and sanitizer products. The company operates approximately 1,850 company-operated and franchised stores globally and a robust e-commerce channel. Spun off from L Brands in 2021, headquartered in Columbus, Ohio. Key brands include Bath & Body Works, White Barn, and Aromatherapy.

Investment Thesis
Bath & Body Works is the dominant U.S. specialty retailer in home fragrance and body care, generating $7.25B in annual revenue and ~$600M in free cash flow from a highly recognizable brand with 1,850+ stores [1]. Q1 2026 results beat guidance with $0.32 adjusted EPS vs. $0.29 expected, and the stock trades at just 8.4x forward earnings — an undemanding valuation for a market-leading consumer franchise. New CEO Daniel Heaf (ex-Nike, where he oversaw growth from $10.4B to $22.3B) brings a transformation playbook focused on the 'Consumer First Formula,' and the 3.5% dividend yield provides income while investors wait for the turnaround [2].
The bear case centers on a business in secular decline — revenue has fallen for four consecutive years from $7.88B to $7.25B, and full-year 2026 guidance of -4.5% to -2.5% sales decline signals continued deterioration [1]. Adjusted EPS guidance of $2.40-$2.65 is well below 2025's $3.21, reflecting tariff headwinds and promotional intensity. The CFO departure in June 2026 creates leadership instability at a critical moment. Negative shareholder equity from aggressive buybacks and L Brands legacy liabilities means the balance sheet provides no margin of safety. The core risk is that BBWI's fragrance/body care category faces secular headwinds from private-label competition, shifting consumer preferences, and macro sensitivity, and no amount of operational optimization can reverse the decline.
