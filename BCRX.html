Company Profile
Healthcare ·Drug Manufacturers - Specialty & Generic ·NASDAQ ·United States
BioCryst Pharmaceuticals, Inc. is a biotechnology company focused on rare diseases, principally hereditary angioedema (HAE). The company's lead product, ORLADEYO (berotralstat), is the first and only oral once-daily prophylactic treatment for HAE. BioCryst acquired Astria Therapeutics and its navenibart subcutaneous HAE treatment to build a comprehensive HAE franchise. Founded in 1986, headquartered in Durham, North Carolina.

Investment Thesis
BioCryst has transformed from a pre-revenue biotech into a profitable rare disease franchise with ORLADEYO generating $601.8M in 2025 revenue (38% YoY growth) and 2026 guidance of $625-645M [1]. The company achieved record non-GAAP operating profit of $54M in Q1 2026 (+25% YoY), and the Astria acquisition adds navenibart as a next-gen subcutaneous HAE treatment with European licensing generating $70M upfront [1]. ROE of 18.7% validates the transition to sustainable profitability, and the analyst consensus Strong Buy with a $21.30 price target implies 114% upside from the current $9.94 stock price. FDA approval of ORLADEYO oral pellets for pediatric patients (ages 2-12) expands the addressable market.
The primary risk is single-product concentration — ORLADEYO represents essentially all of BioCryst's commercial revenue, creating vulnerability to competitive entry, patent expiration, or safety events [1]. Forward P/E of 36.76 reflects growth expectations that could compress if ORLADEYO growth decelerates from the current 21% comparable rate. The Astria acquisition added significant costs that drove a $458M GAAP net loss in the TTM period, and navenibart's commercial viability in a crowded HAE biologics market is unproven. BioCryst ended Q1 2026 with only $261M in cash, and continued investment in the pipeline and integration costs could pressure the balance sheet. The CEO transition from Stonehouse to Gayer, while orderly, introduces execution risk during a critical commercial scaling phase.

  2. Forward P/E of 36.76 prices in continued high growth — any deceleration below the 21% comparable growth rate could trigger significant multiple compression
  3. Cash position of $261M with ongoing integration costs from Astria acquisition and pipeline investment may require additional capital raises
