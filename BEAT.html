Company Profile
Healthcare ·Health Information Services ·NASDAQ ·United States
HeartBeam is a medical technology company developing and commercializing a cable-free, ambulatory 12-lead ECG system that captures the heart's electrical signals in three dimensions. The system consists of a credit card-sized 3D ECG recording device, patient app, physician portal, and cloud-based algorithms for arrhythmia assessment and cardiac monitoring outside healthcare facilities. The company received FDA 510(k) clearance in December 2025.

Investment Thesis
HeartBeam achieved a critical milestone with FDA 510(k) clearance in December 2025 for the first-ever cable-free synthesized 12-lead ECG, creating a new category in home cardiac monitoring [2][3]. The Q1 2026 limited U.S. launch with concierge cardiology practices provides a validation runway, and the heart attack detection indication could expand the addressable market to tens of millions of patients [3]. The Mount Sinai AI collaboration and AIMIGo patent position HeartBeam to evolve from a device company to an AI-powered cardiac intelligence platform [4]. At $48M market cap, successful commercialization could deliver substantial upside from a low base. The $10M April 2026 offering provides runway, and the debt-free balance sheet reduces financial risk [5].
The bear case centers on execution risk and capital needs. HeartBeam is pre-revenue after 10 years of development, with zero commercial track record and $6.9M annual cash burn [1]. The April 2026 offering raised $10M through issuance of 12.5M shares — significant dilution on a ~54M share base [5]. Cash of $4.4M (pre-offering) plus $10M provides approximately 2 years of runway at current burn, but commercialization expenses will increase costs. Competition from established players (iRhythm's Zio with $500M+ revenue, AliveCor's KardiaMobile) creates market penetration risk. Reimbursement uncertainty — whether insurers will pay for home 12-lead ECG monitoring — is the critical unknown. The stock has declined from $1.25 (50-day SMA) to $0.89, suggesting market skepticism about the commercialization timeline [1].
