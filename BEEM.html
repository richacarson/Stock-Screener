Company Profile

Beam Global designs, develops, manufactures, and sells renewably energized infrastructure products and battery solutions, including the EV ARC solar-powered autonomous EV charging station, Solar Tree DCFC off-grid fast charger, and EV-Standard streetlight-integrated charger. The company serves EV charging, energy storage, energy security, and disaster preparedness markets from headquarters in San Diego, California.

Investment Thesis
Beam Global occupies a unique niche as the leading manufacturer of autonomous, off-grid solar-powered EV charging infrastructure, with its patented EV ARC product requiring no construction, permitting, or grid connection [2]. The commercial pivot is showing early signs of success — Q4 2025 revenue of $9.0M grew 7% YoY and 56% sequentially, and commercial customers now represent 72% of revenue versus 38% in 2024, significantly reducing government contract dependency [1]. At $39M market cap with minimal debt (D/E 6.0%), the stock offers high-risk/high-reward exposure to the EV charging infrastructure buildout if commercial traction accelerates. The Inspire Impact Score of 71 (ALIGNED) reflects a business model inherently tied to clean energy, sustainability, and environmental stewardship.
The bear case is severe and existential. Full-year 2025 revenue of $28.2M declined 42.8% from $49.3M in 2024, driven by the collapse of U.S. federal fleet electrification contracts, and the company posted a $27.0M net loss on $28.2M revenue — a -95.6% profit margin that implies the business is burning through cash at an unsustainable rate [1][4]. The $39M market cap and 197 employees leave the company one bad quarter from potential going-concern risk. Competition from well-funded EV charging players (ChargePoint, Blink, Tesla) with broader product portfolios and deeper customer relationships threatens to marginalize Beam's niche autonomous solar product. The company's survival depends on commercial revenue acceleration that has not yet been proven at scale.
