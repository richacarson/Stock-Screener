Company Profile
Real Estate ·Real Estate Services ·NYSE ·China
KE Holdings (Beike) is China's largest integrated online and offline real estate platform, operating across existing home transactions, new home transactions, home renovation and furnishing, home rental services, and emerging services. Founded as Lianjia in 2001, the company pioneered the Agent Cooperation Network (ACN) model connecting brokerages across China.

Investment Thesis
KE Holdings is China's dominant real estate platform trading at 15x forward P/E with analyst consensus of 'Strong Buy' and a $24.36 price target (43% upside) [1]. Despite the Chinese housing downturn, the company maintained 1.2% revenue growth to RMB94.6B through strategic diversification into renovation, rental, and AI-powered services. The $5B buyback program (435M+ shares retired) demonstrates management confidence, and the Hang Seng China Enterprises Index inclusion broadens the investor base [1]. Beike's AI deployment across property visualization, agent training, and home design creates technology differentiation that should drive margin expansion as the housing market stabilizes. At $14.97 with 119,245 employees and RMB3.18T in annual GTV, BEKE offers leveraged exposure to any Chinese housing recovery [2].
The bear case centers on China's structural housing downturn and governance concerns. GTV declined 5.0% YoY with existing home transactions down 4.2% and new home transactions down 8.2%, reflecting persistent market weakness [1]. Earnings declined 83.7% in the latest quarter with profit margin compressing to 3.2% and ROE falling to 4.3%. The securities fraud settlement over overstated platform metrics raises data integrity concerns [3]. Antitrust investigation for anticompetitive 'choose one from two' practices could result in fines or operational restrictions. The SEC HFCAA watchlist creates delisting risk for US-listed shares. China's demographic headwinds (declining population, urbanization slowdown) suggest the housing market may not recover to prior peak levels, limiting long-term GTV growth. The 101% dividend payout ratio is unsustainable without earnings recovery [4].
Key Catalysts
  1. Chinese housing market stabilization driven by government stimulus measures, with any recovery leveraging BEKE's dominant platform position and RMB3.18T annual GTV [1]

Key Risks
  1. Prolonged Chinese housing downturn — GTV down 5% YoY with earnings declining 83.7%, and demographic headwinds suggest structural rather than cyclical weakness [1]
  2. Governance concerns including securities fraud settlement, antitrust investigation for anticompetitive practices, undisclosed founder health condition, and SEC HFCAA watchlist delisting risk [3]
  3. Margin compression — profit margin of 3.2% and ROE of 4.3% with 101% dividend payout ratio are unsustainable without significant earnings recovery [4]
