Company Profile
Healthcare ·Medical Devices ·NYSE ·United States
Butterfly Network, Inc. develops and commercializes handheld, semiconductor-based whole-body ultrasound imaging systems powered by a single silicon chip (Ultrasound-on-Chip). The company's Butterfly iQ+ probe connects to smartphones and tablets, making point-of-care ultrasound (POCUS) accessible to clinicians in emergency rooms, rural clinics, and developing nations. Butterfly integrates AI-powered software for automated image guidance, clinical decision support, and gestational age measurement.
400 Employees ·www.butterflynetwork.com

Investment Thesis
Butterfly Network is executing on a compelling vision of democratizing medical imaging through AI-powered, semiconductor-based handheld ultrasound. Q1 2026 revenue grew 25% to $26.5M with 68.9% gross margin, and software and services revenue surged 68%, validating the transition from hardware sales to a high-margin, AI-driven platform [1]. The company guides 2026 revenue to $117-121M (20-24% growth) and narrowed its adjusted EBITDA loss to $21-25M, suggesting a clear path toward profitability. FDA-cleared AI tools (Gestational Age, HeartFocus cardiac echo) differentiate Butterfly from traditional ultrasound incumbents, and the Butterfly Embedded chip licensing program opens a potentially transformative new revenue stream. The $2.1B market cap reflects investor enthusiasm for the AI-in-healthcare narrative, and the stock's 307% one-year return shows momentum.
The fundamental challenge is that Butterfly remains deeply unprofitable at -73.6% profit margin and -35.2% ROE, with $75.8M in TTM net losses against just $103M in TTM revenue [4]. The $2.1B market cap at 20x trailing revenue requires extraordinary growth and margin expansion to justify. Midjourney Medical's entry into full-body ultrasound scanning signals intensifying competition in the AI-powered imaging space, potentially commoditizing Butterfly's software differentiation [2]. The company's Q1 2026 operating expenses of $32.2M exceeded gross profit of $18.3M, meaning the business consumes cash to operate. With approximately $142M in cash, Butterfly has roughly 2-3 years of runway at current burn rates, but any execution stumble or fundraising dilution could significantly impact the stock. The guided adjusted EBITDA loss of $21-25M for 2026 means GAAP profitability remains distant.
Key Catalysts
  1. Butterfly Embedded Ultrasound-on-Chip licensing initiative could transform the company from device maker to semiconductor IP licensor, unlocking high-margin recurring revenue from third-party device integrations

Key Risks

  2. Midjourney Medical's full-body ultrasound scanner and increasing AI competition in medical imaging could commoditize Butterfly's software differentiation, compressing the premium the market assigns to AI-powered diagnostics
  3. Cash runway of approximately 2-3 years at current burn rates creates potential dilution risk if the company needs to raise additional capital before reaching profitability

The hardware moat is defensible but the software/AI moat faces real competitive pressure.
