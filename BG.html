Bunge Global SA is one of the world's leading agribusiness and food companies, operating in the origination, processing, and distribution of oilseeds, grains, and agricultural commodities. Following its 2024 merger with Viterra, Bunge is the largest global oilseed processor and a leading grain merchandiser, with operations spanning over 40 countries.

Investment Thesis
Bunge Global offers a compelling value proposition as the world's largest oilseed processor trading at 12.0x forward earnings with a 2.23% dividend yield and 20+ years of consecutive dividend payments [1][6]. The transformative Viterra merger — completed in 2024 — created unmatched global scale in oilseed processing and grain merchandising, with Q4 2025 segment EBIT of $756M (up 38% from $546M) demonstrating integration benefits in softseed processing [1]. Revenue of $70.3B and grain merchandising volumes tripling YoY confirm the scale thesis. Management guided to $7.50–$8.00 adjusted EPS for 2026, with significant upside potential as Viterra synergies fully materialize and commodity margins normalize. At $125.83/share, the stock offers food security exposure at a reasonable multiple with income support.
The bear case centers on margin fragility, integration risk, and ESG controversy. Net income fell from $2.24B (2023) to $816M (2025) — a 64% decline — despite revenue growing from $59.5B to $70.3B, revealing the extreme commodity margin sensitivity of the business [1][6]. Profit margin of 1.16% provides virtually no earnings cushion. Free cash flow was negative $7.1B in 2025 due to Viterra integration working capital demands, creating near-term balance sheet pressure at 91% D/E. Management guided 2026 EPS of $7.50–$8.00, below the $8.94 analyst consensus, citing 'limited forward visibility' — a warning sign for earnings sustainability [1]. Deforestation controversies are significant: Global Witness has documented supply chain links to Cerrado deforestation and human rights abuses, and Bunge has resisted the Cerrado Manifesto's 2020 cutoff date despite pressure from Walmart, Tesco, and Unilever [4][5]. These ESG risks create regulatory, reputational, and customer-retention headwinds. EPS growth of -88.8% in 2025 shows the Viterra integration has not yet delivered bottom-line value.
Key Catalysts
  1. Viterra synergy realization — integration of the world's largest oilseed processing network should drive cost savings and volume efficiency as the combined platform matures beyond the first full year [1]
  2. Commodity margin normalization — soybean crush margins and grain trading spreads were depressed in 2025; any recovery in agricultural commodity margins would disproportionately benefit Bunge's expanded processing capacity [1]
  3. Renewable diesel feedstock demand — growing renewable fuel mandates increase demand for soybean oil and other vegetable oils, positioning Bunge's expanded crush capacity as a structural beneficiary [2]
Key Risks
  1. Commodity margin compression — profit margin of 1.16% provides no cushion; a further 50bps decline in agricultural processing margins could eliminate profitability entirely on $70B revenue [1][6]
  2. Deforestation and supply chain ESG controversy — Global Witness, NGOs, and major customers (Walmart, Tesco, Unilever) have pressured Bunge on Cerrado deforestation; failure to resolve creates regulatory and customer-loss risk [4][5]
  3. Viterra integration execution — negative $7.1B FCF in 2025 and below-consensus 2026 guidance suggest integration costs are higher than expected; D/E of 91% limits balance sheet flexibility during the integration period [1][6]

AI Resilience LOW RISK

AI Risk Protection (7/9): Bunge's core business — originating, transporting, processing, and distributing physical agricultural commodities — is structurally immune to AI disruption. You cannot digitize soybean crushing, grain logistics, or crop origination from millions of farmers across 40 countries [1]. The business requires massive physical infrastructure (processing plants, port facilities, storage elevators) and deeply embedded supply chain relationships that AI cannot replicate. AI may improve demand forecasting and logistics optimization, but these are efficiency tools, not substitution risks. Analysts have not flagged AI as a risk for agricultural commodity processors. AI Opportunity (1/1): Bunge is deploying data analytics and technology across its supply chain for operational efficiency, and the Viterra integration creates opportunities for AI-driven supply chain optimization across the combined global network [2].
