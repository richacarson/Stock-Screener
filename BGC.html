Investment Thesis
BGC is executing one of the most impressive platform pivots in financial services: FMX UST now holds 39% US Treasury cash market share (up from 30% YoY) and FMX futures crossed 1% market share in January 2026 with record 200k open interest contracts, challenging CME's Treasury futures monopoly. [1] Full-year 2025 revenue grew 30% to nearly $3B and management guided Q1 2026 revenue growth of 34% at the midpoint. Forward P/E of 7.2 is exceptionally cheap for a company compounding this fast.
Bear case: EPS was -42% YoY in FY2025 due to FMX build-out and transition costs, D/E is elevated at 174%, and profit margin of only 5.5% reflects the heavy reinvestment phase. Founder Howard Lutnick's departure to become US Secretary of Commerce removes a unique operator (37+ year Cantor/BGC tenure) and introduces three Co-CEO governance risk. If FMX futures adoption stalls near current 1% share, the thesis is impaired. Reg/political exposure via Lutnick is an uncommon overhang.
Key Catalysts
  1. FMX Futures Exchange market share accretion above 1% - every additional point of CME futures volume creates material operating leverage
  2. Q1 2026 revenue guidance of $860-920M (34% YoY midpoint) delivery with pretax adjusted earnings up 32%
  3. Continued Fenics Lucera FX/rates growth (+24% YoY) and new fixed income product launches
Key Risks
  1. Three Co-CEO governance structure (Abularrage, Aubin, Windeatt) may struggle vs. single-founder decisiveness of Lutnick era
  2. FMX futures stalling below critical mass as CME defends Treasury futures market
  3. Elevated D/E of 174% constrains flexibility in a financial-markets downturn
