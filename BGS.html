Company Profile
Consumer Defensive ·Packaged Foods ·NYSE ·United States
B&G Foods manufactures, sells, and distributes a portfolio of shelf-stable and frozen foods and household products in the United States, Canada, and Puerto Rico. Brands include Crisco, Green Giant, Ortega, Cream of Wheat, Clabber Girl, Spice Islands, and Dash. The company operates through Specialty, Meals, Frozen & Vegetables, and Spices & Flavor Solutions segments.

Investment Thesis
B&G Foods is a deep value play in packaged foods trading at just 8.2x forward earnings with a 13.7% dividend yield — but only if the turnaround thesis materializes. FY2026 guidance of $1.655B-$1.695B revenue and $265M-$275M adjusted EBITDA reflects a streamlined portfolio after divesting Green Giant frozen, and early Q1 2026 data shows base business growth of ~4% [1]. Free cash flow of $137.5M covers the reduced $60.8M annual dividend ($0.76/share) with room for debt reduction. The College Inn acquisition strengthens the Meals segment, and CEO Keller's focus on core brands (Crisco, Ortega, Spice Islands, Dash) could drive margin improvement as the company exits low-margin, high-complexity frozen operations.
The bear case is compelling: D/E of 442% with $2.0B in debt against a $445M market cap creates existential leverage risk if operating performance deteriorates [5]. Revenue has declined from $2.16B to $1.83B over three years as the portfolio shrinks through divestitures. Profit margins are negative (-2.4%) and ROE is deeply negative (-8.9%), indicating the business does not currently earn its cost of capital. The 13.7% dividend yield signals market skepticism about sustainability — the payout ratio on trailing earnings is 333%, though forward coverage on $0.55-$0.65 EPS is approximately 117-138% [1][5]. Consumer shift away from shelf-stable processed foods toward fresh, organic, and premium alternatives represents a secular headwind. Glassdoor reviews revealing toxic culture and low employee morale suggest operational challenges beyond the balance sheet [2].
Key Catalysts
  1. Base business stabilization — Q1 2026 base business net sales growing ~4% could signal the portfolio restructuring is working, supporting revenue guidance of $1.655B-$1.695B [1]
  2. Debt reduction from Green Giant divestiture proceeds and free cash flow generation could improve the 442% D/E ratio, reducing financial risk and potentially unlocking equity value [1]
  3. Margin improvement from exiting low-margin Green Giant frozen operations and focusing on higher-margin Spices & Flavor Solutions and Specialty segments [1]
Key Risks
  1. Existential leverage risk — $2.0B debt against $445M market cap (4.5x debt-to-equity) with negative profitability creates refinancing risk if interest rates remain elevated or operating performance deteriorates [5]
  2. Secular decline in shelf-stable food categories — consumer shift toward fresh, organic, and premium alternatives pressures volume and pricing power across B&G's entire portfolio [1]
  3. Dividend sustainability — the 13.7% yield at 333% payout ratio on trailing earnings requires significant earnings improvement to avoid a second dividend cut, which would likely trigger further equity price decline [1][5]
