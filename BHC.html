Investment Thesis
Bausch Health is a deep-value deleveraging story with operational momentum. The company posted 2025 revenue of $10.27B (+7%) and adjusted EBITDA of $3.54B (+7%), exceeding guidance, with 11 consecutive quarters of revenue and EBITDA growth excluding Bausch + Lomb [1]. Salix grew 11% to $2.58B led by Xifaxan, and the $9.6B refinancing program reduced near-term debt obligations to below $700M through 2027 [1]. With BHC trading at a forward P/E of 1.4 and an 88% stake in publicly-listed Bausch + Lomb, sum-of-the-parts math suggests meaningful upside if management can execute B+L monetization at a fair price.
The bear case is the $20.2B total debt obligation against impaired equity and a Xifaxan patent cliff that will compress Salix earnings starting later this decade. ROE of 436% and D/E of 5591% are mathematical artifacts of the impaired capital structure rather than signs of strength — a single operating stumble or rate spike would re-price the credit. The B+L monetization is the make-or-break catalyst: a low-priced sale or a delayed transaction would leave BHC stranded with a melting Salix franchise and unsustainable leverage. AI-driven generic discovery [3] is a long-term tail risk to specialty pharma economics. This is an option-like equity for risk-tolerant investors only.
Key Catalysts
  1. Successful Bausch + Lomb monetization at a value-accretive price
  2. Continued Salix revenue growth and Xifaxan duration extension
  3. Further deleveraging — refinancing the remaining 2028+ maturities at lower coupons
Key Risks
  1. $20.2B total debt obligation against thin equity creates extreme financial leverage
  2. Xifaxan patent cliff and generic competition will compress Salix earnings later this decade
  3. Bausch + Lomb monetization could occur at a depressed price if eye-health sector valuations weaken
