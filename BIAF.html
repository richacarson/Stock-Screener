Company Profile
Healthcare ·Diagnostics & Research ·NASDAQ ·United States
bioAffinity Technologies develops non-invasive diagnostic tests to detect early-stage lung cancer and other lung diseases using proprietary flow cytometry and artificial intelligence. Its flagship product, CyPath Lung, uses a fluorescent porphyrin preferentially absorbed by cancer cells combined with AI-driven analysis to identify malignancy in patient sputum samples. The company is expanding into companion diagnostics for asthma and COPD.
57 Employees ·bioaffinitytech.com

Investment Thesis
bioAffinity Technologies is an early-stage AI-enabled diagnostics company with a differentiated product in CyPath Lung — a non-invasive test with 92% sensitivity and 87% specificity for detecting early-stage lung cancer in patients with indeterminate pulmonary nodules [3]. The addressable market is large and growing: 2.9M indeterminate nodules detected annually in the U.S., projected to reach 4.7M by 2030 as CT screening expands [1]. CyPath Lung revenue grew 87% in 2025 with test volumes up 99%, physician ordering sites grew 67%, and 2026 guidance calls for unit sales to more than double [1]. The 2,000-patient longitudinal study across 17 medical centers could provide the clinical evidence needed for broader payer coverage and guideline inclusion. At $18M market cap, the stock offers significant upside if commercialization scales and the clinical trial succeeds.
The bear case is survival risk. bioAffinity has only $6.1M in total revenue, a -242% profit margin, and burns approximately $5M annually in cash [1]. The $16.9M capital raise provides runway, but the company will likely need additional financing to fund the 2,000-patient clinical trial and commercial expansion simultaneously. The 120%+ stock surge in March 2026 was driven by the revenue announcement and creates volatility risk for new investors. With only 57 employees and a single commercial product, execution risk is extreme — any setback in the clinical trial, payer coverage decisions, or sales force expansion could be existential. Average daily volume of 7.3M shares appears inflated by the recent stock surge; normalized liquidity may be significantly lower. The company's micro-cap status ($18M) means institutional investors cannot meaningfully participate, limiting price discovery and creating manipulation risk.

Key Risks

  3. Payer coverage uncertainty — without inclusion in clinical practice guidelines or broad insurance coverage, CyPath Lung adoption may plateau at early-adopter physicians [3]

AI Risk Protection (7/9): bioAffinity's diagnostic business is structurally immune to AI disruption in its core value chain. CyPath Lung requires physical patient sputum samples processed through laboratory-grade flow cytometry equipment — AI cannot replace the physical specimen collection, processing, and cell analysis that form the product's foundation [3]. The diagnostic operates in a regulated environment requiring CLIA laboratory certification and clinical validation studies. AI actually enhances the product: the company's proprietary AI algorithms analyze flow cytometry data to identify cancer cell populations with 92% sensitivity [3]. The indeterminate pulmonary nodule market is projected to grow 62% from 2.9M to 4.7M cases by 2030, driven by increased CT lung cancer screening — a tailwind from AI-improved imaging that generates more nodules requiring further workup [1].
