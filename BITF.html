Investment Thesis
Bitfarms/Keel Infrastructure offers a speculative play on the Bitcoin-miner-to-AI-infrastructure transition, with $533M in liquidity (cash and Bitcoin) providing runway through 2028 to fund development at three key sites — Panther Creek (PA), Sharon (PA), and Moses Lake (WA) [1]. FY2025 revenue grew 72% to $229M, and the company's access to low-cost hydroelectric power and existing energy infrastructure provides a foundation for HPC data center development [1][4]. The March 2026 hiring of six experienced data center executives with 20+ years average experience signals serious commitment to the AI pivot [2].
The bear case is significant execution risk. Q1 2026 revenue declined 23% YoY to $37M as Bitcoin mining economics deteriorated, and the company reported a $98M operating loss [1]. Unlike TeraWulf or Hut 8 which have signed major hyperscaler deals, Bitfarms/Keel has not yet executed a single HPC lease — signing three leases is merely the top 2026 priority, not a done deal [1]. Forward EPS of -$0.01 and a Forward P/E of -198 indicate the market expects continued losses. The complete rebrand and redomiciliation add transition risk, and the 2024 hostile takeover attempt by Riot Platforms exposed governance vulnerabilities [4]. The stock at $4.07 trades on hope rather than demonstrated AI infrastructure capability, and the company faces intense competition from better-capitalized and further-along peers.
Key Catalysts
  1. Signing first HPC/AI leases at Panther Creek, Sharon, and Moses Lake sites in 2026 would validate the infrastructure pivot and potentially transform the revenue profile [1]

  3. Strategic hiring of six data center veterans with 20+ years average industry experience signals institutional capability building for HPC/AI operations [2]
Key Risks
  1. Zero HPC/AI leases signed to date — the entire investment thesis depends on converting existing energy assets into contracted data center revenue, which remains unproven [1]

  3. Intense competition from better-positioned peers (TeraWulf with $9.5B FluidStack deal, Hut 8 with Google-backed $7B deal) that have already secured hyperscaler contracts while Bitfarms/Keel has none [1][2]
