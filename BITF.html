Investment Thesis
Bitfarms holds $814M in liquidity with a 2.1 GW North American power portfolio being converted for HPC/AI workloads, positioning it as a potential beneficiary of the $175B+ in hyperscaler AI capex planned for 2026 [1]. Revenue grew 156% YoY in Q3 2025, the HPC pivot targets Nvidia Vera Rubin GPUs, and the Panther Creek project offers 350 MW of growth capacity. At $2.21 per share (market cap ~$1.3B), the stock trades at a fraction of its power portfolio replacement value.
The bear case centers on execution risk in a highly competitive HPC/AI infrastructure market where Bitfarms has zero track record of hosting AI workloads [1]. Bitcoin mining revenue is deteriorating as BTC fell to ~$60K, well below the estimated $87K mining cost, and the company reported a $46M net loss in Q3 [1][3]. The 2026-2027 transition period creates a revenue gap as mining winds down before HPC revenue ramps, and $588M in convertible notes represent significant dilution risk. Noise pollution complaints and energy sustainability questions add operational uncertainty [3].
Key Catalysts
  1. Washington site HPC/AI conversion completion by December 2026 with Nvidia Vera Rubin GPU hosting [1]
  2. 80 MW substation and 350 MW Panther Creek expansion could attract hyperscaler customers in H2 2026 [1]
  3. Potential rebranding to 'Keel Infrastructure' with U.S. redomiciliation could unlock institutional investor interest [2]
Key Risks
  1. Zero track record in HPC/AI hosting creates execution risk against established competitors like Equinix and Digital Realty [1]
  2. Bitcoin price at ~$60K versus ~$87K mining cost means core mining business is cash-flow negative [3]
  3. $588M convertible notes represent major dilution risk if stock price remains depressed and notes convert at unfavorable terms [1]
