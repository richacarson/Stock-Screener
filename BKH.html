Investment Thesis
Black Hills Corporation is a faith-aligned regulated utility gem with the second-longest dividend growth streak in the industry — 56 consecutive years of increases backed by 84 years of uninterrupted payments since 1942. The 4.15% yield with investment-grade credit ratings (BBB+/Baa2 stable) provides reliable income. The company is well-positioned for AI-era demand with a 3GW data center pipeline, and the Ready Wyoming project integrates 350MW of renewable capacity. FY2025 adjusted EPS of $4.10 with 2026 guidance of $4.25-$4.45 (6% growth) provides visible earnings trajectory, and the NorthWestern Energy merger could create meaningful scale advantages. The +62 Inspire score with zero negative attributions makes BKH among the most faith-aligned utilities available.
The bear case revolves around leverage and growth limitations. D/E of 120.4% is well above optimal levels, and the 67.9% payout ratio leaves limited cushion if earnings disappoint. ROE of 8.0% is mediocre, and the stock's $5.1B market cap makes it a small-cap utility with limited analyst coverage and liquidity (942K avg daily volume). EPS growth of 0.9% YoY in FY2025 was essentially flat, and the 4-6% long-term growth target is unexciting. The NorthWestern Energy merger adds integration risk to a company with limited M&A experience. Rising interest rates directly pressure utility valuations and borrowing costs, and the natural gas exposure creates long-term energy transition risk similar to larger peers.
