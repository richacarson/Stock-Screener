Company Profile
Utilities ·Utilities - Regulated Gas ·NYSE ·United States
Black Hills Corporation is a customer-focused utility company serving approximately 1.35 million natural gas and electric utility customers across over 800 communities in eight states: Arkansas, Colorado, Iowa, Kansas, Montana, Nebraska, South Dakota, and Wyoming. A Dividend King with 55 consecutive years of dividend increases, the company operates 1,386 MW of generation capacity and 44,840 miles of gas distribution mains. A pending merger with NorthWestern Energy is expected to close H2 2026.

Investment Thesis
Black Hills Corporation is a Dividend King with 55 consecutive years of dividend increases, offering a 4.05% yield backed by a regulated utility business serving 1.35 million customers across eight states [1][5]. The company delivered 2025 revenue of $2.31B (up 8.6% YoY) and adjusted EPS of $4.10, initiating 2026 guidance of $4.25-$4.45 (6% growth) at the upper end of its 4-6% long-term target [1]. The pending NorthWestern Energy merger (expected H2 2026) would approximately double the company's scale, creating a combined utility with enhanced geographic diversification and operational synergies. Analyst consensus targets $80.50 (15% upside), with Siebert Williams upgrading to Buy with an $82 target in March 2026 [5]. At 15.2x forward P/E, BKH is attractively valued for a Dividend King with visible earnings growth and AI-driven data center demand tailwinds.
The bear case centers on execution risk from the NorthWestern Energy merger and structural limitations of the utility model. Integration risk is meaningful — merging two multi-state utilities could strain management bandwidth and increase leverage on an already elevated D/E of 120.4% [6]. BKH missed Q4 2025 analyst estimates by 7.0% on revenue and 3.1% on EPS, raising questions about near-term execution consistency [1]. ROE of 8.0% is well below the 15% target for a well-run business, reflecting the capital-intensive nature of regulated utilities with ongoing infrastructure investment needs. Revenue has been lumpy — declining from $2.55B in 2022 to $2.13B in 2024 before recovering to $2.31B in 2025 — reflecting weather sensitivity and commodity price exposure in the gas distribution segment [6]. The 67.9% payout ratio, while sustainable, leaves limited room for dividend acceleration beyond the established 4-6% growth trajectory. Profit margin of 12.6% is below the 20% target, constraining free cash flow generation.
Key Catalysts
  1. NorthWestern Energy merger closing expected H2 2026 — would nearly double scale, add geographic diversification, and create operational synergies across overlapping service territories in Montana, South Dakota, and Nebraska [1]
  2. Data center electricity demand growth — AI infrastructure buildout is driving incremental power demand in BKH's service territories, with management noting data center pipeline opportunities in recent earnings calls [1]
  3. 2026 EPS guidance of $4.25-$4.45 representing 6% growth, supported by three rate reviews completed in 2025 adding $52M in new annual revenue and continued infrastructure capital deployment [1]
Key Risks
  1. NorthWestern Energy merger integration risk — regulatory approvals across multiple state jurisdictions, shareholder vote on April 2, 2026, and execution complexity of combining two multi-state utilities could distract management and delay synergy realization [1]
  2. Interest rate sensitivity — 120.4% D/E ratio means rising rates directly increase borrowing costs, compressing margins for a capital-intensive utility with $900M+ annual infrastructure investment needs [6]
  3. Weather and commodity volatility — revenue fluctuated from $2.55B (2022) to $2.13B (2024) to $2.31B (2025), reflecting BKH's exposure to heating degree days and natural gas prices in its gas distribution segment [6]
