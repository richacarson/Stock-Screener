Company Profile
Utilities ·Utilities - Regulated Gas ·NYSE ·United States
Black Hills Corporation is a customer-focused utility company serving approximately 1.35 million natural gas and electric utility customers across over 800 communities in eight states: Arkansas, Colorado, Iowa, Kansas, Montana, Nebraska, South Dakota, and Wyoming. The company has a pending merger with NorthWestern Energy.

Investment Thesis
Black Hills offers a 4.2% dividend yield at an attractive forward P/E of 14.7x, with 54+ consecutive years of dividend payments and 11 consecutive years of growth [5]. FY2025 adjusted EPS of $4.10 grew 5% YoY, with FY2026 guidance of $4.25-$4.45 implying 6% growth [1]. The 3 GW data center pipeline (600 MW in the five-year plan) provides a meaningful growth vector as AI infrastructure buildout reaches Black Hills' service territories [1]. The pending NorthWestern Energy merger creates scale benefits and geographic diversification, while emission reduction targets (40% by 2030, 70% by 2040) demonstrate responsible utility management [4].
The bear case is significant for this small-cap utility. D/E of 120.4% is well above the 70% target, and the pending NorthWestern merger will likely increase leverage further while creating integration risk [1][5]. Revenue has declined on a 5-year CAGR basis (-2%), reflecting flat underlying utility demand. ROE of 8.0% and profit margin of 12.6% are below peer benchmarks, and EPS growth of 4-6% is at the low end of utility peers [1]. The $325K Kansas consumer protection fine for sharing customer data without consent raises governance concerns [4]. Natural gas explosion lawsuits in Aspen (2022 hospitalization of four workers) and the 2019 gas sabotage incident that left 3,500 customers without heat in single-digit temperatures reveal infrastructure vulnerability [4]. The 5-year dividend growth CAGR of 4.6% falls below the 6% target, trailing peers like Atmos Energy.
