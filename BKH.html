Investment Thesis
Black Hills Corporation offers a compelling value proposition with a forward P/E of 14.7x, a 4.15% dividend yield, and 54 consecutive years of dividend growth. The pending NorthWestern Energy merger could create a larger, more efficient utility platform, while the 3 GW+ data center pipeline driven by Microsoft and Meta AI facilities represents a significant incremental growth catalyst. The company's 2026 EPS guidance of $4.25-$4.45 implies 6% growth, and the long-term 4-6% EPS growth target is supported by a $4.7 billion five-year capital plan.
Key concerns include the elevated 120% debt-to-equity ratio, negative free cash flow, and a 68% payout ratio that stretches dividend coverage. The NorthWestern merger introduces meaningful execution and regulatory risk, with Montana environmental groups already raising concerns about rate impacts. The company's history of consumer protection violations ($325K Kansas fine), safety incidents (Aspen gas explosion), and contentious rate disputes in Pueblo highlight operational risk. Black Hills also retains coal mining exposure through its Gillette, Wyoming operations, creating long-term stranded asset risk.
