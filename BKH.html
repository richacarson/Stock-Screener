Company Profile
Utilities ·Regulated Electric & Gas Utility ·NYSE ·United States
Black Hills Corporation is a regulated electric and natural gas utility serving approximately 1.3 million customers across eight states in the western United States, including South Dakota, Wyoming, Colorado, Iowa, Kansas, Montana, Nebraska, and Arkansas. Also operates a small non-regulated power generation business. Founded in 1941 and headquartered in Rapid City, South Dakota.

Investment Thesis
Black Hills Corporation offers a compelling income investment as a Dividend King with 56 consecutive years of dividend growth and 83 consecutive years of payments, yielding 3.73% at a reasonable 16.89x forward earnings [1][5]. The company's positioning as a power provider for AI data center infrastructure — with a pipeline exceeding 3 GW and 600 MW in the five-year capital plan — provides a rare growth catalyst for a regulated utility [3]. The innovative Wyoming tariff structure for Meta's data center demonstrates management's ability to capture AI-driven demand with minimal capital risk. FY2025 revenue grew 8.08% YoY to $2.31B, and 2026 EPS guidance of $4.25-$4.45 represents approximately 6% earnings growth [1][2]. AI resilience is exceptional: regulated utilities are structurally immune to AI disruption while directly benefiting from AI-driven electricity demand. The stock reached an all-time high of $75.76 in March 2026 on AI infrastructure optimism.
The bear case centers on below-average returns and utility-sector structural constraints. ROE of 8.00% significantly trails the 15% target, reflecting the regulated return environment where state commissions cap profitability [5]. The PEG ratio of 2.80 suggests the stock is fully valued relative to its modest growth rate. Negative free cash flow of -$312.5M highlights the capital-intensive nature of the business and dependence on external financing for growth investments. The 68.4% payout ratio, while sustainable, leaves limited room for dividend acceleration — the 5-year dividend growth CAGR of approximately 4% trails the 6% target. Innovation and inspiration scores are weak at 3/10 each, reflecting the inherent limitations of a regulated utility in generating excitement or breakthrough products. Q1 2026 revenue of $780.7M declined from $805.2M in the prior year period, suggesting quarterly volatility [2]. The data center opportunity, while promising, represents load growth within the existing regulated model rather than a transformative new business.
