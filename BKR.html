Company Profile
Energy ·Oil & Gas Equipment & Services ·NASDAQ ·United States
Baker Hughes Company provides a portfolio of technologies and services to energy and industrial value chains. It operates through Oilfield Services & Equipment (OFSE) and Industrial & Energy Technology (IET) segments. The company designs and manufactures products for exploration, drilling, production, and processing, alongside gas technology, climate technology, and industrial asset management solutions. Baker Hughes has positioned its IET segment as a growth engine serving LNG, gas technology, and emerging energy transition markets.

Investment Thesis
Baker Hughes is a compelling energy technology transformation story, with the IET segment growing 14% YoY and orders surging 54% to $4.89B in Q1 2026, driven by LNG demand, gas technology, and sub-utility power generation [1]. Net income of $930M (up 131% YoY) and adjusted EBITDA of $1.16B (up 12%) demonstrate improving profitability as the company's margin expansion initiatives gain traction. D/E of 82.9% is well-managed, ROE of 17.2% exceeds targets, and CEO Simonelli's 9-year tenure provides strategic continuity. The structural shift toward LNG and gas technology positions Baker Hughes to benefit from the global energy transition while maintaining the cash flow stability of the traditional OFSE business.
The primary risk is the cyclical nature of the energy services industry. Revenue growth of 2.5% YoY at the consolidated level masks weakness in the OFSE segment (down 7%), which remains exposed to oil price volatility and upstream spending cycles [1]. Forward P/E of 21.0 and PEG of 2.34 price in continued IET growth and margin expansion that depend on sustained LNG order flow. Profit margin of 11.2% is below the 20% target and reflects the operational intensity of energy services. The Middle East geopolitical disruptions that boosted Q1 2026 results could reverse, and any moderation in global LNG investment cycles would pressure the IET growth narrative. Additionally, the company's exposure to fossil fuel infrastructure creates long-term ESG reputational risk that could limit multiple expansion [3].
Key Catalysts
  1. LNG equipment order momentum continuing with $4.89B Q1 2026 orders (up 54% YoY), driven by global natural gas demand and energy security investments that provide multi-year revenue visibility
  2. IET segment margin expansion as the higher-margin technology business grows to represent more than half of total revenue, potentially driving consolidated EBITDA margins toward 20%+
  3. Climate technology and industrial AI solutions creating new revenue streams beyond traditional energy, with potential to revalue Baker Hughes as an energy technology company rather than an oilfield services firm
Key Risks
  1. OFSE segment decline (down 7% YoY in Q1 2026) exposes the company to upstream oil & gas spending cycles that could weaken further if commodity prices decline or OPEC+ production cuts are reversed
  2. Forward PEG of 2.34 prices in sustained double-digit IET growth and margin expansion — any deceleration in LNG orders or gas technology demand could trigger valuation compression
  3. Fossil fuel infrastructure exposure creates long-term ESG and regulatory risk, with increasing pressure from investors and policymakers to accelerate the energy transition faster than the company's current strategy supports
