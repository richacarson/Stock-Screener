Company Profile
Industrials ·Specialty Business Services ·NYSE ·United States
BlackSky Technology operates as a space-based intelligence company providing real-time geospatial data, AI-powered analytics, and monitoring services. The company operates a constellation of small satellites delivering high-frequency imagery and uses proprietary AI/ML algorithms to transform satellite imagery into actionable intelligence for defense, government, and commercial customers.

Investment Thesis
BlackSky Technology is a differentiated space-based intelligence company transitioning from growth-stage to potential profitability. FY2025 revenue reached $106.6M with Q4 at $35.2M (up 16% YoY), and the company guided FY2026 revenue of $120-145M representing ~24% growth at midpoint [1]. The Gen-3 constellation delivers very high-resolution AI-enabled analytics, and the $345M contracted backlog provides revenue visibility [1]. International revenues grew 50%+ YoY and now exceed half of total revenue, diversifying away from U.S. government dependence. Adjusted EBITDA inflected positive at $0.9M for FY2025 with Q4 at $8.8M, and FY2026 guidance of $6-18M adjusted EBITDA suggests the path to sustainable profitability is emerging [1]. The stock has surged from $7.86 to $33.83 (330%+ from 52-week lows) as the market prices in the growth trajectory.
The bear case centers on persistent unprofitability and execution risk. BlackSky remains deeply unprofitable with -65.9% profit margins, trailing EPS of -$2.09, and D/E of 221% [5]. The Gen-3 constellation buildout requires $50-60M capex in 2026, and any satellite deployment failure would be severely punished given the small constellation size [1]. Revenue growth decelerated to just 4.4% on an annual basis (FY2025 vs FY2024), and back-half-loaded guidance (55-60% of revenue in H2) creates execution pressure. The stock trades at roughly 12x forward revenue (negative earnings) after its 330% rally, leaving little margin for error. Heavy dependence on U.S. defense and intelligence contracts (~50% of revenue) creates concentration risk, and rising regulatory scrutiny on surveillance technology could constrain international expansion [4]. Competition from Planet Labs, Maxar, and Capella Space is intensifying as the commercial Earth observation market matures.
Key Catalysts
  1. Gen-3 constellation buildout delivering higher-resolution AI-enabled imagery, driving new contract wins and revenue acceleration toward the $120-145M FY2026 guidance [1][2]
  2. Adjusted EBITDA expansion from $0.9M (FY2025) toward the $6-18M FY2026 guidance range, demonstrating the path to sustainable profitability [1]
  3. International revenue growth exceeding 50% YoY with new strategic defense customers diversifying the revenue base beyond U.S. government contracts [1]
Key Risks
  1. Persistent unprofitability with -65.9% profit margins and -$2.09 trailing EPS — any delay in the profitability inflection could pressure the stock after its 330% rally from 52-week lows [5]
  2. Satellite deployment risk — Gen-3 constellation buildout requires $50-60M capex, and a single launch failure could set back the technology roadmap and contract fulfillment [1]
  3. Concentrated customer base with heavy U.S. defense/intelligence dependence and rising regulatory scrutiny on surveillance and data privacy that could constrain international expansion [4]
