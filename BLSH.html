Company Profile
Technology ·Software - Infrastructure ·NYQ ·Cayman Islands
Bullish is a global digital asset platform providing market infrastructure and information services. It operates Bullish Exchange, a digital assets spot and derivatives exchange integrating a central limit order book with automated market making. The company also owns CoinDesk, providing indices, data, analytics, and media coverage of the digital asset ecosystem. Founded in 2020 and headquartered in George Town, Cayman Islands.

Investment Thesis
Bullish is a high-growth institutional crypto exchange riding the derivatives wave — revenue surged 97.9% YoY with Q4 2025 adjusted revenue of $92.5M up 67.6%, driven by the options platform launch that captured 29% of the global Bitcoin options market and $9B+ cumulative volume within months [1]. The 2026 SS&O revenue guidance of $220-250M implies ~50% growth, and forward EPS of $1.02 suggests a profitability inflection after years of losses [1][4]. The CoinDesk media and data business adds diversification and recurring revenue beyond exchange transaction fees [3]. D/E of just 17.6% and $1.1B in IPO proceeds provide a strong balance sheet for investment in product development and regulatory expansion across the US (20 states plus New York BitLicense), Germany, Hong Kong, and Gibraltar [3][4].
The bear case is substantial. Bullish reported a $563.6M Q4 loss and trailing EPS of -$5.99, with GAAP profitability still distant despite forward consensus of $1.02 EPS [1][4]. The crypto exchange industry is hyper-competitive — Coinbase, Binance, Kraken, and Deribit all compete for institutional volume, and crypto market cyclicality means revenue can collapse in bear markets as trading volumes evaporate. Bullish's 97.9% revenue growth is partly a function of launching from a small base during a crypto bull cycle; sustainability of this growth through market downturns is unproven. The Cayman Islands domicile raises governance and regulatory perception concerns. Only 36% of employees have a positive business outlook per Glassdoor, and the 3.1/5 rating suggests cultural challenges that could impair talent retention in a competitive labor market [2]. At $6.5B market cap and ~43x forward earnings, the stock is priced for significant execution on derivatives growth and profitability that remains speculative.
Key Catalysts
  1. Options platform scaling — 29% global Bitcoin options market share captured within months of launch, with 2026 SS&O guidance of $220-250M implying continued explosive derivatives growth [1]
  2. Profitability inflection — forward EPS of $1.02 suggests analysts expect BLSH to turn profitable in 2026, which would be a transformative milestone after years of GAAP losses [4]
  3. US market expansion — Bullish secured the New York BitLicense and launched in 20 US states, providing access to the world's largest institutional investor base as crypto regulation clarifies [3]
Key Risks
  1. Crypto market cyclicality — revenue and trading volumes are highly correlated with cryptocurrency prices, and a bear market could collapse the 97.9% revenue growth trajectory and delay profitability [1][4]
  2. Hyper-competitive exchange landscape — Coinbase, Binance, Kraken, and Deribit compete aggressively for institutional volume, and fee compression or market share loss could undermine revenue guidance [1]
  3. Governance and regulatory risk — Cayman Islands domicile, limited ESG disclosure, and evolving global crypto regulation create uncertainty around operating license continuity and institutional investor appetite for the stock [3]
