Company Profile
Healthcare ·Biotechnology ·NASDAQ ·United States
BioMarin develops and commercializes therapies for rare, serious genetic diseases. Its portfolio includes VOXZOGO (achondroplasia), a suite of enzyme replacement therapies (Naglazyme, Vimizim, Brineura, Aldurazyme), and Palynziq for phenylketonuria. BioMarin withdrew Roctavian (hemophilia A gene therapy) from the market in early 2026 and announced an agreement to acquire Amicus Therapeutics.

Investment Thesis
BioMarin is a rare-disease biologics leader delivering accelerating growth under new CEO Alexander Hardy — 2025 revenue rose 13% to a record $3.22B, with VOXZOGO (achondroplasia) up 26% to $927M and Q4 VOXZOGO growth of 31% [1]. Non-GAAP EPS of $3.15 in 2025, 2026 guidance of $4.95-5.15 (ex-Amicus), and an organic non-GAAP operating margin of ~40% make BMRN one of the more profitable specialty biotechs [1][4]. Six of eight marketed therapies are first-in-disease, creating durable franchises with rare-disease exclusivity and deeply embedded payer/physician relationships [2]. Balance sheet is conservative (D/E 0.10) with $605M debt — unusual discipline for a biotech at this scale [4]. The pending Amicus acquisition (Q2 2026 close) adds a second rare-disease franchise and extends growth visibility into the next decade [1].
The bear case starts with Roctavian. After BioMarin's hemophilia A gene therapy failed to find a buyer post-divestiture exploration, the company voluntarily withdrew it from the market in Q1 2026, a visible capital-allocation miss [1]. Q1 2026 is guided as the lowest revenue quarter of the year after lapping a $30M one-time government VOXZOGO order from Q1 2025, creating near-term optical softness [1]. Hardy-led R&D slim-down and leadership shake-ups during the first 9 months could strain culture, and CSR/ESG disclosure remains Sustainalytics 'average' rather than industry-leading [3]. Amicus integration risk is meaningful — $0.25 per share of pre-close costs are baked into 2026 EPS [1]. At 30x trailing P/E on $10.65B market cap, multiple expansion depends on VOXZOGO label extension hitting the $5B opportunity Hardy outlined and on Amicus closing cleanly [4]. AI Resilience (25%)
AI Resilience LOW RISK
7
AI Risk Protection (6/9): Rare-disease biologics are among the most AI-resistant healthcare franchises — small patient populations, complex biologics manufacturing, and regulatory exclusivity insulate revenue from generative-AI disruption. VOXZOGO, Naglazyme, Vimizim, and Brineura have no direct substitutes, and payer/physician relationships are reputation-based, not price-competitive. AI Opportunity (1/1): AI can accelerate natural-history studies, patient finding, and trial enrichment for ultra-rare diseases, which should benefit the Hardy-era focused R&D strategy; competitive AI-first drug discovery firms are a longer-term threat to novel-target programs but not to BioMarin's installed franchise.
