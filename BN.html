Company Profile
Financial Services ·Asset Management ·NYSE ·Canada
Brookfield Corporation is a global alternative asset manager with over $1 trillion in assets under management across real estate, infrastructure, renewable power, private equity, credit (via Oaktree Capital), and wealth solutions. The company invests its own capital alongside institutional and retail investors, focusing on real assets with long-duration cash flows. Led by CEO Bruce Flatt since 2002, Brookfield operates in 30+ countries.

Investment Thesis
Brookfield Corporation delivered record distributable earnings of $6.0B in 2025 (up 11% per share), with fee-bearing capital growing 12% to $603B and fee-related earnings surging 22% to $3.0B [1]. The company raised $112B in capital, deployed $126B, and completed $91B in asset sales — demonstrating unmatched scale in alternative asset management. At a forward P/E of 6.6× against 20%+ targeted earnings growth, BN appears significantly undervalued relative to its earnings power [1][4]. The $10-20B AI infrastructure fund (NVIDIA as anchor), Wealth Solutions targeting $200B insurance assets by year-end 2026, and $188B of deployable capital provide multiple visible growth vectors. The 17% dividend increase signals management confidence in sustainable earnings growth [1].
The bear case centers on financial complexity, leverage, and ESG credibility. Brookfield's $272.6B in total debt against a $87.6B market cap creates significant leverage risk if asset values decline or credit markets tighten [4]. GAAP financials are nearly impenetrable — trailing P/E of 79× vs forward P/E of 6.6× reflects the disconnect between GAAP net income ($1.3B) and distributable earnings ($6.0B), which complicates valuation for most investors [1][4]. Greenwashing allegations are material — Brookfield's 159M metric tons of CO2 equivalent annual emissions (including Oaktree) are nearly 14× higher than disclosed in sustainability reports, undermining the net-zero-by-2050 commitment and creating regulatory and reputational risk [3]. Revenue growth of 3.5% YoY on the consolidated entity is modest, and profit margin of 1.68% on GAAP basis makes the stock difficult for quantitative screens to identify. The stock trades 22% below its 52-week high ($49.57), reflecting market caution on financial complexity [4].
Key Catalysts

  2. Greenwashing liability — 159M metric tons CO2e annually (14× disclosed levels including Oaktree) creates regulatory, litigation, and reputational risk as climate disclosure requirements tighten globally [3]
  3. Leverage concentration — $272.6B total debt requires continued access to capital markets and stable asset valuations; a credit cycle downturn could compress asset values and restrict refinancing [4]
