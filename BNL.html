Investment Thesis
Broadstone Net Lease offers a defensible 5.8% dividend yield backed by 99.8% occupancy, $454M in trailing revenue, and AFFO growth guidance of approximately 4% for 2026 (AFFO of $1.53-$1.57 per share) [1]. The industrial-focused portfolio strategy differentiates BNL from diversified net lease peers, and the expanded build-to-suit pipeline provides development upside beyond pure acquisitions. Same-store rental revenue growth of 2.9% in Q4 2025 demonstrates embedded rent escalators, while $500-625M in planned 2026 investments should drive external growth [1]. At $20.14 per share, the stock trades at 13.6x 2026 AFFO guidance midpoint, a reasonable valuation for a mid-cap net lease REIT with improving fundamentals.
The bear case centers on BNL's limited scale, modest growth trajectory, and REIT structural constraints. Revenue growth of 5.5% YoY and projected AFFO growth of ~4% trail higher-growth REIT peers, limiting total return potential beyond the dividend [1]. The 83% D/E ratio constrains balance sheet capacity for acquisitions, and rising interest rates would increase refinancing costs on the $2.3B debt stack. GAAP ROE of 3.2% reflects the capital-intensive nature of the business. Tenant concentration risk exists despite 200+ tenants — the top 10 tenants represent a meaningful portion of revenue. The dividend has been paid for only 6 years since the 2020 IPO, and the 3.1% dividend growth rate barely keeps pace with inflation. Competition for net lease properties from larger, better-capitalized REITs (Realty Income, STORE) compresses cap rates and limits acquisition quality [5].
Key Catalysts
  1. Expanded build-to-suit pipeline creating development-driven growth beyond traditional acquisitions, with $500-625M in 2026 investment guidance [1]
  2. Industrial portfolio repositioning increasing exposure to logistics and manufacturing properties benefiting from reshoring and e-commerce trends [1]
  3. Same-store rental revenue growth of 2-3% annually from embedded contractual rent escalators supporting organic AFFO growth [1]
Key Risks
  1. Interest rate sensitivity — rising rates increase BNL's refinancing costs on $2.3B debt and compress property valuations, particularly at 83% D/E leverage [5]
  2. Scale disadvantage versus larger net lease REITs (Realty Income at $70B+ market cap) limits BNL's cost of capital and competitive positioning for high-quality acquisitions [1]
  3. Tenant credit risk in a potential recession — net lease REITs depend on tenant solvency to maintain the 99.8% occupancy rate, and industrial/restaurant tenants face cyclical pressure [1]
