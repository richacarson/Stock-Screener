Company Profile
Energy ·Oil & Gas Drilling ·NYSE ·Bermuda
Borr Drilling Limited is an international offshore drilling contractor that owns and operates a fleet of 29 modern jack-up rigs, one of the youngest fleets in the industry. Founded in 2016 and headquartered in Hamilton, Bermuda, the company provides drilling services to oil and gas exploration and production companies worldwide in water depths up to approximately 400 feet, with operations concentrated in the Middle East, Southeast Asia, West Africa, and Mexico.

Investment Thesis
Borr Drilling operates one of the youngest and highest-specification jack-up fleets in the industry (29 rigs, 97.9% technical utilization) in a structurally undersupplied market — limited new rig orders since 2015 mean existing modern fleets command premium dayrates [1]. FY2025 adjusted EBITDA of $470M (at the top of guidance) on $1.02B revenue demonstrates strong operational leverage, and the $174M Mexico JV expansion adds five premium rigs to capitalize on growing Latin American offshore demand [1]. Forward P/E of 14.8x and a 3.9% dividend yield at $6.05 provide attractive valuation for a company with $613M liquidity and a constructive demand outlook, with Saudi Arabia and Mexico showing signs of jack-up demand inflection into 2027 [1]. The structural supply shortage supports dayrate stability even in softer demand periods.
The bear case centers on cyclicality, leverage, and energy transition risk. Revenue declined -1.4% in the most recent quarter with Q4 EBITDA falling 22.6% sequentially, and profit margin of 4.4% is razor-thin for a company with $2.15B in total debt [1][4]. D/E of 175.9% means any sustained decline in dayrates or utilization could trigger cash flow problems — the company is not yet generating sufficient free cash flow to rapidly deleverage [4]. The offshore drilling industry is inherently cyclical and exposed to oil price volatility; a recession-driven demand decline could compress dayrates 20-30% from current levels. The energy transition poses a secular headwind: ESG-conscious investors may limit valuation multiples, and tightening environmental regulations could restrict offshore drilling activity over time [3]. The Mexico JV adds geopolitical risk in a country with uncertain regulatory frameworks for offshore drilling. Glassdoor's 3.1/5 rating with 41% recommendation rate suggests talent retention challenges that could affect operational reliability [2].
