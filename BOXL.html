Company Profile
Technology ·Consumer Electronics ·NASDAQ ·United States
Boxlight Corporation develops, sells, and services interactive classroom technology products including interactive displays, digital signage, campus audio systems, and collaboration software under the Clevertouch, FrontRow, and Mimio brands. The company serves K-12 education, corporate, and government markets globally, headquartered in Lawrenceville, Georgia. The company completed a 1-for-6 reverse stock split in June 2026 and faces going-concern warnings from auditors.

Investment Thesis
Boxlight Corporation operates in the K-12 interactive classroom technology market with recognized brands (Clevertouch, FrontRow, Mimio) serving a genuine need in education technology [1]. Q1 2026 revenue of $22.4M was flat YoY, suggesting stabilization from the -19.6% FY2025 decline. FrontRow Symphony represents a potentially differentiated campus communication platform. Gross margins of ~31% are reasonable for hardware technology, and the company has historically generated positive operating cash flow. At $2.4M market cap, the stock trades at just 0.02x revenue, implying near-total value destruction — any turnaround would produce outsized returns [5].
The bear case is severe and existential. Management has disclosed substantial doubt about the company's ability to continue as a going concern, citing $34.1M in Whitehawk credit facility debt maturing April 2027 with no clear refinancing path [1][4]. Market cap of $2.4M on 667K post-reverse-split shares reflects the market pricing meaningful probability of equity wipe-out. Revenue declined 19.6% in FY2025 to $109M, profit margin is -24.8%, ROE is -507%, and stockholders' equity is negative [5]. The stock has fallen 94% over the past year, Nasdaq has flagged equity shortfall below $2.5M listing minimum, and insider selling (COO and CTO) signals lack of management conviction [4]. The interactive display market faces both cyclical weakness (flat global demand) and structural threat from AI-native educational platforms. With 159 employees and no R&D budget, Boxlight lacks the resources to compete against well-capitalized rivals.
Key Catalysts
  1. Successful refinancing of the $34.1M Whitehawk credit facility before April 2027 maturity would remove the going-concern overhang and could re-rate the equity significantly
  2. FrontRow Symphony platform adoption in K-12 districts could drive recurring revenue growth and differentiate from commodity display vendors
  3. Potential acquisition target at $2.4M market cap — larger edtech players could acquire Boxlight's brand portfolio and distribution at a premium to current equity value
Key Risks
  1. Going-concern risk — management has disclosed substantial doubt about ability to continue operations; $34.1M Whitehawk debt matures April 2027 with negative stockholders' equity and no clear refinancing path
  2. Nasdaq delisting risk — stockholders' equity below $2.5M minimum listing requirement; 1-for-6 reverse stock split already executed June 2026 to maintain price compliance
  3. Structural obsolescence — AI-powered educational platforms and flat global interactive display demand threaten Boxlight's hardware-centric model, and the company lacks R&D resources (159 employees) to pivot
