Company Profile
Financial Services ·Real Estate - Closed-End Fund ·NYSE ·United States
Bluerock Private Real Estate Fund (BPRE) is a closed-end fund that provides access to private institutional real estate. The fund holds approximately $4.2 billion in assets spanning ~5,500 properties, primarily allocated to private real estate securities (87%) and real estate debt/preferred equity (13%). Listed on the NYSE in December 2025, BPRE was previously known as Bluerock Total Income+ Real Estate Fund and operated as an interval fund with limited liquidity.

Investment Thesis
BPRE trades at a ~35% discount to its stated NAV of $25.52, offering potential upside if the market's valuation skepticism proves unwarranted. Bluerock cites $3.4B in asset sales at prices within ~2% of NAV over the past three years, supporting the accuracy of the fund's carrying values [2]. The annualized distribution yield of ~8.3% ($0.1208/month for May-June 2026) has been increased three consecutive times since the January 2026 transition to monthly payments, suggesting growing management confidence in cash flow [3]. The fund's $4.2B portfolio spans ~5,500 properties with 87% in private real estate and 13% in real estate debt/preferred equity, providing diversified exposure to physical assets that are structurally protected from AI disruption [2].
The bear case is substantial. BPRE opened at a ~38% discount to NAV on its December 2025 listing date, and the market continues to trade the fund at a steep discount — suggesting institutional investors believe the stated NAV is overstated [1]. For over two years prior to listing, Bluerock restricted redemptions, trapping investors who wanted to exit. The White Law Group has opened an investor losses investigation, citing losses investors 'did not anticipate' from the structural transition [5]. The closed-end fund structure eliminates redemption rights entirely — there is no mechanism to realize NAV, only the market price. Private real estate valuations are inherently subjective, and the NAV may not reflect current market conditions. With only 5 months of distribution history as a listed entity, there is no track record to evaluate sustainability. The externally managed structure creates fee drag and potential conflicts of interest between Bluerock and shareholders. No financial metrics (P/E, ROE, D/E) are available for standard fundamental analysis [4].
Key Catalysts
  1. NAV discount narrowing — if quarterly NAV publications and continued asset sales validate the $25.52 stated NAV, the 35% discount could compress, generating significant price appreciation [2]
  2. Continued distribution increases — three consecutive raises since January 2026 establish a positive trajectory that could attract income investors and support price discovery [3]
  3. Private real estate market stabilization — improved transaction volume and price discovery in commercial real estate could validate fund valuations and reduce investor skepticism [2]
Key Risks
  1. NAV impairment — private real estate valuations may be overstated; the 35% market discount suggests institutional investors believe properties are worth less than stated [1]
  2. Governance and litigation risk — White Law Group investor losses investigation and blocked redemptions for 2+ years prior to listing raise serious governance concerns [5]
  3. Distribution sustainability — only 5 months of distribution history as a listed entity, with no long-term track record to evaluate whether the 8.3% yield is sustainable from fund cash flows [3]
