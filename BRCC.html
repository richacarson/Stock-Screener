BRC Inc. (Black Rifle Coffee Company) is a veteran-founded coffee and lifestyle brand that purchases, roasts, and sells coffee and accessories through wholesale, direct-to-consumer, and outpost retail channels. Founded in 2014, the company also offers apparel, brewing equipment, and recently launched Black Rifle Energy beverages through a distribution partnership with Keurig Dr Pepper.

Investment Thesis
Black Rifle Coffee offers a compelling brand story as a veteran-founded coffee company with authentic cultural resonance and a growing distribution footprint. FY2025 revenue of $398.3M (above guidance) with 2026 guidance of at least 7% revenue growth and 30% adjusted EBITDA growth suggests improving momentum [1]. The Black Rifle Energy launch through Keurig Dr Pepper's national distribution network provides a new revenue stream, and the 'land and expand' retail coffee strategy is gaining measurable shelf space [1]. New CEO Chris Mondzelewski brings 28 years of Mars Inc. consumer marketing experience to professionalize operations [2]. At $127M market cap (0.32x revenue), the stock is deeply discounted if BRCC can achieve profitability.
The bear case is severe: BRCC has lost over 90% of its value since the 2022 SPAC listing at ~$20+, and the stock trades at $1.09 — near its all-time low of $0.60 [4]. The company has never been profitable, with -3.0% profit margin, -54.9% ROE, and -$9.8M operating cash flow in FY2025. Forward P/E of 36.3x is expensive for a company with forward EPS of just $0.03 [4]. Daily dollar volume of only $868K makes the stock illiquid and unsuitable for institutional investors. Cash of $4.3M against $62M debt creates going-concern risk if the energy drink launch underperforms. The politically polarized brand limits total addressable market and creates reputational risk — the Kyle Rittenhouse association and 'gun-themed' branding alienate a significant portion of potential consumers [3]. Forecasts suggest profitability will remain elusive for at least three more years. The SPAC-era dilution and ongoing losses make equity recovery extremely difficult for common shareholders.
