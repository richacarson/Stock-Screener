Company Profile
Consumer Cyclical ·Gambling ·NYSE ·United Kingdom
Brightstar Lottery PLC (formerly International Game Technology PLC) is a pure-play global lottery technology and services company following the July 2025 sale of its Gaming & Digital business to Apollo Global Management for $4.1B. The company designs, operates, and services lottery systems, produces instant ticket games, provides iLottery platforms, and offers player engagement and marketing services. Brightstar operates across the U.S., Italy (Lotto Italia concession), and internationally, generating $2.51B in 2025 revenue.

Investment Thesis
Brightstar Lottery is a newly created pure-play global lottery technology leader trading at a cheap 12x forward P/E with a 7.1% dividend yield — a compelling income proposition if the business stabilizes post-restructuring [4]. The company secured the Lotto Italia concession through 2034 ($926M upfront investment) and holds dominant market positions in U.S. state lotteries, providing highly visible, recurring revenue streams [1]. FY2026 guidance of $2.5-$2.55B revenue (5%+ organic growth) and $1.16-$1.19B adjusted EBITDA signals improving profitability as the Italy concession costs amortize. The São Paulo lottery launch and Italy B2C digital expansion provide incremental growth catalysts through 2028. The $1.1B capital return ($3.02/share special dividend) following the gaming sale demonstrates shareholder-friendly capital allocation. Lottery operations benefit from government-granted monopolies with extremely high switching costs and regulatory barriers to entry.
The bear case focuses on leverage, governance risk, and stagnant growth. D/E of 270% is extremely elevated, with $4.3B in debt against a $2.4B market cap — the company is essentially a leveraged play on lottery concessions [4]. The Lotto Italia upfront payment crushed FY2025 profitability (income from continuing operations down 50% to $135M), and margin recovery depends on successful B2C digital ramp. Revenue has been flat to declining for three years ($2.60B to $2.51B), and even the optimistic 2026 guidance implies only mid-single-digit growth. IGT faced controversies including $180K in Texas Lottery contract fines for political contributions and allegations related to lottery terminal deliveries enabling jackpot irregularities [3]. The gambling sector carries inherent regulatory risk — any tightening of lottery regulations could impair concession values. Trailing EPS of -$0.01 means the company is currently unprofitable on a GAAP basis, and the 85% normalized payout ratio provides minimal margin of safety for the dividend. The newly created Brightstar entity has no operating history as a standalone company.
Key Catalysts
  1. Lotto Italia B2C digital expansion — transitioning Italian players to online lottery could drive margin expansion and incremental revenue through 2028 as digital adoption accelerates [1]
  2. São Paulo lottery launch — new market entry provides organic revenue growth opportunity in one of the world's largest lottery markets [1]
  3. Profitability recovery — FY2025 margins were depressed by $926M Lotto Italia concession payment; normalized earnings of $1.08 FY2026 EPS would support the 12x forward multiple and 7%+ dividend yield [4]
Key Risks
  1. Extreme leverage — D/E of 270% with $4.3B debt creates refinancing risk and limits financial flexibility; a credit downgrade could spike borrowing costs on this capital-intensive concession model [4]
  2. Flat organic revenue trajectory — three consecutive years of revenue decline ($2.60B to $2.51B) with only mid-single-digit growth guided for 2026 suggests the core lottery market is mature with limited growth levers [1]
  3. Governance and regulatory risk — IGT/Brightstar's Texas Lottery contract fines and terminal delivery controversies highlight operational risk; lottery concession values are highly sensitive to regulatory changes [3]
