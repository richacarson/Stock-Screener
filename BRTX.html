Company Profile
Healthcare ·Biotechnology ·NASDAQ ·United States
BioRestorative Therapies, Inc. is a clinical-stage biotechnology company developing regenerative medicine products using adult (non-embryonic) stem cell protocols. Its lead candidate BRTX-100 targets chronic lumbar disc disease using autologous cultured mesenchymal stem cells, with a fully enrolled Phase 2 trial of 99 patients. The company also operates a commercial biocosmeceutical platform and develops ThermoStem for obesity and metabolic disorders. Headquartered in Melville, New York, with a market cap of approximately $10 million.
13 Employees ·www.biorestorative.com

Investment Thesis
BioRestorative's BRTX-100 Phase 2 trial is fully enrolled with 99 patients across 15 U.S. sites and has achieved FDA alignment on the BLA pathway, with interim data showing over 74% of subjects achieving greater than 50% improvement in function and no serious adverse events [1]. At a $10M market cap, the stock prices in near-total failure — any positive Phase 2 readout or partnership announcement could generate outsized returns on a percentage basis. The zero-debt balance sheet and biocosmeceutical revenue diversification provide modest financial cushion, and the Inspire Impact Score of 35 (ALIGNED) reflects the socially beneficial nature of regenerative medicine for chronic pain.
The bear case is overwhelming — BioRestorative has 13 employees, $360K annual revenue (down 7.3% YoY), ROE of -241%, and $8M annual cash burn against $3.6M cash, implying approximately 5-6 months of runway without dilutive capital raises [1][4]. The stock trades at $0.41, down 80% from its 52-week high, with real risk of NASDAQ delisting if the price remains below $1.00. Clinical development has been glacially slow — the company was founded over a decade ago with no commercial therapeutic products. Any Phase 2 failure would likely be terminal for equity investors given the company's limited pipeline and resources. Revenue concentration in a single contract manufacturing agreement creates fragility, as demonstrated by the 95% Q3 2025 revenue decline when that contract's deliverables were completed [1].

  3. Biocosmeceutical product line expansion and ThermoStem program advancement could diversify the pipeline and provide additional near-term revenue [2]
