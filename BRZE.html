Braze, Inc. operates a customer engagement platform that provides interactions between consumers and brands worldwide. The platform offers software development kits, REST APIs, partner data integrations, and AI-powered tools for mobile and web notifications, in-app messaging, content cards, and cross-channel campaign orchestration, serving over 2,600 customers reaching 8 billion monthly active users.

Investment Thesis
Braze is the leading next-generation customer engagement platform, displacing legacy marketing clouds with a modern, AI-powered approach that reached 8.0B monthly active users and $738M in FY2026 revenue (28% growth), with $1B+ in remaining performance obligations providing strong forward visibility [1]. The company's BrazeAI products drove a 50%+ increase in quarterly bookings, enterprise customer growth of 29% to 333 customers at $500K+ ARR signals deepening platform adoption, and FY2027 guidance of $884-889M (20-21% growth) with $800M+ ARR demonstrates a clear path to the Rule of 40. At 19.3x forward earnings with minimal debt (D/E 13.2%), Braze offers a rare combination of high growth and reasonable valuation in the SaaS landscape.
The bear case centers on Braze's persistent GAAP losses (-17.8% profit margin, -23.8% ROE) and the question of whether the company can achieve sustainable profitability as revenue growth decelerates from 28% to the guided 20-21% [1][5]. Dollar-based net retention declined from 111% to 109% year-over-year, suggesting some customer spending compression, and AI disruption cuts both ways — while BrazeAI is a product advantage today, generative AI could also empower brands to build in-house engagement capabilities or enable new low-cost competitors. Net insider selling across the C-suite (CFO, CTO, General Counsel all selling in Q1 2026) is a negative signal, and Braze faces intensifying competition from Salesforce, Adobe, and vertical SaaS players embedding similar AI-powered engagement features into their platforms.
Key Catalysts
  1. BrazeAI Agent Console and Operator adoption accelerating enterprise bookings, with AI-powered features becoming a primary driver of land-and-expand motion among the 333 large customers at $500K+ ARR
  2. Path to GAAP profitability as operating leverage kicks in — revenue growth of 20%+ on a $740M base with improving gross margins should narrow losses meaningfully in FY2027
  3. Legacy marketing cloud displacement continuing as enterprises migrate from Salesforce Marketing Cloud and Adobe Campaign to Braze's modern architecture, driving net new customer growth above 14%
Key Risks
  1. Dollar-based net retention declining from 111% to 109% and potentially falling below 100% if enterprise customers consolidate martech spending during economic uncertainty
  2. Generative AI enabling brands to build in-house customer engagement capabilities or empowering new low-cost competitors to replicate Braze's cross-channel orchestration functionality
  3. Failure to achieve GAAP profitability within the next 2-3 fiscal years as revenue growth decelerates, potentially triggering valuation compression and investor flight from unprofitable SaaS names
