Company Profile
Energy ·Oil & Gas E&P ·NYSE ·United States
Black Stone Minerals, L.P. is one of the largest owners of oil and natural gas mineral interests in the United States, with approximately 20 million gross acres spanning mineral interests, nonparticipating royalty interests, and overriding royalty interests across major U.S. basins. As a pure-play mineral and royalty company, BSM collects royalty income from E&P operators who develop its acreage, without bearing drilling or operating costs.

Investment Thesis
Black Stone Minerals offers a high-yield (8.57%) income stream from one of the largest mineral rights portfolios in the U.S. — ~20 million gross acres across every major basin [2]. The royalty model produces 72.5% profit margins with minimal capital requirements and pristine leverage (D/E 17.4%), generating $300M in distributable cash flow on $422M revenue in 2025 [1]. Forward P/E of 12.3x is well below the market average, and the business is structurally immune to AI disruption. The January 2026 co-CEO transition to Fowler Carter and Taylor DeWalch maintains continuity, and director insider purchases exceeding $1.4M in May 2026 signal leadership conviction [3]. The Inspire Insight score of 62 with 4 positive attributions and zero negatives reflects alignment with environmental stewardship principles.
The bear case centers on commodity price sensitivity, declining distributions, and limited growth levers. Revenue declined from $784M (2022) to $422M (2025) as oil and gas prices normalized post-2022 [1]. Distributions have been cut from $0.475/quarter in early 2024 to $0.30/quarter, with a payout ratio of 100.4% leaving zero coverage cushion for further commodity weakness [4]. BSM has no ability to control its revenue trajectory — income depends entirely on commodity prices and third-party operator activity on its acreage. Production guidance is roughly flat YoY for 2026, with growth dependent on natural gas price recovery that may not materialize. The partnership structure limits access to capital markets, and the lack of any innovation, R&D, or strategic optionality means BSM is a pure commodity bet with no pathway to value creation beyond waiting for higher prices.
Key Catalysts
  1. Production expected to grow from Q4 2025 to Q4 2026 as operators increase drilling activity, particularly in natural gas-weighted basins where rising LNG export demand supports higher prices [1]

Key Risks
  1. Payout ratio of 100.4% and declining distributions ($0.475 to $0.30/quarter) indicate current distribution levels may not be sustainable if commodity prices weaken further, threatening the income thesis [1][4]
  2. Zero control over revenue drivers — BSM's income depends entirely on commodity prices and third-party operator drilling decisions on its acreage, with no ability to create value through operational improvement [2]
  3. Revenue has declined 46% from the 2022 peak ($784M to $422M in 2025), demonstrating extreme commodity cyclicality that makes BSM unsuitable for investors requiring stable or growing income [1]
