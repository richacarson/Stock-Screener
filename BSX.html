Company Profile

Boston Scientific Corporation develops, manufactures, and markets medical devices for use in various interventional medical specialties worldwide. The company operates in MedSurg and Cardiovascular segments, offering devices for gastrointestinal, urological, pulmonary, cardiac rhythm management, electrophysiology, and structural heart conditions.

Investment Thesis
Boston Scientific represents a premier medtech compounder trading at a compelling PEG of 0.65 with forward P/E of just 14x, suggesting the market has significantly over-discounted temporary segment headwinds. Structural growth drivers in electrophysiology (FARAPULSE PFA adoption), structural heart (WATCHMAN), and AI-guided procedural platforms remain intact, supported by the largest venture portfolio in medtech and 19% operational growth achieved in 2025.
The guidance trim to 6.5-8% organic growth in 2026 reflects cyclical challenges in CRM (down 3% in Q1) and urology (1% organic growth) rather than structural deterioration. Adjusted EPS of $3.34-$3.41 still represents solid double-digit growth. Long-term, BSX's AI integration strategy, regulatory moat from FDA-cleared devices, and expanding operating margins (28% adjusted in 2025) create durable competitive advantages in a healthcare market with secular tailwinds from aging demographics and procedure volume recovery.
Key Catalysts
  1. FARAPULSE pulsed field ablation system gaining rapid market share in electrophysiology with potential to disrupt the multi-billion dollar thermal ablation market
  2. AI-guided procedural platforms and robotic-assisted surgery pipeline approaching commercial milestones across cardiology and endoscopy
  3. Operating margin expansion continuing beyond 28% as high-growth, high-margin Cardiovascular segment becomes a larger revenue mix
Key Risks
  1. WATCHMAN growth deceleration could persist if competitive left atrial appendage closure alternatives gain traction or Medicare reimbursement tightens
  2. Acquisition-heavy strategy creates integration risk and potential culture friction across 59,000 employees in a post-acquisition environment
  3. Tariff and supply chain headwinds contributing to revised 2026 guidance could worsen in escalating trade conflict scenarios
