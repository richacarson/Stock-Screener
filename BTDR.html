Company Profile
Technology ·Software - Application ·NASDAQ ·Singapore
Bitdeer Technologies is a vertically integrated blockchain and high-performance computing company. It designs proprietary ASIC mining chips (SEALMINER), operates Bitcoin mining data centers across Singapore, the US, Bhutan, and Norway, and is expanding into AI/HPC infrastructure. The company has a 3.0 GW global power pipeline with 1.66 GW currently online.

Investment Thesis
Bitdeer Technologies is executing an ambitious vertical integration strategy — designing proprietary SEALMINER ASIC chips, scaling self-mining hashrate 626% YoY to 63.2 EH/s, and pivoting into AI/HPC infrastructure with a 3.0 GW global power pipeline [1]. Q4 2025 revenue of $224.8M grew 225.8% YoY with self-mining revenue up 306% as Bitcoin mining economics improved. The company mined 668 BTC in January 2026 (430% YoY increase) and deployed NVIDIA GB200 NVL72 AI infrastructure in Malaysia with ~$10M GPU cloud ARR [1]. CEO Jihan Wu's 20.44% ownership ($519.8M) provides significant alignment, and the dual-use thesis — crypto mining infrastructure supporting AI workloads — offers optionality if the AI data center boom continues [2].
The bear case is dominated by governance, execution, and financial risk. A securities fraud class action filed in December 2025 alleges Bitdeer misled investors about SEALMINER A4 chip efficiency and SEAL04 development timeline, with early samples testing above the 5 J/TH target and next-gen chip 'significantly delayed' [3]. The company reported a $266.7M net loss, and the negative forward P/E indicates analysts expect continued losses near-term [3][4]. D/E of 126.7% includes $375M in convertible notes, creating dilution risk. Management tenure of only 1.8 years and board tenure of 2.7 years raise execution concerns during a critical strategic pivot. Bitcoin price volatility directly drives revenue — a sustained BTC price decline would compress margins on the self-mining business. The AI/HPC pivot puts Bitdeer in competition with vastly better-resourced cloud providers (AWS, CoreWeave) with no established competitive moat in AI infrastructure [2].
