Company Profile
Technology ·Software - Application ·NASDAQ ·Singapore
Bitdeer Technologies Group is a vertically integrated blockchain and high-performance computing company. It designs proprietary ASIC mining chips (SEALMINER), operates Bitcoin mining data centers across Singapore, the US, Bhutan, Norway, and Ethiopia, and is expanding into AI/HPC cloud infrastructure. The company has a 3.0 GW global power pipeline and mined 2,033 BTC in Q1 2026 with 63.2 EH/s self-mining hashrate.

Investment Thesis
Bitdeer Technologies is executing an ambitious vertical integration strategy — proprietary SEALMINER ASIC chips, self-mining hashrate of 63.2 EH/s producing 2,033 BTC in Q1 2026, and a rapidly growing AI cloud business reaching ~$69M ARR by April 2026 [1]. Q1 2026 revenue of $188.9M (+169% YoY) demonstrates explosive topline growth, and the 3.0 GW global power pipeline provides visible infrastructure runway. CEO Jihan Wu's ~20% ownership ($519M+) aligns founder interests with shareholders. The dual-use thesis — crypto mining infrastructure supporting AI workloads — offers genuine optionality. The stock has doubled from $8.37 to $17.23 since March 2026, and analyst consensus target of $21.52 implies further 25% upside [5].
The bear case centers on profitability, governance, and competitive risk. Q1 2026 produced a $159.5M net loss despite $188.9M revenue, with cost of revenue exceeding sales due to aggressive infrastructure buildout [1]. TTM operating cash flow of -$1.8B and free cash flow of -$2.1B represent extreme capital consumption, funded by $1.9B in borrowings including convertible notes that create dilution risk [5]. The COO and CBO resigned in June 2026, a new CFO was appointed in May 2026, and a securities fraud class action remains pending — three C-suite transitions in one year signals organizational instability [2][4]. D/E of 278% and Altman Z-Score of 1.15 reflect elevated financial risk. Short interest of 21.5% indicates substantial market skepticism [5]. Bitcoin price volatility directly drives self-mining revenue (the largest segment), and the AI/HPC pivot puts Bitdeer against vastly better-resourced competitors (AWS, CoreWeave) with no established competitive moat.
