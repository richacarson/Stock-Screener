Company Profile

B2Gold is an international mid-tier gold producer operating four mines: Fekola (Mali), Masbate (Philippines), Otjikoto (Namibia), and the newly commissioned Goose Mine (Canada). The company produced 237,763 ounces in Q1 2026 and generated $1.16B in gold revenue. Headquartered in Vancouver, Canada.

Investment Thesis
B2Gold offers compelling value at 4.2x forward P/E with Q1 2026 gold revenue of $1.16B (more than doubling YoY), operating cash flow of $539M, and free cash flow of $362M in a single quarter [1]. The fortress balance sheet (D/E of 14%) provides financial flexibility, and all four operating mines outperformed budget in Q1 2026 with 237,763 ounces produced (23% above prior year). The Goose Mine ramp to ~250K oz in 2026 and ~330K oz in 2027 provides a clear production growth trajectory, and gold prices above $3,000/oz generate substantial free cash flow at B2Gold's cost structure [1][5].
The bear case centers on 2026 being a production transition year — guidance of 820,000-970,000 ounces is below 2025's ~980,000 ounces due to Otjikoto step-down and Fekola stripping requirements [1]. CEO Clive Johnson's retirement in June 2026 creates leadership transition risk as CFO Mike Cinnamond takes over — the new CEO has never run a mining operation. Geopolitical risk in Mali (military government, potential nationalization of the Fekola Mine) remains the most significant tail risk. The dividend was cut 50% in 2025 (from $0.04/quarter to $0.02/quarter), signaling capital allocation pressure despite strong gold prices [5]. Gold price sensitivity is extreme — B2Gold's margins compress rapidly below $2,500/oz given elevated AISC of $2,400-2,580/oz [1].
Key Catalysts
  1. Goose Mine ramp to ~250K oz in 2026 and ~330K oz in 2027 restores production growth and validates the multi-billion dollar Nunavut investment [1]
  2. Gold prices sustaining above $3,000/oz generate exceptional free cash flow at B2Gold's cost structure, with Q1 2026 FCF of $362M annualizing to $1.4B+ [1]
  3. New CEO Mike Cinnamond (effective June 2026) could bring fresh strategic direction including potential M&A or diversification beyond pure gold production [4]
Key Risks
  1. Mali geopolitical instability — military government could impose new taxes, royalties, or partial nationalization of the Fekola Mine, which represents ~40% of production capacity [1]
  2. Gold price decline below $2,500/oz would compress margins to near breakeven given AISC guidance of $2,400-2,580/oz, eliminating free cash flow generation [1]
  3. CEO leadership transition risk — founder Clive Johnson retiring June 2026, replaced by CFO with no operational mining experience, during a critical Goose Mine ramp period [4]
