Investment Thesis
AB InBev is the world's largest brewer executing a disciplined deleveraging and organic-growth playbook under Michel Doukeris, with Q4 2025 EPS beating consensus ($0.95 vs. $0.91), full-year EBITDA up 4.9% with 101 bps of margin expansion, and net debt / EBITDA below 3x for the first time since the SABMiller acquisition [1]. Non-alcohol beer revenue grew 34% in 2025 and gained share in 70% of AB InBev's top 14 non-alcohol markets, positioning the company for the secular shift toward wellness and lower-alcohol consumption [1][3]. Forward P/E of 15.6x with 11.5% profit margins, a ~1.5% dividend yield, and continued productivity capture make BUD a reasonable mature-CPG compounder. Material ESG execution (67.6% renewable electricity, 39.2% Scope 1+2 emissions reduction vs. 2017, net-zero by 2040) supports institutional ownership [4].
The bear case centers on brand fragility and product category headwinds. The 2023 Bud Light controversy cost ~$1.4B in US sales, handed the #1 US beer position to Modelo Especial (Constellation Brands), and exposed the limits of reconciling values-based marketing with a working-class consumer base [3]. Alcohol is an Inspire Insight 'Alcohol' negative attribution — meaningful for values-driven investors despite the small -1 net score. Revenue growth of 2% organic / 4.8% reported is below CPG peer growth rates, and US beer volumes continue to decline secularly as younger consumers drink less alcohol. D/E of 75% reflects still-elevated leverage; earnings are sensitive to FX transactional headwinds [5][1]. The premium non-alcohol bet (34% growth) is the most credible offset but remains a small portion of total revenue.
Key Catalysts
  1. Non-alcohol beer portfolio sustaining 30%+ growth, driving mix improvement and share gains in 70% of top 14 non-alcohol markets [1][3]
  2. Continued deleveraging with net debt / EBITDA below 3x, unlocking dividend growth and potential buyback capacity [1]
  3. Productivity-driven margin expansion (101 bps in 2025) as Doukeris' organic-growth strategy compounds through 2026 [1]
Key Risks
  1. Brand fragility and consumer controversy — the 2023 Bud Light backlash ceded the #1 US beer spot to Modelo Especial and cost ~$1.4B in US sales [3]
  2. Secular decline in US beer volumes as Gen Z drinks less alcohol, and alcohol-category ESG/values pressure on the Inspire score [3][4]
  3. FX transactional headwinds and emerging-market exposure (Mexico, Brazil, South Africa) creating reported-basis revenue volatility [1]
