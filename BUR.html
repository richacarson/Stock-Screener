Company Profile
Financial Services ·Asset Management ·NYSE ·Guernsey
Burford Capital is the world's largest provider of litigation finance, offering capital against the underlying value of commercial litigation and arbitration matters. The company operates in two segments: Principal Finance (deploying capital directly into legal claims) and Asset Management (managing third-party legal finance assets). Burford also provides legal risk management and adverse cost insurance services.
172 Employees ·www.burfordcapital.com

Investment Thesis
Burford Capital is the world's largest litigation funder trading at a deeply discounted 5.0x forward P/E, with new definitive commitments up 39% in FY2025 and portfolio modeled realizations growing $700M to $5.2B at year-end [1]. The massive gap between forward P/E (5.0x) and trailing P/E (17.8x) reflects analyst expectations for a significant earnings rebound as the litigation resolution pipeline matures. At $4.99 per share (0.45x book value), the market prices extreme skepticism about the company's ability to realize the $5.2B in modeled portfolio value. CEO Bogart's 16.5-year tenure and the company's position as category creator in institutional litigation finance provide competitive advantages that should compound as the legal finance market matures [2].
The bear case centers on Burford's extreme earnings volatility, opaque accounting, and governance concerns. Revenue swung from $853.9M (FY2023) to $193.8M (FY2025) — a 77% decline — driven by litigation resolution timing that is fundamentally unpredictable [1][5]. The business model requires continuous debt issuance ($2.1B outstanding) to fund new commitments, creating refinancing risk in tight credit markets. Sustainalytics assigns a 'Severe' ESG controversy level, and critics argue the litigation finance model incentivizes excessive litigation and economic coercion [4]. The Muddy Waters short attack in 2019 raised legitimate questions about financial reporting transparency that have not been fully resolved. The stock has declined 67% from its $15.10 52-week high, suggesting the market may be pricing in structural concerns beyond just a bad revenue year. Glassdoor culture ratings (2.9/5 for culture and values) are concerning for a 172-person knowledge business [3][5].
Key Catalysts
  1. Portfolio realizations from $5.2B in modeled value that could drive a dramatic earnings recovery if major cases resolve favorably in 2026-2027 [1]
  2. YPF-related litigation resolution — Burford holds a significant position in the Argentine YPF nationalization case that could generate hundreds of millions in gains [4]
  3. New definitive commitments up 39% in FY2025, indicating strong deal origination that will feed future revenue as cases mature over 2-4 year horizons [1]
Key Risks
  1. Extreme revenue volatility — FY2025 revenue of $193.8M was 77% below FY2023's $853.9M, and future years are similarly unpredictable based on litigation resolution timing [1][5]
  2. Debt-funded business model with $2.1B outstanding creates refinancing risk; the company has burned through $1B+ in the last 5 years financed by borrowing [4][5]
  3. Sustainalytics 'Severe' ESG controversy rating and ongoing criticism of litigation finance as ethically problematic could limit institutional investor appetite and regulatory scrutiny could constrain the business [4]
