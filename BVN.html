Company Profile
Basic Materials ·Other Precious Metals & Mining ·NYSE ·Peru
Compañía de Minas Buenaventura is Peru's largest publicly traded precious and base metals mining company, founded in 1953. The company explores for and produces gold, silver, copper, zinc, and lead across multiple Peruvian operations, including the newly commissioned San Gabriel gold mine and the flagship Uchucchacua silver mine. BVN also holds a significant stake in Cerro Verde, one of Peru's largest copper operations.

Investment Thesis
Buenaventura is a Peruvian mining company riding a historic precious metals supercycle — Q4 2025 EPS of $1.62 crushed consensus of $0.59 by $1.03, revenue of $623.4M beat by 38%, and the newly commissioned San Gabriel mine is guiding 48,000-55,000 gold ounces in 2026 [1]. At 10.4x forward P/E with a 45.2% profit margin, 21.4% ROE, and 108% YoY revenue growth, BVN offers compelling value exposure to gold prices (management guides $1.8B-$2.0B revenue at $4,500/oz gold) and Peru's copper resources [4]. The minimal 16.6% D/E ratio, 13.2% payout ratio, and 300%+ dividend increase in 2025 signal financial strength and management confidence in sustained earnings power.
The bear case is dominated by commodity price dependency and Peru country risk. BVN's financial profile is entirely leveraged to gold and silver prices — if gold retreats from $4,500/oz to $2,500/oz, the revenue and EBITDA guidance collapses proportionally [1]. Peru's chronic mining social license issues have already impacted BVN: contract worker protests at Uchucchacua, production costs rising 15% YoY, and new royalty hikes threaten margins [3]. The $3.4B Trapiche copper project carries significant execution and permitting risk in Apurímac, an area with both mineral reserves and sensitive ecosystems. The dividend payment history is inconsistent (skipped 2020-2021), limiting income investor confidence. BVN has no AI adoption or operational digitization, leaving it vulnerable to more technologically advanced mining competitors in the long term [2].
Key Catalysts
  1. San Gabriel gold mine ramp-up to full production (48,000-55,000 oz guided for 2026) adding high-margin ounces to BVN's portfolio at a time of record gold prices [1]
  2. Gold price momentum above $4,500/oz driving BVN's guided $800M-$1.0B EBITDA for 2026, with potential upside if prices remain elevated [1]
  3. Trapiche copper project ($3.4B) environmental approval advancing toward construction, positioning BVN for long-term copper exposure as AI data center buildout drives copper demand [3]
Key Risks
  1. Gold and silver price dependency — a sustained decline in precious metals prices from current record levels would compress margins and revenue proportionally, as BVN has no hedging program disclosed [1]
  2. Peru political and social license risk — community protests, new mining royalty hikes, and labor unrest at Uchucchacua and other operations could disrupt production and inflate costs [3]
  3. Trapiche copper project execution risk — the $3.4B capex requirement, environmental sensitivities in Apurímac, and multi-year construction timeline create significant capital allocation risk [3]
