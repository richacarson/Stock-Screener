Company Profile
Real Estate ·REIT - Mortgage ·NYSE ·United States
Blackstone Mortgage Trust is an externally managed commercial mortgage REIT that originates, acquires, and manages senior floating-rate mortgage loans secured by commercial real estate. Managed by Blackstone, the world's largest alternative asset manager, BXMT operates globally across the U.S., Europe, and Australia. The company was formerly Capital Trust, Inc. and rebranded in 2013.

Investment Thesis
Blackstone Mortgage Trust offers a 9.36% dividend yield backed by improving fundamentals — Q4 2025 revenue surged 39.2% to $159.3M (beating estimates by 47%), impaired loans dropped from $2.3B to $700M, and distributable earnings now cover the dividend at 102% [1][2]. The forward P/E of 10.3x implies earnings of $1.94/share, a major recovery from trailing $0.64, creating potential for significant total return if CRE markets stabilize. Blackstone's $1T+ AUM platform provides unmatched deal flow, underwriting capability, and institutional credibility that independent mortgage REITs cannot replicate [3]. The floating-rate portfolio benefits from elevated interest rates through higher net interest margins. The Inspire score of 63 (ALIGNED) with zero negative attributions supports faith-based ownership. At $20.08 and near 52-week highs, the stock reflects early recovery recognition.
The bear case is dominated by leverage and CRE credit risk. D/E of 461.7% and $16.2B total debt against a $3.4B market cap creates extreme financial fragility — even modest credit deterioration could wipe out equity value [5]. The 24% dividend cut in Q3 2024 demonstrates that management will reduce distributions when necessary, and the GAAP payout ratio of 294% remains deeply unsustainable. ROE of 3.0% is abysmal for the risk taken, reflecting the impaired loan drag. The CRE market remains under pressure from remote work reducing office demand, higher interest rates stressing borrower debt service, and slowing transaction volumes. The externally managed structure creates management fee conflicts — Blackstone earns fees regardless of BXMT stock performance [4]. Blackstone's own AI risk framework warns that rule-based businesses face 'profound' disruption — CRE underwriting has rule-based elements that could be automated, potentially compressing lending spreads over time [3]. The $700M in remaining impaired loans could still generate significant losses.
Key Catalysts
  1. Earnings recovery from $0.64 trailing EPS to $1.94 forward EPS, driven by impaired loan resolution and higher net interest margins on floating-rate portfolio [1][5]
  2. Continued impaired loan reduction — decline from $2.3B to $700M demonstrates portfolio cleanup that could eliminate the credit loss overhang [2]
  3. 9.36% dividend yield now covered at 102% by distributable earnings, with potential for dividend increases if earnings recovery materializes [1]
Key Risks
  1. Extreme leverage — $16.2B debt on $3.4B market cap (461.7% D/E) creates existential risk in a CRE downturn or if refinancing costs increase [5]
  2. CRE market structural risks — remote work reducing office demand, elevated interest rates stressing borrower debt service, and slowing transaction volumes threaten portfolio credit quality [4]
  3. External management conflicts — Blackstone earns management fees regardless of BXMT shareholder outcomes, creating misaligned incentives between manager and shareholders [4]
