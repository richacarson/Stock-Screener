Blackstone Secured Lending Fund is an externally managed business development company (BDC) that invests primarily in first-lien senior secured debt of private US middle-market companies. As of September 30, 2025, BXSL held approximately $13.8B of investments and is advised by Blackstone Credit & Insurance.

Investment Thesis
Blackstone Secured Lending is one of the largest publicly traded BDCs with $13.8B of investments as of Q3 2025, backed by Blackstone's ~$395B private credit platform [1][2]. The ~13.2% yield ($3.08 annual, $0.77 Q1 2026) is covered by net investment income of $0.82 per share (107% coverage), and the portfolio is concentrated in first-lien senior secured debt — the top of the capital stack and lowest risk tranche within private credit [1]. For income investors, BXSL offers a high-yield vehicle with Blackstone's underwriting brand and disciplined first-lien focus. Blackstone Inc. is a Great Place to Work certified with 91% positive employee rating, indicating the adviser's institutional quality [2].
Bear case dominates the AI-era narrative: Morgan Stanley warns private credit direct lending default rates could surge to 8% versus the 2-2.5% historical average, with AI disruption concentrated in software/technology sectors that represent 20%+ of BDC portfolios [2]. BCRED (Blackstone's retail private credit fund with overlapping portfolio construction) posted its first monthly loss in three years in February 2026 and saw a surge in redemption requests — an early warning signal [2]. Morgan Stanley estimates software exposure in direct lending at ~26%, and agentic AI is disrupting SaaS unit economics, potentially leading to margin compression and defaults among BXSL's borrowers. BXSL explicitly does not pursue an ESG-based strategy and has a 'poor' MSCI ESG rating, which limits its appeal to ESG-oriented capital [4]. BDC structure forces 90%+ distribution of taxable income, leaving little retained capital cushion if losses accelerate. Payout ratio of ~94% means any NII decline pressures dividend coverage quickly.
Key Catalysts
  1. Net investment income coverage of dividend — $0.82 NII vs $0.77 dividend (107% coverage) if maintained supports distribution stability [1]
  2. Blackstone's $395B private credit platform scale enables selective origination and workout capability in a stress cycle [2]
  3. Potential rate cuts reducing cost of BDC leverage and boosting net interest margin if the Fed eases through 2026
Key Risks
  1. AI-driven software defaults — Morgan Stanley warns private credit default rates could surge to 8% vs 2-2.5% historical, concentrated in BXSL's software/tech exposure (~26% of direct lending) [2]
  2. BCRED February 2026 first monthly loss in 3 years and redemption surge signal broader private credit stress beginning to materialize [2]
  3. High dividend payout ratio (~94%) leaves minimal buffer for NII deterioration; any credit event pressures distribution [1]
