Company Profile

Kanzhun Limited operates BOSS Zhipin, China's largest online recruitment platform by monthly active users. The platform offers AI-powered recruitment services connecting job seekers with enterprise customers through direct real-time chat, AI resume polishing, interview coaching, and AI-enhanced job descriptions. Founded in 2014 by Peng Zhao and headquartered in Beijing.
4,884 Employees ·ir.zhipin.com

Investment Thesis
Kanzhun operates China's dominant recruitment platform (60.7M MAU) with exceptional financials — 33.1% profit margins, 85.1% gross margins, and a fortress balance sheet with $20B cash and virtually no debt [1][4]. Net profit surged 72.6% YoY to RMB2.74B in 2025 while revenue grew 12.4% to RMB8.27B [1]. Forward P/E of 9.3x with 43% FCF yield represents deep value for a profitable, growing tech platform with active AI deployment (DeepSeek-R1, Nanbeige model, Hammer agent) [2][4]. The company's blue-collar and Tier 3+ city moat creates durable network effects that competitors cannot easily replicate. Management's commitment to return 50%+ of adjusted profit through buybacks and dividends starting 2026 provides shareholder return visibility [1].
The bear case centers on China macro risk, regulatory uncertainty, and AI disruption. China's youth unemployment rate remains elevated, and the slowing economy could pressure employer recruitment spending — Q1 2026 guidance of 6.6-8.8% YoY growth represents a meaningful deceleration from 14% [1]. As a Chinese ADR listed on NASDAQ, Kanzhun faces VIE structure risk, potential delisting threats under HFCAA, and regulatory unpredictability from the PRC government [3]. The company was subject to a class action lawsuit, adding legal risk [3]. AI is both an enabler and potential disruptor — generalist AI tools could eventually bypass specialized recruitment platforms by matching candidates directly [2]. The stock has declined from $15.99 (50-day SMA) to $13.50, reflecting China tech sentiment weakness. Despite strong fundamentals, the China discount may persist indefinitely given geopolitical tensions between the U.S. and China [4].
Key Catalysts
  1. AI chatbot for 'big batch recruitment' launching within 1-2 months could accelerate blue-collar hiring efficiency and drive employer monetization [2]

Key Risks
  1. China macro and regulatory risk — VIE structure, potential NASDAQ delisting under HFCAA, and PRC government regulatory actions could destroy shareholder value regardless of fundamentals [3]
  2. Revenue growth deceleration — Q1 2026 guidance of 6.6-8.8% vs 14% in 2025 signals potential maturation of the Chinese recruitment market [1]
  3. AI platform disruption — generalist AI tools (ChatGPT, DeepSeek) could disintermediate specialized recruitment platforms by enabling direct employer-candidate AI matching [2]
