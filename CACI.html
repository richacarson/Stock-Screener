Investment Thesis
CACI International is a mid-cap defense technology company in the midst of a strategic transformation from IT services provider to AI-enabled mission systems integrator. The $2.6B Arka acquisition positions CACI at the intersection of three secular growth trends in defense: AI-powered intelligence automation, space-based sensing, and electronic warfare. With a $33.9B backlog (+4.6% YoY), 11.2% Q1 FY2026 revenue growth, and a management team that has compounded revenue from $2B to $8.6B through disciplined M&A, CACI is executing a credible transition to higher-margin technology products. The company's AI capabilities — particularly in geospatial intelligence (NGA $290M contract) and agentic AI (via Arka) — position it to capture increasing defense AI budgets.
The primary concerns are valuation and margin profile: at 26x trailing earnings with 5.8% profit margins and 81.9% debt-to-equity (elevated by the Arka acquisition), CACI is priced for continued execution. The forward P/E of 19x is more reasonable given 14.5% EPS growth and the expanding technology mix. The defense budget environment remains supportive under the FY2026 NDAA, but any sequestration risk or contract delays could pressure the backlog-to-revenue conversion. For IOWN's framework, CACI scores well on innovation, mission-driven inspiration, and AI resilience, but the lack of dividend income and thin margins limit its appeal for income-oriented portfolios. Best suited as a Growth sleeve holding for investors seeking defense-sector AI exposure with demonstrated execution history.
