Company Profile
Consumer Defensive ·Packaged Foods ·NYSE ·United States
Conagra Brands, Inc. operates as a consumer packaged goods food company in the United States through four segments: Grocery & Snacks, Refrigerated & Frozen, International, and Foodservice. Key brands include Birds Eye, Marie Callender's, Duncan Hines, Healthy Choice, Slim Jim, Reddi-wip, and Angie's BOOMCHICKAPOP. Founded in 1919, headquartered in Chicago, Illinois.

Investment Thesis
Conagra trades at just 8.2x forward earnings with a 10.3% dividend yield, offering deep value if the turnaround materializes under new CEO John Brase. The company generates $1.24B in operating cash flow and $655M in free cash flow — more than sufficient to cover the approximately $670M annual dividend. Q3 FY2026 showed organic sales returning to growth at +2.4%, and 'Project Catalyst' AI-driven efficiency gains could expand the 10.6% adjusted operating margin toward the company's 11.0-11.5% target range [1][3]. The MSCI AA ESG rating and faith-aligned Inspire score of +46 add portfolio fit for values-oriented investors [2].
Conagra faces structural headwinds in packaged foods as consumers shift toward fresh, less-processed alternatives. Revenue has declined from $12.3B (FY2023) to $11.6B (FY2025), profit margins turned negative on a GAAP basis, ROE is essentially zero, and the dividend has been frozen at $1.40 since FY2024 [1][5]. The 79% payout ratio on operating earnings and $7.3B debt load leave minimal margin for error — any further revenue decline could force a dividend cut that would devastate the stock given that most holders own it for yield. The CEO transition to John Brase adds execution uncertainty during a critical period of volume decline and consumer trade-down [3]. Innovation and Inspiration scores of 3 each reflect a company that is managing its portfolio rather than building for the future.

AI Resilience (25%)
AI Resilience LOW RISK
8
AI Risk Protection (8/9): Conagra's business is structurally immune to AI disruption. Packaged food manufacturing, distribution logistics, retailer relationships, and brand management are fundamentally physical operations that AI cannot substitute [5]. Food production requires factories, supply chains, cold storage, and regulatory compliance — none of which can be digitized or automated away by AI. Consumer packaged goods purchasing decisions are driven by brand loyalty, shelf placement, price, and taste — factors where AI has no competitive vector. The only minor AI risk exposure comes from potential AI-enabled private label optimization by retailers, which could incrementally pressure branded products. AI Opportunity (0/1): 'Project Catalyst' uses AI for internal predictive modeling and process optimization with a 40-person digital organization [4]. However, this is purely operational efficiency — no AI-driven revenue streams, AI-enhanced products, or AI-enabled consumer experiences have been launched or quantified. AI remains a back-office tool rather than a growth driver.
