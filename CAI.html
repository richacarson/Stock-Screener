Company Profile
Healthcare ·Diagnostics & Research ·NASDAQ ·United States
Caris Life Sciences is a precision oncology and molecular science company that performs Whole Exome and Whole Transcriptome Sequencing (WES/WTS) for cancer therapy selection. The company operates one of the largest clinico-genomic databases in oncology and applies AI/ML bioinformatics to classify cancer at the molecular level, predict therapy response, and develop blood-based early detection assays.

Investment Thesis
Caris Life Sciences is the breakout story in precision oncology — Q4 2025 revenue surged 125% YoY to $292.9M with gross margin expanding 2,000 bps to 75%, and the company turned profitable with $82M net income and $106.1M Adjusted EBITDA in the quarter [1]. Management guides full-year 2026 revenue to $1.0-1.02B (+23-26%) with continued 20% volume growth in clinical therapy selection [1]. The competitive moat is durable: Caris owns one of the largest clinico-genomic databases in oncology paired with proprietary AI/ML bioinformatics that classify cancers and predict therapy response — a flywheel where each additional case improves model accuracy [2]. The blood-based Caris Assure early detection program adds a multi-billion dollar TAM expansion thesis on top of the molecular profiling base. With $5.43B market cap on accelerating $1B revenue and best-in-class 75% gross margins, Caris is one of the few diagnostics firms positioned to compound at 20%+ for a decade [4].
The bear case rests on valuation, culture, and reimbursement risk. At 6.7x P/S the stock trades above the 5.1x peer average and trailing P/E is still negative, leaving little room for execution stumbles [4]. Glassdoor culture/values rating of just 2.7/5 and employee complaints of 'toxic culture' and 'favoritism' are red flags for a company that needs to scale its workforce; the recent CSO/CFO/Chief People Officer hires hint that the board sees problems [3]. The 2022 $2.8M False Claims Act settlement for delaying test submissions shows historical compliance gaps that must not recur as Medicare reimbursement is a critical revenue source [5]. Tempus AI, Foundation Medicine (Roche), Guardant Health, and Natera all compete aggressively with deeper pockets, and any payer pricing pressure on comprehensive genomic profiling could compress the gross margin gains. Recent IPO lockup expirations could also create supply overhang.
Key Catalysts
  1. Achieving 2026 revenue guidance of $1.0-1.02B (+23-26%) with continued 75% gross margin profile, validating the operating leverage thesis [1]
  2. Caris Assure blood-based early detection commercial expansion contributing to the $75-85M Pharma & Research revenue line in 2026 [1]
  3. Sustained 20% case volume growth in clinical therapy selection, demonstrating share gains versus Tempus, Foundation Medicine, and Guardant [1]
Key Risks
  1. Valuation risk — 6.7x P/S vs 5.1x peer average and negative trailing P/E leaves limited margin for execution misses [4]
  2. Culture and management risk — Glassdoor 2.7/5 culture rating with 'toxic culture' employee feedback could impair scaling and the recent C-suite turnover signals concern [3]
  3. Reimbursement / regulatory risk — the 2022 $2.8M False Claims Act settlement underscores Medicare billing exposure, and any rate cut on comprehensive genomic profiling would directly compress margins [5]
