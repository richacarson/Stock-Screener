Company Profile
Technology ·Computer Hardware ·NASDAQ ·Singapore
Canaan Inc. is a technology company that designs ASIC high-performance computing chips and produces bitcoin mining equipment under the Avalon brand. Founded in 2013 by Nangeng Zhang, Canaan shipped the world's first ASIC bitcoin mining machines. The company operates bitcoin self-mining operations with installed hashrate of 10.97 EH/s and holds a treasury of 1,826 BTC and 3,952 ETH as of April 2026. Canaan recently exited its AI semiconductor business to refocus on core crypto infrastructure. Listed on NASDAQ since 2019, headquartered in Singapore.

Investment Thesis
Canaan delivered explosive 121% revenue growth to $529.7M in FY2025, driven by the bitcoin bull cycle and expanding self-mining operations reaching 10.97 EH/s installed hashrate [1]. The company holds a bitcoin treasury of 1,826 BTC and 3,952 ETH providing leveraged crypto exposure, and its strategic refocus on core bitcoin mining after exiting AI chips simplifies the business model. At $0.53/share ($393M market cap) with minimal debt (D/E of 12.6%), CAN offers high-beta exposure to bitcoin price appreciation through both equipment sales and self-mining operations, with the landmark U.S. order in early 2026 validating demand recovery.
The bear case is severe. Despite 121% revenue growth, Canaan remains deeply unprofitable with -39.7% profit margins, -59.8% ROE, and negative free cash flow of -$194M [1][4]. The forward P/E is negative (-15x), meaning analysts expect continued losses even at current elevated bitcoin prices. The company's revenue is entirely dependent on bitcoin price cycles — revenue collapsed from $651M to $211M between 2022 and 2023, demonstrating existential vulnerability to crypto downturns. The failed AI chip venture ($900K revenue against 15% of opex) raises questions about capital allocation discipline [2]. Competitively, Canaan is a distant second to Bitmain in mining equipment market share, and the 399-person workforce limits engineering scale. The stock trades at $0.53 — down over 95% from its IPO price — reflecting the market's assessment of structural unprofitability in a commoditized, cyclical business.
Key Catalysts
  1. Bitcoin price appreciation above $100K would dramatically improve self-mining profitability and mining equipment demand, with Canaan's 10.97 EH/s providing direct BTC price exposure
  2. U.S. bitcoin mining recovery and landmark equipment orders signal potential market share gains as North American miners expand capacity post-halving
  3. Operational focus on cost efficiency after exiting unprofitable AI chip business could improve path to profitability — the Avalon A15 Hydro's energy efficiency enhances competitive positioning
Key Risks
  1. Deeply unprofitable at -39.7% profit margin with negative free cash flow of -$194M despite 121% revenue growth — achieving sustained profitability remains unproven at any bitcoin price level
  2. Complete dependence on bitcoin price cycles creates existential risk — revenue collapsed 68% from $651M to $211M in the prior downturn, and another crypto winter would devastate the business
  3. Distant second to Bitmain in mining equipment market share with only 399 employees, limiting R&D scale and competitive positioning in ASIC chip design innovation
