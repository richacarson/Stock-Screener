Company Profile
Technology ·Computer Hardware ·NASDAQ ·Singapore
Canaan Inc. is a technology company focused on ASIC high-performance computing chip design for bitcoin mining equipment. The company designs and sells Avalon-branded bitcoin mining machines and also operates its own bitcoin mining facilities across North America, the Middle East, and Africa. Founded in 2013, Canaan shipped the world's first ASIC-based bitcoin mining machine. Recently discontinued AI semiconductor operations to focus exclusively on bitcoin mining. Incorporated in the Cayman Islands, headquartered in Singapore.

Investment Thesis
Canaan is a speculative bitcoin mining hardware play trading at $0.42 per share ($311M market cap) with a clean balance sheet (D/E 12.6%) and cryptocurrency treasury of 1,808 BTC and 3,952 ETH providing downside support [1]. FY2025 revenue doubled to $529.7M as bitcoin price strength drove mining hardware demand, and the company's ASIC chip design expertise (first 7nm mining chip, first commercial ASIC miner) represents genuine technological capability [3]. Installed computing power across joint-mining projects reached 11 EH/s (up 10.7% sequentially), and the company's vertical integration from chip design to mining operations provides exposure to both hardware sales and bitcoin production. At current depressed levels, the stock offers asymmetric upside if bitcoin sustains above $80K and mining hardware demand remains strong through the 2025-2026 halving cycle.
The bear case is severe and multi-dimensional. Canaan has never sustained profitability — the company lost $210M in FY2025, $250M in FY2024, and $414M in FY2023, with the sole profitable year being FY2022 ($70M net income) [2]. ROE of -59.8% and profit margin of -39.7% reflect a business that systematically destroys shareholder value. Q2 2026 revenue guidance of $35-45M represents a severe sequential decline from Q1's $62.7M, suggesting demand softening [1]. The stock trades at $0.42 — micro-cap territory with high volatility and potential NASDAQ delisting risk if the price remains below $1. The decision to discontinue AI semiconductor operations eliminates the company's most differentiated technology and concentrates all risk on bitcoin mining cycles [3]. Canaan competes against dominant Bitmain and well-funded MicroBT with a fraction of their market share. Regulatory risk from potential cryptocurrency restrictions, energy consumption scrutiny, and China-related geopolitical concerns compound the investment risk profile.
Key Catalysts
  1. Bitcoin price sustained above $80K driving renewed demand for Avalon mining hardware and increasing the value of Canaan's 1,808 BTC + 3,952 ETH cryptocurrency treasury

Key Risks
  1. Persistent unprofitability with cumulative losses exceeding $870M over FY2023-2025, -59.8% ROE, and Q2 2026 guidance of $35-45M implying continued revenue deterioration from Q1 levels
  2. Micro-cap status ($311M market cap) and sub-$1 stock price create NASDAQ delisting risk, extreme volatility, and limited institutional investor interest — the stock has lost over 95% from its 2021 peak
  3. Concentrated bitcoin mining exposure with no diversification after discontinuing AI semiconductor operations — a sustained bitcoin bear market would be existential, and competition from Bitmain and MicroBT limits pricing power

AI Resilience (25%)
AI Resilience HIGH RISK
4
AI Risk Protection (3/9): Canaan's core business — designing ASIC chips for bitcoin mining hardware — faces mixed but meaningful AI-adjacent disruption dynamics. While AI cannot directly replace bitcoin mining hardware demand (miners need physical chips), the company's competitive position is threatened by the broader semiconductor industry's pivot toward AI chips [3]. TSMC, Samsung, and other foundries are prioritizing AI chip manufacturing capacity, which could squeeze supply allocation for bitcoin mining ASICs. More fundamentally, Canaan's decision to discontinue its AI semiconductor business (Kendryte) means it has no participation in the AI value chain — it is a pure bitcoin mining play in a world where AI is capturing an increasing share of semiconductor industry investment and talent [3]. If capital markets continue favoring AI-focused semiconductor companies, Canaan faces multiple compression and reduced access to capital. Bitcoin mining hardware demand is also highly cyclical and correlated to bitcoin prices, creating business model fragility. AI Opportunity (1/1): Canaan previously developed genuine AI chip technology (Kendryte K510 RISC-V edge AI chip) but has discontinued this business line to focus on bitcoin mining. The company's ASIC design expertise could theoretically be redeployed toward AI applications, but management has explicitly chosen not to pursue this path [3].
