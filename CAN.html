Company Profile
Technology ·Computer Hardware ·NASDAQ ·Singapore
Canaan Inc. engages in the research and development, design, and sale of ASIC-based bitcoin mining equipment and related components. The company also operates its own bitcoin mining facilities. It has operations across the United States, the UAE, Ethiopia, Hong Kong, Malaysia, Kazakhstan, Canada, and other countries. Founded by Nangeng Zhang in 2013.

Investment Thesis
Canaan offers leveraged exposure to the bitcoin mining cycle at a depressed $0.44/share valuation. Q4 2025 revenue surged 121% YoY to $196.3M driven by strong mining equipment demand, self-mining operations exceeded 10 EH/s of deployed hashrate, and the company holds a cryptocurrency treasury of 1,750 BTC and 3,951 ETH [1]. With bitcoin prices elevated and the next halving cycle approaching, Canaan's ASIC chip design expertise and vertically integrated mining operations could generate significant operating leverage if the company achieves breakeven profitability.
The bear case is severe. Canaan has been consistently unprofitable with -39.7% profit margins, -59.8% ROE, and a forward P/E that is negative, meaning analysts expect continued losses through 2026 [1]. The 3-year revenue CAGR is -6.6% despite the recent boom, reflecting extreme cyclicality where revenue can collapse 70% (from $650M in 2022 to $210M in 2023). The decision to abandon AI semiconductors eliminates the only diversification pathway and leaves the company entirely dependent on bitcoin economics [2]. At $0.44/share and a $325M market cap, Canaan faces potential delisting risk if the stock remains below $1.00, and the $13.9M in Q4 inventory write-downs suggest margin pressure even during favorable market conditions. The 399-employee company with operations in 10+ countries has minimal organizational resilience against a prolonged crypto downturn.
Key Catalysts
  1. Bitcoin price appreciation above $120K would drive mining equipment demand and improve self-mining economics, potentially pushing Canaan toward GAAP breakeven for the first time since 2021
  2. Self-mining hashrate expansion beyond 10 EH/s could generate increasing mining revenue at favorable bitcoin prices, with Q4 2025 mining revenue already reaching $30.4M quarterly [1]
  3. Next-generation ASIC chip release with improved energy efficiency (joules/terahash) could capture market share from Bitmain and MicroBT if performance benchmarks are competitive
Key Risks
  1. Bitcoin price decline below $60K would simultaneously crush mining equipment demand and self-mining profitability, potentially triggering another revenue collapse similar to the 70% drop from 2022 to 2023
  2. Persistent unprofitability — the company has not achieved consistent GAAP profitability in recent years despite revenue recovery, and forward estimates project continued losses through 2026
  3. NASDAQ delisting risk — at $0.44/share, Canaan faces compliance risk with NASDAQ's minimum bid price requirement, which could force a reverse stock split or delisting
