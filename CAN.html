Company Profile
Technology ·Computer Hardware ·NASDAQ ·Singapore
Canaan Inc. is a technology company focusing on ASIC chip design for bitcoin mining and self-operated bitcoin mining. The company designs, manufactures, and sells Avalon-branded bitcoin mining machines (ASIC miners), and operates its own mining facilities. Canaan also holds cryptocurrency assets including 1,870 BTC and 3,950 ETH as of May 2026. Founded in 2013, the company shipped the world's first batch of ASIC bitcoin miners. Headquartered in Singapore.

Investment Thesis
Canaan's bull case rests on bitcoin price appreciation and the company's position as one of three major ASIC mining hardware manufacturers globally. FY2025 revenue grew 97% to $530M as bitcoin prices rose, and the company accumulated 1,870 BTC and 3,950 ETH on its balance sheet (worth approximately $190M+ at current prices) [1]. Self-mining hashrate grew 34.6% YoY to 10.97 EH/s, and monthly bitcoin production of 90 BTC demonstrates operational execution. The balance sheet is clean with D/E of just 14.8%, and the $72M capital raise provides liquidity for growth. If bitcoin sustains $100K+ pricing, Canaan's ASIC hardware demand and self-mining profitability could drive a return to positive earnings.
The bear case is severe. Canaan has posted three consecutive annual net losses totaling $874M ($414M FY2023, $250M FY2024, $210M FY2025), with trailing profit margin of -41.7% and ROE of -67.9% [5]. At $0.34 per share ($257M market cap), CAN has lost over 95% of its value from its 2021 peak, reflecting structural challenges in bitcoin mining economics post-halving. The forward PE is negative (-1.5x), meaning analysts expect continued losses. Q1 2026 revenue declined 24.3% sequentially, and the company discontinued its AI semiconductor unit — eliminating its only diversification pathway beyond bitcoin mining [2]. Canaan trails Bitmain and MicroBT in ASIC efficiency, competing for third place in a consolidating market. The company's entire valuation thesis depends on bitcoin prices, which are inherently unpredictable and outside management's control. Energy cost inflation and AI data center competition for power capacity create additional headwinds for mining economics.
Key Catalysts
  1. Bitcoin price appreciation above $100K sustaining demand for Avalon ASIC miners and improving self-mining profitability, with 1,870 BTC on balance sheet providing direct price exposure
  2. Self-mining hashrate expansion beyond 10.97 EH/s through 2026-2027, with monthly production of 90 BTC compounding cryptocurrency holdings and generating operating cash flow
  3. AI semiconductor unit discontinuation reducing operating expenses by 15%, improving path to breakeven if bitcoin mining margins expand
Key Risks
  1. Three consecutive years of net losses totaling $874M with negative forward earnings expectations, creating existential risk if bitcoin prices decline or mining economics deteriorate further
  2. Structural competitive disadvantage versus Bitmain and MicroBT in ASIC chip efficiency, with Canaan's market share at risk of further erosion in a consolidating industry
  3. Total dependency on bitcoin price — the company discontinued its only diversification path (AI semiconductors), leaving no alternative revenue stream if crypto mining economics become permanently unviable

AI Risk Protection (4/9): Canaan faces indirect but meaningful AI disruption risk. However, bitcoin mining hardware is a physical product that AI cannot directly substitute.
