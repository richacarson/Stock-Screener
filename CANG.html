Investment Thesis
Cango represents a high-risk speculative bet on Bitcoin mining economics. The company generated $688.1M in revenue in FY2025 (87.9% YoY growth) driven by its rapid buildout of mining operations across four continents following its November 2024 pivot from auto financing [1]. However, this came at a staggering cost: a net loss of $452.8M and adjusted EBITDA of just $24.5M for the year. Q4 2025 recorded a $156.3M adjusted EBITDA loss as transformation costs and fair-value adjustments overwhelmed mining revenue [1]. The forward P/E of 1.97x suggests the market prices significant earnings improvement in 2026 as one-time costs fade.
The bear case is severe: the stock trades at $0.45 with minimal liquidity ($566K daily dollar volume), making institutional ownership impossible and creating significant execution risk for any position [4]. The -90.4% profit margin and -95.2% ROE indicate the business is currently destroying capital. Bitcoin price dependence creates binary risk — a sustained crypto downturn could render mining operations uneconomic given the $68,216 average cash cost per BTC reported in March 2026 [3]. The complete leadership overhaul, 96-person team operating global mining infrastructure, and absence of any competitive moat beyond scale create operational fragility. The company's pivot from auto financing to mining was opportunistic rather than strategic, and there is no evidence of sustainable competitive advantage.
Key Catalysts
  1. Bitcoin price appreciation above $80K would dramatically improve unit economics given the $68,216 average cash cost per BTC in March 2026 [3]
  2. Mining efficiency improvements — management targeting further cost reductions after 19.3% decrease in cash cost per BTC from February to March 2026 [3]
  3. Potential AI/HPC computing revenue from distributed computing pilot projects, which could diversify beyond pure Bitcoin mining [2]
Key Risks
  1. Bitcoin price decline below mining cash cost ($68,216) would make operations unprofitable and could force asset write-downs on mining infrastructure [1][3]
  2. Severe illiquidity — $566K daily dollar volume makes the stock untradeable for institutional investors and creates wide bid-ask spreads [4]
  3. Operational fragility with 96 employees managing global mining operations across 4 continents, plus complete leadership overhaul in 2025 creating execution risk [1][2]
