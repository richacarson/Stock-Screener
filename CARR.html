Company Profile
Industrials ·Building Products & Equipment ·NYSE ·United States
Carrier Global Corporation is a global leader in intelligent climate and energy solutions, operating across HVAC, refrigeration, and building automation. The company serves commercial, residential, and data center markets with heating, cooling, and refrigeration products under brands including Carrier, Toshiba, and Viessmann. After divesting fire & security and acquiring Viessmann Climate Solutions for $13.3B, Carrier is transforming into a pure-play climate technology company. Data center cooling revenue crossed $1B for the first time in Q1 2026 with orders up 500%.

Investment Thesis
Carrier Global is transforming into a pure-play climate technology leader positioned at the intersection of two powerful secular tailwinds: AI data center infrastructure and the global energy transition [1]. Data center cooling orders surged 500% in Q1 2026 with revenue crossing $1B for the first time, and the company entered 2026 with a $1B data center backlog targeting $1.5B in segment revenue — a 50% growth rate [1]. Commercial HVAC is on track for its sixth consecutive year of double-digit growth. The $13.3B Viessmann acquisition positions Carrier as the European heat pump leader. Q1 2026 results beat estimates on both revenue ($5.34B, +$326M above consensus) and adjusted EPS ($0.57, +12.1% beat), and full-year adjusted EPS guidance of $2.80 was reaffirmed [1]. CEO Gitlin has delivered 330% total shareholder return since the 2020 spin-off, validating the strategic vision [2].
The bear case centers on valuation stretch, residential HVAC weakness, and integration execution. The stock has risen 30% from $54.25 to $70.75 since March 2026, pushing the forward P/E to 25.25x and PEG to 2.69 — premium pricing that leaves limited margin of safety [5]. GAAP operating margin declined to 4.8% from 12.1% in Q1 2026, and profit margin of 5.99% is thin for an industrial leader during a portfolio transformation [1]. Americas residential HVAC volumes remain depressed with recovery timing uncertain. The $13.3B Viessmann acquisition carries ongoing integration risk with D/E of 91% limiting financial flexibility. European heat pump demand is subject to regulatory uncertainty and natural gas price volatility. The data center cooling thesis depends on sustained AI capex spending — any hyperscaler budget discipline could impact the $1.5B target. Glassdoor reviews (3.5/5) flag 2026 layoff concerns and cultural challenges during restructuring [4].
Key Catalysts
  1. Data center cooling revenue projected to grow 50% from $1B to $1.5B in 2026, driven by AI infrastructure buildout with Q1 2026 orders surging 500% — this segment is becoming Carrier's fastest-growing business [1]
