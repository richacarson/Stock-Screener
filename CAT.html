Investment Thesis
Caterpillar is uniquely positioned at the intersection of infrastructure spending and AI-driven demand, with record revenue of $67.6 billion, a $51 billion backlog, and Power Generation sales exceeding $10 billion (30%+ growth) driven by data center buildouts. The company's 800+ autonomous mining trucks and NVIDIA partnership for AI-powered construction equipment create deep competitive moats. With 7% guided sales growth for 2026 and a services revenue target of $30 billion by 2030, CAT offers compelling long-term growth supported by $5.8 billion in annual free cash flow.
The primary risks center on the $2.6 billion in expected tariff costs for 2026, which compress margins even as revenue grows. The forward P/E of 24.6x is premium for a cyclical industrial, leaving limited margin of safety if construction or mining demand softens. The 207% debt-to-equity ratio is elevated, and the MSCI ESG index removal highlights ongoing governance and environmental scrutiny. EPS declined 11.4% year-over-year in 2025 despite revenue growth, raising questions about margin sustainability amid inflationary cost pressures.
