Company Profile
Healthcare ·Biotechnology ·NASDAQ ·United States
C4 Therapeutics is a clinical-stage biopharmaceutical company developing novel targeted protein degradation therapeutics. Its lead candidate Cemsidomide targets IKZF1/3 for multiple myeloma, and it is advancing CFT8919 for EGFR L858R-mutant non-small cell lung cancer. The company has strategic collaborations with Roche (degrader-antibody conjugates), Betta Pharmaceuticals, and Merck KGaA.

Investment Thesis
C4 Therapeutics is a clinical-stage protein degradation company with validated platform technology, evidenced by the $1B+ Roche degrader-antibody conjugate collaboration signed in April 2026 [2]. Lead candidate Cemsidomide is in Phase 2 (NEXUS study) targeting multiple myeloma with analyst estimates of $2.5-4B peak revenue potential in combination with BCMA BiTEs, and CFT8919 addresses EGFR L858R NSCLC [1]. The company's $248.5M cash position provides roughly 2+ years of runway, and the low D/E of 23.4% reflects conservative capital management for a clinical-stage company [3]. Revenue of $35.9M (up 112.8% YoY) is driven by collaboration milestones rather than product sales.
The bear case is binary clinical risk: Cemsidomide Phase 2 failure would eliminate the primary value driver, and the company burns ~$99M annually in operating cash with no path to profitability without clinical success [3]. At $285M market cap, the stock prices in significant platform optionality but remains highly speculative. Competition in the protein degradation space from Arvinas (ARV-471 approved), Kymera, and larger pharma entrants could compress C4's competitive positioning. The volatile collaboration revenue ($20.8M in 2023, $35.6M in 2024, $35.9M in 2025) depends on partner milestones that C4 cannot control. With only 104 employees, key-person risk is elevated — loss of scientific leadership could derail programs [4].
