Coca-Cola Europacific Partners is the world's largest Coca-Cola bottler by revenue, producing, distributing, and selling Coca-Cola branded and other non-alcoholic beverages across Western Europe, Australia, the Pacific, and Indonesia. The company serves approximately 600 million consumers across 31 countries with brands including Coca-Cola, Fanta, Sprite, Monster Energy, and Costa Coffee.

Investment Thesis
Coca-Cola Europacific Partners is the world's largest Coca-Cola bottler, offering defensive consumer staples exposure with improving profitability and growing shareholder returns. 2025 revenue of $20.9B grew 2.3% with earnings surging 37% to $1.94B, reflecting successful margin expansion from the Amatil integration [1]. 2026 guidance targets 3-4% revenue growth with 7% operating profit growth, at least EUR 1.7B free cash flow, and an additional EUR 1B share buyback [1]. At 16.7x forward earnings with a 2.45% dividend yield growing at 18.4% CAGR over five years, CCEP offers an attractive total return profile for a defensive compounder. ROE of 22.9% demonstrates efficient capital allocation, and the exclusive Coca-Cola bottling agreements across 31 countries create a durable competitive moat with predictable cash flows. The MSCI AA ESG rating and CDP A List status attract ESG-focused capital (25%+ of float) [5].
The bear case centers on low organic growth and commodity exposure. Revenue growth of 2.3% in 2025 and 3-4% guidance for 2026 falls well below the company's own midterm 7% target, suggesting consumer volume headwinds in mature European markets from health-conscious consumption trends and sugar taxes [1]. D/E of 130% reflects Amatil acquisition leverage that constrains financial flexibility and increases interest expense sensitivity. Profit margin of 9.3% is structurally limited by the bottling model's high raw material costs (sugar, PET plastic, aluminum) and energy-intensive manufacturing — input cost inflation directly compresses margins. The PEG ratio of 2.79 suggests the stock may be overvalued relative to its modest growth trajectory. Currency risk is significant with operations across 31 countries and earnings reported in euros but stock priced in dollars. The alcohol-ready-to-drink category expansion (Jack Daniel's & Coca-Cola RTD) diversifies revenue but creates regulatory and brand risk [6].
Key Catalysts
  1. Operating profit growth of 7% in 2026 outpacing 3-4% revenue growth, demonstrating continued margin expansion from Amatil integration synergies and operational efficiency gains [1]
  2. EUR 1B share buyback program in 2026 reducing share count and boosting per-share metrics, on top of the 2.45% dividend yield with 18.4% 5-year dividend growth CAGR [1]
  3. Asia-Pacific volume growth in Indonesia and Australia providing a higher-growth offset to mature European markets as the Amatil integration matures [1]
Key Risks
  1. Secular health-conscious consumer trends and sugar taxes in European markets pressuring core carbonated soft drink volumes, with 2025 revenue growth of just 2.3% already below the midterm 7% target [1]
  2. Raw material and energy cost inflation — sugar, PET plastic, aluminum, and energy prices directly compress the 9.3% profit margin with limited ability to pass through fully to retailers [6]
  3. Currency translation risk across 31 countries with EUR-reported earnings and USD-priced stock creating earnings volatility from FX movements beyond management control [6]

AI Resilience (25%)
AI Resilience LOW RISK
8
AI Risk Protection (7/9): Beverage bottling and distribution is a physical-world logistics operation that AI structurally cannot displace — manufacturing, warehousing, and delivering billions of servings of beverages across 31 countries requires massive physical infrastructure, cold-chain logistics, local regulatory compliance, and deeply embedded retail relationships [1]. The exclusive bottling agreements with The Coca-Cola Company create contractual moats that AI cannot circumvent. Consumer beverage demand is non-discretionary and AI-immune — people will continue drinking Coca-Cola regardless of AI advances. AI may actually improve CCEP's operations through route optimization, demand forecasting, and production scheduling. AI Opportunity (1/1): CCEP generates measurable AI-driven value through AI-powered procurement analytics spanning its complete supply chain, an AI-driven talent platform for 41,000 employees, and AI-based demand forecasting systems across 31 markets [3].
