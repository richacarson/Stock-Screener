Company Profile
Consumer Cyclical ·Restaurants ·NASDAQ ·Malaysia
CCH Holdings Ltd operates specialty hotpot restaurant chains in Malaysia, primarily under the Chicken Claypot House and Zi Wei Yuan brands offering chicken hotpot and fish head hotpot. The company also operates or licenses outlets under 888 Family Food Court, Bibixian, Banbudian Bistro, and Chao Liu Xian Hotpot brands, and sells food ingredients, condiments, and franchise licenses. Founded in 2015 in Bukit Mertajam, Malaysia, IPO'd on Nasdaq in October 2025 at $4.00/share, with approximately 30 total outlets and a market cap of approximately $15 million.

Investment Thesis
CCH Holdings is the largest chicken hotpot brand in Malaysia by outlet count with 30 outlets across multiple brands, and the Nasdaq listing provides access to international capital markets for expansion [1]. At $0.49 per share with a $15M market cap, the stock prices in severe distress — any successful execution on the announced acquisition strategy or international expansion could generate outsized percentage returns from this depressed base. Revenue grew 10.1% YoY to $9.6M in 2025, and the appointment of Co-CEO Mhlengi Prevail Mafu with AI transformation and African market experience could open new growth vectors [2].
The bear case is overwhelming and existential. At $685K cash against $5M debt, CCH Holdings faces immediate liquidity risk — Nasdaq issued a non-compliance notice in February 2026 for minimum bid price, and the stock trades at $0.49, down 88% from IPO [2][3]. The founding CEO departed in March 2026 after just 5 months as a public company, and the replacement leadership team lacks public company experience in U.S. capital markets. Revenue stagnated at approximately $9.6M over three years (2-year CAGR of -1.0%), ROE is -35%, profit margin is -28%, and the company swung from profitability to loss in H1 2025 [1][3]. Plans to expand to the U.S. and Africa with a $15M market cap and 207 employees are aspirational to the point of being unrealistic. Nasdaq delisting is a material near-term risk that would effectively eliminate institutional investability.
