Company Profile
Energy ·Uranium ·NYSE ·Canada
Cameco is one of the world's largest uranium producers, accounting for approximately 15% of global output. The company operates across the full nuclear fuel cycle through three segments: Uranium (mining and milling), Fuel Services (refining and conversion), and Westinghouse (reactor technology and plant services). Cameco is headquartered in Saskatoon, Canada and serves nuclear utilities globally.

Investment Thesis
Cameco is the Western world's premier full-cycle nuclear company, uniquely positioned to benefit from a structural nuclear renaissance driven by AI data center electricity demand, energy security concerns, and decarbonization mandates. The $80B+ Westinghouse-Brookfield-U.S. government partnership provides decades of reactor construction visibility, while Cameco's 15% share of global uranium production benefits from a tightening supply-demand balance with 3.1 billion pounds of uranium demand uncontracted through 2045 [1][2]. FY2025 revenue of $3.48B CAD with $480M FCF and a fortress balance sheet ($1.21B cash, 14.7% D/E) provide financial durability. Consensus expects 52% EPS growth in 2026 as Westinghouse synergies and rising uranium prices flow through [1].
The bear case centers on extreme valuation and execution risk. At 60.9x forward earnings and 114.6x trailing P/E, Cameco is priced for a nuclear renaissance that must actually materialize over the next decade [4]. The Westinghouse integration adds complexity — the resignation of Westinghouse's President and CEO creates leadership uncertainty at a critical subsidiary [2]. Revenue grew only 1.5% YoY in 2025, and 2026 guidance of CAD $3.13-3.37B implies potential revenue decline, suggesting the uranium price cycle may be plateauing near term [1]. Geopolitical risk is significant: Russian enrichment sanctions, Kazakhstan production uncertainties, and trade war tariffs could disrupt uranium supply chains in unpredictable ways. New reactor construction timelines are measured in decades, creating a long duration between investment thesis and cash flow realization. Uranium price volatility could compress margins if spot prices correct from current elevated levels [1].
