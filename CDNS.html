Company Profile
Technology ·Software - Application ·NASDAQ ·United States
Cadence Design Systems is one of two dominant providers (with Synopsys) of electronic design automation (EDA) software, semiconductor IP, and system design tools used by every major chipmaker, hyperscaler, and systems company in the world. Cadence's tools are mission-critical to designing AI accelerators, GPUs, ASICs, and increasingly to physical AI applications in robotics, automotive, and aerospace under its 'Intelligent System Design' strategy.

Investment Thesis
Cadence is one of two dominant EDA franchises (with Synopsys) without which no modern AI chip can be designed. FY2025 revenue of $5.3B grew 14% YoY with a 45% non-GAAP operating margin, and the company ended the year with a record $7.8B backlog — ~1.5x trailing revenue, providing exceptional visibility into 2026 and beyond [1]. The IP segment grew ~25% in 2025 driven by AI/HPC/automotive demand, and the new 'three-layer cake' AI strategy plus the Joint Enterprise AI partnership with NVIDIA are accelerating customer adoption of Cerebrus, Verisium, and ChipStack Super Agent [1][2]. Devgan (CEO since 2021) has compounded shareholder value at 327% over five years and has been appointed to the Lam Research board, signaling industry validation [2]. With ~30% global EDA share in a structural duopoly (~61% combined with Synopsys) and AI workloads driving more chip starts and more design complexity per chip, Cadence has multi-year revenue visibility under almost any AI scenario [3].
The bear case rests on valuation, China exposure, and competitive parity. At forward P/E of 30.6 and PEG of ~2.1, Cadence prices in continued mid-teens revenue growth and 45%+ operating margins; any deceleration in chip-design starts or hardware emulation would compress the multiple meaningfully. China was historically ~12-15% of EDA revenue and has become a pinch point given U.S. export-control rules that limit which Chinese customers and product nodes Cadence can serve [3]. Synopsys's Ansys acquisition arguably gives that competitor a stronger system-analysis story, and customers increasingly negotiate for combined tool flows, putting pricing pressure on both vendors. The recurring-revenue mix means quarterly revenue can be lumpy as hardware (Palladium) ships, creating headline volatility. P/E of 71x trailing reflects elevated expectations.
Key Catalysts
  1. $7.8B record backlog (~1.5x revenue) plus the Joint Enterprise AI partnership with NVIDIA driving 2026 revenue toward the $5.9B-$6B guidance and beyond [1][2]
  2. IP business growth (~25% in 2025) accelerating as hyperscalers, automotive OEMs, and AI startups adopt Cadence IP for custom silicon programs [1]
  3. Physical AI expansion — Cadence's tools moving into robotics, autonomous vehicles, and drones extends the addressable market beyond traditional chip design [2]
Key Risks
  1. Valuation — forward P/E of 30.6 and PEG of ~2.1 leave little margin for error; any deceleration in chip starts or AI capex would compress the multiple
  2. China export-control risk — ~12-15% of revenue subject to evolving U.S. rules limiting which customers and product nodes Cadence can serve [3]
  3. Synopsys-Ansys competitive dynamic — the combined Synopsys-Ansys offering may pressure Cadence's system-analysis pricing and accelerate consolidation pressure [3]
