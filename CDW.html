Investment Thesis
CDW is a recovering IT cycle play trading at 12.5x forward earnings, with 2025 revenue of $22.42B (up 6.8% YoY) and Q4 gross margin expanding 50bps to 22.8% as the company benefits from the enterprise AI infrastructure buildout [1]. The company generates $920M in annual free cash flow, maintains a 43.0% ROE, and has a 11-year consecutive dividend growth streak with a conservative 31% payout ratio [4]. CDW's strategic pivot from hardware reselling to AI advisory, cloud solutions, and managed services positions it to capture the multi-year enterprise AI spending cycle. CEO Chris Leahy's early AI investment (2020 digital transformation, 10,000 Copilot deployments) and the 2025 CDW AI Report demonstrate thought leadership in helping mid-market customers navigate AI adoption [2]. At 1.33 PEG, the stock is reasonably valued for a company targeting 200-300bps of IT market outgrowth in 2026.
The bear case centers on leverage, thin margins, and secular disruption risk. D/E of 236.9% with $6.17B total debt limits balance sheet flexibility and creates interest rate sensitivity [4]. Profit margin of 4.8% — while typical for IT distribution — leaves minimal buffer for revenue shortfalls, and a negative 3-year revenue CAGR of -1.9% (from $23.75B in 2022 to $22.42B in 2025) shows the business contracted during the recent IT spending downturn. CDW's reseller model faces structural risk as procurement AI tools could automate product selection, cloud migration reduces on-premise hardware demand, and OEMs increasingly sell direct to large enterprises. Employee sentiment is declining — Glassdoor ratings dropped 4% in the past year with concerns about layoffs, outsourcing, and prioritization of stockholder interests over employee wellbeing [2]. Revenue growth guidance for 2026 remains modest (mid-single-digit), and the company has yet to demonstrate the double-digit growth that would justify a premium re-rating [1].
Key Catalysts
  1. Enterprise AI infrastructure spending cycle — CDW's AI solutions practice, GPU server deployments, and advisory services position the company to capture a meaningful share of the multi-year enterprise AI hardware and software buildout [2]
  2. Gross margin expansion — the pivot from commodity hardware reselling to higher-margin solutions, advisory, and managed services is driving margin improvement (Q4 gross margin +50bps to 22.8%), with potential for continued expansion [1]
  3. IT spending recovery — with the 2023-2024 downturn creating pent-up demand and enterprises needing to modernize infrastructure for AI workloads, CDW could see revenue acceleration above the current mid-single-digit trajectory [1]
Key Risks
  1. AI-driven disintermediation — procurement AI tools and vendor-direct sales could structurally reduce the need for IT resellers/distributors, threatening CDW's core business model over the medium term [2]
  2. High leverage vulnerability — D/E of 236.9% with $6.17B debt creates interest rate risk and limits strategic flexibility for acquisitions or investments during an IT market recovery [4]
  3. Revenue stagnation risk — negative 3-year revenue CAGR of -1.9% and only 6.3% YoY growth in 2025 suggest CDW may be losing relevance in an IT market shifting toward cloud, SaaS, and direct vendor relationships [1]
