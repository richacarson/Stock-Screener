Company Profile
Healthcare ·Medical Devices ·NASDAQ ·United States
Cerus Corporation develops and commercializes the INTERCEPT Blood System, a proprietary pathogen reduction technology designed to enhance blood safety by inactivating blood-borne pathogens in donated blood components intended for transfusion. Products include INTERCEPT systems for platelets, plasma, cryoprecipitation, and red blood cells, sold globally through direct sales and distributors.

Investment Thesis
Cerus is approaching a meaningful profitability inflection after 30+ years of development investment, with 2025 product revenue of $206M (up 14% YoY), seven consecutive quarters of positive adjusted EBITDA ($9.5M for full year), and $2.5M in free cash flow — the company is transitioning from cash-burning R&D firm to self-sustaining commercial enterprise [1]. The INTERCEPT Blood System is the category leader in pathogen reduction, an underadopted technology with significant room for market expansion as more blood centers adopt pathogen-reduced components. 2026 product revenue guidance of $224-228M (9-11% growth) with IFC contributing $20-22M provides visibility into continued growth, and the red blood cell system represents a significant additional market opportunity [1]. At a $373M market cap and 54.5% gross margins, the stock offers compelling value if GAAP profitability arrives.
The bear case centers on the company's persistent inability to achieve GAAP profitability despite 30+ years of operations and $206M in revenue. GAAP net loss was $15.6M in 2025, ROE is negative 25.7%, and the stock trades at $1.94 — down 34% from its 52-week high of $2.96 [6]. D/E of 149% with $97M debt and $83M cash creates meaningful leverage risk for a company that is still GAAP-unprofitable. Revenue growth is decelerating from 16% in 2025 to guided 9-11% in 2026 [1]. The CEO transition from Greenman to Jayaraman in July 2026 introduces leadership uncertainty at a critical juncture. The single-platform INTERCEPT dependence means any regulatory issue, competitive technology, or market adoption slowdown could severely impact the entire business [2]. At $1.94, the stock has limited margin of safety if the profitability timeline extends further.

AI Resilience (25%)
AI Resilience LOW RISK
8
AI Risk Protection (8/9): Blood safety and pathogen reduction is a deeply physical, biochemistry-based process that AI cannot substitute — you cannot digitize the inactivation of blood-borne pathogens in donated platelets, plasma, or red blood cells [1]. The INTERCEPT system uses photochemistry (amotosalen/UVA light) to crosslink nucleic acids and inactivate pathogens; this is physical product intervention that requires actual devices and chemistry at the point of blood processing. Blood banking is heavily regulated by the FDA and European authorities, creating structural regulatory moats. AI cannot replace the need for pathogen-reduced blood components in surgical and trauma settings. AI Opportunity (0/1): No evidence of AI-generated revenue; Cerus's technology platform is based on photochemistry, not AI [2].
