The Carlyle Group Inc. is a global alternative asset management firm with $477 billion in total AUM across four segments: Corporate Private Equity, Real Assets, Global Credit, and Solutions. Investment Thesis
Carlyle Group is undergoing a transformation under CEO Harvey Schwartz that is producing tangible results: record FRE of $1.24B (12% organic growth), FRE margin of 47% (all-time high), fee-earning AUM of $337B (up 11%), and total AUM of $477B [1]. At 9.0x forward P/E with $5.35 forward EPS, CG trades at a significant discount to peers despite improving fundamentals. The February 2026 Shareholder Update set specific three-year targets — $1.9B+ FRE, $200B+ inflows, $6.00+ DE/share by 2028 — backed by a $2B share repurchase authorization [3]. The 2.9% dividend yield and diversifying revenue streams (credit, solutions, insurance) create a compelling risk/reward for patient investors.
The bear case focuses on earnings cyclicality, leverage, and competitive positioning. Revenue swung from $3.4B (2024) to $1.3B (2023) to $3.4B (2024) again, with a 5-year CAGR of -1.4%, underscoring how performance fees create volatile earnings streams [5]. D/E of 197% reflects $13.9B in balance sheet debt that amplifies downside in a credit cycle downturn. The payout ratio of 64% limits dividend growth flexibility — the dividend was held flat at $1.40 for two consecutive years after being cut in 2020. Carlyle remains the smallest of the four major publicly traded alternative managers (behind BX, KKR, APO), creating a scale disadvantage in fundraising and deal sourcing. Schwartz's restructuring is producing results, but the PE industry faces secular pressure from LP fee compression and increased competition from sovereign wealth funds investing directly [1][5].
Key Catalysts
  1. Execution on three-year targets: $1.9B+ FRE and $200B+ inflows by 2028 would demonstrate sustainable platform growth and likely drive multiple expansion toward peers [3]
  2. Credit platform expansion — Global Credit AUM grew 10% in 2025 and is positioned to benefit from bank disintermediation and CLO market growth, providing more durable fee streams [1]
  3. Share repurchase acceleration — $2B authorization at a 9x forward P/E represents highly accretive capital return that compounds distributable earnings per share [3]
Key Risks
  1. Earnings cyclicality — realization-dependent revenue swung from $3.4B to $1.3B to $3.4B over three years; a PE exit drought would collapse distributable earnings [5]
  2. Balance sheet leverage of 197% D/E ($13.9B debt) amplifies risk in a credit cycle downturn, particularly for the balance sheet investment portfolio [5]
  3. Scale disadvantage versus Blackstone, KKR, and Apollo in fundraising, deal sourcing, and talent retention as the alternative asset industry consolidates around larger platforms [1]
