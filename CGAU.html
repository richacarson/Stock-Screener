Company Profile
Basic Materials ·Gold ·NYSE ·Canada
Centerra Gold engages in the operation, development, exploration, and acquisition of gold and copper properties in North America, Turkey, and internationally. Principal assets include the Mount Milligan gold-copper mine in British Columbia, the Öksüt gold mine in Turkey, the Kemess project, and molybdenum operations including the Thompson Creek and Endako mines.

Investment Thesis
Centerra Gold is a mid-tier miner executing a strong operational turnaround with exceptional financial metrics — $1.38B revenue (up 14% YoY), 42% profit margins, 31% ROE, and a near-zero debt balance sheet with $540M cash [1][5]. Gold prices near $4,450/oz and copper at $5.80/lb create a highly favorable pricing environment, while the Kemess project ($1.1B NPV, 16% IRR) provides a clear self-funded growth catalyst without dilution [1]. At 6.8x trailing P/E and 9.4x forward P/E, the stock trades at a significant discount to gold mining peers despite superior margins and growth profile. Scotiabank recently raised FY2026 EPS estimates to $1.81, reflecting confidence in sustained execution [1].
The bear case centers on commodity price dependence and geopolitical risk. Gold and copper prices are cyclical — a meaningful pullback from current highs would compress margins and free cash flow despite operational excellence. Centerra's Öksüt mine in Turkey carries geopolitical risk given regional instability, and the company still bears reputational scars from the 2021 Kumtor mine seizure by Kyrgyzstan. The Kemess project requires $1.1B in development capital over several years with execution risk on permitting, construction timelines, and cost overruns typical of large mining projects. Free cash flow was negative $8.3M in the most recent period despite strong revenue, suggesting heavy capital investment that may pressure near-term returns [5]. Insider selling of $1.7M in the last 3 months is a cautionary signal [4].
Key Catalysts
  1. Gold and copper price strength — gold near $4,450/oz and copper at $5.80/lb provide significant margin tailwinds, with AI data center buildout driving sustained copper demand [1]
  2. Kemess project development — $1.1B NPV and 16% IRR provide a transformative growth asset that could significantly expand Centerra's production profile over the next 3-5 years [1]
  3. Goldfield District project advancement in Nevada adds further optionality to the pipeline with U.S.-based operations reducing geopolitical risk concentration [1]
Key Risks
  1. Commodity price cyclicality — a correction in gold or copper prices from current highs would compress margins on a business with 100% commodity exposure and no hedging visibility [1][5]
  2. Geopolitical risk at Öksüt (Turkey) and residual Kumtor overhang — operational disruption from political instability or regulatory changes in foreign jurisdictions [1]
  3. Kemess execution risk — the project requires multi-billion dollar development capital over several years with permitting, construction, and cost overrun risks typical of large greenfield mining developments [1]
