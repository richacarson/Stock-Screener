Company Profile

Cognex Corporation designs, develops, manufactures, and markets machine vision products used to automate manufacturing and distribution tasks worldwide. Its products capture and analyze visual information to locate, identify, inspect, and measure discrete items in industries including automotive, logistics, consumer electronics, and semiconductor manufacturing.

Investment Thesis
Cognex is the global leader in industrial machine vision, recovering from a cyclical trough with Q4 2025 revenue of $252M (up 9.9% YoY) beating analyst estimates by $13M, driving shares up 48.6% [1]. CEO Moschner's 'AI-First' strategy positions Cognex at the intersection of manufacturing automation and AI, with the OneVision cloud platform transforming the business from hardware sales to AI-as-a-service [2]. Management targets 25-31% adjusted EBITDA margins by late 2026, a significant improvement from the current 11.5% net margin, through product line exits and operational discipline [1]. The fortress balance sheet (D/E 5.1%, $337M cash) provides flexibility for R&D investment and the $500M buyback authorization [1][5]. The plan to double the served customer base over five years suggests a long runway for growth in logistics, automotive, and semiconductor end markets.
The bear case centers on valuation and cyclical risk. At 35.5x forward earnings and 80x trailing earnings, Cognex is pricing in a dramatic margin expansion and growth acceleration that has yet to materialize [5]. ROE of 7.6% is well below cost of capital, and the 5yr revenue CAGR of only 4.2% reflects significant cyclicality — revenue peaked at $1.006B in 2022, fell 17% to $838M in 2023, and has only recovered to $994M [5]. The semiconductor and automotive end markets remain cyclically sensitive, and tariff escalation could disrupt global manufacturing investment that drives Cognex's order book. Cloud-based AI vision tools from hyperscalers pose a long-tail disruption risk to Cognex's proprietary edge models. The new leadership team, while internally promoted, is untested in executing the ambitious AI-First transformation and margin targets simultaneously [2].
Key Catalysts
  1. EBITDA margin expansion to 25-31% target by late 2026 through $22M product line exits and operational restructuring, potentially doubling profitability [1]
  2. OneVision cloud AI platform adoption accelerating the shift to recurring revenue and enabling non-expert customers to deploy machine vision [2]
  3. Manufacturing capex recovery cycle driving order growth across automotive, semiconductor, and logistics end markets as AI-driven automation demand increases [2]
Key Risks
  1. Extreme valuation at 80x trailing and 35.5x forward earnings requires flawless execution on margin targets and growth acceleration to avoid multiple compression [5]
  2. Cyclical revenue vulnerability — revenue fell 17% from 2022 peak to 2023 trough, and global manufacturing capex remains sensitive to tariffs and trade disruption [5]
  3. Cloud AI competition — hyperscaler vision APIs from AWS, Google, and Azure could erode Cognex's traditional on-premise moat in lower-complexity machine vision applications [2]
