Company Profile
Healthcare ·Biotechnology ·NASDAQ ·United States
CG Oncology is a late-stage clinical biopharmaceutical company focused on developing and commercializing cretostimogene grenadenorepvec, an investigational oncolytic immunotherapy for patients with bladder cancer. The company has multiple Phase 3 trials in non-muscle invasive bladder cancer (NMIBC), holds FDA Fast Track and Breakthrough Therapy Designations, and initiated a rolling BLA submission in 2026.
142 Employees ·cgoncology.com

Investment Thesis
CG Oncology represents a compelling late-stage biotech opportunity with cretostimogene advancing toward FDA approval for bladder cancer with Breakthrough Therapy and Fast Track Designations [1]. The company initiated a rolling BLA submission in 2026 with completion expected this year, and PIVOT-006 Phase 3 topline data in intermediate-risk NMIBC is expected H1 2026 — nearly a year ahead of schedule. With $903M in cash providing runway through H1 2029, the company is exceptionally well-funded to execute through BLA completion, potential approval, and commercial launch without dilution. The addressable market in NMIBC is estimated at $3B+ peak sales, and cretostimogene's bladder-sparing approach addresses a devastating unmet need where the alternative is radical cystectomy [1].
The bear case centers on the binary nature of regulatory outcomes and the significant valuation premium already embedded in the $5.8B market cap. While Phase 2 data has been encouraging, Phase 3 trials can produce unexpected results — and any failure in the PIVOT-006 or BOND-003 programs would likely result in a catastrophic stock decline given the single-asset structure [1]. The net loss widened to $161M in 2025 with R&D expenses of $116.6M and G&A of $73.5M, reflecting the cost of running four concurrent Phase 3 programs [1]. Competition from Ferring's nadofaragene firadenovec (Adstiladrin, already FDA-approved for BCG-unresponsive NMIBC) and emerging therapies could limit cretostimogene's market share even with positive data. The February 2026 ATM equity raise, while extending runway, signals that the company may need additional capital for commercialization [1].
Key Catalysts
  1. PIVOT-006 Phase 3 topline data in intermediate-risk NMIBC expected H1 2026 — accelerated nearly a year ahead of schedule, representing the potentially largest market expansion opportunity
  2. Rolling BLA submission completion expected in 2026 for BCG-unresponsive NMIBC, with potential FDA approval in 2027 given Breakthrough Therapy Designation and Fast Track status
  3. CORE-008 Cohort CX Phase 2 combination data (cretostimogene + gemcitabine) expected H1 2026 could demonstrate enhanced efficacy and differentiate from competitors in high-risk NMIBC
Key Risks
  1. Binary Phase 3 trial risk across four concurrent programs — a single pivotal trial failure could eliminate the regulatory pathway for a specific indication and significantly impair the company's valuation
  2. Ferring's Adstiladrin already holds FDA approval for BCG-unresponsive NMIBC, establishing a first-mover advantage that cretostimogene must overcome with superior efficacy or safety data
  3. Net loss widening to $161M in 2025 with $190M in combined R&D and G&A expenses — commercialization costs for a bladder cancer launch would require significant additional investment beyond the current $903M cash position
