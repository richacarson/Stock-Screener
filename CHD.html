Investment Thesis
Church & Dwight delivered $6.2B in 2025 revenue with adjusted EPS of $3.53, gaining market share in 12 of 17 power brands despite a challenging consumer environment [1]. The 2026 outlook calls for 3-4% organic sales growth and 5-8% adjusted EPS growth, supported by 100bps gross margin expansion and continued acquisition-driven growth [1]. The balance sheet is healthy with D/E of 60%, and the dividend track record of 28 consecutive years of growth with a sustainable 39% payout ratio makes CHD a reliable income compounder. The appointment of Chief Technology & Analytics Officer signals commitment to AI-driven marketing efficiency [3].
The bear case centers on valuation and growth deceleration. At 26.3x forward earnings and PEG of 3.66, CHD is priced as a premium compounder despite delivering only 1.6% revenue growth in 2025 and 2.6% EPS growth [1]. Organic sales growth of 0.7% in 2025 (excluding VMS exit impact) reveals tepid underlying demand in mature CPG categories. The 1.24% dividend yield, while growing, is below the 2% threshold for pure income investors. Competition from private label and smaller DTC brands continues to pressure shelf space in a deflationary consumer environment. The CEO transition introduces execution risk as Dierker establishes his strategic priorities [3].
Key Catalysts
  1. 3-4% organic sales growth guidance for 2026 driven by new product launches across OxiClean, Arm & Hammer, and international expansion, with 100bps gross margin expansion from productivity initiatives
  2. AI-driven marketing and analytics capabilities under new Chief Technology & Analytics Officer could improve media ROI and accelerate digital commerce growth across the brand portfolio
  3. Continued bolt-on acquisition strategy targeting high-growth personal care and household brands to supplement low-single-digit organic growth
Key Risks
  1. Premium valuation at 26.3x forward P/E and PEG of 3.66 leaves minimal margin of safety against any organic growth shortfall below the 3-4% 2026 guidance
  2. Secular category deceleration in mature CPG segments (laundry, personal care) as consumers shift toward private label and value alternatives in an inflationary environment
  3. CEO transition from Matthew Farrell to Richard Dierker introduces strategic execution risk as the new leader sets priorities for acquisition pipeline and organic growth initiatives
