Investment Thesis
Church & Dwight is a well-managed consumer staples company with 28 consecutive years of dividend increases, a healthy 59.5% D/E ratio, and a +56 Inspire score with zero negative attributions for faith-aligned investors. FY2025 net income grew 25.9% to $737M, exceeding management's own outlook, and FY2026 guidance of 18-22% reported EPS growth signals continued momentum. The digital transformation (82% of marketing spend digital, 21.4% online sales) positions CHD ahead of consumer staples peers in modern retail. The newly hired CTO from Kimberly-Clark and Simporter AI partnership create data-driven product innovation capabilities. The 39% payout ratio provides significant dividend growth runway.
The bear case centers on growth limitations and premium valuation. At 31.4x trailing P/E and 23.5x forward P/E, CHD is priced at a premium for a company with 3.9% revenue growth and 11.9% profit margins. The $400M in business exits will create revenue headwinds in FY2026 that may mask underlying organic growth. GAAP EPS declined 20.8% YoY due to business exit costs, and the 1.3% dividend yield is among the lowest in this screen — barely competitive with Treasury yields. Core categories like baking soda and household cleaning face private label competition from Costco and Amazon, and the relatively small 5,550-person workforce limits scale advantages versus P&G or Unilever. TOUCHLAND integration adds execution risk to a company without extensive M&A experience.
Key Catalysts
  1. FY2026 reported EPS growth of 18-22% as business exit costs from FY2025 roll off, with second-half weighted EPS acceleration providing positive momentum for valuation re-rating
  2. TOUCHLAND brand integration and scaling through CHD's distribution infrastructure, adding a Gen-Z-focused personal care brand to the portfolio and reaching new consumer demographics
  3. New CTO driving AI-powered product innovation pipeline through the Simporter partnership, potentially accelerating product launches and reducing R&D cycle times
Key Risks
  1. Revenue headwinds from $400M in business exits may offset organic growth in FY2026, making it difficult to demonstrate top-line momentum to investors focused on growth rates
  2. Private label competitive pressure — retailer brands from Costco, Amazon, and Walmart are increasingly competitive in baking soda, cleaning, and personal care categories, potentially eroding pricing power over time
  3. Premium valuation vulnerability — at 31.4x trailing P/E, any earnings miss or guidance reduction could trigger significant multiple compression, particularly given the modest 1.3% dividend yield that provides limited downside support
