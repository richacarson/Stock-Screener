Investment Thesis
ChowChow Cloud delivered strong H1 2025 results with revenue surging 81.3% YoY to US$22.8 million and net income of US$1.6 million, driven by three new cloud CDN and server farm customers contributing HK$83.5 million [1]. The company is expanding across Asia-Pacific with operations in seven countries (Hong Kong, Singapore, Philippines, Macau, Japan, Malaysia, Indonesia) and offers a growing AI-powered cloud managed services portfolio. With minimal debt (D/E 6.94%) and a historically strong 3-year revenue CAGR of 32.6%, the company has demonstrated an ability to scale its cloud consulting and infrastructure business.
The bear case is substantial. ChowChow Cloud is a $15 million market cap company with only 17 employees, trading at $0.44 per share — down 89% from its $4.00 IPO price in September 2025 [1][2]. The stock has no analyst coverage, no earnings estimates, and yfinance data shows TTM revenue declining 12.6% with negative profit margins (-9.4%) and deeply negative ROE (-57.5%). Customer concentration risk is severe — three new customers drove nearly half of H1 2025 revenue. The company competes against hyperscalers (AWS, Azure, Google Cloud) and hundreds of regional cloud providers with vastly greater resources. At this market cap and share price, the stock carries extreme liquidity and delisting risk.
Key Catalysts
  1. Continued geographic expansion across Asia-Pacific markets, particularly in the Philippines, Japan, and Indonesia where cloud adoption is accelerating
  2. Growth in AI-powered cloud managed services driving higher-margin revenue and differentiation from commodity cloud resellers
  3. Potential new large enterprise customer wins for cloud CDN and server farm projects similar to the three customers secured in H1 2025
Key Risks
  1. Extreme customer concentration — three customers drove 46.9% of H1 2025 revenue, creating binary revenue risk if any relationship is lost
  2. Stock has declined 89% from IPO price with $15M market cap and no analyst coverage, creating severe liquidity risk and potential delisting exposure on NYSE American
  3. 17-employee company competing against hyperscalers and well-funded regional cloud providers in a commoditizing market where AI automation tools increasingly reduce the need for third-party cloud consultants
