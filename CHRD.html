Company Profile
Energy ·Oil & Gas E&P ·NASDAQ ·United States
Chord Energy is an independent exploration and production company focused on the acquisition, exploration, development, and production of crude oil, natural gas, and natural gas liquids in the Williston Basin of Montana and North Dakota. Formed from the merger of Oasis Petroleum and Whiting Petroleum in 2022, later merging with Enerplus in 2024.

Investment Thesis
Chord Energy is a well-capitalized Williston Basin pure-play generating approximately $2.3B adjusted EBITDA and $700M adjusted free cash flow at $64/bbl WTI, with a forward P/E of just 10.1x reflecting deep value for normalized earnings [1]. The Enerplus integration is complete, creating operational efficiencies across 157-161 MBopd of oil production for approximately $1.4B CapEx in 2026. Low debt-to-equity (18.7%) and $2.0B operating cash flow provide financial durability through commodity cycles [4]. The 3.48% dividend yield and share repurchase program offer meaningful total shareholder returns even in a flat oil price environment.
The bear case is commodity price risk and structural decline in trailing metrics. Full-year 2025 earnings collapsed (EPS $0.75 vs $16.02 in 2024) due to impairment charges and lower realized prices, creating a distorted trailing P/E of 199x [4]. Revenue declined 19.4% YoY reflecting lower commodity prices and production mix changes post-Enerplus integration. The variable dividend was cut from $2.94 to $1.30/quarter, signaling management's response to lower cash flows. The Williston Basin is a mature producing region with limited inventory upside compared to the Permian; if oil prices remain below $65/bbl, the company's capital program and return targets compress. The company's 0.97% trailing profit margin (though impairment-distorted) highlights commodity price sensitivity that creates earnings volatility.
Key Catalysts
  1. Normalization of trailing earnings metrics in 2026 — forward EPS of $14.74 implies P/E compression from 199x to 10x as impairment charges roll off [4]
  2. Enerplus synergy realization and extended lateral drilling program (4+ mile laterals) driving lower per-unit costs [2]
  3. Stable oil price environment above $65/bbl WTI enabling full capital return program restoration and potential dividend increase [1]
Key Risks
  1. WTI crude price decline below $60/bbl would compress free cash flow below dividend coverage, forcing further dividend cuts and reduced capital returns [1]
  2. Williston Basin inventory concerns — mature basin with limited Tier 1 drilling locations relative to Permian peers, constraining long-term production growth
  3. Trailing financial metrics distorted by impairment charges create uncertain forward visibility and may deter value-oriented investors despite low forward P/E [4]
