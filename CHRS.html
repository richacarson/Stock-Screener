Company Profile
Healthcare ·Biotechnology ·NASDAQ ·United States
Coherus Oncology (formerly Coherus BioSciences) is a commercial-stage biopharmaceutical company focused on immuno-oncology. Its lead product LOQTORZI (toripalimab) is a PD-1 inhibitor approved for nasopharyngeal carcinoma. The pipeline includes Casdozokitug (IL-27 antagonist) and CHS-114 (CCR8 antibody). The company divested its UDENYCA biosimilar franchise in 2025 to focus exclusively on oncology.
147 Employees ·www.coherus.com

Investment Thesis
Coherus Oncology is a high-risk turnaround story with a promising clinical pipeline. The lead asset Casdozokitug demonstrated 38% ORR and 17.2% complete response rate in Phase 2 HCC, suggesting meaningful clinical differentiation in a large addressable market [2]. LOQTORZI is expected to grow 10-15% quarter-over-quarter with management targeting $150-200M peak sales by mid-2028 [1]. Post-UDENYCA divestiture, the company has approximately $250M in projected cash with runway exceeding two years, and debt was reduced 90% to $38.8M [1][2]. At a $282M market cap, the stock prices in significant risk but offers substantial upside if Casdozokitug Phase 3 data succeeds.
The bear case is severe. Revenue collapsed 84% YoY to $42.2M as UDENYCA was divested, and the company burns approximately $69M annually in free cash flow [1][5]. Consensus expects a -$0.50 EPS loss in FY2026 on just $96.2M revenue [1]. LOQTORZI competes in the PD-1 space against Keytruda ($25B+ annually) and Opdivo, making commercial scale extremely difficult for a 147-person company. Casdozokitug remains in Phase 2 with significant clinical and regulatory risk — Phase 3 failure would be existential. The 30% headcount reduction in 2025 signals financial distress, and Sustainalytics has flagged ESG controversy risk [2][4]. Cash runway of two years creates a hard deadline for clinical or commercial milestones.
Key Catalysts
  1. Casdozokitug Phase 2/3 progression in hepatocellular carcinoma — 38% ORR and 17.2% complete response rate represent potentially best-in-class efficacy data [2]
  2. LOQTORZI commercial ramp targeting 10-15% quarterly demand growth and $150-200M peak sales by mid-2028, with potential label expansions into lung and skin cancers [1][2]
  3. CHS-114 (CCR8 antibody) combination study initiation in HNSCC and gastric cancer could expand the immuno-oncology pipeline [2]
Key Risks
  1. Cash burn of ~$69M annually against $172M cash creates an approximately two-year survival window — failure to achieve clinical milestones or raise capital would be existential [2][5]
  2. LOQTORZI faces overwhelming competition from Keytruda and Opdivo in the PD-1 space, and $19.1M FY2024 revenue suggests limited commercial traction [2]
  3. Casdozokitug Phase 3 clinical risk — HCC trials have a high failure rate, and negative data would eliminate the primary value driver for the company [2]
