Investment Thesis
Chewy is the dominant US online pet retailer with 21.3M active customers, 83.3% Autoship penetration, and a strengthening financial profile — $12.6B revenue, $719M adjusted EBITDA (5.7% margin), and $562M free cash flow in FY2025 [1]. At 13.1x forward P/E with FY2026 guidance of $13.6-13.75B revenue (8-9% growth) and 6.6-6.8% EBITDA margin, the stock offers reasonable value for a consumer platform transitioning to profitability [1]. Growth vectors include Chewy Vet Care (18 clinics, expanding), pet pharmacy, sponsored advertising, and AI-driven efficiencies targeting $50M+ annualized savings by 2027 [2]. The $860M cash position and $453M FCF provide a balance sheet cushion. Singh's track record of growing revenue from $900M to $12.6B while achieving consistent profitability is impressive for an e-commerce company.
The bear case centers on Chewy's thin margins and limited growth runway. Profit margin of just 1.8% leaves minimal room for error — any execution misstep, supply chain disruption, or competitive price pressure could swing the company back to losses [1]. Pet household formation is stagnant, with management expecting 'little or no pricing contribution' in FY2026, meaning growth must come entirely from share gains and ARPU expansion. The trailing P/E of 50x is expensive relative to specialty retail peers at 25x. Competition from Amazon's Subscribe & Save and Walmart's pet offerings creates ongoing pricing pressure. The CTO retirement at a 'pivotal moment' for AI integration introduces technology leadership risk [2]. Gross margin expansion is expected to moderate in FY2026, limiting near-term profitability improvement. International expansion beyond Canada remains unproven, and the Vet Care clinic model is capital-intensive with uncertain unit economics.
Key Catalysts
  1. Chewy Vet Care expansion from 18 to potentially 30+ clinics in 2026, serving as a customer acquisition engine and high-margin healthcare revenue stream [2]
  2. AI-driven fulfillment and customer experience efficiencies targeting $50M+ annualized savings by 2027, contributing to EBITDA margin expansion toward 6.6-6.8% in FY2026 [2]
  3. Autoship penetration at 83.3% of sales with $591 net sales per active customer, creating a sticky recurring revenue base that grows with pet premiumization trends [1]
Key Risks
  1. Thin profit margins (1.8% net margin) with limited pricing power — management expects 'little or no pricing contribution' in FY2026, exposing profitability to cost inflation or competitive pressure [1]
  2. Amazon and Walmart competitive threat — Subscribe & Save and expanding pet offerings from scaled competitors with superior logistics could erode Chewy's market position [2]
  3. CTO departure during critical AI integration period — technology leadership vacuum may slow fulfillment automation and customer experience innovation at a time when AI capabilities are strategically important [2]
