Investment Thesis
Cipher Digital has secured $9.3B in contracted HPC data center revenue through long-term leases with AWS and Google, with delivery commencing July 2026 and projected $669M average annualized NOI through 2036 [1]. The company holds $754M in liquidity, has fully funded its Barber Lake and Black Pearl facilities, and Morgan Stanley rates the stock Overweight at $38 — implying 170% upside from current levels. The pivot from mining to infrastructure hosting captures the secular AI demand wave.
The bear case centers on extreme leverage ($3.73B in bonds), execution risk on simultaneous multi-hundred-megawatt builds, and the gap between contracted future revenue and current $822M net losses [1]. Revenue from HPC leases won't begin until H2 2026, creating a multi-quarter dead zone. The D/E ratio of 331% leaves no margin for construction delays or cost overruns. If AI infrastructure spending slows, Cipher's take-or-pay contracts provide protection but the equity could be crushed by debt service obligations.
Key Catalysts
  1. AWS Black Pearl 300 MW delivery commencing July 2026 with rent starting August 2026 — first material HPC revenue recognition
  2. Barber Lake 300 MW facility with Google/Fluidstack completing build-out in Q4 2026, doubling HPC capacity
  3. Potential additional hyperscaler contracts beyond the current 600 MW could drive re-rating toward data center peer multiples
Key Risks
  1. Construction delays at Barber Lake or Black Pearl could defer revenue recognition and strain liquidity despite $754M cash position
  2. D/E ratio of 331% with $3.73B in bonds creates significant interest expense burden — rising rates or credit downgrades could pressure equity value
  3. Customer concentration risk: two hyperscaler relationships drive entire $9.3B contracted backlog, and loss of either would be catastrophic
