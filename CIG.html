Company Profile
Utilities ·Utilities - Regulated Electric ·NYSE ·Brazil
Companhia Energetica de Minas Gerais (CEMIG) is one of the largest integrated electric power companies in Brazil, headquartered in Belo Horizonte, Minas Gerais. With over 70 years of history, CEMIG operates across the full energy value chain: generation (32 hydro plants, 2 wind farms, 12 solar stations totaling ~4,674 MW), transmission, distribution, and commercialization of electric energy, as well as natural gas distribution. The company serves millions of customers in the state of Minas Gerais and trades as CIG (NYSE ADR) and CMIG3/CMIG4 on Brazils B3 exchange.

Investment Thesis
CEMIG is a well-positioned Brazilian integrated utility trading at an attractive forward P/E of 10.18 with a 7.68% dividend yield, offering a compelling combination of value and income for investors comfortable with Brazilian country risk. The company delivered solid Q1 2026 results with BRL 10.46B in net operating revenue (+6.3% YoY) and is executing a record BRL 6.72B investment plan focused on grid modernization, smart meters, and renewable energy expansion [1]. CEMIGs core business is structurally immune to AI disruption -- physical electricity infrastructure, regulated concessions, and essential service delivery create near-impenetrable competitive moats. The company is proactively deploying AI for operational efficiency, achieving a 20-point NPS improvement through IBM watsonx.ai-powered virtual assistants [2]. With ROE of 17.04%, debt-to-equity of 69%, and a 5-year revenue CAGR of ~11% in BRL terms, CEMIG combines solid financial fundamentals with growth from Brazils expanding electricity demand and free-market energy commercialization.
The primary risks are concentrated in Brazilian macro and regulatory factors rather than company-specific weaknesses. CEMIGs ADR returns are heavily influenced by BRL/USD exchange rate movements -- the ~11% BRL revenue CAGR has historically been partially offset by real depreciation. As a state-influenced entity (the state of Minas Gerais is a major shareholder), CEMIG is exposed to political risk from government transitions and potential interference in tariff-setting, capital allocation, and management appointments -- the recent CEO change in May 2026 illustrates this dynamic [3]. The Q1 2026 results showed EBITDA declining 2.1% YoY despite revenue growth, reflecting cost pressures from the accelerated investment program and higher net debt (2.45x EBITDA vs. lower levels previously). Profit margin of 11.15% is below peer averages, and the elevated capex program could strain free cash flow generation if regulatory returns do not keep pace. ADR dividend payments have been inconsistent -- paid in only 5 of the last 10 years -- making CIG unreliable as an income investment for US-based investors despite the headline yield [7].
Key Catalysts
  1. Record BRL 6.72B investment plan for 2026 targeting grid modernization with smart meters and ADMS technology, which should drive operational efficiency gains, reduce losses, and improve revenue protection
  2. Growing free-market electricity commercialization segment as Brazil continues deregulating its power market, allowing CEMIG to capture higher-margin sales beyond its regulated tariff base
  3. Attractive valuation at 10.18x forward P/E and 7.68% dividend yield, with potential for re-rating as Brazils political and regulatory environment stabilizes under the new CEOs leadership
Key Risks
  1. Brazilian macroeconomic and currency risk -- BRL depreciation against USD can significantly erode ADR returns, and Brazils fiscal policy uncertainty creates ongoing volatility for Brazilian equities
  2. State government influence on management and capital allocation -- as a state-controlled entity, CEMIG faces political risk from gubernatorial transitions, potential tariff interference, and management changes driven by political rather than operational considerations
  3. ADR dividend inconsistency -- payments in only 5 of the last 10 years makes CIG unreliable as an income investment, and the 64% payout ratio combined with elevated capex (BRL 6.72B in 2026) could further strain distributions to ADR holders
