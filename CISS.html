Company Profile
Industrials ·Marine Shipping ·NASDAQ ·Greece
C3is Inc. provides international seaborne transportation services, owning and operating drybulk carriers and tankers that transport iron ore, coal, grains, and crude oil. Founded in 2021 and headquartered in Athens, Greece, the company is expanding its fleet with MR product tanker acquisitions to reach six vessels by mid-2026.

Investment Thesis
C3is offers an unusually clean balance sheet for a shipping company — D/E of just 2.6% with zero bank debt after repaying $59.2M in CapEx obligations entirely from operating cash flow [1]. Net income surged 481% in 2025 to $10.5M, and the company is aggressively expanding its fleet with two MR product tanker acquisitions expected Q1-Q3 2026, growing capacity by 387% from inception [2]. The stock trades at an extreme discount to book value (P/B of 0.0005x), and daily TCE rates of $19,469 in Q4 2025 demonstrate the fleet's earning power in a favorable tanker market [1].
The bear case is dominated by micro-cap risk and structural concerns. Market cap of just $2.2M makes this effectively a nano-cap with severe liquidity risk despite technically passing the $1M liquidity floor [4]. Repeated reverse stock splits (the stock trades at $0.82) signal chronic dilution and value destruction for shareholders. The company is managed externally by Stealth Maritime Corporation, creating potential conflicts of interest and limited governance oversight [3]. Revenue is highly cyclical and dependent on volatile charter rates — full-year 2025 voyage revenues declined 18% due to drydocking and idle days [1]. Greek shipping micro-caps have a poor track record of rewarding minority shareholders, and the extreme P/B discount may reflect rational skepticism about governance and capital allocation rather than a valuation opportunity.
Key Catalysts
  1. Two MR product tanker deliveries in Q1-Q3 2026 expanding fleet capacity by 387% from inception, with tanker sector rates remaining elevated [2]
  2. Debt-free balance sheet providing flexibility for additional acquisitions without dilution as fleet-expansion strategy continues [1]
  3. Rising tanker day rates driven by Middle East crude oil flows and aging global tanker fleet creating favorable supply-demand dynamics
Key Risks
  1. Nano-cap market cap of $2.2M with extreme reverse split history signals chronic shareholder dilution and governance risk [4]
  2. External management by Stealth Maritime Corporation creates potential conflicts of interest and limited transparency for minority shareholders [3]
  3. Cyclical charter rate exposure — a downturn in dry bulk or tanker rates would severely compress earnings given the small fleet size and lack of long-term contracts [1]
