Investment Thesis
Colgate-Palmolive offers one of the most defensible competitive positions in consumer staples with 41.3% global toothpaste market share, record $3.6 billion free cash flow, and 62 consecutive years of dividend growth. The new 2030 strategy under CEO Noel Wallace emphasizes AI-driven demand generation, omnichannel expansion, and organizational optimization. The Hill's Pet Nutrition business provides high-margin, veterinary-channel growth, and the company's global diversification across 200+ countries provides natural hedging against regional economic weakness.
The negative Inspire score of -49 due to LGBT Activism significantly weighs on faith alignment for values-focused investors. GAAP EPS declined 25% in 2025 due to the $794 million skin health impairment charge, exposing acquisition integration risk. The 2,344% debt-to-equity ratio (reflecting share buyback-depleted equity) limits financial flexibility, and the 78% payout ratio constrains dividend growth to a 3.2% CAGR that barely exceeds inflation. The 2026 organic growth guidance of 1-4% is below the company's own 3-5% long-term target, suggesting the challenging category environment may persist. The forward P/E of 20.5x offers limited upside for a low-single-digit organic grower.
