Company Profile
Basic Materials ·Steel ·NYSE ·United States
Cleveland-Cliffs Inc. is the largest flat-rolled steel producer in North America, operating an integrated portfolio spanning iron ore mining, pellet production, direct reduced iron, steelmaking, and downstream finishing including stamping, tooling, and tubing. Serves automotive, infrastructure, manufacturing, and distribution markets. Transformed from an iron ore mining company through acquisitions of AK Steel (2020) and ArcelorMittal USA (2020). Founded in 1847, headquartered in Cleveland, Ohio, with a market cap of approximately $7.6B.

Investment Thesis
Cleveland-Cliffs offers a compelling cyclical recovery opportunity at $13.27 per share with a Forward PEG of 0.43, implying significant upside if steel prices and earnings recover as management expects [1][4]. The company's position as North America's largest flat-rolled steel producer and primary automotive steel supplier provides structural advantages, and Q1 2026 revenue of $4.9B (up 6.3% YoY) with guided sequential improvement suggests the trough is behind [1]. The Palantir AI partnership should drive operational efficiencies, tariff protections support domestic pricing, and guided $10/ton cost reductions in 2026 should expand margins. At $7.6B market cap against $18.6B in annual revenue, the stock trades at just 0.4x sales.
The bear case is severe: Cleveland-Cliffs reported a GAAP net loss of $229M in Q1 2026 with negative ROE of -18.6% and negative profit margins, reflecting the deep trough in steel pricing and the burden of $5B+ in debt from the AK Steel and ArcelorMittal acquisitions [1][4]. Revenue has declined from $23.0B (2022) to $18.6B (2025), a -6.8% CAGR that reflects both lower steel prices and volume compression. The company eliminated its dividend after 2020 and generates negative free cash flow (-$60.5M TTM), leaving no shareholder returns beyond potential stock appreciation [4]. Goncalves's aggressive acquisition strategy has left the balance sheet stretched at 129% D/E, limiting financial flexibility if the steel cycle worsens further. CFO selling $2.9M in stock in June 2026 does not inspire confidence in management's near-term outlook.
Key Catalysts
  1. Steel price recovery driving sequential earnings improvement throughout 2026, with each quarter guided to be better than the last as order book momentum and pricing translate into margins
  2. Tariff protections on foreign steel imports supporting domestic pricing and protecting Cleveland-Cliffs' market position as the largest domestic flat-rolled producer
  3. Operational efficiency gains from the Palantir AI partnership and $10/ton guided cost reductions reducing breakeven steel prices and expanding margins into positive territory
Key Risks
  1. Cyclical steel downturn deepening beyond management expectations — D/E of 129% and negative free cash flow leave no buffer if steel prices remain depressed, potentially threatening debt covenants
  2. Revenue decline of -6.8% CAGR (2022-2025) may reflect structural demand erosion as automotive lightweighting trends and electric vehicle adoption reduce steel content per vehicle
  3. CFO insider selling of $2.9M and management's inability to generate positive free cash flow despite $18.6B in annual revenue raise questions about the company's ability to service its acquisition debt burden through the cycle
