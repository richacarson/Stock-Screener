Company Profile
Basic Materials ·Steel ·NYSE ·United States
Cleveland-Cliffs Inc. is the largest flat-rolled steel producer in North America, with approximately 30 million net tons of annual steelmaking capacity. Through four major acquisitions (AK Steel, ArcelorMittal USA, Ferrous Processing & Trading, and Stelco), the company has transformed from an iron ore miner into a vertically integrated steel producer serving automotive, infrastructure, and manufacturing markets. The company also mines iron ore and produces pellets, HBI, and coke. Founded in 1847, headquartered in Cleveland, Ohio.

Investment Thesis
Cleveland-Cliffs is a cyclical turnaround with the wind at its back. Q1 2026 revenue of $4.9B (up $600M QoQ) was driven by HRC prices averaging $980/ton (24% YoY increase), with the CEO declaring the 'order book is full' [1]. U.S. steel tariffs have materially reduced imports, driving domestic demand and pricing power. The slab contract termination delivers an estimated $500M annual EBITDA benefit. Full-year shipment guidance of 16.5-17.0M net tons with $10/ton cost reductions points to meaningful profitability recovery. At $10.15 per share with a PEG of 0.43 and $5.8B market cap against $18.9B in revenue, the stock is pricing in no recovery — any return to normalized margins would drive substantial re-rating.
The bear case is that two consecutive years of net losses ($760M in 2024, $1.48B in 2025) have severely damaged the balance sheet — D/E of 129% with $7.8B debt against just $45M cash creates acute financial vulnerability if the recovery stalls [1]. The tariff-dependent business model means any policy shift could collapse domestic steel pricing overnight. Automotive exposure (~30% of revenue) faces EV transition uncertainty that may suppress steel demand for traditional body-in-white applications. CEO Goncalves's $37M+ insider selling in February 2026 contradicts the bullish narrative, and the POSCO partnership remains unconfirmed. The 5yr revenue CAGR of -6.9% reflects a structurally declining top line despite acquisitions, and free cash flow is negative at -$60.5M TTM. At $10/share, the market is not wrong to demand proof of recovery before re-rating [3].
Key Catalysts
  1. HRC steel prices sustaining above $1,000/ton through H2 2026 as tariff enforcement and reshoring activity drive domestic demand, delivering return to GAAP profitability
  2. Slab contract termination generating $500M+ annual EBITDA benefit, transforming the cost structure and enabling deleveraging from the current 129% D/E ratio
  3. Definitive POSCO partnership agreement providing access to EV-grade advanced high-strength steel and diversifying revenue beyond traditional automotive applications
Key Risks
  1. Balance sheet fragility: $7.8B debt against $45M cash and negative FCF creates existential risk if steel prices decline or automotive demand fails to recover in 2026
  2. Tariff policy dependence: any relaxation, exemption, or trade deal that reopens U.S. markets to imported steel could collapse domestic HRC pricing from $980/ton toward $700-800/ton
  3. CEO Goncalves's $37M+ insider selling in February 2026, combined with combative public persona and aggressive acquisition strategy, creates corporate governance risk and potential succession concerns

AI Resilience LOW RISK

AI Risk Protection (8/9): Steel manufacturing is one of the most AI-immune industries in existence. Converting iron ore into flat-rolled steel through blast furnaces, basic oxygen furnaces, or electric arc furnaces is a capital-intensive, physical-world process that AI cannot substitute [1][2]. Cleveland-Cliffs' vertically integrated operations — from iron ore mining through pelletization, HBI production, primary steelmaking, and downstream finishing — involve massive physical infrastructure, complex metallurgy, and union labor that have no AI replacement. AI adoption in data centers, automotive, infrastructure, and manufacturing actually increases steel demand (server racks, construction materials, EV bodies). Tariff policy, not AI, is the primary risk factor cited by analysts [1]. AI Opportunity (0/1): No AI-generated revenue. The company uses standard industrial process optimization tools but does not sell AI products or services.
