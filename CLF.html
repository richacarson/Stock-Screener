Company Profile
Basic Materials ·Steel ·NYSE ·United States
Cleveland-Cliffs Inc. is the largest flat-rolled steel producer in North America, vertically integrated from iron ore mining and pellet production through primary steelmaking and downstream finishing, stamping, tooling, and tubing. The company serves direct automotive, infrastructure and manufacturing, distributors, and steel producer customers.

Investment Thesis
Cleveland-Cliffs is positioned for cyclical recovery with Q1 2026 revenue of $4.9B (up 7% YoY), multi-year fixed-price contracts with all major automotive OEMs securing high-margin revenue, and U.S. steel tariffs providing structural support for domestic pricing. The stock trades at $10.34 with a forward P/E of 22.3x on trough earnings, and the PEG ratio of 0.43 is attractive if the earnings recovery from the -$2.91 trailing EPS to the $0.46 forward estimate materializes [1][4]. The company's vertically integrated model — from iron ore mining to finished steel — provides cost advantages that peers lack, and the Palantir AI partnership could deliver operational efficiencies that compress the $10/ton cost reduction target.
The bear case is substantial: Cleveland-Cliffs posted consecutive annual net losses ($760M in 2024, $1.48B in 2025) with negative ROE of -18.6% and profit margins of -6.4%, and the forward EPS of $0.46 represents a fragile earnings recovery dependent on steel pricing maintaining tariff-supported levels [1][4]. D/E of 129% is elevated for a cyclical business, and free cash flow is negative at -$60.5M, leaving limited financial flexibility during a prolonged steel downturn. Revenue has contracted from $23.0B in 2022 to $18.6B in 2025 (5-year CAGR of -6.9%), and the absence of a dividend means shareholders bear full cyclical downside with no income cushion. If tariffs are rolled back or auto production weakens, the earnings recovery thesis collapses [1].
Key Catalysts
  1. U.S. steel tariffs providing structural pricing support for domestic producers, with Section 232 tariffs and potential additional trade protection enhancing margins
  2. Multi-year fixed-price automotive contracts with all major OEMs increasing market share and providing revenue visibility through 2027 and beyond
  3. Palantir AI partnership delivering operational cost reductions supporting management's target of $10/net ton unit cost improvement in 2026
Key Risks
  1. Tariff rollback or trade policy reversal under future administrations would eliminate the pricing floor that supports Cleveland-Cliffs' domestic cost structure
  2. Consecutive annual losses in 2024-2025 and negative free cash flow with 129% D/E create balance sheet fragility in a prolonged steel cycle downturn
  3. U.S. automotive production weakness from EV transition disruption or consumer spending slowdown would directly impact the 28% of revenue from direct auto customers
