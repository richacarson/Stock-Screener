Company Profile
Basic Materials ·Steel ·NYSE ·United States
Cleveland-Cliffs Inc. is the largest flat-rolled steel producer in North America, operating an integrated steelmaking platform from iron ore mining and pellet production through primary steelmaking, downstream finishing, stamping, tooling, and tubing. The company serves automotive, infrastructure, manufacturing, and distributor end markets across the United States and Canada.

Investment Thesis
Cleveland-Cliffs at 22.3x forward P/E with a 0.43 PEG ratio offers asymmetric upside if steel prices recover and the integrated model delivers on its profitability promise. Q1 2026 showed meaningful improvement — steelmaking adjusted EBITDA swung from -$179M to +$95M YoY, and the operating loss narrowed from $543M to $213M [1]. The company's unique vertical integration from iron ore mining through automotive stamping provides competitive advantages in quality, supply chain control, and customer relationships. Tariff protection supports domestic pricing power, and the company already achieved its 2030 GHG emissions reduction goal ahead of schedule [3].
The bear case is substantial: CLF remains unprofitable with a $229M net loss in Q1 2026, negative ROE (-18.6%), negative profit margins (-6.4%), and 129% D/E from aggressive debt-funded acquisitions [1][4]. Revenue has declined at a -6.8% 5-year CAGR, reflecting the structural challenge of growing in a mature, cyclical industry. The eliminated dividend removes income support, and the stock at $10.31 is priced for a recovery that may not materialize if steel demand softens or tariff protections are rolled back. The automotive end market faces its own disruption from EV transition and potential volume declines, threatening CLF's highest-margin business.
Key Catalysts
  1. Steelmaking adjusted EBITDA recovery from -$179M to +$95M YoY in Q1 2026, with further improvement expected as steel prices stabilize and Stelco synergies materialize [1]
  2. Tariff protection on imported steel supporting domestic pricing power and improving spread between raw material costs and finished steel prices [1]
  3. Full-year 2026 shipment guidance of 16.5-17.0 million net tons with operating leverage positioned to generate significant EBITDA at normalized steel prices [1]
Key Risks
  1. Negative ROE (-18.6%), negative profit margins, and $229M net loss in Q1 2026 demonstrate that current steel prices are insufficient for profitability despite vertical integration advantages [1]
  2. 129% D/E ratio from debt-funded acquisitions limits financial flexibility and increases bankruptcy risk if the steel cycle downturn extends beyond current expectations [4]
  3. Automotive end market disruption from EV transition and potential volume declines threatens CLF's highest-value customer segment and premium product mix [1]

AI presents moderate opportunities and limited direct disruption risk for steel production. AI can optimize blast furnace operations, predictive maintenance, supply chain logistics, and scrap quality assessment — areas where CLF's vertically integrated model provides rich data for machine learning applications [2]. However, CLF has shown limited public investment in AI capabilities compared to peers.
