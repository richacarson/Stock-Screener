Company Profile
Basic Materials ·Steel ·NYSE ·United States
Cleveland-Cliffs Inc. is the largest flat-rolled steel producer in North America, with approximately 30 million net tons of annual steelmaking capacity. The company produces hot-rolled, cold-rolled, coated, stainless, and electrical steel products serving automotive, infrastructure, and manufacturing markets through vertically integrated operations including iron ore mining and steelmaking.

Excellence Evaluation — Think Like an Owner (50%)
Innovation WEAK
3
Cleveland-Cliffs has made incremental innovation in sustainability via Hot Briquetted Iron (HBI) production — the sole Great Lakes HBI producer reducing GHG emissions versus blast furnace routes [4]. However, the company cancelled a $500M green steel project in 2025 and the POSCO partnership for EV-grade advanced high-strength steel remains unconfirmed [1]. No meaningful AI deployment in customer-facing products; the COO oversees a Research and Innovation Center but output is conventional steel grades, not differentiated materials [3]. Market share leadership (largest flat-rolled in North America) is the single notable strength.
Inspiration DEVELOPING

The company supports communities in Cleveland, Pittsburgh, and steel towns where it is often the largest employer. However, Glassdoor reviews describe mixed culture with concerns about 'lack of quality leadership' and employees 'just showing up to collect a paycheck' [3]. Insider buying is absent — Goncalves has net sold shares recently.
Infrastructure WEAK
3

Steelmaking Adjusted EBITDA improved to $95M from -$179M, signaling the turnaround is underway but far from complete [1]. D/E of 129%, negative ROE of -18.6%, and negative profit margins of -6.4% reflect the hangover from catastrophic FY2025 [2]. Forward P/E of 22.3x is expensive for a loss-making cyclical. Investment Thesis
Cleveland-Cliffs is a cyclical turnaround with identifiable 2026 catalysts: Q1 Steelmaking EBITDA swung from -$179M to +$95M YoY, revenue grew 6% to $4.92B, and management expects positive free cash flow starting Q2 2026 [1]. The slab contract termination delivers an estimated $500M annual EBITDA benefit, tariff protections provide pricing floors, and the PEG ratio of 0.43 suggests significant earnings recovery is priced in below fair value if the turnaround materializes.
The bear case remains formidable: Q1 net loss of $229M means the company is still losing money despite the EBITDA improvement, D/E of 129% limits financial flexibility, and forward P/E of 22.3x is expensive for a cyclical that hasn't proven sustainable profitability [2]. Chinese steel overcapacity, EV transition uncertainty for automotive demand (~30% of revenue), and management credibility erosion from the magnitude of the 2025 collapse all weigh against the recovery thesis. A no-dividend, high-leverage cyclical requires near-perfect execution across all catalysts simultaneously.
Key Catalysts
  1. Positive free cash flow achievement in Q2 2026 as guided, with sequential EBITDA improvement validating the slab contract termination benefit
  2. U.S. steel tariff expansion providing sustained pricing protection for domestic flat-rolled products above $800/ton HRC
  3. Automotive OEM order book strengthening as fixed-price multi-year contracts and EV body-in-white demand materialize
Key Risks
  1. Automotive production remaining weak through 2026 as OEMs manage EV transition inventory — Cleveland-Cliffs derives ~30% of revenue from auto
  2. D/E of 129% limiting financial flexibility if recovery is slower than guided, potentially requiring refinancing at unfavorable rates
  3. Chinese steel overcapacity and global trade redirection eroding domestic pricing power despite tariff protections
