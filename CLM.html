Company Profile
Financial Services ·Asset Management ·NYSE American ·United States
Cornerstone Strategic Investment Fund (formerly Cornerstone Strategic Value Fund) is a diversified closed-end equity mutual fund managed by Cornerstone Advisors, LLC. Incorporated in Maryland in 1987, the fund invests in global public equities using a balanced value/growth approach and is best known for its 21% managed distribution policy that returns NAV to shareholders monthly.

Investment Thesis
Cornerstone Strategic Investment Fund (CLM) is a closed-end equity mutual fund best known for its 21%-of-NAV managed distribution policy, paying $0.1215 per share monthly in early 2026 [1][3]. For income-focused investors who understand the mechanics, CLM offers a high headline yield and consistent monthly cash flow that can be harvested even when the underlying market is volatile. The fund holds a diversified global equity portfolio managed by Cornerstone Advisors, LLC, with operations dating back to 1987, providing a long operating track record and reasonable secondary market liquidity [2].
The bear case is fundamental to the structure: the 21% distribution rate vastly exceeds any reasonable equity return, so a substantial portion of monthly distributions is return-of-capital — investors are simply getting their own money back, which steadily erodes NAV over time [1][3]. The fund routinely trades at a premium to NAV that compresses when distributions are reset lower, and recurring dilutive rights offerings further pressure per-share NAV [1]. There is no Just Cause, no innovation, and no courageous capital-allocation discipline — the structure exists primarily to perpetuate AUM-based fees for the adviser. AI-driven robo-advisors and low-cost ETFs continue to commoditize the high-yield-equity wrapper that CLM occupies. For total-return-focused investors, CLM is a poor vehicle relative to direct equity indexing [2].
Key Catalysts
  1. 2026 distribution rate maintained at 21% of NAV with monthly $0.1215 payments providing predictable cash flow for income-seekers [1]
  2. Sustained equity market rally would lift NAV and reduce the return-of-capital component of distributions [1][3]
  3. Premium-to-NAV expansion in income-starved environments could deliver short-term capital appreciation independent of underlying portfolio returns [1]
Key Risks
  1. Return-of-capital distributions structurally erode NAV — a meaningful portion of the 21% yield is the investor's own principal being returned [1][3]
  2. Premium-to-NAV compression and dilutive rights offerings have historically reduced per-share NAV regardless of market direction [1]
  3. Secular shift to low-cost ETFs and direct indexing continues to commoditize the closed-end equity fund category, pressuring premiums and AUM [2]
