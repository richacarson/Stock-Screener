Company Profile
Energy ·Oil & Gas Refining & Marketing ·NASDAQ ·United States
Clean Energy Fuels is the largest provider of renewable natural gas (RNG) fuel for transportation in North America. The company supplies RNG, compressed natural gas (CNG), and liquefied natural gas (LNG) for medium and heavy-duty vehicle fleets, designs and operates fueling stations, and develops dairy farm RNG production projects to convert livestock waste into renewable fuel.

Investment Thesis
Clean Energy Fuels is the dominant provider of renewable natural gas for heavy-duty transportation in North America, positioned at the intersection of decarbonization mandates and practical fleet fuel economics [1]. The 2026 outlook shows meaningful operational progress: RNG production expected to nearly double from dairy projects, fuel volumes targeting 324M gallons with 250M gallons RNG, and adjusted EBITDA guidance of $70-75M represents improving unit economics [1]. At $502M market cap (1.2x P/S), the stock trades at a significant discount to clean energy peers. The LCFS and RIN credit regulatory framework creates revenue tailwinds, and AI/data center-driven electricity demand growth may increase natural gas infrastructure value long-term. Clay Corbus's 20-year tenure as CEO provides strategic continuity.
The bear case is anchored in persistent unprofitability: Clean Energy has operated for 25 years without achieving sustained GAAP profitability, guiding to a $66-71M net loss in 2026 despite $420-440M in revenue [1][4]. The -52.3% profit margin and near-zero free cash flow ($3M) suggest the business model may be structurally unprofitable. Revenue has been flat at ~$420M for four consecutive years, raising questions about market size limitations for natural gas transportation fuel. Electric vehicle adoption in trucking (Tesla Semi, Daimler eActros, Nikola) represents a long-term existential threat if battery technology improves for long-haul applications. The company's dependence on government credits (LCFS, RINs) for revenue creates regulatory risk — any reduction in credit values directly impacts profitability. At $2.29 per share, the stock is a micro-cap with limited institutional ownership and higher volatility [4].
Key Catalysts
  1. Near-doubling of RNG production in 2026 through South Fork and East Valley dairy projects, with upstream business expected to achieve positive adjusted EBITDA for the first time [1]
  2. Expansion of total fuel volumes to 324M gallons in 2026, with RNG comprising 250M gallons, demonstrating the transition from fossil CNG/LNG to renewable fuel [1]
  3. Hydrogen fueling infrastructure exploration as a potential new growth vertical, leveraging existing station network and fleet relationships [2]
Key Risks
  1. Structural unprofitability — 25 years without sustained GAAP profitability and guided net loss of $66-71M in 2026, with unclear path to breakeven [1][4]
  2. Electric vehicle disruption of heavy-duty trucking as Tesla Semi and other battery-electric trucks improve range and economics for long-haul applications
  3. Government credit dependency — LCFS and RIN credit values are subject to regulatory changes that could materially impact revenue and margins [1]
