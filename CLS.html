Investment Thesis
Celestica is one of the cleanest pure-play hyperscaler AI infrastructure beneficiaries in EMS/ODM. FY2025 revenue grew 28% to $12.39B, and 2026 guidance was raised to $17.0B revenue (+37%) and adjusted EPS of $8.75 (+45%) — with Q1 2026 guide of +51% YoY at midpoint confirming acceleration [1]. CCS hardware platform solutions revenue (custom AI servers, networking, storage for hyperscalers) surged 79% YoY to $1.4B in a single quarter [1]. CEO Rob Mionis's 10-year tenure and Honeywell/StandardAero operating discipline have transformed the company from low-margin contract manufacturing to high-value custom design + supply chain solutions. The $1B 2026 capex (~6% of revenue) accelerates AI hardware capacity ahead of demand. SBTi 1.5°C science-based emissions targets and RBA membership provide ESG credibility [3]. At a forward P/E in the low-20s for 37%+ revenue growth and 45%+ EPS growth, valuation remains reasonable.
Risks center on AI capex cyclicality and customer concentration. EMS/ODMs are leverage plays on hyperscaler capex, and any pause in AI infrastructure spending (whether from a Big Tech reset, a model-efficiency breakthrough that reduces compute needs, or macro deceleration) would hit Celestica disproportionately. Customer concentration on a small number of hyperscalers (Meta, Google, Microsoft, Amazon) creates single-customer-loss risk; one design loss could materially impact growth. The high revenue growth rate (37% in 2026 guide) is unlikely to sustain at this level beyond 2027, and multiple compression is possible if growth normalizes. Margin expansion to date has been impressive but EMS economics remain structurally lower than pure-play hardware OEMs. Capex acceleration to $1B+ creates execution risk if the AI demand profile shifts.
