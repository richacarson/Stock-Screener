Company Profile
Technology ·Bitcoin Mining / Digital Infrastructure ·NASDAQ ·United States
CleanSpark, Inc. is a US-based digital infrastructure company operating the largest fleet of Bitcoin mining machines among public US miners, reaching 50 EH/s operational hashrate and approximately 5.8% of global Bitcoin network hashrate as of late 2025. The company generated $766.3M in FY2025 revenue (fiscal year ended September 30, 2025), a 102% year-over-year increase. CleanSpark is now pivoting beyond pure-play Bitcoin mining toward high-performance compute (HPC) and AI data center development, having acquired 270+ acres in Austin County and up to 447 acres in Brazoria County, Texas, targeting 890+ MW of aggregate potential utility capacity for AI and HPC workloads by 2027. Headquartered in Henderson, Nevada. CEO Matt Schultz (founder) returned in August 2025 following the resignation of Zachary Bradford.

Investment Thesis
CleanSpark is the largest US public Bitcoin miner by operational hashrate (50 EH/s, 5.8% of global network), having grown revenue from $39.3M in FY2021 to $766.3M in FY2025 — a 110% four-year CAGR — through relentless infrastructure buildout and a differentiated low-cost energy strategy (5.2 cents/kWh, 90%+ clean energy) [1][2]. With $925M in Bitcoin holdings and $260M in cash, the company carries a $1.0B working capital position providing financial cushion heading into its AI and HPC pivot [6]. The pivot is the central thesis: CleanSpark has secured 890+ MW of aggregate Texas data center capacity targeting H1 2027 energization, raised $1.15B at zero-coupon to fund buildout, and hired dedicated AI data center leadership — positioning itself as a vertically integrated digital infrastructure platform serving both Bitcoin network security and AI workload demand [3]. If AI data center demand continues to outpace supply (as hyperscaler capex plans consistently indicate), CleanSpark's early Texas land and power position could prove transformative and justify a significant valuation re-rating from Bitcoin miner to digital infrastructure company.
The risks are substantial and immediate. Q2 FY2026 revenue declined 24.9% year-over-year to $136M as Bitcoin prices fell from ~$100K to ~$76K between quarters, demonstrating the violent earnings sensitivity to Bitcoin price volatility that will persist for the next 12-18 months until AI data center revenue commences [6]. The net loss of $378.3M includes material non-cash fair value adjustments, but even adjusted EBITDA of -$241.2M signals real cash consumption. Debt-to-equity of 181.6% reflects the $1.15B convertible note issuance, creating refinancing risk if the HPC pivot takes longer than expected. Gross margin contracted from 55% in FY2025 to 40% in Q2 FY2026, entirely reflecting Bitcoin price sensitivity. The August 2025 CEO resignation of Zachary Bradford and return of founder Matt Schultz adds organizational execution risk during the most critical strategic transition in the company's history [2]. ROE of -34.8% and profit margin of -67.7% reflect the financial reality of a company absorbing large depreciation on mining hardware, fair value losses on Bitcoin, and growth capex simultaneously — fundamentals that will not normalize until AI revenue begins [1][6].
Key Catalysts
  1. Bitcoin price recovery toward $100K+ would restore gross margins above 50% and dramatically reduce reported losses, potentially tripling operating cash flow given the mining model's operating leverage [1][6]
  2. First AI and HPC data center tenant announcements with multi-year power purchase agreements at the Texas campuses would validate the infrastructure pivot thesis and de-risk the $1.15B capital deployment [3]
  3. Global Bitcoin hashrate growth flattening — Q2 FY2026 saw only 22 fewer BTC mined versus the prior quarter despite network difficulty growth — improving per-coin economics and protecting mining revenue at current BTC prices [6]
Key Risks
  1. Bitcoin price volatility is the primary earnings driver — a sustained decline below $60K would compress gross margins materially and accelerate cash burn against the $1.15B convertible debt load, creating potential liquidity pressure before AI revenue commences
  2. HPC and AI data center pivot execution risk: Texas campus energization delays, failure to sign anchor tenants before 2027, or intensifying competition from CoreWeave, Equinix, and hyperscaler-owned capacity could leave CleanSpark with stranded infrastructure capital at elevated leverage [3]
  3. CEO transition instability — Zachary Bradford's August 2025 resignation and Matt Schultz's return introduces leadership uncertainty precisely when the company needs deep HPC operating expertise, raising questions about execution on hyperscaler negotiations and data center commissioning [2]
