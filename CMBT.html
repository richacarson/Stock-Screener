Company Profile
Energy ·Oil & Gas Midstream ·NYSE ·Belgium
CMB.TECH NV is a diversified maritime group operating approximately 250 vessels across crude oil tankers (Euronav brand), dry bulk carriers (Bocimar), chemical tankers, container ships, offshore wind support vessels, and port vessels. The company also develops green hydrogen and ammonia infrastructure for maritime decarbonization, including dual-fuel ammonia-powered vessels and green molecule supply chains. Formerly known as Euronav NV, the company rebranded in October 2024.

Investment Thesis
CMB.TECH is a uniquely positioned maritime conglomerate operating ~250 vessels across tankers, bulkers, containers, and offshore wind, with a pioneering hydrogen/ammonia infrastructure division. TTM revenue surged 160.6% to $1.67B, driven by the Euronav combination and strong tanker rates [1]. The company booked $192.6M in vessel sale capital gains in 2025 and announced $269.3M more in Q1 2026, monetizing peak tanker values [1]. Contract backlog of $3.05B provides multi-year revenue visibility. At a forward P/E of 10.3x with 88.6% insider ownership by the Saverys family, the stock is cheaply valued with aligned management [3][4]. The green hydrogen/ammonia strategy positions CMB.TECH for the maritime energy transition, with 80+ hydrogen-ready vessels and ammonia-powered ships on order.
The bear case centers on shipping cyclicality, leverage, and hydrogen viability. D/E of 212% is significantly elevated even by shipping standards, creating vulnerability in a tanker rate downturn [4]. Profit margin of 9.7% is heavily reliant on vessel sale gains rather than recurring operating income — Q4 2025 profit of $90.1M included significant asset sales [1]. Earnings declined 35.3% YoY as the fleet-expansion acquisition costs flowed through. The green hydrogen business is pre-profit and faces fundamental economic challenges — ammonia fuel costs remain 3-5x higher than traditional bunker fuel, and ammonia bunkering infrastructure is virtually nonexistent globally. The Bocimar subsidiary faces legal scrutiny over ship scrapping practices in Bangladesh, including a worker fatality [3]. ROE of 7.3% is below cost of equity for a capital-intensive shipping business [4].
Key Catalysts
  1. Delivery of ammonia-powered Newcastlemax bulkers in 2026-2027, proving commercial viability of dual-fuel ammonia shipping and establishing first-mover advantage [1]
  2. Continued vessel sale program monetizing peak tanker values — $269.3M in Q1 2026 capital gains already announced, with potential for further fleet rationalization [1]
  3. IMO 2030 greenhouse gas regulations tightening, potentially advantaging CMB.TECH's ammonia-ready fleet versus competitors with conventional vessels
Key Risks
  1. Tanker rate cyclical downturn — crude oil tanker rates are near cyclical highs and a correction would pressure the Euronav division that generates the majority of operating income [1]
  2. Hydrogen/ammonia economics remain unproven at scale — ammonia fuel costs 3-5x conventional bunker fuel, and bunkering infrastructure is virtually nonexistent, creating execution risk on the decarbonization thesis
  3. Elevated leverage with D/E of 212% and $1.4B bridge loan recently repaid creates financial vulnerability if asset values decline or rates weaken [1][4]
