Investment Thesis
Comcast is deeply undervalued at 5.4x trailing P/E and 7.3x forward P/E with $19.2 billion in FCF, a 4.6% yield covered at just 18.4% payout, and $37.4 billion in adjusted EBITDA [1][2]. The post-Versant spin-off company is a focused connectivity and entertainment platform where wireless growth (1.5M net adds), Peacock approaching profitability (40M+ paid subs), and Epic Universe theme park provide multiple growth vectors. The simplified broadband pricing transition, while painful near-term, should stabilize the subscriber base by H2 2026 as free wireless lines convert to paid.
The bear case includes persistent broadband subscriber losses (587,000 in 2025) from 5G fixed wireless competition and the 'largest broadband investment year' consuming capital without guaranteed subscriber stabilization [1]. Adjusted EPS declined 53% due to the Versant spin-off, and revenue growth of just 1.2% demonstrates a mature business facing structural headwinds. Content investment in Peacock, NBA rights, and Epic Universe requires billions with uncertain returns, and 2.3x net leverage limits acquisition flexibility. The media landscape continues to fragment, and Comcast's success depends on the convergence thesis (broadband + wireless + streaming) that remains unproven at scale.
Key Catalysts
  1. Peacock profitability expected in 2026, with Super Bowl and Winter Olympics driving subscriber growth and advertising revenue
  2. Conversion of free wireless lines to paid in H2 2026 creates a material revenue tailwind from the 9M total wireless line base
  3. Epic Universe theme park opening drives incremental parks revenue and cross-sells Peacock streaming subscriptions
Key Risks
  1. Broadband subscriber losses accelerating beyond 587K/year as 5G fixed wireless and fiber competitors expand coverage
  2. Content investment in Peacock, NBA rights, and Epic Universe totaling billions may not generate adequate returns in a fragmenting media landscape
  3. Post-Versant spin-off earnings comparisons create confusing YoY optics (EPS -53%) that may depress the stock despite underlying business health
