Company Profile
Real Estate ·REIT - Diversified ·NASDAQ ·United States
Creative Media & Community Trust Corporation is a real estate investment trust that acquires, owns, and operates premier multifamily and creative office assets in vibrant communities throughout the United States. Managed by affiliates of CIM Group, L.P., CMCT focuses on properties catering to technology, media, and entertainment industries. The company has undergone significant restructuring including two 1-for-10 reverse stock splits in 2026 and the sale of its lending division.

Investment Thesis
CMCT's only positive signal is improving multifamily occupancy at 91.4% (up 1,120 basis points YoY), suggesting the underlying real estate assets have stabilizing fundamentals [1]. The sale of the lending division to Peachtree Group for $44.9M net proceeds provides short-term liquidity, and CIM Group's 30+ years of real estate management expertise offers operational know-how. The company's undepreciated book value of $147.22 per share against a $3.25 stock price suggests extreme NAV discount if the underlying assets can be stabilized.
The overwhelming weight of evidence is negative. CMCT is in severe financial distress with a $9.6M market cap, two 1-for-10 reverse stock splits in March and April 2026, Q1 2026 net loss of $34.7M, FFO of negative $28.8M, and revenue declining 9% YoY [1]. The external management structure by CIM Group creates inherent conflicts of interest — management fees are charged regardless of shareholder returns. D/E of 195.3%, ROE of -96.3%, and the cancellation of director equity awards signal a company where insiders are not aligned with shareholders. The $3.25 stock price (post two 100:1 cumulative reverse splits) represents a 97.8% discount to undepreciated book value, but this discount may be warranted given the company's inability to generate positive cash flow and the structural challenges facing creative office real estate in an AI-enabled remote work environment [5].
Key Catalysts
  1. Multifamily occupancy improvement to 91.4% (up 1,120 bps YoY) suggests underlying asset stabilization that could eventually translate to positive FFO if operating expenses are contained
  2. Sale of lending division for $44.9M provides near-term liquidity to fund operations and potentially reduce debt, extending the company's survival runway
  3. Extreme 97.8% discount to undepreciated book value ($3.25 vs $147.22) creates deep value optionality if the company can stabilize operations or if CIM Group pursues a take-private transaction
Key Risks
  1. Two 1-for-10 reverse stock splits in March and April 2026 signal NASDAQ listing compliance risk — continued stock price weakness could result in delisting, eliminating institutional investor access
  2. External management by CIM Group creates structural conflict of interest — management fees are charged regardless of shareholder returns, and strategic decisions may serve CIM Group's broader portfolio rather than CMCT shareholders
  3. Creative office space faces secular headwinds from AI-enabled remote work and hybrid models, potentially reducing demand for the company's core creative office assets in technology and media markets
