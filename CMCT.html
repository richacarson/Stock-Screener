Investment Thesis
Creative Media & Community Trust is a severely distressed micro-cap REIT trading at $0.59/share with a market cap under $16M, triggered by declining office occupancy (68.1%), revenue contraction (-8.7% in 2025), and sustained net losses ($17.7M in Q4 2025 alone) [1][2]. The company has executed a 1-for-10 reverse stock split and is converting preferred shares to common stock to improve FFO by ~$16M/year, but at the cost of massive common equity dilution [1].
The lending division sale ($44.9M to Peachtree Group) and multifamily pivot provide a strategic direction, but CMCT lacks the scale, capital, and market position to execute a meaningful turnaround [1]. CIM Group's management track record — including bottom-quartile fund returns across its last four real estate funds and documented tenant mistreatment controversies — provides little confidence in operational improvement [4]. The liquidity floor is triggered (daily dollar volume ~$353K), making this position effectively illiquid for institutional investors. This is a speculative turnaround story with significant downside risk.
Key Catalysts
  1. Preferred stock redemption improving FFO by approximately $16M annually, potentially moving toward FFO breakeven if office occupancy stabilizes [1]
  2. Multifamily portfolio expansion (85.3% occupied, new Echo Park completion) in markets with favorable demographic trends could drive organic growth [1]
  3. Balance sheet deleveraging from the $44.9M lending division sale proceeds and simplified corporate structure could attract value-oriented investors if execution improves [1]
Key Risks
  1. Liquidity floor triggered — daily dollar volume of ~$353K makes positions effectively illiquid and vulnerable to large price swings on minimal volume [2]
  2. Office occupancy of 68.1% in a secular work-from-home trend could deteriorate further, requiring write-downs on the 1.3 million sq ft office portfolio [1]
  3. CIM Group management controversies (tenant displacement, ESG misrepresentation, bottom-quartile fund performance) create ongoing reputational and operational risk [4]
