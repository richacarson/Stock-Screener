Investment Thesis
Compass Therapeutics is a sub-$1B clinical-stage oncology biotech with a validated lead asset: tovecimig (CTX-009), a DLL4/VEGF-A bispecific antibody, met its primary efficacy endpoint in the COMPANION-002 Phase 2/3 biliary tract cancer trial in April 2025 with a 17.1% overall response rate vs 5.3% for paclitaxel alone [1]. Pivotal PFS/OS analyses are on track for late Q1 2026 and, if confirmatory, could enable a regulatory filing for 2L biliary tract cancer — a high-unmet-need indication with limited options [1]. Analyst consensus forecasts FY2027 revenue of ~$65M (+678%) consistent with a commercial launch scenario [1]. The StitchMabs and common light chain platforms support multi-asset pipeline extension beyond tovecimig. CEO Dr. Thomas Schuetz (MD/PhD) was promoted from President of R&D, preserving scientific continuity with 20+ years of oncology drug development experience [2].
The bear case reflects the binary nature of clinical biotech. Compass remains pre-revenue with 0% profit margin, ROE of -41.3%, and forward P/E of -11.3x — the full $980M market cap is optionality on tovecimig [4]. Late Q1 2026 PFS/OS results must confirm the April 2025 ORR signal; anything short of convincing survival benefit could compress shares substantially. Commercial risk includes payer reimbursement in a small 2L biliary tract cancer population, potential competitive entry, and manufacturing scale-up challenges for a ~35-employee company. Glassdoor rating of 3.1/5 (18 reviews) is modest though qualitative feedback is positive. Cash runway and potential dilution are meaningful considerations given the -$82M consensus 2026 loss estimate [1][4]. WATCH ahead of the late Q1 2026 readout; upgrade conviction on confirmatory survival data.

  3. Cash runway and dilution — consensus FY2026 loss of ~$82M (range -$40M to -$124M) may require additional financing, creating potential dilution overhang [1][4]
