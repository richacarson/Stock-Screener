Company Profile
Technology ·Software - Application ·NASDAQ ·United States
Commerce.com, Inc. (formerly BigCommerce Holdings) provides an AI-driven commerce ecosystem through its SaaS platform enabling merchants to orchestrate digital commerce experiences across owned and third-party channels for B2B, B2C, and small business use cases. The company operates three products: BigCommerce (open commerce engine), Feedonomics (AI-powered product data optimization), and Makeswift (visual storefront editor). Headquartered in Austin, Texas.

Investment Thesis
Commerce.com is at an inflection point, guiding to its first-ever GAAP profitability in FY2026 with revenue of $347.5-$369.5M (2-8% growth) and non-GAAP operating income of $34-$53M [1]. At $3.14 per share and 6.8x forward P/E, the stock is deeply discounted relative to SaaS peers, pricing in minimal growth expectations. The company is increasing R&D spending 30% to build AI-driven commerce capabilities — Feedonomics AI catalogue enrichment, conversational search, and agent-enabled checkout — positioning for the emerging agentic commerce era [2]. Enterprise ARR grew 10% to $287M, demonstrating traction in higher-value segments. Director Ellen Siminoff purchased $294K in shares in February 2026, signaling insider conviction at current levels [3]. If the AI commerce pivot gains traction and revenue growth reaccelerates above 10%, the stock offers significant upside from current depressed levels.
The bear case is substantial: Commerce.com has never been GAAP profitable in its history, trailing profit margin is -5.7%, and ROE is -53.2% [4]. Revenue grew only 2.9% in FY2025 — well below the growth rates needed to justify a SaaS valuation. The company faces existential competitive pressure from Shopify (which has 10x the merchant base and far greater AI investment resources), Adobe Commerce, and emerging AI-native platforms that could render traditional e-commerce platforms obsolete [2]. D/E of 420.6% reflects a heavily leveraged balance sheet with $165.5M in debt against minimal equity. The rebranding from BigCommerce to Commerce.com risks confusing existing customers without attracting new ones. At $3.14 per share and $258M market cap, the stock has lost over 90% from its 2021 IPO highs, and further dilution risk exists if the company cannot sustain profitability. The 30% R&D increase could further pressure margins if revenue growth does not materialize.
Key Catalysts
  1. First-ever GAAP profitability in FY2026 would validate the turnaround thesis and could rerate the stock from deep-value territory [1]
  2. AI-driven commerce products (Feedonomics AI, Commerce Companion, agentic checkout) reaching commercial traction and driving revenue acceleration above 8% growth [2]
  3. Enterprise ARR growth continuation above 10% demonstrating successful upmarket migration and improving unit economics [1]
Key Risks
  1. Shopify's dominant market position and massive AI investment ($1B+ annually) could further erode BigCommerce's competitive position in mid-market e-commerce [2]
  2. Revenue growth stagnation — 2.9% YoY growth is dangerously slow for a SaaS company and insufficient to cover operating costs if profitability targets are missed [1]
  3. AI disruption to traditional e-commerce platforms — if AI agents can autonomously create and manage commerce experiences, the need for dedicated platform subscriptions could decline structurally [2]

AI Risk Protection (3/9 — EXPOSED): Commerce.com faces significant AI disruption risk to its core e-commerce platform business.
