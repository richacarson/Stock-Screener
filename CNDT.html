Investment Thesis
Conduent's bull case rests on a turnaround under new CEO Harsha Agadi, who has experience restructuring distressed companies (Church's Chicken, Quiznos, Friendly's) and has put personal capital at risk with $497K in open-market purchases [3]. Adjusted EBITDA improved to $164M in 2025 (up 32% from $124M), the Government segment delivers 24% EBITDA margins, and the qualified ACV pipeline of $3.2B (up 4% YoY, Government up 29%) suggests improving demand [1]. The AI Experience Center could differentiate Conduent's BPO offerings through GenAI-powered automation [2]. At $264M market cap with $3.04B revenue, the stock trades at 0.09x revenue — extreme cheapness if operational improvements materialize.
The bear case is formidable. Revenue has contracted for five consecutive years from $4.16B (2020) to $3.04B (2025), a 27% decline reflecting structural loss of market share to larger IT services competitors [1]. Profit margins are negative (-5.6%), ROE is deeply negative (-18.8%), and the balance sheet carries $841M debt against $233M cash [4]. The January 2025 ransomware breach exposing 25 million records has triggered class action lawsuits, state attorney general investigations, and potential regulatory penalties that could consume years of free cash flow [3]. Conduent's core BPO services face direct AI disruption risk as large language models commoditize document processing, customer service, and claims administration. The $1.70 share price and $264M market cap reflect severe market skepticism about Conduent's viability as a standalone entity.
