Company Profile
Industrials ·Farm & Heavy Construction Machinery ·NYSE ·United Kingdom
CNH Industrial N.V. designs, manufactures, and sells agricultural and construction equipment worldwide under the Case IH, New Holland, STEYR, Case Construction, and Eurocomach brands. The company also provides financial services to dealers and end customers. Founded in 1842 and headquartered in Basildon, UK.

Investment Thesis
CNH Industrial is a 184-year-old agricultural and construction equipment manufacturer navigating the trough of a severe agricultural equipment downturn, with revenue declining 27% from the 2023 peak of $24.7B to $18.1B in 2025. However, the company is positioned for a powerful recovery through its AI and precision technology strategy — SenseApply sprayer technology reduces herbicide use by 80%, AI-powered combines optimize harvest in real time, and a roadmap targets nearly doubling Precision Tech revenue by 2030 [1][2]. The forward PEG of 0.59x suggests the market prices in significant earnings recovery, with forward PE of 14.3x reflecting cyclical trough expectations.
Key concerns center on the highly leveraged balance sheet (D/E 334%, driven by the captive Financial Services segment carrying $26.3B in debt), compressed profit margins of 2.1%, and management guiding for flat to slightly declining 2026 industrial sales with a further 5% decline expected in global ag equipment demand [3]. CEO Gerrit Marx (appointed July 2024) faces the challenge of executing a technology transformation while managing through a cyclical downturn. The DJSI-leading ESG score (88/100) and Inspire Insight score of 64 (ALIGNED) make this attractive for values-aligned investors who can tolerate cyclical volatility. The $230M in 2025 savings and $550M cumulative target through 2030 provide cost discipline as the company awaits demand recovery [3].
