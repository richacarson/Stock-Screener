Company Profile
Industrials ·Farm & Heavy Construction Machinery ·NYSE ·United Kingdom
CNH Industrial N.V. designs, produces, and sells agricultural and construction equipment worldwide through its Case IH, New Holland Agriculture, STEYR, CASE, and New Holland Construction brands. The company is undergoing a major technology transformation, embedding AI, autonomy, and precision agriculture across its product portfolio. FY2025 revenue was $18.1B (down 8.8% YoY) with net income declining to $386M from $2.5B in FY2024 due to industry downturn and higher input costs. Headquartered in Basildon, UK with 34,197 employees.

Investment Thesis
CNH Industrial offers compelling cyclical recovery value at 14.0x forward P/E and 0.6x PEG, reflecting market expectations that FY2025's earnings trough ($386M net income, down from $2.5B in FY2024) will reverse as the agricultural equipment cycle normalizes [1][5]. The technology transformation under CEO Marx is genuinely differentiated: the 2030 strategy to nearly double precision tech sales share, R4 autonomous robot, and AI integration across every leadership team's operations position CNH to capture higher-margin, recurring revenue from precision agriculture [2][3]. 11 consecutive years in the DJSI and the industry's highest ESG score (88/100) reflect strong governance and sustainability practices [4]. Q2 2026 guidance calls for agriculture net sales approximately flat YoY with construction up mid-teens percentage.
The bear case is the severity and duration of the agricultural equipment downturn: revenue has declined from $24.7B in FY2023 to $18.1B in FY2025 (3-year CAGR of -8.4%), net income collapsed 85% from $2.5B to $386M, and Q1 2026 showed continued deterioration to just $10M in net income [1][5]. The D/E of 333.7% reflects the captive financial services arm's leverage but creates balance sheet vulnerability in a prolonged downturn. Profit margin of 2.1% and ROE of 4.8% are far below IOWN targets, and the dividend yield of 0.98% offers minimal income support while the payout ratio of 78% suggests even this modest dividend could face pressure. Construction segment turned to an adjusted EBIT loss of $28M in Q1 2026 as tariffs and weak North/South American volumes compound the challenge [1]. Recovery depends on commodity price normalization and farmer replacement cycles that remain uncertain.
Key Catalysts
  1. Agricultural equipment cycle recovery: farmer equipment replacement cycles typically normalize within 2-3 years of downturn, and pent-up demand from aging fleet could drive a sharp V-shaped revenue recovery toward $22B+ levels
  2. Precision technology adoption acceleration: the 2030 strategy to nearly double precision tech sales share within agriculture creates a pathway to structurally higher margins as recurring software/data revenue supplements equipment sales
  3. Construction segment recovery with mid-teens percentage growth guided for Q2 2026 and potential infrastructure spending acceleration in key markets
Key Risks
  1. Prolonged agricultural equipment downturn: if commodity prices remain depressed and farmer income does not recover, the revenue decline from $24.7B to $18.1B could extend further, with net income approaching break-even levels
  2. Financial services portfolio risk: D/E of 333.7% reflects captive financing leverage, and rising agricultural loan defaults in a sustained downturn could impair the financial services segment's $74M quarterly net income
  3. Tariff escalation: Q1 2026 results cited higher tariffs as a margin headwind, and further trade policy disruption could increase input costs while reducing export competitiveness in key South American markets
