Company Profile
Industrials ·Farm & Heavy Construction Machinery ·NYSE ·United Kingdom
CNH Industrial N.V. designs, manufactures, and distributes agricultural and construction equipment under the Case IH, New Holland, and Case Construction brands. Agriculture represents 82% of industrial net sales. The company also operates a financial services segment. Q1 2026 revenue was $3.8B with net income collapsing to $10M from $132M YoY as tariff headwinds (210-220 bps on ag margins, 600 bps on construction) compounded the worst agricultural equipment downturn in a decade.

Investment Thesis
CNH Industrial trades at 14.6x forward P/E with a PEG of 0.61, pricing in significant earnings recovery from the current agricultural equipment trough [4]. The company reaffirmed full-year 2026 guidance of -4% to flat industrial net sales and 2.5-3.5% adjusted EBIT margin, with Q1 expected as the low point [1]. North America remains the profit engine, and precision agriculture technology (30+ releases through 2027, AI Tech Assistant deployed to dealer network) positions CNH to capture more value per unit as the cycle turns. The Inspire Impact Score of 64 (ALIGNED) reflects industry-leading ESG practices across 17 positive attributions. The Iveco spin-off created a focused pure-play with a genuine long-term purpose around food security.
The near-term outlook is severely challenged. Revenue has fallen from $24.7B (2023) to $18.1B (2025), a -8.4% 3-year CAGR, with further declines guided for 2026 [4]. Q1 2026 net income collapsed to $10M from $132M YoY, and the industrial segment posted a $45M adjusted EBIT loss due to tariff impacts of 210-220 bps on agriculture and 600 bps on construction margins [1]. D/E of 334% reflects captive finance leverage but constrains financial flexibility. Global agricultural equipment demand is expected to decline another 5% in 2026. Profit margins of 2.1% and ROE of 4.8% indicate the company is earning inadequate returns on its massive capital base. CEO Marx's 1.75-year tenure means the current strategy is unproven through a full cycle, and Glassdoor reviews describe the company as 'slow to innovate' and 'content to follow market leaders' [2].
Key Catalysts
  1. Agricultural equipment demand recovery beginning in 2027 as dealer inventory destocking completes and farmer income stabilizes, creating significant operating leverage on CNH's fixed cost structure
  2. Precision agriculture technology monetization — AI Tech Assistant and integrated crop management platform driving software/service revenue at margins above hardware business
  3. Tariff resolution or supply chain restructuring eliminating the 210-600 bps margin headwind that depressed Q1 2026 results
Key Risks
  1. Agricultural equipment downturn extending beyond 2026 with global demand declining a further 5%, creating additional earnings pressure on already-thin margins and potentially forcing restructuring charges
  2. Tariff escalation on CNH's cross-border supply chain (manufacturing in US, UK, Brazil, India) creating persistent margin headwinds that management estimated at 210-220 bps on agriculture and 600 bps on construction
  3. John Deere's autonomous and electric equipment roadmap and superior precision agriculture ecosystem potentially widening the competitive gap, relegating CNH to a value-tier follower position
