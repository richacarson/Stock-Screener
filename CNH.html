Company Profile
Industrials ·Farm & Heavy Construction Machinery ·NYSE ·Netherlands
CNH Industrial N.V. is a global capital goods company that designs, manufactures, and distributes agricultural and construction equipment under iconic brands including Case IH, New Holland, and Case Construction. The company is executing a strategy it calls 'Breaking New Ground,' combining AI, autonomy, and precision technology with its legacy iron equipment to modernize farming and construction.

Investment Thesis
CNH Industrial is a high-quality franchise navigating a cyclical trough with visible recovery on the horizon. The company's Q4 2025 showed 6% revenue growth and 21% YoY industrial adjusted EBIT improvement, and management has guided for agricultural demand recovery beginning in 2027[2]. CNH's precision agriculture technology stack — with 30+ new product releases planned by 2027 and every global leader running AI pilots — positions it to capture greater value per unit as the cycle turns. At a discounted valuation reflecting current earnings trough, patient investors are buying a global agricultural equipment leader at cycle-trough multiples.
The near-term bear case is severe: industry retail demand is forecast down another 5% in 2026 to historic trough levels, high-horsepower North American tractors specifically face 15-20% demand declines, and adjusted EPS is guided down to $0.35-$0.45 from $1.05 in FY2024[2]. Tariff costs created 110 basis points of EBIT margin headwind in 2025 with expectations for 2026 to worsen. Q1 2026 is expected to be near break-even. CNH carries elevated leverage in an industrial company which limits financial flexibility during the downturn.
Key Catalysts
  1. Agricultural equipment demand recovery beginning in 2027 as inventory destocking completes and farmer income stabilizes with normalized commodity prices
  2. Precision agriculture technology monetization — CNH's AI Tech Assistant and integrated crop management platform driving meaningful software/service revenue above hardware margins
  3. Margin recovery as high-horsepower equipment volumes recover — the most profitable segment showing the deepest declines creates significant operating leverage on the upturn
Key Risks
  1. Agricultural equipment demand trough extending beyond 2026 as high commodity prices fail to materialize, delaying farmer capital expenditure recovery and creating further earnings pressure
  2. Tariff escalation on international supply chains (CNH manufactures across the US, UK, Brazil, India) creating margin headwinds that management has flagged as worsening in 2026
  3. John Deere and AGCO accelerating autonomous/electric equipment rollouts that position competitors ahead of CNH in the precision agriculture software monetization race
