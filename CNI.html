Company Profile
Industrials ·Railroads ·NYSE ·Canada
Canadian National Railway operates the largest rail network in Canada and the only transcontinental network in North America, spanning approximately 20,000 route miles connecting the Atlantic and Pacific oceans through Canada and the U.S. Midwest. CN transports petroleum, grain, forest products, metals, automotive, and intermodal freight across three coasts.

Investment Thesis
Canadian National Railway is a best-in-class infrastructure franchise operating the only transcontinental rail network in North America with 20,000 route miles, generating C$17.3B in 2025 revenue with a 27.3% profit margin and 22.2% ROE [1]. The company delivered 8% EPS growth to C$7.57 in 2025 with Q4 adjusted earnings up 12%, while maintaining a 27-year dividend growth streak with an 8.8% 5yr CAGR and manageable 46.9% payout ratio [1]. At 17.0x forward P/E, CN trades at a reasonable valuation for a regulated infrastructure monopoly with predictable cash flows, and the Google Cloud AI partnership provides a technology edge over railroad peers [2][5].
The bear case centers on CN's anemic revenue growth — 2.4% YoY and near-zero 5yr CAGR reflect a mature industry with limited volume upside in a tariff-uncertain trade environment [1]. CN pulled its 2024-2026 outlook amid tariff uncertainty, signaling that management lacks visibility on near-term demand [1]. D/E of 100.3% is elevated for a capital-intensive business with $21.6B in total debt against limited cash reserves of $350M [5]. The PEG ratio of 2.52x suggests the stock is fully valued relative to its growth rate. Railroad labor disruptions remain a recurring risk, and Canadian regulatory changes could constrain pricing power. Climate transition costs — battery-electric locomotives, zero-emission trucks, emission reduction targets — represent significant capital deployment with uncertain returns over the medium term [3].
Key Catalysts
  1. Google Cloud AI partnership driving measurable improvements in network optimization, predictive maintenance, and fuel efficiency across 20,000 route miles, with potential to close the operating ratio gap versus industry leader CSX [2]
  2. Q1 2026 earnings beat expectations with EPS of $1.85 vs $1.78 estimate, suggesting operational momentum continuing into 2026 with potential for guidance improvement [1]
  3. 27-year consecutive dividend growth streak with 8.8% 5yr CAGR and 46.9% payout ratio providing significant runway for continued dividend increases [5]
Key Risks
  1. Tariff uncertainty causing CN to pull its 2024-2026 outlook — trade policy disruptions could materially reduce cross-border freight volumes on CN's transcontinental network [1]
  2. Near-zero 5yr revenue CAGR and 2.4% YoY growth reflect structural maturity in railroad volumes, limiting upside to pricing and efficiency improvements [5]
  3. D/E of 100.3% with $21.6B total debt against $350M cash creates refinancing risk if interest rates remain elevated, while capital-intensive sustainability investments require ongoing heavy spending [5]
