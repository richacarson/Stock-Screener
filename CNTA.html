Company Profile

Centessa Pharmaceuticals plc discovers, develops, and delivers orexin-based neuroscience therapeutics. Its lead program ORX750 is an orexin receptor 2 agonist in Phase IIa for narcolepsy type 1 and 2 and idiopathic hypersomnia. The company is being acquired by Eli Lilly for approximately $6.3 billion.

Investment Thesis
Centessa Pharmaceuticals represents a validated biotech innovation story — the company's pivot to orexin-based neuroscience produced ORX750, which demonstrated 87% weekly cataplexy rate reduction in Phase 2a NT1 patients, a potential best-in-class profile that attracted Eli Lilly's $6.3B acquisition offer in March 2026 [1][2]. The pipeline extends beyond ORX750 with ORX142 (neurodegenerative) and ORX489 (neuropsychiatric) entering clinical studies in 2026, all built on proprietary OX2R agonist chemistry. Cash of $349M funds operations into mid-2027. With the Lilly deal pending, the stock trades near the acquisition price, limiting both upside and downside for new positions.
The bear case centers on acquisition risk — if the Lilly deal fails to close due to regulatory objections or other factors, CNTA would face significant downside as a pre-revenue biotech with negative ROE of -42.6% and $193M annual cash burn [4]. The company has governance baggage including a securities lawsuit over misleading statements about discontinued programs (lixivaptan and ZF874) and the departure of co-founder Moncef Slaoui following harassment allegations [5]. Even if the deal closes, the $6.3B price may undervalue the orexin platform if ORX750 achieves blockbuster status in narcolepsy (estimated $2-4B peak revenue opportunity). For investors who already own CNTA, the deal provides a clean exit; for new positions, the risk-reward is primarily a merger arbitrage play rather than a fundamental investment.
Key Catalysts
  1. Eli Lilly acquisition at ~$6.3B expected to close in 2026, providing near-term price support and validating the orexin platform's commercial potential
  2. ORX750 registrational program initiating Q1 2026, with Phase 2a data showing best-in-class 87% WCR reduction in NT1 — positive Phase 3 readouts could drive deal sweeteners or standalone upside if the acquisition fails
  3. ORX142 and ORX489 entering clinical studies in Q1 2026, expanding the orexin platform into neurodegenerative and neuropsychiatric indications beyond sleep-wake disorders
Key Risks
  1. Eli Lilly acquisition failure would expose a pre-revenue biotech burning $193M annually with no near-term path to profitability, likely triggering a 50%+ stock decline
  2. Securities litigation history — Centessa faced lawsuits over allegedly misleading statements about lixivaptan and ZF874 safety profiles, raising governance concerns about management credibility despite dismissal of the lawsuit
  3. Clinical-stage binary risk: ORX750 Phase 3 failure would eliminate the company's primary value driver and likely strand the remaining pipeline without the Lilly acquisition infrastructure
