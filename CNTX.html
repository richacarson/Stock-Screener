Company Profile
Healthcare ·Biotechnology ·NASDAQ ·United States
Context Therapeutics is a clinical-stage biopharmaceutical company focused on developing next-generation T-cell engaging (TCE) bispecific antibody therapies for solid tumors. The pipeline includes CTIM-76 (Claudin-6 x CD3) for ovarian, endometrial, and testicular cancers, CT-95 (Mesothelin x CD3) for multiple solid tumors, and CT-202 (Nectin-4 x CD3) in preclinical development. Founded in 2015 and headquartered in Philadelphia, PA.
50 Employees ·www.contexttherapeutics.com

Investment Thesis
Context Therapeutics offers high-risk, high-reward exposure to the rapidly expanding TCE bispecific antibody space for solid tumors. The company has three clinical programs with defined catalysts in 2026: CTIM-76 Phase 1a interim data in June, CT-95 interim data in September, and CT-202 first patient in Q3 [1]. Early CTIM-76 data is encouraging — 12 patients treated with preliminary anti-tumor activity and no dose-limiting toxicities or Grade 2+ CRS [1]. With $66M cash runway into mid-2027, the company is funded through all three catalysts. At $208M market cap, positive clinical readouts could drive significant upside if any program demonstrates meaningful efficacy in hard-to-treat solid tumors.
The bear case is the fundamental reality of pre-revenue clinical-stage biotech: binary risk with asymmetric downside. Context burned $36.1M in 2025 (up 35% from 2024) with zero revenue and no path to profitability until at least 2029-2030 at the earliest [1]. The $66M cash position requires successful data readouts to raise additional capital — a negative trial result would likely require severe dilution or could threaten the company's viability. The TCE bispecific space is fiercely competitive, with Amgen, Roche, and other well-capitalized players pursuing similar Claudin-6, Mesothelin, and Nectin-4 targets with greater resources and manufacturing scale [2]. ROE of -46.4% and $0.38 loss per share reflect the capital-consumptive reality of clinical-stage drug development. At $2.26 per share with negative forward P/E, the stock is a speculative option on clinical data, not an investment in a functioning business [4].
