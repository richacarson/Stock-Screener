Company Profile
Technology ·Semiconductors & Photonics ·NYSE ·United States
Coherent Corp. is a global leader in photonics, advanced materials, and optical networking solutions. The company designs and manufactures lasers, optical transceivers, silicon carbide substrates, and advanced fiber optics serving data center, telecommunications, industrial, and electronics markets. Formed from the 2022 merger of II-VI Incorporated and Coherent Inc., the company is a critical supplier of optical interconnect technology for AI data centers. Multi-year strategic partnership with NVIDIA includes multi-billion-dollar purchase commitments. Market cap of $74.6 billion.
29,000 Employees ·www.coherent.com

Investment Thesis
Coherent is the premier pure-play beneficiary of AI datacenter optical networking buildout, with a record backlog, 4x book-to-bill ratio, and customer commitments extending to 2028+ [1]. Q3 FY2026 revenue of $1.81B (+20.5% YoY) with non-GAAP EPS of $1.41 (+55% YoY) demonstrates accelerating growth, and Q4 guidance of $1.91-$2.05B confirms continued momentum [1]. The multi-billion-dollar NVIDIA partnership positions Coherent as the optical backbone of next-generation AI infrastructure. Vertical integration from raw materials through modules creates barriers to entry that competitors cannot easily replicate. Management expects FY2027 growth to exceed FY2026, supported by contracted demand and capacity expansion. At PEG of 1.14, the stock is reasonably valued relative to its growth trajectory, and the 78% renewable energy achievement aligns with sustainable business practices [3].
The primary risk is valuation and cyclicality. At 51.21x forward P/E and $74.6B market cap on $6.6B TTM revenue, Coherent is priced for flawless execution of AI demand projections [4]. The stock has risen 369% in 52 weeks, creating substantial downside risk if hyperscaler CapEx spending pauses or slows. Free cash flow is negative (-$538M) due to massive capacity investments ($678.6M CapEx), meaning the company is consuming cash to fund growth [4]. Customer concentration risk is significant — increasing dependence on NVIDIA and a handful of hyperscalers creates vulnerability to demand cycles. The II-VI/Coherent merger integration is still ongoing with cultural challenges reflected in 59% Glassdoor recommendation rate [2]. Historical cyclicality in the photonics industry suggests that the current AI-driven demand boom will eventually moderate, and capacity built during the boom could become stranded if demand normalizes. Export controls on advanced photonics could disrupt supply chains.

  3. Customer concentration in NVIDIA and hyperscalers (Microsoft, Google, Meta) creates vulnerability to demand cycles and pricing pressure from a small number of critical buyers
