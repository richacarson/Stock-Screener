Company Profile
Technology ·Scientific & Technical Instruments ·NYSE ·United States
Coherent Corp. is a global leader in materials, networking, and lasers, manufacturing advanced optical components including indium phosphide lasers, transceivers, and optical circuit switches essential for AI datacenter interconnects. The company operates through three segments: Networking (72% of revenue), Materials, and Lasers. A $2B strategic partnership with NVIDIA cements Coherent as a primary architect of optical interconnects for AI clusters.

Investment Thesis
Coherent is the optical backbone of the AI revolution — the company's indium phosphide lasers, 800G/1.6T transceivers, and optical circuit switches are essential physical infrastructure for every AI datacenter being built globally [1][2]. Q2 FY2026 revenue of $1.69B grew 17% YoY (22% pro forma), with datacenter & communications surging 33.5% to 72% of total revenue [1]. Non-GAAP EPS of $1.29 beat consensus by 13%, and the $2B NVIDIA strategic partnership validates Coherent's technology as mission-critical for next-generation AI clusters [2]. CEO Jim Anderson brings a proven 10x stock price turnaround track record from Lattice Semiconductor. At 32.8x forward P/E with ~30% earnings growth (PEG ~1.1x), Coherent offers AI infrastructure exposure at a reasonable valuation relative to the growth trajectory. Management expects strong growth through FY2027 with capacity expansion underway for the 1.6T transition [1].
The bear case centers on cyclicality, concentration, and profitability. GAAP profit margin of 4.66% and ROE of 3.24% are weak, reflecting $3.5B in acquisition-related debt and ongoing amortization from the Finisar/Coherent deals [4]. The deep NVIDIA dependency (blockbuster $2B partnership) is a double-edged sword — Coherent's fortunes are increasingly tied to a single customer's product cycles and capex decisions [2]. Hyperscaler capex pauses could create significant revenue 'air pockets' — datacenter & communications at 72% of revenue creates concentration risk if AI buildout slows [1]. The 6-inch indium phosphide wafer ramp and 1.6T transceiver capacity expansion carry execution risk — yield, timing, or quality missteps could cede share to Lumentum and Broadcom [2]. Trailing P/E of 239x reflects GAAP earnings still suppressed by acquisition charges; any miss in the earnings recovery trajectory could trigger a sharp de-rating from the current $46B market cap. Insider selling of $4.1M in March 2026 by CTO and directors, while likely routine, is notable [4].

  3. 6-inch indium phosphide wafer ramp execution risk — yield, quality, or timing missteps in the critical 1.6T transition could cede market share to Lumentum, Broadcom, and emerging competitors [2]
