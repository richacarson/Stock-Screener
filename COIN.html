Investment Thesis
Coinbase is the dominant regulated US crypto exchange with S&P 500 inclusion, $11.6B in cash, all-time high trading volumes (up 156% YoY), and a diversified revenue base of 12 products generating $100M+ each. Subscription and services revenue of $2.8B (up 5.5x from 2021) reduces cyclicality, while the Deribit acquisition and AI-managed transactions open new growth vectors. The favorable regulatory shift — SEC dismissal with prejudice and pro-crypto administration — removes the largest historical overhang.
The bear case is severe: net income fell 51% in 2025, Q4 showed a GAAP loss, and analyst EPS estimates for 2026 have been cut 42% in the past month. The 44x trailing P/E and 33x forward P/E price in aggressive crypto market recovery that may not materialize. Revenue remains highly correlated to crypto trading volumes and Bitcoin price, creating extreme earnings volatility. The $7.9B debt load from Deribit acquisition and Armstrong's aggressive AI mandate (firing resistors) create operational risks. Crypto's inherent volatility makes sustainable earnings power difficult to forecast.
