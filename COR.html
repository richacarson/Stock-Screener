Company Profile
Healthcare ·Medical Distribution ·NYSE ·United States
Cencora (formerly AmerisourceBergen) sources and distributes pharmaceutical products in the United States and internationally through two segments: U.S. Healthcare Solutions and International Healthcare Solutions. Founded in 1871, the company distributes generics, specialty pharmaceuticals, biologics, and animal health products, and recently acquired OneOncology to expand into oncology practice management.

Investment Thesis
Cencora is a $60B pharmaceutical distribution leader trading at 15.9x forward P/E with a PEG of 0.74, reflecting undervaluation relative to its defensive growth profile [4]. Q1 FY2026 revenue of $85.9B (up 5.5% YoY) with adjusted diluted EPS of $4.08 (up 9.4%) demonstrates steady execution, and management raised FY2026 adjusted operating income guidance to 11.5-13.5% growth following the OneOncology acquisition [1]. The 'Big Three' pharmaceutical distribution oligopoly provides structural competitive advantages through scale, regulatory barriers, and entrenched customer relationships. Free cash flow of $3.5B and operating cash flow of $4.3B provide substantial capital return capacity despite the dividend yield of only 0.77% [4]. FY2026 revenue guidance of 7-9% growth on a $326B base represents massive dollar-value expansion.
The bear case centers on margin vulnerability, leverage, and legacy liabilities. Profit margin of 0.5% is structurally thin — pharmaceutical distribution is a low-margin, high-volume business where any disruption to the wholesaler model (manufacturer direct-to-pharmacy, Amazon Pharmacy expansion) could be devastating [4]. D/E of 473% reflects the capital structure burden of a $326B revenue business, and the OneOncology acquisition further increased leverage while halting share repurchases. The trailing P/E of 37.4x vs. forward P/E of 15.9x suggests significant earnings seasonality or one-time items that complicate valuation. The opioid settlement created ongoing compliance costs and reputational damage, and any new opioid-related litigation could resurface financial and brand risk [3]. The company's Inspire Impact Score of -75 (MISALIGNED) reflects controversial product distribution categories.
