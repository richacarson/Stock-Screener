Investment Thesis
Costco delivered Q2 FY2026 revenue of $69.6B (up 9.3%) with 7% comparable sales growth, EPS of $4.58 (up 14%), and membership fee revenue surging 14% from new sign-ups and fee increases [1][2]. Renewal rates of 92.1% (US/Canada) and 89.7% (worldwide) are among the stickiest recurring revenue streams in consumer retail [2]. Digital sales grew 22.6% in Q2, showing the omnichannel strategy is working while preserving the in-warehouse treasure-hunt experience [1]. CEO Ron Vachris — who rose from forklift driver to the top job — is preserving the founder culture while thoughtfully deploying AI 'the Costco way' for member loyalty and efficiency [5]. The plan to open 30+ clubs per year provides a clear multi-year growth runway, and the $254B revenue base supports continued 8-10% growth [2][5].
The bear case centers on valuation. At 43.4x forward P/E, Costco is priced at a premium to most retail peers — a 8-10% revenue grower with 3% profit margins needs to keep compounding to justify the multiple [7]. The membership fee price hike is a short-term tailwind that will lap unfavorably. Tariff and inflation pressure on imported goods could squeeze the thin product margin if Costco chooses to absorb costs rather than pass them to members (likely choice, given culture) [3]. ESG concerns include lagging deforestation commitments vs. retail peers (Walmart, Target, Kroger) and Mighty Earth / NRDC campaigns on supply chain exposure [6]. Inspire Impact Score of -62 reflects Abortifacients concern despite a deep list of 14 positive attributions on labor, supply chain ethics, energy management, and product sustainability. Faith-aligned investors should weigh the misalignment carefully.
Key Catalysts
  1. Continued membership fee growth at 14% — recurring revenue flywheel with 92%+ renewal rates compounds earnings power [2]
  2. 30+ new warehouses per year plus international expansion providing a multi-year high-single-digit revenue growth runway [2]
  3. Digital/omnichannel momentum at 22.6% growth blending with in-warehouse experience without cannibalizing core traffic [1]
Key Risks
  1. Valuation risk — 43.4x forward P/E leaves little margin for disappointment; any slowdown in comp sales could trigger multiple compression [7]
  2. Tariff and inflation pressure on imported Kirkland and branded goods — Costco culture will absorb rather than pass, pressuring thin 3% margin [3]
  3. Deforestation and supply chain ESG concerns — Costco lags Walmart, Target, Amazon, Kroger on published deforestation commitments [6]
