Company Profile
Consumer Defensive ·Household & Personal Products ·NYSE ·United States
Coty is a global beauty company operating through Prestige (68% of revenue) and Consumer Beauty segments. The Prestige portfolio includes fragrances under licensed brands such as Burberry, Calvin Klein, Hugo Boss, Marc Jacobs, and Gucci, plus owned brands like philosophy and Lancaster. Consumer Beauty includes CoverGirl, Rimmel, Sally Hansen, and Max Factor. Coty is a subsidiary of JAB Beauty B.V.

Investment Thesis
Coty trades at a deep value multiple of 5.3x forward earnings, reflecting severe pessimism after CEO departure, withdrawn guidance, and 70%+ stock decline [4]. The Prestige fragrance portfolio (Burberry, Calvin Klein, Hugo Boss, Marc Jacobs) generates 68% of revenue with stronger margins than Consumer Beauty, and the $750M Wella divestiture reduced leverage from 3.7x to 2.7x net debt/EBITDA [1]. H1 FY2026 free cash flow of $524M exceeds prior year, demonstrating underlying cash generation capacity despite revenue headwinds. Interim CEO Strobel's turnaround plan targeting procurement savings, innovation pipeline streamlining, and Consumer Beauty brand repositioning could unlock value if executed — the stock prices in no recovery.
The bear case is substantial and multi-layered. Coty faces securities fraud class action lawsuits from multiple law firms alleging misleading disclosures about growth prospects while the business deteriorated, with a May 2026 lead plaintiff deadline [3]. Consumer Beauty revenue declined 6% like-for-like in Q2 FY2026, and the mass-market segment faces structural competitive pressure from indie beauty brands and private label [1]. The loss of the Gucci beauty license in 2028 removes a significant prestige revenue stream that will be difficult to replace. GAAP net loss of $126.9M in Q2 FY2026 (vs. $20.4M profit a year earlier) and negative ROE of -12.6% demonstrate the depth of financial deterioration [1]. The interim CEO structure creates leadership uncertainty — permanent CEO search adds another variable. JAB Beauty's majority ownership limits governance independence and may not align with minority shareholder interests [2].

AI Risk Protection (6/9): Coty's core business — manufacturing and selling physical beauty products (fragrances, cosmetics, skincare) — has meaningful defensive moats against AI disruption.
