Investment Thesis
Coursera is a mission-driven edtech platform trading at a compelling 10.8x forward P/E with $793M cash (80% of market cap) and approaching profitability, guided for FY2026 revenue of $805–$815M (6-8% growth) with adjusted EBITDA of $70–$76M [1]. The pending Udemy merger would create the world's largest online learning platform with $115M in annual cost synergies, dramatically improving the combined entity's margin profile [1]. New CEO Greg Hart (ex-Amazon Alexa, 23 years at Amazon, technical advisor to Bezos) brings AI-native product thinking, while Chairman Andrew Ng provides unmatched AI credibility in the education market [3]. The demand for AI skills training is a secular tailwind — Coursera's AI-related courses are among its fastest-growing offerings, and the 15% Platform Fee effective January 2026 should improve monetization of the marketplace model [1].
The bear case centers on AI disruption risk and growth deceleration. Revenue growth has slowed from 20%+ post-IPO to 6-8% guidance for FY2026, with enterprise net retention at a concerning 93% (below 100% implies net contraction in existing accounts) [1]. AI-powered tutors like ChatGPT, Khanmigo, and Google's education tools threaten to displace structured online courses by providing personalized, free-form learning at near-zero cost [3]. The $985M market cap and GAAP losses raise viability questions if the Udemy merger fails or integration proves difficult. Consumer segment growth of 12% in Q4 may not sustain as AI alternatives proliferate. The edtech market is brutally competitive with LinkedIn Learning (Microsoft), Google Career Certificates, and university-direct offerings all vying for the same learners. Coursera's 55% gross margin is mediocre for a software platform, reflecting the revenue share with university partners that compresses economics [5].
Key Catalysts
  1. Udemy merger creating the world's largest online learning platform with $115M in annual cost synergies achievable within 24 months, transforming the margin profile [1]
  2. AI skills training demand — secular tailwind as enterprises invest in upskilling workforces for AI adoption, with Coursera's Generative AI Academy and Andrew Ng's AI credibility as differentiators [3]
  3. Profitability inflection — adjusted EBITDA guidance of $70–$76M for FY2026 with 15% Platform Fee and operating leverage improvements pointing toward sustainable profitability [1]
Key Risks
  1. AI disruption — ChatGPT, Khanmigo, and AI-powered learning tools threaten to commoditize structured online courses, and enterprise net retention of 93% suggests some customers are already finding alternatives [1][3]
  2. Growth deceleration from 20%+ (post-IPO) to 6-8% FY2026 guidance with sub-$1B market cap raises questions about platform viability and investor patience [1]
  3. Udemy merger integration risk — combining two edtech platforms with different content models, cultures, and cost structures could distract management and delay synergy realization [1]

AI Resilience (25%)
AI Resilience NEUTRAL
5
AI Risk Protection (4/9): AI poses a dual-edged existential risk to Coursera. On one hand, AI tutoring tools (ChatGPT, Google's AI-powered education tools, Khan Academy's Khanmigo) could displace structured online courses by providing personalized, on-demand learning at zero marginal cost. AI-generated content could commoditize the course creation that Coursera's university partners provide [3]. On the other hand, Coursera is actively leveraging AI through its Generative AI Academy, AI-powered recommendations, and the credentialing/certification layer that pure AI tutors cannot replicate. Enterprise net retention at 93% suggests some customers are already finding alternatives [1]. AI Opportunity (1/1): CEO Greg Hart's Amazon Alexa background and Andrew Ng's AI expertise position Coursera to lead AI-enhanced education, and the demand for AI skills training is a direct growth driver — AI-related courses are among Coursera's fastest-growing categories [3].
