Company Profile
Healthcare ·Biotechnology ·NASDAQ ·United States
Catalyst Pharmaceuticals is a commercial-stage biopharmaceutical company focused on developing and commercializing therapies for rare neurological and neuromuscular diseases. The company's portfolio includes FIRDAPSE (amifampridine) for Lambert-Eaton myasthenic syndrome, AGAMREE (vamorolone) for Duchenne muscular dystrophy, and FYCOMPA (perampanel) for epilepsy.

Investment Thesis
Catalyst Pharmaceuticals is a rare disease franchise with exceptional financial metrics — 36.4% profit margin, 25.5% ROE, virtually no debt (D/E 0.3%), and $690M cash on hand — trading at just 9.1x forward earnings [1][4]. The company delivered record 2025 revenue of $589M (up 19.8% YoY) and guides to $615-645M in 2026 with promoted product growth of 20%+ [1]. FIRDAPSE dominates the LEMS market and AGAMREE is scaling rapidly in DMD ($140-150M guided for 2026 from $82M in its first nine months). The company's rare disease focus provides durable competitive advantages: orphan drug exclusivity, small patient populations that deter generics, and deep physician relationships. With $690M cash and active evaluation of 100+ acquisition targets, Catalyst has firepower for portfolio expansion. At 9.1x forward P/E with a 35.9% 5yr revenue CAGR, the stock is materially undervalued relative to its growth profile and cash generation.
The primary risk is FIRDAPSE concentration and generic threat. FIRDAPSE represents approximately 70% of revenue ($435-450M guided for 2026), creating single-product dependency [1]. The Hetero generic trial scheduled for March 2026 could invalidate patent settlements expiring in 2035, potentially introducing generic competition years earlier and collapsing FIRDAPSE revenue. The historical FIRDAPSE pricing controversy — charging $80,000/year for a compound previously available at a fraction of the cost — creates ongoing reputational and political risk, particularly as drug pricing scrutiny intensifies [3]. Revenue growth is decelerating from 19.8% in 2025 to guided 7-10% in 2026 (though promoted products grow 20%+), and EPS growth was negative on a TTM basis (-6.4%). The thin internal R&D pipeline means growth depends on acquisitions rather than organic innovation, creating execution and integration risk. CEO Daly is relatively new (appointed January 2024) and the strategic direction post-McEnany remains uncertain. FYCOMPA faces patent expiration pressure that could erode its $40-45M revenue contribution.

  3. Drug pricing scrutiny — FIRDAPSE's history of pricing a previously available compound at $80,000/year creates political and reputational risk as pricing reform gains momentum [3]
