Company Profile
Financial Services ·Insurance - Life ·NYSE ·United States
Corebridge Financial is one of the largest providers of retirement solutions and insurance products in the United States, with $385 billion in assets under management and administration. Spun off from AIG in 2022, the company offers annuities, life insurance, retirement plans, and wealth management services. Formerly known as AIG Life & Retirement.

Investment Thesis
Corebridge Financial trades at a strikingly low 4.1x forward earnings for one of the largest US retirement solutions providers with $385B AUM and record $42B in annual product sales [1]. The company generated adjusted ATOI of $2.4B in 2025, returned $2.6B to shareholders (110% payout ratio including $2.1B buybacks), and is simplifying its business model by exiting variable annuities and asset management. The 4.02% dividend yield with consecutive increases signals income appeal. AIG's stake reduction to ~5% removes the overhang of forced selling and positions Corebridge as a fully independent company. Q4 2025 revenue surged 35.7% YoY, and the RILA product line is growing 13% [1].
The bear case centers on GAAP financial opacity and cultural dysfunction. GAAP profit margin of -1.97% and ROE of -2.97% obscure the underlying economics, making it difficult for investors to assess true earnings quality [4]. The 43% Glassdoor recommendation rate — among the lowest in financial services — signals deep cultural issues including outsourcing, leadership instability, and a lack of employee trust [2]. CEO Marc Costantini is Corebridge's third CEO in four years as a public company, creating strategic continuity risk. The insurance industry faces secular pressure from rising interest rate volatility, potential annuity regulation changes, and insurtech disruption. Revenue growth of 5.7% is modest for a company trading at such a low multiple, suggesting the market sees structural challenges. The $2.6B shareholder return at 110% payout ratio may not be sustainable if adjusted earnings decline.
Key Catalysts
  1. Full AIG separation — reduction to ~5% stake eliminates forced selling overhang and enables fully independent governance and strategic decision-making [3]
  2. Simplified business model post-VA reinsurance and SunAmerica divestiture, focusing on capital-light retirement products with more predictable earnings streams [1]
  3. RILA product growth at 13% premiums increase, positioning Corebridge in the fastest-growing annuity category as baby boomers enter peak retirement years [1]
Key Risks
  1. Cultural dysfunction — 43% Glassdoor recommendation rate, outsourcing concerns, and leadership instability with three CEOs in four years threaten operational execution and talent retention [2]
  2. GAAP earnings opacity — insurance accounting makes it difficult to assess true earnings quality; GAAP profit margin and ROE are negative despite strong adjusted metrics, creating investor uncertainty [4]
  3. Interest rate sensitivity — annuity product economics are highly sensitive to rate movements; a prolonged rate decline could compress spreads and reduce the attractiveness of fixed annuity products relative to alternatives [1]
