Company Profile
Energy ·Oil & Gas E&P ·NYSE ·United States
California Resources Corporation is an independent energy and carbon management company operating exclusively within California. It is the largest oil and natural gas E&P company in California on a gross-operated basis, with operations in two segments: Oil and Natural Gas, and Carbon Management (Carbon TerraVault CCS). The company was incorporated in 2014 and is based in Long Beach, California.

Investment Thesis
CRC offers a differentiated energy investment as California's largest oil producer with an emerging carbon management platform. The 42% increase in 2026 EBITDAX guidance to $1,450M midpoint reflects operational momentum from increased drilling activity and improved efficiencies [1]. At 14.4× forward P/E with $1,276M liquidity and a growing dividend (2.3% yield), CRC provides value characteristics with optionality from Carbon TerraVault CCS — a potential multi-billion dollar asset as carbon credit markets mature [1]. Production of 154 MBoe/d (81% oil) with plans to exit 2026 at ~175 MBoe/d demonstrates growth trajectory.
The bear case centers on California concentration risk: the state's aggressive regulatory environment increases compliance costs and could restrict future drilling permits. Revenue declined 13.8% YoY and EPS fell 61.5%, with Q1 2026 sales missing analyst estimates despite the EBITDAX guidance raise [1]. Glassdoor reviews revealing toxic culture (2.9/5, 38% recommend) and retaliation dynamics could impair execution during the critical CCS buildout phase [3]. Carbon TerraVault's commercial viability depends on carbon credit pricing and regulatory frameworks that remain uncertain. The company's post-bankruptcy history (emerged 2020) and limited operating track record under current management add execution risk.
Key Catalysts
  1. 42% EBITDAX guidance raise to $1,450M midpoint with accelerated H2 2026 drilling activity building momentum into 2027 [1]
  2. Carbon TerraVault CCS commercialization progress as carbon credit markets develop under California's cap-and-trade system
  3. Production growth trajectory targeting ~175 MBoe/d exit rate in 2026, representing ~1% entry-to-exit growth with upside from expanded drilling [1]
Key Risks
  1. California regulatory concentration risk — restrictive drilling permits, environmental regulations, and compliance costs could constrain production growth and margins
  2. Commodity price sensitivity with 81% oil production mix and no geographic diversification to hedge against California-specific pricing dislocations [1]
  3. Severely impaired organizational culture (2.9/5 Glassdoor, 38% recommend) and toxic leadership dynamics could undermine execution of the CCS transformation strategy [3]
