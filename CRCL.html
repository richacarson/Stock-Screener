Company Profile
Financial Services ·Financial Technology ·NYSE ·United States
Circle Internet Group is the issuer of USDC, the world's second-largest stablecoin with $75.3B in circulation at year-end 2025 (up 72% YoY). The company went public in June 2025 at $31/share (IPO), and earns revenue primarily from interest income on the U.S. Treasury reserves backing USDC. FY2025 total revenue and reserve income was $2.7B, up 64% YoY, with Q4 2025 net income of $133M. USDC onchain transaction volume in Q4 2025 was $11.9 trillion, up 247% YoY.

Investment Thesis
Circle is the leading regulated stablecoin infrastructure company at a historical moment of institutional and regulatory adoption. The GENIUS Act codifies USDC's compliance framework into law, Visa and Intuit integrations create real-world payment utility, and $75.3B in circulation growing 72% YoY represents a rapidly expanding interest income base [1][2]. The $29.5B market cap at 14.5× P/S appears stretched but is supported by 64% revenue growth, 77% Q4 YoY growth, and a path toward $9B in USDC reserve income by 2029 per institutional projections. The stablecoin TAM is projected at $1.9T by 2030.
The primary structural risk is Coinbase: Coinbase earns approximately 56% of Circle's USDC reserve revenue — a dependency that could be renegotiated, creating significant earnings risk [2]. Any interest rate decline materially reduces USDC reserve income (the primary revenue driver), since the reserve income depends on U.S. Treasury rates applied to $75B+ in reserves. At a forward P/E of approximately 60×, the stock has priced in substantial optimism; any execution miss or multiple compression would be painful. The GENIUS Act regulation could also enable bank-issued stablecoins that compete with USDC.
Key Catalysts
  1. USDC circulation growth above $100B — would unlock a step-change in reserve income and validate Circle's stablecoin dominance in the regulated category
  2. Coinbase revenue sharing renegotiation reducing the ~56% distribution cost — would significantly improve Circle's net revenue retention and profitability
  3. Circle Payments Network (CPN) scaling beyond $5.7B annualized to $100B+ — would diversify revenue away from interest income and reduce rate sensitivity
Key Risks
  1. Coinbase dependency: ~56% of USDC gross revenue distributed to Coinbase — any change in this arrangement (upward or downward) creates significant earnings uncertainty
  2. Interest rate sensitivity: a 100bps Fed funds rate decline would reduce USDC reserve income by approximately $750M+ annually based on $75B in circulation
  3. Regulatory risk: while the GENIUS Act is positive, it also enables bank-issued stablecoins that could challenge USDC's position in the regulated stablecoin market
