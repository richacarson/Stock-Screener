Company Profile
Financial Services ·Capital Markets ·NYSE ·United States
Circle Internet Group is the issuer of USDC, the world's second-largest stablecoin with $75.3B in circulation at year-end 2025 (up 72% YoY). The company went public in June 2025 at $31/share (IPO) and earns revenue primarily from interest income on the U.S. Treasury reserves backing USDC. FY2025 total revenue and reserve income was $2.7B (+64% YoY). Q1 2026 revenue was $694.1M with net income of $55.3M. CEO Jeremy Allaire was named to the TIME100 list in 2026, and former AWS CEO Adam Selipsky joined the board.

Investment Thesis
Circle is the leading regulated stablecoin infrastructure company at a pivotal moment of institutional adoption. The GENIUS Act codifies USDC's compliance framework into law, Visa and Stripe integrations create real-world payment utility, and $75.3B in circulation growing 72% YoY represents a rapidly expanding interest income base[1]. Q1 2026 showed a turn to profitability with $55.3M net income, and FY2026 revenue is forecast to grow ~21.5% with earnings growth of ~52.6%. The stablecoin TAM is projected at $1.9T by 2030, giving Circle a massive runway if it maintains regulatory advantage and USDC adoption momentum.
The primary structural risk is Coinbase dependency: Coinbase earns approximately 50%+ of Circle's USDC reserve revenue under the distribution agreement — a concentration that creates significant earnings risk if renegotiated upward[1]. Interest rate sensitivity is acute: a 100bps Fed funds decline would reduce USDC reserve income by ~$750M+ annually. At forward P/E of ~50x and PEG of 4.34, the stock prices substantial optimism with no margin of safety. The Drift Protocol hack ($230M cross-chain exploit) and related class-action lawsuit create legal and reputational overhang[4]. Operating expenses jumped 76% in FY2025, raising concerns about cost discipline as the company scales. The GENIUS Act may also enable bank-issued stablecoins that compete with USDC using superior retail distribution.
Key Catalysts
  1. USDC circulation growth above $100B in 2026 would unlock a step-change in reserve income and validate Circle's dominance in the regulated stablecoin category
  2. Sustained quarterly profitability through FY2026 after Q1 2026's $55.3M net income, proving the business model works beyond the high-rate environment
  3. Circle Payments Network (CPN) scaling beyond $5.7B annualized volume to diversify revenue away from interest income and reduce rate sensitivity
Key Risks
  1. Coinbase revenue-sharing dependency: any renegotiation increasing distribution costs would directly compress Circle's net revenue retention and profitability
  2. Interest rate sensitivity: Fed rate cuts would reduce USDC reserve income — the primary revenue driver — by approximately $750M+ per 100bps on $75B+ in circulation
  3. Legal and security risk: the Drift Protocol hack class-action lawsuit and ARC Token governance conflicts of interest create legal liability and regulatory scrutiny overhang
