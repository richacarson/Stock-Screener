Company Profile
Financial Services ·Capital Markets ·NYSE ·United States
Circle Internet Group is the issuer of USDC, the world's second-largest stablecoin with $77 billion in circulation as of Q1 2026 (up 28% YoY). The company went public in 2025 and earns revenue primarily from interest income on U.S. Treasury reserves backing USDC. Q1 2026 revenue was $694 million with USDC onchain transaction volume of $21.5 trillion. Circle is also building the Arc blockchain network and Circle Payments Network for programmable financial infrastructure.

Investment Thesis
Circle is the leading regulated stablecoin infrastructure company at a pivotal moment of institutional adoption. Q1 2026 revenue of $694 million grew 20% YoY, USDC circulation reached $77 billion (up 28%), and onchain transaction volume surged to $21.5 trillion (up 263%) [1]. Adjusted EBITDA of $151 million grew 24% YoY, and the company is building new revenue streams through the Arc blockchain network, ARC token ($222 million presale), and the Agent Stack for AI-native economic activity. The stock has corrected 50% from its highs to $111, creating a more reasonable entry point at 49.7x forward earnings. The GENIUS Act codifies USDC's compliance framework into law, creating a regulatory moat against non-compliant competitors.
The bear case is substantial: Circle's revenue is fundamentally interest income on U.S. Treasury reserves — any Fed rate cuts directly compress revenue, with a 100bps decline potentially reducing annual income by $770M+ on $77 billion in reserves [3]. Forward P/E of 49.7x and PEG of 4.34 price in extraordinary growth that may not materialize in a declining rate environment. Net income of $55 million in Q1 2026 was down 15% YoY despite revenue growth, as stock-based compensation and infrastructure investment weighed on profitability (-2.8% net margin). The March 2026 wallet blacklist controversy triggered a 20% single-session collapse, exposing centralization risk that undermines the decentralization value proposition [3]. The GENIUS Act also enables bank-issued stablecoins that could challenge USDC's dominance, and Coinbase distribution costs remain a significant drag on net revenue retention.
Key Catalysts
  1. USDC circulation growth toward $100 billion milestone, with management projecting 40% CAGR through the cycle, which would drive proportional reserve income growth assuming stable interest rates
  2. Arc blockchain network and Agent Stack achieving meaningful traction in the AI agent economy, diversifying revenue away from interest income dependency
  3. Circle Payments Network scaling beyond the $5.7 billion annualized run rate toward enterprise adoption, creating fee-based recurring revenue that reduces interest rate sensitivity
Key Risks
  1. Interest rate sensitivity: a 100bps Fed funds rate decline would reduce USDC reserve income by approximately $770M+ annually, directly compressing the primary revenue driver on $77B in circulation
  2. Centralization risk: the March 2026 wallet blacklist of 16 business wallets triggered a 20% stock collapse and reignited debate over whether USDC's centralized control model is sustainable long-term
  3. Regulatory competition: the GENIUS Act enables bank-issued stablecoins (JPMorgan, Bank of America) that could challenge USDC's position in the regulated stablecoin market with existing customer bases and trust
