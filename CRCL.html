Company Profile
Financial Services ·Capital Markets ·NYSE ·United States
Circle Internet Group is the issuer of USDC, the world's second-largest stablecoin with $77B in circulation (up 28% YoY). The company went public in June 2025, and earns revenue primarily from interest income on U.S. Treasury reserves backing USDC. Q1 2026 total revenue and reserve income was $694M (up 20% YoY) with adjusted EBITDA of $151M. USDC onchain transaction volume surged 263% YoY to $21.5T in Q1 2026. Recently received conditional OCC approval for a national trust bank charter.

Investment Thesis
Circle is the leading regulated stablecoin infrastructure company at a historical moment of institutional and regulatory adoption. Q1 2026 revenue of $694M grew 20% YoY with adjusted EBITDA of $151M at 53% margin, while USDC circulation reached $77B (up 28% YoY) and onchain transaction volume surged 263% to $21.5T [1]. The OCC conditional approval for a national trust bank charter and first-mover MiCA compliance create deepening regulatory moats. The Circle Agent Stack positions USDC as the default payment rail for AI-to-AI agentic commerce (99.8% of x402-protocol transactions), representing a potentially transformative new revenue stream. Management affirms a long-term 40% CAGR target for USDC circulation [1].
The primary structural risk is interest rate dependency: the majority of Circle's revenue comes from interest income on ~$77B in U.S. Treasury reserves — a 100bps Fed funds rate decline would reduce annual revenue by ~$770M [1]. Coinbase remains a critical distribution partner consuming a significant share of USDC gross revenue, creating concentration risk if terms are renegotiated unfavorably. At 57x forward earnings and 4.3x PEG, the stock is priced for perfection with minimal margin of safety; the current negative profit margin and -3.0% ROE mean profitability has not yet been proven at scale as a public company [4]. The CLARITY Act stablecoin regulation, while broadly positive, could enable well-capitalized bank-issued competitors (JPMorgan, Goldman) to enter the regulated stablecoin market with superior distribution and trust [3].
Key Catalysts
  1. USDC circulation growth above $100B, validating the 40% CAGR target and unlocking step-change reserve income that could drive profitability inflection
  2. Circle Agent Stack scaling to meaningful revenue as agentic AI commerce grows, diversifying beyond interest income and reducing rate sensitivity
  3. OCC national trust bank charter full approval, elevating Circle's regulatory status to a federally supervised institution and deepening competitive moat against unregulated stablecoin issuers
Key Risks
  1. Interest rate sensitivity: a 100bps Fed funds rate decline would reduce USDC reserve income by ~$770M annually, directly threatening the path to profitability given current -2.8% profit margin
  2. Extreme valuation: 57x forward earnings and 4.3x PEG leave no margin of safety — any execution miss, revenue slowdown, or market re-rating of crypto assets would trigger severe multiple compression
  3. Regulatory competition: CLARITY Act and GENIUS Act stablecoin frameworks could enable bank-issued competitors (JPMorgan, Goldman Sachs) with superior distribution, brand trust, and existing customer relationships to challenge USDC's institutional market share
