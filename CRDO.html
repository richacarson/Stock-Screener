Company Profile
Technology ·Semiconductors ·NASDAQ ·Cayman Islands
Credo Technology provides high-speed connectivity solutions for AI, cloud, and hyperscale data center networks. The company designs active electrical cables (AECs), optical PAM4 DSPs, SerDes chiplets, line card PHYs, and SerDes IP licensing, serving hyperscalers, OEMs, and optical module manufacturers. Credo pioneered AEC technology that has become critical infrastructure for AI cluster interconnects.
622 Employees ·credosemi.com

Investment Thesis
Credo Technology is the premier pure-play on AI data center connectivity, having pioneered the active electrical cable category that has become essential infrastructure for GPU cluster buildouts. Q3 FY2026 revenue of $407M (up 201.5% YoY) with Q4 guidance of $425-435M implies full-year revenue of ~$1.3B, more than tripling FY2025's $436.8M [1]. Gross margins of 67.8% and net margins of ~45% demonstrate exceptional pricing power in specialized connectivity silicon, while D/E of 0.88% reflects a virtually debt-free balance sheet [5]. At 21.7x forward P/E for a company growing revenue 170%+ annually with 30%+ profit margins, CRDO offers a rare combination of hypergrowth and profitability. The 1.6T networking transition and continued AI infrastructure buildout provide a multi-year demand runway as every new AI cluster requires Credo's interconnect solutions [3].
The bear case focuses on customer concentration, competition, and valuation risk. Credo's revenue is heavily concentrated among a small number of hyperscaler customers — a single customer shift in supplier could materially impact results. Broadcom, Marvell, and other larger semiconductor companies are aggressively entering the AEC and high-speed connectivity market, and Credo's small scale (622 employees) may become a disadvantage as larger competitors bring R&D resources to bear [3]. The trailing P/E of 56x reflects embedded growth expectations that leave little room for execution misses. Revenue growth of 201.5% YoY is extraordinary but likely to decelerate as the company scales — management's Q4 guidance of mid-single-digit sequential growth already signals moderation. A slowdown in hyperscaler AI capex spending, whether from ROI concerns or macro headwinds, would directly impact Credo's order book given its near-total dependence on AI infrastructure demand. The Cayman Islands domicile also creates governance complexity for U.S. investors.
Key Catalysts
  1. 1.6T networking transition beginning in 2026-2027, requiring next-generation AECs and optical DSPs that represent Credo's core product roadmap [3]
  2. Continued hyperscaler AI infrastructure buildout with Microsoft, Google, Meta, and Amazon collectively spending $200B+ annually on data centers, driving sustained demand for high-speed connectivity [1]
  3. Potential customer diversification as enterprise and HPC markets adopt AI infrastructure, expanding Credo's addressable market beyond the top hyperscalers [2]
Key Risks
  1. Customer concentration risk — a small number of hyperscaler accounts generate the majority of revenue, and a single design-out or supplier shift could cause a significant revenue decline [1]
  2. Competitive entry from Broadcom, Marvell, and other large semiconductor companies with greater R&D budgets and existing customer relationships in the connectivity silicon market [3]
  3. AI capex cycle dependency — a slowdown in hyperscaler data center spending due to ROI concerns or macroeconomic headwinds would directly reduce demand for Credo's products given near-total AI exposure [1]
