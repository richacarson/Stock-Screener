Company Profile
Energy ·Oil & Gas E&P ·NYSE ·United States
Crescent Energy Company is a diversified oil and gas exploration and production company focused on acquiring and developing assets in the Eagle Ford Shale, Uinta Basin, and Permian Basin. Formed by KKR in December 2021 through a merger of Contango Resources and Independence Energy, the company has grown rapidly through acquisitions including the $2.6B SilverBow Resources merger (2024) and Vital Energy acquisition. Headquartered in Houston, Texas.

Investment Thesis
Crescent Energy offers compelling value at 4.3x forward earnings with record production of 341K boepd and $690M in Q1 2026 adjusted EBITDA, demonstrating that the acquisition-driven growth strategy is generating substantial cash flow [1]. The Vital Energy merger and SilverBow integration have created a diversified mid-cap E&P platform across the Eagle Ford, Uinta, and Permian basins, with Q1 revenue surging 24% to $1.2B. The 4.1% dividend yield provides income while the company's low forward valuation offers significant upside if oil prices stabilize and GAAP losses (driven by $706.6M in derivative losses) normalize [1][3].
The bear case is substantial: Crescent posted a $419.8M GAAP net loss in Q1 2026 despite strong operations, as a $706.6M derivative loss overwhelmed operating income [1]. ROE of -5.66% and profit margin of -7.47% reflect structural accounting challenges from rapid M&A integration and commodity hedging volatility. Long-term debt of $5.24B against a $3.47B market cap creates significant leverage risk if oil prices decline. The 2026 revenue forecast was cut from $5.0B to $4.7B, and the EPS estimate shifted from $1.80 profit to $0.18 loss. As a KKR-backed consolidation vehicle, Crescent faces governance questions about alignment between the PE sponsor's return timeline and public shareholders' long-term interests. Commodity price exposure makes all financial projections inherently uncertain.
Key Catalysts
  1. Full integration of Vital Energy and SilverBow acquisitions expected to unlock $150M+ in annual cost synergies by mid-2027, driving margin expansion from current negative territory
  2. Record production of 341K boepd with rising oil prices could generate $2.5B+ in annual adjusted EBITDA, enabling rapid deleveraging from 112% D/E
  3. Mineral acquisitions provide royalty-like cash flows with no operating cost exposure, diversifying the revenue base beyond working interest production
Key Risks
  1. Oil price decline below $60/bbl WTI would compress cash flows and stress the $5.24B debt load, potentially forcing asset sales or dividend cuts from the already modest $0.48 annual payout
  2. GAAP losses from $706.6M+ derivative swings create earnings volatility that obscures underlying operating performance and may deter institutional investors
  3. Rapid M&A integration risk across Contango, Independence, SilverBow, and Vital Energy workforces, systems, and asset portfolios — with $670M+ quarterly investment pace outstripping proven integration capacity
