Company Profile
Basic Materials ·Building Materials ·NYSE ·Ireland
CRH plc is the largest building materials company in North America and among the largest globally, producing aggregates, cement, ready-mixed concrete, asphalt, and infrastructure products. The company operates 3,961 locations across 28 countries, with 75% of EBITDA derived from North America. CRH joined the S&P 500 in December 2025.

Investment Thesis
CRH is the dominant North American building materials company, delivering record FY2025 results — $37.4B revenue (up 5%), $7.7B adjusted EBITDA (up 11%, 20.5% margin), and $5.51 EPS (up 10%) — with FY2026 guidance projecting $8.1-8.5B EBITDA [1]. The company benefits from structural tailwinds: US infrastructure spending (IIJA, CHIPS Act), data center construction, reshoring/reindustrialization, and aging infrastructure replacement. CRH's local logistics moats across 3,961 locations create durable competitive advantages with pricing power. S&P 500 inclusion (December 2025) drives index fund demand and valuation re-rating. At 15.9x forward P/E, the stock offers reasonable value for a company with 10%+ EPS growth, strong cash generation ($5.8B deployed in growth investments plus $2.2B to shareholders), and SBTi-validated decarbonization leadership [1][3].
The bear case centers on cyclical exposure and carbon-intensive operations. At $105.12, the stock has declined from the SMA-50 of $115.24, reflecting macro uncertainty around US construction spending and tariff impacts on input costs. CRH's cement operations produce 33.9M tonnes of CO2e (Scope 1), making it one of the most carbon-intensive companies in the S&P 500 — tightening carbon regulations and emissions caps could significantly increase compliance costs [3]. Historical anti-competitive behavior (Irish cement cartel fine, Polish price-fixing, West Virginia allegations) creates governance risk [3]. D/E of 77.3% reflects acquisition-driven leverage, and the 32% residential revenue exposure creates housing cycle sensitivity. The dividend yield of approximately 1.5% is modest for income investors, though the 5% annual increase and buyback program enhance total returns. A construction downturn or infrastructure spending pullback would directly impact the top line [4].
Key Catalysts
  1. US infrastructure spending acceleration (IIJA, CHIPS Act, data center construction) driving sustained demand for aggregates, cement, and asphalt — 40% of CRH revenue from infrastructure [1]
  2. FY2026 EBITDA guidance of $8.1-8.5B (5-10% growth) with continued margin expansion from pricing power and operational efficiency across 3,961 locations [1]
  3. S&P 500 inclusion (December 2025) driving index fund buying, analyst coverage expansion, and US investor base broadening for a $70B market cap company [1]
Key Risks
  1. Carbon-intensive operations (33.9M tonnes CO2e Scope 1) expose CRH to tightening emissions regulations, carbon taxation, and increasing compliance costs in the EU and potentially US [3]
  2. Cyclical construction exposure — 32% residential and 28% non-residential revenue creates vulnerability to housing downturns, rising interest rates, and construction spending pullbacks [1]
  3. Historical anti-competitive behavior (cartel fines in Ireland and Poland, West Virginia allegations) creates ongoing governance and regulatory risk [3]
