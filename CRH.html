Company Profile
Basic Materials ·Building Materials ·NYSE ·Ireland
CRH plc is the global leader in building materials solutions, operating across North America and Europe with leadership positions in aggregates, cement, ready-mixed concrete, asphalt, and paving. The company serves critical infrastructure, commercial, and residential construction markets through approximately 3,100 operating locations in 28 countries. CRH is the number one infrastructure play in North America, benefiting from structural demand drivers including the IIJA, water infrastructure upgrades, and data center construction. Market cap of approximately $75 billion.

Investment Thesis
CRH is the dominant global building materials franchise with unmatched scale across North America and Europe, positioned at the intersection of multiple structural demand tailwinds including the Infrastructure Investment and Jobs Act (IIJA), water infrastructure modernization, data center construction, and reindustrialization [1][5]. FY2025 delivered $37.4B revenue with 11% EBITDA growth, and the company enters 2026 with accelerating momentum — Q1 2026 showed 9% revenue growth and 18% EBITDA growth [1][2]. The $5.6B Arcosa acquisition announced in June 2026 further consolidates CRH's position in high-growth US construction materials. With a Forward P/E of 18.97 (below the 20 target), ROE of 15.81%, and a conservative 23% payout ratio supporting a growing dividend, CRH offers a rare combination of infrastructure-grade durability and growth-market exposure.
The primary risk is CRH's acquisition-dependent growth model — the company deployed $4.1B across 38 acquisitions in 2025, and integration risk accumulates at this pace. Profit margins of 9.65% reflect the capital-intensive nature of building materials and limit upside to earnings growth relative to asset-light business models. D/E of 83% is manageable but rising with the Arcosa acquisition, and the 5.3% YoY revenue growth in FY2025 (below the 10% target) suggests organic growth alone may not sustain the current multiple. CRH's dividend yield of 1.39% is below the 2% threshold typically expected for income investors, placing it in an awkward position between pure growth and income strategies. Geographic concentration in North America and Europe leaves CRH exposed to regional construction cycles and policy changes affecting infrastructure spending [1][3].
Key Catalysts
  1. IIJA and infrastructure spending acceleration in 2026-2027 driving sustained demand for aggregates, cement, and paving across CRH's North American operations, with $1.2 trillion in authorized spending still being deployed [1][5]
  2. Arcosa acquisition closing expected to add significant aggregates and construction products capacity, potentially driving 10%+ revenue growth in FY2026 with guided net income of $3.9B-$4.1B and adjusted EBITDA of $8.1B-$8.5B [2][3]
  3. Data center construction boom requiring massive quantities of concrete, aggregates, and infrastructure materials — CRH is the primary building materials beneficiary of the AI infrastructure buildout [5]
Key Risks
  1. Acquisition integration risk is accumulating — 38 deals in 2025 plus the $5.6B Arcosa acquisition in 2026 strain management bandwidth and create execution risk if construction markets soften [1][3]
  2. Profit margin of 9.65% reflects structural limitations of the building materials industry, and rising D/E from acquisition-funded debt could compress returns if interest rates remain elevated
  3. Dependence on US federal infrastructure policy creates political risk — IIJA spending could slow under future administrations, and tariff changes could affect material costs and cross-border operations [5]

AI Resilience (25%)
AI Resilience LOW RISK
8
AI Risk Protection (8/9): CRH's core business is structurally immune to AI disruption. Building materials manufacturing, aggregates extraction, cement production, asphalt paving, and infrastructure construction are physical-world operations that AI cannot substitute [1][5]. The delivery and installation of concrete, aggregates, and road surfaces requires heavy equipment, supply chain logistics, and on-site labor that no AI system can replicate. Furthermore, AI-driven demand tailwinds benefit CRH directly — data center construction requires massive quantities of concrete, aggregates, and infrastructure materials, making CRH a beneficiary of the AI buildout rather than a victim [5]. Analysts see zero substitution risk for building materials; if anything, AI increases demand for CRH's products through the data center and infrastructure construction boom. AI Opportunity (0/1): While CRH Ventures has made strategic investments in AI-adjacent companies (Voda.ai, Fido AI), the company does not generate quantifiable revenue from AI products or services. AI investments are venture-stage and do not yet contribute to CRH's revenue base [4].
