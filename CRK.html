Company Profile
Energy ·Oil & Gas E&P ·NYSE ·United States
Comstock Resources is a Frisco, Texas-based natural gas exploration and production company with a core position in the Haynesville Shale of Northwest Louisiana and the developing Western Haynesville play in East Texas. Dallas Cowboys owner Jerry Jones is the largest shareholder (~71%), having committed over $1B to fund the Western Haynesville development that sits adjacent to the Gulf Coast LNG corridor and the Dallas-Houston AI data center buildout.

Investment Thesis
Comstock Resources is a unique infinite-game bet on US natural gas at the intersection of the AI data center buildout and the Gulf Coast LNG export corridor. The Western Haynesville play in East Texas — backed by Dallas Cowboys owner Jerry Jones's $1B+ personal investment (now ~71% ownership) — has been described as 'the holy grail of supply' for Big Tech's AI power needs given its proximity to the Dallas-Houston data center cluster and Gulf Coast LNG facilities [2]. CEO Jay Allison is the longest-tenured public E&P CEO and has steered Comstock through multiple natural gas cycles with intellectual honesty and patient capital allocation [2]. Q4 2025 non-GAAP EPS of $0.16 beat consensus by 38.1%, and TTM revenue of $1.92B (up from $1.25B in 2024) demonstrates production growth [1]. The active exploration of a NextEra power-generation partnership opens a contracted-revenue path to AI data center load beyond spot gas pricing [2]. MiQ methane certification supports responsible-sourcing claims as buyers screen for emissions intensity [3].
The bear case is grounded in commodity price exposure, leverage, and execution timing. Q4 2025 revenue missed analyst estimates by 21.9%, signaling that top-line momentum is heavily natural-gas-price dependent [1]. Total debt of ~$3.05B with Debt/EBITDA of 1.27x is manageable but levered FCF of -$335.56M TTM reflects the heavy outspend on Western Haynesville drilling — management has had to sell $445M of legacy Haynesville/Cotton Valley assets to fund the development [3][4]. ROE of 6.71% and net margin of 3.14% are modest [1]. Without a recovery in natural gas prices or a material direct-to-AI power deal, the buildout could compress equity returns. ESG pressures on natural gas access to capital remain a structural headwind despite the MiQ certification [3]. No dividend means investors are entirely dependent on production growth and multiple expansion. Concentrated ownership (Jones at ~71%) limits float and creates governance dynamics where minority shareholders ride alongside Jones's strategic preferences.
Key Catalysts
  1. Western Haynesville production ramp and well-results disclosure validating Allison/Jones's 'last great play' thesis on reserves and breakeven economics [2]
  2. Direct-to-data-center power offtake or NextEra partnership signing, converting commodity gas exposure into contracted recurring revenue [2]
  3. Natural gas price recovery driven by LNG export expansion and AI data center load growth, leveraging Comstock's strategic Gulf Coast / Dallas-Houston position [2]
Key Risks
  1. Commodity price exposure — Q4 2025 revenue missed by 21.9% and the company has no dividend cushion; sustained low natural gas prices compress margins and FCF [1]
  2. Leverage and outspend — $3.05B debt with -$335.56M levered FCF (TTM) requires asset sales (e.g., $445M legacy Haynesville sale) and capital markets access during the Western Haynesville buildout [3][4]
  3. ESG access-to-capital risk — natural gas E&P faces increasing scrutiny from lenders and equity investors despite MiQ certification, potentially limiting financing options [3]
