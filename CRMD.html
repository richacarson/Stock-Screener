Company Profile
Healthcare ·Drug Manufacturers - Specialty & Generic ·NASDAQ ·United States
CorMedix (recently branded CorMedix Therapeutics) is a diversified specialty pharmaceutical company whose lead asset, DefenCath, is the first FDA-approved catheter lock solution indicated to reduce catheter-related bloodstream infections in chronic hemodialysis patients. The August 2025 acquisition of Melinta Therapeutics added six marketed anti-infectives (plus TOPROL-XL), transforming CRMD from a single-product company to a diversified commercial operation. Market cap is approximately $579M.

Investment Thesis
Bull case: CorMedix is a profitable, under-followed specialty pharma trading at ~6x forward earnings with a transformative multi-product platform. FY2025 pro forma revenue of $401M, net income of $163M, and ROE of 23.2% represent a durable financial foundation [1][4]. DefenCath is the only FDA-approved catheter lock solution for its indication with category-defining market share in preventing CRBSIs in chronic hemodialysis patients [1]. The August 2025 Melinta acquisition ($390M intangibles) diversified the portfolio to six anti-infectives plus TOPROL-XL, with ~$35M in annual operational synergies [2]. 2026 guidance of $300-320M revenue and $100-125M adjusted EBITDA implies strong free cash generation even through the TDAPA reimbursement transition. $148M cash balance plus $150M convertible notes provide M&A firepower. REZZAYO Phase 3 data in Q2 2026 is a potential catalyst.
Bear case: the DefenCath TDAPA reimbursement transition on July 1, 2026 to a post-TDAPA Add-On Adjustment will cut net pricing in Q3 and Q4 2026, driving the 2026 guidance reset that caused a 28-35% stock crash [3]. Beyond 2026, investors must model further potential step-downs in DefenCath economics. Melinta's anti-infective portfolio competes in crowded markets with established generics pressure. Customer concentration on large dialysis organizations (Fresenius, DaVita) creates bargaining leverage risk. The convertible note structure creates future dilution. Sustainalytics rates CRMD's ESG material risk management as Weak, though no specific controversy is active. The Vizient Innovative Technology Designation mitigates reimbursement risk somewhat but does not eliminate it [5]. Stock volatility has been severe (52-week range $5.60-$17.43).
Key Catalysts
  1. REZZAYO Phase 3 data readout expected Q2 2026, potential label expansion or new indication catalyst [1][3]
  2. Melinta synergies realization (~$35M operational) and full-year integration benefits flowing through 2026 financials [2]
  3. Post-TDAPA Add-On Adjustment pricing clarity from CMS after July 1 2026 transition, potentially better than current conservative guidance assumes [1][3]
Key Risks
  1. DefenCath TDAPA reimbursement transition July 1 2026 — net price decline in 2H 2026 already causing 2026 guidance reset and 28-35% stock crash [3]
  2. Large-dialysis customer concentration (Fresenius, DaVita) creates commercial bargaining risk for both volumes and pricing [1]
  3. Convertible notes ($150M, 4.00%, due 2030) create future dilution risk and constrain capital allocation flexibility [4]
