Company Profile
Materials ·Other Industrial Metals & Mining ·NASDAQ ·Austria/Greenland
Critical Metals Corp. is a development-stage mining company advancing the Wolfsberg Lithium Project in Carinthia, Austria—Europe's first fully permitted lithium mine—and the Tanbreez Project in southern Greenland, one of the world's largest rare earth element deposits. The company is a subsidiary of European Lithium Limited and is developing lithium and rare earth resources to supply European battery manufacturers under binding offtake arrangements.

Investment Thesis
Critical Metals holds a unique strategic asset—Europe's only fully permitted lithium mine—at a time when the EU is actively incentivizing domestic critical mineral supply through the Critical Raw Materials Act. With battery-grade lithium prices recovering (CNY 168,000-170,000/tonne) and the Austrian government extending Wolfsberg's license by 2 years, the decision-to-mine timeline (end of 2026) has specific binding offtake arrangements from European battery manufacturers already in place[1]. The $30M Tanbreez acceleration and 60° North Greenland acquisition add rare earth element optionality beyond lithium.
The bear case is that Critical Metals is entirely pre-revenue (beyond $560K in royalties), burning cash (-$51.87M net loss in FY2025), and the mine production decision is subject to both lithium prices (volatile) and financing availability (uncertain). Previous lithium mining companies with 'fully permitted' assets have struggled to reach production due to cost overruns, environmental reviews, and financing gaps. The stock has already ranged from $1.23 to $32.15 in the past year—extreme volatility indicating speculative rather than fundamental pricing. With only ~85 employees and reliance on external contractors, the operational risk is high.
Key Catalysts
  1. Formal decision to commence Wolfsberg mining announced by end of 2026 with binding financing secured, triggering a re-rating from exploration to development/production company
  2. Tanbreez rare earth element resource update confirming large-scale, high-grade deposit with metallurgical test results supporting processing design
  3. EU Critical Raw Materials Act strategic project designation for Wolfsberg enabling access to streamlined permitting and potential EU co-financing
Key Risks
  1. Lithium price decline below $1,500/tonne spodumene equivalent making Wolfsberg uneconomic to develop and delaying the production decision indefinitely
  2. Capital requirements for Wolfsberg construction ($400M+ estimated) requiring multiple equity raises that dilute current shareholders significantly below current price
  3. Austrian community opposition to underground lithium mining or environmental review complications extending the timeline beyond the 2026 decision window
