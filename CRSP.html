Company Profile
Healthcare ·Biotechnology ·NASDAQ ·Switzerland
CRISPR Therapeutics is a gene editing company developing transformative gene-based medicines using its CRISPR/Cas9 platform. The company's lead product CASGEVY, developed in partnership with Vertex Pharmaceuticals, is the world's first approved CRISPR gene therapy, treating sickle cell disease and transfusion-dependent beta-thalassemia. The pipeline spans hemoglobinopathies, oncology (allogeneic CAR-T), cardiovascular disease (in-vivo gene editing), autoimmune disorders, and type 1 diabetes. Founded in 2013 by Nobel laureate Emmanuelle Charpentier, the company is headquartered in Zug, Switzerland.

Investment Thesis

CASGEVY revenue is expected to reach $100M in 2025 with significant growth anticipated in 2026 as treatment centers expand globally [1]. The $2.0B cash position provides 3+ years of runway at current burn rates, eliminating near-term dilution risk. The pipeline represents massive optionality: CTX112/CTX131 (allogeneic CAR-T for oncology/autoimmune), CTX310/CTX320 (in-vivo cardiovascular gene editing), and CTX211 (T1D) each address multi-billion-dollar markets. At a $5.6B market cap with $2.0B in cash ($37.99 net cash-adjusted price), the market is valuing the entire pipeline at ~$3.6B — potentially a fraction of the total addressable market if even one additional program succeeds. The stock gained 19% in the past month on pipeline optimism [1].
The bear case centers on clinical and execution risk. CRSP lost $582M in the trailing period with ROE of -30.2% and profit margin of 0% — the company is years from profitability even with CASGEVY ramping [4]. Revenue declined 97.6% YoY due to collaboration milestone timing, and Q4 EPS of -$1.37 missed estimates by 14% [1]. CASGEVY's commercial trajectory faces headwinds: the treatment requires complex manufacturing, costs approximately $2.2M per patient, and addresses a limited initial patient population (sickle cell and beta-thal). The Glassdoor evidence of cultural toxicity (2.6/5 senior management, 42% CEO approval) raises concerns about organizational resilience during the multi-year commercial ramp [2]. Pipeline programs are pre-clinical or early-stage with binary success/failure risk. Competition from Intellia (in-vivo CRISPR), Beam (base editing), and large pharma gene therapy programs could erode CRISPR Therapeutics' first-mover advantage. The stock's 1.79 beta amplifies downside volatility in risk-off markets.

AI Resilience (25%)
AI Resilience MODERATE
7
AI Risk Protection (6/9): CRISPR gene editing involves physical biological processes — collecting patient stem cells, editing them with CRISPR/Cas9 in manufacturing facilities, and reinfusing them — that AI cannot substitute for [1]. The therapeutic product is a living biological material, not a digital service. CRISPR Therapeutics' patents on its Cas9 platform provide legal moats that AI cannot circumvent. However, AI is increasingly used in drug discovery and target identification, which could accelerate competing platforms from Intellia Therapeutics, Editas Medicine, and Beam Therapeutics. AI-driven protein engineering could also create next-generation gene editing tools that improve on Cas9 accuracy and efficiency, potentially eroding CRISPR Therapeutics' platform advantage over time. The company is protected but not immune — its moat is biological and regulatory, not technological exclusivity. AI Opportunity (1/1): While CRISPR Therapeutics does not generate AI-related revenue, AI tools for target identification, guide RNA optimization, and clinical trial design could meaningfully accelerate the company's pipeline development timelines. The intersection of AI and gene editing represents a significant opportunity for enhanced therapeutic discovery.
