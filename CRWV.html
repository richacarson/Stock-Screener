Investment Thesis
CoreWeave achieved the fastest cloud revenue growth in history ($5.1B in 2025), has $60.7B in remaining performance obligations, and is expected to nearly triple revenue to approximately $12B in 2026 [1]. The company operates 43 data centers with 3.1 GW of contracted capacity, and 98% take-or-pay contracts provide revenue certainty. Strategic partnerships with Microsoft, Meta, OpenAI, and Nvidia position CoreWeave at the center of the AI infrastructure buildout. The Weights & Biases acquisition creates an end-to-end AI platform. At Morningstar's $97 fair value estimate, the stock has 19% upside from current levels.
The bear case is existential leverage risk: 894% D/E, $8B+ in debt, 9x net debt/EBITDA, and $1.2B net losses make CoreWeave among the most leveraged technology companies [1][3]. Microsoft accounts for 67% of revenue — loss or reduction of this contract would be catastrophic. DitchCarbon gives CoreWeave just 28/100 on sustainability despite claims of renewable energy usage. If AI infrastructure spending moderates or interest rates rise, CoreWeave's equity could be wiped out by debt obligations. Morningstar assigns no economic moat, and the company's return on invested capital fails to exceed cost of capital despite explosive growth. Competition from hyperscalers with vastly superior balance sheets poses long-term margin pressure.
Key Catalysts
  1. 2026 revenue execution toward approximately $12B, demonstrating that $60.7B RPO converts to actual recognized revenue at projected rates
  2. Path to profitability — adjusted EBITDA margins improving as data centers reach operational maturity and utilization rises
  3. Weights & Biases integration creating a differentiated end-to-end AI platform that reduces customer churn and increases wallet share
Key Risks
  1. Extreme leverage — 894% D/E with 9x net debt/EBITDA means even small increases in interest rates could make equity value zero
  2. Microsoft customer concentration at 67% of revenue — contract modification or loss would be existential; no diversification achieved despite scale
  3. AI infrastructure spending slowdown — if hyperscaler capex moderates, CoreWeave's take-or-pay contracts protect revenue but debt service obligations remain fixed
