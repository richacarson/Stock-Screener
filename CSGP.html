Company Profile
Real Estate ·Real Estate Services ·NASDAQ ·United States
CoStar Group is the leading provider of commercial real estate information, analytics, and online marketplaces. Its platforms include CoStar Suite (commercial data/analytics), Apartments.com (#1 multifamily marketplace), Homes.com (residential portal), LoopNet (commercial listings), and Ten-X (online auctions). The company holds 40 years of proprietary commercial real estate data and serves brokers, investors, lenders, and property managers globally.

Investment Thesis
CoStar Group is the undisputed leader in commercial real estate data and analytics, with 40 years of proprietary data, $3.2B in 2025 revenue (19% growth), and a clear path to $3.8B+ in 2026 [1]. The company is inflecting toward profitability with Adjusted EBITDA guidance of $740-800M for 2026 (83% YoY growth), and medium-term targets of $1.25B EBITDA by 2028 and $2.3B by 2030 at 35% margins [1]. The stock has fallen ~50% from highs, creating a potential entry point at 22.4x forward P/E with earnings expected to surge from near-zero to $175-215M in 2026. The $700M buyback signals management confidence, and Homes.com reaching #2 residential portal status with 108M monthly visitors validates the investment [1]. AI deployment across the platform is driving meaningful efficiency gains and differentiated products like Homes AI [2].
The bear case is substantial. Near-zero net income ($7M on $3.2B revenue) reflects massive Homes.com investment whose ROI remains unproven — activist investors D.E. Shaw and Third Point have publicly challenged the strategy and transparency of reporting [3]. Jim Cramer flagged CoStar as an 'AI victim,' warning that proprietary data becomes less valuable when AI can aggregate equivalent information [2]. The company's toxic culture (32% Glassdoor recommendation rate, 2.4/5 culture score) creates employee retention and recruitment challenges that could undermine execution [2]. AI-driven layoffs of 100+ employees while simultaneously claiming to invest in growth sends mixed signals. Homes.com must overcome massive network effects favoring Zillow (10x+ monthly visitors) to justify billions in cumulative investment. Trailing P/E of nearly 2000x reflects the current profitability gap.

AI Resilience (25%)
AI Resilience MODERATE
6
AI Risk Protection (5/9): CoStar faces a genuine AI disruption debate. Jim Cramer called CoStar 'another victim of AI,' warning that proprietary CRE data 'maybe becomes a lot less valuable when AI can easily write code to gather the same data' [2]. Market concerns include AI agents potentially disintermediating traditional real estate advertising models, directly threatening Homes.com and Apartments.com revenue [2]. However, CoStar's 40-year proprietary commercial real estate database has no public equivalent (unlike residential MLS), creating a defensible data moat that AI cannot easily replicate from public sources [2]. The core CRE analytics business is likely more protected than the residential portal, which faces greater substitution risk. AI Opportunity (1/1): CoStar is actively deploying AI across its platforms — Homes AI provides conversational property search, AI drives content creation and lease data extraction efficiencies, and the company appointed AI board expertise (Nana Banerjee) to accelerate adoption [1][2].
