Company Profile
Real Estate ·Real Estate Services ·NASDAQ ·United States
CoStar Group, Inc. provides information, analytics, and online marketplaces for the commercial and residential real estate industries. Products include CoStar (commercial data platform), Apartments.com (multifamily listings), Homes.com (residential marketplace), LoopNet (commercial listings), STR (hospitality benchmarking), Ten-X (online auction), and Matterport (3D digital twin technology). Founded in 1986 by Andy Florance, headquartered in Arlington, Virginia. Member of the S&P 500 and NASDAQ 100.

Investment Thesis
CoStar Group is a founder-led platform company delivering 23% revenue growth to $897M in Q1 2026, with full-year guidance of $3.8B (17% growth), demonstrating accelerating momentum from Homes.com AI innovation and Matterport integration [1]. The AI-powered Homes.com search application drove 119% organic traffic growth in Q1, validating the billions invested in residential real estate. Adjusted EBITDA doubled YoY to $132M, signaling the inflection from investment phase to profitability. With virtually no debt (D/E 0.15%), $5.0M in founder-CEO insider buying, and a PEG ratio of 0.54, CoStar offers compelling growth at a reasonable valuation. Analyst consensus target of $48.85 implies 60% upside from the current $33.66 [4].
The primary risks are competitive and legal. Multiple antitrust class-action lawsuits filed in 2026 allege CoStar monopolized commercial real estate data through exclusionary contracts and technological barriers — if successful, these could force CoStar to open its data moat, undermining the core competitive advantage [3]. GAAP profitability remains near-zero (0.73% profit margin, ROE 0.30%) as heavy Homes.com investment compresses earnings, requiring continued investor patience for the residential bet to pay off. Homes.com still trails Zillow significantly in residential market share, and the path to profitability in residential requires sustained double-digit traffic growth and agent monetization improvements. Forward P/E of 20.8x assumes significant earnings acceleration — any deceleration in Homes.com traction could compress the multiple.
