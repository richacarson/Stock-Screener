Company Profile
Technology ·Solar ·NASDAQ ·Canada
Canadian Solar Inc. is a vertically integrated solar energy and battery energy storage company operating through two segments: CSI Solar (manufacturing solar modules, battery storage solutions, and providing EPC services) and Recurrent Energy (developing, constructing, and operating solar power and battery storage projects). The company delivered 24.3 GW of solar modules and a record 7.8 GWh of energy storage in 2025. Founded in 2001 by Dr. Shawn Qu, headquartered in Kitchener, Canada.

Investment Thesis
Canadian Solar is a deeply discounted global solar and storage leader trading at 10.5x forward earnings after a 64% decline from its 52-week high of $34.59 to $12.53, creating potential deep-value entry into a company with $5.6B revenue and a $3.6B e-STORAGE contracted backlog [1][5]. The 2026 outlook is compelling: energy storage shipments are guided to nearly double (14-17 GWh vs. 7.8 GWh in 2025), and U.S. market shipments of 6.5-7.0 GW solar plus 4.5-5.5 GWh batteries benefit from IRA incentives and reshoring trends [1]. Founder-CEO Dr. Shawn Qu's 24-year tenure provides strategic continuity through the cycle, and the company's vertical integration from ingots to project ownership creates margin capture across the value chain. Forward EPS of $1.19 implies return to profitability after the 2024 loss year, and AI-driven data center demand is creating a structural tailwind for solar and battery storage deployment.
The bear case is substantial: Canadian Solar's 2024 results were a net loss (EPS -$2.50) with -1.9% profit margins as solar module ASPs collapsed amid massive Chinese overcapacity, and revenue fell 20% YoY [5]. D/E of 157.9% with $6.7B debt against only $1.4B cash creates financial fragility — operating cash flow of -$253M and free cash flow of -$1.4B mean the company is burning cash while servicing significant debt [5]. The Xinjiang forced labor controversy — with documented ties to GCL-Poly and initial resistance to independent supply chain audits — creates ESG, regulatory, and reputational risk, particularly as U.S. customs enforcement under the Uyghur Forced Labor Prevention Act intensifies [4]. Tariff uncertainty threatens U.S. market access, and the $839M market cap against $5.6B revenue signals the market deeply discounts the company's earnings power. Q1 2026 guidance of 13-15% gross margin (down from 17.2% in Q3 2025) suggests continued margin pressure.
