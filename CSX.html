Company Profile
Industrials ·Rail Transportation ·NASDAQ ·United States
CSX Corporation is one of the largest rail transportation networks in the eastern United States, operating approximately 20,000 miles of track connecting the Southeast, Midwest, and Northeast. The company delivers approximately 3.2 million carloads annually of chemicals, agricultural products, automotive, coal, and intermodal freight. Full-year 2025 revenue was $14.09 billion (down 3.1%), reflecting weak industrial demand; CEO Steve Angel (appointed October 2025 following a leadership transition) is targeting improved productivity and cost discipline for 2026.

Investment Thesis
CSX offers a compelling combination of durable competitive moat (physical rail infrastructure in the eastern US is irreplaceable), strong financial metrics (23.87% profit margin, 22.59% ROE, 19.43x forward P/E, 15+ years of dividend growth), and a recovery setup for 2026: approximately $100M of non-recurring disruption costs won't repeat, new CEO Steve Angel is focused on productivity gains, and industrial demand historically recovers from the trough that impacted 2025 volumes. The stock's PEG of 2.32 and dividend yield of 1.47% are reasonable for a high-quality infrastructure business [1][2].
The bear case focuses on persistent volume headwinds: mill and plant closures in housing and automotive markets are dragging volumes through Q3 2026; aluminum shortages in automotive supply chains add pressure; and the CEO transition in October 2025 creates execution uncertainty. Revenue declined 3.08% in 2025, and the full-year 2025 adjusted operating margin of 33.2% declined from 2024 levels — suggesting PSR efficiency gains are plateauing. At 22.97x trailing P/E for a company with declining revenue, the valuation requires clear evidence of volume recovery [1].
Key Catalysts
  1. Q3 2026 volume inflection — management expects housing and automotive-related headwinds to lap by Q3 2026; first quarter of positive YoY volume growth would confirm the recovery thesis
  2. Operating ratio improvement below 65% — any quarterly operating ratio improvement demonstrating productivity and cost management under CEO Angel would validate the 2026 improvement thesis
  3. Industrial production index recovery above 1% growth — CSX's volumes are highly correlated to US industrial production; a broader manufacturing recovery would lift all freight categories
Key Risks
  1. Volume recovery delayed beyond Q3 2026: if housing and automotive demand recovery is pushed out to 2027, the revenue trajectory remains negative for the full year and the 2026 earnings improvement narrative collapses
  2. Labor relations deterioration: the union's objection to Trip Optimizer automation could escalate to work stoppages or regulatory pushback; a work slowdown during the volume recovery window would be costly
  3. CEO transition execution risk: Steve Angel has been in the role since October 2025 (5 months); the October CEO dismissal over merger mismanagement signals internal governance issues that could resurface
