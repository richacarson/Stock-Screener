Investment Thesis
Castellum is a micro-cap defense technology company in strategic transition under CEO Glen Ives, with revenue growing 15.2% to $52.9M in 2025 and management guiding 10-12% growth for 2026 [1]. The company achieved a historic $103.3M Navy contract, reduced debt by 70%, and earned CMMC Level 2 certification that opens new DoD contract opportunities [1][2]. The government cybersecurity market is growing as federal agencies increase spending on information assurance, electronic warfare, and AI/ML services. At $0.69, the stock trades at a $65M market cap — barely above annual revenue — creating potential upside if the company achieves profitability. The clean balance sheet ($14.9M cash, only $1.2M debt) provides stability for the transition to organic growth [1].
The bear case is dominated by scale, profitability, and liquidity concerns. Despite 15% revenue growth, Castellum remains unprofitable with -4.5% profit margin, -$2.5M net loss, and negative free cash flow of -$1.9M [1]. The company fails the liquidity floor test ($739K average daily trading value), making it unsuitable for institutional investors and subject to extreme price volatility. At 34.5x forward P/E, the market is pricing in a profitability inflection that has not materialized after years of promises. The government contracting space is dominated by firms 100-300x larger (Leidos, SAIC, Booz Allen) with superior relationships, security clearance pools, and pricing power. The 244-person workforce limits the company's ability to staff large contracts simultaneously, creating capacity constraints. A single contract loss or delay could materially impact the revenue base. The CEO's heavy equity compensation (83.8% of total) could motivate short-term stock promotion over long-term value creation [2].
