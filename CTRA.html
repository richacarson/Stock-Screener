Company Profile
Energy ·Oil & Gas E&P ·NYSE ·United States
Coterra Energy is an independent oil and gas exploration and production company operating primarily in three U.S. basins: the Permian Basin (Delaware Basin, ~345,000 net acres in west Texas and southeast New Mexico), Marcellus Shale (~186,000 net acres in northeast Pennsylvania), and Anadarko Basin (~208,000 net acres in Oklahoma). Formed in October 2021 through the merger of Cabot Oil & Gas and Cimarex Energy, Coterra produces a roughly 50/50 mix of oil and natural gas/NGLs.

Investment Thesis
Coterra Energy is a premier low-cost E&P operator with top-tier acreage in the Delaware Basin (Permian) and Marcellus Shale, generating strong cash flows from a diversified oil/gas production mix. Full-year 2025 revenue of $7.64B (up 23.4% YoY) with net income of $1.72B and profit margins of 24.6% demonstrate the quality of Coterra's asset base [1]. At 12.0x forward P/E with a 2.55% dividend yield, D/E of just 27%, and 35+ consecutive years of dividend payments, CTRA offers attractive income with valuation support from low-cost operations. The pending Devon Energy merger targets $1B/year in pre-tax synergies by year-end 2027, creating a 'super-independent' E&P company with enhanced scale and operational efficiency [1]. 2026 production guidance of 770-810 MBoe/day provides near-term visibility.
The bear case centers on commodity price exposure, merger execution risk, and environmental liabilities. Coterra's revenue is entirely driven by oil and natural gas prices — a sustained decline in commodity prices would compress margins despite low-cost operations. The Devon merger carries significant integration risk; achieving $1B in annual synergies requires flawless execution across two distinct corporate cultures, and E&P mega-mergers have a mixed track record. ROE of 12.3% is below the 15% target, reflecting the capital-intensive nature of E&P even with low leverage. The Dimock, Pennsylvania water contamination case (criminal no-contest plea, $16M settlement) and resumption of drilling in the affected township create ongoing reputational and legal risk [4]. A 74% shareholder vote in favor of a methane disclosure resolution signals investor concern about emissions transparency, and actual emissions may be 50-100% higher than reported figures [4]. Innovation score of 4 reflects the limited differentiation available in commodity E&P — Coterra is an excellent operator but not an innovator.
Key Catalysts
  1. Devon Energy merger completion creating a 'super-independent' E&P company with $1B/year in targeted pre-tax synergies by year-end 2027 and enhanced Permian Basin scale [1]
  2. Natural gas demand growth from AI data center power generation and LNG export capacity additions, supporting higher gas prices that benefit Coterra's 50% gas production mix [1]
  3. 2026 production guidance of 770-810 MBoe/day with capital spending modestly down YoY, demonstrating improving capital efficiency from operational optimization [1]
Key Risks
  1. Commodity price exposure — oil and natural gas price declines would directly compress margins and cash flow, with no hedging or diversification beyond hydrocarbon production [1]
  2. Devon merger integration risk — achieving $1B/year in synergies requires combining two corporate cultures and operational teams; failure to deliver would undermine the strategic rationale [1]
  3. Environmental and legal liability — the Dimock, PA water contamination case (criminal no-contest plea) and 74% shareholder methane disclosure vote signal ongoing regulatory, reputational, and litigation risk [4]
