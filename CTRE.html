Company Profile
Real Estate ·REIT - Healthcare Facilities ·NYSE ·United States
CareTrust REIT is a self-administered, publicly traded healthcare REIT that owns, acquires, finances, and leases skilled nursing facilities, senior housing communities, and other healthcare-related properties. As of December 31, 2025, the company owned 407 properties with 37,628 operational beds and units across 32 U.S. states and the United Kingdom, leased to independent operators under triple-net leases.
43 Employees ·www.caretrustreit.com

Investment Thesis
CareTrust REIT has transformed into a premier healthcare REIT through $3.3B in acquisitions over 2024-2025 at disciplined 8.6% average yields, growing its portfolio to 407 properties with 37,628 beds across 32 states and the U.K. [1]. Revenue surged 62% to $369.4M in 2025 with net income nearly doubling to $320.5M, while leverage remains conservative at 0.7x [1]. The 2026 guidance of $1.90-$1.95 normalized FFO per share (~9% growth) with a ~$600M acquisition pipeline provides continued growth visibility. The 3.96% dividend yield with 8% 5-year growth CAGR and 12 consecutive years of payments offers reliable income backed by secular aging demographics and essential healthcare real estate [5]. ISS ESG Prime status reflects governance quality [4].
The bear case centers on acquisition integration risk and sector-specific vulnerabilities. CareTrust's rapid portfolio expansion ($3.3B deployed in two years) creates operator concentration and integration risk — the company's 43-person team must manage relationships with operators across 407 properties in 32 states and the U.K. [1][2]. The 85% payout ratio leaves limited cushion for dividend sustainability if tenant defaults increase. Skilled nursing facilities face persistent staffing shortages that could pressure operator coverage ratios. The U.K. care home entry introduces currency risk and unfamiliar regulatory environments. At 23x forward P/E ($39.42), the stock prices in continued growth execution — any acquisition stumble or operator distress could compress the multiple. Rising interest rates would increase borrowing costs and reduce cap rate spreads on new acquisitions, potentially slowing the growth engine [5].
Key Catalysts
  1. ~$600M identified acquisition pipeline with three operational growth engines (U.S. SNF, U.K. care homes, SHOP) driving continued portfolio expansion at disciplined yields [1]
  2. 2026 normalized FFO guidance of $1.90-$1.95 per share represents ~9% growth, with the most recent quarterly dividend increase to $0.39/quarter (16.4% increase) signaling management confidence [1][5]
  3. Secular aging demographics — U.S. 65+ population growing ~3% annually — creating sustained demand for skilled nursing and senior housing real estate
Key Risks
  1. Operator concentration and integration risk from rapid $3.3B portfolio expansion — tenant defaults or coverage ratio deterioration across 407 properties could impair rental income [1]
  2. Skilled nursing staffing shortages and rising labor costs pressuring operator economics and lease coverage ratios across the portfolio
  3. U.K. care home exposure introduces currency risk, regulatory unfamiliarity, and geographic management complexity for a 43-person team [2]
