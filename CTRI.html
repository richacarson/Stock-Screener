Company Profile
Utilities ·Utilities - Regulated Gas ·NYSE ·United States
Centuri Holdings is a utility infrastructure services company operating across North America, providing maintenance, replacement, repair, and installation services for gas distribution and electric transmission/distribution utilities. Serves electric, gas, and combination utility providers, as well as distributed power projects and data centers. A subsidiary of Southwest Gas Holdings, founded in 1909, headquartered in Phoenix, Arizona.
9,687 Employees ·centuri.com

Investment Thesis
Centuri Holdings is a utility infrastructure services company at an inflection point — record 2025 revenue of $3.0B, a 59% increase in backlog to $5.9B, and a $13B opportunity pipeline fueled by utility modernization, clean energy transition, and AI-driven data center power demand [1][2]. The 2026 guidance of $3.24B-$3.54B revenue with $280M-$310M adjusted EBITDA signals meaningful margin expansion from the current 0.75% profit margin. The PEG ratio of 1.01 with 175% earnings growth suggests the stock is reasonably valued on a growth-adjusted basis despite the optically high 139x trailing P/E [5]. With 85%+ of 2026 base revenue already in backlog and a book-to-bill target of 1.1x-1.2x, revenue visibility is exceptional for an infrastructure contractor.
The bear case centers on profitability and leverage. Centuri's 0.75% profit margin and 8.3% gross margin are among the thinnest in the industrial sector, meaning even modest cost overruns or weather delays could erase profits entirely [5]. D/E of 109% with $957M debt creates meaningful interest expense burden, and free cash flow of just $4.6M against a $3.5B market cap indicates minimal shareholder return capacity near-term. The forward P/E of 35.4x requires significant margin expansion to justify, and the company's history as a Southwest Gas subsidiary raises questions about capital allocation independence. The 117-year-old company only recently went public, and the management team under new CEO Fehrman is unproven at this scale. Infrastructure spending is inherently cyclical and dependent on utility capex budgets that could contract in an economic downturn. The stock has nearly doubled from its 52-week low of $17.49 to $34.74, suggesting much of the near-term upside may already be priced in [5].
Key Catalysts
  1. Margin expansion from 0.75% toward 2-3% as the $5.9B backlog converts to revenue with improved operational efficiency and pricing discipline [1][2]
  2. AI and data center infrastructure demand accelerating the $13B opportunity pipeline as power-hungry facilities require grid upgrades [2][3]
  3. Book-to-bill target of 1.1x-1.2x in 2026 extending backlog growth momentum and providing multi-year revenue visibility [2]
Key Risks
  1. Razor-thin margins — 0.75% profit margin and 8.3% gross margin mean any cost overrun, weather disruption, or project delay could eliminate profitability entirely [5]
  2. Leverage risk — D/E of 109% with $957M debt creates meaningful interest expense burden and refinancing risk in a higher-rate environment [5]
  3. Cyclical utility capex dependence — infrastructure spending could contract in an economic downturn, and backlog cancellations could reduce the $5.9B forward pipeline [1][2]

AI Resilience (25%)
AI Resilience LOW RISK
7
AI Risk Protection (6/9): Centuri's core business — physical utility infrastructure construction and maintenance — is among the most AI-resistant industries. Digging trenches, replacing gas pipelines, upgrading electric transmission lines, and performing skilled field work cannot be automated by AI in any foreseeable timeframe [1]. Furthermore, AI adoption is a direct demand driver — data centers require massive power infrastructure that Centuri builds, and management explicitly highlighted AI-driven load demand as a growth catalyst [2][3]. The 59% backlog growth partly reflects this AI-infrastructure nexus. Competitive moats include utility relationships, safety certifications, and skilled labor availability. AI Opportunity (1/1): AI tools for project management, workforce optimization, predictive maintenance scheduling, and safety monitoring could improve Centuri's thin operating margins if adopted across 9,687 field employees [4].
