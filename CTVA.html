Company Profile
Basic Materials ·Agricultural Inputs ·NYSE ·United States
Corteva operates through Seed and Crop Protection segments, providing advanced germplasm, trait technologies, herbicides, insecticides, and digital agriculture solutions globally. Spun off from DowDuPont in 2019, the company is planning to separate into two independent businesses by Q4 2026. Founded in 1802 and headquartered in Indianapolis, Indiana.

Investment Thesis
Corteva delivered record 2025 results with operating EBITDA of $3.85B (up 14%), margins above 22%, and free cash flow of $2.9B, demonstrating strong execution in a challenging agricultural market [1]. The 2026 guide of $4.0-4.2B EBITDA (7% growth) is underpinned by Conkesta/corn licensing ramp ($120M royalty improvement), mid-single-digit Crop Protection volume growth, and $200M productivity savings [1]. The Bayer licensing agreement contributes ~$1B aggregate earnings upside over the next decade and accelerates royalty neutrality to 2026 [1]. At 20.7x forward P/E with a pristine balance sheet (D/E 12%, $4.5B cash), Corteva offers defensive growth exposure to the agricultural cycle with improving earnings quality.
The bear case centers on legacy liabilities and execution risk. Corteva's PFAS exposure — including a $2B New Jersey settlement shared with DuPont and Chemours — creates ongoing litigation tail risk, and the Enlist Duo cancellation removes a key crop protection product [3]. Profit margin of 6.3% and ROE of 4.97% remain below investment-grade thresholds, suggesting the business model generates modest returns on capital despite strong EBITDA [4]. The planned Q4 2026 separation into two companies introduces execution risk, potential dissynergies, and could delay AI capability rollout as data and digital platforms are split [2]. Revenue declined 1.7% YoY in Q4 2025, and the trailing P/E of 47.9x reflects depressed earnings that may not fully recover. FTC antitrust investigation into distributor practices and ongoing chlorpyrifos litigation add regulatory uncertainty [3].
Key Catalysts
  1. Planned separation into two independent companies by Q4 2026, potentially unlocking value as Seed (higher-margin, technology-driven) trades at a premium multiple independently [1]
  2. Royalty neutrality achievement in 2026 via Bayer agreement, eliminating a ~$120M annual drag and improving Seed segment profitability [1]
  3. EBITDA margin expansion above 22% as Conkesta soybean technology ramps and $200M productivity savings flow through, driving guided 7% EBITDA growth [1]
Key Risks
  1. PFAS legacy liability — Corteva's 14.5% share of the $2B+ New Jersey settlement and exposure to additional PFAS lawsuits across multiple states creates material litigation tail risk [3]
  2. Business separation execution risk — splitting AI/digital platforms, supply chains, and corporate functions across two entities could erode operational efficiency and delay technology rollout [2]
  3. Low profitability metrics — 6.3% profit margin and 4.97% ROE suggest the agricultural inputs business generates modest capital returns, limiting valuation upside if growth slows [4]
