Company Profile
Energy ·Oil & Gas Refining & Marketing ·NYSE ·United States
CVR Energy is a diversified holding company engaged in petroleum refining, renewable fuels, and nitrogen fertilizer manufacturing. The company operates two crude oil refineries in Kansas and Oklahoma with combined throughput capacity of approximately 206,500 bpd, a renewable diesel facility, and two nitrogen fertilizer plants. CVR Energy is a subsidiary of Icahn Enterprises Holdings L.P.

Investment Thesis
CVR Energy offers deep value characteristics at a forward P/E of 26.1x (elevated due to trough earnings) with Carl Icahn's $16.4M insider purchase in February 2026 signaling conviction in a cyclical recovery [2]. The company's mid-continent refining position and nitrogen fertilizer diversification provide exposure to both energy and agricultural commodity cycles. When crack spreads normalize, CVR has demonstrated the ability to generate substantial cash flow — $769M net income in 2023 on $9.25B revenue, versus just $27M in 2025 on $7.16B [1]. The renewable diesel segment provides optionality on energy transition, and the PEG ratio of 0.71 suggests the market is pricing in a meaningful earnings recovery. At $31.52/share, CVR trades well below its 52-week high of $41.67, offering significant upside if refining margins improve [4].
The bear case centers on structural decline and governance risk. Revenue has contracted from $10.9B in 2022 to $7.16B in 2025, a 34% decline over three years reflecting both crack spread normalization and secular demand headwinds for petroleum products [1]. Profit margin of 0.4% is essentially breakeven, and the trailing P/E of 116.7x reflects near-zero earnings power at current refining margins. D/E of 203.7% creates meaningful financial risk during prolonged margin troughs. Dividends appear effectively suspended since August 2024, undermining the income thesis — the variable dividend model means shareholders only receive cash when crack spreads are elevated [4]. ESG-related funding constraints and energy transition policies threaten long-term asset values, with Sustainalytics rating CVR's ESG risk management as 'Weak' [3]. Icahn's controlling ownership introduces governance risk — strategic decisions are driven by the controlling shareholder's financial objectives rather than independent corporate strategy.
