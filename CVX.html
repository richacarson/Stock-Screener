Investment Thesis
Chevron offers defensive energy exposure with a 39-year dividend growth streak, $20.2B in adjusted free cash flow (up 35% YoY), and record production of 4.05M bpd. The company targets 7-10% production growth in 2026 with $3-4B in structural cost savings, while the 3.5% dividend yield and aggressive buybacks ($3.0B in Q4 alone) provide compelling total shareholder return. Low debt-to-equity of 24% and Venezuela/Libya production upside provide optionality.
The bear case centers on cyclical commodity exposure — revenue fell 6.8% and EPS declined 24% in 2025 as oil prices dropped 15%. The trailing P/E of 30x reflects depressed earnings rather than growth, and the GAAP payout ratio exceeding 100% is unsustainable if oil prices remain soft. Greenwashing allegations, the Ecuador environmental legacy, and a 'Severe' Sustainalytics risk rating create reputational headwinds.
