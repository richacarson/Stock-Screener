Company Profile
Utilities ·Utilities - Renewable ·NYSE ·United States
Clearway Energy, Inc. is one of the largest owners of clean energy generation assets in the United States, with a portfolio of approximately 12.7 GW of gross capacity across 27 states, including 9.9 GW of wind, solar, and energy storage and 2.8 GW of dispatchable natural gas generation providing grid reliability services. The company operates as a yieldco, acquiring and operating contracted renewable energy assets to generate stable cash available for distribution (CAFD).

Investment Thesis
Clearway Energy offers a compelling clean energy income vehicle with a 4.46% dividend yield, 11.0% 5yr dividend growth CAGR, and 6 consecutive years of dividend increases [1][4]. The 12.7 GW portfolio across 27 states provides stable contracted cash flows, with management reaffirming 2026 CAFD guidance of $470-$510M and long-term targets of $2.90-$3.10 CAFD/share by 2030 [1]. Revenue growth of 21.1% YoY to $1.43B reflects successful asset acquisitions, and the AI-driven surge in electricity demand creates a structural tailwind for renewable energy asset owners [3]. At 24.2x forward P/E, the stock is reasonably valued for a utility-like business with above-average growth visibility and clean energy policy support.
The bear case centers on leverage and execution risk. D/E of 159.7% with $9.4B total debt creates significant interest rate sensitivity — rising rates increase refinancing costs and reduce the present value of contracted cash flows [4]. ROE is negative at -4.0%, and GAAP payout ratio of 124% exceeds 100%, though CAFD-based distribution is sustainable. Q4 2025 results missed analyst estimates with -$0.89 EPS versus -$0.21 expected, highlighting quarterly earnings volatility driven by weather-dependent renewable generation [1]. The company depends on its sponsor (Clearway Energy Group/Global Infrastructure Partners) for growth assets — a sponsor relationship that introduces conflicts of interest and limits independent strategic flexibility. Regulatory risk remains: changes to renewable energy tax credits (IRA) or state-level clean energy mandates could impact future asset economics. The PEG ratio of 3.67 suggests the market is pricing in growth that may not materialize if asset acquisition pace slows [4].
Key Catalysts
  1. AI data center-driven electricity demand surge creating structural tailwind for renewable energy assets, with utility-scale solar and wind procurement accelerating [3]
  2. CAFD growth trajectory from $470-$510M in 2026 to $2.90-$3.10/share by 2030 driven by continued asset acquisitions and organic CAFD improvements [1]
  3. Energy storage expansion adding grid flexibility and premium pricing to the renewable portfolio, complementing intermittent wind and solar generation [3]
Key Risks
  1. Interest rate sensitivity — $9.4B debt load means rising rates increase refinancing costs and compress the spread between contracted cash flows and cost of capital [4]
  2. Weather and generation variability — Q4 2025 EPS miss of -$0.89 vs. -$0.21 expected demonstrates exposure to wind and solar resource intermittency [1]
  3. Sponsor dependency and regulatory risk — growth depends on Clearway Energy Group's asset pipeline, and changes to IRA clean energy tax credits could impair future acquisition economics [3]
