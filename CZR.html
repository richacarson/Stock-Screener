Caesars Entertainment is the largest casino entertainment company in the United States, operating properties in 18 states with approximately 50,000 employees. The company operates casinos, hotels, dining, entertainment venues, and the Caesars Sportsbook digital platform across 24 states and Washington D.C. Founded in 1937 and headquartered in Reno, Nevada.

Investment Thesis
Caesars Entertainment is the largest U.S. casino operator executing a digital transformation that is gaining traction — Caesars Digital adjusted EBITDA surged 102% to $236M in 2025 from $117M in 2024, with the Sportsbook now live in 24 states [1]. Q4 2025 net revenues grew to $2.9B with same-store EBITDA of $901M, and CEO Reeg guided for continued digital growth in 2026 alongside lower capex and interest expense, targeting strong free cash flow for debt paydown [1]. At forward P/E of 24.7x and an enterprise value reflecting the massive debt, the stock prices in significant execution risk but offers leveraged upside if digital profitability scales. The $420M buyback program (14.7M shares at depressed prices) is accretive. Regional casino revenue rose 4% in Q4, demonstrating stable core operations. The combination of 50+ physical properties, 65M+ Caesars Rewards members, and an expanding digital platform creates a multi-channel ecosystem with cross-selling potential that pure-play digital competitors cannot match [1][2].
The bear case is dominated by the crushing $25.6B debt load — among the highest in the gaming industry — which generated a $502M net loss in 2025 despite $11.5B in revenue [1][5]. Same-store adjusted EBITDA actually declined from $3.7B to $3.6B year-over-year, suggesting the brick-and-mortar business is stagnating while interest costs consume operating income. Las Vegas segment revenue fell 3.4% in Q4, a concerning signal for the company's highest-margin market [1]. The digital segment, while growing rapidly, is competing against DraftKings and FanDuel which have deeper technology stacks and larger digital market share. The D/E ratio of 695% and negative ROE (-10.8%) reflect a balance sheet that leaves virtually no room for strategic error — any recession or sustained downturn in gaming spend could threaten debt covenants [5]. The path from $502M net loss to positive earnings requires both sustained digital growth and significant interest expense reduction through debt paydown, a multi-year process with no margin for operational setbacks.
Key Catalysts
  1. Digital EBITDA trajectory — growth from $117M to $236M in 2025, with guidance for continued strong growth in 2026, could prove the digital transformation thesis [1]
  2. Debt reduction and interest expense savings — CEO Reeg targeting FCF deployment for debt paydown, which directly improves bottom line as each $1B retired saves ~$60-70M annually [1]
  3. Regional casino stability and new Sportsbook state launches (Missouri December 2025, Summerlin March 2026) expand the revenue base [1]
Key Risks
  1. Extreme leverage — $25.6B total debt at D/E of 695% creates existential risk in a downturn and generated $502M net loss in 2025 despite $11.5B revenue [1][5]
  2. Digital competition — DraftKings and FanDuel hold larger digital market share with deeper technology stacks, and Caesars Sportsbook may struggle to achieve scale economics [1]
  3. Las Vegas softening — Q4 Vegas revenue declined 3.4%, and any sustained weakness in the highest-margin segment would disproportionately impact EBITDA and debt reduction capacity [1]
