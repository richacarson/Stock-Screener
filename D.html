Company Profile
Utilities ·Regulated Electric Utilities ·NYSE ·United States
Dominion Energy, Inc. provides regulated electric and natural gas utility services to approximately 4.5 million customers in Virginia, North Carolina, and South Carolina. The company operates the world's largest data center market in Northern Virginia with 51 GW of contracted data center capacity. In May 2026, Dominion agreed to a $67 billion all-stock merger with NextEra Energy to create the nation's largest regulated electric utility. Founded in 1983, headquartered in Richmond, Virginia.

Dominion Energy under CEO Bob Blue demonstrates meaningful infinite game characteristics — a regulated utility inherently operates on multi-decade time horizons, and the company has made transformative strategic decisions including the 2020 gas transmission divestiture and the $67B NextEra merger. Investment Thesis
Dominion Energy sits at the epicenter of the AI-driven electricity demand supercycle, operating the world's largest data center market in Northern Virginia with 51 GW of contracted capacity — tripling since mid-2023 [1][3]. Q1 2026 revenue surged 23% YoY to $5.02B with operating EPS of $0.95 beating consensus of $0.86, and the pending $67B merger with NextEra Energy will create the nation's largest regulated utility with enhanced scale for renewable energy and grid infrastructure investment [7]. At forward P/E of 19.33, dividend yield of 3.84%, and 5-7% long-term EPS growth guidance biased to the upper half through 2030, Dominion offers a compelling regulated utility profile uniquely levered to the AI infrastructure buildout [1].
The bear case centers on execution risk and the broken dividend story. The NextEra merger requires 12-18 months of regulatory approvals across FERC, NRC, and three state commissions — any of which could impose costly conditions or block the transaction [7]. Dominion's dividend has 0 consecutive years of growth and a negative 5-year CAGR of -3.19% after the 2020 cut, with a 79.25% payout ratio limiting future increases, making it an impaired income holding relative to utility peers with 10+ year dividend growth streaks [5][6]. D/E of 155% combined with $65B in planned five-year capex and $6-9.5B in 2026 debt issuance creates balance sheet risk if rate recovery lags investment or if data center demand growth moderates [1][5]. The $11.4B offshore wind project has only $123M in remaining contingency (6% of remaining costs), leaving minimal buffer for final construction overruns [1].
Key Catalysts
  1. NextEra merger completion (expected 2027-2028) creating the nation's largest regulated utility with $2.25B in customer bill credits and combined scale for data center, renewable energy, and grid modernization investment [7]
  2. Data center contracted capacity of 51 GW with 10.4 GW under executed Electric Service Agreements driving multi-year rate base growth, with commercial sales already surging 8.4% in Q1 2026 [1][3]
  3. Coastal Virginia Offshore Wind project reaching completion at $11.4B budget with levelized cost of $84/MWh — well below the $149/MWh prudency cap — unlocking clean energy capacity and regulatory goodwill [1]
Key Risks
  1. NextEra merger regulatory risk — FERC, NRC, and three state utility commissions must approve; failure or extended delay could create prolonged uncertainty and limit Dominion's strategic flexibility as a standalone entity [7]
  2. Broken dividend track record — 0 years of consecutive growth, -3.19% five-year CAGR, and 79.25% payout ratio make Dominion an impaired income holding that lags utility peers with established dividend growth streaks [5][6]
  3. Capital intensity of $65B over five years combined with D/E of 155% and $6-9.5B in planned 2026 debt issuance creates balance sheet pressure if interest rates remain elevated or data center demand growth decelerates [1][5]

AI Resilience (25%)
AI Resilience LOW RISK
9
AI Risk Protection (8/9): Dominion Energy's core business — physical electric generation, transmission, and distribution infrastructure serving 4.5 million customers — is structurally immune to AI disruption [1]. Power generation requires physical plants, turbines, transformers, and thousands of miles of transmission lines that no software can replace. The business is deeply regulated by state public utility commissions, creating additional structural barriers. Most critically, AI is a direct and powerful demand driver for Dominion: Northern Virginia hosts the world's largest data center market, and contracted capacity has tripled to 51 GW since July 2023, with commercial sales surging 8.4% in Q1 2026 driven primarily by data center expansion [1][3]. The AI compute buildout requires massive amounts of electricity, directly increasing Dominion's rate base and earnings power. Scoring 8 rather than 9 because Dominion is not itself building AI infrastructure — it is a beneficiary, not a creator, and execution risk in building generation capacity fast enough to meet demand is real. AI Opportunity (1/1): AI data center demand is generating quantifiable revenue growth — Q1 2026 revenue surged 23% YoY to $5.02B, driven in significant part by data center load growth [1][3]. The 51 GW pipeline and $65B five-year capital plan are directly tied to serving AI infrastructure demand.
