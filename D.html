Company Profile
Utilities ·Regulated Electric ·NYSE ·United States
Dominion Energy is one of the largest regulated electric and natural gas utilities in the United States, serving approximately 7 million customers across 20 states. The company operates 30.7 GW of generating capacity and is the primary power supplier to northern Virginia's data center corridor, which accounts for 13% of global data center capacity. Dominion is investing $65 billion over 2026-2030 in grid modernization, offshore wind, and data center connectivity.

Investment Thesis
Dominion Energy is uniquely positioned as the primary power supplier to northern Virginia's data center corridor — 13% of global data center capacity — making it a regulated utility play on AI infrastructure demand [1][2]. Q4 2025 revenue surged 20% to $4.09B with net income of $567M (up from $59M YoY), driven by hyperscale customer demand. The company has 10 GW of signed data center contracts with 30 GW in negotiation, and the $65B 2026-2030 capital plan including a $500M Amazon nuclear SMR joint venture positions Dominion for sustained 5-7% EPS growth through 2030 [1]. At 16.3x forward P/E with a 4.39% dividend yield, Dominion offers attractive income plus growth optionality from the AI energy supercycle.
The bear case centers on execution and financial risk. D/E of 150% is elevated, and the $65B capital plan will require significant debt issuance and potential equity dilution. The Coastal Virginia Offshore Wind project has already generated $258M in unrecoverable cost charges, and further overruns could erode returns [1]. The 2020 dividend cut from $0.94 to $0.63/quarter — with only modest recovery to $0.668 — destroyed income investor trust and dividend growth metrics remain weak (1.2% 5yr CAGR, 77% payout ratio). ROE of 9.7% is below the 15% target for quality utilities. Concentration risk is real: if data center demand growth slows or hyperscale customers develop on-site power solutions, Dominion's growth thesis weakens. Regulatory risk looms as Virginia ratepayers increasingly bear costs of data center-driven grid expansion [2][3].
Key Catalysts
  1. Data center demand acceleration — 10 GW signed contracts with 30 GW in negotiation, driven by AI computing buildout in northern Virginia [2]

Key Risks
  1. CVOW execution risk — the largest US offshore wind project has already incurred $258M in unrecoverable charges with potential for further cost overruns and regulatory challenges [1]
  2. Leverage and dilution — D/E of 150% with a $65B capital plan will require significant debt and potential equity issuance, pressuring the balance sheet [4]
  3. Data center concentration — if hyperscale customers develop on-site power generation or AI demand growth slows, Dominion's growth premium could compress rapidly [2]
