Company Profile
Consumer Cyclical ·Auto Parts ·NYSE ·United States
Dana Incorporated provides power-conveyance and energy-management solutions for on-highway vehicles worldwide. The company supplies axles, driveshafts, transmissions, e-axle and e-transmission systems, inverters, electric motors, and thermal management products to light vehicle, commercial vehicle, and off-highway OEMs across North America, Europe, South America, and Asia Pacific.

Investment Thesis
Dana Incorporated at 10.3x forward earnings offers deep value exposure to an underappreciated EV drivetrain transition play with a $750M three-year new-business backlog and guided 2026 EBITDA of $750M-$850M representing 250bps of margin expansion over 2025 [1]. The company's e-axle and thermal management capabilities position it to benefit from the long-term electrification trend across light vehicle, commercial, and off-highway markets, with $200M in incremental new-business growth expected in 2026 from next-gen OEM platforms.
The bear case is severe: D/E of 394% represents critical leverage risk, negative ROE of -4.3% reflects a business that is destroying shareholder value, and revenue has declined from $10.2B to $7.5B in three years [2]. The November 2024 CEO replacement suggests the board lost confidence in the prior strategic direction, and new CEO McDonald's restructuring may not be sufficient to restore profitability. The EV transition creates binary risk — if EV adoption slows, Dana's electrification investments may not generate returns, while if EVs accelerate, the margin compression from new platform launches could persist. The 91% payout ratio on a 1.04% yield signals a dividend that is barely sustainable and unlikely to grow, while the PEG of 4.21 suggests the market sees limited growth relative to the price.
