Investment Thesis
Darling Ingredients is the world's largest processor of animal byproducts and food waste, converting approximately 15% of global meat-industry waste streams into feed, food, renewable fuel, and fertilizer across 250+ plants in 17 countries [4]. The bull case rests on a genuine circular-economy Just Cause, a 22-year CEO tenure under Randy Stuewe, improving top-line momentum (Q4 2025 revenue +20.6% YoY to $1.71B, FY +7.4% to $6.14B), and recovery optionality in Diamond Green Diesel as biofuel credit conditions normalize [1]. The Inspire Insight score of +53 reflects ALIGNED positive attributions including Business Resilience, Product Sustainability, and Waste and Hazmat Management. At ~17-19x forward P/E on recovery earnings, valuation is reasonable if the renewable-fuel earnings bridge holds [5].
The bear case is material. FY2025 net income collapsed to $62.8M ($0.39 EPS) from $278.9M ($1.73 EPS) — a 77% drop — as the Diamond Green Diesel JV was hit by biofuel-credit headwinds and policy uncertainty [1]. D/E has climbed to ~86.5% with net debt of ~$4.07B, and the company carries no dividend cushion for investors waiting on a rebound [5]. Glassdoor reviews describe 'horrible upper management' and 'culture is bad,' and the executive team has been graded 'C-' on Comparably [3]. Renewable-fuel policy volatility (RINs, Section 45Z tax credits, Low Carbon Fuel Standard pricing) could keep earnings depressed well into 2026-27, and Q1 2026 guidance for core ingredients adjusted EBITDA of only $240-250M is below historical levels [1]. Trailing P/E of ~140x on depressed earnings makes the stock optically expensive and amplifies multiple risk.
Key Catalysts
  1. Q1 2026 core ingredients adjusted EBITDA guide of $240-250M establishes a recovery baseline from the 2025 earnings trough [1]
  2. Renewable-fuel policy normalization (RINs, Section 45Z credits) would restore Diamond Green Diesel JV profitability and drive EPS rebound to the ~$3+ analyst expectation [1]
  3. Scope 1&2 emissions reduction of 42% by 2030 (SBTi-aligned) and DarLinQ Used Cooking Oil technology demonstrate continued sustainability execution that supports ESG-screen inflows [2][4]
Key Risks
  1. Biofuel-credit and renewable-fuel policy volatility — 2025 net income fell 77% YoY as Diamond Green Diesel headwinds hit, and policy uncertainty could persist [1]
  2. Leverage and liquidity — $4.16B total debt, $88.7M cash, D/E ~86.5% leave limited balance-sheet flexibility if earnings stay depressed [5]
  3. Culture and retention — Glassdoor 3.3/5 with negative commentary on 'upper management' and 'culture,' plus Comparably executive team grade of C-, could hamper talent retention in AI/innovation roles [3]
