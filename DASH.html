Investment Thesis
DoorDash is the dominant U.S. local commerce platform with 68% food delivery market share, accelerating revenue growth (Q4 2025: $4.0B, up 38% YoY), and a clear path to margin expansion as the business scales [1]. The company generated $2.38B in FCF in 2025 with adj. EBITDA of $780M in Q4 alone (up 38%), demonstrating operating leverage in the core business [1]. At 19.6x forward P/E with 37.7% revenue growth and 47.7% earnings growth, DASH offers attractive growth-adjusted valuation [4]. The 35M+ DashPass membership base creates recurring revenue with strong retention, while expansion into grocery, retail, and international markets (Deliveroo contributing $200M EBITDA in 2026) extends the addressable market well beyond restaurant delivery [1]. The proprietary AI logistics platform and real-world commerce data represent a widening competitive moat [2].
The bear case centers on margin sustainability and competitive risk. DoorDash's announcement to invest 'several hundred million dollars' more in 2026 prompted analyst price target cuts (Oppenheimer to $280, JPMorgan to $300, Cantor to $270), signaling concern that profitability improvement will plateau [1]. Profit margin of 6.8% remains thin for a company with $65B market cap, and gig worker reclassification legislation (California AB5 precedent) could structurally increase costs [3]. The stock has fallen 15% from its SMA-50 of $177.30, reflecting near-term investor unease. Uber Eats maintains aggressive competition globally, and autonomous delivery technology could disintermediate DoorDash's Dasher network over time. Glassdoor reviews revealing 'fear-driven toxicity' and poor middle-management culture (58% recommend, 2.4/5 for Data Scientists) suggest talent retention risk as the company scales [2].
