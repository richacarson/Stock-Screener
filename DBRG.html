Company Profile
Financial Services ·Asset Management ·NYSE ·United States
DigitalBridge Group, Inc. is a global alternative asset manager dedicated exclusively to digital infrastructure investments, including data centers, cell towers, fiber networks, small cells, and edge infrastructure. The firm manages approximately $108 billion in infrastructure assets with $80.1 billion in AUM and $32.8 billion in fee-earning equity under management. Founded by Marc Ganzi, DigitalBridge has been at the forefront of the convergence between power and data infrastructure. In December 2025, SoftBank Group announced a definitive agreement to acquire DigitalBridge for approximately $4.0 billion ($16.00 per share), with the transaction expected to close in the second half of 2026.

Investment Thesis
DigitalBridge is a uniquely positioned pure-play digital infrastructure asset manager with $80.1 billion AUM (up 52% YoY) and $32.8 billion in fee-earning equity under management [1]. Fee revenue of $375 million grew 14% YoY in FY2025 with fee-related earnings of $142 million up 33%, demonstrating improving operating leverage as the platform scales [6]. The SoftBank acquisition at $16.00/share (closing expected H2 2026) validates the thesis that AI infrastructure is the defining asset class of the decade — SoftBank explicitly cited DigitalBridge's capabilities to address the projected $6.7 trillion AI infrastructure market by 2030 [3]. With a $43 billion capex plan, the DBP III fund at $11.7 billion in commitments, and portfolio companies including hyperscale data centers and the Yondr Group acquisition, DigitalBridge sits at the intersection of the two most powerful secular trends in technology: AI and infrastructure.
The primary risk is execution uncertainty through the SoftBank acquisition process. While the deal has received stockholder approval and is expected to close by July 2026, integration risks remain — SoftBank's own financial leverage and strategic shifts could redirect DigitalBridge's capital allocation priorities [3]. The stock trades at $15.80 against the $16.00 acquisition price, offering minimal upside and capping the investment case to a risk arbitrage spread. If the deal were to break, the standalone valuation at 37x forward earnings is elevated for an asset manager with sub-7% ROE and 11.6% profit margins. The company's GAAP financials are complex and difficult for generalist investors to parse, with total revenue declining 84.5% in 2025 due to deconsolidation — a structural transformation that obscures the underlying fee revenue growth story.
Key Catalysts
  1. SoftBank acquisition closing expected H2 2026 at $16.00/share, providing near-term price certainty and long-term platform expansion through SoftBank's global AI infrastructure ambitions
  2. DBP III fund at $11.7 billion in commitments positions DigitalBridge to deploy capital into AI-era data centers, with the projected $6.7 trillion AI infrastructure market by 2030 providing a multi-decade tailwind
  3. Fee revenue growth of 14% YoY with FRE margin expansion to 38% demonstrates the operating leverage of the asset-light model as AUM scales past $80 billion
Key Risks
  1. SoftBank acquisition risk — any regulatory delay or deal break would expose a standalone business trading at 37x forward earnings with sub-7% ROE, creating significant downside from current levels
  2. GAAP revenue declined 84.5% in 2025 due to deconsolidation of portfolio companies, creating investor confusion and potential for mispricing even as underlying fee revenues grow 14% annually
  3. Concentration risk in digital infrastructure — a slowdown in hyperscale cloud/AI capex spending or data center overbuilding could compress fee revenue growth and carried interest realizations
