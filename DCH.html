Company Profile

Dauch Corporation (formerly American Axle & Manufacturing) designs, engineers, and manufactures driveline and metal forming technologies for electric, hybrid, and internal combustion vehicles. Following the transformational acquisition of Dowlais Group (GKN Automotive) in Q1 2026, Dauch became one of the world's largest automotive driveline suppliers with approximately $10.5B in guided 2026 revenue. Headquartered in Detroit, Michigan.

Investment Thesis
Dauch's transformational acquisition of Dowlais Group (GKN Automotive) creates a global driveline powerhouse with $10.3-10.8B in 2026 guided revenue, up from ~$5.8B standalone. The combined company has leading positions in both traditional driveline and electric drive units, with $50-75M in year-one synergies targeting $100M+ run rate. At just 8.58x forward earnings and $5.68/share (61% below $9.16 analyst consensus target), the stock prices in significant execution risk but offers substantial upside if integration succeeds [1][2]. Adjusted EBITDA guidance of $1.3-1.425B implies ~13% margins, competitive with Tier 1 peers.
The bear case centers on execution risk and balance sheet strain. With $5.25B in debt against $1.50B equity (D/E ~350%) and a projected 2026 net loss of $180-335M, Dauch has minimal margin for error [1][2]. Integration of Dowlais — a UK-based company with different culture and operations — adds complexity. The auto industry faces cyclical headwinds including potential tariff disruptions and EV adoption uncertainty. Adjusted free cash flow guidance of $235-325M must cover $210-275M in restructuring and synergy implementation payments, leaving thin coverage for debt reduction. If vehicle production declines or synergies underdeliver, the leverage could become unsustainable.
Key Catalysts
  1. Realization of $100M+ annual synergies from Dowlais integration, with $50-75M flowing through in 2026 and full run-rate expected by year-end
  2. Ramp of electric drive unit (eDrive) programs across OEM customers as EV adoption accelerates, leveraging GKN Automotive's leading eDrive technology
  3. Deleveraging path from current D/E of ~350% as adjusted free cash flow of $235-325M enables debt reduction toward investment-grade metrics
Key Risks
  1. Acquisition integration failure — combining Dauch's North American operations with Dowlais's global footprint involves cultural, operational, and technology harmonization risk across 26,000+ employees
  2. Cyclical automotive production downturn or tariff disruption could compress revenues while the company carries $5.25B in debt with limited financial flexibility
  3. EV transition timing mismatch — if EV adoption slows, the premium paid for GKN's eDrive capabilities may not generate expected returns; if it accelerates too fast, legacy ICE driveline revenues erode before eDrive scales
