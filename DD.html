Company Profile

DuPont de Nemours provides technology-based materials and solutions across two segments: Healthcare & Water Technologies (water filtration, medical device components) and Diversified Industrials (construction materials, engineered products, adhesives). Following the 2019 DowDuPont split and 2025 Electronics separation (Qnity), DuPont is focused on healthcare, water, and industrial applications.

Investment Thesis
DuPont is emerging as a focused specialty materials company after years of restructuring, with 2026 guidance of $7.08-$7.14B revenue and $2.25-$2.30 adjusted EPS representing meaningful recovery from the restructuring-depressed 2025 base [1]. The Healthcare & Water Technologies segment offers mid-single-digit growth in structurally attractive markets — water scarcity drives filtration demand and aging demographics support medical devices. At 18.0x forward P/E with 8-10% medium-term EPS CAGR guidance, the valuation is reasonable for a specialty chemicals company with improving margins (60-80bps EBITDA margin expansion guided for 2026) and >90% FCF conversion [1]. The $2B buyback authorization and $1.2B Aramids divestiture proceeds provide near-term capital return support.
The bear case centers on execution risk and legacy liabilities. DuPont's PFAS litigation exposure remains a material overhang — the $2.5B New Jersey settlement, $1.185B public water system settlement, and ongoing state AG lawsuits from Texas and North Carolina could produce additional billions in liabilities [3]. Organic growth of only 2-3% is modest for a company trading at 18x forward earnings, and the Diversified Industrials segment faces cyclical headwinds with low-single-digit growth expectations. GAAP profitability in 2025 was razor-thin ($57M net income on $6.85B revenue) due to restructuring charges, and the transition to forward estimates requires faith in management's execution. Revenue missed Q4 consensus by 2.6%, suggesting demand softness. The stock has risen 40% over the past year, pricing in much of the turnaround [1].
Key Catalysts
  1. 2026 adjusted EPS of $2.25-$2.30 represents a major step-up from the restructuring-depressed 2025 base of $1.68, with 60-80bps of EBITDA margin expansion guided [1]
  2. Healthcare & Water Technologies segment growing mid-single digits in structurally attractive end markets driven by water scarcity and aging demographics [1]
  3. $2B share buyback authorization plus $1.2B Aramids divestiture proceeds provide significant capital return capacity on a ~$19B market cap [1]
Key Risks
  1. PFAS litigation exposure beyond existing settlements — Texas AG, North Carolina AG, and additional state and federal lawsuits could produce billions in additional liabilities [3]
  2. Organic revenue growth of only 2-3% is below the company's medium-term target of 3-4% CAGR, with Diversified Industrials facing cyclical weakness [1]
  3. Serial portfolio restructuring (DowDuPont split, Electronics separation, Aramids sale) creates integration risk and management distraction as the company stabilizes around two remaining segments [1]
