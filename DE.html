Deere & Company manufactures and distributes agricultural, construction, forestry, and turf care equipment worldwide. The company operates through Production & Precision Ag, Small Ag & Turf, Construction & Forestry, and Financial Products segments. Deere is a leader in precision agriculture technology, autonomous machinery, and AI-powered farming solutions.

Investment Thesis
Deere is a generational franchise positioned at the intersection of agriculture and AI, with 189 years of brand equity, 60% North American large equipment market share, and a transformative precision agriculture platform [1][2]. Q1 2026 revenue of $9.61B beat estimates by 26.6%, with Small Ag & Turf and Construction both growing 15%, signaling the cyclical trough is behind [1]. Management guided FY2026 net income of $4.5B-$5.0B, above consensus. The autonomous farming commitment by 2030, See & Spray AI weed detection reducing herbicide use 77%, and OpenAI partnership position Deere to capture recurring software/subscription revenue on top of equipment sales. ROE of 19.6%, 53+ consecutive years of dividends, and a 15.3% five-year dividend CAGR demonstrate durable capital allocation discipline [4].
The bear case rests on cyclical risk, valuation, and the pace of AI adoption. FY2025 revenue declined 11.1% and net income fell 29.2% to $5.03B as the agricultural equipment cycle peaked [1]. D/E of 376% — driven by John Deere Financial's lending operations — creates leverage risk if agricultural credit quality deteriorates. Forward P/E of 25.7x and PEG of 1.75 embed significant recovery expectations; if the cycle trough extends, multiple compression could drive meaningful downside [4]. Profit margin of 10.3% has contracted from cycle-peak levels above 16%, and further compression is possible. CEO John May's $20.8M insider sale in January 2026 raises questions about near-term confidence. The 'right to repair' movement threatens Deere's aftermarket revenue and data moat as legislation enables third-party access to equipment software [2]. Technology democratization risk is real — as AI and computer vision commoditize, AGCO and CNH Industrial could close the precision agriculture gap, eroding Deere's technology premium [2].
Key Catalysts
  1. Agricultural cycle recovery — Small Ag & Turf and Construction both guided up 15% for FY2026, with management signaling 2026 represents the cycle bottom and foundation for accelerated growth [1]
  2. Autonomous farming milestone — fully autonomous corn and soybean production cycle targeted by 2030, with autonomous 9RX tractor and expanded See & Spray deployment creating new recurring revenue streams [2]
  3. Precision agriculture platform monetization — software subscriptions, data analytics, and AI-powered services layered on top of the installed equipment base, shifting revenue mix toward higher-margin recurring streams [2]
Key Risks
  1. Extended agricultural downturn — if commodity prices decline or farm income deteriorates further, equipment purchases could be deferred beyond FY2026, invalidating the cycle recovery thesis [1]
  2. Technology democratization — AGCO, CNH Industrial, and precision agriculture startups deploying comparable AI/autonomous capabilities at lower cost, eroding Deere's technology premium and justification for higher equipment pricing [2]
  3. Right-to-repair legislation enabling third-party software access to Deere equipment, threatening the data moat and aftermarket revenue that underpin the precision agriculture strategy [2]
