Investment Thesis
Deckers is one of the best-executed consumer brand platforms in the world — Q3 FY2026 revenue hit a record $1.96B (+7.1%) with HOKA up 18% and UGG reaching a record $1.3B quarterly at 5% growth [1]. Gross margin of 59.8% on full-price selling and raised FY2026 guidance ($5.4-$5.425B revenue; $6.80-$6.85 EPS, 7-8% growth) demonstrate pricing power rare in apparel/footwear [1]. The acquisition and scaling of HOKA from ~$30M to multi-billion dollars is a transformative brand-platform pivot, and simultaneous UGG reinvention from seasonal slipper to year-round lifestyle franchise shows rare operational excellence. Forward P/E near 15x for this growth profile with net-cash balance sheet and ROE of ~38% makes Deckers an attractive compounder. Inspire score of +61 (ALIGNED) reinforces the quality story.
The bear case: DECK has pulled back meaningfully in 2025 on concerns about HOKA growth deceleration (from 30%+ to 18%) and UGG cycle maturity. Athletic footwear is competitive — Nike is working through a reset, On Holding (ONON) is scaling fast, and consumer discretionary spending is sensitive to macro conditions. Tariff exposure on Asian manufacturing is real. The new CEO Stefano Caroti (August 2024) is still establishing his operating cadence and has guided conservatively. HOKA saturation in specialty-run wholesale and DTC could cap growth if product innovation cycles slow. While faith-alignment is positive, the premium valuation requires sustained execution — any disappointment in HOKA's performance running narrative could compress the multiple significantly. Overall 75 = HOLD with meaningful upside if HOKA reaccelerates.

  3. Gross margin expansion and full-price-selling discipline at 59.8% supporting EPS guidance of $6.80-$6.85 FY2026, with buyback capacity from net-cash balance sheet [1]
Key Risks

  2. Asian manufacturing tariff exposure and supply-chain disruption risk for brands heavily dependent on Vietnam/China production [3]
  3. Premium-footwear competitive intensity from Nike reset, On Holding scaling, Brooks specialty-run dominance — share-loss risk in key HOKA channels [2]
