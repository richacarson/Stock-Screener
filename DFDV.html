Company Profile
Financial Services ·Asset Management ·NASDAQ ·United States
DeFi Development Corp. (formerly Janover Inc.) focuses on building its treasury around Solana (SOL) cryptocurrency. The company operates through two segments: Digital Asset Treasury (purchasing, holding, and compounding SOL through staking and validator operations) and Real Estate Platform (a technology platform connecting commercial mortgage borrowers with lenders). Founded in 2018, headquartered in Boca Raton, Florida. Renamed from Janover Inc. in April 2025.

Investment Thesis
DeFi Development's bull case is simple: leveraged exposure to Solana at a discount. The company holds 2.2M SOL tokens (worth ~$300M+ at current SOL prices) against a $127M market cap, potentially trading at a significant discount to net asset value [1]. FY2025 revenue grew 442% to $11.4M from SOL staking income, and the validator operations provide a yield on the SOL treasury. COO Parker White's $171K in open-market purchases signals insider conviction [4]. If SOL appreciates significantly, the leveraged structure (convertible debt + equity) could generate substantial shareholder returns. The $5B equity line of credit provides additional capital raising capacity to grow the SOL position, and management's background in crypto markets may enable sophisticated treasury management [1].
The bear case is severe. DeFi Development is essentially a leveraged cryptocurrency bet structured as a public company. The $73.8M net loss in FY2025 demonstrates the extreme volatility of fair-value accounting for digital assets [1]. D/E of 128.4% with $127M in convertible debt means the company faces potential forced SOL liquidation in a crypto downturn, creating reflexive downside risk. SPS guidance was already cut from 0.165 to 0.085 as 'mNAV compression' pressured the strategy [1]. The company has only 16 employees and virtually no operational moat — any fund or ETF can replicate the SOL treasury strategy without the equity dilution and management fees. The April 2025 pivot from Janover (lending) to crypto treasury raises governance questions about management's long-term commitment. Free cash flow is deeply negative (-$55M), and the company depends entirely on capital market access to fund operations and SOL purchases. If crypto markets enter a bear cycle, the leveraged structure could result in permanent capital loss [4].
Key Catalysts
  1. Solana price appreciation — with 2.2M SOL held, every $1 increase in SOL price adds ~$2.2M to treasury value, creating leveraged upside at the current $127M market cap [1]
  2. SOL staking yield from validator operations provides recurring revenue independent of SOL price movements, growing from $2.1M in 2024 to $11.4M in 2025 [1]
  3. $5B equity line of credit provides capital raising capacity to grow the SOL position accretively if market conditions and SOL prices are favorable [1]
Key Risks
  1. Leveraged cryptocurrency exposure — $127M in convertible debt financing SOL purchases creates reflexive downside risk if Solana prices decline, potentially forcing asset liquidation [1]
  2. No operational moat — the SOL treasury strategy can be replicated by any fund, ETF, or individual investor, making the company's value proposition fragile [1]
  3. Extreme earnings volatility — the $73.8M net loss in FY2025 from unrealized digital asset losses demonstrates that fair-value accounting for crypto assets produces uncontrollable P&L swings [1][4]
