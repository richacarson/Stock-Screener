Investment Thesis
T3 Defense is a newly rebranded defense holding company (formerly Nukkleus, a fintech firm) attempting to capitalize on rising global defense spending by acquiring and integrating small Israeli and US defense businesses. The company reported $4.2M in Q1 2026 revenue — its first quarter as a defense entity — and reaffirmed $26M full-year 2026 guidance with a $12.1M backlog and $12.0M in active RFPs [1]. The portfolio spans counter-drone systems, tactical power, AI software (Tiltan), and missile defense components, including claimed Iron Dome supply chain exposure. The geopolitical backdrop is favorable, with NATO defense budgets expanding and global demand for integrated air defense rising significantly.
The bear case is severe. T3 Defense carries a net operating loss of $32.6M, negative working capital of ~$30M, stockholders' deficit of $15.6M, and a current ratio of 0.3 as of year-end 2025 [2]. FCF is deeply negative at -$133M, and the stock has collapsed 97% from its 52-week high of $26.21 to $0.76 [3]. The pivot from fintech to defense in February 2026 is only two months old — the company has no established track record in its current industry. CEO Menny Shalom operates under a consulting agreement rather than traditional employment, and the company faces a pending lawsuit over success fees [2]. The $26M revenue target requires significant back-half acceleration from the $4.2M Q1 pace. At $29M market cap, this is a highly speculative micro-cap with existential financial risk that overwhelms the favorable defense demand environment.
