Investment Thesis
Digi Power X offers pure-play exposure to AI infrastructure build-out with an unusually clean balance sheet: $93M liquidity, zero debt, and a 400MW capacity pipeline with 60MW of hydroelectric power already approved in upstate NY [1][2]. The ARMS 200 modular Tier III data center platform with ANSI/TIA-942 certification and NVIDIA B200/B300 engineering differentiates from generic colocation, and the partnership with former Verizon CEO Hans Vestberg through U.S. Data Centers provides enterprise credibility. First AI revenues are expected by end-April 2026, with 100MW of live infrastructure targeted by 2027 and a projected $282M run-rate at full activation [1]. At $218M market cap, the company is a leveraged bet on AI data center power scarcity.
The bear case is pre-revenue execution risk and competitive intensity. FY2025 revenue of $34.2M came primarily from legacy Bitcoin mining operations being wound down — the AI business has not yet generated meaningful revenue and 'first AI revenues' are contingent on GPU testing completion [1][2]. GAAP net loss of $28.4M with a -83% net margin and no clear forward P/E anchor valuation to multiples of speculative 2027-2028 revenue [1]. CoreWeave, IREN, Core Scientific, Applied Digital, and Iris Energy all have larger capacity pipelines, deeper customer contracts, and more operational history — DGXX must execute flawlessly against incumbents. Stock volatility is extreme (micro-cap with low liquidity), and any delay in Columbiana GPU commissioning would damage the 2026 investment thesis.
Key Catalysts
  1. First AI revenues by end-April 2026 from Columbiana, Alabama GPU commissioning — execution milestone that validates the entire pivot thesis [1][2]
  2. 60MW upstate NY hydroelectric capacity deployment — specific carbon-advantaged power supply that monetizes into high-value AI colocation contracts [2]
  3. Hans Vestberg / U.S. Data Centers partnership expansion — enterprise anchor tenant announcements would materially de-risk the 400MW capacity pipeline [2]
Key Risks
  1. Execution risk on first AI revenue — any delay in Columbiana GPU commissioning beyond April 2026 would damage the growth narrative and accelerate cash burn [1]
  2. Competitive intensity from CoreWeave, IREN, Core Scientific, Applied Digital, and Iris Energy, all with larger pipelines, deeper customer contracts, and operational history [2]
  3. Pivot-era strategic instability — a company that went from Bitcoin mining to AI data centers in 18 months may pivot again if AI colocation economics deteriorate, signaling finite-game capital allocation [2]
