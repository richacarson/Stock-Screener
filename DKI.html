Company Profile
Communication Services ·Electronic Gaming & Multimedia ·NASDAQ ·Hong Kong
DarkIris Inc. is a comprehensive technology enterprise engaged in the development, publishing, and operating of mobile digital games on various platforms in Hong Kong, Macau, and Taiwan. Founded in 2017 and headquartered in Sheung Wan, Hong Kong, the company is expanding into AI-Generated Content (AIGC) for gaming and film production through its Singapore-based AETHER INTELLIGENCE R&D subsidiary. The company completed a 1-for-16 reverse stock split in May 2026 and has a market capitalization of approximately $5.8 million.

Investment Thesis
DarkIris Inc. is a micro-cap mobile game developer headquartered in Hong Kong with FY2025 revenue of $10.08 million (up 27.3% YoY) but a net loss of $8.64 million, resulting in a deeply negative profit margin of -85.75% [1]. The company is attempting a strategic pivot from pure mobile gaming to an AIGC-powered content creation platform spanning gaming and film production, with a Singapore-based R&D hub (AETHER INTELLIGENCE) targeting Beta 1.0 by Q3 2026 [3][4]. CEO Zhifang Hong personally participated in a $3.8 million PIPE financing at $0.35 per share in April 2026, demonstrating personal commitment, but the deeply discounted placement price also reflects significant dilution and market skepticism [5].
The fundamental challenges are severe. With a $5.8 million market cap, 19 employees, and $1.8 million cash (supplemented by the PIPE), DarkIris is attempting to compete in AI content generation against companies with thousands of engineers and billions in resources. The AIGC platform vision — evolving into a MaaS/PaaS offering for global content creators — is extraordinarily ambitious for a company of this size and financial position. The 1-for-16 reverse stock split in May 2026 to maintain NASDAQ listing compliance, combined with ongoing cash burn and reliance on equity dilution for financing, raises serious going-concern questions [6]. Average daily dollar volume of approximately $247,000 makes the stock essentially untradeable for institutional investors and fails the IOWN liquidity floor, resulting in an Infrastructure score of 0. This is a speculative micro-cap with an unproven pivot strategy, not a suitable investment for the IOWN framework.
