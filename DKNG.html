Company Profile
Consumer Cyclical ·Gambling ·NASDAQ ·United States
DraftKings Inc. operates as a digital sports entertainment and gaming company in the United States and internationally. The company offers online and retail sports betting, daily fantasy sports, digital lottery couriers, prediction markets, and iGaming products including blackjack, roulette, baccarat and slot machines. Also designs and develops sports betting and casino gaming software for online and retail sportsbooks. Founded in 2011, headquartered in Boston, Massachusetts.

Investment Thesis
DraftKings has reached a profitability inflection, delivering its second consecutive quarter of positive net income in Q1 2026 with 17% revenue growth to $1.65B and full-year guidance of $6.5B-$6.9B revenue and $700M-$900M Adjusted EBITDA [1]. The forward P/E of 15.4x with a PEG of 0.1x offers compelling growth-adjusted valuation as the business transitions from land-grab to profit-harvesting. Free cash flow reached $603M TTM, and the TAM continues expanding as additional states legalize online sports betting and iGaming. The 47% 5-year revenue CAGR demonstrates category-creating execution [1][4].
The bear case centers on DraftKings' precarious balance sheet — D/E of 317% and trailing profit margin under 1% leave minimal buffer if customer acquisition costs increase or hold rates normalize [1]. The gambling industry faces structural regulatory risk as states may increase tax rates on online gaming revenue, with New York's 51% rate as the cautionary example. Competition from FanDuel, ESPN BET, and international operators keeps promotional spending elevated. Revenue growth is decelerating from 47% CAGR to 17% YoY in Q1 2026, and the Gambling negative attribution in the Inspire score reflects genuine social harm risk from addiction [3][5].
Key Catalysts
  1. Additional state legalizations for iGaming (currently only 7 states) could double the addressable market for DraftKings' highest-margin product line
  2. Full-year 2026 EBITDA guidance of $700M-$900M represents a step-change in profitability that could catalyze institutional investor interest and multiple expansion
  3. Prediction markets and digital lottery courier expansion into non-sports verticals diversifying revenue beyond traditional sports betting seasonality
Key Risks
  1. State tax rate increases on online gaming revenue could materially compress margins — New York's 51% rate demonstrates legislative risk that could spread to other high-value states
  2. D/E of 317% with $1.9B debt against $999M cash creates refinancing risk if profitability trajectory stalls or capital markets tighten
  3. Revenue growth deceleration from 47% CAGR to 17% YoY signals maturation of the US sports betting market, limiting the growth premium in valuation
