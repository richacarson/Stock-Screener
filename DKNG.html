Investment Thesis
DraftKings achieved first-ever full-year GAAP profitability in 2025, with revenue growing to $6.055B (+27%), adjusted EBITDA tripling to $600M+, and Q4 EBITDA margin expanding 1,000 bps to 17% [1]. The company is deploying DraftKings Predictions — a CFTC-regulated event contracts platform targeting a $10B TAM — which represents a second growth engine alongside sports betting. At 4M monthly unique payers with $125 ARPMUP and only 33 of 41 legalized sports betting jurisdictions active, the organic growth runway remains significant.
The Gambling negative attribution in the Inspire score reflects a fundamental values concern: sports betting and gambling create addiction risks that cause real harm to a significant percentage of users [2]. From a financial perspective, 2026 guidance of $6.5-6.9B revenue came below some analyst expectations due to Predictions investment drag, and the forward P/E of 45× reflects full optimistic pricing. Revenue growth is decelerating from 60%+ (2022-2023) to 27%, and each incremental state legalization adds less revenue than earlier states.
Key Catalysts
  1. DraftKings Predictions CFTC approval and launch — event contracts on elections, economic data, and sports outcomes could add hundreds of millions in revenue by 2027-2028
  2. New state legalizations: additional U.S. states (Texas, California, Florida) adopting online sports betting would materially expand the TAM
  3. Adjusted EBITDA reaching $800M-900M in 2026 upper guidance range — would validate the profitability trajectory and support multiple expansion
Key Risks
  1. Gambling harm and regulatory risk: states could impose additional restrictions on gambling advertising or product features, compressing marketing ROI
  2. Revenue growth deceleration: YoY growth trending from 63% (2023) to 27% (2025) to projected 8-14% (2026) — the law of large numbers creates multiple compression
  3. Gambling negative attribution: IOWN faith alignment concerns around addiction risk and social harm associated with digital gambling products
