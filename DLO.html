Company Profile
Technology ·Software - Infrastructure ·NASDAQ ·Uruguay
DLocal is an emerging markets payments technology platform that enables global enterprise merchants to accept and disburse payments in over 40 countries across Africa, Asia, and Latin America. The company processes local payment methods including cards, bank transfers, cash, and alternative payment methods, serving commerce, streaming, ride-hailing, financial services, and gaming industries.

Investment Thesis
DLocal is the dominant emerging markets payments platform with explosive growth metrics — 65.2% revenue growth, 87% net income growth, and TPV surging 70% to $13.1B in Q4 2025, crossing $1B annual revenue for the first time [1]. At 12.3x forward P/E, the stock is a fraction of fintech peers (Adyen 40x+, Stripe private at higher multiples) despite superior growth. The $843M cash balance (nearly debt-free), $332M annual FCF, and newly announced $300M buyback plus $57M dividend demonstrate financial strength and shareholder return commitment [1][4]. The 2026 guidance of 50-60% TPV growth (beating 41% consensus) signals continued acceleration across 40+ countries. The $3.95B market cap represents a compelling entry point for a high-growth fintech with proven profitability.
The bear case revolves around governance and fraud risk: Muddy Waters Research called DLocal 'likely a fraud' in November 2022, the Argentine government investigated a possible $400M fraud in May 2023, and Hollenden Square Research raised money laundering concerns in February 2025 [3]. While independent investigations found allegations 'not substantiated,' the recurring pattern creates a permanent credibility discount and legal liability risk. Emerging market concentration creates currency risk — Brazil, Mexico, and Egypt represent significant revenue exposure to volatile FX environments and potential capital controls. The 18% profit margin, while solid, is below mature payment processors (Visa 50%+, Adyen 25%+), and take rates face secular pressure as competition intensifies in higher-margin emerging markets. The company's Uruguay domicile creates jurisdictional complexity for shareholder protections, and the dual-class share structure limits minority shareholder governance rights [3][4].
Key Catalysts
  1. 2026 TPV growth of 50-60% (beating 41% consensus), driven by expansion in Africa and Asia and deepening penetration with existing enterprise merchants like Amazon, Microsoft, and Google [1]
  2. New $300M share buyback program and $57M annual dividend, initiating material capital returns that could attract income-oriented investors to the high-growth story [1]
  3. Stablecoin on-ramp and BNPL product launches expanding the addressable market beyond traditional card and bank transfer payments in emerging economies [2]
Key Risks
  1. Recurring fraud and governance allegations — three separate short-seller reports since 2022 create permanent credibility discount and class action lawsuit liability (ongoing Eastern District NY case) [3]
  2. Emerging market currency and regulatory risk — concentration in Brazil, Mexico, Egypt, and Argentina exposes revenue to FX volatility, capital controls, and sudden regulatory changes [1]
  3. Competitive pressure from Adyen, Stripe, and MercadoPago entering emerging markets with superior brand credibility and deeper pockets, potentially compressing DLocal's take rates [2]
