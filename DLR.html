Investment Thesis
Digital Realty is a premier AI-infrastructure REIT trading at the intersection of two megatrends: insatiable AI compute demand and constrained data center capacity. Full year 2025 revenue of $6.11B grew 10% with Q4 accelerating to 13.9% ($1.63B), driven by record bookings of $1.2B in new leases — 70% above the five-year average [1]. The $1.4B record backlog with $634M of leases commencing in 2026 provides exceptional revenue visibility. Core FFO of $7.39/share in 2025 (up 10%) with 2026 guidance of $7.90-8.00 implies 8% FFO growth. The NVIDIA partnership on AI and quantum facilities, 5GW+ development runway, and 66% renewable energy usage position DLR as the physical foundation layer for AI adoption [2]. The 2.4% dividend yield on 22 consecutive years of payments provides income while the company deploys $3.25-3.75B in growth CapEx [5].
The bear case focuses on valuation and capital intensity. At 62.9x forward P/E (though ~25.5x FFO), DLR is richly valued relative to historical REIT multiples, pricing in significant AI-driven growth that must materialize [5]. The dividend has been flat at $4.88 since 2022 with a 1.7% 5yr CAGR — income investors receive no growth while capital is consumed by $3.25-3.75B annual CapEx that may take years to generate returns [1]. The 136% GAAP payout ratio, while normal for REITs on an FFO basis, constrains financial flexibility. Competition from Equinix, CyrusOne, QTS (Blackstone), and hyperscaler-built data centers could compress margins as supply eventually catches demand. Power and water constraints in key markets could slow development timelines. Rising interest rates disproportionately pressure REITs, and DLR's 81.7% D/E could become more costly to service. AI workload durability beyond the current investment cycle is unproven — a pullback in AI CapEx by hyperscalers could leave excess capacity [1].
Key Catalysts
  1. Record $1.4B lease backlog converting throughout 2026 with $634M scheduled to commence ratably — providing visibility to 10%+ revenue growth and 8% FFO growth guidance [1]
  2. NVIDIA partnership on next-generation AI and quantum data center facilities positions DLR at the frontier of AI infrastructure demand as compute requirements scale exponentially [2]
  3. 5GW+ development runway in power-constrained markets creates structural supply advantage — power availability is the defining constraint for AI data center expansion, and DLR's secured capacity is a durable competitive moat [2]
Key Risks
  1. Valuation premium risk — at ~25.5x forward FFO and 62.9x GAAP P/E, DLR prices in substantial AI-driven growth that must materialize to sustain current multiple levels [5]
  2. Capital intensity and dividend stagnation — $3.25-3.75B annual CapEx absorbs cash flow while the dividend has been flat since 2022, creating tension between growth investment and income investor expectations [1]
  3. Hyperscaler self-build competition — Microsoft, Google, Amazon, and Meta are increasingly building their own data centers, which could reduce demand for third-party colocation and hyperscale leasing over time [1]
