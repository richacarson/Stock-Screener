Company Profile
Energy ·Oil & Gas E&P ·NASDAQ ·United States
Dorchester Minerals, L.P. acquires, owns, and administers royalty properties across the United States. The partnership holds producing and nonproducing mineral, royalty, overriding royalty, net profits, and leasehold interests in 594 counties and parishes in 28 states. Founded in 1982 and headquartered in Dallas, Texas, DMLP operates as a pure-play mineral royalty company with minimal operating costs and only 26 employees.

Investment Thesis
Dorchester Minerals is a conservatively managed mineral royalty partnership with zero debt, 40.9% profit margins, and a diversified portfolio spanning 594 counties in 28 states — a unique combination of financial safety and commodity exposure. Q1 2026 revenue surged 36% YoY to $58.9M with net income of $29.1M ($0.59/unit), demonstrating strong leverage to improving commodity prices [1]. The 11.1% distribution yield provides substantial income appeal, and the D/E of 0.24% is effectively zero leverage — rare in the energy MLP space [4]. The Inspire Impact Score of 47 (ALIGNED) with positive attributions for Business Resilience, Employee Wellbeing, GHG Emissions, and Product Access reflects sound governance. CEO Ehrman's 22-year tenure and the board's 11.9-year average tenure provide exceptional leadership continuity. The mineral royalty business model is maximally immune to AI disruption while benefiting from AI-driven natural gas demand for data center power generation.
The bear case centers on commodity price dependence, distribution volatility, and structural revenue decline. Full-year 2025 revenue declined 5.4% to $152.8M, and the 3-year revenue CAGR is approximately -3.7%, reflecting the fundamental challenge of a royalty business tied to volatile commodity prices [1]. Distributions have ranged from $2.78/unit (2025) to $3.50+/unit (2022), creating income unpredictability that may frustrate income-seeking investors despite the high current yield. The calculated payout ratio exceeds 200% of GAAP earnings, which is typical for MLPs but raises sustainability concerns if commodity prices decline further. With only 26 employees and no operational control over drilling activity, DMLP is entirely dependent on third-party operators' capital allocation decisions — a decline in Permian Basin drilling activity or shift to alternative energy could reduce royalty volumes regardless of mineral rights quality. The trailing P/E of 18.1x is elevated for a declining-revenue commodity business, suggesting the market may be overvaluing the income stream relative to its volatility.
