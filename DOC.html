Company Profile
Real Estate ·REIT - Healthcare Facilities ·NYSE ·United States
Healthpeak Properties is an S&P 500 real estate investment trust focused on healthcare discovery and delivery, owning and operating 703 properties across outpatient medical (530), lab (139), CCRC (15), and other (19) segments in the United States. The company merged with Physicians Realty Trust in March 2024 and completed the Janus Living senior housing REIT IPO in March 2026, generating approximately $880M in net proceeds while retaining 81.6% ownership. Founded in 1985, headquartered in Denver, Colorado.
411 Employees ·www.healthpeak.com

Investment Thesis
Healthpeak offers a 6.07% dividend yield supported by FFO adjusted guidance of $1.71-1.75/share (well above the ~$1.22 annual dividend), with the Janus Living IPO generating approximately $880M while retaining 81.6% ownership (~$5.7B market value of the subsidiary) [1]. Q1 2026 revenue of $738.8M beat estimates by 7.6%, EPS of $0.28 beat by 369%, and management raised full-year EPS guidance to $0.46-0.50 — a dramatic improvement from 2025's $0.10 [1]. The diversified 703-property healthcare portfolio provides recession-resistant rental income across outpatient medical, life science lab, and senior housing, with 39 consecutive years of dividend payments demonstrating income reliability. Aging U.S. demographics with 10,000 Baby Boomers turning 65 daily through 2030 create structural demand tailwinds for healthcare facilities [2].
The bear case centers on structurally weak financial metrics despite the attractive yield. ROE of 2.8% is very low even by REIT standards, the forward P/E of 130.4x and GAAP payout ratio of 381% — while distorted by REIT depreciation accounting — deter investors using traditional fundamental screens [1][4]. The dividend has shown zero growth over 5 years, offering yield without capital appreciation — a potential value trap. Life science lab vacancy rates have risen in key markets (San Francisco, Boston) as biotech funding has tightened and construction pipelines deliver new inventory. D/E of 118.2% creates leverage risk if interest rates remain elevated, and net debt leverage of approximately 5x adjusted EBITDA leaves limited buffer against credit deterioration. With only 411 employees managing 703 properties, operational depth is limited compared to vertically integrated healthcare companies [4].
Key Catalysts

Key Risks
  1. Life science lab vacancy rates rising in key innovation markets (San Francisco, Boston, San Diego) as biotech funding tightens, IPO markets remain subdued, and construction pipelines deliver new competing inventory [1]
  2. Zero dividend growth over 5 years and GAAP payout ratio of 381% create value trap dynamics — high current yield but no income growth or capital appreciation for investors requiring total return [4]
  3. D/E of 118.2% and net debt leverage of approximately 5x adjusted EBITDA create interest rate sensitivity — sustained higher rates increase borrowing costs and reduce the relative attractiveness of the 6.07% yield [1][4]
