Company Profile
Technology ·Software - Infrastructure ·NASDAQ ·United States
DocuSign, Inc. provides electronic signature and AI-powered intelligent agreement management (IAM) solutions worldwide. The company's platform enables organizations to prepare, sign, act on, and manage agreements through e-signature, contract lifecycle management, document generation, and AI contract agents. DocuSign serves over 1.5 million customers across industries. Founded in 2003, headquartered in San Francisco, California.

Investment Thesis
DocuSign is executing a critical strategic pivot from e-signature market leader to Intelligent Agreement Management platform company. FY2026 delivered $3.22B revenue (8% growth), net income of $309M, and free cash flow exceeding $1B, demonstrating strong profitability from the mature e-signature business [1]. Billings exceeded $1B for the first time, and the company was named to Fast Company's Most Innovative Companies 2026 list for the IAM platform [3]. At 12.84x forward earnings with a PEG ratio of 0.86, the stock is attractively valued relative to its growth trajectory and near-debt-free balance sheet (D/E of 10%) [5]. The IAM platform, powered by AI trained on DocuSign's massive agreement data corpus, has the potential to expand the company's total addressable market from $7B (e-signatures) to $50B+ (agreement management).
The bear case centers on cultural deterioration and competitive risk. Under CEO Thygesen, DocuSign laid off approximately 700 employees, removed health benefits, and eliminated holiday closure weeks, triggering Glassdoor reviews describing a 'broken' culture [4]. For a company whose strategic pivot to IAM requires innovation and employee engagement, cultural erosion is a material risk. Revenue growth has decelerated to 8% from the pandemic-era 40%+ growth rates, and the core e-signature business faces increasing commoditization pressure from Microsoft, Google, and Adobe embedding signing capabilities into their productivity suites [1][2]. The IAM platform is early-stage with limited proof points on customer adoption and willingness to pay for agreement intelligence beyond basic e-signatures. With an overall IOWN score of 54 driven by a weak Inspiration score (2/10) and Finite mindset classification, DocuSign earns a WATCH recommendation pending evidence that the IAM pivot is gaining traction and the cultural foundation is being rebuilt.
Key Catalysts
  1. IAM platform adoption accelerating beyond early adopters, with management disclosing specific IAM revenue contribution or customer count metrics that validate the strategic pivot [1][2]
  2. Q1 FY2027 earnings (quarter ended April 2026) demonstrating reacceleration of revenue growth above 10% and continued billings momentum above $1B, confirming the IAM-driven expansion narrative [2]

Key Risks
  1. Core e-signature commoditization as Microsoft 365, Google Workspace, and Adobe Acrobat embed native signing capabilities, potentially eroding DocuSign's 70% market share and pricing power over time
  2. Cultural deterioration under current leadership — layoffs, benefit cuts, and declining employee morale risk undermining the innovation capacity needed to execute the IAM platform transformation [4]
  3. IAM platform execution risk: the agreement management market is crowded (Icertis, Ironclad, Agiloft, Adobe) and DocuSign's expansion from e-signatures to full lifecycle management is unproven at scale
