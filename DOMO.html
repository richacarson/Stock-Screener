Investment Thesis
Domo is a niche cloud BI/analytics platform showing meaningful operational improvement: FY2026 non-GAAP operating margin reached +6% (from negative), operating cash flow turned positive at $7.9M, and billings grew 8.0% YoY to $111.2M in Q4, signaling improving forward demand [1]. Net retention improved for six consecutive quarters to over 96%, and subscription RPO rose 8.0% to $437.9M, providing revenue visibility [1]. At $175M market cap with $40.6M free cash flow, the stock trades at ~4.3x FCF — exceptionally cheap for a SaaS company with 75% gross margins if the profitability trajectory continues. The AI integration into the platform and Domo Labs innovation hub could differentiate the company in a market where AI-powered analytics is becoming table stakes [3].
The bear case is severe: Domo's revenue has been essentially flat at ~$319M for three consecutive fiscal years while competing against Microsoft, Salesforce, and Google — companies spending more on AI in a single quarter than Domo's entire market cap [1]. The company carries $143M in debt against $43M cash with negative stockholders' equity, and profit margin remains deeply negative at -18.6% GAAP [4]. AI disruption is an existential risk: as AI-native analytics capabilities are embedded into Power BI, Tableau, and Looker by hyperscaler competitors, Domo's standalone BI platform may become redundant for a growing share of the market. The company has reduced headcount from 1,500+ to 876, and the $175M market cap creates acquisition risk where Domo is more valuable as a technology acquisition target than an independent company. CEO Josh James' 16-year tenure without achieving sustained profitability raises execution questions [2].
