Company Profile
Technology ·Computer Hardware ·NASDAQ ·Canada
Draganfly Inc. develops, manufactures, and sells unmanned and remote data collection platforms including quad-copters, fixed-wing aircraft, and ground-based robots. The company serves defense, public safety, agriculture, telecom, and humanitarian sectors with drone hardware, software, and AI systems. Its flagship Outrider drone offers 7-hour flight duration with 100-lb payload capacity.

Investment Thesis
Draganfly is a 20+ year drone pioneer positioned at the intersection of defense, AI, and autonomous systems — sectors with strong secular tailwinds. Full-year 2025 revenue reached $7.7M (up 17.8% YoY), with Q4 revenue growing 18.5% to $1.91M [1]. The company's Outrider drone (7-hour endurance, 100-lb payload) has attracted military orders from U.S. and Canadian armed forces, a Fortune 50 telecom standardization, and border management contracts across multiple jurisdictions [2]. The balance sheet was dramatically strengthened with $90.1M CAD cash plus a $50M registered direct offering, providing ample runway to execute on a 'predetermined revenue ramp' through 2026 with Q1 2026 guidance of $2.42M and Q4 2026 projection of $5.7M [1].
The bear case is substantial: Draganfly has been operating for over two decades yet generates only $7.7M in annual revenue with a $22.9M net loss (-297% profit margin) [1]. Gross margins are thin — only 17.2% adjusted — for what is ostensibly a technology company, suggesting limited pricing power. The stock trades at a $217M market cap (28x trailing revenue) for a company with no path to near-term profitability and a history of dilutive capital raises. The drone industry is increasingly competitive with well-funded players like AeroVironment ($4B+ market cap), Joby Aviation, and defense primes entering the space. Revenue has been largely flat over 5 years ($7.6M in 2022 to $7.7M in 2025), and the projected 2026 ramp to $5.7M quarterly has no precedent in the company's history [1][3]. Execution risk is extremely high.
Key Catalysts
  1. U.S. and Canadian military drone contract expansions, with Outrider's 7-hour endurance and 100-lb payload addressing the DOD's shift toward expendable/reusable autonomous platforms [2]
  2. 2026 revenue ramp toward $5.7M quarterly (Q4 2026 target), driven by defense orders, Fortune 50 telecom standardization, and border management contracts [1]
  3. Palladyne AI swarming technology partnership enabling multi-drone autonomous operations for defense and commercial applications [2]
Key Risks
  1. Persistent unprofitability — $22.9M annual loss on $7.7M revenue with only 17.2% adjusted gross margins leaves no margin for error on the revenue ramp [1]
  2. Dilution risk — the company has funded operations through repeated capital raises ($50M registered direct offering) rather than operating cash flow, diluting shareholders [1]
  3. Competitive intensity — AeroVironment, Kratos, Shield AI, and defense primes are investing billions in autonomous drone platforms, dwarfing Draganfly's $7.7M revenue base [2]
