Company Profile
Healthcare ·Biotechnology ·NASDAQ ·United States
Dermata Therapeutics, Inc. is a late-stage medical dermatology company focused on identifying, developing, and commercializing pharmaceutical product candidates for the treatment of medical skin diseases and aesthetic applications. The company's lead candidate, XYNGARI, met all three co-primary endpoints in the Phase 3 STAR-1 trial for moderate-to-severe acne. In 2025, Dermata pivoted from the pharmaceutical pathway to a direct-to-consumer skincare strategy, withdrawing the XYNGARI IND and launching a new brand called Tome, with its first OTC Foundational Treatment product expected mid-2026.

Investment Thesis
Dermata Therapeutics possesses a validated clinical asset in XYNGARI, which met all three Phase 3 co-primary endpoints in the STAR-1 acne trial with early-onset efficacy at week four — a genuine scientific achievement for a 9-person team [1]. CEO Gerry Proehl brings a track record of building dermatology biotechs into multi-billion dollar exits (Santarus to Salix for $2.6B), and the 2025 pivot to DTC skincare under the Tome brand targets a mid-2026 product launch that could generate the company's first commercial revenues and reduce dependence on dilutive equity raises [1][3].
The risk profile, however, is extreme. Dermata has a $5.4 million market cap, zero revenue, -194.2% ROE, and $7.5 million cash runway projected to last only through Q1 2027 [1][4]. The company raised $15.4 million in equity in 2025–2026 — nearly three times its current market cap — representing severe shareholder dilution. Competing in DTC skincare against Galderma, J&J, and well-funded digital-native brands with a nine-person team and minimal marketing capital faces long odds, and the withdrawal of the XYNGARI IND forfeits the most defensible value driver. At current metrics, DRMA does not meet the IOWN framework's thresholds for portfolio inclusion.
