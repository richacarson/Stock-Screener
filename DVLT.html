Company Profile
Technology ·Software - Infrastructure ·NASDAQ ·United States
Datavault AI Inc. is a data sciences technology company that owns and operates data management platforms with high computing capabilities. The company focuses on delivery of cyber-secure, privacy-protected data management and monetization technologies, as well as AI-driven agents including Data Vault, DataValue, DataScore, and Data Vault Bank. It also offers wireless audio standards and data-over-sound technology across North America, Asia Pacific, Europe, and internationally.

Investment Thesis
Datavault AI is a high-risk, high-reward micro-cap AI company that achieved its first profitable quarter in Q4 2025 and is targeting explosive ~400% revenue growth to $200M in FY2026. The company's 70+ patent portfolio and focus on data sovereignty, edge computing, and RWA tokenization position it in differentiated niches within the AI infrastructure landscape.
The bull case rests on management's execution against its ambitious $200M target and the thesis that enterprises will demand decentralized, sovereignty-first AI data management [1][2]. However, the stock trades at $0.56 (down 86% from its 52-week high of $4.10), full-year profitability has not been demonstrated, and the company competes against vastly better-funded hyperscalers in the broader AI infrastructure market. The -202% profit margin, -48.8% ROE, and single profitable quarter make this a speculative position appropriate only for investors with high risk tolerance.
Key Catalysts
  1. Management reiterating $200M FY2026 revenue target representing ~400% YoY growth, driven by AI data monetization and RWA tokenization platform expansion [1]
  2. First profitable quarter in Q4 2025 with $33.8M revenue and $4.2M operating profit demonstrates the business model can generate positive economics at scale [1]
  3. CEO Bradley's 70+ patent portfolio and flagship keynotes at CONV3RGENCE London and AssetRush Zurich 2026 signal growing institutional recognition of the platform [3]
Key Risks
  1. Deep unprofitability on a full-year basis with -202% profit margin and -48.8% ROE — one profitable quarter does not establish sustainable economics [1]
  2. Stock has collapsed 86% from $4.10 52-week high to $0.56, reflecting severe market skepticism about the $200M revenue target and execution risk
  3. Micro-cap ($479M market cap) competing against hyperscaler giants (AWS, Azure, Google Cloud) in AI infrastructure — existential competitive risk if larger players enter data sovereignty/edge AI niches [2]
