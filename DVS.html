Company Profile

Dolly Varden Silver Corporation was a Canadian exploration-stage silver and gold mining company focused on the Kitsault Valley district in British Columbia. The company held 100% ownership of approximately 100,000 hectares of prospective land. DVS completed a merger with Contango ORE in December 2025, creating a combined mid-tier precious metals company now trading as CTGO. DVS was delisted in March 2026.

Investment Thesis
Dolly Varden Silver assembled a compelling district-scale silver exploration position in British Columbia's Kitsault Valley, growing from a $20M junior miner to a ~$600M company under CEO Shawn Khunkhun [1]. The merger with Contango ORE in December 2025 created a diversified precious metals platform combining Contango's 30% stake in the high-grade Manh Choh gold mine (expected 60,000 oz annual production) with DVS's 100,000-hectare silver exploration portfolio [5]. The combined entity holds over US$100M in cash and expects ~US$100M in annual free cash flow, providing non-dilutive funding for exploration and development.
DVS was delisted in March 2026 following the completed Contango merger, making this a legacy screen only [4]. The bear case centers on the fundamental limitations of a pre-revenue exploration company: zero revenue, -$23.15M net loss, no earnings visibility, and complete dependence on commodity prices and drilling success. Silver exploration carries binary risk — resource estimates may disappoint, permitting timelines can extend indefinitely, and the capital intensity of moving from discovery to production typically requires further dilution or sale. Investors seeking exposure to this portfolio should evaluate CTGO (Contango Silver & Gold) as the successor entity. DVS as a standalone entity no longer exists.
