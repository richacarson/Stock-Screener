Company Profile
Real Estate ·REIT - Mortgage ·NYSE ·United States
Dynex Capital is a mortgage real estate investment trust (mREIT) that invests in agency and non-agency mortgage-backed securities (MBS) on a leveraged basis. The company generates income primarily from the interest rate spread between its MBS portfolio and its borrowing costs, managed through interest rate hedging. Founded in 1987, headquartered in Glen Allen, Virginia, with approximately $9B in investment portfolio.
28 Employees ·www.dynexcapital.com

Investment Thesis
Dynex Capital offers a 15.79% dividend yield at 8.85x forward earnings, making it an attractive income vehicle for investors willing to accept mREIT volatility [1]. FY2025 delivered exceptional results with revenue up 147% and net income up 191%, driven by favorable MBS spread compression during the Fed easing cycle. Book value per share of $13.31 provides 3% upside from current price of $12.92. Co-CEOs Byron Boston and Smriti Popenoe have deep fixed-income expertise, and the company's $9B agency MBS portfolio is positioned to benefit from continued spread normalization. With 18 consecutive years of dividend payments and analyst consensus at $14.80 (15% upside), Dynex offers yield-focused total return potential.
The bear case is structural. Dynex's 134% payout ratio means the company pays more in dividends than it earns in GAAP income, funding the gap through continuous share issuance that dilutes existing shareholders [2]. The 15.79% yield itself signals market skepticism about sustainability — if rates reverse or MBS spreads widen, book value erosion could force another dividend cut like the 76% reduction in 2022. D/E of 565% means small movements in asset values create outsized equity swings — a 5% decline in portfolio value would wipe out nearly 30% of equity. The company has no competitive moat, no pricing power, and no control over the interest rate environment that determines its profitability. Only 28 employees and no technology differentiation make Dynex a pure interest rate bet rather than a business with sustainable competitive advantages.
Key Catalysts
  1. Continued Fed rate normalization creating favorable MBS spread environment and potential book value accretion from current $13.31 per share
  2. Potential dividend increase from current $2.04/year as portfolio income recovers and payout ratio normalizes below 100%
  3. Housing market stability supporting agency MBS valuations and reducing prepayment risk in the portfolio
Key Risks
  1. Interest rate reversal or unexpected Fed tightening could rapidly erode book value and force another dividend cut — the 76% cut in 2022 from $0.56 to $0.13/quarter demonstrates this fragility
  2. Payout ratio of 134% is unsustainable — the company must issue shares to fund dividends, creating a dilution cycle that destroys long-term per-share value for existing shareholders
  3. D/E of 565% creates existential leverage risk — a 5% decline in MBS portfolio values would eliminate approximately 30% of shareholders' equity, potentially triggering margin calls on repo financing
