Company Profile
Healthcare ·Medical Devices ·NASDAQ ·United States
DexCom, Inc. designs, develops, and commercializes continuous glucose monitoring (CGM) systems for people with diabetes and those seeking metabolic health insights. Products include the Dexcom G7 (prescription CGM for diabetes management) and Stelo (over-the-counter biosensor for general wellness). Founded in 1999 and headquartered in San Diego, California, DexCom has pioneered CGM technology for over 25 years.

Investment Thesis
DexCom delivered Q1 2026 revenue of $1.192B (up 15% YoY) with GAAP net income of $199.5M and EPS of $0.51, beating consensus estimates by 17% [1]. Full-year 2026 guidance of $5.16-5.25B implies 11-13% growth with continued margin expansion — profit margin has improved from 10% to 17% YoY in Q1. The balance sheet is pristine with net cash of $1.03B and D/E of just 47%. At a forward PEG of 1.19 and forward P/E of 26x, the stock is attractively valued for a medtech leader with 19%+ 5-year revenue CAGR, 35.6% ROE, and a $100B+ TAM expansion opportunity via Stelo targeting 100M+ Americans with prediabetic conditions [4]. The AI-powered Smart Food Logging and Smart Basal integration create platform stickiness that differentiates DexCom from commodity sensor competitors.
The primary risk is the stock's steep decline from its 2024 highs above $130 to approximately $70 today, reflecting market skepticism about DexCom's ability to execute the Stelo-driven TAM expansion while maintaining margins. The Q2 2024 guidance cut damaged management credibility, and the recent CEO transition from Kevin Sayer to Jacob Leach introduces execution risk during a critical growth phase [3]. Competitive pressure from Abbott's FreeStyle Libre (which holds majority global CGM share at lower price points) and potential entry from big tech companies into biometric sensing could compress margins. The class action lawsuit related to G7 CGM connectivity issues and two FDA recalls of Dexcom mobile apps in 2025-2026 highlight product quality risks that could erode the brand trust that underpins DexCom's premium pricing [5]. Revenue growth deceleration from 24% in 2023 to 15% in Q1 2026 may continue if Stelo adoption falls short of expectations.

AI Resilience (25%)
AI Resilience LOW RISK
8
AI Risk Protection (7/9): DexCom's core business is manufacturing and selling physical biosensors that are FDA-regulated Class II medical devices — AI structurally cannot replace the need for a physical sensor adhered to the body that measures interstitial glucose via electrochemistry [5]. The CGM market is protected by deep regulatory moats (FDA 510(k) clearance, CE marking), switching costs (insulin pump integrations, physician prescribing habits), and proprietary sensor chemistry developed over 25+ years. Analysts specifically note that DexCom is "gaining from AI, not disrupted by it" with AI integration into CGM platforms actually increasing the value proposition and expanding the addressable market [5]. The physical-world nature of glucose monitoring — requiring hardware on the body — makes this business structurally immune to AI substitution. The one point deducted reflects theoretical risk from AI-enabled competitors (Apple, Samsung) potentially entering biometric sensing, though none have achieved FDA clearance for glucose monitoring. AI Opportunity (1/1): DexCom generates meaningful AI-related revenue through its Stelo biosensor built with Google AI, AI-powered Smart Food Logging, and Smart Basal FDA-cleared insulin therapy integration. These AI features are shipping to customers and generating revenue, differentiating DexCom's CGM platform from competitors [2][3].
