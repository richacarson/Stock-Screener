Company Profile
Financial Services ·Asset Management ·NYSE ·United States
Destiny Tech100 is a non-diversified closed-end management investment company that provides public market investors access to private venture-backed technology companies. The fund targets a portfolio of up to 100 companies, currently holding 32 positions with significant exposure to SpaceX (16.2%), Shield AI (4.1%), Databricks (4.0%), xAI (3.5%), and Anthropic. Founded in November 2020, the fund began trading on the NYSE on March 26, 2024.

Investment Thesis
Destiny Tech100 provides unique public market exposure to pre-IPO tech unicorns including SpaceX (16.2%), Shield AI (4.1%), Databricks (4.0%), xAI (3.5%), and Anthropic [1]. The fund's NAV per share surged 210% from FY2024 to FY2025 driven by AI and space sector valuations, and recent $127M deployment into Anthropic and other holdings expands the portfolio toward AI leadership [1]. Potential Databricks and SpaceX IPOs in 2026 could unlock significant value as private holdings convert to liquid public equities. For investors seeking pre-IPO tech exposure without accredited investor requirements, DXYZ offers a rare publicly traded vehicle [1].
The bear case is compelling. DXYZ trades at a 73%+ premium to its December 2025 NAV of $19.97 per share, meaning investors pay $1.74 for every $1.00 of underlying assets — a structural wealth transfer from shareholders to the fund [1][3]. The fund returned -7.3% in 2023 versus 43.4% for the Nasdaq Composite, demonstrating poor relative performance during a tech boom [2]. The closed-end structure means there is no NAV convergence mechanism (unlike ETFs), so the premium can persist indefinitely or collapse unpredictably. SpaceX concentration at 16.2% (previously 25.9% before remediation) creates single-name risk that exceeds typical diversification standards [3]. The fund has no employees, no operating revenue, and generates earnings only from mark-to-market changes in illiquid private company valuations that are inherently difficult to verify. Competing vehicles like VCX have emerged, potentially compressing DXYZ's premium as investor alternatives increase [1]. Morningstar and Seeking Alpha analysts have both rated the fund as a sell despite its AI/SpaceX holdings [2].
Key Catalysts
  1. Databricks IPO expected in 2026 could unlock liquidity and establish public market valuations for DXYZ's fourth-largest holding (4.0% of portfolio) [1]

Key Risks
  1. 73%+ premium to NAV — investors pay $34.71 for $19.97 of underlying assets; any premium compression would destroy shareholder value regardless of portfolio performance [1][3]
  2. Illiquid private holdings with opaque valuations — the fund's NAV depends on subjective markups of private company stakes that may not reflect realizable values [2][3]
  3. Concentration risk — SpaceX at 16.2% and previous diversification test non-compliance (25.9%) create regulatory and single-name exposure risks for a fund with only 32 holdings [3]
