Company Profile

Electronic Arts is a global interactive entertainment company developing and publishing games, content, and services across consoles, PC, mobile, and cloud. Core franchises include EA SPORTS FC, Madden NFL, College Football, The Sims, Apex Legends, and Battlefield. EA has agreed to be acquired by a consortium led by Saudi Arabia's PIF, Silver Lake, and Affinity Partners in a $55B take-private deal at $210/share, expected to close by June 30, 2026 [1].

Investment Thesis
EA is a deal-arbitrage situation: shareholders approved the $55B take-private to PIF/Silver Lake/Affinity at $210/share in December 2025, with the final regulatory clearance achieved and closing expected by June 30, 2026 [1]. With the stock near deal value, the upside is capped at the merger premium (~$1-2/share spread depending on close timing) and the cash flows are essentially a short-duration arbitrage trade. Operationally, EA SPORTS FC, Madden, College Football, and The Sims continue to generate strong recurring revenue, with FY26 guidance of $7.6-8.0B in net bookings and operating cash flow of $2.2-2.4B [2]. AI integration is genuinely advanced (100+ projects, 60% of dev impacted) and gross margin of 83.3% is industry-leading [3]. For new money, EA is a HOLD until close — the deal will deliver $210/share in cash by mid-2026.
The bear case centers on deal break risk and post-close opacity. While regulatory clearance has occurred, geopolitical tensions or US national-security review could still delay or scuttle the transaction. If the deal breaks, EA likely retraces to $130-150 (pre-announcement levels), creating ~25-30% downside [1]. Long-term concerns include heavy reliance on Ultimate Team monetization (gambling-adjacent mechanics under EU/UK regulatory scrutiny), Apex Legends decline, and the cultural risk of Saudi PIF stewardship over Western IP. Inspire score of -69 reflects negative attributions for gambling, abortion activism, and LGBT activism, plus PIF ownership raising additional faith-based and human-rights concerns. The take-private also removes EA from public markets entirely, ending 37 years of shareholder transparency.
