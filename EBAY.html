Iannone's emphasis on serving passionate communities (collectors, enthusiasts, fashion resellers) gives the platform cultural meaning beyond transactions. However, eBay's purpose has evolved from its original democratic marketplace vision (connecting anyone to anything) to a more narrowly defined recommerce positioning, and the cause hasn't been tested through crisis. Investment Thesis
eBay delivered a strong Q4 2025 with revenue of $3.0B (up 15%), non-GAAP EPS of $1.41 (beating consensus by 14%), and GMV of $21.2B (up 10%) [1]. At 13.6x forward P/E with 13-15% guided revenue growth, 71.4% gross margins, 40.8% ROE, and a 90-100% FCF return policy ($2B buybacks + dividends annually), eBay offers an attractive combination of growth re-acceleration and capital return [1][5]. Iannone's strategic refocus on recommerce (40%+ of inventory) and vertical expertise has driven stock outperformance of 50% in one year. Fashion grew 10% in the US ($500M incremental GMV), and the $1.2B Depop acquisition positions eBay in the high-growth Gen Z C2C fashion market [1]. AI-powered listing tools are directly reducing seller friction across 2.4B listings, leveraging 30 years of proprietary pricing data that competitors cannot replicate [2].
The bear case centers on eBay's persistent buyer attrition — 13 consecutive quarters of declining active buyers since Q1 2018, suggesting the platform is monetizing a shrinking user base more efficiently rather than growing the marketplace [2]. GAAP EPS declined 18.4% YoY despite revenue growth, and the Q4 2025 earnings decline of 22.2% reflects Depop-related costs and margin pressure [5]. D/E of 155.6% and $7.2B in debt (versus $2.9B cash) create balance sheet risk if the growth re-acceleration stalls. The Depop acquisition ($1.2B) will be dilutive to EPS until 2028 and carries integration risk in the notoriously competitive Gen Z fashion market (Depop vs Poshmark, Vinted, ThredUp) [1]. Revenue 5yr CAGR of ~4% reflects structural stagnation that the recent 15% acceleration may not sustain. Competition from AI-powered shopping experiences on Amazon, Google, and TikTok Shop could further erode buyer traffic. The Inspire Impact Score of -100 (maximum misalignment) limits the faith-aligned investor base [3]. Heavy insider selling in March 2026 (CEO and multiple officers) signals near-term caution despite strong operating results [4].
Key Catalysts
  1. Depop acquisition closing Q2 2026 — adds 1-2 percentage points to GMV growth and positions eBay in the $100B+ Gen Z resale market, with accretion targeted by 2028 [1]
  2. AI-powered listing and pricing tools scaling across 2.4B listings, leveraging 30 years of proprietary data to reduce seller friction and increase conversion rates [2]
  3. Q1 2026 guidance of 10-12% GMV growth and 13-15% revenue growth with $2B in buybacks, demonstrating sustained acceleration from the recommerce/vertical strategy [1]
Key Risks
  1. Persistent buyer attrition — 13 consecutive quarters of declining active buyers creates existential risk if eBay cannot reverse the trend through Depop, AI tools, and vertical expansion [2]
  2. Depop integration risk — $1.2B acquisition in the crowded Gen Z fashion resale market (Poshmark, Vinted, ThredUp) will be dilutive until 2028 and carries execution risk in a demographic eBay has struggled to attract [1]
  3. AI-powered competition — Amazon, Google Shopping, and TikTok Shop are deploying AI-driven product discovery and recommendation engines that could further erode eBay's buyer traffic and marketplace relevance [2]

AI Resilience (25%)
AI Resilience MODERATE
6
AI Risk Protection (5/9): eBay's marketplace business faces moderate AI disruption risk on two fronts. First, AI-powered search and recommendation engines from Amazon, Google Shopping, and TikTok Shop could reduce buyer traffic to eBay by offering superior product discovery. Second, AI tools that help anyone create and manage online storefronts (Shopify with AI, social commerce) could disintermediate eBay's seller base [2]. However, eBay's 30 years of proprietary pricing data on 2.4B listings, its dominant position in used/collectible goods where authenticity verification matters, and the network effects of 134M active buyers create meaningful defensive moats. The recommerce focus is harder to replicate than general e-commerce. AI Opportunity (1/1): eBay generates meaningful value from AI — AI-powered listing tools, pricing recommendations, image recognition for collectibles, and AI-enhanced search directly improve platform engagement and conversion. Iannone described AI as 'a real game-changer' for reducing listing friction [2].
