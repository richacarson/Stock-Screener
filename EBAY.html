Company Profile
Consumer Discretionary ·Internet Retail ·NASDAQ ·United States
eBay Inc. operates a global e-commerce marketplace connecting buyers and sellers across 190 markets worldwide. The platform enables consumer-to-consumer and business-to-consumer sales through its Marketplace segment, specializing in non-new, collectible, and enthusiast inventory categories. Key verticals include motors, sneakers, luxury goods, trading cards, and refurbished electronics. The company also operates Depop (acquired 2021), a fashion resale platform. Founded in 1995, headquartered in San Jose, California, with a market cap of approximately $47.9 billion.

Investment Thesis
eBay delivered strong Q1 2026 results with revenue of $3.09B (up 19% as-reported, 17% FX-neutral) and GMV of $22.2B (up 18%), demonstrating that the focused category strategy under CEO Jamie Iannone is generating real momentum [1]. Non-GAAP EPS of $1.66 was up 21% YoY, with operating margins expanding to 29.4% non-GAAP. The marketplace model generates exceptional economics — 71.8% gross margins, 42.9% ROE, and sufficient free cash flow to fund a 5.4% buyback yield plus a growing 1.15% dividend. At 17.5x forward earnings, eBay trades at a meaningful discount to e-commerce peers despite accelerating growth. The AI Activate programme with OpenAI, eBay Live streaming commerce, and authenticated luxury verticals provide multiple vectors for continued GMV expansion. The Depop acquisition strengthens eBay's position in the high-growth Gen Z resale market.
The primary risk is that eBay's growth acceleration proves temporary rather than structural. Q2 2026 guidance of 8-10% FX-neutral revenue growth represents a significant deceleration from Q1's 17%, and the full-year GMV guidance of only 7-7.5% FX-neutral growth suggests management itself expects moderation [1]. The 5-year revenue CAGR of approximately 1.6% reveals that eBay has essentially been flat over a multi-year period, with the current upturn following a post-pandemic contraction. More fundamentally, agentic AI commerce poses a structural threat to marketplace intermediation — if AI shopping agents can search, compare, and transact across platforms autonomously, eBay's value as a discovery and transaction layer could erode [7]. The debt-to-equity ratio of 163% reflects aggressive financial engineering through buybacks, and the Inspire Impact Score of -100 (MISALIGNED) may concern faith-based investors. CEO insider selling of $8.4M in recent months, while conducted under a pre-arranged trading plan, does not inspire confidence in near-term upside [6].
Key Catalysts
  1. Continued GMV acceleration driven by eBay Live streaming commerce expansion, AI-powered listing tools increasing seller productivity, and authenticated luxury/sneaker vertical growth
  2. AI Activate programme with OpenAI could meaningfully improve small seller conversion rates and listing quality, driving platform-wide GMV growth beyond the current 7-7.5% FX-neutral guidance
  3. Depop integration and Gen Z resale market capture, combined with circular economy positioning, provides secular growth tailwind as younger consumers shift toward secondhand commerce
Key Risks
  1. Agentic AI commerce from Perplexity, OpenAI, and Google threatens marketplace intermediation by enabling autonomous cross-platform shopping that could bypass eBay's discovery and transaction layer entirely
  2. Q1 2026 growth of 17-19% may prove unsustainable as Q2 guidance moderates to 8-10%, and the 5-year revenue CAGR of 1.6% suggests structural low-single-digit organic growth is the base case
  3. Debt-to-equity of 163% driven by aggressive buybacks creates balance sheet risk if marketplace economics deteriorate, while $3.35B net debt limits financial flexibility for acquisitions or defensive investment

AI Resilience (25%)
AI Resilience MODERATE
5
AI Risk Protection (4/9): eBay faces meaningful AI disruption risk. Agentic commerce — where AI shopping agents autonomously search, compare, and purchase across platforms — threatens eBay's core marketplace intermediation model. Retail Dive and Payments Dive have reported that large language models from Perplexity AI, OpenAI, and Google moving into agentic commerce are "potentially disruptive" to eBay's model [7]. Consumer shopping agents are expected to move from product research to fully autonomous transactions, which could bypass eBay's platform entirely. The BCG Consumer AI Disruption Index identifies e-commerce marketplaces as a moderately exposed category [8]. However, eBay's focus on non-new, unique, and collectible inventory creates some defensibility — AI agents need structured catalogs to function, and eBay's one-of-a-kind inventory is harder to intermediate than commodity goods. The physical nature of the goods themselves provides a floor of protection. AI Opportunity (1/1): eBay is actively deploying AI across its platform — AI-powered listing tools, personalized search, the AI Activate programme with OpenAI, and AI-driven authentication and fraud detection [3]. While specific AI revenue figures are not disclosed, AI is integrated into the core product experience and measurably improving seller productivity.
