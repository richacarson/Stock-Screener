Company Profile
Financial Services ·Asset Management ·NYSE ·United States
Eagle Point Credit Company is an externally managed, non-diversified closed-end investment company focused on equity and junior debt tranches of collateralized loan obligations (CLOs) collateralized by below-investment-grade US senior secured loans. The fund is managed by Eagle Point Credit Management ($14B AUM, founded 2012 by Thomas Majewski and Stone Point Capital) and is one of the largest CLO equity holders in the world.

Investment Thesis
ECC is a high-yield income vehicle for investors specifically seeking CLO equity exposure. Eagle Point Credit Management ($14B AUM, founded 2012) is one of the most experienced CLO equity managers in the world, led by 31-year credit veteran Tom Majewski [2]. The portfolio is defensively positioned: triple-C exposure at 4.6% vs market 4.8%, loans below 80 at 2.7% vs market 3.4%, weighted average junior OC cushion at 4.6% vs market 3.7%, and look-through default exposure of just 34bps vs market 1.5% TTM [1]. Q3 2025 generated $77M ($0.59/share) of recurring cash flows with NAV at $6.69-$6.79. The monthly $0.14 distribution annualizes to $1.68 per share, producing a high stated yield. CEO insider buying [2] supports alignment.
Risks dominate the case. ECC has cut dividends multiple times since 2024 [3] and recurring cash flows fell from $0.69/share in Q2 to $0.59/share in Q3 2025 [1]. Net investment income of $0.24/share quarterly does not cover the $0.42/share distribution — implying distributions are partially funded from capital, eroding NAV over time. The 26%+ stated yield largely reflects dividend cuts and a falling share price rather than sustainable income. CLO equity is structurally late-cycle leveraged credit — a corporate default cycle (recession-driven, AI-disruption-driven, or rates-driven) would compound losses through CLO subordination. Closed-end fund mechanics add layered leverage and trading-discount risk. Eagle Point's defensive portfolio positioning helps but does not eliminate downside in a credit downturn. Income-only investors with full understanding of CLO equity mechanics and capital risk may consider; broad-market investors should stay watchful.
Key Catalysts
  1. Stable senior loan market with low default activity — Q3 portfolio look-through default of just 34bps vs market 1.5% TTM supports continued recurring cash flow [1]
  2. Defensive portfolio positioning (4.6% triple-C, 2.7% loans below 80, 4.6% OC cushion) outperforms market averages, providing relative downside protection [1]
  3. Falling Treasury rates would compress CLO debt costs and expand CLO equity arbitrage spreads, supporting NAV recovery
Key Risks
  1. Distribution sustainability — multiple dividend cuts since 2024 [3] and Q3 2025 NII of $0.24/share well below the $0.42/share distribution implies return-of-capital risk and continued NAV erosion [1]
  2. Credit cycle risk — a recession or AI-driven corporate default wave would cascade through CLO equity subordination, compounding losses through structural leverage
  3. Treasury volatility / spread widening — rising rates or widening loan spreads pressure CLO equity NAV and increase financing costs for the fund's own preferred capital structure [3]
