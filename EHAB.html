Company Profile
Healthcare ·Medical Care Facilities ·NYSE ·United States
Enhabit, Inc. provides home health and hospice services across the United States, operating 255 home health and 110 hospice locations in 34 states. The Home Health segment offers nursing, therapy, and chronic disease management. The Hospice segment provides palliative care, symptom management, and family counseling. Spun off from Encompass Health in 2022, the company is the fourth-largest U.S. home health provider. A definitive merger agreement with Kinderhook Industries at $13.80/share was announced February 2026.
10,800 Employees ·www.ehab.com

Investment Thesis
Enhabit is effectively a merger arbitrage situation with a pending Kinderhook Industries acquisition at $13.80/share announced February 2026 [1]. The stock trades at $13.75, essentially at the deal price, offering minimal upside but high certainty of completion. Pre-deal, the company showed improving operational trends: home health admissions grew 7.3% YoY with non-Medicare admissions surging 16.0%, hospice ADC grew 9.9%, cost per patient day declined 3.5%, and Adjusted EBITDA grew 11.6% to $28M in Q4 2025 [1]. The Kinderhook acquisition at ~10.7x EBITDA and 1.05x revenue represents reasonable but not generous takeout pricing for a turnaround-stage home health provider.
The bear case is largely moot given the pending acquisition, but risks remain. The Kinderhook deal could fail to close due to regulatory or financing issues, which would leave shareholders with a $13.75 stock on a company generating negligible earnings (TTM EPS of -$0.09) and flat revenue [5]. Pre-deal, Enhabit was structurally challenged: spun off with 80% Medicare fee-for-service revenue into an industry rapidly shifting to Medicare Advantage, operating at near-breakeven margins with $500M debt [4]. The home health industry faces continued CMS reimbursement pressure, labor shortages for nurses and aides, and competitive intensity from larger players. Had the deal not materialized, Enhabit would likely have continued to struggle as an under-scaled, under-diversified operator in a consolidating industry.

AI Resilience (25%)
AI Resilience MODERATE
7
AI Risk Protection (6/9): Home health and hospice care is inherently physical, hands-on work — nurses, therapists, and aides providing bedside care in patients' homes cannot be replaced by AI [3]. Healthcare is the most labor-intensive sector in the U.S. economy, and in-home care is among the most AI-resistant segments within it. However, AI could compress administrative margins through automated scheduling, documentation, and billing, and AI-powered remote patient monitoring could reduce the frequency of in-home visits for some conditions, creating modest volume risk for home health specifically. Regulatory barriers (Medicare/Medicaid certification, state licensing) provide additional protection against AI-driven new entrants. AI Opportunity (1/1): AI documentation tools, predictive analytics for patient deterioration, and automated care planning could meaningfully reduce Enhabit's cost per patient day (already down 3.5% YoY) and improve clinical outcomes, generating operational savings even if not direct AI revenue [1].
