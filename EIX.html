Company Profile
Utilities ·Utilities - Regulated Electric ·NYSE ·United States
Edison International, through its subsidiary Southern California Edison (SCE), generates and distributes electric power across approximately 50,000 square miles of southern, central, and coastal California. The regulated utility serves approximately 5 million customer accounts and is investing $40 billion over five years in grid modernization and clean energy infrastructure.

Investment Thesis
Edison International is a deeply discounted regulated utility trading at 11.3x forward P/E (vs historical median ~27x) with 24.1% ROE and 23.1% profit margins — strong fundamentals obscured by wildfire liability overhang [1]. FY2025 revenue grew 9.8% to $19.3B, and management guides 5-7% EPS growth supported by a $40B five-year capital plan targeting grid modernization, clean energy, and data center electrification [1]. The AI data center buildout creates structural demand growth for California electricity — SCE's service territory is home to major hyperscalers. The $0.8775 quarterly dividend (4.8% yield, 29% payout ratio) provides income while the valuation normalizes. At 6.4x trailing P/E, the stock prices in significant wildfire losses that may not fully materialize given California's $21B Wildfire Fund backstop [3].
The bear case is dominated by wildfire liability. The January 2025 Eaton fire killed 17 people and destroyed 9,414 structures, with SCE facing $77M+ in DOJ lawsuits and $10B+ in private claims [3]. LA County alleges Edison 'deliberately prioritized profits over safety.' If found negligent, Edison could owe $3.9B+ to California's Wildfire Fund, and the $1B self-insurance fund is projected depleted by 2026 [3]. D/E of 215.7% limits financial flexibility for absorbing large settlements. The Altman Z-Score of 0.81 indicates financial distress risk. Revenue growth of 3.9% CAGR (3-year) is modest for a Growth sleeve stock. Regulatory uncertainty — wildfire liability rules, rate case outcomes, and clean energy mandates — creates multi-year earnings visibility challenges. The stock is down ~33% from pre-fire levels, suggesting the market still prices meaningful probability of adverse outcomes [3].
Key Catalysts
  1. AI data center electricity demand creating structural load growth in SCE's California service territory, with hyperscaler capacity additions driving incremental rate base growth [1]

  3. $40B five-year capital investment plan driving 5-7% EPS growth through grid modernization, renewable interconnection, and EV charging infrastructure [1]
Key Risks
  1. Eaton fire liability — $10B+ in private claims and DOJ lawsuits could result in material losses exceeding Wildfire Fund coverage, with negligence finding triggering $3.9B+ fund reimbursement [3]
  2. Financial distress indicators — Altman Z-Score of 0.81, D/E of 215.7%, and $1B self-insurance fund projected depleted by 2026 create balance sheet vulnerability [1]
  3. Regulatory and climate risk — increasing California wildfire frequency from climate change raises recurring liability exposure, while rate case outcomes may not fully recover wildfire mitigation costs [3]
