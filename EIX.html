Company Profile
Utilities ·Utilities - Regulated Electric ·NYSE ·United States
Edison International is one of the largest electric utility holding companies in the United States, operating primarily through subsidiary Southern California Edison (SCE). SCE serves approximately 15 million people across a 50,000-square-mile service area in Southern California, with a focus on grid modernization, clean energy transition, and wildfire mitigation.

Investment Thesis
Edison International trades at a deeply discounted 10.7x forward earnings despite guiding to 5-7% compound annual core EPS growth through 2028-2030, with a $38-41B capital plan requiring no equity issuance [1]. The stock offers a compelling risk/reward with a 5.0% dividend yield (22 consecutive years of growth), 29% payout ratio providing significant safety margin, and California's $21B wildfire fund providing balance sheet backstop [1][4][5]. Full-year 2025 core EPS of $6.55 (up 33% YoY) demonstrated strong earnings power, and the return to 5-7% growth in 2027 after a muted 2026 creates a visible re-rating catalyst [1]. AI data center electricity demand provides a structural tailwind for rate base growth in SCE's 50,000-square-mile service territory [3].
The bear case centers on the existential wildfire liability risk — the Eaton Fire has already generated $1B+ in recorded liabilities, 2,405 claims, and potential exposure exceeding the $21B wildfire fund under California's inverse condemnation doctrine (strict liability regardless of negligence) [5]. S&P revised the credit outlook to negative, and the stock has fallen 35% since the wildfires [5]. 2026 core EPS guidance of $5.90-6.20 represents a 7.6% decline from 2025, with $0.25 in regulatory headwinds and wildfire cost absorption creating near-term earnings pressure [1]. D/E of 215.7% is elevated even for a utility, and the ongoing fire investigations (12-18 months to determine cause) create prolonged uncertainty that could suppress the stock for an extended period [5]. If wildfire liability exceeds fund capacity, Edison could face PG&E-style balance sheet stress.
