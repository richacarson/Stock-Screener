Investment Thesis
Edison International offers a 5% dividend yield at a deeply discounted forward P/E of 10.7x, with management guiding for 5-7% core EPS CAGR through 2028-2030 and 7% rate base growth via the $38-41B capital plan. The 90% grid hardening completion and SB 254 wildfire fund framework provide financial backstops, while AI data center electricity demand creates a secular growth tailwind in Southern California. No equity issuance is expected through 2030, protecting per-share economics.
The bear case is dominated by Eaton Fire liability — $1.1B+ recorded losses with additional 'probable but not estimable' damages that could exceed California's $21B wildfire fund. Shares have fallen 35% since the fires, and S&P's negative outlook reflects real credit deterioration risk. California's inverse condemnation doctrine imposes strict liability regardless of negligence, creating an existential tail risk. Debt-to-equity of 216% leaves limited balance sheet flexibility, 2026 core EPS guidance of $5.90-$6.20 represents a 7.6% decline from 2025, and negative free cash flow signals heavy capital spending requirements.
