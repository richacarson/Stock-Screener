Company Profile
Utilities ·Utilities - Regulated Electric ·NYSE ·United States
Edison International is the parent company of Southern California Edison (SCE), one of the largest electric utilities in the United States, serving approximately 5 million customer accounts across 50,000 square miles of central, coastal, and southern California. The company focuses on regulated electric utility operations, clean energy transition, and grid modernization, with significant investments in wildfire mitigation technology and infrastructure hardening.

Investment Thesis
Edison International offers a compelling value proposition at $74.72 with a trailing P/E of just 6.04 and forward P/E of 11.42, deeply discounted relative to utility sector averages and the broader market [6]. The core investment case rests on three pillars: first, the resolution of wildfire overhang as shareholder lawsuits were dismissed in 2026 and SCE's $5B+ wildfire mitigation investment has fundamentally improved the risk profile [3][4]; second, AI-driven data center electricity demand creating a secular growth tailwind in SCE's Southern California service territory, with CEO Pizarro actively engaging technology leaders on grid-AI integration [5]; and third, strong financial fundamentals including ROE of 24.10%, profit margin of 23.08%, and 24 consecutive years of dividend growth at 5.75% CAGR, with FY2026 core EPS guided at $5.90-$6.20 [1][6]. The 5.03% dividend yield provides meaningful current income while investors wait for multiple expansion as wildfire risk perception normalizes.
The bear case centers on Edison's elevated leverage and lingering tail risks. Debt-to-equity of 216% is the highest among large-cap utilities, amplifying risk if future wildfire events create new liabilities despite mitigation investments [6]. California's regulatory environment remains politically complex, with rate case outcomes uncertain and potential for unfavorable cost recovery decisions that could compress earned returns [2]. The trailing P/E of 6.04 appears artificially depressed by one-time wildfire-related charges and insurance recoveries that inflate TTM earnings, making the forward P/E of 11.42 a more representative valuation — still cheap, but less dramatically so [6]. Revenue growth of 7.7% (Q1 2026 YoY) and 5-year CAGR of 5.1% are adequate but not exceptional for a Growth-sleeved stock, and the estimated PEG of 1.90 suggests the growth-adjusted valuation is less compelling than the headline P/E implies [1]. Climate change could intensify wildfire, wind, and heat events in SCE's service territory, creating tail risk that financial metrics cannot fully capture.
