Investment Thesis
E-Home Household Service operates in China's large household services market, providing physical services (appliance repair, housekeeping, senior care) with inherent AI resilience. H1 FY2026 revenue was $26.3M (flat YoY) with a dramatically narrowed net loss of just $0.11M vs. $5.06M a year prior, driven by higher gross margins (23.4%) and a ~50% cut in sales and marketing costs [1]. The company holds $262.7M in cash against $1.4M debt, and the planned Yunding IoT acquisition (500+ projects, 3M+ users) provides a pathway into smart community services [3].
The bear case is overwhelming. E-Home has a $5.2M market cap trading at 0.016x book value despite $262.7M in reported cash — the market deeply distrusts asset quality and governance under the VIE structure common to Chinese ADRs [5]. The stock collapsed from $1,262.50 to $1.61, destroying 99.9%+ of shareholder value. Daily dollar volume of ~$113K makes the stock effectively untradeable. Revenue has stagnated around $49M with negative ROE (-0.35%) and negative operating cash flow (-$1.8M) [1][5]. No analyst coverage, no forward estimates, and $1.61 price well below Nasdaq's $5 minimum bid threshold creates imminent delisting risk. The Chinese regulatory environment (HFCAA compliance, VIE oversight) adds existential uncertainty for US-listed shareholders.
