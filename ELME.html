Company Profile
Real Estate ·REIT - Residential ·NYSE ·United States
Elme Communities (formerly Washington Real Estate Investment Trust) was a multifamily REIT operating approximately 8,900 apartment homes in the Washington, DC metro and Atlanta/Sunbelt regions. The company is in active liquidation following shareholder approval of a Plan of Sale and Liquidation in October 2025, with an initial $14.67/share distribution paid in January 2026. The $280M Riverside Apartments sale collapsed in June 2026, causing ELME to withdraw all remaining distribution estimates and creating significant uncertainty around the dissolution timeline.

Investment Thesis
Elme Communities is a liquidation play, not a going-concern investment. Shareholders approved a Plan of Sale and Liquidation in October 2025, paid an initial $14.67/share distribution in January 2026, and have sold 5 properties for approximately $252.7M in 2026 with 4 more under contract for approximately $431.3M [1]. At $1.48/share, the stock represents a bet on remaining distributions from the final property sales. However, the June 17, 2026 collapse of the $280M Riverside Apartments sale to The Beitel Group during inspection forced ELME to withdraw all remaining distribution estimates, introducing material uncertainty into the dissolution timeline and final payout [1]. The $350M Green Bond portfolio, ISS Prime rating, and GRESB 'A' rating indicate the assets being sold are institutional-quality [3].
The bear case has intensified since the Riverside sale collapse. The 1,222-unit Riverside Apartments complex in Alexandria, VA was one of ELME's largest remaining assets, and the buyer's withdrawal during inspection raises questions about property condition, market pricing, or financing availability [1]. ELME has withdrawn all distribution estimates that previously guided to $16.74-17.02/share total, and the dissolution timeline (originally Q3-Q4 2026) is no longer assured. The remaining $251M of the $520M Goldman Sachs term loan must be repaid from sale proceeds, creating senior claims on the asset pool. D/E has risen to 165.5% as equity shrinks through distributions. The stock's extreme illiquidity (market cap of just $129M, daily turnover barely $1.5M) and the binary nature of the liquidation outcome make this unsuitable for most investment strategies. The stock has fallen from $2.02 to $1.48 since the Riverside collapse, and regular dividends have been suspended [4].
Key Catalysts
  1. Successful re-marketing and sale of the 1,222-unit Riverside Apartments complex after the June 2026 buyer withdrawal could restore confidence in the liquidation thesis and unlock the largest remaining asset [1]
  2. Completion of 4 properties currently under contract for approximately $431.3M, including Elme Bethesda, The Kenmore, and 3801 Connecticut Ave for approximately $168M [1]
  3. Repayment of remaining $251M of the $520M Goldman Sachs term loan, clearing the path for final shareholder distributions [1]
Key Risks
  1. Riverside Apartments sale collapse introduces material uncertainty — the $280M deal failure forced withdrawal of all distribution estimates and dissolution timeline, and re-marketing may yield a lower price [1]
  2. Liquidation timeline now uncertain — delays beyond Q4 2026 increase administrative costs, extend holding periods on remaining assets, and may require additional debt service payments [1]
  3. Extreme illiquidity risk — market cap of only $129M with daily turnover barely exceeding the $1M liquidity floor, making position entry/exit difficult and creating vulnerability to forced selling [4]
