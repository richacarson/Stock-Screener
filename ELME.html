Company Profile
Real Estate ·REIT - Residential ·NYSE ·United States
Elme Communities (formerly Washington Real Estate Investment Trust) is a multifamily REIT that owned and operated approximately 9,400 apartment homes in the Washington, DC metro and Atlanta metro regions, plus approximately 300,000 square feet of commercial space. The company is currently in liquidation — shareholders approved a Plan of Sale and Liquidation in October 2025, with an initial $14.67/share distribution paid in January 2026 and total estimated distributions of $17.02-17.47/share.

Investment Thesis
Elme Communities is a liquidation play, not a going-concern investment. Shareholders approved a Plan of Sale and Liquidation in October 2025, and the company has completed the sale of 19 multifamily communities for $1.6B, paid an initial $14.67/share liquidating distribution in January 2026, and estimates total distributions of $17.02-17.47/share [1]. At $2.02/share, the remaining implied value is $2.35-2.80/share in additional distributions, representing 16-39% upside if remaining property sales execute on schedule by mid-2026. The $350M Green Bond portfolio, ISS Prime rating, and GRESB 'A' rating indicate the assets being sold are institutional-quality [3].
The risk in any liquidation is timing, pricing, and hidden liabilities. The remaining 7 unsold properties may face buyer resistance if multifamily cap rates expand or credit markets tighten, compressing the final distribution below the $17.02-17.47 estimate [1]. The $520M term loan must be fully repaid from sale proceeds, creating senior claims on the asset pool. Legal, administrative, and wind-down costs will consume a portion of remaining value. The stock's extreme illiquidity (market cap of just $179M) and the binary nature of the liquidation timeline make this unsuitable for most investment strategies. The 35.8% dividend yield is misleading — it reflects the collapsed stock price, not sustainable income, and regular dividends will cease as the liquidation completes [4].
Key Catalysts
  1. Completion of remaining 7 property sales by mid-2026, releasing final liquidating distributions of $2.35-2.80/share above current price [1]
  2. Strong multifamily transaction market in DC metro and Atlanta could drive final sale prices above management's base case estimates [1]
  3. Repayment of $520M term loan removing the largest liability claim and clearing the path for shareholder distributions [1]
Key Risks
  1. Multifamily cap rate expansion or credit market tightening could reduce sale prices on remaining 7 properties below estimates, compressing final distributions [1]
  2. Liquidation timeline risk — delays beyond mid-2026 increase administrative costs and extend the period of uncertainty for shareholders [1]
  3. Hidden liabilities — environmental remediation, lease obligations, or legal claims could emerge during the wind-down process, reducing distributable proceeds [1]
