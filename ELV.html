Company Profile
Healthcare ·Healthcare Plans ·NYSE ·United States
Elevance Health is one of the largest managed care organizations in the United States, serving approximately 117 million people through its affiliated health plans and Carelon services business. The company operates Blue Cross Blue Shield plans in 14 states, along with Carelon (health services including pharmacy, behavioral health, and care delivery) and CarelonRx. Formerly known as Anthem, the company rebranded to Elevance Health in 2022.

Investment Thesis
Elevance Health is a deeply discounted large-cap managed care compounder trading at 10.9x forward earnings despite delivering $197.6B in operating revenue (up 13% YoY) and transforming into an integrated health company through Carelon ($71.7B revenue, up 33%) [1]. The dividend profile is exceptional — 15 consecutive years paid, 12.5% 5-year CAGR, 27.1% payout ratio, and 2.15% yield provide a growing income stream well-covered by earnings [6]. At $25.50+ guided 2026 adjusted EPS, the stock trades at a significant discount to managed care peers and the broader market. The Blue Cross Blue Shield franchise in 14 states provides durable competitive positioning, and Carelon's health services expansion creates a second growth engine that reduces dependence on insurance margin alone [1].
The bear case centers on medical cost trend pressure and regulatory risk. The benefit expense ratio rose 150bps to 90.0% in 2025, and management expects a further 125bps Medicaid margin decline in 2026 due to elevated utilization and acuity trends [1]. Q4 2025 saw an adjusted operating loss, signaling the cost pressure is accelerating. Profit margin of 2.84% leaves minimal room for error — a 50bps increase in the benefit expense ratio would erase most earnings. Government policy changes (Medicaid redeterminations, Medicare Advantage rate adjustments, potential ACA modifications) create binary regulatory risk. The company's 96,000+ employee workforce shows cultural strain per Glassdoor, with layoffs and management instability potentially undermining the Carelon transformation [3]. UnitedHealth's Optum platform has a multi-year head start in health services, and Elevance must invest aggressively in Carelon to close the gap, pressuring near-term margins [1].
Key Catalysts
  1. Carelon health services revenue growth — $71.7B in 2025 (up 33% YoY) creating a high-margin second growth engine that reduces insurance margin dependence [1]
  2. Deeply discounted valuation at 10.9x forward P/E providing margin of safety if medical cost trends normalize in 2H 2026 [6]
  3. Dividend growth trajectory — 12.5% 5-year CAGR with 27.1% payout ratio providing substantial room for continued double-digit increases [6]
Key Risks
  1. Elevated medical cost trends — benefit expense ratio rose to 90.0% in 2025 (up 150bps) with further Medicaid margin pressure expected in 2026, creating earnings risk on thin 2.84% profit margin [1]
  2. Regulatory risk from Medicaid redeterminations, Medicare Advantage rate changes, and potential ACA modifications that could compress member enrollment and reimbursement rates [1]
  3. Cultural and operational execution risk — Glassdoor reviews cite understaffing, layoffs, and leadership instability that could undermine the Carelon transformation strategy [3]
