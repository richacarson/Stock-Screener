Company Profile
Consumer Staples ·Household Products ·NYSE ·United States
Energizer Holdings, Inc. is a global manufacturer and marketer of batteries, portable lighting, and auto care products. The company's portfolio includes Energizer, Eveready, Rayovac, and VARTA battery brands, as well as auto care brands including Armor All, STP, and A/C Pro acquired through the 2024 APS (Automotive Products and Solutions) acquisition.

Investment Thesis
Energizer Holdings offers a compelling deep-value proposition at 5.0x forward P/E with a 6.48% dividend yield, trading at $18.52 with FY2026 adjusted EPS guidance of $3.30-$3.60 and adjusted EBITDA of $580-$610M [1]. The APS acquisition added $900M+ in auto care revenue (Armor All, STP, A/C Pro), diversifying beyond the mature battery category and driving Q1 FY2026 revenue growth of 6.5% to $779M [1]. Management expects sequential gross margin recovery of 300bps Q1 to Q2 and 300-400bps by year-end, targeting low-40% gross margins, while Project Momentum restructuring should deliver $15-20M in savings plus $15-25M in tariff mitigation [1]. The battery business generates stable, recession-resistant cash flows in a category with high barriers to entry and the dividend payout ratio of 40.4% is conservative relative to the yield.
The bear case is dominated by catastrophic leverage — D/E of 2,424% creates existential risk if EBITDA falls short of targets or interest rates remain elevated [4]. Q1 FY2026 organic sales declined 4.3% despite the APS contribution, revealing underlying weakness in the core battery business from softer U.S. demand [1]. Adjusted EPS fell from $0.67 to $0.31 YoY in Q1, with a reported net loss of -$3.4M, and Q2 guidance of $0.40-$0.50 EPS with organic sales down 4-5% suggests the first half will remain challenging [1]. The flat $0.30/quarter dividend since 2023 signals zero growth — at $1.20/year, the 6.48% yield reflects the depressed stock price rather than a generous distribution policy. The battery category faces structural headwinds from rechargeable alternatives, device miniaturization, and private-label competition. At $1.27B market cap against $5B+ in debt, equity holders are highly subordinated and any debt covenant violation or refinancing difficulty could severely impair equity value.

AI Risk Protection (8/9): Energizer's core businesses — manufacturing alkaline batteries, portable lighting, and auto care chemicals — are fundamentally physical products immune to AI substitution. Consumers and businesses will continue to need batteries for remote controls, smoke detectors, flashlights, and countless devices regardless of AI advancement [1]. Auto care products (Armor All, STP, A/C Pro) require physical chemical formulations applied to physical vehicles. The manufacturing, distribution, and retail channels for these products are deeply physical and cannot be digitized. AI data center expansion may even increase demand for backup battery systems. No analyst has flagged AI as a substitution risk to the battery or auto care industries. AI Opportunity (0/1): No evidence of AI-generated revenue.
