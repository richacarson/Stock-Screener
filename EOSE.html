Investment Thesis
Eos Energy represents a high-risk/high-reward play on the non-lithium energy storage revolution. Revenue grew 7x to $114.2M in 2025, the $701.5M backlog provides near-term visibility, and the $23.6B commercial pipeline (91 GWh) suggests enormous long-term potential [1]. The zinc-bromine chemistry offers non-flammable, domestically manufactured, conflict-mineral-free storage at a time when AI data center demand (22% of pipeline) and grid infrastructure investment are accelerating. 2026 guidance of $300-400M revenue (up to 250% growth) with new Marshall Township manufacturing facility opening mid-2026 could accelerate the path to profitability [1].
The bear case is severe: Eos only recently resolved its going concern doubt, reported a $969.6M net loss in 2025 (including $746.8M non-cash), and has no clear profitability timeline [1]. The 2026 revenue guidance of $300-400M fell short of expectations, causing a stock plunge despite record growth. Gross margins remain negative despite improvement, and the company must scale manufacturing rapidly while competing against battery giants with vastly superior resources. The stock has fallen from $11.33 (50-day SMA) to $5.19 — a 54% decline — reflecting market skepticism about execution and timeline to profitability. Over $1B raised in Q4 2025 through dilutive equity and debt adds financial burden [1].
