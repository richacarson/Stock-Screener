Company Profile
Financial Services ·Asset Management ·NYSE ·United States
Equitable Holdings operates as a diversified financial services company through six segments: Individual Retirement, Group Retirement, Asset Management, Protection Solutions, Wealth Management, and Legacy. Founded in 1859 (formerly AXA Equitable), the company manages $1.1 trillion in AUM/A and provides annuities, life insurance, investment management, and employee benefits to individuals and institutions.

Investment Thesis
Equitable Holdings is a deeply undervalued financial services franchise trading at 4.4x forward earnings with $1.1 trillion in AUM/A (up 10% YoY), a targeted 12-15% non-GAAP EPS CAGR through 2027, and cash generation growing from $1.6B (2025) to $1.8B (2026) to $2.0B (2027) [1]. Non-GAAP operating earnings of $1.9B ($6.21/share) in 2025 demonstrate a highly profitable core business obscured by GAAP mark-to-market volatility on the Legacy GMxB book. The dividend has grown 7 consecutive years at a 9.73% CAGR, and CEO Pearson's NWOW transformation — featured in Harvard Business Review — is creating a more agile organization capable of adapting to AI and fintech disruption [2]. At $38.84/share ($10.9B market cap), Equitable trades at a significant discount to book value and peers.
The bear case centers on GAAP earnings volatility and balance sheet complexity. The $1.4B GAAP net loss in 2025 (vs. $1.3B profit in 2024) reflects the Legacy segment's exposure to equity market movements through variable annuity guarantees, creating earnings unpredictability that suppresses the multiple [1]. D/E of 429.6% is extreme even by insurance standards, and the $7.7B debt load against volatile mark-to-market equity creates leverage risk. Revenue declined 14.2% YoY and the 3-year CAGR is -2.7%, reflecting both GAAP accounting distortions and competitive pressures in the asset management and annuity markets. The asset management segment (AllianceBernstein) faces secular fee compression from passive investing and AI-driven strategies. The Legacy GMxB book is a long-tail liability that could produce further GAAP losses in equity market downturns [1]. EQH's GAAP payout ratio of 76.7% appears stretched, though the non-GAAP payout is sustainable.
Key Catalysts
  1. Cash generation ramp from $1.6B (2025) to $1.8B (2026) to $2.0B (2027) supporting dividend growth and share buybacks [1]
  2. 12-15% non-GAAP EPS CAGR target for 2023-2027 — if achieved, the 4.4x forward P/E would imply substantial multiple expansion potential [1]
  3. NWOW organizational transformation improving agility, operational efficiency, and competitive positioning against fintech disruptors [2]
Key Risks
  1. Legacy GMxB book generating GAAP earnings volatility — equity market downturn could produce another multi-billion dollar net loss, suppressing the stock multiple [1]
  2. D/E of 429.6% creates leverage risk in a rising-rate environment, though insurance company capital structures differ from industrial companies [5]
  3. Asset management fee compression from passive investing and AI-driven investment strategies threatening the AllianceBernstein segment [1]
