Company Profile
Energy ·Oil & Gas E&P ·NYSE ·United States
EQT Corporation is the largest natural gas producer in the United States, accounting for approximately 6% of total U.S. output. The company operates primarily in the Appalachian Basin (Marcellus and Utica shales) and holds a ~53% stake in the Mountain Valley Pipeline. EQT focuses exclusively on natural gas, NGLs, and oil production.

Investment Thesis
EQT is the dominant U.S. natural gas producer at 14.4x forward earnings with analysts projecting 67% EPS growth to $5.27 in 2026 driven by rising gas prices and expanding LNG export demand [1]. The company's unhedged positioning for 2026+ provides full upside exposure to structurally bullish gas markets, supported by data center electricity demand doubling by 2030 and slowing associated gas growth from oil basins [2]. The 53% Mountain Valley Pipeline stake connects production to premium Southeast markets, and management targets $4B+ FCF enabling debt reduction to $5B by mid-2026 and potential share buyback acceleration.
The bear case centers on commodity price volatility — natural gas prices remain highly cyclical, and EQT's unhedged position means earnings could collapse 50%+ in a $2.00/MMBtu scenario [1]. The 28.9% D/E is manageable but $7.9B in total debt creates leverage risk if gas prices decline, and ROE of 9.0% remains below cost of capital [2]. Regulatory risk around methane emissions regulations and potential LNG export policy changes under shifting political winds could constrain growth. The trailing P/E of 20.2x already reflects significant earnings recovery expectations, and competition from Permian associated gas production could cap Appalachian gas pricing.
Key Catalysts
  1. Natural gas price appreciation toward $4-5/MMBtu driven by LNG export facility startups (Golden Pass, Plaquemines) adding 5+ Bcf/d of demand by 2027 [1]
  2. Data center electricity demand doubling by 2030 creating structural natural gas baseload demand growth, with EQT as the largest domestic supplier [2]
  3. Mountain Valley Pipeline 53% stake generating $200M+ annual midstream EBITDA while providing premium market access for EQT's Appalachian production [1]
Key Risks
  1. Natural gas price collapse below $2.50/MMBtu would severely compress margins given EQT's fully unhedged position, potentially reducing EPS by 40-60% from consensus estimates [1]
  2. Methane emissions regulations and potential restrictions on LNG export permits could constrain production growth and increase compliance costs by $100-200M annually [2]
  3. Appalachian Basin production growth from competitors could overwhelm takeaway capacity improvements and suppress regional pricing differentials [1]
