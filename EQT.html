Company Profile
Energy ·Oil & Gas E&P ·NYSE ·United States
EQT Corporation is the largest natural gas producer in the United States, accounting for approximately 6% of total U.S. output. The company operates primarily in the Appalachian Basin (Marcellus and Utica shales) and holds a ~53% stake in the Mountain Valley Pipeline. Founded in 1888, EQT focuses exclusively on natural gas, NGLs, and oil production, with vertically integrated midstream operations following the Equitrans acquisition. Headquartered in Pittsburgh, Pennsylvania.

Investment Thesis
EQT is the dominant U.S. natural gas producer at just 10.9x forward earnings, with Q1 2026 delivering record $1.8B free cash flow on $3.38B revenue (up 94% YoY, beating estimates by 5.3%) and EPS of $2.33 (beating $2.14 consensus) [1]. The company is uniquely positioned at the intersection of energy and AI — CEO Rice projects 10-18 BCF/day of new gas demand from AI data centers, representing a 10-20% increase in total U.S. natural gas demand [2]. The vertically integrated model (Equitrans midstream + 53% Mountain Valley Pipeline stake) connects Appalachian production to premium Southeast markets. D/E of just 20.8% provides balance sheet strength, and the 35.1% profit margin demonstrates operational efficiency. Analysts project $4.95 EPS for FY2026 and $5.40 for FY2027, with LNG export facility startups (Golden Pass, Plaquemines) adding 5+ BCF/day of structural demand by 2027 [1].
The bear case centers on commodity price volatility — natural gas remains highly cyclical, and EQT's largely unhedged position means earnings could collapse 50%+ in a $2.00/MMBtu scenario [1]. The revenue 5yr CAGR of -11.7% (distorted by the 2022 price spike to 2023-2024 trough) illustrates this volatility risk. ROE of 13.4% is improving but still below the 15% cost-of-capital threshold. CEO Rice sold $5.4M in shares in June 2026, and multiple officers sold in recent months, suggesting insiders view the stock as fairly valued near current levels [3]. Regulatory risk around methane emissions regulations and potential LNG export policy changes under shifting political dynamics could constrain growth. The PEG of 2.3 suggests the market has already partially priced in the earnings recovery, and competition from Permian Basin associated gas production could cap Appalachian gas pricing differentials [1][4].
Key Catalysts
  1. AI data center electricity demand driving structural 10-18 BCF/day of new natural gas demand by 2030, with EQT as the largest domestic supplier positioned to capture outsized share of this secular tailwind [2]
  2. LNG export facility startups (Golden Pass, Plaquemines) adding 5+ BCF/day of demand by 2027, structurally tightening the U.S. gas market and supporting higher realized prices [1]
  3. Mountain Valley Pipeline 53% stake generating growing midstream EBITDA while providing premium market access that commands basis differentials above Henry Hub for EQT's Appalachian production [1]
Key Risks
  1. Natural gas price collapse below $2.50/MMBtu would severely compress margins given EQT's largely unhedged position, potentially reducing EPS by 40-60% from consensus estimates — the same volatility that generated record Q1 results works in reverse [1]
  2. Regulatory risk from methane emissions regulations, potential LNG export permit restrictions, and shifting federal energy policy could constrain production growth and increase compliance costs [2]
  3. Net insider selling of $6.5M+ across CEO Rice and multiple officers in recent months suggests insiders may view the stock as fully valued, and the PEG of 2.3 indicates the market has partially priced in the earnings recovery [3][4]
