Company Profile
Basic Materials ·Gold ·NYSE ·Canada
Equinox Gold Corp. is a mid-tier gold mining company with operations across the Americas. The company explores, develops, and operates gold and silver mines, with key assets including the Valentine Gold Mine and Greenstone Mine in Canada, Mesquite in California, and El Limon and Libertad in Nicaragua. Equinox Gold completed the transformative $1.97B acquisition of Calibre Mining in 2025, achieving record production of 922,827 ounces. Following the divestiture of its Brazil operations, the company targets 700,000-800,000 ounces in 2026 with an 80% increase in Canadian gold production. Headquartered in Vancouver, Canada.

Investment Thesis
Equinox Gold offers leveraged gold exposure at a deeply discounted 6.49x forward earnings with a conservative balance sheet (D/E 12.9%) following $1.1B in debt reduction from the Brazil divestiture [1]. The company expects $1.4B in free cash flow in 2026 at current gold prices, with Valentine ramping to full capacity and the AI-supported Minotaur Zone discovery (2.68 g/t over 32m, 700m+ strike length) providing near-mill exploration upside with 15,000-20,000 metres of drilling planned [2]. TTM profit margin of 25.2% at AISC of $1,833/oz demonstrates operational efficiency, and the pending Orla Mining merger would create a diversified Americas-focused gold producer with enhanced scale. Senior officer insider buying ($100,800 in June 2026) and Ross Beaty's 3.5% ownership (~27M shares) align management with shareholders [3].
The bear case centers on gold price sensitivity and execution risk. AISC guidance of $1,775-$1,875/oz means a return to $2,000/oz gold would compress margins to near breakeven, potentially halting the dividend and growth investment programs [1]. The 2026 production step-down to 700-800K oz from 922K oz following the Brazil divestiture creates a negative year-over-year comparison, and ROE of just 5.2% remains well below the 15% target despite favorable gold prices above $3,400/oz, reflecting the dilutive cost of equity-financed acquisitions. The trailing P/E of 28.4x versus forward P/E of 6.49x implies the market expects a dramatic earnings inflection — if Valentine or Greenstone underperform, the trajectory could disappoint. Three CEO transitions in nine years create leadership continuity risk, and the pending Orla merger adds integration complexity [3].
