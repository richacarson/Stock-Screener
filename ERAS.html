Company Profile
Healthcare ·Biotechnology ·NASDAQ ·United States
Erasca is a clinical-stage precision oncology company focused on discovering, developing, and commercializing therapies for patients with RAS/MAPK pathway-driven cancers. The company's lead programs include ERAS-0015 (pan-RAS molecular glue) and ERAS-4001 (pan-KRAS inhibitor), targeting a pathway that affects approximately 5.5 million cancer patients annually worldwide.

Investment Thesis
Erasca is a high-potential clinical-stage oncology bet targeting the $29B RAS-driven cancer market with two differentiated lead compounds. ERAS-0015, a pan-RAS molecular glue, showed early confirmed responses across multiple tumor types and RAS mutations in Phase 1, with monotherapy topline data expected H1 2026 [1]. ERAS-4001, a potential first-in-class pan-KRAS inhibitor, has Phase 1 data expected H2 2026 [1]. The January 2026 offering raised $258.8M, bringing pro forma cash to ~$434M — sufficient runway into H2 2028 to reach key clinical milestones without dilution [1]. CEO Jonathan Lim has a proven track record, having led Ignyta to achieve the first 'triple crown' of breakthrough designations with ROZLYTREK [2]. Analyst sentiment is positive with Mizuho reaffirming Outperform and price targets trending toward $20 [1]. Patents protect both lead compounds through 2043, providing a long runway for commercialization.
The bear case reflects the inherent binary risk of clinical-stage biotech. Erasca is entirely pre-revenue with quarterly operating expenses of ~$32.6M (Q3 2025), burning approximately $130M annually with no commercial revenue to offset losses [1]. Clinical trial outcomes are inherently uncertain — early Phase 1 dose-escalation responses do not guarantee efficacy in larger trials, and the company's own risk disclosures note that targeting the active state of RAS is 'a novel and unproven approach' [2]. The $4.7B market cap implies significant value for unproven compounds, creating substantial downside risk if clinical data disappoints. Competition is intensifying as large pharma (Amgen, BMS) and other biotechs advance their own RAS-targeting programs with deeper resources. The singular focus on RAS/MAPK pathway leaves no revenue diversification — a single clinical failure could materially impair the investment thesis. Dilution risk remains if trials require longer timelines than the H2 2028 cash runway allows [1].
Key Catalysts
  1. ERAS-0015 Phase 1 monotherapy topline data expected H1 2026 — positive results could validate the pan-RAS molecular glue approach and drive significant re-rating [1]
  2. ERAS-4001 Phase 1 monotherapy topline data expected H2 2026 — potential first-in-class pan-KRAS inhibitor data across multiple tumor types [1]
  3. Expansion and combination cohorts for both lead programs in H2 2026 and 2027, with Tango Therapeutics collaboration targeting MTAP-deleted RAS-mutant cancers [1]
Key Risks
  1. Binary clinical trial risk — Phase 1 responses may not translate to meaningful efficacy in expansion cohorts, and the pan-RAS approach remains novel and unproven at scale [1][2]
  2. Cash burn of ~$130M annually with zero revenue — H2 2028 runway could require additional dilutive financing if timelines extend [1]
  3. Competitive pressure from Amgen (sotorasib), BMS/Mirati (adagrasib), and emerging RAS-targeting biotechs with deeper resources and more advanced programs [1]
