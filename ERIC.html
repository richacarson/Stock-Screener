Investment Thesis
Ericsson offers a compelling turnaround story with EBITA margins expanding to 18.3% (up from 11.5% two years ago), a net cash position of SEK 61.2B, and ROE of 28.3% driven by aggressive cost restructuring [1]. The company's market leadership in 5G RAN and expansion into high-growth enterprise and defense markets provide multiple growth vectors as global 5G deployment continues. The proposed SEK 15B buyback and dividend increase signal management confidence, while a forward P/E of 16.5 and conservative 33.5% payout ratio offer a reasonable entry point for a cyclical recovery in telecom capex [1].
The bear case centers on a structurally flat RAN market that may not recover meaningfully until 6G deployment cycles begin, leaving Ericsson dependent on cost-cutting rather than top-line growth for margin expansion [1]. Revenue declined 5% reported in 2025 with negative 5-year revenue CAGR, and ongoing layoffs erode institutional knowledge and innovation capacity. Open RAN initiatives and Huawei's market dominance outside the West create long-term competitive threats, while forward P/E of 16.5 is elevated relative to the flat growth profile and suggests limited margin of safety [1][3].
Key Catalysts
  1. Enterprise and defense 5G revenue ramp could accelerate beyond current Cloud Software & Services growth rate of 12% organically [1]
  2. Completion of cost restructuring in 2026 with full benefit of 10,000+ headcount reductions flowing through to margins [3]
  3. Potential 6G standards development cycle beginning to generate next-generation R&D investment returns from Ericsson's patent leadership [1]
Key Risks
  1. Flat-to-declining RAN market creates a structural ceiling on core revenue growth until the next capex cycle [1]
  2. Aggressive layoffs of 10,000+ employees risk degrading R&D capabilities and institutional knowledge that underpin technology leadership [3]
  3. Open RAN adoption by carriers could commoditize radio access network hardware, eroding Ericsson's pricing power and competitive moat [1]
