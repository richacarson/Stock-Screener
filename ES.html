Company Profile
Utilities ·Regulated Electric ·NYSE ·United States
Eversource Energy is New England's largest energy delivery company, serving approximately 4.4 million electric, natural gas, and water customers across Connecticut, Massachusetts, and New Hampshire. Following the 2025 exit from offshore wind, Eversource is now a pure-play regulated transmission and distribution utility with a $26.5 billion 2026-2030 capex plan focused on grid modernization.

Investment Thesis
Eversource is now a cleaner story post-offshore-wind exit: a pure-play regulated New England electric/gas utility with 4.4M customers, a 26-year dividend growth streak (4.66% yield, 5.81% 5yr CAGR), and a $26.5B 2026-2030 capex plan supporting 5-7% EPS growth through 2030 [1][4]. 2026 guidance of $4.80-$4.95 EPS and 2025 revenue of $13.5B (+13.8%) confirm the base business is healthy [1]. Data-center-driven load growth across New England is a multi-decade tailwind that transmission investment captures through rate base expansion, and AI tools like SmartInspect and digital-twin outage prevention are reducing operating costs (40,000 avoided outages in one pilot) [2]. Forward P/E of ~13.9x is attractive for a regulated monopoly with a 26-year dividend streak and a visible capex runway.
The bear case centers on balance sheet, regulatory, and offshore-wind overhang. D/E of 186% is 40% above the 10-year median and finances a heavy capex program that competes with dividend coverage; ROE of just 5.4% is well below the 15% target and was just reduced again by FERC's allowed-ROE cut, forcing Eversource to trim 2026 EPS view [5]. The company still carries offshore wind liability after the Trump administration stop-work order on Revolution Wind, and Fitch has flagged renewed financial risk [3]. Connecticut rate politics (post-July 2025 bill-shock) remain adversarial, and management's culture metrics are middling (multiple Glassdoor complaints of 'overloaded employees' and 'clicky' management) [2]. Utility sector will also compete with higher rates for yield-seeking capital.
Key Catalysts
  1. AI data-center load growth across New England driving transmission capex expansion and rate base growth under the $26.5B 2026-2030 plan [1]
  2. 26-year dividend growth streak continuing with 5-7% annual EPS growth through 2030, supporting a 4.66% yield [4]
  3. Resolution of Revolution Wind stop-work order and final offshore-wind liability reduction removing a persistent overhang [3]
Key Risks
  1. FERC allowed-ROE cut already forced a 2026 EPS trim; further regulatory reductions would pressure the dividend trajectory [5]
  2. Offshore wind residual liability — Revolution Wind stop-work order and $2.4B in realized losses continue to cap upside; Fitch flagged financial risk [3]
  3. Connecticut rate politics — July 2025 bill-shock has politicized energy policy and threatens future rate-case outcomes [3]
