Company Profile
Healthcare ·Drug Manufacturers - Specialty & Generic ·NASDAQ ·United States
Esperion Therapeutics is a commercial-stage biopharmaceutical company focused on cardiovascular and cardiometabolic therapies. Its flagship product bempedoic acid (Nexletol/Nexlizet) is the first oral ACLY inhibitor for lowering LDL cholesterol, approved in 50+ countries. The company is expanding into rare/orphan diseases with ESP-2001 for primary sclerosing cholangitis and advancing a triple-combination therapy targeting 2027 commercialization.
294 Employees ·www.esperion.com

Investment Thesis
Esperion Therapeutics is approaching a critical inflection point as bempedoic acid gains momentum through 2026 ACC/AHA Class 1 guideline recommendations, 38% revenue growth in 2025 to $408M, and 34% prescription growth [1]. The company expects to become cash-generating in 2026 with forward P/E of 9.3x, offering significant upside if profitability materializes [1]. The pipeline is diversifying meaningfully — triple-combination therapy targeting 2027, ESP-2001 for PSC entering clinical trials by end of 2026, and the Corstasis acquisition expanding into rare diseases [1]. International expansion through Daiichi Sankyo (30 countries, royalties up 51% in Q4) provides a growing royalty stream with minimal incremental cost. Analysts maintain a consensus 'Moderate Buy' with $8.25 average price target, representing 200%+ upside from $2.74 [1].
The bear case centers on financial fragility and management quality concerns. Esperion carries $548M in debt against $168M cash with negative equity, negative operating cash flow of -$13.1M, and negative FCF of -$52.6M — the 'cash-generating in 2026' guidance is aspirational [3]. Q4 2025 revenue of $168.4M was heavily inflated by a $90M Otsuka milestone payment that masks underlying product revenue of only $43.7M [1]. The stock trades at $2.74 versus its SMA-50 of $3.08, reflecting weak momentum. Employee culture is severely problematic — Comparably's 'F' executive team rating and Glassdoor descriptions of 'culture of fear' and 'worst management team in the industry' raise retention and execution risk [2]. Bempedoic acid faces competition from PCSK9 inhibitors (Repatha, Praluent) and novel therapies entering the LDL-lowering space, and the company lacks the commercial scale of larger pharma competitors [2]. ESG disclosure is notably weak with no carbon emissions data or sustainability targets [3].
