Investment Thesis
EVgo is one of the fastest-growing EV infrastructure companies in the US, with revenue surging 75.5% YoY to $384M in 2025 and achieving its first positive adjusted EBITDA quarter ($25M in Q4, a $33M YoY improvement) [1]. The company operates 1,200+ DC fast charging locations across 47 states, serving 1.6M customer accounts, and guides to $410M-$470M revenue in 2026 with 1,400-1,650 stall deployments. Gross margins of 41% on charging network revenue demonstrate the unit economics are viable at scale [1]. The long-term targets of 50-60% charging-network profit CAGR and 25-30% adjusted EBITDA margins suggest substantial upside if EV adoption continues its trajectory. At $554M market cap (1.4x trailing revenue), the stock prices in significant execution risk.
The bear case centers on capital intensity, profitability uncertainty, and competitive dynamics. EVgo plans ~$200M gross CapEx in 2026 while guiding to EBITDA of -$20M to +$20M — the path to sustained profitability remains unclear [1]. Free cash flow is -$117M annually, requiring ongoing capital raises or debt issuance [4]. Tesla Supercharger's expanding third-party access and ChargePoint's asset-light model pose competitive threats. Fuzzy Panda Research alleged in 2022 that EVgo chargers were broken 25%+ of the time and that management misled government officials on uptime [3]. Federal EV tax credit expiration could slow EV adoption and reduce charging demand. Glassdoor reviews citing two rounds of layoffs, 'directionless' management, and leadership toxicity suggest internal execution risks [2]. The stock has declined 31% from its 50-day SMA of $2.56 to $1.77.
Key Catalysts
  1. Next-generation NACS charging architecture rollout in 2026 doubling EVgo's addressable market by serving Tesla and all major OEM vehicles on a single standard [1]
  2. Path to sustained EBITDA profitability — Q4 2025 positive adjusted EBITDA of $25M demonstrates viability; full-year 2026 EBITDA guidance of -$20M to +$20M is the key metric to watch [1]
  3. Autonomous vehicle partnerships — OEM collaborations position EVgo to capture high-utilization autonomous fleet charging revenue as self-driving vehicles scale [2]
Key Risks
  1. Capital intensity trap — $200M gross CapEx in 2026 with uncertain EBITDA, -$117M free cash flow, and $311M debt creates financing risk if EV adoption slows [1][4]
  2. Tesla Supercharger competition — Tesla's expanding third-party access and brand trust advantage could commoditize EVgo's charging network value proposition [1]
  3. Organizational instability — two rounds of layoffs (2023-2024), 1.8-year average management tenure, and Glassdoor reviews describing 'directionless' leadership raise execution risk during a critical scaling phase [2]
