Company Profile
Industrials ·Security & Protection Services ·NASDAQ ·United States
Evolv Technologies develops, manufactures, and sells AI-powered security screening systems for high-traffic venues. Its flagship Evolv Express uses advanced sensors and machine learning to detect weapons in unstructured people flows without requiring individuals to stop, remove items, or empty pockets. The company serves 1,200+ customers across education, healthcare, sports, entertainment, and government sectors and has scanned over 4 billion people since 2019.

Investment Thesis
Evolv Technologies is the category leader in AI-powered touchless weapon detection, serving 1,200+ customers including NFL stadiums, schools, and hospitals with technology that has screened over 4 billion people [2]. The company grew revenue 40% YoY to $145.9M in 2025, turned Adjusted EBITDA positive for the first time at $11.1M, and guided 2026 revenue to $172-$178M (18-22% growth) with ARR reaching $145-$150M [2]. The transition to ~50% pure subscription model strengthens recurring revenue quality. With $69M cash and improving unit economics, Evolv is approaching a profitability inflection point in a growing market driven by school safety mandates and venue security regulations.
The bear case centers on profitability uncertainty and leadership instability. Despite 40% revenue growth, Evolv posted a -$33.1M net loss in 2025 and ROE of -28% — the path to GAAP profitability remains unclear beyond Adjusted EBITDA [1][2]. The forced CEO termination in October 2024 for cultural reasons, followed by two leadership changes in three months, creates organizational risk and execution uncertainty under new CEO John Kedzierski [3]. The $1.1B market cap implies ~6x forward revenue, which demands sustained 20%+ growth that could be challenged by traditional security companies (Smiths Detection, Garrett) adding AI capabilities to their established customer relationships. Evolv's technology has faced scrutiny over detection accuracy, and any high-profile failure at a protected venue would be catastrophic for the brand. Stock-based compensation remains elevated, diluting shareholder value.
Key Catalysts
  1. 2026 revenue guidance of $172-$178M with ARR of $145-$150M represents 20%+ growth and continued subscription model transition driving recurring revenue quality [2]
  2. School safety mandate legislation in multiple U.S. states could accelerate education sector adoption, Evolv's fastest-growing vertical
  3. Full-year GAAP profitability inflection as subscription revenue scales and operating leverage improves from the $11.1M Adjusted EBITDA base [2]
Key Risks
  1. Leadership instability — three CEO changes in 14 months (George terminated, Ellenbogen interim, Kedzierski appointed) creates execution risk during a critical growth phase [3]
  2. Detection accuracy scrutiny — any high-profile weapon incident at an Evolv-protected venue would severely damage brand credibility and could trigger customer defections
  3. GAAP profitability remains elusive despite Adjusted EBITDA turning positive, with -$33.1M net loss in 2025 and elevated stock-based compensation diluting shareholder value [2]

AI Resilience LOW RISK

AI Risk Protection (7/9): Evolv IS an AI company — its entire product line is built on proprietary AI/ML weapon detection algorithms trained on millions of scans across 1,200+ customer sites [2]. AI does not threaten this business; AI IS the business. The physical hardware (sensors, screening gates) combined with proprietary training data from 4+ billion scans creates a moat that pure-software AI cannot replicate. The shift toward subscription/SaaS model with Evolv Insights analytics further embeds AI into recurring revenue. Venues cannot deploy a ChatGPT alternative to screen for weapons — this requires purpose-built hardware-software integration. AI Opportunity (1/1): 100% of Evolv's $145.9M revenue is AI-derived, with Evolv Express and eXpedite being AI-native products and Evolv Insights providing AI-powered analytics as an upsell [2].
