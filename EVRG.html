Company Profile
Utilities ·Utilities - Regulated Electric ·NASDAQ ·United States
Evergy is a regulated electric utility serving 1.7 million customers across Kansas and Missouri. The company generates, transmits, distributes, and sells electricity from a diversified fuel mix including coal, natural gas, nuclear, wind, and solar. Formed in 2017 through the merger of Westar Energy and Kansas City Power & Light, Evergy is headquartered in Kansas City, Missouri.

Investment Thesis
Evergy is a stable regulated utility positioned to benefit from AI-driven electricity demand. FY2025 adjusted EPS of $3.83 grows to guided $4.14-$4.34 in FY2026 (8-13% growth), with a long-term 6-8%+ EPS growth target through 2030 accelerating above 8% starting in 2028 [1]. The $21.6B capital investment plan and signed LLPS agreements with data center customers provide visible load growth catalysts. At 17.9x forward P/E with a 3.41% dividend yield and 7 consecutive years of dividend growth, Evergy offers reasonable income and moderate growth for a utility [5]. The company's regulated monopoly in Kansas and Missouri provides earnings stability and recession resistance.
The bear case centers on ESG credibility damage and regulatory risk. Evergy backtracked on its 2045 net-zero commitment, delayed coal plant retirements by 4-5 years, and removed 2030 emissions targets from SEC filings — earning Sierra Club's 'greenwashing' label [4]. D/E of 149.9% with $15.4B in total debt creates interest rate sensitivity, and the elevated 73.7% payout ratio limits dividend growth flexibility [5]. Revenue growth of just 2.0% YoY and 3.7% 5yr CAGR reflect the sluggish organic growth reality of regulated utilities. ROE of 8.6% is constrained by regulatory rate caps and below investor expectations for the sector. Negative free cash flow of -$862M reflects the massive capital requirements that make the 6-8% EPS growth target dependent on favorable regulatory outcomes for rate recovery [5].
Key Catalysts
  1. Large Load Power Service (LLPS) agreements with data center customers driving incremental load growth, with potential for additional agreements as AI infrastructure demand expands [1]
  2. FY2026 EPS growth of 8-13% (guided $4.14-$4.34 vs. $3.83 in FY2025) with acceleration above 8% starting in 2028, supported by $21.6B capital investment plan [1]
  3. Favorable regulatory rate case outcomes in Kansas and Missouri to recover capital investments and maintain authorized returns [1]
Key Risks
  1. ESG credibility erosion from backtracking on net-zero commitments and coal plant retirement delays, potentially affecting institutional investor interest and regulatory relationships [4]
  2. Interest rate sensitivity on $15.4B debt with D/E of 149.9% — higher-for-longer rates compress utility valuations and increase borrowing costs for the $21.6B capital plan [5]
  3. Regulatory risk — the 6-8% EPS growth target depends on Kansas and Missouri regulators approving rate increases to recover massive capital investments, with no guarantee of favorable outcomes [1]
