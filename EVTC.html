Company Profile
Technology ·Information Technology Services ·NYSE ·Puerto Rico
EVERTEC, Inc. is the leading full-service transaction processing company in Latin America and the Caribbean, providing payment technology and merchant acquiring, business process management, and financial technology solutions. Founded in Puerto Rico and publicly traded since 2013, the company serves financial institutions, merchants, and government agencies across 26 countries. Following its transformative $412M acquisition of Sinqia in 2023, EVERTEC has expanded from a Caribbean-centric payments processor to a diversified LatAm fintech platform with ~40% of revenue from Latin America.

Investment Thesis
EVERTEC is a compelling mid-cap fintech value play trading at just 8.1x forward earnings with 10%+ organic revenue growth and a clear path to $1B+ annual revenue in 2026. The $412M Sinqia acquisition successfully transformed EVERTEC from a Caribbean payments processor into a diversified LatAm fintech platform — LATAM now represents ~40% of revenue versus 10% in 2015 — with cross-sell opportunities across Sinqia's banking software client base providing multi-year growth runway [1][2]. Q1 2026 revenue of $247.9M (up 8% YoY) and raised full-year guidance to $1,073–$1,085M demonstrate execution on the integration thesis. Adjusted EBITDA margin of ~40%, ROE of 23.6%, and Forward PEG of 1.02 suggest the stock is significantly undervalued relative to its growth profile and dominant Caribbean market position [1].
The bear case centers on execution risk and geographic concentration. Integration of Sinqia, Grandata, Nubity, and Tecnobank simultaneously across multiple LatAm markets creates operational complexity — Q1 2026 GAAP net income declined 27% YoY to $23.8M despite revenue growth, reflecting acquisition-related costs and currency headwinds [2]. D/E of 155% is elevated, though deleveraging from 3.8x to ~2.8x net leverage shows progress. EVERTEC remains dependent on Puerto Rico's government payment flows (~25% of revenue) and Banco Popular's transaction volumes, creating concentration risk. The stock's recent 16% decline signals market skepticism about near-term growth trajectory, and CEO insider selling of ~$4M in 2025 raises alignment concerns. LatAm currency volatility (constant currency revenue growth of 5% in Q1 vs. 8% reported) creates earnings unpredictability [2].
Key Catalysts
  1. Cross-sell of Sinqia banking software across EVERTEC's merchant acquiring and payment processing client base in LatAm, targeting $50M+ in incremental annual revenue synergies by 2027
  2. Continued deleveraging from 2.8x toward sub-2x net leverage, unlocking capital for additional tuck-in acquisitions and potential dividend growth from a 5.8% payout ratio
  3. Digital payments adoption acceleration in Latin America — card penetration in Brazil, Colombia, and Mexico remains below 40% versus 75%+ in the US, providing a secular tailwind for transaction volume growth
Key Risks
  1. GAAP profitability compression from simultaneous integration of four acquisitions (Sinqia, Grandata, Nubity, Tecnobank) across different LatAm regulatory and cultural environments — Q1 2026 GAAP net income fell 27% despite 8% revenue growth
  2. Puerto Rico government contract concentration risk — approximately 25% of revenue depends on government payment processing contracts that face periodic renegotiation and political risk
  3. LatAm currency depreciation against the USD erodes reported revenue and earnings — Q1 2026 constant currency growth of 5% vs. 8% reported highlights the ~3 percentage point FX headwind

AI Resilience (25%)
AI Resilience MODERATE
7
AI Risk Protection (6/9): EVERTEC's core business — payment processing, merchant acquiring, and government payment flows — involves deeply embedded financial infrastructure with high switching costs, regulatory requirements (PCI-DSS, local banking regulations across 26 countries), and physical network effects (ATH network in Puerto Rico, POS terminal installations) that AI cannot substitute [1][3]. The company processes transactions for financial institutions under long-term contracts with complex integration requirements. However, AI-native fintech competitors could theoretically streamline onboarding and underwriting, and EVERTEC's business process management segment (document processing, claims management) faces moderate AI automation risk. The Sinqia banking software platform could face competitive pressure from AI-powered core banking alternatives in the LatAm market [2]. AI Opportunity (1/1): EVERTEC is actively deploying AI/ML fraud detection tools across its payment processing platform to reduce chargeback costs and false positives for merchant clients. Management has discussed AI-enhanced data analytics capabilities through the Grandata acquisition, which uses behavioral data and AI for credit risk scoring in Mexico — a direct AI revenue product [3].
