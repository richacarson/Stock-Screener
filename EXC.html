Company Profile
Utilities ·Utilities - Regulated Electric ·NASDAQ ·United States
Exelon Corporation is a utility services holding company that distributes and transmits electricity and natural gas through six regulated utility subsidiaries (ComEd, PECO, BGE, Pepco, DPL, ACE) serving approximately 10 million customers across the mid-Atlantic and Midwest regions. The company is positioned as a primary beneficiary of AI-driven data center electricity demand, with its service territories including key data center markets. Incorporated in 1999 and headquartered in Chicago, Illinois, Exelon spun off its generation business as Constellation Energy in 2022 to focus exclusively on regulated transmission and distribution.

Investment Thesis
Exelon offers a defensive utility investment with a compelling AI-demand tailwind — the company's data center load queue surged from 6 GW to 19 GW, directly benefiting its mid-Atlantic service territories which include key data center markets [2]. FY2025 adjusted EPS of $2.77 and 2026 guidance of $2.81-$2.91 demonstrate consistent execution, with the $41.3B capex plan driving 7.9% rate base growth and targeting near the top end of 5-7% EPS CAGR through 2029 [1]. The 3.56% dividend yield with 4 consecutive years of growth and a 5% annual increase target, combined with forward P/E of 15.1, positions Exelon for 8-10% total returns. The AI-driven electricity demand growth — projected to triple U.S. data center demand by 2032 — provides a secular growth catalyst rare among regulated utilities [2].
The bear case revolves around capital intensity, regulatory execution risk, and balance sheet leverage. The $41.3B capex plan requires $3.4B in equity issuance through 2029 (creating dilution risk) and significant additional debt on top of the already elevated D/E of 174.8% [1]. If rate case outcomes are unfavorable — particularly the critical 2026 Maryland legislative push — Exelon's ability to earn adequate returns on its massive capital deployment could be impaired. The 5-year dividend growth CAGR of just 0.81% reflects the dividend reset at the 2022 Constellation spinoff and means income investors have not yet been rewarded for the post-spinoff strategy. ROE of 9.8% is constrained by regulatory allowed returns, limiting upside even in favorable demand environments. Rising interest rates increase the cost of Exelon's substantial debt load and make the 3.56% yield less competitive against risk-free alternatives [1].
Key Catalysts
  1. Data center electricity demand projected to triple by 2032 directly benefits Exelon's mid-Atlantic service territories, with 19 GW of high-probability data center load in queue driving the $5B new business allocation within the $41.3B capex plan [2]
  2. 2026 Maryland legislative push could authorize Exelon to re-enter the generation business to serve critical data center loads — success would represent a significant strategic expansion beyond regulated T&D [2]
  3. EPS CAGR targeting near the top end of 5-7% through 2029, supported by 7.9% rate base growth, pending rate cases across subsidiaries, and operational efficiency gains from AI-driven grid management [1]
Key Risks
  1. D/E of 174.8% and $51.2B total debt against $713M cash create significant balance sheet leverage — the $3.4B equity issuance program adds dilution risk that could offset EPS growth if utility multiples compress in a rising rate environment [1]
  2. Regulatory pushback risk from rising retail electricity prices, potentially resulting in lower allowed ROEs or disallowed costs in rate case proceedings across the six utility subsidiaries
  3. 5-year dividend growth CAGR of just 0.81% reflects the post-spinoff dividend reset — if the 5% annual growth target is not sustained, income investors may rotate to higher-yielding utility peers with longer dividend growth track records
