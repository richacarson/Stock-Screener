Investment Thesis
Exelon offers a defensive utility investment with 5-7% EPS growth, 3.5% dividend yield, and 5% annual dividend increases targeting 10-12% total returns through 2029 [1]. The $41.3B capital plan drives 7.9% rate base growth supported by surging data center electricity demand in key mid-Atlantic markets. The Q4 2025 EPS beat ($0.59 vs. $0.55 consensus) and 2026 guidance of $2.81-$2.91 demonstrate execution, while 13.5% credit metrics exceed Moody's thresholds by 150 basis points, supporting the A-rated balance sheet.
The bear case revolves around capital intensity and regulatory execution risk. The $41.3B capex plan requires $3.4B in equity issuance and $16B in debt, which could pressure returns if rate case outcomes are unfavorable [1]. D/E of 174% is already elevated, and retail electricity prices increased 4.8% in 2025, creating regulatory pushback risk. Revenue declined 1.1% in 2025 despite growing demand, reflecting the lag between investment and rate recovery. The 15.9x forward P/E offers limited valuation discount relative to the sector, and rising interest rates increase the cost of Exelon's massive debt load.
Key Catalysts
  1. Data center electricity demand projected to triple by 2032 directly benefits Exelon's mid-Atlantic service territories, with the $41.3B capex plan sized to capture incremental load growth [1]
  2. ComEd's $243M rate increase approved by ICC effective January 2026, plus pending rate cases across subsidiaries, should drive 2026-2027 earnings growth
  3. EPS CAGR targeting near the top end of 5-7% through 2029, supported by 7.9% rate base growth and operational efficiency gains [1]
Key Risks
  1. Rising retail electricity prices (4.8% in 2025) could trigger regulatory pushback, resulting in lower allowed ROEs or disallowed costs in rate case proceedings [1]
  2. The $3.4B equity issuance program creates dilution risk that could offset EPS growth if utility multiples compress in a rising rate environment
  3. Concentration in mid-Atlantic service territories exposes Exelon to regional economic slowdowns and potential customer migration to rooftop solar/distributed generation
