Company Profile
Energy ·Oil & Gas E&P ·NASDAQ ·United States
Expand Energy Corporation (formerly Chesapeake Energy) is the largest independent natural gas producer in the United States. The company operates in the Marcellus Shale (Pennsylvania, Ohio, West Virginia), Utica Shale, and Haynesville/Bossier Shales (Louisiana, Texas). Rebranded in October 2024 following the $7.4B merger with Southwestern Energy. Founded in 1989 and headquartered in Oklahoma City.
1,600 Employees ·www.expandenergy.com

Investment Thesis
Expand Energy is the largest independent U.S. natural gas producer (~7.40 Bcfe/d) at an inflection point where AI-driven electricity demand and LNG exports are creating structural tailwinds for natural gas [1]. Full-year 2025 net income of $1.82B ($7.57/share) with 38.3% revenue growth demonstrates the Southwestern merger's accretive impact [1]. Forward P/E of 11.1x with D/E of only 27.5% offers compelling value for a category leader targeting $500M in annual synergies by end of 2026 [1][4]. The 3.07% dividend yield provides income while the company grows free cash flow. Analyst consensus projects $9.13 EPS for 2026, representing 21% growth [1]. The company's basin diversification (Marcellus, Haynesville) provides optionality to serve both domestic and LNG export demand.
The bear case highlights leadership uncertainty, commodity exposure, and legacy governance risk. CEO Nick Dell'Osso's sudden departure in February 2026, replaced by interim CEO Michael Wichterich, raises questions about strategic continuity during a critical integration period [2]. Natural gas prices remain volatile — ROE of 10.1% is below the 15% target despite strong revenue growth, suggesting margin compression from commodity swings [4]. Chesapeake's history includes the 2020 bankruptcy, Aubrey McClendon's governance scandals, EPA environmental fines, and felony fraud charges in Michigan — while the company has been transformed, the legacy creates reputational overhang [3]. Profit margin of 15.6% is moderate for an E&P company, and the payout ratio of 42.1% with variable special dividends makes income unreliable. The stock has declined from $106.90 (50-day SMA) to $103.55, reflecting market uncertainty about the leadership transition [4].
