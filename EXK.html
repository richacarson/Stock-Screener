Company Profile
Basic Materials ·Silver ·NYSE ·Canada
Endeavour Silver Corp. is a mid-tier silver mining company engaged in the acquisition, exploration, development, extraction, processing, and reclamation of mineral properties in Mexico, Chile, Peru, and the United States. The company primarily explores for gold and silver deposits and operates the Guanacevi and Bolanitos mines in Mexico, the newly commissioned Terronera mine in Jalisco, Mexico, and the Kolpa mine in Peru acquired in 2025.

Investment Thesis
Endeavour Silver is executing a transformative growth phase, having successfully brought the Terronera mine into production and acquired Kolpa in Peru, driving Q1 2026 revenue up 230% to $209.7M with net earnings swinging to $64.9M profit from a $32.9M loss [1]. Silver equivalent production surged 78% to 3.34M ounces in Q1, with full-year 2026 guidance targeting 14.6-15.6M ounces (30% growth). At forward P/E of just 7.4x with analysts projecting $0.58 full-year EPS, the stock appears undervalued relative to its production growth trajectory. The balance sheet is strong with $231.8M cash and $173.4M working capital. Silver prices remain supported by both industrial demand (solar, electronics, AI data centers) and precious metals safe-haven flows, providing a favorable commodity backdrop [3].
The bear case centers on execution risk and commodity dependence. Trailing profit margin of -3.5% and ROE of -3.9% reflect the company's recent transition period — if Terronera or Kolpa encounter operational difficulties (grade variability, labor issues, permitting), the growth thesis could stall [4]. Silver's dual nature as both industrial and precious metal creates price volatility; a global recession could simultaneously reduce industrial demand while potentially supporting safe-haven flows. Mining in Mexico and Peru carries political and regulatory risk — both jurisdictions have seen increasing resource nationalism. The Inspire Impact Score of 0 with no positive or negative attributions provides no ESG differentiation. Operating margins of 40% in Q1 2026 may prove unsustainable if silver prices normalize from current elevated levels, and analyst EPS of $1.32 forward may prove optimistic if commodity prices decline.
