Company Profile
Financial Services ·Insurance Brokers ·NASDAQ ·United States
Reliance Global Group, Inc. acquires, owns, and manages insurance distribution and technology-oriented businesses in the United States. Operates RELI Exchange, a B2B InsurTech platform for insurance agents, and 5MinuteInsure.com, a direct-to-consumer insurance comparison platform. Also operates EZRA International Group, a division acquiring controlling stakes in high-tech Israeli companies in cybersecurity, AI, FinTech, and MedTech. Changed ticker from RELI to EZRA in 2025. Founded in 2013, headquartered in Lakewood, New Jersey.

Investment Thesis
EZRA represents a speculative micro-cap with ambitions far exceeding its current scale. CEO Ezra Beyman has assembled an interesting portfolio of early-stage technology bets — Enquantum's post-quantum encryption and Scentech Medical's AI diagnostics alongside the core InsurTech platform [2]. Q1 2026 net loss narrowed 15% YoY to $1.47M, and the CEO has been buying shares in the open market at $0.17-$0.28 per share [5]. The EZRA International Group structure could create optionality if any portfolio company achieves product-market fit in cybersecurity, AI diagnostics, or InsurTech.
The bear case is overwhelming: EZRA has a $1.9M market cap — below its $2.3M cash balance — signaling the equity market sees negative enterprise value or questions going-concern viability [1][5]. Revenue has declined from $14M (2024) to an annualized $15.3M run-rate but with -55.9% profit margins generating persistent losses. The stock is deeply illiquid at $330K average daily dollar volume, making any meaningful position impossible to enter or exit without moving the price. D/E of 73% on a tiny capital base with negative cash flow means the company requires ongoing capital raises that dilute existing shareholders. The strategy of acquiring Israeli tech startups while running a small insurance brokerage creates conglomerate complexity that is inappropriate for a company this size [1][4].
