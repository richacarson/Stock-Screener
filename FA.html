Excellence Evaluation — Think Like an Owner (50%)
Innovation DEVELOPING
6
First Advantage is transitioning from a background screening service to a comprehensive capital risk management platform, targeting $2B revenue by 2028 [1]. The company's platforms leverage proprietary AI for faster, deeper screening and digital identity solutions, and management has publicly embraced responsible AI practices that prioritize fairness and transparency [3]. However, the core business remains screening commoditized data with limited R&D differentiation, and the AI-driven replacement of verification specialists suggests efficiency gains over genuine innovation [3].
Inspiration DEVELOPING
5
CEO Scott Staples holds 68% approval rating with employees and is actively setting new core values alongside the Chief People & Culture Officer [3]. However, Glassdoor rating of 3.3/5 with only 45% of employees recommending the company reveals widespread dissatisfaction. Reviews cite leadership departures, unresponsive managers, and information misrouting [3]. The AI-driven layoff of an entire Verification Specialist team — replaced with AI and offshore workers — demonstrates prioritization of cost efficiency over employee welfare.
Infrastructure DEVELOPING
4

FY2026 guidance of $1.625-1.7B with adjusted EBITDA of $460-480M indicates ~28% margins on adjusted basis. However, GAAP profit margin remains negative at -2.2% and D/E of 159.5% reflects heavy acquisition-related leverage [2]. Forward P/E of 9.2x on adjusted earnings is cheap, but the stock at $12.97 reflects market skepticism about organic growth sustainability and integration execution.

Investment Thesis
First Advantage offers a transformational growth story at 9.2x forward adjusted earnings, with the Sterling acquisition creating the world's largest background screening platform targeting $2B revenue by 2028. AI-powered screening capabilities are driving operational efficiency and enabling expansion from commodity checks into comprehensive capital risk management across 200+ countries [1].
The investment case requires patience through acquisition integration and GAAP profitability normalization. D/E of 159.5% from the Sterling deal creates financial risk, organic growth has decelerated to ~3%, and employee sentiment (Glassdoor 3.3/5, 45% recommend) suggests cultural integration challenges [3]. The WATCH recommendation reflects cheap valuation offset by execution uncertainty — upgrading to HOLD requires evidence of sustained organic growth acceleration and margin expansion from combined platform synergies.
Key Catalysts
  1. Sterling integration synergies driving adjusted EBITDA margin expansion toward 30%+ as duplicate infrastructure is consolidated
  2. AI-powered platform differentiation enabling deeper screening capabilities and new product lines (continuous monitoring, digital identity) driving cross-sell revenue
  3. Hiring market recovery in 2H 2026 boosting organic screening volumes beyond the current 3% growth trajectory toward the $2B by 2028 target
Key Risks
  1. Organic revenue growth decelerating to 3% suggests underlying demand softness that acquisition-driven growth is masking, risking the $2B by 2028 target
  2. D/E of 159.5% from Sterling acquisition limits strategic flexibility and increases vulnerability to hiring market downturns or rising interest costs
  3. FCRA compliance history and background check accuracy complaints create regulatory and reputational risk as AI-driven automation scales screening volume
