Company Profile
Healthcare ·Biotechnology ·NASDAQ ·United States
Fate Therapeutics is a clinical-stage biopharmaceutical company developing programmed cellular immunotherapies for cancer and autoimmune disorders using its proprietary induced pluripotent stem cell (iPSC) platform. Key programs include FT819 (off-the-shelf CAR T-cell therapy for autoimmune diseases), FT836 (solid tumors), and FT839 (multi-antigen dual-CAR T-cell). Headquartered in San Diego, California.
161 Employees ·www.fatetherapeutics.com

Investment Thesis
Fate Therapeutics is a clinical-stage biotech with a differentiated iPSC-derived cell therapy platform that has achieved meaningful clinical milestones — FT819 demonstrated outpatient dosing in 20 autoimmune patients without conditioning chemotherapy, and FT836 showed early anti-tumor activity in solid tumors [1]. The company's 500+ patent portfolio, proprietary Sword and Shield technology, and first-mover position in off-the-shelf iPSC-derived CAR T-cells create substantial scientific moat. With $205M cash providing runway through year-end 2027, planned discussion of an FT819 registrational trial under RMAT designation in 2026, and a $159M market cap, the risk-reward is asymmetric if clinical data supports approval — the autoimmune cell therapy market alone is projected to be multi-billion dollar [1][2].
The bear case is substantial. Fate is burning $136M annually on $6.6M in revenue, with no approved products and a stock that has declined 98%+ from its highs above $100 [1][4]. The $205M cash runway through 2027 means dilutive financing is likely needed before any product reaches market, and the $159M market cap provides minimal equity cushion. Clinical-stage biotech risk is acute — FT819's autoimmune data, while promising, is from a small number of patients and a registrational trial could fail. Competition from autologous CAR-T therapies (Kymriah, Yescarta) and other off-the-shelf approaches (Allogene, CRISPR) is intensifying. Insiders are selling, not buying, at $1.06 per share — a signal that even management may lack conviction in the near-term outlook [4]. The -51.8% ROE and 0% profit margin with no path to profitability before 2029+ make this a speculative option on clinical success.
Key Catalysts
  1. FT819 registrational trial discussion planned for 2026 under RMAT designation — if initiated, could accelerate path to approval in autoimmune indications with breakthrough therapy potential [1]
  2. FT836 solid tumor data showing anti-tumor activity without conditioning chemotherapy, potentially expanding the addressable market beyond autoimmune diseases [1]
  3. Autoimmune cell therapy market emerging as multi-billion dollar opportunity with Fate's off-the-shelf iPSC approach offering manufacturing and scalability advantages over autologous CAR-T [2]
Key Risks
  1. Cash runway through year-end 2027 with $136M annual burn rate makes dilutive financing inevitable before any product commercialization, potentially devastating the $159M market cap [1]
  2. Clinical-stage binary risk — FT819 data from only 20 autoimmune patients; registrational trial failure would be catastrophic given the concentrated pipeline [1]
  3. 98%+ stock decline from highs and insider selling at $1.06 suggest market and management skepticism about near-term value creation [4]
