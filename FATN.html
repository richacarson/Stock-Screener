Company Profile
Technology ·Software - Infrastructure ·NASDAQ ·United States
FatPipe, Inc. is a leading developer of enterprise-class, application-aware, Secure Software-Defined Wide Area Network (SD-WAN) solutions for organizations including enterprises, communication service providers, security service providers, and government organizations. Founded in 2010 by Dr. Ragula Bhaskar and headquartered in Salt Lake City, Utah, FatPipe pioneered the concept of SD-WAN and hybrid WANs and holds 14 patents in networking technology. The company listed on the Nasdaq in 2025.

FatPipe is a founder-led SD-WAN pioneer showing accelerating momentum: Q3 FY2026 revenue grew 30% YoY with MRR surging 48%, the company turned cash flow positive, and D. Boral Capital initiated coverage with an $8.00 price target (280%+ upside) citing its #1 midmarket ranking and expanding cybersecurity product suite [1][7]. The company's 14 patents, minimal debt (D/E 27%), and founder-CEO with deep domain expertise create a defensible niche in a $17.6B+ addressable market.
The bear case centers on scale and execution risk: with only $16M in trailing annual revenue and 4.1% profit margins, FatPipe remains a micro-cap ($29M market cap) competing against Cisco, VMware, and Fortinet in SD-WAN [2]. Glassdoor reviews (3.2/5) highlight organizational dysfunction that could impede the sales force expansion needed to capture enterprise customers. The P/E of 41.8x prices in significant growth that may not materialize if the company cannot scale its go-to-market operation beyond the midmarket niche, and the class action lawsuit from Rosen Law represents an additional overhang [5][8].
