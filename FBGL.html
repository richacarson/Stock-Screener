Investment Thesis
FBS Global is a Singapore-based green building contractor that IPO'd on NASDAQ in February 2025 at $4.50, now trading at $0.63 — an 86% post-IPO decline reflecting deep investor skepticism [4]. The S$104.8M secured pipeline and new exclusive rights for international commercialization of green/intelligent building technologies provide some strategic optionality [3]. The company's 20+ year Finebuild Systems operating heritage in Singapore institutional/commercial fit-out gives it a credible niche in green construction [3]. However, the financial profile is alarming: revenue declined -7.3% YoY in H1 2024, losses are widening at ~51% CAGR, debt-to-equity is 788%, and the market cap has collapsed to ~$8.6M [1][4].
The bear case dominates. FBS Global is a sub-$10M market cap micro-cap with negative earnings, shrinking revenue, extreme leverage (788% D/E), and daily trading volumes that often fall below the $1M liquidity threshold [4]. Post-IPO shareholders have lost 86% of capital in ~14 months. The core business is Singapore construction subcontracting — a fragmented, low-margin, labor-intensive trade with limited pricing power. The new 'intelligent building technologies' commercialization rights are promising on paper but require capital and operational scale the company does not possess. Singapore construction sector faces cyclical pressures and rising labor costs. For U.S. investors, this is a speculative micro-cap with governance, liquidity, and execution risks that collectively warrant a SELL recommendation until the company demonstrates sustained revenue growth, loss reduction, and balance sheet repair.
