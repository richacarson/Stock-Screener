Company Profile
Financial Services ·Banks - Regional ·NYSE ·United States
First BanCorp is the bank holding company for FirstBank Puerto Rico, providing consumer, commercial, mortgage, and treasury banking services across Puerto Rico, the U.S. Virgin Islands, and Florida. Founded in 1948 and headquartered in San Juan, it operates through six segments including mortgage banking, retail, commercial, treasury, U.S. operations, and Virgin Islands operations.

Investment Thesis
First BanCorp is a well-managed Puerto Rico-headquartered regional bank trading at an attractive 9.9x forward P/E with 19% ROE and 3.25% dividend yield [1][6]. Q1 2026 demonstrated strong execution: net income grew 21% YoY to $88.8M, pretax pre-provision income hit an all-time record of $131M, and management reiterated 3-5% loan growth guidance with NIM expansion of 2-3 bps per quarter [1][2]. The bank benefits from a geographic moat in Puerto Rico, where limited competition and regulatory barriers create sticky customer relationships. At 1.85x book value with earnings growth exceeding valuation, FBP offers a value proposition for income-oriented investors seeking Caribbean banking exposure.
The bear case centers on geographic concentration and structural growth limitations. Puerto Rico's population has declined roughly 12% since 2010, creating a shrinking domestic market that caps organic growth potential [1]. The island's ongoing fiscal crisis and PROMESA oversight board create macroeconomic uncertainty. The 5-year revenue CAGR of just 6% and PEG ratio of 4.15 suggest the stock may be fairly valued despite the low absolute P/E [6]. Florida expansion provides diversification but puts FirstBank in direct competition with much larger mainland banks. The lack of visible AI or digital transformation investment creates operational efficiency risk as fintech competitors deploy automated lending and servicing platforms. Interest rate sensitivity remains elevated — NIM compression in a falling rate environment could reverse recent earnings momentum [2]. No D/E ratio is available from yfinance, limiting full balance sheet assessment.
