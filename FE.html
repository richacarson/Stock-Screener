Investment Thesis
FirstEnergy delivered Q1 2026 operating EPS of $0.72 (up 7.5% YoY) and Q1 revenue of $4.2B (up 10.5% YoY), reaffirming full-year guidance of $2.62-$2.82 and signaling improving execution under CEO Tierney's leadership [1]. The $36B Energize365 program targeting 10% rate base CAGR through 2030 provides visible, multi-year earnings growth supported by 6-8% EPS CAGR guidance, with Q1 2026 capital deployment of $1.4B (up 33% YoY) and H1 2026 operating cash flow of $1.7B (up 60% YoY). At 17.0x forward P/E with a 3.9% dividend yield across 26 consecutive years of payments, the valuation is reasonable for a utility with improving fundamentals [5]. AI-driven electricity demand growth from data center buildout in FE's Ohio and Pennsylvania service territories provides a secular tailwind, with CEO Tierney explicitly citing AI as a demand driver [2].
The bear case is dominated by the HB6 bribery scandal's lingering overhang and balance sheet concerns. FirstEnergy paid a combined $580.7M in fines and restitution ($230M DOJ DPA, $100M SEC settlement, $250.7M PUCO penalty), and the March 2026 mistrial of former CEO Jones and SVP Dowling — with a September 2026 retrial scheduled and the U.S. Supreme Court declining to hear the appeal in May 2026 — keeps governance risk elevated [3]. D/E of 222% is very high even for utilities, and the $6B annual Energize365 capex generates negative free cash flow (FY2024 FCF was -$1.14B), creating refinancing risk [5]. The payout ratio of 96.5% is dangerously elevated — leaving virtually no retained earnings for debt service flexibility. While the dividend has been paid for 26 consecutive years and grown for 4 consecutive years with a 2.67% 5yr CAGR, the 2014 cut from $2.20 to $1.44 and the scandal-era disruptions create credibility concerns for income investors. Profit margin of 6.9% and Glassdoor ratings of just 2.7/5 (with culture and values at 2.3/5) signal ongoing organizational challenges [2][5].
