Company Profile
Financial Services ·Banks - Regional ·NYSE ·United States
First Horizon Corporation is a financial services holding company operating through its banking subsidiary First Horizon Bank, providing commercial banking, wealth management, and capital markets services. Founded in 1864 and headquartered in Memphis, Tennessee, the company operates 416 banking centers across 12 southeastern U.S. states with approximately $82 billion in assets.

Investment Thesis
First Horizon is a well-managed southeastern regional bank trading at 9.5x forward earnings with a 3.1% dividend yield, delivering 8.1% revenue growth that beat consensus by 3.2% — roughly double the regional bank average beat [1][2]. The 2026 outlook is compelling: 3-7% revenue growth with flat expenses, positive operating leverage, a $100M+ PPNR improvement opportunity, and ROTCE already at 15% in H2 2025 [1]. The $1.2B share repurchase program (~$1B remaining) adds meaningful capital return upside.
The bull case rests on First Horizon's strong southeastern franchise, reputation (No. 5 Most Reputable U.S. Bank), and disciplined execution under CEO Bryan Jordan's 15+ year tenure [3]. The bear case includes limited technology differentiation, post-merger cultural challenges, and the typical regional bank exposure to credit cycle deterioration and rate sensitivity [3]. At forward P/E of 9.5x with improving fundamentals and a 160-year operating history, FHN offers a reasonable risk-reward for income-oriented investors seeking quality regional bank exposure.
Key Catalysts
  1. 2026 PPNR improvement of $100M+ from balance sheet optimization and positive operating leverage, with 3-7% revenue growth on flat expenses [1]
  2. Share repurchase program with ~$1B remaining authorization, potentially retiring 9%+ of market cap at current prices, supplementing the 3.1% dividend yield [1]
  3. Mid-single-digit loan growth in the growing southeastern U.S. footprint, supported by favorable demographic and business migration trends [1]
Key Risks
  1. Credit cycle deterioration — a southeastern economic slowdown or commercial real estate stress could drive provision increases that offset operating leverage gains [1]
  2. Interest rate sensitivity — the 3-7% revenue guidance range reflects uncertainty around the rate path; a faster-than-expected rate decline could compress net interest margin [1]
  3. Post-TD merger cultural healing remains incomplete — mixed Glassdoor reviews citing 'fear culture' and excessive overtime could impact talent retention and operational quality [3]

AI Resilience (25%)
AI Resilience NEUTRAL
6
AI presents both opportunities and moderate threats to First Horizon's regional banking model. On the opportunity side, AI-driven credit underwriting, fraud detection, and customer service automation could improve efficiency — especially as the bank guides flat expenses with growing revenue [1]. The threat is that fintech competitors and large-bank AI investments could erode First Horizon's competitive position in lending, payments, and wealth management over time. However, the bank's relationship-based southeastern footprint, physical branch network, and community trust provide insulation against pure digital disruptors. First Horizon has not publicized a formal AI strategy, which is a concern given peers' investments in the technology [3].
