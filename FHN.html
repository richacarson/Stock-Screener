Company Profile
Financial Services ·Banks - Regional ·NYSE ·United States
First Horizon Corporation is a financial services holding company providing banking, wealth management, and capital markets services through its subsidiary First Horizon Bank. Operating primarily across the southeastern United States with ~400 banking locations, the company offers commercial and consumer banking, mortgage lending, trust and investment management, and fixed-income securities. Founded in 1864 and headquartered in Memphis, Tennessee, First Horizon has a market cap of $12.1 billion.

Investment Thesis
First Horizon delivered a strong Q1 2026 with $257M net income (up 21% YoY), EPS of $0.53 (beating estimates of $0.49), and ROTCE above 15% for the third consecutive quarter [1]. Net interest income of $667M grew 6% with NIM expanding to 3.52%, while non-interest income rose 7% driven by service charges, mortgage banking, and fee income. The bank reaffirmed full-year 2026 guidance for 3-7% revenue growth with flat expenses, projecting EPS of $2.13 for 2026 and $2.19 for 2027. The $100M technology investment including ChatFHN AI deployment, combined with the $1.2B share buyback program and 13.3% dividend increase to $0.68/share, signals management confidence in the standalone growth trajectory post-TD Bank deal [2].
First Horizon's 5-year revenue CAGR of approximately -0.2% and 5-year dividend growth CAGR of just 0.34% expose a bank that has essentially treaded water for half a decade through the disruption of the failed TD Bank acquisition [1]. At $12.1B market cap, FHN is caught between national banks with scale advantages and community banks with local nimbleness — the 'super-regional' model faces structural questions about long-term viability. The 2.66% dividend yield, while adequate, lacks the growth trajectory of true Dividend Aristocrats. CEO Jordan's net insider selling of $2.3M across 29 transactions with zero purchases over the past 6 months undermines the bullish narrative [3]. Rate sensitivity remains a risk — the expanded NIM could compress if the Fed eases aggressively, and southeastern real estate exposure adds cyclical vulnerability.
Key Catalysts
  1. Continued NIM expansion above 3.52% as the rate environment and loan repricing drive net interest income growth toward the high end of 3-7% full-year guidance
  2. Successful execution of the $1.2B share buyback program reducing shares outstanding and boosting EPS, building on the 6.29% reduction already achieved YoY
  3. ChatFHN and $100M technology investment delivering measurable cost savings and customer acquisition improvements, differentiating First Horizon from technology-lagging regional peers
Key Risks
  1. Aggressive Fed rate cuts could compress the 3.52% NIM that is driving earnings growth, reversing the net interest income momentum that produced 21% net income growth
  2. The super-regional banking model faces structural competitive pressure from both national banks with scale and technology advantages and fintech disruptors targeting profitable retail banking segments
  3. Near-flat 5-year revenue CAGR of -0.2% suggests the bank has not yet proven sustainable organic growth independent of rate-cycle tailwinds, and the post-TD Bank rebuild may have front-loaded easy gains
