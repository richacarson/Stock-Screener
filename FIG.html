Company Profile
Technology ·Software - Application ·NYSE ·United States
Figma, Inc. is the leading collaborative design platform used by 95% of Fortune 500 companies and 13 million monthly active users. Founded in 2012 by Dylan Field and Evan Wallace, Figma provides a browser-based interface for product design, prototyping, and development handoff, and went public in July 2025 in the largest U.S. VC-backed tech IPO in four years.

Investment Thesis
Figma is the rare combination of dominant market position (95% Fortune 500 penetration, 136% NDR), high growth (41% revenue growth in FY2025, 30% guided in 2026), zero debt, and $1.7B cash — entering an AI monetization phase where 75% of enterprise customers are already consuming AI credits weekly[2]. The company crossed $1B in ARR for the first time in 2025 and is guiding to $1.37B in 2026, with analyst consensus buy ratings and a $50.50 price target representing 113% upside from the current ~$23 price after post-IPO volatility. The AI credits monetization beginning in March 2026 is not yet reflected in guidance and represents a meaningful upside catalyst.
The bear case centers on valuation risk after the post-IPO euphoria — the stock fell from $140 to $23, reflecting real investor concerns about P/S multiple compression as growth normalizes from 41% toward 30% and eventually lower. Gross margin is structurally declining (92% to 86%) as AI inference costs scale with usage, which could accelerate if AI credit pricing disappoints. The $975.7M IPO stock compensation expense creates GAAP losses that confuse accounting-based investors, and any slowdown in NDR from the 136% peak would signal enterprise budget pressure.
Key Catalysts
  1. AI credits monetization at scale — 75% of enterprise customers already consuming credits weekly means any meaningful pricing creates immediate ARR acceleration not yet reflected in FY2026 guidance
  2. Dev Mode and Slides product suite expansion driving Figma from 'design tool' to 'product development platform,' increasing per-seat pricing and expanding the total addressable market beyond designers
  3. International revenue acceleration — international grew 45% YoY in Q4 2025 versus 40% total, with significant underpenetration in Europe and Asia suggesting a multi-year runway of geographic expansion
Key Risks
  1. AI-generated design tools (Midjourney, Adobe Firefly, OpenAI's Sora) reducing the need for human designers and therefore Figma's core user base over a 3-5 year horizon as generative AI matures
  2. Gross margin structural decline from AI inference costs — if costs grow faster than AI credit pricing, the 12% non-GAAP operating margin could compress further, raising questions about the path to higher profitability
  3. Post-IPO lockup expiration creating significant insider selling pressure — early investors and employees received shares at $33 IPO price versus the $140 spike, and the sell-down could create persistent overhang
