Company Profile
Financial Services ·Capital Markets ·NASDAQ ·United States
Figure Technology Solutions is a financial technology company that develops blockchain-based products and solutions for lending, trading, and capital markets. The company operates Figure Connect, a distribution marketplace for consumer credit and digital assets, and owns Provenance Blockchain, which holds approximately 75% market share in tokenized private credit. Products include HELOCs, cash-out refinance, DSCR loans, the YLDS SEC-registered yield-bearing stablecoin, and Democratized Prime on-chain financing.

Investment Thesis
Figure Technology Solutions is building the infrastructure layer for blockchain-based capital markets, with 75% market share in tokenized private credit and explosive growth — Q4 2025 marketplace volume of $2.7B (+131% YoY), full-year adjusted net revenue of $506.9M, and $133.9M net income [1]. At 24.4x forward P/E with 99% revenue growth and 31% profit margins trending toward management's 60%+ EBITDA margin target, Figure is reasonably valued for its growth trajectory. The 2026 product roadmap — first-lien mortgages, auto finance via Agora, and the OPEN network replacing DTC clearing — provides multiple expansion vectors [1]. The $200M buyback program and IPO proceeds of $787.5M provide capital flexibility [1][3].
The bear case centers on execution risk and competitive threats. Figure's blockchain infrastructure thesis requires institutional adoption at scale — if traditional finance firms build competing tokenization platforms (JPMorgan's Onyx, Goldman's GS DAP), Figure's first-mover advantage could erode [2]. The January 2026 data breach exposing nearly 1 million customers' SSNs and personal data represents a significant reputational and legal risk, with class-action lawsuits and ShinyHunters claiming responsibility [3]. Founder Mike Cagney's departure from SoFi amid harassment allegations creates ongoing governance scrutiny [3]. Revenue concentration in HELOCs makes Figure sensitive to interest rate movements and housing market conditions. The stock has declined 25% from IPO highs, reflecting market skepticism about blockchain-fintech premiums in a rising rate environment [4].
Key Catalysts
  1. First-lien mortgage expansion in 2026, potentially opening the largest consumer lending market ($12T outstanding) to Figure's blockchain marketplace [1]
  2. OPEN network launch aiming to replace DTC clearing infrastructure, which if adopted could dramatically expand Figure's addressable market from consumer lending to all securities settlement [1]
  3. Democratized Prime on-chain financing marketplace growing from $20M to $200B+ in matched offers, with 1,000+ active participants indicating network effect acceleration [1]
Key Risks
  1. January 2026 data breach affecting nearly 1 million customers exposed SSNs, emails, and loan details — ShinyHunters claimed responsibility and class-action lawsuits have been filed, creating material legal and reputational liability [3]
  2. Institutional adoption risk — blockchain-based capital markets require banks, asset managers, and regulators to change entrenched workflows, and competing tokenization platforms from JPMorgan and Goldman Sachs could limit Figure's TAM [2]
  3. HELOC concentration and interest rate sensitivity — rising rates compress HELOC demand and home equity values, potentially slowing Figure's core marketplace volume [4]
