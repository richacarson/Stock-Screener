Company Profile
Financial Services ·Capital Markets ·NASDAQ ·United States
Figure Technology Solutions operates a blockchain-native capital markets platform connecting loan origination, funding, and secondary market trading. Founded in 2018 by ex-SoFi CEO Mike Cagney, the company provides consumer and institutional lending products including HELOCs, cash-out refinance, DSCR loans, and crypto-backed loans through its Figure Connect consumer credit marketplace with 160+ lending partners. The company IPO'd in September 2025 at $25/share and reported $506.9M in FY2025 revenue, growing 81% year-over-year. Figure's Provenance Blockchain serves as the infrastructure layer for origination, custody, and trading of financial assets.
500 Employees ·www.figure.com

Investment Thesis
Figure Technology Solutions at a PEG ratio of 0.55 and Forward P/E of 26.57x offers a rare growth-at-a-reasonable-price opportunity in blockchain-native fintech [4]. Revenue surged 81% in FY2025 to $506.9M and accelerated to 98% YoY growth in Q1 2026, with Consumer Loan Marketplace volume reaching $2.9B in Q1 alone — up 113% YoY — demonstrating that demand for the platform is expanding, not decelerating [1][2]. The 35.2% profit margin and 21.67% ROE prove this is not a growth-at-any-cost story — Figure is scaling profitably with $107.8M in LTM net income less than a year after IPO [4]. The 160+ lending partner network on Figure Connect creates compounding network effects: more partners attract more borrowers, which attracts more capital providers, reinforcing the platform's dominance in blockchain-native lending [3]. The $200M share repurchase authorization announced alongside FY2025 results signals management confidence in the stock's value at current levels [2].
The bear case centers on valuation, leverage, and regulatory uncertainty for a blockchain-centric business model. At 46.48x trailing earnings, the stock prices in substantial continued hyper-growth — any deceleration below 50% revenue growth would compress the multiple sharply, and the company has minimal history as a public entity to establish credibility with institutional investors [4]. D/E of 102% reflects a lending platform that holds loans on balance sheet, creating credit cycle exposure — a recession or housing downturn could simultaneously impair loan quality and restrict funding access, as happened to multiple fintech lenders in 2022-2023. The blockchain thesis, while differentiated, faces regulatory headwinds: SEC scrutiny of tokenized securities, evolving state lending regulations, and potential CFPB enforcement actions could disrupt operations or require costly compliance investments. Finally, Figure's 500-person workforce and $5.9B market cap create key-person risk — the company remains heavily dependent on founder Mike Cagney's vision and relationships, and his prior departure from SoFi amid controversy introduces governance uncertainty [3].
Key Catalysts
  1. Consumer Loan Marketplace volume acceleration — Q1 2026 volume of $2.9B (+113% YoY) suggests the network effect flywheel is strengthening as more lending partners join the platform, potentially driving revenue growth above 80% for full-year 2026 [1]
  2. Institutional adoption of Provenance Blockchain beyond Figure's own lending products — third-party asset managers, banks, or government entities tokenizing assets on Provenance would validate the infrastructure thesis and create a high-margin, recurring revenue stream independent of lending cycles [3]
  3. Expansion into new lending verticals and geographies leveraging the existing 160+ partner network and blockchain infrastructure, with DSCR loans and crypto-backed lending still in early scaling phases [3]
Key Risks
  1. Credit cycle exposure — as a lending platform with $15B+ in originated loans, a recession or housing downturn could impair asset quality and restrict warehouse funding lines, particularly for the HELOC and DSCR loan portfolios that depend on home values [2][3]
  2. Regulatory risk for blockchain-native financial services — SEC scrutiny of tokenized securities, evolving state lending regulations, and potential classification of Provenance Blockchain tokens could require structural changes to the business model or create material compliance costs [3]
  3. Concentration and key-person risk — the company has ~500 employees, is less than a year into its public market life, and remains heavily dependent on founder Mike Cagney's strategic vision and institutional relationships, with limited operating history through an economic downturn [3]

AI Resilience (25%)
AI Resilience MODERATE
7
AI Risk Protection (6/9): Figure's core business sits at the intersection of lending, capital markets, and blockchain infrastructure — a positioning that carries moderate AI disruption risk with meaningful structural defenses [3]. The blockchain infrastructure layer (Provenance) provides AI-resistant properties: immutable ledgers, smart contract execution, and cryptographic verification are complementary to rather than replaceable by AI systems. The 160+ partner network on Figure Connect creates multi-sided network effects that are difficult for an AI-first competitor to replicate without building equivalent marketplace liquidity [1][3]. However, the lending and underwriting components face real AI disruption risk — competitors like Upstart and SoFi are deploying increasingly sophisticated AI credit models that could offer faster decisioning and tighter spreads. Traditional lenders investing in AI could narrow Figure's speed advantage in loan origination. The company's moat is ultimately the integrated blockchain infrastructure, not any single lending product. AI Opportunity (1/1): Figure actively deploys AI and machine learning models across its credit underwriting, fraud detection, and marketplace matching algorithms [3]. The Provenance Blockchain's data layer — with $15B+ in originated loans creating a proprietary training dataset — positions Figure to build increasingly accurate credit models over time. AI enhances rather than threatens the blockchain infrastructure thesis.
