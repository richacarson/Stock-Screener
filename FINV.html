Investment Thesis
FinVolution trades at a remarkably cheap 3.9x trailing P/E and 3.8x forward P/E with a 5.6% dividend yield, RMB 7.3B cash against minimal debt, and 15.7% ROE. The AI-powered credit platform serving 216M+ users has a genuine technology moat (98% fraud detection accuracy), and international revenue growing 32% YoY to 25% of total revenue provides a diversification hedge against China regulatory headwinds. The $182M in total shareholder returns (dividends + buybacks) in 2025 demonstrates capital allocation discipline.
The bear case is dominated by China regulatory risk — the company guided for 5-15% revenue decline in 2026 as new internet lending regulations tighten standards and compress margins. Q4 2025 showed deteriorating metrics: China 30-day collection rates fell from 88% to 86%, and transaction volumes declined 2.9% YoY. The VIE structure and Chinese ADR status expose investors to delisting risk and capital controls. Philippine interest rate caps and emerging market expansion into Pakistan create new regulatory complexities. The Inspire score of 0 (no data) limits faith alignment assessment.
