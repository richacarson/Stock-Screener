Company Profile
Financial Services ·Credit Services ·NYSE ·China
FinVolution Group is a leading fintech company connecting consumers and SMEs with financial institutions across China, Indonesia, the Philippines, Pakistan, and Australia. Founded in 2007 as PPDAI Group (a P2P lending platform), the company has evolved into an AI-driven credit technology platform with 223.6 million cumulative registered users and operations spanning five countries.

Investment Thesis
FinVolution is a deep value fintech opportunity trading at just 3.8x forward earnings with a clean balance sheet (D/E 7.9%), 18.7% profit margins, and a 15.7% ROE — metrics that would command a much higher multiple in any other geography [1][4]. Full-year 2025 revenue grew 3.8% to RMB 13.6B with net profit up 6.6% to RMB 2.5B despite China regulatory headwinds [1]. International operations grew 38.6% in transaction volume and now represent 31% of quarterly revenue, with a target of 50% by 2030, progressively de-risking the China concentration [1][3]. The $107M share buyback (8% of market cap) and $0.306/ADS dividend (10.5% YoY increase) provide aggressive capital return, and the Australian market entry via Fundo acquisition adds a developed-market growth vector [1].
The bear case is dominated by China regulatory risk — 2026 revenue is guided to decline 5-15% as regulatory tightening continues to compress domestic lending volumes [1]. The company's P2P lending origins (PPDAI), where the broader Chinese P2P industry was shut down due to predatory practices including 36-55% effective interest rates, creates permanent reputational and regulatory overhang [5]. The Philippines interest rate cap effective April 2026 adds a second regulatory headwind in a key growth market [1]. At $5.48/share with a $1.35B market cap, FINV trades like a distressed equity, and the deep discount reflects legitimate concerns about Chinese ADR delisting risk, VIE structure uncertainty, and the potential for further government intervention in consumer lending [4][5]. International expansion, while promising, is capital-intensive and unproven at scale in developed markets.
