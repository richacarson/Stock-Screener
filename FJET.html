Company Profile
Industrials ·Aerospace & Defense ·NYSE American ·United States
Starfighters Space, Inc. operates a commercial fleet of flight-ready F-104 supersonic aircraft for pilot and astronaut training, in-flight testing, and launch services. The company provides airborne testbed services for hypersonic R&D and is developing the STARLAUNCH air-launch system for small satellite delivery to orbit. Customers include Lockheed Martin, GE Aerospace, Space Florida, and the U.S. Air Force Research Laboratory.

Investment Thesis
Starfighters Space occupies a genuinely unique niche — the only commercial operator of sustained Mach 2+ aircraft with customers including Lockheed Martin, GE Aerospace, and the USAF Research Laboratory [2]. The STARLAUNCH air-launch system and Blackstar partnership for reusable hypersonic systems represent optionality on the growing commercial space and hypersonic testing markets. At $237M market cap, the stock up 85% YTD at one point, the market is pricing in substantial future revenue from government demand and commercial space expansion [1].
The bear case is overwhelming at this stage. FJET is a pre-revenue company with -$6.9M net income, $11.3M debt against only $3.9M cash, and zero disclosed revenue pipeline with timelines [1]. The founder's resignation in February 2026 over fundamental disagreements with the board, combined with Johnson Fistel's investor investigation, signal serious governance risk [3][4]. The stock has fallen from an all-time high of $31.50 (December 2025) to $5.39, an 83% decline. Seeking Alpha analysis labeled it 'a hypersonic and space launch hype to sell' [4]. Without revenue generation, cash runway is limited and dilution is likely. The F-104 fleet (1950s-era aircraft) represents aging infrastructure with maintenance risk. This is a speculative micro-cap with existential execution risk.
