Financial Services ·Banks - Regional ·NYSE ·United States
Flagstar Bank, N.A. (formerly New York Community Bancorp/Flagstar Financial) is a regional bank headquartered in Hicksville, New York, offering commercial and consumer banking products including multifamily and CRE lending, C&I loans, mortgage origination, and deposit services. The bank nearly collapsed in early 2024 due to CRE losses and received a $1.05B emergency equity infusion led by Steven Mnuchin's Liberty Strategic Capital.

Investment Thesis
Flagstar Bank returned to profitability in Q4 2025 ($0.05 diluted EPS) after a tumultuous 2024 that saw a near-collapse, $2.4B impairment charge, and $1.05B emergency equity injection [1][3]. Trading at 0.72x book value ($13.17 vs $18.37 book) and 8.0x forward earnings, FLG offers deep value if the Otting-led turnaround executes — C&I loan originations surged 41% QoQ, net interest margin improved for three consecutive quarters, and the bank achieved investment-grade ratings in March 2026 [1]. Revenue grew 19% YoY to $1.88B from a depressed base, and 13 analysts have an average 'Buy' rating with a $14.62 target (12% upside) [1]. The plan to hire 100 commercial bankers and build a diversified regional bank franchise has credible leadership in Otting (former Comptroller of the Currency) and Mnuchin-backed financial support.
The bear case is substantial for a bank still recovering from existential crisis. Non-performing loans continue growing even as the balance sheet shrinks, signaling that CRE credit losses are not yet fully resolved [3]. The CRE portfolio still contains $38.3B in exposure (down from $50.6B) with particular vulnerability to NYC rent-regulated multifamily properties whose values collapsed after 2019 rent reform legislation [3]. The dividend has been effectively eliminated ($0.04 annual vs $2.04 pre-crisis), and operating cash flow is negative at -$181M [4]. Governance scandals including discriminatory lending findings by the OCC, whistleblower retaliation allegations, and executive misconduct complaints create reputational and legal risk [3]. The bank's institutional identity remains unsettled after three name changes in two years, and the concentration of insider ownership from the Mnuchin-led rescue creates principal-agent tensions. Profitability at $0.05 EPS quarterly remains fragile — one bad CRE quarter could reverse the recovery trajectory.
Key Catalysts
  1. Investment-grade rating upgrade (March 2026) reduces funding costs and expands the depositor and counterparty base, supporting NIM expansion and deposit growth [1]
  2. C&I loan growth acceleration — originations up 41% QoQ with plans to hire 100 commercial bankers, diversifying away from the CRE-heavy legacy portfolio [1]
  3. 0.72x price-to-book discount implies $5+ per share of embedded value if ROE normalizes to peer levels of 8-10% [5]
Key Risks
  1. CRE credit deterioration — $38.3B in remaining CRE exposure with growing non-performing loans, particularly in NYC rent-regulated multifamily properties affected by 2019 rent reforms [3]
  2. Governance and legal risk — OCC discriminatory lending findings, whistleblower retaliation lawsuit, and executive misconduct allegations create ongoing regulatory and litigation exposure [3]
  3. Turnaround execution risk — profitability at $0.05 quarterly EPS is razor-thin, operating cash flow is negative, and the bank has changed names three times and CFOs twice in two years [1][2]
