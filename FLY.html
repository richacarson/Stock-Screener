Company Profile
Industrials ·Aerospace & Defense ·NASDAQ ·United States
Firefly Aerospace is a space and defense technology company providing integrated launch and space services for national security, government, and commercial customers. Products include the Alpha responsive launch vehicle, Eclipse medium-lift rocket, Blue Ghost lunar delivery platform, Elytra space maneuverability craft, and Ocula lunar imaging service. The company went public via IPO in 2025.

Investment Thesis
Firefly Aerospace is a rare pure-play space company with validated capabilities across launch (Alpha), lunar delivery (Blue Ghost), and defense — posting record 2025 revenue of $159.9M (up 163% YoY) with gross margin inflecting to 19.2% from gross loss the prior year [1]. Management guides $420-450M revenue for 2026 (172% growth) driven by expanded Alpha launches, Blue Ghost lunar missions, Eclipse medium-lift development, and growing national security contracts [1]. With $893M cash, conservative D/E of 25.9%, and a multi-year runway, Firefly has the financial resources to execute on its ambitious pipeline. The U.S. Space Force rapid-launch wins and $5.6B market cap position Firefly as an emerging challenger in the $400B+ global space economy [1].
The bear case centers on execution risk and valuation. The $334M net loss and $237.8M free cash burn in 2025 shows Firefly is years away from profitability, with 2026 guidance requiring near-flawless execution on multiple simultaneous programs [1]. Trading at 34.9x trailing sales, the stock prices in years of hypergrowth with zero margin for error. The Alpha rocket has a limited launch track record, and any launch failure could devastate customer confidence and stock price. Competition from SpaceX (Falcon 9, Starship), Rocket Lab (Electron, Neutron), and Blue Origin (New Glenn) is intense, with SpaceX's Starship potentially making small-lift vehicles economically obsolete [4]. Insider ownership of 21% means potential future dilution or selling pressure. The stock has fallen 42% from its 52-week high of $73.80, suggesting the market has cooled on the hypergrowth narrative [4].
Key Catalysts
  1. Execution on $420-450M 2026 revenue guidance (172% YoY growth) through expanded Alpha launches and Blue Ghost lunar missions would validate the growth trajectory [1]
  2. U.S. Space Force and national security contract wins expanding Firefly's defense backlog and creating sticky, high-margin government revenue [1]
  3. Eclipse medium-lift vehicle development milestones creating a pathway to the larger addressable market currently dominated by SpaceX Falcon 9 and ULA Vulcan [1]
Key Risks
  1. Any Alpha or Blue Ghost mission failure could severely damage customer confidence, contract pipeline, and stock price in a company with limited operational history [1]
  2. Cash burn of $237.8M in 2025 with continued capital-intensive development requiring potential future equity raises that would dilute existing shareholders [4]
  3. SpaceX Starship disruption risk — if Starship achieves routine low-cost launch, it could render small-lift vehicles like Alpha economically uncompetitive [1]
