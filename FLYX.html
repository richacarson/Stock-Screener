flyExclusive, Inc. is a vertically integrated private jet operator offering jet charter, fractional ownership, jet club membership, aircraft management, and maintenance services. Founded in 2015 by Jim Segrave and based in Kinston, North Carolina, the company operates one of the largest private jet fleets in North America.

Investment Thesis
Bull case: flyExclusive is transitioning from growth-at-any-cost to operational discipline. FY2025 revenue of $376M grew 15% on 14% fewer aircraft, net loss narrowed to $67M, and Q4 posted positive EBITDA [1]. 2026 guidance targets full-year EBITDA profitability, 15% flight hour growth to a 100k annualized run rate, ~20 aircraft additions, and $25M annualized improvement from dispatch reliability gains [1]. Founder-CEO Jim Segrave has strengthened the executive bench (new CFO Brad Garner, COO Matt Lesmeister) and the vertically integrated MRO/FBO model differentiates flyExclusive from pure-charter peers [2].
Bear case: flyExclusive has significantly negative stockholder equity with short-term liabilities of $275.9M and long-term of $250.9M, making the infrastructure score effectively 0 by IOWN's methodology [3]. The January 2026 dilutive secondary offering underwritten by Lucid Capital Markets sent the stock down 14%, reflecting ongoing capital structure fragility [3]. Glassdoor 2.7/5 with specific employee reports of 'constant threats from managers' and only 39% willing to recommend working there is a serious culture red flag [2]. Private aviation demand is cyclical and tightly correlated with high-net-worth wealth effects; any equity market correction would compress fractional and jet card sales. Competition from NetJets, Flexjet and Wheels Up is well-capitalized.
Key Catalysts
  1. Full-year 2026 EBITDA profitability achieved vs. 2025 $67M net loss would re-rate the stock [1]
