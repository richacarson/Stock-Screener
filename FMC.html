Company Profile
Basic Materials ·Agricultural Inputs ·NYSE ·United States
FMC Corporation is a global agricultural sciences company focused on crop protection solutions including insecticides, herbicides, fungicides, and biologicals. Key products include Rynaxypyr (diamide insecticide, now off-patent) and a pipeline of new active ingredients including Fluindapyr, Isoflex, and Dodhylex. The company is exploring strategic alternatives including a potential sale.

Investment Thesis
FMC offers deep value at 7.6x forward earnings with a potential turnaround catalyst: 2026 guidance of $3.6-$3.8B revenue and $1.63-$1.89 adjusted EPS would represent a meaningful recovery from the -$17.59 trailing EPS disaster [1]. New active ingredient sales are expected to grow 75%+ to $300-$400M in 2026, diversifying beyond the post-patent Rynaxypyr franchise. The $1B planned debt reduction via asset sales improves balance sheet flexibility. The board's exploration of strategic alternatives including a potential sale provides takeout optionality — FMC's global regulatory registrations, manufacturing infrastructure, and new AI pipeline make it an attractive acquisition target for BASF, Syngenta, or private equity. At $17.15, the stock trades at a 70% discount to its 2022 peak near $57 [4].
The bear case is formidable. FMC has destroyed enormous shareholder value — revenue declined 40% from $5.8B to $3.5B in three years, trailing losses of -$17.59 EPS dwarf the $17.15 stock price, and ROE of -66.6% reflects a business in crisis [4]. D/E of 200% creates financial fragility, and the 86% dividend cut (from $2.32 to $0.32) eliminated the income thesis that attracted many holders. The 2026 guidance of $3.6-$3.8B revenue is far below the 2023 Investor Day target of $5.5-$6.0B, suggesting management has lost credibility on forward projections [1]. Generic competition for Rynaxypyr will intensify, compressing margins on what remains FMC's largest product franchise. The restructuring requires $1B in asset sales/licensing that may not achieve fair value in a distressed context. A potential sale could occur at a price well below historical intrinsic value, locking in permanent capital destruction for long-term holders.
