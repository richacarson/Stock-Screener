Floor & Decor is a high-growth specialty retailer of hard-surface flooring (tile, wood, laminate, vinyl, stone) operating 270 warehouse-format stores across 38 U.S. states. Investment Thesis
Floor & Decor is a category-killing specialty retailer with a multi-year runway of store expansion (20 new stores planned in 2026, targeting 500+ long-term) taking share in a highly fragmented hard-surface flooring market [1]. FY2025 delivered $4.684B in sales (5.1% growth) and diluted EPS of $1.92 despite a challenging housing/remodeling backdrop — a testament to the warehouse format's pro loyalty and DIY appeal [1]. FY2026 guidance of $4.88-$5.03B sales (4-7% growth) and $1.98-$2.18 EPS represents continued expansion even with comparable sales guidance of -2% to +1% reflecting softer macro [1]. Founder-inspired culture under new CEO Bradley Paulsen (former President) maintains the category-killer focus, and the physical, pro-oriented model is AI-resilient.
The bear case is valuation meets macro. At ~$51 and 26.6x earnings, FND trades at a premium to market for ~5% revenue growth and low single-digit EPS growth in 2026 [4]. A prolonged housing slowdown, persistent high mortgage rates, or weakening consumer spending would compress comparable sales further. The California PAGA labor class action creates financial and reputational risk if it expands [3]. Transition from Tom Taylor (CEO 2012-2025) to Bradley Paulsen, while carefully managed, introduces execution risk on the ambitious store expansion plan [2]. Home Depot and Lowe's have more sophisticated omnichannel/digital capabilities and could eventually erode FND's specialty advantage. No dividend means investors rely entirely on capital appreciation for returns. The combination of premium multiple, slow growth, and housing market uncertainty merits a HOLD rather than BUY at current price.
