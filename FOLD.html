Company Profile
Healthcare ·Biotechnology ·NASDAQ ·United States
Amicus Therapeutics is a patient-dedicated biotechnology company focused on discovering, developing, and delivering novel medicines for rare diseases. Key products include Galafold for Fabry disease and Pombiliti + Opfolda for late-onset Pompe disease. The company is being acquired by BioMarin Pharmaceutical for $14.50 per share in a transaction expected to close Q2 2026.

Investment Thesis
Amicus Therapeutics is a rare disease biotech delivering accelerating revenue growth — full-year 2025 revenue of $634.2M grew 17% at CER with Pombiliti + Opfolda surging 42% and Galafold contributing $521.7M (14% growth) [1]. The company is approaching profitability with Q4 2025 net income of $1.7M and forward P/E of 15.7x, attractively valued for a biotech with 24% revenue growth and a patient-dedicated mission rooted in the founder's personal experience [1][2]. The BioMarin acquisition at $14.50/share provides a near-term floor, and the DMX-200 pipeline expansion into FSGS kidney disease broadens the therapeutic platform. At current prices near the acquisition offer, downside is limited while the rare disease portfolio continues to grow.
The bear case is dominated by acquisition dynamics and fundamental growth sustainability. The pending BioMarin acquisition at $14.50/share caps upside — investors are effectively buying a merger arbitrage spread rather than a growth story, with closing expected Q2 2026 subject to regulatory approval [1]. If the deal breaks, the stock faces significant downside given negative ROE (-11.6%), negative profit margin (-4.3%), and D/E of 161.3% [4]. The company reported a full-year 2025 loss of $27.1M despite $634M revenue, highlighting that profitability remains elusive even at scale. Product concentration risk is acute — Galafold and Pombiliti + Opfolda account for virtually all revenue, serving ultra-rare patient populations where market size is inherently limited. AI-driven drug discovery competitors could develop next-generation rare disease therapies that challenge Amicus's current products [2].
Key Catalysts
  1. BioMarin acquisition closing expected Q2 2026 at $14.50/share, providing near-term value realization for shareholders [1]
  2. Pombiliti + Opfolda revenue acceleration — 42% growth at CER in Q3 2025 with continued global launch driving Pompe disease market expansion [1]
  3. DMX-200 Phase 3 results for FSGS kidney disease could validate pipeline diversification beyond lysosomal storage disorders [2]
Key Risks
  1. BioMarin acquisition regulatory or shareholder approval failure would remove the $14.50/share floor, exposing downside given ongoing operating losses and high leverage [1]
  2. Product concentration in Galafold (82% of 2025 revenue) creates acute dependency on a single rare disease therapy with limited addressable patient population [1]
  3. Persistent negative profitability — full-year 2025 loss of $27.1M despite $634M revenue — raises questions about the business model's ability to generate sustainable earnings at scale [1]
