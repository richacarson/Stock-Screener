Amicus Therapeutics is a biotechnology company focused on discovering, developing, and delivering treatments for rare diseases. Key products include Galafold (migalastat) for Fabry disease and Pombiliti + Opfolda (cipaglucosidase alfa-atga + miglustat) for Pompe disease. Investment Thesis
Amicus Therapeutics has been acquired by BioMarin Pharmaceutical for $14.50 per share in an all-cash transaction valued at $4.8B, with the deal completing in Q2 2026 [2]. The stock trades near the acquisition price, offering minimal upside but validating the value of the rare disease franchise. Prior to acquisition, Amicus demonstrated strong commercial execution with 2025 revenue of $634M (up 20% YoY), Galafold reaching $522M in sales (up 14%), and Pombiliti + Opfolda gaining meaningful Pompe market share [1]. The company was approaching GAAP profitability with losses narrowing 52% to just $27M in 2025, and revenue was forecast to grow 17%+ annually.
With the BioMarin acquisition complete, FOLD stock offers no meaningful investment thesis as a standalone equity. The $14.50/share price represents a modest premium to the pre-announcement share price but arguably undervalues a franchise with $634M revenue growing 20% with a clear path to profitability [1]. For investors who held through the acquisition, the deal crystallized gains but eliminated exposure to what could have been a rare disease compounder. The primary risk is deal completion failure (now resolved) and the broader concern that rare disease biotechs of Amicus's size remain vulnerable to acquisition at premiums that undervalue long-term franchise potential.
Key Catalysts
  1. BioMarin acquisition complete at $14.50/share, providing certain liquidity to shareholders — limited further upside from this position
  2. Post-acquisition, BioMarin's global commercial infrastructure could accelerate Galafold and Pombiliti + Opfolda revenue growth beyond Amicus's standalone capabilities
  3. Gene therapy pipeline for Fabry and Pompe diseases may advance faster with BioMarin's development resources and gene therapy expertise
Key Risks
  1. Stock trades at acquisition price ($14.50) with no meaningful upside — this is an investment thesis that has been fully realized through the buyout
  2. Pre-acquisition, Amicus had negative ROE (-6.9%) and profit margin (-2.3%), indicating the business model had not yet proven sustainable profitability as a standalone entity
  3. Rare disease competitive landscape evolving rapidly with gene therapy approaches from Sarepta, BioMarin itself, and others potentially displacing enzyme replacement therapies over the next decade
