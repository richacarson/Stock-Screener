Investment Thesis
Shift4 Payments is one of the fastest-growing payment processors in the US, now going global through the transformative 2025 Global Blue acquisition. 2025 revenue less network fees grew 46% to $1.98B, adjusted EBITDA +43% to $970M (49% margin), and 2026 guidance calls for 26-31% GRLNF growth with 47% EBITDA margin and $5.50-5.70 non-GAAP EPS. [1] Global Blue adds a #1 market-share tax-free-shopping business with 4x lead over competitors and high-margin take rates, plus 80k+ international merchants. Forward P/E of 6.7 is compelling for a 30%+ compounder.
Bear case: GAAP profit margin collapsed to 2.8% and trailing EPS was -72% YoY due to Global Blue integration costs and interest burden (D/E 236%). Founder Jared Isaacman's June 2025 transition to Executive Chairman (he was also briefly the NASA Administrator nominee) puts new CEO Taylor Lauber to the test in his first full year. Payments competition from Toast (restaurants), Block, and Stripe remains intense. If Global Blue integration stumbles or international take rates compress, the high-growth narrative breaks.
Key Catalysts
  1. Global Blue integration delivering targeted $2.5-2.6B GRLNF in 2026 with international revenue doubling
  2. All-in-one terminal launch across 15 countries in 2026 expanding addressable SMB merchant base
  3. Adjusted free cash flow scaling from $500M 2025 toward $1B+ enabling deleveraging
Key Risks
  1. High D/E of 236% from Global Blue acquisition constrains balance-sheet flexibility in a recession
  2. New CEO Taylor Lauber's execution in first full year vs. founder Isaacman
  3. Competitive pressure from Toast, Block, Stripe compressing take rates below 60bps floor
