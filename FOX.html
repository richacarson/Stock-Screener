Company Profile

Fox Corporation operates as a news, sports, and entertainment company through four segments: Cable Network Programming (Fox News, Fox Business, Fox Sports), Television (FOX broadcast network, Tubi AVOD streaming), Credible (consumer finance marketplace), and The FOX Studio Lot (production services). The company was spun off from 21st Century Fox in 2019.

Investment Thesis
Fox Corporation offers a compelling value proposition at 11.6x forward earnings with dominant franchises in live news (Fox News #1 in cable) and premium sports (NFL, MLB, college football) that command pricing power in both advertising and affiliate distribution [1]. Q1 FY2026 revenue of $3.74B (up 5%) and Q2 of $5.18B (up 2%) demonstrate stable top-line performance despite the secular decline of linear TV [1][2]. Tubi's continued growth as a free streaming platform provides a digital hedge, and the Fox One AI-driven advertising platform positions the company for the converged media future. Free cash flow of $1.77B supports share buybacks and a growing dividend (7 consecutive years of increases), and the $2B cash position provides financial flexibility [5].
The bear case centers on structural media disruption and earnings volatility. Linear TV viewership continues its secular decline, with cord-cutting accelerating as streaming platforms proliferate — Fox's cable network programming segment faces long-term subscriber erosion that pricing increases cannot offset indefinitely [1]. Q2 FY2026 net income fell 36% to $247M and adjusted EPS declined from $0.96 to $0.82, driven by rising sports rights costs and lower political advertising in a non-election quarter [2]. Revenue growth of just 2% YoY in Q2 and 5% in Q1 is below the broader market, and the company lacks a paid streaming strategy that could replace declining linear economics. The Murdoch family's dual-class control structure limits corporate governance accountability. The $787.5M Dominion settlement precedent and ongoing legal exposure from defamation risks create tail risk for the news division [4].
Key Catalysts
  1. Tubi AVOD streaming platform scaling — continued digital growth driving advertising revenue increases and offsetting linear TV subscriber declines [1][3]
  2. Live sports rights portfolio (NFL, MLB, college football) providing irreplaceable content that commands premium pricing in both advertising and affiliate fees [1]
  3. Fox One AI-driven converged media platform enabling precision advertising and higher CPMs across Fox's portfolio [3]
Key Risks
  1. Secular cord-cutting acceleration — linear TV subscriber erosion in cable network programming segment threatens the distribution revenue base that provides 40%+ of total revenue [1]
  2. Rising sports rights costs — escalating NFL, MLB, and college sports fees compressing margins while creating dependence on content Fox doesn't own [2]
  3. Dual-class share structure and Murdoch family control limiting corporate governance accountability and strategic flexibility for outside shareholders [3]

AI Resilience (25%)
AI Resilience MODERATE
5
AI Risk Protection (4/9): Fox's core business faces a mixed AI disruption profile. Live sports and live news — Fox's two strongest franchises — are structurally protected from AI substitution because audiences demand real-time, authentic human-driven content [1]. However, AI-generated news summaries, automated sports highlights, and AI-powered content recommendation algorithms on competing platforms (YouTube, TikTok) threaten Fox's traditional distribution advantage and audience engagement patterns. The broader linear TV ecosystem faces secular decline as AI-personalized streaming platforms erode viewership. AI tools could also reduce production costs industry-wide, compressing the competitive advantage of Fox's studio operations [3]. AI Opportunity (1/1): Fox is generating measurable AI-driven revenue through the Fox One converged media platform, which uses AI for ad targeting, personalization, and measurement, and through Tubi's AI-powered content recommendation engine that drives engagement and advertising revenue [3].
