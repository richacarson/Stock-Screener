Company Profile
Industrials ·Electrical Equipment & Parts ·NYSE ·United States
Forgent Power Solutions designs and manufactures electrical distribution equipment for data centers, the power grid, and energy-intensive industrial facilities. Products include transformers, switchgear, transfer switches, power distribution units, and prefabricated power solutions. Formed in 2023 as a PE-backed rollup of four legacy companies (MGM Transformers, States Manufacturing, PwrQ, VanTran Transformers) by Neos Partners. IPO'd in February 2026, raising $1.06B.

Investment Thesis
Forgent Power Solutions is a pure-play beneficiary of the AI data center and grid electrification megatrend. Q2 FY2026 revenue surged 69% YoY to $296M with bookings accelerating 268%, creating a $1.5B backlog that provides multi-year revenue visibility [1]. FY2026 guidance of $1.275-1.325B revenue with $300-310M adjusted EBITDA implies ~23% EBITDA margins at scale. The capacity expansion to support $5B annual revenue signals management's conviction in the demand cycle's durability. At 28.5x forward P/E against 73% guided revenue growth, the valuation is reasonable for an infrastructure company riding the most powerful demand cycle in a generation. Eight analysts rate the stock Buy with a $42.63 price target (52% upside) and DCF models suggest $59 intrinsic value [1][2].
The bear case centers on execution and cyclicality risks. Forgent is a PE-created rollup of four companies that has been public for less than two months — integration risks remain, and the GAAP profit margin of just 1.45% shows profitability is fragile at current scale [3]. D/E of 120% reflects the leveraged PE-backed capital structure. Neos Partners retains voting control as a 'controlled company,' limiting shareholder protections. Data center capex is inherently cyclical — if hyperscalers pause AI infrastructure buildouts, the 268% bookings growth could reverse sharply. The Tax Receivable Agreement may divert significant cash to legacy PE holders. P/S of 9.1x is materially above electrical equipment peers and implies perfection. The company has no operating history as a public entity, no established track record of managing through downturns, and key executives were assembled recently rather than having built the culture over decades [1][3].
Key Catalysts
  1. Capacity expansion to $5B revenue footprint completing by end of FY2026, removing the primary constraint on growth [1]
  2. AI data center demand acceleration — 268% YoY bookings growth with $1.5B backlog provides multi-year revenue visibility [1]
  3. Margin expansion trajectory from current 1.45% GAAP to guided 23% adjusted EBITDA margin as production volumes drive overhead absorption [1]
Key Risks
  1. Data center capex cyclicality — if hyperscalers slow AI infrastructure investment, the explosive bookings growth could reverse and the $9.3B market cap built on growth expectations would compress rapidly [1]
  2. PE-backed controlled company structure with Neos Partners retaining voting control and Tax Receivable Agreement diverting cash to legacy holders [3]
  3. Integration risk from four acquired companies operating as a unified entity for less than 3 years, with GAAP profit margin of just 1.45% showing fragile profitability [3]
