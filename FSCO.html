Company Profile
Financial Services ·Asset Management ·NYSE ·United States
FS Credit Opportunities Corp. is a closed-end fixed income fund launched by Franklin Square Capital Partners and managed by FS Global Advisor, LLC. The fund invests across public and private credit markets globally with a focus on Europe and the United States, targeting non-traditional credit opportunities including secured and unsecured loans, bonds, event-driven credit, special situations, and private capital solutions. The fund has approximately $2.2 billion in assets under management.

Investment Thesis
FSCO offers an attractive 13.4% annualized distribution yield on market price, backed by a $2.2B credit portfolio that delivered a 10.89% net NAV return in 2025 and a 3-year NAV total return of 14.3% — double its peer group [3]. The fund trades at a significant discount to NAV (YTD market price return of -19.0% vs +1.1% NAV return), creating a potential value opportunity for patient investors who believe the discount will narrow [3]. Monthly distributions of $0.0583/share have been fully covered by net investment income on a tax basis throughout 2026, and the fund's focus on complex credit situations (event-driven, special situations, private capital) provides differentiated exposure [3]. Under Michael Forman's leadership since 2013, the fund has maintained one of the sector's lowest leverage ratios while outperforming peers.
The bear case is structural and fundamental. FSCO is a closed-end fund with virtually no measurable financial metrics for IOWN infrastructure scoring — D/E, ROE, forward P/E, revenue growth, and profit margin are all unavailable or zero, producing a mechanical infrastructure score of 0 [4]. Revenue has contracted 38% from $263M in 2023 to $164M in 2025, and was negative in 2022, reflecting the volatility of mark-to-market credit investing [3]. The payout ratio of 104.9% exceeds net investment income, raising distribution sustainability concerns — a dividend cut would devastate the share price for yield-seeking investors [4]. The persistent and widening NAV discount (-19% YTD) signals the market views the portfolio as overvalued or the distribution as unsustainable. As a closed-end fund without operating leverage or competitive moats, FSCO lacks the characteristics IOWN screens for: it has no innovation pipeline, no mission beyond returns, and no path to growing intrinsic value over time.
Key Catalysts
  1. NAV discount narrowing from the current -19% gap between market price and NAV, which could provide 20%+ upside if the market revalues the portfolio correctly [3]

Key Risks
  1. Distribution sustainability — the 104.9% payout ratio exceeds net investment income, and a distribution cut would likely trigger 15-20% downside as yield investors exit [4]
  2. Persistent NAV discount widening — closed-end fund discounts can become self-reinforcing as selling pressure from disappointed investors deepens the discount further [3]
  3. Credit cycle deterioration — a recession would impair the loan and bond portfolio, reducing NAV and potentially forcing the fund to sell assets at distressed prices to fund distributions [3]
