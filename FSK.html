Company Profile
Financial Services ·Asset Management ·NYSE ·United States
FS KKR Capital Corp. is a publicly traded business development company (BDC) providing customized credit solutions to private middle market U.S. companies. Externally managed by FS/KKR Advisor LLC (a partnership between FS Investments and KKR), the company invests primarily in senior secured first-lien loans and to a lesser extent second-lien, subordinated debt, and asset-based financing. Total portfolio originated $5.6B in 2025.

Investment Thesis
FS KKR Capital is the largest publicly traded BDC backed by KKR's global credit platform, trading at a 49% discount to NAV ($10.73 vs $20.89 NAV) with a 24.55% trailing dividend yield [1][4]. The reduced quarterly distribution of $0.48 ($0.45 base + $0.03 supplemental) still provides a ~9% annualized yield on NAV, supported by Q1 2026 GAAP NII guidance of $0.45/share. KKR's sourcing network originated $5.6B in 2025, with expansion into asset-based finance diversifying revenue streams. Insider buying by CEO Forman ($155K), President Pietrzak ($208K combined), and directors signals management conviction in the recovery. The forward P/E of 6.5x and extreme NAV discount price in significant credit loss expectations that may prove overly pessimistic if the economy avoids recession.
The bear case centers on portfolio credit deterioration and structural BDC headwinds. NAV per share fell 5% QoQ to $20.89 with rising nonaccruals, and management guided to a lower 2026 dividend (~9% of NAV vs. prior 10% target), acknowledging 'a tougher credit and earnings backdrop' [1]. Trailing ROE of 0.2% and profit margin of 0.7% reflect near-zero earnings after mark-to-market losses. Revenue declined 14.5% YoY. The external management structure creates a misalignment — FS/KKR Advisor earns fees on assets regardless of shareholder returns, incentivizing portfolio growth over quality. KKR's parent entity faces ESG controversy over gas infrastructure investments (Coastal GasLink pipeline, Cameron LNG terminal) that could create reputational risk for the fund [3]. The 24.55% yield reflects the market's assessment that the dividend may face further cuts if credit losses accelerate, and base interest rate declines would compress NII across the portfolio.
Key Catalysts
  1. NAV stabilization as credit losses normalize, potentially triggering a re-rating from the extreme 49% NAV discount toward historical averages of 10-20% discount [1]
  2. Expansion into asset-based finance (ABF) providing more defensive, collateral-backed income streams that perform better through credit cycles [1]
  3. Base rate environment supporting NII — if rates remain elevated, the floating-rate portfolio generates strong current income to support the $0.48 quarterly distribution [1]
Key Risks
  1. Rising nonaccruals and further NAV deterioration could force additional dividend cuts below the $0.48 quarterly level, triggering investor selling [1]
  2. External management fee structure creates misaligned incentives — FS/KKR Advisor profits from asset growth regardless of credit quality or shareholder returns [2]
  3. Interest rate cuts compressing net investment income across the primarily floating-rate portfolio, reducing distributable earnings below dividend levels [1]
