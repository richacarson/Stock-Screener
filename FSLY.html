Investment Thesis
Fastly has executed one of the most successful strategic pivots in tech, transforming from a struggling CDN into an essential AI infrastructure layer — Q4 2025 revenue of $172.6M (+23% YoY), RPO up 55% to $353.8M, NRR at 110%, and its first profitable fiscal year [1]. The AI Accelerator product suite using semantic caching addresses a genuine bottleneck in AI deployment costs, and recognition as a Forrester Leader validates the technology positioning. With 14% revenue growth guided for 2026 and consensus shifting from 'Hold' to 'Strong Buy,' Fastly has meaningful momentum.
The bear case centers on valuation and execution risk: after a 92.7% monthly surge, the forward P/E of 78.5x prices in flawless AI pivot execution with little margin for error [4]. $117M in annual SBC dilutes shareholders at a rate disproportionate to $650M+ annual revenue. Customer concentration remains a structural risk, and the core CDN delivery business faces pricing pressure from Cloudflare and AWS. If enterprise AI spending decelerates or Fastly fails to convert RPO into realized revenue, the premium valuation could compress rapidly from these elevated levels.
Key Catalysts
  1. AI Accelerator product suite commercialization driving enterprise adoption and RPO conversion from the $353.8M backlog through 2026 [1]
  2. Path to sustained GAAP profitability after first non-GAAP profitable fiscal year, with 14% revenue growth guided for 2026 [1]
  3. Forrester Leader recognition and agentic AI positioning could drive new enterprise wins as AI inference moves to the edge [1][2]
Key Risks
  1. Extreme valuation risk at 78.5x forward P/E after 92.7% monthly gain — any revenue miss or AI spending slowdown could trigger sharp multiple compression [4]
  2. Customer concentration in a small number of 'whale' accounts creates binary revenue risk if a major customer churns or reduces usage [1]
  3. $117M annual stock-based compensation versus ~$650M revenue represents excessive dilution that depresses real per-share economics [1]
