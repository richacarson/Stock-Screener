Company Profile
Industrials ·Rental & Leasing Services ·NASDAQ ·United States
FTAI Aviation owns, leases, and maintains commercial aircraft engines, primarily CFM56 and V2500 powerplants. The company operates two segments: Aviation Leasing (47 aircraft, 243 engines) and Aerospace Products (engine maintenance, repair, overhaul, and module factory). FTAI is also developing FTAI Power, an aeroderivative gas turbine for power generation.
985 Employees ·www.ftaiaviation.com

Investment Thesis
FTAI Aviation is a high-growth aviation aftermarket platform delivering 32.7% YoY revenue growth to $2.51B, with Aerospace Products EBITDA surging 76% to $671M in 2025 [1]. The proprietary module factory model — disassembling, repairing, and reassembling CFM56 engines at scale — addresses a $10B annual maintenance market with faster turnaround and lower cost than OEM shops. Management raised 2026 segment EBITDA guidance to $1.625B (37% growth), raised the module target to 1,050 units (39% growth), and increased the dividend to $0.40/quarter [1]. At 21x forward P/E with forward EPS of $12.27 (vs. trailing $4.61), the valuation is reasonable for the growth trajectory. FTAI Power's Mod-1 aeroderivative turbine (first delivery Q4 2026, 100 units planned 2027) opens a new TAM in data center and distributed power generation [1].
The bear case centers on extreme leverage, governance concerns, and execution risk. D/E of 1,046% creates significant interest rate sensitivity — even modest rate increases could compress the spread between asset yields and funding costs [4]. The January 2025 Muddy Waters short-seller report raised allegations about related-party transactions and accounting practices, and while the company disputed them, the stock dropped significantly and the controversy highlighted governance opacity [3]. FTAI's entire aftermarket thesis depends on the aging CFM56 fleet remaining in service — if airlines retire narrow-body aircraft faster than expected (due to new-generation engine availability from LEAP/GTF), the addressable market shrinks. The FTAI Power aeroderivative is an unproven product entering a competitive market (GE, Siemens) with first delivery not until Q4 2026. Trailing P/E of 55.8x reflects significant one-time charges that obscure underlying earnings quality. With only 985 employees managing a $26B market cap, operational concentration risk is elevated [4].
Key Catalysts
  1. Aerospace Products module target of 1,050 units in 2026 (39% growth) driving toward $1.05B segment EBITDA — each quarterly beat could propel continued re-rating [1]
  2. FTAI Power Mod-1 aeroderivative first delivery in Q4 2026, with 100-unit production in 2027, opening an entirely new revenue stream in data center power generation [1]
  3. ~$915M free cash flow guidance for 2026 after new investments, enabling deleveraging and continued dividend growth from the recently raised $0.40/quarter ($1.60 annualized) [1]
Key Risks
  1. Extreme leverage at 1,046% D/E creates material interest rate sensitivity and refinancing risk — rising rates directly compress leasing spreads and increase debt service costs [4]
  2. Muddy Waters short-seller allegations (January 2025) regarding related-party transactions and accounting opacity remain an overhang on governance credibility and could resurface with additional reports [3]
  3. CFM56 fleet retirement risk — if airlines accelerate narrow-body fleet renewal to LEAP/GTF-powered aircraft, FTAI's $10B addressable aftermarket could shrink faster than module factory capacity ramps [1]
