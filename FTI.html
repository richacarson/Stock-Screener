Company Profile
Energy ·Oil & Gas Equipment & Services ·NYSE ·United Kingdom
TechnipFMC is the largest provider of offshore oilfield services, offering integrated deep-water subsea development solutions spanning subsea equipment design, engineering, procurement, manufacturing, fabrication, installation, and life-of-field services. The company also provides surface technologies for land and shallow water exploration and production. Formed from the 2017 merger of Technip and FMC Technologies, TechnipFMC operates globally with approximately 22,000 employees.

Investment Thesis
TechnipFMC is the dominant integrated subsea platform with a record $16.6B backlog and $29B 24-month opportunity pipeline providing exceptional multi-year revenue visibility [1]. FY2025 revenue of $9.9B (up 9.4% YoY) with Subsea margins expanding to 20.1% and guidance raised to 21-22% for 2026 represents a structural margin inflection that could be the highest sustained Subsea margin in the company's history [1]. Adjusted free cash flow of $1.3-1.45B for 2026 with 70% return to shareholders and a low 39.4% D/E ratio creates a compelling capital return story. The iEPCI model — where TechnipFMC provides full subsea field development from design through installation — has become the industry standard, with direct awards and iEPCI representing over 80% of Subsea inbound [1]. At 20.1x forward P/E, the stock is reasonably valued for a market leader with double-digit revenue CAGR and expanding margins. Susquehanna raised its price target to $82, citing robust offshore outlook through the decade [4].
The bear case centers on cyclical exposure and valuation. TechnipFMC's revenue is ultimately tied to offshore oil and gas capital expenditure, which is cyclical and dependent on commodity prices — a sustained oil price decline below $60/bbl would slow subsea project sanctioning and pressure the $29B opportunity pipeline [1]. Revenue growth decelerated from 16.1% (2024) to 9.4% (2025), and profit margin of 9.7% is thin compared to asset-light business models, leaving limited room for error if cost overruns occur on the $16.6B backlog. The 30.5x trailing P/E is elevated for a cyclical industrial company, creating downside risk if offshore spending peaks. Insider selling has been exclusively one-directional — EVP Rounce's $10.7M sale and Director Rousset's $440K sale in March 2026 without any offsetting insider buying creates a cautious signal [4]. Energy transition risk looms over the long-term thesis: as the world shifts toward renewable energy, the addressable market for new offshore oil and gas development could structurally decline over the next decade, though near-term demand remains robust.
