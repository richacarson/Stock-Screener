Company Profile
Communication Services ·Entertainment — Live TV Streaming ·NYSE ·United States
FuboTV is a live TV streaming platform focused on sports programming, serving 1.63 million paid North American subscribers as of Q3 2025. The company is in the process of merging its operations with Hulu + Live TV to form a larger streaming entity controlled by Disney. Prior to merger completion, FuboTV operates independently, offering sports-first vMVPD bundles including a lower-priced 'skinny' sports bundle launched in 2025.

Investment Thesis
FuboTV's pending merger with Hulu + Live TV under Disney creates the largest live TV streaming platform in the US by subscriber count, combining Fubo's sports-first DNA with Hulu's broad content library. The deal's strategic logic is sound: combined scale reduces content licensing costs, improves technology amortization, and creates a credible alternative to YouTube TV. Fubo's achievement of consecutive positive adjusted EBITDA quarters in 2025 demonstrates the path to profitability was real before the merger [1].
The bear case is structural: even in the merged entity, Fubo shareholders are minority partners in a Disney-controlled company, limiting independent upside capture. The stock has declined 63% in 52 weeks and trades at $1.13 with a forward P/E of 91.52x — implying the market expects minimal near-term earnings even post-merger. Short interest at 15.78% of shares reflects skepticism about merger closing terms and post-merger value creation. The company missed Q4 2025 EPS expectations by 300%, highlighting ongoing execution risk [1][2].
Key Catalysts
  1. Disney/Hulu merger closing — definitive terms announcement and shareholder approval timeline will determine the exchange ratio and value realization for current shareholders
  2. Subscriber growth above 2 million North American paid subscribers would validate the sports-first strategy and demonstrate the skinny bundle is working
  3. Disney's first combined streaming earnings report showing cost synergies and margin improvement would validate the strategic rationale
Key Risks
  1. Merger risk: regulatory or structural complications in the Hulu + Live TV merger could delay or derail value realization, leaving FuboTV as a standalone company with insufficient scale
  2. Content cost inflation: sports rights negotiations with major leagues represent the single largest cost driver; any escalation in NBA, NFL, or MLB rights costs would compress margins sharply
  3. Subscriber churn: with 15.78% short interest, the market expects subscriber losses; any sequential decline in paid subscribers would trigger further stock pressure
