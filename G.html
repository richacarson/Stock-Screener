Investment Thesis
Genpact offers a compelling value proposition at 8.3x forward P/E with accelerating AI-driven growth. Full-year 2025 revenue grew 6.6% to $5.08B, with ATS revenue surging 17% to $1.204B, demonstrating successful positioning at the intersection of AI and business process transformation [1]. Adjusted EPS of $3.65 grew 11.3% YoY, free cash flow of $768M represents a 15% FCF yield on the $6.2B market cap, and 2026 guidance of at least 7% revenue growth with margin expansion signals confidence [1]. The 2.05% dividend yield with 21.7% payout ratio and 11.6% 5-year dividend growth CAGR provides growing income. The stock trades 27% below its 52-week high of $50.41, creating a potential value entry point [5].
The primary risk is that AI disruption may ultimately reduce demand for outsourced business processes faster than Genpact can transition to AI-enabled services. The Digital Operations segment (52% of revenue, growing only 4.1%) is the exposed legacy business, and the question is whether ATS growth can offset potential Digital Operations contraction as clients insource AI-automated processes [1]. Revenue growth of 6.6% is modest for a 'technology transformation' company, and the 5.0% 5-year revenue CAGR suggests the AI acceleration is recent rather than deeply established. Genpact competes against much larger rivals (Accenture at $65B revenue, TCS, Infosys) with greater scale and client relationships. The Bermuda incorporation and India-heavy operations create geopolitical risk, and the 0.6% insider ownership means management has limited skin in the game relative to the company's size [5].
Key Catalysts
  1. ATS revenue acceleration — high-teens growth guidance for 2026 in AI-powered services could accelerate as enterprise AI adoption increases [1]
  2. Margin expansion from AI-driven automation — 2026 guidance of 50bps gross margin expansion to 36.5% demonstrates operating leverage as machine-processed work scales [1]
  3. Valuation re-rating — at 8.3x forward P/E, the stock is priced for disruption, and evidence of successful AI transition could drive multiple expansion toward peers trading at 15-20x [5]
Key Risks
  1. AI substitution risk — clients may use AI tools (ChatGPT, Microsoft Copilot) to insource work currently outsourced to Genpact, compressing the Digital Operations segment [2]
  2. Competitive pressure from larger IT services firms (Accenture, TCS, Infosys) with greater R&D budgets and client relationships in AI transformation [1]
  3. Revenue growth deceleration — 6.6% total growth is modest for the AI narrative, and failure to accelerate ATS growth could validate the market's disruption concerns reflected in the 8.3x forward P/E [5]

AI Resilience MODERATE

AI Risk Protection (4/9): Genpact operates in the eye of the AI disruption storm. As a business process outsourcing firm, its core revenue depends on human labor performing repetitive, rule-based tasks — precisely the work that AI agents, robotic process automation, and large language models are designed to automate [2]. The company's ATS segment (24% of revenue, growing 17%) shows it is actively building AI capabilities, but the Digital Operations segment (52% of revenue) remains exposed to substitution risk as clients adopt AI tools to insource previously outsourced work. Management acknowledges this by framing the strategy as 'intentionally disrupting ourselves' [2]. The transition from human-processed to machine-processed work could compress margins even if revenue grows. AI Opportunity (1/1): ATS revenue of $1.204B and the AP agentic suite represent meaningful, quantifiable AI revenue — Genpact is monetizing AI, not just talking about it [1].
