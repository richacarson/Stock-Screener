Company Profile
Technology ·Semiconductors ·NYSE ·United States
GCT Semiconductor is a fabless designer and supplier of 4G LTE, cellular IoT, and 5G semiconductor solutions. Products include RF and modem chipsets used in smartphones, tablets, routers, USB dongles, and M2M/IoT applications. The company sells primarily through distributors to OEMs in Asia, Europe, and the Americas. Founded in 1998, headquartered in San Jose, California.

Investment Thesis
GCT Semiconductor is a high-risk, high-reward bet on 5G commercialization. The company shipped its first commercial 5G chipsets in January 2026, validated by Gogo's live in-flight network, and signed a satellite licensing deal targeting 'million unit plus' annual quantities [1]. Management expects sequential revenue and shipment growth through 2026 with gross margins reaching high-30s/low-40s% at scale. At $82M market cap, even modest 5G success could drive significant equity appreciation. The $9.4M cash position (as of Feb 2026) plus $20M convertible facility and $75M ATM program provide near-term runway. The 17 US patents on RF technology represent genuine intellectual property differentiation [2].
The bear case is severe. GCT generated just $2.9M revenue in FY2025 while losing $43.4M — a burn rate that is existentially threatening. The company had only $0.6M cash at year-end and is dependent on dilutive equity raises and convertible debt to survive [1]. Competing against Qualcomm ($200B+ market cap), MediaTek, and Samsung in 5G modems with 139 employees is a David vs. Goliath mismatch where the giants have orders of magnitude more engineering resources, carrier relationships, and manufacturing scale. The satellite deal quantities are aspirational — 'million unit plus' contrasts sharply with the 1,900 units shipped in Q4 2025. EBITDA breakeven is not expected until Q1 2027, requiring continued cash raises. The stock trades at 75x forward earnings, pricing in optimistic 5G ramp scenarios that are far from guaranteed [4].
Key Catalysts

  2. Satellite licensing deal with major provider targeting million-unit annual shipments starting H2 2026, potentially transforming the revenue base [1]
