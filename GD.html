Investment Thesis
General Dynamics offers a compelling combination of defense spending tailwinds and financial discipline, with FY2025 revenue growing 10.1% to $52.6B, EPS up 13.4% to $15.45, and a record $118B backlog ($179B total estimated contract value, up 24% YoY) [1]. FY2026 guidance of $54.3-54.8B revenue and $16.10-$16.20 EPS implies continued mid-single-digit growth with margin expansion to 10.4% [1]. The Gulfstream segment is inflecting with G700/G800 deliveries ramping to 160 units, while the Columbia-class submarine program provides decades of revenue visibility. At 19.1x forward earnings with a 1.76% yield and 30+ years of consecutive dividend growth, GD offers attractive risk-adjusted returns in an elevated geopolitical environment [6].
Bear case focuses on the inherent margin constraints of cost-plus defense contracting, with profit margins of just 8.0% limiting upside even as revenue scales [6]. The Technologies segment faces margin compression (9.2% guided for FY2026, down 30bps) from competitive pressure in government IT services. Marine Systems continues to experience labor and supply chain challenges that have historically caused cost overruns on submarine programs [1]. CEO insider selling of $11.7M in March 2026 raises questions despite strong operational performance [6]. Additionally, any de-escalation of geopolitical tensions or defense budget normalization could pressure the elevated backlog conversion timeline.
Key Catalysts
  1. Record $179B total estimated contract value (up 24% YoY) provides unprecedented multi-year revenue visibility across all four segments [1]
  2. Gulfstream G700/G800 delivery ramp to 160 units in 2026 with 14% operating margins drives Aerospace segment earnings growth [1]
  3. Elevated global defense spending driven by NATO rearmament commitments and geopolitical tensions supports sustained demand for combat vehicles and naval platforms
Key Risks
  1. Marine Systems labor shortages and supply chain constraints could cause Columbia-class submarine cost overruns, pressuring segment margins below the guided 30bps improvement [1]
  2. Technologies segment margin compression to 9.2% reflects competitive pressure from AI-native IT providers challenging GDIT's government services business
  3. Profit margins of 8.0% are structurally constrained by cost-plus contracting, limiting earnings leverage even as revenue grows mid-single digits
