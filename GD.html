Investment Thesis
General Dynamics enters 2026 with a record $118B backlog (+30% YoY) and $179B total estimated contract value, providing the best revenue visibility in its history. Full-year 2025 revenue of $52.6B grew 10.1% with net earnings up 11.3% to $4.2B. The 2026 guidance of $54.3-54.8B revenue with 25% segment operating profit growth signals meaningful margin expansion. GDIT's AI Centers of Excellence and Astris AI subsidiary position GD as a defense AI leader with quantifiable revenue. The 32-year dividend growth streak with a conservative 38.8% payout ratio provides reliable income with ample growth room. The +38 Inspire score supports faith-aligned investing, and the forward P/E of 19.1 offers reasonable valuation for a defense prime with unprecedented backlog visibility.
The bear case centers on margin structure and execution risk. Profit margin of 8.0% is structurally low compared to commercial peers, and the Marine Systems segment faces well-documented production challenges at Electric Boat (submarine construction delays). The stock has already risen 30% in 2026, pricing in much of the backlog strength. EPS growth of just 0.4% in FY2025 despite 10.1% revenue growth reveals margin compression that the 25% operating profit growth guidance must overcome. Government budget uncertainty — particularly with potential defense spending debates — could freeze new orders even if backlog protects near-term revenue. The 1.8% dividend yield is modest for income investors. Defense spending optimism may be peaking as geopolitical dynamics evolve.

Key Catalysts
  1. Marine Systems production ramp — resolving Electric Boat submarine construction challenges would unlock the largest single margin improvement opportunity, with the segment guiding $17.3-17.7B revenue in 2026
  2. GDIT AI revenue acceleration through Astris AI government platform and expanded Centers of Excellence, positioning GD as the preferred AI vendor for classified defense and intelligence applications
  3. Gulfstream Aerospace delivering 160+ aircraft in 2026 at 14% operating margins, with strong demand from ultra-high-net-worth individuals and corporate fleet renewal providing premium-margin revenue
Key Risks
  1. Electric Boat submarine production delays and cost overruns could continue to compress Marine Systems margins, the company's largest revenue segment at $17B+, and damage customer relationships with the U.S. Navy
  2. Government budget uncertainty — defense spending debates, continuing resolutions, and potential sequestration could freeze new contract awards even as the existing $118B backlog protects near-term revenue
  3. Business aviation cycle risk — Gulfstream's 14% operating margin segment is exposed to luxury discretionary spending, and any economic slowdown among ultra-high-net-worth clients could reduce order rates and delivery volumes
