Investment Thesis
GoDaddy is a deeply undervalued FCF machine trading at 7.2x forward P/E and 0.68 PEG, generating $1.61B in annual free cash flow from 21M+ SMB customers [1][6]. The AI-first transformation through Airo is driving Applications & Commerce segment growth of 17% YoY, and 2026 guidance projects $5.2B-$5.3B revenue with normalized EBITDA margins exceeding 33% and $1.8B FCF [1]. CEO Bhutani's culture of experimentation (20-30 A/B tests monthly) is systematically improving customer lifetime value and attachment rates [3]. The stock at $84.26 (down ~59% from highs) prices in minimal growth, creating substantial re-rating upside if Airo adoption accelerates and the company's value-creation story gains market recognition. EPS growth of 31.7% YoY demonstrates operating leverage despite modest top-line growth.
The bear case focuses on extreme leverage, modest growth, and competitive threats. D/E of 1,795.7% from aggressive share buybacks creates significant balance sheet risk — $3.7B in debt leaves little margin of safety if the business declines [6]. Revenue growth of just 6.8% YoY and conservative 2026 guidance (~6% growth) suggest GoDaddy may be a mature, low-growth business masquerading as a tech platform. The FTC settlement over data security failures (multiple breaches 2019-2022) raises governance concerns about security practices for a company hosting millions of small business websites [7]. Generic AI tools (ChatGPT, Wix AI, Shopify Magic) increasingly offer website creation and marketing capabilities that could erode GoDaddy's value-added services premium. The stock's 59% decline reflects market skepticism about the growth trajectory and AI transformation narrative [1]. Promotional term-mix shifts and the .CO registry contract expiration create near-term revenue headwinds.
