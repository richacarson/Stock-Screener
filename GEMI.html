Company Profile
Financial Services ·Capital Markets ·NASDAQ ·United States
Gemini Space Station operates the Gemini cryptocurrency exchange platform, offering trading, custody, staking, yield products, and payments infrastructure for digital assets. Founded by Cameron and Tyler Winklevoss in 2014, the company went public in September 2025 at $28 per share. Gemini has since exited international markets (UK, EU, Australia), cut 25% of staff, and is pivoting toward prediction markets.

Investment Thesis
Gemini's sole positive metric is revenue growth of 39.4% YoY to $179.6M, reflecting recovery in crypto trading volumes during the 2025 bull market [4]. The company holds a recognized brand in US crypto markets with institutional custody and regulatory compliance infrastructure that took years to build. The SEC's dismissal of its Earn program lawsuit (January 2026) removed one major legal overhang [3]. The pivot to prediction markets could capture growing demand for event-based trading products if executed successfully. At $4.71 per share (83% below IPO price), the stock could offer asymmetric upside if the company stabilizes operations and crypto markets recover.
The bear case is overwhelming. GEMI has collapsed over 80% from its $28 IPO price in just seven months, triggering a securities class action lawsuit alleging the company misled investors about its strategic direction — allegedly planning a prediction markets pivot while marketing an international exchange growth story [1]. Three C-suite executives (CFO, COO, CLO) departed simultaneously within months of the IPO, signaling internal dysfunction [2]. The company burned $218.1M in operating cash, cut 25% of its workforce, and exited three international markets (UK, EU, Australia) — all within six months of going public. Zero profit margin, -$15.52 EPS, and $660.8M in debt against $252.6M cash create financial fragility [4]. The $565M market cap on a deeply unprofitable, strategically confused company with active securities litigation makes GEMI uninvestable for a disciplined framework like IOWN. Competitive positioning behind Coinbase (COIN) in the US and Binance internationally leaves Gemini without a clear moat in any market.
