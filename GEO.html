Company Profile
Industrials ·Security & Protection Services ·NYSE ·United States
The GEO Group is a leading provider of secure facilities management, processing centers, secure transportation, electronic monitoring, and reentry services for U.S. federal, state, and local government agencies, including U.S. Immigration and Customs Enforcement (ICE), the U.S. Marshals Service, and the Federal Bureau of Prisons. The company operates approximately 100 facilities with ~81,000 beds and ~440,000 individuals on its BI electronic monitoring platform.

Investment Thesis
GEO Group is the dominant beneficiary of the federal government's expanded immigration enforcement build-out, with $520M of new annualized revenue contracted in 2025 — the largest single-year wins in company history [1]. Five facility activations representing ~$400M annualized capacity, plus a new ICE skip-tracing contract worth up to $60M annually, drive 2026 revenue guidance of $2.9B-$3.1B (+10-18% YoY) and adjusted EBITDA of $490M-$510M [1]. At a forward P/E of just 9.95 against EPS guidance of $0.99-$1.07, the stock trades at a deep discount to industrial services peers, reflecting policy and ESG overhangs rather than fundamental weakness. ROE of 17.9% and profit margin of 9.7% are healthy for the sector, and the founder-CEO transition (Zoley returning March 1, 2026, with multi-year contract through 2029) provides continuity through the ramp [2].
The bear case is acute and structural. The February 2026 Supreme Court ruling that GEO is not entitled to government immunity opens the door to detainee lawsuits across multiple jurisdictions, including pending forced-labor claims, with potentially material litigation exposure [3]. Major banks have moved to 'debank' private prison operators on ESG grounds, forcing GEO to spend $1.37M lobbying for the Fair Access to Banking Act in 2025 to protect access to capital [3]. Detention contracts are concentrated with a small number of federal customers (ICE, USMS, BOP), and a future change in U.S. administration or immigration enforcement priorities could compress the contract pipeline materially. Recurring human-rights incidents — including facility deaths — expose the company to reputational, regulatory, and legal risk that ESG-mandated funds cannot underwrite. D/E of 114.7% requires continued strong free cash flow to de-lever.
Key Catalysts
  1. Ramp of five newly activated ICE facilities (~$400M annualized) and the secure-transportation expansion driving 2026 revenue toward the $3.1B high end of guidance [1]
  2. BI Inc. electronic monitoring growth and the new $60M annual skip-tracing contract creating a higher-margin tech-enabled services layer [1][3]
  3. Continued de-leveraging from strong free cash flow at lower interest rates supporting potential dividend reinstatement or accelerated buybacks
Key Risks
  1. February 2026 Supreme Court decision allowing detainee lawsuits to proceed could expose GEO to material litigation costs, including pending forced-labor and conditions-of-confinement claims [3]
  2. Bank debanking pressure on ESG grounds threatens access to capital; GEO is spending heavily on lobbying for Fair Access to Banking Act with no certainty of passage [3]
  3. Concentration risk — a change in U.S. administration or immigration enforcement priorities could meaningfully compress ICE contract revenue, the largest single growth driver
