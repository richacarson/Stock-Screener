Company Profile
Healthcare ·Biotechnology ·NASDAQ ·United States
Geron Corporation is a commercial-stage biopharmaceutical company focused on its telomerase inhibitor imetelstat (marketed as RYTELO). RYTELO became the first and only telomerase inhibitor approved by the FDA, cleared in June 2024 for lower-risk myelodysplastic syndromes (MDS) in patients who have relapsed or refractory disease after erythropoiesis-stimulating agents. FY2025 RYTELO net revenue was $183.6M, up 140% YoY, with 2026 guidance of $220-240M.

Investment Thesis
Geron's RYTELO is the first and only telomerase inhibitor approved anywhere in the world, targeting an 8,000-patient U.S. second-line MDS market with $183.6M in FY2025 revenue — a 140% improvement over 2024 — and 2026 guidance of $220-240M [1]. The IMpactMF myelofibrosis trial with 85% enrollment and H2 2026 interim OS readout represents a major potential catalyst for expansion into a significantly larger patient population. Net loss improved dramatically from $174.6M to $85.8M in 2025, with the best-ever 9% demand growth in Q4.
Key risks center on the narrow indication and cash position: the $401.1M cash balance against $254.7M in operating expenses provides an approximately 18-month runway [1][2]. The Altman Z-Score distress signal, December 2025 workforce reduction, and the limited 8,000-patient eligible U.S. MDS population cap near-term revenue. European commercialization launches in 2026 create execution risk without a commercial partner. IMpactMF OS readout in H2 2026 is binary — a failure would significantly impair the growth thesis.
Key Catalysts
  1. IMpactMF Phase 3 interim overall survival readout in H2 2026 — positive data in myelofibrosis would open a significantly larger addressable market and potential accelerated approval
  2. European commercial launch in select EU countries commencing 2026 — international revenue would improve the path to profitability
  3. Q4 2026 revenue demonstrating sustained demand growth and trajectory to profitability — reducing dilution risk and extending cash runway
Key Risks
  1. IMpactMF failure: a negative OS readout in myelofibrosis would limit GERN to the narrow MDS indication, making the path to profitability extremely challenging
  2. Cash runway of approximately 18 months necessitates near-term profitability; any revenue shortfall would force a dilutive capital raise
  3. Competitive MDS pipeline from luspatercept (already dominant in first line) and emerging agents could limit RYTELO's second-line market penetration
