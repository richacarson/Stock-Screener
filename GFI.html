Investment Thesis
Gold Fields delivered record results in 2025 — revenue up 68% to $8.75B, profit up 187%, and adjusted FCF surging to $2.97B — driven by gold prices averaging $3,281/oz and 16% production growth. The stock trades at just 7x forward P/E with a 5.9% dividend yield, 20% payout ratio, and $1.7B capital return program. Net debt of just 0.26x EBITDA provides exceptional balance sheet strength, while the Windfall Project and 2026 guidance of 2.4-2.6M ounces maintain production momentum.
The bear case is gold price dependency — the record $3,281/oz average in H1 2025 drove the entire earnings surge, and a reversion to $2,000/oz would compress margins dramatically despite declining AISC. The trailing P/E of 9.9x and forward P/E of 7x already discount significant gold price risk. South African operational challenges (South Deep has historically underperformed), geopolitical risk across Ghana and Peru, and the historical SEC/FCPA investigation create governance overhang. Mining is inherently a depleting asset business requiring constant capital reinvestment ($1.9-2.1B capex guided for 2026).
