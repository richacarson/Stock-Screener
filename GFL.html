Company Profile
Industrials ·Waste Management ·NYSE ·United States
GFL Environmental provides non-hazardous solid waste management services across Canada and the United States, including collection, transportation, transfer, recycling, and disposal for municipal, residential, commercial, and industrial customers. Founded in 2007 by Patrick Dovigi, GFL has grown rapidly through acquisitions to become one of North America's largest waste services companies.

Investment Thesis
GFL Environmental at $40.36 offers exposure to a top-4 North American waste franchise with improving operational execution — adjusted EBITDA margin reached 30% for the first time in 2025, up 130bps YoY, with 2026 guidance targeting $2.14B adjusted EBITDA (+10%) on $7.0B revenue (+8%) [1]. The divestiture of Environmental Services reshapes GFL as a pure-play solid waste company with higher margins and more predictable cash flows. Management targets $835M adjusted free cash flow in 2026 (+14%), and the waste industry benefits from recession-resistant demand, high regulatory barriers, and limited new landfill permitting. The stock's 0.527 beta provides defensive characteristics.
The bear case rests on valuation and governance. At 40.5x forward earnings, GFL trades at a steep premium to Waste Management (~28x) and Republic Services (~30x) despite weaker margins and higher leverage at 106% D/E [2]. Normalized ROE of just 3.3% does not support the premium multiple — the elevated 2025 profit margin of 57.9% is entirely driven by the one-time divestiture gain and will normalize to under 3%. The dual-class share structure concentrating control with founder Dovigi until 2040, combined with immediate-vesting executive compensation and Glassdoor culture scores of 2.9/5, raises governance concerns [3]. Past environmental violations (200,000-gallon oil spill, federal fine for chemical sales) add regulatory tail risk. If the premium valuation compresses toward peers, significant downside exists from current levels.
