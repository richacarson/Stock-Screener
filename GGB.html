Company Profile
Basic Materials ·Steel ·NYSE ·Brazil
Gerdau is one of the largest steel producers in the Americas, operating long steel mills in Brazil, North America, and other markets. Full-year 2025 revenue was R$69.86B (+4.2% YoY) with North America contributing 51% of consolidated revenue and 62% of EBITDA. Net income fell 69% to R$1.4B due to R$2B in Brazilian segment impairments. CEO Gustavo Werneck leads a company navigating a structural divergence between strong North American performance and a challenged Brazilian market facing record Chinese steel imports.

Investment Thesis
Gerdau trades at 10.5x forward P/E with a 5.8% dividend yield and 0.76x net debt/EBITDA — one of the cheapest steel companies globally relative to its financial strength [1]. The North America segment now generates 62% of EBITDA with high-quality margins supported by Section 232 steel tariffs and 90-day order backlogs [2]. The Miguel Burnier mining platform coming online in H1 2026 adds R$1.1B in annual EBITDA starting 2027, and the 24% capex reduction to R$4.7B in 2026 frees cash for buybacks and dividends [1].
The bear case centers on Brazil structural headwinds: record Chinese steel import penetration (6.4M tonnes, +7.4% YoY) has effectively destroyed Brazil segment profitability (87% gross profit decline in Q4 2025), and anti-dumping duties may not be sufficient to restore domestic steel economics [2]. The R$2B impairment charge signals management expects persistent, not cyclical, weakness in the Brazilian business. The BRL/USD exchange rate creates earnings translation risk for ADR investors, and the legacy tax fraud investigation continues to create ESG and governance concerns. EPS declined 69% YoY — far below IOWN's 10% growth threshold.
Key Catalysts
  1. Miguel Burnier mining platform reaching full capacity in H2 2026: expected R$1.1B in annual EBITDA improvement starting 2027 from lower iron ore input costs — directly addressable profitability improvement [1]
  2. Brazilian anti-dumping duty implementation: Commerce Ministry investigation could impose meaningful import quotas or duties that restore Brazilian steel pricing and partially reverse the Q4 Brazil gross profit collapse [2]
  3. North American steel tariff expansion: Section 232 extensions or new tariffs under the current administration would strengthen Gerdau's North America pricing power and order backlog duration [2]
Key Risks
  1. Chinese steel import surge continuing: if Brazilian domestic market pricing remains depressed by subsidized Chinese imports, the Brazil segment's R$2B impairment could be the first in a series — management has de facto written off the segment's prior asset values [1]
  2. BRL depreciation: Gerdau reports in BRL, and sustained BRL weakness vs. USD compresses ADR dividend payments and reduces the USD-equivalent market cap valuation even if BRL financials improve
  3. North America steel demand slowdown: residential construction weakness from elevated mortgage rates could reduce long steel demand in Gerdau's most profitable segment — the order backlog at 90 days would compress rapidly if end-market demand falls [2]
