Gerdau S.A. is a major steel producer operating across Brazil, North America, and South America, offering rebars, bars, wires, plates, hot rolled coils, structural profiles, and special steel products for agricultural, automotive, construction, energy, and mining markets. Founded in 1901 and headquartered in Sao Paulo, Brazil.

Investment Thesis
Gerdau is a 124-year-old steel producer and the largest long steel manufacturer in the Americas, trading at an attractive forward P/E of 8.3x with a 3.1% dividend yield. The company benefits from structural tailwinds including AI-driven data center construction demand and infrastructure spending across Brazil and North America. AI deployment in operations has delivered quantifiable cost savings ($3/ton alloy reduction, 15% quality variation improvement), demonstrating operational excellence under CEO Gustavo Werneck and new Chief Data & AI Officer Rodrigo Silveira [1][2].
However, Gerdau is navigating a cyclical steel downturn with revenue declining from R$82.4B (2022) to R$69.9B (2025), profit margins compressed to 2.4%, and ROE at just 3.1% — well below the company's historical average. The dividend has declined significantly from $0.56/share (2022) to $0.14/share (2024), and the 74% payout ratio is elevated relative to depressed earnings. While Q3 2025 showed sequential improvement in EBITDA (+7% QoQ) and steel shipments (+6.1% YoY for 9M2025), a sustained earnings recovery depends on steel price stabilization and demand improvement in Brazil and North America. The low D/E ratio of 29% provides balance sheet safety for the cycle [3][6].
Key Catalysts
  1. AI data center construction boom driving incremental steel demand for structural and specialty products across North and South America
  2. Sequential EBITDA improvement in Q3 2025 (+7% QoQ) and 6.1% YoY volume growth suggest cyclical trough may be forming
  3. R$6.0B capex program in 2025 positions Gerdau for production efficiency gains and capacity expansion when demand recovers
Key Risks
  1. Cyclical steel downturn has compressed profit margins to 2.4% and ROE to 3.1%, with revenue down 15% from 2022 peak
  2. Brazilian Real volatility creates currency translation risk for ADR holders — BRL depreciation reduces USD-denominated earnings
  3. Dividend declining from $0.56 (2022) to $0.14 (2024) with elevated 74% payout ratio; further cuts possible if earnings remain depressed
  4. Forward PEG of 90.95 suggests limited earnings growth visibility despite low forward P/E
