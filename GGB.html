Company Profile
Basic Materials ·Steel ·NYSE ·Brazil
Gerdau S.A. is one of the largest steel producers in the Americas, operating long steel mills across Brazil, North America, and South America. The company produces rebars, bars, wires, thick plates, and special steel products from 70% recycled scrap inputs. Q1 2026 net income rose 34% YoY to R$1.01B with North America driving 75% of EBITDA growth, delivering the best first-quarter EBITDA since 2022.

Investment Thesis
Gerdau trades at 8.2x forward P/E with a 3.14% dividend yield and excellent D/E of 29%—one of the cheapest steel companies relative to its balance sheet strength [1]. Q1 2026 delivered best-in-class first-quarter EBITDA (R$3.0B, +25% QoQ) driven by North American operations benefiting from Section 232 tariff protection and infrastructure/data center construction demand. The company's low-cost 70% recycled scrap model provides structural cost advantages.
The bear case centers on structural Brazilian headwinds: record Chinese steel imports have compressed domestic profitability to near-breakeven levels, and ROE of 3.1% is far below the 15% target [1]. Trailing profit margin of 2.4% and negative 5-year dividend growth CAGR reflect a company whose earnings power has deteriorated significantly from peak levels. The BRL/USD exchange rate creates translation risk for ADR investors, and the legacy tax fraud investigation continues to impair ESG credibility [3]. Revenue growth remains negative at -3.8% YoY.
Key Catalysts
  1. North American infrastructure spending acceleration (data centers, bridges, roads) extending the segment's 75% EBITDA contribution with protected pricing [1]
  2. Brazilian anti-dumping duty implementation against Chinese steel imports could restore domestic pricing and partially reverse margin compression
  3. Completion of Miguel Burnier mining platform adds R$1.1B in annual EBITDA from reduced iron ore input costs starting 2027
Key Risks
  1. Chinese steel import surge continuing to suppress Brazilian segment profitability, keeping overall ROE below 5% and preventing meaningful earnings recovery
  2. BRL depreciation vs. USD compresses ADR dividend value and reduces market cap equivalent even if BRL financials improve
  3. North American steel demand slowdown from housing weakness or tariff policy reversal could erode the company's most profitable geographic segment [1]
