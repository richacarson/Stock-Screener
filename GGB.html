Company Profile
Basic Materials ·Steel ·NYSE ·Brazil
Gerdau S.A. is one of the largest steel producers in the Americas, operating through Brazil, North America, and South America segments. The company produces rebars, bars, wires, plates, hot rolled coils, and structural steel primarily from recycled scrap (70% of production). North America now drives 75% of consolidated EBITDA growth. Q1 2026 net income reached BRL 1 billion, up 34% YoY, led by North American operations generating a 24.1% EBITDA margin.

Investment Thesis
Gerdau trades at 8.3x forward P/E with a 3.07% dividend yield and low leverage (D/E of 29%) — among the cheapest steel valuations globally relative to financial strength [4]. North America now drives 75% of EBITDA growth with an expanding 24.1% margin, supported by structural steel demand from data center construction and infrastructure spending [1]. Q1 2026 showed meaningful recovery with BRL 1B net income (+34% YoY) and BRL 3B adjusted EBITDA (+25% QoQ). The Miguel Burnier mining platform approaching completion will reduce iron ore input costs, adding approximately BRL 1.1B in annual EBITDA from 2027. The scrap-based production model (70% recycled inputs) provides a structural cost advantage and environmental differentiation.
The bear case centers on structural deterioration in Brazil and income quality erosion. Revenue has declined at a -5.4% 3-year CAGR, profit margins are compressed to 2.4%, and ROE of 3.1% is far below the 15% target — reflecting a company earning inadequate returns on capital [4]. The dividend has collapsed from $0.56/ADR in 2022 to $0.06 in 2025, a -47% 5-year CAGR that violates IOWN's income growth criteria. Brazilian operations face persistent headwinds from record Chinese steel imports that management describes as 'subsidized and unfairly competitive,' with anti-dumping duties providing uncertain relief [1]. The trailing P/E of 27.7 (versus 8.3 forward) reflects depressed current earnings, and the forward estimate requires significant profit recovery that depends on North American demand persistence and Brazilian market stabilization. BRL/USD exchange rate creates additional translation risk for ADR investors.
Key Catalysts
  1. Miguel Burnier mining platform reaching full production in H2 2026, reducing iron ore input costs and adding approximately BRL 1.1B in annual EBITDA starting 2027
  2. Brazilian anti-dumping duty enforcement on Chinese steel imports restoring domestic pricing power and partially reversing the 87% gross profit decline in the Brazil segment
  3. North American infrastructure and data center construction demand sustaining 24%+ EBITDA margins through 2026-2027 as AI buildout accelerates structural steel demand
Key Risks
  1. Chinese subsidized steel imports continuing to flood the Brazilian market, permanently impairing Gerdau's home market profitability and potentially triggering additional impairment charges beyond the BRL 2B recognized in 2025
  2. Dividend collapse from $0.56/ADR (2022) to $0.06 (2025) may not reverse if Brazilian profitability remains structurally impaired, eliminating the income case for Dividend sleeve investors
  3. North American steel demand vulnerability to construction slowdown from elevated interest rates or recession — the 90-day order backlog that supports current margins would compress rapidly if end-market demand falls

AI Resilience (25%)
AI Resilience LOW RISK

AI Risk Protection (8/9 — IMMUNE): Steel manufacturing is a capital-intensive physical-world process that AI structurally cannot displace. Gerdau operates electric arc furnaces processing recycled scrap metal into structural steel — this requires physical infrastructure, energy systems, logistics networks, and skilled labor that exist entirely outside AI's substitution capability [1]. AI actually increases demand for Gerdau's products: data center construction (the physical infrastructure of AI) requires massive quantities of structural steel, rebar, and plates. North America's 75% EBITDA contribution is partly driven by infrastructure and data center construction demand. Analysts have identified zero AI substitution risk for the steel industry. AI Opportunity (0/1): Gerdau's digital twins and CDAO appointment represent internal operational efficiency gains rather than revenue-generating AI products [2].
