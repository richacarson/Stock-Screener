Company Profile
Basic Materials ·Steel ·NYSE ·Brazil
Gerdau S.A. is one of the largest steel producers in the Americas, operating long steel mills across Brazil, North America, and South America. The company produces rebars, bars, wires, plates, hot rolled coils, and structural profiles for construction, automotive, energy, and mining markets. Founded in 1901, Gerdau is family-controlled (Johannpeter family) and produces 70% of its steel from recycled scrap. Q1 2026 net sales were R$16.7B with adjusted net income of R$1.0B, up 33.6% YoY.

Investment Thesis
Gerdau trades at 8.1x forward P/E with a 2.8% dividend yield and low leverage (D/E 29%) — deeply discounted relative to North American steel peers [1][4]. Q1 2026 showed improving profitability with adjusted net income up 33.6% YoY to R$1.0B and gross profit up 17.8% despite a 3.8% revenue decline, driven by cost management and higher capacity utilization [1]. The Miguel Burnier mining platform coming online in H1 2026 adds R$1.1B in annual EBITDA by 2027 from lower iron ore costs. North America's 62% EBITDA contribution provides exposure to U.S. infrastructure spending and data center construction under Section 232 tariff protection.
The bear case centers on structural weakness in Brazil, which still generates roughly half of revenue but far less profitability. Record Chinese steel import penetration has effectively destroyed Brazil segment margins, and anti-dumping duties may be insufficient to restore pricing [1][2]. Profit margin of 2.4% and ROE of 3.1% are well below IOWN thresholds, reflecting the Brazil drag on consolidated results [4]. Dividends have declined significantly from peak levels (5yr CAGR approximately -15%), and the 74.2% payout ratio is elevated for a cyclical steel producer. The BRL/USD exchange rate creates earnings translation risk for ADR investors, and the legacy 2015 tax fraud investigation continues to weigh on governance perceptions.
Key Catalysts
  1. Miguel Burnier mining platform reaching full capacity in H2 2026, expected to add R$1.1B in annual EBITDA by 2027 from lower iron ore input costs — directly addressable margin improvement
  2. Brazilian anti-dumping duty implementation against Chinese steel imports, which could restore domestic pricing and partially reverse Brazil segment profit compression
  3. U.S. infrastructure spending acceleration and data center construction driving incremental long steel demand in Gerdau's most profitable North American segment
Key Risks
  1. Continued Chinese steel import surge in Brazil: if subsidized imports persist, the R$2B impairment could be the first in a series, and Brazil's 30,000-employee manufacturing base becomes a structural cost burden
  2. Consolidated profit margin of 2.4% and ROE of 3.1% leave minimal buffer against any demand downturn — a North American construction slowdown would expose the full weakness of the Brazil-dependent cost structure
  3. Dividend sustainability concerns with a 74.2% payout ratio, declining annual payments, and cyclical earnings that could force further dividend cuts in the next steel downcycle
