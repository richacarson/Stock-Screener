Company Profile
Consumer Cyclical ·Internet Retail ·NASDAQ ·Israel
Global-e Online provides a direct-to-consumer cross-border e-commerce platform enabling international shoppers to buy online and merchants to sell worldwide. The platform handles localization, payments, logistics, and compliance for cross-border transactions. Founded in 2013 and headquartered in Petah Tikva, Israel, the company serves major brands including Disney, Shopify, and luxury retailers.

Investment Thesis
Global-e is the category-defining platform for cross-border e-commerce, delivering extraordinary growth — revenue of $962M in 2025 (28% YoY), GMV of $6.57B (35% YoY), and free cash flow of $281M (29% FCF yield on revenue) [1]. The company achieved its first profitable year with $68.3M net income, up from a $75.5M loss in 2024, proving the unit economics work at scale. 2026 guidance of $1.21-$1.27B revenue implies growth acceleration to ~30%, with adjusted EBITDA of $259-$284M showing margin expansion [1]. The balance sheet is fortress-like with $623M cash, $24M debt, and D/E of just 2.5% [4]. At 17.3x forward P/E with 30% revenue growth and expanding margins, the valuation is compelling. The Shopify partnership provides embedded distribution, and the co-founder leadership team's 12-year tenure ensures strategic continuity [2].
The bear case centers on platform dependency and competitive risk. Global-e's Shopify integration is both its greatest asset and its largest concentration risk — if Shopify builds native cross-border capabilities or renegotiates terms, Global-e's growth trajectory could decelerate sharply. Insider selling by all three co-founders in April 2026 signals that management may view the stock as fairly valued [4]. Profit margins at 7.1% remain thin for a platform business, and the 81x trailing P/E reflects earnings that are still early-stage. Cross-border e-commerce is growing but faces macro headwinds from tariffs, trade tensions, and potential regulatory changes in key markets. Amazon and other large e-commerce platforms investing in cross-border infrastructure could compress Global-e's competitive moat over time. The 1,219-employee headcount serving nearly $1B in revenue is impressively lean but may limit the company's ability to scale support and innovation simultaneously.
