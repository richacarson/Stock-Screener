Company Profile
Energy ·Oil & Gas Midstream ·NASDAQ ·Bermuda
Golar LNG Limited designs, converts, owns, and operates marine infrastructure for the liquefaction of natural gas. The company operates floating liquefaction natural gas (FLNG) vessels and LNG carriers, providing upstream gas monetization solutions through long-term charter contracts. Golar owns three FLNG units with a combined $17 billion adjusted EBITDA backlog.

Investment Thesis
Golar LNG is a unique pure-play on floating LNG liquefaction infrastructure with a $17B adjusted EBITDA backlog across three FLNG vessels secured under 20-year take-or-pay charters [1]. Revenue grew 51% to $394M in 2025 with adjusted EBITDA of $265M, and management projects EBITDA scaling to approximately $800M annually once the fleet is fully deployed — representing a 3x increase from current levels [1]. The two 20-year Argentina contracts worth approximately $14B in future EBITDA provide exceptional long-term visibility. Golar operates in a market it effectively created — there are very few operational FLNG vessels globally — giving it a dominant competitive position with deep engineering expertise that is extremely difficult to replicate. AI-driven data center expansion is a structural tailwind for LNG demand, as natural gas power plants are the primary backup and baseload fuel for new data center capacity.
The bear case centers on execution risk, valuation, and energy transition uncertainty. The forward P/E of 58.4x and PEG of 7.69 reflect a stock priced for the projected $800M EBITDA rather than the current $265M — any delays in fleet deployment or Argentina project execution would expose significant downside [5]. D/E of 134% reflects the capital-intensive nature of FLNG conversions, and the $17B backlog requires decades of operational execution to realize. The GAAP payout ratio of 167% exceeds earnings, suggesting the current $1.00 annual dividend may not be sustainable from net income alone [5]. Geopolitical risk is material — the Argentina contracts depend on stable government policy, and the Greater Tortue Ahmeyim project in Mauritania/Senegal faces political complexity in West Africa. Long-term energy transition risk exists if renewable generation and battery storage accelerate faster than expected, reducing LNG demand beyond the 2040s. Chairman Troim's controversial public comments on diversity create ESG headline risk for institutional investors [4].
Key Catalysts
  1. Hilli Episeyo redeployment to Argentina following shipyard upgrade, commencing 20-year charter and unlocking new EBITDA contribution toward the $800M annual target [1]
  2. FLNG Gimi production ramp-up at Greater Tortue Ahmeyim as the field develops, driving increasing revenue and EBITDA through 2026-2027 [1]
  3. AI-driven data center expansion increasing global LNG demand for gas-fired power generation, providing structural tailwind for Golar's contracted volumes [1]
Key Risks
  1. Execution risk on $17B EBITDA backlog — fleet deployment delays, shipyard conversion cost overruns, or operational issues with FLNG vessels could materially reduce realized value versus projections [1][5]
  2. Argentina geopolitical and regulatory risk — 20-year contracts depend on stable Argentine energy policy, currency stability, and political continuity in a historically volatile jurisdiction [1]
  3. Premium valuation — forward P/E of 58.4x and PEG of 7.69 price the stock for the fully deployed fleet; any shortfall in the ramp to $800M EBITDA would compress the multiple significantly [5]

AI Resilience (25%)
AI Resilience LOW RISK
9
AI Risk Protection (8/9): Golar's core business — designing, converting, and operating floating LNG liquefaction vessels in some of the world's most challenging offshore environments — is structurally immune to AI disruption [1]. AI cannot replace 200,000-ton FLNG vessels, maritime crew operations, or the complex engineering required to liquefy natural gas offshore. The business requires physical-world infrastructure, decades of maritime expertise, and regulatory approvals that AI cannot replicate. Furthermore, AI's explosive growth in data centers directly increases global electricity demand, which increases demand for LNG as a transitional fuel — AI is a tailwind for Golar's business, not a threat [1]. The company's 20-year take-or-pay charter contracts eliminate revenue volatility from AI disruption to the broader energy market. AI Opportunity (1/1): While Golar does not sell AI products, the company benefits from AI-driven energy demand growth — AI data center expansion requires massive electricity generation, and LNG is a primary fuel for new gas-fired power plants serving these facilities [1].
