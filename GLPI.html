Company Profile
Real Estate ·REIT - Specialty ·NASDAQ ·United States
Gaming and Leisure Properties is the first REIT to specialize in the acquisition, financing, and ownership of gaming-industry real estate, leasing properties to leading regional gaming operators under long-term, triple-net master leases. The portfolio spans 60+ properties across 19 states with tenants including PENN Entertainment, Bally's, Boyd Gaming, Caesars, and tribal operators.

Investment Thesis
GLPI is the original gaming REIT and remains a high-quality, recession-resistant income vehicle with multiple growth levers. 2026 AFFO guidance of $4.06–$4.11 per unit (up ~5% from 2025's $3.88) is supported by a $2.6B development pipeline, the closed $700M Lincoln acquisition, and a $467M Cordish commitment, plus continuing tribal-relationship expansion [1][2]. Triple-net master leases with 15+ year terms and CPI-linked or fixed escalators give GLPI predictable, contractually senior cash flow that is structurally protected from tenant operating volatility. The current ~6% dividend yield is well-covered by ~80% AFFO payout, and 12 consecutive years of dividend payments since the 2013 spin (with growth in ~10 of those years) make this a credible Dividend-sleeve income holding. Forward P/E of 13.9x and AFFO multiple in the low double-digits are reasonable for a low-volatility specialty REIT with investment-grade credit.
The bear case centers on tenant concentration and rate sensitivity. PENN Entertainment remains a meaningful share of rent, and any tenant credit deterioration would pressure cash flow even with master-lease seniority. The Inspire Impact Score of -1 reflects gambling's mixed faith alignment and may exclude GLPI from values-based mandates. The Sinek Just Cause score is modest — the business is fundamentally a financial structure rather than a transformative purpose, and growth depends on continued M&A in a market where VICI Properties is a larger, often-better-capitalized competitor. Higher-for-longer rates would compress refinancing economics on the $7B+ debt stack and pressure the spread on incremental acquisitions. Finally, secular shifts toward online sports betting and iGaming could, over a 10–20 year horizon, weigh on land-based casino visitation and affect tenant lease coverage at renewal.
Key Catalysts
  1. 2026 AFFO guidance of $4.06–$4.11 per unit (up ~5%) supported by Lincoln, Cordish, and a $2.6B development pipeline [1][2]
  2. Continued tribal-gaming relationship expansion as a differentiated growth channel vs. VICI [1]
  3. Stable ~6% dividend yield with 12-year payment streak and ~80% AFFO payout providing room for further growth [1]
Key Risks
  1. Tenant concentration with PENN Entertainment — any credit deterioration would pressure cash flow despite master-lease seniority [1]
  2. Rate-sensitive REIT with $7B+ debt stack; higher-for-longer rates would compress refinancing spreads and acquisition economics [2]
  3. Faith alignment headwind — gambling exposure (Inspire score -1) may limit investor base and creates long-term iGaming-substitution risk for land-based casino real estate
