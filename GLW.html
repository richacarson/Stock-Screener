Corning Incorporated is a 175-year-old materials science company operating across optical communications, display technologies, specialty materials, environmental technologies, and life sciences. The inventor of optical fiber (1970) and Gorilla Glass, Corning is now the essential infrastructure provider for AI data center connectivity, with a $6B+ Meta partnership, a multi-billion dollar Amazon fiber deal, an NVIDIA co-development agreement, and 2025 revenue of $15.6B up 19% YoY. Investment Thesis
Corning is the essential 'picks and shovels' play for the AI infrastructure buildout, with 2025 Optical Communications revenue of $6.3B (up 35%), enterprise data center revenue growing 61%, and segment profit reaching a record $1B (up 71%) [1]. The $6B+ Meta partnership (January 2026), multi-billion dollar Amazon fiber deal (June 2026), similar deals with two additional hyperscalers, and the May 2026 NVIDIA co-development agreement provide multi-year revenue visibility [2]. Q1 2026 core sales grew 18% to $4.35B with core EPS up 30% to $0.70 and operating margin expanding to 20.2% [1]. Management's Springboard plan targets $6.5B in incremental annualized sales by end of 2026 and $11B by 2028, reaching a $20B run rate. CEO Weeks stated 'Next year the hyperscalers will be our biggest customers.' The company's 175-year innovation legacy, CEO Weeks' 43-year tenure, and worldwide market leadership in optical fiber provide an unmatched combination of growth and institutional durability.
The bear case centers on valuation and cyclical risk. At 98.9x trailing P/E and 46.6x forward P/E, Corning is priced for sustained AI infrastructure spending acceleration with no margin for error [4]. The stock has surged ~550% over two years, pricing in multi-year growth that depends on hyperscaler CapEx continuing at record levels. Morningstar noted 'Corning's AI fiber boom is real, but its stock price may be getting out of hand' [2]. If AI data center buildout decelerates — as has occurred in every previous technology infrastructure cycle — Optical Communications growth could stall, triggering sharp multiple compression. Profit margin of 11.1% remains thin for a company commanding near-100x trailing earnings, and the materials manufacturing business model has structural margin limitations. Customer concentration risk is growing as Meta, Amazon, and a small number of hyperscalers drive a disproportionate share of optical growth. Insider selling activity — including CEO Weeks selling 100,000 shares at $186 in June 2026 — suggests company insiders recognize the valuation premium [3].
