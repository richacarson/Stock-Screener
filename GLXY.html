Company Profile
Financial Services ·Capital Markets ·NASDAQ ·United States
Galaxy Digital Holdings Ltd is a technology-driven financial services and investment management firm focused on digital assets, cryptocurrency, and blockchain technology. The company operates through three segments: Global Markets (institutional trading and lending), Asset Management ($5B AUM in digital asset funds), and Digital Infrastructure Solutions (Helios AI/HPC data center campus in West Texas). Founded by former Goldman Sachs partner Mike Novogratz in 2018, Galaxy completed its NASDAQ listing in 2025 after redomiciling from Canada.

Investment Thesis
Galaxy Digital is executing a compelling strategic pivot from pure-play crypto to diversified digital asset and AI infrastructure platform, with the Helios data center campus valued at $15B representing the most significant growth vector [2]. Q1 2026 showed $10B revenue with $49M Digital Assets adjusted gross profit, and management guided Q2 2026 adjusted EBITDA of approximately $90M as data center revenues begin ramping [1]. With $5B AUM, $3.2B assets under stake, $1.53B cash, and the GalaxyOne retail platform expanding the addressable market, Galaxy has assembled a diversified platform spanning institutional crypto, asset management, and AI infrastructure. The 43% stock gain over the past year reflects growing recognition of the Helios opportunity at the intersection of crypto infrastructure and AI compute demand.
Galaxy Digital's financials are extraordinarily difficult to analyze — $58.7B TTM revenue on an $11B market cap reflects principal trading volume rather than economic revenue, and the -$67M TTM net loss masks volatile underlying economics [1]. The $4.65B in total debt (D/E 167%) includes $1.15B in exchangeable notes raised at steep cost during the October 2025 crypto downturn. CEO Novogratz's registration of 202.3M shares for potential sale in May 2026 creates massive overhang on the 389.9M share base — potential dilution of over 50% if fully exercised [4]. The business carries extreme crypto price sensitivity (3.60 beta), and the Helios data center valuation of $15B has not been validated by third-party appraisal. Key-person risk is severe — Novogratz IS Galaxy's brand, investor relationships, and strategic direction, with governance concerns raised by proxy advisory firms [3].

AI Risk Protection (7/9): Galaxy Digital's business is structurally protected from AI disruption across its three segments. The Global Markets trading business requires regulatory licenses, institutional relationships, and capital markets infrastructure that AI cannot substitute. The Asset Management business ($5B AUM) is built on human relationships, regulatory compliance, and investment judgment in the specialized digital asset space. Most significantly, the Helios data center campus directly serves AI demand — Galaxy IS building AI infrastructure, making it a beneficiary rather than a victim of AI adoption [1][2]. The primary exposure is that crypto trading could become more commoditized by algorithmic competitors, but the institutional and regulatory barriers provide meaningful protection. AI Opportunity (1/1): The Helios AI/HPC data center campus, valued at $15B according to CEO Novogratz, represents one of the most significant AI infrastructure investments in the digital asset industry [2]. This positions Galaxy as both a crypto company and an AI infrastructure company.
