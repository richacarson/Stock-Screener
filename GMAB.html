Company Profile
Healthcare ·Biotechnology ·NASDAQ ·Denmark
Genmab A/S is a Danish biotechnology company specializing in antibody-based therapeutics for cancer and other diseases. The company has eight approved antibody medicines on the market, including DARZALEX (multiple myeloma, via J&J), Kesimpta (multiple sclerosis, via Novartis), EPKINLY/TEPKINLY (lymphoma), and Tivdak (cervical cancer). Genmab's proprietary DuoBody bispecific technology platform and extensive partnership network with J&J, AbbVie, BioNTech, and Novo Nordisk drive its royalty-heavy revenue model.

Investment Thesis
Genmab is a premier antibody engineering platform company with eight approved medicines generating $3.72B in 2025 revenue (up 19% YoY), anchored by DARZALEX royalties (~$2.5B from $14.3B J&J net sales) and rapidly scaling proprietary products EPKINLY ($468M, +67%) and Tivdak ($164M, +26%) [1]. The 2026 guidance of $4.1-4.4B revenue (10-18% growth) provides visibility, while the deep late-stage pipeline (epcoritamab expansions, tisotumab vedotin, acasunlimab) offers multiple value inflection points. At 15.3x forward P/E with 25.9% profit margins, 93.6% gross margins, and $1.07B free cash flow, Genmab is attractively valued for a high-growth biotech with proven commercial execution [4]. The DuoBody bispecific platform powering four approved medicines represents a durable technology moat that partners continue to validate through new collaborations.
The bear case centers on DARZALEX concentration risk and rising operating expenses. DARZALEX royalties represent approximately 67% of 2025 revenue, creating significant dependency on a single J&J-controlled product that will eventually face biosimilar competition (patent expiration timeline uncertain but approaching within the next decade) [1]. Operating expenses are guided to increase 23-32% in 2026 ($2.7-2.9B vs $2.2B) as Genmab invests in late-stage trials and commercial launch readiness, compressing operating margins and potentially causing operating profit to decline from $1.3B to as low as $0.9B [1]. The PEG ratio of 1.75 suggests the stock is not cheap relative to near-term earnings growth, particularly given the -94.4% earnings growth in the most recent period. The transition from royalty-dependent to commercial company carries execution risk in building sales forces and market access capabilities across multiple geographies [4].
Key Catalysts
  1. EPKINLY expansion into first-line DLBCL and FL — potential to transform the product from $468M to a multi-billion dollar franchise as earlier treatment lines are captured [1]
  2. DARZALEX continued growth toward $16B+ annual net sales in 2026, driving royalty revenue expansion for Genmab [1]
  3. Late-stage pipeline readouts — tisotumab vedotin in solid tumors, acasunlimab in NSCLC, and rinatabart sesutecan in ovarian cancer represent significant market opportunities [1]
Key Risks
  1. DARZALEX concentration risk — royalties from a single J&J-controlled product represent ~67% of revenue, with eventual biosimilar competition threatening this stream [1]
  2. Operating expense ramp — $2.7-2.9B guided 2026 opex (up 23-32%) for pipeline and launch investments may compress operating margins and disappoint earnings-focused investors [1]
  3. Clinical trial execution risk — multiple late-stage programs running simultaneously across indications creates binary event risk if key readouts disappoint [1]

AI Resilience (25%)
AI Resilience LOW RISK
8
AI Risk Protection (7/9): Genmab's core business — discovering, developing, and commercializing antibody-based therapeutics — is structurally immune to AI disruption. Drug development requires wet-lab biology, multi-year clinical trials, regulatory approval (FDA, EMA), and physical manufacturing of biologic medicines that AI cannot replace [2]. The antibody engineering process involves proprietary biological platforms (DuoBody, HexaBody, DuoHexaBody) that represent decades of accumulated know-how. If anything, AI increases demand for Genmab's capabilities by accelerating target identification and enabling more efficient clinical trial design. The company's partnership-heavy model with J&J, AbbVie, and Novo Nordisk creates contractual moats that are independent of AI dynamics. AI Opportunity (1/1): Genmab is actively deploying AI in its Discovery platform for target identification and antibody candidate design using in silico approaches, which should accelerate pipeline development and reduce preclinical costs, contributing to long-term productivity gains [2].
