Company Profile
Real Estate ·REIT - Diversified ·NYSE ·United States
Global Net Lease is an internally managed net lease REIT that acquires and manages single-tenant, net lease assets backed by creditworthy tenants across the U.S. and Western/Northern Europe. The company is executing a capital recycling strategy to reduce office exposure and redeploy into industrial and retail net lease properties. GNL completed its internalization and merger with The Necessity Retail REIT in 2023.

Investment Thesis
Global Net Lease is a net lease REIT executing a disciplined turnaround after its 2023 merger with Necessity Retail REIT. The company delivered a 32% total return in 2025, nearly triple the 12% net lease peer average, validating its capital recycling strategy [1]. Net debt has been reduced by $2.0B since Q3 2024, and the company achieved an investment-grade credit rating. The 8.0% dividend yield is attractive for income investors, supported by 11 consecutive years of dividend payments. Contract backlog across the global portfolio provides rental income visibility, and the shift from office to industrial/retail net lease assets positions the portfolio for better long-term fundamentals. FY2026 guidance of $0.80-$0.84 AFFO/share suggests stabilization [1].
The bear case centers on GAAP profitability, leverage, and dividend sustainability. GAAP net income was -$225.5M with a -45.5% profit margin, reflecting impairments and depreciation that mask the underlying cash flow business [3]. Revenue declined 15.1% YoY to $495.3M as asset dispositions outpaced acquisitions — the company is shrinking to improve quality. D/E of 153.9% remains elevated despite the $2.0B deleveraging, and the forward P/E is negative, meaning the company cannot cover its dividend from GAAP earnings [3]. The dividend was materially cut from historical levels and has zero consecutive years of growth. The 56-employee team must manage a global portfolio across the U.S. and Europe, creating operational risk. Interest rate sensitivity is significant — if rates remain elevated, GNL's cost of debt and cap rate spreads will compress, limiting accretive acquisition opportunities.
Key Catalysts
  1. Continued capital recycling — disposing of remaining office assets and redeploying into industrial/retail net lease at accretive spreads, improving portfolio quality and reducing lease risk [1]
  2. Investment-grade credit rating enables lower-cost debt refinancing, potentially improving net interest margins and AFFO per share as legacy debt matures [1]
  3. FY2026 AFFO guidance of $0.80-$0.84/share, if achieved, would demonstrate earnings stabilization and support the current $0.76/year dividend level [1]
Key Risks
  1. Revenue declining 15.1% YoY due to asset dispositions may continue if office exit proceeds exceed acquisition deployment, shrinking the portfolio and rental income base [1][3]
  2. Elevated D/E of 153.9% and negative GAAP earnings create refinancing risk if credit markets tighten or the investment-grade rating is downgraded [3]
  3. Office exposure (being disposed) carries vacancy and re-leasing risk as remote work trends persist, potentially requiring capital expenditure or rent concessions on remaining office assets
