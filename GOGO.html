Company Profile

Gogo Inc. is the leading provider of broadband connectivity services for the business aviation market. Following its 2024 acquisition of Satcom Direct, Gogo now offers integrated air-to-ground (ATG), LEO satellite (via its partnership on Galileo), and 5G solutions to private and fractional jet operators globally.

Investment Thesis
Gogo is executing a high-stakes transformation from a pure North American ATG provider into a global business-aviation connectivity platform following the Satcom Direct acquisition. Full-year 2025 revenue doubled to $910.5M and Q4 revenue grew 67% YoY to $230.6M, with adjusted EBITDA up 53% to $217.8M and free cash flow more than doubling to $89.2M [1]. 2026 guidance calls for $905M-$945M revenue, $198M-$218M adjusted EBITDA, and $90M-$110M FCF, with 1,000+ Galileo/5G unit shipments expected to build the installed base toward ~700 Galileo aircraft by year-end [1]. At 5.85x forward P/E, Gogo is priced for skepticism — if management executes the LEO rollout and converts shipments into recurring service revenue, there is meaningful upside as the debt deleverages.
The bear case is substantial. Debt-to-equity of 895.7% leaves very little margin for execution missteps, and a single missed deployment year could trigger covenant stress [4]. Starlink Aviation is arming business jets with LEO connectivity that bypasses Gogo's ATG network — a direct existential threat to the legacy service revenue base [2]. Glassdoor reviews citing that CEO Chris Moore's profitability-first vision has 'worsened the work culture' raise retention risk during a critical integration year [2]. The Q4 2025 EPS miss ($0.09 below consensus) shows that the operational story is still noisy [1]. An ISS Compensation QualityScore of 10 (worst possible) and Audit score of 9 signal governance concerns [3]. With trailing P/E of 52x and thin 1.4% profit margins, Gogo is priced for perfect execution — and the evidence so far is mixed.
Key Catalysts
  1. Galileo HDX/FDX LEO + 5G product ramp in 2026 — 1,000+ unit shipments and ~700 Galileo aircraft installed base creating high-margin recurring service revenue [1]
  2. Free cash flow guidance of $90M-$110M in 2026 with declining CapEx, accelerating debt paydown from current ~9x leverage [1]
  3. Satcom Direct integration synergies unlocking international expansion in Europe, Middle East, and Asia business aviation markets [1]
Key Risks
  1. Starlink Aviation competitive threat — LEO-direct service bypasses Gogo ATG network and could erode the legacy service revenue base [2]
  2. Extreme leverage (D/E 895.7%) with thin 1.4% profit margin leaves little buffer for execution missteps or macro downturn in business jet demand [4]
  3. Cultural/retention risk — Glassdoor reviews citing CEO profitability focus 'worsened culture' creates talent flight risk during critical integration year [2]
