Investment Thesis
Alphabet is the most undervalued mega-cap AI platform at 22.4x forward earnings with $403B revenue growing 18%, Google Cloud accelerating to 48% growth with 30% margins, and a $240B Cloud backlog that grew 55% quarter-over-quarter [1]. Gemini's 750M MAU and 300% growth in partner AI solutions revenue demonstrate AI monetization at scale, while 78% reduction in serving costs shows margin expansion potential. The $126.8B cash position and $165B annual operating cash flow fund the $175-185B capex plan without leverage concerns.
The bear case centers on the $175-185B 2026 capex plan — potentially the largest capital investment in corporate history — with uncertain returns if enterprise AI demand plateaus or AI capabilities commoditize [1]. Emissions rose 22% as data center expansion conflicts with sustainability goals, and Google quietly removed net-zero targets from its website [3]. Antitrust scrutiny remains an overhang, with ongoing DOJ cases that could force structural changes. At $301, the stock is 14% below its $349 52-week high, suggesting some market skepticism about the AI capex cycle's returns. Low governance scores from ESG raters highlight ongoing concerns about data privacy, algorithmic bias, and content moderation [3].
