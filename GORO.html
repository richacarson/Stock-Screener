Company Profile
Basic Materials ·Other Precious Metals & Mining ·NYSE ·United States
Gold Resource Corporation is a gold and silver producer, developer, and explorer headquartered in Denver, Colorado. The company operates the Don David Gold Mine in Oaxaca, Mexico, which has pivoted to predominantly silver production (~80% of revenue), and holds 100% interest in the Back Forty polymetallic project in Menominee County, Michigan.

Investment Thesis
Gold Resource Corporation is a speculative turnaround play benefiting from the precious metals bull market. Revenue rebounded to $99.8M as the Don David mine pivoted to silver-dominant production (80% of revenue), with operating cash flow of $21.7M and virtually no debt on the balance sheet [1]. The Three Sisters zone is expected to contribute 40-50% of production by 2026, and improved mining methods have reduced dilution from 40% to 13-17%, potentially unlocking higher-grade ore [1]. H.C. Wainwright raised its price target to $2 (70% upside) citing favorable commodity price trends, and with gold near all-time highs and silver surging, the macro backdrop is supportive [1].
The bear case is severe. GORO's own management warned that continued operations may not be possible beyond Q3 2026 if new mining areas are not successfully developed — this is an existential going concern risk [1]. The company remains unprofitable with -6.5% profit margins and -18.1% ROE despite the revenue recovery, and the $38.5M capital investment needed for new deposits exceeds the $25M cash reserve [1]. The Back Forty project in Michigan faces intense environmental opposition from the Coalition to Save the Menominee River, indigenous community resistance from the Menominee Tribe, and an overturned wetlands permit after data manipulation was revealed during contested hearings [3]. Labor relations risk at the Oaxaca mine was demonstrated by the January 2026 blockade. With a $190M market cap, negative FCF of -$14.1M, and a history of dilutive capital raises, GORO is a high-risk speculation that requires both operational execution and commodity price cooperation to survive [1][3].
