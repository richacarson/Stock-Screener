Company Profile

Gossamer Bio is a late-stage clinical biopharmaceutical company focused on the development of seralutinib for pulmonary arterial hypertension (PAH) and pulmonary hypertension associated with interstitial lung disease (PH-ILD). The company's Phase 3 PROSERA study reported topline results in February 2026 that narrowly missed the primary endpoint (p=0.0320, vs. 0.025 threshold), triggering a strategic reassessment. Cash of approximately $258M provides runway into H1 2027. The company has a global collaboration with Chiesi Group for seralutinib development.

Investment Thesis
The bull case for Gossamer rests on the subgroup analyses from PROSERA: in the intermediate- and high-risk PAH subgroup (REVEAL Lite 2 ≥6), seralutinib showed +20 meters improvement at Week 24 with three of four secondary endpoints hitting nominal significance [2]. In CTD-PAH, the improvement was +37 meters (nominal p=0.0104). If FDA engagement leads to a path toward approval in a defined subgroup, seralutinib could address an unmet need in high-risk PAH patients with approximately $258M in cash to fund operations.
The PROSERA miss is a critical setback: the trial did not meet its prespecified statistical threshold of p=0.025 in the overall population, and the company has paused SERANATA enrollment [2]. The regulatory path forward is highly uncertain — a June 2026 Type C FDA meeting is the key near-term milestone, and a negative outcome could end the program. The $170M FY2025 loss with runway only into H1 2027 creates acute capital pressure. Strategic options review may result in a sale at a significant discount to historical valuations.
Key Catalysts
  1. Positive June 2026 FDA Type C meeting outcome — a defined regulatory path for subgroup approval in high-risk PAH would significantly restore value
  2. Strategic partnership or licensing deal for seralutinib — Chiesi or another partner acquiring global rights would provide non-dilutive capital
  3. Positive SERANATA PH-ILD data when enrollment resumes — a clean win in a second indication would rescue the overall program
Key Risks
  1. FDA rejects a subgroup approval path at the June 2026 Type C meeting — would effectively end the seralutinib program and force asset liquidation or sale
  2. Cash runway only into H1 2027 with $170M annual cash burn — without a partnership or regulatory clarity, the company faces a capital raise below current book value
  3. SERANATA enrollment pause: the PH-ILD program cannot advance without understanding the regulatory implications of PROSERA, creating at least 6-12 months of lost development time
