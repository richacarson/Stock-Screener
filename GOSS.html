Company Profile
Healthcare ·Biotechnology ·NASDAQ ·United States
Gossamer Bio, Inc. is a clinical-stage biopharmaceutical company focused on developing seralutinib, an inhaled tyrosine kinase inhibitor targeting PDGFR, CSF1R, and c-KIT, for the treatment of pulmonary arterial hypertension (PAH) and pulmonary hypertension associated with interstitial lung disease (PH-ILD). Founded in 2015, incorporated in San Diego. Currently facing securities class action lawsuits following the Phase 3 PROSERA trial failure in February 2026.

Investment Thesis
Gossamer Bio retains speculative value through seralutinib's subgroup data showing meaningful efficacy signals in specific PAH patient populations despite the Phase 3 PROSERA primary endpoint miss (p=0.032 vs. required 0.025) [1]. The treatment effect of +13.3 meters 6MWD improvement and the planned Pre-NDA meeting with FDA create a narrow but non-zero path to approval. If the FDA accepts the subgroup data or the ongoing PH-ILD Phase 3 trial succeeds, seralutinib addresses a $3B+ PAH market with a differentiated inhaled delivery mechanism. R&D expenses of $43.1M in Q1 2026 demonstrate continued clinical investment, and collaboration revenue of $14.9M provides some cash flow.
The risks overwhelmingly dominate. The Phase 3 primary endpoint miss triggered an 80% stock collapse and multiple securities fraud class action lawsuits alleging that management concealed trial design issues — specifically that Latin American clinical sites enrolled patient populations inconsistent with publicly stated selection criteria [4]. Cash of $99M against quarterly burn of ~$47M provides only approximately two quarters of runway without additional financing, which at $0.18/share would be severely dilutive. The FDA path forward is uncertain — a Pre-NDA meeting does not guarantee acceptance, and the failed primary endpoint is a significant regulatory burden. The company's market cap of $87.5M prices in near-total failure. At $0.18/share (down 95% from 52-week high of $3.87), this is a binary biotech option with a high probability of worthlessness.
