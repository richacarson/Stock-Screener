Company Profile
Industrials ·Metal Fabrication ·NYSE ·United States
GPGI (formerly CompoSecure) is a diversified multi-industry platform operating two segments: CompoSecure (premium metal payment cards, security and authentication solutions) and Husky Technologies (engineered injection molding equipment and aftermarket tooling for food, beverage, medical, and packaging applications). The company completed its $7.4B business combination with Husky Technologies in January 2026 under Executive Chairman Dave Cote's Resolute Operating System.

Investment Thesis
GPGI represents a compelling industrial transformation story under Dave Cote's proven leadership. The January 2026 Husky Technologies combination created a $2.2B revenue platform with ~70% recurring revenues, 29% EBITDA margins, and $325-375M projected free cash flow [1]. CompoSecure's Q4 organic revenue grew 17% with 189% net income growth, demonstrating the metal payment card franchise's strength [1]. At 14.9x forward P/E, the stock trades at a meaningful discount to industrial compounder peers, and the Resolute Operating System offers a clear pathway to margin expansion through H2 2026. The $5B market cap provides substantial upside if Cote replicates even a fraction of his Honeywell playbook.
The bear case centers on execution risk from a complex integration. The Husky CEO and CFO are departing in 2026, and CompoSecure just installed a new segment CEO — three leadership vacancies during the most critical integration phase is concerning [3][4]. Reported financials are severely distorted by merger accounting, making it difficult to assess true operating performance. The company's debt load from the $7.4B acquisition is not yet fully transparent in public filings. Husky operates in a cyclical injection molding market where demand is tied to consumer packaging volumes; a recession would pressure the largest revenue segment. Additionally, the stock has declined 35% from its 52-week high of $26.78 to $17.26, reflecting market skepticism about the integration timeline [5].
Key Catalysts
  1. H2 2026 margin expansion as Resolute Operating System gains traction across Husky operations, with guided EBITDA growth of ~17% YoY [1]
  2. CompoSecure segment continuation of 17% organic revenue growth driven by expanding premium metal card partnerships with banks and fintechs [1]
  3. Successful Husky leadership transition and first full-year pro forma financial reporting providing transparency on combined entity performance [3]
Key Risks
  1. Integration execution risk — three senior leadership departures (Husky CEO, CFO; CompoSecure CEO transition) during the critical first year of the merger [3][4]
  2. Cyclical exposure in injection molding — Husky's equipment sales tied to consumer packaging capex cycles that are vulnerable to economic downturns [2]
  3. Opaque financial reporting — merger accounting distorts TTM metrics, preventing clear assessment of true leverage ratios and operating performance until full-year 2026 results [5]
