Company Profile
Financial Services ·Specialty Business Services ·NYSE ·United States
Global Payments is a payment technology and software solutions provider serving merchants, issuers, and consumers worldwide. Following the completion of the Worldpay acquisition and Issuer Solutions divestiture, the company is repositioning as a pure-play merchant solutions provider offering authorization, settlement, point-of-sale software, analytics, and value-added services across the Americas, Europe, and Asia-Pacific.

Investment Thesis
Global Payments trades at a deeply discounted 4.0x forward P/E after a 15% stock decline on modest 2026 guidance, creating a potential value opportunity if the Worldpay integration and pure-play merchant repositioning deliver results [1]. The company guided FY2026 adjusted operating margin expansion of 150 basis points to ~46%, 13-15% EPS growth, and plans to return $2B+ to shareholders through buybacks and dividends [1]. Q4 2025 adjusted EPS of $3.18 beat estimates, and the 44.7% adjusted operating margin demonstrates strong underlying profitability. At $18.2B market cap with $1.75B in free cash flow and $8.3B in cash, the balance sheet supports the transformation thesis. The merchant solutions market is growing mid-to-high single digits globally, providing a stable base for margin-focused value creation.
The bear case is that Global Payments is a subscale legacy payments processor losing ground to technology-native competitors. Revenue has declined from $8.98B in 2022 to $7.71B in 2025, and 5% guided growth for 2026 compares poorly to Stripe, Adyen, and Square's double-digit organic growth [1]. GAAP ROE of 4.8% reflects the goodwill-laden balance sheet from serial acquisitions ($22.2B in total debt), and GAAP EPS of $4.43 versus adjusted EPS of $16.23 reveals substantial restructuring noise that obscures true earnings power [4]. The dividend has been frozen at $0.25/quarter since 2021, signaling that management prioritizes debt reduction and buybacks over income growth. Competition from integrated commerce platforms (Shopify, Toast) and vertical software providers threatens GPN's merchant relationships. The frequent strategic pivots — TSYS merger (2019), Worldpay acquisition (2025), Issuer Solutions sale (2025) — suggest a company searching for an identity rather than executing on a clear vision [1].
Key Catalysts
  1. Worldpay integration synergies — margin expansion to ~46% adjusted operating margin in FY2026 with 150 bps of improvement, potentially accelerating as integration matures [1]
  2. Share buyback accretion — $2.5B authorization and $550M accelerated buyback at deeply depressed prices could drive 10%+ EPS growth from capital allocation alone [1]
  3. Pure-play re-rating — as the market recognizes the higher-margin merchant solutions focus and integration noise subsides, multiple expansion from current 4.0x forward P/E could drive significant returns [1]
Key Risks
  1. Fintech competitive erosion — Stripe, Adyen, Square, and vertical software providers are winning the next generation of merchants with superior technology and developer experience, threatening GPN's core revenue base [2]
  2. Integration execution risk — Worldpay is a complex acquisition requiring cultural, technological, and operational integration across 26,000 employees and multiple geographies [1]
  3. Revenue stagnation — 5% guided growth and flat-to-declining revenue trajectory since 2022 suggests structural market share loss that margin optimization alone cannot offset [1][4]
