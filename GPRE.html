Company Profile
Basic Materials ·Chemicals ·NASDAQ ·United States
Green Plains is a leading producer of low-carbon fuels and high-protein animal feed ingredients in the United States. The company operates through Ethanol Production and Agribusiness & Energy Services segments, producing ethanol, ultra-high protein distillers grains (Sequence), and renewable corn oil. Green Plains is advancing carbon capture and storage (CCS) under the Advantage Nebraska program.

Investment Thesis
Green Plains is at an inflection point with a compelling transformation thesis. Q4 2025 marked the return to profitability ($11.9M net income, $0.17 EPS beating estimates by 750%), and 2026 is set to be a breakout year with $188M in expected 45Z carbon credit EBITDA and $150M+ in annual CCS EBITDA from Advantage Nebraska [1]. The balance sheet is clean at 60.2% D/E, and forward P/E of 16.1x is attractive if carbon earnings materialize. The Sequence ultra-high protein product opens a higher-margin specialty ingredients channel, and CEO Osowski (promoted from COO) brings operational continuity [2]. At $1.1B market cap, the stock is pricing in minimal value for the carbon capture and specialty ingredients optionality. Director Sweeney's $101K open-market purchase at $15.87 signals insider confidence [3].
The bear case centers on policy risk and cyclical exposure. The $188M in 45Z carbon credits and $150M CCS EBITDA are entirely dependent on federal clean energy policy — a change in administration or legislation could eliminate these revenue streams [1]. Revenue has declined 43% from 2022 ($3.66B to $2.09B), reflecting structural headwinds in commodity ethanol pricing. Profit margin is -5.8% and ROE is -14.7%, with the company generating net losses for most of 2025 [3]. The ethanol industry faces long-term secular decline risk as EV adoption reduces gasoline demand and with it ethanol blending volumes. Green Plains competes against much larger operators (POET, ADM) with greater scale and diversification. The CEO transition in August 2025 creates execution risk during a critical transformation period, and 642 employees must execute a complex carbon capture buildout while maintaining ethanol production [2].
Key Catalysts

Key Risks
  1. Federal clean energy policy risk — $188M in 45Z carbon credits and $150M CCS EBITDA are entirely dependent on government incentives that could be reduced or eliminated with policy changes [1]
  2. Secular decline in corn ethanol demand as EV adoption reduces gasoline consumption and ethanol blending requirements over the medium-to-long term [1]
  3. Revenue declined 43% from 2022 peak ($3.66B to $2.09B) with -5.8% profit margin, and the transformation to low-carbon fuels carries execution risk during a CEO transition [2][3]
