Company Profile
Industrials ·Aerospace & Defense ·AMEX ·United States
Hyperscale Data, Inc. (formerly Ault Alliance) is a diversified holding company pivoting toward AI data center infrastructure. Its subsidiaries span crane operations, commercial lending, Bitcoin mining, hotel real estate, and defense/aerospace. The company operates a 617,000 sq ft Michigan data center facility targeting 340 MW capacity for AI and HPC workloads through its Alliance Cloud Services subsidiary.

Investment Thesis
Hyperscale Data is attempting a dramatic transformation from a sprawling conglomerate into a pure-play AI data center company, with Q1 2026 revenue of $44 million (76% YoY growth) and full-year guidance of $180-200 million representing 80-100% growth [3]. The 617,000 sq ft Michigan facility with 340 MW power target and NVIDIA GPU deployments position the company at the intersection of AI infrastructure demand [2].
However, fundamental concerns overwhelm the growth narrative. The company trades at $0.12 per share with a $53 million market cap, ROE of -102%, and profit margin of -65%, reflecting a deeply unprofitable business [6]. SEC enforcement actions against founder Todd Ault III for misleading disclosures erode governance credibility [4]. The company competes against hyperscalers with effectively unlimited capital in a commoditizing market. Revenue quality is questionable — Q1 included $10 million from a litigation settlement and contributions from a recently-bankrupt subsidiary (Gresham Worldwide). The path to Q4 2026 profitability remains highly uncertain given the capital intensity of data center operations at this scale.
Key Catalysts
  1. Successful scaling of Michigan data center to full 340 MW capacity could establish the company as a meaningful regional AI hosting provider [2]
  2. Completion of non-core asset divestitures would simplify the corporate structure and potentially unlock value for the remaining AI infrastructure business [5]
  3. Achievement of positive EBITDA by Q4 2026 as guided would represent a critical inflection point for investor confidence [6]
Key Risks
  1. SEC governance concerns from the 2023 enforcement action against founder Todd Ault III for misleading disclosures spanning 2016-2021 remain an overhang on institutional investor confidence [4]
  2. Capital intensity of data center expansion at 340 MW requires significant financing that may further dilute the already micro-cap stock at $0.12 per share
  3. Revenue quality risk — Q1 2026 revenue included $10 million from a one-time litigation settlement and contributions from recently-bankrupt Gresham Worldwide, raising sustainability concerns [3]
