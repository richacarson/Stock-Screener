Investment Thesis
Hyperscale Data offers extreme speculative upside at $0.12 with 2026 revenue guidance of $180-200 million representing 80-100% growth, driven by Gresham's full-year contribution (~$40M via Ballista), Ault Lending expansion, and nascent AI data center operations through Sentinum [1][2]. Q1 2026 preliminary revenue of ~$44M (+76% YoY) validates the growth trajectory, and the Michigan AI data center's NVIDIA GPU deployment with a major cloud provider demonstrates early traction in AI infrastructure [3]. If profitability is achieved by Q4 2026 as targeted, the stock could re-rate significantly from its current $53M market cap.
The bear case is severe and multidimensional: GPUS has -65% profit margins, -102% ROE, and has never achieved sustained profitability as a diversified holding company [5]. Revenue declined from $135M in 2023 to $107M in 2024 before the Gresham-driven rebound, and the 3-year revenue CAGR remains negative at -4.7%. The conglomerate of cranes, lending, Bitcoin mining, and AI data centers lacks strategic coherence and faces massive competitors in every segment. The $53M market cap against $106M in total debt creates existential financial risk, and the stock's decline from $1.96 to $0.12 over the past year reflects persistent market skepticism. Dilution risk from potential share issuance to fund operations could further compress the stock price, and the founder's planned departure adds leadership uncertainty.

  2. Michigan AI data center expansion and deepened partnership with the unnamed major Silicon Valley cloud provider could establish credible AI infrastructure revenue at higher margins
  3. Planned divestiture of Ault Capital Group would simplify the corporate structure, release capital, and reduce conglomerate discount
