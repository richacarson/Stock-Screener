Company Profile
Healthcare ·Drug Manufacturers - General ·NYSE ·United Kingdom
GSK is a global biopharma company headquartered in London, focused on vaccines, specialty medicines (HIV, oncology, respiratory/immunology), and general medicines. Following the 2022 demerger of consumer healthcare into Haleon, GSK operates as a pure-play biopharma with FY2025 sales of £32.7 billion across 36 markets worldwide.

Pipeline (1/2): Pipeline includes weight loss (35Pharma/HS235), next-gen HIV regimens, and oncology expansion, with a 2031 sales target of £40B+. R&D Intensity (1/1): R&D investment is substantial at industry-competitive levels, with AI-enhanced drug discovery capabilities and a commitment to invest $30B+ in the US by 2030 [2].

Existential Flexibility
10
The 2022 Haleon demerger is a textbook example of existential flexibility — GSK abandoned its profitable consumer healthcare business (Sensodyne, Advil, Centrum) to focus entirely on biopharma, a painful short-term decision that aligned with the Just Cause of getting ahead of disease [1]. The subsequent 35Pharma acquisition to enter weight loss therapeutics shows continued willingness to pivot into new therapeutic areas. This is a transformative reinvention, not incremental adaptation. (2/2 points)
Courage to Lead
6
The Haleon demerger required genuine courage — walking away from stable consumer healthcare cash flows to bet on higher-risk biopharma R&D [1]. Walmsley's decision to step down and ensure an orderly transition shows leadership maturity. The $2.2B Zantac settlement, while financially painful, resolved uncertainty rather than prolonging litigation [3]. However, the Zantac governance failure — allegedly suppressing internal safety findings since 1982 — represents a profound courage deficit in prior leadership eras. (1/2 points)

Investment Thesis
GSK delivered strong FY2025 performance with £32.7B in sales (up 4%) and core EPS growth of 12%, driven by Specialty Medicines surging 17% to £13.5B with Oncology up 43% [1]. At 10.8x forward P/E with 2026 guided 7-9% Core EPS growth and a 3.3% dividend yield (raised to 70p for 2026), GSK offers compelling value for a top-5 global pharma with a refocused biopharma strategy, AI-enhanced drug discovery, and a £40B+ 2031 sales target [1]. The Haleon demerger successfully transformed GSK into a pure-play biopharma, and the 35Pharma weight loss acquisition signals strategic ambition. Cash from operations of £8.9B and £4.0B free cash flow provide firepower for pipeline investment and shareholder returns, with £1.4B of the £2B buyback already executed [1]. Five FDA approvals in 2025 validate pipeline productivity.
The bear case centers on patent expiry headwinds that management warns will slow 2026 sales growth to 3-5%, creating a challenging transition period before next-generation products scale [1]. The $2.2B Zantac settlement resolves most litigation but ongoing cases (bellwether trials not until March 2028) and a shareholder class action alleging suppression of internal safety data since 1982 create lingering legal and reputational risk [3]. Net debt rose 10% to £14.5B, constraining acquisition flexibility. US Shingrix revenue fell 17% from market saturation, and the vaccine franchise faces pricing pressure globally. The CEO transition from Walmsley to Miels introduces execution risk during a pivotal year. 5yr USD ADR dividend CAGR is negative (-5.4%) due to the Haleon demerger cut, undermining the income case despite the recent 70p target [4]. GSK's environmental emissions targets (80% reduction by 2030) appear behind schedule with only 14% of the target achieved in the first three years [3].
Key Catalysts
  1. Specialty Medicines mix shift — at 41% of revenue growing 17% with Oncology up 43%, the margin-accretive portfolio transformation could accelerate core operating profit expansion toward the £40B+ 2031 target [1]
  2. AI-enhanced drug discovery potentially doubling success rates from 10% to 20%, which would transform R&D economics and pipeline productivity across all therapeutic areas [2]
  3. 35Pharma weight loss acquisition (HS235 for fat-selective weight loss with muscle preservation) positions GSK in the highest-growth therapeutic category in pharma [1]
Key Risks
  1. Patent expiry headwinds in 2026-2028 could compress revenue growth below the 3-5% guided range if new product launches fail to offset genericization of legacy medicines [1]
  2. Zantac litigation tail risk — $2.2B settlement resolves 93% of cases, but bellwether trials in 2028, shareholder class action, and the $70M whistleblower settlement signal ongoing legal exposure and governance reputational damage [3]
  3. CEO transition from Walmsley to Miels during a pivotal year introduces execution risk, particularly around pipeline prioritization, capital allocation, and the ambitious 2031 sales target [2]

AI Resilience (25%)
AI Resilience MODERATE
7
AI Risk Protection (6/9): Pharmaceutical R&D, manufacturing, and distribution are deeply regulated and capital-intensive — AI cannot replace the clinical trial process, FDA approval pathway, or physical drug manufacturing infrastructure [2]. However, AI-native drug discovery companies (Recursion, Insilico) and big tech (Google DeepMind) are entering drug discovery, potentially compressing timelines and reducing the competitive advantage of large pharma's traditional R&D scale. GSK's moat is its commercial infrastructure, regulatory expertise, and existing approved drug portfolio, which AI startups cannot easily replicate. AI Opportunity (1/1): GSK generates indirect AI-related value through AI-enhanced drug discovery that has contributed to pipeline advancement. Kim Branson's team uses ML across genetic analysis, molecular design, and clinical imaging, with Walmsley noting AI could double drug development success rates from 10% to 20% — a transformative impact on pharma economics [2].
