Company Profile
Basic Materials ·Other Industrial Metals & Mining ·NASDAQ ·United Kingdom
Ferroglobe PLC produces and sells silicon metal, silicon-based ferroalloys, and manganese-based ferroalloys globally. The company supplies silicon metal to aluminum, silicone chemical, and solar industries, and operates quartz mines, coal mines, and a hydroelectric power plant across the U.S., Europe, South Africa, and Canada.

Investment Thesis
Ferroglobe is a deep-value turnaround play trading at 6.6x forward earnings with a healthy balance sheet (D/E 38.9%) and management guiding to 20% revenue growth in 2026 ($1.5-$1.7B) [1]. The recovery thesis is anchored by three structural catalysts: a new 10-year energy contract in France reducing costs and enabling year-round production; EU safeguard duties and U.S. anti-dumping rulings protecting margins from Chinese import competition; and growing demand for high-purity silicon from the semiconductor, solar, and EV battery industries. Q4 2025 showed early momentum with alloy shipments up 13% to 165,000 tons and revenue up 6% to $329M. Chairman Lopez Madrid's insider purchase at $3.86 signals management confidence in the turnaround [4]. The company's silicon metal is an indirect beneficiary of AI infrastructure buildout, as semiconductors and data center components require silicon inputs.
The bear case is substantial. Ferroglobe posted a net loss of -$170.7M in 2025 with profit margin of -12.8% and ROE of -23.2%, reflecting the severity of the commodity cycle downturn [1]. Revenue declined 10.4% YoY, and the 5yr CAGR of 7.0% masks extreme volatility — revenue peaked at $2.3B in 2022 and has contracted to $1.4B. The turnaround depends on commodity price recovery that is outside management's control — silicon metal and ferroalloy prices are cyclical and subject to Chinese supply dynamics despite trade protections. Ferroglobe is a sub-$1B market cap company ($839M) with thin liquidity ($5.7M daily), creating volatility risk for any meaningful position. The company lacks a distinctive competitive advantage beyond cost structure — trade protections can be reversed, energy contracts expire, and Chinese producers have repeatedly found ways around import duties. Environmental regulatory risk is elevated given smelting operations and Sustainalytics' 'highest controversy level' designation [3].
Key Catalysts
  1. New 10-year energy contract in France starting 2026, reducing costs and enabling year-round production at French smelting facilities — management's top cited competitive advantage [1]
  2. EU ferrosilicon safeguard duties and U.S. anti-dumping rulings protecting domestic producers from Chinese imports, supporting pricing recovery in 2026 [1]
  3. Growing demand for high-purity silicon from EV battery, semiconductor, and solar industries, with Ferroglobe developing silicon-rich anodic material for lithium-ion batteries [2]
Key Risks
  1. Deep operating losses in 2025 (net loss -$170.7M, profit margin -12.8%) with turnaround dependent on commodity price recovery outside management's control [1]
  2. Sub-$1B market cap ($839M) with thin daily liquidity ($5.7M), creating significant position sizing constraints and price volatility for institutional investors [5]
  3. Trade protection dependence — EU safeguard duties and U.S. anti-dumping orders can be reversed or circumvented by Chinese producers through third-country routing [1]
