Company Profile
Consumer Cyclical ·Auto Parts ·NasdaqGS ·United States
The Goodyear Tire & Rubber Company develops, manufactures, distributes, and sells tires and related products and services worldwide. The company offers tires for automobiles, trucks, buses, aircraft, motorcycles, earthmoving equipment, mining and industrial equipment, and various other applications under the Goodyear, Cooper, Dunlop, Kelly, and Mastercraft brands. Founded in 1898 and headquartered in Akron, Ohio, Goodyear operates approximately 1,000 tire and auto service center outlets and is one of the world's largest tire companies with a $1.8B market cap.

Investment Thesis
Goodyear presents a deep-value turnaround opportunity at 0.43x PEG and 8.2x forward earnings, pricing in substantial pessimism that the Goodyear Forward transformation can reverse [1][2]. CEO Mark Stewart's restructuring plan delivered $107M in Q1 2026 benefits with further cost savings expected through 2027, and the company is gaining OE market share in both Americas (up 2 points) and EMEA (9 consecutive quarters of gains) despite severe volume headwinds [1]. The multi-brand portfolio (Goodyear, Cooper, Dunlop, Kelly, Mastercraft) provides coverage across premium and value segments, and the 128-year brand heritage carries enduring consumer trust in a safety-critical product category. Forward EPS estimates of $0.77 imply a return to profitability — if Stewart can stabilize margins, begin reducing the $8B debt load, and convert operational improvements into GAAP earnings, the stock at $6.29 offers asymmetric upside from trough valuations. The physical nature of tire manufacturing provides a structural moat against AI disruption, favorable price/mix trends partially offset volume declines, and the company's strong ESG positioning (Sustainalytics 13th percentile risk, Inspire Impact score of 47) is notable for an industrial manufacturer [4][5].
The bear case is formidable and centers on balance sheet stress and deteriorating volumes. Goodyear carries $8.0B in debt against only $723M cash and a $1.8B market cap — the enterprise is leveraged at 253% D/E, and any sustained deterioration in operating performance could trigger a liquidity crisis [2]. Q1 2026 painted a concerning picture: revenue fell 8.8% YoY to $3.88B, tire shipments declined 11.6% with replacement volume down a staggering 17.8%, and the net loss widened to $246M ($0.86/share) [1]. A $200M raw material cost headwind projected for H2 2026 threatens to offset Goodyear Forward savings before they fully materialize [1]. Trailing EPS of -$7.25, ROE of -50%, and profit margin of -11.6% confirm the company is deep in loss territory. The dividend was suspended in 2020 with no reinstatement timeline, removing an income floor for value investors. Structural risks compound the near-term challenges: Chinese tire manufacturers are aggressively expanding into Western markets with lower-cost offerings, EV adoption changes tire specifications and wear dynamics, and autonomous vehicles could eventually reduce individual car ownership. The Goodyear Forward plan must deliver flawlessly just to reach breakeven — at current leverage levels, there is minimal margin for execution error or macroeconomic deterioration.

AI Risk Protection (7/9): Goodyear's core business is deeply protected from AI disruption — tire manufacturing is a physical, capital-intensive process requiring specialized rubber compounding, materials science expertise, and a global factory infrastructure that AI cannot replicate or substitute [3].
