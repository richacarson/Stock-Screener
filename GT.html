Company Profile
Consumer Cyclical ·Auto Parts ·NASDAQ ·United States
Goodyear is one of the world's largest tire manufacturers, producing tires for automobiles, trucks, buses, aircraft, and motorcycles under the Goodyear, Cooper, Kelly, Mastercraft, and other brands. Founded in 1898 and headquartered in Akron, Ohio, the company operates approximately 750 retail outlets and is investing in smart tire technology and sustainable materials as part of its Goodyear Forward transformation plan.

Investment Thesis
Goodyear is a deep-value turnaround play trading at 5.8x forward earnings with a $1.85B market cap on $18.3B revenue. CEO Stewart's Goodyear Forward plan is delivering results: record Q4 2025 SOI of $416M (8.5% margin, best in 7+ years), five consecutive quarters of margin expansion, and positive free cash flow of $340M [1]. The company expects $250M+ in carryover cost savings and $200-300M raw material benefit in 2026, revenue per tire grew 4% with consumer replacement up 8% showing pricing power, and the premium 18-inch-plus segment share reached 50% [1]. Smart tire technology and the PlusAI autonomous freight partnership position Goodyear for the connected vehicle future. At $6.44 per share (near the 52-week low of $6.14), the downside appears limited if the turnaround execution continues.
The bear case is anchored in leverage and volume risk. Goodyear carries $7.26B in debt against an $801M cash balance and a $1.85B market cap — the enterprise is heavily leveraged, and any deterioration in operating performance could stress the balance sheet [1]. Revenue is flat YoY with management guiding ~4% volume decline in 2026 due to elevated channel inventories and soft demand. Tariff headwinds of ~$200M annualized could compress margins. Profit margin remains -9.4% and ROE is -41.3%, meaning the company is still losing money on a GAAP basis despite record segment profitability [5]. Low-cost tire imports continue to erode market share in the value segment. The dividend remains suspended, removing an income floor for investors. Only 40% of employees have a positive business outlook, and the executive team rates in the bottom 15% on Comparably — a turnaround requires engaged workers [2]. Factory inefficiencies and commercial segment weakness could derail the recovery trajectory in early 2026.
Key Catalysts
  1. Goodyear Forward cost savings of $250M+ plus $200-300M raw material benefit in 2026, potentially driving the first full-year GAAP profit since the Cooper acquisition [1]
  2. Premium mix shift — 50% share in 18-inch-plus tires (up from 42%) with 8% consumer replacement revenue per tire growth demonstrates pricing power in the fastest-growing segment [1]
  3. Smart tire technology and PlusAI autonomous freight partnership position Goodyear as a platform for connected and autonomous vehicle tire intelligence [2]
Key Risks
  1. Extreme leverage — $7.26B debt against $1.85B market cap and $801M cash; any margin deterioration or volume shortfall could trigger balance sheet distress [1]
  2. Volume headwinds — management guides ~4% global volume decline in 2026 with elevated channel inventories, soft commercial demand, and $200M annualized tariff impact [1]
  3. Employee disengagement — bottom 15% executive team rating on Comparably and 40% positive business outlook on Glassdoor suggest workforce trust deficit that could impair turnaround execution [2]

AI Resilience LOW RISK

AI Risk Protection (8/9): Tire manufacturing is a physical-world industrial process that AI structurally cannot displace — vehicles of all types (human-driven, autonomous, electric) require tires [2]. Autonomous vehicles may actually increase demand for advanced tires: AVs need higher-performance, sensor-equipped tires for safety, and shared autonomous fleets generate higher mileage per vehicle (more tire replacement). No analyst has flagged AI as a displacement threat to tire manufacturers. Goodyear's 128-year brand, global manufacturing footprint, and regulatory barriers (FMVSS standards) provide additional moats. AI Opportunity (0/1): While Goodyear is investing in smart tire technology and the PlusAI autonomous freight partnership, no quantifiable AI revenue has been reported. The smart tire platform and connected vehicle data are in early stages without specific revenue contribution [2].
