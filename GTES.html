Gates Industrial manufactures highly engineered power transmission belts and fluid power products (hoses, couplings, pumps) sold into industrial and automotive replacement and OEM markets worldwide. Investment Thesis
Gates Industrial is a global leader in engineered power-transmission and fluid-power components benefiting from two accelerating secular tailwinds — AI data-center liquid cooling (4x growth in 2025, guided to multiples of that in 2026) and personal mobility (25%+ growth in 2025) [1]. FY2025 was a record year for adjusted EBITDA and adjusted EPS despite a soft industrial demand environment, demonstrating mix-driven earnings power [1][2]. 2026 guidance of 1-4% core sales growth, $775-835M adjusted EBITDA, and $1.52-$1.68 adjusted EPS represents mid-single-digit earnings growth supported by $3.44B trailing revenue and 7.3% profit margin [7]. At $25.48 and 17.4x forward P/E, GTES trades at a reasonable multiple for a cash-generative industrial directly exposed to AI infrastructure buildout. The +55 Inspire score and SASB/GRI-aligned sustainability program add faith-alignment appeal [5].
The bear case centers on cyclical industrial demand, leverage, and culture. Core sales growth of 3.2% in Q4 and 1-4% guided for 2026 reflect a lingering soft industrial cycle in automotive aftermarket and diversified industrial end markets [1]. Net debt of ~$1.58B and D/E of 0.65 is manageable but limits flexibility if demand deteriorates [7]. Glassdoor feedback (3.6/5) flags 'poor management,' 'changing goals,' and a disconnected headquarters culture — a red flag for long-term execution [4]. No dividend means total return depends on share-price appreciation and buybacks, making the stock more sensitive to multiple compression. Competition from ContiTech, Continental, Parker Hannifin, and Eaton pressures pricing in core industrial categories. Personal-mobility growth could moderate if consumer e-bike demand cools, and data-center cooling is tied to the hyperscaler capex cycle, which is strong today but not immune to correction.
Key Catalysts
  1. Data-center liquid-cooling revenue growing 'multiples' of 2025 levels in 2026, turning GTES into a direct AI-infrastructure beneficiary [1]
  2. Personal mobility (e-bike Carbon Drive belts) sustaining 20%+ growth and mix-shifting the company toward higher-margin secular verticals [1]
