Investment Thesis
Chart Industries is a global leader in cryogenic and process technology positioned at the nexus of LNG infrastructure, hydrogen, carbon capture, and AI data center cooling — four secular growth megatrends. The company generated $4.26B in revenue for 2025, with management targeting mid-teens organic revenue growth through 2026 and mid-30% gross margins [1]. The Forward PEG of 0.62 suggests the stock is significantly undervalued relative to its earnings growth trajectory, and forward EPS of $12.86 represents a dramatic improvement from the trailing $0.33 depressed by Howden integration costs. The pending Baker Hughes acquisition at approximately the current price provides downside support.
The bear case focuses on execution risk from the massive Howden integration — trailing ROE of just 1.2% and profit margin under 1% reflect the burden of $3.7B in acquisition debt and integration costs. If the Baker Hughes deal closes, upside is capped near current levels (~$208/share). If the deal fails, the stock could face significant downside given the leveraged balance sheet (D/E 109%) and the need for continued deleveraging [1]. Employee culture issues (Glassdoor culture score 2.9/5) and CEO turnover create organizational risk during a critical integration period. Q4 2025 Specialty Products orders declined 3.5% with hydrogen revenue timing concerns, suggesting the hydrogen economy is materializing slower than expected [4].
Key Catalysts
  1. Baker Hughes acquisition expected to close mid-2026 provides near-term price support and potential long-term platform expansion within Baker Hughes' energy technology ecosystem
  2. AI data center cooling demand is creating a new growth vertical for Chart's heat exchanger and cryogenic technology platforms
  3. Forward PEG of 0.62 with consensus earnings growth of 26% suggests significant undervaluation if Howden integration delivers on margin expansion targets
Key Risks
  1. Baker Hughes acquisition could fail to close due to regulatory or shareholder objections, exposing Chart to downside with a heavily leveraged balance sheet (D/E 109%, $3.7B debt)
  2. Howden integration execution risk — trailing profit margin under 1% and ROE of 1.2% must improve dramatically to justify the acquisition premium and current valuation
  3. Hydrogen economy commercialization delays — Q4 2025 Specialty Products orders declined 3.5% due to customer timing in hydrogen markets, and the green hydrogen buildout remains dependent on policy incentives
