Company Profile
Energy ·Oil & Gas Equipment & Services ·NYSE ·United States
Halliburton Company provides products and services to the energy industry worldwide through two segments: Completion and Production, and Drilling and Evaluation. Services include hydraulic fracturing, cementing, drilling fluids, wireline logging, drill bits, artificial lift, and cloud-based digital solutions including AI-powered automation platforms. Founded in 1919, headquartered in Houston, Texas, operating in approximately 70 countries.

Investment Thesis
Halliburton offers compelling value at 12.7x forward earnings with a PEG of 0.97, pricing in minimal growth for the #2 global oilfield services provider with $22.2B in annual revenue and $2.0B in free cash flow [1][5]. Q1 2026 delivered $5.4B revenue with 13% operating margins, and the Drilling & Evaluation segment grew 4% YoY driven by international activity, partially offsetting Completion & Production weakness. The balance sheet is manageable at 74.7% D/E with $2.0B cash, and the ZEUS IQ and LOGIX automation platforms position Halliburton to capture efficiency-driven spending even in a flat activity environment. The stock trades 15% below its 52-week high of $43.59, suggesting a margin of safety at current levels.
The bear case centers on structural cyclicality and margin fragility. Full-year 2025 revenue declined 3% to $22.2B, and Completion & Production operating income dropped 17% in Q1 2026 as North America operators reduced activity amid lower commodity prices [1]. Profit margin of 6.95% is thin by industrial standards, leaving minimal buffer against further revenue declines given high operating leverage. HAL's heavy North America weighting (over 40% of revenue) makes it more cyclically exposed than SLB's internationally diversified model. The 2024-2025 dividend plateau at $0.68/share signals management caution about forward earnings. Total debt of $8.1B against $2.0B cash requires sustained FCF generation, and the energy transition creates secular uncertainty for traditional oilfield services demand that HAL has not diversified away from.
Key Catalysts
  1. North America completion activity stabilization in H2 2026 as operator budgets reset and natural gas demand growth from LNG exports and AI data center power needs supports drilling economics
  2. International revenue acceleration in the Middle East and Latin America, where multi-year development programs provide visibility beyond the short-cycle North American market
  3. ZEUS IQ electric fracturing and LOGIX automation adoption expanding HAL's digital services revenue mix and supporting margin recovery toward the 15%+ operating margin achieved in 2023-2024
Key Risks
  1. Sustained WTI decline below $60/bbl would trigger E&P spending cuts and compress Halliburton's already-thin 6.95% profit margin, given high operating leverage in the services business
  2. Completion & Production segment deterioration continues — Q1 2026 operating income fell 17% YoY, and further North America activity declines could accelerate margin erosion
  3. Energy transition creates secular demand uncertainty for traditional oilfield services, and HAL's lack of diversification into adjacent energy segments (unlike SLB's carbon management and new energy businesses) increases long-term structural exposure
