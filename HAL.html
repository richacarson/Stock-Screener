Company Profile
Energy ·Oil & Gas Equipment & Services ·NYSE ·United States
Halliburton is one of the world's largest oilfield services companies, providing products and services for the exploration, development, and production of oil and natural gas. The company operates through two segments: Completion and Production, and Drilling and Evaluation, serving energy companies globally with leading positions in hydraulic fracturing, completions, and drilling technologies.

Investment Thesis
Halliburton offers a compelling value proposition at 14.5x forward earnings with a PEG of 1.09, supported by a dramatic profitability improvement story. Q1 2026 net income more than doubled YoY despite flat revenue, demonstrating the leverage of automation and cost discipline on margins. The company's leading position in North American completions and growing international exposure (Latin America +22% YoY) provide diversified growth vectors.
The bear case centers on cyclical commodity exposure and near-term North American activity headwinds (-4% NA revenue in Q1 2026). However, the forward P/E of 14.5 already prices in significant pessimism, and the operating margin expansion from automation investments (ZEUS IQ, LOGIX) creates a structural earnings uplift independent of rig count. International growth at 3% and Latin American strength at 22% provide offsets to domestic weakness. For patient investors, Halliburton offers cyclical upside with improving structural profitability.
Key Catalysts
  1. Continued operating margin expansion from automation and digital platforms (ZEUS IQ, LOGIX) driving earnings growth despite flat revenue
  2. Latin America and international revenue acceleration as national oil companies increase investment spending in 2026-2027
  3. North American activity recovery if natural gas prices strengthen and LNG export capacity additions drive completions demand
Key Risks
  1. Cyclical commodity exposure: prolonged oil price weakness below $60/bbl would compress activity levels and service pricing across all regions
  2. North America revenue declining 4% YoY in Q1 2026 could accelerate if US rig count continues falling on weaker natural gas economics
  3. Energy transition long-term risk: stricter decarbonization policies and capital reallocation toward renewables could structurally reduce demand for oilfield services over the next decade

AI risk protection scores 6/9: the physical nature of oilfield services (equipment, crews, chemicals) cannot be replaced by software, but AI could enable smaller competitors to match Halliburton's operational efficiency advantage over time.
