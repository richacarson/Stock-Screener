Company Profile
Energy ·Oil & Gas Equipment & Services ·NYSE ·United States
Halliburton is North America's largest oilfield services company by market share, providing technology products and services to the upstream oil and gas industry worldwide through two segments: Completion and Production (hydraulic fracturing, completion tools) and Drilling and Evaluation (drilling, formation evaluation, software). FY2025 revenue was $22.2B with adjusted operating margin of 14%, generating $2.9B in operating cash flow and $1.9B in free cash flow. Management is cautious on 2026 as North American activity softens, targeting $100M in quarterly cost savings from restructuring.

Investment Thesis
Halliburton trades at 14.6x forward P/E with a PEG of 1.11 — reasonable for the #1 North American fracturing company generating $1.9B in annual free cash flow and committed to returning >50% to shareholders [1]. The $100M quarterly savings restructuring will significantly improve margins when oilfield activity recovers, creating operating leverage from a leaner cost base. Q4 2025 adjusted operating margin of 15% with revenue of $5.7B beat estimates, demonstrating execution quality even in a soft market. The 1.61% dividend yield, 37.6% payout ratio, and $1B+ annual share repurchases provide shareholder returns while waiting for the cycle to turn [1][4].
The bear case is cyclical: FY2025 revenue declined 3.3% as North American land activity softened, and management has guided 'notably cautious' near-term expectations with first-half 2026 margin compression expected [1]. North American rig count remains at multi-year lows, directly limiting HAL's highest-margin fracturing services. A sustained oil price decline below $55/bbl WTI would materially reduce E&P capital budgets and compress completion services demand globally. The profit margin of 6.95% is thin relative to the 20% target, reflecting the capital-intensive nature of oilfield services and the current cyclical trough. Energy transition acceleration poses a long-term structural headwind if fossil fuel investment declines faster than expected.
Key Catalysts
  1. $100M quarterly savings realization from 2026 restructuring — at full run-rate, this adds ~$400M annually to operating income, providing significant margin leverage when revenue recovers
  2. International oilfield spending recovery: Middle East, offshore, and deepwater activity rebound could offset North American softness and drive revenue growth in H2 2026
  3. Oil price recovery above $70 WTI — would trigger increased E&P capital budgets and drive completion services demand, particularly in HAL's dominant North American fracturing franchise
Key Risks
  1. Oilfield services cyclicality: management guided 'notably cautious' for 2026; sustained weakness in North American land rig count at multi-year lows would compress fracturing revenue and margins further
  2. Oil price risk: a sustained decline below $55/bbl WTI would trigger E&P spending cuts across HAL's entire client base, reducing revenue 15-25% from current levels
  3. Energy transition structural headwind: accelerating shift away from fossil fuel investment could create long-term demand erosion for oilfield services, though near-term global energy demand continues rising
