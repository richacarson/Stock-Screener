Company Profile
Energy ·Oil & Gas Equipment & Services ·NYSE ·United States
Halliburton is North America's largest oilfield services company by market share, providing technology products and services to the upstream oil and gas industry worldwide. The company operates through two segments: Completion and Production (hydraulic fracturing, completion tools, and production enhancement) and Drilling and Evaluation (drilling, formation evaluation, and software). FY2025 revenue was $22.18B, down 3.3%, with Q4 2025 revenue of $5.7B, beating estimates, and management guiding for approximately $100M quarterly savings from operational restructuring.

Investment Thesis
Halliburton trades at a forward P/E of approximately 9.5× with a 2.76% dividend yield — significant value relative to peers given its #1 position in North American hydraulic fracturing, a $5.7B Q4 2025 revenue beat, and $100M quarterly savings target from ongoing restructuring [1]. The company committed to returning >50% of annual free cash flow to shareholders. When the oilfield services cycle recovers from its current softness, Halliburton's operating leverage should drive significant EPS expansion from the current restructured cost base.
The bear case is the oilfield services cycle: FY2025 revenue declined 3.3% as North American land activity softened and international growth decelerated [1]. CEO Miller noted the market would be 'softer than previously expected over the short to medium term.' A sustained oil price decline below $50/barrel would materially reduce upstream spending budgets, directly impacting Halliburton's completion services revenue. The 30% capex reduction to ~$1B in 2026 signals management itself expects a softer environment.
Key Catalysts
  1. $100M quarterly savings realization from restructuring — would significantly improve margins when revenue recovers, demonstrating operating leverage
  2. International oilfield spending recovery: Middle East and Asia-Pacific activity rebound could offset North American softness and drive consolidated revenue growth
  3. Oil price recovery above $70 WTI — would trigger an increase in E&P capital budgets and drive completion services demand globally
Key Risks
  1. Oilfield services market 'softer than expected over short to medium term' per CEO Miller — any further global E&P spending cuts would reduce revenue and EBITDA below guidance
  2. North American land rig count at multi-year lows, limiting the highest-margin fracturing services segment that represents ~50% of revenue
  3. Energy transition acceleration: a faster-than-expected decline in fossil fuel investment would create long-term structural headwinds for oilfield services companies
