Company Profile
Industrials ·Electrical Equipment & Parts ·NYSE ·United States
Hayward Holdings designs, manufactures, and markets pool equipment and associated automation systems for residential and commercial swimming pools in North America, Europe, and internationally. Products include pumps, filters, robotic cleaners, heaters, heat pumps, salt chlorine generators, LED lighting, safety equipment, and the OmniX IoT-enabled pool automation ecosystem. Founded in 1925 and headquartered in Charlotte, North Carolina.

Investment Thesis
Hayward Holdings delivered record FY2025 performance — $1.122B revenue (up 7%), record 48% gross margin, and 24.1% EPS growth — demonstrating the operating leverage in its pool equipment business as the industry recovers from post-COVID normalization [1]. The OmniX automation platform targeting 3.5M unautomated U.S. pools represents a significant growth runway, with 2026 guidance of ~4% net sales growth, $0.82-$0.86 adjusted EPS, and ~$200M free cash flow [1]. At 16.3x forward P/E with a dominant market position in a non-cyclical aftermarket (pools require ongoing maintenance regardless of economic conditions), Hayward offers stable compounding with margin expansion optionality. The company's products reduce energy usage by 90% and chlorine by 50%, positioning it favorably as energy efficiency regulations tighten [4].
The bear case centers on Hayward's modest growth profile and post-COVID hangover. Revenue has declined from $1.314B in 2022 to $1.122B in 2025 (5-year CAGR of -5.1%), and 2026 guidance of only 4% growth suggests the industry recovery will be gradual [1][5]. New pool construction is sensitive to housing starts, interest rates, and consumer discretionary spending, creating macro risk. The $1.018B in debt (D/E 63.9%) carries refinancing risk if rates remain elevated. ROE of 10.0% is below cost of equity, suggesting the business does not yet earn adequate returns on its capital base. Competition from Pentair (larger, more diversified) and Fluidra (European leader) limits pricing power. The OmniX platform, while promising, has not yet demonstrated the revenue acceleration needed to justify a premium multiple — Hayward remains primarily a replacement equipment company growing at GDP-like rates [5].
Key Catalysts
  1. OmniX automation platform adoption targeting 3.5 million unautomated U.S. pools, which could drive revenue per pool significantly higher than replacement equipment alone [1]
  2. Energy efficiency regulation tailwinds — Department of Energy mandates for variable speed pumps and state-level environmental requirements create forced upgrade cycles [4]
  3. Margin expansion from 48% gross margin toward management's long-term gross margin targets, supported by manufacturing automation and mix shift toward higher-ASP connected products [1]
Key Risks
  1. Slow industry recovery — FY2025 revenue of $1.122B remains 15% below 2022 peak, and 4% guided 2026 growth suggests multiple years before prior peak volumes are reached [1][5]
  2. Interest rate sensitivity — new pool construction and discretionary pool upgrades are correlated with housing activity and consumer confidence, both pressured by elevated rates [5]
  3. Competitive pressure from Pentair and Fluidra in automation and IoT connectivity could limit Hayward's differentiation and compress pricing power in the connected pool equipment category [1]

AI Risk Protection (8/9): Hayward manufactures physical pool equipment — pumps, filters, heaters, cleaners, and salt chlorine generators — that AI cannot substitute [2].
