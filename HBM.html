Company Profile
Basic Materials ·Copper ·NYSE ·Canada
Hudbay Minerals Inc. is a diversified mining company focused on copper, gold, zinc, and silver production across North and South America. The company operates the Constancia mine in Peru, the Snow Lake operations in Manitoba, and the Copper Mountain mine in British Columbia. Hudbay is developing the Copper World project in Arizona and plans to double copper production to 250,000+ tonnes by 2030.

Investment Thesis
Hudbay Minerals is a high-quality mid-tier copper producer trading at 9.3x forward P/E, delivering record results ($2.2B revenue, $1B+ EBITDA, $380M+ FCF) with a clear path to double copper production to 250,000+ tonnes by 2030 [1]. The company sits at the intersection of two secular megatrends — AI data center copper demand and electrification — with negative cash costs (-$0.10 to -$0.30/lb) reflecting valuable gold and zinc byproduct credits [1]. S&P's upgrade to BB- validates the improving credit profile, and the $600M Mitsubishi joint venture for Copper World de-risks the company's largest growth project [2]. At $7.6B market cap with 45.6% EBITDA margins and a 25.7% profit margin, Hudbay offers compelling value for copper exposure in a structurally tight supply market [1].
The bear case focuses on execution and ESG risk. The plan to double production requires $575M+ annual capex and successful Copper World feasibility/sanction in 2026 — capital-intensive growth in mining carries permitting, construction, and commissioning risk [1]. Gold production is expected to decline 9% in 2026 due to Pampacancha depletion, reducing a key byproduct credit that supports negative cash costs. The Guatemala human rights settlement (involving alleged gang rapes, murder, and forced displacement of Indigenous communities) represents a severe historical ESG liability, even though the mine was sold in 2011 [3]. Constancia operations in Peru face periodic community disruption that can impact production. Copper price cyclicality means that a global recession or China slowdown could compress the 9.3x forward P/E multiple. The minimal dividend (0.08% yield) provides no income cushion during commodity downturns [1][4].
