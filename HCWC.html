Company Profile
Consumer Defensive ·Packaged Foods ·NYSE American ·United States
Healthy Choice Wellness Corp. operates natural and organic retail grocery stores across the United States through six brands: Ada's Natural Market, Paradise Health & Nutrition, Mother Earth's Storehouse, Greens Natural Foods, Ellwood Thompson, and GreenAcres Market. The company also operates wellness centers offering IV vitamin therapy and sells supplements online through TheVitaminStore.com. Spun off from Healthier Choices Management Corp. in September 2024, HCWC is headquartered in Hollywood, Florida with 19 locations across six states.

Investment Thesis
Healthy Choice Wellness is a natural and organic grocery roll-up with 19 locations across six states, growing revenue 39% annually (3-year CAGR) through acquisitions of established local brands like Ellwood Thompson, Mother Earth's Storehouse, and GreenAcres Market [1]. Full-year 2025 revenue reached a record $78.2M with gross margins of 39%, demonstrating the viability of the multi-brand natural grocery model. The company trades at an extraordinary 0.08x price-to-sales ratio ($6.1M market cap on $78.2M revenue), and management cites a 'rich pipeline of acquisition opportunities' to continue scaling nationally [1]. CEO Holman's 18-year tenure provides strategic continuity as the company invests in data analytics, loyalty programs, and wellness center expansion [2].
The bear case is severe. The stock fails the IOWN liquidity floor at $391K daily dollar volume, making it uninvestable for institutional portfolios [4]. D/E of 247% reflects dangerous leverage for a grocery retailer — $18.0M total debt against $6.1M market cap and only $3.0M cash [4]. Profit margin of -5.0% and ROE of -81.3% indicate the company is destroying value despite revenue growth, as acquisitions are funded with debt and equity dilution without generating operating profits. Operating cash flow of only $1.0M on $78.2M revenue is razor-thin. The 'Buy and Build' model requires continued access to capital markets for acquisitions — at a $6.1M market cap and penny-stock valuation, this access is severely constrained. Integration risk across six distinct store brands with different cultures, systems, and local identities creates operational complexity. Competition from Whole Foods (Amazon), Sprouts, Trader Joe's, and conventional grocers expanding organic sections pressures margins in an already low-margin industry [1].
