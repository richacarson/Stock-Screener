Company Profile
Financial Services ·Capital Markets ·NASDAQ ·United States
HIVE Digital Technologies Ltd. operates green-energy-powered data centers engaging in Bitcoin mining and high-performance computing hosting. The company provides computational capacity to blockchain networks and is aggressively pivoting toward AI infrastructure, including a planned 320 MW AI Gigafactory in the Greater Toronto Area expected to house 100,000+ NVIDIA GPUs. Formerly HIVE Blockchain Technologies Ltd., the company rebranded in July 2023. Operations span Canada, Sweden, and Paraguay, with headquarters in San Antonio, Texas.

Investment Thesis
HIVE Digital Technologies is executing an ambitious pivot from commodity Bitcoin mining to AI/HPC infrastructure, with FY2026 revenue surging 158% to $297.8M and BUZZ HPC revenue growing 94% to $19.5M [1]. The $220M Bell Canada/Cohere sovereign AI contract for NVIDIA Grace Blackwell GPU deployment validates demand, and contracted HPC ARR of $35M with a target of $225M by early 2027 provides revenue visibility [2]. The planned 320 MW AI Gigafactory in Greater Toronto Area could house 100,000+ NVIDIA GPUs, positioning HIVE as a meaningful North American AI infrastructure player. Low debt-to-equity of 13.4%, daily liquidity of $101.5M, and Forward P/E of 17.75 support the growth trajectory, with analysts at B. Riley ($8), Cantor ($7), and Rosenblatt ($5.50) all seeing significant upside from the current $4.26 [5].
The bear case centers on execution risk and current unprofitability. HIVE lost $0.66/share in FY2026 with -49.85% profit margins and -30.34% ROE despite 158% revenue growth -- the company is burning cash faster than it generates recurring revenue [1]. The CAD $3.5B Gigafactory investment is enormous relative to the $1.14B market cap, creating substantial dilution and financing risk [2]. Bitcoin mining revenue ($278.3M, 93% of total) remains subject to halving events, energy costs, and cryptocurrency volatility with a beta of 3.72. HIVE must compete against hyperscalers (AWS, Azure, Google Cloud) with infinitely deeper pockets and established customer relationships in AI infrastructure. Net insider selling -- including COO Rossy disposing of direct holdings at $3.97/share -- raises questions about management conviction at current prices [3]. Only 29 employees manage a billion-dollar enterprise, creating key-person risk and organizational fragility.
Key Catalysts
  1. Bell Canada/Cohere sovereign AI GPU deployment going live in late 2026 to early 2027, expected to add ~$70M ARR and validate HIVE's AI infrastructure business model at scale
  2. BUZZ HPC ARR scaling from $35M contracted to $225M target by early 2027, driven by NVIDIA H200 and B200 GPU cluster demand across enterprise and sovereign AI customers
  3. 320 MW AI Gigafactory in Greater Toronto Area beginning operations in H2 2027, potentially housing 100,000+ NVIDIA GPUs and establishing HIVE as a major North American AI data center operator
Key Risks
  1. Current unprofitability (-$0.66 trailing EPS, -49.85% profit margin) with massive capital requirements for the CAD $3.5B AI Gigafactory, creating dilution and financing risk that could impair shareholder value
  2. Bitcoin mining revenue (93% of total) is subject to halving cycles, energy cost volatility, and cryptocurrency price swings -- a 50% BTC decline would eliminate the majority of current revenue
  3. Competition from hyperscalers (AWS, Azure, Google Cloud) and established GPU cloud providers (CoreWeave, Lambda) who offer superior scale, reliability, and enterprise sales infrastructure
