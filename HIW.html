Company Profile

Highwoods Properties is an office REIT focused on the best business districts (BBDs) of fast-growing Sun Belt markets including Raleigh, Nashville, Tampa, Charlotte, Atlanta, Dallas, and Pittsburgh. The company owns approximately 27 million square feet of office space and is known for its BBD-focused strategy and strong ESG practices.

Investment Thesis
Highwoods Properties is a Sun Belt-focused office REIT trading at an attractive 7.7% dividend yield with well-covered FFO ($3.54 midpoint, 56% payout ratio) and visible near-term growth drivers — projected 200 bps occupancy gain and $50-60M NOI potential across eight buildings by year-end 2026 [1][4]. Q4 2025 leasing activity was strong with 526,000 sq ft leased and a 20% increase in net effective rents, suggesting improving demand in the company's BBD markets [1]. FFO guidance of $3.40-3.68/share (midpoint up 5.7% YoY) indicates the portfolio is stabilizing after post-pandemic headwinds. The 100 ENERGY STAR certified properties and strong ESG profile provide tenant appeal in an increasingly sustainability-conscious corporate real estate market [3].
The bear case centers on secular office demand decline and leverage. The post-pandemic shift to remote and hybrid work has structurally reduced office demand, and year-end 2026 occupancy of 87.5% remains below pre-pandemic levels [1]. D/E of 149.2% creates significant interest rate sensitivity — if rates remain elevated, refinancing costs could pressure FFO and dividend coverage [5]. Revenue declined -1.6% YoY, and same-property cash NOI is expected flat for 2026, suggesting the recovery is slow despite positive leasing spreads [1]. The dividend has not grown, with $2.00/share annual payout unchanged, offering no inflation protection to income investors [4]. Forward P/E of 31.4x appears expensive on a P/E basis, though this metric is less relevant for REITs where FFO is the standard measure. Glassdoor reviews citing management's resistance to cultural adaptation raise concerns about the company's ability to evolve as workplace needs change [2].
Key Catalysts
  1. Projected 200 basis point occupancy gain by year-end 2026 and $50-60M NOI growth across eight buildings, potentially driving FFO above guidance midpoint [1]
  2. Sun Belt migration trends continuing to drive office demand in Raleigh, Nashville, Tampa, and Charlotte — Highwoods' core markets [1]
  3. Interest rate cuts in 2026 could reduce financing costs on the 149.2% D/E capital structure and drive multiple expansion for office REITs [5]
Key Risks
  1. Secular office demand decline from remote/hybrid work — 87.5% occupancy target remains below pre-pandemic norms and recovery may stall [1]
  2. Interest rate sensitivity — 149.2% D/E means elevated rates directly pressure FFO, dividend coverage, and refinancing costs [5]
  3. No dividend growth — flat $2.00/share annual dividend provides no inflation protection and signals limited financial flexibility [4]
