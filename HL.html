Hecla Mining is the largest silver producer in the United States and Canada, operating mines in Idaho, Alaska, and Quebec. The company produced 17 million ounces of silver and generated record revenue of $1.4 billion in 2025, pivoting toward a pure-play silver strategy with the sale of its Casa Berardi gold mine.

Investment Thesis
Hecla delivered transformational 2025 results: record $1.4B revenue (up 53%), $310M free cash flow (vs $4M prior year), and net debt reduced to just $34M [1]. The strategic pivot to silver (73% of revenue post-Casa Berardi sale) positions Hecla as the premier North American silver pure-play at a time when industrial silver demand is surging from solar panels, electronics, and data center infrastructure [1]. With Lucky Friday at record production, Greens Creek at AISC under negative $2/oz after by-product credits, and Keno Hill achieving first full-year profitability, the operational portfolio is firing on all cylinders. Forward P/E of 14.1x with near-zero net leverage offers a margin of safety [6].
The bear case centers on commodity price dependence — silver at $54/oz and gold at $3,818/oz in Q4 2025 are at historical highs, and any meaningful price correction would compress Hecla's margins significantly [1]. 2026 silver production guidance of 15.1-16.5M oz is actually a step down from 2025's 17M oz as the portfolio transitions post-Casa Berardi sale. Hecla's environmental track record — including the $263M Bunker Hill Superfund settlement, 1,500+ spills at Greens Creek, and Clean Water Act violations at Lucky Friday — creates ongoing regulatory and reputational risk [3]. The high trailing P/E of 35.2x suggests the market has already priced in the earnings improvement.
Key Catalysts
  1. Silver price remaining above $40/oz driven by industrial demand from solar and electronics, supporting $1B+ annual revenue

  2. 2026 silver production guidance of 15.1-16.5M oz represents a temporary decline from 2025's 17M oz during portfolio transition
  3. Ongoing environmental liability risk from historical contamination at Bunker Hill, Greens Creek, and Lucky Friday operations
