Hamilton Lane is a leading private markets investment management firm with $142 billion in AUM and a $1 trillion total asset footprint. The firm provides direct investments, fund of fund solutions, evergreen products, and private credit across private equity, venture capital, real assets, and credit. Its Evergreen products have driven significant growth in retail/private wealth access to private markets. Founded in 1991 and headquartered in Philadelphia, Pennsylvania.

Investment Thesis
Hamilton Lane is a best-in-class private markets platform with exceptional financial characteristics — 32.4% ROE, 32.8% profit margin, 24.3% D/E, and Forward P/E of 11.1x represent a rare combination of quality and value [1][4]. The Evergreen product platform is a structural growth engine, with specialized fund fee-earning AUM growing 24% YoY to $41B as private wealth access to alternatives accelerates [1]. Total fee-related revenue grew 31% in FY2026, the asset footprint reached $1T, and the 11% dividend increase reflects management confidence. The Inspire Insight score of 62 with 16 positive ESG attributions is among the highest in the screening universe, consistent with the firm's ESG leadership since 2008 and Novata co-founding [3]. The 2.92% dividend yield with 36.5% payout ratio and 11.7% 5yr dividend growth CAGR provides an attractive and growing income stream.
The bear case centers on fee compression risk, CEO transition friction, and cyclical exposure. Glassdoor reviews citing cultural deterioration under the Co-CEO structure (Hirsch and Delgado-Moreira, effective January 2024) are concerning for a relationship-intensive business where talent retention is the product [2]. Revenue growth YoY of -2.2% in the latest quarter reflects the lumpy nature of performance fees, and any sustained private markets downturn would compress both management fees (via lower AUM) and performance fees. The trailing P/E of 13.9x looks cheap, but private markets valuations face mark-to-market risk in downturns — NAV-based fees could decline even without redemptions. Competition from Blackstone, KKR, and Apollo in the Evergreen/retail alternatives space is intensifying, potentially compressing fee rates. The $1T asset footprint, while impressive, includes assets under advisement that generate lower fees than direct AUM. PEG ratio is unavailable, limiting growth-adjusted valuation assessment.

  1. Evergreen product platform growing specialized fund fee-earning AUM at 24% YoY to $41B, driven by structural tailwind of private wealth access to alternatives — total addressable market of retail alternatives is estimated in the trillions [1]

  2. Private markets cyclical exposure — revenue growth YoY of -2.2% reflects lumpy performance fees; a sustained downturn in PE/VC valuations would compress both management and performance fee revenue [4]
  3. Fee compression from intensifying competition in retail alternatives as Blackstone, KKR, and Apollo all scale their own Evergreen-style products, potentially pressuring Hamilton Lane's fee rates and distribution leverage [1]
