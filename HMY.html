Company Profile
Basic Materials ·Gold ·NYSE ·South Africa
Harmony Gold Mining explores, extracts, and processes gold, uranium, silver, and copper across nine underground operations in South Africa's Witwatersrand Basin, the Hidden Valley open-pit mine in Papua New Guinea, and copper development projects in Australia. Founded in 1950 and headquartered in Randfontein, Harmony is transforming from a pure gold producer into a diversified gold-copper mining company with the CSA Copper Mine acquisition and $1.55B Eva Copper project.

Investment Thesis
Harmony Gold is a rare combination of value and strategic transformation. Trading at just 4.8x forward earnings with 33.5% ROE, 20% profit margins, and only 25.5% D/E, Harmony offers deep value in a sector benefiting from elevated gold prices [4]. H1 FY2026 revenue grew 20% to R44.4B with EBITDA surging 39% and operating cash flow rising 55% to $417.9M [1]. The copper diversification strategy — CSA acquisition plus $1.55B Eva Copper project targeting 40% of production by 2035 — positions Harmony as a beneficiary of AI-driven copper demand (data center wiring and cooling). The 2.83% dividend yield with a sustainable 14.6% payout ratio provides income while retaining capital for growth projects [4]. CEO Beyers Nel's 22 years at the company ensures strategic continuity.
The bear case centers on gold price dependence, execution risk, and South African operational challenges. H1 FY2026 gold production declined 9% YoY with underground recovered grades falling 11% to 5.7 g/t, driving all-in sustaining costs higher to $2,115/oz [1]. The company's profitability is heavily leveraged to the gold price — a sustained decline below $2,000/oz would compress margins significantly. The $1.55B Eva Copper and CSA ramp-up represent substantial capital commitments ($244.9M capex in H1, up 92.5%) that must execute on time and budget in a capital-intensive industry with a history of cost overruns. South African operations face structural challenges including illegal mining, unreliable electricity supply (Eskom), and currency volatility (ZAR/USD). Dividend history is inconsistent — Harmony paid no dividends from 2014-2020 and 2025 declared dividend ($0.12) was below 2024 ($0.13), undermining the dividend growth narrative [4].
Key Catalysts
  1. Gold price resilience above $2,500/oz driving record profitability — Harmony's leverage to gold price means every $100/oz increase flows almost directly to earnings and cash flow [1]
  2. $1.55B Eva Copper project approval and CSA ramp-up positioning Harmony as a gold-copper producer with 40% copper production by 2035, benefiting from AI-driven copper demand [1][2]
  3. Extremely low valuation at 4.8x forward P/E with 33.5% ROE and 2.83% dividend yield — significant multiple expansion potential if copper strategy de-risks and gold prices hold [4]
Key Risks
  1. Gold price decline — Harmony's profitability is heavily leveraged to gold prices, and a sustained drop below $2,000/oz would compress margins and threaten the copper investment thesis [1]
  2. Execution risk on $1.55B Eva Copper and CSA ramp-up — capital-intensive mining projects in Queensland and New South Wales face permitting, construction, and operational risks with a 10-year payback horizon [1][2]
  3. South African operational challenges including declining underground grades (down 11% to 5.7 g/t), illegal mining, Eskom electricity unreliability, and ZAR currency volatility affecting USD-reported results [1][3]
