Company Profile
Healthcare ·Health Information Services ·NYSE ·United States
Hinge Health is a digital health company building a technology-driven health system to scale and automate the delivery of musculoskeletal (MSK) care. Its platform addresses acute injury, chronic pain, and post-surgical rehabilitation through AI-powered motion tracking technology and a proprietary electrical nerve stimulation wearable device, primarily serving self-insured employers.

Investment Thesis
Hinge Health is the market leader in digital MSK care with 51% revenue growth to $588M in 2025 and 2026 guidance of $732-742M (25% growth), significantly exceeding analyst expectations [1]. The company's AI-powered motion tracking and wearable nerve stimulation technology create a differentiated platform serving 2,000+ enterprise clients. Forward P/E of 19.2x on forward EPS of $2.32 is reasonable for a healthcare tech company growing 25%+ with guided non-GAAP operating margins of 21%, and the virtually debt-free balance sheet (D/E 2.1%) provides financial flexibility [2].
The bear case centers on GAAP profitability — trailing EPS of -$7.77 and profit margin of -89.9% reflect massive stock-based compensation costs post-IPO, and trailing ROE of -132.9% indicates the company has not yet generated positive returns on invested capital on a GAAP basis [2]. The digital MSK market faces competitive pressure from Big Tech health platforms, traditional PT providers going digital, and AI-native startups (Sword Health). Employee concerns about AI-based performance surveillance without transparency could create cultural issues that impair execution. As a recently public company (IPO in 2025), Hinge Health lacks a track record of managing public market expectations, and any miss on the aggressive 2026 guidance could trigger a significant derating from the current $3.5B market cap.
