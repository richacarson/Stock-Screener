Company Profile
Consumer Cyclical ·Recreational Vehicles ·NYSE ·United States
Harley-Davidson is an iconic American motorcycle manufacturer founded in 1903, producing heavyweight cruiser and touring motorcycles. The company operates through three segments: Harley-Davidson Motor Company (HDMC), Harley-Davidson Financial Services (HDFS), and LiveWire (electric motorcycles). HOG also operates the Harley Owners Group (H.O.G.), one of the world's largest brand communities.

Investment Thesis
Harley-Davidson is a deeply discounted American icon trading at 11.2x forward P/E and 7.6x trailing P/E — a valuation that implies permanent earnings impairment. The $2.5B market cap company generates $4.47B in revenue, owns the dominant U.S. heavyweight motorcycle brand with 120+ years of heritage, operates a profitable financial services arm (HDFS reported record $490M operating income in 2025), and pays a 3.5% dividend yield with a conservative 25.9% payout ratio [1]. Management's $150M annual cost savings program beginning H2 2026 could restore HDMC profitability, and the deep value multiple provides asymmetric upside if motorcycle demand normalizes [1]. The H.O.G. community of 1M+ members creates brand loyalty unmatched in consumer cyclicals.
The bear case is substantial and multi-layered. Revenue declined 27.8% YoY to $4.47B, HDMC turned loss-making in Q4 2025 with a $30M gross loss on tariffs and incentives, and 2026 guidance of +$10M to -$40M HDMC operating income signals continued near-term pain [1]. The core customer demographic is aging — the average Harley buyer is 50+ years old — and the company has failed to attract younger riders despite years of trying. LiveWire's $70-80M annual operating loss drains capital with minimal electric motorcycle sales [1]. Leadership instability (CEO change, C-suite reshuffling, shareholder activism), the DEI controversy that alienated both progressive and conservative customers, and a 'transition year' posture without a clear strategic vision create significant execution risk [2][3]. Total debt of $3.05B against a $2.5B market cap means the balance sheet offers limited cushion if the downturn deepens. Revenue has declined from $5.84B (2023) to $4.47B (2025) — a structural decline, not a cyclical dip [4].
Key Catalysts
  1. $150M annual cost savings program beginning H2 2026 could restore HDMC profitability and expand margins if management executes on restructuring [1]
  2. Housing and consumer confidence recovery driving discretionary spending on big-ticket items like motorcycles, potentially normalizing unit volumes from depressed 2025 levels [1]
  3. Deep value multiple of 11.2x forward P/E with 3.5% dividend yield provides asymmetric upside if the turnaround gains traction [4]
Key Risks
  1. Structural demand decline — aging core demographic (average buyer 50+) and failure to attract younger riders suggests the revenue decline from $5.84B to $4.47B may be permanent, not cyclical [1]
  2. LiveWire electric motorcycle losses of $70-80M annually drain capital with no clear path to profitability, representing a strategic bet that has not paid off [1]
  3. Leadership instability and governance concerns — CEO turnover, C-suite reshuffling, and shareholder activism undermine strategic continuity during a critical transition period [2][3]
