Investment Thesis
Robinhood has transformed from a struggling meme-stock platform into an S&P 500 diversified fintech generating $4.47B in revenue with 42% profit margins and 22% ROE. Revenue grew at a 49% CAGR over three years, and the forward PEG of 0.76x suggests the market has not fully priced the durability of this growth. With 27M funded customers, $324B in platform assets, and expansion into prediction markets, venture investing, credit cards, and international crypto (Bitstamp), Robinhood has multiple growth vectors beyond volatile crypto trading. Analysts project 20-22% revenue growth in 2026 with consensus price targets implying 40%+ upside.
The primary risk is revenue quality: crypto trading revenue remains volatile and cyclical, declining meaningfully from Q3 to Q4 2025 peaks. The company has paid over $150M in cumulative regulatory fines and faces ongoing SEC, FINRA, and state-level scrutiny that could intensify as it expands into prediction markets and tokenized assets. D/E of 136% reflects significant leverage for a financial services firm. The stock trades at 34.6x trailing earnings, pricing in significant growth that depends on sustained market activity levels. Competition from Schwab, Fidelity, and SoFi in crypto trading could compress Robinhood's transaction revenue margins.
