Investment Thesis
HPP is a deeply contrarian play on the West Coast office recovery, trading at just $460M market cap — a fraction of the portfolio's replacement cost — with AI-driven leasing demand providing a potential inflection catalyst [1][3]. The company reported Q4 2025 revenue of $256M (beating estimates by 52%) and guided FY2026 FFO of $0.96-$1.06 with sequential improvement starting Q2 2026, suggesting the trough may be forming [1]. AI companies like Databricks and Tools for Humanity are driving new lease signings in HPP's core San Francisco, Silicon Valley, and Seattle markets, where AI is the fastest-growing source of office demand [3]. Management has stabilized the balance sheet with $1B liquidity, 100% fixed/capped debt, and no maturities until H2 2026, while targeting $200M-$300M of FFO-accretive asset sales [1]. The studio/sound stage portfolio provides entertainment industry exposure that diversifies beyond pure office risk.
The bear case is severe and reflects deep fundamental distress. Trailing EPS of -$12.81, profit margin of -66.4%, and ROE of -18.6% indicate a company that has destroyed enormous value — the stock has declined over 90% from 2022 highs [5]. D/E of 117% with $3.76B debt against $142M cash creates significant financial risk, and debt maturities beginning H2 2026 represent a near-term refinancing hurdle. The suspended dividend eliminates the income thesis that traditionally supports REIT valuations, and the 5-year average yield of 6.3% is gone. Office occupancy guidance of 80-82% remains well below pre-pandemic levels, and structural remote work trends may permanently impair demand for West Coast office space. Glassdoor ratings of 3.3/5.0 and complaints about cliquish culture suggest organizational challenges during a period requiring exceptional execution [3]. The beta of 1.53 amplifies market downside, and the negative forward P/E indicates no near-term path to GAAP profitability [5].

Key Risks
  1. Severe financial distress — trailing EPS of -$12.81, D/E of 117%, and $3.76B debt with H2 2026 maturities approaching create refinancing and solvency risk [5]
  2. Structural remote work headwinds — office occupancy guidance of 80-82% may represent a permanent impairment to West Coast office demand, not a cyclical trough [1]
  3. AI disruption to studio assets — AI-generated content and virtual production could reduce demand for physical sound stages, threatening the entertainment portfolio hedge [3]
