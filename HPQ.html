Company Profile

HP Inc. provides personal computing, printing, 3D printing, and related technologies globally. The company operates through Personal Systems (desktops, notebooks, workstations), Printing (consumer/commercial printers, supplies, solutions), and Corporate Investments segments. Founded in 1939, HP split from Hewlett-Packard Enterprise in 2015.

Investment Thesis
HP Inc. offers a compelling value proposition with a 5.77% dividend yield, trailing P/E of 7.9x, and forward P/E of 7.0x against FY2026 EPS guidance of $2.90-$3.20[1]. Q1 FY2026 revenue of $14.4B (+6.9% YoY) was driven by Personal Systems growth of 11% as AI PCs reached 35% of shipments amid a Windows 11 refresh cycle. Free cash flow guidance of $2.8-3.0B supports the dividend and continued buybacks.
However, the investment case faces structural headwinds: negative stockholders' equity from aggressive buybacks, declining printing revenue (-2% YoY), memory cost inflation compressing margins (DRAM/NAND at 35% of PC BOM vs. historical 15-18%), and a leadership vacuum with interim CEO Bruce Broussard navigating the transition[1][2]. HP is a mature hardware company offering income rather than growth, but cultural deterioration and AI-driven secular decline in printing prevent a HOLD rating despite attractive valuation.
Key Catalysts
  1. AI PC penetration reaching 50% of shipments by year-end 2026 could drive meaningful ASP uplift and margin expansion in Personal Systems
  2. Resolution of leadership transition with permanent CEO appointment could restore strategic clarity and employee confidence
  3. Windows 11 enterprise refresh cycle expected to sustain elevated demand through FY2026-2027
Key Risks
  1. Memory cost inflation (DRAM/NAND at 35% of BOM vs. 15-18% historically) compressing margins with management guiding to low end of FY2026 EPS range
  2. Structural decline in printing (30% of revenue) accelerating as AI-driven digital workflows reduce physical printing demand
  3. Negative stockholders' equity (-$346M) from debt-funded buybacks creates fragility if PC demand cycle turns negative
