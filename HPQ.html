Investment Thesis
HP Inc. offers compelling income characteristics with a 5.7% dividend yield, 9.1% 5-year dividend growth CAGR, and a deeply discounted forward P/E of 7.1x — among the cheapest valuations in large-cap tech [1]. The $1B AI-driven transformation program targeting operational savings by FY2028, combined with emerging growth in industrial graphics ($1.8B+ revenue with nine consecutive quarters of YoY growth) and 3D printing, provides incremental upside beyond the core PC/printing business. FQ1 2026 revenue of $14.4B grew 6.9% YoY with non-GAAP EPS of $0.81, up 9.5% [1].
The bear case centers on secular decline in HP's core printing business (down 4% YoY in Q4) and commoditized PC hardware with thin 4.5% profit margins, creating vulnerability to any macroeconomic slowdown [1]. Negative stockholders equity (-$346M) from aggressive buybacks raises questions about balance sheet health, and the chronic CEO instability (Lores departed 2026, replaced by interim CEO Broussard) creates strategic uncertainty during a critical AI transformation period [2]. Revenue has declined from $62.9B in FY2022 to $55.3B in FY2025, and the 3-year CAGR of -4.2% suggests the organic business is shrinking even as the company claims AI-driven growth.
Key Catalysts
  1. AI PC upgrade cycle could accelerate Personal Systems revenue growth beyond the 8% YoY achieved in Q4 FY2025 as enterprises refresh aging fleets [1]
  2. $1B AI transformation program reaching $300M in gross savings by end of FY2026 could materially expand profit margins from the current thin 4.5% [2]
  3. Industrial graphics and 3D printing businesses reaching scale ($1.8B+) could diversify revenue mix away from declining traditional printing [1]
Key Risks
  1. Secular printing decline accelerates as AI-powered digital workflows further reduce physical printing volumes, compressing HP's higher-margin supplies revenue [1]
  2. CEO transition from Enrique Lores to interim CEO Bruce Broussard creates strategic uncertainty during the critical AI transformation execution window [2]
  3. Negative stockholders equity and $10.9B total debt limit financial flexibility if PC/printing demand deteriorates or the AI transformation restructuring costs escalate beyond $650M [3]

AI Risk Protection (4/9): HP faces meaningful AI disruption risk across its core business lines. The PC segment benefits from AI PC upgrade cycles in the near term, but generative AI tools and cloud computing increasingly shift computing workloads away from local hardware, threatening long-term PC demand [2]. The printing business faces secular decline as AI-powered digital workflows reduce physical printing volumes — a structural headwind that AI accelerates rather than mitigates. HP's own acknowledgment of AI-driven workforce reductions (4,000-6,000 jobs by FY2028) confirms that AI displaces labor-intensive functions within its own operations [2]. The industrial graphics and 3D printing segments are more defensible but remain small relative to core PC/printing revenue. AI Opportunity (1/1): HP generates revenue from AI-enabled PCs and is deploying AI across operations for $1B in targeted savings by FY2028, representing tangible AI-driven value creation [1].
