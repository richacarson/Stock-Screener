Investment Thesis
HealthEquity is the dominant independent HSA platform with 17.8M accounts and $36B+ in assets, delivering record FY2026 results — $1.31B revenue (9% growth) with net income surging 123% to $215.2M as margins expanded from 8% to 16% [1]. At 15.4x forward P/E with PEG of 1.19, the stock is attractively valued for a company with durable growth drivers: secular HSA adoption (employer shift to high-deductible plans), custodial revenue benefiting from interest rates, and investment platform expansion. The Purple Culture creates sustainable competitive advantages through employee retention, member satisfaction, and employer relationships that compound annually through enrollment cycles [2]. Adjusted EBITDA grew 20% to $566M, demonstrating operating leverage in a capital-light model.
The bear case centers on revenue growth deceleration and interest rate sensitivity. Revenue growth of 9% YoY is moderate for a stock trading at 33x trailing earnings, and custodial revenue ($637M, 49% of total) is sensitive to interest rate direction — rate cuts could compress margins [1]. The CEO transition from founder-era leader Jon Kessler to Scott Cutler in January 2025 introduces execution risk during a critical scaling period. Competition from Fidelity, Optum, and fintech disruptors could pressure pricing and market share in employer HSA administration. EPS of $0.58 in Q4 missed some estimates despite revenue beats, and the company's history of acquisition-driven growth (WageWorks debt) constrains capital allocation flexibility with 47.5% D/E [1][5].
Key Catalysts
  1. Continued margin expansion from 16% net income margin toward 20%+ as AI-powered automation reduces per-account servicing costs and custodial revenue scales [1][2]
  2. HSA asset growth compounding — $36B+ growing with contributions, investment returns, and new account additions creating durable custodial revenue flywheel [1]
  3. Raised FY2027 guidance reflecting confidence in sustained growth from new employer wins and deepening existing relationships through platform expansion [1]
Key Risks
  1. Interest rate sensitivity — custodial revenue ($637M, 49% of total) depends on cash deposit spread income that would compress in a falling rate environment [1]
  2. Revenue growth deceleration to 9% YoY may not justify 33x trailing P/E, creating multiple compression risk if growth doesn't reaccelerate [5]
  3. Competitive pressure from Fidelity and fintech platforms offering lower-cost HSA solutions with superior digital experiences could pressure market share gains [2]
