Company Profile
Real Estate ·REIT - Healthcare Facilities ·NYSE ·United States
Healthcare Realty Trust is a real estate investment trust (REIT) focused on owning, managing, and developing outpatient medical properties. The company owns approximately 660 properties totaling over 40 million square feet across major healthcare markets, primarily leased to hospital systems, physician groups, and other healthcare providers.

Investment Thesis
Healthcare Realty offers a 5.92% dividend yield and trades at approximately 10.7x FFO ($17.29 / $1.61 FFO), providing an attractive entry point for a pure-play medical outpatient REIT with 660+ properties in major healthcare markets [1]. The company's deleveraging to 5.4x net debt/EBITDA with $1.4B liquidity provides financial stability, and 2026 guidance of 3.5-4.5% same-store NOI growth demonstrates organic portfolio improvement [1]. Healthcare real estate benefits from secular tailwinds — aging demographics, shift from inpatient to outpatient care, and hospital system expansion into suburban markets. The GRESB 2 Green Star rating and seventh consecutive Corporate Responsibility Report reflect strong ESG practices that attract institutional capital [4]. If the company stabilizes leadership (permanent CEO appointment) and sustains NOI growth, the stock could rerate toward the 12-14x FFO range typical of high-quality healthcare REITs.
The bear case centers on leadership instability, dividend deterioration, and balance sheet concerns. The CEO departed in November 2024 with no permanent replacement — interim CEO Connie Moore and a wholesale leadership restructuring create strategic uncertainty [2]. The 22.6% dividend cut in 2025 (from $0.31 to $0.24/quarter) signals earnings pressure, and the GAAP payout ratio of 251% reflects the gap between accounting income and cash flow [5]. Revenue has declined from $1.33B (2023) to $1.15B (2025) as the company disposes of non-core assets, creating a smaller portfolio with uncertain growth trajectory. Total debt of $4.15B against minimal cash requires ongoing capital markets access. 2026 FFO guidance midpoint of $1.61 is flat YoY, with management acknowledging the ~5% core earnings growth is offset by dilution from asset sales and deleveraging [1]. The broader REIT sector faces headwinds from elevated interest rates that compress cap rate spreads and increase refinancing costs. Competition from Physicians Realty (now merged with Healthpeak) and other healthcare REITs for premium medical office assets continues to intensify [5].
Key Catalysts
  1. Permanent CEO appointment providing strategic clarity and restoring investor confidence after 18+ months of interim leadership [2]
  2. Same-store NOI growth of 3.5-4.5% in 2026 driven by occupancy improvement, re-leasing spreads, and embedded rent escalators across the 660-property portfolio [1]
  3. Completion of deleveraging program with $1.4B liquidity and 5.4x net debt/EBITDA positioning the company for opportunistic acquisitions as interest rates stabilize [1]
Key Risks
  1. Leadership vacancy risk — no permanent CEO 18+ months after Todd Meredith's departure, with interim management and wholesale executive restructuring creating strategic drift [2]
  2. Dividend sustainability — the 22.6% cut in 2025 and flat FFO guidance for 2026 suggest limited capacity for dividend restoration, and further cuts are possible if NOI growth disappoints [1][5]
  3. Interest rate sensitivity — $4.15B total debt with REIT refinancing needs creates exposure to elevated rates, which compress cap rate spreads and increase cost of capital for acquisitions [5]
