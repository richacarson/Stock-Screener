Investment Thesis
H&R Block is executing a credible AI-powered transformation under new CEO Curtis Campbell (former TaxAct CEO, Intuit product leader). Q2 FY2026 revenue grew 11.1% to $198.9M, driven by higher volume and net average charge in assisted tax, Wave subscription growth, and DIY software gains [1]. The OpenAI partnership for AI Tax Assist (consumer) and Sidekick (professional) turns AI from a threat into a force multiplier across 60,000 tax pros and millions of DIY users [2]. FY2026 guidance of $3.88-$3.90B revenue, $1.02B+ EBITDA, and $4.85-$5.00 adjusted EPS supports continued aggressive buybacks and a ~2.3% dividend yield with 27 consecutive years paid [1]. At ~12x forward earnings, HRB offers quality cash flow, AI optionality, and disciplined capital return.
The bear case is secular AI substitution. As AI tax assistants become more capable, the value of the 9,000-office assisted channel could compress — Seeking Alpha and Simply Wall St have both flagged HRB as 'high yield, high uncertainty in an AI-driven future' [2]. Intuit TurboTax continues to gain DIY share with superior software economics, and free IRS Direct File expansion could pressure pricing for simple filers. Balance sheet leverage is optically high (debt far exceeds negative book equity from years of buybacks), though $1B+ EBITDA supports the structure. Revenue growth is mid-single-digit, limiting upside unless Campbell successfully expands into year-round financial services. Glassdoor compensation scores are low (3.1/5) and seasonal preparer retention is a perennial challenge [2].
Key Catalysts
  1. OpenAI partnership delivering AI Tax Assist and Sidekick at scale, potentially increasing NAC and retention across 60,000 tax pros [2]
  2. Curtis Campbell's year-round financial platform strategy — if executed, could unlock new revenue streams beyond tax season [2]
  3. Continued aggressive buybacks driving EPS growth above revenue growth at ~12x forward multiple [1]
Key Risks
  1. AI-driven displacement of assisted tax — if AI DIY becomes materially better, premium pricing power erodes [2]
  2. IRS Direct File expansion and Intuit TurboTax competitive pressure on DIY segment [2]
  3. Seasonality and concentration — the business remains heavily dependent on Q3/Q4 fiscal tax season, limiting visibility [1]
