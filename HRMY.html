Investment Thesis
Harmony Biosciences represents a rare value opportunity in biotech — a profitable, growing rare disease company trading at just 4.8x forward earnings with $882.5M in cash (roughly 50% of market cap) and WAKIX revenue guided to exceed $1 billion in 2026 [1][6]. The company has delivered six consecutive years of revenue growth (21.1% YoY to $868M in 2025) and profitability, with patent protection securing the WAKIX franchise through at least September 2029 (or March 2030 with pediatric exclusivity) [1][4]. The pipeline offers significant upside optionality — the orexin-2 agonist (BP1.15205) Phase 1 data in mid-2026, Pitolisant GR NDA in Q2 2026, and ONSTRIDE Phase 3 trials could substantially expand the addressable market beyond the current 8,500 patients out of 80,000 diagnosed narcolepsy patients [1][3].
The bear case centers on single-drug concentration risk — WAKIX accounts for 100% of revenue, and any safety signal, competitive entry, or patent challenge could be devastating [1]. The Scorpion Capital short-seller report in March 2023 alleged safety concerns including cardiac events, kickback schemes, and patent vulnerabilities, causing a 27% stock drop; while the company has defended against these allegations, the securities fraud investigations by Wolf Popper and Rosen Law remain unresolved [5]. CEO and CFO insider selling totaling $2.7M+ in late 2025/early 2026 raises questions about near-term conviction despite strong operational results [6]. GAAP net income declined from $181M (2022) to $159M (2025) despite revenue doubling, suggesting rising costs from pipeline investments and potential margin pressure as the company transitions from single-drug to platform model [6].
Key Catalysts
  1. WAKIX revenue crossing $1 billion in 2026 validates blockbuster status and supports continued penetration of the 80,000 diagnosed narcolepsy patient population [1]
  2. Orexin-2 agonist (BP1.15205) Phase 1 clinical data in mid-2026 could establish a next-generation narcolepsy treatment with best-in-class potential [3]
  3. Pitolisant GR NDA submission in Q2 2026 with PDUFA target Q1 2027 provides near-term regulatory catalyst for franchise lifecycle extension [1]
Key Risks
  1. 100% revenue concentration in WAKIX creates existential risk from any safety signal, competitive displacement by orexin agonists (Takeda/Jazz), or successful generic challenge before 2029 patent expiry [1][4]
  2. Unresolved securities fraud investigations following Scorpion Capital's March 2023 short-seller report alleging cardiac safety concerns and kickback schemes [5]
  3. CEO and CFO insider selling of $2.7M+ in late 2025/early 2026 despite strong operational performance and deeply discounted valuation [6]
