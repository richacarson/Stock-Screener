Company Profile
Healthcare ·Biotechnology ·NASDAQ ·United States
Harmony Biosciences is a commercial-stage pharmaceutical company focused on developing and commercializing therapies for patients with rare neurological diseases. Its lead product WAKIX (pitolisant) is the first and only FDA-approved treatment that increases histamine signaling in the brain for excessive daytime sleepiness and cataplexy in narcolepsy, with a pipeline expanding into Prader-Willi Syndrome, Myotonic Dystrophy, and other rare conditions.
293 Employees ·www.harmonybiosciences.com

Investment Thesis
Harmony Biosciences is a rare disease pharma franchise approaching blockbuster status with WAKIX, guiding $1.0-$1.04B in 2026 revenue (17.5% growth at midpoint) after delivering $868.5M in FY2025 (22% YoY growth) [1]. At 4.8x forward earnings on $5.79 forward EPS, the stock is remarkably cheap for a profitable biotech with 20.75% ROE, 19.4% D/E, and a differentiated mechanism of action in narcolepsy [6]. The pediatric narcolepsy FDA approval expands the addressable market, patent settlements delay generic competition to at least March 2030, and the pitolisant GR NDA submission in Q2 2026 could open additional commercial opportunities [1][2]. With 293 employees generating $868M in revenue ($2.96M per employee), this is one of the most capital-efficient pharma franchises in the market. HC Wainwright maintains a Buy rating, and the consensus price target of $43.78 implies 57% upside [4].
The bear case centers on single-product concentration and patent risk. WAKIX represents essentially all of Harmony's revenue, creating binary risk if generic competition arrives earlier than the 2030 settlement timeline [1][4]. The AET Pharma bench trial in Delaware District Court remains unresolved, and Deutsche Bank downgraded to Hold citing concerns that the judge's early post-trial comments could indicate a negative outcome, potentially triggering generic entry as early as 2026 [4]. The Scorpion Capital short-seller report from 2023 alleging 'potentially improper behavior' continues to weigh on investor sentiment despite strong financial execution [5]. Q4 2025 saw operating expenses surge 50% YoY with G&A costs nearly doubling to $57.6M, compressing operating margins from 27.7% to 15.8% — raising questions about cost discipline [1]. Earnings growth was -55% in Q4 despite 21% revenue growth. CFO Sandip Kapadia's full liquidation of direct holdings ($752,800) in January 2026, while pre-planned, adds to the cautious insider sentiment [3].
