Investment Thesis
Harmony Biosciences is an exceptionally undervalued biotech at 4.8x forward P/E with $1B+ WAKIX revenue guidance, $882.5M cash, 20.8% ROE, and a forward PEG of ~0.27. The pediatric cataplexy FDA approval (Feb 2026) expands the addressable market, generic competition is settled until March 2030 with lifecycle management targeting the 2040s, and the pipeline includes five Phase 3 programs plus a potential best-in-class orexin-2 agonist. The 293-person company generates $868M in revenue with exceptional capital efficiency, and the Inspire ALIGNED score (42) supports faith-based investment considerations. HC Wainwright ($55 target) and Needham ($44 target) maintain Buy ratings.
The bear case centers on single-product concentration risk—WAKIX generates essentially 100% of revenue, and any unexpected safety signal, competitive displacement, or accelerated generic entry would be catastrophic. Q4 earnings missed by $0.46 as G&A expenses nearly doubled, suggesting cost discipline issues during pipeline expansion. The five simultaneous Phase 3 programs represent significant binary clinical risk, and the orexin-2 agonist is only in first-in-human studies. UBS trimmed its target to $36 with neutral rating, and the stock has declined significantly, suggesting the market is concerned about execution risk and rising costs. The 858K daily volume creates modest liquidity constraints for larger institutional investors.
