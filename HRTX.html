Company Profile
Healthcare ·Biotechnology ·NASDAQ ·United States
Heron Therapeutics is a commercial-stage biotechnology company focused on acute care and oncology therapeutics using its proprietary Biochronomer drug delivery technology. Key products include ZYNRELEF (non-opioid post-surgical pain), CINVANTI and APONVIE (chemotherapy-induced nausea/vomiting prevention), and SUSTOL (extended-release anti-emetic). The company is headquartered in Cary, North Carolina.
128 Employees ·www.herontx.com

Investment Thesis
Heron Therapeutics offers a speculative turnaround story with 2025 revenue of $154.9M (up 7.4% YoY) and positive adjusted EBITDA of $14.7M for the first time, exceeding guidance [1]. ZYNRELEF — the company's non-opioid post-surgical pain treatment — is the growth catalyst, rising 35% sequentially in Q4 2025 to ~$12.5M and benefiting from CMS pass-through payment status expanding hospital outpatient reimbursement [1][3]. The 2026 guidance of $173-183M revenue (12-18% growth) with $10-20M adjusted EBITDA suggests a credible path toward GAAP profitability. At a forward P/E of 2.0x and $192M market cap, the stock prices in extreme pessimism — if Heron achieves the $0.51 forward EPS estimate, the stock would re-rate significantly from $1.02 [4].
The bear case is severe. Heron's D/E ratio of 981% with $140.6M debt against a $192M market cap creates existential financial risk if revenue growth stalls or the path to profitability takes longer than expected [4]. The company has been unprofitable since its founding in 1983, burning through investor capital for four decades with cumulative net losses well exceeding $1B. Profit margin of -13.0% and negative free cash flow of -$28.5M in 2025 demonstrate the company still consumes more cash than it generates [4]. CINVANTI, the largest revenue contributor (~$22.9M Q4), faces price erosion that management expects to offset unit volume growth, resulting in flat sales [1]. The $1.02 stock price carries meaningful delisting risk if it remains below exchange minimums. With only $46.6M cash and $140.6M debt, the balance sheet provides minimal margin of safety if product launches disappoint [4].
