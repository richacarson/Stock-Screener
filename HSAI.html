Investment Thesis
Hesai is the world's first profitable LiDAR company and the dominant player in automotive LiDAR with 40%+ market share, delivering transformative FY2025 results — $433M revenue (+46% YoY), $62M net income (vs. $102M loss in 2024), and 1.62M units shipped (+223%) [1]. The company is guiding to 3-3.5M unit shipments in 2026 (nearly doubling again), with production capacity scaling to 4M+ units, and Chinese OEMs are adopting multi-LiDAR configurations (3-6 units per vehicle) that multiply the addressable market per car [1]. With $1.07B in cash, clean D/E of 9%, 41.8% gross margins, and a PEG of 0.82x, Hesai offers exceptional value for a 40%+ growth company at 25x forward P/E [5]. The robotics segment (+426% YoY to 239K units) represents a second growth vector beyond automotive [1].
The bear case centers on technology risk, geopolitics, and ASP pressure. Tesla's camera-only autonomous driving approach directly challenges the LiDAR value proposition — if pure-vision FSD proves sufficient, the LiDAR industry could face existential demand destruction [2]. ASP declined from ~RMB 1,867 in FY2025 to ~RMB 1,585 in Q4 2025, reflecting mix shift toward lower-cost products and OEM pricing pressure, which could compress margins even as volumes grow [1]. As a Chinese company listed on NASDAQ, Hesai faces delisting risk under the HFCAA, US-China trade restrictions, and potential tariff escalation that could limit international expansion [5]. Revenue concentration in Chinese automakers creates country-specific cyclical risk, and the autonomous driving timeline remains uncertain — a key safety incident could slow industry adoption. Free cash flow was negative at -$875M due to heavy capex investment in manufacturing capacity [5].
Key Catalysts
  1. 2026 shipment guidance of 3-3.5M units represents near-doubling from FY2025, with multi-LiDAR configurations (3-6 units per vehicle) from Li Auto, Xiaomi, and Changan driving volume acceleration [1]
  2. Robotics segment inflection — 239K units shipped in FY2025 (+426% YoY) demonstrates LiDAR demand expanding beyond automotive into delivery robots, AGVs, and industrial applications [1]

Key Risks
  1. Camera-only autonomous driving (Tesla FSD pure-vision approach) could prove sufficient, undermining the core value proposition of LiDAR as a necessary sensor for autonomous driving [2]
  2. Geopolitical risk — Chinese company on NASDAQ faces potential delisting under HFCAA, US export controls, and tariff escalation that could constrain international expansion and investor access [5]
  3. Average selling price compression — ASP declined ~15% from FY2025 average to Q4 2025 due to mix shift and OEM pricing pressure, threatening margin sustainability even as volumes scale [1]

AI Resilience (25%)
AI Resilience MODERATE
6
AI Risk Protection (5/9): Hesai occupies a complex position in the AI ecosystem — its LiDAR hardware is critical infrastructure for autonomous driving AI, but the company faces existential risk from AI-driven technological substitution [2][3]. Camera-only autonomous driving approaches (championed by Tesla's pure-vision FSD) represent a direct threat to the LiDAR value proposition; if AI perception algorithms advance enough to make cameras sufficient, LiDAR could become unnecessary for ADAS applications. Current market dynamics favor LiDAR inclusion (Chinese OEMs are adding 3-6 units per vehicle), but the long-term technology debate remains unresolved [1]. Additionally, as a Chinese company listed in the US, Hesai faces geopolitical risks including potential delisting, export controls, and tariff escalation that could constrain growth [5]. AI Opportunity (1/1): Hesai generates 100% of its revenue from AI-enabling hardware — its LiDAR products are essential sensors for autonomous driving, robotics, and spatial intelligence applications, representing $433M in FY2025 AI-adjacent revenue [1].
