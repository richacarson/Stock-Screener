Investment Thesis
Hershey offers a compelling income story backed by 96 consecutive years of dividend payments, a 3.0% yield, and 11.7% five-year dividend growth CAGR — one of the strongest income profiles in consumer staples [5]. Q4 2025 revenue grew 7% to $3.09B with the Salty Snacks segment surging 28%, demonstrating the success of portfolio diversification beyond confectionery [1]. The 2026 guidance of $8.20-$8.52 adjusted EPS on $12.2B+ revenue implies significant margin recovery from the cocoa-cost-compressed 2025, and at 19.5x forward P/E, the stock is attractively valued for a company with dominant U.S. confectionery market share and a century-long brand moat [1]. New CEO Kirk Tanner brings CPG execution expertise from PepsiCo and Wendy's, and the 'One Hershey' restructuring should unlock operational efficiencies.
The bear case centers on cocoa cost inflation that may prove structural rather than cyclical. Cocoa futures tripled from 2023 to 2025, crushing trailing profit margins to 7.6% from historical ~16% levels [1]. If cocoa costs remain elevated, the $8.20-$8.52 EPS guidance assumes pricing actions that could sacrifice volume — Hershey already experienced volume declines in 2024. The CEO transition creates execution risk as Kirk Tanner restructures during a period of margin stress, and Glassdoor reviews suggest employee morale is suffering [2]. The Hershey Trust's aggressive selling of $24M+ in shares in April 2026 alone raises questions about even the controlling shareholder's near-term conviction [4]. International revenue remains a modest ~10% of total, limiting diversification against U.S. consumer spending weakness. The payout ratio of 126% on trailing earnings is technically unsustainable, though it normalizes on forward estimates.
Key Catalysts
  1. Margin recovery in 2026 as pricing actions flow through and cocoa cost comparisons ease, with guidance implying gross margin expansion of 400-600bps from Q4 2025 trough [1]
  2. Salty Snacks segment reaching critical mass — 28% Q4 growth and LesserEvil integration could drive the segment toward $1.5B annual revenue, diversifying away from confectionery concentration [1]
  3. New CEO Kirk Tanner's 'One Hershey' restructuring unlocking $100M+ in operational efficiencies through streamlined division structure and supply chain optimization [2]
Key Risks
  1. Cocoa cost inflation remains structural — West African crop disease and climate disruption could keep cocoa futures elevated, preventing the margin recovery embedded in 2026 guidance [1]
  2. CEO transition execution risk — Kirk Tanner is restructuring leadership, strategy, and operations simultaneously during the worst margin environment in a decade, risking organizational fatigue and talent attrition [2]
  3. Volume-price tradeoff — aggressive pricing to offset cocoa costs risks consumer trade-down to private label or non-chocolate alternatives, particularly among price-sensitive shoppers in an inflationary environment [1]
