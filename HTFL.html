Investment Thesis
HeartFlow is a high-growth AI-powered medical technology company that delivered 40% revenue growth in FY2025 to $176M, with guidance for $218M-$222M in 2026 (24-26% growth) [1]. The company's non-invasive cardiac diagnostic platform is a genuine category creator, with gross margins expanding to 79.5% in Q4 and guided to 80-81% for 2026. The Plaque Analysis product received a Category 1 CPT code with 57% commercial coverage, opening a significant new revenue stream. Cash of $280.2M provides adequate runway, and the technology addresses a $10B+ cardiovascular diagnostic market [1][3].
The bear case centers on persistent unprofitability and cultural dysfunction. HeartFlow lost $116.8M in FY2025 on $176M revenue (-66% profit margin), with operating cash flow of -$54M [4]. After 19 years in business, the path to GAAP profitability remains unclear despite improving unit economics. Glassdoor reviews (2.9/5.0) reveal significant internal challenges including low morale, forced culture, and leadership credibility issues [2]. Forward P/E is meaningless at -101x. Competition from AI-enabled cardiac imaging entrants could pressure pricing, and the company's reliance on favorable reimbursement decisions creates regulatory risk. The $2.6B market cap at 15x trailing revenue requires sustained 25%+ growth and a credible profitability timeline to justify current valuation.

Key Risks

  2. Cultural dysfunction with Glassdoor rating of 2.9/5.0 and only 43% employee recommendation rate could impair talent retention and execution quality at a critical growth stage [2]
  3. Reimbursement dependency — the commercial viability of Plaque Analysis and FFRCT depends on continued favorable Medicare and commercial payer coverage decisions, which could be reversed [1]
