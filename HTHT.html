Investment Thesis
H World Group is China's second-largest hotel operator with 10,000+ properties and a proven franchise model generating 33.5% adjusted EBITDA margins [1]. Full-year 2025 results showed adjusted net income surging 32.9% to RMB 4.9B on revenue of RMB 25.3B (up 5.9% YoY), with the higher-margin managed and franchised segment growing faster than the legacy-owned business [1]. At 16.7x forward P/E with a PEG of 0.27, the stock is compellingly valued for a company guiding 12-16% managed/franchised revenue growth in 2026 with 2,200-2,300 planned hotel openings [1]. ROE of 40.5% and free cash flow of RMB 6.2B demonstrate capital efficiency, while the shift toward asset-light franchising should continue to improve returns and margins over time [4].
The bear case centers on China macro risk and leverage. D/E of 278.4% reflects the capital-intensive hotel model, and while RMB 15.3B in cash provides a buffer, the RMB 36.1B debt load creates refinancing risk in a rising rate environment [4]. Revenue growth decelerated to 5.9% in 2025 from 36.4% in 2024 as COVID recovery normalized, and 2026 guidance of 2-6% group revenue growth suggests further moderation [1]. RevPAR is guided flat to slightly positive for 2026, indicating limited pricing power in a competitive Chinese hospitality market [1]. The Legacy-DH international segment remains a drag on margins. As a China-domiciled company trading as ADRs on NASDAQ, HTHT carries regulatory and geopolitical risk including potential delisting concerns and capital controls [4].
Key Catalysts
  1. Managed and franchised revenue guided to grow 12-16% in 2026 as asset-light model scales, with 2,200-2,300 planned hotel openings driving network expansion [1]
  2. Adjusted EBITDA margin expanded 4.9pp to 33.5% in 2025, with continued franchise mix shift expected to drive further margin improvement [1]
  3. PEG ratio of 0.27 suggests significant upside potential if earnings growth trajectory of 30%+ continues through 2026-2027 [4]
Key Risks
  1. China macro slowdown and consumer confidence deterioration could compress RevPAR and occupancy rates, particularly in Tier 1/Tier 2 business travel markets [1]
  2. D/E of 278.4% with RMB 36.1B in debt creates refinancing risk and limits financial flexibility if the operating environment deteriorates [4]
  3. ADR/VIE structure carries inherent China regulatory and geopolitical risk, including potential forced delisting from US exchanges [4]

AI Resilience (25%)
AI Resilience LOW RISK
7
AI Risk Protection (6/9): Hotel operations involve physical-world services -- cleaning rooms, managing properties, providing hospitality -- that AI cannot replace. H World's core business of operating and franchising 10,000+ hotels across China is fundamentally protected from AI substitution [1]. However, AI-powered travel platforms (e.g., AI trip planners, dynamic pricing aggregators) could increase pricing pressure and reduce direct booking advantages, creating moderate channel disruption risk. The company's franchise-heavy model (managed and franchised revenue growing 12-16%) provides structural insulation since franchisees bear property risk [1]. AI Opportunity (1/1): H World is actively deploying AI through its intelligent hotel assistant (first in China's hotel industry), voice-controlled room features, and automated housekeeping dispatch systems, generating operational efficiencies and enhanced guest experience [2].
