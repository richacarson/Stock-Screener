Company Profile
Basic Materials ·Chemicals ·NYSE ·United States
Huntsman Corporation is a global manufacturer of differentiated and specialty chemicals operating through three segments: Polyurethanes, Performance Products, and Advanced Materials. The company produces MDI, polyols, amines, epoxies, and other specialty chemicals used in construction, automotive, aerospace, electronics, and consumer products, operating 55+ facilities across 25 countries.

Investment Thesis
Huntsman trades at $12.66 with a market cap of $2.2B on a deeply depressed earnings base — full-year 2025 adjusted EBITDA was just $146M (4% margin) on $3.7B in revenue [1]. The value case rests on cyclical recovery: D/E of 81% and continued dividend payments (even at reduced $0.088/quarter) suggest the balance sheet can survive the trough, and a normalization of polyurethanes demand could drive significant earnings leverage given the fixed-cost operating structure. The Tienen innovation center and Technology Portal investments position Huntsman for eventual product-led recovery [2].
The bear case is severe: Huntsman reported a $96M net loss in Q4 2025 with adjusted EBITDA of just $35M, and Q1 2026 guidance of $25-40M EBITDA signals no recovery in sight [1]. Revenue has declined from $8.0B in 2022 to $5.78B TTM, a 28% contraction. The dividend was slashed 65% to $0.088/quarter in Q4 2025, and the payout ratio of 180% suggests even the reduced dividend may not be sustainable from earnings. Forward P/E of 83.6x reflects analyst estimates of just $0.15 EPS for 2026, with the highest estimate at only $0.26 [1]. The 10% workforce reduction signals structural cost problems beyond cyclical weakness. Management acknowledged 'meaningful changes may not occur in the immediate term,' offering no catalyst for recovery [1]. The Comparably D- executive team rating and 33% positive employee business outlook suggest internal confidence is collapsing [2].
Key Catalysts
  1. Chemical industry cyclical recovery — polyurethanes and MDI demand tied to construction, automotive, and consumer spending could normalize as interest rate cuts stimulate end markets [1]
  2. Cost restructuring benefits from the 10% workforce reduction and operational efficiencies flowing through to margins, potentially improving EBITDA even without revenue growth [1]
  3. Innovation center in Tienen, Belgium driving new specialty product development and higher-margin formulations in advanced materials and performance products [2]
Key Risks
  1. Prolonged chemical industry downcycle — management guidance of $25-40M Q1 EBITDA with 'no meaningful near-term improvement' suggests the trough could extend through 2026, exhausting the balance sheet and forcing further dividend cuts or debt restructuring [1]
  2. Dividend sustainability — payout ratio of 180% on negative EPS means the reduced $0.088/quarter dividend is funded from cash reserves rather than earnings, creating a ticking clock on income payments [5]
  3. Structural demand decline — secular shifts away from traditional polyurethanes in construction (energy efficiency regulations favoring alternatives) and automotive (EV lightweighting preferences) could impair the cyclical recovery potential [1]
