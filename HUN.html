Company Profile
Basic Materials ·Specialty Chemicals ·NYSE ·United States
Huntsman Corporation manufactures and sells differentiated organic chemical products worldwide. The company operates through three segments: Polyurethanes (MDI-based products for insulation, automotive, adhesives, coatings), Performance Products (amines and maleic anhydride for energy, agriculture, pharmaceuticals), and Advanced Materials (epoxy and acrylic-based formulations for aerospace, electronics, industrial). Founded in 1970 by Jon M. Huntsman Sr., headquartered in The Woodlands, Texas.

Investment Thesis
Huntsman Corporation is a specialty chemicals manufacturer navigating a challenging cyclical trough with Q1 2026 revenue of $1.42B (essentially flat YoY) and a net loss of $53M [1]. Bright spots include Advanced Materials segment growth of 12% driven by aerospace demand, AI-enabled R&D acceleration through the Citrine Informatics partnership, and early-stage semiconductor materials qualification expecting $5M+ in 2026 [2][3]. The company maintains $0.9B in combined liquidity, and management guides toward sequential profitability improvement in Q2 2026 with 'stronger than expected demand' and margin expansion from pricing initiatives [3]. CEO Peter Huntsman's 5.09% personal ownership stake and net insider buying of 116,762 shares over 18 months provide alignment with shareholders [5]. The 3.09% dividend yield is supported by 18 consecutive years of payment.
The bear case is substantial. Revenue has declined from $8.0B in 2022 to $5.7B in 2025 — a 29% contraction reflecting broad-based demand weakness across construction, automotive, and industrial end markets [6]. Negative ROE (-8.89%), negative profit margin (-5.83%), and a forward P/E of 72x make valuation difficult to justify on current fundamentals. The debt load of $2.49B against a $1.98B market cap creates leverage risk if the cyclical recovery is delayed. European operations face structural headwinds from elevated natural gas costs, and the UK aniline facility operates under unfavorable energy policies with the Ethyleneamines facility at only ~50% capacity [3]. The dividend, while maintained for 18 years, is not covered by earnings or cash flows, raising sustainability concerns if the downturn extends into 2027.
Key Catalysts
  1. Q2 2026 sequential profitability improvement driven by seasonal demand pickup, pricing initiatives, and stronger-than-expected order activity in Advanced Materials aerospace applications
  2. Semiconductor-grade specialty amines qualification reaching $5M+ revenue contribution in 2026, with potential for significant growth as AI data center buildout accelerates demand for advanced packaging materials
  3. European operations transitioning to positive EBITDA as pricing actions offset elevated feedstock costs, potentially restoring consolidated profitability by H2 2026
Key Risks
  1. Revenue has contracted 29% from the 2022 peak ($8.0B to $5.7B) with no clear catalyst for recovery to prior levels — secular demand decline in core construction and automotive end markets could persist beyond the cyclical trough
  2. Dividend sustainability at risk: $0.35/share annual dividend requires approximately $61M against negative earnings and negative operating cash flow of -$53M in Q1 2026 — extended unprofitability could force a dividend cut
  3. Leverage risk: $2.49B total debt against $1.98B market cap with $1.69B net debt creates refinancing pressure if credit markets tighten or the cyclical recovery timeline extends beyond management's expectations
