Investment Thesis
Hut 8 is executing one of the most ambitious corporate transformations in public markets — pivoting from a Canadian Bitcoin miner to a Google-backed AI data center infrastructure platform. The $7B Fluidstack lease (backstopped by Google) for 245 MW at River Bend, scalable to 2,300 MW and $17.7B in potential value, provides contracted long-term revenue visibility that is transformative for a $5.3B market cap company [1][2]. FY2025 revenue grew 45% to $235.1M with gross margins expanding from 47% to 54%, while the 8,500 MW development pipeline provides years of growth runway [1]. CEO Genoot calls the Google deal 'the first domino to fall' with plans to replicate the model, and Roth Capital raised its price target to $80 with a Buy rating. Institutional ownership surged from sub-10% to approximately 70%, signaling broad institutional validation of the transformation [1].
The bear case centers on execution risk and current profitability. The net loss of $248M in FY2025 (vs. $331M net income in 2024) and adjusted EBITDA loss of $135.4M reflect a company burning cash during transformation, with ROE of -18.58% and profit margin of -96.19% [1][6]. The River Bend campus represents a massive $10B Phase I commitment with operations not commencing until Q2 2027, creating significant construction and timeline risk [2]. Bitcoin price volatility continues to impact results through unrealized gains/losses on the company's Bitcoin holdings. Q4 2025 revenue of $121.4M missed analyst estimates by 5.25%, suggesting the market may be getting ahead of actual execution [1]. Environmental and community opposition to data center expansion in Louisiana could create permitting delays. The forward consensus EPS of -$0.41 for Q1 2026 indicates profitability remains distant, and the company's rapid strategic shifts (Bitcoin → energy → AI) raise questions about long-term strategic coherence [1][3].

  1. River Bend AI campus construction milestones — operations commencing Q2 2027 for the $10B Phase I development, with potential scaling from 245 MW to 2,300 MW ($17.7B total lease value) [1][2]

Key Risks

  2. Sustained cash burn — $248M net loss in FY2025 with negative forward EPS consensus, requiring continued access to capital markets during a period of potential market volatility [1][6]
  3. Bitcoin price and regulatory risk — unrealized BTC gains/losses continue to distort reported financials, and American Bitcoin Corp. subsidiary maintains crypto exposure while the AI pivot is underway [1]
