Investment Thesis
Howmet Aerospace is a best-in-class aerospace supplier riding three powerful multi-year tailwinds: commercial-aero build-rate recovery, a rich aftermarket/spares mix, and surging industrial gas turbine demand tied to data-center electrification [1]. FY2025 revenue grew 11% to $8.3B with adjusted EBITDA +26% and adjusted EPS +40% to $3.77, and 2026 guidance calls for another ~10% revenue growth ($9.0-9.2B) and EPS of $4.35-$4.55 [1]. Debt-to-equity has been methodically reduced from 142% to 57% over five years, and the company is AI-immune — its mission-critical engine airfoils, fasteners, and engineered structures are physical parts that AI cannot displace [4]. Howmet is ALIGNED on Inspire Insight with broad positive attributions including Air Quality, Business Resilience, and Product Sustainability.
The bear case is almost entirely valuation-driven. At $252.67 the stock trades at roughly 55-57x forward earnings and ~3x PEG — demanding multiples that require uninterrupted aerospace growth [4]. Any airline capex air-pocket, Boeing 737 MAX production hiccup, or widebody-delivery slippage would compress multiples materially. Culture metrics are only average (Glassdoor 3.3/5, 52% recommend) with pockets of 'toxic culture' commentary, and CEO compensation of $22.4M is 92% variable pay — large and scrutinized [2]. The recent $1.2B debt raise for acquisitions reintroduces leverage risk, and CFO Ken Giacobbe's retirement after 21 years (Dec 2025) removes a steady financial hand during this high-multiple phase.
Key Catalysts
  1. 2026 revenue guidance of $9.0-9.2B (~10% growth) and EPS $4.35-4.55 (16-21% growth) backed by commercial aero, aftermarket, and industrial gas turbine demand [1]
  2. Continued balance sheet discipline — D/E already reduced from 142% to 57%, supporting capital return expansion and investment-grade trajectory [4]
  3. Data-center / AI electricity demand lifting industrial gas turbine orders, one of three named 2026 growth engines [1]
Key Risks
  1. Rich valuation — ~55-57x forward P/E and ~3x PEG leaves little margin for aerospace production misses at Boeing/Airbus or engine OEMs [4]
  2. Concentrated aerospace customer base — any narrowbody/widebody build-rate slippage or aftermarket softness would compress revenue and multiples simultaneously [1]
  3. Management transition risk — CFO Ken Giacobbe retired after 21 years (Dec 2025) and CEO comp ($22.4M, 92% variable) has drawn employee criticism over elusive bonuses despite record earnings [2]

AI Resilience (25%)
AI Resilience LOW RISK
8
AI Risk Protection (8/9): Howmet makes physical-world mission-critical metal parts — jet engine airfoils, fasteners, engineered structures, forged aluminum wheels — that cannot be replaced by software or AI [1]. Extreme regulatory barriers (FAA/EASA certification), capital-intensive precision-casting and forging processes, and decades-long engine program lifecycles create enormous defensive moats. If anything, AI and data-center demand indirectly increase industrial gas turbine demand (one of Howmet's named 2026 growth engines), and AI-optimized engine design increases content per aircraft [1]. Analysts show essentially no AI-displacement concern. AI Opportunity (0/1): Howmet does not report meaningful AI-driven revenue — internal AI adoption for yield improvement is operational rather than a revenue line.
