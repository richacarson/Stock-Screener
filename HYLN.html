Company Profile
Consumer Cyclical ·Auto Parts ·NYSE American ·United States
Hyliion Holdings designs and develops the KARNO Power Module, a fuel-agnostic linear generator for stationary and mobile power generation. The system can operate on natural gas, propane, diesel, hydrogen, ammonia, and waste fuels including landfill gas. The company targets distributed power for data centers, military applications, and prime power installations, with commercial deployment expected in late 2026.

Investment Thesis
Hyliion is a speculative clean energy play with its KARNO Power Module targeting the growing distributed power market for data centers, military, and prime power applications. The fuel-agnostic design — capable of running on natural gas, hydrogen, ammonia, diesel, and waste fuels — provides unique flexibility as the energy transition unfolds [1]. Five KARNO units are operational at 175 kW, with full 200 kW power and commercial deployment expected late 2026. Military contracts of $40-50M being pursued could provide meaningful validation, and $10M 2026 revenue guidance represents the beginning of commercialization [1]. The balance sheet carries minimal debt ($4.4M) with $152.4M in cash and investments providing runway through commercialization. At a $364M market cap, the stock prices in significant optionality if KARNO achieves commercial traction.
The bear case is substantial for a company with $3.5M FY2025 revenue that declined 52.8% YoY while burning $47M annually in operations [1][4]. Hyliion has already pivoted once from a failed hybrid powertrain product, raising questions about whether KARNO will succeed commercially where the original vision did not. Short seller Bonitas Research has challenged the company's technology claims, and the stock has fallen over 97% from its 2020 SPAC-era peak [3]. At $2.04/share, the stock is barely above $1 and faces potential delisting risk. Competition from established players like Cummins, Caterpillar, and Bloom Energy — companies with existing manufacturing scale, distribution networks, and customer relationships — presents a formidable commercial barrier. The workforce contraction from ~300 to 113 employees raises execution risk for scaling manufacturing. Cash runway of approximately 3 years at current burn rate provides limited margin for error if commercialization is delayed beyond 2026 [4].
Key Catalysts
  1. KARNO Power Module commercial deployment targeted for late 2026, with 200 kW design power rating and Tier 4 diesel emissions compliance achieved — successful deployments would validate the technology and open the $10B+ distributed power market [1]
  2. U.S. military contracts worth $40-50M being pursued in 2026, which would provide meaningful revenue and credibility for the KARNO platform in defense applications [1]
  3. Data center power demand surge driven by AI infrastructure buildout creating growing market for distributed generation solutions that KARNO's fuel flexibility is positioned to address [1]
Key Risks
  1. Commercialization execution risk — KARNO must transition from 5 prototype units to commercial production while scaling manufacturing with only 113 employees, a challenge that has defeated many hardware startups [1][2]
  2. Cash burn sustainability — $47M annual operating cash burn with $152.4M in reserves provides approximately 3 years of runway; any commercialization delay could force dilutive financing at distressed share prices [4]
  3. Competitive disadvantage versus Cummins, Caterpillar, and Bloom Energy who have established manufacturing, distribution, and customer relationships in power generation markets that Hyliion must build from scratch [1]
