Company Profile
Communication Services ·Internet Content & Information ·NASDAQ ·United States
IAC is a diversified media and internet holding company led by Chairman Barry Diller. Its primary operating asset is People Inc. (formerly Dotdash Meredith), which publishes digital content across 40+ iconic brands including People, Better Homes & Gardens, Allrecipes, Investopedia, and The Daily Beast. IAC also operates Vivian Health (healthcare staffing), IAC Films, and search properties (Ask.com). The company sold Care.com in March 2026.

Finite vs Infinite Game — Sinek (25%)
Mindset: MIXED Overall: 7 /10
IAC under Barry Diller is one of the clearest examples of infinite game thinking in corporate America — Diller has been building, acquiring, and spinning off companies for 30+ years, treating IAC not as a business to optimize but as a perpetual company-creation engine. The willingness to spin off successful businesses (Expedia, Match, Vimeo) rather than holding them for conglomerate scale demonstrates an unusual commitment to long-term value creation over short-term empire building. However, the current IAC faces finite challenges — AI disruption of its core content business and declining revenue require immediate strategic response.

This is not a mission statement — it's a demonstrated pattern of behavior that has created tens of billions of shareholder value. The cause endures beyond any single business, which is the hallmark of an infinite-minded organization.

Investment Thesis
IAC trades at $44.97 ($3.4B market cap) and is transitioning to a focused digital media company anchored by People Inc. (Dotdash Meredith), which showed 14% digital revenue growth to $355M in Q4 2025 — demonstrating organic strength in its core content business [1]. At 18.8x forward P/E with an expected return to profitability in 2026 ($2.39 forward EPS), IAC offers exposure to one of digital media's strongest brand portfolios (People, Better Homes & Gardens, Allrecipes, Investopedia) at a discount to NAV, given the company's $960M cash position and $335M free cash flow [5]. Barry Diller's 30+ year track record of identifying trends and creating multi-billion-dollar businesses (Expedia, Match, Vimeo) provides a proven capital allocator at the helm [3]. The OpenAI licensing partnership positions Dotdash Meredith to monetize AI rather than be disrupted by it, and the reduction of Google search dependence from 60% to 33% of traffic shows proactive risk management [4].
The bear case is substantial. IAC's core digital publishing business faces existential pressure from AI — Google AI Overviews appear on one-third of searches related to Dotdash Meredith content, and ChatGPT/Perplexity can directly answer queries that previously drove ad-supported traffic [4]. Revenue declined 10.5% YoY to $2.39B (partly from Care.com divestiture), and the company reported a $104M net loss in 2025 with -4.35% profit margin [5]. 2026 EBITDA guidance of $297.5M at the midpoint was below consensus, suggesting margin recovery will be slower than expected [1]. The PEG ratio of 12.91 implies minimal growth relative to valuation. Diller is 83 years old and succession planning remains a key risk — IAC's value creation model is deeply dependent on his judgment and relationships. The search properties (Ask.com, Reference.com) face near-total disruption from AI search, and the revenue mix is shifting toward lower-growth digital advertising in a commoditizing market [1][4].
Key Catalysts
  1. Dotdash Meredith digital revenue growth acceleration — Q4 2025's 14% growth driven by advertising, performance marketing, and licensing suggests the content monetization strategy is working despite AI headwinds [1]
  2. OpenAI licensing partnership expansion and potential additional AI content licensing deals that monetize the 40+ brand content library as AI training data [4]
  3. Capital allocation — with $960M cash and $335M free cash flow post-Care.com sale, Diller has significant firepower for transformative acquisitions or aggressive buybacks that could unlock NAV discount [5]
Key Risks
  1. AI disruption of digital publishing — Google AI Overviews on one-third of relevant searches and ChatGPT substitution threaten Dotdash Meredith's ad-supported traffic model, with 3% session declines already evident [4]
  2. Succession risk — Barry Diller's 30+ year track record is irreplaceable at age 83, and IAC's value creation model depends heavily on his strategic judgment and deal-making ability [3]
  3. Revenue and profitability recovery — 10.5% revenue decline, $104M net loss, and below-consensus 2026 EBITDA guidance create uncertainty about whether the focused media strategy can deliver sustainable profitable growth [1][5]

AI Resilience (25%)
AI Resilience HIGH RISK
4
AI Risk Protection (3/9): IAC's core business — digital content publishing and search — is directly exposed to AI disruption. Google AI Overviews appear on roughly one-third of searches related to Dotdash Meredith content, and the company reported a 3% YoY decline in core user sessions attributed to 'weakening referral traffic from search platforms' [4]. AI chatbots (ChatGPT, Perplexity, Gemini) can directly answer queries that previously drove traffic to Investopedia, Verywell, Allrecipes, and other Dotdash Meredith sites, creating real substitution risk for ad-supported content monetization. The company has reduced Google search dependence from 60% of traffic (2021) to ~33%, which is defensive but still significant [4]. Ask.com and search properties face existential pressure from AI search alternatives. The Daily Beast's journalism is somewhat more defensible but still vulnerable to AI-generated news summaries. AI Opportunity (1/1): The OpenAI licensing deal provides direct AI revenue — IAC is monetizing its content library through AI training data licensing, and ChatGPT links back to Dotdash Meredith content [4]. This creates a nascent but quantifiable AI revenue stream.
