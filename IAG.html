Company Profile
Basic Materials ·Gold ·NYSE ·Canada
IAMGOLD Corporation is a mid-tier gold producer and developer operating in Canada and Burkina Faso. The company's flagship Cote Gold mine in northeastern Ontario is one of the largest gold mines in Canada with a 20+ year mine life, achieving nameplate capacity of 36,000 tonnes per day ahead of schedule in June 2025. In 2025, IAMGOLD delivered record production of 765,900 attributable gold ounces with revenue of $2.85B. Incorporated in 1990, headquartered in Toronto, Canada.

Investment Thesis
IAMGOLD is a transformed gold producer riding the successful Cote Gold ramp-up and a historically strong gold price environment. Record 2025 production of 765,900 attributable ounces drove revenue of $2,852.8M, and Q1 2026 revenue of $1,030.1M (up 116% YoY) signals continued momentum [1]. The financial profile is compelling: Forward P/E of 6.97, ROE of 27.97%, profit margin of 29.49%, and D/E of just 14.71% after aggressive deleveraging from $1.15B to $651M total debt [2]. Free cash flow of $971.6M funds continued debt reduction and the Cote expansion study (Q4 2026), which could unlock additional production capacity beyond the current 36,000 tpd nameplate. With AISC of $2,000-$2,150/oz against ~$4,000/oz gold prices, margins are substantial. CEO insider buying ($299K in June 2026) and analyst consensus of Strong Buy with $24.90 mean target (47% upside) reinforce conviction [3][5].
The primary risk is gold price dependency -- at AISC of $2,000-$2,150/oz, margins compress rapidly if gold retreats from ~$4,000/oz toward $2,500-$3,000/oz levels [1]. EPS declined 24% from $1.50 to $1.14 in 2025 despite 74.7% revenue growth due to one-time gains in the 2024 base, and the PEG ratio of 13.52 reflects earnings volatility that makes traditional growth valuation challenging [2]. Geopolitical risk from Burkina Faso operations (Essakane mine) is significant -- West African security concerns and resource nationalism could disrupt production or require asset impairments [4]. The stock's beta of 2.2 and 52-week range of $6.69-$24.87 reflect extreme volatility. Insider ownership of just 0.128% means management has limited financial alignment with shareholders beyond Adams' recent purchases. Competition from better-capitalized senior producers (Barrick, Newmont) for assets and talent could limit growth opportunities [3].

AI Risk Protection (8/9): Gold mining is a physical-world, resource-extraction business that AI structurally cannot displace [2]. Mining requires geological expertise, heavy equipment operation, environmental management, regulatory compliance, and community relations across remote locations in Canada and West Africa -- none of which AI can substitute. The core product (gold) is a physical commodity with demand driven by central bank reserves, jewelry, and investment hedging, all immune to AI disruption. AI may serve as an operational efficiency enabler through autonomous trucks, predictive maintenance, and geological modeling, but this increases the value of mining operations rather than threatening them. Analysts have cited zero AI substitution risk for gold mining companies [2]. AI Opportunity (0/1): IAMGOLD does not generate quantifiable AI-related revenue. AI deployment in mining operations is at an early stage and represents a cost-reduction tool rather than a revenue generator [2].
