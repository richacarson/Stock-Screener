Investment Thesis
Interactive Brokers is a high-quality financial services compounder delivering 15.2% revenue growth and 30.3% earnings growth with $6.2B in annual revenue [1]. The company's technology-first DNA, 150+ market global access, and lowest-cost execution model create a durable competitive moat reinforced by proprietary data assets uniquely suited for AI integration [2]. At 24.5x forward P/E with consensus 2026 revenue of $6.94B (11.7% growth), IBKR is reasonably valued for a best-in-class franchise with expanding total addressable market through retail, crypto, and sustainable investing initiatives. The 95% renewable energy procurement and AA-rated ESG tools position IBKR well for the growing sustainable investing trend [3].
The bear case centers on market sensitivity and competitive pressure. IBKR's revenue is highly correlated with trading volumes and market volatility — a sustained low-volatility environment would pressure commission and interest income [1]. The stock trades below its 50-day SMA of $71.37, reflecting near-term weakness. Forward earnings growth of 7.3% EPS is modest relative to the 30x trailing P/E, suggesting limited upside if growth decelerates. Glassdoor ratings of 3.7/5 with executive team rated C- on Comparably signal cultural issues that could impair talent retention as AI-native fintech competitors offer more dynamic work environments [2]. Peterffy's eventual full retirement creates key-person risk, as his engineering vision has driven the company's strategic direction for 47 years. The brokerage industry's shift toward zero commissions compresses margins despite IBKR's scale advantages [4].
