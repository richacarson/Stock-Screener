Investment Thesis
ImmunityBio offers exposure to a potentially transformative immunotherapy platform with ANKTIVA revenue surging 700% YoY to ~$113 million, expanded to 33 countries in under two years, and multiple label expansion catalysts in 2026 [1]. The stock is up 332% YTD and 185% over 12 months, yet at $8.47 with an $8.7B market cap, significant upside remains if ANKTIVA becomes a standard-of-care in bladder cancer and lung cancer. The papillary BLA resubmission, BCG-naive trial enrollment ahead of schedule, and Saudi NSCLC approval create a dense catalyst calendar. Q4 EPS beat of 33% shows progression toward profitability.
The bear case includes single-product concentration risk — 100% of revenue comes from ANKTIVA, and any safety signal, competitive displacement, or regulatory setback would be devastating [1]. The company remains unprofitable with ~$250 million cash and ongoing R&D burn, meaning dilution risk persists. Dr. Soon-Shiong's controversial history (NantHealth stock decline, complex corporate structures) raises governance red flags. The stock's 332% YTD rise prices in substantial success, and any pipeline disappointment could trigger a sharp correction. The FDA previously rejected the papillary bladder cancer label expansion, and resubmission acceptance is not guaranteed.
Key Catalysts
  1. FDA decision on resubmitted papillary bladder cancer sBLA could expand ANKTIVA's addressable patient population by 2-3x in 2026
  2. BCG-naive NMIBC trial enrollment ahead of schedule with full enrollment by Q2 2026 and BLA submission targeted by year-end 2026
  3. International revenue from 33 countries expected to generate 'substantive revenue' beginning 2027, with multiple ex-US accelerated approval submissions in 2026
Key Risks
  1. Single-product risk — 100% revenue dependence on ANKTIVA means any safety signal, reimbursement challenge, or competitive displacement is existential
  2. FDA could reject the papillary sBLA resubmission, repeating the prior pushback and undermining the label expansion thesis
  3. Founder-led governance with Dr. Soon-Shiong's complex corporate history (NantKwest, NantHealth) creates key-person and governance risk
