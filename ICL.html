Company Profile
Basic Materials ·Agricultural Inputs ·NYSE ·Israel
ICL Group is a global specialty minerals and chemicals company operating across Industrial Products, Potash, Phosphate Solutions, and Growing Solutions segments. The company extracts bromine and potash from the Dead Sea, produces specialty phosphates for food and industrial applications, and develops precision agriculture fertilizer solutions serving pharmaceutical, food, agriculture, and industrial markets worldwide.

Investment Thesis
ICL Group at 12.1x forward earnings offers a low-leverage specialty minerals company (D/E 44%) positioned at the intersection of food security and sustainability. Q4 2025 sales grew 6% to $1.70B with adjusted EBITDA up 10% to $380M, and 2026 guidance targets $1.4-1.6B adjusted EBITDA with potash volumes of 4.5-4.7M metric tons [1]. The Dead Sea concession provides irreplaceable bromine and potash resources, while the specialty pivot into food-grade phosphates, battery materials (LFP), and precision agriculture positions ICL for higher-margin growth. The Inspire Impact Score of 64 (ALIGNED) with 19 positive attributions reflects strong faith-based governance.
The bear case centers on ICL's severely depressed profitability — profit margin of just 3.2% and ROE of 4.6% versus the 2022 peak when commodity prices generated $10B revenue and $2.2B net income [1]. The 96.4% payout ratio leaves virtually no earnings cushion for the dividend, and the 5yr dividend CAGR is negative at -1.7% as payouts were slashed from the 2022 highs. Potash prices remain well below 2022 peaks, and geopolitical risk from Israeli operations adds a structural discount. If commodity markets remain depressed, ICL may need to further reduce dividends, and the specialty pivot may take years to generate meaningful margin improvement.
Key Catalysts
  1. Q1 2026 earnings (May 13) could validate the EBITDA recovery trajectory toward the $1.4-1.6B 2026 guidance range, with potash volume targets of 4.5-4.7M metric tons [1]
  2. Battery materials (LFP) commercialization could open a high-margin revenue stream as EV and energy storage demand accelerates globally
  3. Potash supply disruptions from Belarus/Russia sanctions or reduced competitor output could drive commodity price recovery and margin expansion
Key Risks
  1. Profit margin of 3.2% and payout ratio of 96.4% create dividend sustainability risk — another earnings miss would likely force a dividend cut [2]
  2. Geopolitical risk from Israeli operations including Dead Sea concession, regional conflicts, and potential regulatory changes to extraction rights
  3. Potash and phosphate commodity prices remain well below 2022 levels, and a global agricultural recession could further compress margins and volumes [1]
