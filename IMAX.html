Company Profile
Communication Services ·Entertainment ·NYSE ·Canada
IMAX Corporation is an entertainment technology company that designs, manufactures, and installs proprietary large-format cinema projection and sound systems. The company operates through a network of 1,798 IMAX theater systems in over 90 countries, generating revenue through system sales, leases, maintenance agreements, and box office revenue-sharing arrangements with exhibitors.

Investment Thesis
IMAX Corporation offers a compelling growth-adjusted valuation at 0.93x PEG and 18.4x forward P/E, with management guiding for a record $1.4B global box office in 2026 supported by network expansion to 1,798 systems across 90+ countries [1]. Outside Greater China, the business is thriving — Q1 2026 box office grew 67% with 75% North America growth, and the expanded content slate positions IMAX to capture an increasing share of the premium theatrical experience market [1]. Revenue has grown at a 10.9% CAGR from 2022-2025, and adjusted EPS rose 33% in Q1 2026 to $0.17, demonstrating operating leverage as the installed base scales [1]. The IMAX brand is a defensible moat — there is no substitute for the immersive large-format experience, and the 679-employee capital-light model generates $124M operating cash flow on $410M revenue [4].
The bear case centers on China dependency and content cyclicality. Greater China box office collapsed 62% in Q1 2026 after the Ne Zha 2 comparison, demonstrating the volatility of IMAX's largest growth market [1]. Q1 total revenue declined 6.1% YoY despite strong ex-China performance, and the 9.1% profit margin is thin for a premium brand — suggesting limited pricing power or high fixed costs in the system deployment model [4]. The trailing P/E of 54.5x reflects a depressed earnings base, and at $2B market cap, IMAX remains a small-cap with limited institutional ownership. The 3.4/5 Glassdoor rating, D+ executive team rating, and three CPOs in four years signal internal cultural dysfunction that could impair execution [2]. Geopolitical tensions between the U.S. and China create regulatory risk for IMAX's ~700 Chinese theater systems, and any escalation could permanently impair the company's largest growth market [1].
Key Catalysts

Key Risks

  2. Content cyclicality creates unpredictable quarterly results — IMAX revenue is dependent on Hollywood and Chinese film studios releasing blockbusters suited to the large-format experience [1]
  3. Internal culture dysfunction — Glassdoor D+ executive rating, 49% recommendation rate, three CPOs in four years — creates execution risk in a company that requires 679 employees to operate at a high level globally [2]
