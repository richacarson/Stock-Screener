Company Profile
Healthcare ·Biotechnology ·NASDAQ ·Australia
Immutep is an Australian clinical-stage biotechnology company pioneering novel immunotherapies based on Lymphocyte Activation Gene-3 (LAG-3) for cancer and autoimmune diseases. Its lead product candidate eftilagimod alfa (efti) is a soluble LAG-3 fusion protein and first-in-class MHC Class II agonist being developed in combination with anti-PD-1 therapies and chemotherapy across multiple oncology indications including non-small cell lung cancer, head and neck squamous cell carcinoma, metastatic breast cancer, and soft tissue sarcoma.

Investment Thesis
Immutep offers speculative exposure to a pioneering LAG-3 immunotherapy platform at a deeply discounted valuation following the March 2026 TACTI-004 Phase 3 failure in NSCLC. At $77M market cap and $0.52 per share, the stock is trading near trough levels against $99M in cash and the $370M Dr. Reddy's licensing deal (with an A$30.2M upfront received in January 2026) [1][2]. The remaining pipeline retains potential value — FDA Orphan Drug Designation for efti in soft tissue sarcoma (April 2026), ongoing Phase IIb trials in HNSCC and metastatic breast cancer, and new data accepted for ASCO 2026 presentation [1][3]. CSO Triebel's position as the original discoverer of LAG-3 with 59 patents and 156 publications gives Immutep unique scientific credibility, and the extended cash runway following TACTI-004 discontinuation provides time to pursue remaining indications without immediate dilution pressure.
The bear case is severe. The TACTI-004 Phase 3 failure — the company's most advanced and valuable program — destroyed the near-term investment thesis and raised fundamental questions about eftilagimod alfa's efficacy profile as a LAG-3 agonist in combination immunotherapy [1][3]. The stock has collapsed from over $2 to $0.52, and the remaining pipeline consists of earlier-stage programs (Phase IIb) with years of development and additional capital required before potential commercialization. Immutep is pre-revenue with a $36M annual cash burn, and while the $99M cash position and extended runway provide breathing room, the company will likely need additional capital raises if any remaining programs advance to Phase 3 [4]. The competitive landscape in immuno-oncology is dominated by large pharma companies (BMS, Merck, Roche) with vastly greater resources, and the LAG-3 mechanism remains unproven as a standalone commercial opportunity beyond BMS's Opdualag. At $0.52 per share, the stock is a penny stock with extreme volatility and limited institutional sponsorship, making it suitable only for highly speculative investors.
