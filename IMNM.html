Company Profile
Healthcare ·Biotechnology ·NASDAQ ·United States
Immunome is a clinical-stage targeted oncology company developing antibody-drug conjugates (ADCs), gamma secretase inhibitors, and radioligand therapies. Lead asset Varegacestat is a gamma secretase inhibitor in Phase 3 for desmoid tumors with NDA submission planned for Q2 2026. Pipeline includes IM-1021 (ROR1 ADC, Phase 1), IM-3050 (FAP radioligand), and three preclinical solid tumor ADCs.

Investment Thesis
Immunome's primary value driver is Varegacestat, which demonstrated an 84% reduction in disease progression or death in the Phase 3 RINGSIDE trial for desmoid tumors — a rare condition with no currently approved standard-of-care therapy [1]. NDA submission is planned for Q2 2026, with potential FDA approval creating a first-mover commercial opportunity in a market with significant unmet medical need. The company holds $653.5M in cash (runway into 2028), a deep ADC pipeline (IM-1021 Phase 1, IM-3050 IND cleared, three additional ADC INDs planned for 2026), and a leadership team with proven ADC commercialization experience from Seagen (ADCETRIS, PADCEV) [3]. The ASCO 2026 oral presentation of RINGSIDE data provides near-term catalyst visibility [1].
The bear case centers on binary clinical/regulatory risk typical of clinical-stage biotech. Immunome burned $190.9M in operating cash in 2025 with only $6.9M in revenue, and even with $653.5M in cash, commercialization of Varegacestat would require significant salesforce buildout for an orphan indication with limited market size [3]. CEO Clay Siegall's 2022 domestic assault arrest (charges dropped) creates reputational and governance risk [5]. The $2.7B market cap on a pre-revenue company prices in substantial Varegacestat success — any NDA delay or FDA concerns could severely compress the valuation. Pipeline diversification is early-stage (Phase 1 and preclinical), leaving the company heavily dependent on a single asset for the next 2-3 years. Competitive risk from nirogacestat (SpringWorks/Bayer) in the gamma secretase inhibitor space could limit market share [1].
