Investment Thesis
INBS is delivering accelerating commercial traction in fingerprint-based drug testing — H1 fiscal 2026 revenue of $2.0M up 36% YoY, with Q2 specifically up 48% YoY and reader sales doubling (104%) [1][2]. Gross margin expanded to 49% from 39% on operational leverage, and the razor/razor-blade model (cartridges now 58% of revenue) is starting to compound. The Syrma Johari MedTech manufacturing partnership and ongoing FDA progress create real near-term catalysts, and the addressable market in workplace and criminal justice drug testing is genuinely large. AI cannot disrupt the underlying chemistry, giving the company structural protection from the dominant technology risk facing healthcare diagnostics today.
The bear case is severe and IOWN-disqualifying. Liquidity is below the IOWN floor: 126K shares per day at ~$2.46 = ~$311K of daily dollar volume, well under the $1M minimum required to trade in size [3]. The company executed a 1-for-10 reverse stock split and a $10M dilutive private placement to maintain Nasdaq listing compliance, which destroys per-share value and signals capital-structure stress [4]. ROE is -150%, profit margin is 0%, and the entire enterprise value is $4.9M — smaller than the cost of a single late-stage clinical trial. Revenue is growing fast in percentage terms but tiny in dollar terms; the company has years of cash burn ahead before breakeven and will likely need additional dilutive raises. This is a venture-stage equity, not a screenable IOWN holding.
Key Catalysts
  1. Continued reader-installed-base growth driving recurring cartridge revenue at 49% gross margin, with Q2 FY2026 reader sales already up 104% YoY [2]
  2. Syrma Johari MedTech manufacturing partnership scaling production capacity ahead of FDA cartridge menu expansion [3]
  3. Potential FDA clearance of additional drug panels expanding the addressable market in clinical and law-enforcement settings [3]
Key Risks

  3. Tiny scale ($4.9M market cap, ~$2M H1 revenue) means a single failed pilot, slow FDA decision, or large-customer churn could be existential [3]
