Company Profile
Technology ·Semiconductors ·NASDAQ ·United States
indie Semiconductor is a fabless automotive semiconductor company providing mixed-signal SoCs, software, and sensor solutions for ADAS, autonomous vehicles, connected car, user experience, and electrification applications. Products include radar and vision processors, LiDAR components, ultrasonic sensors, in-cabin wireless charging, and cybersecurity microcontrollers. The company is headquartered in Aliso Viejo, California.
800 Employees ·www.indie.inc

Investment Thesis
indie Semiconductor offers pure-play exposure to the automotive ADAS megatrend with a $7.4B design-win backlog that dwarfs its $217M trailing revenue, providing multi-year growth visibility [1]. The Gen8 radar and iND880 vision processors, each expected to contribute 'well in excess of $100M in incremental annual revenue,' represent potential step-function revenue growth as production ramps accelerate through 2026 [1]. The Wuxi divestiture ($135M proceeds, $8-10M quarterly OpEx reduction) creates a leaner company targeting breakeven at $65M quarterly revenue — achievable if the ADAS ramp delivers [1]. The serviceable addressable market is projected to grow from $19B (2021) to $40B (2026), and indie's 49.6% non-GAAP gross margin demonstrates the pricing power of differentiated automotive silicon. At $3.08 with a $570M market cap, the stock trades at a fraction of its backlog value, offering significant upside if execution materializes [4].
The bear case is significant. indie remains deeply unprofitable with -65.8% profit margin, -36.3% ROE, and trailing losses of $143M on $217M revenue [4]. Revenue growth stalled at 0% YoY in 2025, and the Wuxi divestiture removes 43% of Q3 revenue, creating a near-term revenue hole that must be filled by ADAS ramps that have not yet materialized at scale [1]. The company competes against NXP ($14B revenue), Infineon ($16B), Renesas ($14B), and TI ($16B) — each with 50-100x more revenue, established OEM relationships, and 25-40% operating margins. indie's path to the $65M quarterly breakeven requires 21% growth from the current $54M base, which is uncertain given flat 2025 performance. Cash burn rate and potential dilution risk remain concerns, and the $7.4B backlog is spread over many years with no guarantee of conversion. Automotive production cycles are long and can be disrupted by macro or geopolitical shocks [1].
Key Catalysts
  1. Gen8 radar and iND880 vision processor production ramps in 2026, each targeting $100M+ in incremental annual revenue from the $7.4B design-win backlog [1]
  2. Path to profitability — Wuxi divestiture reduces quarterly OpEx by $8-10M, lowering breakeven to ~$65M quarterly revenue (21% above current levels) [1]
  3. Automotive ADAS content growth — SAM projected to double from $19B to $40B by 2026 as vehicles add more sensors and processing for L2+ autonomy [1][2]
Key Risks
  1. Competitive pressure from NXP, Infineon, Renesas, and TI — each 50-100x larger with established OEM relationships and operating profitability [1]
  2. Revenue stagnation — 0% YoY growth in 2025 and Wuxi divestiture removing 43% of Q3 revenue creates near-term revenue decline risk [1][4]
  3. Pre-profit cash burn of ~$143M annually with uncertain timeline to breakeven, creating potential dilution risk if ADAS ramps are delayed [1]
