Company Profile
Energy ·Oil & Gas E&P ·NYSE ·Indonesia
Indonesia Energy Corporation Limited is a micro-cap oil and gas exploration and production company operating in Indonesia. Its principal assets include the Kruh Block, a producing block covering 258 square kilometers in South Sumatra, and the Citarum Block, an exploration block covering approximately 3,925 square kilometers in onshore West Java. The company was incorporated in 2018 and is headquartered in Jakarta.

Investment Thesis
Indonesia Energy Corporation offers a speculative option on Indonesian oil and gas assets at a $45.6M market cap with $8.6M in cash and minimal debt ($712K, D/E of 3.2%) [3]. The Citarum Block covers 3,925 square kilometers of onshore West Java exploration acreage, representing significant upside if hydrocarbon discoveries materialize. The company benefits from Indonesia's growing energy demand and the NDAA-era focus on energy security in Southeast Asia. Average daily trading volume of 3.1M shares provides surprising liquidity for a company of this size, and the stock has demonstrated volatility that creates trading opportunities [1].
The bear case is overwhelming. Revenue has declined 44% from $4.1M in 2022 to $2.3M trailing, reflecting production decline at the Kruh Block without offsetting growth from exploration [1]. The company is deeply unprofitable with ROE of -40%, operating cash burn of -$4.9M annually, and only $8.6M in cash — giving approximately 2 years of runway before the company needs external capital [3]. The $45.6M market cap on $2.3M revenue yields a price-to-sales ratio of 31.7x, an absurd valuation for a declining micro-cap E&P company with no growth visibility. With only 31 employees and a single producing block, Indonesia Energy has extreme concentration risk — any operational disruption, regulatory change, or commodity price decline could eliminate the entire revenue base. The combined Chairman/CEO/controlling shareholder structure creates governance concerns for minority investors. Oil and gas exploration is inherently high-risk, and the Citarum Block remains unproven with no disclosed development timeline or resource estimates. This appears to be a speculative shell with limited fundamental value.
Key Catalysts
  1. Citarum Block exploration success — a meaningful hydrocarbon discovery on the 3,925 sq km West Java acreage could transform the company's asset base and justify the current market cap
  2. Oil price appreciation driven by AI data center energy demand and geopolitical supply constraints, which would increase revenue and margins from the Kruh Block producing asset
  3. Strategic partnership or farm-out agreement for Citarum Block development, bringing in capital and technical expertise to advance the exploration program without dilutive equity raises
Key Risks

  2. Cash runway exhaustion within 2 years at current -$4.9M annual burn rate against $8.6M in cash, likely requiring highly dilutive equity raises or convertible debt at unfavorable terms for a $3 stock [3]
  3. Governance concentration risk — CEO Dr. Wirawan Jusuf serves as Chairman, CEO, and controlling shareholder simultaneously, creating potential conflicts of interest and limiting minority shareholder protections [2]
