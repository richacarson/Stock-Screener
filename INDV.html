Investment Thesis
Indivior is a focused opioid-use-disorder specialty pharma with SUBLOCADE as a genuine long-duration growth asset — the drug grew 13% to $856M in FY2025 with Q4 up 30% YoY, and management projects 11% SUBLOCADE growth at the midpoint for FY2026 plus 35% adjusted EBITDA growth and 14-point EBITDA margin expansion [1]. Peak SUBLOCADE revenue of $1.5-$2.0B implies significant upside over current levels. New CEO Laura Crossley's focused leadership team is running a portfolio concentration strategy: capping opex at $450M, targeting ~$300M operating cash flow, returning capital via buyback, and unwinding low-return legacy products [1]. The balance sheet is clean ($89M net cash), the forward PE of ~11.3x is undemanding for a mid-teens SUBLOCADE dispense-unit growth trajectory, and the 35% adjusted EBITDA margin is attractive for a specialty pharma.
The bear case is real. FY2026 total revenue is guided DOWN 6% at the midpoint as the legacy Sublingual film franchise erodes faster than SUBLOCADE replaces it [1]. Two 2024 profit warnings shook investor confidence, the 2025 Opvee withdrawal under NY AG pressure revealed governance weaknesses, and Glassdoor culture feedback about 'bro culture' and compliance issues raises execution risk on the leadership reset [2][3]. Brixadi and eventual generic competition to SUBLOCADE will compress the growth runway. The $86M opioid settlement is a reminder that a company whose cause is treating opioid addiction has faced allegations its own products fueled addiction [3]. No dividend and concentrated product risk (SUBLOCADE is the whole story) make INDV a higher-risk holding than the PE suggests.
Key Catalysts

  2. Execution on the $150M buyback and $300M operating cash flow target, returning capital efficiently while the growth story matures [1]
  3. New CEO Laura Crossley's leadership reset showing up in improved Glassdoor sentiment, operational discipline, and the Q1 2026 earnings report on April 30 [2]
Key Risks
  1. FY2026 total revenue declines 6% at the midpoint as legacy Sublingual film erodes faster than SUBLOCADE replaces it — a known but negative optical [1]
  2. Governance and culture risk — Glassdoor citations of 'bro culture,' nepotism, compliance concerns, plus $86M opioid settlement and Opvee withdrawal [2][3]
  3. SUBLOCADE competitive intensity from Brixadi and future generics could erode the 11% growth runway and pressure the $1.5-$2.0B peak sales ambition [3]
