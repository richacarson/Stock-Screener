Company Profile
Technology ·Software - Infrastructure ·NYSE ·United States
Infleqtion is a full-stack quantum technology company specializing in neutral-atom quantum computing, sensing, networking, and security. Founded in 2007 as ColdQuanta by physicist Dana Anderson at the University of Colorado Boulder, the company commercializes ultra-cold atom technology across four product lines: quantum computers (Sqale platform), quantum optical clocks, quantum gravity sensors, and the Superstaq software stack. Customers include the U.S. Department of Defense, NASA, the UK Government, and enterprise partners including Nvidia. The company generated approximately $29M in trailing twelve-month revenue with an ~80% CAGR over the prior two years and went public via SPAC merger with Churchill Capital Corp X in February 2025.

Investment Thesis
Infleqtion is one of the few quantum computing companies with genuine commercial traction — approximately $29M in trailing twelve-month revenue at an ~80% two-year CAGR, a blue-chip government customer base including NASA, DOD, and the UK Government, and a full-stack platform spanning hardware and software that creates durable switching costs [1][2]. Its neutral-atom approach offers inherently lower error rates and higher projected qubit counts at scale compared to trapped-ion and superconducting alternatives, and the Superstaq software abstraction layer captures value regardless of which hardware architecture ultimately prevails [2]. The Nvidia partnership at GTC 2026 explicitly targets quantum-classical hybrid AI workloads, giving Infleqtion access to the dominant AI infrastructure platform as quantum enters the enterprise stack [1].
The company's diversification into quantum sensing — gravity sensors, optical clocks, GPS-denied inertial navigation — provides near-term revenue durability that pure quantum computing plays lack, as these products serve defense and aerospace markets with government contract funding largely independent of quantum computing adoption timelines [2]. At a $2.2B market cap against an 80% revenue CAGR trajectory, a technically defensible moat, and a business update call on April 8, 2026 that could catalyze a re-rating toward peer multiples, the stock offers asymmetric upside for investors with a 5-10 year horizon — though the path to profitability is undefined and the stock will remain highly volatile in the near term [1].
