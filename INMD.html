Company Profile

InMode designs, develops, manufactures, and markets minimally invasive and non-invasive aesthetic medical platforms based on proprietary radiofrequency (RF) technology. Products include BodyTite, FaceTite, Morpheus8, EmpowerRF, and hands-free solutions for body contouring, skin tightening, fat reduction, and women's health procedures, sold through direct sales and distributors globally.

Investment Thesis
InMode is a highly profitable medical device company trading at deep value multiples — 9.4x forward P/E and 0.83 PEG — with a fortress balance sheet ($555M cash, near-zero debt) and 75%+ gross margins on its proprietary RF-based aesthetic platforms [1][3]. Q4 2025 international sales surged 38% YoY to a record $48.5M (47% of revenue), and consumables grew to 22% of revenue (~128,000 units), suggesting the recurring revenue model is gaining traction. Management's 2026 guidance of $365-375M with 75-76% gross margins indicates stabilization after two years of decline. The stock has fallen from ~$40 to $14, creating a potential value opportunity if the international growth inflection and consumable adoption continue [1].
The bear case is significant: revenue has declined from $492M in 2023 to $370M in 2025, a 25% contraction that raises questions about whether InMode's products are losing relevance in a competitive aesthetic device market [1]. The U.S. business remains under pressure from GLP-1 weight-loss drugs (Ozempic, Mounjaro), which reduce demand for body contouring procedures — a core InMode use case. EPS declined 63.5% YoY, and 2026 guidance is essentially flat, suggesting no near-term catalyst for revenue reacceleration [1]. The Glassdoor reviews (3.2/5 with 2.5/5 work-life balance) indicate cultural issues that could impair talent retention in a competitive med-tech labor market [2]. The company is Israel-based, adding geopolitical risk. The $555M cash pile ($8.61/share against a $14.41 stock price) raises capital allocation questions — management has not articulated a plan to deploy this capital productively or return it to shareholders [3].
Key Catalysts
  1. International revenue inflection — record Q4 2025 international sales of $48.5M (up 38% YoY) with Europe at an all-time high, potentially offsetting U.S. weakness [1]
  2. Consumable-driven recurring revenue reaching 22% of sales with ~128,000 units sold in 2025, providing procedure volume visibility and reducing capital equipment cyclicality [1]
  3. Deep value rerating opportunity — at $14.41 with $8.61/share in cash, the market is pricing the operating business at ~$5.80/share despite $94M net income and 75%+ gross margins [3]
Key Risks
  1. GLP-1 weight-loss drug adoption (Ozempic, Mounjaro) reducing demand for body contouring procedures, directly threatening InMode's core use case in the U.S. market [1]
  2. Continued U.S. revenue decline as the domestic aesthetic market matures and physicians shift spending to competing platforms from Cynosure, Cutera, and emerging entrants [1]
  3. Capital allocation inaction — $555M in cash earning below-market returns while the stock trades near all-time lows, with no clear plan for deployment, acquisitions, or meaningful buybacks [3]
