Company Profile
Healthcare ·Medical Devices ·NYSE ·United States
Inspire Medical Systems develops and commercializes a minimally invasive neurostimulation platform for obstructive sleep apnea (OSA). The Inspire system is an implantable device that stimulates the hypoglossal nerve to keep the airway open during sleep, offering an alternative to CPAP therapy for patients who cannot tolerate it.

Investment Thesis
Inspire Medical Systems is a monopoly medical device company with an exceptional financial profile — $912M in FY2025 revenue (13.6% growth), 85.4% gross margins, $145.4M net income (up 2.7x YoY), and a pristine balance sheet with $276M net cash [1]. The stock trades at just 10.9x trailing P/E and 20.7x forward P/E — a significant discount to medtech peers — after a ~60% drawdown driven by the DOJ investigation, securities lawsuits, and GLP-1 competition fears [4]. At $53.41, the market prices in substantial risk to a business with no direct competitor, FDA regulatory moats, 85%+ gross margins, and a trained surgeon network that creates switching costs. The $200M buyback authorization and 2026 guidance of $950M-$1B in revenue with 6-8% adjusted operating margins suggest management confidence in the business trajectory [1].
The bear case is substantial and multi-faceted. The DOJ civil investigation into marketing, promotion, and reimbursement practices (disclosed January 2025) creates regulatory overhang that could result in fines, consent decrees, or restrictions on selling practices [5]. Securities class action lawsuits since December 2025 add legal uncertainty. GLP-1 drugs (Ozempic, Wegovy) are reducing obesity-driven OSA severity in a meaningful patient subset, potentially shrinking Inspire's addressable market over time [2]. Revenue growth is decelerating sharply — from 30%+ CAGR to 2026 guidance of just 4-10%, with Q1 2026 expected flat YoY [1]. The company also experienced execution difficulties with its newest-generation device rollout. As a single-product company with no pipeline diversification, any setback to the Inspire platform directly impacts the entire business. Analyst price target consensus of ~$64-78 suggests limited upside from current levels if growth continues to decelerate [2].
Key Catalysts
  1. Resolution of DOJ civil investigation could remove the primary overhang — Truist notes CIDs are routine and may result in manageable settlement or dismissal, potentially unlocking 20%+ re-rating [5]
  2. International expansion acceleration as Inspire penetrates European and Asian markets where OSA treatment awareness is growing and CPAP failure rates are similar to the U.S. [1]
  3. Profitability inflection with 2026 adjusted operating margins of 6-8% and the $200M buyback program reducing share count, supporting EPS growth even as revenue growth moderates [1]
Key Risks
  1. DOJ investigation into marketing and reimbursement practices could result in significant fines, corporate integrity agreements, or restrictions that impair the commercial sales model [5]
  2. GLP-1 drug competition — as obesity pharmacotherapy improves, the population of CPAP-intolerant OSA patients requiring surgical intervention may structurally decline [2]
  3. Revenue growth deceleration from 30%+ CAGR to 4-10% 2026 guidance, with Q1 2026 expected flat YoY, suggesting market penetration is slowing faster than anticipated [1]

AI Resilience (25%)
AI Resilience LOW RISK
8
AI Risk Protection (8/9): Inspire's business is fundamentally physical — implanting a neurostimulation device in a patient's chest and tongue requires a surgeon, an operating room, and a medical device. AI cannot substitute for the surgical implantation procedure, the physical hardware, or the ongoing patient management [2]. The company's monopoly position in hypoglossal nerve stimulation is protected by FDA regulatory approval, 15+ years of clinical evidence, trained surgeon networks, and implantable device manufacturing capabilities — none of which are AI-disruptable. AI could theoretically improve patient selection algorithms or device programming optimization, but these would enhance rather than threaten Inspire's business. The primary competitive threat is pharmacological (GLP-1 drugs reducing OSA severity by reducing obesity), not AI-driven [2]. AI Opportunity (0/1): Inspire does not generate revenue from AI products or services. The device is a traditional implantable neurostimulator without AI-powered features [2].
