Investment Thesis
Intapp is a high-growth SaaS platform delivering 27-28% SaaS revenue growth with Cloud ARR of $433.6M (up 31% YoY) and 124% cloud net revenue retention, serving a sticky professional services niche (legal, private capital, consulting) [1]. At 15.7x forward P/E and a PEG of 0.45x — the stock is down 67% from its highs — the valuation is compelling for a company growing revenue 17%+ with a clear path to profitability (forward EPS $1.48 vs -$0.29 trailing) [5]. The virtually debt-free balance sheet ($191M cash, $22M debt) provides financial stability, and the AI-powered product roadmap (Intapp Assist) positions the company as an AI enabler rather than victim in the professional services software market [1][3]. FY2026 guidance of $570-574M revenue confirms sustained double-digit momentum.
The bear case centers on profitability, competitive risk, and AI disruption. Intapp remains unprofitable with -4.4% net margin after 25 years of operation — the narrowing loss trajectory is encouraging but not guaranteed to continue [5]. Microsoft Copilot integration into M365 (which Intapp's collaboration products depend on) could cannibalize Intapp's value proposition if Microsoft builds professional services features natively. Salesforce Einstein and AI-native CRM startups represent additional competitive threats in relationship intelligence and deal management [3]. The 67% stock decline from peak levels reflects investor skepticism about the path to profitability and the sustainability of growth rates. Glassdoor reviews citing constant re-orgs and unclear direction suggest organizational execution risk at a critical juncture, and the company's small size (1,336 employees, $1.9B market cap) limits its ability to invest at the pace needed to compete with platform giants [3][5].

  2. Persistent unprofitability — 25 years of operation without sustained profitability raises questions about long-term business model viability despite narrowing losses [5]
  3. AI disruption from above — Salesforce, Microsoft, and AI-native competitors could replicate Intapp's relationship intelligence and compliance features using generative AI, compressing Intapp's addressable market [3]
