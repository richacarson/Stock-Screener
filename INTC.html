Investment Thesis
Intel is executing a credible turnaround under CEO Lip-Bu Tan, with Q4 2025 revenue of $13.7B beating guidance for the fifth consecutive quarter and DCAI growing at its fastest sequential pace this decade [1]. The 18A process node is in manufacturing, the custom ASIC business surpassed $1B annualized run rate, and Intel expects positive adjusted free cash flow for full-year 2026 [1]. The $37.4B cash position, NVIDIA's $5B investment, and a projected 70-80% EPS growth trajectory support the turnaround thesis. OpEx reduction to $16B target in 2026 and $2.5B debt retirement demonstrate financial discipline [1].
The bear case centers on Intel's still-fragile margins — non-GAAP gross margin of 37.9% remains far below the 60%+ levels of Intel's heyday, and Q1 2026 guidance of breakeven non-GAAP EPS with gross margins declining to 34.5% signals continued pressure [1]. The foundry business lost $2.5B in Q4 alone as 18A ramp costs mount. Intel has lost the AI GPU training market entirely, and competing with TSMC for external foundry customers requires years of yield improvement and trust-building. Revenue declined 4.1% YoY in 2025, and the 20% workforce reduction risks brain drain at a critical moment. If 18A yields disappoint or the foundry strategy fails to attract external customers, Intel's turnaround could stall [2].
Key Catalysts
  1. 18A process node volume ramp in H2 2026, with early yield data and external foundry customer announcements as key validation milestones [1]
  2. DCAI segment targeting strong growth in 2026 with server market recovery and custom ASIC business scaling beyond $1B annualized run rate [1]
  3. OpEx reduction to $16B in 2026 and positive adjusted free cash flow generation, demonstrating the turnaround is producing financial results [1]
Key Risks
  1. 18A yield risk — any drop in manufacturing yields could crater gross margins and delay foundry customer adoption, undermining the entire turnaround thesis [1]
  2. Foundry competition with TSMC — Intel must prove it can deliver competitive yields, capacity, and reliability to win external customers who have trusted TSMC for decades [1]
  3. AI GPU market share loss — Intel shelved Falcon Shores and has no competitive AI training GPU, leaving it dependent on custom ASICs and inference workloads in the highest-growth semiconductor segment [2]
