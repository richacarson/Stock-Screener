Company Profile
Financial Services ·Banks - Regional ·NASDAQ ·Brazil
Inter & Co is a Brazilian digital banking platform offering a Super App ecosystem that includes banking, investments, insurance, and e-commerce. Founded in 1994 as a traditional bank, the company transformed into a fully digital platform and listed on NASDAQ in 2022. With 25 million active clients and expanding into the US market, Inter competes with Nubank and traditional Brazilian banks.

Investment Thesis
Inter & Co is a Brazilian digital banking Super App delivering exceptional growth and approaching premium profitability metrics. Full-year 2025 saw 45% net income growth, 36% credit portfolio expansion (3x Brazil market growth), record NIM of 9.6%, and 4.4M new active clients bringing the total to 25M [1]. At a forward P/E of 7.9x, the stock trades at a fraction of US fintech peers despite 26.4% revenue growth, 21.9% profit margin, and ROE approaching 15% with a clear path to 20%+ [3]. The Super App model (banking + investments + insurance + e-commerce) creates multi-product cross-sell opportunities with low incremental customer acquisition cost. 2026 guidance targets 25-30% loan growth, and US market expansion via global digital accounts opens a large incremental addressable market [1]. The Menin family's long-term ownership structure supports patient capital allocation.
The bear case centers on Brazil country risk, currency exposure, and competitive intensity. The Brazilian Real's volatility directly impacts USD-denominated returns — INTR shares are US-listed but earnings are primarily in BRL, creating significant FX translation risk [3]. Brazil's macroeconomic environment (high interest rates, political uncertainty, fiscal concerns) could slow consumer lending growth or increase credit losses. Competition is fierce: Nubank (100M+ clients, 4x Inter's scale), Itaú, Bradesco, and other digital banks all target the same Brazilian consumer. Credit quality risk is elevated — 36% credit growth in a single year could seed future NPL problems if the Brazilian economy weakens, and the company's risk-adjusted NIM expansion may not be sustainable [1]. The stock's deep discount to global fintech peers may reflect legitimate emerging market risk rather than mispricing. No disclosed employee count or R&D data limits transparency. D/E is unavailable, making balance sheet risk assessment incomplete for a leveraged financial institution.
Key Catalysts
  1. ROE trajectory toward management's 20%+ target — current 14.4% ROE with improving NIM (9.6%) and operating leverage suggests significant profitability upside that could trigger valuation re-rating [1]
  2. US market expansion via global digital account — capturing Brazilian diaspora and cross-border financial flows opens a large incremental market with high revenue per client [2]
  3. Super App monetization — cross-selling insurance, investments, and e-commerce to 25M active clients at low incremental cost, with 2026 guidance of 25-30% loan growth sustaining top-line momentum [1]
Key Risks
  1. Brazilian Real depreciation — BRL/USD volatility directly impacts USD-denominated returns for NASDAQ-listed shares, and Brazil macro deterioration could simultaneously weaken the currency and increase credit losses [3]
  2. Credit quality deterioration — 36% credit portfolio expansion in 2025 far exceeds market growth and could seed NPL problems if Brazil's economy slows, particularly in unsecured consumer lending [1]
  3. Competitive pressure from Nubank (100M+ clients, 4x scale), traditional bank digitization (Itaú, Bradesco), and potential US fintech entrants into the Brazilian market [2]
