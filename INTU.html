Intuit Inc. provides AI-powered financial management, payments, compliance, and marketing products through four segments: Global Business Solutions (QuickBooks, Mailchimp), Consumer (TurboTax), Credit Karma, and ProTax. Investment Thesis
Intuit is a rare triple-category leader — TurboTax in consumer tax prep, QuickBooks in SMB accounting, Credit Karma in personal finance — generating $20.1B in TTM revenue growing 17.4% YoY with 80.9% gross margins and $5.3B free cash flow [1][4]. The forward P/E of 15.8x represents exceptional value for a dominant software franchise after a 30% stock decline driven by AI disruption fears, despite CEO Goodarzi aggressively transforming every product into an AI-native experience [2]. Q2 FY2026 showed acceleration: QuickBooks Online Accounting +24%, Credit Karma +23%, and Global Business Solutions +18%, all powered by AI-driven customer engagement and pricing expansion [1]. FY2026 guidance of $21.0-$21.2B (12-13% growth) with continued operating margin expansion provides visibility on sustained profitable growth. D/E of 36.1%, ROE of 23.5%, and profit margin of 21.6% demonstrate financial strength befitting a platform franchise [4].
The bear case centers squarely on AI disruption risk — the same force driving Intuit's growth could eventually destroy its moat. Free AI tools from ChatGPT, Gemini, and emerging startups can already perform basic tax preparation and bookkeeping, potentially commoditizing TurboTax's DIY tier and QuickBooks' simpler use cases [2]. CEO Goodarzi himself acknowledges that 'SaaS companies will either be disrupted or they will be disruptors' with no middle ground, and the stock's 30% decline reflects market uncertainty about which side Intuit lands on [2]. The FTC investigated Intuit for deceptive TurboTax 'free' advertising, and while a federal appeals court tossed the order, the regulatory attention highlights reputational risk around Intuit's pricing practices [3]. The 1,800-person layoff (10% of workforce) in 2024 for AI transformation creates execution risk and cultural disruption. Credit Karma's ad-based revenue model is inherently cyclical, and TurboTax growth of 8% is decelerating relative to the AI-powered segments. Consumer revenue guidance of 8-9% growth lags the 12-13% company average, suggesting the highest-profile brand is the slowest grower [1].
Key Catalysts
  1. AI-native product transformation driving QuickBooks Online Accounting revenue growth of 24% — AI-powered features are expanding ARPU and reducing churn across the small business platform [1]
  2. Credit Karma revenue growth of 23% with expansion into personal loans, auto insurance, and financial products beyond credit monitoring, powered by 100M+ user data advantage [1]
  3. Valuation re-rating potential at 15.8x forward P/E for a dominant software platform growing 12-13% with expanding margins — the 30% stock decline creates asymmetric upside if AI fears prove overblown [2][4]
Key Risks
  1. AI disruption of core tax and accounting products — free AI tools can already perform basic tax preparation and bookkeeping, threatening TurboTax DIY and QuickBooks entry tiers [2]
  2. Regulatory and reputational risk from FTC scrutiny of TurboTax 'free' advertising practices and potential future enforcement actions around pricing transparency [3]
  3. Execution risk on AI transformation — laying off 1,800 employees to hire AI talent requires successful reorientation of product development without disrupting platform stability or customer experience [2]
