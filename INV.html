Company Profile
Financial Services ·Asset Management ·NASDAQ ·United States
Innventure identifies, funds, and operates companies built around disruptive sustainable technologies licensed from multinational corporations. Its three portfolio companies are Accelsius (two-phase liquid cooling for data centers), AeroFlexx (flexible liquid packaging), and Refinity (advanced plastic-waste recycling). Founded in 2015 and headquartered in Orlando, Florida.
169 Employees ·www.innventure.com

Investment Thesis
Innventure is an early-stage platform commercializing three sustainable technologies with significant market potential, led by Accelsius two-phase liquid cooling which reported 433% revenue growth in 2025 and $50M+ in Q1 2026 bookings [1][2]. Management targets a $100M Accelsius annual revenue run rate by year-end 2026 and cash flow positivity by late 2026, representing a potential inflection from pre-revenue to meaningful scale [1]. The company sits at the intersection of AI infrastructure demand (liquid cooling for high-density data centers) and sustainability (packaging waste reduction, plastic recycling), with a low D/E of 6.9% and $60.4M cash providing near-term runway [7]. At a $496M market cap, the stock offers significant upside if Accelsius achieves its revenue targets.
The bear case is substantial: Innventure carries a going-concern qualification from its auditors, burned $80.7M in operating cash flow in 2025 against only $2.1M in consolidated revenue, and posted a $475M net loss driven by a $346.6M goodwill impairment [4][6]. The company has no path to profitability without Accelsius executing flawlessly on its $100M run rate target, and revenue guidance is heavily back-end weighted in 2026 with customer construction timelines creating execution risk [1]. Commonwealth Asset Management filed a Schedule 13D in February 2026, suggesting activist pressure that could distract management [5]. AeroFlexx and Refinity remain pre-meaningful-revenue, and the company needs continued capital market access to fund operations — dilution risk is real for a company with negative operating cash flow and a $496M market cap [4].
Key Catalysts
  1. Accelsius achieving $100M annual revenue run rate by year-end 2026, validating the data center liquid cooling thesis and potentially triggering cash flow positivity [1]
  2. Conversion of $50M+ Q1 2026 bookings backlog into recognized revenue through H2 2026, demonstrating commercial traction at scale [2]
  3. AeroFlexx and Refinity reaching independent capital formation milestones, reducing parent-level cash burn and validating the multi-platform model [1]
Key Risks
  1. Going-concern qualification and $80.7M annual operating cash burn with only $60.4M cash — potential dilutive capital raises or liquidity crisis if Accelsius revenue ramp is delayed [4][6]
  2. Back-end-weighted 2026 revenue guidance tied to customer data center construction timelines creates significant execution risk and potential for missed targets [1]
  3. Commonwealth Asset Management activist involvement (Schedule 13D filing) could force management into value-destructive short-term decisions or proxy fight [5]
