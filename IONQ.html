Company Profile
Technology ·Computer Hardware ·NYSE ·United States
IonQ develops and commercializes quantum computing systems using trapped-ion technology. The company sells access to quantum computers of various qubit capacities through cloud platforms and on-premises installations, serving enterprise, government, and research customers in the United States, Switzerland, and internationally.

Investment Thesis
IonQ is the leading pure-play quantum computing company with explosive commercial traction — Q1 2026 revenue of $64.7M (755% YoY growth, beating consensus by 30%), $470M in RPOs (up 554% YoY), and raised full-year guidance to $260-$270M [1]. The company sold its first sixth-generation 256-qubit chip-based system to Cambridge and is targeting 2 million qubits by 2030. With D/E of just 0.61% and a pristine balance sheet, IonQ has the financial flexibility to sustain its technology buildout. The SkyWater merger provides vertical semiconductor manufacturing integration [1].
The bear case is valuation and governance. At $56.89 per share ($21.2B market cap), IonQ trades at 145.9x trailing earnings (inflated by a one-time gain) and deeply negative forward P/E, with forward EPS of -$1.04 indicating the underlying business remains cash-destructive [1]. Short sellers Kerrisdale Capital labeled the company 'hype' with 'limited, error-prone systems,' and Wolfpack Research alleged $396.6M in insider selling through newly created 10b5-1 plans [3]. The CEO transition from Chapman to de Masi in February 2025 introduces execution uncertainty. Quantum computing commercialization timelines remain highly uncertain, and the technology may take longer than the market expects to deliver enterprise-grade reliability. Competition from IBM, Google, and well-funded rivals adds pressure [2][3].
