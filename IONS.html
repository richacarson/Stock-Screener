Company Profile
Healthcare ·Biotechnology ·NASDAQ ·United States
Ionis Pharmaceuticals is a commercial-stage biotech pioneer in antisense oligonucleotide RNA-targeted therapeutics. Its independent commercial portfolio includes TRYNGOLZA (olezarsen for FCS), WAINUA (eplontersen for ATTRv-PN, partnered with AstraZeneca), and the upcoming donidalorsen launch for hereditary angioedema. Olezarsen is expected to launch for severe hypertriglyceridemia in 2026.

Investment Thesis
Ionis Pharmaceuticals is transitioning from a platform-royalty biotech to an independent commercial-stage company with three in-market drugs (TRYNGOLZA, WAINUA, SPINRAZA royalty) and two 2026 launches (donidalorsen for HAE, olezarsen for severe hypertriglyceridemia) [1]. Q3 2025 revenue of $157M (+17% YoY) and nearly 70% sequential TRYNGOLZA growth validate the early commercial uptake, and management guides to 20% annual revenue growth on the new launches [1]. CEO Brett Monia — a founding scientist and 2025 Oligonucleotide Therapeutics Society Lifetime Achievement Award recipient — leads an employee base that rates Ionis 4.0/5 on Glassdoor with 94% CEO approval [2]. The 93% TRYNGOLZA price reduction to ~$40K ahead of SHTG expansion is a deliberate patient-access move that reinforces the +35 Inspire Impact Score and 11 positive ESG attributions [3][CSV].
The bear case centers on execution and cash burn. 2026 operating loss guidance of $500–550M means several more years before GAAP profitability, profit margin is -40.4%, ROE is -70.8%, and debt-to-equity at 534.8% reflects convertible-note leverage on a modest equity base [1][5]. Trailing revenue is down 10.3% due to SPINRAZA royalty erosion and partnership lumpiness — the forward 20% growth story depends entirely on TRYNGOLZA SHTG expansion, donidalorsen HAE launch, and WAINUA ramp executing to plan [1][5]. AstraZeneca (WAINUA), Novartis (pelacarsen), and Alnylam (competing RNAi therapies) are all credible rivals, and the 93% TRYNGOLZA price cut signals payer pressure on orphan pricing. At ~$12.7B market cap and a negative forward P/E of -123x, the stock is priced on pipeline execution rather than current earnings.
Key Catalysts
  1. Donidalorsen FDA approval and HAE commercial launch in 2026, adding a third independently commercialized Ionis drug [1]
  2. Olezarsen (TRYNGOLZA) label expansion into severe hypertriglyceridemia — a market orders of magnitude larger than FCS — following the strategic 93% price reset [3]
  3. WAINUA ramp with AstraZeneca in ATTRv-PN, building toward competitive positioning against Alnylam's Amvuttra and Pfizer's Vyndaqel [1]
Key Risks
  1. Operating loss of $500–550M in 2026 with negative forward P/E means the investment case rests entirely on commercial execution; a slow donidalorsen or SHTG launch would extend cash burn and pressure the balance sheet [1][5]
  2. Competitive pressure — Alnylam RNAi therapies (Amvuttra) and partner/competitor dynamics with AstraZeneca and Novartis could cap Ionis's revenue per drug [1]
  3. Payer and pricing pressure — the 93% TRYNGOLZA price cut demonstrates orphan-drug repricing risk can materially affect revenue trajectory even with volume growth [3]
