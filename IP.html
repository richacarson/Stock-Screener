Company Profile
Consumer Cyclical ·Packaging & Containers ·NYSE ·United States
International Paper produces and sells renewable fiber-based packaging in North America, Latin America, Europe, South America, and North Africa. Following the January 2025 acquisition of DS Smith, the company operates through Packaging Solutions North America and Packaging Solutions EMEA segments, offering linerboard, containerboard, and corrugated packaging for food, beverage, agriculture, and industrial customers.

Investment Thesis
International Paper is the world's largest packaging company following the DS Smith acquisition, offering a 5.22% dividend yield at 12.2x forward P/E with analyst-projected EPS surging 746% from $0.28 (FY2025) to $2.37 (FY2026) as integration charges roll off [1][4]. The $710M cost-out program and $400M in combined commercial and operational benefits expected in 2026 provide clear earnings visibility toward the $5B EBITDA target by 2027 [1]. E-commerce growth structurally increases corrugated packaging demand, and IP's renewable fiber-based products are positioned to benefit from plastic-to-paper substitution trends. The 64-year unbroken dividend streak and 62,602-person global workforce demonstrate institutional durability [4].
The bear case focuses on integration execution risk and current financial weakness. Negative ROE (-24.7%), negative profit margin (-14.9%), and Q4 2025 EPS of -$0.08 reflect a company deep in restructuring mode [4]. Total debt of $10.5B against $1.1B cash creates significant leverage, and the 158% payout ratio means the dividend is currently uncovered by earnings — sustainability depends entirely on the earnings recovery materializing [4]. IP's $498M+ in cumulative environmental penalties since 2000 and the recent $500K Bogalusa mill fine for Clean Air Act violations undermine the sustainability narrative [3]. The planned separation into two independent companies creates execution complexity and potential value destruction. Organic revenue has been declining (2023: $16B, 2024: $15.8B) with the top-line growth entirely acquisition-driven [1]. EMEA site restructuring and >$160M in run-rate savings imply further workforce reductions in the European operations.
