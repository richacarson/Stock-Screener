Company Profile
Industrials ·Specialty Industrial Machinery ·NYSE ·United States
Ingersoll Rand Inc. is a diversified industrial company providing mission-critical flow creation and industrial solutions. Products include compressors, blowers, pumps, vacuum systems, and power tools across diverse end markets including aerospace & defense, food & beverage, water management, and life sciences. The company was formed from the 2020 merger of Gardner Denver and Ingersoll-Rand's Industrial segment.

Investment Thesis
Ingersoll Rand is a premier industrial compounder delivering consistent growth through disciplined M&A and operational execution. FY2025 revenue of $7.65B (+6%) with Q4 accelerating to +10% demonstrates the growth engine is working [1]. The company closed 16 acquisitions in 2025 ($525M invested, ~$275M annualized revenue), and guides to $3.45-$3.57 adjusted EPS for 2026 (up 3-7%) [1]. The balance sheet is strong at 50.7% D/E, PEG of 0.72 indicates the stock is cheap relative to growth, and the company's products are mission-critical in growing end markets (data centers, water infrastructure, life sciences). Ingersoll Rand's decade-long sustainability track record (37% GHG reduction, 45% water reduction) and DJSI World Index inclusion position it favorably for ESG-focused institutional capital [3]. The Inspire score of 68 (ALIGNED) reflects a clean profile with 18 positive attributions and zero negatives.
The bear case focuses on acquisition dependence and margin compression. Organic growth guidance of flat to 2% for 2026 is anemic for a stock trading at 54x trailing P/E and 20x forward P/E — the growth story is almost entirely M&A-driven [1][4]. Profit margin of 7.6% and ROE of 5.77% are weak for a company of this scale, suggesting that serial acquisitions are creating goodwill bloat and integration costs that depress returns on capital [4]. The 16-deal pace of M&A raises execution risk — integrating $275M in inorganic revenue annually requires significant management bandwidth. CEO decision centralization ('every decision goes to the CEO') could become a bottleneck as the organization scales [2]. At $31B market cap, the stock needs continued M&A execution and margin expansion to justify the premium valuation. A slowdown in industrial capex or difficulty finding accretive acquisition targets at reasonable prices could stall the growth narrative.
Key Catalysts
  1. Continued M&A execution in 2026 — management expects acquisitions to contribute 400-500 bps of revenue growth, and the company's track record of 16 deals in 2025 suggests a deep pipeline [1]
  2. Margin expansion as recent acquisitions integrate and synergies realize, potentially lifting profit margin above the current 7.6% toward the industrial peer average of 12-15% [4]
  3. Data center and AI infrastructure buildout driving demand for Ingersoll Rand's compressors, cooling systems, and fluid handling equipment — a multi-year secular tailwind [1]
Key Risks
  1. Acquisition-dependent growth — organic revenue guidance of flat to 2% means the growth narrative collapses if the M&A pipeline dries up or target multiples become uneconomic [1]
  2. Margin compression risk — 7.6% profit margin and 5.77% ROE are below industrial peers, and rapid M&A pace may continue to dilute returns on capital through goodwill accumulation [4]
  3. Valuation risk — at 54x trailing P/E and 20x forward P/E, the stock is priced for execution perfection; any disappointment in acquisition integration or organic growth could trigger meaningful multiple compression [4]

AI Risk Protection (8/9): Ingersoll Rand's core business — manufacturing physical compressors, pumps, blowers, and industrial equipment — is structurally immune to AI disruption [1]. These are mission-critical, engineered-to-order products that require metallurgy, precision manufacturing, installation, and field service.
