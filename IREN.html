Investment Thesis
IREN represents a uniquely positioned play on AI infrastructure with 2,910 MW of grid-connected power, a $9.7 billion Microsoft cloud contract providing $1.9 billion in ARR, and a pipeline targeting $3.4 billion ARR from 140,000 GPUs by end of 2026 [1]. Revenue grew 355% YoY in Q1 FY26 to $240 million with record 51.5% net margins, and the strategic shift from 64%-margin Bitcoin mining to 85%-margin AI cloud creates a structural margin expansion story. The 50 EH/s Bitcoin mining capacity provides downside protection while AI scales. Multiple AI cloud customers (Together AI, Fluidstack, Fireworks) diversify beyond Microsoft.
The bear case includes extreme execution risk — scaling from 23,000 to 140,000 GPUs in one year while managing a $9.7 billion Microsoft contract with just 257 employees is extraordinarily ambitious [1]. The $3.4 billion AI Cloud ARR target is described by HC Wainwright as 'lofty' (maintaining a sell rating despite a $56 price target), and Q1 AI Cloud revenue of just $7.3 million versus $232.9 million from mining shows the pivot is nascent. D/E of 153% reflects heavy capital spending with uncertain returns, Bitcoin mining revenue provides 97% of current income and remains volatile. If AI CapEx spending slows or GPU prices decline, IREN's infrastructure investments could become stranded assets.
Key Catalysts
  1. $9.7B Microsoft contract deployment beginning at Childress, Texas, with 20% customer prepayment reducing execution risk and validating AI cloud economics
  2. GPU fleet scaling from 23,000 to 140,000 by end of 2026 could generate $3.4B ARR at 85% project EBITDA margins if on schedule
  3. Additional hyperscaler contracts beyond Microsoft could diversify customer concentration and validate the neocloud infrastructure model
Key Risks
  1. GPU scaling execution — deploying 117,000 additional GPUs in 12 months with 257 employees requires flawless supply chain and construction execution
  2. Bitcoin price decline below $50K would compress the $232.9M/quarter mining revenue that currently funds AI infrastructure expansion
  3. AI CapEx slowdown from hyperscalers (Microsoft, Google, Meta) would reduce demand for IREN's infrastructure before the pivot is complete
