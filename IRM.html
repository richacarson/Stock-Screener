Investment Thesis
Iron Mountain is a rare REIT successfully pivoting from legacy physical storage into high-growth data center infrastructure, riding the AI-driven demand wave for colocation capacity. FY2025 revenue hit a record $6.9B with data center and digital units growing 40%+ in Q4, and 2026 guidance of $7.6-7.8B (12% growth) with AFFO/share of $5.69-$5.79 significantly beat consensus [1].
The 400MW data center pipeline positions Iron Mountain to capture AI-driven demand through 2027+, while the legacy physical storage business provides highly recurring revenue and 95% Fortune 1000 penetration [1]. The dividend yield of 2.93% with 17+ years of consecutive payments and ~6% 5-year growth rate provides income quality. The primary risk is execution on the capital-intensive data center buildout in a competitive market, with elevated leverage and a forward P/E of 46x requiring sustained growth to justify the valuation.
Key Catalysts
  1. 400MW of new data center capacity coming online over the next 24 months, with 25%+ data center revenue growth expected in 2026 [1]
  2. AFFO/share guidance of $5.69-$5.79 for 2026 significantly above consensus estimate of $4.22, suggesting analyst upgrades ahead [1]
  3. AI-driven enterprise demand for data digitization and intelligent document management creating cross-sell opportunities across 240,000+ customer base [2]
Key Risks
  1. Elevated valuation at 46x forward P/E requires sustained double-digit growth; any data center demand slowdown could compress multiples significantly
  2. Capital-intensive data center buildout with unknown D/E ratio creates balance sheet risk — REIT structure limits retained earnings for organic funding
  3. Competition from hyperscalers (AWS, Azure, Google) with deeper pockets and larger data center footprints could limit market share gains in colocation
