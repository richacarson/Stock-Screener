Gartner, Inc. is the world's leading research and advisory company for IT, enterprise, and C-suite leaders. It operates through three segments: Research (subscription-based insights, data, and expert access), Conferences (Symposium/Xpo series and peer-driven events), and Consulting (technology-driven strategic initiatives and IT optimization). Investment Thesis
Gartner is the undisputed global leader in enterprise research and advisory, trading at a compelling 10.2x forward P/E (PEG 0.82) for a subscription-based business with 68.4% gross margins, 86.9% ROE, and $1.2B in annual free cash flow [1][4]. Full-year 2025 revenue of $6.5B grew 4% as reported, and contract value growth is expected to accelerate throughout 2026 as the Digital Markets divestiture sharpens focus and easing federal government headwinds improve the demand environment [1]. CEO Eugene Hall's 22-year tenure provides extraordinary leadership continuity, and the Magic Quadrant franchise gives Gartner unmatched pricing power in the enterprise advisory market. At $150.55/share, the company generates forward EPS of $14.72 and is aggressively returning capital through buybacks.
The bear case focuses on AI disruption to Gartner's own business model and decelerating growth. Revenue grew just 2.2% in 2025 and guidance calls for only 2% FX-neutral growth in 2026 — the weakest organic growth in years as the company digests the Digital Markets divestiture and faces federal government spending headwinds [1]. EPS declined 34.2% YoY in 2025 due to restructuring charges and business mix shifts. D/E of 1046% reflects aggressive financial engineering — while serviceable given $1.2B FCF, a revenue shock could strain the $3.35B debt load [4]. Most critically, large language models and AI-powered research tools pose genuine long-term substitution risk to expensive Gartner subscriptions. If enterprise decision-makers can get 80% of Gartner's insights from AI tools at 10% of the cost, the subscription value proposition weakens. Gartner advises others on AI disruption but has not publicly addressed how AI threatens its own model — a significant blind spot for a $10.2B company [2].
Key Catalysts
  1. Contract value growth accelerating throughout 2026 as federal government headwinds ease and enterprises increase IT research spending amid AI adoption complexity [1]
  2. Forward P/E of 10.2x with PEG of 0.82 representing valuation re-rating opportunity as earnings recover from 2025's -34.2% decline and approach $14.72 forward EPS [4]
  3. Continued expansion of research coverage into new C-suite functions (AI strategy, supply chain resilience) driving wallet share growth within existing enterprise clients [2]
Key Risks
  1. AI-powered research substitution — LLMs and tools like Perplexity Enterprise or ChatGPT can synthesize publicly available research into executive-ready insights, threatening the value of $50K+ annual Gartner subscriptions [2]
  2. Revenue growth stalling at 2% FX-neutral with EPS declining 34.2% YoY, creating risk that the business has reached a growth ceiling in its core enterprise research market [1]
  3. Leverage risk from D/E of 1046% — $3.35B debt fueled by buybacks could become burdensome if FCF generation deteriorates in a recession or competitive displacement scenario [4]

AI Risk Protection (5/9): Gartner occupies a complex position in the AI disruption landscape.
