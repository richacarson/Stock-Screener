Integra Resources Corp. is a gold and silver producer operating the Florida Canyon Mine in Nevada and advancing the DeLamar Project in southwestern Idaho. The company produced 70,927 gold ounces in 2025 and is developing a multi-asset growth pipeline targeting mid-tier producer status with 1.1 million gold-equivalent ounces in DeLamar's 10-year mine plan.

Investment Thesis
Integra Resources is a compelling gold producer with a dramatically undervalued growth profile — Florida Canyon produced 70,927 oz gold in 2025 generating $243.9M revenue and $72.3M operating cash flow, while the DeLamar feasibility study shows $1.9B NPV at current gold prices ($4,500/oz Au) with a 97% IRR [1]. At just $604M market cap and 4.2x forward P/E ($0.71 forward EPS), the stock trades at a fraction of its net asset value: DeLamar alone is worth $774M-$1.9B depending on gold prices, plus Florida Canyon's $72M annual cash flow and Nevada North's optionality [1]. The company is debt-free with $63M cash after converting the $50M convertible in December 2025, and production guidance of 70,000-75,000 oz in 2026 growing to 80,000-90,000 oz by 2027-2028 provides clear earnings visibility [1]. The recent GDXJ ETF inclusion broadens the institutional investor base, and BLM's establishment of a federal permitting schedule for DeLamar (Record of Decision expected Q3 2027) de-risks the development timeline.
The bear case centers on execution risk, cost inflation, and commodity price dependency. AISC of $2,693/oz in 2025 is above industry average — if gold prices retreat from $3,400+ levels to $2,000-2,500/oz, Florida Canyon's operating margins compress dramatically or turn negative, and DeLamar's $774M base-case NPV assumes $3,000/oz gold [1]. The GAAP net loss of $2.2M (despite $47.3M adjusted earnings) and -1.4% ROE highlight the gap between cash mining economics and reported profitability. DeLamar requires $389M initial capital and a successful NEPA permitting process through Q3 2027 — any environmental or tribal opposition, permitting delays, or capital cost overruns could significantly impair the project's value [1]. The $604M market cap reflects a micro-cap with limited liquidity (avg daily volume $9.1M), making the stock susceptible to sharp drawdowns. Integra's entire value proposition depends on elevated gold prices persisting; a secular decline in gold sentiment would be devastating given the above-average cost structure [4].
Key Catalysts
  1. DeLamar permitting milestones — BLM Notice of Intent expected Q2 2026 and Record of Decision targeted Q3 2027, progressively de-risking the $774M-$1.9B NPV project [1]

Key Risks

  2. DeLamar permitting and construction execution risk — $389M initial capital requirement with NEPA environmental review and tribal engagement creates multi-year timeline uncertainty [1]
  3. Micro-cap liquidity and dilution risk — $604M market cap with limited institutional ownership; future capital raises for DeLamar construction ($389M+) could significantly dilute existing shareholders [4]
