Company Profile
Healthcare ·Biotechnology ·NASDAQ ·Ireland
Iterum Therapeutics is a biopharmaceutical company that developed ORLYNVAH (oral sulopenem), the first and only FDA-approved oral penem antibiotic for uncomplicated urinary tract infections in adult women with limited oral treatment options. The company filed a petition for winding up in the High Court in Ireland on March 27, 2026, and was delisted from Nasdaq on April 1, 2026. Joint Provisional Liquidators have been appointed to oversee the wind-down process.

Investment Thesis
Iterum Therapeutics achieved a genuine scientific milestone with FDA approval of ORLYNVAH, the first and only oral penem antibiotic, addressing the critical public health need of drug-resistant urinary tract infections [1]. The product secured payer access for 60M+ insured lives and signed a 40M-life PBM deal, with projected 2026 net sales of $5-15M [1]. The antimicrobial resistance market represents a large and growing unmet need as bacterial resistance to existing antibiotics increases globally.
The company is in terminal decline. Iterum filed a petition for winding up in the Irish High Court on March 27, 2026, with Joint Provisional Liquidators appointed from Teneo Restructuring [3]. The stock was delisted from Nasdaq on April 1, 2026, after shareholders failed to approve a reverse stock split needed to maintain the $1.00 minimum bid price [3]. The financial profile is unsustainable: $390K TTM revenue, $33.7M debt, $11.0M cash, and -$19.5M operating cash flow [4]. With a market cap of just $1.6M and shares trading at $0.04, shareholders are 'very unlikely to receive any meaningful return' per the company's own disclosure [3]. The ORLYNVAH asset may have value in a liquidation sale, but equity holders sit behind $33.7M in debt claims.
Key Catalysts
  1. Potential acquisition of ORLYNVAH asset by a larger pharma company during the liquidation process, though any proceeds would go to creditors before equity holders [3]
  2. ORLYNVAH's unique position as the only oral penem could attract a buyer willing to invest in commercial infrastructure that Iterum could not build [1]
  3. Growing antimicrobial resistance crisis could increase the strategic value of the ORLYNVAH franchise to potential acquirers [2]
Key Risks
  1. Company is in liquidation — filed for winding up on March 27, 2026, with Joint Provisional Liquidators appointed; shareholders are very unlikely to receive any return [3]
  2. Delisted from Nasdaq on April 1, 2026; stock trades at $0.04 with essentially zero liquidity for institutional investors [3][4]
  3. Debt of $33.7M against $11.0M cash means creditors have priority over any asset sale proceeds, leaving equity worthless in most scenarios [4]
