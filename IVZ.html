Company Profile
Financial Services ·Asset Management ·NYSE ·United States
Invesco is a global investment management firm with $2.2 trillion in assets under management, serving retail, institutional, and high-net-worth clients in over 120 countries. The company offers actively managed funds, ETFs (including the flagship QQQ), index strategies, fixed income, alternatives, and private markets. Invesco has a significant China joint venture ($132 billion AUM) and is transitioning toward a hybrid investment platform.

Investment Thesis
Invesco offers deep value at 7.7× forward P/E with $2.2 trillion AUM growing 19% YoY, driven by record net long-term inflows of $81.2 billion in FY2025 and a 5% annualized organic growth rate [1]. The ETF and index business grew revenue 22% and the China JV reached record $132 billion AUM with 36% annualized organic growth [1]. Balance sheet improvement is underway — $1.5 billion in preferred stock repurchased and $240 million in debt repaid — with management targeting a 60% total payout ratio in 2026. The 3.6% dividend yield with 31 consecutive years of payment provides income support. Adjusted operating margin of 36.4% demonstrates underlying profitability obscured by the one-time $1.8 billion impairment charge [1].
The bear case centers on structural headwinds and governance risk. The $1.8 billion non-cash impairment of intangible assets from prior acquisitions (OppenheimerFunds) signals that value-destructive M&A has eroded shareholder wealth [1]. Active equity outflows of $5.5 billion in FY2025 reflect secular industry shifts toward passive investing that threaten Invesco's higher-fee products [1]. Net revenue yield continues to decline as the product mix shifts toward lower-fee ETFs and index funds. The $17.5 million SEC fine for misleading ESG integration claims damaged credibility and suggests weak internal governance controls [3]. GAAP profitability is negative with -4.4% net margin and -1.2% ROE on a trailing basis. The dividend was cut during COVID and has only recently resumed modest growth ($0.21/quarter vs pre-cut $0.31), leaving the payout ratio elevated at 56%. Competition from BlackRock, Vanguard, and fintech disruptors continues to compress margins across the industry.
Key Catalysts
  1. Hybrid Investment Platform completion by end 2026 expected to reduce operating costs and enable personalized portfolio capabilities, supporting margin expansion toward the 60% payout ratio target [1]
  2. China JV momentum — $132 billion AUM with 36% annualized organic growth rate and $8.9 billion in net inflows provides a high-growth lever that most Western competitors lack [1]
  3. Preferred stock buyback benefit — $1.5 billion repurchased in 2025 expected to generate $0.20 EPS accretion once associated debt is fully repaid, directly improving earnings per share [1]
Key Risks
  1. Secular active-to-passive shift — $5.5 billion in fundamental equity outflows in FY2025 may accelerate as AI-driven investing tools and low-cost ETFs erode demand for actively managed products [1]
  2. Acquisition integration risk — the $1.8 billion intangible impairment from OppenheimerFunds signals ongoing value destruction from legacy M&A, and the pending Canadian partnership may create additional short-term earnings drag starting Q3 2026 [1]
  3. Governance and compliance risk — the $17.5 million SEC fine for misleading ESG claims reveals weak internal controls; future regulatory actions could further damage institutional client trust and trigger additional AUM outflows [3]
