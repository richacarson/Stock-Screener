Company Profile
Healthcare ·Biotechnology ·NASDAQ ·United States
Jaguar Health, Inc. is a commercial-stage pharmaceuticals company developing plant-based prescription medicines for gastrointestinal distress in humans and animals. Key products include Mytesi (crofelemer) for HIV/AIDS-related diarrhea and Canalevia-CA1 for chemotherapy-induced diarrhea in dogs. The pipeline includes Phase III trials of crofelemer for cancer therapy-related diarrhea and Phase II trials for short bowel syndrome and IBS-D.

Investment Thesis
Jaguar Health offers a speculative bet on the commercial expansion of crofelemer, the only FDA-approved plant-based anti-secretory antidiarrheal, with Phase III trials in cancer therapy-related diarrhea (CTD) potentially opening a market 10x larger than the current HIV/AIDS indication [1]. Revenue has grown from $4.3M (2021) to $11.8M (TTM) demonstrating the Mytesi commercial ramp, and Q2 2025 revenue grew 35% sequentially and 10% YoY [1]. The pipeline includes Phase II trials for orphan indications (short bowel syndrome, microvillus inclusion disease) that could command premium pricing. CEO and board insider buying of $3.45M at-the-market signals leadership conviction [2]. Gross margins of 81.9% on Mytesi sales demonstrate the unit economics work if volume scales.
The bear case is existential. Jaguar Health has a $5.1M market cap, $3.5M cash, $31.9M debt, and burns ~$26M annually in operating cash flow — the company faces immediate going-concern risk without additional financing [3]. D/E of 731.8% and negative ROE of -411.6% reflect a balance sheet in distress. The $0.41 stock price and penny stock status severely limit institutional participation and create dilution risk from inevitable capital raises. Revenue growth has stalled at -0.8% YoY despite years of Mytesi commercialization, suggesting a ceiling on the HIV/AIDS diarrhea market. Phase III CTD trial success is uncertain, and even positive results require years and additional capital to commercialize. Operating losses of ~$8M per quarter are expanding, not contracting [1]. The company's survival depends entirely on securing financing and achieving clinical milestones before capital is exhausted.
Key Catalysts
  1. Phase III CTD trial readout for crofelemer in cancer therapy-related diarrhea — a positive result could open a significantly larger market than the current HIV/AIDS indication [1]
  2. Orphan drug designations for short bowel syndrome and microvillus inclusion disease, which could provide regulatory fast-track and premium pricing if trials succeed [1]
  3. Potential partnership or licensing deal for crofelemer with a larger pharma company, providing non-dilutive capital and commercial infrastructure
Key Risks
  1. Going-concern risk — $3.5M cash with $26M annual cash burn and $31.9M debt creates imminent financing need; failure to raise capital could force restructuring or bankruptcy [3]
  2. Dilution risk — at $0.41/share with a $5.1M market cap, any equity raise will be massively dilutive to existing shareholders [3]
  3. Clinical trial failure — Phase III CTD results are binary; a negative readout would eliminate the primary growth catalyst and likely trigger further stock decline [1]
