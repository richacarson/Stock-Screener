Company Profile
Real Estate ·REIT - Residential ·NYSE ·United States
Janus Living, Inc. is the only U.S. publicly traded REIT focused exclusively on the senior housing sector, with its entire portfolio owned and operated under RIDEA structures. The company's initial portfolio consists of 34 senior housing communities comprising 10,422 units across 10 states, concentrated in Florida and Texas retirement markets. Spun off from Healthpeak Properties and IPO'd in March 2026.

Investment Thesis
Janus Living is the only US pure-play senior housing REIT, positioned to capture the multi-decade Baby Boomer aging wave with 34 communities (10,422 units) concentrated in high-demand Florida and Texas markets [1][4]. Q1 2026 delivered 13.8% same-store NOI growth with 150bps margin expansion, $400M acquisition pipeline at 7.5-8.5% yields, and Morgan Stanley forecasts sector-leading 18% annual FFO growth through 2027 [1][6]. The fortress balance sheet ($949M cash, no debt, $600M undrawn credit) provides exceptional acquisition firepower.
Key concerns center on valuation (Forward P/E 44.5x is premium for a REIT), limited operating history as an independent entity (IPO March 2026), and geographic concentration risk with 69% of units in Florida and Texas [2]. The RIDEA structure provides operational upside but also carries higher risk than triple-net lease models if occupancy or labor costs deteriorate. As a newly public company with only one quarter of independent results, investors lack visibility into management's ability to execute the acquisition-driven growth strategy without overpaying or diluting returns.
Key Catalysts
  1. Successful closure of $400M acquisition pipeline in Q2 2026 at targeted 7.5-8.5% stabilized cash NOI yields, demonstrating accretive capital deployment capability [1][3]
  2. Continued same-store NOI growth above 11% guidance as senior housing occupancy recovers toward pre-pandemic levels, driven by 75M+ aging Boomers entering the demographic sweet spot
  3. Analyst coverage expansion and REIT index inclusion driving institutional capital flows into the only US pure-play senior housing REIT, potentially compressing cap rates and expanding valuation multiples [6]
Key Risks
  1. Elevated valuation at 44.5x Forward P/E (FFO-adjusted P/FFO may be more reasonable) creates downside risk if growth disappoints or interest rates remain elevated, compressing REIT valuations broadly [2]
  2. Geographic concentration: 69% of units in Florida and Texas exposes the portfolio to regional risks including insurance costs (hurricane exposure), property taxes, and local regulatory changes
  3. Execution risk on acquisition-driven growth: $400M pipeline must close at attractive yields without integration issues, and continued capital deployment requires demonstrating accretive acquisitions without overpaying in a competitive market for senior housing assets [1][3]
