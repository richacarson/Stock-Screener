Company Profile
Real Estate ·REIT - Residential ·NYSE ·United States
Janus Living, Inc. is the only U.S. publicly traded REIT focused exclusively on the senior housing sector, spun off from Healthpeak Properties in early 2026. The portfolio comprises 40 senior housing communities with approximately 10,422 units across 10 states, concentrated in Florida and Texas (69% of units). All communities operate under RIDEA structures with resident-pay models. Led by CEO Scott Brinker (also Healthpeak's former CEO). Headquartered in Denver, Colorado. Market cap ~$5.0B.

Investment Thesis
Janus Living offers pure-play exposure to the secular 'silver tsunami' demographic tailwind — the 80+ population is growing rapidly while senior housing construction starts dropped 18% in 2025, creating a supply-demand imbalance that drives occupancy and pricing power [1][2]. Q1 2026 results confirm the thesis: same-store revenue grew 7.6% on 230bps occupancy gains and 4.7% RevPOR growth, with adjusted NOI increasing 13.8% and margins expanding 150bps [1]. The zero-debt balance sheet with $949M unrestricted cash provides substantial acquisition firepower, and the $714M in post-IPO acquisitions demonstrates management's ability to deploy capital aggressively. FFO guidance of $0.93-0.97/share for 2026 with 11-15% same-store NOI growth implies strong cash flow trajectory.
The primary concern is valuation — at a forward P/E of 63.6x and a $5.0B market cap for a portfolio generating ~$200M quarterly revenue with effectively zero GAAP net income, Janus Living is priced for flawless execution of its growth strategy [1]. The company has no independent operating history, having existed as a standalone entity since March 2026. Geographic concentration in Florida and Texas (69% of units) creates climate and regulatory risk. The RIDEA structure, while providing operating upside, also exposes JAN to operating risk that triple-net lease REITs avoid — labor costs, regulatory compliance, and resident satisfaction directly impact earnings [2][3]. The senior housing sector also carries regulatory and reputational risks from elder care quality issues, and the company's reliance on third-party operators introduces execution risk beyond direct management control. The 2.15% dividend yield with no growth history provides limited income support for the premium valuation.
Key Catalysts
  1. Silver tsunami demographics driving occupancy toward 90%+ as the 80+ population grows 3-4% annually while senior housing supply remains constrained by historically low construction starts
  2. Continued acquisition-driven growth deploying the $949M cash position into accretive senior housing communities at attractive cap rates before capital markets competition intensifies
  3. Same-store NOI growth of 11-15% guided for 2026 with margin expansion as occupancy gains and RevPOR growth compound within the RIDEA operating structure
Key Risks
  1. Forward P/E of 63.6x and zero GAAP profitability leave no margin of safety — any operational shortfall, acquisition integration issues, or occupancy slowdown could trigger severe multiple compression
  2. Geographic concentration with 69% of units in Florida and Texas exposes the portfolio to climate events (hurricanes, extreme heat), state regulatory changes, and regional economic cycles
  3. No independent operating history as a standalone entity — the company has existed for only months, and management's ability to execute acquisitions, integrate portfolios, and manage operators without Healthpeak's infrastructure is unproven
