Investment Thesis
Jabil has successfully transformed from a consumer electronics contract manufacturer into a critical AI infrastructure provider, with $12.1B in AI-related revenue projected for FY2026. The company's expertise in liquid cooling, optical interconnects, and server platforms positions it as a structural beneficiary of hyperscaler capex cycles.
Under CEO Dastoor's capital-efficient 'Jabil 3.0' strategy, the company targets 5.7% core operating margins and $1.3B+ adjusted free cash flow in FY2026. However, thin profit margins typical of EMS businesses (2.5%) and elevated leverage (D/E 329%) temper the thesis. The stock trades at a forward PEG of 0.82, suggesting the market has not fully priced in AI-driven growth acceleration.
Key Catalysts
  1. AI infrastructure revenue growing 35% YoY to $12.1B in FY2026 with potential upside from hyperscaler capex acceleration
  2. GLP-1 drug delivery device manufacturing contracts providing healthcare diversification with secular growth tailwinds
  3. Raised FY2026 core EPS outlook to $12.25 from $11.55, signaling continued margin expansion and operational leverage
Key Risks
  1. Customer concentration risk — top-10 customers including Apple and Amazon represent outsized revenue share; loss of any key relationship would materially impact results
  2. Elevated debt-to-equity of 329% funded by aggressive share repurchases creates financial fragility if AI infrastructure spending cycles down
  3. Structurally thin profit margins (~2.5%) typical of EMS industry leave limited buffer against cost inflation or pricing pressure from hyperscaler customers
