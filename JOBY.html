Investment Thesis
Joby is the global leader in eVTOL certification progress with first FAA-conforming aircraft testing underway, selection for the White House pilot program across 10 states, and $1.4B in liquidity providing multi-year runway [1]. Strategic partnerships with Toyota (manufacturing), Delta (distribution), and Uber (platform) create a uniquely integrated air mobility ecosystem. The Xwing autonomy acquisition positions Joby for future autonomous operations. At $9.23 after significant pullback from highs, the risk-reward profile improves for investors with a 5+ year horizon.
The bear case is significant: Joby remains pre-commercial with no certified aircraft, $350M+ quarterly cash burn, and $9.0B market cap on $53.4M TTM revenue [1]. The 2026 guidance of $105-150M is primarily from Blade's existing helicopter business, not eVTOL operations. FAA certification requires completing Stage 5 and final regulatory acceptances — delays are common in aerospace. Production scaling to four aircraft per month by 2027 is aggressive for a company that hasn't yet produced a certified unit. Cash runway of 4-5 years at current burn means profitability must be demonstrated or additional dilutive capital raised.
Key Catalysts
  1. FAA Type Inspection Authorization completion and progress toward final certification — the single most important de-risking event for the stock
  2. Commencement of passenger operations in UAE and eIPP locations providing first revenue from Joby-branded eVTOL services
  3. Production scale-up toward four aircraft per month by 2027, demonstrating manufacturing viability for the Bevirt/Toyota collaboration
Key Risks
  1. FAA certification delays — Stage 5 and final regulatory acceptance timelines are uncertain and historically subject to extensions in new aircraft categories
  2. Cash burn of $350M+ quarterly against $1.4B liquidity means 4-5 years of runway without additional capital, which would be dilutive
  3. Production scaling risk — transitioning from prototype to serial manufacturing of a novel aircraft type presents significant engineering and supply chain challenges
