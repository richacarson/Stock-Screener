Investment Thesis
Jet.AI offers a speculative optionality play at $0.10/share: the flyExclusive merger (expected close April 2026) could create a combined entity with meaningful aviation revenue, the Convergence Compute data center JV could tap into hyperscale AI infrastructure demand, and the $5 million buyback signals management confidence [1]. Net income of $4.6 million in 2025 (vs. -$12.7 million in 2024) shows financial trajectory improvement, and $13.7 million in cash with no debt provides a modest runway.
The bear case is severe: JTAI is a 7-employee company trading at $0.10 with declining revenue (-44.3% YoY), negative gross margins, and a chaotic strategic pivot from aviation AI to data centers [1]. The flyExclusive merger adds another struggling company's problems rather than solving Jet.AI's. Data center development requires billions in capital that Jet.AI cannot access at its scale, making the Convergence Compute JV aspirational at best. The $250 million shelf facility suggests future dilution, and the reported positive metrics (50% margin, 32% ROE) are one-time artifacts, not sustainable operations. At $0.10/share, the risk of total loss is substantial.
