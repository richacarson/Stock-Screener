It operates through Government Solutions (Mission Technology Solutions) focused on defense, intelligence, and space, and Sustainable Technology Solutions providing proprietary process technologies for ammonia, petrochemicals, and decarbonization.

Investment Thesis
KBR trades at a compelling 8.4x forward P/E with a PEG ratio of 0.49, offering significant value for a defense/government services company with $18.9B in Mission Tech backlog and $17B in bids awaiting awards (80% new business) [1]. The company delivered 52.4% EPS growth in FY2025 with improving Mission Tech EBITDA margins from 9.8% to 10.4%, and 82% of 2026 guidance is covered by existing contracts with no material recompetes [1]. The planned SpinCo separation of Mission Tech ($5.3B revenue, 10.5% EBITDA margins) could unlock substantial conglomerate discount value. Free cash flow of $298M easily covers the $0.66/year dividend (18.9% payout ratio), with capacity for buybacks and debt reduction [4]. ROE of 30.7% demonstrates capital efficiency despite the engineering/services sector's thin operating margins.
The bear case centers on revenue volatility and leverage. Q4 2025 revenue declined $223M YoY due to award timing in Mission Tech and UCOM contingency scope reductions, and full-year revenue growth was essentially flat at 1% despite strong EPS growth driven by margin improvement [1]. D/E of 191.7% creates interest expense sensitivity — $2.9B in debt against $511M cash leaves limited balance sheet flexibility if contract awards slow [4]. Profit margin of 5.3% is thin, leaving little room for execution missteps. The SpinCo separation creates near-term uncertainty about organizational structure, stranded costs, and which entity retains which assets/contracts. KBR's traditional engineering culture and bureaucratic processes (noted in Glassdoor reviews) could hinder its ability to compete for technology-intensive contracts requiring rapid AI and digital innovation [2]. Government spending priorities under changing administrations create cyclical risk for defense services revenue.
Key Catalysts
  1. SpinCo separation of Mission Tech ($5.3B revenue, $18.9B backlog) expected to unlock conglomerate discount and create two focused entities with clearer investment profiles [1]
  2. Bidding pipeline expansion to $25B+ in 2026 (double-digit YoY increase) with $17B in bids awaiting awards, 80% representing new business growth [1]
  3. Margin expansion trajectory — Mission Tech EBITDA margins improved from 9.8% to 10.4% with further expansion expected as program mix shifts toward higher-value technology work [1]
Key Risks
  1. Government contract award timing volatility — Q4 2025 revenue declined $223M YoY, and lumpy award patterns create quarterly earnings unpredictability typical of defense contractors [1]
  2. High leverage (D/E 191.7%) with $2.9B in debt creates interest expense sensitivity and limits balance sheet flexibility if contract revenue declines [4]
  3. SpinCo separation execution risk — organizational restructuring could cause talent attrition, stranded costs, and customer uncertainty during the transition period [1]
