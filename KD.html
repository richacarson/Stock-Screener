Investment Thesis
Kyndryl is a deep-value IT infrastructure services play trading at 6.0x forward earnings as it transitions from declining IBM legacy managed services to an AI-powered consulting model [1]. The Kyndryl Bridge platform now has 1,400+ onboarded customers generating 16 million+ AI insights per month, the applications/data/AI practice grew double digits, and agentic AI capabilities are deployed across 200,000+ customer devices [2]. FY2026 signings of $13.5B included 38 contracts over $50M with new-scope deals doubling to 30%+ of total. Adjusted EBITDA of $2.7B (up 6% YoY) and adjusted pretax income of $581M (up 21% YoY) demonstrate the margin expansion story is working beneath the surface [1]. At $2.5B market cap against $15.1B revenue, the valuation embeds substantial pessimism.
The bear case is compelling: GAAP net income fell 21% to $198M, EPS declined 19% to $0.85, and constant-currency revenue was down 3% in FY2026 [1]. The stock has collapsed 73% from its $44.20 high to $11.32, reflecting market skepticism about Kyndryl's ability to grow while competing against hyperscalers building their own managed services. D/E of 383% with $4.96B total debt (including $1.8B current maturities) against $1.3B equity creates refinancing risk in a higher-rate environment [4]. The $200M workforce rebalancing eliminating ~10,000 positions signals that the AI-driven efficiency story comes at the cost of organizational stability. Insider selling, the absence of insider buying despite the collapse, and the -4.8% 5-year revenue CAGR raise serious questions about whether the platform pivot can offset structural legacy contract erosion.
Key Catalysts
  1. Workforce rebalancing targeting $400-500M in annualized operating expense savings by FY2028, potentially driving adjusted pretax margins above 5% and meaningful EPS acceleration [1]
  2. Kyndryl Bridge platform scaling — 1,400+ customers with 190+ new services and $3B in annual customer savings, positioning for AI infrastructure buildout revenue growth [2]
  3. Agentic AI commercialization across mainframe, IT operations, and digital twin deployments creating new high-margin recurring revenue streams [2]
Key Risks
  1. Hyperscaler disintermediation — AWS, Azure, and GCP are building managed services that directly compete with Kyndryl's core offerings, and the -4.8% 5yr revenue CAGR suggests legacy erosion is not yet offset by new business [1]
  2. Balance sheet fragility — D/E of 383% with $4.96B total debt including $1.8B in current maturities against only $1.3B equity creates significant refinancing risk [4]
  3. Workforce instability — the $200M rebalancing eliminating ~10,000 positions combined with insider selling and 73% stock collapse undermine employee retention and morale during a critical transformation period [1][2]
