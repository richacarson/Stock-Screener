Investment Thesis
KeyCorp at 9.8x forward earnings with a 3.9% dividend yield offers compelling value in a recovering regional bank. Q1 2026 delivered 33% EPS growth, 10% revenue growth, and record investment banking fees, with full-year guidance projecting 7% revenue growth and 9-10% NII expansion [1]. The Scotiabank strategic investment provides capital support, and priority fee-based businesses growing 12% YoY demonstrate successful diversification beyond net interest income dependency. AUM reaching $70B positions the wealth management franchise for continued growth.
The bear case centers on structural regional banking headwinds: ROE at 10% remains below cost of equity for many investors, the 5-year revenue CAGR of just 1.2% reveals anemic underlying growth, and dividend growth has stalled since 2023 [1][4]. The CIO departure at a critical juncture for digital banking transformation is concerning [2]. Credit quality risks loom if commercial real estate or consumer lending deteriorate in a slowing economy. The PEG ratio of 1.99 suggests the market already prices in modest growth, limiting upside potential unless KEY can accelerate fee income and improve returns on equity.
