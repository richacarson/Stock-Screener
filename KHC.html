Investment Thesis
Kraft Heinz offers deep value at 11.2x forward earnings with a 6.8% dividend yield, supported by $3.2B in annual free cash flow and a manageable balance sheet with D/E of just 50%. The FY2025 net loss was driven by $7.8B in non-cash goodwill impairments rather than operational deterioration, and forward EPS estimates of $2.11 suggest normalized profitability is intact. New CEO Steve Cahillane's $600M investment program and rejection of the breakup plan could catalyze a return to volume-led growth, and the stock trades near multi-year lows with significant upside if execution improves [1][4].
The bear case is significant: revenue has contracted for three consecutive years with 5-year CAGR of -1.5%, reflecting secular decline in processed food as consumers shift toward fresh, less-processed alternatives. The dividend has been frozen at $1.60/share annually since the 2019 cut from $2.50, making the 6.8% yield a potential value trap if revenue erosion accelerates. Negative ROE of -12.6% and profit margins of -23% — even if impairment-driven — highlight the fragility of the earnings base, and the company faces continued private-label competition and input cost inflation in categories with limited pricing power [1][3].
Key Catalysts
  1. Successful execution of $600M commercial investment program driving volume recovery and return to organic revenue growth in FY2026
  2. Normalization of earnings to approximately $2.11 forward EPS after FY2025 impairment charges, potentially re-rating the stock from deep value
  3. NotCo AI partnership and smart factory automation delivering measurable cost savings and accelerating product development cycles
Key Risks
  1. Secular decline in processed food categories as consumers shift to fresh, health-conscious alternatives eroding the core Kraft, Oscar Mayer, and Maxwell House portfolios
  2. Frozen dividend at $1.60/share since 2019 with zero growth trajectory signals lack of confidence in sustainable earnings power and dividend growth resumption
  3. Berkshire Hathaway's gradual position reduction removes the implicit floor under the stock and signals diminishing conviction from the company's largest shareholder
