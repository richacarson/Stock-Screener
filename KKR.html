Company Profile
Financial Services ·Asset Management ·NYSE ·United States
KKR is a global alternative asset manager with $758B in assets under management (Q1 2026) spanning private equity, credit, real estate, infrastructure, insurance (Global Atlantic), and energy. Founded in 1976 by Henry Kravis and George Roberts, the firm is led by co-CEOs Scott Nuttall and Joe Bae with a target to reach $1T AUM by 2030. KKR has committed $42B of equity into digital infrastructure across 23 investments and hired former AWS CEO Adam Selipsky to guide its AI infrastructure strategy.

Investment Thesis
KKR is a generational alternative asset manager with $758B AUM (up 14% YoY), trading at a compelling 14.5x forward P/E and 0.69 PEG ratio, backed by $46M in insider buying from both co-CEOs and three directors in early 2026 [1][2]. Q1 2026 delivered total revenues of $4.32B with adjusted net income of $1.25B, fee-related earnings of $1.02B (up 24% YoY), and management fees of $1.19B (up 30% YoY) [1]. The firm raised $28B of new capital in Q1 alone, with $125B of uncalled commitments providing future deployment runway toward the $1T AUM target by 2030. KKR's $42B digital infrastructure commitment (155+ data center facilities, 15GW development pipeline, former AWS CEO as advisor) positions the firm as a picks-and-shovels beneficiary of the AI buildout [2]. The 25% stock decline from its 52-week high of $153.87 to $91.51 creates a potential entry point for a franchise business with multiple growth vectors across PE, credit, real estate, infrastructure, and insurance.
The bear case centers on execution complexity, revenue volatility, and valuation compression risk. KKR's revenue is inherently volatile — 2025 full-year revenue of $25.65B was down 2.92% YoY despite strong Q1 2026, reflecting lumpy carried interest and investment income recognition patterns that make earnings prediction difficult [1][4]. The consolidated accounting with Global Atlantic compresses reported metrics: profit margin of 11.68% and ROE of 7.66% understate the management company's standalone profitability (~27% operating margin). The 5yr revenue CAGR of approximately 1% reflects extreme accounting volatility rather than underlying business trajectory, but it creates optical weakness. ESG credibility risk is material — KKR's $45B in sustainability investments conflicts with Coastal GasLink Pipeline ownership, Port Arthur LNG terminal investment, and environmental justice concerns, resulting in an MSCI ESG rating of only BB [3]. The $42B digital infrastructure deployment carries concentration risk — a hyperscaler capex pause, power permitting delays, or technology shifts could impair returns across interconnected investments. At $82B market cap, KKR must sustain AUM growth and realize carried interest to justify the premium to book value (2.91x P/B) [4].
