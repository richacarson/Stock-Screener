Investment Thesis
KKR is a generational alternative asset manager trading at a compelling valuation — 11.3x forward P/E with $744B AUM, targeting $1T by 2030 — backed by $46M in insider buying from both co-CEOs and three directors in February-March 2026 [1][2]. The firm's $42B digital infrastructure commitment (155+ data center facilities, 15GW development pipeline, former AWS CEO as advisor) positions KKR as a picks-and-shovels beneficiary of the AI buildout, differentiated from Blackstone's standalone infrastructure approach [2]. Q4 2025 EPS of $1.12 beat consensus, and forward EPS of $7.84 implies a 3.3x earnings recovery from trailing levels as performance fees and carried interest normalize [1]. KKR's transformation into a diversified platform (PE, credit, real estate, infrastructure, insurance via Global Atlantic) creates multiple growth vectors and reduces dependence on any single market cycle. The $10B ST Telemedia data center acquisition in January 2026 demonstrates continued execution against the AI infrastructure thesis [1].
The bear case centers on execution complexity, fee compression, and fossil fuel exposure. KKR's ambitious $1T AUM target requires massive fundraising execution in a potentially crowded alternative asset market — fee compression from institutional investors and competition from Blackstone, Apollo, and Carlyle could squeeze management fee margins [1]. Revenue is inherently volatile — full-year 2025 revenue declined 11% YoY to $19.2B despite the Q4 76% quarterly surge, reflecting the lumpy nature of investment income and carried interest [1]. The consolidated accounting with Global Atlantic (insurance) compresses reported margins to 9.24% and ROE to 8.56%, making the business appear less profitable than the management company alone [4]. ESG credibility risk is material — KKR's $45B in sustainability investments conflicts with its Coastal GasLink Pipeline ownership, Port Arthur LNG terminal investment, and environmental justice concerns in affected communities [3]. The stock's 12% decline from SMA-50 may reflect broader concerns about private equity valuations, fundraising headwinds, or macro uncertainty. At $82B market cap, KKR must sustain AUM growth, realize carried interest, and deploy the $42B digital infrastructure commitment successfully to justify the premium to book value (2.8x P/B) [4].
