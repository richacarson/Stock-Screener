Company Profile
Energy ·Oil & Gas Midstream ·NYSE ·United States
Kinder Morgan, Inc. operates as one of the largest energy infrastructure companies in North America. It owns and operates approximately 79,000 miles of pipelines and 139 terminals transporting natural gas, refined petroleum products, crude oil, and CO2. The company operates through Natural Gas Pipelines, Products Pipelines, Terminals, and CO2 segments.

Investment Thesis
KMI offers an attractive income-and-growth combination as the largest U.S. natural gas pipeline operator, benefiting directly from the AI-driven power demand cycle. Full-year 2025 net income rose 17% to $3.05B, adjusted EPS grew 13%, and the $10B approved project backlog with ~60% tied to power-related demand provides multi-year earnings visibility [1]. The 3.5% dividend yield is well-covered by distributable cash flow, 2026 guidance calls for 4% EBITDA growth and 8% EPS growth, and rising LNG export volumes (feed gas demand expected at 19.8 Bcf/day in 2026) underpin the natural gas demand thesis [1].
The bear case focuses on valuation and structural limitations. The forward P/E of 22.0x is above KMI's historical range, suggesting the AI-data-center demand story may be priced in. The 5-year revenue CAGR is negative (-4.1%) reflecting commodity price sensitivity, the 78.5% payout ratio limits dividend growth capacity, and the 2.4% 5-year dividend growth CAGR barely matches inflation [4]. KMI's debt-to-equity of 99% remains elevated for a capital-intensive business, and the transition away from fossil fuels — while slow — represents a multi-decade structural headwind. The moderate ESG controversy score of 5 from Sustainalytics suggests ongoing environmental scrutiny of pipeline operations [3].
Key Catalysts
  1. AI data center power demand driving record natural gas transportation volumes, with U.S. LNG feed gas expected to reach 19.8 Bcf/day in 2026, directly benefiting KMI's largest segment [1]
  2. Execution of the $10B approved project backlog, with approximately $6B tied to power-generation and LNG-related infrastructure expected to generate returns over 2026-2029
  3. Energy Transition Ventures — RNG production capacity at 6.9 Bcf/year and hydrogen-ready pipeline retrofits position KMI for regulatory tailwinds and long-term fuel diversification
Key Risks
  1. Natural gas price volatility could compress revenue and margins, as seen in the 2022-2024 period when commodity price swings created a negative 3-year revenue CAGR despite volume growth
  2. Regulatory and environmental risk — pipeline permitting delays, methane emission regulations, and potential carbon pricing could increase operating costs and slow project development
  3. Valuation risk at 22x forward earnings — the AI-power-demand narrative has lifted KMI's stock 23% in 6 months, and any softening in data center growth forecasts could trigger a multiple contraction
