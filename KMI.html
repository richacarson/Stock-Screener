Company Profile
Energy ·Oil & Gas Midstream ·NYSE ·United States
Kinder Morgan, Inc. operates as one of the largest energy infrastructure companies in North America, owning and operating approximately 79,000 miles of pipelines and 139 terminals. Segments include Natural Gas Pipelines, Products Pipelines, Terminals, and CO2. The company transports approximately 40% of U.S. natural gas consumption and is a key beneficiary of rising LNG export and data center power demand. Headquartered in Houston, Texas.

Investment Thesis
KMI delivered a strong Q1 2026 with $4.83B revenue (up 14% YoY), net income of $976M (up 36%), and adjusted EPS of $0.48 (up 41%), crushing analyst estimates [1]. The company sits at the intersection of two secular tailwinds: surging LNG export demand and AI-driven data center power consumption, both of which require massive natural gas transportation infrastructure. KMI's $10.1B project backlog (92% natural gas) provides multi-year revenue visibility, and the 3.4% dividend yield with 8 consecutive years of growth offers income investors a growing cash return. At 22x forward earnings, KMI trades at a reasonable premium for a utility-like business with improving growth dynamics and near-zero AI disruption risk.
The bear case centers on valuation and structural growth limitations. At a PEG ratio of 4.03 and Forward P/E of 22.4, KMI is priced for growth that may not materialize — management's own FY2026 guidance calls for only 5% adjusted EPS growth and 4% EBITDA growth, a modest step-down from Q1 outperformance [1]. The payout ratio of 78.5% leaves limited room for dividend acceleration, and the 5-year dividend growth CAGR of 2.1% trails inflation. ROE of 10.6% is below cost of capital for an infrastructure business, and the 3-year revenue CAGR is negative (-4.1%) due to commodity pass-through volatility from the FY2022 peak [2]. KMI also faces long-term energy transition risk — while natural gas is currently benefiting from data center and LNG demand, policy shifts toward electrification and renewables could erode the terminal value of pipeline assets over a 20-30 year horizon.
Key Catalysts
  1. AI data center power demand driving incremental natural gas transportation volumes, with KMI's pipeline network uniquely positioned to serve power generation facilities across the Gulf Coast and Southeast
  2. LNG export capacity expansion at Sabine Pass, Golden Pass, and other Gulf Coast terminals increasing feed gas demand through KMI's pipeline network, with multiple new facilities under construction for 2027-2028 start-up
  3. Project backlog of $10.1B (92% natural gas) converting to revenue and EBITDA through FY2027-2028, providing visible organic growth beyond the base dividend
Key Risks
  1. PEG ratio of 4.03 and management guidance of only 5% EPS growth suggest the stock is fully valued for its growth profile — any execution miss could trigger multiple compression from the 22x forward P/E
  2. 78.5% payout ratio and 2.1% dividend growth CAGR trail inflation, limiting the dividend growth story that has attracted income investors and creating pressure to maintain rather than accelerate distributions
  3. Long-term energy transition risk from electrification and renewable energy adoption could erode natural gas transportation volumes over a 20-30 year horizon, reducing terminal value of pipeline assets

AI cannot transport natural gas, operate compressor stations, or replace physical infrastructure [1]. Moreover, AI is a direct demand catalyst: data centers powering AI workloads require enormous electricity, and natural gas is the primary fuel source for U.S. power generation. KMI's project backlog of $10.1B is 92% natural gas-related, with significant exposure to LNG feed gas and power generation interconnects driven by AI data center demand [1]. The only modest risk is that AI-driven energy efficiency could slightly reduce per-unit gas demand over the very long term. AI Opportunity (1/1): KMI generates indirect but meaningful AI-related revenue through natural gas transportation to AI-driven data center power plants. The company's Palantir partnership for pipeline optimization and digital twin technology also represents genuine AI-enabled operational efficiency [3].
