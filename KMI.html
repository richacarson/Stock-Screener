Company Profile
Energy ·Oil & Gas Midstream ·NYSE ·United States
Kinder Morgan, Inc. operates as one of the largest energy infrastructure companies in North America, owning and operating approximately 79,000 miles of pipelines and 139 terminals. The company transports natural gas, refined products, crude oil, and CO2 through its Natural Gas Pipelines, Products Pipelines, Terminals, and CO2 segments. Headquartered in Houston, Texas.

Investment Thesis
Kinder Morgan is the premier play on AI-driven natural gas demand, operating 40% of U.S. natural gas pipeline capacity with a $10.1B project backlog that is 92% natural gas-related [1]. Q1 2026 delivered adjusted EPS of $0.48 (up 41% YoY) on $4.83B revenue (up 14%), crushing estimates and prompting full-year guidance of $1.37 adjusted EPS and $8.7B EBITDA [1]. The stock yields 3.7% with 16 consecutive years of payments and 8 years of consecutive growth since the 2016 rebuild. AI data center power demand is creating a secular tailwind for natural gas infrastructure that could sustain above-trend growth through the end of the decade, and KMI's dominant pipeline network gives it first-mover advantage on expansion projects.
The bear case centers on valuation relative to growth and long-term demand risk. At 20.9x forward earnings with a PEG of 3.72, KMI is priced for perfection despite a modest 2026 EBITDA growth guide of only 4% and a negative 3-year revenue CAGR of -4.1% [5]. The 78.9% payout ratio leaves limited room for dividend acceleration, and the 5-year dividend growth CAGR of just 2.1% underperforms inflation. ROE of 10.6% and D/E of 98.8% reflect the capital-intensive nature of midstream infrastructure. Long-term, if renewable energy displaces natural gas for power generation over the next 15-20 years, KMI's asset base could face stranding risk. Near-term, any slowdown in AI data center buildouts would remove the primary growth narrative supporting the current multiple.
Key Catalysts
  1. AI data center natural gas demand accelerating pipeline expansion project approvals, with $10.1B backlog (92% gas) converting to contracted revenue through 2027-2028
  2. LNG export capacity additions in 2026-2027 driving incremental natural gas transportation volumes through KMI's Gulf Coast pipeline network
  3. Q1 2026 EPS beat of 41% YoY potentially signaling upside to full-year $1.37 adjusted EPS guidance, with operating leverage from fee-based contract escalators
Key Risks
  1. Forward PEG of 3.72 and 20.9x forward P/E reflect premium valuation that leaves minimal margin of safety if AI data center demand growth disappoints or regulatory delays stall expansion projects
  2. 78.9% payout ratio constrains dividend growth potential, with 5-year CAGR of just 2.1% — well below inflation — risking income investor disappointment if growth capital needs increase
  3. Long-term natural gas demand destruction from renewable energy buildout and potential carbon regulation could strand pipeline assets over a 15-20 year horizon, with limited evidence of transformative business model adaptation
