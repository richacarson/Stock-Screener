Company Profile
Energy ·Oil & Gas Midstream ·NYSE ·United States
Kinder Morgan, Inc. is one of the largest energy infrastructure companies in North America, operating approximately 79,000 miles of pipelines and 139 terminals. The company transports natural gas, refined petroleum products, crude oil, condensate, CO2, and other products. It operates through four segments: Natural Gas Pipelines, Products Pipelines, Terminals, and CO2. KMI's natural gas pipeline network handles approximately 40% of U.S. natural gas consumption. Founded in 1997 and headquartered in Houston, Texas.

Investment Thesis
KMI delivered an exceptional Q1 2026 with net income surging 36% to $976M and adjusted EPS up 41% to $0.48, driven by natural gas pipeline demand from LNG exports and AI data center power requirements [1]. The $10.1B project backlog (92% natural gas) provides multi-year growth visibility, and LNG feed gas demand is expected to reach 19.8 Bcf/day in 2026, up 19% from 2025. At a 3.5% dividend yield with 6 consecutive years of growth and a forward P/E of 22x, KMI offers an attractive combination of income and infrastructure-driven growth as America's largest natural gas pipeline operator handling ~40% of U.S. consumption.
The bear case centers on valuation and structural concerns. The forward P/E of 22x and PEG of 3.98 are elevated for a midstream infrastructure company with mid-single-digit organic growth, reflecting significant AI-driven demand expectations already priced in [4]. The 78.5% payout ratio leaves limited room for dividend acceleration, and the 5-year dividend growth CAGR of only 2.4% reflects the slow recovery from the traumatic 75% cut in 2016 that destroyed shareholder trust. D/E of 98.8% and $32.2B in total debt against only $76M in cash create refinancing risk if interest rates remain elevated. Long-term, the energy transition could reduce natural gas demand if renewables and battery storage achieve cost parity, though this risk is offset by near-term AI and LNG demand tailwinds.

  3. Continued dividend growth trajectory with 2026 budget calling for $1.36 adjusted EPS (up 5%), supporting further dividend increases toward full restoration of pre-2016 credibility
Key Risks
  1. Elevated payout ratio of 78.5% and slow 2.4% 5-year dividend growth CAGR limit dividend acceleration potential, with the 2016 cut still fresh in income investors' memory
  2. Forward PEG of 3.98 suggests AI-driven natural gas demand growth is already priced into the stock at 22x forward earnings, creating downside risk if data center buildout slows
  3. $32.2B total debt with only $76M cash creates refinancing exposure in a sustained high-rate environment, and any commodity price collapse could pressure pipeline throughput volumes
