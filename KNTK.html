Company Profile
Energy ·Oil & Gas Midstream ·NYSE ·United States
Kinetik Holdings Inc. operates as a midstream energy company in the Texas Delaware Basin, providing gathering, compression, processing, stabilization, treating, transportation, and storage services for natural gas, NGLs, and crude oil. The company also offers water gathering and disposal services and sells condensates, natural gas residue, and NGLs through its Midstream Logistics and Pipeline Transportation segments.

Investment Thesis
Kinetik offers a compelling combination of 6.5% dividend yield and mid-single-digit growth from its dominant Delaware Basin midstream position. Revenue grew 18.9% YoY to $1.76B in 2025, and management guides for 7% adjusted EBITDA growth in 2026 to $950M-$1.05B with planned 3-5% annual dividend increases [1][2]. The company benefits from secular tailwinds — Permian Basin production continues to grow, and AI-driven data center demand is increasing natural gas consumption. CEO Jamie Welch's 6% ownership stake ($412M) provides strong alignment with shareholders [5].
The bear case centers on payout sustainability and leverage. The 118.6% payout ratio (on a net income basis) means the dividend currently exceeds earnings, relying on distributable cash flow which can be volatile with commodity prices [2]. D/E of 132% reflects $3.9B in debt against a $3.4B market cap, and the negative free cash flow of -$130M in 2025 demonstrates that growth capex is consuming cash. Forward P/E of 24.3x prices in substantial growth that depends on continued Permian Basin volume expansion — any production slowdown from lower oil prices would pressure throughput volumes and dividend coverage.
Key Catalysts
  1. Permian Basin production growth driving higher throughput volumes across KNTK's gathering, processing, and transportation infrastructure through 2027
  2. AI data center buildout increasing natural gas demand and supporting long-term volume growth for midstream operators
  3. Completion of 2026 growth capex projects ($450-510M) transitioning to free cash flow generation and improved dividend coverage in 2027
Key Risks
  1. Oil price decline below $55/bbl reducing Permian Basin drilling activity and pressuring throughput volumes across KNTK's system
  2. Elevated payout ratio of 118.6% on net income basis leaves dividend vulnerable if distributable cash flow falls short of management projections
  3. Customer concentration risk in the Delaware Basin — a slowdown in this specific sub-basin would disproportionately impact KNTK relative to more diversified midstream peers
