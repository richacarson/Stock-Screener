Company Profile
Consumer Staples ·Beverages—Non-Alcoholic ·NYSE ·United States
The Coca-Cola Company is the world's largest non-alcoholic beverage company, owning or licensing more than 500 beverage brands sold in over 200 countries. Coca-Cola operates a franchise-bottling model, concentrating on brand ownership, marketing, and concentrate production while relying on bottling partners for manufacturing and distribution. The company has paid dividends for over 60 consecutive years and grown them for 62+ consecutive years.

Investment Thesis
Coca-Cola is a classic compounding dividend machine with 62 consecutive years of dividend growth, $12.2B projected free cash flow in 2026, and record operating margins of 31.2%[1]. The brand's 19 consecutive quarters of global value share gains prove pricing power even in inflationary environments. New CEO Henrique Braun's digital-first strategy—creating a Chief Digital Officer role and investing in AI personalization—positions the company to deepen consumer relationships in ways the legacy franchise model never could. The 3.1% dividend yield with low-single-digit growth provides ballast in volatile markets.
The bear case centers on the $18B IRS tax dispute, which represents significant financial liability the company has partially offset with a $6B Treasury deposit but has not resolved[1]. New excise taxes in Mexico (2026), tariff headwinds, and continued weakness in Converse and BODYARMOR brand (written down in Q4 2025) represent near-term earnings pressure. Organic revenue growth of only 5% and reported growth of 2% suggest the 'total beverage' transformation is not yet translating into accelerated volume gains. The stock's 22x forward P/E leaves limited margin of safety for execution missteps.
Key Catalysts
  1. Favorable resolution of the $18B IRS tax dispute releasing the $6B Treasury deposit and removing overhang from the stock
  2. New Chief Digital Officer driving measurable improvement in digital direct-to-consumer revenues and loyalty program engagement
  3. Coke Zero Sugar sustaining 10%+ volume growth validating the zero-sugar platform as a structural growth driver
Key Risks
  1. Adverse IRS ruling requiring up to $18B in tax payments that could force leverage increase and dividend growth interruption for the first time in 62 years
  2. Mexican sugar tax headwinds and health/wellness trend acceleration reducing Coca-Cola's core carbonated beverage volumes in key emerging markets
  3. New CEO transition execution risk as Henrique Braun assumes leadership in March 2026 while simultaneously managing digital transformation and portfolio restructuring
