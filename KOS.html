Company Profile
Energy ·Oil & Gas E&P ·NYSE ·United States
Kosmos Energy Ltd. is a deepwater exploration and production company focused on offshore oil and natural gas in West Africa and the Gulf of America. The company operates production assets in Ghana (Jubilee and TEN fields), Equatorial Guinea, and the Greater Tortue Ahmeyim (GTA) LNG project in Mauritania/Senegal, with exploration acreage across multiple basins.

Investment Thesis
Kosmos Energy achieved record Q1 2026 production of 75,000 boepd — up 25% YoY — driven by GTA LNG ramp-up and new Jubilee wells in Ghana, with operating expenses falling 47% to under $20/BOE demonstrating operational leverage as production scales [1]. Management guides Jubilee toward the upper end of 70-80K bpd gross for 2026, with three new producer wells expected online in June-July adding ~20K bpd gross. The stock trades at just $3.10 per share ($1.8B market cap) despite a production base that, at current oil prices, should generate meaningful free cash flow as GTA and Jubilee mature. Analysts project a return to profitability in 2026 with 32% revenue growth. The Inspire Insight score of 54 (ALIGNED) with zero negative attributions and the Kosmos Innovation Center's community programs in West Africa reflect responsible E&P operations.
The bear case is dominated by extreme financial leverage, commodity price sensitivity, and geopolitical risk. D/E of 580% ($3.0B debt against $130M cash) represents existential balance sheet risk — a sustained oil price decline below $60/bbl could impair the company's ability to service debt and fund development capex [4]. Q1 2026 adjusted net loss of $36M despite record production demonstrates that operational improvement has not yet translated to profitability due to derivatives losses and interest expense [1]. Profit margin of -59.5% and ROE of -101% reflect a company that has been value-destructive to shareholders. Geopolitical risk is concentrated — Ghana, Equatorial Guinea, Mauritania, and Senegal are emerging-market jurisdictions where regulatory, political, and operational risks are elevated. The Western Sahara controversy — though Kosmos withdrew from the territory in 2018 — demonstrated reputational and ethical risks of frontier E&P operations [3]. The energy transition poses a secular demand risk to deepwater oil assets with 20-30 year production horizons.
