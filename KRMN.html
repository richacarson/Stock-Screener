Company Profile
Industrials ·Aerospace & Defense ·NYSE ·United States
Karman Holdings Inc. designs, tests, manufactures, and sells mission-critical systems for space and defense applications, including payload protection and deployment systems, aerodynamic interstage systems, and propulsion systems. The company serves hypersonics, strategic missile defense, tactical missile, integrated defense, space, and launch markets. Incorporated in 2020 and headquartered in Huntington Beach, California, Karman operates as a subsidiary of TCFIII Spaceco SPV LP.

Investment Thesis
Karman Holdings is a pure-play defense subcontractor riding a generational increase in missile, hypersonics, and space launch demand. FY2025 revenue grew 37% to $471.5M with adjusted EBITDA of $145.3M (30.8% margin), and FY2026 guidance of $715-730M revenue (52-55% growth) with $207-218M EBITDA demonstrates accelerating momentum [1]. The $1B+ funded backlog provides multi-year revenue visibility, and the January 2026 Seemann Composites acquisition expands into maritime defense composites. Under new CEO Jon Rambeau (30-year defense veteran from L3Harris), Karman is positioned as a critical supplier of payload protection, propulsion, and interstage systems for programs that cannot be delayed or cancelled given the geopolitical environment [2]. The U.S. defense budget trajectory and missile stockpile replenishment needs provide secular tailwinds.
The bear case centers on extreme valuation, leverage, and execution risk. At 676x trailing P/E and 89.9x forward P/E on just $0.13 trailing EPS, Karman is priced for perfection — any execution stumble would trigger severe multiple compression [4]. The stock has already declined 15.4% from its $118.38 high despite raising guidance, suggesting the market is skeptical of the valuation [1]. D/E of 153.5% with $587M debt and negative free cash flow of -$41M creates balance sheet fragility if defense spending stalls [4]. Profit margin of just 3.7% on a GAAP basis raises questions about actual earnings quality versus adjusted EBITDA storytelling. As a PE-backed company (TCFIII Spaceco SPV LP), overhang from potential secondary offerings could pressure the stock. The acquisition integration of Seemann Composites adds execution risk to an already complex operational ramp.
