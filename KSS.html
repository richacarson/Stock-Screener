Company Profile
Consumer Cyclical ·Department Stores ·NYSE ·United States
Kohl's Corporation operates as an omnichannel retailer in the United States, offering apparel, footwear, accessories, beauty, and home products through approximately 1,150 stores and kohls.com. The company partners with Sephora (in-store shops in ~900 locations) and Amazon (returns program) to drive traffic. FY2025 net sales were approximately $15.5 billion, with the company navigating a multi-year turnaround amid leadership instability.

Investment Thesis
Kohl's offers deep value at 5.96x trailing P/E and 10.47x forward P/E with a 3.52% dividend yield, trading near $14 per share with $15.5B in annual revenue [1][4]. Q1 FY2026 showed the best comparable sales performance in over four years (-1.1% vs prior -3.1%), suggesting the turnaround is gaining traction. Digital sales grew 4.0% to 26% of net sales, and the Sephora partnership (in ~900 stores) continues to drive incremental traffic. The 29% payout ratio provides dividend coverage with room for growth, and the company has $674M in cash [1].
The bear case is compelling. Kohl's faces secular decline in department store retail — FY2025 comparable sales fell 3.1%, FY2026 guidance projects flat to -2% sales, and EPS guidance of $1.00-$1.60 reflects continued margin pressure [1]. The CEO-for-cause termination in May 2025 and ongoing interim leadership create strategic uncertainty — no permanent CEO has been named over a year later [2]. D/E of 164% with $6.63B debt and only 1.75% profit margin leaves minimal buffer against further sales declines. The stock has declined from a 52-week high above $20 as investors question the turnaround's viability. Employee count fell 3.45% to 84,000, suggesting ongoing cost-cutting that may further degrade customer experience. Off-price retailers (TJX, Ross) and e-commerce continue taking share from the mid-tier department store format.
Key Catalysts
  1. Naming a permanent CEO to replace interim leadership could restore strategic clarity and investor confidence, potentially triggering a re-rating from severely depressed valuation multiples
  2. Sephora partnership expansion to ~900 locations driving beauty category growth and incremental store traffic as Kohl's differentiates from pure-play apparel peers
  3. Q1 FY2026 comparable sales of -1.1% represents best performance in 4+ years, suggesting turnaround initiatives may be gaining traction with digital sales growing 4% to 26% of net sales
Key Risks
  1. Secular decline in mid-tier department store retail accelerating as off-price (TJX at $54B revenue), fast fashion, and AI-enabled e-commerce continue taking market share from the traditional format
  2. Leadership vacuum with no permanent CEO named 14+ months after the for-cause termination of Ashley Buchanan creates strategic drift and governance uncertainty that may deter institutional investors
  3. D/E of 164% with $6.63B debt against 1.75% profit margin creates balance sheet fragility — any further comparable sales deterioration could trigger dividend cuts, credit downgrades, or liquidity concerns
