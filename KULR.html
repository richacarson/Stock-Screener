Investment Thesis
KULR Technology offers speculative upside through its battery safety and thermal management platform, which grew revenue 51% YoY to $16.1M in 2025 with NASA-heritage technology serving defense, drone, and emerging AI data center markets [1]. The KULR ONE platform targeting 10,000 drone packs per month in H2 2026, combined with the $100M AI Server Rack Battery Backup Unit development opportunity and partnerships with Hylio and Caban, provides a visible growth pipeline [1][3]. Battery platform revenue of $7.3M provides a commercial baseline, and the minimal D/E of 1.2% means the balance sheet is not over-leveraged on traditional debt. Average revenue per product customer grew 56% to $108,000, suggesting deepening customer relationships [1].
The bear case is substantial. KULR massively missed its own 2024 revenue guidance ($10.7M actual vs. $26-34M projected), destroying management credibility on forward projections [4]. The $62M GAAP net loss in 2025 on $16.1M revenue means the company is burning cash at an unsustainable rate. The Bitcoin treasury strategy — $60M in cryptocurrency representing 90% of cash reserves — adds enormous balance sheet volatility unrelated to the core business and raises governance concerns [4]. A Grizzly Research short report documented revenue inflation through licensing agreement pull-forwards and misrepresentation of DoD contract relationships [4]. A class action lawsuit alleging insider trading is pending. The stock has collapsed from its 2024 highs, declining approximately 75% in 2025. At $119M market cap with negative earnings, the company faces dilution risk if it needs to raise capital, and the 47-person team may lack the organizational capacity to execute on multiple simultaneous growth initiatives [5].
