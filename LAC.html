Company Profile
Basic Materials ·Other Industrial Metals & Mining ·NYSE ·Canada
Lithium Americas Corp. is a pre-revenue mining development company focused on building the Thacker Pass lithium mine in Humboldt County, Nevada — the largest known lithium deposit in the United States. The company is developing Phase 1 of Thacker Pass with an estimated capital cost of $2.93 billion, supported by a $2.23 billion DOE loan facility, targeting first lithium production in late 2027 to 2028.

Investment Thesis
Lithium Americas controls the largest known lithium deposit in the United States at Thacker Pass, positioning it as a cornerstone of domestic critical mineral supply as the U.S. pursues energy independence [5]. The $2.23B DOE loan facility secured in October 2025 de-risks the capital structure and validates the project's national strategic importance [2]. With Phase 1 targeting mechanical completion in late 2027 and first lithium production in 2028, LAC offers asymmetric upside if lithium prices recover and domestic sourcing premiums materialize. The $1.55B market cap against a $2.93B Phase 1 project with 40+ year mine life implies significant optionality if execution proceeds on schedule [1][4].
The bear case is substantial: LAC is a zero-revenue company burning through cash at $60.6M annually in operating costs while facing $1.3B-$1.6B in 2026 capex alone, creating acute execution and financing risk [1][4]. Lithium prices have collapsed from 2022 peaks, and the forward P/E of -24x reflects deep market skepticism about profitability timelines. The indigenous rights controversy at Thacker Pass — flagged by Amnesty International, Human Rights Watch, and the ACLU — poses ongoing legal, regulatory, and reputational risk that could delay or halt construction [3]. Single-asset concentration means any technical, permitting, or market setback has existential consequences for the company, and the 94-person team must manage a mega-project that would strain organizations ten times its size.
Key Catalysts
  1. Phase 1 processing plant mechanical completion targeted for late 2027, with first lithium production in 2028 representing the transformative inflection from developer to producer [1]
  2. Rising AI data center buildout and EV adoption driving incremental lithium demand, potentially supporting price recovery from current depressed levels [4]
  3. Continued DOE loan drawdowns and potential federal support for domestic critical mineral production under energy independence policies [2]
Key Risks
  1. Single-asset concentration in Thacker Pass means any permitting reversal, construction delay, or technical failure has existential consequences for the entire enterprise [5]
  2. Indigenous rights opposition backed by Amnesty International, Human Rights Watch, and the ACLU creates ongoing legal and regulatory risk that could delay construction or impose costly remediation requirements [3]
  3. Depressed lithium prices and $1.3B-$1.6B in 2026 capex against zero revenue create severe cash burn, and any delay in DOE loan drawdowns or cost overruns could force dilutive equity raises at the current $4.42 share price [1][4]

AI Resilience (25%)
AI Resilience LOW RISK
9
Physical lithium mining and chemical processing are completely immune to AI disruption — AI cannot extract lithium from clay deposits, build processing plants, or replace the physical infrastructure required for mine construction [5]. LAC's core business involves geological extraction and chemical engineering that have no digital substitute. Moreover, the AI boom is a direct demand tailwind: AI data centers require massive energy storage capacity, driving incremental lithium battery demand that strengthens LAC's long-term market [4]. No AI revenue prevents the full 10/10, but the combination of zero AI risk and indirect AI-driven demand uplift makes this among the most AI-resilient business models available.
