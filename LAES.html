Company Profile
Technology ·Semiconductors ·NASDAQ ·Switzerland
SEALSQ Corp designs, manufactures, and sells tamper-resistant secure microcontrollers, post-quantum cryptography (PQC) chips, and digital identity infrastructure for IoT devices, critical infrastructure, and government/defense applications. The company's QS7001 is the first hardware-embedded post-quantum secure chip, integrating NIST-standardized ML-KEM and ML-DSA algorithms directly in silicon. Spun off from WISeKey International Holding AG in December 2023.
185 Employees ·www.sealsq.com

Investment Thesis
SEALSQ is a first-mover in the emerging post-quantum cryptography semiconductor market, with the QS7001 being the industry's first hardware-embedded PQC chip integrating NIST-standardized algorithms directly in silicon [2]. Q1 2026 revenue tripled YoY to $4.1M, and management reaffirmed FY2026 guidance of 50-100% revenue growth ($27-37M) backed by an active pipeline exceeding $200M for 2026-2029, with $60M tied directly to QS7001 and QVault TPM programs [1]. With $525M+ cash, minimal debt (D/E 2%), and regulatory tailwinds from France's ANSSI PQC mandate and US CNSA 2.0 requirements, SEALSQ has the financial runway and market positioning to capture the hardware PQC buildout. The appointment of a dedicated Group AI Officer and development of AI-integrated security chipsets position the company at the convergence of AI, quantum, and cybersecurity.
The bear case is dominated by dilution and execution risk at extreme valuations. Shares outstanding grew 304% in one year through aggressive equity raises including a $125M registered direct offering, massively diluting existing shareholders [3]. At $642M market cap on $18.3M TTM revenue (35x P/S), the stock is priced for significant future growth that remains unproven. Net losses of $34.2M in FY2025 with -187% profit margin show the company is far from breakeven. Revenue has been erratic — swinging from $30M (FY2023) to $11M (FY2024) to $18.3M (FY2025) — making projections unreliable [1]. Insider activity shows 33 sales and 0 purchases in the past 6 months, signaling limited executive conviction [4]. Competition from semiconductor giants NXP, Infineon, and STMicroelectronics with established manufacturing scale poses a serious threat once NIST PQC standards mature and larger players integrate PQC into existing product lines.

AI Risk Protection (7/9): SEALSQ manufactures physical semiconductor chips — tamper-resistant secure microcontrollers and PQC processors — that AI software structurally cannot displace [2]. AI systems themselves need hardware security; as AI deployments scale (Physical AI, robotics, autonomous systems), demand for hardware roots of trust increases. The post-quantum security thesis is actually strengthened by AI: more AI-generated data and AI-connected devices means more attack surface requiring hardware-level security. The only exposure is that AI could accelerate competitor chip design timelines from NXP, Infineon, and STMicroelectronics. AI Opportunity (1/1): SEALSQ appointed a dedicated Group AI Officer in November 2025, is developing AI-powered chipsets integrated with post-quantum semiconductors at the SEALQuantum lab, and positions PQC chips as foundational infrastructure for Physical AI systems and robotics [2].
