Company Profile
Basic Materials ·Other Industrial Metals & Mining ·NYSE ·Switzerland
Lithium Argentina AG is a lithium resource company focused on advancing lithium projects in Argentina. The company owns interests in the Cauchari-Olaroz project in Jujuy province (40,000 tpa capacity, JV with Ganfeng Lithium) and the Pastos Grandes project in Salta Province. The company produced over 34,000 tonnes of lithium carbonate in 2025 and is headquartered in Zug, Switzerland.

Investment Thesis
Lithium Argentina is a compelling early-stage lithium producer transitioning from pre-revenue exploration to meaningful profitability. The Cauchari-Olaroz project produced 34,100 tonnes of lithium carbonate in 2025, reaching the high end of guidance, while cash costs plummeted from $8,000+/tonne to $5,618/tonne by Q4 — a 30% reduction demonstrating operational maturity [1]. Q4 2025 revenue of $92M and net income of $31M proved the business model, and 2026 projections are transformational: at $20,000/tonne lithium prices (with Q1 2026 realized prices already at ~$17,000/tonne), EBITDA could reach $460M against a $1.25B market cap — a sub-3x EV/EBITDA if sustained [1]. The low D/E ratio of 29% and 2026 production guidance of 35,000-40,000 tonnes provide financial and operational cushion.
The bear case centers on lithium price volatility and execution risk. Lithium carbonate prices collapsed from $80,000/tonne in 2022 to below $10,000/tonne in late 2024 before recovering to ~$17,000/tonne — a 78% decline that drove LAR's full-year 2025 loss of $39M despite strong production [1]. The company's 40% JV interest in Cauchari-Olaroz (with Ganfeng holding 51%) limits economic upside and strategic control. ESG risks are significant: lithium mining in Argentina's Lithium Triangle faces documented concerns about water consumption in arid regions, Indigenous community consultation failures (lack of Free Prior Informed Consent), and environmental degradation of sensitive salt flat ecosystems [3]. Management turnover is elevated with average tenure of only 1.7 years, and the Pastos Grandes second project remains early-stage with no production timeline [2].
Key Catalysts
  1. Lithium price recovery — Q1 2026 realized prices of ~$17,000/tonne versus $7,500/tonne average in Q3 2025 could drive 2026 EBITDA to $460M at current production levels [1]
  2. Production ramp to 35,000-40,000 tonnes in 2026, a 3-18% increase over 2025 output, with potential for further cost reductions below $5,600/tonne [1]
  3. Pastos Grandes project advancement following the Q3 2025 Scoping Study completion, providing a second production asset for long-term growth [1]
Key Risks
  1. Lithium price volatility — prices have swung from $80,000 to $10,000/tonne in recent years; a return to 2024 lows would eliminate profitability despite operational improvements [1]
  2. ESG and community risk — Argentine lithium mining faces water consumption concerns, Indigenous consultation failures, and regulatory enforcement gaps in the Lithium Triangle [3]
  3. JV dependency — Ganfeng Lithium holds 51% of Cauchari-Olaroz, limiting LAR's economic capture and strategic control of its primary producing asset [1]
