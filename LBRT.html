Company Profile
Energy ·Oil & Gas Equipment & Services ·NYSE ·United States
Liberty Energy is an integrated energy services and technology company providing hydraulic fracturing services, wireline, proppant delivery, field gas processing, and compressed natural gas delivery to onshore oil and gas producers in North America. The company operates ~40 active frac fleets and two sand mines in the Permian Basin. Through Liberty Power Innovations, it is expanding into utility-scale power generation for data centers and industrial customers.
5,800 Employees ·www.libertyenergy.com

Investment Thesis
Liberty Energy is a leading North American completions provider navigating a cyclical trough while building a potentially transformative power generation business. Full year 2025 revenue of $4.01B with $634M adjusted EBITDA and D/E of just 29.7% demonstrate a clean balance sheet, though profit margins have compressed to 3.7% with EPS declining 75% YoY to $0.89 [1]. The Liberty Power Innovations pivot — anchored by a 1 GW deal with Vantage Data Centers and a 3 GW target by 2029 — positions Liberty to capture AI-driven electricity demand growth as data centers expand [1]. At $27.92 per share, the stock trades at 7.1x 2025 EBITDA, reasonable if the power business delivers on its 2027 revenue inflection.
The bear case is severe near-term earnings deterioration. Management guided for flat 2026 revenue with lower EBITDA, driven by completions pricing headwinds and $15-20M in LPI scaling costs [1]. Consensus expects near-zero or negative EPS in 2026 (forward P/E is meaningless at -145x), meaning Liberty must survive a profit drought before power revenues materialize in 2027. The core completions business faces structural pressure from flat North American drilling activity and E&P capital discipline. Chris Wright's departure to the U.S. Energy Department removes the visionary founder, though succession was well-planned [2]. Oil price volatility and potential trade war impacts on energy demand create macro risk that could extend the downturn beyond 2026 [1].
Key Catalysts
  1. Liberty Power Innovations delivering 400 MW firm reservation to Vantage Data Centers in 2027, validating the power business model and creating recurring revenue [1]
  2. Completions pricing stabilization in H2 2026 as North American gas-directed drilling activity recovers on higher natural gas prices [1]
  3. Expansion of power portfolio toward 3 GW by 2029, with potential additional data center and industrial customer contracts announced [1]
Key Risks
  1. Near-term earnings collapse — consensus expects negative EPS in 2026 as completions pricing headwinds fully materialize and LPI development costs ramp [1]
  2. Oil price decline below $60/bbl could force E&P customers to cut completion activity further, reducing fleet utilization and revenue below management's flat guidance [1]
  3. Power business execution risk — LPI is pre-revenue and the 2027 EBITDA inflection depends on successful deployment of utility-scale generation assets, a capability Liberty has not previously demonstrated at scale [1]
