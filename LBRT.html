Company Profile
Energy ·Oil & Gas Equipment & Services ·NYSE ·United States
Liberty Energy Inc. is North America's second-largest hydraulic fracturing company, providing integrated oilfield services including hydraulic fracturing, wireline, and related completions services. The company operates a large fleet of conventional and next-generation electric (digiFrac) fracturing equipment. Founded in 2011 by Chris Wright (now U.S. Secretary of Energy), headquartered in Denver, Colorado.
5,800 Employees ·www.libertyenergy.com

Investment Thesis
Liberty Energy is North America's second-largest hydraulic fracturing company generating $4.0B in FY2025 revenue with $634M adjusted EBITDA, returning $77M to shareholders through dividends (2.59% yield) and buybacks [1]. The company's digiFrac electric fleet technology positions it at the forefront of lower-emission completions services, and the developing power business opens adjacent revenue opportunities. The conservative 23.88% payout ratio and 18.63% 5-year dividend growth CAGR provide income investors with a growing yield on a well-managed, low-leverage balance sheet (D/E 80%) [3]. At $26.27, the stock trades near its 52-week low, potentially offering a contrarian entry point if U.S. drilling activity stabilizes.
Liberty faces severe cyclical headwinds — FY2025 revenue declined 7.2% to $4.0B, net income fell 53% to $148M, and Q1 2026 adjusted EBITDA of $126M was down 25% from Q1 2025 [1]. Analysts forecast EPS of -$0.24 for FY2026 (range: -$0.62 to +$0.38), implying the company may post a full-year loss as U.S. drilling activity contracts [4]. Founder and visionary CEO Chris Wright departed in February 2025 to serve as U.S. Secretary of Energy, leaving successor Ron Gusek untested through a full down cycle [2]. The oilfield services industry faces structural challenges from upstream capital discipline, potential shifts in U.S. energy policy, and long-term demand uncertainty as the energy transition progresses. The stock's 100.5x forward P/E (on minimal expected earnings) offers limited margin of safety.
Key Catalysts
  1. Stabilization in U.S. drilling activity and frac fleet utilization rates in H2 2026, driven by sustained oil prices above $70/barrel supporting E&P capital spending recovery
  2. DigiFrac electric fleet deployment expansion, capturing market share from conventional fleets through lower emissions, reduced fuel costs, and increasingly stringent environmental regulations
  3. Development of the power business as an adjacent revenue stream, leveraging Liberty's energy infrastructure expertise and fleet of natural gas-powered generation equipment
Key Risks
  1. Analysts forecast FY2026 EPS of -$0.24, implying a potential full-year loss as U.S. drilling activity contracts and frac pricing remains under pressure from excess fleet capacity
  2. Departure of founder/CEO Chris Wright to serve as U.S. Secretary of Energy creates leadership uncertainty — successor Ron Gusek is untested through a full commodity downturn
  3. Structural decline in U.S. onshore drilling activity from upstream capital discipline and potential energy transition headwinds could permanently impair the premium completions services business model
