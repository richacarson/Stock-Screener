Company Profile
Financial Services ·Banks - Regional ·NYSE ·United States
LendingClub Corporation operates as a digital marketplace bank in the United States. The company provides personal loans, auto refinancing, patient financing, and small business loans originated through its platform. LendingClub transformed from a peer-to-peer lending platform into a full-service digital bank following its 2021 acquisition of Radius Bank.

Investment Thesis
LendingClub is a high-growth digital bank trading at a remarkably cheap 7.1x forward P/E with a Forward PEG of 0.15, making it one of the most attractively valued growth stories in financial services [3]. The transformation from marketplace lender to digital bank is bearing fruit — FY2025 originations grew 33% to $10.1B, revenue grew 27% to $998.8M, and EPS surged 158% to $1.16 [1]. AI-driven underwriting reduced charge-offs by 44% while supporting volume growth, validating the technology platform [2]. Management guides to $1.65-$1.80 EPS for 2026 (42-55% growth) with $11.6-$12.6B in originations [1]. Insider buying is exceptionally strong with 1.05M net shares purchased in 6 months (38.9% of float traded) [3]. The bank charter provides deposit funding advantages and regulatory credibility that pure fintech competitors lack.
The bear case centers on credit risk, competition, and cultural challenges. Consumer credit deterioration in a slowing economy could spike charge-offs and provision expenses, wiping out the margin expansion that drove 2025's earnings recovery [1]. The 3-year revenue CAGR of -5.6% reflects the severe contraction from 2022's $1.19B peak, reminding investors this business is highly cyclical. AI dramatically lowers barriers to entry in digital lending — Apple, Google, and AI-native fintechs can deploy competitive credit models, compressing LendingClub's marketplace margins [2]. The 3.1/5 Glassdoor rating with employee complaints about excessive politics, unclear strategy, and leadership quality suggests organizational dysfunction that could hamper execution [2]. At $1.9B market cap and 1,600 employees, LendingClub is small and vulnerable to regulatory changes or a single bad credit vintage. The company pays no dividend, providing zero downside cushion.

AI Resilience (25%)
AI Resilience MODERATE
5
AI Risk Protection (4/9): LendingClub operates in the consumer lending space where AI is both an enabler and a direct competitive threat. The company uses AI for underwriting, which is a strength — AI-driven credit models reduced charge-offs by 44% [2]. However, AI dramatically lowers barriers to entry in digital lending. Big tech companies (Apple, Google), neobanks, and AI-native fintechs can deploy sophisticated credit models without LendingClub's platform. The core business of matching borrowers with capital through algorithmic underwriting is precisely what AI automates and commoditizes. Regulatory requirements for banks provide some moat, but the lending marketplace model faces compression from AI-enabled competitors who can offer similar services at lower cost [2]. AI Opportunity (1/1): LendingClub generates measurable value from AI — its machine learning underwriting platform is core to the business, with AI-driven credit scoring directly improving loan performance (44% charge-off reduction) and enabling the 40% origination growth [2].
