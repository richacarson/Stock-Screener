Company Profile

Leidos is a Fortune 500 technology, engineering, and science company serving defense, intelligence, homeland security, civil, and health markets. Operating through four segments — National Security & Digital, Health & Civil, Commercial & International, and Defense Systems — the company provides AI/ML solutions, cybersecurity, digital modernization, air traffic control systems, security screening, and space-based sensors to U.S. and international government customers.

Investment Thesis
Leidos is a well-positioned defense and technology services company trading at an attractive 11.3x forward P/E with a $49B backlog providing exceptional revenue visibility [1]. FY2025 operating income surged 15% to $2.11B with net income of $1.45B, and 2026 guidance of $17.5-17.9B revenue with EPS of $12.05-12.45 implies continued earnings growth [1]. The company is strategically pivoting toward AI-enabled solutions — hiring an AI-focused CTO, appointing a Chief AI Officer, and deploying AI across defense intelligence, cybersecurity, and digital modernization programs [2]. The $2.4B Entrust acquisition extends Leidos into commercial identity and security software, diversifying revenue beyond government. With ROE of 31%, free cash flow of $1.1B, and a conservative 14.6% dividend payout ratio, the company generates strong returns while investing for growth. Defense spending tailwinds from geopolitical tensions provide a supportive macro backdrop [1].
The bear case focuses on revenue growth deceleration and acquisition risk. Revenue grew only 3.1% in FY2025 and guidance implies 2-4% growth for 2026 — modest for a technology company [1]. The $2.4B Entrust acquisition requires $1.4B in bridge financing that will temporarily increase D/E from 107.7% to an estimated 150%+, creating integration and leverage risk [1]. Government budget sequestration or spending cuts under the current administration's DOGE efficiency mandate could directly impact Leidos's contract pipeline. The stock has declined from $200+ to $149, reflecting market concerns about defense spending sustainability. Profit margin of 8.4% is structurally limited by the cost-plus and fixed-price government contracting model. Revenue growth of -3.6% in the most recent quarter (possibly timing-related) creates near-term uncertainty. The Commercial & International segment remains a small portion of revenue, and the Entrust integration must execute to validate the diversification thesis [4].
Key Catalysts
  1. $49B backlog with strong book-to-bill ratio providing multi-year revenue visibility, supported by bipartisan defense spending and national security priorities [1]
  2. AI-enabled solutions becoming a core growth driver — CTO Ted Tanner and Chief AI Officer Ron Keesing are embedding AI across the enterprise, aligning with government demand for AI modernization [2]
  3. $2.4B Entrust acquisition expanding into commercial mission-critical identity and security software, diversifying revenue beyond government dependence [1]
Key Risks
  1. Government spending cuts or sequestration under DOGE efficiency mandates could directly impact the defense and health IT contract pipeline that generates the majority of revenue [1]
  2. Entrust acquisition integration risk — $1.4B bridge loan increases leverage, and commercial software is a new market requiring different go-to-market capabilities than government services [1]
  3. Revenue growth deceleration — 3.1% FY2025 growth and 2-4% 2026 guidance are modest for a $149 stock, creating multiple compression risk if the AI and commercial growth thesis fails to materialize [1]
