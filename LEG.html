Company Profile
Consumer Cyclical ·Furnishings, Fixtures & Appliances ·NYSE ·United States
Leggett & Platt designs, manufactures, and sells engineered components for bedding (innersprings, specialty foam, adjustable beds), furniture (mechanisms, seat suspensions), automotive (lumbar support, seat systems), and flooring (carpet cushion, underlayment). Founded in 1883 in Carthage, Missouri, the company has been restructuring through asset divestitures including the 2025 sale of its Aerospace Products Group, and generated $4.06B in 2025 revenue.

Investment Thesis
Leggett & Platt is a deep value situation trading at 6.7x trailing earnings and 9.6x forward P/E — a historically cheap multiple for a company with 143 years of operating history and strong ROE of 27.5% [5]. The restructuring thesis has merit: the Aerospace divestiture completed in August 2025 simplifies the portfolio, the dividend cut from $1.82 to $0.20 preserves $220M+ annually for deleveraging (net leverage improved to 2.4x), and 2026 guidance for $1.00-$1.20 adjusted EPS demonstrates the core business generates meaningful earnings [1]. Non-GAAP Q4 2025 EPS of $1.05 significantly beat consensus. A residential housing recovery would be a powerful catalyst — approximately half of revenue is tied to housing and furnishings markets that are in a 'multi-year depression' with demand well below cycle averages. The low ESG controversy score (14, 9th percentile) and Inspire alignment score of 45 provide value-aligned appeal [3].
The bear case is that Leggett is a structurally declining business in secular headwinds. Revenue has fallen 21% from $5.15B in 2022 to $4.06B in 2025, and 2026 guidance of $3.8-$4.0B implies further 1-6% decline [1]. The bedding components market faces permanent disruption from DTC mattress brands, foam alternatives to innersprings, and consolidation among mattress manufacturers. Profit margin of 5.8% is thin and deteriorating. D/E of 162% is elevated, and the $1.66B debt load constrains strategic flexibility. The dividend — once a Dividend Aristocrat with 50+ consecutive years of increases — was slashed 89%, destroying the income investment thesis that supported the stock's valuation premium for decades. No insider buying during the stock's decline from $50+ to $11 suggests insiders lack conviction [4]. The company has no AI strategy and no visible innovation pipeline to drive a return to growth [2]. This may be a value trap rather than a turnaround.
Key Catalysts
  1. Residential housing recovery — approximately half of revenue tied to bedding and furniture markets in a multi-year depression; any normalization of housing starts drives meaningful volume recovery [1]
  2. Deleveraging progress — dividend cut frees $220M+ annually for debt reduction, with net leverage already improving to 2.4x; reaching investment-grade metrics could re-rate the stock [1]
  3. Restructuring earnings leverage — portfolio simplification and cost reductions could drive margin expansion; 2026 adjusted EPS guidance of $1.00-$1.20 may prove conservative if volumes stabilize [1]
Key Risks
  1. Secular decline in bedding components — DTC mattress brands, foam alternatives to innersprings, and mattress manufacturer consolidation permanently reduce demand for Leggett's core products [1]
  2. Elevated leverage with shrinking revenue — D/E of 162% and $1.66B total debt against declining revenue creates refinancing risk and limits strategic investment capacity [5]
  3. Value trap risk — 21% revenue decline over three years with 2026 guidance for further contraction; absence of any growth catalyst or innovation pipeline suggests the business may continue shrinking [1]

AI Risk Protection (6/9): Leggett & Platt's core business involves physical manufacturing of steel components, innersprings, foam products, and automotive seat systems — operations that require physical-world manufacturing processes AI cannot displace [1]. The bedding and furniture component markets are driven by housing cycles and consumer spending, not information goods that AI can substitute. The automotive segment's lumbar and seat systems could integrate AI-powered comfort personalization for OEM customers, creating incremental value.
