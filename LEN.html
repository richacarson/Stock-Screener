Company Profile
Consumer Cyclical ·Residential Construction ·NYSE ·United States
Lennar Corporation is the second-largest homebuilder in the United States, constructing and selling single-family and attached homes across multiple segments including East, Central, South Central, and West regions. The company also operates financial services (mortgage, title, insurance), multifamily rental properties, and the LenX technology/innovation arm. In February 2025, Lennar completed the transformative spin-off of Millrose Properties, contributing $5.5B in land assets to become an asset-light homebuilder.

Investment Thesis
Lennar is executing a once-in-a-generation business model transformation — the February 2025 Millrose Properties spin-off ($5.5B land + $1.0B cash) is creating the industry's first pure-play asset-light homebuilder, modeled on NVR's proven capital efficiency [1][3]. At 11.5x forward P/E with $8.9B cash (net cash of $3.4B), LEN trades at a deep cyclical discount despite a structural improvement in capital efficiency and return on equity that should emerge as the asset-light model scales. The 2.3% dividend yield with a conservative 28.8% payout ratio and 39-year payment streak provides income stability. The 27% 5-year dividend growth CAGR signals aggressive shareholder return commitment [4].
The bear case is near-term earnings deterioration and execution risk on the asset-light pivot. Q1 FY2026 revenue fell 13.3% YoY to $6.3B with EPS plunging 56% to $0.93, missing consensus estimates on both lines [1]. Profit margin has compressed to 5.4% from historical levels above 10%, and ROE at 8.1% is far below the 15% target. Management acknowledged affordability challenges will persist with elevated mortgage rates, and the Millrose relationship introduces a new dependency — Lennar is currently Millrose's only customer, creating concentration risk for both entities. The 5-year revenue CAGR of 0.5% reveals zero organic growth through the housing cycle. The stock's beta of 1.49 amplifies cyclical downside, and a recession would pressure both closings volumes and the Millrose option contract economics [4]. Glassdoor's declining 3.4/5 rating with culture scored 3.1 suggests internal organizational challenges during the transformation [2].
Key Catalysts
  1. Asset-light model scaling — as Millrose supplies finished lots via option contracts, Lennar's capital efficiency and ROE should improve structurally, driving multiple re-rating toward NVR-like valuations [1][3]
  2. Mortgage rate relief below 6% would directly improve affordability, drive order growth, and allow Lennar to reduce margin-compressing sales incentives [1]
  3. Millrose attracting additional builder customers beyond Lennar, validating the public land-banking REIT model and creating a platform value premium [3]
Key Risks
  1. Profit margin compression to 5.4% with Q1 EPS down 56% — elevated incentives and affordability constraints may persist if mortgage rates remain above 6.5% [1]
  2. Millrose execution risk — Lennar is currently the only customer, option contract economics are untested through a full cycle, and land valuation risk transfers but doesn't disappear [3]
  3. Declining employee engagement (Glassdoor down 7%, culture 3.1/5) during a complex organizational transformation could impact operational execution and talent retention [2]
