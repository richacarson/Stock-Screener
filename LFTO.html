Company Profile
Communication Services ·Advertising Agencies ·NASDAQ ·United States
Liftoff Mobile is a mobile advertising and monetization platform that uses its proprietary Cortex neural network prediction engine to optimize ad campaigns and improve return on investment. The company operates a demand-side platform (DSP) for user acquisition and a supply-side platform (SSP) for app monetization. Cortex makes over two billion predictions per second across the platform. Liftoff went public in June 2026 and is headquartered in Redwood City, California.

Investment Thesis
Liftoff Mobile is a high-growth mobile advertising platform riding the convergence of AI and programmatic ad tech. Q1 2026 delivered 37% core advertising revenue growth with dramatic profitability improvement — 24% net income margin and 58% adjusted EBITDA margin versus negative margins just two years prior [1]. The Cortex neural network engine making 2B+ predictions per second is a genuine AI product generating direct revenue, and the integrated DSP+SSP model (post-Vungle acquisition) creates a differentiated full-stack platform rare among independent ad tech players [2]. With $685M FY2025 revenue growing to a $741M TTM run rate, S&P expects ~23% revenue growth in 2026. Glassdoor ratings of 4.3-4.4/5 with 87% recommendation rate suggest a healthy organizational culture [2].
The bear case centers on leverage, valuation opacity, and platform risk. Liftoff carries $1.82B in debt against negative stockholders' equity of -$951M, a legacy of its PE-backed acquisition strategy [3]. Trailing P/E of 205x on $0.13 GAAP EPS obscures the improving economics — Q1 2026 showed 24% net margin — but forward P/E estimates are unavailable, making valuation difficult for a newly public company. The existential risk is platform disintermediation: Apple's ATT framework already disrupted mobile ad targeting, and Google, Meta, and Apple are building increasingly sophisticated in-house AI ad optimization that could compress margins for independent ad tech. AppLovin's emergence as a dominant force in mobile ad tech creates direct competitive pressure. The company has only 3 years of disclosed financial history, providing limited visibility into durability through economic cycles.
