Company Profile
Communication Services ·Telecom Services ·NASDAQ ·Bermuda
Liberty Latin America Ltd. provides fixed, mobile, and subsea telecommunications services across Latin America and the Caribbean, including Puerto Rico, Panama, Costa Rica, Jamaica, the Bahamas, and Chile. The company operates through C&W Caribbean, C&W Panama, Liberty Networks, Liberty Puerto Rico, and Liberty Costa Rica segments, offering video, broadband, fixed-line, mobile, and enterprise connectivity under the Flow, +movil, Liberty, and BTC brands.

Investment Thesis
Liberty Latin America offers deep value exposure to Caribbean and Latin American telecom infrastructure at a Forward P/E of just 6.67x. The company's adjusted OIBDA grew 9% in Q4 2025 with adjusted FCF of $150M (+29% YoY), signaling operational improvement beneath the headline losses [1]. The Liberty Networks subsea cable network connecting approximately 30 markets provides a physical infrastructure moat that is difficult to replicate, and the company's FTTH expansion and mobile network investments position it for eventual revenue stabilization. If management can delever the balance sheet and convert OIBDA improvement into positive GAAP earnings as forward estimates suggest ($0.72 EPS), the stock trading below $5 could offer significant upside from current compressed levels.
The bear case is dominated by extreme leverage — $8.9B in debt against a $967M market cap produces D/E of 877% and ROE of -34.9%, leaving virtually no margin for error [4]. Revenue has declined from $4.81B in 2022 to $4.44B in 2025, with the company guiding only 2.8% average annual growth over the next three years — well below the 4.8% telecom industry forecast [1]. Hurricane exposure in Caribbean markets adds unpredictable revenue risk, as demonstrated by Hurricane Melissa's $12M Q1 2026 impact. The Inspire Impact score of -62 (MISALIGNED) with a 'Sexually Explicit' negative attribution may conflict with values-based investment mandates. At the current debt load, even modest interest rate increases or revenue shortfalls could threaten the capital structure, making this a speculative turnaround story rather than a conviction holding.

AI Risk Protection (6/9): Telecom infrastructure is fundamentally physical — fiber optic networks, cell towers, submarine cables, and last-mile connections cannot be replicated by software AI [2]. LLA's subsea cable network connecting approximately 30 Caribbean and Latin American markets through Liberty Networks represents a physical moat that AI cannot displace. However, portions of the business face AI exposure: customer service operations are already being automated via AI call centers, and content aggregation/video services face substitution risk from AI-curated alternatives. The telecom industry broadly faces margin pressure as AI enables competitors to operate leaner. AI Opportunity (1/1): LLA is deploying AI internally for call center optimization, text-to-speech engines, and has established an AI governance framework with locally managed use cases across operations [2]. While not generating direct AI product revenue, these deployments represent meaningful operational AI adoption that generates quantifiable cost savings.
