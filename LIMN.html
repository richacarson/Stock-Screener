Company Profile
Healthcare ·Biotechnology ·NASDAQ ·United States
Liminatus Pharma, Inc. is a pre-clinical stage biopharmaceutical company developing IBA101, a next-generation CD47-blocking humanized monoclonal antibody for advanced solid cancers including non-small cell lung cancer. The company went public via SPAC merger with Iris Acquisition Corp in April 2025 and began trading on NASDAQ May 1, 2025. Founded in 2018 and based in Cerritos, California with 1 employee. The stock has declined 99.6% from its $33.66 peak to ~$0.12, and management has disclosed substantial doubt about the company's ability to continue as a going concern.

Investment Thesis
Liminatus holds a single pre-clinical oncology asset (IBA101, a next-generation CD47-blocking antibody) in the high-value immuno-oncology space, with a Phase 1 trial announced in March 2026 targeting lung cancer using a seamless monotherapy-to-combination design with PD-1/PD-L1 therapies [1]. The second-generation approach is engineered to avoid the hematological toxicities (anemia, thrombocytopenia) that hampered first-generation CD47 blockades from Gilead and others. At a $5.3M market cap, any clinical validation — even initial safety data — could represent significant upside from a de minimis base. The February 2026 equity offering provided $3.44M in net proceeds, and the $7.36M Clear Street settlement improved the balance sheet. If IBA101 demonstrates a differentiated safety profile in Phase 1, the asset could attract licensing interest from larger pharma partners.
The overwhelming weight of evidence points to extreme risk across every dimension. The company has 1 employee, zero revenue, a going-concern qualification, accumulated deficit of $40.0M, and cash of only $1.9M against $1.44M in related-party debt [1]. The stock has collapsed 99.6% from its $33.66 SPAC-merger peak to $0.12. Nasdaq has issued deficiency notices for MVLS, MVPHS, and Bid Price Rule violations, with delisting risk imminent if compliance is not regained by mid-2026 deadlines [1]. Shares outstanding nearly doubled in Q1 2026 alone through dilutive issuances (17.8M shares from Clear Street settlement and equity offering). The Phase 1 trial has not yet initiated patient dosing, and successful Phase 1 to Phase 2 progression is anticipated within 2-3 years — during which the company will require substantial additional financing that will further dilute existing shareholders. Major pharma competitors including Gilead have abandoned or scaled back similar CD47 programs after spending exponentially more, raising fundamental questions about the mechanism's viability. No analyst coverage exists for this stock [1][2].
