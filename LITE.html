Company Profile
Technology ·Communication Equipment ·NASDAQ ·United States
Lumentum Holdings designs and manufactures optical and photonic products for cloud and networking, commercial lasers, and 3D sensing markets. The company is a critical supplier of lasers and optical components for AI data center interconnects, cloud transceivers, and optical circuit switches, and holds the position of sole source laser supplier for Nvidia's co-packaged optics technology.

Investment Thesis
Lumentum is one of the purest AI infrastructure plays in public markets, delivering explosive 65% YoY revenue growth in Q2 FY2026 ($665.5M) with Q3 guidance of $780-830M representing 85%+ growth — both all-time records [1]. The company holds 50-60% market share in 200G EMLs, is Nvidia's sole source laser supplier for co-packaged optics, and secured a landmark $2B Nvidia investment with multibillion-dollar purchase commitments [2]. Operating margins expanded 1,700bps to 25.2% and are guided to 30-31% in Q3, demonstrating the operating leverage inherent in optical manufacturing at scale [1]. The OCS backlog exceeds $400M with the $10M quarterly milestone achieved three months early, and indium phosphide wafer fab capacity is expanding 40% to meet surging demand [1]. At 43.7x forward P/E, the stock is expensive in absolute terms but growing into its valuation rapidly as the path to $3B+ annual revenue at 30%+ margins becomes visible.
The bear case centers on cyclicality, concentration, and capital structure risks. Customer concentration is severe — Nvidia and two hyperscalers represent approximately 43% of revenue, and the sole-source Nvidia relationship, while currently advantageous, creates existential risk if Nvidia diversifies suppliers or shifts technology direction [2]. The $3.18B in convertible notes triggered conversion thresholds during the 416% stock rally, creating potential dilution overhang [3]. Morgan Stanley has warned that stretched valuations may temper 2026 gains, and at 188x trailing P/E, any deceleration in AI capex spending could trigger a violent multiple compression [2]. The optical industry is historically cyclical — the current AI supercycle could moderate if hyperscaler capex budgets tighten or if silicon photonics advances make discrete optical components less critical. Geopolitical risk from U.S.-China export controls could also impact operations, though Lumentum has shifted production to Thailand [2]. Execution risk on the $400M+ OCS backlog (mostly H2 FY2026) is non-trivial.
Key Catalysts
  1. Q3 FY2026 earnings delivery on $780-830M revenue guidance with 30-31% operating margins would confirm the AI optics supercycle thesis and support the elevated valuation [1]
  2. OCS and co-packaged optics revenue ramp — meaningful contributions from these next-gen products in H2 FY2026 would demonstrate Lumentum's ability to extend its technology leadership beyond current EML dominance [1]
  3. Nvidia partnership deepening — additional purchase commitments or expanded sole-source arrangements for next-gen Nvidia GPU platforms would extend the revenue visibility runway [2]
Key Risks
  1. Customer concentration — Nvidia and two hyperscalers representing ~43% of revenue creates existential risk if key relationships deteriorate or AI capex cycles slow [2]
  2. Convertible note dilution — $3.18B in convertible notes that triggered conversion thresholds could dilute shareholders significantly as the stock trades above conversion prices [3]
  3. AI capex cyclicality — the optical industry is historically boom-bust, and any moderation in hyperscaler AI infrastructure spending could trigger sharp revenue declines given Lumentum's concentration in AI optics [2]
