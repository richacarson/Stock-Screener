Existential Flexibility
10
Lilly's transformation from a diversified pharma company to a GLP-1-dominated cardiometabolic leader is one of the most dramatic strategic pivots in pharmaceutical history [1]. The company invested in incretin biology for over two decades before Mounjaro's 2022 launch generated explosive growth — from $28.5B revenue in 2022 to $65.2B in 2025. Divesting the animal health business (Elanco, 2018) to focus on human pharma, and now the oral obesity pill pivot (orforglipron) to expand the addressable market beyond injectables, show ongoing transformative flexibility [4].

Investment Thesis
Eli Lilly is the world's fastest-growing major pharmaceutical company, delivering $65.2B in 2025 revenue (up 45%) driven by the GLP-1 franchise — Mounjaro ($23.9B) and Zepbound ($12.6B) — which addresses the massive global obesity and diabetes epidemics [1]. The company guides $80-83B revenue for 2026 (25% growth) with 40%+ adjusted EPS growth, fueled by continued GLP-1 demand, Medicare Part D coverage expansion, and the expected launch of an oral GLP-1 obesity pill (orforglipron) in Q2 2026 [1][4]. At 22.7x forward earnings with 25%+ revenue growth, 83% gross margins, and 31.7% profit margins, Lilly is reasonably valued for a franchise that is still in early innings of penetrating the estimated 800M+ global obesity patient population. The Chief AI Officer appointment and $11B+ annual R&D investment ensure continued pipeline innovation.
The bear case focuses on concentration risk and pricing headwinds. GLP-1 drugs represent 56% of total revenue — any safety signal, competitive displacement, or manufacturing disruption would be catastrophic [1]. The Trump administration's pricing deals create 'low- to mid-teens' pricing headwinds, and Lilly opposes codifying 'most favored nation' drug pricing into law, creating ongoing regulatory uncertainty [4]. At $853B market cap, the stock requires sustained 20%+ revenue growth to justify the premium. Novo Nordisk's oral semaglutide pill could launch ahead of orforglipron, and at least 30 companies are developing GLP-1 competitors that could erode Lilly's first-mover advantage. D/E of 165% ($43.9B debt) limits financial flexibility if growth decelerates. The stock is down 13% in 2026, reflecting market concerns about pricing policy and competition [4].
Key Catalysts
  1. Oral GLP-1 obesity pill (orforglipron) FDA approval expected Q2 2026, opening the massive oral obesity market and expanding the addressable patient population beyond injectable-tolerant patients [1]
  2. 2026 revenue guidance of $80-83B with 40%+ adjusted EPS growth, driven by Medicare Part D coverage, international expansion, and continued Mounjaro/Zepbound demand [1]
  3. Deep pipeline readouts including next-generation obesity combination therapies, donanemab for Alzheimer's, and oncology programs that could diversify revenue beyond GLP-1 [4]
Key Risks
  1. GLP-1 concentration risk — Mounjaro and Zepbound represent 56% of total revenue; any safety signal, supply disruption, or competitive displacement would materially impact the business [1]
  2. Drug pricing regulatory risk — Trump administration pricing deals create low- to mid-teens pricing headwinds, and proposed 'most favored nation' legislation could further compress margins [4]
  3. Intensifying competition — 30+ GLP-1 competitors in development including Novo Nordisk's oral semaglutide, Amgen's MariTide, and Viking Therapeutics' VK2735, which could erode market share and pricing power
