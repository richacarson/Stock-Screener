Investment Thesis
Lockheed Martin offers exceptional defense exposure with a record $194 billion backlog providing 2.5x revenue coverage and compelling 2026 guidance: 5% revenue growth, 25%+ operating profit growth, and 36%+ EPS growth to $29.35-$30.25. The Missiles and Fire Control segment is projected to deliver double-digit revenue CAGR through the decade driven by unprecedented global demand for precision munitions. Free cash flow of $6.9 billion and a 22-year dividend growth streak at 7.5% CAGR provide reliable shareholder returns. At 19.6x forward earnings, the stock is reasonably valued below the defense sector average.
The negative Inspire score of -49 due to LGBT Activism creates headwinds for faith-based investors. The 339% debt-to-equity ratio, while typical for defense, limits financial flexibility. LMT's 2025 EPS declined 4% despite revenue growth, driven by one-time charges, and the company faces execution risk on the F-35 production ramp. Defense spending is politically sensitive and could face pressure from budget negotiations. The inherent ESG controversy of weapons manufacturing may limit the investor base, and the company's 'Significant' Level 3 controversy rating on some assessments reflects meaningful reputational risk.
