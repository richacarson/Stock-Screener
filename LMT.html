Investment Thesis
Lockheed Martin enters 2026 with the strongest demand profile in its history — a record $194B backlog, trailing twelve-month revenue of $75B, and stock up 30% YTD. The company expects 2026 revenue of ~$79B (5% growth) with 25% segment operating profit growth, suggesting significant margin expansion. AI innovation is tangible and revenue-generating: Project Overwatch flew the first tactical AI on an F-35, STAR.OS enables AI interoperability, and Astris AI serves government agencies. FCF guidance of $6.5-6.8B supports the 2.15% dividend yield with a 62% payout ratio and 22 consecutive years of increases. The forward P/E of 19.6 is reasonable for a defense prime with decade-plus backlog visibility and rising geopolitical demand.
The bear case is structural and political. Profit margin of 6.7% is structurally low and constrained by cost-plus government contracts, limiting earnings leverage on revenue growth. D/E of 338.8% (negative equity from buybacks) creates balance sheet opacity. F-35 production challenges have been persistent, and the program's complexity makes margin improvement difficult. Government budget uncertainty — potential defense spending debates, continuing resolutions, or shifting political priorities — could freeze new contract awards. The stock's 30% YTD gain may have priced in the favorable demand environment. International revenue exposes LMT to ITAR regulations and export control restrictions. The -49 Inspire score from LGBT activism limits appeal for faith-aligned investors.

Key Catalysts
  1. 25% segment operating profit growth in 2026 on 5% revenue growth, implying significant margin expansion as production efficiency improves and higher-margin sustainment revenue grows in the F-35 program
  2. International order acceleration as European NATO allies increase defense spending toward 3%+ GDP targets, driving new Aeronautics and Missile orders from $194B backlog pipeline
  3. AI defense platform adoption — Project Overwatch, STAR.OS, and Astris AI creating new revenue streams and competitive moats as the DoD accelerates autonomous systems and AI-enabled warfare programs
Key Risks
  1. F-35 production and sustainment cost challenges — the program's complexity has historically generated margin pressure, and any further delays or cost overruns in the 3,600+ aircraft order book could constrain the margin expansion needed to hit 2026 profit growth targets
  2. Government budget risk — defense spending debates, continuing resolutions, or political shifts could delay new contract awards even as the existing $194B backlog protects near-term revenue, creating a gap in mid-term growth visibility
  3. Geopolitical deescalation risk — any reduction in global security tensions could slow the defense spending supercycle that has driven LMT's 30% YTD stock appreciation, potentially compressing the premium multiple
