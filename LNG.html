Company Profile
Energy ·Oil & Gas Midstream ·NYSE ·United States
Cheniere Energy is the largest producer of LNG in the United States and the second-largest LNG operator globally. The company operates the Sabine Pass and Corpus Christi liquefaction facilities in Louisiana and Texas, exporting approximately 10% of the world's LNG with long-term contracts to Asian and European buyers.

Investment Thesis
Cheniere Energy is the dominant U.S. LNG exporter at a pivotal moment — the AI data center build-out is driving record natural gas demand, Europe needs non-Russian supply for decades, and Asia is switching from coal to gas [3]. FY 2025 delivered record revenues of ~$20.0B, net income of ~$5.3B, and Distributable Cash Flow of $5.3B; 2026 guidance of $6.75-$7.25B Consolidated Adjusted EBITDA with 51-53 million tons production reflects Corpus Christi Stage 3 coming online [1]. At 11x earnings, LNG trades at a value multiple for a company with long-duration contracted cash flows, 26.5% net margins, and completed '20/20 Vision' capital plan returning $6.1B to shareholders in 2025 [1]. The new buyback authorization and growing dividend (yield 0.84%) signal capital allocation discipline.
The bear case is commodity oversupply and geopolitical risk. U.S. LNG capacity is scaling rapidly — Venture Global's Plaquemines, Sempra's Port Arthur, and NextDecade's Rio Grande are coming online — which pressures spot cargo margins as management flagged in 2026 guidance [1]. European demand could weaken faster than expected if renewables scale rapidly. Methane leak scrutiny, while improving (2025 UNEP Gold Standard), remains a policy risk if EU methane regulations tighten [3]. The CEO/Chairman combined role under Fusco raises governance concerns [2]. Rising interest rates elevate the cost of the capital-intensive expansion pipeline. However, the 15-20+ year take-or-pay contract book with investment-grade counterparties provides substantial downside protection, and the structural demand from AI data center electrification creates a long-duration tailwind that the market may be underappreciating at 11x earnings [4].
Key Catalysts
  1. Corpus Christi Stage 3 completion — three remaining trains online in 2026, driving production to 51-53 million tons and supporting $6.75-$7.25B EBITDA guidance [1]

Key Risks
  1. U.S. LNG oversupply — Venture Global, Sempra, and NextDecade projects pressure spot margins as flagged in 2026 guidance [1]
  2. European demand weakness if renewables scale faster than expected or gas prices spike enough to accelerate substitution [1]
  3. Methane emissions regulation in EU and US could raise compliance costs despite Cheniere's OGMP 2.0 Gold Standard recognition [3]
