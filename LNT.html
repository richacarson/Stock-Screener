Company Profile
Utilities ·Utilities - Regulated Electric ·NASDAQ ·United States
Alliant Energy is a regulated electric and natural gas utility serving approximately 1 million electric and 425,000 natural gas customers in Iowa and Wisconsin through its IPL and WPL subsidiaries. The company is transitioning from coal to renewables and is aggressively contracting data center demand (3 GW signed) to drive load growth.

Investment Thesis
Alliant Energy is a well-run regulated utility with one of the clearest AI-era tailwinds: 3 GW of contracted data center demand representing 50% of peak load growth by 2030, funded by a $13.4B 4-year capex plan (up 17%) and a 6-8% EPS CAGR trajectory through 2029 [1]. The 2026 EPS guidance of $3.36-$3.46 continues a 10-year record of 6% compound EPS growth, supported by constructive Iowa/Wisconsin regulatory jurisdictions [1]. The dividend is one of the most durable in the sector — 45+ years of consecutive payments, 22+ years of growth, ~6% 5-year CAGR, and ~3.2% yield at a 57% payout ratio [3]. For income investors seeking an AI-levered utility, LNT offers regulated-return visibility on data center load growth.
Bear case: As a regulated utility, LNT's ROE (~11%) sits below IOWN's 15% target and growth ceiling is capped by regulatory commissions. D/E of ~155% reflects heavy rate-base financing — rising long-term rates compress equity returns and raise the cost of the $13.4B capex plan [1]. The Glassdoor culture signal is a real concern: 2.4/5 overall rating (34% below industry), 2.3 on culture/values, with employee feedback citing disconnected leadership — poor culture in a regulated-execution business creates operational risk (outages, rate-case friction, attrition) [2]. Data center contracts introduce concentration risk if hyperscaler AI capex cycles slow or if large customers renegotiate; commission-approved cost-recovery mechanisms mitigate but do not eliminate this. Forward P/E of ~18x and PEG of ~2.8x leave little valuation cushion relative to pure growth utilities.
Key Catalysts
  1. Data center load monetization — 3 GW contracted, representing 50% of peak load growth by 2030, flowing through regulated rate base [1]
  2. 2027-2029 EPS growth acceleration to 7-8% (vs. 6% historical) driven by $13.4B capex plan and data center revenue ramp [1]
  3. Iowa and Wisconsin rate case outcomes through 2026-2027 determining ROE and cost recovery on accelerated renewables and transmission investment [1]
Key Risks
  1. Employee culture weakness — Glassdoor 2.4/5 rating (34% below industry) with leadership disconnect feedback creates operational execution and attrition risk in a rate-regulated business [2]
  2. Interest rate and cost-of-capital pressure on a ~155% D/E balance sheet financing $13.4B of 4-year capex; higher long rates compress equity returns [1]
  3. Data center customer concentration and counterparty risk if hyperscaler AI capex slows or if contracted load fails to materialize on schedule [1]
