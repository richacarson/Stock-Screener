Company Profile
Basic Materials ·Other Precious Metals & Mining ·NYSE American ·United States
Comstock Inc. is a systemic decarbonization company operating through Fuels, Metals, Mining, Strategic Investments, and Corporate Services segments. The company develops technology to extract and convert lignocellulosic biomass into renewable fuels, operates solar panel recycling facilities, and holds mining rights in Nevada.
45 Employees ·www.comstock.inc
Excellence Evaluation — Think Like an Owner (50%)
Innovation DEVELOPING
5
Comstock has executed a meaningful business model transformation over the past 5 years, pivoting from legacy mining operations to a clean technology and recycling platform—solar panel recycling, Bioleum renewable fuels, and critical metals recovery represent genuinely new revenue streams [1]. The acquisition of GenMat for physics-based AI materials discovery demonstrates forward-looking R&D investment [2]. However, commercial traction remains nascent—total 2025 revenues of $1.4M are trivial, and the solar recycling facility is still scaling from 5,000 to 100,000 tons annually [1]. Market share leadership is absent; the company is a small participant in emerging recycling markets. R&D intensity is high relative to revenue but absolute spending is modest given the 45-person headcount.
Inspiration DEVELOPING
5
Comstock's mission centers on 'commercial decarbonization solutions,' positioning the company as an environmental steward—a genuine purpose beyond profit that aligns with secular sustainability trends [3]. CEO Corrado De Gasperis has provided 15+ years of stable leadership, bringing deep industrial manufacturing and governance experience [2]. Glassdoor reviews (3.9/5, 83% recommend) indicate a reasonably positive culture with strong compensation (4.1/5) and career opportunities (4.0/5) [2]. The R2v3/RIOS responsible recycling certification—the first for a solar panel recycler in North America—validates the company's commitment to its environmental mission [3].
Infrastructure AT RISK
1
Financial infrastructure is severely stressed. Revenue tripled to $1.4M in 2025 but remains negligible, with net losses exceeding $13M and EPS of -$1.17 trailing [1][4]. ROE is deeply negative at -50.6%, profit margin is 0%, and revenue declined 76.6% YoY on a quarterly basis [4]. The $34.5M equity raise provides near-term runway but dilutes existing shareholders [1]. Forward P/E of 5.2 suggests analyst expectations of profitability by Q2 2026, but this is speculative. D/E of 16.9% is low. Liquidity floor: $3.13 x 1,825,318 = $5.71M > $1M, clearing the threshold. The company targets operational profitability by Q2 2026 but has no track record of achieving it.

Investment Thesis
Comstock represents a speculative bet on the intersection of solar panel recycling, renewable fuels, and AI-driven materials discovery. The company's decarbonization pivot is strategically sound—solar panel waste is a massive and growing problem, and first-mover advantage in responsible recycling could create durable competitive positioning.
However, the investment case rests almost entirely on future execution. With only $1.4M in 2025 revenue, >$13M in annual losses, and aggressive scaling plans (5,000 to 100,000 tons of recycling capacity), the gap between vision and reality is wide. The $34.5M raise provides runway, but profitability targets for Q2 2026 are aspirational. Suitable only for highly risk-tolerant investors who believe in the decarbonization thesis and can tolerate multi-year holding periods.
Key Catalysts
  1. Solar panel recycling facility commissioning in Q1 2026, with scaling to 100,000 tons annually and operational profitability targeted by Q2 2026
  2. Master Service Agreements with major utility and recycling aggregators could accelerate revenue ramp in southwest U.S. markets
  3. GenMat physics-based AI platform for materials discovery could unlock high-value IP and licensing opportunities
Key Risks
  1. Revenue remains negligible at $1.4M with >$13M annual losses—failure to achieve profitability by Q2 2026 would further erode credibility and require additional dilutive capital raises
  2. Solar panel recycling economics are unproven at scale—processing costs, metal recovery yields, and end-market pricing all carry significant uncertainty
  3. 45-employee company attempting multi-segment growth across fuels, metals, mining, and AI is stretched thin operationally

AI Resilience (25%)
AI Resilience LOW RISK
7
AI is more enabler than threat for Comstock's physical recycling and materials processing operations—solar panel dismantling, metals recovery, and biomass conversion are inherently physical processes not susceptible to digital disruption [3]. The GenMat acquisition positions the company to use physics-based AI for accelerated materials discovery, and CEO De Gasperis has stated that 'anyone not integrating AI into their core competencies will likely be disrupted or replaced' [2]. This proactive AI posture is notable for a 45-person company. The primary AI opportunity is in optimizing recycling yields and discovering new material recovery pathways through GenMat's platform.
