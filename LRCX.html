Investment Thesis
Lam Research is one of the purest plays on AI semiconductor spending, with record TTM revenue of $20.6B (up 27% YoY), record gross margins of 49.9%, and Q2 FY2026 revenue hitting $5.34B — the 10th consecutive quarter of growth [1]. Q3 FY2026 guidance of $5.7B (±$300M) implies ~30% YoY growth acceleration, with management projecting WFE spending to reach $135B in 2026 driven by AI, advanced packaging (>40% growth), and NAND recovery [1]. The company's competitive position is nearly unassailable — 'nearly every advanced chip is built with Lam technology' — and the AI capex cycle is still in early innings, with major hyperscalers committing $150B+ to data center buildout in 2026-2027 [3]. At 30.2% profit margins, 65.6% ROE, and conservative 44.2% D/E, Lam combines elite profitability with a strong balance sheet [6].
The bear case centers on semiconductor equipment cyclicality — Lam's revenue declined 14% in FY2024 before recovering, demonstrating that even secular AI spending cannot fully insulate the business from fab investment cycles [6]. Forward P/E of 33.1x prices in sustained 20%+ growth, and the trailing P/E of 47x leaves limited room for execution misses or customer spending delays. China export restrictions remain a risk factor, with any tightening of semiconductor equipment export controls potentially impacting a significant revenue stream. CFO Bettinger's $20.5M in insider selling in March 2026 is notably large even by semiconductor industry standards [6]. WFE growth in 2026 is expected to be constrained by clean room space availability, creating a natural ceiling on near-term upside. The dividend yield of 0.46% provides minimal income support despite 9 consecutive years of growth.
Key Catalysts
  1. WFE spending projected at $135B in 2026 with advanced packaging growing >40%, driven by AI chip manufacturing demand across TSMC, Samsung, and Intel foundry expansions [1]
  2. NAND recovery inflection beginning in 2026 adds a cyclical growth driver on top of the secular AI spending tailwind [1]
  3. New COO Varadarajan appointment to 'increase company velocity for the AI era' signals organizational focus on capturing the accelerating semiconductor equipment opportunity [3]
Key Risks
  1. Semiconductor equipment cyclicality risk — FY2024's 14% revenue decline demonstrates vulnerability to fab investment cycle downturns despite secular AI tailwinds [6]
  2. Forward P/E of 33.1x prices in sustained 20%+ growth; any deceleration from WFE spending constraints or customer delays would trigger significant multiple compression
  3. China export control risk — tightening of semiconductor equipment restrictions could impact revenue, and geopolitical tensions create ongoing uncertainty for cross-border equipment sales [1]
