Company Profile
Technology ·Semiconductor Equipment & Materials ·NASDAQ ·United States
Lam Research Corporation designs, manufactures, and services semiconductor processing equipment used in the fabrication of integrated circuits. The company is a global leader in etch, deposition (CVD and ALD), and cleaning/surface preparation systems critical to advanced chip manufacturing. Lam's equipment enables leading-edge nodes, 3D NAND, advanced packaging, and high-bandwidth memory (HBM) production for AI chips. Founded in 1980, headquartered in Fremont, California.

Investment Thesis
Lam Research delivered record FY2025 revenue of $20.6B (up 27% YoY) with 49.9% gross margin — the highest since the Novellus merger — and non-GAAP EPS of $4.89, up 46% from $3.36 [1]. The company outgrew the wafer fab equipment industry by 4x in 2025 (40% tool revenue growth vs. 10% industry) and continues to gain share in AI-critical applications. Advanced packaging revenue is growing 40%+ in 2026, directly driven by AI chip demand for HBM, chiplets, and heterogeneous integration [1]. Fiscal Q3 2026 delivered $5.84B revenue (up 24% YoY), and the March 2026 quarter guidance of $5.70B with 49% gross margin confirms sustained momentum. The balance sheet is pristine ($1.02B net cash, 35% D/E), and the dividend has grown 15% CAGR over 5 years with only an 18.91% payout ratio [3].
Valuation is the primary concern. At 36.55x forward earnings (P/E of 51.64 trailing), LRCX is priced for continued semiconductor equipment demand growth driven by AI [4]. Any slowdown in AI infrastructure spending, fab construction delays, or U.S.-China export restriction expansion could materially impact revenue growth and compress the multiple. The semiconductor equipment industry is inherently cyclical — the 2022-2023 NAND downturn demonstrated that even dominant players can see significant revenue declines. China export restrictions represent an ongoing revenue risk as ~30% of semiconductor equipment industry revenue comes from Chinese customers [1]. The 0.36% dividend yield is negligible for income investors despite the 15% growth rate, and the stock's 248% increase over the past 52 weeks has pushed valuation to levels that require sustained ~25%+ revenue growth to justify.
Key Catalysts
  1. Advanced packaging revenue growing 40%+ in 2026, driven by AI chip demand for high-bandwidth memory, chiplets, and heterogeneous integration across NVIDIA, AMD, and custom ASIC programs
  2. NAND industry recovery creating an incremental demand cycle for etch and deposition equipment as memory makers resume capacity expansion for AI and enterprise storage workloads
  3. Gate-all-around transistor transition at TSMC, Samsung, and Intel requiring incremental etch and deposition process steps, expanding Lam's served addressable market at each new node
Key Risks
  1. Forward P/E of 36.55x and 248% stock price appreciation over 52 weeks reflect AI-driven demand assumptions — any deceleration in AI infrastructure spending or fab construction delays could trigger significant multiple compression
  2. U.S. export restrictions on semiconductor equipment to China represent an ongoing revenue risk, with potential for expanded restrictions that could impact ~30% of industry revenue from Chinese customers
  3. Inherent cyclicality of the semiconductor equipment industry — the 2022-2023 NAND downturn reduced revenue significantly, and the current AI-driven super-cycle could reverse if AI capital spending moderates
