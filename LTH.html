Company Profile

Life Time Group Holdings operates large-format athletic country clubs across the United States and Canada, offering fitness, wellness, sports, and social programming. The company operates 189 centers with over 822,000 memberships, generating revenue through membership dues, in-center services (personal training, spa, cafe), and media/entertainment.

Investment Thesis
Life Time is a compelling growth story in the premium fitness and wellness space, with no direct competitor operating at its scale and price point. Full-year 2025 revenue grew 14.3% to $2,995M with net income surging 139% to $374M, while 2026 guidance calls for $3.3B+ revenue (10.7% growth) and 12-14 new club openings adding 1.2M sq ft [1]. The company's transition to an 'athletic country club' model with average revenue per membership of $3,531 (+11.7% YoY) creates a durable pricing power moat, and the L*AI*C digital wellness platform adds a recurring engagement layer that should drive lifetime member value higher [1][2].
The bear case centers on capital intensity and macroeconomic sensitivity. With $891.5M in capex in 2025 and aggressive 2026 expansion plans, the company is betting heavily on continued demand for premium fitness at $200+/month membership rates [1]. Debt-to-equity of 134% is manageable at current EBITDA levels but leaves limited margin for error if a recession compresses discretionary consumer spending. Persistent insider selling ($7M+ in three months with zero insider purchases) is a concerning signal that leadership may not share the market's optimism about the stock's valuation [4]. The back-half-loaded 2026 club opening schedule (6-7 of 12-14 clubs in Q4) creates execution risk and limits near-term revenue visibility.
Key Catalysts
  1. Accelerated club expansion with 12-14 new openings in 2026, nearly doubling annual square footage additions and creating significant embedded revenue growth as clubs ramp to maturity over 3-4 years [1]
  2. Continued revenue per membership growth through premium pricing, AI-personalized wellness services via L*AI*C, and expansion of high-margin ancillary offerings like personal training, spa, and pickleball programming [1][2]
  3. $500M share repurchase authorization signals management confidence and provides EPS accretion support at current valuation of ~15x forward earnings [1]
Key Risks
  1. Consumer discretionary spending compression in a recessionary environment could pressure membership retention and pricing power at $200+/month dues, particularly among the 822K member base that skews toward affluent but not ultra-high-net-worth households [1]
  2. Capital-intensive expansion model with $891.5M capex in 2025 and accelerating spend in 2026 creates significant execution risk and constrains free cash flow generation to $207M despite $871M in operating cash flow [1]
  3. Persistent insider selling (22 sells, zero buys in the past year) and back-half-loaded 2026 club openings suggest management may have limited near-term conviction while facing concentrated execution risk in Q4 [4]
