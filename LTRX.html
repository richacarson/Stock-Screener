Company Profile
Technology ·Communication Equipment ·NASDAQ ·United States
Lantronix, Inc. develops, markets, and sells industrial and enterprise IoT products including embedded compute modules, edge AI solutions, out-of-band management servers, and SaaS platforms for IoT deployment management. The company also has a growing autonomous drone components business through its partnership with Safe Pro Group.

Investment Thesis
Lantronix is a small-cap IoT company undergoing a strategic transformation under award-winning CEO Saleel Awsare, pivoting from legacy connectivity products toward edge AI compute and autonomous drone components[1][3]. Q2 FY2026 delivered $29.8M revenue with non-GAAP gross margins expanding to 45.3%, and the company raised its drone revenue outlook to $8-12M for FY2026 with drones expected to reach 15-20% of total revenue by FY2027[1]. The PEG ratio of 0.91 suggests the market is pricing in a credible earnings inflection, supported by the forward P/E of 21.1 and positive non-GAAP EPS of $0.04 in Q2.
The bear case rests on Lantronix's persistent GAAP losses — four consecutive years of net losses totaling over $30M — and a declining revenue trajectory with FY2025 revenue of $123M down from $160M in FY2024[2]. The company's $274M market cap leaves it vulnerable to competitive pressure from much larger IoT platform providers. Revenue guidance for Q3 FY2026 of $28.5-32.5M shows a business still searching for consistent growth, and the drone opportunity, while promising, remains small relative to the overall revenue base and carries execution risk in an emerging market.
Key Catalysts
  1. Drone revenue outlook raised to $8-12M for FY2026, with autonomous systems expected to reach 15-20% of revenue by FY2027[1]
  2. Non-GAAP gross margin expansion to 45.3% in Q2 FY2026 signals improving product mix toward higher-value edge AI solutions[1]
  3. PEG ratio of 0.91 implies the market sees a realistic path to profitability as the strategic pivot gains traction
Key Risks
  1. Four consecutive years of GAAP net losses with FY2025 loss widening to $11.4M, raising questions about path to sustained profitability[2]
  2. Revenue declining from $160M (FY2024) to $123M (FY2025), with core IoT connectivity business losing share to larger competitors
  3. Small scale ($274M market cap, 351 employees) limits R&D investment capacity in the capital-intensive edge AI and autonomous systems markets
