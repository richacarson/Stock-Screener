Company Profile
Financial Services ·Credit Services ·NYSE ·China
Lufax Holding is a Chinese fintech company backed by Ping An Insurance, providing credit facilitation, consumer finance, and wealth management services. Originally a peer-to-peer lending platform, Lufax pivoted to institutional credit facilitation and consumer finance after China's P2P lending crackdown. The company is navigating significant revenue declines, an accounting scandal, and a CEO transition.
35,875 Employees ·www.lufaxholding.com

Investment Thesis
Lufax trades at an extreme discount — 3.3x forward P/E with a $1.66B market cap against net cash of RMB 10.2B ($1.4B) — offering deep value if the business stabilizes [4]. The consumer finance segment grew 27.8% YoY in Q3 2024, and forward EPS of $0.58 implies a return to profitability [1]. The Ping An Insurance backing provides financial stability and distribution access. If incoming CEO Xiang Ji (McKinsey-trained retail banking strategist) successfully pivots toward consumer finance growth while managing credit quality, the stock's distressed valuation leaves room for significant re-rating. Cash-rich balance sheet (RMB 57.8B cash vs RMB 47.6B debt) limits downside [4].
The bear case is overwhelming. Revenue has declined 65% over three years — from RMB 54.6B to RMB 19.2B — with no visible inflection point [4]. The company is currently unprofitable with -13.6% profit margin and -4.1% ROE. The CEO transition creates strategic vacuum during a critical period. China's regulatory environment for fintech remains hostile, with ongoing uncertainty about credit facilitation rules, data privacy requirements, and consumer lending caps [3]. Competition from Ant Group, WeBank, and traditional bank fintech units is intensifying. The accounting scandal damaged institutional trust. Lufax's ADR structure adds delisting risk under US-China tensions. At $1.92 per ADS, the stock is deeply distressed and may be a value trap rather than a turnaround opportunity — three consecutive years of revenue decline with no clear catalyst for reversal [1][4].
