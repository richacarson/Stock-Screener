Company Profile

Intuitive Machines designs, manufactures, and operates lunar landers and space infrastructure, serving as NASA's most prolific commercial lunar delivery provider under the CLPS program with five task orders. Investment Thesis
Intuitive Machines occupies a unique position as the most prolific commercial lunar landing company, with Q1 2026 revenue of $186.7M (nearly 3x YoY) and management reaffirming $900M-$1B full-year 2026 guidance — a nearly 5x increase driven by the transformative Lanteris acquisition [1]. The $1.1B backlog (27% national security), five NASA CLPS contracts, $6.2B-ceiling Space Force Andromeda IDIQ, and $151B-ceiling MDA SHIELD IDIQ provide exceptional contract visibility across civil, commercial, and defense markets [3]. The company's integrated space services platform — combining lunar delivery, satellite manufacturing, autonomous navigation, and in-space data processing — creates competitive barriers no other commercial space company outside SpaceX can match.
The bear case centers on extreme execution risk: the nearly 5x revenue growth guidance depends on successful Lanteris integration and large contract deliveries, and Q1 2026 revenue of $186.7M already missed the $200-205M consensus [1]. The company remains deeply unprofitable with -32.7% profit margin and -30.3% ROE, and the path to positive adjusted EBITDA requires flawless execution across multiple complex programs. Director Ghaffarian's $3.57M share sale and CFO McGrath's share dispositions signal that insiders are monetizing rather than accumulating [4]. Balance sheet fragility persists with $455M in total debt against $232M cash, and the $175M strategic investment and potential shelf offering suggest further dilution ahead. Government budget risk remains real — NASA and DoD contracts are subject to congressional appropriation and potential spending cuts that could delay or cancel missions.

AI Resilience LOW RISK

AI Risk Protection (7/9): Intuitive Machines' core business — designing, building, and landing spacecraft on the Moon — is among the most AI-immune businesses in existence. No software-only AI product can replicate the physical engineering of lunar landers, the vertical integration of satellite manufacturing, or the operational complexity of deep-space missions [1]. The company's expanding national security space contracts (Space Force Andromeda IDIQ with $6.2B ceiling, MDA SHIELD IDIQ with $151B ceiling) involve highly classified physical infrastructure that AI structurally cannot displace [3]. AI actually increases demand for the company's services — AI data centers need satellite communications infrastructure, and AI-driven space domain awareness relies on the kind of hardware Intuitive Machines builds. AI Opportunity (1/1): The company generates meaningful AI-adjacent revenue through its Lanteris 1300 series platform targeting on-orbit data processing and edge computing for AI applications, and the KinetX acquisition brings AI-powered autonomous navigation capabilities that are embedded in revenue-generating mission contracts [2].
