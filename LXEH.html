Investment Thesis
Lixiang Education is a micro-cap Chinese education company trading at $0.98 with a $1.9M market cap, operating five schools across China's high school and vocational education segments [1]. The education sector provides some defensive characteristics, and the company showed marginal revenue growth of 0.3% YoY. The expansion into vocational education aligns with Chinese government policy prioritizing vocational training.
The bear case is overwhelming: the company is losing more money than it earns (net loss of RMB 16.1M vs. RMB 15.4M revenue in H1 2025), faces imminent NASDAQ delisting risk with a May 18, 2026 compliance deadline for the $1.00 minimum bid price, and daily dollar volume of ~$164K makes the stock essentially untradeable for institutional investors [1][3]. D/E of 49% is manageable, but the -97.8% profit margin and -14.8% ROE indicate a business model that is not generating economic value. This is a speculative micro-cap with existential risk.
