Company Profile
Technology ·Software - Application ·NASDAQ ·United States
Lyft is a North American transportation network company offering ridesharing, bike/scooter sharing, and autonomous vehicle partnerships. Under CEO David Risher since 2023, the company is pivoting from pure-play rideshare to a 'global hybrid transportation platform,' combining human drivers with AV partnerships through Waymo, Mobileye, and Baidu, and expanding internationally via the FreeNow acquisition covering nine European countries. FY2025 gross bookings of $18.5B grew 15% YoY with record adjusted EBITDA of $528.8M, and Q1 2026 revenue of $1.7B grew 14% YoY.

Investment Thesis
Lyft achieved record financials in FY2025 — $18.5B gross bookings (+15% YoY), $528.8M adjusted EBITDA, and its first year of sustained GAAP profitability — while Q1 2026 revenue of $1.7B grew 14% YoY confirming acceleration [1]. At 6.58x forward P/E with a PEG of 0.15, the stock trades at a massive discount to both Uber (20x+) and the broader market, reflecting the AV disruption overhang. The AV fleet management model — earning fees for managing third-party autonomous fleets through Waymo, Mobileye, and Baidu partnerships — could become a high-margin recurring revenue stream if AV adoption scales. California insurance reform and FreeNow European expansion provide near-term catalysts independent of the AV narrative [1][4].
The bear case is that Lyft's AV disruption risk is structural and asymmetric: Waymo's planned 20+ new city launches in 2026 could directly cannibalize Lyft's human driver network in exactly the densely populated markets where Lyft is most profitable [2]. Wedbush's Underperform downgrade specifically cited Lyft as 'most at risk' due to its U.S. rideshare concentration and undiversified business mix — unlike Uber which has food delivery and freight diversification. Trailing financial metrics (1.95x P/E, 43.8% margin, 147.8% ROE) are distorted by the transition from losses to profitability and do not reflect normalized earnings power; forward EPS of $2.03 suggests a more modest business. The MISALIGNED Inspire score (-67) reflects material values concerns. Driver classification litigation and regulatory reserves create ongoing earnings volatility risk.
Key Catalysts
  1. AV fleet management revenue materializing from Mobileye Nashville/Atlanta deployments and expanded Waymo partnership — would validate the 'hybrid transportation platform' thesis and justify re-rating toward Uber-like multiples
  2. FreeNow European expansion generating international gross bookings growth and reducing dangerous U.S. rideshare concentration risk
  3. Continued adjusted EBITDA margin expansion toward 4%+ in 2027, driven by California insurance reform (SB 371) and operational efficiency gains from DoorDash partnership scale
Key Risks
  1. Autonomous vehicle disruption: Waymo's 20+ city expansion and Tesla robotaxi in Austin could displace human driver rides before Lyft's AV fleet management model generates sufficient replacement revenue — Wedbush calls this 'most at risk' scenario [2]
  2. Driver classification litigation and regulatory reserve requirements creating $100M+ quarterly earnings surprises that distort reported revenue and erode investor confidence [1]
  3. Normalized earnings far below trailing GAAP figures: forward EPS of $2.03 vs trailing EPS of $6.84 suggests the trailing profitability was inflated by one-time items; true earning power remains unproven at scale
