Company Profile
Consumer Cyclical ·Department Stores ·NYSE ·United States
Macy's is an omni-channel retail organization operating department stores, websites, and mobile applications under the Macy's, Bloomingdale's, and Bluemercury brands. The company sells apparel, accessories, cosmetics, home furnishings, and other consumer goods. Founded in 1830, Macy's is executing a 'Bold New Chapter' strategy under CEO Tony Spring, closing underperforming locations and investing in reimagined stores and luxury expansion.

Investment Thesis
Macy's Q4 FY2025 results beat expectations with adjusted EPS of $1.67 vs. $1.56 consensus and comparable OLM sales growth of 1.8% vs. expectations for flat to -2.5%, demonstrating that the Bold New Chapter strategy is gaining traction [1]. At 8.2x forward P/E and 4.27% dividend yield with a conservative 31.5% payout ratio, the stock offers deep value and income in a turnaround context [4]. The 125 Reimagine stores posted 1.0% comp growth, Bluemercury grew 1.6%, and the company's 35+ AI use cases targeting $7.6B in margin gains by 2027 provide operational improvement optionality [1][2]. Stock appreciation of ~45% YoY suggests the market is beginning to recognize the turnaround progress.
The bear case centers on secular decline and the fundamental question of whether department stores have a long-term future. Revenue has declined from $25.4B (FY2022) to $22.6B (FY2025), a 3.2% annual decline that the Bold New Chapter has not yet reversed [4]. Fiscal 2026 guidance of $21.4-$21.65B in net sales implies another ~5% revenue decline, with adjusted EPS of $1.90-$2.10 falling from FY2025's $2.32 [1]. Profit margin of just 2.84% leaves almost no buffer for execution missteps or economic downturn [4]. The $0.10-$0.20 tariff EPS headwind could worsen if trade tensions escalate. E-commerce penetration and off-price retailer expansion (TJX, Ross) continue to structurally compress department store demand. The company suspended its dividend during COVID, and while it has been growing since resumption, the 5-year track record since restoration is short [4].
Key Catalysts
  1. Reimagine store program expansion — 125 stores delivering 1.0% comp growth vs. broader Macy's decline, with potential rollout to additional locations [1]
  2. AI-driven operational efficiency targeting $7.6B in cumulative sales/margin gains by 2027 through dynamic pricing, predictive inventory, and supply chain optimization [2]
  3. Bloomingdale's and Bluemercury luxury positioning providing insulation from mass-market retail pressure and higher-margin revenue mix improvement [1]
Key Risks
  1. Secular department store decline — revenue has contracted from $25.4B to $22.6B over 3 years with FY2026 guidance implying another ~5% decline to $21.4-$21.65B [1]
  2. Tariff headwind of $0.10-$0.20 EPS impact in FY2026 could accelerate if trade tensions escalate, directly pressuring the already-thin 2.84% profit margin [1][4]
  3. AI-powered e-commerce competitors (Amazon, Shein, Temu) using personalization and dynamic pricing at scale, structurally compressing department store addressable market [2]

AI Resilience (25%)
AI Resilience HIGH RISK
4
AI Risk Protection (4/9): Department store retail faces meaningful AI-driven disruption risk through multiple vectors. AI-powered recommendation engines and personalized shopping platforms (Amazon, Stitch Fix) directly compete for Macy's core apparel and home goods customers by offering superior convenience and personalization [2]. AI enables e-commerce competitors to optimize pricing, inventory, and marketing at a speed and precision that legacy retailers struggle to match. The in-store experience — Macy's key differentiator — provides some defense, but AI-driven virtual try-on, conversational commerce, and automated customer service reduce the experiential advantage of physical stores. Macy's is exposed but not vulnerable because the luxury segment (Bloomingdale's) and beauty (Bluemercury) have higher touch requirements that resist digital displacement [2]. AI Opportunity (0/1): While Macy's has identified 35+ AI use cases and targets $7.6B in margin gains by 2027, these are internal efficiency applications — the company does not generate meaningful revenue from AI products or services [2].
