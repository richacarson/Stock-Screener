Company Profile
Real Estate ·REIT - Retail ·NYSE ·United States
Macerich is a fully integrated, self-managed, self-administered real estate investment trust (REIT). As a leading owner, operator, and developer of high-quality retail real estate in densely populated and attractive U.S. markets, Macerich's portfolio is concentrated in California, the Pacific Northwest, Phoenix/Scottsdale, and the Metro New York to Washington, D.C. corridor, currently owning 39 million square feet across 38 retail centers.

Investment Thesis
Macerich offers exposure to Class A retail real estate in top-tier U.S. markets (California, Pacific Northwest, Metro NYC-DC corridor) at a significant discount to NAV, with shares trading at $22.30 against properties in affluent, high-barrier-to-entry locations [1][7]. The 'Path Forward Plan' targets $30M incremental contribution in 2026 growing to $45-50M by 2028 from signed leasing, and the 3.05% dividend yield provides income while investors wait for the turnaround to materialize [2]. GRESB #1 ranking for 10 consecutive years in U.S. retail and net-zero-by-2035 commitment position the company well for ESG-focused capital flows [4].
The bear case centers on deeply negative fundamentals: -18.9% profit margin, -7.5% ROE, 203% D/E ratio, and a 189% payout ratio that makes the current dividend mathematically unsustainable without improvement [7]. Revenue declined 17.4% YoY, the third CEO in a decade signals strategic instability, and the structural shift toward e-commerce continues to pressure mall-based retail despite Class A positioning [1][2]. With negative forward EPS and no clear path to GAAP profitability, the REIT structure that forces dividend payments from depleting FFO creates a value trap risk that could result in another dividend cut.
Key Catalysts
  1. Path Forward Plan delivering $30M incremental NOI in 2026 from signed leasing activity, validating the turnaround thesis and potentially restoring positive earnings
  2. Mixed-use redevelopment completions converting underperforming retail into residential and dining, diversifying revenue streams and increasing property values in high-demand urban markets
  3. Potential interest rate cuts reducing refinancing costs on the $4.8B debt load, directly improving FFO and making the dividend more sustainable
Key Risks
  1. Payout ratio of 189% is unsustainable — if FFO does not improve by 2027, a second dividend cut becomes likely, potentially triggering a 20-30% share price decline as income investors exit
  2. Third CEO in a decade (Jackson Hsieh appointed March 2024) creates strategic execution risk; 'Path Forward Plan' targets could be revised or abandoned under new leadership priorities
  3. Continued secular shift to e-commerce and experiential retail could reduce tenant demand, with anchor store closures or rent concessions further compressing NOI across the 38-property portfolio
