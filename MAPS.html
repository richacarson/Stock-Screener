Investment Thesis
WM Technology operates Weedmaps, the leading US cannabis marketplace, but the business is in structural decline with revenue falling 9.7% YoY to an expected $42-44M quarterly run rate [1]. The company is voluntarily delisting from Nasdaq to OTCQX, reducing transparency and liquidity while management focuses on cost-cutting rather than growth. At a market cap of just $39M against $57M in cash, there is asset value, but the SEC fraud settlement ($1.5M for inflating user metrics), 2.1/5 Glassdoor rating, and serial leadership turnover create significant governance risk [2][3]. The cannabis industry remains federally illegal, limiting the total addressable market and preventing access to normal banking/payment rails.
The bull case rests on federal cannabis legalization unlocking significant market expansion, the company's $57M cash position exceeding its market cap, and the potential for a successful go-private transaction at a premium. The forward P/E of 2.1x is exceptionally cheap if profitability stabilizes. However, the company's Nasdaq delisting removes institutional investor access, the SEC enforcement action damaged credibility, and declining revenue without clear growth drivers suggests a value trap. The co-founder CEO's failed $1.70/share take-private attempt in 2025 indicates even insiders see limited upside from current levels [1][3].
Key Catalysts
  1. Federal cannabis legalization or rescheduling could dramatically expand the addressable market and unlock banking/payment infrastructure [1]
  2. Cash position of $57M exceeds $39M market cap, providing asset-backed downside protection and potential for special dividends or buybacks [1]
  3. Successful transition to OTCQX could reduce compliance costs by $5-10M annually, improving profitability on the reduced cost structure [1]
Key Risks
  1. Voluntary Nasdaq delisting eliminates institutional investor access and reduces liquidity below the $1M daily threshold, creating a permanent liquidity trap [1]
  2. SEC fraud settlement and governance failures ($1.5M fine for misleading MAU data) signal systemic integrity issues that may continue under co-founder leadership [3]
  3. Cannabis industry revenue decline of 9.7% YoY with no visible growth catalysts as state markets mature and pricing pressure intensifies [1]
