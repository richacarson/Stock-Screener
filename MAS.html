Company Profile
Industrials ·Building Products & Equipment ·NYSE ·United States
Masco Corporation manufactures and distributes home improvement and building products worldwide through two segments: Plumbing Products (Delta, Hansgrohe, Brizo) and Decorative Architectural Products (Behr paints, Kichler lighting divested in 2024). It serves wholesale, retail, and homebuilder channels primarily in North America and Europe.

Investment Thesis
Masco is a well-run mature building-products business with two high-quality franchises — Delta/Hansgrohe/Brizo plumbing and Behr architectural paints at Home Depot — that have weathered a difficult housing cycle. 2025 revenue fell 3% to $7.56B but adjusted EPS held at $3.96, and 2026 guidance of $4.10-$4.30 implies modest growth off a trough [1]. The Plumbing segment grew 3% to $4.99B on pricing, and a new $2.0B buyback authorization (replacing the prior) plus a 2.3% dividend yield with 12+ years of consecutive payments frame Masco as a total-return, capital-return story [1]. The new CEO Jon Nudi and restructured Executive Committee installed in December 2025 are positioned to 'accelerate growth' via portfolio focus after the 2024 Kichler divestiture [2].
The bear case: Masco is structurally tied to North American housing turnover and R&R (repair & remodel) demand, both of which are cyclically weak. Q4 2025 North American sales fell 5% and gross margin compressed 80 bps to 33.9% on input costs and unfavorable mix [1]. Decorative Architectural (Behr) revenue fell 14% in 2025 to $2.57B, reflecting the Kichler divestiture plus soft paint demand — and analysts have flagged tariff exposure and housing-market weakness as a 'high-risk scenario' for shareholders [4]. Debt/equity is elevated due to buyback-driven capital structure, and 2026 EPS guidance implies only low-single-digit growth. Innovation is incremental rather than transformative, and Inspire faith-alignment is solidly positive (+55) but overall score at 61 reflects a HOLD — quality but not yet a BUY catalyst.

  3. Decorative Architectural segment (Behr paints) posted a 14% 2025 revenue decline with 2026 guidance flat — a secular signal rather than a single-cycle drawdown [1]
