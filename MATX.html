Company Profile
Industrials ·Marine Shipping ·NYSE ·United States
Matson, Inc. is a leading U.S. carrier in the Pacific providing ocean transportation and logistics services. Founded in 1882, the company serves as a vital lifeline to Hawaii, Alaska, Guam, Micronesia, and the South Pacific, while also operating a premium China-Long Beach Express service and Matson Logistics for intermodal and supply chain services.

Investment Thesis
Matson offers compelling value at 10.4x forward P/E with a clean 26% debt-to-equity balance sheet and strong 2025 earnings of $13.81 EPS. The Jones Act-protected domestic routes to Hawaii and Alaska provide stable, recession-resistant revenue, while the China-Long Beach Express service benefits from e-commerce demand growth. The ultra-low 10% payout ratio gives management flexibility to grow dividends at 15%+ CAGR, invest in fleet renewal ($425 million in 2026), and return capital through buybacks. The company's 140-year history and essential lifeline service role provide exceptional business durability.
Revenue declined 4.3% in 2025 to $3.34 billion as China trade volumes softened, and 2026 operating income guidance is roughly flat with 2025. The average daily volume of 308,000 shares raises modest liquidity concerns (though passes the $1M floor at $48M daily). The February 2026 shipbreaking controversy, where Matson reversed its 2015 anti-beaching commitment, damages ESG credibility and could attract regulatory scrutiny. Shipping is inherently cyclical with freight rate sensitivity, and Matson's Pacific concentration creates geographic risk from trade disruptions or natural disasters. The 'C' executive team rating and below-average Glassdoor scores suggest leadership challenges.
