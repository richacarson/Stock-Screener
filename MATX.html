Company Profile
Industrials ·Marine Shipping ·NYSE ·United States
Matson provides ocean transportation and logistics services in the Pacific. The company operates containership and barge services between the U.S. West Coast, Hawaii, Alaska, Guam, and Micronesia, and between China and the U.S. The company also provides intermodal rail and trucking services through its logistics subsidiary.

Investment Thesis
Matson is a deeply undervalued Pacific shipping franchise trading at just 10.4x forward earnings with a fortress balance sheet (26.4% D/E, $547M operating cash flow, $361M total debt). The Jones Act provides an irreplaceable competitive moat for Hawaii and Alaska routes that cannot be disrupted by competitors, AI, or digital substitution. FY2025 net income of $444.8M on $3.3B revenue demonstrates strong profitability, and the $1.26B buyback program (one-third of shares) dramatically enhances per-share economics. EPS growth of 21.2% YoY shows momentum despite revenue decline. The 10% payout ratio provides enormous dividend growth runway from the 0.9% yield. The +39 Inspire score with zero negative attributions supports faith-aligned investing.
The bear case centers on growth limitations and cyclical exposure. Revenue declined 4.3% YoY to $3.3B, and 2026 operating income guidance of 'approaching 2025 levels' implies no growth. The China-U.S. transpacific trade is vulnerable to tariff disruptions, trade policy shifts, and e-commerce demand normalization that could rapidly compress freight rates. The 0.9% dividend yield is negligible for income investors despite the dividend sleeve classification. Average daily volume of 301K shares creates significant liquidity constraints for institutional investors. The $4.8B market cap makes MATX a small-cap stock with limited analyst coverage. Jones Act legislative risk — any weakening of cabotage requirements would immediately destroy Matson's competitive moat on core routes, though repeal remains politically unlikely.
Key Catalysts
  1. China-U.S. e-commerce demand continuation driving transpacific freight rate strength, with Q4 2025 already exceeding management expectations on volume and pricing
  2. New vessel deliveries from the $425M construction program improving fuel efficiency, capacity, and environmental compliance, extending the fleet's competitive positioning for decades
  3. Dividend growth acceleration — with a 10.1% payout ratio and $547M operating cash flow, management has enormous capacity to dramatically increase the $1.44 annual dividend while maintaining buyback programs
Key Risks
  1. Transpacific trade disruption — escalating U.S.-China tariffs, trade war retaliation, or a significant reduction in Chinese e-commerce exports could rapidly compress Matson's China service revenue and freight rates
  2. Jones Act legislative risk — while politically unlikely, any weakening of cabotage requirements would immediately open Hawaii and Alaska routes to foreign competition, destroying Matson's core moat
  3. Small-cap liquidity risk — 301K average daily volume and $4.8B market cap limit institutional interest, creating potential for volatile price action on modest position changes and wider bid-ask spreads
