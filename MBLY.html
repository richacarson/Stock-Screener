Company Profile
Technology ·Auto Parts ·NASDAQ ·Israel
Mobileye Global Inc. develops and deploys advanced driver-assistance systems (ADAS) and autonomous driving technologies. The company's EyeQ system-on-chip platform powers ADAS features in over 200 million vehicles from more than 50 OEMs worldwide. Products include SuperVision (hands-free highway driving), Mobileye Chauffeur (eyes-off L4 autonomy), and the Mobileye Drive self-driving system for robotaxis and commercial vehicles. Founded in 1999 by Prof. Amnon Shashua in Jerusalem, Israel. Intel holds approximately 97% voting control through Class B shares. Market cap of approximately $6.6 billion.

Investment Thesis
Mobileye is the dominant ADAS technology provider with EyeQ chips deployed in 200M+ vehicles across 50+ OEMs, a $24.5B future revenue pipeline, and a clear technology roadmap from L2 ADAS through L4 autonomous driving [1][2]. Q1 2026 delivered 27% revenue growth to $558M with adjusted operating income up 61% to $95M, prompting management to raise full-year 2026 guidance to $1.935B-$2.015B [1]. The balance sheet is exceptionally clean — $1.34B cash against $62M debt — and the Forward PEG of 0.88 suggests the stock is undervalued relative to projected 21% EPS growth [6]. The expansion into vertically integrated robotaxis and Physical AI (via Mentee Robotics acquisition) positions Mobileye for revenue streams beyond its traditional Tier-1 supplier model. At $7.79 per share, down 53% from 52-week highs, much of the execution risk appears priced in.
The primary concerns center on profitability and strategic control. GAAP financials are heavily distorted by a $3.9B goodwill impairment charge in Q1 2026, resulting in a -204% net margin and -41% ROE that do not reflect underlying economics [1][6]. Even on an adjusted basis, operating margins of 17% are modest for a technology company, and the path to sustained GAAP profitability remains unclear as R&D spending stays elevated. Intel's 97% voting control creates an overhang — Intel has sold over $1B in Mobileye shares and its own financial difficulties could force further sales or strategic decisions misaligned with Mobileye's long-term vision [5]. The competitive landscape is intensifying: Tesla FSD is scaling rapidly with a pure vision approach, Waymo has demonstrated commercial L4 robotaxi service, and Nvidia's DRIVE platform enables OEMs to reduce dependence on Mobileye. The 2024 revenue decline (from $2.08B to $1.65B) due to customer inventory corrections exposed cyclical vulnerability in the OEM supply chain model [3].
Key Catalysts
  1. Q1 2026 momentum with 27% revenue growth and raised full-year guidance to $1.935B-$2.015B, driven by 28% increase in EyeQ SoC volumes and normalization of customer safety stock levels
  2. SuperVision hands-free driving system launching across 17 new vehicle models from 2026, expanding Mobileye's revenue per vehicle from ~$50 (EyeQ ADAS) to $500-1,000+ for premium automated driving systems
  3. Vertically integrated robotaxi business and Mentee Robotics acquisition positioning Mobileye in the Physical AI market beyond automotive, with CES 2026 demonstrations showing working prototypes
Key Risks
  1. Intel's 97% voting control and history of share sales creates an overhang — Intel's own financial pressures could lead to forced divestiture at unfavorable terms, strategic decisions that prioritize Intel over Mobileye, or continued share dilution through secondary offerings
  2. Tesla FSD, Waymo, and Nvidia DRIVE represent intensifying competition from AI-native platforms, and OEMs increasingly building in-house ADAS capabilities could erode Mobileye's dominant 70%+ market share in front-camera ADAS
  3. GAAP profitability remains elusive with -21.4% operating margin, a $3.9B goodwill impairment charge, and heavy R&D investment needed for L4 autonomy and robotaxi commercialization — the path from adjusted profitability to sustained GAAP profitability is uncertain

AI Resilience (25%)
AI Resilience LOW RISK
9
AI Risk Protection (8/9): Mobileye is an AI-native company — AI is the core product, not a peripheral tool. The company's Compound AI System, combining deep learning perception with Responsibility-Sensitive Safety (RSS) and Road Experience Management (REM) mapping, represents a deeply integrated technology stack that is itself the competitive moat [2]. The EyeQ6 chip (176 TOPS) is purpose-built for automotive AI inference, and the safety validation requirements of ADAS/autonomous driving create barriers that generic AI cannot easily surmount. Mobileye's installed base of 200M+ vehicles generates proprietary mapping data that compounds over time. However, the company faces genuine AI-native competitors: Tesla FSD uses a pure vision end-to-end neural network approach that could prove superior, Waymo has demonstrated L4 commercial robotaxi service, and Nvidia's DRIVE platform enables OEMs to build competing in-house solutions [3]. The risk is not that AI disrupts Mobileye but that better AI implementations from competitors erode its dominant ADAS market position. AI Opportunity (1/1): AI expansion beyond automotive is underway — the acquisition of Mentee Robotics extends Mobileye's Compound AI System into humanoid robotics, positioning the company in the broader Physical AI market that Prof. Shashua presented at CES 2026 [2]. The robotaxi vertical integration initiative creates new revenue streams. AI is not just the moat — it is the entire growth thesis.
