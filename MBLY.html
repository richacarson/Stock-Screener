Company Profile

Mobileye Global develops and deploys advanced driver-assistance systems (ADAS) and autonomous driving technologies. The company designs proprietary EyeQ system-on-chips and software for collision avoidance, lane keeping, and autonomous driving across the global automotive industry. Founded in 1999 by Prof. Amnon Shashua, Mobileye serves major OEMs worldwide with an 8-year revenue pipeline of $24.5 billion.

Investment Thesis
Mobileye is the dominant global provider of AI-powered ADAS technology with ~70% market share in vision processing, an $24.5B 8-year revenue pipeline (up 42%), and a visionary founder in Prof. Amnon Shashua who has led the company for 27 years [1][2]. Full-year 2025 revenue grew 15% to $1,894M with operating cash flow surging 51% to $602M, demonstrating the business model's leverage as it scales [1]. The forward P/E of 17.6x is attractive for a company with dominant market position, massive pipeline visibility, and expanding TAM through robotaxi (VW partnership) and Physical AI (Mentee Robotics acquisition). At $5.7B market cap, Mobileye trades at 3x revenue with $1.8B cash, providing substantial downside protection and optionality on autonomous driving adoption acceleration.
The bear case centers on near-term growth deceleration and profitability timeline uncertainty. 2026 guidance of flat to 5% revenue growth significantly disappointed after 2025's 15% growth, suggesting OEM ordering patterns remain lumpy and unpredictable [1]. GAAP losses persist with -20.7% profit margin and -3.3% ROE, and the $900M Mentee Robotics acquisition depletes cash reserves while adding execution risk in an unproven market (humanoid robotics). Intel's 88% voting control creates governance risk — Mobileye's strategic decisions may be influenced by Intel's financial pressures rather than Mobileye's long-term interests. Competition from Qualcomm Snapdragon Ride, NVIDIA DRIVE, and Tesla's vertical integration threatens market share in next-generation autonomous systems. The stock's 76% decline from IPO highs ($41 to $6.73) reflects persistent market skepticism about the path to profitability [4].
Key Catalysts
  1. Surround ADAS program win with major U.S. OEM and VW robotaxi expansion could accelerate revenue ramp beyond the conservative flat-to-5% 2026 guidance [1]
  2. Margin inflection as higher-ASP SuperVision and Chauffeur programs scale — operating cash flow already surged 51% in 2025 to $602M, signaling operating leverage [1]
  3. Mentee Robotics integration opens Physical AI/humanoid robotics market, potentially expanding Mobileye's TAM by orders of magnitude beyond automotive ADAS [1][2]
Key Risks
  1. Intel's 88% voting control creates governance overhang — potential forced sale, strategic misdirection, or delayed investment decisions driven by Intel's financial restructuring needs rather than Mobileye's optimal strategy [1]
  2. 2026 guidance of flat-to-5% growth suggests OEM ordering patterns remain volatile, with risk of further inventory corrections or program delays that extend the unprofitable period [1]
  3. Competitive pressure from Qualcomm Snapdragon Ride, NVIDIA DRIVE Orin/Thor, and Tesla FSD threatens Mobileye's dominant ADAS market share as automakers diversify chip suppliers [2]
