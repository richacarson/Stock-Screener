Investment Thesis
Mister Car Wash surpassed $1B in full-year revenue for the first time in FY2025 with 2.3M Unlimited Wash Club members (up 7% YoY) — a genuine subscription services business wrapped in a physical asset base [1][2]. However, the company has announced a definitive take-private agreement with Leonard Green & Partners (majority owner since 2014) at $7.00/share, a 29% premium to 90-day VWAP but well below the 2021 IPO price [4]. At a $3.1B enterprise value, the deal likely closes in 1H 2026 and delists the stock. For public shareholders, MCW is effectively a merger-arbitrage situation — downside protection is the $7.00 cash consideration, and the only remaining upside would come from a superior competing bid (unlikely given LGP's 67% ownership).
There is no meaningful long-term public-equity thesis for MCW — the stock is functionally going away. The bear case is that the deal breaks (regulatory, shareholder lawsuits investigating minority protection, or financing) and MCW trades back toward mid-$5s on fundamentals: decelerating 4% Q4 revenue growth, 1.6% comps, modest ~8% net margin, and a high net-leverage capital structure [1][2]. Independent of the deal, MCW faces secular pressure from AI-accelerated EV/autonomous adoption (modest), rising operating costs, and membership churn risk if consumer discretionary spending weakens. Shareholder law firms (Robbins Geller, Halper Sadeh) are investigating whether the $7.00 price adequately protects minority holders [4] — a successful challenge could raise the price marginally but unlikely by more than 5-10%. Faith-aligned score of +53 is a positive but does not overcome the IOWN Return-on-Intention framework's low growth/leverage concerns.
