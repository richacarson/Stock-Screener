Company Profile
Industrials ·Aerospace & Defense ·NYSE ·Canada
MDA Space is a leading space technology company providing satellite communications, Earth observation, and robotics solutions. The company builds satellite constellations (Telesat Lightspeed, Globalstar), develops the Canadarm3 robotic system for the Lunar Gateway, operates the RADARSAT-2 Earth observation satellite, and offers defense C4ISR solutions. Founded in 1969, MDA is the creator of the original Canadarm and Canadarm2 for the International Space Station.

Investment Thesis
MDA Space is a high-growth space technology company riding the LEO satellite constellation boom, with revenue surging 51.2% YoY to C$1.63B in 2025 driven by Telesat Lightspeed and Globalstar manufacturing contracts [1]. The $4.0B backlog provides 90% coverage of C$1.7-1.9B 2026 revenue guidance, while a $40B pipeline (including $10B in down-selected/follow-on opportunities) offers substantial long-term growth visibility [1]. MDA's 57-year heritage in space robotics (Canadarm to Canadarm3) and Earth observation (RADARSAT-2, CHORUS) creates deep competitive moats in specialized markets. CEO Mike Greenley, named Canada's 2024 CEO Innovator of the Year, has tripled revenue and more than doubled headcount since taking the helm [2]. At 26.1x forward P/E with 50%+ revenue growth, the valuation is compelling relative to the growth trajectory and backlog visibility.
The bear case centers on margin compression and execution risk at scale. Profit margin of 2.2% on C$1.63B revenue (net income C$108.5M) reflects the margin-dilutive nature of satellite manufacturing — MDA is building physical hardware at lower margins than its legacy services business [1]. FCF guidance for 2026 is neutral to slightly negative as the company invests C$225-275M in capex for manufacturing facilities. The 2026 revenue growth deceleration to ~10% at midpoint (after 51% in 2025) raises questions about whether the constellation manufacturing peak is approaching. Concentration risk is significant — Telesat Lightspeed and Globalstar represent the majority of satellite systems revenue, and delays or cancellations in either program could materially impact results. Defense programs face geopolitical and budget risk. D/E and ROE metrics are unavailable, limiting visibility into balance sheet leverage. At $4.3B market cap, the stock requires continued execution on large, complex satellite programs with tight delivery timelines [3].
Key Catalysts
  1. Telesat Lightspeed satellite deliveries beginning in 2026 with ramp-up in 2027, representing the largest LEO constellation manufacturing program for MDA and driving sustained revenue growth [1]
  2. Canadarm3 production contract execution for the Lunar Gateway, deepening MDA's dominant position in space robotics with a multi-decade program spanning exploration of the Moon and Mars [2]
  3. $40B pipeline conversion — with $10B including down-selected government opportunities and follow-on contracts — provides significant visibility for backlog replenishment beyond current programs [1]
Key Risks
  1. Customer concentration risk — Telesat Lightspeed and Globalstar constellation contracts drive the majority of satellite systems revenue; program delays, technical issues, or customer financial difficulties could materially impact results [1]
  2. Margin compression from satellite manufacturing scale-up — profit margin at 2.2% with FCF neutral to negative in 2026 creates limited financial cushion if execution challenges arise during production ramp [1]
  3. Revenue growth deceleration to ~10% in 2026 (from 51% in 2025) as the constellation build phase matures, potentially compressing the forward valuation multiple if the market reprices growth expectations [1]
