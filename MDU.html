The company serves approximately 1.2 million customers across the northern Great Plains and Pacific Northwest.

Investment Thesis
MDU Resources is a stable regulated utility offering 2.5% dividend yield with 80+ consecutive years of dividend payments and a 6-8% long-term EPS CAGR target backed by $3.1B in planned capital investments (2026-2030) [1]. The 2023 spin-offs of construction businesses refocused the company on its regulated utility core, and the results are showing: natural gas utility earnings grew 19.6% in 2025 and pipeline earnings hit a record $68.2M [1]. Rate base growth of 16% YoY (including Badger Wind Farm) drives the regulated earnings flywheel. At 20.7x forward P/E with beta of 0.37, MDU offers defensive income with inflation-protected dividend growth (~11% 5yr CAGR). The 1-2% annual customer growth in the northern Great Plains, bolstered by Bakken energy activity and data center demand, provides organic volume upside [1].
The bear case reflects the limitations of a small regulated utility. Revenue growth YoY of -0.3% and 3yr CAGR of 2.4% demonstrate the inherently slow-growth nature of regulated gas and electric distribution [1]. ROE of 7.01% is below the 10% utility average, suggesting suboptimal regulatory outcomes or capital structure inefficiency. Forward P/E of 20.7x is not cheap for a utility growing EPS at 6-8% — peers like ATO trade at similar multiples with better growth profiles. The 2026 EPS guidance of $0.93-$1.00 implies flat-to-modest growth from 2025's $0.93, disappointing for a utility in the midst of a rate base expansion cycle. Negative free cash flow (-$365M in 2025) reflects heavy capex, and D/E of 97.8% with $2.7B debt requires continuous capital market access. Geographic concentration in the northern Great Plains limits the addressable market, and warm winters directly impact natural gas distribution revenues [4].
Key Catalysts
  1. $3.1B capital investment plan (2026-2030) driving 6-8% rate base and EPS CAGR through regulated infrastructure expansion, including pipeline capacity additions and distribution upgrades [1]
  2. AI data center demand — hyperscale computing facilities require massive electricity and gas consumption, potentially increasing load growth in MDU's service territories beyond the baseline 1-2% customer growth [1]
  3. Dividend growth trajectory — 11% 5yr CAGR with 60-70% payout ratio target provides a growing income stream supported by 80+ years of uninterrupted payments [1]
Key Risks
  1. Regulatory risk — ROE of 7.01% suggests MDU may struggle to achieve favorable rate case outcomes; adverse regulatory decisions could compress allowed returns below the cost of capital [1]
  2. Geographic concentration in the northern Great Plains exposes MDU to regional economic downturns, warm winter weather patterns reducing gas demand, and limited customer growth ceiling [4]
  3. Negative free cash flow (-$365M in 2025) from heavy capex requires continuous debt and equity issuance, diluting existing shareholders and creating refinancing risk if capital markets tighten [1]
