Investment Thesis
MetLife is a deeply undervalued large-cap dividend payer trading at 6.5x forward P/E with a 3.21% dividend yield, offering a compelling value proposition for income investors [4]. The company delivered $77.1B in 2025 revenue (up 8.6% YoY) with Q4 adjusted earnings up 13% and adjusted EPS excluding notables rising 24% to $2.58, demonstrating operational leverage [1]. Forward EPS of $10.94 implies 14.9% earnings growth, and the company returned $4.4B to shareholders in 2025 with BVPS increasing 14% to $39.02 [1]. MetLife's diversified global platform across group benefits, retirement, and international markets provides multiple growth vectors, while its AI investment through a proprietary platform positions it for efficiency gains in claims processing and underwriting [2].
The bear case focuses on cyclical exposure and structural challenges. MetLife's Q4 2025 GAAP EPS missed analyst estimates despite revenue growth, with earnings quarterly growth declining 36.3% [4]. Profit margin of 4.4% is thin, and D/E of 226% reflects the insurance industry's leveraged balance sheet structure that amplifies downside in market stress scenarios. Variable investment income — a significant earnings contributor — is inherently volatile and dependent on capital market conditions. International operations (Asia, Latin America, EMEA) face currency risk and geopolitical uncertainty. The insurance industry faces long-term disruption from insuretech startups using AI for automated underwriting and instant claims. MetLife stock has declined 15.3% from its 52-week high of $83.85, underperforming the S&P 500's 32.7% gain, suggesting the market sees limited growth upside [4].
Key Catalysts
  1. New Frontier strategy execution delivering on targeted 14.9% earnings growth and 17.6% EPS growth in 2026, potentially driving multiple expansion from the current 6.5x forward P/E [1]
  2. AI platform deployment across claims processing and underwriting creating measurable efficiency gains and expanding margins beyond the current 4.4% profit margin [2]
  3. Continued shareholder returns through dividend growth (~4.7% 5yr CAGR) and share buybacks, with $4.4B returned in 2025 providing a strong income floor [1]
Key Risks
  1. Variable investment income volatility — MetLife's earnings are significantly exposed to capital market conditions, and a market downturn could compress both investment returns and book value [4]
  2. Insuretech disruption risk from AI-powered challengers automating underwriting, claims, and distribution in simpler insurance lines like term life and auto [2]
  3. International exposure to currency risk and geopolitical uncertainty across Asia, Latin America, and EMEA segments representing a material portion of revenue [1]
