Investment Thesis
Meta Platforms is executing one of the most impressive growth-at-scale stories in market history — $201B in 2025 revenue growing 33% YoY in Q1 2026 to $56.3B, with 32.8% profit margins, 32.9% ROE, and a forward PEG of 0.87x [1]. The AI transformation is directly monetizable through advertising improvements (ad impressions +19%, price per ad +12% in Q1), Meta AI reaching 1B+ monthly users, and AI-powered content recommendations driving engagement across 3.3B+ daily active users. At a forward P/E of 16.7x with 62% earnings growth, META trades at a significant discount to its growth rate, and the $125-145B AI infrastructure investment positions the company as a defining platform for the AI era [1].
The bear case centers on capital intensity risk — $125-145B in 2026 capex (raised from prior guidance of $115-135B) represents an unprecedented bet on AI infrastructure, and any slowdown in advertising demand or AI monetization would expose massive overcapacity [1]. Reality Labs continues to burn $20B+ annually with uncertain returns on the metaverse vision. Regulatory risk is significant with ongoing EU antitrust proceedings, content moderation scrutiny, privacy fines ($5B FTC, $263M EU), and a US antitrust trial in April 2026 that could force structural changes [4]. Total debt has grown to $83.9B from $49.1B a year ago, reflecting the aggressive infrastructure buildout. The company's deeply negative Inspire Impact Score (-93) and controversies around platform harm to minors, misinformation, and content moderation failures create reputational and regulatory overhang [4].
