Investment Thesis
Meta is executing at an extraordinary level: Q1 2026 revenue of $56.3B (+33% YoY) with net income of $26.8B (+61% YoY) demonstrates that the company's AI investments are producing immediate returns through its advertising engine[1]. The AI ad creative tools have doubled adoption to 8M advertisers, Value Optimization exceeds $20B run-rate, and the Llama ecosystem with 600M+ downloads positions Meta as both an AI platform and AI beneficiary. At a PEG of 0.89 and forward P/E of 17.0x, the stock is arguably undervalued relative to its 33% growth rate.
The primary debate is whether $125-145B in 2026 capex represents visionary investment or overbuilding. Meta's history suggests the former — every major capital allocation decision (mobile pivot, Reels, AI ad engine) has generated superior returns[1]. Key risks include regulatory pressure (EU €800M fine, FTC appeal, privacy lawsuits), Reality Labs losses (~$5B/quarter), and concentration in advertising revenue (~98% of total). However, the combination of dominant market position, AI monetization evidence, and attractive valuation on forward earnings makes Meta a compelling BUY at current levels[3].
