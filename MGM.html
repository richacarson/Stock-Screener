MGM Resorts International operates casino resorts offering gaming, hotel, convention, dining, entertainment, and retail amenities across Las Vegas Strip properties, regional U.S. operations, MGM China (Macau), and the BetMGM digital gaming platform. The company manages approximately 37,000 guest rooms on the Las Vegas Strip, representing about one-fourth of all units in the market.

Investment Thesis
MGM Resorts is a global gaming and hospitality leader trading at an attractive 15.8x forward P/E with multiple growth vectors — Macau recovery driving record MGM China performance, BetMGM scaling to $3.1B+ net revenue in 2026 with improving profitability, and the Japan Osaka integrated resort providing a decades-long growth runway [1]. Q4 2025 Adjusted EBITDA surged 20% to $635M with adjusted EPS of $1.60 (vs. $0.45 prior year), demonstrating strong earnings momentum. The company commands ~25% of Las Vegas Strip room inventory and operates a customer database 'on par with any major bank,' creating a data-driven competitive moat [2]. BetMGM's transition to positive Adjusted EBITDA validates the digital gaming strategy that initially weighed on earnings. The analyst consensus price target of $42.67 implies 15% upside from current levels [1].
The bear case highlights structural concerns despite the earnings improvement. D/E of 963% reflects extreme leverage typical of the VICI Properties sale-leaseback structure, leaving limited financial flexibility if consumer spending weakens [4]. Profit margin of 1.2% on $17.5B revenue means MGM earns pennies on every dollar — well below the 20% target — and is vulnerable to cost inflation in labor-intensive hospitality operations. Las Vegas performance was mixed in Q4 2025 with key hotel metrics declining on the Strip, suggesting normalization after post-pandemic pent-up demand [1]. Consumer sentiment is falling amid inflation concerns, creating headwinds for discretionary spending on gambling and entertainment. The Inspire score of -77 (MISALIGNED) reflects gambling, sexually explicit content, and LGBT activism concerns that limit the faith-aligned investor universe [CSV]. Legacy technology systems and hierarchical culture cited by employees suggest operational modernization has lagged competitors [2]. The Japan Osaka project, while visionary, ties up billions in capital for years before generating returns.
