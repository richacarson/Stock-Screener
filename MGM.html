Company Profile
Consumer Cyclical ·Resorts & Casinos ·NYSE ·United States
MGM Resorts International operates as a gaming and entertainment company in the United States, China, and internationally. It owns and operates casino resorts including Bellagio, MGM Grand, Mandalay Bay, and The Cosmopolitan on the Las Vegas Strip, plus regional properties and the MGM China joint venture in Macau. The company also operates the BetMGM digital sports betting and iGaming platform.

Investment Thesis
MGM Resorts is executing a multi-pronged transformation from a pure casino operator into an integrated entertainment and digital gaming platform. Q1 2026 posted record quarterly revenue of $4.5B (+4% YoY), BetMGM delivered a $470M EBITDA turnaround with first cash distribution of $135M, and MGM Digital revenue surged 43% YoY [1][2]. The AI-powered MGM Rewards platform with 50M+ members is driving measurable non-gaming revenue uplift. Forward P/E of 21.2x with PEG of 1.32 represents reasonable valuation for a company with multiple growth vectors including the Osaka Japan IR (opening 2030), BetMGM guidance of $300-350M EBITDA in 2026, and international digital expansion through LeoVegas [2][4]. Management repurchased $1.2B in shares in 2025 and has $2B remaining in buyback authorization.
The bear case centers on extreme leverage and margin compression. D/E of 951% with $31.7B in debt against $2.5B cash creates significant financial fragility in a downturn — a recession would simultaneously reduce gaming revenue and increase refinancing risk [6]. GAAP profit margin of just 1.03% ($206M net income on $17.5B revenue) reflects the heavy interest burden, and Q1 2026 EBITDA declined 9% to $580M on margin compression from higher gaming taxes, self-insurance reserves, and payroll costs [1]. The September 2023 Scattered Spider cyberattack ($100M impact, $45M settlement) exposed operational vulnerability [5]. Las Vegas Strip RevPAR was flat in Q1 2026, and Macau operations face geopolitical risk from U.S.-China tensions. GAAP EPS declined from $3.49 in 2022 to $0.76 in 2025, a 78% decline driven by margin erosion and one-time charges. The Osaka IR represents $10B+ in capital commitment with no revenue until 2030 [4].
Key Catalysts
  1. BetMGM EBITDA guidance of $300-350M in 2026 with continued digital revenue growth (+43% YoY in Q1) and expanding international digital platform through LeoVegas (+35% net revenue growth) [1][2]

Key Risks
  1. Extreme leverage at 951% D/E with $31.7B debt creates existential refinancing risk in a recession — GAAP profit margin of just 1.03% leaves virtually no cushion for revenue decline or interest rate increases [6]
  2. GAAP EPS deteriorated 78% from $3.49 in 2022 to $0.76 in 2025, with Q1 2026 EBITDA declining 9% YoY on margin compression from gaming taxes, insurance reserves, and rising labor costs [1][2]
  3. Cybersecurity remains a critical operational risk after the $100M Scattered Spider ransomware attack ($45M settlement) — another breach could devastate customer trust in the 50M-member loyalty ecosystem [5]
