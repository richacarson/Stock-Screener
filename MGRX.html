Company Profile
Healthcare ·Health Information Services ·NASDAQ ·United States
Mangoceuticals, Inc. develops, markets, and sells men's wellness products and services through a telemedicine platform in the United States. The company offers erectile dysfunction products under the Mango brand, hair loss products under the Grow brand, hormone balance and therapy products under the Mojo brand, weight loss products under the Slim brand, and oral testosterone replacement therapy under the Prime brand. In April 2025, the company acquired smokeless technology IP to enter the oral pouch delivery market for stimulant and functional wellness products. It also operates PeachesRx, a sister brand for women's wellness.

Investment Thesis
Mangoceuticals is a nano-cap telehealth company ($6.2M market cap) that has failed to achieve commercial traction after three years of operations, generating just $456K in FY2025 revenue — a 26% decline from FY2024 — while accumulating $20.6M in net losses [2]. The company operates with only 3 employees and relies on outsourced pharmacy fulfillment for its men's health product lines (Mango ED, Grow hair loss, Mojo hormones, Slim weight loss, Prime testosterone) [5][6]. The core telehealth business faces insurmountable competitive disadvantages against Hims & Hers, Ro, and Amazon, which operate at 1,000x+ revenue scale with established brand recognition, physician networks, and marketing budgets. CEO compensation of $630K exceeds total company revenue, and share dilution of 376% YoY has destroyed shareholder value [7].
The April 2025 pivot into the oral stimulant pouch market through the acquisition of Smokeless Technology Corp. IP represents a Hail Mary strategic shift [3]. While the $3.1B U.S. nicotine pouch market is growing rapidly (led by Zyn's $1.6B in sales), Mangoceuticals faces an even more daunting competitive landscape than telehealth — competing against Philip Morris (Zyn), Altria, Reynolds, and other tobacco giants with decades of distribution relationships, regulatory expertise, and marketing capabilities. The hiring of ex-JUUL Canada president Tim Corkum brings relevant expertise but cannot compensate for the company's lack of manufacturing capability, distribution network, and capital [3]. The stock fails the liquidity floor test (daily dollar volume ~$119K vs. $1M threshold), making position sizing impractical for most investors. The Z-score of 1.92 indicates elevated bankruptcy risk [2]. There is no institutional analyst coverage, no forward earnings estimates, and no visibility into a path to profitability.

AI Resilience (25%)
AI Resilience MODERATE
4
AI Risk Protection (4/9): Mangoceuticals' telehealth-based business model faces moderate AI disruption risk. The core model — connecting patients to licensed physicians for prescription medications via telemedicine — retains a structural moat because prescription authority requires human physicians and regulated pharmacy fulfillment [5]. AI cannot legally prescribe medications or dispense controlled substances. However, AI-powered health platforms from larger competitors (Hims, Amazon Pharmacy) could dramatically improve customer acquisition, personalization, and operational efficiency, widening the competitive gap against a 3-employee company with declining revenue [2][6]. The compounding pharmacy model itself faces regulatory risk as the FDA has moved to restrict compounded GLP-1 and other medications. The planned oral pouch business would face less AI disruption risk as it involves physical product manufacturing and distribution [3]. AI Opportunity (0/1): No evidence of AI deployment or AI-enabled product development. The company's extremely small scale (3 employees, $456K revenue) makes meaningful AI investment impractical at this stage [2][6].
