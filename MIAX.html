Company Profile
Financial Services ·Capital Markets ·NYSE ·United States
Miami International Holdings operates regulated financial exchanges across options, equities, futures, and international listings. The company's platforms include MIAX Options, MIAX Pearl, MIAX Emerald, and MIAX Sapphire for options; MIAX Pearl Equities for U.S. equities; MIAX Futures for derivatives; and the Bermuda Stock Exchange (BSX) and The International Stock Exchange (TISE) for international listings.

Investment Thesis
Miami International Holdings is the fastest-growing U.S. options exchange operator, reaching a record 18.2% market share with 11.1M contracts daily in Q4 2025 (up 46.5% YoY) [1]. Revenue grew to $1.36B in FY2025 (3-year CAGR of 19.6%), and adjusted EBITDA in Q4 doubled to $62.2M with 50% margins [1]. The August 2025 IPO raised $396.8M, leaving the balance sheet with $478M cash against just $24.8M debt — a fortress capital position [2]. At 27.2x forward P/E, the stock prices in the transition to normalized profitability after one-time IPO-related charges. Multi-asset-class expansion (equities up 95% YoY, international up 586% YoY) diversifies revenue beyond options, and secular growth in options trading volumes (driven by retail participation and hedging demand) provides a structural tailwind [2].
The bear case centers on competition, post-IPO risks, and cultural execution. MIAX competes against deeply entrenched incumbents — Cboe (33% market share), NYSE (20%), and Nasdaq (19%) — with far greater resources, brand recognition, and institutional relationships [1]. The company's GAAP profitability is distorted by $107.7M in one-time debt extinguishment charges, and trailing profit margins remain negative at -5.1% [2]. The options exchange business is inherently commoditized — differentiation is primarily on price and speed, creating a race to the bottom on transaction fees. CEO Gallagher's $9.7M in stock sales and $1.3M from the General Counsel just months post-IPO signal potential insider liquidity harvesting [4]. Glassdoor reviews flag concerning trading operations culture with micromanagement complaints, and only 65% of employees would recommend the company [3]. At $4.5B market cap, the valuation assumes sustained market share gains and margin expansion that may prove challenging against well-resourced competitors [4].
Key Catalysts
  1. Options market share trajectory toward 20%+ as MIAX Sapphire (4th exchange) ramps and retail options participation continues to grow, driving volume-based revenue acceleration [1]
  2. GAAP profitability normalization as one-time IPO charges ($107.7M debt extinguishment) roll off, revealing underlying adjusted EBITDA margins of 50% and potentially triggering institutional re-rating [1]
  3. Multi-asset-class diversification with equities (95% growth) and international listings (586% growth) reducing dependence on the U.S. options market [2]
Key Risks
  1. Exchange fee compression from incumbent competitors (Cboe, NYSE, Nasdaq) with deeper pockets who could engage in pricing wars to defend market share against MIAX's gains [1]
  2. Post-IPO lock-up expirations and continued insider selling (CEO sold $9.7M, General Counsel $1.3M in April 2026) could create selling pressure on the stock [4]
  3. Regulatory risk from SEC market structure reforms that could alter options exchange economics, including changes to maker-taker fee models or payment for order flow regulations [2]

AI Risk Protection (5/9): MIAX operates regulated financial exchanges — a business model protected by SEC licensing, regulatory barriers, and network effects (liquidity begets liquidity) [2]. However, AI poses meaningful structural risks to exchange economics: AI-driven trading firms already account for a significant share of options volume, and AI could commoditize market-making, compress bid-ask spreads, and reduce per-contract transaction fees. AI-powered alternative trading systems could emerge to compete with traditional exchanges. The exchange model is also exposed to AI-driven shifts in market structure regulation. That said, the physical infrastructure and regulatory licenses create meaningful moats against full AI displacement [2]. AI Opportunity (1/1): MIAX's exchange technology inherently processes algorithmic and AI-driven order flow, and increased AI trading activity actually drives higher volumes on the platform.
