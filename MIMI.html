Investment Thesis
Mint Incorporation is a Hong Kong-based micro-cap ($7M market cap) that IPO'd on Nasdaq in January 2025 at $4.00/share and has since declined 93% to $0.26, reflecting deep market skepticism about its fundamentals and pivot strategy [1]. The core fit-out business generated just $3.27M TTM revenue with a -44.7% profit margin, while CEO Damian Chan (MIT-trained architect, Asia Top 40 Under 40) is attempting an ambitious pivot to AI infrastructure, robotics, and smart facility management through subsidiary Axonex Intelligence and a February 2026 Rice Robotics JV [2]. The balance sheet provides limited comfort: $4.6M cash against $1.25M debt, but with -$3.27M operating cash flow, runway is approximately one year without additional capital raises [1].
The bear case is overwhelming at this stage. MIMI faces Nasdaq non-compliance for minimum bid price, creating real delisting risk that would destroy remaining shareholder value [3]. Revenue declined 33.8% YoY with no analyst coverage, no earnings estimates, and no visibility on when (or if) the AI/robotics pivot will generate revenue. The company's $7M market cap makes it vulnerable to manipulation — The Bear Cave has flagged pump-and-dump risks in small-cap Chinese stocks listed on U.S. exchanges [3]. While the Axonex AI subsidiary and Rice Robotics JV represent optionality, the probability-weighted outcome for a pre-revenue AI pivot by a micro-cap construction company with one year of cash runway is unfavorable. Institutional investors are absent, and the stock trades with extreme volatility typical of penny stocks.
