Company Profile
Healthcare ·Biotechnology ·NASDAQ ·Canada
Milestone Pharmaceuticals is a biopharmaceutical company focused on cardiovascular medicines. Its lead product, CARDAMYST (etripamil) nasal spray, received FDA approval December 2025 as the first and only self-administered treatment for paroxysmal supraventricular tachycardia (PSVT). U.S. retail launch began Q1 2026; the EMA accepted the European MAA, and additional indications (atrial fibrillation with rapid ventricular rate) are in development.

Investment Thesis
Milestone Pharmaceuticals is a freshly approved commercial-stage biotech with CARDAMYST (etripamil) — the first and only self-administered nasal spray for paroxysmal supraventricular tachycardia (PSVT) — receiving FDA approval December 12, 2025 and launching in U.S. retail pharmacies through January-February 2026 [1][2]. The product addresses 2M+ Americans who previously had to go to the ER for PSVT episodes, a clear unmet need with effective monopoly in the niche. EMA MAA acceptance opens a second major market, and Phase II development for atrial fibrillation with rapid ventricular rate provides pipeline optionality [2]. Cash position is healthy: $106M at year-end 2025 plus a $75M RTW royalty payment in January 2026 plus $19M of additional ATM/warrant proceeds provides 18-24 months of launch runway [1][3]. CEO Joe Oliveto's ~9-year tenure and July 2025 insider purchase signal conviction. Wall Street consensus price target of $8.00 implies meaningful upside.
The bear case centers on commercial execution and ongoing dilution. 2025 revenue was just $1.5M and 2026 analyst forecasts call for losses of $44.7M with EPS of -$0.52 — meaningful CARDAMYST sell-through must materialize quickly to validate the launch [4]. The recent $52.5M equity raise was 'poorly timed and dilutive' and follows multiple smaller financings that have steadily diluted shareholders [3]. CEO sold 34,523 shares in January 2026 alongside the launch, sending mixed insider signals despite the prior July 2025 buy [3]. PSVT is a relatively small addressable market versus large cardiology categories — even at full penetration the single-product franchise has a finite ceiling. The company has no revenue diversification: a slow CARDAMYST ramp would force additional dilution. AFib-RVR Phase II results are years away.
Key Catalysts
  1. CARDAMYST commercial uptake metrics through 2026 — early prescription data from the late January U.S. retail launch and February national sales deployment will set the trajectory for the entire investment case [1][2]
  2. EMA decision on the European etripamil MAA, opening a second major commercial market and supporting partnership/royalty economics [2]
  3. Phase II readouts for etripamil in atrial fibrillation with rapid ventricular rate (AFib-RVR), which would multiply the addressable market well beyond PSVT [2]
Key Risks
  1. Slow CARDAMYST commercial uptake — the product is the entire franchise and a soft launch would force additional dilutive financings beyond the recent $52.5M raise [3][4]
  2. Single-asset, single-indication concentration risk — PSVT addressable market is finite and any safety signal or formulary access challenge would be material [2][4]
  3. Continued shareholder dilution — repeated ATM offerings, warrant exercises, and equity raises have steadily expanded the share count and may continue if the launch ramp is slow [3]
