Company Profile
Technology ·Scientific & Technical Instruments ·NASDAQ ·United States
MKS Inc. (formerly MKS Instruments) provides foundational technology solutions for semiconductor manufacturing, electronics and packaging, and specialty industrial applications. Operating through Vacuum Solutions (VSD), Photonics Solutions (PSD), and Material Solutions (MSD) segments, the company's products touch 85% of semiconductor manufacturing steps and 70% of advanced packaging manufacturing steps. Founded in 1961 and headquartered in Andover, Massachusetts.
10,200 Employees ·www.mks.com

Investment Thesis
MKS Inc. is a 'picks-and-shovels' beneficiary of the AI semiconductor buildout, with products touching 85% of chip manufacturing steps and 70% of advanced packaging processes [1]. Revenue of $3.93B in 2025 grew 9.6% YoY with semiconductor segment leading at $435M quarterly (up 8.8%), and full-year 2026 analyst estimates of $4.5B imply 16% growth as AI-driven demand for advanced chips and packaging accelerates [1]. Forward EPS of $12.15 versus trailing EPS of $4.38 reflects a dramatic earnings recovery trajectory, making the 23.1x forward P/E and 1.32 PEG attractive for a semiconductor equipment company with structural AI tailwinds [4]. The Atotech integration is delivering synergies with the Electronics & Packaging segment growing 19.3% YoY, and the company's photonics design wins for advanced lithography metrology tools position it for faster growth than the overall WFE market [1]. At $280.98, the stock has recovered 314% from its 52-week low of $67.87, signaling market recognition of the AI-driven turnaround.
The bear case centers on elevated leverage, cyclical risk, and competitive threats. D/E of 164.7% and $4.5B in total debt from the Atotech acquisition constrain strategic flexibility and consume significant operating cash flow in interest payments, suppressing profit margins to 7.5% versus a 20% target [4]. Revenue growth 5yr CAGR of just 3.5% reflects the company's cyclical exposure — the 2023 semiconductor downturn drove a -$1.84B net loss from goodwill write-downs, demonstrating how quickly AI optimism can reverse in a cyclical business [4]. Advanced Energy's AI data center power solutions and Coherent's explosive growth in datacom lasers show nimbler competitors capturing high-growth segments, while MKS's debt levels constrain its ability to match competitor R&D spending [1]. Trailing P/E of 64x remains elevated even as forward estimates improve, and any semiconductor demand shortfall could trigger significant multiple compression. China exposure creates tariff and export control risk — China-led sales have been a material growth driver that could be disrupted by geopolitical policy changes [1].
