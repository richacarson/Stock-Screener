Investment Thesis
Melco is a leveraged play on Macau's continued recovery and the return of premium-mass gaming, with 2025 revenue of $5.16B (up 11.3%) and 2026 guidance to $5.31B and Adjusted EBITDA of ~$1.3B (vs. $1.1B in 2024) [1][2]. The dividend will return in 2026, and Moody's expects meaningful leverage reduction through end-2026 on solid revenue growth and profitability [2][6]. Forward P/E of 7.07x and analyst 12-month price target of $10.10 (+81% from $5.68) reflect significant upside if the Macau thesis plays out [5][1]. Lawrence Ho's founder-led operating model is in 'full competition mode,' with City of Dreams Mediterranean in Cyprus and Studio City Phase 2 providing diversification beyond pure Macau exposure [3].
The bear case is substantial. Gaming is the entire business model — a -1 Inspire score and 'Gambling' negative attribution reflect genuine social concerns about the product. Macau concession renewal locked Melco into non-gaming investment commitments that pressure returns on capital, and China's regulatory posture toward junket VIP gaming remains hostile [1]. Profit margin of 3.58% is thin, and the absence of reported D/E and ROE data in the yfinance pull reflects a capital-intensive balance sheet still recovering from the COVID shock [5]. Competition from Sands China, Galaxy, Wynn Macau, and SJM is brutal for market share, and Manila and Cyprus properties are sub-scale versus Macau. iGaming and sports betting expansion globally could compress the long-term margin structure of land-based concession casinos.
Key Catalysts
  1. Dividend reinstatement in 2026 per management, signaling balance sheet confidence and providing a yield floor [2]
  2. Adjusted EBITDA acceleration to ~$1.3B in 2026 from $1.1B in 2024 as Macau premium mass continues to recover [2][6]
  3. City of Dreams Mediterranean (Cyprus) and Studio City Phase 2 ramp providing non-Macau revenue diversification [1]
Key Risks
  1. China regulatory risk — further crackdowns on junket VIP, cross-border capital flows, or currency controls could materially impair Macau GGR [1]
  2. Debt load — leverage remains elevated post-COVID and reduction is scheduled through 2026, leaving limited cushion if Macau recovery stalls [6]
  3. Gaming product category risk — addiction-related regulation, problem gambling lawsuits, and secular ESG pressure on casino investors limit multiple expansion [4]
