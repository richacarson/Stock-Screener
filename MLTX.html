Company Profile
Healthcare ·Biotechnology ·NASDAQ ·Switzerland
MoonLake Immunotherapeutics is a clinical-stage biopharmaceutical company developing sonelokimab, an investigational Nanobody targeting IL-17A and IL-17F for the treatment of inflammatory diseases. The company's lead program focuses on hidradenitis suppurativa (HS), with Phase 3 trials (VELA-1 and VELA-2) completed. Founded in 2021, MoonLake is headquartered in Zug, Switzerland and has no approved products or meaningful revenue.

Investment Thesis
MoonLake is a high-risk single-asset biotech bet on sonelokimab, a tri-specific Nanobody targeting IL-17A and IL-17F, with a BLA submission planned for H2 2026 in hidradenitis suppurativa and potential US launch in H2 2027 [1]. The bull case hinges on the VELA-1 and VELA-2 Phase 3 trials demonstrating clinically meaningful HiSCR75 responses in HS, supporting a viable path to FDA approval despite the September 2025 stock crash when results fell short of elevated investor expectations [1]. If approved, sonelokimab could capture meaningful share in the underserved HS market where existing anti-IL-17 therapies have limitations. At $18.34 per share and $1.3B market cap — down from pre-crash highs — the stock prices in significant skepticism, potentially creating asymmetric upside if the BLA is accepted and approved. D/E of 24.9% and $394M cash provide ~1.5 years of runway [4].
The bear case is overwhelming. MoonLake's stock crashed 80-90% in September 2025 when Phase 3 HS results fell short of investor expectations, triggering a securities fraud lawsuit alleging the company made materially misleading statements about sonelokimab's clinical prospects [3]. The company generates virtually zero revenue, lost $227M in 2025, and analysts forecast -$3.69 EPS in 2026 with no path to profitability before 2028 at earliest [1]. CEO Jorge Santos da Silva sold $2.8M in shares in April 2026, undermining insider conviction at a critical juncture [3]. STAT News reported the company explored a sale in mid-2025, characterizing it as 'a cry for help' — suggesting management itself questioned the standalone viability [3]. The $394M cash position funds only ~1.5 years of operations at $65M/quarter burn, meaning a dilutive capital raise is likely before potential launch revenue materializes. Single-asset concentration means a BLA rejection or CRL would be existential. Sustainalytics rates MoonLake's ESG risk management as 'Weak' [3]. This is a binary outcome stock unsuitable for the IOWN framework's emphasis on durable, infinite-game businesses.
