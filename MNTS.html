Company Profile
Industrials ·Aerospace & Defense ·NASDAQ ·United States
Momentus Inc. is a commercial space company providing last-mile satellite transportation, hosted payloads, and in-orbit services using its Vigoride orbital service vehicle powered by proprietary Microwave Electrothermal Thruster technology. The company went public via SPAC in 2021 at a $1B+ valuation and is headquartered in San Jose, California. It has experienced severe financial deterioration and recurring going-concern warnings.

Investment Thesis
Momentus occupies a legitimate niche in the growing space economy — last-mile satellite transportation using proprietary water-plasma propulsion technology. The company successfully launched Vigoride 7 in March 2026, was selected for the MDA SHIELD program ($151B contract ceiling), and secured a NASA Space Storms contract, demonstrating continued government interest in its capabilities [1][2]. At a $5.6M market cap, any single contract win could represent a massive multiple expansion. The pivot toward defense and government contracts under CEO John Rood (former Under Secretary of Defense) provides strategic credibility for securing classified and sensitive space missions.
The overwhelming risk is that Momentus may not survive long enough to realize any of these opportunities. The company has disclosed going-concern warnings since Q2 2023, burned $30.5M in net losses against just $1.11M in FY2025 revenue (down 47% YoY), and ended 2025 with only $12.8M cash [1][2]. A 1-for-17.85 reverse stock split in December 2025 was required to avoid Nasdaq delisting, and shareholders rejected a 10M share increase proposal, limiting future capital raising capacity [3]. The SEC previously settled charges against the company for falsifying prototype test results, and founder Mikhail Kokorich was removed by CFIUS over national security concerns — a governance history that undermines credibility [3]. Market cap has collapsed from $1B+ at SPAC debut to $5.6M, representing 99.4% value destruction. The space transportation market, while growing, is dominated by SpaceX and well-funded competitors. Without imminent and substantial revenue materialization from SHIELD or NASA contracts, equity holders face near-total dilution or delisting.
Key Catalysts

  3. Governance and credibility damage — SEC settlement for falsified test results, CFIUS-forced founder removal, 1-for-17.85 reverse split, and 99.4% value destruction from $1B SPAC valuation undermine investor confidence [3]
