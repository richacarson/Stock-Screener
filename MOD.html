Modine Manufacturing Company is a global leader in thermal management solutions, providing heat transfer products and systems for data centers, HVAC, commercial vehicles, and automotive markets. The company is rapidly pivoting toward data center cooling, which grew from 4% to 25% of net sales between FY2021 and FY2025, with a target of $2B+ in data center revenue by FY2028.

Investment Thesis
Modine Manufacturing is executing a transformative strategic pivot from legacy auto parts thermal management to high-growth data center cooling, with data center revenue growing from 4% to 25% of net sales between FY2021 and FY2025 and a clear path to $2B+ in data center revenue by FY2028 [1][2]. Q3 FY2026 results showed Climate Solutions segment revenue up 51% YoY, adjusted EBITDA of $119.6M (up 37%), and the company raised full-year EBITDA guidance to $455-475M — growth of 16-21% [1]. The AI infrastructure buildout is a secular tailwind as high-density AI chips generate significantly more heat, driving demand for advanced cooling including Modine's liquid cooling and Cooling AI Control System [2]. CEO Brinker expects 50-70% annual data center revenue growth over the next two years, and the creation of a dedicated data centers division with Art Laszlo's leadership signals organizational commitment to this opportunity [2].
The bear case centers on execution risk and valuation. At 33x forward P/E and 133x trailing P/E, the stock prices in substantial growth that must materialize — any deceleration in data center buildout or market share loss to Vertiv and Schneider Electric would compress the multiple significantly [4]. Profit margin of 3.4% is extremely thin for a company trading at this valuation, and the capital-intensive nature of the business (over $100M in capacity expansion committed) means free cash flow was negative at -$109M [4]. The planned spin-off of the automotive thermal business creates execution and organizational risk during a critical growth phase. ROE of 10% is below the 15% target, reflecting the low-margin manufacturing model. Revenue growth of 30.5% YoY is heavily concentrated in data center cooling — a single end market whose growth depends on continued AI capex spending by hyperscalers, which could slow if AI model efficiency improves faster than expected or macroeconomic conditions deteriorate [1].
Key Catalysts
  1. Data center cooling revenue on track to exceed $2B by FY2028, with CEO Brinker expecting 50-70% annual growth driven by AI infrastructure buildout and $100M+ capacity expansion [2]
  2. Cooling AI Control System — patent-pending AI technology using deep learning and digital twins to optimize data center energy efficiency, creating differentiated product offering [2]
  3. Planned spin-off of legacy automotive thermal business to create a pure-play data center cooling company, potentially unlocking valuation premium [2]
Key Risks
  1. Valuation risk at 33x forward P/E with only 3.4% profit margin — any deceleration in data center cooling demand or margin improvement would trigger significant multiple compression [4]
  2. Concentration risk with data center cooling as the primary growth driver — a slowdown in hyperscaler AI capex or faster-than-expected model efficiency gains could reduce demand [1]
  3. Execution risk from the planned automotive business spin-off during a critical growth phase, plus negative free cash flow from capital-intensive capacity expansion [4]
