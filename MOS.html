Company Profile
Basic Materials ·Agricultural Inputs ·NYSE ·United States
The Mosaic Company produces and markets concentrated phosphate and potash crop nutrients through three segments: Phosphates, Potash, and Mosaic Fertilizantes. The company operates mines and processing facilities in the U.S., Brazil, and Canada, serving global agricultural markets. It also produces MicroEssentials premium fertilizer and is expanding into biological crop nutrition through Mosaic Biosciences, which doubled revenue to $68M in 2025.

Investment Thesis
Mosaic at 12.5x forward earnings with a 4.1% dividend yield represents a cyclical value play positioned for recovery as global phosphate and potash shipments approach record levels [1]. The company's low-cost potash production ($75/ton at Esterhazy, highest output since 2019), record phosphate rock production at Miski Mayo, and $150M in cost savings achieved ahead of schedule demonstrate operational excellence. Revenue grew 14.4% YoY to $12.05B in 2025, and the Biosciences business is targeting $130M+ in 2026 with 40%+ margins. China's agreement to buy at least $17B per year of U.S. agricultural products in 2026-2028 provides a demand tailwind for fertilizer application. RBC Capital upgraded Mosaic to Outperform with a $27 target, and multiple board members purchased shares in 2025-2026 — Kuzenko ($210K), Westbrook ($108K), and Beebe ($175K) — signaling insider conviction [3].
The bear case reflects deep cyclical challenges at trough-level profitability. Q1 2026 EPS of $0.05 missed the $0.24 consensus as soaring sulfur and ammonia costs from the Strait of Hormuz disruption created a projected $250M EBITDA headwind for the phosphate segment, crushing stripping margins and forcing production curtailments at Bartow and Louisiana [1]. The trailing profit margin of 0.36% and ROE of 0.59% are near historic lows, and the 629% payout ratio is mathematically unsustainable at current earnings — the dividend depends entirely on earnings recovery. Free cash flow turned negative at -$290M as working capital absorbed $960M in 2025, and net debt increased $829M. Brazilian operations remain idled at Araxa and Fospar with no restart timeline. North American fertilizer demand declined well beyond normal seasonal softness in Q4, and Brazilian credit constraints plus increased Chinese phosphate import competition weighed on Fertilizantes volumes. If sulfur costs persist or fertilizer prices decline further, the dividend could face a cut [1].
Key Catalysts
  1. Global phosphate and potash shipments expected to reach record levels in 2026, supported by China's $17B annual U.S. agricultural trade commitment and stronger grain demand lifting crop prices and fertilizer application rates [1]
  2. Mosaic Biosciences targeting $130M+ revenue in 2026 (doubling from $68M in 2025) with 8-10 new microbial technology product launches providing high-margin diversification from commodity fertilizers [1]
  3. Sulfur price normalization from Strait of Hormuz disruption levels and $100M additional cost savings targeted for 2026, potentially restoring free cash flow and validating the forward P/E of 12.5x
Key Risks
  1. Trailing payout ratio of 629% is unsustainable — the dividend depends entirely on forward earnings recovery, and persistent sulfur cost headwinds ($250M projected Q1 EBITDA impact) or fertilizer price weakness could force a dividend cut [1]
  2. Brazilian Fertilizantes operations idled at Araxa and Fospar with no restart timeline, while credit constraints and increased Chinese phosphate import competition reduce segment contribution and strand invested capital
  3. Strait of Hormuz conflict escalation could sustain elevated sulfur and ammonia input costs that compressed Q1 2026 margins below variable costs, potentially extending production curtailments into H2 2026 [1]

AI Resilience (25%)
AI Resilience LOW RISK
8
AI Risk Protection (8/9): Mosaic's core business — mining phosphate and potash from underground deposits, processing them into fertilizer, and shipping physical product to agricultural markets — is structurally immune to AI disruption [1]. The operations require massive physical infrastructure (mines, chemical processing plants, port terminals, railways) that AI cannot substitute. Fertilizer demand is driven by biological necessity — crops need phosphorus and potassium regardless of technological advancement, and the global population continues to grow. No analysts have flagged AI as a disruption risk for Mosaic; instead, AI-driven agricultural optimization (precision agriculture) improves fertilizer application efficiency without reducing overall demand. The AI data center buildout increases demand for copper and rare earth elements, providing indirect tailwind to Mosaic's rare earth extraction partnership with Rainbow Rare Earths [1]. AI Opportunity (0/1): Mosaic does not generate revenue from AI-related products or services. The company uses standard industrial automation in mining and processing operations but has not deployed commercially meaningful AI capabilities.
