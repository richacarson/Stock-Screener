Company Profile
Energy ·Oil & Gas Midstream ·NYSE ·United States
MPLX LP is a midstream energy master limited partnership that owns and operates crude oil and natural gas gathering, processing, transportation, and logistics infrastructure across the United States. Operating through Crude Oil and Products Logistics and Natural Gas and NGL Services segments, MPLX serves as the midstream arm of Marathon Petroleum Corporation. The company operates pipelines, terminals, storage caverns, processing plants, and an inland marine fleet.

Investment Thesis
MPLX LP is a premier midstream MLP offering a 7.73% distribution yield with 12% annual growth guidance, supported by a 1.4x coverage ratio and $2.0B in annual free cash flow [1][5]. The partnership generated $12.0B in revenue (up 9.1%) and $3.5B in net income (up 9.4%) in 2025, with 2026 adjusted EBITDA guidance of $7.0-$7.3B reflecting continued mid-single-digit growth from natural gas and NGL processing capacity expansions [1]. At 11.3x forward P/E with 41.6% profit margins, MPLX is attractively valued as an essential infrastructure business that benefits from AI-driven power demand growth — the MARA Holdings partnership targets 1.5 GW of gas-fired power for data centers [2]. The $2.7B capital spending program ($2.4B growth) provides multi-year distribution growth visibility.
The bear case centers on energy transition risk, leverage, and MLP structural limitations. D/E of 180.1% reflects $26.2B in total debt, creating refinancing risk if interest rates remain elevated [5]. The 81.9% payout ratio leaves limited margin of safety for distribution maintenance if throughput volumes decline. Revenue 5-year CAGR of just 2.9% (excluding the strong 2025) suggests the business grows slowly absent acquisitions [5]. As an MLP, MPLX issues K-1 tax forms, limiting the investor base to those willing to accept tax complexity. The energy transition narrative creates long-term demand uncertainty for fossil fuel infrastructure, though natural gas is positioned as a transition fuel. Pipeline operations face ongoing environmental scrutiny and indigenous community protests that could delay or block expansion projects [3]. The MPC general partner structure creates potential conflicts of interest, as MPC could prioritize its refining operations over MPLX unitholder interests.
Key Catalysts
  1. 12% annual distribution growth guidance for at least two more years, supported by a 1.4x coverage ratio and $7.0-$7.3B adjusted EBITDA outlook for 2026 [1]
  2. AI data center power demand tailwind — MARA Holdings partnership targeting 1.5 GW of gas-fired power generation from Delaware Basin natural gas, directly increasing MPLX throughput volumes [2]
  3. Natural Gas and NGL Services segment growth — $2.4B growth capex with 90% directed at NGL processing capacity expansion to capture rising Permian and Appalachian Basin volumes [1]
Key Risks
  1. Elevated leverage (D/E 180.1%, $26.2B total debt) creates refinancing risk in a higher-for-longer interest rate environment and limits financial flexibility [5]
  2. Energy transition and regulatory risk — long-term demand uncertainty for fossil fuel infrastructure, plus pipeline permitting challenges and environmental opposition to expansion projects [3]
  3. MPC general partner conflict — Marathon Petroleum's 64% economic interest could lead to decisions that prioritize refining operations over MPLX unitholder distributions [1]

AI Resilience (25%)
AI Resilience LOW RISK
9
AI Risk Protection (8/9): MPLX's core business — owning and operating physical midstream energy infrastructure (pipelines, processing plants, terminals, marine vessels) — is structurally immune to AI disruption. AI cannot replace pipelines, fractionation plants, or storage caverns. The business is further protected by regulatory requirements, long-term contracts, right-of-way assets that are nearly impossible to replicate, and the essential nature of natural gas and NGL transportation for the U.S. economy [1]. AI actually increases demand for MPLX's infrastructure: AI data centers require massive power generation, much of it natural gas-fired, which drives throughput volumes through MPLX's gathering and processing systems. The MARA Holdings partnership to develop 1.5 GW of gas-fired power for data centers directly monetizes this AI tailwind [2]. AI Opportunity (1/1): The MARA Holdings collaboration generates revenue by supplying natural gas for AI data center power generation, creating a direct linkage between AI demand growth and MPLX throughput volumes [2].
