Company Profile
Technology ·Software - Infrastructure ·NASDAQ ·United States
Marqeta is a modern card-issuing platform that provides the infrastructure and tools for companies to create and manage customized payment card programs. The company's cloud-native, API-first platform processed $383 billion in total payment volume in 2025 and is certified to operate in 40+ countries. Key clients include Block (Cash App), DoorDash, Klarna, and a growing roster of Fortune 500 enterprises.
800 Employees ·www.marqeta.com

Investment Thesis
Marqeta is the leading modern card-issuing platform with $383B in TPV (+31% YoY), approaching GAAP profitability after years of losses — 2025 net loss narrowed to just $14M with $110M adjusted EBITDA, and 2026 guidance calls for ~$10M in GAAP net income [1]. The enterprise pivot is bearing fruit with three Fortune 500 clients signed in 2025 and average deal size up 20%+, positioning Marqeta in the $2T embedded finance opportunity. The AI-powered Real-Time Decisioning product launched in March 2026 adds a high-value fraud prevention capability that differentiates from legacy processors [2]. At $1.7B market cap (~2.7x 2025 revenue), Marqeta trades at a significant discount to its historical multiples and fintech peers, creating upside if the enterprise strategy drives re-acceleration.
The bear case centers on customer concentration, growth deceleration, and AI disruption risk. Block represents 44% of Q4 net revenue and hit a lower pricing tier in December 2025 that will reduce 2026 growth by ~3 percentage points, with the assumed loss of Cash App new issuance adding further risk [1]. 2026 guidance of 12-14% net revenue growth and 10-12% gross profit growth represents a sharp deceleration from 23-24% in 2025, and management warned that H2 2026 gross profit growth will slow to high single digits. The Forward P/E of 56x is demanding for a company just reaching breakeven. Analysts flag an 'AI Margin Pressure Score of 7/10,' warning that AI-native entrants could replicate Marqeta's API-first platform capabilities [2]. Three CEO changes in three years create strategic continuity risk. The $3.93 stock price — down from a $35+ IPO — reflects significant market skepticism about Marqeta's ability to diversify beyond Block and achieve sustainable profitability.
Key Catalysts
  1. GAAP profitability inflection in 2026 (~$10M net income guidance) after years of losses, potentially triggering index inclusion and institutional buying interest [1]
  2. Enterprise customer expansion — three Fortune 500 clients signed in 2025, 40 new logos over two years, and 20%+ increase in average deal size signal move upmarket toward stickier revenue [1]

Key Risks

  2. Growth deceleration — 2026 guidance of 12-14% net revenue growth vs. 23% in 2025, with H2 2026 gross profit expected to grow only in high single digits as BNPL and TransactPay tailwinds fade [1]
  3. AI disruption risk rated 7/10 by analysts — cloud-native API platform that was once differentiated could be replicated by AI-native entrants with lower integration friction, compressing Marqeta's pricing power [2]

AI Risk Protection (4/9): Marqeta occupies an exposed position in the AI landscape.
