Company Profile
Healthcare ·Biotechnology ·NASDAQ ·United Kingdom
Mereo BioPharma Group plc is a UK-based clinical-stage biopharmaceutical company focused on rare diseases. Lead programs include setrusumab for osteogenesis imperfecta (OI) and alvelestat for alpha-1 antitrypsin deficiency-associated lung disease (AATD-LD). The company's Phase 3 ORBIT and COSMIC studies for setrusumab failed to meet primary endpoints in December 2025, triggering a securities class action lawsuit. Market cap is approximately $39M with $36M in cash.
39 Employees ·www.mereobiopharma.com

Investment Thesis
Mereo retains residual scientific value through its alvelestat program for AATD-LD, which has active FDA engagement and addresses a $2B+ potential market with approximately 100,000 patients globally [2]. The setrusumab data showed statistically significant bone mineral density improvements despite missing the primary fracture endpoint, leaving open the possibility of a regulatory path in a defined OI subgroup. The vantictumab partnership with Ashibio provides additional option value. Cash of $36M provides runway, and zero debt means no near-term solvency risk [4].
The bear case dominates. The Phase 3 ORBIT and COSMIC studies — Mereo's primary value drivers — both failed their primary endpoints of reducing clinical fracture rates, destroying the company's most advanced program [1]. The stock has collapsed, triggering a securities class action lawsuit alleging misleading disclosures about the setrusumab program [3]. Market cap of $39M and daily dollar volume of $435K breach IOWN's liquidity floor, making the stock untradeable at institutional scale. With $36M in cash and approximately $27M in annual burn rate, Mereo has roughly 15 months of runway without dilutive financing. Analyst consensus expects continued losses through 2026, with EPS forecasts ranging from -$0.06 to +$0.04 [1]. The company is a speculative biotech bet on pipeline assets that have yet to demonstrate clinical success.
