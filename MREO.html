Investment Thesis
Mereo BioPharma is a clinical-stage rare disease biotech with a 10-year track record of pipeline development but no approved products. The company's lead asset setrusumab (partnered with Ultragenyx) targets osteogenesis imperfecta, a rare bone disorder with limited treatment options. At $0.28 per share, the $45M market cap trades below the $41M cash position, but this discount reflects valid concerns: a securities fraud class action alleging misleading statements about the ORBIT and COSMIC Phase 3 programs, and persistent cash burn of ~$42M annually.
The cash runway extends to mid-2027, but without positive clinical readouts or a partnership deal, Mereo will need additional capital raises that would further dilute shareholders. The stock fails the IOWN liquidity floor test with daily dollar volume of approximately $472K. While the rare disease pipeline offers potential binary upside if clinical trials succeed, the governance concerns, litigation risk, and pre-revenue status make this unsuitable for the IOWN framework. The forward EPS estimate of -$0.04 suggests the market expects continued losses through 2026.
