Company Profile
Healthcare ·Biotechnology ·NASDAQ ·United Kingdom
Mereo BioPharma Group plc is a UK-based clinical-stage biopharmaceutical company focused on rare diseases. Lead programs include setrusumab for osteogenesis imperfecta (OI), alvelestat for alpha-1 antitrypsin deficiency-associated lung disease (AATD-LD), and vantictumab (partnered with Ashibio) for autosomal dominant osteopetrosis type 2 (ADO2). Founded in 2015, headquartered in London, with 39 employees and a $38M market cap.

Investment Thesis
Mereo's residual value rests on two clinical assets: alvelestat in a Phase 3 pivotal trial for AATD-LD (~220 patients, 18-month treatment) that could support dual regulatory submissions, and setrusumab where the company is engaging regulators on a pediatric OI path leveraging statistically significant BMD gains from the Orbit/Cosmic studies [1][3]. Vantictumab Phase 2 with Ashibio in ADO2 expected H2 2026 provides additional optionality. Cash of $36.2M with runway into mid-2027 supports near-term operations, and a zero-debt balance sheet avoids covenant risk.
The bear case is dominant: the stock triggers IOWN's liquidity floor with only $386K in daily dollar volume versus the $1M minimum, making it uninvestable at institutional size [5]. The $38M market cap, Nasdaq compliance deficiency, and class action lawsuit (Dodge v. Mereo) create structural overhang. The Phase 3 setrusumab miss eliminated the primary value driver — the BMD data, while encouraging, faces an uncertain regulatory path. FY2025 net loss of $41.9M on $500K revenue reflects a company that is deeply pre-commercial, and the $36.2M cash position requires either a partnership, asset sale, or dilutive capital raise before mid-2027 [1][3].
Key Catalysts

  2. Positive FDA/EMA regulatory guidance on a defined path forward for setrusumab in pediatric OI, restoring partial program value from the Phase 3 studies
  3. Strategic acquisition of Mereo or individual asset licensing deals that provide non-dilutive capital and value realization for shareholders
