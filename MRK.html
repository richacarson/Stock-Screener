Merck & Co., Inc. operates as a global healthcare company, offering pharmaceutical products led by Keytruda (pembrolizumab), the world's top-selling drug at $32B+ annual run-rate. Investment Thesis
Merck's Keytruda franchise delivered $8.03B in Q1 2026 (12% YoY growth) with the subcutaneous Keytruda Qlex adding $128M in its first meaningful quarter, driving a raised full-year guidance of $65.8-$67B revenue and $5.04-$5.16 adjusted EPS [1]. At 12.0x forward earnings with $14B annual free cash flow, a 2.95% dividend yield supported by 14 consecutive years of growth and 60+ consecutive years of payment, Merck offers pharma-grade defensiveness with meaningful pipeline upside [1][4]. The post-Keytruda strategy targeting $70B+ annual revenue by mid-2030s through 20 new drug launches — including Winrevair (PAH, expanding into heart failure), Capvaxive (vaccines), three Daiichi Sankyo ADC candidates, and MK-7240 (IBD from Prometheus acquisition) — provides visible diversification drivers [1][3]. CEO Davis has invested $28B+ in acquisitions to build this pipeline, and R&D spending of $15B+/year (~23% of revenue) demonstrates sustained commitment to innovation.
Merck's Keytruda concentration risk is existential — the franchise generates approximately 49% of total revenue, and US loss of exclusivity beginning in 2028-2029 could result in significant generic erosion of the world's largest drug franchise [3]. Biosimilar developers including Samsung Bioepis, Amgen, and Indian manufacturers are advancing programs, with analysts projecting 15-30% EU price erosion in Year 1 declining to 45-60% by Year 3 [3]. The trailing P/E of 32.4x (distorted by 2023 one-time charges) versus forward P/E of 12.0x reflects market concern about the earnings cliff. Revenue growth of 4.9% YoY and 6.3% 5yr CAGR are modest for a company needing to replace $32B+ in Keytruda revenue within 3-5 years. The Inflation Reduction Act's Medicare drug price negotiation directly threatens Keytruda and other high-revenue drugs, potentially accelerating margin pressure before pipeline replacements reach commercial scale [3]. CEO Davis's net insider selling ($6M in February 2026) provides no conviction signal from leadership.
Key Catalysts

  2. Post-Keytruda pipeline of 20 new drug launches by 2030 targeting $70B+ annual revenue by mid-2030s, led by Winrevair (PAH/heart failure expansion), three Daiichi Sankyo ADC candidates, MK-7240 (IBD), and Capvaxive (pneumococcal vaccine)
  3. Forward P/E of 12.0x with $14B annual FCF and 2.95% dividend yield creates attractive valuation entry point if pipeline diversification proves successful in offsetting Keytruda LOE headwinds
Key Risks
  1. Keytruda patent cliff beginning 2028-2029 in the US threatens ~$32B+ in annual revenue — biosimilar developers project 15-30% EU price erosion Year 1 and 45-60% by Year 3, requiring pipeline diversification to replace the world's largest drug franchise within a compressed timeline
  2. Inflation Reduction Act Medicare drug price negotiation could directly impact Keytruda and future high-revenue products, compressing margins before replacement revenues achieve commercial scale
  3. D/E of 106.9% reflects financial stretch from $28B+ in acquisitions, and the PEG ratio of 5.36 suggests the market sees limited growth relative to earnings — pipeline execution risk is substantial if 20 planned drug launches underperform clinical or commercial expectations

AI Resilience (25%)
AI Resilience LOW RISK
8
AI Risk Protection (7/9): Pharmaceutical drug development, clinical trials, FDA regulatory approval, and GMP manufacturing are deeply regulated physical-world processes that AI structurally cannot substitute [1]. Drug molecules must physically interact with biological systems — no AI model can replace the $2B+ and 10+ years required to bring a new drug through Phase I-III trials and FDA approval. Keytruda's competitive moat is protected by clinical data exclusivity, manufacturing complexity, and physician trust built over a decade of real-world evidence across 30+ tumor types [1]. AI actually enhances drug discovery through faster target identification and trial design optimization, making pharma an AI beneficiary. However, AI-driven drug discovery could lower barriers to entry for competitors over time, and AI tools may accelerate biosimilar development timelines for the approaching Keytruda patent cliff — preventing an IMMUNE rating [3]. AI Opportunity (1/1): GPTeal AI platform deployed to 50,000+ employees generating operational productivity gains across R&D, clinical, and corporate functions. Mayo Clinic AI drug discovery collaboration and Merck Digital Sciences Studio incubating AI/ML startups demonstrate systematic AI adoption. Ranked #2 in Pharma AI readiness by CB Insights [2].
