Investment Thesis
Merck is a best-in-class pharmaceutical franchise at a reasonable valuation: 36.88% ROE, $65B in revenue, 14.5%+ consecutive years of dividend growth, and a 15.86x trailing P/E[1]. Keytruda will reach peak sales of ~$32.7B in 2026 before patent erosion, providing extraordinary cash generation to fund pipeline building. The company has identified $70B+ in annual revenue opportunities from 20+ next-generation drugs across oncology ($25B), cardiometabolic ($20B), and infectious diseases ($15B). Winrevair alone generated $1.4B in its first full year, validating pipeline quality.
The central bear case is the Keytruda patent cliff: IV formulation patents expire in 2028, and without successful lifecycle management and pipeline execution, Merck could face $15-20B+ in revenue erosion within 3-5 years after 2028[1]. The subcutaneous Keytruda reformulation and combination therapy expansion mitigate but don't eliminate this risk. Forward P/E of 22.55x prices in significant pipeline success that may not materialize at the scale needed. Q1 2026 EPS is projected to be -$1.49, reflecting anticipated charges. Revenue growth deceleration to 1.31% in FY2025 from mid-single digits previously signals the transition year has begun.
Key Catalysts
  1. Keytruda subcutaneous formulation FDA approval and rapid uptake shift from IV to SC administration, extending effective patent protection beyond 2028 and reducing biosimilar competition risk from the primary revenue source
  2. Daiichi Sankyo ADC approvals — patritumab deruxtecan and ifinatamab deruxtecan represent potential multi-billion-dollar blockbusters in lung cancer that would significantly offset Keytruda patent cliff losses
  3. Winrevair continued market penetration in pulmonary arterial hypertension — at $1.4B in first-year sales, successful expansion into additional PAH patient segments could make this a $3-5B peak revenue asset
Key Risks
  1. Keytruda biosimilar entry post-2028 causing faster-than-expected revenue erosion — if biosimilar competition captures 50% of market share within 2 years of entry (common in EU markets), Merck faces a $15B+ revenue gap that even the full pipeline may not close by 2030
  2. Pipeline clinical failures — Merck's $70B opportunity depends on 20+ clinical programs succeeding; a pattern of Phase III failures (as seen with some mRNA vaccine candidates) would dramatically compress the post-Keytruda growth outlook
  3. IRA Inflation Reduction Act drug pricing negotiations — Merck's Januvia (diabetes) is already under IRA price cuts, and future inclusion of Keytruda or Winrevair in IRA pricing would reduce profitability of the key growth drivers

AI Resilience (25%)
AI Resilience MODERATE
7
Pharmaceutical drug development — Merck's core business — requires FDA approval, clinical trial data, and complex biochemistry that AI cannot replace with currently available tools[1]. However, AI drug discovery is creating structural disruption in the industry: faster lead identification, protein structure prediction (AlphaFold), and AI-designed molecules (like some of the Daiichi Sankyo ADC partnership candidates) could eventually compress Merck's R&D productivity advantage. The most immediate AI risk is cost pressure: AI improves research efficiency across all players, potentially reducing the barrier for smaller biotech to compete with Merck's pipeline. Merck is deploying AI in its own R&D and manufacturing — earning the tiebreaker point. Scores 6 on AI Risk Protection and 1 for documented AI deployment in research.
