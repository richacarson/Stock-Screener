Company Profile
Healthcare ·Drug Manufacturers - General ·NYSE ·United States
Merck & Co., Inc. is one of the world's largest pharmaceutical companies, founded in 1891 and headquartered in Rahway, New Jersey. Keytruda (pembrolizumab), the world's best-selling cancer drug, generated $8.0B in Q1 2026 alone. The company is managing Keytruda's 2028 patent cliff through pipeline expansion including ADC partnerships with Daiichi Sankyo, Winrevair for PAH ($525M Q1), and a targeted $3B cost reduction program. Also operates a significant Animal Health segment.

Investment Thesis
Merck surpassed Q1 2026 expectations with Keytruda family sales of $8.0B (up 8%), Winrevair reaching $525M (up 88%, ahead of $487M consensus), and raised full-year guidance to $65.8-67.0B revenue and $5.04-5.16 EPS [1]. At a forward P/E of 12.0 with an 18.9% ROE, 2.98% dividend yield, and 64 consecutive years of dividend payments, Merck offers compelling value among mega-cap pharma. The company's GPTeal AI platform and Mayo Clinic collaboration position Merck at the forefront of AI-enabled drug discovery [2]. Multiple near-term catalysts — WELIREG + KEYTRUDA adjuvant RCC decision in June 2026, three additional PDUFA dates through October, and continued Winrevair market penetration — provide upside potential.
The central bear case remains the Keytruda patent cliff: IV formulation patents expire in 2028, and Keytruda alone generates ~$32B annually — roughly half of total revenue [1]. Even with subcutaneous reformulation extending effective protection, biosimilar competition could erode $15-20B+ in revenue within 3-5 years. The payout ratio of 93.5% is dangerously high, suggesting dividend growth may slow or the dividend could come under pressure if pipeline assets underperform. Profit margin of 13.6% is below pharma peers, depressed by $3B restructuring charges and acquisition costs. Trailing P/E of 32.2x vs. forward P/E of 12.0 reflects the market expectation of dramatic earnings improvement that depends entirely on pipeline execution. IRA drug pricing negotiations could further compress profitability on key assets [3]. The 6,000-position workforce reduction signals cost pressure that could impair innovation capacity.
Key Catalysts
  1. Keytruda subcutaneous formulation approval and rapid adoption, extending effective patent protection beyond 2028 IV biosimilar entry and preserving the $32B annual franchise
  2. Daiichi Sankyo ADC partnership approvals — I-DXd priority review and additional oncology ADC candidates represent multi-billion dollar blockbuster potential that would meaningfully offset the patent cliff
  3. Winrevair market penetration continuing at $500M+/quarter pace, with expansion into additional PAH patient segments targeting $3-5B peak revenue by 2028-2029
Key Risks
  1. Keytruda biosimilar entry post-2028 causing faster-than-expected revenue erosion — if biosimilar competition captures 50% of market share within 2 years (common in EU markets), Merck faces a $15B+ revenue gap that even the full pipeline may not close by 2030
  2. Payout ratio of 93.5% leaves virtually no dividend safety margin — any earnings miss or pipeline failure could force a dividend cut that would trigger institutional selling from income-focused portfolios
  3. IRA drug pricing negotiations potentially including Keytruda or Winrevair in future rounds, compressing profitability on the two most important growth drivers during the critical patent cliff transition period

AI Resilience (25%)
AI Resilience MODERATE
7
AI Risk Protection (6/9): Pharmaceutical drug development — Merck's core business — requires FDA approval, decade-long clinical trials, complex biochemistry, and manufacturing at scale that AI cannot substitute [2]. However, AI drug discovery is creating structural change across the industry: faster lead identification, protein structure prediction (AlphaFold), and AI-designed molecules could compress Merck's traditional R&D productivity advantage. AI-native biotech firms (Recursion, Insilico Medicine) and big tech AI labs are entering drug discovery, potentially reducing barriers for smaller competitors. The most immediate AI risk is not substitution of Merck's products but acceleration of competitive pipeline development. AI Opportunity (1/1): Merck's GPTeal platform serves 50,000+ employees, the Mayo Clinic AI collaboration targets drug discovery and precision medicine, and AI is integrated into manufacturing and quality control processes — qualifying for the AI tiebreaker [2].
