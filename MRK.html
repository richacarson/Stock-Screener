Investment Thesis
Merck represents a high-quality pharma franchise trading at an attractive 11.7x forward earnings, with Keytruda generating $8B+ quarterly revenue growing 12% YoY and management guiding to $65.8-67.0B in 2026 revenue. The subcutaneous Keytruda formulation ($128M Q1 revenue) and deep pipeline provide visibility into extending franchise value beyond the 2028 patent exclusivity loss, while the 3.05% dividend yield offers income during the transition.
The primary investment tension is the 2028 Keytruda patent cliff — management expects peak sales of $35B before exclusivity loss, requiring the pipeline (Welireg, Winrevair, oncology combinations) to backfill a potential $30B+ revenue hole. The 93.5% payout ratio signals limited dividend growth capacity until new revenue streams mature. At $111/share and 11.7x forward earnings, the market is pricing in meaningful patent cliff risk, creating a margin of safety if pipeline execution succeeds or if subcutaneous Keytruda extends franchise economics beyond initial loss-of-exclusivity timelines.
Key Catalysts
  1. Keytruda subcutaneous formulation scaling — $128M in first quarter suggests multi-billion annual run rate potential that could extend franchise beyond 2028 LOE
  2. Pipeline approvals and data readouts: Welireg, Winrevair, and oncology combination programs expected to contribute $10B+ in peak revenue collectively
  3. Raised 2026 guidance to $65.8-67.0B revenue and $5.04-5.16 adjusted EPS reflects underlying business momentum above market expectations
Key Risks
  1. 2028 Keytruda patent cliff creates a potential $30B+ revenue hole that pipeline must backfill — concentration risk is severe with Keytruda at ~50% of total revenue
  2. Payout ratio of 93.5% leaves minimal room for dividend growth and constrains capital allocation flexibility for pipeline acquisitions
  3. Drug pricing reform (IRA negotiations) and international reference pricing could compress margins on key products including Keytruda and vaccines
