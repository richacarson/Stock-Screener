Company Profile
Healthcare ·Drug Manufacturers - General ·NYQ ·United States
Merck & Co., Inc. is one of the world's largest pharmaceutical companies, founded in 1891 and headquartered in Rahway, New Jersey. The company's flagship product Keytruda (pembrolizumab) is the world's best-selling cancer drug, generating $8.0B in Q1 2026 revenue (+12% YoY). Merck is managing Keytruda's 2028 patent cliff through aggressive pipeline expansion including ADC partnerships with Daiichi Sankyo, Winrevair for pulmonary arterial hypertension, and 20+ Phase III programs targeting $70B+ in annual revenue opportunities.

Investment Thesis
Merck is a best-in-class pharmaceutical franchise at an attractive valuation: 18.9% ROE, forward P/E of 11.87, $65B in annual revenue, 65 consecutive years of dividend payments with 15 years of consecutive growth, and Keytruda generating $8.0B in Q1 2026 alone (+12% YoY) [1][5]. The company has identified $70B+ in annual revenue opportunities from 20+ next-generation drugs across oncology ($25B from ADC partnerships with Daiichi Sankyo), cardiometabolic ($20B), and infectious disease ($15B). Winrevair's $1.4B first-full-year revenue validates pipeline quality, and the $3B cost restructuring program demonstrates proactive management of the 2028 Keytruda patent cliff [1][2].
The central bear case is the Keytruda patent cliff: IV formulation patents expire in 2028, and biosimilar entry could erode $15-20B+ in annual revenue within 3-5 years [1][2]. The trailing P/E of 31.8x reflects one-time acquisition charges that inflated the ratio, but the 93.5% payout ratio signals genuine earnings pressure in the transition period. Revenue growth has decelerated to 4.9% YoY from prior mid-to-high single digits, and the 5-year CAGR of 6.3% may not accelerate without successful pipeline execution. The PEG ratio of 5.25 suggests the market does not expect meaningful earnings growth near-term. IRA drug pricing negotiations could reduce profitability of key growth drivers including Keytruda and Winrevair, compressing the post-cliff recovery potential [1][5].
Key Catalysts
  1. Keytruda subcutaneous formulation FDA approval enabling extended effective patent protection beyond 2028 through differentiated administration and reduced biosimilar competition for the primary $30B+ revenue source
  2. Daiichi Sankyo ADC approvals — patritumab deruxtecan and ifinatamab deruxtecan represent potential multi-billion-dollar blockbusters in lung cancer that would materially offset Keytruda patent cliff revenue loss
  3. Winrevair market expansion into broader pulmonary arterial hypertension patient segments — at $1.4B in first-year sales, successful label expansion could drive $3-5B in peak annual revenue, establishing a second franchise-scale asset
Key Risks
  1. Keytruda biosimilar entry post-2028 causing faster-than-expected revenue erosion — if biosimilar competition captures 50% market share within 2 years (common in EU markets), Merck faces a $15B+ annual revenue gap that even the full $70B pipeline opportunity may not close by 2030
  2. Pipeline concentration risk — the ADC partnership with Daiichi Sankyo represents the single largest post-Keytruda revenue opportunity, and Phase III failures of patritumab or ifinatamab deruxtecan would dramatically compress the growth outlook with limited alternative assets of comparable scale
  3. IRA pricing pressure expanding to include Keytruda or Winrevair in Medicare price negotiations, which would reduce profitability of the two most important current and future revenue drivers at precisely the moment Merck needs maximum cash generation to fund the transition

AI Risk Protection (7/9): Pharmaceutical drug development requires FDA approval, randomized clinical trials, complex biochemistry, and manufacturing at biological scale — AI structurally cannot replace these regulatory and scientific processes [3].
