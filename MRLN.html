Company Profile
Industrials ·Aerospace & Defense ·NASDAQ ·United States
Merlin, Inc. develops AI-powered autonomous flight software and systems for aircraft. Its main product, the Merlin Pilot, is an integrated hardware and software platform that manages navigation, communication with air traffic control, obstacle avoidance, and real-time decision-making using sensor technologies and natural language processing. The company supports both military (C-130J, KC-135) and civil aviation programs and went public via SPAC merger on March 17, 2026.

Investment Thesis
Merlin occupies a unique position as the only publicly traded pure-play AI autonomous flight company, with the Merlin Pilot platform designed to retrofit legacy military and civilian aircraft with full autonomous capability from takeoff to touchdown [2]. Revenue is projected to accelerate from ~$8.5M (2025E) to ~$32M (2026E), a 276% growth rate driven by C-130J and KC-135 military integration programs [1]. The $3B identified pipeline, $80M PIPE investment in April 2026, and $183M total cash resources provide runway and growth visibility [1][3]. Named to Fast Company's 2026 Most Innovative Companies alongside Google and Nvidia, validating the technology platform [2]. The defense AI spending tailwind, global pilot shortage (projected 80,000 pilot shortfall by 2030), and cargo automation opportunity create a large addressable market. At ~$760M market cap, the stock prices in significant execution risk but offers asymmetric upside if programs deliver.
The bear case centers on pre-revenue economics, execution risk, and binary program outcomes. Merlin burned $55M in FY2024 with only $1.2M in revenue, and even with the PIPE raises, the company has roughly 3 years of runway at current burn rates [4]. Forward P/E is negative, gross margins are -21.6%, and there is no clear path to profitability without significant revenue scale-up. Military contract timelines are notoriously unpredictable — program delays, budget cuts, or shifting DoD priorities could push revenue milestones further out. The SPAC-origin creates overhang from warrant dilution and lock-up expiries. Competition from Shield AI, Reliable Robotics, and major defense primes (Lockheed Martin, Northrop Grumman) developing their own autonomous capabilities could limit market share. FAA certification for civilian autonomous flight remains years away, constraining the commercial aviation opportunity to distant future revenue. The stock trades 12% below its 50-day SMA ($10.21), reflecting market skepticism about near-term execution.
Key Catalysts
  1. $80M PIPE investment closed April 2026 bringing total cash to $183M, accelerating C-130J and KC-135 program execution and unlocking new growth opportunities [1]
  2. Revenue projected to reach ~$32M in 2026 (276% YoY growth) as initial military integration deliveries and early license payments ramp from C-130J and KC-135 programs [1]

Key Risks
  1. Pre-revenue economics with $55M annual cash burn against $1.2M FY2024 revenue — even with $183M cash, runway is limited without successful program execution and follow-on capital raises [4]
  2. Binary military contract risk — revenue growth is concentrated in C-130J and KC-135 programs; delays, cancellations, or DoD budget shifts could eliminate near-term revenue projections [1]
  3. SPAC-origin dilution risk from warrants and lock-up expiries, combined with the April 2026 PIPE creating additional share count expansion that pressures per-share economics [1][3]
