Company Profile
Technology ·Software - Application ·NASDAQ ·United States
Strategy Inc (formerly MicroStrategy) operates as a bitcoin treasury company and enterprise analytics software provider. The company holds approximately 818,334 bitcoins valued at over $64 billion, representing about 3.9% of all Bitcoin that will ever exist. It also offers AI-powered enterprise analytics platforms including Strategy One and Strategy Mosaic.

Investment Thesis
Strategy Inc is the world's largest corporate bitcoin holder with 818,334 BTC (~$64 billion), offering leveraged exposure to bitcoin through public equity markets. The company pioneered the 'bitcoin treasury' corporate model, raising $25.3 billion in 2025 alone through innovative capital markets strategies including convertible notes and ATM equity offerings, delivering a 22.8% BTC yield in 2025 and 9.4% already in early 2026 [1][3].
However, the company is essentially a single-asset bet with no meaningful operating business diversification. The legacy analytics software generates minimal revenue, GAAP profitability is nonexistent due to bitcoin fair value accounting ($12.54B Q1 loss), and the stock trades at a significant premium to NAV that could compress as bitcoin ETFs proliferate [5]. The recent shift from 'never sell bitcoin' to active balance sheet management signals pragmatism but also undermines the original thesis [4]. At a Forward P/E of 50x and with extreme bitcoin price sensitivity (beta of 3.6), the risk-reward is challenging for IOWN's framework, which values durable, diversified business models.
Key Catalysts
  1. Bitcoin price appreciation above $100K could drive significant NAV growth and potential BTC yield acceleration beyond the 9.4% achieved in early 2026 [1]
  2. Successful execution of the 42/42 capital plan to raise $84 billion would further consolidate Strategy's dominance in the corporate bitcoin treasury category [6]
  3. Potential inclusion in major stock indices like the S&P 500 as the company's market cap exceeds $68 billion, which would drive significant passive fund inflows
Key Risks
  1. A sustained bitcoin price decline below $40K could trigger a debt spiral, as $25+ billion in convertible notes and obligations create significant fixed-income servicing requirements [6]
  2. Regulatory risk from SEC or global regulators targeting corporate bitcoin treasury structures could undermine the company's business model entirely [7]
  3. Premium compression risk as bitcoin ETFs become more accessible and cost-effective, reducing investor willingness to pay a premium for MSTR as a bitcoin proxy [4]
