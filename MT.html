Company Profile
Basic Materials ·Steel ·NYSE ·Luxembourg
ArcelorMittal is the world's largest steel producer, operating integrated steel and mining operations across the Americas, Europe, Asia, and Africa. The company produces flat products (slabs, coils, plates), long products (bars, structural sections, rails), and tubular products, serving automotive, construction, energy, and machinery industries. It also operates iron ore mining in Brazil, Liberia, Mexico, South Africa, and Canada.

Investment Thesis
ArcelorMittal is the world's largest steel producer trading at a compelling valuation — 9.3x forward P/E and 0.42x PEG ratio — with $5.4B in cash and strategic growth projects expected to add $2.1B in EBITDA including $0.8B targeted in 2026 [1][5]. Q4 2025 EPS of $4.11 TTM represents significant recovery from the 2024 cyclical trough ($1.69), and management is optimistic on 2026 fundamentals citing stabilizing markets and supportive industry policies in key regions. The stock has rallied from $27.42 52-week low to $62.41, but at 0.78x book value the market still prices significant skepticism about the steel cycle recovery. D/E of just 23.7% provides balance sheet flexibility for both shareholder returns and growth investment.
The bear case centers on ArcelorMittal's cyclicality and structural margin compression. Revenue has declined from $79.8B in 2022 to $61.4B in 2025 as post-COVID steel prices normalized, and 5-year revenue CAGR of -8.4% reflects secular challenges in European steel demand [1][5]. Profit margin of 5.1% and ROE of 6.0% are below cost of capital, raising questions about whether the business earns adequate returns through the cycle. Chinese steel overcapacity continues to depress global pricing, and tariff uncertainty creates demand volatility. The green steel transition requires massive capital investment ($10B+ industry-wide) with uncertain returns and long payback periods. Negative free cash flow of -$2.2B in the latest period is concerning despite the low valuation. The Mittal family's controlling interest (36%+) may not align with minority shareholder interests on capital allocation decisions [5].
Key Catalysts
  1. Strategic growth projects targeting $2.1B in incremental EBITDA, with $0.8B expected in 2026 from capacity expansions in India and Brazil [1]
  2. European steel market recovery supported by infrastructure spending, EU carbon border adjustment mechanism (CBAM), and potential trade protections against Chinese imports [1]
  3. Valuation re-rating potential at 9.3x forward P/E and 0.42x PEG if steel cycle recovery materializes and margins expand toward mid-cycle levels [5]
Key Risks
  1. Chinese steel overcapacity and export dumping continue to suppress global steel prices, preventing margin recovery despite ArcelorMittal's cost optimization efforts [1]
  2. European energy costs and decarbonization mandates create structural cost disadvantages versus Asian producers, compressing margins in ArcelorMittal's largest market [4]
  3. Cyclical revenue decline from $79.8B to $61.4B over 3 years may not fully reverse — secular steel demand weakness in developed markets could persist [1][5]

AI Resilience LOW RISK

AI Risk Protection (8/9): Steel production is fundamentally a physical-world industrial process involving blast furnaces, electric arc furnaces, mining, and complex logistics that AI cannot substitute [2]. ArcelorMittal's core business — smelting iron ore into steel products — requires massive capital infrastructure, regulatory permits, and physical supply chains that are structurally immune to AI disruption. If anything, AI-driven demand for data centers and energy infrastructure increases steel consumption. The company's automotive and construction end markets also face minimal AI substitution risk.
