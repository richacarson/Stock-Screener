Company Profile
Energy ·Oil & Gas E&P ·NYSE ·United States
Matador Resources is an independent E&P company focused on the acquisition, exploration, development, and production of oil and natural gas resources, primarily in the Delaware Basin (Wolfcamp and Bone Spring plays) in Southeast New Mexico and West Texas. The company also operates midstream assets providing gathering, processing, and transportation services.

Investment Thesis
Matador Resources offers deep value in the Permian Basin with a forward P/E of 6.9x, D/E of 59%, and profit margin of 20.8% — one of the most financially sound small-cap E&P companies [1]. The 2026 operating plan delivers 3% oil production growth to ~123,000 bbl/d while cutting total capex by 11% and achieving $795/lateral foot D&C costs (6% reduction), demonstrating improving capital efficiency [2]. Q4 2025 EPS of $0.87 beat consensus by 10%, and the dividend has grown from $0.10/year in 2021 to $1.50/year in 2025, reflecting management's confidence in free cash flow generation. The $1.9B Ameredev II acquisition positions Matador as a scaled Delaware Basin operator with decades of drilling inventory.
The bear case centers on commodity price exposure and leadership risk. Revenue declined 12.5% sequentially in Q4 2025 driven by lower realized oil prices, and free cash flow turned negative (-$170M) as Ameredev integration costs and elevated capex consumed operating cash flow [1]. Glassdoor reviews paint a troubling culture picture — 2.2/5 work-life balance and 2.4/5 culture ratings with employees describing CEO Foran's management as autocratic and toxic [3]. The company has no HR department, creating succession risk given Foran's central role. Net debt of $3.5B against $15M cash leaves minimal financial cushion if commodity prices decline materially. The 483-person workforce is lean but the 9.3% short interest suggests meaningful skepticism from the market.
Key Catalysts
  1. 2026 capital efficiency gains — 11% capex reduction with 3% oil production growth and $795/lateral foot D&C costs demonstrate operational leverage that should expand free cash flow as Ameredev integration completes [2]
  2. Dividend growth trajectory — quarterly dividend has increased 15x since initiation in 2021, signaling management's confidence in sustainable free cash flow generation and potential for further increases [1]
  3. Delaware Basin inventory depth — Ameredev II acquisition provides multi-decade drilling runway in the core of the Permian Basin, supporting sustained production at lower capital intensity [2]
Key Risks
  1. Commodity price dependence — Matador has no hedging program details disclosed, and revenue is directly tied to WTI oil and Henry Hub natural gas prices, which could decline materially in a global recession [1]
  2. Leadership and culture risk — CEO Joseph Foran's autocratic management style (no HR department, all decisions centralized) creates key-man risk and limits organizational scalability, with Glassdoor rating of 2.4/5 for culture [3]
  3. Negative free cash flow — FCF of -$170M in 2025 driven by Ameredev integration costs and elevated capex; if capital efficiency targets are not met, the balance sheet could come under pressure given $3.5B net debt [1]
