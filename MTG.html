Company Profile
Financial Services ·Insurance - Specialty ·NYSE ·United States
MGIC Investment Corporation, through its subsidiary Mortgage Guaranty Insurance Corporation, provides private mortgage insurance and other mortgage credit risk management solutions in the United States. The company insures mortgage default risk for lenders on residential first mortgages with loan-to-value ratios exceeding 80%, enabling homebuyers to purchase homes with less than 20% down payments.

Investment Thesis
MGIC at 8.1x forward earnings with a 60.8% profit margin and 12.6% D/E ratio offers deep value in a regulated mortgage insurance franchise with structural demand from the conventional mortgage market [1][2]. The 17.8% payout ratio provides massive headroom for dividend growth — the quarterly dividend has already grown 50% from $0.10 to $0.15 over three years. Share buybacks further enhance capital return. With housing supply remaining structurally tight and homeownership demand resilient among millennials, MGIC's insurance-in-force should remain stable even in a higher-rate environment.
The bear case centers on cyclical housing risk and business model stagnation. Revenue declined 0.9% YoY with analyst forecasts projecting only 1.5% annual revenue growth and 3.7% annual earnings decline over the near term [1]. MGIC's business is entirely dependent on new mortgage origination volumes, which remain suppressed by elevated interest rates — any prolonged housing downturn would directly impact insurance-in-force and premiums written. The company has no product diversification beyond private mortgage insurance, making it fully exposed to GSE policy changes that could reduce or eliminate the PMI requirement. ROE of 14.3% is adequate but not exceptional for a financial services company, and the innovation score of 1 reflects a company that has not evolved its business model in decades.
Key Catalysts
  1. Federal Reserve interest rate cuts in 2026 could revive mortgage origination volumes, directly increasing new insurance written and reversing the revenue decline trend [1]
  2. Continued aggressive dividend growth from the 17.8% payout ratio — management has tripled the quarterly dividend from $0.05 to $0.15 since 2022, with room for further increases
  3. Structural housing supply shortage and millennial household formation sustaining demand for mortgage insurance even in a higher-rate environment
Key Risks
  1. Prolonged high interest rates suppressing mortgage origination volumes — every 50bp increase in mortgage rates reduces purchase applications by an estimated 5-8%, directly impacting new insurance written [1]
  2. GSE reform or policy changes reducing or eliminating the private mortgage insurance requirement for conventional loans, which would be an existential threat to the business model
  3. Rising unemployment or housing price correction increasing loss incidence and severity on the $293B insurance-in-force portfolio, echoing the 2008-2012 crisis that nearly destroyed the company [1]

AI Resilience (25%)
AI Resilience LOW RISK
7
Private mortgage insurance is a deeply regulated financial product — PMI is mandated by law for conventional mortgages with less than 20% down payment, and mortgage insurers must maintain state-approved capital reserves and actuarial standards [1]. AI cannot substitute for the regulatory requirement to carry mortgage insurance, making the core business structurally protected (7 points risk protection). However, AI-powered underwriting and automated risk assessment tools from insurtechs could gradually pressure MGIC's traditional actuarial processes, and GSE policy changes could alter the PMI requirement landscape. MGIC generates no revenue from AI products or services (0 points AI opportunity).
