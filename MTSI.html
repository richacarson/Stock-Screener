Company Profile
Technology ·Semiconductors ·NASDAQ ·United States
MACOM Technology Solutions designs and manufactures analog semiconductor solutions for wireless and wireline applications across the RF, microwave, millimeter wave, and lightwave spectrum. The company serves data center, industrial & defense, and telecom markets with integrated circuits, multi-chip modules, diodes, amplifiers, switches, and subsystems used in 5G infrastructure, radar, electronic warfare, fiber optics, and AI data center interconnects.

Investment Thesis
MACOM Technology Solutions is riding a powerful secular growth wave in AI data center infrastructure, with FQ1 2026 revenue of $271.6M growing 24.5% YoY and management raising full-year data center growth guidance from 20% to 35-40% on hyperscaler AI capital investment [1]. The company's 800G and 1.6T optical components and high-speed analog ICs are essential building blocks for AI interconnects, while Industrial & Defense revenue hit a record $117.7M. The balance sheet is conservatively managed with $768M cash, D/E of 42.5%, and improving profitability — adjusted gross margins expanded to 55.9% with FQ2 guidance of 57-59% implying continued margin expansion [1]. The strategic IQE investment and STMicroelectronics GaN partnership strengthen MACOM's compound semiconductor technology position for long-term growth [3].
The bear case centers on extreme valuation and execution risk. At 126x trailing P/E and 50x forward P/E with a PEG of 2.05, MACOM is priced for perfection in a cyclical semiconductor industry — any miss on the 35-40% data center growth target or slowdown in hyperscaler spending could compress the multiple severely [4]. Profit margins of 15.9% remain below semiconductor peers, and GAAP profitability lags adjusted metrics significantly. Massive insider selling — over $175M by 10%+ owner John Ocampo and additional sales by the COO and CFO in early 2026 — signals that insiders may view current prices as stretched [4]. MACOM's telecom segment remains weak amid industry consolidation, and geopolitical risks including export controls on advanced semiconductors could disrupt the data center growth trajectory. The company's relatively small scale (2,000 employees, $1B revenue) makes it vulnerable to customer concentration risk and supply chain disruptions in compound semiconductors.

AI Resilience (25%)
AI Resilience LOW RISK
8
AI Risk Protection (7/9): MACOM's core business — physical analog semiconductor design and manufacturing — is structurally protected from AI disruption. The company's RF, microwave, and photonic components require deep physics expertise, specialized fabrication processes (GaN, InP, SiGe), and years of customer qualification that AI cannot replicate or substitute [3]. Defense and industrial applications are governed by ITAR and regulatory requirements creating additional moats. MACOM is not only immune to AI displacement but is a direct beneficiary — its 800G and 1.6T optical components and high-speed analog ICs are essential building blocks of AI data center interconnects, and management raised data center growth guidance to 35-40% specifically citing hyperscaler AI investment [1]. The primary risk is that AI chip architectures could evolve to integrate optical/analog functions on-chip, reducing demand for discrete analog components, but this remains years away. AI Opportunity (1/1): MACOM generates meaningful and rapidly growing revenue from AI-related infrastructure — data center revenue hit a record $85.8M in FQ1 2026, driven by AI hyperscaler demand for 800G optical transceivers and high-speed analog components, with full-year growth guidance of 35-40% [1].
