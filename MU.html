Company Profile
Technology ·Semiconductors ·NASDAQ ·United States
Micron Technology designs, develops, and manufactures memory and storage products including DRAM, NAND, and High Bandwidth Memory (HBM) for AI, data center, mobile, and automotive applications. Founded in 1978 and headquartered in Boise, Idaho, the company employs 53,000 people. Micron is in the midst of a historic AI-driven profit supercycle, with Q2 FY2026 revenue of $23.86 billion (up 196% YoY), HBM4 volume production underway for NVIDIA's Vera Rubin platform, and $906 billion market cap reflecting a near-doubling of stock price since March 2026.

Investment Thesis
Micron is in a historic AI-driven profit supercycle with Q2 FY2026 revenue of $23.86B (up 196% YoY), GAAP net income of $13.79B ($12.07 EPS), and Q3 guidance of $33.5B revenue with 81% gross margins that would represent the most profitable quarter in semiconductor history [1]. At 7.9x forward P/E and 0.26x PEG, the stock remains remarkably cheap for a company delivering triple-digit revenue growth — the market appears to be pricing in a cycle peak despite Micron's entire 2026 HBM supply being sold out under binding agreements [1][4]. The HBM TAM of $100B by 2028 (40% CAGR from $35B in 2025) provides a multi-year growth runway, $6.4B in CHIPS Act grants de-risk the $25B+ CapEx program, and Micron's D/E of just 14.9% with $14.6B cash against $10.8B debt provides a fortress balance sheet. The stock has risen from $300 to $803 since March 2026, yet forward multiples have actually compressed as earnings growth has outpaced the stock price.
The bear case centers on cyclicality and valuation. Memory is historically the most volatile semiconductor segment, and Micron's stock has historically peaked with margins — 81% gross margins are unprecedented and any normalization would trigger a significant correction [1]. At $803 per share and $906B market cap, the stock prices in near-perfect execution. CEO Mehrotra and other insiders sold $45.5 million in shares over the last three months, which — while conducted under pre-arranged 10b5-1 plans — signals that management recognizes the stock may be approaching fair value at these levels [5]. The 5-year revenue CAGR of only 6.7% (dragged down by the FY2023 cyclical trough of $15.5B) reminds investors that memory is inherently cyclical. Hyperscaler CapEx concentration means Micron's fortunes are tied to AI infrastructure budgets at Microsoft, Meta, Google, and Amazon — if ROI from AI investments disappoints, CapEx could be cut and HBM demand could fall below supply. Competition from Samsung and SK Hynix remains fierce, and any NVIDIA procurement shift toward rivals could reduce Micron's premium HBM share.
Key Catalysts
  1. Q3 FY2026 results expected June 24 — if $33.5B revenue and 81% gross margin guidance is met or exceeded, it would confirm the supercycle and justify further re-rating toward $1,000+
  2. HBM4 volume production ramp for NVIDIA Vera Rubin throughout 2026, with HBM4e targeting 2027, extending technology leadership and pricing power
  3. CHIPS Act MegaFab in Idaho on track for 2027 production, adding domestic capacity that reduces geopolitical supply chain risk and positions Micron for the next decade of AI demand
Key Risks
  1. Memory cycle peak risk — HBM gross margins of 81% are historically unprecedented and any normalization toward 50-60% would cause significant earnings decline and stock correction
  2. Insider selling of $45.5M in three months signals management awareness of elevated valuation, and concentrated hyperscaler customer base means any slowdown in AI CapEx budgets could rapidly reduce HBM demand
  3. Samsung and SK Hynix competition in HBM could pressure pricing and market share — any NVIDIA procurement shift toward rivals would directly impact Micron's premium revenue stream
