Investment Thesis
Micron is in a profit supercycle with record Q2 FY26 revenue of $23.86B (up 196% YoY), 74.9% gross margins expanding to guided 81% in Q3, and Q3 revenue guidance of $33.5B that would represent 260%+ YoY growth [1]. At just 4.3x forward P/E, the stock is astonishingly cheap for a company delivering these growth rates — the market appears to be pricing in a cycle peak that may not arrive soon given HBM demand far exceeds supply through 2026. The $62 billion HBM market in 2026 growing to $100B by 2028 provides a multi-year growth runway, and $6.4B in CHIPS Act grants de-risk the $25B+ CapEx program.
The bear case centers on cyclicality — memory is historically the most volatile semiconductor segment, and Micron's stock has historically peaked with margins. At $422/share with a $477B market cap, any demand slowdown from hyperscalers (Microsoft, Meta, Google) would trigger a sharp correction [1]. Rumors of NVIDIA prioritizing SK Hynix and Samsung for Rubin could reduce Micron's share of the most premium HBM market. CapEx above $25B creates operating leverage risk if the HBM cycle shortens. The stock tripled in 2025 and is up 62% YTD — momentum is extreme, and any earnings miss could cause a significant pullback. Concentration risk in a few hyperscaler customers means Micron's fortunes are tied to AI CapEx budgets that could be cut if ROI disappoints.
Key Catalysts
  1. Q3 FY26 guidance of $33.5B revenue with 81% gross margins, if met or exceeded, would confirm the profit supercycle and justify further re-rating
  2. HBM4 volume production for NVIDIA Vera Rubin ramping through 2026, with HBM4e targeting 2027, extends the technology leadership timeline
  3. CHIPS Act MegaFab in Idaho on track for 2027 production, adding domestic capacity that reduces geopolitical supply chain risk
Key Risks
  1. Memory cycle peak risk — HBM margins of 75-81% are historically unprecedented and any normalization would cause significant stock decline
  2. NVIDIA potentially shifting HBM procurement toward SK Hynix and Samsung for Rubin flagship could reduce Micron's premium market share
  3. Hyperscaler CapEx concentration — if Microsoft, Meta, or Google slow AI infrastructure spending, HBM demand could fall below supply
