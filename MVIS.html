Company Profile
Technology ·Scientific & Technical Instruments ·NASDAQ ·United States
MicroVision develops and commercializes lidar sensors and perception solutions for automotive, industrial, and defense applications. Products include MAVIN (long-range MEMS-based lidar), MOVIA (flash-based short/mid-range), and associated perception software. The company acquired Luminar Technology and Scantinel to consolidate the lidar industry.
190 Employees ·www.microvision.com

Investment Thesis
MicroVision is a high-risk speculative play on lidar industry consolidation, having acquired Luminar Technology and Scantinel to create what management calls 'the most comprehensive lidar portfolio in the industry' [1]. Revenue guidance of $10-15M for 2026 represents significant improvement from $1.21M in 2025, primarily from industrial applications and retained Luminar contracts [1]. The MOVIA-S sensor launching Q4 2026 targets the industrial market as a bridge to automotive revenue expected in the 2029-2031 timeframe. Next-gen sensors at $200-300 price points (2028 launch) could unlock mass adoption if the technology proves commercially viable. D/E of 66% is manageable and the $74.8M cash position provides a runway, but annual burn of $65-70M creates urgency [1].
The bear case is substantial. MicroVision has existed for 30+ years without achieving commercial viability, generating just $1.21M in 2025 revenue against $95M in losses [1]. The $178M market cap prices in significant hope — the company needs to execute a 10x+ revenue increase in 2026 just to reach guidance. Tesla's camera-only approach to autonomous driving challenges the fundamental thesis that lidar is necessary for ADAS. Automotive revenue is 3-5 years away (2029-2031 SOP), during which cash burn continues at $65-70M annually [1]. The Luminar acquisition adds integration complexity and dilution risk. CEO DeVos is the latest in a series of leadership changes, and the 2.6-year average board tenure suggests governance instability [2]. At $0.58/share, the stock has declined 39% over 52 weeks and risks further dilution via the ATM facility. The Sustainalytics ESG risk score of 19.46 is low risk, but fundamental business risk overwhelms ESG considerations [3].
Key Catalysts
  1. MOVIA-S industrial sensor launch in Q4 2026 could validate commercial demand and accelerate revenue toward the $10-15M guidance target [1]
  2. Quantum Leap Energy IPO spinoff from Luminar acquisition and defense contract wins could create new revenue streams and valuation catalysts [1]
  3. Automotive RFQ conversions for 2029-2030 start-of-production would validate the long-term autonomous driving thesis and de-risk the investment [1]
Key Risks
  1. Cash runway of approximately one year at current $65-70M annual burn rate — failure to hit $10-15M revenue guidance forces dilutive capital raises or ATM facility usage [1]
  2. Tesla's camera-only autonomous driving approach challenges the fundamental lidar necessity thesis, potentially rendering dedicated lidar hardware obsolete for automotive ADAS [2]
  3. 30+ year history of failing to achieve commercial scale despite multiple technology pivots creates substantial execution risk on yet another strategic transformation [1]

The defense and industrial pivot provides some insulation, but core automotive lidar faces meaningful AI-driven substitution risk. AI Opportunity (1/1): MicroVision's perception software uses machine learning for 3D object detection and tracking, and the MAVIN/MOVIA sensors feed AI autonomy stacks — the company's entire product line enables AI-powered autonomous systems [2].
