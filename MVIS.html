Company Profile
Technology ·Scientific & Technical Instruments ·NASDAQ ·United States
MicroVision, Inc. develops and commercializes lidar sensors and perception solutions for autonomous vehicles, industrial automation, and defense applications. Products include MOVIA (short-range), IRIS and HALO (long-range), and FMCW ultra-long-range lidar sensors acquired from Luminar Technologies and Scantinel Photonics. The company serves automotive OEMs, industrial equipment manufacturers, and security/defense customers.
190 Employees ·www.microvision.com

Investment Thesis
MicroVision is a lidar sensor company that has significantly expanded its product portfolio through the $33.2M Luminar Technologies asset acquisition, now offering a complete tri-lidar architecture spanning short-range (MOVIA), long-range (IRIS, HALO), and ultra-long-range (FMCW from Scantinel) sensors [1]. The Caterpillar Master Development Agreement signed June 2026 for autonomous mining hauling represents the first major commercial validation from a Fortune 50 OEM [2]. Q1 2026 gross margin improved dramatically to 39% from 7% in Q1 2025, and FY2026 revenue guidance of $10-15M (mostly H2-weighted) would represent a 10x increase from FY2025's $1.2M [1]. New CEO Glen DeVos brings 30+ years of Aptiv/Delphi automotive technology experience to drive the transition from R&D to scaled commercialization [3]. The 100+ customer pipeline across automotive, industrial, and defense verticals provides multiple pathways to revenue ramp.
The bear case is severe. MicroVision has operated for 30+ years without achieving commercial viability — TTM revenue of $1.6M against $60M annual cash burn creates a runway of 12-18 months ($46.1M cash plus $42M ATM capacity) before additional dilutive financing is required [1][4]. The stock trades at $0.28, down 84% from its 52-week high of $1.73, and a proposed reverse stock split (1:5 to 1:15) is needed to avoid NASDAQ delisting [4]. The $40.1M convertible note matures October 2026 with a $1.596/share conversion price — at current prices, this note is deeply out-of-the-money and will require cash repayment or renegotiation. The entire lidar industry faces existential risk from Tesla's camera-only autonomous driving approach — if AI-powered vision proves sufficient without lidar, the addressable market collapses [2]. ROE of -197%, profit margin near 0%, and negative forward P/E reflect a company that has never demonstrated the ability to generate profit. Insider ownership of just 1.9% provides minimal alignment with shareholders [4].

AI Resilience MODERATE

AI Risk Protection (5/9): MicroVision's lidar sensors provide the perception hardware layer for AI-powered autonomous systems — AI is the fundamental demand driver for lidar technology, making the company an indirect AI beneficiary rather than a target [2].
