Company Profile
Utilities ·Utilities - Renewable ·NASDAQ ·United States
SOLV Energy is a leading utility-scale solar and battery storage EPC (engineering, procurement, construction) provider in North America. The company designs, builds, and maintains solar energy projects exceeding 200 MWdc capacity along with related transmission, distribution, and energy storage infrastructure. SOLV has built more than 12.8 GW and manages over 20 GW under O&M contracts across 30+ states. Founded in 2008 and headquartered in San Diego, the company IPO'd on NASDAQ in early 2026.

Investment Thesis
SOLV Energy is the largest pure-play utility-scale solar and battery storage EPC in North America, riding the most powerful demand cycle in renewable energy history. Revenue of $2.49B in 2025 grew 35% YoY with 2026 guidance of $3.72-3.82B implying 51% growth, supported by an $8B backlog that increased 87% year-over-year [1]. AI data center buildouts are driving unprecedented electricity demand that flows directly to SOLV's order book. ROE of 35.3% and forward P/E of 26.7x on $1.52 forward EPS offer reasonable valuation for hyper-growth. The 4.9/5 Glassdoor culture rating and 96% employee recommendation rate provide an organizational foundation that competitors cannot easily replicate [2]. The O&M business (20+ GW under contract) creates a growing recurring revenue base alongside the project-based EPC work.
The bear case centers on margin compression and execution risk at scale. 2026 guidance implies gross margin contraction from 18.6% to 15.6-16.2%, suggesting the $8B backlog was won with thinner terms as SOLV prioritized volume growth [1]. Profit margins of 6.0% leave minimal cushion for cost overruns on billion-dollar projects. The EPC business model is inherently project-based with lumpy revenue recognition and concentration risk in any single large project delay. Tariff policy uncertainty on solar panels and equipment creates input cost volatility that SOLV may not be able to pass through on fixed-price contracts. The company's recent IPO (early 2026) means limited public market track record and potential for insider selling as lockup periods expire. NLRB scrutiny over alleged labor retaliation and below-market wages on certain projects creates reputational and legal risk for a company whose brand depends on its social license to operate [3]. At $40.65/share and $8.2B market cap, the stock has already priced in significant growth expectations.
Key Catalysts
  1. AI data center electricity demand driving unprecedented utility-scale solar and storage buildouts — SOLV's $8B backlog (up 87%) directly captures this secular tailwind [1]
  2. 2026 revenue guidance of $3.72-3.82B (51% growth) with adjusted EBITDA of $400-420M demonstrating operating leverage on the scaled platform [1]
  3. O&M business scaling past 20 GW under contract, creating a recurring revenue base that smooths the project-based EPC revenue profile [2]
Key Risks
  1. Gross margin contraction from 18.6% to guided 15.6-16.2% in 2026 signals that backlog growth came at the expense of profitability — execution risk on thin-margin mega-projects is significant [1]
  2. Tariff policy volatility on solar panels and equipment could create cost overruns on fixed-price EPC contracts, compressing already thin 6% net margins
  3. NLRB labor retaliation allegations and below-market wage controversies threaten the company's social license and could increase labor costs if regulatory action follows [3]
