Investment Thesis
Myriad Genetics is a speculative turnaround play in precision medicine with a strong pipeline — AI-enhanced Prolaris (Q2 2026), Precise MRD for breast cancer (launched March 2026), and FirstGene prenatal screening (H2 2026) — that could reignite revenue growth toward the guided $860-880M for 2026 (6% growth) [2]. New CEO Sam Raha (ex-Agilent, Illumina) brings deep diagnostics industry credibility and has articulated a 'Cancer Care Continuum' strategy targeting mid-to-long-term high-single-digit to low-double-digit revenue growth. The balance sheet is nearly debt-free (D/E 32.8%) with $150.7M in cash, providing runway for the transformation. At $5.14/share and $485M market cap, MYGN trades at a fraction of its 52-week high ($8.59) and offers significant upside if the product launches succeed [1]. The PEG of 1.24 suggests the forward earnings path is reasonably priced relative to expected growth.
The bear case is substantial and multi-dimensional. Four consecutive years of net losses totaling $868M (2022-2025) with -41.3% profit margins and -$46M free cash flow demonstrate fundamental execution failure [1]. The 13.1% short interest reflects significant market skepticism. Revenue actually declined 1.6% in FY2025 despite the company being in supposed 'growth mode.' Competitive pressure from Guardant Health, Exact Sciences, Tempus, and Foundation Medicine threatens Myriad's market position in every segment — hereditary cancer testing has been commoditized since the 2013 Supreme Court BRCA patent ruling [5]. CEO Raha is the third CEO in five years, creating leadership continuity risk just as the company attempts a strategic pivot. The $485M market cap and $5.14 share price create micro-cap volatility risk, and the company must demonstrate a path to profitability before the $150.7M cash reserve is depleted by ongoing losses [1].
