Company Profile
Technology ·Information Technology Services ·NYSE ·United States
N-able, Inc. provides unified endpoint management, security operations, and data protection solutions for managed service providers (MSPs) and IT organizations worldwide. The company's cloud-based platform enables MSPs to remotely monitor and manage their clients' IT infrastructure, secure endpoints, protect data, and automate workflows. Spun off from SolarWinds in 2021.
1,847 Employees ·www.n-able.com

Investment Thesis
N-able offers a compelling value proposition at $976M market cap: a cloud-based SaaS platform with 80.4% gross margins, $540M in ARR growing 12% YoY, 30% adjusted EBITDA margins, and a forward P/E of just 10.6x as it crosses into GAAP profitability in 2026 [1][4]. The MSP market provides structural tailwinds as SMBs increasingly outsource IT management and cybersecurity — N-able's integrated platform (RMM, security, data protection) benefits from vendor consolidation trends. Revenue of $511M in 2025 with guided $554-559M in 2026 demonstrates consistent high-single to low-double-digit growth, and the transition from negative to positive GAAP earnings could drive a rerating [1].
The bear case focuses on competitive intensity and the overhang of the SolarWinds heritage. N-able competes against well-funded rivals — ConnectWise, Kaseya/Datto, and fast-growing NinjaOne — in a market where MSPs are consolidating vendors and switching costs are moderate [2]. The company is not yet GAAP profitable with trailing EPS of -$0.09 and profit margin of -3.3%, meaning any revenue slowdown could delay the profitability inflection investors are pricing in at 10.6x forward P/E [4]. Revenue growth is decelerating from 12% in Q4 2025 to guided 8-9% for full-year 2026. The $430M debt load at 53.5% D/E creates leverage risk for a sub-$1B market cap company. AI-native IT management tools could disintermediate the traditional MSP tooling layer that N-able provides, and the company's small size ($5.18/share) limits institutional ownership and creates liquidity risk.
Key Catalysts
  1. GAAP profitability inflection in 2026 with forward EPS of $0.49, potentially triggering institutional rerating of a stock trading at 10.6x forward P/E with 80%+ gross margins [1][4]
  2. Cybersecurity module expansion driving ARR growth above the 8-9% full-year guidance as SMBs face increasing cyber threats [1]
  3. AI integration across the N-able platform enabling MSPs to automate workflows and improve margins, deepening platform stickiness and reducing churn [2]
Key Risks
  1. Competitive displacement from ConnectWise, Kaseya/Datto, or AI-native RMM tools that offer superior automation at lower cost, particularly as growth decelerates to 8-9% [2]
  2. GAAP profitability delay if revenue growth slows further or operating expenses increase, undermining the forward P/E thesis at $5.18/share [4]
  3. SolarWinds heritage creating lingering brand trust deficit with security-conscious MSPs, despite 5 years of independent operation [1]

AI Resilience (25%)
AI Resilience MODERATE
6
AI Risk Protection (5/9): N-able operates in a space with mixed AI exposure. The core RMM (remote monitoring and management) platform has meaningful protection — MSPs need integrated tooling with established workflows, compliance requirements, and client relationships that AI cannot easily replicate [2]. However, AI-powered IT automation tools from Microsoft (Copilot), emerging AI-native RMM competitors, and large language models that can diagnose and remediate IT issues pose real substitution risk for portions of the MSP workflow. The cybersecurity and data protection modules face competition from AI-native security platforms. CEO Pagliuca acknowledges AI is 'accelerating the convergence' of data, cloud, infrastructure, and security — positioning N-able to benefit but also facing disruption [2]. AI Opportunity (1/1): N-able is integrating AI into its platform for MSP workflow automation and security operations, contributing to the 12% ARR growth. While specific AI revenue is not broken out, AI-driven features are a meaningful part of the product roadmap and customer value proposition [1][2].
