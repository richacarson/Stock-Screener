Northern Dynasty Minerals is a single-asset mineral exploration company holding 100% interest in the Pebble copper-gold-molybdenum deposit in southwest Alaska, one of the world's largest undeveloped copper-gold resources. The project has been vetoed by the EPA and is currently in litigation.

Investment Thesis
Northern Dynasty represents a highly speculative binary option on the Pebble Mine's permitting outcome, with the deposit containing 57B lbs copper and 71M oz gold in one of the world's largest undeveloped resources [1]. Under a Trump administration that has shown openness to reconsidering the EPA veto and issued executive orders supporting domestic critical mineral production, there is a non-zero probability of a regulatory path forward [2]. The stock trades at $1.24 with a $686M market cap — a fraction of the deposit's theoretical value — offering extreme upside if permitting materializes.
The bear case is overwhelming: the Pebble Mine has been vetoed by the EPA, denied permits by the Army Corps under both Biden and Trump administrations, abandoned by four global mining partners, opposed by 80%+ of Bristol Bay residents and 100% of Alaska's congressional delegation [2][3]. The DOJ filed a February 2026 court brief supporting the EPA's veto, settlement talks failed in 2025, and multiple investor law firms are investigating potential claims [1]. Northern Dynasty has never generated revenue, has no other assets, and has never had the financial capacity to build the mine independently. A court decision is not expected until end of 2026 at earliest, and even a favorable ruling would restart years of permitting and environmental review.
Key Catalysts
  1. Federal court ruling on EPA veto challenge expected by end of 2026, which could either vindicate or permanently close the permitting path [1][2]
  2. Trump administration executive orders on domestic critical minerals could theoretically influence regulatory reconsideration of the Pebble project [2]
  3. Copper price exceeding $5/lb and critical mineral supply concerns could increase political pressure to reconsider domestic resource development
Key Risks
  1. DOJ's February 2026 court brief supporting the EPA veto signals the Trump administration is defending the previous determination, contrary to market hopes for reversal [1]
  2. Multiple law firms investigating potential investor claims following stock decline, suggesting possible litigation risk on top of regulatory risk [1]
  3. Zero diversification — single-asset company with no revenue, no operations, and no Plan B if the Pebble litigation fails permanently [1][2]
