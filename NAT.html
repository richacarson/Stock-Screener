Company Profile
Energy ·Oil & Gas Midstream ·NYSE ·Bermuda
Nordic American Tankers is an international tanker shipping company focused exclusively on owning, operating, and chartering Suezmax crude oil tankers. The company operates a fleet of approximately 20 Suezmax vessels primarily on the spot market and has paid quarterly dividends since 1997. NAT is incorporated in Bermuda and listed on the NYSE.

Investment Thesis
NAT offers a pure-play exposure to Suezmax crude oil tanker spot rates with an 8.0% dividend yield and 114 consecutive quarterly dividends since 1997 [1]. Q4 2025 showed a recovery with $59.5M revenue, $34.7M EBITDA, and $35,000/day rates, while the company has booked nearly two-thirds of Q1 2026 spot days at ~$55,000/day — a significant rate improvement [1]. The stock is up 63% YTD from year-end 2025, reflecting the tanker rate recovery. CEO Hansson and Vice-Chairman Alexander Hansson purchased a combined 400,000 shares at $5.70 in March 2026, signaling family conviction in the rate cycle [2]. Fleet expansion with two new Suezmax orders for 2028 delivery and a one-year contract at >$50,000/day with a major oil company provide near-term visibility.
The bear case is formidable. NAT's full-year 2025 revenue collapsed 47% to $183.3M from $349.7M in 2024, demonstrating the extreme cyclicality that makes this business nearly impossible to value with confidence [1]. The trailing P/E of 95.5x and dividend payout ratio of 971% are deeply unsustainable — the company is paying dividends far in excess of earnings, effectively returning capital rather than distributing profits [4]. The $5.73 share price sits above the $2.67 DCF fair value estimated by Simply Wall St, suggesting the current price bakes in the rate recovery. Both Sustainalytics ('Weak' management) and MSCI ('Poor' industry score) rate NAT's ESG risk management negatively, and the fleet age of ~13 years is among the highest in the peer group [3]. The long-term structural risk of declining global oil demand from energy transition threatens the entire business model over a 10-20 year horizon. Jim Cramer has characterized NAT as a 'play with house money' stock, reflecting its speculative rather than investment-grade nature [2].
Key Catalysts
  1. Suezmax spot rate recovery — nearly two-thirds of Q1 2026 days booked at ~$55,000/day vs. $35,000/day in Q4 2025, with bookings extending into Q2 2026 [1]
  2. Fleet expansion — two new Suezmax vessels under construction for 2028 delivery and two recent acquisitions expand capacity into a tight vessel supply market [1]
  3. Insider buying — Hansson family purchased 400,000 shares at $5.70 in March 2026, the strongest conviction signal from management [2]
Key Risks
  1. Extreme earnings cyclicality — revenue swung from $392M (2023) to $350M (2024) to $183M (2025), demonstrating that tanker spot rates can halve in a single year, making earnings essentially unpredictable [1][4]
  2. Unsustainable dividend — 971% payout ratio on trailing earnings means the $0.68/share annual dividend is being funded from cash reserves rather than earnings, raising risk of a dividend cut if rates don't recover sustainably [1]
  3. Long-term energy transition risk — declining global oil demand over 10-20 years from electrification and renewable energy threatens the structural relevance of crude oil tanker shipping [3]
