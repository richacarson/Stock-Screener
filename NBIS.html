Company Profile
Technology ·Cloud Computing / AI Infrastructure ·NASDAQ ·Netherlands
Nebius Group N.V. is a full-stack AI cloud infrastructure company that builds large-scale GPU clusters and operates an AI-native cloud platform for training, deploying, and running AI models. Originally Yandex N.V., the company sold its Russian assets for $5.4 billion in 2024 and rebranded under founder Arkady Volozh's leadership. Nebius operates data centers across Europe and North America, backed by a $2 billion NVIDIA investment and multi-billion dollar agreements with Meta Platforms and Microsoft. The company also owns TripleTen (edtech) and Avride (autonomous driving).

Investment Thesis
Nebius is one of the purest plays on AI infrastructure outside of NVIDIA itself. Q1 2026 revenue of $399M (684% YoY) and adjusted EBITDA of $130M demonstrate that the company has crossed from speculative to operationally validated [1]. The $45B backlog with Meta and Microsoft, NVIDIA's $2B investment and priority Vera Rubin access, and 2026 guidance of $3.0-3.4B revenue with 40% EBITDA margins provide rare visibility for a hypergrowth company. Founder Arkady Volozh's 12.5% stake (~$4.9B) and the extraordinary Yandex-to-Nebius transformation validate leadership commitment [2]. At a PEG of 0.63, the stock is attractively priced relative to its growth trajectory despite the headline forward P/E of 547x.
The bear case centers on capital intensity and valuation risk. Nebius is guiding for $20-25B in 2026 CapEx while operating near breakeven on continuing operations, requiring continuous capital raises that dilute shareholders [1]. The $9.5B in debt against $9.3B cash leaves a thin liquidity cushion relative to the investment program. Customer concentration is extreme — Meta and Microsoft represent the bulk of the $45B backlog, and loss or delay in either contract would be devastating. The 547x forward P/E and $50B market cap on $878M TTM revenue prices in flawless execution with zero room for disappointment. Significant insider selling ($100M+ in May 2026) raises questions about whether insiders believe the current valuation is sustainable. The company's Yandex heritage still creates geopolitical friction in government contract bids and ESG evaluations [3].
Key Catalysts
  1. 2026 revenue trajectory hitting $3B+ and adjusted EBITDA margin reaching 40% would validate the business model and likely trigger institutional re-rating and index inclusion
  2. NVIDIA Vera Rubin platform priority access in late 2026 provides next-generation hardware advantage, potentially widening the gap against competing neoclouds like CoreWeave
  3. European sovereign cloud expansion — EU data residency requirements create structural demand that US hyperscalers struggle to serve, positioning Nebius for government and regulated-industry wins
Key Risks
  1. Capital structure risk: $20-25B 2026 CapEx against near-breakeven operations requires additional capital raises that will dilute shareholders, with convertible notes already adding dilution overhang
  2. Customer concentration: Meta and Microsoft anchor the $45B backlog; loss, delay, or renegotiation of either contract would materially impair revenue guidance and investor confidence
  3. Execution risk at unprecedented scale: building gigawatt-scale data centers across multiple geographies simultaneously while managing GPU supply chains, power procurement, and 1,500+ employee scaling creates compounding operational complexity
