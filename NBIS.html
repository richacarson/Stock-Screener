Company Profile
Technology ·Cloud Computing / AI Infrastructure ·NASDAQ ·Netherlands
Nebius Group N.V. is a full-stack AI cloud infrastructure company that builds large-scale GPU clusters and operates an AI-native cloud platform for training, deploying, and running AI models and applications. Originally rebranded from Yandex N.V. after selling its Russian assets for $5.4 billion in 2024, the company operates data centers across Europe and North America, backed by a $2 billion NVIDIA investment and a landmark multi-billion dollar agreement with Meta Platforms signed in March 2026.

Investment Thesis
Nebius is the purest play on AI infrastructure outside of NVIDIA itself — a company growing revenue 479% YoY, backed by NVIDIA ($2B investment), contracted to supply Meta (multi-billion dollar deal), and targeting $3–3.4B in 2026 revenue with 40% adjusted EBITDA margins by year-end. The founder-led transformation from Yandex into a Western AI cloud is validated by the pedigree of its investors and customers. With analysts targeting $168 average price and 'Strong Buy' consensus, the market sees a path to $7–9B in ARR by end-2026 [1][2].
The bear case is significant: Nebius is guiding for $16–20B in 2026 CapEx while remaining EBIT-negative, requiring continuous capital raises that dilute shareholders. The $4.3B convertible note raise already dilutes equity holders substantially. The company's 11-month operating history in its current form means management has never navigated a downturn or a GPU shortage. The P/E ratio of 256x on a loss-making company reflects speculative pricing, and any delay in the Meta contract or NVIDIA GPU shipments could trigger severe stock volatility — the 52-week range of $0.63 to $14.65 (pre-surge) illustrates this volatility risk [1].
Key Catalysts
  1. 2026 revenue hitting $3B+ and adjusted EBITDA margin reaching 40% would validate the business model and likely trigger significant re-rating by institutional investors
  2. NVIDIA Vera Rubin platform priority access in late 2026 provides competitive hardware advantage and customer pipeline visibility for 2027
  3. European sovereign cloud expansion — EU data residency laws create a structural moat that US hyperscalers struggle to replicate, driving enterprise healthcare and financial services customer wins
Key Risks
  1. Capital structure risk: $16–20B 2026 CapEx funded only 60% by existing cash and commitments requires additional capital raises that will dilute current shareholders at potentially unfavorable terms
  2. Customer concentration: the Meta agreement and NVIDIA relationship represent massive client concentration; loss or delay in either would materially impair 2026 revenue guidance
  3. Execution risk: building data centers at gigawatt scale across multiple geographies simultaneously, with >2,000 contracted staff and supply chain dependencies, creates significant operational execution risk
