Company Profile
Financial Services ·Financial Data & Stock Exchanges ·NASDAQ ·United States
Nasdaq, Inc. operates as a global technology company serving capital markets and other industries. It operates through three segments: Capital Access Platforms (listings, index, workflow and insights), Financial Technology (anti-financial crime technology via Adenza, regulatory technology, and capital markets technology), and Market Services (equity and derivatives trading, clearing, and settlement). Serves approximately 6,000 clients across 50+ countries.

Investment Thesis
Nasdaq is executing a successful transformation from exchange operator to technology company, with Q1 2026 delivering $1.4B net revenue (up 14% YoY), non-GAAP EPS of $0.96 (up 22%), and all three divisions posting double-digit growth [1]. The Financial Technology segment ($517M, up 20%) is the growth engine, driven by Adenza cross-selling into the existing 6,000+ client base. ARR of $3.2B growing 13% provides revenue visibility and predictability [1]. Forward P/E of 18.6 is reasonable for a company with 35.3% profit margins, 16.2% ROE, and structural competitive advantages including regulatory moats, brand recognition, and network effects. The 14-year dividend growth streak (10.2% 5yr CAGR) and 32.5% payout ratio demonstrate capital allocation discipline alongside growth investment.
The primary risk is execution of the Adenza integration and deleveraging from $9.5B in acquisition debt against only $531M cash and $1.56B annual free cash flow [5]. While the 79.1% D/E is manageable, interest expense pressures earnings growth during a rising-rate environment. The Forward PEG of 1.92 suggests the stock is not cheap relative to its growth rate, leaving limited margin of safety if organic growth decelerates from the current 13% pace [5]. Market Services revenue (trading and clearing) remains tied to market activity levels, introducing cyclicality that could mask weakness in more strategic segments during quiet markets. Additionally, the financial technology market is increasingly competitive, with Bloomberg, Refinitiv, and AI-powered fintech startups all targeting the same regulatory and compliance spending pool that Adenza addresses.
Key Catalysts
  1. Adenza cross-sell acceleration into the existing 6,000+ client base driving Financial Technology segment growth above 20% as anti-financial crime and regulatory technology adoption increases
  2. ARR growth above 13% compounding into a $3.5B+ recurring revenue base by year-end 2026, supporting multiple expansion as investors recognize the recurring revenue quality
  3. AI-driven product enhancements across surveillance, compliance, and market analytics platforms, positioning Nasdaq to capture growing regulatory technology spending globally
Key Risks
  1. Adenza integration execution risk with $9.5B in acquisition debt requiring deleveraging from $1.56B annual free cash flow, limiting strategic flexibility and making the company sensitive to interest rate movements
  2. Forward PEG of 1.92 provides limited margin of safety — any deceleration in organic revenue growth from the current 13% pace could trigger multiple compression from the 18.6x forward P/E
  3. Competitive pressure from Bloomberg, Refinitiv/LSEG, and AI-powered fintech startups targeting the same regulatory compliance and financial technology spending pool that Adenza serves

AI Resilience LOW RISK
8
AI Risk Protection (7/9): Financial market infrastructure is deeply protected from AI disruption through regulatory moats, institutional trust, and network effects that AI cannot replicate [5]. Stock exchange operations, clearing and settlement, and market surveillance require regulatory licenses in every jurisdiction and represent essential infrastructure that AI cannot substitute. The Nasdaq exchange handles trillions in daily trading volume through systems that require regulatory approval to modify. The Financial Technology segment (anti-financial crime, regulatory compliance) actually benefits from AI as regulatory complexity increases demand for Nasdaq's surveillance and compliance tools [1]. However, some data analytics and workflow products face competition from AI-enabled fintech startups that could offer faster, cheaper alternatives to legacy market technology. AI Opportunity (1/1): Nasdaq serves as critical infrastructure for AI companies to list, trade, and access capital — the Nasdaq-100 index captures the core AI ecosystem. Index products ($220M Q1 2026 revenue, up 14%) generate licensing fees from AI-related ETFs [1]. The company is actively building AI capabilities across its surveillance, compliance, and analytics platforms.
