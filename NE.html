Company Profile
Energy ·Oil & Gas Drilling ·NYSE ·United States
Noble Corporation is an offshore drilling contractor operating a fleet of mobile offshore drilling units (floaters and jackups) for the oil and gas industry worldwide. The company serves clients in Africa, Far East Asia, the North Sea, Oceania, South America, and the U.S. Gulf of America, with a $7.5B contract backlog providing multi-year revenue visibility.

Investment Thesis
Noble Corporation offers compelling offshore drilling exposure with a $7.5B backlog providing multi-year revenue visibility and a clear path to $1.3B EBITDA and $600M free cash flow by 2027 [1]. The D/E ratio of 46.9% is conservative for the sector, and the fleet of 15 tier-one drillships positions Noble well in a tightening offshore supply environment where backlog grew 30% YoY even as Brent crude fell 15% [1]. The forward P/E of 20.8x is reasonable given the earnings recovery trajectory, and the 4.1% dividend yield provides income while waiting for the ramp.
The bear case centers on cyclicality and near-term earnings weakness. Revenue declined 20% YoY in the latest quarter due to rig transitions and scheduled downtime, and profit margins of 7% are well below what the backlog implies [4]. The 148% payout ratio is unsustainable — the dividend either gets cut or earnings must recover significantly. Offshore drilling remains tied to oil prices and E&P capital budgets, which are volatile and subject to energy transition pressures. The 2020 bankruptcy is a reminder that this is a boom-bust industry. ESG pressures could constrain financing and raise compliance costs, with Noble's 20% carbon-intensity reduction target by 2030 adding execution risk [3].
Key Catalysts
  1. $7.5B backlog with $2.3B rolling into 2026 revenue provides near-term earnings visibility and supports recovery to targeted $1.3B EBITDA by 2027 [1]
  2. Tightening offshore rig supply — fleet additions are minimal industry-wide, giving Noble pricing power on contract renewals as 13 of 15 drillships target full utilization [1]
  3. AI data center boom driving incremental global electricity demand, supporting long-term oil and gas exploration activity in deepwater basins [1]
Key Risks
  1. Oil price volatility and E&P budget cuts — a sustained drop in Brent below $60 could trigger contract deferrals and lower day rates, compressing margins further from the current 7% profit margin [4]
  2. Dividend sustainability — the 148% payout ratio is unsustainable at current earnings; a dividend cut would damage investor confidence and the stock price [4]
  3. ESG and regulatory pressure on offshore drilling could constrain financing access, increase compliance costs, and limit the company's ability to win contracts with ESG-focused operators [3]
