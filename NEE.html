Company Profile
Utilities ·Utilities - Regulated Electric ·NYSE ·United States
NextEra Energy is the world's largest publicly traded electric utility by market capitalization, operating through two segments: Florida Power & Light (FPL), a regulated utility serving ~6 million customer accounts, and NextEra Energy Resources (NEER), the world's largest generator of wind and solar power. The company operates ~36 GW of net generating capacity across renewables, nuclear, natural gas, and battery storage, with a 30 GW renewables backlog.

Investment Thesis
NextEra Energy is the dominant clean energy franchise — the world's largest publicly traded utility and the world's largest generator of wind and solar power — positioned at the intersection of two secular megatrends: the energy transition and AI-driven electricity demand. Adjusted EPS grew 8.2% to $3.71 in 2025, with management targeting at least 8% EPS CAGR through 2035 and 10% annual dividend growth through 2026 [1]. The December 2025 landmark partnership with Google Cloud to develop gigawatt-scale data center campuses transforms NextEra from an energy seller into an integrated energy-technology infrastructure provider, with 3.5 GW of existing Google deals and additional campuses under development [2]. The 30 GW renewables backlog (record 13.5 GW added in 2025) and FPL's $4–5B annual storm resilience capex provide multi-year earnings visibility. At 21.1x forward earnings with a 2.7% yield, 10.1% 5yr dividend CAGR, and 25% profit margins, NEE offers rare growth-at-a-reasonable-price in the utility sector [5].
The bear case centers on policy risk, interest rate sensitivity, and execution complexity. IRA tax credit uncertainties could impact 10–15% of planned projects, with 40% of the 2026–2028 pipeline remaining exposed to potential phase-outs that would raise project costs 15–25% [3]. Rising interest rates disproportionately impact capital-intensive utilities — NEE's 146% D/E ratio makes financing costs a material earnings sensitivity. The $1.2B Mountain Valley Pipeline impairment and $350M antitrust lawsuit for allegedly obstructing a competitor's clean energy project highlight governance risks [3]. ROE of 8.4% is below the 15% target, reflecting the capital-intensive nature of the business. Revenue of $27.4B includes energy trading gains that may not recur, making the 20.7% YoY growth potentially misleading. The NextEra Energy Partners (NEP) subsidiary carries additional leverage and complexity. Local opposition to solar, battery, and wind projects could slow permitting, while wind turbine collapses have generated negative publicity and potential litigation exposure [3].
Key Catalysts
  1. Google Cloud landmark partnership developing gigawatt-scale data center campuses with accompanying generation and capacity — building on 3.5 GW of existing deals and representing a new integrated energy-technology business model [2]
  2. 30 GW renewables backlog with record 13.5 GW added in 2025 and regulated transmission growing >20% CAGR through 2032, providing exceptional multi-year earnings visibility in a sector typically offering low single-digit growth [1]
  3. AI-driven electricity demand — data centers driving roughly one-third of U.S. power demand growth, with North American power demand expected to grow 50%+ over 15 years, directly benefiting NextEra's generation and transmission infrastructure [2]
Key Risks
  1. IRA tax credit policy risk — potential phase-outs or modifications could impact 40% of the 2026–2028 project pipeline, raising costs 15–25% on uncontracted capacity and compressing renewable project returns that currently depend on tax credits for 20% of IRRs [3]
  2. Interest rate sensitivity — 146% D/E ratio and capital-intensive utility business model make financing costs a material earnings driver; sustained high rates would compress spreads between project returns and capital costs
  3. Governance and litigation exposure — $1.2B Mountain Valley Pipeline impairment, $350M antitrust lawsuit, investigations into political donations related to Jacksonville utility bid, and wind turbine collapse investigations create headline and financial risk [3]
