Investment Thesis
NextEra Energy is uniquely positioned at the intersection of renewable energy and AI-driven electricity demand growth. The 30 GW renewable backlog, Google Cloud AI partnership, and $90-100 billion infrastructure investment plan through 2032 provide exceptional long-term growth visibility. The company's 8%+ adjusted EPS CAGR through 2032 (potentially extending through 2035) is best-in-class for utilities, supported by FPL's regulated earnings growth and record renewable origination. The 2.8% dividend yield with 10% growth CAGR through 2026 and 30 consecutive years of increases provides compelling total return potential.
The massively negative free cash flow of -$15.2 billion reflects the enormous capital requirements of the growth plan, requiring sustained access to capital markets at favorable rates. The 146% debt-to-equity ratio adds leverage risk in a rising rate environment. Several legal controversies, including the $350 million antitrust lawsuit and investigations into political donations related to the Jacksonville utility bid, create governance overhang. The lobbying against rooftop solar policies and wind turbine collapse investigations suggest operational challenges. At 20.4x forward earnings, the stock is priced as a growth stock rather than a utility, leaving limited margin for execution missteps.
