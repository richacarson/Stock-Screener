Investment Thesis
NextEra Energy sits at the intersection of the two most powerful secular trends in energy: clean energy transition and AI-driven electricity demand. The company's adjusted EPS grew 8.2% in FY2025 to $3.71, with management guiding for $3.92-$4.02 in 2026 and an 8%+ EPS CAGR through 2032 [1]. The landmark Google Cloud partnership for GW-scale data center campuses, building on 3.5 GW of existing energy deals, positions NEE as the go-to energy infrastructure partner for hyperscalers [2]. With 55 consecutive years of dividend payments, 30 years of growth, and ~10% annual dividend growth guidance through 2026, the company offers rare combination of growth and income at a forward P/E of 20.4 [1][4].
The bear case hinges on NEE's premium valuation for a utility — at 27x trailing P/E and 20.4x forward P/E, any deceleration in the AI data center buildout narrative would compress multiples significantly [1]. D/E of 146% reflects substantial leverage that could become problematic in a rising rate environment, and ROE of 8.4% is below average, typical for regulated utilities but a drag on capital efficiency [1]. Political and regulatory risks include the $350M antitrust lawsuit, investigation into political donations tied to the Jacksonville utility bid, and community opposition to solar/battery projects [3]. NextEra's renewable portfolio faces execution risk as it scales from GW to multi-GW deployments, with permitting delays and supply chain constraints potentially limiting growth.
