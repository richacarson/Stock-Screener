Company Profile
Utilities ·Utilities - Regulated Electric ·NYSE ·United States
NextEra Energy, Inc. generates, stores, transmits, distributes, and sells electric power to retail and wholesale customers in North America. Operates through Florida Power & Light Company (FPL), the largest electric utility in Florida serving approximately 6 million customer accounts, and NextEra Energy Resources (NEER), the world's largest generator of wind and solar energy. Generates electricity from wind, solar, nuclear, natural gas, and other clean energy sources.

Investment Thesis
NextEra Energy is the premier clean energy utility, combining FPL's regulated earnings stability (6 million Florida customer accounts) with Energy Resources' record 33 GW contracted renewables backlog, the largest in company history [1][2]. Q1 2026 adjusted EPS of $1.09 beat consensus by 12.4%, and management targets the high end of its $3.92-$4.02 full-year 2026 EPS guidance with 8%+ CAGR through 2032 [1]. The AI data center megatrend is a structural tailwind — multi-gigawatt deals with tech companies (including an 860 MW Google agreement) position NEE as the energy backbone of AI growth [2][3]. The 30-year dividend growth streak (10.1% 5yr CAGR) with a sustainable 58.9% payout ratio and 2.89% yield make this a core dividend growth holding. Forward P/E of 19.6 is reasonable for a company with this growth visibility and defensive characteristics.
The bear case centers on leverage, valuation, and cultural risk. D/E of 156.7% reflects $104.4B total debt against equity, making NEE sensitive to interest rate movements and capital market conditions — a prolonged high-rate environment would pressure interest expense and reduce the attractiveness of the yield [5]. Revenue growth of 7.3% YoY and 9.4% 3yr CAGR, while solid for a utility, undershoot the 10%+ threshold, and the Forward PEG of 1.93 suggests limited margin of safety at current valuation levels. Glassdoor reviews describing 'absolutely terrible' senior management and an 'old school good old boy culture' reveal significant cultural rot beneath the visionary clean energy narrative, creating execution and retention risk [4]. Additionally, ESG controversies including opposition to solar projects, lobbying against rooftop solar, and a class-action lawsuit over wind and drilling operations in Florida could create reputational headwinds [4]. Insider selling of $3.7M by officers in Q1 2026 is a cautionary signal.
Key Catalysts
  1. Record 33 GW contracted renewables backlog converting to revenue as projects are completed, with AI data center demand accelerating new contract signings beyond the current pipeline
  2. Management guidance for 8%+ adjusted EPS CAGR through 2032 (and through 2035), supported by FPL's regulated rate base growth and Energy Resources' backlog execution
  3. AI Infrastructure Partnership participation and multi-gigawatt tech company contracts (860 MW Google deal) positioning NEE as the primary clean energy supplier for the fastest-growing electricity demand segment
Key Risks
  1. Elevated D/E of 156.7% ($104.4B total debt) creates interest rate sensitivity — any sustained rate increase would pressure interest expense and potentially slow the renewables development pace that drives growth
  2. Cultural disconnect between the visionary clean energy mission and employee experience ('absolutely terrible' management, 'old school good old boy culture' on Glassdoor) creates execution and talent retention risk
  3. Regulatory and ESG headwinds including opposition to utility-scale solar projects, lobbying against rooftop solar policies, and environmental class-action lawsuits that could slow permitting and project development
