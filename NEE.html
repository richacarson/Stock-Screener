Investment Thesis
NextEra Energy is the premier utility play on the AI electricity supercycle, with 15-30GW of data center power capacity contracted with Google, Meta, and Exxon Mobil through 2035. As the world's largest renewable energy generator with 30GW of renewables in backlog, NEE is uniquely positioned at the intersection of decarbonization and AI infrastructure buildout. Adjusted EPS of $3.71 in 2025 grew 8%+ YoY with 2026 guidance of $3.92-$4.02 and a 8%+ growth CAGR targeted through 2035. The 30-year dividend growth streak with 10% annual increases and a $90-100B investment plan through 2032 provide visible, funded growth. The 2.8% yield is attractive for a utility with 8%+ earnings growth, and the +44 Inspire score supports faith-aligned investing.
The bear case centers on leverage, interest rate sensitivity, and execution risk. D/E of 146.2% reflects the massive capital deployment required for the renewable buildout, and rising interest rates directly pressure both borrowing costs and utility valuations. ROE of 8.4% is mediocre for the premium P/E of 27x, and the 68.7% payout ratio leaves limited flexibility for unexpected capital needs. The $90-100B investment plan requires sustained capital markets access and regulatory cooperation — any disruption could derail the growth trajectory. The nuclear restart (Duane Arnold) introduces operational risk in a technology NEE hasn't operated for years. Revenue of $27B is expected to grow to $42B by 2030, requiring flawless execution on a massive project pipeline. Political risk from renewable energy policy changes could slow permitting and interconnection queues.

Key Catalysts
  1. AI data center power demand acceleration — 15-30GW contracted with Google, Meta, and Exxon Mobil, with electricity demand projected to grow 25% by 2030, providing secular growth beyond traditional utility rate base expansion
  2. Google nuclear PPA for Duane Arnold Energy Center restart, creating a blueprint for additional nuclear capacity additions that could accelerate clean baseload power deployment for AI hyperscalers
  3. 30GW renewable backlog conversion to operational capacity driving 8%+ annual EPS growth through 2032, with $90-100B investment plan providing funded visibility into decade-long earnings trajectory
Key Risks
  1. Interest rate sensitivity — at 146% D/E, NEE's borrowing costs and equity valuation are highly leveraged to rate movements, and sustained higher rates could compress the premium multiple and increase project financing costs across the $90-100B plan
  2. Renewable project execution risk — supply chain constraints, permitting delays, and interconnection queue backlogs could slow the conversion of the 30GW backlog to operational capacity, undermining the 8%+ growth trajectory
  3. Political and regulatory risk — changes in renewable energy tax credits (IRA), state-level permitting restrictions, or shifts in federal energy policy could reduce project economics and slow new development activity
