Company Profile
Basic Materials ·Gold ·NYSE ·United States
Newmont Corporation is the world's largest gold producer, also exploring for copper, silver, lead, zinc, and other metals. The company has operations and assets in the United States, Papua New Guinea, Australia, Ghana, Suriname, Argentina, Dominican Republic, Chile, Peru, Ecuador, Mexico, and Canada. Founded in 1916 and headquartered in Denver, Colorado.

Investment Thesis
Newmont is the world's largest gold producer riding a historic gold price cycle, delivering extraordinary financial results — Q1 2026 revenue of $7.31B (+45.9% YoY), $3.3B net income, and record $3.1B quarterly free cash flow [1]. The balance sheet is a fortress with $8.78B cash against $5.53B debt, D/E of just 15.76%, and ROE of 25.83% [4]. At a forward P/E of 9.11x with forward EPS of $11.39, the stock appears significantly undervalued relative to earnings power. The 2023 Newcrest acquisition created an unmatched global portfolio of tier-one gold and copper assets, and AI Resilience scores at 8/10 because physical gold mining is immune to AI disruption while AI-driven inflation and energy demand support gold's safe-haven premium. The Inspire Insight score of 52 with 17 positive attributions and zero negatives, along with #1 DJSI ranking for 8 consecutive years, confirms strong ESG alignment [3].
The bear case centers on gold price dependency, dividend deterioration, and cultural concerns. Newmont's exceptional financials are primarily driven by gold price appreciation rather than operational volume growth — a sustained gold price correction would compress revenue, margins, and FCF simultaneously [1][4]. The dividend has been cut significantly from $2.20 to $1.04 annualized over four years, a -17.9% CAGR that erodes income investor confidence despite the company's 20+ year payment history [4]. Glassdoor reviews citing 'constant layoffs,' 'no job security,' and 'growing disconnect between corporate and ground level' suggest cultural challenges that could impact operational execution and talent retention [2]. The PEG ratio of 2.78 indicates that growth is priced in, and Sustainalytics' 'Significant Controversy Level' rating and unmet human rights targets create ESG headline risk despite the strong DJSI ranking [3]. Mining companies inherently face geopolitical, regulatory, and environmental risks across their global operations.
