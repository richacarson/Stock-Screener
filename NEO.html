Company Profile
Healthcare ·Diagnostics & Research ·NASDAQ ·United States
NeoGenomics, Inc. operates a network of cancer-focused testing laboratories in the United States and the United Kingdom. The company provides cytogenetics, FISH, flow cytometry, immunohistochemistry, molecular testing, and next-generation sequencing (NGS) services to hospitals, academic centers, oncologists, pharmaceutical companies, and clinical laboratories. Founded in 2001, headquartered in Fort Myers, Florida.

Investment Thesis
NeoGenomics offers pure-play exposure to the precision oncology diagnostics market, with revenue growing 10.6% YoY to $727M driven by 22-23% NGS growth and an expanding test menu [1][2]. The company guides to $793-801M revenue and $0.19 adjusted EPS in 2026, representing a profitability inflection from -$0.84 trailing EPS [2][7]. RaDaR ST (minimal residual disease monitoring) launch adds a high-margin, clinically differentiated product to the portfolio, while gross margin expansion of 100-120 bps signals improving operating leverage [2]. At $8.35/share with forward P/E of 25.8x on $0.32 forward EPS, the stock is reasonably valued for a diagnostics company approaching profitability.
The bear case centers on persistent unprofitability and execution risk. Despite $727M in revenue, NeoGenomics still generates -14.9% profit margin and -12.4% ROE — the path to GAAP profitability remains unproven [7]. CEO Tony Zook was appointed only in April 2025 and faces a Glassdoor culture score of 3.3/5 with employees citing 'resistance to innovation' and 'getting lapped by other labs' — a dangerous perception in a rapidly evolving genomics market [3]. AI-powered pathology from Paige AI, PathAI, and Google Health poses medium-term disruption risk to NeoGenomics' image-based diagnostics business lines. Competition from Quest, Labcorp, and Foundation Medicine with vastly greater scale and resources could compress margins as NGS testing commoditizes. The $1.1B market cap offers limited upside if the profitability inflection takes longer than expected.
Key Catalysts
  1. RaDaR ST minimal residual disease monitoring launch contributing mid-single-digit millions in 2026 revenue, establishing NeoGenomics in the high-growth liquid biopsy market segment
  2. Profitability inflection with guided $55-57M adjusted EBITDA in 2026 and $0.19 adjusted EPS, potentially triggering re-rating from unprofitable growth to profitable diagnostics platform

Key Risks

  2. Persistent GAAP unprofitability (-14.9% profit margin) despite $727M revenue creates cash burn risk; if the profitability inflection delays, the company may need capital raises that dilute shareholders at depressed prices
  3. CEO Tony Zook's April 2025 appointment marks the latest in a series of leadership transitions, creating strategic execution risk; Glassdoor reviews suggest cultural headwinds that could impair the innovation pipeline

AI Resilience (25%)
AI Resilience MODERATE
5
AI Risk Protection (4/9): Cancer diagnostics faces meaningful AI exposure. AI-powered pathology and diagnostic tools from companies like Paige AI, PathAI, and Google Health are demonstrating ability to read tissue slides, identify genetic markers, and interpret molecular data — core activities that NeoGenomics performs with human pathologists and technicians [3]. While regulatory barriers (CLIA certification, FDA approval) provide near-term protection, the long-term trajectory points toward AI augmenting or partially replacing anatomic pathology workflows. NeoGenomics' molecular/NGS testing is more defensible since it involves complex wet-lab chemistry that AI cannot replicate, but image-based diagnostics (IHC, cytogenetics) face real substitution risk as AI pathology matures. AI Opportunity (1/1): NeoGenomics is actively deploying AI internally through its digital strategy initiative, leveraging automation for operational efficiency in lab workflows, and AI capabilities could enhance its diagnostic accuracy and throughput — generating measurable value even if not yet separately reported revenue [3].
