Company Profile
Healthcare ·Medical Devices ·NASDAQ ·United States
Neogen Corporation develops, manufactures, and markets products dedicated to food and animal safety worldwide. The Food Safety segment offers diagnostic test kits for pathogen detection, allergen testing, mycotoxin testing, and environmental monitoring. The Animal Safety segment provides animal genomics services, veterinary instruments, and biosecurity products. Neogen completed a transformative merger with 3M's Food Safety business in 2023.

Investment Thesis
Neogen is a food and animal safety diagnostics company in the early innings of a turnaround following its transformative 2023 acquisition of 3M's Food Safety business. Q2 FY2026 results showed encouraging signs: core organic revenue grew 2.9%, adjusted EBITDA margin improved 500bps sequentially to 21.7%, and management raised full-year guidance to $845-855M revenue and ~$175M adjusted EBITDA [1]. The Petrifilm platform is an industry-standard consumable with recurring revenue characteristics, and food safety testing benefits from secular tailwinds including increasing regulatory requirements worldwide. At $2.1B market cap, the stock trades at ~12x FY2026 adjusted EBITDA, reasonable if the integration delivers the expected synergies and organic growth accelerates under new CEO Mike Nassif [2].
The bear case centers on integration execution risk and a bloated balance sheet. Neogen took a $1.09B goodwill impairment in FY2025 — representing over half the company's current market cap — acknowledging the 3M deal was overpriced [4]. ROE is deeply negative at -25.3% and reported revenue declined 2.8% YoY despite the 2.9% core growth, suggesting currency headwinds and portfolio rationalization are masking underlying weakness [1]. Forward P/E of 29.6x is rich for a company with no GAAP earnings and minimal organic growth. The CEO transition in 2025 adds leadership risk during a critical integration period. Total debt of $894M against a $2.1B market cap creates leverage risk if the macro environment weakens food industry capital spending. Competition from bioMérieux and emerging molecular testing platforms could pressure Neogen's legacy culture-based testing products [2].

  2. New CEO Mike Nassif's turnaround playbook from Siemens Healthineers could accelerate organic growth and operational efficiency [2]

  3. Forward P/E of 29.6x with no GAAP profitability leaves little margin of safety if organic growth disappoints or competition intensifies from molecular testing platforms [4]
