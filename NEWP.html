Company Profile
Basic Materials ·Silver ·AMEX ·Canada
New Pacific Metals is a Canadian exploration and development company with three precious metal projects in Bolivia, including the flagship Silver Sand silver project (one of the world's largest undeveloped silver deposits) and the Carangas Silver-Gold project. The company is pre-revenue, focused on advancing toward feasibility studies and environmental approvals, with a 2026 work plan comprising over 30,000 metres of drilling.

Investment Thesis
New Pacific Metals offers leveraged exposure to one of the world's largest undeveloped silver deposits during a period of structural silver supply deficit. The Silver Sand PFS reported a post-tax NPV(5%) of $740M and 37% IRR based on 157 million ounces of silver, representing substantial potential value against the current $1.04B market cap [1][3]. The balance sheet is pristine — zero debt, $42M cash, and 47% insider ownership signal strong conviction from those closest to the assets [4][5]. The 2026 work plan targets community framework agreements by mid-2026, 30,000+ metres of drilling, and advancement of the EEIA environmental process at both Silver Sand and Carangas [1]. Silver demand is supported by secular trends in solar energy, electronics, and AI infrastructure, creating a favorable macro backdrop for the company's eventual production.
The bear case is dominated by Bolivia country risk. The company operates exclusively in one of Latin America's most politically volatile countries, with parliamentary approval required for mining concessions and a history of contested transfers — Hindenburg Research published a critical report questioning the legality of New Pacific's Bolivian entity [4]. The company generates zero revenue and has been in exploration for years with no clear timeline to production or cash flow. The $1.04B market cap on zero revenue means the stock is entirely an option on project development, and any regulatory setback, community conflict, or political change could destroy significant value overnight. The Carangas and Silver Sand projects face environmental permitting hurdles, resettlement requirements, and infrastructure needs that could push first production well beyond 2030 [1]. Cash burn of ~$4.7M annually is manageable but will require eventual capital raises for mine construction, likely diluting shareholders [5].
Key Catalysts
  1. Community framework agreements at Silver Sand targeted for mid-2026, which would unblock the environmental assessment process and advance toward feasibility study completion [1]
  2. Rising silver prices driven by structural supply deficit and growing AI/solar/electronics demand, directly increasing the NPV of Silver Sand's 157M oz resource [3]
  3. Bolivia mining law reform and improved Western investment relations, potentially de-risking the regulatory path for concession approvals and mine permitting [4]
Key Risks
  1. Bolivia country risk — political instability, contested concessions, parliamentary approval requirements, and potential nationalization threats could delay or destroy project value [4]
  2. Pre-revenue status with no production timeline — mine construction at Silver Sand could require $500M+ in capital and take 5+ years, with no revenue until completion [1]
  3. Community and environmental permitting delays — artisanal mining conflicts and EEIA environmental assessments are ongoing hurdles that have historically caused multi-year setbacks [1][4]
