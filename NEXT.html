Company Profile
Energy ·Oil & Gas Equipment & Services ·NASDAQ ·United States
NextDecade Corporation is an energy company developing the Rio Grande LNG facility, a 27 million metric ton per annum (mtpa) LNG export project located near Brownsville, Texas. The company has taken final investment decisions on Trains 1-5 and is also developing NEXT Carbon Solutions, one of the largest carbon capture and storage projects in North America. NextDecade is pre-revenue with first LNG production targeted for early 2027.
360 Employees ·www.next-decade.com

Investment Thesis
NextDecade offers asymmetric upside as a pre-revenue LNG developer approaching its first commercial production in early 2027. The company has secured FIDs on 5 trains at Rio Grande LNG representing 27 mtpa of capacity, with long-term LNG sale and purchase agreements providing contracted revenue visibility [1]. A director buying shares in April 2026 at ~$8 signals insider conviction, and the 45.5% insider ownership aligns management with shareholders [4]. At $2.2B market cap, the stock trades at a fraction of the facility's replacement cost. The carbon capture integration differentiates NextDecade from peers and may command premium LNG pricing from ESG-conscious buyers [3]. Global LNG demand growth driven by European energy security needs and Asian coal-to-gas switching provides structural tailwinds.
The bear case is severe and multi-dimensional. NextDecade carries $8.7B in debt against zero revenue, making it entirely dependent on construction completion and LNG market conditions [4]. Analysts warn that LNG supply growth could outpace demand, creating an oversupplied market by late 2026 that would compress margins on NextDecade's first cargoes [1]. Construction delays or cost overruns at Rio Grande LNG could burn through remaining cash and require dilutive capital raises. Regulatory risks include environmental permits, export authorizations, and potential policy changes under shifting political winds. The stock's $8.31 price represents a 31% decline from its 52-week high of $12.12, reflecting market concerns about execution risk. With $169M annual operating cash burn and $4.5B in capex outflows, the company has no margin for error [4].
Key Catalysts
  1. First LNG production from Train 1 targeted early 2027, transforming NextDecade from a construction-phase developer to a revenue-generating LNG exporter [1]
  2. Additional long-term LNG sale and purchase agreements for Trains 4 and 5, de-risking future revenue and providing contracted cash flow visibility [1]
  3. Rising global LNG prices driven by European energy security demand and Asian coal-to-gas switching, improving prospective margins on first cargoes [1]
Key Risks
  1. Construction and commissioning risk — delays at Rio Grande LNG could push first revenue past 2027, extending the cash burn period with $8.7B in outstanding debt [4]
  2. LNG oversupply — analysts project supply growth could exceed demand by late 2026, compressing margins on NextDecade's initial cargoes and reducing project economics [1]
  3. Financing risk — with $143.8M cash against $169M annual operating burn and $4.5B capex, any construction cost overruns could require dilutive capital raises at depressed share prices [4]
