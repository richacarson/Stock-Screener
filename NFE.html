Company Profile
Energy ·Oil & Gas Midstream ·NASDAQ ·United States
New Fortress Energy Inc. is an integrated gas-to-power energy infrastructure company that provides LNG logistics, regasification, and natural gas-fired power generation to end-users in emerging markets. The company operates through Terminals and Infrastructure (natural gas procurement, liquefaction, shipping, and power generation) and Ships (FSRUs and LNG carriers) segments. Founded in 1998 by Wesley Edens, headquartered in New York. The company is currently in financial distress with a $6.5B debt restructuring underway and going-concern warnings.

Investment Thesis
NFE's sole investment case is a distressed restructuring optionality play. The UK High Court is considering a $6.5B debt restructuring plan supported by 97% of creditors that would extinguish approximately 90% of existing debt, splitting the company into BrazilCo and CoreCo [1][2]. If the restructuring succeeds, the remaining operational assets — LNG terminals in Puerto Rico, Mexico, Jamaica, and Brazil, plus FLNG capabilities — could represent significant value relative to the $175M current market cap. CEO Edens' $50M+ personal investment and 27.7% ownership stake aligns his interests with shareholders through the restructuring [5]. Forward P/E of 8.8x suggests analysts project profitability post-restructuring.
The bear case is existential. NFE has acknowledged 'substantial doubt' about its ability to continue as a going concern, with $8.6B in debt against $92M cash and a -$55M stockholders' deficit as of Q1 2026 [1]. Revenue collapsed 50.8% YoY in Q1 2026 to $227M, and net losses totaled $1.8B in FY2025 and $400M in Q1 2026. Multiple debt facilities are in payment default. The restructuring plan, even if approved, would result in massive dilution for existing equity holders through $2.46B in CoreCo Convertible Preferred shares. A class-action lawsuit alleges misrepresentations by management, and the Halper Sadeh investigation into fiduciary duty breaches adds legal risk [4]. Nasdaq delisting risk from missed filing deadlines could further destroy liquidity. At $0.61 per share, the stock reflects near-total equity destruction, and the restructuring outcome for common shareholders is highly uncertain.
Key Catalysts
  1. UK High Court approval of the $6.5B debt restructuring plan, which 97% of creditors support, could eliminate 90% of debt and provide a viable path forward for the operating assets
  2. Successful commissioning of FLNG2 and Brazilian power assets post-restructuring could restore revenue growth and demonstrate the viability of the original infrastructure thesis
  3. Resolution of regulatory and legal issues — including the class-action lawsuit, fiduciary duty investigation, and Nasdaq compliance — could stabilize the equity and restore market access
Key Risks
  1. Going-concern risk is real and acknowledged by management — $8.6B debt against $92M cash with multiple facilities in payment default means bankruptcy or equity wipeout is a plausible outcome
  2. Restructuring terms could result in near-total dilution for common shareholders via $2.46B CoreCo Convertible Preferred, with creditors receiving substantially all residual value
  3. Class-action lawsuit and Halper Sadeh fiduciary duty investigation create additional legal liability, while Nasdaq delisting from missed filing deadlines could eliminate remaining equity liquidity
