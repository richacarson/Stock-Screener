Company Profile
Energy ·Oil & Gas Midstream ·NASDAQ ·United States
New Fortress Energy Inc. is an integrated gas-to-power energy infrastructure company that provides energy and development services worldwide. It operates through Terminals and Infrastructure (natural gas procurement, liquefaction, logistics, and power generation) and Ships (FSRUs and LNG carriers) segments. Founded in 1998 by Wes Edens, headquartered in New York. Currently undergoing $6.5B debt restructuring through UK courts with Chapter 15 filings in the US.

Investment Thesis
NFE's investment case is a high-risk restructuring play. The company's proposed $6.5B debt restructuring through UK courts, with Chapter 15 recognition in the US, would slash debt from $5.7B to $527M, potentially creating a viable post-restructuring entity with valuable physical LNG infrastructure assets including liquefaction facilities, FSRUs, and gas-to-power plants across the Caribbean, Latin America, and beyond [3]. Wes Edens' $50M in personal share purchases over 24 months signals insider conviction in the underlying asset value. The Forward P/E of 7.55 implies analysts expect a return to profitability post-restructuring, and growing global demand for LNG-to-power infrastructure — amplified by AI data center electricity needs — could support a recovery narrative [1][4].
The bear case is overwhelming. NFE's financial disclosures reveal 'pervasive financial misstatements' and systemic mismanagement: $8.9B in debt accumulated without generating free cash flow, $1B+ in dividends steered to shareholders and insiders, poor operational performance at Mexico's only LNG plant, and probable abandonment of a second Mexican facility [3]. Q1 2026 revenue collapsed 52% YoY to $227M, and net loss expanded to $400M from $178M. The stock trades at $0.53, down 89% from its 52-week high of $4.96, reflecting near-total destruction of equity value. IEEFA has characterized NFE as a threat to renewable energy in Puerto Rico, and the current ratio of 0.13 with $7.1B in current debt against $92M cash confirms the balance sheet is in extremis [1][3]. Post-restructuring equity ownership and governance remain uncertain, and the $151M market cap may still overstate the equity value if restructuring outcomes are unfavorable.
