Company Profile
Energy ·Oil & Gas Midstream ·NASDAQ ·United States
New Fortress Energy Inc. is an energy infrastructure company that develops, finances, and operates natural gas and liquefied natural gas (LNG) facilities to provide energy to power, industrial, and transportation markets. The company operates terminals, power plants, and an FLNG facility across the Americas, including key operations in Puerto Rico, Jamaica (recently divested), and Brazil. Founded in 2014 by Wes Edens, headquartered in New York. The company faces severe financial distress with $8.6B in debt and a stock price of $0.69.

Investment Thesis
NFE is a high-risk turnaround speculation centered on whether the FLNG commissioning and Klondike data center initiative can generate sufficient cash flow to service $8.6B in debt. The FLNG facility has exited construction and entered commissioning, which if successful could materially improve EBITDA [1]. The Klondike Digital Infrastructure pivot positions NFE at the intersection of gas-fired power and AI hyperscaler demand, a potentially large addressable market [2]. The Jamaica divestiture generated $800M in net proceeds and a $430M gain, providing near-term liquidity. At $0.69/share and $197M market cap, the stock prices in near-total equity wipeout, creating asymmetric upside if the company avoids bankruptcy.
The bear case is existential. NFE has $8.6B in total debt with $6.5B due within one year, only $92M in cash, and negative free cash flow of -$280M [5]. Q1 2026 produced a net loss of $400M ($1.40/share loss), and revenue has declined 50.6% YoY [1]. IEEFA has explicitly warned of bankruptcy risk, and the company is already behind on $500M in payments [4]. The Fast LNG projects have suffered chronic cost overruns and delays. Even if FLNG commissioning succeeds, the debt maturity wall within 12 months creates a binary outcome — either the company successfully refinances under duress or faces restructuring. The 'private equity culture' with 16-hour workdays and a 2.8/5 Glassdoor culture score suggests an unsustainable organizational model for a company that needs flawless execution to survive [2].
Key Catalysts
  1. FLNG 1 successful commissioning and first commercial LNG cargo delivery, which would validate the Fast LNG concept and materially improve EBITDA toward the $1.25-1.5B 2025 target
  2. Klondike Digital Infrastructure securing binding commitments from AI hyperscalers for gas-powered data center development, creating a new revenue stream
  3. Successful debt refinancing or extension of the $6.5B in near-term maturities, removing the immediate bankruptcy overhang
Key Risks
  1. Bankruptcy or restructuring — $8.6B in debt with $6.5B due within one year, $92M cash, negative FCF, and already behind on $500M in payments creates an imminent solvency crisis
  2. FLNG commissioning failure or further delays could eliminate the primary EBITDA growth catalyst and accelerate creditor action
  3. Puerto Rico gas cargo disputes and regulatory challenges in emerging market operations create revenue uncertainty in the core business at the worst possible time
