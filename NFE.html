Company Profile
Energy ·Oil & Gas Midstream ·NASDAQ ·United States
New Fortress Energy Inc. is an integrated gas-to-power energy infrastructure company providing LNG terminals, floating liquefaction (FLNG), regasification units, and natural gas-fired power generation to end-users worldwide. Founded by Wes Edens in 2014, the company aims to replace heavy fuel oil and diesel with cleaner natural gas in emerging markets.

Investment Thesis
New Fortress Energy represents a high-risk, high-reward turnaround in LNG infrastructure. The company's Fast LNG Unit 1 is producing above its 1.4 MTPA nameplate capacity, validating the core technology thesis, with 2026 revenue guidance of $605M+ and EBITDA of $305M+ as the asset ramps[1]. At $0.70/share, the market is pricing in near-certainty of severe dilution or restructuring given $8.6B in debt against a $199M market cap.
The bull case requires FLNG Unit 1 sustaining above-nameplate production, FLNG Unit 2 commissioning on schedule in H1 2027, and debt-to-EBITDA declining below 4x as guided. If executed, the stock could re-rate dramatically from current levels. The bear case is that widening losses ($1.40/share in Q1 2026) and unsustainable leverage force a restructuring that wipes out common equity. This is a speculative position requiring high conviction in Wes Edens' execution ability and the Fast LNG economics[1][2].
Key Catalysts
  1. FLNG Unit 1 sustained production above 1.4 MTPA nameplate demonstrating economic viability and generating cash flow to service debt
  2. FLNG Unit 2 commissioning on schedule in H1 2027 would double liquefaction capacity and dramatically improve EBITDA trajectory
  3. Debt refinancing or asset sales that reduce leverage below 4x debt/EBITDA, removing existential risk from the equity
Key Risks
  1. Unsustainable leverage: $8.6B debt vs. $183M equity (4,692% D/E) with widening quarterly losses of $1.40/share in Q1 2026
  2. FLNG Unit 2 construction delays or cost overruns could exhaust remaining liquidity and trigger covenant violations
  3. Sub-$1 stock price creates vulnerability to NASDAQ delisting, further institutional selling, and inability to raise equity capital
