Investment Thesis
NFE's bull case rests on contracted EBITDA from existing terminals (2025 target was $1.3B) and the Brazil buildout expected to contribute $470M in contracted EBITDA by 2026, which at a forward P/E of 8.6x prices in a dramatic earnings recovery [2]. The company's physical LNG infrastructure across Puerto Rico, Mexico, Brazil, and Nicaragua represents real assets with long-term contracted revenues, and the global energy transition's demand for natural gas as a bridge fuel provides secular tailwinds. If NFE can restructure its $8.6B debt burden and bring Brazil assets online, the equity could recover substantially from its $0.60 level.
The bear case is severe and immediate: management has acknowledged events of default under debt agreements and substantial doubt about the company's ability to continue as a going concern [1]. Revenue collapsed -50.8% YoY with Q2 2026 revenue of $301.7M falling 46% short of analyst expectations. The debt-to-equity ratio exceeds 4,600%, stockholders equity has been destroyed from $1.88B to $183M in a single year, and profit margins are -164% [4]. With no insider buying at $0.60 per share and the stock down over 95% from its 2023 highs, NFE represents a potential restructuring or bankruptcy scenario where equity holders face total loss. The company's ambitious infrastructure buildouts in developing markets have consumed capital far faster than they have generated returns.
