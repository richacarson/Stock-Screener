Company Profile
Utilities ·Utilities - Regulated Electric ·NYSE ·United Kingdom
National Grid operates electricity transmission and distribution networks in England, Wales, and the northeastern United States (New England and New York). The company also operates gas distribution in the UK and US, electricity interconnectors, and LNG importation. National Grid is a critical infrastructure owner at the heart of the UK and US clean energy transitions.

Investment Thesis
National Grid is a critical infrastructure monopoly positioned at the nexus of the clean energy transition and AI-driven electricity demand growth, deploying a record GBP 5.1B in H1 FY2026 (+12% YoY) with operating profit rising 13% to GBP 2.3B and underlying EPS growing 6% to 29.8p [1]. The company's regulated monopoly assets in UK transmission/distribution and US Northeast electricity and gas create a durable earnings base with 6-8% underlying EPS growth guidance, supported by approximately 10% annual investment growth [1]. At 14.4x forward P/E with a 3.53% dividend yield, NGG offers a compelling combination of value, yield, and secular growth from grid modernization and AI data center demand expansion. The $88B market cap reflects the scale of National Grid's irreplaceable infrastructure assets across both the UK and US.
The bear case focuses on financial strain from the massive capital investment program. Negative free cash flow of GBP 3.6B reflects spending that far exceeds operating cash generation, requiring continued debt issuance at 123.4% D/E [4]. Revenue declined 11.3% YoY (partly energy pass-through fluctuations) and the dividend has not grown consistently — the 5-year CAGR is essentially flat at -0.1% with the 2022 dividend reduction breaking the growth streak [4]. ROE of 7.9% is constrained by regulated returns, limiting profitability metrics. The CEO transition to Zoe Yujnovich in November 2025 introduces execution uncertainty. Currency risk affects US ADR holders as GBP/USD fluctuations impact reported earnings. Regulatory risk in both the UK (Ofgem) and US (state regulators) could compress allowed returns, particularly as political pressure on energy costs intensifies [1].
Key Catalysts
  1. AI data center buildout in US Northeast service territory driving incremental grid connection revenue and capacity upgrade investment opportunities [2]
  2. Record GBP 11B+ annual infrastructure investment program supporting 6-8% underlying EPS growth and 10% annual investment growth trajectory [1]
  3. Clean energy transition requiring massive grid modernization in both UK and US, creating a multi-decade secular demand tailwind for transmission and distribution assets [1]
Key Risks
  1. Negative free cash flow of GBP 3.6B from capital-intensive investment program, requiring continued debt issuance and increasing financial leverage at 123.4% D/E [4]
  2. Regulatory risk in UK (Ofgem) and US (state regulators) could compress allowed returns on equity, constraining earnings growth below the 6-8% target [1]
  3. Inconsistent dividend growth history — 5-year CAGR of -0.1% with 2022 reduction — undermines income reliability for dividend-focused investors despite 3.53% current yield [4]
