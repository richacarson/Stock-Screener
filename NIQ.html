Investment Thesis
NIQ Global Intelligence is the world's leading consumer intelligence company with unmatched data coverage across 90+ countries, serving CPG manufacturers and retailers with retail measurement, consumer panels, and analytics. FY2025 revenue of $4.20B grew 5.7% YoY, with Intelligence segment growing 7.7% organically [1]. The $400M technology investment under CTO Kapoor is deploying AI-powered analytics across the platform [2]. 2026 guidance of 5.0-5.3% organic revenue growth with improving EBITDA margins (23.5-23.8%) and $235-250M levered free cash flow signals a path toward sustainable profitability [1]. At 9.5x forward P/E, the stock is deeply discounted, reflecting both risk and potential upside if the company delivers on its deleveraging and margin expansion roadmap.
The bear case is dominated by NIQ's catastrophic debt burden — D/E of 1,175% from the GfK acquisition financing creates existential risk if revenue growth stalls or interest rates remain elevated [4]. The company posted a net loss of -$353M in FY2025 despite $4.2B in revenue, with negative profit margins and ROE [1]. Employee morale is deteriorating, with Glassdoor ratings declining and multiple rounds of layoffs creating organizational instability [2]. The core business faces structural AI disruption risk as CPG companies build in-house analytics capabilities and AI-native platforms offer cheaper alternatives to traditional panel and retail measurement data. Revenue growth of 5.7% is modest for a company carrying this level of leverage, and any economic downturn that reduces CPG companies' analytics budgets would severely pressure cash flows needed for debt service. The stock has traded down to $11.36 from higher post-IPO levels, reflecting persistent market skepticism about the company's ability to grow its way out of its debt burden [4].

  3. Deteriorating employee morale with declining Glassdoor ratings, multiple layoff rounds, and aggressive cost cutting undermining organizational capability during a critical integration period [2]
