Company Profile
Energy ·Oil & Gas Equipment & Services ·NYSE ·United States
NOV Inc. designs, manufactures, and sells equipment and components used in oil and gas drilling, completion, and production operations worldwide. The company operates through Energy Products & Services and Energy Equipment segments, providing rig systems, wellbore technologies, completion tools, and process and flow technologies. Founded over 150 years ago, NOV is also expanding into low-carbon solutions including electrified drilling rigs, offshore wind installation vessels, and carbon capture technologies. Headquartered in Houston, Texas.

Investment Thesis
NOV reported Q1 2026 revenue of $2.05B with $177M adjusted EBITDA (8.6% margin) and $734M TTM free cash flow, demonstrating cash generation durability even as revenue declined 2% YoY due to Middle East disruptions impacting $54M in revenue and $32M in EBITDA [1]. Capital equipment backlog of $4.23B and Q1 Energy Equipment bookings of $520M—the strongest Q1 since 2019—signal a potential multi-year capital equipment upcycle as management guides [1]. The forward P/E of 17.9x and PEG of 1.72 represent attractive valuation relative to expected earnings recovery, with $339M in energy transition revenue (electrified rigs, offshore wind, carbon capture) providing diversification [4]. The company returned $100M to shareholders in Q1 through buybacks and dividends while maintaining a conservative 37% D/E ratio [1][3].
The bear case centers on cyclical vulnerability and profitability deterioration. GAAP profit margin of 1.05% and ROE of 1.52% are extremely thin, with Q1 2026 net income collapsing to $19M ($0.05 EPS) on Middle East disruptions [1][3]. Q2 2026 guidance of 4-6% revenue decline YoY suggests continued near-term headwinds. The payout ratio of 170% on trailing earnings is unsustainable without earnings recovery, and the dividend was previously cut during the 2020 downturn (only 2 consecutive years of payment) [3][5]. The Altman Z-Score of 1.9 flags increased bankruptcy risk, and cyclical oil and gas spending remains vulnerable to energy transition pressures, geopolitical disruptions, and OPEC production decisions that NOV cannot control [3]. Energy Equipment book-to-bill of 80% in Q1 suggests orders are not keeping pace with revenue.
Key Catalysts
  1. Multi-year capital equipment upcycle materializing as management guides, with Q1 2026 Energy Equipment bookings ($520M) marking the strongest Q1 since 2019 and $4.23B backlog providing revenue visibility
  2. Middle East conflict resolution reducing the $54M quarterly revenue and $32M EBITDA headwind, enabling margin recovery toward historical 12-14% EBITDA margins
  3. Energy transition revenue scaling beyond $339M annually as electrified drilling rigs, offshore wind installation vessels, and carbon capture technologies gain market adoption
Key Risks
  1. Cyclical oil and gas spending downturn driven by OPEC production decisions, energy transition momentum, or global recession—NOV's 1.05% profit margin provides zero buffer against revenue decline
  2. Energy Equipment book-to-bill of 80% in Q1 2026 indicates orders are not keeping pace with revenue, risking backlog erosion and future revenue decline if trend persists
  3. Geopolitical risk remains elevated with Middle East disruptions already costing $54M quarterly revenue and $32M EBITDA, with no clear timeline for resolution
