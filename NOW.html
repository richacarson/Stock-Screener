Investment Thesis
ServiceNow is the enterprise AI platform that benefits from — rather than is threatened by — the AI boom. Q1 2026 delivered 22% subscription revenue growth to $3.67B with raised FY2026 guidance to $15.74-15.78B and AI revenue target elevated 50% to $1.5B [1]. Post-10:1 split at $95, the forward P/E of 18.9x and PEG of 0.88 represent the most attractive valuation in years for a consistent 20%+ grower with 32% operating margins. McDermott's $3M personal share purchase, 2030 contract extension, and TIME100-caliber leadership signal maximum conviction in the AI execution layer thesis [2][3].
The bear case is narrowing but real: the 14-17% stock decline post-Q1 earnings reflected margin compression fears as the company invests aggressively in AI infrastructure and the Armis acquisition adds complexity [1]. Geopolitical headwinds from the Iran conflict delayed several large Middle East deals (75bps subscription impact), and any escalation could pressure international revenue further. The trailing P/E of 56.6x (vs. 18.9x forward) highlights the transition period as GAAP earnings catch up to non-GAAP performance. If agentic AI matures to replace workflow orchestration rather than require it, the entire thesis inverts.
Key Catalysts
  1. AI revenue accelerating toward $1.5B target in 2026 — 50% above original guidance — validating enterprise AI agent adoption at scale
  2. Armis cybersecurity acquisition closing H2 2026, expanding platform TAM to security operations and adding ~1% incremental revenue
  3. Forward P/E compression to 18.9x with PEG of 0.88 creating compelling entry point for a consistent 20%+ subscription revenue grower
Key Risks
  1. Agentic AI systems maturing to replace enterprise workflow orchestration within 2-3 years, inverting the 'execution layer' thesis
  2. Middle East geopolitical escalation extending deal delays beyond the 75bps Q1 impact into material full-year subscription growth headwinds
  3. Margin compression from aggressive AI investment and Armis integration costs disappointing investors who expect expanding profitability
