Investment Thesis
ServiceNow is the enterprise AI platform that benefits from — rather than is threatened by — the AI boom. Q1 2026 subscription revenue of $3.67B grew 22% YoY (accelerating from 19.5% prior quarter), with cRPO of $12.64B growing 22.5% and 16 deals over $5M in net new ACV representing 80% YoY growth [1]. FY2026 subscription guidance raised to $15.74-15.78B (~21% growth) with expanding operating margins. At 18x forward earnings and 0.88 PEG following the stock split, ServiceNow offers rare value for a 22% growth enterprise software leader. CEO McDermott's $3M personal share purchase and 2030 contract extension signal maximum long-term conviction [2]. Now Assist is the fastest-selling new product in company history, validating AI as revenue additive.
The bear case centers on geopolitical and execution risk. Q1 2026 saw approximately 75 basis points of subscription revenue headwind from delayed Middle East on-premise deal closings due to the Iran conflict, and the stock sank 14% post-earnings despite strong results [1]. If agentic AI matures faster than McDermott's 'execution layer' thesis anticipates, enterprise workflows could be replaced rather than orchestrated by AI systems — a low-probability but high-impact disruption scenario. Profit margin of 12.6% on a GAAP basis remains below the 20% target, reflecting heavy stock-based compensation that dilutes reported profitability. The Armis acquisition (expected H2 2026 close) adds integration complexity and potential execution risk in a high-multiple stock where any stumble triggers outsized sell-offs.
Key Catalysts
  1. FY2026 subscription revenue guidance raise beyond $15.78B at Q2 report, demonstrating sustained 22%+ growth acceleration driven by AI workflow adoption and Now Assist ACV momentum
  2. Armis cybersecurity acquisition closing in H2 2026, expanding platform TAM to security operations and adding incremental revenue to the Rule of 55+ framework
  3. AI agent adoption metrics materializing with specific customer workflow automation data showing Now Assist driving measurable enterprise productivity gains and contract expansions
Key Risks
  1. Agentic AI systems maturing to replace enterprise workflow orchestration platforms within 2-3 years, faster than McDermott's 'execution layer' thesis anticipates — a structural disruption risk that would invalidate the platform's value proposition
  2. Geopolitical disruption: Q1 2026 already showed 75bps subscription headwind from Middle East conflict; escalation could delay further large enterprise deals and compress growth below 20%
  3. GAAP profit margin of 12.6% reflects heavy stock-based compensation; if SBC grows faster than revenue, the gap between non-GAAP (32% operating margin) and GAAP profitability will widen, pressuring the stock on an economic earnings basis
