Company Profile

NRG Energy is a Houston-based Fortune 200 integrated power and home services company serving residential, small business, and commercial/industrial customers across the US and Canada. Following the LS Power acquisition (18 natural gas/dual-fuel facilities, ~13 GW), NRG operates one of the largest competitive generation fleets in the US and is positioning to serve the AI data center power demand surge in ERCOT and PJM.

Investment Thesis
NRG is one of the cleanest pure-play infrastructure beneficiaries of the AI data center power-demand super-cycle. The November 2023 CEO transition to Larry Coben (board director since 2003) catalyzed a strategic pivot validated by ~240% stock returns since [2] and #1 S&P 500 performance in 2025 (~75%). 2025 results: adjusted EPS $8.24 (+21%), adjusted EBITDA $4.087B (+8%) [1]. 2026 guide is materially higher: adjusted EBITDA midpoint $5.575B (+37%), adjusted EPS midpoint $8.90, free cash flow before growth $2.8-$3.3B [1]. The LS Power acquisition added 18 natural gas/dual-fuel facilities (~13 GW) doubling generation capacity. Cumulative 445 MW of data center retail power agreements signed across ERCOT and PJM; targeting 1 GW+ of additional contracts in 2026 not yet in guidance [1]. Long-term: 14%+ annual EPS/FCF/share growth target through 2030. Net-zero by 2050 ambition with science-based targets [3] and Inspire +44 alignment add further quality.
Risks center on policy, leverage, and execution. The LS Power acquisition added significant debt and integration risk; D/E is elevated and a power market dislocation could pressure ratings. The data center power demand thesis is consensus and partially priced in — if hyperscaler capex moderates or model-efficiency breakthroughs (e.g., DeepSeek-style optimization) reduce compute needs, NRG's premium multiple could compress. Coben himself has warned that tight power markets risk consumer price spikes from data center diversion [2] — a politically charged issue that could invite regulatory intervention or windfall-profit policies. Natural gas exposure raises long-term carbon-transition risk despite the 2050 net-zero ambition. Multi-year construction schedules (data center initial powering 2028, fully online 2032) introduce execution risk around permitting, equipment supply (gas turbines), and cost overruns.
