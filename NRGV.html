Company Profile

Energy Vault develops and deploys utility-scale energy storage solutions including battery (B-Vault), gravity (G-Vault), and hybrid (H-Vault) systems, complemented by AI-powered energy management software (Vault-OS, Vault-Bidder, Vault-Manager). Investment Thesis
Energy Vault is a high-growth energy storage company that achieved 340%+ revenue growth to $203.7M in 2025, with a $1.3B contract backlog expected to yield $60M in recurring EBITDA [1]. The company's 2026 guidance of $225M-$300M (30%+ growth at midpoint) is supported by 540 MW of contracted capacity across BESS, gravity, and AI digital infrastructure. The AI-powered energy management software platform (Vault-OS, Vault-Bidder) adds recurring revenue potential and differentiation in the rapidly growing utility-scale storage market, which benefits from AI data center demand and renewable energy mandates [1][2]. S&P Global awarded Energy Vault the highest ESG score (74/100) in the energy storage sector for the second consecutive year, positioning it well for ESG-focused capital flows [3].
The bear case centers on persistent unprofitability, dilution risk, and technology credibility. Profit margin of -50.9% and negative ROE of -96.5% reflect a company burning cash to scale, with $97M in debt and $59M cash requiring continued capital raises [4]. The $150M convertible notes offering dilutes existing shareholders at depressed prices. The original gravity storage technology was criticized by Bleecker Street Research as a 'middle-school science fair project,' and while Energy Vault has responded, the pivot to commoditized BESS reduces the company's technological differentiation [3]. At a $545M market cap with $3.15 share price, the stock is highly speculative. Competition from Tesla Megapack, Fluence, and BYD in BESS is intense, and Energy Vault's 142-person team must execute against companies with orders of magnitude more resources.
Key Catalysts
  1. 2026 revenue guidance of $225M-$300M representing 30%+ growth, with potential upside from AI Digital Infrastructure portfolio expansion [1]
  2. Contract backlog of $1.3B converting to $60M recurring EBITDA, accelerating to $100M-$150M by 2029, providing long-term revenue visibility [1]
  3. AI data center power demand explosion driving incremental need for utility-scale energy storage, directly benefiting Energy Vault's pipeline [1][2]
Key Risks
  1. Persistent unprofitability with -50.9% profit margin and negative ROE requiring continued dilutive capital raises to fund operations [4]
  2. Intense competition from Tesla Megapack, Fluence, and BYD in the commoditized BESS market where Energy Vault's 142-person team lacks scale [1]
  3. Technology credibility concerns from the abandoned gravity storage concept and short-seller criticism, creating investor skepticism about management's execution track record [3]
