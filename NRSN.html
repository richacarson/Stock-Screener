Company Profile
Healthcare ·Biotechnology ·NASDAQ ·Israel
NeuroSense Therapeutics Ltd. is a clinical-stage biotechnology company focused on the discovery and development of targeted innovative therapeutics for neurodegenerative diseases, primarily amyotrophic lateral sclerosis (ALS) and Alzheimer's disease. Lead candidate PrimeC is a novel extended-release oral fixed-dose combination of ciprofloxacin and celecoxib targeting multiple ALS disease mechanisms including inflammation, iron accumulation, and impaired RNA regulation. Founded in 2017 by Alon Ben-Noon in Herzliya, Israel, following a personal encounter with an ALS patient.

Investment Thesis
NeuroSense Therapeutics holds a potentially transformative asset in PrimeC, which demonstrated a statistically significant 65% reduction in risk of death and >14-month median survival benefit in its Phase 2b PARADIGM study for ALS, with results published in JAMA Neurology [1]. FDA clearance for the Phase 3 PARAGON trial was received in November 2025, and the company is pursuing a potential accelerated Canadian NDS pathway following a pre-NDS meeting with Health Canada [1]. PrimeC's orphan drug designation from both FDA and EMA provides 7-10 years of market exclusivity upon approval, and patent protection extends through 2042 [3]. The ALS therapeutic market represents significant unmet need with limited competition, and PrimeC's dual-mechanism approach (ciprofloxacin + celecoxib) targeting inflammation, iron accumulation, and RNA dysregulation offers differentiation [3]. CEO and CFO insider buying at premium prices in April-June 2026 signals management conviction [6][7][8].
The bear case is existential: NeuroSense had only $166K in cash as of December 2025 against $11.1M in annual operating losses, creating immediate going-concern risk [1][2]. The company is funding survival through highly dilutive small PIPE financings ($600K in April 2026) that have expanded share count from 12M to 32.6M in three years [2][6]. Nasdaq has issued non-compliance notices for both minimum bid price and minimum market value with a September 29, 2026 deadline, raising concrete delisting risk [4]. A Phase 3 ALS trial will require tens of millions in capital that the company does not have and cannot currently raise at scale given its micro-cap status and sub-$1 stock price. Daily dollar volume of ~$106K makes the stock essentially untradeable for institutional investors, and the 69% decline in share price over the past 12 months reflects the market's skepticism that NeuroSense can bridge the funding gap to Phase 3 completion [9].
Key Catalysts
  1. Phase 3 PARAGON trial initiation for PrimeC in ALS, which if partnered or funded could transform the company's value trajectory and validate the Phase 2b survival data published in JAMA Neurology
  2. Health Canada pre-NDS meeting outcome (May 2026) could enable accelerated Canadian market approval based on existing Phase 2b data, potentially generating near-term revenue without completing Phase 3
  3. Strategic partnership or licensing deal with a larger pharmaceutical company to fund Phase 3 development and commercialization, which would resolve the existential funding gap
Key Risks
  1. Cash position of $166K against $11.1M annual burn creates immediate going-concern risk; without material fundraising, the company cannot sustain operations through 2026, let alone fund a Phase 3 trial
  2. Nasdaq non-compliance with minimum bid price ($1.00) and market value ($35M) requirements with September 29, 2026 deadline could result in delisting, further restricting capital access and investor participation
  3. Severe and ongoing share dilution (shares outstanding grew 172% from 2022 to 2025) will continue to erode per-share value as the company relies on equity issuance for survival, with no revenue to offset dilution
