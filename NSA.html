Company Profile
Real Estate ·REIT - Industrial ·NYSE ·United States
National Storage Affiliates Trust is a self-storage REIT headquartered in Greenwood Village, Colorado, focused on the ownership, operation, and acquisition of self-storage properties in the United States. As of December 31, 2025, the company owned and operated 1,063 self-storage properties across 37 states and Puerto Rico with approximately 69.4 million rentable square feet, making it one of the largest self-storage owners and operators among public and private companies in the U.S.

Investment Thesis
NSA offers a unique investment opportunity driven by the proposed Public Storage acquisition announced in March 2026 at approximately $10.5B, providing significant near-term value realization for shareholders [1]. Independently, NSA is a well-managed self-storage REIT with 1,063 properties across 37 states, a 5.4% dividend yield supported by 10 consecutive years of growth (11.1% 5-year CAGR from $0.54 to $2.28/share), and an Inspire score of 42 reflecting strong ESG alignment [3][4]. Q4 2025 showed improving fundamentals with same-store revenue decline narrowing to -70bps from -260bps in Q3, and year-over-year occupancy improving from -140bps to -70bps [1]. The digital transformation — algorithmic pricing, cloud analytics, 70%+ digital lease signing — positions NSA's portfolio for operational efficiency gains [2]. FY2026 guidance of $2.13-2.25 EPS with same-store revenue growth between -0.3% and +2.1% suggests a return to positive growth [1].
The bear case focuses on the acquisition uncertainty, fundamental headwinds, and REIT valuation challenges. The Public Storage acquisition faces investigation by law firm Kahn Swick & Foti over the adequacy of price and process, creating deal risk — if the merger fails, NSA's stock could decline significantly from the current $42.21 (near its 52-week high of $42.60) [3]. Without the acquisition premium, NSA's fundamentals are deteriorating: revenue declined 2.3% YoY, same-store revenue was negative through most of 2024-2025, and GAAP EPS of just $0.69 barely covers the $2.28 annual dividend [4]. Forward P/E of 111.9x and P/B of 5.4x are expensive even for a REIT, and the 330% GAAP payout ratio (while distorted by REIT accounting) signals that reported earnings do not cover distributions. D/E of 127% with $2.0B in debt against $19.5M cash creates leverage risk in a rising rate environment [4]. Self-storage supply additions in key markets could further pressure occupancy and rental rates if new construction outpaces demand growth.
Key Catalysts
  1. Public Storage acquisition closing at approximately $10.5B, providing near-term value realization and potential upside if terms are improved during the review process [1]
  2. Same-store revenue inflection from negative to positive in 2026, with guidance of -0.3% to +2.1% growth following sequential improvement through 2025 [1]
  3. Digital transformation payoff — algorithmic pricing and 70%+ digital leasing driving operational efficiency improvements across the 1,063-property portfolio [2]
Key Risks
  1. Public Storage acquisition failure risk — law firm investigation into price and process adequacy could delay or derail the merger, potentially triggering a 20%+ stock decline from acquisition-premium levels [3]
  2. Continued same-store revenue pressure from self-storage supply additions in key metropolitan markets, with negative occupancy trends only recently stabilizing [1]
  3. Leverage vulnerability with $2.0B debt and 127% D/E in a sustained high-interest-rate environment, compressing the spread between cap rates and borrowing costs [4]
