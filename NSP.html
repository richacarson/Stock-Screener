Company Profile
Industrials ·Staffing & Employment Services ·NYSE ·United States
Insperity is the largest professional employer organization (PEO) in the United States, providing comprehensive HR outsourcing solutions including payroll, benefits administration, compliance, and talent management to small and medium-sized businesses. The company co-employs approximately 300,000 worksite employees across its client base and offers HR360, HRCore, and the new Workday-powered HRScale platform.

Investment Thesis
Insperity is the largest U.S. PEO with a 40-year track record under founder-CEO Paul Sarvadi, trading at a depressed forward P/E of 11.5 as it navigates an earnings trough with Q1 2026 EPS of $1.31 missing estimates of $1.69 [1]. The HRScale platform launch with Workday targets mid-market clients and expects ~6,000 worksite employees live by Q4 2026, representing a meaningful expansion of the addressable market. The 6.75% dividend yield and 21-year payment history provide income floor while the margin recovery plan takes hold, with analysts expecting 3.9% revenue growth over the next 12 months.
The bear case is significant: D/E of 641.8% reflects the capital-light but liability-heavy PEO model, trailing ROE is -26.9%, and the payout ratio of 500% on trailing earnings is unsustainable without a rapid return to profitability [3]. AI-powered HR platforms from Rippling, Deel, and Gusto are competing aggressively for SMB clients, potentially compressing PEO pricing power. Worksite employee counts declined in Q1 2026 even as revenue grew marginally, suggesting client attrition is offsetting price increases. If the earnings recovery stalls, the dividend could face a cut despite its 21-year history.
Key Catalysts
  1. HRScale platform launch with Workday targeting ~6,000 worksite employees by Q4 2026, opening the mid-market segment and increasing revenue per client
  2. Margin recovery plan for 2026 with management expressing satisfaction with Q1 execution on cost optimization and pricing discipline
  3. Forward P/E of 11.5 implies significant earnings recovery priced in — if achieved, the stock re-rates with both earnings growth and multiple expansion
Key Risks
  1. Worksite employee count decline despite revenue growth signals client attrition that could accelerate if SMBs shift to AI-powered HR platforms like Rippling or Gusto
  2. Debt-to-equity of 641.8% and trailing payout ratio of 500% create financial fragility — a sustained earnings shortfall could force a dividend reduction
  3. Q1 2026 EPS of $1.31 missed consensus of $1.69, raising questions about the pace and magnitude of the margin recovery plan

Insperity's Workday partnership for HRScale is a defensive response to this pressure [1].
