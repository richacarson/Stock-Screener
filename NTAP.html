Investment Thesis
NetApp is successfully repositioning as enterprise AI data infrastructure. Q3 FY2026 revenue of $1.71B beat guidance with record non-GAAP operating margins, and the All-Flash Array segment delivered $1.0B in quarterly revenue (+11% YoY) [1]. The company closed ~200 AI infrastructure and data lake modernization deals in Q2 FY2026 (up from ~125 last quarter), demonstrating real AI traction. AFX (NVIDIA SuperPOD-certified) and AI Data Engine (AIDE) position ONTAP as the data backbone for enterprise AI workloads that need to live on-premises or in hybrid environments [1][2]. FY2026 guidance of $6.625-$6.875B revenue (3% growth), 29.5-30.5% non-GAAP operating margin, and $7.75-$8.05 EPS supports continued cash flow growth. CEO George Kurian's 10+ year tenure and 'embrace disruption' philosophy add execution credibility.
The bear case is modest top-line growth and competitive pressure. FY2026 revenue growth of just 3% reflects AI tailwind offsetting legacy hybrid storage decline rather than meaningful net acceleration [1]. Pure Storage, Dell, and hyperscaler-native storage solutions all compete for AI data infrastructure dollars, and NetApp must continually prove AFX wins versus alternatives. Capex demands for AI-grade infrastructure are rising, and customer ROI cycles for new AI deployments are still being established. ESG profile is strong (EcoVadis GOLD, top 5%) [3], but the Inspire Insight score of -49 (driven by LGBT activism attribution) places NetApp in the MISALIGNED category for faith-aligned screens despite a long list of positive attributions across data security, employee wellbeing, and environmental responsibility. HOLD: solid AI enabler with reasonable valuation but limited near-term growth acceleration.
