Company Profile
Healthcare ·Biotechnology ·NASDAQ ·United States
Intellia Therapeutics is a clinical-stage biopharmaceutical company focused on developing curative genome editing treatments using CRISPR/Cas9 technology. Lead programs include lonvo-z (NTLA-2002) for hereditary angioedema (HAE) and nex-z (NTLA-2001) for transthyretin amyloidosis (ATTR). Founded in 2014, co-founded by CRISPR Nobel laureate Jennifer Doudna, headquartered in Cambridge, Massachusetts.
377 Employees ·www.intelliatx.com

Investment Thesis
Intellia Therapeutics offers asymmetric upside as the leader in in vivo CRISPR gene editing approaching its first commercial launch. Lonvo-z for HAE is expected to report HAELO topline data by mid-2026 with a BLA submission planned for H2 2026 and commercial launch in H1 2027 [1]. If successful, this would be the first in vivo CRISPR therapy approved, establishing Intellia's platform value across multiple indications. The company has $605.1M in liquidity providing runway through launch, Q4 2025 collaboration revenue surged 78.4% YoY to $23.0M (beating expectations by $10.85M), and the stock trades at $12.82 — down 55% from its 52-week high of $28.25 — offering a discounted entry point [1][4]. Cathie Wood's ARK Invest has been actively buying shares, signaling institutional conviction in the CRISPR platform [2].
The bear case is severe and binary. A patient death in the MAGNITUDE ATTR-CM trial triggered an FDA clinical hold, and while the hold has been lifted with enhanced safety measures, the root cause of the liver toxicity remains unknown [2][3]. This creates existential regulatory risk — another serious adverse event could permanently derail the nex-z program. Intellia burned $412.7M in 2025 with $605.1M remaining, giving approximately 18 months of runway, and will likely need to raise capital (diluting shareholders) unless lonvo-z is approved and generating revenue by late 2027 [1][4]. The company has only 377 employees and must build an entire commercial infrastructure (sales teams, distribution, treatment centers) from scratch. With a market cap of $1.5B, the stock is pricing in significant probability of commercial success, but biotech Phase 3 trials have historically high failure rates. Competition from established gene therapy companies and emerging gene editing platforms adds further risk [2].

  3. Cash runway of ~18 months ($605.1M as of December 2025 with -$394.7M annual operating cash burn) will likely require dilutive capital raise before commercial revenue materializes [1][4]
