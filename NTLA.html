Company Profile
Healthcare ·Biotechnology ·NASDAQ ·United States
Intellia Therapeutics is a clinical-stage genome editing company focused on developing potentially curative therapies using CRISPR/Cas9 technology. Lead programs include nexiguran ziclumeran (nex-z) for transthyretin amyloidosis (ATTR) in partnership with Regeneron, with Phase 3 MAGNITUDE trial ongoing after FDA lifted a clinical hold in March 2026. Pipeline spans in vivo and ex vivo gene editing across ATTR, hemophilia A, and other genetic diseases using proprietary lipid nanoparticle delivery. Founded in 2014, headquartered in Cambridge, Massachusetts. Post-restructuring headcount of approximately 400 following 27% workforce reduction in 2025.

Investment Thesis
Intellia is pioneering in vivo CRISPR gene editing with nex-z (nexiguran ziclumeran) for ATTR amyloidosis, the first-ever systemic CRISPR therapy to reach Phase 3 clinical trials. Phase 1 data published in NEJM demonstrated 87% serum TTR reduction at the highest dose, validating the proprietary lipid nanoparticle delivery platform [1]. The FDA lifted the clinical hold on the MAGNITUDE Phase 3 trial in March 2026, clearing the path for pivotal data that could support regulatory approval for a one-time curative treatment in a multi-billion dollar ATTR market [4]. The Regeneron partnership provides financial and development resources, and the pipeline extends into hemophilia A (Phase 1) and next-generation base editing and DNA writing platforms [1]. At $2.4B market cap, the stock prices in significant clinical risk but offers asymmetric upside if Phase 3 data are positive.
The bear case centers on existential clinical and financial risk. The nex-z clinical hold was triggered by a patient death from a grade 4 liver adverse event, raising fundamental safety questions about the lipid nanoparticle delivery platform that underpins the entire pipeline [4]. Cash of $376M against annual burn of $364M provides only ~12 months of runway, likely requiring dilutive equity financing in 2027 at potentially distressed valuations [3]. Two consecutive years of workforce reductions (15% in 2024, 27% in 2025) have hollowed out R&D capabilities beyond the lead program, narrowing the pipeline optionality that justifies a platform biotech valuation. Revenue of $66M (entirely collaboration-dependent) with -$395M net loss and -56% ROE reflect a business with no path to standalone profitability without an approved product [3]. Competition from Alnylam's approved RNAi therapy (patisiran/vutrisiran) for ATTR gives physicians an established alternative if CRISPR safety concerns persist.
Key Catalysts
  1. MAGNITUDE Phase 3 trial data for nex-z in ATTR-CM expected to generate pivotal efficacy and safety data following FDA's March 2026 lift of clinical hold, potentially supporting regulatory filing
  2. Hemophilia A Phase 1 IND filing and initial clinical data expanding the in vivo CRISPR platform beyond ATTR into a $12B+ therapeutic market
  3. Base editing and DNA writing technology maturation providing next-generation precision editing capabilities beyond CRISPR/Cas9 knockdown approaches
Key Risks
  1. Nex-z safety profile remains uncertain following the grade 4 liver adverse event that triggered the FDA clinical hold—any additional serious adverse events in MAGNITUDE could permanently derail the program and the entire LNP platform
  2. Cash runway of approximately 12 months ($376M cash, $364M annual burn) will likely require dilutive equity financing in 2027 at a time when the stock is near 52-week lows
  3. Competition from Alnylam's approved RNAi therapies (vutrisiran) for ATTR provides physicians with a proven alternative, and the established safety profile of RNAi may limit CRISPR adoption even if nex-z demonstrates efficacy
