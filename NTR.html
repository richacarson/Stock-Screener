Company Profile
Basic Materials ·Agricultural Inputs ·NYSE ·Canada
Nutrien is the world's largest provider of crop inputs and services, operating through four segments: Retail (crop nutrients, protection, seed, and agronomic services), Potash (mining and processing), Nitrogen (ammonia, urea, UAN production), and Phosphate (solid/liquid fertilizers). Formed in 2018 through the merger of PotashCorp and Agrium, Nutrien operates the world's largest potash production capacity and the largest agricultural retail network.

Investment Thesis
Nutrien is the world's largest crop inputs company with dominant positions in potash mining, nitrogen production, and agricultural retail. Full-year 2025 results showed adjusted EBITDA of $6.05B with record upstream fertilizer sales volumes, and 2026 guidance projects continued momentum driven by strong global potash demand (74-77Mt global shipments) and 200,000 tonnes of brownfield potash expansion [1]. At 15.6x forward P/E with a 2.92% dividend yield and 6 consecutive years of dividend growth, NTR offers attractive income with valuation support from improving fundamentals. The structural need to feed a growing global population creates durable demand for Nutrien's products, while the company's integrated mine-to-farm model provides a competitive moat that pure-play producers cannot match.
The bear case centers on commodity cyclicality, weak profitability metrics, and leadership instability. Revenue has declined from the $37.9B peak in 2022 to $26.9B in 2025, a 29% decline driven by fertilizer price normalization after the Russia-Ukraine supercycle, and ROE of 9.2% is well below the 15% target [5]. Profit margin of 8.7% is thin for a company with Nutrien's scale advantages. CEO turnover — three leadership transitions since 2018 — creates strategic uncertainty and undermines long-term execution confidence [3]. Potash prices remain well below 2022 peaks, and a further decline would compress margins given the high fixed-cost nature of mining operations. Environmental controversies including waterway pollution at the Mokelumne River, the Aurora phosphate mine accident, and criticism of ESN polymer-coated nitrogen products create regulatory and reputational risk [4]. The agricultural inputs market is increasingly competitive with Mosaic, CF Industries, and ICL all expanding capacity. Dividend growth has been modest (4.2% 5yr CAGR) relative to the income investor expectations embedded in the 2.92% yield.
Key Catalysts
  1. Global potash demand growth to 74-77Mt in 2026 with Nutrien targeting 14.1-14.8Mt sales volumes, supported by strong crop affordability and offshore consumption growth [1]
  2. Brownfield potash expansion of 200,000 tonnes in 2026 and nitrogen product optimization projects that increase capacity without greenfield capex risk [1]
  3. Digital agronomy platform expansion and precision agriculture services that differentiate Nutrien's retail network from commodity-only competitors [3]
Key Risks
  1. Fertilizer price cyclicality — potash and nitrogen prices remain well below 2022 peaks, and further declines would compress margins on high-fixed-cost mining operations [1]
  2. CEO and leadership instability — three CEO transitions since Nutrien's 2018 formation (Mayo Schmidt, interim, Ken Seitz) creates strategic uncertainty [3]
  3. Environmental and regulatory risk — waterway pollution allegations, Aurora mine accident, and ESN polymer-coated nitrogen criticism expose Nutrien to tightening environmental regulations and potential remediation costs [4]
