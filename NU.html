Company Profile
Financial Services ·Banks - Regional ·NYSE ·Brazil
Nu Holdings Ltd. operates the largest independent digital banking platform in the world, headquartered in Sao Paulo, Brazil. Serving over 100 million customers across Brazil, Mexico, and Colombia, the company provides a full suite of digital financial services including credit cards, personal accounts, loans, investments, insurance, and cryptocurrency trading through its mobile-first platform. Founded in 2013 by David Velez.

Investment Thesis
Nu Holdings is the world's largest independent digital bank with 100M+ customers, delivering Q4 2025 net income of $895M (+50% YoY), record 33% ROE, and revenue of $4.9B (+45%) with unit economics steadily improving to $15 ARPAC (+27% YoY) [1]. The financial profile is remarkable: 41% profit margins, 30% ROE, 44% revenue growth, and a Forward PEG of 0.81 making it one of the most attractively valued high-growth names globally at just 11.8x forward earnings [4]. Management has declared 2026 an 'inflection year' for AI, international expansion, and product diversification, with next earnings on May 14, 2026 expected to show continued momentum [1].
The bear case centers on geographic concentration and currency risk: the vast majority of revenue comes from Brazil, where economic volatility, currency depreciation (BRL), and regulatory changes could materially impact dollar-denominated returns [1]. Credit risk is a structural concern—unsecured lending surpassed $8B with record $4B quarterly originations, and a Brazilian economic downturn could trigger significant loan losses. The stock trades at 23x trailing P/E which, while modest for growth, embeds expectations of continued 40%+ growth that may decelerate as the Brazilian market matures. Expansion into Mexico and Colombia requires heavy investment with uncertain returns in markets where Nubank lacks the brand dominance it has in Brazil [2].
Key Catalysts
  1. Q1 2026 earnings on May 14 expected to show $5.06B revenue, with ARPAC growth and expanding product attach rates demonstrating improving unit economics at 100M+ customer scale [1]
  2. Mexico and Colombia expansion reaching inflection points with customer acquisition accelerating and path to profitability clearing—creating a multi-country growth story beyond Brazil [2]
  3. AI-driven personalization and credit underwriting improvements expected to further reduce cost-to-serve and improve loan loss rates, with CEO Velez targeting global leadership in AI for financial services [2]
Key Risks
  1. Brazilian macroeconomic and currency risk: economic volatility, BRL depreciation, and potential regulatory changes in Brazil's banking sector could compress dollar-denominated earnings [1]
  2. Credit quality risk: unsecured lending of $8B+ with record originations creates exposure to a potential Brazilian economic downturn driving elevated loan losses [1]
  3. Growth deceleration risk: as the Brazilian market matures toward saturation (100M+ customers in a country of 215M), maintaining 40%+ revenue growth becomes increasingly difficult without successful international scaling [2]
