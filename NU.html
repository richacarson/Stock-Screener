Company Profile
Financial Services ·Banks - Regional ·NYSE ·Brazil
Nu Holdings operates the world's largest digital banking platform by customer count, serving over 131 million customers across Brazil, Mexico, and Colombia. The company offers digital bank accounts, credit cards, personal loans, investments, insurance, and a growing suite of financial products through a mobile-first, AI-powered platform that has disrupted traditional banking in Latin America.

Investment Thesis
Nu Holdings is the world's largest digital bank by customers (131M) with exceptional financial metrics: FY2025 revenue of $10.6B (+45% YoY), net income of $2.9B (+46% YoY), ROE of 30.3%, and profit margins of 41% [1]. At 12.0x forward earnings with a PEG of 0.81, Nubank is arguably the most attractively valued high-growth financial platform globally — growing revenue 44% while trading cheaper than traditional banks growing at low single digits. The $32.7B credit portfolio is expanding 40% YoY, ARPAC is growing 27% to $15/month, and geographic expansion into Mexico and Colombia provides a multi-year growth runway with 660M+ people across Latin America [1][3].
The bear case centers on emerging market macro risk and credit quality. Brazil's economic volatility — currency depreciation, inflation, and political instability — creates earnings translation risk for a company reporting in USD but earning in BRL [2]. The $32.7B credit portfolio growing 40% YoY in a potential economic slowdown could lead to rising non-performing loans; Nubank's track record through severe credit cycles is limited since it only became profitable in 2023. Competition from traditional banks investing in digital (Itau, Bradesco) and Big Tech (Apple, Google) entering financial services creates market share risk. Regulatory risk is acute as Nubank seeks a US banking charter, which invites heightened scrutiny. At $67B market cap, the valuation requires sustained 40%+ growth in a region prone to macroeconomic shocks [1][6].
Key Catalysts
  1. Mexico and Colombia customer growth acceleration — these markets have even lower banking penetration than Brazil, providing a multi-year TAM expansion opportunity with 130M+ potential customers [1][3]
  2. ARPAC expansion from $15/month toward developed-market digital bank levels through cross-selling insurance, investments, and credit products to the existing 131M customer base [1]
  3. US banking charter pursuit could open the largest banking market globally and validate Nubank's platform for international expansion beyond Latin America [4]
Key Risks
  1. Brazilian macroeconomic volatility — BRL depreciation, inflation spikes, and political instability create earnings translation risk and potential credit quality deterioration in the $32.7B loan portfolio [2]
  2. Credit cycle risk — Nubank has never been tested through a severe Latin American recession as a scaled lender; 40% credit portfolio growth during a potential slowdown could drive NPL spikes [1]
  3. Competitive intensification from traditional banks digitizing rapidly (Itau, Bradesco) and Big Tech players entering Latin American financial services could compress Nubank's growth and margin advantages [3]

AI Resilience LOW RISK
7
AI Risk Protection (6/9): Banking is structurally protected from AI disruption by deep regulatory moats (banking licenses, capital requirements, deposit insurance), switching costs (salary deposits, credit history), and the physical-world nature of financial infrastructure [3]. Nubank's digital-native architecture actually benefits from AI advances — AI improves credit scoring, fraud detection, and customer service at scale. However, AI-powered fintechs and Big Tech (Apple, Google Pay) could compete for digital banking customers in emerging markets, creating moderate competitive pressure [4]. AI Opportunity (1/1): Nubank generates quantifiable AI-driven value through the Hyperplane acquisition — AI powers credit underwriting for $32.7B in loans, fraud prevention across 131M accounts, and personalized product recommendations that drive ARPAC growth of 27% YoY [1][4].
