Investment Thesis
Nubank is the world's largest digital bank with 131M customers, delivering exceptional financial performance — 45% revenue growth, 62% net income growth, 41% profit margin, 30.3% ROE, and a 19.9% efficiency ratio that is best-in-class globally [1]. At a forward P/E of 12.0x, the stock is attractively valued for a 40%+ growth company with expanding margins and multiple growth vectors (Mexico, Colombia, U.S. entry, SME banking, insurance) [6]. Total credit portfolio growth of 40% YoY to $32.7B with risk-adjusted NIM stable at 10.5% demonstrates disciplined lending growth [1]. ARPAC of $15 (up 27% YoY) confirms deepening monetization per customer, and the digital-native platform provides structural cost advantages over traditional banks that are nearly impossible to replicate.
The bear case centers on Latin American macro risk and credit cycle exposure. Brazil's economic volatility, currency risk (BRL/USD), and potential regulatory changes could impact Nubank's growth trajectory [1]. The credit portfolio grew 40% YoY to $32.7B — rapid credit expansion in emerging markets historically correlates with eventual asset quality deterioration. The U.S. banking expansion is high-risk against entrenched incumbents and fellow digital challengers. Return-to-office investments and AI spending will pressure the efficiency ratio near-term [1]. The stock has fallen from $16.39 to $13.94, and the $67.7B market cap requires sustained 40%+ growth to justify. International expansion to Mexico and Colombia is still early-stage with limited profitability, and political risk across Latin America remains elevated.
Key Catalysts
  1. U.S. banking charter approval and launch, opening the world's largest banking market to Nubank's proven digital-first model [1]
  2. AI acceleration in 2026 improving credit decisions, fraud detection, and operational efficiency across 131M customers [1]
  3. Mexico and Colombia inflection — deepening monetization (ARPAC growth) and achieving country-level profitability in these markets [1]

  3. U.S. market entry execution risk — competing against JPMorgan, Bank of America, and established neobanks in the most competitive banking market globally [1]
