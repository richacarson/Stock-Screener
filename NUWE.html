Company Profile
Healthcare ·Medical Devices ·NASDAQ ·United States
Nuwellis is a medical device company focused on ultrafiltration therapy for fluid overload. Its Aquadex SmartFlow system removes excess fluid from patients with hypervolemia unresponsive to diuretics, indicated for both adult and pediatric patients weighing 20 kg or more. The company sells through a 24-person direct sales force in the U.S. and recently exited international operations to focus on domestic growth.
38 Employees ·www.nuwellis.com

Investment Thesis
Nuwellis is a niche medical device company with the only FDA-cleared ultrafiltration system specifically designed for fluid overload in heart failure patients. The company achieved a meaningful milestone with pediatric revenue reaching ~50% of U.S. sales in 2025, and U.S. console sales surged 208% in Q4 2025 [1]. The first outpatient Aquadex treatment under a 297% higher CMS reimbursement rate could unlock an entirely new revenue channel beyond hospital settings [1]. With 38 employees and a focused 24-person direct sales team covering 24 U.S. territories, the company is lean and commercially focused. At a market cap of $3.2M against $8.3M in revenue, the stock is trading well below revenue — offering significant upside if commercial execution accelerates.
The bear case is existential. Nuwellis has a $3.2M market cap, burns $13M in cash annually against $8.3M in revenue, and has seen its stock price decline 97% from its 52-week high of $45.36 to $1.23 [3]. Revenue has stagnated or declined for four consecutive years ($8.5M in 2022, $8.9M in 2023, $8.7M in 2024, $8.3M in 2025), suggesting fundamental demand challenges despite the clinical need for ultrafiltration therapy. The company will almost certainly need additional capital raises to survive, which would further dilute the already-tiny float. The exit from international operations in 2025 reduces the addressable market. Forward EPS of -$2.59 against a $1.23 stock price implies the company is expected to lose more than 2x its stock price per share in the next year [3]. This is a speculative micro-cap with binary outcomes — either the outpatient reimbursement opportunity drives a commercial inflection, or the company runs out of cash.

  2. Pediatric category reaching 50% of U.S. revenue provides a second growth vector with higher clinical urgency and less competition from diuretic alternatives [1]

  1. Cash burn of $13M annually against $8.3M revenue will necessitate additional dilutive capital raises within 12-18 months, with uncertain financing availability at a $3.2M market cap [3]
  2. Four consecutive years of stagnant/declining revenue ($8.5M-$8.9M range) despite niche monopoly position suggests the addressable market may be fundamentally smaller than management's estimates [3]
  3. Stock has declined 97% from 52-week high; NASDAQ minimum bid price compliance risk could force reverse stock splits or delisting if shares remain below $1.00 [3]
