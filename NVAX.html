Company Profile
Healthcare ·Biotechnology ·NASDAQ ·United States
Novavax is a biotechnology company that discovers, develops, and commercializes vaccines to prevent serious infectious diseases. Products include Nuvaxovid (COVID-19 vaccine) and the R21/Matrix-M adjuvant malaria vaccine. The company has collaboration agreements with Sanofi, Pfizer, Takeda, and Serum Life Sciences. Founded in 1987 and headquartered in Gaithersburg, Maryland.
749 Employees ·www.novavax.com

Investment Thesis
Novavax offers a speculative turnaround play anchored by its proprietary Matrix-M adjuvant platform and the transformative Sanofi partnership. Q1 2026 revenue of $140M beat estimates with supply sales surging 139%, and the company achieved a +75% EPS surprise demonstrating improving execution [1]. The Sanofi flu-COVID combination vaccine partnership provides a clear path to value realization through defined milestones — $75M for tech transfer (received), $125M upon Phase III initiation, and $225M upon US launch — potentially totaling $425M+ in milestone payments before royalties [2]. The Matrix-M adjuvant platform has been validated by the WHO-recommended R21 malaria vaccine and is being licensed to multiple partners, creating a technology-licensing revenue stream independent of Novavax's own commercialization capabilities [4]. With $790M in cash, positive free cash flow of $58.6M, and a market cap of just $1.46B, the stock trades at roughly 1.7x cash — implying the market assigns minimal value to the Sanofi partnership, pipeline, or platform technology [5].
The bear case is severe and centers on fundamental business deterioration. Revenue is collapsing from $1,123M in FY2025 (inflated by one-time Sanofi deal payments) to guidance of just $230-270M in FY2026, a decline of 76-78% that overstates the company's true earning power [1]. Negative equity of -$127.8M, negative profit margins of -14.73%, and inability to generate operating profit (operating cash flow -$91.6M) indicate a company burning through its cash reserves with no clear path to self-sustaining profitability [5]. The 27.6% short interest signals significant institutional skepticism. Management credibility is impaired — a major shareholder is opposing CEO Jacobs' pay package, citing 'destruction of shareholder value' and 'chronic operational shortcomings,' while Glassdoor reviews describe leadership instability and frequent layoffs [3]. Novavax's fate is now largely in Sanofi's hands: if the flu-COVID combination vaccine fails clinical trials or Sanofi deprioritizes the program, Novavax has no viable standalone commercial path. The 749-person workforce and negative equity leave minimal margin for further setbacks [5].
