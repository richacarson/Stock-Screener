Company Profile

Novavax is a biotechnology company focused on the discovery, development, and commercialization of vaccines to prevent serious infectious diseases. The company commercializes Nuvaxovid (COVID-19 vaccine) and has a partnership-driven strategy leveraging its proprietary Matrix-M adjuvant technology platform through collaborations with Sanofi, Pfizer, and the Serum Institute.

Investment Thesis
Novavax is a speculative turnaround play centered on the Matrix-M adjuvant platform — a proven technology licensed to Pfizer ($30M upfront, up to $500M in milestones) and partnered with Sanofi ($225M in 2025 milestones earned) [1]. The company ended 2025 with $857M in cash, claims funding runway 'into 2028,' and is aggressively cutting costs ($325M R&D/SG&A target in 2026, down from $685M) [1]. At $1.3B market cap and $8.14/share, the stock prices in significant downside — if the Pfizer milestones and Sanofi partnership execute on schedule, the risk/reward is asymmetric. The protein-based vaccine platform provides an alternative for mRNA-contraindicated patients, and the malaria vaccine collaboration addresses one of the world's largest unmet health needs.
The bear case is substantial: 2026 adjusted revenue guidance of $230-270M (vs. $1.1B in 2025 which included $625M in non-cash APA resolutions) represents the true revenue run rate, and forward EPS is negative [1]. The Phase 3 clinical hold on flu and combo COVID/flu vaccines (lifted but delayed) damaged pipeline credibility. Novavax has lost 90%+ of market value over five years as mRNA competitors captured the vaccine market [2]. Beta of 2.63 makes this extremely volatile. Glassdoor rating of 3.1/5 with 27% recommendation rate and constant layoffs suggest organizational dysfunction that could impair execution [2]. The company is entirely dependent on partnership milestones — if Pfizer or Sanofi timelines slip, the cash runway shrinks. No dividend, negative forward earnings, and the speculative nature of biotech milestone revenue make this unsuitable for income or conservative portfolios [4].
Key Catalysts
  1. Pfizer Matrix-M licensing milestones — up to $500M in development and sales milestones beyond the $30M upfront payment, with two infectious disease programs in development [1]
  2. Sanofi partnership execution for COVID-19 vaccine commercialization, building on $225M in 2025 milestones earned and ongoing supply agreements [1]
