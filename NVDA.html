Investment Thesis
NVIDIA is the defining technology company of the AI era, delivering $215.9B in FY2026 revenue (up 65%), $58.1B in free cash flow, and 101% ROE with 90% AI chip market share. Q1 FY2027 guidance of $78B implies continued acceleration, and the company has $500B revenue visibility through end of 2026 from Blackwell and Rubin platforms. The forward P/E of 15.5x on forward EPS of $11.13 is remarkably reasonable for a company growing revenue 65%+ with $62.6B in net cash. Hyperscaler capex approaching $700B annually provides massive demand runway.
The bear case centers on concentration risk — 50%+ of data center revenue comes from hyperscalers who are developing custom ASICs (Google TPU, Amazon Trainium, Microsoft Maia). The Inspire score of -49 creates a significant faith alignment headwind. Gross margins declining from peak to 75% as Blackwell ramps suggest product transition pressures, and China export restrictions eliminate a major addressable market. If AI spending growth decelerates or proves cyclical, NVIDIA's $172 stock price at $4.2T market cap would face severe compression. The company's minimal 0.02% dividend yield provides no income cushion.
