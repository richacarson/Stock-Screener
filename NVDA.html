Investment Thesis
NVIDIA is the undisputed infrastructure backbone of the AI revolution, with FY2026 revenue of $215.9B (+65%), net income of $120.1B, and free cash flow of $96.6B. At a forward P/E of 15.5x with forward PEG of 0.12x, the stock is remarkably cheap relative to its growth trajectory. Q1 FY2027 guidance of $78B (+15% sequential) exceeded consensus by $5B, and supply commitments doubling to $95.2B signal multi-year demand visibility. The Vera Rubin architecture and expanding inference market provide runway for sustained growth as enterprise AI adoption accelerates.
The primary risks are concentration and cyclicality: 91% of revenue comes from data centers, and hyperscaler capex cycles could create demand volatility if AI monetization disappoints. China export restrictions cost $5.5B in FY2025 charges and limit a major growth market. NVIDIA's greenhouse gas footprint doubled from 2023 to 2025 as AI energy consumption surges, creating potential regulatory headwinds. Custom silicon efforts by Google, Amazon, and Microsoft threaten to erode NVIDIA's dominance in inference workloads over time, though CUDA's software moat remains formidable.
