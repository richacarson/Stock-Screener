Company Profile
Industrials ·Waste Management ·NYSE ·United States
Enviri Corporation (formerly Harsco) provides environmental solutions for industrial and specialty waste streams through three segments: Harsco Environmental (steel mill services, resource recovery, and recycling), Clean Earth (hazardous/non-hazardous specialty waste processing), and Harsco Rail (railway track maintenance equipment and services). Founded in 1853, the company operates globally from Philadelphia.

Investment Thesis
Enviri is a deeply contrarian environmental services play trading at $1.6B market cap for a company generating $2.24B in revenue with global leadership in steel mill environmental services across 150+ customer sites in 30+ countries [1]. The inclusion in Corporate Knights' 2026 Global 100 Most Sustainable Corporations validates the environmental positioning, and 100% of revenue deriving from environmental solutions provides secular tailwind exposure as circular economy and waste processing regulations tighten globally [4]. Clean Earth's 4% growth and strong market position in specialty hazardous waste processing addresses a growing regulatory-driven demand. The stock has bounced above its 50-day SMA of $18.81, and the potential Clean Earth sale could generate significant proceeds to address the overleveraged balance sheet. At essentially breakeven forward EPS, any operational improvement could drive meaningful upside from a depressed base [1].
The bear case is dominated by financial distress and structural challenges. D/E of 576% with $1.70B debt against $105M cash is dangerously leveraged — the company generates only $101M operating cash flow against a massive debt burden, and any downturn in steel production volumes would threaten debt covenants [5]. Trailing EPS of -$2.03, ROE of -44%, and profit margin of -7.5% indicate the business is destroying value at current operating levels. Revenue declined 0.8% in 2025 and 2026 guidance projects essentially flat performance, with no identified catalysts for a return to growth [1]. The potential sale of Clean Earth — the best-performing segment — could generate deleveraging proceeds but would remove the growth engine, leaving the company with declining Harsco Environmental and troubled Rail segments. Gross margins of 19.1% provide minimal buffer against cost inflation or volume declines. The beta of 1.72 amplifies downside risk, and the suspended dividend (5-year avg 4.67% yield) eliminates income support for the share price [5].
Key Catalysts
  1. Potential Clean Earth sale generating significant deleveraging proceeds to reduce the $1.70B debt burden and reset the capital structure [1]
  2. Global 100 Most Sustainable Corporations recognition and tightening environmental regulations driving secular demand for waste processing and circular economy services [4]
  3. Restructuring and cost actions in Rail segment addressing ETO contract risks, with potential for margin improvement as problematic contracts roll off [1]
Key Risks
  1. Extreme leverage — D/E of 576% with $1.70B debt creates significant refinancing and covenant risk if steel mill volumes decline or interest rates remain elevated [5]
  2. Revenue decline and flat guidance — 2025 revenue fell 4% with 2026 projecting no growth, indicating the business is shrinking under its debt burden [1]
  3. Potential Clean Earth sale removing the growth engine — if the best-performing segment is divested for deleveraging, the remaining business has declining revenue and weaker margins [1]

AI Risk Protection (5/9): Enviri's business is highly physical and resistant to AI disruption — steel mill waste processing, soil remediation, and railway maintenance require on-site equipment operation and manual labor that AI cannot replace [3]. The company's relationships with steel mills are deeply embedded (operating at 150+ customer sites) and switching costs are high. Competitors could use AI for route optimization and predictive maintenance scheduling, but these are incremental efficiency gains rather than existential threats. AI Opportunity (0/1): AI applications in Enviri's business are limited — potential uses include predictive equipment maintenance for railway services and process optimization for waste treatment, but these are marginal improvements unlikely to move the revenue or margin needle meaningfully [3].
