Company Profile
Industrials ·Electrical Equipment & Parts ·NYSE ·United Kingdom
nVent Electric designs, manufactures, and services electrical connection, protection, and thermal management solutions used across data centers, industrial, commercial, residential, and infrastructure markets. Spun off from Pentair in 2018, nVent has emerged as a leading supplier of liquid cooling, power distribution, and enclosures to AI data centers — often called 'a mini Vertiv' by Wall Street. Led by CEO/Chair Beth Wozniak.

Investment Thesis
nVent Electric is one of the purest and best-executed AI data center electrification plays in the public markets — FY2025 revenue grew 30% to a record $3.9B with data center revenue doubling to ~$1B and year-end backlog surging 3x to $2.3B [1]. Q4 2025 sales +42% (+24% organic), adjusted EPS +35% for the full year, and record $561M free cash flow demonstrate both growth and capital discipline [1]. Wozniak's leadership has produced a 99% CEO approval Glassdoor rating, Great Place To Work certification, and explicit 'new product vitality' KPI that crossed 20% with a 25% target — a rare infinite-game culture in industrials [2]. 2026 guidance of 15-18% reported sales growth and $4.00-$4.15 adjusted EPS (+20-24%) with infrastructure vertical expected >50% of sales validates the trajectory [1]. At 27.2x forward P/E, the stock is premium but reasonable relative to the growth rate.
The bear case centers on AI capex cyclicality and tariff exposure. 2026 tariff headwinds of ~$80M (concentrated in H1) add near-term pressure [1]. Data center revenue now ~25% of total and growing fast — a slowdown in hyperscaler AI capex (from any of Microsoft, Meta, Google, Amazon, or Oracle) could materially impact nVent's growth rate. At 51.7x trailing and 27.2x forward P/E, the stock prices in sustained 20%+ earnings growth, leaving no margin for error [4]. Competition from Vertiv (category leader), Eaton, and Schneider Electric in liquid cooling and power distribution is intensifying. Currency translation (Ireland-domiciled, global operations) and global trade tensions add secondary risk. That said, the combination of Wozniak's disciplined operating model, 99% CEO approval, clean ESG profile, AI-aligned TAM expansion, and accelerating order backlog supports a BUY rating despite the valuation.
Key Catalysts
  1. Data center revenue trajectory — from ~$1B in 2025 toward potentially $1.5B+ in 2026 given 3x backlog surge and accelerating liquid cooling adoption [1]
  2. Q1 2026 guidance of 34-36% reported sales growth and $0.90-$0.93 adjusted EPS provides a near-term catalyst to confirm the AI infrastructure trajectory [1]
  3. Infrastructure vertical crossing 50% of sales in 2026 would re-rate nVent toward pure-play AI infrastructure peers like Vertiv [1][3]
Key Risks
  1. Tariff absorption of ~$80M in 2026 (mainly H1) compresses near-term margins and creates execution risk on EPS guidance [1]
  2. AI capex cyclicality — a hyperscaler capex slowdown or pause would materially impact the data center segment now ~25% of total revenue [1]
  3. Forward P/E of 27.2x leaves no margin for growth deceleration; any quarter of AI-infrastructure order weakness could trigger multiple compression [4]
