Company Profile
Technology ·Semiconductors ·NASDAQ ·United States
Navitas Semiconductor is a pure-play next-generation power semiconductor company specializing in gallium nitride (GaN) power ICs and silicon carbide (SiC) technology. The company serves AI data center, EV, grid infrastructure, and industrial markets through its 'Navitas 2.0' strategy, pivoting from mobile/consumer toward high-power applications. Q1 2026 revenue of $8.6M was up 18% sequentially with high-power markets growing 35% YoY.

Investment Thesis
Navitas is the only pure-play next-generation power semiconductor company positioned at the intersection of AI data center power demand, EV electrification, and grid infrastructure — three secular growth markets requiring advanced GaN and SiC solutions [1]. Q1 2026 showed inflection: revenue up 18% sequentially to $8.6M, high-power markets growing 35% YoY, and gross margins expanding to 39% [1]. With $221M cash, minimal debt (D/E 1.5%), and new CEO Chris Allexandre's semiconductor scaling expertise from Renesas, the company has both runway and leadership to execute [2]. The India GaN partnership with Cyient and NVIDIA data center collaboration provide additional growth vectors. The stock has surged 88% YTD to $22.65, reflecting growing AI power infrastructure confidence.
The bear case is significant. Revenue declined 38.7% YoY to $40.5M with 2026 consensus of just $41.3M, meaning the revenue trough could extend through 2026 before meaningful recovery in 2027 [1]. The $5.3B market cap represents 130x TTM revenue — an extreme valuation requiring near-perfect execution. Seeking Alpha analysts warn this could be 'an AI value trap' with '$20M quarterly cash burn' needing '$150M+ liquidity to sustain a 2027 timeline' [2]. The leadership overhaul — founder CEO and CTO both departing — risks losing institutional knowledge during the critical 'Navitas 2.0' transformation. Competition from Infineon, TI, and STMicro with 100x+ resources targeting the same AI/EV power markets means Navitas must win design-ins against vastly better-resourced rivals. The stock has already priced in substantial AI optionality at $22.65 [2].

  3. Competition from Infineon, TI, and STMicro with 100x+ resources investing in GaN/SiC targets the exact same AI data center and EV power markets Navitas pursues [2]
