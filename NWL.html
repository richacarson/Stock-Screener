Company Profile
Consumer Cyclical ·Household & Personal Products ·NASDAQ ·United States
Newell Brands Inc. is a global consumer goods company with a portfolio of iconic brands including Sharpie, Rubbermaid, Yankee Candle, Coleman, Graco, NUK, Paper Mate, Elmer's, Contigo, and Oster. The company operates across three segments: Home and Commercial Solutions, Learning and Development, and Outdoor and Recreation. Headquartered in Atlanta, Georgia with approximately 25,000 employees. Undergoing a multi-year turnaround and portfolio restructuring under CEO Chris Peterson.

Investment Thesis
Newell Brands is a deep value turnaround play trading at 10.4x normalized forward earnings with a 4.75% dividend yield, offering significant upside if CEO Chris Peterson's restructuring succeeds [2][5]. Q1 2026 revenue of $1.55B beat expectations by $40M, gross margin improved to 33.1% (up 100bps YoY), and management raised full-year normalized EPS guidance to $0.56-$0.60 while guiding flat to +2% revenue growth — the first potential year of stabilization after five years of decline [2]. The company generates $433M in annual free cash flow despite net losses, owns iconic consumer brands (Sharpie, Rubbermaid, Yankee Candle, Coleman, Graco) with strong consumer recognition, and CEO Peterson's AI-forward strategy including generative AI for product development could accelerate margin improvement. At $2.5B market cap against $7.2B in revenue, the price-to-sales ratio of 0.34x implies extreme pessimism already priced in.
The bear case is formidable. Newell has posted three consecutive years of net losses totaling $889M (FY2023-FY2025), with revenue contracting 32% from $10.6B to $7.2B over five years [1]. The $5.3B debt load at D/E of 243% creates significant financial fragility — interest expense consumes a substantial portion of operating income. ROE of -11.17% and profit margin of -3.91% reflect a company that is destroying shareholder value. The dividend was already cut 70% in 2023 and remains vulnerable to further reduction if losses persist. Core categories (home organization, writing instruments, outdoor recreation) face secular headwinds from private-label competition, digital-native brands, and shifting consumer preferences. Morgan Stanley's 'Underweight' rating with a $4.94 average analyst target (below the current price after the recent surge) suggests limited institutional confidence [5]. The turnaround depends on cost restructuring generating enough savings to offset ongoing volume declines — a difficult equation with no margin for error.
Key Catalysts
  1. Revenue stabilization in FY2026 (guided flat to +2%) would end five consecutive years of decline and could re-rate the stock significantly from its deeply depressed 0.34x price-to-sales valuation
  2. Gross margin expansion from productivity actions and pricing optimization — Q1 2026 margins improved to 33.1%, with management targeting meaningful annual savings beginning in FY2027 from plant closures
  3. AI-driven product development and cost efficiency under CEO Peterson's strategic AI initiative, with potential to accelerate speed-to-market and reduce R&D costs across the brand portfolio
Key Risks
  1. Heavy debt burden of $5.3B (D/E 243%) constrains financial flexibility and consumes operating income through interest expense, with refinancing risk if turnaround progress stalls
  2. Five consecutive years of revenue decline ($10.6B to $7.2B) may not have bottomed — core categories face secular headwinds from private-label competition, e-commerce disruption, and shifting consumer preferences away from legacy brands
  3. Three consecutive years of net losses ($889M cumulative) and -11.17% ROE demonstrate that the current cost structure remains unprofitable, with risk of further dividend cuts if free cash flow deteriorates
