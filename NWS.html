Investment Thesis
News Corp offers a diversified media portfolio at a reasonable valuation ($16.5B market cap) anchored by the Wall Street Journal/Dow Jones franchise, REA Group's dominant Australian real estate platform, and HarperCollins' record publishing results [1]. The OpenAI content licensing partnership creates a new AI-driven revenue stream, and the digital transformation (50%+ digital revenue) positions the company to monetize its premium content assets in an AI-first media landscape. Q2 FY2026 revenue beat estimates at $2.36B with 6% YoY growth, showing operational momentum [1].
The bear case rests on structural media industry decline: 3-year revenue CAGR of -6.6% (partly due to divestitures) reflects a shrinking legacy business, while the 24x forward P/E and 3.53 PEG ratio suggest the market already prices in recovery that may not materialize [6]. AI-generated content poses an existential threat to traditional journalism economics, as AI search summaries reduce website traffic and advertisers shift budgets to AI-powered platforms. The Murdoch family's controlling stake creates governance risk, and the 0.66% dividend yield provides minimal income support. At 6.3% ROE, the company is not generating adequate returns on invested capital to justify its premium valuation.
Key Catalysts
  1. OpenAI content licensing revenue scaling and potential expansion to additional AI platform partnerships, monetizing News Corp's proprietary data and journalism assets
  2. HarperCollins maintaining record revenue momentum as publishing benefits from social media-driven book discovery and the 'Heated Rivalry' franchise success
  3. Dow Jones digital subscription growth accelerating as Wall Street Journal and Barron's expand international reach and AI-enhanced data analytics products
Key Risks
  1. AI-generated content and AI search summaries (Google AI Overviews, ChatGPT) cannibalizing website traffic and undermining the advertising and subscription model that funds professional journalism
  2. Murdoch family succession and governance concentration creating strategic uncertainty, with the dual-class share structure limiting minority shareholder influence
  3. Structural decline in print media and traditional advertising accelerating faster than digital revenue growth can offset, particularly in the News Media segment

AI Resilience (25%)
AI Resilience MODERATE

AI Risk Protection (4/9): News Corp faces meaningful AI disruption risk across its media and publishing segments. AI-generated content threatens traditional journalism economics, while AI search summaries (Google AI Overviews, ChatGPT) reduce click-through traffic to news websites, directly pressuring digital advertising and subscription growth [2]. HarperCollins faces AI-generated book competition and potential disintermediation of traditional publishing. However, News Corp's premium data assets (Factiva, Dow Jones Newswires, WSJ) have defensible value as AI training data, and the company has proactively monetized this through its OpenAI licensing deal. AI Opportunity (1/1): News Corp is generating measurable AI revenue through its content licensing partnership with OpenAI, which pays for access to News Corp's premium journalism and data corpus for AI model training. The Dow Jones data and analytics division is well-positioned to sell AI-enhanced financial intelligence products [2].
