Company Profile

Nextdoor Holdings operates a hyperlocal social networking platform connecting neighbors, local businesses, and public agencies across 11 countries. The platform enables local news, community discussions, goods exchange, and local business discovery. Founded in 2011, the company went public via SPAC in 2021 and has approximately 21 million weekly active users.

Investment Thesis
Nextdoor trades at a compelling valuation on a cash-adjusted basis — $405M cash against a $549M market cap means the market values the operating business at just $144M, or 0.6x revenue [1]. The company achieved positive full-year adjusted EBITDA a year ahead of schedule, and net losses narrowed from $98M in 2024 to $54M in 2025. CEO Tolia's return brings founder energy to a product overhaul. The neighborhood network occupies a unique niche with 21M WAU across 11 countries, and local advertising is a $150B+ addressable market. If the product transformation succeeds in reversing user decline, the stock has significant upside from current levels.
The bear case is substantial. Revenue grew only 4% in FY2025 — barely above inflation — while weekly active users declined 5% YoY, indicating the platform is losing relevance [1]. With just 463 employees, Nextdoor lacks the engineering resources to compete with Meta, Google, and emerging AI platforms for local information and advertising. The -21% profit margin means the company continues burning cash despite cost cuts. AI assistants pose an existential threat: as consumers increasingly ask AI for local recommendations and information, the core use case for visiting Nextdoor erodes. The stock has lost ~90% from its 2021 SPAC highs, and there is no evidence the product overhaul will reverse structural user decline. Glassdoor reviews suggest internal dysfunction that may prevent effective execution [2].
Key Catalysts
  1. Product overhaul under returning CEO Tolia could reignite user growth and reverse the 5% WAU decline if new features resonate with neighborhoods [2]
  2. Cash-rich balance sheet ($405M cash, $549M market cap) provides runway and potential for strategic M&A or shareholder returns [1]
  3. Continued path to profitability — adjusted EBITDA turned positive in FY2025, and further margin improvement could re-rate the stock [1]
Key Risks
  1. AI substitution risk — Google AI, ChatGPT, and Meta AI increasingly serve the 'local information discovery' use case that is Nextdoor's core value proposition [2]
  2. Declining user base (21M WAU, -5% YoY) signals platform irrelevance and makes the advertising flywheel harder to sustain [1]
  3. Minimal workforce of 463 employees constrains engineering capacity for product transformation while competing against trillion-dollar platforms [1]
