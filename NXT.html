Company Profile
Technology ·Solar ·NASDAQ ·United States
Nextpower Inc. (formerly Nextracker Inc.) provides intelligent solar tracker technologies, foundation systems, and software solutions for utility-scale and distributed generation solar applications worldwide. The company's products include NX Horizon trackers, TrueCapture energy yield optimization, NX Navigator monitoring platform, and NX Anchor foundation systems. Rebranded in November 2025, the company has shipped over 150 GW of tracker systems across 40 countries.
1,300 Employees ·www.nextpower.com

Investment Thesis
Nextpower (formerly Nextracker) is the global market leader in solar trackers with 150 GW shipped, a $5B+ record backlog, and the strongest financial profile in the solar supply chain — 33.2% ROE, near-zero leverage, $908M net cash, and $454M in trailing free cash flow [1][2]. The company sits at the intersection of two structural tailwinds: accelerating global solar deployment and surging electricity demand from AI data centers, both of which directly drive tracker demand.
The HOLD rating reflects exceptional business quality and positioning, tempered by a PEG ratio of 2.96 that suggests the market already prices in much of the growth story. FY26 guidance of $3.3-3.5B revenue (10-16% growth) is decelerating from 33.9% YoY, and the Nextpower rebranding with AI/robotics expansion introduces execution risk in adjacent markets where the company has no track record. Tariff and trade policy uncertainty around solar components remains a persistent overhang. The $5B+ backlog and founder-CEO leadership provide strong downside protection, making this a conviction growth holding for investors willing to pay the premium [1][3].
Key Catalysts
  1. Record backlog exceeding $5B with Q2 FY26 revenue up 42% YoY to $905M, driven by surging electricity demand from AI data centers accelerating solar deployment [1][2]
  2. Launch of new AI and robotics business unit with $40M+ in technology acquisitions and first Chief AI and Robotics Officer, positioning Nextpower as an intelligent clean energy platform [3]
  3. Nextpower rebranding signals strategic expansion beyond trackers into a broader clean energy technology ecosystem, potentially expanding addressable market significantly [5]
Key Risks
  1. PEG ratio of 2.96 near the floor threshold signals stretched valuation — FY26 revenue guidance of $3.3-3.5B implies growth deceleration from 33.9% to 10-16%, which could compress multiples if the market expects sustained high growth [1][2]
  2. Tariff and trade policy uncertainty on solar components could disrupt supply chains and increase costs, particularly given Nextracker's global manufacturing and deployment footprint across 40 countries [4]
  3. AI/robotics business expansion introduces execution risk in adjacent markets where the company has no proven track record, and Glassdoor reports of reactive layoffs suggest workforce management growing pains [3][7]
