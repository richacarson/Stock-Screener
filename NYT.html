Investment Thesis
The New York Times Company delivered 2025 revenue of $2.825B (up 9.2%) with 450,000 net digital subscribers added in Q4 alone and average digital ARPU rising to $9.72 as subscribers rolled off promotional pricing [1]. Subscription revenue of $1.95B (up 9.1%) is now 76.7% of total revenue, creating a high-quality recurring revenue flywheel [1]. 2026 Q1 guidance calls for digital-only subscription revenue growth of 14-17% and total subscription 9-11%, with digital advertising growing high-teens to low-20s [1]. CEO Meredith Kopit Levien has positioned NYT on the right side of the AI era — suing OpenAI/Microsoft and Perplexity for copyright infringement while signing a lucrative licensing deal with Amazon (May 2025) [2]. The bundle strategy (News + Games + Cooking + Wirecutter + The Athletic) and 173-year editorial brand create durable retention advantages. Debt-to-equity of just 2.4% gives the balance sheet exceptional flexibility [4].
The bear case centers on AI disintermediation. ChatGPT, Perplexity, and Google AI Overviews can summarize news without delivering traffic to NYT, eroding both subscription and advertising economics over time [2]. The OpenAI litigation is in its third year with no settlement, creating legal cost overhang [2]. At 25.8x forward P/E, NYT trades at a premium to publishing peers — justified by subscription quality but vulnerable to any subscriber growth slowdown [4]. Political polarization creates narrative risk as NYT's coverage choices can alienate subscribers on either side of the spectrum. Recession risk to advertising revenue (22% of total) adds cyclical exposure.
