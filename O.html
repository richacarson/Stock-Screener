Company Profile
Real Estate ·REIT - Retail ·NYSE ·United States
Realty Income Corporation is a real estate investment trust that invests in single-tenant commercial properties under long-term net-lease agreements. Branded as 'The Monthly Dividend Company,' Realty Income owns over 15,600 properties across the US, UK, and Europe, leased to ~1,550 clients across 90 industries. It is the largest net-lease REIT by market cap (~$58.5B) and an S&P 500 member.

Investment Thesis
Realty Income is the gold standard of net-lease REITs — a $58.5B market cap S&P 500 member with 55+ years of consecutive monthly dividends, 30+ years of dividend growth, 113 consecutive quarterly increases, and 98.9% portfolio occupancy across 15,600+ properties [1]. The 5.16% dividend yield at $65.50 provides compelling income, with raised AFFO guidance of $4.41-$4.44 for 2026 representing 3.0-3.7% growth and ~8-9% total operational return [1]. Q1 2026 results were strong: revenue of $1.55B beat expectations of $1.39B by 12%, and net income surged 25% YoY [1]. The investment volume target was raised from $8B to $9.5B at 7.1% initial cash yields, supported by the $1.5B GIC sovereign wealth partnership and private capital vehicles that expand Realty Income's investment capacity without proportional equity dilution [1]. D/E of 76% is well-managed, and the triple-net-lease model creates a highly durable cash flow stream with improving profit margins.
The bear case focuses on growth deceleration, valuation, and structural REIT challenges. AFFO per share growth guidance of 3.0-3.7% for 2026 is below Realty Income's long-stated ~5% target, raising questions about whether the company can grow per-share metrics at scale with a 933M+ share base [1]. Forward P/E of 39.99x (GAAP) creates sticker shock for non-REIT investors, though P/AFFO of ~15x is more reasonable. The ~$29.5B in total debt creates meaningful interest rate sensitivity — if rates stay elevated, the spread between investment yields (7.1%) and cost of capital narrows [4]. Employee culture strain persists: Glassdoor shows 49% recommendation rate with complaints about below-market pay and loss of the 'family feel' as headcount scales [2]. Tenant concentration risk remains: Walgreens (largest tenant) faces pharmacy closures, and Dollar General/Dollar Tree face operational headwinds. The Gambling negative attribution in Inspire scoring reflects exposure to casino/gaming properties, a category Realty Income is actively expanding into.
Key Catalysts
  1. Raised $9.5B 2026 investment volume target (up from $8B) at 7.1% initial cash yields, with raised AFFO guidance of $4.41-$4.44 reflecting accelerating deployment capacity [1]
  2. Interest rate decline cycle could compress cap rates and expand Realty Income's valuation multiple while reducing its ~$29.5B net debt cost burden [4]
  3. European expansion, data center acquisitions, and $1.5B GIC sovereign wealth partnership diversifying the investment universe and capital sources beyond traditional US retail net-lease [1]
Key Risks
  1. AFFO per share growth of 3.0-3.7% guided for 2026 is below the ~5% long-term target, and the 933M+ share base makes per-share accretion increasingly difficult at scale [1]
  2. Interest rate sensitivity — ~$29.5B total debt means sustained higher-for-longer rates compress investment spread and increase refinancing cost, while Forward P/E of 39.99x reflects premium valuation [4]
  3. Tenant credit risk — largest tenants including Walgreens (pharmacy closures), Dollar General, and Dollar Tree face operational headwinds that could increase lease defaults above the guided 40-50bps credit loss [1]

AI Resilience (25%)
AI Resilience LOW RISK
8
AI Risk Protection (8/9): Realty Income's business is structurally immune to AI disruption — the company owns physical commercial properties under long-term triple-net leases where tenants (Walgreens, Dollar General, FedEx, Wynn Resorts) pay rent regardless of AI's impact on the broader economy [1]. Unlike real estate services firms (CBRE, JLL) that face AI disruption in brokerage and property management, net-lease REITs are landlords collecting contractual rent — AI cannot replicate or substitute physical property ownership [2]. The 15,600+ property portfolio with 98.9% occupancy across 90 industries provides maximum diversification against any single-sector AI disruption. AI may even benefit Realty Income indirectly by increasing demand for data center properties, a growing asset class in the portfolio [1]. The raised $9.5B investment volume target for 2026 includes data center acquisitions that directly benefit from the AI infrastructure buildout [1]. AI Opportunity (0/1): No AI-related revenue; the company uses predictive analytics internally for underwriting but does not sell AI products or generate AI-specific revenue [2].
