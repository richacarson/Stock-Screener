Company Profile
Real Estate ·REIT - Retail ·NYSE ·United States
Realty Income Corporation is a real estate investment trust that invests in single-tenant commercial properties under long-term net-lease agreements. Branded as 'The Monthly Dividend Company,' Realty Income owns over 15,600 properties across the US, UK, and Europe, leased to ~1,550 clients across 90 industries. It is the largest net-lease REIT by market cap (~$56.6B) and an S&P 500 member.

Investment Thesis
Realty Income is the gold standard of net-lease REITs — a $56.6B market cap S&P 500 member with 55+ years of consecutive monthly dividends, 30+ years of dividend growth, and 98.9% portfolio occupancy across 15,600+ properties [1]. The 5.33% dividend yield at $60.69 provides compelling income in a declining rate environment, with AFFO guidance of $4.38-$4.42 for 2026 representing ~2.8% growth and ~9% total operational return [1]. The $8B 2026 investment target at 7.1% initial cash yields is highly accretive, supported by the new $1.5B GIC sovereign wealth partnership and private capital vehicles that expand Realty Income's investment capacity without proportional equity dilution [1]. D/E of 73.5% is well-managed, and the triple-net-lease model with 92.6% gross margins creates a highly durable cash flow stream.
The bear case focuses on growth deceleration and structural REIT challenges. AFFO per share growth guidance of ~2.8% for 2026 is well below Realty Income's long-stated ~5% target, raising questions about whether the company can grow per-share metrics at scale with a 933M+ share base [1]. Forward P/AFFO of ~14x is reasonable but not cheap versus historical averages, and the 34.6x GAAP P/E creates sticker shock for non-REIT investors. The $29.5B in total debt creates meaningful interest rate sensitivity — if rates stay elevated, the spread between investment yields (7.1%) and cost of capital narrows [4]. Employee culture strain is a risk: Glassdoor shows 49% recommendation rate with complaints about below-market pay and loss of the 'family feel' as headcount scales from ~350 to 544 [2]. Tenant concentration risk remains: Walgreens (largest tenant) faces pharmacy closures, and Dollar General/Dollar Tree face operational headwinds. The Gambling negative attribution in Inspire scoring reflects exposure to casino/gaming properties.
Key Catalysts
  1. Record $8B 2026 investment volume target at 7.1% initial cash yields, supported by $1.5B GIC sovereign wealth partnership and US Core Plus fund, driving AFFO accretion [1]
  2. Interest rate decline cycle could compress cap rates and expand Realty Income's valuation multiple while reducing its 5.4x net debt cost burden [1]
  3. European expansion and data center/gaming property diversification expanding the addressable investment universe beyond traditional US retail net-lease [1]
Key Risks
  1. AFFO per share growth of ~2.8% guided for 2026 is significantly below the ~5% long-term target, and the 933M+ share base makes per-share accretion increasingly difficult at scale [1]
  2. Interest rate sensitivity — $29.5B total debt means sustained higher-for-longer rates compress investment spread and increase refinancing cost [4]
  3. Tenant credit risk — largest tenants including Walgreens (pharmacy closures), Dollar General, and Dollar Tree face operational headwinds that could increase lease defaults above the guided 40-50bps credit loss [1]

AI Resilience (25%)
AI Resilience LOW RISK
8
AI Risk Protection (8/9): Realty Income's business is structurally immune to AI disruption — the company owns physical commercial properties under long-term triple-net leases where tenants (Walgreens, Dollar General, FedEx, Wynn Resorts) pay rent regardless of AI's impact on the broader economy [1]. Unlike real estate services firms (CBRE, JLL) that saw 12-14% stock drops on AI disruption fears in February 2026, net-lease REITs are landlords collecting contractual rent — AI cannot replicate or substitute physical property ownership [2]. The 15,600+ property portfolio with 98.9% occupancy across 90 industries provides maximum diversification against any single-sector AI disruption. AI may even benefit Realty Income indirectly by increasing demand for data center properties, a growing asset class in the portfolio [1]. AI Opportunity (0/1): No AI-related revenue; the company uses predictive analytics internally for underwriting but does not sell AI products or generate AI-specific revenue [2].
