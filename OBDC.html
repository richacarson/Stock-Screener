Company Profile
Financial Services ·Asset Management ·NYSE ·United States
Blue Owl Capital Corporation is a business development company (BDC) focused on direct lending to U.S. middle-market and upper-middle-market companies with EBITDA between $10M-$250M. As of December 31, 2025, the portfolio consisted of 234 companies with $16.5B aggregate fair value. Externally managed by Blue Owl Capital's Credit platform, which has ~$157.8B AUM.

Investment Thesis
OBDC is the third-largest publicly traded BDC offering a 13.9% dividend yield backed by a $16.5B portfolio of direct loans to 234 U.S. middle-market companies [1]. The January 2026 Moody's upgrade to Baa2 validates the portfolio's credit quality, while non-accruals improved to 2.3%/1.1% (cost/fair value) [1]. Forward P/E of 8.3x and NAV of $14.81 per share (stock trades at 74% of NAV) create a value proposition for income investors [1][5]. Blue Owl's Credit platform manages $157.8B AUM with a highly selective process (5% close rate on 11,000+ opportunities), and the average LTV of 42% provides meaningful downside protection [2]. The $300M buyback authorization at a 26% discount to NAV is accretive to book value [1]. The Q4 2025 opportunistic asset sale of $400M at ~99.7 cents on the dollar validated portfolio marks [3].
The bear case centers on dividend sustainability and governance concerns. The 125.8% payout ratio and -10.65% 1-year dividend decline suggest the current $0.37 quarterly distribution may face further cuts as base rates normalize and supplemental dividends have already been reduced from $0.06 to zero [1][5]. Earnings per share declined 40.9% YoY, with FY2026 consensus EPS of $1.33 representing further compression [1]. The OBDC II saga — suspended redemptions, continued fee collection during the freeze, and the failed NAV-for-NAV merger — raised serious governance questions about Blue Owl's alignment with shareholder interests [3]. Glendon Capital's March 2026 allegations that BDCs including Blue Owl were misrepresenting portfolio loss rates adds credibility risk [3]. As an externally managed BDC, the structural fee conflict between Blue Owl Capital (the manager) and OBDC shareholders is permanent. Rising credit defaults in a slowing economy could pressure the middle-market borrowers that comprise the portfolio [1].
Key Catalysts
  1. Moody's Baa2 upgrade reduces funding costs and expands OBDC's access to investment-grade debt markets, potentially improving net interest margin [1]
  2. $300M share repurchase program at 74% of NAV is directly accretive to book value per share and signals management confidence in portfolio quality [1]
  3. Improving non-accrual trends (down to 2.3%/1.1% from 2.7%/1.3%) suggest portfolio credit quality is stabilizing [1]
Key Risks
  1. Dividend sustainability at risk — 125.8% payout ratio with declining EPS (-40.9% YoY) and shrinking supplemental dividends suggests further distribution cuts ahead [1][5]
  2. OBDC II governance controversy — suspended redemptions, continued fee collection, and failed merger attempt eroded investor trust and raised questions about management alignment [3]
  3. Credit cycle exposure — middle-market borrowers face stress from high rates, and Glendon Capital's allegations of misrepresented portfolio loss rates add uncertainty around true asset quality [3]
