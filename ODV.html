Company Profile
Basic Materials ·Gold ·NYSE ·Canada
Osisko Development Corp. is a North American gold development company focused on advancing past-producing mining properties. Its flagship asset is the fully-permitted, 100%-owned Cariboo Gold Project in British Columbia, Canada, alongside the Tintic Project in Utah. The company aims to become an intermediate gold producer through responsible and sustainable mining practices.

Investment Thesis
Osisko Development offers speculative upside as a well-funded gold development company with $422M CAD cash, a fully-permitted flagship project (Cariboo Gold) in British Columbia, and early-stage production at Tintic generating $35.5M revenue in FY2025 from 6,240 ounces of gold [1]. CEO Sean Roosen's track record of building and monetizing mining assets (Osisko Mining Corp sold to Agnico Eagle, Osisko Gold Royalties) provides credibility, and the company's low D/E ratio of 21% provides balance sheet flexibility for the capital-intensive development phase ahead.
The bear case centers on significant execution risk: Cariboo Gold requires substantial additional capital expenditure ($40.2M in underground development alone for 2026) before reaching commercial production, and the company burns cash with negative ROE of -25% and zero profit margin [1]. Gold price sensitivity is extreme for a pre-production miner — a sustained gold price decline could make the Cariboo project uneconomic. Dilution risk is material given the company's pattern of equity raises ($143.8M USD offering in February 2026, $82.5M private placement in Q4 2024) that erode per-share value for existing shareholders.
Key Catalysts
  1. Cariboo Gold Project advancing through underground development with $40.2M allocated for 2026, moving toward commercial production decision
  2. Gold prices at or near record highs in 2025-2026 supporting project economics and potential for expanded resource estimates
  3. Tintic Project heap leach operations scaling from 6,240 ounces in 2025 to potential interim cash flow generation while Cariboo develops
Key Risks
  1. Pre-production stage with negative earnings and sustained cash burn requiring continued equity dilution — $226M raised across two offerings in late 2024/early 2025
  2. Gold price sensitivity: Cariboo project economics are highly leveraged to gold prices, and a sustained decline below project cost curves could strand the asset
  3. Execution risk on Cariboo underground development: mining at depth in a historical district carries geological uncertainty and potential cost overruns
