Company Profile

Organon is a global pharmaceutical company spun off from Merck in 2021, focused on women's health (Nexplanon contraceptive), biosimilars, and established brands across 60+ medicines. The company operates in over 140 countries with $6.2B in annual revenue, though it carries significant legacy debt from the spinoff.

Investment Thesis
Organon trades at a deeply discounted 8.3x trailing P/E and 1.6x forward P/E, reflecting the market's severe skepticism following the CEO departure over improper sales practices, 93% dividend cut, and Audit Committee financial statement review [1][2]. The women's health franchise anchored by Nexplanon (now FDA-approved for 5-year duration) provides recurring revenue with high switching costs, and the biosimilars portfolio offers low-single-digit growth [1]. The Jada divestiture for ~$390M proceeds dedicated to debt reduction demonstrates balance sheet discipline under Interim CEO Morrissey. Revenue of $6.2B and $1.91B Adjusted EBITDA (30.7% margin) show the underlying business generates meaningful cash flow despite the headline challenges [1].
The bear case dominates. D/E of 1,170% with $8.64B in debt at 4.3x net leverage leaves virtually no margin for error — the Altman Z-Score of 1.15 places Organon in the bankruptcy distress zone [2]. Revenue declined 3% in 2025 with flat 2026 guidance, as Established Brands face mid-single-digit declines from loss of exclusivity on Atozet and respiratory products [1]. The CEO scandal and Audit Committee review of biosimilar purchase timing create financial statement reliability concerns — Piper Sandler called it 'significant control failures' and slashed the target to $5 [2]. Net income collapsed 78% from $864M to $187M in 2025. The 93% dividend cut from $1.12 to $0.08 annually destroyed the income investment thesis that initially attracted shareholders. No permanent CEO has been named, leaving strategic direction uncertain. The Inspire score of -75 (MISALIGNED) reflects concerns about abortifacients in the women's health portfolio [CSV].
