Company Profile
Real Estate ·REIT - Healthcare Facilities ·NYSE ·United States
Omega Healthcare Investors is a Real Estate Investment Trust providing financing and capital to the long-term healthcare industry in the United States and United Kingdom, with a focus on skilled nursing and assisted living facilities. As of late 2025, Omega's portfolio includes 1,024 operating facilities in 42 states, the District of Columbia, and the UK/Jersey, operated by 88 different operators.

Investment Thesis
Omega Healthcare Investors offers a compelling 5.72% dividend yield backed by 33 consecutive years of payments and a recovering skilled nursing portfolio. Q4 2025 revenue climbed to $319M (+14.3% YoY) with net income jumping to $172M ($0.55/share vs. $0.41 prior year), and 2026 AFFO guidance of $3.15-$3.25/share implies continued earnings expansion [1]. The $1.1B capital deployment in 2025 — with two-thirds directed toward senior housing and UK assets — demonstrates successful diversification beyond U.S. skilled nursing, reducing operator concentration risk. The aging demographic tailwind (65+ population growing ~3% annually) provides secular demand support, and Omega's 1,024-facility portfolio across 88 operators creates scale advantages in deal sourcing and underwriting. At a forward P/E of 22.7x and 0.58 beta, the stock offers defensive income characteristics.
The bear case centers on dividend stagnation and operator credit risk. Omega's dividend has been frozen at $2.68/share since 2020 — six consecutive years without a raise — while inflation has eroded the real yield by approximately 20% [5]. The GAAP payout ratio of 138% is unsustainable (though FFO-based payout is more relevant for REITs), and AFFO guidance of $3.15-$3.25 leaves only modest coverage. Genesis Health Care's bankruptcy exposed the operator concentration risk inherent in triple-net healthcare REITs, and any future large operator distress could force asset write-downs. The RIDEA strategy, while diversifying revenue, introduces operating risk that pure triple-net REITs avoid. ROE of 12% trails peers, and the PEG ratio of 12.0x suggests the market prices in limited growth. Skilled nursing facilities face persistent staffing shortages that pressure operator profitability and, ultimately, rent coverage ratios.
Key Catalysts
  1. Resumption of dividend growth — if AFFO reaches the high end of $3.25/share guidance, Omega would have sufficient coverage to announce its first dividend increase since 2019, which could catalyze a meaningful re-rating among income-focused investors [1]
  2. RIDEA strategy execution — direct senior housing operations could deliver higher risk-adjusted returns than triple-net leases, potentially expanding the addressable market and improving portfolio-level growth rates [1]
  3. Aging population demographic wave — the 65+ cohort is the fastest-growing segment of the U.S. population, driving secular demand for skilled nursing beds and post-acute care facilities that directly benefits Omega's occupancy and rent coverage metrics
Key Risks
  1. Operator credit deterioration — Omega's triple-net lease model depends on operator profitability, and persistent skilled nursing staffing shortages (vacancy rates above 20% nationally) pressure operator margins and rent coverage ratios, creating potential for lease restructurings or defaults [1]
  2. Interest rate sensitivity — as a high-yield REIT, OHI competes with fixed income for capital allocation, and sustained higher-for-longer rates compress the yield spread that attracts income investors, while increasing Omega's borrowing costs on the 79% D/E balance sheet [5]
  3. Dividend stagnation risk — six consecutive years without a dividend increase has frustrated income growth investors, and if AFFO growth disappoints, Omega may face another year of flat payments while peers like CareTrust and NHI resume growth distributions
