Company Profile

O-I Glass, Inc. manufactures and sells glass containers to food and beverage manufacturers globally. The company produces glass containers for beer, spirits, wine, food, non-alcoholic beverages, and pharmaceuticals, operating approximately 70 plants across the Americas, Europe, and Asia-Pacific.

Investment Thesis
O-I Glass is executing a credible operational turnaround under new CEO Gordon Hardie, with adjusted EBITDA rising 11% to $1.22B in FY2025, margins expanding from 16.8% to 19.0%, and free cash flow swinging from negative $128M to positive $168M [1]. The Fit to Win program delivered $275M+ in cost savings, and 2026 guidance calls for $1.25-1.30B adjusted EBITDA, $1.65-1.90 adjusted EPS (up 3-19%), and $200M FCF [1]. At 5.0x forward P/E, the stock is priced for distress, offering significant upside if the turnaround continues — MAGMA flexible manufacturing technology could be a long-term differentiator for glass packaging sustainability.
The bear case is substantial: D/E of 359.5% creates existential refinancing risk if credit markets tighten, and negative ROE (-7.8%) and profit margins (-2.0%) on a GAAP basis mean the company is still destroying value [4]. Revenue has declined from $7.1B in 2023 to $6.4B in 2025, reflecting glass packaging's secular headwinds from aluminum and plastic alternatives. Volumes are expected flat to slightly down in 2026 with pricing headwinds from customer negotiations [1]. The stock has fallen 37% from its 52-week high of $16.91 to $10.59, and with no dividend and massive leverage, there is no margin of safety. A recession-driven volume decline could stress covenant compliance on $5.5B+ of debt [4].
Key Catalysts
  1. Fit to Win Phase 2 delivering $275M+ incremental cost savings in 2026, potentially lifting adjusted EBITDA to $1.30B and expanding margins toward 20% [1]
  2. MAGMA flexible manufacturing technology achieving commercial scale deployment, enabling smaller production runs and new customer segments in craft beverages and premium spirits [1]
  3. Deleveraging trajectory — if $200M FCF target is achieved in 2026, net debt reduction could begin improving the D/E ratio and reducing refinancing risk [1]
Key Risks
  1. Debt-to-equity of 359.5% with $5.5B+ total debt creates acute refinancing risk — any credit market disruption or covenant breach could force dilutive equity raises or restructuring [4]
  2. Secular decline in glass packaging market share as beverage customers shift to lighter, cheaper aluminum cans and plastic containers, pressuring volumes and pricing power [1]
  3. Negative GAAP profitability (profit margin -2.0%, ROE -7.8%) means the turnaround is incomplete — adjusted metrics mask real economic value destruction including impairments and restructuring charges [1][4]
