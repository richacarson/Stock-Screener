Company Profile
Energy ·Oil & Gas Midstream ·NYSE ·United States
ONEOK, Inc. is a leading midstream energy company operating approximately 60,000 miles of pipeline infrastructure across the United States. Following transformative acquisitions of Magellan Midstream Partners ($18.8B, 2023) and EnLink Midstream ($8.5B, 2025), the company provides gathering, processing, fractionation, transportation, storage, and marine export services for natural gas, natural gas liquids, refined products, and crude oil. Headquartered in Tulsa, Oklahoma.

Investment Thesis
ONEOK delivered strong Q1 2026 results with $9.62B revenue (up 20% YoY), $774M net income (up 22%), and raised full-year 2026 adjusted EBITDA guidance to a midpoint of $8.25B with diluted EPS midpoint of $5.53 [1][2]. The company has successfully integrated the Magellan ($18.8B) and EnLink ($8.5B) acquisitions, creating a diversified midstream platform with ~60,000 miles of pipeline infrastructure and dominant positions in NGL processing, refined products, and crude oil transportation. NGL raw feed throughput rose 15% YoY and refined products volume shipped increased 12%, demonstrating organic growth on top of acquired assets [1]. With a forward P/E of 16.2x, 4.89% dividend yield, 56 consecutive years of dividend payments, and direct secular tailwinds from AI-driven energy demand growth, ONEOK offers an attractive combination of value, income, and infrastructure exposure.
The bear case centers on growth deceleration and leverage risk. ONEOK's 2026 EPS guidance midpoint of $5.53 is only 2% above 2025's $5.42, signaling a growth pause as acquisition synergies plateau and the earnings base normalizes [1][2]. The forward PEG of 2.18 implies the market is pricing in more growth than current guidance supports. D/E of 150% and $32B in long-term debt from the Magellan acquisition create significant financial leverage, making the company vulnerable to rising interest rates or commodity price declines. Profit margin of 10% reflects the structurally lower-margin midstream pass-through revenue model. The 76% payout ratio leaves limited cushion for dividend safety if volumes decline. Major growth projects (Medford fractionator rebuild, Bighorn Processing Plant, Texas City terminal) won't meaningfully contribute to earnings until 2027-2028, creating an earnings vacuum in 2026 [4]. Net insider selling of $6.3M in the last 12 months suggests management may not see near-term upside at current valuations [3].
Key Catalysts
  1. AI-driven data center buildout and LNG export expansion creating secular tailwind for natural gas and NGL demand through ONEOK's 60,000-mile pipeline network
  2. Medford fractionator rebuild (Q4 2026/Q1 2027), Bighorn Processing Plant (mid-2027), and Texas City export terminal expected to drive earnings reacceleration in 2027-2028
  3. Continued integration synergies from Magellan and EnLink acquisitions plus organic volume growth in Permian Basin and Rocky Mountain regions supporting adjusted EBITDA expansion toward $8.5B+
Key Risks
  1. Growth deceleration — 2026 EPS guidance midpoint of $5.53 is only 2% above 2025's $5.42, with major growth projects not contributing meaningfully until 2027-2028
  2. Elevated D/E of 150% and $32B long-term debt from acquisitions limit financial flexibility and create vulnerability to rising interest rates or sustained commodity price weakness
  3. Forward PEG of 2.18 implies growth expectations that current guidance doesn't support, creating downside risk to the stock's 16x forward earnings multiple
