Investment Thesis
ONEOK offers a compelling combination of growth and income as the most diversified independent midstream operator, with 90% fee-based earnings insulating against commodity volatility. Revenue grew 50% from $22.4B to $33.6B over three years through transformative M&A, with management guiding for 15%+ EPS growth in 2026 as $250M in annual synergies flow through. The stock trades at 14.8x forward earnings with a 4.7% dividend yield growing 3-4% annually, and AI-driven data center demand for natural gas creates a secular tailwind estimated at 3-20 bcf/day of incremental demand by 2030.
The bear case focuses on ONEOK's elevated D/E ratio of 146.5% and integration risk from absorbing three major acquisitions in rapid succession, which could strain management bandwidth and culture. Profit margins of 10.1% are thin for an S&P 500 company, and the forward PEG of 1.29x suggests the stock is fairly valued rather than cheap on a growth-adjusted basis. Long-term structural risks include potential carbon regulation increasing operating costs for fossil fuel infrastructure, declining NGL demand if petrochemical production shifts overseas, and the possibility that renewable energy growth reduces natural gas's role faster than expected.
