Investment Thesis
ONEOK is a transformed midstream infrastructure giant positioned at the nexus of AI-driven energy demand and NGL/refined products growth. The company generated $8B+ in adjusted EBITDA in 2025 (up 18% YoY) with a 12-year streak of EBITDA growth at a 17% CAGR [1]. Management guides for >15% EPS growth in 2026, supported by $250M in realized acquisition synergies exceeding initial forecasts [1][2]. The 4.8% dividend yield with 3-4% annual growth, combined with a forward P/E of 14.8, offers compelling value for a fee-based business model where 90%+ of earnings are decoupled from commodity prices [1]. The emerging AI data center demand for natural gas — estimated at 3-20 Bcf/d by 2030 — provides a multi-decade secular tailwind that directly benefits ONEOK's 50,000-mile pipeline network [2].
The bear case centers on ONEOK's elevated leverage at 146.5% D/E following the acquisition spree, with management targeting 3.5x debt/EBITDA by 2026 but remaining above that level currently [1]. Revenue growth is expected to decelerate to ~3% YoY in 2026 after the 55% surge in 2025 was primarily acquisition-driven, with organic growth not expected to reaccelerate until major projects come online in 2028 [1]. Profit margin of just 10.1% is thin for the midstream sector, reflecting the high-volume, low-margin nature of refined products (Magellan). The energy transition represents a long-term structural risk if natural gas demand peaks earlier than expected, though AI data center demand is currently extending the gas demand timeline [2]. ONEOK's own disclosure of potential 'greenwashing' scrutiny risk reflects the tension between its ESG commitments and its fossil fuel infrastructure business [3].
Key Catalysts
  1. AI data center natural gas demand estimated at 3-20 Bcf/d by 2030, directly increasing utilization of ONEOK's 50,000-mile pipeline network [2]
  2. $1B Texas City Logistics export terminal and Eiger Express Pipeline (3.7 Bcf/d) entering service in early/mid-2028, reaccelerating growth [1]
  3. Continued synergy realization exceeding $250M from Magellan/EnLink/Medallion acquisitions, with potential for additional upside [1][2]
Key Risks
  1. Elevated leverage at 146.5% D/E and above-target debt/EBITDA could constrain financial flexibility if commodity prices decline or volumes disappoint [1]
  2. Growth deceleration to ~3% revenue growth in 2026 between acquisition completion and major project startups in 2028 could compress valuation multiples [1]
  3. Energy transition risk if natural gas demand peaks earlier than expected, potentially stranding long-lived pipeline infrastructure assets [2][3]
