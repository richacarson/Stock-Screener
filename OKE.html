Company Profile
Energy ·Oil & Gas Midstream ·NYSE ·United States
ONEOK is one of the largest midstream operators in the United States, providing gathering, processing, fractionation, transportation, and storage services for natural gas, NGLs, refined products, and crude oil through approximately 60,000 miles of pipeline infrastructure. The company dramatically expanded its scale through the 2023 Magellan Midstream Partners acquisition, transforming from an NGL-focused operator into a comprehensive midstream platform.

Investment Thesis
ONEOK is a premier midstream infrastructure platform trading at an attractive 14.5x forward P/E with a 4.6% dividend yield, positioned to benefit from secular growth in energy demand driven by AI data centers and continued U.S. hydrocarbon production growth [1]. The Magellan integration is generating $150M+ in incremental synergies, and adjusted EBITDA of $8B+ (up 18% in 2025) demonstrates the scale and cash flow power of the combined platform [1]. Revenue of $33.6B (up 55% YoY) and the 60,000-mile pipeline network create a dominant competitive position in NGL gathering, processing, and fractionation. The 4% quarterly dividend increase to $4.28 annualized shows management's confidence in sustained cash flow, supported by $2.7-3.2B in growth capital projects including the Medford fractionator rebuild, Bighorn Processing Plant, and Texas City export terminal [1]. EPS has grown at a 13% CAGR since 2017, and the 25-year dividend payment history provides income reliability.
The bear case centers on growth deceleration and balance sheet risk. ONEOK's 2026 guidance of $3.19-3.71B net income (midpoint essentially flat vs. 2025's $3.39B) signals a growth pause as acquisition synergies plateau and producer activity moderates at $55-60 WTI crude prices [1][2]. Long-term debt of $32B (D/E 146%) creates significant financial leverage — the debt tripled since 2023 from the Magellan acquisition, making the company vulnerable to rising interest rates and potential credit rating pressure. The Mont Belvieu fractionator explosion in October 2025 (three injuries, $930M insurance-funded repairs) highlights operational safety risks inherent to the business [4]. The stock fell 27% in 2025, suggesting investor skepticism about the growth trajectory post-acquisition. Profit margin of 10.1% is structurally low for a midstream operator with significant pass-through revenue. The Medford fractionator rebuild and Texas City terminal projects won't meaningfully contribute to earnings until 2027-2028, creating a growth vacuum in 2026 [1].
