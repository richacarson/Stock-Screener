Investment Thesis
ONEOK offers a compelling income play with a 4.7% dividend yield growing at 3-4% annually, 26-year payment track record, and fee-based earnings model (>90%) reducing commodity volatility. Adjusted EBITDA of $8.02B (up 18%) and $475M in acquisition synergies demonstrate execution strength. The 60,000-mile pipeline network benefits from AI data center gas demand (estimated 3-20 Bcf/d by 2030). The Eiger Express expansion and potential LPG Export Terminal provide growth visibility. The Inspire score of 45 supports faith alignment.
The bear case centers on leverage — $33B in total debt (146% debt-to-equity) constrains financial flexibility, and the 76% payout ratio leaves limited margin for dividend coverage in a downturn. 2026 EBITDA guidance of $7.9-8.3B implies modest growth after the acquisition-driven 2025 surge. EPS declined 1.5% in 2025 despite revenue growing 29.5%, reflecting integration costs and interest expense. The fossil fuel midstream infrastructure model faces long-term energy transition risk, and trade policy uncertainty could impact NGL export economics. Free cash flow of $725M is thin relative to the $33B debt stack.
