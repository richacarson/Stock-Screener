Company Profile
Utilities ·Utilities - Independent Power Producers ·NYSE ·United States
Oklo Inc. develops advanced fission power plants to provide clean, reliable, and affordable energy at scale. Founded in 2013 by MIT-trained nuclear engineers Jacob and Caroline DeWitte, the company's Aurora powerhouse uses fast-fission reactor technology with passive safety features. Oklo targets AI data center and industrial customers with a ~15 GW order book.

Investment Thesis
Oklo represents a high-conviction bet on the nuclear energy renaissance driven by AI data center demand, with a ~15 GW order book (Meta, Switch), $2.5B cash position, and transformative Aurora fast-fission technology that could redefine clean baseload power [1][2]. CEO DeWitte's PCAST appointment alongside top AI leaders validates the technology-policy positioning at the highest level. The Atomic Alchemy isotope business adds near-term revenue potential. If the Aurora reactor achieves commercial operations by 2028, Oklo could establish first-mover advantage in the advanced nuclear space with a 20-year order pipeline.
The bear case is fundamental: Oklo is a pre-revenue company with zero revenue, $139.3M in 2025 operating losses, and a $9B market cap that peaked near $194/share during the nuclear hype cycle before correcting to $51.81 [1][4]. First-of-a-kind nuclear projects historically suffer severe cost overruns and multi-year delays — the NRC has never licensed a commercial fast-fission reactor. The 2022 NRC application denial for 'lack of information' raised legitimate safety review concerns [3]. HALEU fuel supply availability remains a 'single point of failure' for the entire advanced nuclear industry, and the Pike County site controversy highlights community environmental concerns that could delay future deployments.
Key Catalysts
  1. Aurora powerhouse pre-construction at Idaho National Laboratory advancing toward 2028 commercial operations, with DOE oversight providing credibility pathway [1][2]
  2. CEO DeWitte's PCAST appointment positioning Oklo to influence nuclear regulatory modernization under the Trump administration [2]
  3. Atomic Alchemy isotope pilot plant agreement with DOE targeting near-term revenue from medical and industrial radioisotope production [1]
Key Risks
  1. First-of-a-kind nuclear project execution risk — the NRC has never licensed a commercial fast-fission reactor, and the 2028 commercial timeline is ambitious given historical nuclear construction delays [1][3]
  2. HALEU fuel supply chain dependency — domestic enrichment is not yet at scale, creating a 'single point of failure' for Aurora deployment [1]
  3. Extreme valuation risk at $9B market cap on zero revenue — any milestone delay, regulatory setback, or AI spending pullback could trigger sharp repricing from speculative levels [4]
