Investment Thesis
OLB Group serves 10,300+ merchants across 50 states and is authorized to integrate PayPal checkout solutions, which could potentially stabilize the merchant services business. The planned DMint Bitcoin mining spinoff could unlock value if cryptocurrency markets remain strong, and the at-the-market program provides access to up to $15M in capital. At a micro-cap valuation of ~$8M, any successful stabilization of revenue could generate outsized percentage returns.
The bear case is existential — OLB had $3,540 in cash at September 30, 2025, and management has disclosed substantial doubt about the company's ability to continue as a going concern through November 2026 [1]. Revenue has declined 58% in 2024 and another 35% in H1 2025, profit margin is -92.7%, and the 15-person team faces overwhelming competition from Square, Stripe, and hundreds of better-capitalized payment processors. The Moola Cloud vendor transition has disrupted revenue, and there is no clear path to profitability. The most likely outcome is continued dilution through the ATM program, potential delisting, and eventual dissolution [1].
Key Catalysts
  1. Successful completion of Moola Cloud vendor transition could stabilize fintech revenue from its declining trajectory [1]
  2. DMint Bitcoin mining spinoff could unlock value and provide a separate capital-raising vehicle [1]
  3. PayPal integration across SecurePay gateway could attract new merchant signups and stabilize the customer base [1]
Key Risks
  1. Going concern doubt — $3,540 in cash and management's own disclosure of survival uncertainty through November 2026 [1]
  2. Revenue decline of 58% (2024) and 35% (H1 2025) suggests structural loss of merchant base that may not be recoverable [1]
  3. NASDAQ delisting risk from sustained low share price and potential non-compliance with listing standards [1]
