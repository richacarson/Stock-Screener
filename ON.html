Investment Thesis
Onsemi at 15.1x forward earnings is positioned at the inflection point of a semiconductor recovery, with automotive inventory digestion 'largely behind us,' industrial revenue growing YoY for the first time in eight quarters, and AI data center revenue exceeding $250M and growing high teens sequentially [1]. Record $1.4B free cash flow (24% margin), a new $6B buyback authorization, and plans to exit $300M in non-core revenue demonstrate disciplined capital allocation. Manufacturing underutilization charges of 700bp in Q4 will dissipate as utilization rises from 68% to normalized levels, providing significant margin expansion potential.
The bear case centers on cyclical and competitive risks. Trailing P/E of 210x and 2.0% profit margin reflect the depth of the current downturn, and recovery timing remains uncertain despite management optimism [1]. Revenue declined 11.2% in 2025, and Q1 2026 guidance of $1.44-$1.54B suggests gradual rather than sharp recovery. Wolfspeed's distress and Chinese SiC competitors expanding capacity could pressure pricing even as volumes recover. The exit of $300M in non-core revenue creates a headwind that must be offset by growth in focus areas. Automotive EV adoption rates could moderate if consumer incentives are reduced or charging infrastructure investment slows.
Key Catalysts
  1. AI data center power management revenue growing high teens sequentially in Q1 2026, with 30+ GaN device samples in 2026 expanding the addressable market [1]
  2. Automotive inventory digestion ending after eight quarters of YoY declines positions onsemi for high single-digit content growth per vehicle [1]
  3. Manufacturing utilization recovery from 68% toward normalized 80%+ would remove ~700bp of underutilization charges, potentially expanding gross margins by 500+ basis points in 2H 2026
Key Risks
  1. Chinese SiC competitors (BYD Semiconductor, StarPower) are rapidly expanding capacity and could undercut pricing in the automotive SiC market, compressing onsemi's margins during the recovery [1]
  2. Exit of $300M in non-core revenue in 2026 creates a 5% revenue headwind that must be offset by growth in AI data center and automotive focus areas
  3. EV adoption rate moderation due to reduced consumer incentives, tariffs, or charging infrastructure delays could slow the automotive recovery that drives 50%+ of onsemi's revenue
