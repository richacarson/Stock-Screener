Company Profile
Technology ·Semiconductors ·NASDAQ ·United States
ON Semiconductor Corporation (onsemi) provides intelligent sensing and power solutions across automotive, industrial, AI data center, computing, and mobile end markets. The company operates through Power Solutions Group, Analog and Mixed-Signal Group, and Intelligent Sensing Group segments, specializing in silicon carbide (SiC) and gallium nitride (GaN) power semiconductors, CMOS image sensors, and analog/mixed-signal ICs. Founded in 1992 and headquartered in Scottsdale, Arizona.

Investment Thesis
Onsemi is emerging from a three-year semiconductor cyclical downturn with its strategic transformation largely complete and recovery accelerating. Revenue growth has turned positive at 4.7% YoY — the first positive quarter since the downturn began — with AI data center revenue more than doubling year-over-year and Q1 2026 guidance of $1.44-1.54B suggesting continued momentum [1]. The PEG ratio of 0.4 indicates expected earnings growth substantially exceeds the valuation multiple, and operating income increased 10% YoY, outpacing revenue growth by 2x as margins recover from cyclical lows [1]. The company's leadership position in SiC power semiconductors positions it at the intersection of two secular megatrends: automotive electrification and AI infrastructure power management. Record $1.4B FCF and a new $6B buyback authorization demonstrate financial strength, while the 200mm SiC production ramp provides a structural cost advantage competitors are years behind replicating. The Inspire Insight score of 71 with 20 positive ESG attributions reflects strong ALIGNED faith alignment, and the EcoVadis top 1% ranking confirms genuine ESG commitment [3].
The bear case centers on valuation stretch, competitive risk, and cyclical uncertainty. The stock has doubled from ~$61 to $122 in three months, pushing the forward P/E from 15.1x to 28.4x — much of the recovery is now priced in, and trailing P/E of 89.4x reflects the depth of the earnings trough [4]. Profit margin at 9.5% is recovering but still well below historical peaks, and the timing and magnitude of full margin recovery through manufacturing utilization normalization remains uncertain. Chinese SiC competitors (BYD Semiconductor, StarPower) are rapidly expanding capacity and could undercut pricing in the automotive SiC market, compressing onsemi's margins even during recovery [1]. The strategic exit of $300M in non-core revenue creates a 5% topline headwind that must be offset by growth in AI and automotive focus areas. Automotive concentration at 50%+ of revenue creates vulnerability to EV adoption rate moderation from reduced consumer incentives, tariffs, or charging infrastructure delays. The semiconductor industry's inherent cyclicality means the recovery could stall if macro conditions deteriorate.
