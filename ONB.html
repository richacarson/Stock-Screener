Company Profile
Financial Services ·Banks - Regional ·NASDAQ ·United States
Old National Bancorp operates as the bank holding company for Old National Bank, providing consumer and commercial banking services across the Midwest and Southeast United States. The bank offers deposit accounts, commercial and residential loans, wealth management, and treasury services, with approximately $53 billion in total assets following strategic acquisitions.

Investment Thesis
Old National Bancorp is a well-managed Midwest/Southeast regional bank delivering strong earnings momentum — Q4 2025 adjusted EPS of $0.62 was up 27% YoY, with full-year net income of $669M on $2.52B in revenue [1]. The CapStar acquisition expanded the franchise into the high-growth Nashville market, and management targets 15% earnings growth with high-teens ROATCE in 2026 [1]. At 8.1x forward P/E, the stock is attractively valued versus the bank's improving profitability trajectory. A 31.3% payout ratio provides ample room for dividend increases, and $5.0B of repricing loan volume plus $2.9B in securities cash flow should support net interest margin expansion [1]. Six consecutive Ethisphere awards and a 37-year dividend track record demonstrate institutional quality.
The bear case centers on acquisition integration risk and sub-par capital returns. Back-to-back major acquisitions (CapStar, Bremer Financial) create substantial execution risk — integration costs, cultural friction, and management bandwidth constraints are real concerns [3]. ROE of 9.0% is below the 15% industry benchmark, reflecting goodwill dilution from acquisitions that may take years to earn back. The dividend has been flat at $0.56/year since 2020 despite growing earnings, meaning dividend investors have received zero income growth through a period of significant inflation. Revenue growth of 33.9% is largely acquisition-driven rather than organic, making the underlying growth rate harder to assess. Regional banking faces secular headwinds from deposit competition, fintech disruption, and potential credit deterioration as commercial real estate and consumer portfolios season through the economic cycle [1].
Key Catalysts
  1. Repricing tailwinds from $5.0B of new loan volume at higher yields and $2.9B in securities cash flow supporting NIM expansion through 2026 [1]
  2. Potential dividend increase — payout ratio of 31.3% and 15% earnings growth target create capacity for the first dividend raise since 2020 [1]
  3. CapStar integration synergies fully realized by mid-2026, with Nashville market exposure adding a high-growth geography to the franchise [1]
Key Risks
  1. Integration execution risk from consecutive major acquisitions (CapStar, Bremer) straining management bandwidth and causing employee turnover [3]
  2. Commercial real estate exposure in a rising-rate environment — potential credit deterioration as CRE loans season and refinancing pressure builds [1]
  3. ROE dilution from acquisition goodwill may persist longer than expected, keeping returns below cost of equity and limiting valuation re-rating [5]
