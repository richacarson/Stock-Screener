Company Profile
Healthcare ·Health Information Services ·NASDAQ ·United States
OneMedNet is a healthcare data company that curates regulatory-grade imaging Real World Data (iRWD) from a network of 2,130+ healthcare provider sites encompassing 186 million clinical exams. The company provides de-identified medical imaging and electronic health records to pharmaceutical companies, medical device manufacturers, and AI developers for drug development, clinical validation, and AI model training.

Investment Thesis
OneMedNet is positioned at the intersection of healthcare data and AI — two of the fastest-growing markets in technology. The company's iRWD platform provides regulatory-grade de-identified medical imaging data from 2,130+ healthcare sites (186 million clinical exams) to pharma, device, and AI companies [1][2]. 2025 bookings surged 4.1x to $2.79M, and the Palantir partnership adds AI-powered subscription search and analytics capabilities that could transform the business model from episodic data licensing to recurring subscription revenue [1]. The FDA and EMA are increasingly encouraging Real-World Evidence in drug approvals, creating a secular tailwind for the company's data assets. At $44M market cap, ONMD offers significant upside if execution delivers on the bookings pipeline.
The bear case is severe. OneMedNet generates only $510K in trailing revenue with a -206.1% profit margin and negative equity of -$3.94M — this is a pre-revenue startup with existential financial risk [4]. The stock trades at $0.84 with a consensus 'Sell' rating from analysts, and the company has been burning cash consistently [1]. Larger, better-funded competitors (Flatiron Health/Roche, Tempus AI, Veracyte) are building competing healthcare data platforms with deeper provider relationships and stronger balance sheets. The 2024 CEO transition raises leadership continuity questions. Converting $2.79M in bookings to recognized revenue and then to profitability is a multi-year journey with no guarantee of success. The micro-cap stock is highly volatile (52-week range $0.30-$4.22) and could require further dilutive capital raises to fund operations [4].
Key Catalysts
  1. Palantir-powered Subscription Search and Data License product launches in early 2026 — successful conversion of $2.79M bookings backlog to recurring revenue would validate the business model transition [1]
  2. FDA/EMA regulatory momentum toward accepting Real-World Evidence in drug approvals — creates secular demand tailwind for iRWD platform and imaging data [2]
  3. Expansion into the $1.5B healthcare data annotation market via MedCase — opens an adjacent revenue stream leveraging the existing provider network [2]
Key Risks
  1. Existential financial risk — $510K TTM revenue, -206.1% profit margin, and negative equity require continued capital raises that dilute shareholders and may not materialize in adverse market conditions [4]
  2. Competitive displacement — Flatiron Health (Roche), Tempus AI, and other well-funded healthcare data platforms could replicate OneMedNet's provider network with superior resources and established pharma relationships [2]
  3. Bookings-to-revenue conversion risk — $2.79M in 2025 bookings represents potential, not recognized revenue; execution on the Palantir-powered subscription model remains unproven at scale [1]
