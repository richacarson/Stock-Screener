Company Profile
Consumer Cyclical ·Footwear & Accessories ·NYSE ·Switzerland
On Holding AG develops and distributes premium performance sports products including athletic footwear, apparel, and accessories under the On brand. Founded in 2010 in Zurich, the company sells through wholesale and direct-to-consumer channels across running, outdoor, training, and tennis categories. On is known for its CloudTec cushioning technology and has rapidly scaled to CHF 3.0B in annual revenue.
3,963 Employees ·www.on.com

Investment Thesis
On Holding is a rare high-growth consumer brand trading at a reasonable valuation — CHF 3.0B revenue growing 30% YoY with record 63.9% Q4 gross margins and CHF 1.0B+ cash on the balance sheet [1]. At 16.9x forward earnings, the market is pricing in growth deceleration, but 2026 guidance of 23%+ constant-currency growth (historically conservative) with 18.5-19% adjusted EBITDA margin targets suggests continued premium execution [1]. The DTC channel, retail expansion, and apparel category scaling provide multiple growth vectors, while the Cyclon circularity platform and materials science innovation create long-term differentiation against Nike and Adidas. D/E of just 31.9% and the strongest cash position in company history provide financial resilience [4].
The bear case centers on growth deceleration and governance risk. Revenue growth is slowing from 35.6% constant-currency in 2025 to guided 23%+ in 2026, and GAAP profit margin of 6.8% remains thin for a $11B market cap company [1]. CEO Martin Hoffmann's departure after just one year, coupled with a securities fraud investigation by the Schall Law Firm, raises governance concerns — the stock fell 11.2% on the leadership announcement and is down 25% YTD [2]. Co-founders returning as co-CEOs is unorthodox for a public company and introduces dual-leadership execution risk. ONON trades at 44.8x trailing earnings (despite reasonable forward multiples), leaving limited margin for error. Tariff exposure (20% assumed in guidance) on footwear manufactured in Vietnam and other Asian countries could compress margins if trade policy worsens. Competition from HOKA (Deckers) and Nike's renewed focus on performance running could slow market share gains [1][2].
Key Catalysts
  1. 2026 revenue guidance of CHF 3.44B+ (23%+ constant currency growth) is historically conservative — On has beaten guidance in every year since IPO, suggesting upside to Street consensus of CHF 3.75B [1]

  3. Growth deceleration from 35.6% to 23%+ constant currency with trailing P/E of 44.8x leaves limited margin for error — any miss could trigger significant multiple compression [1]
