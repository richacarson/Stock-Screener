Company Profile
Consumer Cyclical ·Footwear & Accessories ·NYSE ·Switzerland
On Holding AG designs, develops, and markets premium performance athletic footwear, apparel, and accessories. Founded in Zurich by former athletes, the company is known for its patented CloudTec cushioning technology, LightSpray robotic manufacturing, and Superfoam materials innovation. On sells through direct-to-consumer channels and wholesale partners globally, competing in the premium running, outdoor, tennis, and lifestyle segments.

Finite vs Infinite Game — Sinek (25%)
Mindset: MIXED Overall: 6 /10
On Holding exhibits genuine infinite game characteristics — a founder-led company built around authentic product innovation and a clear purpose rooted in athletic performance. The co-founders' return as Co-CEOs reinforces the connection between purpose and leadership. However, the company is young (founded 2010, IPO 2021) and has not yet been tested through a full economic cycle or leadership transition crisis. Its category expansions are promising but incremental rather than transformative. The competitive approach — innovating past Nike and Adidas rather than attacking them — reflects infinite thinking, though the company's finite age limits how much endurance can be demonstrated.
Just Cause
7
On was founded in Zurich by former Ironman champion Olivier Bernhard and friends David Allemann and Caspar Coppetti, driven by an authentic desire to create a better running shoe through Swiss engineering innovation [2]. The purpose of 'igniting the human spirit through movement' is genuine and has guided 14 years of consistent product innovation — from the first CloudTec running shoe to LightSpray manufacturing technology to expansion into tennis, outdoor, and lifestyle. The purpose shapes product decisions and brand identity. However, On's cause, while authentic, is fundamentally product-obsession — a passion for building the perfect running shoe — rather than Sinek's definition of a Just Cause that envisions a future state so appealing people will sacrifice to advance it. The company is too young to have proven this purpose through crisis or multi-decade consistency. (2/3 points)

Worthy Rivals

Existential Flexibility

On has shown meaningful strategic evolution — expanding from pure running shoes into tennis (partnering with Roger Federer), outdoor performance, lifestyle/fashion, and now apparel and owned retail stores [2][3]. The DTC pivot from wholesale-dependent distribution represents a significant business model evolution. Each expansion was guided by the core innovation purpose rather than opportunism. However, these are adjacent category expansions and distribution channel shifts — incremental evolution rather than the transformative reinvention Sinek describes (abandoning a core business entirely in service of the Just Cause). The company has not yet faced a situation requiring true existential flexibility. (1/2 points)
Courage to Lead
6
On has demonstrated meaningful courage in its investment decisions — pouring resources into the proprietary LightSpray robotic manufacturing technology and Superfoam materials innovation rather than relying on commodity contract manufacturing like most competitors [2]. The aggressive expansion into apparel and retail stores while still primarily a shoe company represents a willingness to invest ahead of proven demand. Investment Thesis
On Holding is the fastest-growing premium athletic brand in the world, delivering CHF 3,014M in FY2025 revenue (+30.0%) with Q1 2026 constant currency growth accelerating to 26.4% and full-year 2026 guidance of at least 23% constant currency growth [1][3]. The company's financial profile is rapidly improving — Q1 2026 gross margin expanded 430bps to 64.2%, adjusted EBITDA margin reached 21.0% (up from 16.5% YoY), and net income surged 82.2% [1]. At 17.38x forward P/E with a 0.72 PEG ratio, the stock offers rare growth-at-a-reasonable-price in the consumer discretionary space [4]. The balance sheet is fortress-like: $1.02B cash against $541.9M debt, net cash positive, with $240M in free cash flow funding growth without dilution. CloudTec and LightSpray represent genuine technological moats in an industry dominated by incremental foam chemistry. The DTC expansion, apparel growth pillar, and retail store rollout provide multiple vectors for sustained 20%+ revenue growth through the decade [2][3].
The bear case centers on consumer cyclicality and execution risk at scale. On's premium positioning ($150-250 price points) makes it acutely vulnerable to consumer spending pullbacks — during a recession, discretionary athletic footwear purchases are among the first items cut, and the brand has never been tested through a downturn [4]. Profit margin of 8.02% remains thin for a premium brand, and the gap between gross margin (64.2%) and net margin suggests significant operating expense growth as the company scales its DTC infrastructure, retail stores, and global headcount [1]. Significant Swiss franc appreciation versus USD creates persistent translation headwinds — Q1 2026 reported growth of 14.5% versus 26.4% constant currency illustrates the magnitude of this risk [1]. The CEO transition (Martin Hoffmann departing, co-founders assuming dual-CEO structure) introduces governance risk, as dual-CEO arrangements have a mixed track record. At $12.4B market cap, the stock prices in continued exceptional execution — any deceleration in growth or margin expansion would compress the multiple significantly. Competition from Nike's innovation reset and emerging brands like Hoka creates category crowding risk [3][4].
Key Catalysts
  1. Gross margin expansion toward 65%+ as LightSpray manufacturing scales and DTC mix increases — Q1 2026 already reached 64.2%, up 430bps YoY, with full-year guidance of at least 64.5% [1]
  2. Apparel category expansion as a second growth pillar — On is leveraging its brand equity and DTC infrastructure to build a multi-billion-dollar apparel business alongside footwear [2][3]
  3. Constant currency revenue growth of 23%+ guided for FY2026 with margin leverage driving net income growth well above revenue growth, as demonstrated by Q1's 82.2% net income surge on 26.4% CC revenue growth [1][3]
Key Risks
  1. Consumer cyclicality exposure — premium $150-250 athletic footwear is highly discretionary, and On has never navigated a recession as a public company, leaving downside earnings sensitivity unknown [4]
  2. Swiss franc appreciation creating persistent translation headwinds — Q1 2026 showed a 12 percentage point gap between reported (14.5%) and constant currency (26.4%) growth, compressing reported results despite strong underlying demand [1]
  3. CEO transition and dual-CEO governance risk — co-founders David Allemann and Caspar Coppetti assuming Co-CEO roles from departing CEO Martin Hoffmann introduces an unconventional leadership structure with limited precedent for sustained success [2]
