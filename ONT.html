Onterris, Inc. (formerly Montrose Environmental Group) operates as a global environmental services company providing assessment, permitting, emergency response, environmental testing, laboratory services, and remediation solutions. Investment Thesis
Onterris is an environmental services platform positioned to benefit from secular regulatory tailwinds including PFAS remediation mandates, infrastructure spending, and tightening environmental standards. FY2025 revenue of $830.5M grew 19% YoY, and management guides FY2026 revenue of $840M-$900M (~8% organic growth) with Adjusted EBITDA of $125M-$130M [2]. The forward P/E of 9.9x implies the market expects a dramatic earnings inflection from $0.15 trailing EPS to $1.74 forward EPS, supported by margin expansion as acquisition integration matures. Insider buying by Director Peter Graham (30,000 shares) and CFO Allan Dicks (6,535 shares) at prices between $15-$17 signals leadership conviction in the turnaround [4]. The company's physical environmental services are structurally immune to AI disruption, and environmental regulation is a secular growth driver regardless of political cycles.
The bear case centers on execution risk, thin profitability, and acquisition integration. Despite $830M in revenue, Onterris reported just $0.15 in trailing EPS with a 0.7% profit margin — the company has not yet demonstrated it can convert revenue scale into meaningful bottom-line earnings [5]. The Q1 2026 revenue decline of 5.2% to $168.5M raises questions about organic growth quality beneath the acquisition-driven topline [2]. D/E of 88% reflects acquisition-related leverage that constrains financial flexibility. The trailing P/E of 114.6x is misleading given near-zero earnings, but the forward P/E of 9.9x embeds aggressive earnings expectations — any shortfall in the EPS ramp to $1.74 could reprice the stock significantly. The environmental services market is highly fragmented with intense competition from larger, better-capitalized firms like Tetra Tech and Clean Harbors.
