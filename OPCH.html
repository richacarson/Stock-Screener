Investment Thesis
Option Care Health is the largest independent home infusion provider in the U.S. with $5.6B in 2025 revenue (up 13%), trading at an attractive 13.5x forward P/E with a $4.5B market cap [1][6]. The company benefits from the secular site-of-care shift as payers increasingly push infusion therapy from hospitals ($300+/hour) to home settings ($100-150/hour), a structural tailwind that has multiple years of runway. The 5-year revenue CAGR of ~17% demonstrates sustained growth execution, while the Palantir AI partnership positions the company to optimize clinical operations and reduce costs [4]. Adjusted EBITDA guidance of $480-505M for 2026 (8.5% margin) and operating cash flow of $340M+ (30%+ above 2025) show improving cash generation despite the low profit margin [1][2].
The bear case centers on margin fragility and biosimilar headwinds. Profit margin of only 3.67% leaves almost no room for execution errors — drug pricing pressure, payer reimbursement cuts, or supply chain disruptions could quickly turn profits negative [6]. The STELARA biosimilar conversion creates a ~400-basis-point revenue growth headwind and $25-35M gross profit impact in 2026, illustrating how drug lifecycle dynamics directly threaten Option Care's economics [1][2]. The 2026 revenue guidance of $5.8-6.0B represents a sharp deceleration to ~4% growth from 13% in 2025, raising questions about whether the easy growth from the site-of-care shift is maturing. D/E of 96% is elevated for a thin-margin business, creating balance sheet vulnerability in a downturn. Glassdoor reviews citing management turnover and poor organizational culture (3.1/5) suggest operational fragility that could manifest in clinical quality or customer retention issues [4]. Competition from CVS Health's Coram division and UnitedHealth's Optum infusion services brings scale advantages that could pressure margins further.
Key Catalysts
  1. Continued site-of-care shift from hospital to home infusion driven by payer cost pressures and patient preference, expanding Option Care's addressable market by mid-single digits annually [1][2]
  2. Palantir AI platform deployment optimizing clinical operations, pharmacy logistics, and patient scheduling — potential for measurable cost savings as implementation matures in 2026-2027 [4]
  3. Infusion clinic network expansion opening new ambulatory sites that capture patients who cannot receive therapy at home, diversifying the delivery model and increasing geographic reach [2]
Key Risks
  1. STELARA biosimilar conversion creating ~400bps revenue headwind and $25-35M gross profit hit in 2026, with future biosimilar launches in the chronic therapy portfolio posing ongoing margin risk [1][2]
  2. Thin profit margin (3.67%) leaves minimal buffer for drug pricing pressure, payer reimbursement cuts, or operational disruptions — a 1% margin compression would eliminate over 25% of net income [6]
  3. Management and cultural challenges — Glassdoor reviews citing 'revolving door' management and below-average employee satisfaction could impair clinical quality and customer retention in a labor-intensive business [4]

AI Resilience (25%)
AI Resilience LOW RISK
8
AI Risk Protection (8/9): Home and alternate site infusion services are fundamentally physical-world healthcare delivery — registered nurses travel to patients' homes, mix and administer IV medications, monitor for adverse reactions, and manage complex therapy protocols [1]. AI cannot replace the hands-on clinical work of infusion nursing, the cold-chain pharmacy logistics, or the regulatory compliance (state pharmacy licensing, controlled substance handling) that underpin the business. The site-of-care shift trend (hospital to home) is structurally driven by payer cost pressures, not technology substitution. AI Opportunity (0/1): The Palantir partnership for enterprise-wide digital transformation is in early stages with no quantified revenue contribution or cost savings reported yet — promising but not yet generating measurable AI revenue [4].
