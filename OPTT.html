Company Profile
Industrials ·Specialty Industrial Machinery ·NYSE American ·United States
Ocean Power Technologies, Inc. is a maritime domain awareness company providing autonomous maritime solutions including PowerBuoy wave-powered sensor platforms, WAM-V autonomous unmanned surface vehicles, and the Merrows AI-enabled command and control system. The company serves defense agencies, offshore energy operators, and commercial interests through Data as a Service (DaaS), Robotics as a Service (RaaS), and Power as a Service (PaaS) offerings. Founded in 1984, headquartered in Monroe Township, New Jersey.
53 Employees ·www.oceanpowertechnologies.com

Investment Thesis
Ocean Power Technologies has executed a credible pivot from wave energy to maritime domain awareness, building an AI-enabled product suite (PowerBuoy, WAM-V, Merrows) that has attracted defense contracts from the U.S. Navy, DHS, and NATO engagement [1][2]. Backlog surged 184% YoY to $15M in Q1 FY2026 with a $133.5M pipeline, and FY2025 revenue grew 6% to $5.9M. The company's AI-capable autonomous maritime solutions address growing defense demand for persistent ocean surveillance, and the optionality is in converting pipeline into recurring DaaS/RaaS revenue streams as government procurement cycles accelerate.
The bear case is overwhelming at current metrics. Revenue of $5.9M against $23.3M in operating expenses produces massive cash burn, with only $7.1M cash and -$15.8M annual free cash flow implying less than 6 months of runway without capital raises [1][3]. The stock trades at $0.315 — penny stock territory — with a $73M market cap. Revenue declined 37.8% YoY in the most recent period despite pipeline growth, indicating severe conversion challenges likely tied to government procurement delays. CEO compensation of $5.95M at a company with 53 employees and $5.9M revenue is a significant governance red flag, compounded by the Paragon Technologies proxy battle where a Delaware court found 'credible basis to suspect wrongdoing' by directors [3]. Analyst forecasts show the company remaining unprofitable for at least 3 more years, and dilutive capital raises are virtually certain.

  2. NATO Digital Ocean initiative and European defense expansion could open allied navy markets and provide credibility beyond U.S. DoD contracts
  3. AI-enabled PowerBuoy and Merrows platform deployments for DHS and Navy could accelerate procurement as maritime domain awareness becomes a higher defense priority post-2025
Key Risks
  1. Imminent dilution risk: $7.1M cash against -$15.8M annual free cash flow implies capital raise within 6-12 months, likely at deeply dilutive sub-dollar prices for existing shareholders
  2. Revenue declined 37.8% YoY despite 184% backlog growth — severe pipeline-to-revenue conversion failure suggests government procurement timing risk and limited execution capacity with only 53 employees
  3. Governance concerns from Paragon Technologies proxy battle including Delaware court findings, plus CEO compensation ($5.95M) exceeding total company revenue ($5.9M), undermine confidence in management alignment with shareholders
