Company Profile
Industrials ·Specialty Industrial Machinery ·NYSE American ·United States
Ocean Power Technologies, Inc. is a maritime domain awareness company providing intelligent maritime solutions including Data as a Service (DaaS), Robotics as a Service (RaaS), and Power as a Service (PaaS). The company designs, manufactures, and operates PowerBuoy sensor and power solutions, wave adaptive modular vessels (WAM-V), and the Merrows AI-capable command and control system for defense, security, offshore energy, and marine research markets.

Investment Thesis
Ocean Power Technologies occupies a unique niche at the intersection of autonomous maritime surveillance, defense technology, and clean ocean power — markets experiencing growing demand from both military modernization and offshore energy development. The company's strategic pivot under CEO Stratmann from hardware manufacturing to recurring maritime intelligence services has doubled funded backlog to $12.5M (up 158% YoY) with a $137.5M pipeline (up 88% YoY), while operating expenses declined 27% [1]. The AI-capable Merrows platform and autonomous WAM-V vehicles address real defense and commercial needs for persistent ocean surveillance without emissions. The Inspire Impact Score of 54 with 13 positive attributions reflects the company's environmentally beneficial mission. At $0.335/share with $77.5M market cap, the stock is priced for significant skepticism about execution.
The bear case is compelling: OPT remains deeply unprofitable with -$21.5M net loss on only $5.9M revenue, ROE of -147%, and operating margins of -1,777% [4]. Cash of $7.1M against $15.8M annual burn implies dilutive financing within 6 months absent dramatic revenue acceleration. The Paragon Technologies governance dispute in 2024 exposed board-level dysfunction [3]. Revenue growth decelerated sharply from triple-digit rates to just 6% YoY in the most recent fiscal year, raising questions about backlog conversion timelines. The company has only 53 employees and faces competition from well-capitalized defense primes (L3Harris, Northrop Grumman) and offshore energy incumbents. Stock analyst consensus target of $1.50 implies significant upside but requires faith in execution that the financial track record does not yet support.
