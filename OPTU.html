Optimum Communications (formerly Altice USA) is one of the largest broadband communications and video services providers in the U.S., delivering broadband, video, mobile, proprietary content, and advertising services to approximately 4.5 million residential and business customers across 21 states. The company also operates the Lightpath fiber-based B2B infrastructure business.

Investment Thesis
Optimum Communications is a structurally challenged cable incumbent whose 2026 'offensive' plan is really a defensive triage. Revenue fell 2.3% YoY to $2.18B in Q4 2025 with a margin collapse producing a $3.47 EPS loss, and broadband subscriber trends remain under pressure against fiber and fixed-wireless overbuilders [1][4]. The balance sheet carries heavy debt and negative stockholders' equity, leaving almost no margin for error on the operational turnaround [2]. Forward P/E of 2.4x and a $1.43 stock price reflect market skepticism that management can stabilize broadband ARPU and restart growth before the debt load forces a restructuring [5]. Lightpath fiber B2B and mobile show pockets of momentum, but they are not yet large enough to offset residential cable decline [1].
The bull case rests on Lightpath growth, debt reduction execution, and the convergence-led pricing strategy stabilizing broadband net adds in 2026 [2]. AI-driven data consumption is a structural tailwind for broadband pipes, and deeply negative sentiment means any positive surprise could re-rate the equity. However, Glassdoor sentiment (44% recommend, 2.9/5 culture) and an announced executive leadership transition plan indicate organizational fragility as the turnaround runs [2]. Against fiber overbuilders with superior unit economics and fixed-wireless competitors with zero last-mile capex, Optimum's structural disadvantages are deepening, not easing. This is a highly leveraged equity stub on a telecom business in secular decline — SELL.
Key Catalysts
  1. Lightpath fiber B2B and mobile momentum continuing to offset residential cable declines and contribute to EBITDA growth [1]
  2. Convergence-led pricing and packaging stabilizing broadband net adds in 2026, easing the 2.3% revenue decline [2]
  3. Meaningful debt reduction or refinancing that removes near-term balance-sheet risk and allows equity to re-rate from distressed levels [2]
Key Risks
  1. Heavy debt load with negative stockholders' equity — continued EBITDA deterioration could trigger a restructuring that wipes out the equity [2]
  2. Fiber overbuilders (Verizon Fios, AT&T Fiber) and fixed-wireless entrants (T-Mobile, Verizon) continuing to erode broadband market share in Optimum's 21-state footprint [2][3]
  3. Execution risk on the CEO-led turnaround, with an announced executive leadership transition plan and below-average Glassdoor sentiment (44% recommend) [2]
