Company Profile
Basic Materials ·Gold ·NYSE ·Canada
OR Royalties Inc. (formerly Osisko Gold Royalties) acquires and manages precious metal royalties, streams, and other interests in mining operations primarily in Canada and internationally. Its primary asset is a 3-5% net smelter return royalty on the Canadian Malartic complex. The company also holds options on offtake and exclusive rights to participate in future royalty/stream financings. Founded in 2014, headquartered in Montreal, Canada.

Investment Thesis
OR Royalties offers one of the cleanest financial profiles in the mining sector — 74.3% profit margins, 96.7% gross margins, $151M free cash flow, and D/E of 0.35% — all leveraged to the gold price environment [1]. Q1 2026 revenue of $102.8M (87% YoY growth) with 97% cash margins demonstrates the extraordinary operating leverage of the royalty model when gold is at $4,873/oz [1]. Revenue grew 59.4% in FY2025 to $277M, and the five-year plan targeting 110,000-125,000 GEOs by 2029 provides organic production growth independent of commodity prices. The fortress balance sheet ($142M cash, $5M debt) and low 19.3% payout ratio give management flexibility for accretive acquisitions [4]. At 23.9x forward P/E with 59% revenue growth and industry-leading ESG credentials ('AA' MSCI, Sustainalytics Top-Rated), OR represents a premium gold exposure vehicle with downside protection from the royalty model's zero operating cost structure [3].
The bear case centers on gold price dependency and valuation sensitivity. OR Royalties' revenue surge is primarily driven by gold at $4,873/oz — a historical extreme [1]. A gold price correction to $3,000/oz would compress revenue by ~40% and earnings by a larger percentage given the fixed overhead structure. The trailing P/E of 37x reflects the current gold euphoria and could contract sharply in a risk-on environment. The CEO transition from founder Roosen to Jason Attew in 2024 introduces execution uncertainty — Attew is experienced but has not yet executed a major transformative deal [2]. Dividend yield of 0.55% is nominal and provides minimal income support. The Canadian Malartic complex concentration (primary asset) creates single-asset risk despite the 175+ royalty portfolio. Competing royalty/streaming companies (Franco-Nevada, Wheaton) have larger portfolios and longer track records. The 'OR Royalties' rebrand signals potential diversification beyond gold, which could dilute the pure-play gold exposure that attracts investors to the stock [2].

  1. Q1 2026 revenue of $102.8M with 97% cash margins implies a $400M+ annual revenue run rate at current gold prices, representing a step-change in financial scale [1]
