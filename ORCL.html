Investment Thesis
Oracle's Q3 FY2026 results showed accelerating momentum with cloud infrastructure revenue growing 84% YoY and AI revenue up 243% [1]. RPO of $553B — quadrupled from a year earlier — provides unprecedented revenue visibility. The new co-CEO structure positions Oracle to simultaneously capture AI infrastructure (Magouyrk) and industry-specific AI applications (Sicilia). FY2027 revenue guidance of $90B far exceeds analyst expectations. At 18.8x forward earnings after a 50%+ decline from highs, Oracle offers compelling value for the leading enterprise AI platform.
The bear case centers on Oracle's massive $50B FY2026 capex commitment generating negative $13.18B TTM free cash flow, funded by $415% D/E leverage [1][3]. If AI infrastructure spending decelerates — as some fear from DeepSeek-style efficiency gains — Oracle's heavy fixed-cost structure would amplify earnings pressure. The stock has already fallen 50%+ from September 2025 highs, suggesting the market is pricing in execution risk. The dual-CEO model is unproven, and Oracle's cloud market share still trails AWS and Azure significantly. Carbon emissions increased 18% as data center expansion accelerates.
Key Catalysts
  1. Q4 FY2026 guidance for $1.92-1.96 EPS with 19-20% revenue growth, and FY2027 revenue target of $90B representing major acceleration
  2. AI infrastructure revenue trajectory from $4.9B quarterly run rate with six hyperscaler customers and $553B RPO backlog
  3. Co-CEO model successfully capturing both infrastructure (Magouyrk) and industry applications (Sicilia) revenue streams simultaneously
Key Risks
  1. Massive capex cycle creating $13.18B negative FCF — if AI demand disappoints, Oracle faces years of debt servicing without proportional revenue growth
  2. D/E ratio of 415% leaves minimal margin for error; rising interest rates or credit downgrades could significantly increase financing costs
  3. Dual-CEO model risk: accountability dilution and innovation tension if Ellison's continued presence stifles fresh leadership momentum
