Investment Thesis
Oracle's Q3 FY2026 results showed accelerating momentum with total revenue of $17.2B (+22% YoY), cloud revenue of $8.9B (+44%), and the first quarter in 15 years where organic revenue and EPS both grew 20%+ [1]. RPO of $553B — quadrupled from a year earlier — provides multi-year revenue visibility driven by AI infrastructure demand from hyperscalers. The new co-CEO structure positions Oracle to capture both AI infrastructure (Magouyrk) and industry-specific AI applications (Sicilia) simultaneously. FY2027 revenue guidance raised to $90B, and at a forward PEG of 1.24, Oracle offers compelling growth-adjusted value for an enterprise AI infrastructure play with 57.6% ROE and 25.3% profit margins [1][7].
The bear case centers on Oracle's massive capex commitment generating -$22.3B TTM free cash flow, funded by 415% D/E leverage ($162B debt vs. $39B cash) [7]. If AI infrastructure spending decelerates — whether from DeepSeek-style efficiency gains or macro slowdown — Oracle's heavy fixed-cost structure amplifies earnings pressure. The 5-year revenue CAGR of 8.0% reflects that the current hyper-growth is recent and unproven at scale. The dual-CEO model is untested, and Ellison's continued boardroom presence may constrain fresh leadership. Cloud market share still trails AWS and Azure significantly, and at 24.4x forward earnings with negative FCF, the valuation assumes flawless execution on an unprecedented infrastructure build [1][3][7].
Key Catalysts
  1. Q4 FY2026 guidance for 19-21% revenue growth and 46-50% cloud revenue growth, with FY2027 revenue target raised to $90B representing a $30B+ increase from FY2025 [1]
  2. RPO of $553B converting to revenue as AI infrastructure demand from hyperscalers (OpenAI, Meta, others) accelerates through 2026-2027 [1][2]
  3. Co-CEO model unlocking simultaneous growth in infrastructure (OCI) and industry-specific AI applications, with multicloud database revenue growing 531% [1][3]
Key Risks
  1. D/E ratio of 415% and -$22.3B TTM free cash flow leave minimal margin for error — rising interest rates or AI demand disappointment could trigger credit pressure on $162B in debt [7]
  2. AI infrastructure commoditization risk as AWS, Azure, and Google Cloud invest in custom silicon and expand capacity, potentially compressing OCI's pricing power [2]
  3. Dual-CEO model accountability risk compounded by Ellison/Catz continued boardroom presence — if strategic clarity falters, the organizational structure makes course correction harder [3]
