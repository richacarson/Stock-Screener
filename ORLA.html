Company Profile

Orla Mining is a Canadian gold producer operating the Camino Rojo mine in Zacatecas, Mexico, the Musselwhite mine in Canada (acquired 2025), and the South Railroad development project in Elko, Nevada. Led by CEO Jason Simpson since 2018, Orla is a mid-tier growth gold producer targeting 340,000-360,000 ounces in 2026.

Investment Thesis
Orla Mining is a mid-tier gold growth story with Q1 2026 production of ~81,200 ounces on track for full-year guidance of 340,000-360,000 oz — meaningful growth from 300,620 oz delivered in 2025 (which itself exceeded revised guidance) [1][2]. The balance sheet is strong with $427.3M cash and $96.0M net cash, providing ample flexibility to develop the South Railroad Nevada project [1]. Revenue growth of 308% TTM and earnings growth of 170% reflect the transformational 2025 Musselwhite acquisition and rising gold prices [6]. Forward P/E of 10.1x is reasonable for a growth gold miner with multi-asset production in stable jurisdictions (Canada, Mexico, Nevada) and industry-leading GHG intensity of 0.24 tCO2e/oz at Camino Rojo [5][6]. CEO Jason Simpson (since 2018) has a strong operational track record from his Torex Gold COO role.
The bear case centers on the March 2026 USMCA Rapid Response Labor Mechanism panel finding of 'severe' denial of rights at Camino Rojo, with the panel naming Camino Rojo as the 'main promoter in the Denial of Rights' for facilitating worker disaffiliation from the Los Mineros union [5]. This is a significant governance and reputational overhang and could trigger additional trade remedies or operational restrictions. Gold price sensitivity remains the primary swing factor — at current elevated gold prices Orla generates strong margins, but a reversal could quickly compress the forward P/E setup [6]. Mexico concentration (Camino Rojo as the flagship asset) adds jurisdictional and security risk; the panel's own finding referenced 'organized crime in the area of the mine' [5]. P/E of 59.3x trailing reflects near-peak earnings power on current gold prices; execution on South Railroad and Musselwhite integration is necessary to justify the forward multiple.
Key Catalysts
  1. 2026 gold production growth to 340,000-360,000 oz (mid-point +17% YoY), leveraging Musselwhite full-year contribution plus Camino Rojo and South Railroad progress [1]
  2. South Railroad Nevada development providing jurisdictional diversification and multi-year production growth runway [4]
  3. Sustained elevated gold prices (driven in part by AI-era monetary dynamics and central bank demand) supporting forward margins [3]
Key Risks
  1. USMCA labor rights violation finding at Camino Rojo (March 2026) — reputational damage, potential trade remedies, and governance overhang [5]
  2. Gold price reversal — Orla's forward P/E of 10.1x assumes sustained current gold prices; a 15-20% decline would materially compress margins [6]
  3. Mexico concentration and security risk — organized crime in the Camino Rojo area was explicitly cited in the USMCA panel finding [5]

AI Resilience (25%)
AI Resilience MODERATE
6
AI Risk Protection (5/9): Gold mining is fundamentally resistant to AI disruption — physical ore extraction and processing cannot be automated away. Simpson notes the 'dual benefit' that AI consumes minerals (data centers) while also being a tool Orla can deploy [3]. Gold itself may benefit as a monetary asset during AI-driven economic transitions. However, AI does not create meaningful competitive differentiation among gold miners, so the moat is commodity-level, not AI-specific. AI Opportunity (1/1): Operational AI for exploration targeting, predictive maintenance, and process optimization offers modest efficiency improvement; AI infrastructure demand supports sustained gold price as alternative store of value [3].
