Company Profile
Technology ·Software - Infrastructure ·NASDAQ ·United States
OneStream delivers a unified, AI-enabled enterprise finance platform that replaces fragmented legacy tools for financial close, consolidation, planning, budgeting, forecasting, and ESG reporting. The company's Digital Finance Cloud platform serves over 1,700 customers including 18% of Fortune 500 companies and was recognized as a 5x Gartner Magic Quadrant Leader. OneStream was acquired by Hg Capital in a $6.4B all-cash deal ($24/share) that closed on April 1, 2026; the stock is no longer publicly traded on NASDAQ.

Investment Thesis
NOTE: OneStream was acquired by Hg Capital on April 1, 2026 at $24/share ($6.4B equity value) and is no longer publicly traded. This analysis reflects the company's last available public data and is provided for reference only. OneStream demonstrated strong category leadership — 5x Gartner Magic Quadrant Leader, 24% YoY revenue growth to $602M in FY2025, subscription revenue of $550M (up 28%), and a rapidly scaling SensibleAI portfolio with 60% YoY AI bookings growth [1][2]. GAAP operating margin improved from -36% to -3% in Q4 2025, suggesting an inflection toward profitability. ARR of $699M and RPO of $1.4B provided strong forward revenue visibility [1]. Founder-CEO Tom Shea's 14-year tenure and 12.7% ownership aligned leadership with long-term value creation.
The acquisition by Hg removes OneStream from public market evaluation and limits ongoing transparency. At the time of the deal, the key risks included sustained unprofitability (-8.4% profit margin, -12.4% ROE), intense AI competitive pressure from Microsoft Copilot for Finance, SAP, Oracle, and Workday — all with vastly greater resources — and the existential uncertainty CEO Shea acknowledged about 'winners and losers' emerging in the AI finance landscape within 24-36 months [2][3]. The Hg deal validated the platform's strategic value at 10.6x EV/revenue but also reflected urgency to invest in AI capabilities without quarterly earnings scrutiny, suggesting the path to profitability requires sustained investment that may not have been well-received in public markets.
