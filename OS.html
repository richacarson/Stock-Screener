Company Profile
Technology ·Software - Infrastructure ·NASDAQ ·United States
OneStream delivers a unified, AI-enabled enterprise finance platform that replaces fragmented legacy tools for financial close, consolidation, planning, budgeting, forecasting, and ESG reporting. The company's Digital Finance Cloud platform serves mid-to-large enterprises globally and was recognized as a 5x Gartner Magic Quadrant Leader. OneStream is in the process of being taken private by Hg Capital in a $6.4B all-cash deal at $24 per share.

Investment Thesis
OneStream is a high-growth enterprise finance platform with strong category leadership — 5x Gartner Magic Quadrant Leader, 24% YoY revenue growth to $602M in 2025, and a rapidly scaling SensibleAI portfolio with 60% YoY AI bookings growth [1][2]. The company's GAAP operating margin improved dramatically from -36% to -3% in Q4 2025, suggesting an inflection point toward profitability. The $6.4B Hg acquisition at $24/share provides a near-term price floor and validates the platform's strategic value. Founder-CEO Tom Shea's 14-year tenure and 12.7% ownership align leadership with long-term value creation [2].
The bear case is defined by the pending take-private at $24/share, which effectively caps near-term upside for public shareholders and creates regulatory approval risk if the deal fails. OneStream remains unprofitable with -8.4% profit margin and -12.4% ROE, and the Forward P/E of 51x prices in significant growth expectations that may not materialize in a competitive market [4]. AI disruption risk is real — Microsoft Copilot for Finance, SAP's AI offerings, and Workday's ML capabilities all target OneStream's customer base with vastly greater resources. CEO Shea's own acknowledgment that the AI finance landscape will produce 'winners and losers' in 24-36 months underscores the existential competitive pressure [2]. The Hg deal removes OneStream from public market transparency, making ongoing evaluation difficult.
Key Catalysts
  1. Hg Capital acquisition at $24/share expected to close in 2026, providing a price floor and enabling accelerated AI investment without quarterly earnings pressure [2]
  2. SensibleAI portfolio expansion with 60% YoY AI bookings growth and 30+ plug-and-play AI routines driving customer expansion and net retention [2]
  3. Path to profitability — GAAP operating margin improved from -36% to -3% in Q4 2025, suggesting potential GAAP profitability in 2026 [1]
Key Risks
  1. Pending Hg acquisition at $24/share caps public market upside — if the deal closes, returns are limited; if it fails, the stock could face significant downside from deal-premium unwind [2]
  2. AI competitive pressure from Microsoft, SAP, Oracle, and Workday — all are integrating AI into finance platforms with resources orders of magnitude greater than OneStream's $602M revenue base [2]
  3. Sustained unprofitability — negative profit margin and ROE after 12+ years suggest the business model may require continued heavy investment to maintain competitive position, with no guarantee of reaching durable profitability [1][4]
