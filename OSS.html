Company Profile

One Stop Systems designs and manufactures rugged enterprise-class compute, storage, and interconnect platforms for AI, ML, sensor processing, and autonomy at the edge — with a deep footprint in U.S. defense programs (P-8 Poseidon, Army combat vehicles) and commercial robotics. CEO Mike Knowles (since June 2023) has refocused the company on AI/ML and rugged edge after divesting the Bressner commercial business.

Investment Thesis
OSS has executed a clean strategic pivot under CEO Mike Knowles — divesting the lower-margin Bressner commercial business and refocusing on rugged AI edge compute for defense and commercial robotics [1][3]. Q4 2025 continuing-ops revenue jumped 70.2% YoY to $12.0M at a record 58.5% gross margin, and FY25 continuing revenue rose 31.2% to $32.2M [1][2]. 2026 guidance of 20-25% revenue growth with ~40% gross margin and positive EBITDA is supported by multi-year P-8 Poseidon and Safran Federal Systems awards plus an emerging U.S. Army AI-enabled video concentrator program for Stryker, Bradley, and Abrams combat vehicles [1][3]. The balance sheet is debt-free (D/E 3.2%) and the Inspire Impact Score of +53 with eight positive attributions (Customer Privacy, Data Security, Employee Wellbeing, Ethical Supply Chain, GHG Emissions, Materials Efficiency, Product Access, Regulatory Adherence) adds faith-alignment tailwind [5].
The bear case is small-cap execution risk and valuation. ROE is negative at -8.5% and forward P/E of 116.8 reflects the market pricing in future profitability that hasn't yet materialized in GAAP earnings [5]. Market cap of only $217M and average daily volume of 1.9M shares create liquidity and volatility risk — any defense budget disruption or single-program slip could materially impact guidance. Defense revenue concentration (P-8 Poseidon, Army, Safran) creates program-specific risk, and CEO Knowles has only been in place since mid-2023, so the strategic pivot has not yet been tested across multiple procurement cycles [3][1]. Gross margin is expected to compress from Q4's 58.5% to ~40% in 2026 as mix shifts toward defense programs. Competition from larger primes (Curtiss-Wright, Mercury Systems) could pressure wins over time.
Key Catalysts
  1. 2026 revenue growth of 20-25% with positive EBITDA, supported by P-8 Poseidon, Safran Federal Systems, and U.S. Army AI video concentrator programs [1][2]
  2. Expansion of U.S. Army AI-enabled video concentrator from initial purchase order into broader Stryker/Bradley/Abrams deployments [1][3]
  3. Commercial robotics programs diversify the customer base away from pure defense dependency [1]
Key Risks
  1. Negative ROE (-8.5%) and forward P/E of 116.8 leave little valuation cushion if execution slips [5]
  2. Defense revenue concentration — any P-8 Poseidon or Army program delay could materially impact 2026 guidance [1][3]
  3. Small-cap liquidity risk (market cap $217M) and competitive pressure from larger rugged compute primes (Curtiss-Wright, Mercury) [5]
