Company Profile
Financial Services ·Asset Management ·NYSE ·United States
Blue Owl Technology Finance Corp. (OTF) is an externally managed business development company (BDC) focused on direct lending to U.S. enterprise software and technology companies. The portfolio holds 199 investments at $14.3B aggregate fair value, with average borrower revenue of $945M and EBITDA of ~$300M. OTF listed on the NYSE in June 2025 after operating as a non-traded BDC since 2018.

Investment Thesis
OTF offers a ~12% dividend yield from a Blue Owl-managed BDC that grew its portfolio to $14.3B across 199 enterprise software and technology borrowers in 2025, with revenue up 67% and earnings up 126% [1][2]. The borrower base is high-quality — average revenue of $945M and EBITDA of ~$300M with low- to mid-teens revenue and EBITDA growth — well above typical middle-market direct lending. The June 2025 NYSE listing gives OTF permanent capital and public-market liquidity, and the fund deployed $4.8B in 2025 with backlog positioning it at the bottom of targeted leverage entering Q1 2026. Forward P/E of 8.5 and a ~12% yield with stable distribution history (7 years of payments since the 2018 non-traded launch) make OTF look attractively priced for a senior-secured tech-credit play managed by Blue Owl Capital's experienced credit team led by Co-CEO Marc Lipschultz (ex-KKR, 20+ years).
The bear case is structural and acute right now. Roughly 70% of OTF's portfolio is in enterprise software — exactly the sector under the most aggressive AI disruption pressure in 2026, and Bloomberg reporting plus an April 8, 2026 Moody's negative outlook on a Blue Owl fund highlight that medium-term software loans (3–5 years to maturity) are being repriced for AI-displacement default risk [5][6]. Blue Owl's stock closed at a record low in early April 2026 amid private-credit redemption fears across the $1.8 trillion direct-lending market. While OTF's listed structure insulates it from interval-fund redemption mechanics, NAV and dividend coverage would deteriorate quickly if software-borrower defaults materialize. The externally managed BDC structure means fees flow to Blue Owl regardless of NAV trajectory, creating principal-agent tension. Faith alignment is neutral (Inspire score 0). For now, OTF is a watch-list yield idea pending evidence that software-credit losses remain contained.
