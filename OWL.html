Investment Thesis
Blue Owl trades at 8.5x forward earnings with a 9.9% dividend yield following a 42.8% YTD decline, despite $307B in AUM, $56B in 2025 capital raises, and $28.4B in fee-eligible but not-yet-paying AUM that embeds 13% revenue growth [1]. The $27B Meta AI data center JV positions Blue Owl as the leading private capital provider for hyperscaler infrastructure buildout. FRE margin guidance of 58.5% in 2026 with acceleration into 2027 suggests improving profitability trajectory.
The bear case centers on the Century Capital Partners collapse that exposed private credit risk oversight gaps, permanent redemption restrictions on Blue Owl Capital Corp II that signal structural liquidity concerns, and the Saba Capital tender offer at a 33% NAV discount [3]. The 42.8% YTD stock decline reflects market skepticism about private credit durability. The payout ratio appears unsustainably high at current earnings levels, and a dividend cut would remove a key holder incentive. AUM growth could slow if private credit markets face a recession-driven default cycle.
Key Catalysts
  1. Fee-eligible AUM conversion: $28.4B in AUM not yet paying fees represents $325M in incremental annual management fees as capital is deployed
  2. Meta Hyperion data center JV ($27B) and broader AI infrastructure capital deployment generating new fee streams
  3. FRE per share growth acceleration into 2027 as fundraising seasons and new strategies contribute to earnings
Key Risks
  1. Private credit default cycle risk: if economic conditions deteriorate, Blue Owl's lending portfolios could face impairments that pressure NAV and fee income
  2. Century Capital Partners collapse and OBDC II redemption restrictions highlight governance and liquidity risks in private credit vehicles
  3. Dividend sustainability: 855% payout ratio at current GAAP earnings is unsustainable; distributable earnings basis is more favorable but still demands growth
