Company Profile
Financial Services ·Asset Management ·NYSE ·United States
Blue Owl Capital Inc. is an alternative asset manager with $315 billion in AUM across three multi-strategy platforms: Credit (direct lending, alternative credit, liquid credit), GP Strategic Capital (GP minority stakes, GP debt financing, professional sports stakes), and Real Assets (triple net lease real estate, data center infrastructure). The firm emphasizes permanent capital vehicles and long-dated private funds.

Investment Thesis
Blue Owl Capital offers a rare combination of hyper-growth and permanent capital durability: AUM surged from ~$52B at IPO to $315B by Q1 2026, driven by record $56B annual fundraising and transformative acquisitions like IPI Partners and the $27B Meta Hyperion JV [1][2]. The stock trades at just 10.5x forward earnings and a 0.15x PEG ratio — absurdly cheap for an asset manager compounding AUM at 30%+ with 96% permanent or long-dated capital. The 8.7% dividend yield (which has grown 25%+ annually since IPO) provides income while the underlying business scales toward fee-related earnings of $1.00+ per share [7]. Blue Owl's GP Strategic Capital platform has no direct public-market competitor at scale, providing structural moat around a unique business model.
The bear case centers on GAAP profitability distortion and governance concerns: trailing profit margin is just 3.0% due to $2.1B+ in annual equity-based compensation and intangible amortization from acquisitions, making the true economics difficult to assess [7]. The April 2026 fee lawsuit alleging excessive charges at Blue Owl Credit Advisors and the OBDC II redemption restriction signal potential stakeholder trust erosion [8]. Q1 2026 revenue of $397M missed the $426M estimate, and the investment portfolio shrank from $16.5B to $15.3B in fair value as credit spreads widened [1]. The 88x trailing P/E (versus 10.5x forward) highlights how dependent the investment case is on forward earnings materialization. Blue Owl's rapid growth through acquisitions creates integration risk, and any sustained downturn in alternatives fundraising would pressure management fees and the dividend.
Key Catalysts
  1. Meta Hyperion data center campus ($27B JV with 80% Blue Owl ownership) generating long-term rental income as AI infrastructure demand accelerates through 2027-2030
  2. Fee-related earnings convergence toward $1.00+ per share as permanent capital AUM matures and acquisition-related amortization fades, compressing the gap between GAAP and distributable earnings

Key Risks
  1. April 2026 fee lawsuit and OBDC II redemption restriction signal governance and stakeholder trust concerns that could damage the firm's fundraising brand with institutional allocators
  2. GAAP profitability is opaque — 3.0% trailing profit margin masks underlying economics, and heavy equity-based compensation dilutes existing shareholders by ~2-3% annually
  3. Credit spread widening reduced the investment portfolio from $16.5B to $15.3B in Q1 2026, and a sustained credit downturn would impair both portfolio value and new lending activity
