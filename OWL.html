Investment Thesis
Blue Owl Capital manages $315B in AUM with Q1 2026 revenues of $753.8M (up 13% YoY), fee-related earnings of $393.6M (up 14%), and FRE margins expanding to 58.4% [1]. The firm's permanent capital model generates predictable management fees, and $29.9B of AUM not yet paying fees represents ~$349M in annualized fee upside once deployed. The $7B Digital Infrastructure Fund III and $15B Crusoe data center joint venture position Blue Owl as a beneficiary of AI infrastructure spending [2].
The bear case is severe and immediate: AI disruption fears triggered a $5.4B redemption surge, forced the firm to halt withdrawals at Blue Owl Capital Corporation II, and resulted in a dividend cut from $0.37 to $0.31 per share [1][2]. Bloomberg described shares as near 'doomsday levels' ahead of Q1 earnings. The GAAP payout ratio of 750% (distorted by intangible amortization) and trailing profit margin of 3% obscure the underlying economics, but the gap between GAAP and distributable earnings creates investor confusion. The stock at $10.08 ($6.8B market cap) with a 9.1% yield prices in significant distress — either the market is wrong about the software portfolio risk, or Blue Owl faces a prolonged de-rating of its asset management franchise [1][2].
Key Catalysts
  1. Deployment of $29.9B in AUM not yet paying fees, which would add ~$349M in annualized management fee revenue once fee holidays expire [1]
  2. Digital Infrastructure Fund III ($7B) and Crusoe data center JV ($15B) generating carried interest and management fees from AI infrastructure tailwinds [2]
  3. Resolution of software portfolio AI fears — if AI disruption proves limited (as Blue Owl, Blackstone, and Ares jointly stated), the 9.1% yield and 10x forward P/E represent significant re-rating potential [2]
Key Risks
  1. AI disruption risk to the ~8% software lending portfolio could accelerate credit losses and trigger further redemption waves beyond the $5.4B already requested [2]
  2. Forced redemption caps and dividend cuts erode investor confidence in Blue Owl's permanent capital model — the firm's core value proposition of stable, long-duration capital is being tested [1]
  3. GAAP payout ratio of 750% and trailing profit margin of 3% create fundamental confusion about Blue Owl's true earnings power, deterring institutional investors [1]
