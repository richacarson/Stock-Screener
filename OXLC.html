Company Profile
Financial Services ·Asset Management ·NASDAQ ·United States
Oxford Lane Capital Corp. is a publicly-traded closed-end management investment company registered under the Investment Company Act of 1940. The company seeks attractive risk-adjusted total returns by investing primarily in debt and equity tranches of CLO (collateralized loan obligation) vehicles collateralized by diversified portfolios of senior secured loans.

Investment Thesis
Oxford Lane Capital offers an exceptionally high current yield of approximately 24% through monthly distributions of $0.20/share, backed by a diversified portfolio of CLO equity and debt tranches [1][3]. The recent distribution cut from $0.40 to $0.20 retains roughly 40% of core net investment income ($1.12/share quarterly core NII) to rebuild NAV and pursue opportunistic CLO equity purchases at market dislocations [1]. The forward P/E of 2.25x suggests significant upside if earnings stabilize, and the extended weighted average reinvestment period to August 2029 provides cash flow visibility. Conservative leverage (D/E 37.8%) and $263M in newly acquired CLO equity awaiting first distributions provide a pipeline of future income [1].
The bear case is significant: NAV declined sharply from $19.19 to $15.51 per share in Q3 FY2026 as CLO equity valuations fell amid credit spread widening and tariff-driven economic uncertainty [1]. The 50% distribution cut signals that prior payout levels were unsustainable, and the payout ratio near 96-105% historically suggests the fund was returning capital rather than income. GAAP NII fell to $0.16/share from $0.22 YoY while core NII declined from $0.41 to $0.29, revealing deteriorating underlying cash generation [1]. As a leveraged CLO equity vehicle, OXLC is highly sensitive to credit cycles — a recession or wave of leveraged loan defaults could devastate NAV further. The stock's premium to NAV (~$10 vs. estimated $13.48-$13.78 NAV) has compressed, and further NAV erosion could push the stock well below current levels [4].
Key Catalysts
  1. Deployment of $263M in newly acquired CLO equity awaiting first distributions, which could materially boost future core NII once cash flows begin [1]
  2. Extended weighted average reinvestment period to August 2029 provides longer cash flow runway and potential for CLO equity appreciation as deals season [1]
  3. Opportunistic CLO equity purchases at discounted valuations during credit market dislocations, enabled by retained earnings from the distribution cut [1]
Key Risks
  1. NAV erosion risk — CLO equity valuations are highly sensitive to credit spreads, leveraged loan default rates, and macroeconomic conditions; a recession could accelerate NAV declines from the current $15.51/share [1]
  2. Income sustainability — GAAP NII of $0.16/share is far below the $0.20/share monthly distribution, meaning the fund relies on core NII adjustments and unrealized gains to support payouts [1]
  3. Interest rate and credit cycle risk — rising defaults in the leveraged loan market would reduce CLO equity cash flows and trigger further distribution cuts, while the external management fee structure ($0.20/share monthly) reduces alignment with shareholders [2][3]
