Company Profile
Financial Services ·Asset Management ·NASDAQ ·United States
Oxford Square Capital Corp. is a publicly traded business development company operating as a closed-end, non-diversified management investment company. The firm invests primarily in corporate debt securities of technology-related companies and collateralized loan obligation (CLO) structured finance investments, targeting companies with revenues under $200 million and enterprise values under $300 million.

Investment Thesis
Oxford Square offers a 23.2% yield and trades at a deeply discounted forward P/E of 6.5x, potentially appealing to income-focused investors willing to accept NAV erosion in exchange for high current cash flow [2]. The company's 23-year dividend payment history provides some comfort that distributions will continue in some form, and CEO Cohen's technology sector expertise positions the portfolio to benefit if tech lending conditions improve. Debt-to-equity of 104% is moderate, and the $2.5M daily dollar volume provides adequate liquidity for individual investors.
The bear case is overwhelming: OXSQ's 466% payout ratio means distributions dramatically exceed net investment income, funded by return of capital that systematically destroys NAV [2]. Return on equity is -12.2% with a -46.4% net margin, meaning the portfolio is generating losses, not income. Q1 2026 reported EPS of -$0.17 versus the $0.07 consensus estimate, a massive miss accompanied by negative revenue of -$10.7M versus $10.2M expected — indicating unrealized losses swamping income. The stock has lost approximately 80% of its value since listing on NASDAQ, and CLO equity yields are compressing, threatening the primary income source. Analysts at Seeking Alpha have explicitly labeled OXSQ a 'dividend trap with an unjustified risk-reward profile,' and the mathematical reality of distributions exceeding income makes further NAV erosion and dividend cuts virtually inevitable.
Key Catalysts
  1. Stabilization of CLO equity yields and tech credit markets could slow NAV erosion and improve net investment income coverage of the monthly distribution
  2. Tech sector lending recovery driven by AI adoption increasing borrowing demand from OXSQ's target market of sub-$300M enterprise value technology companies
  3. Potential strategic alternatives including merger with a larger BDC platform that could reduce management costs and improve portfolio diversification
Key Risks
  1. Further dividend cuts are virtually certain given the 466% payout ratio and -$0.17 EPS versus $0.07 consensus in Q1 2026, likely triggering additional share price declines as income investors exit
  2. Continued NAV erosion from distributing capital in excess of earnings, compressing the equity base and increasing leverage ratios over time — an 80% cumulative value decline since listing demonstrates this structural deterioration
  3. CLO equity concentration risk — compressed CLO yields and rising default rates in leveraged loans could simultaneously reduce OXSQ's income and impair its largest asset class, creating a reinforcing downward spiral
