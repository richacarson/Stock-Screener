Company Profile
Energy ·Oil & Gas E&P ·NYSE ·United States
Occidental Petroleum Corporation is an integrated oil and gas company with operations in the United States, Middle East, and North Africa. The company operates through three segments: Oil and Gas (exploration and production), OxyChem (chemical manufacturing), and Midstream and Marketing. OXY is also a major investor in direct air capture (DAC) carbon removal technology through its 1PointFive subsidiary. New CEO Richard Jackson succeeded Vicki Hollub effective June 1, 2026. Warren Buffett's Berkshire Hathaway is the largest shareholder.

Investment Thesis
OXY at 13.2x forward earnings with a PEG of 1.13 offers value exposure to the energy sector with a unique carbon capture optionality. Q1 2026 net income of $3.2B ($3.13 reported EPS, $1.06 adjusted) demonstrates strong underlying profitability, with record production of 1.434M BOE/day in 2025 growing to 1.45M targeted for 2026 [1]. Warren Buffett's Berkshire Hathaway holding ~28% of shares provides an institutional quality endorsement. Profit margin of 22.4%, D/E of 42%, and $3.0B free cash flow demonstrate financial health. The 1PointFive DAC portfolio — including the STRATOS plant and $500M ADNOC partnership — provides unique exposure to the emerging carbon removal economy that no other publicly traded E&P company offers [3].
The bear case centers on commodity price sensitivity and revenue decline. Revenue fell 27% YoY to $20B TTM from the 2022 commodity supercycle peak of $36.6B, and the 3-year revenue CAGR of -16.2% reflects structural normalization [1]. Trailing P/E of 71.7x versus forward P/E of 13.2x signals volatile earnings that depend on oil prices outside OXY's control. The $16.6B debt load, while manageable at 42% D/E, requires sustained commodity prices to service. The DAC strategy faces significant criticism as greenwashing — Carbon Market Watch calls it a 'license to pollute' — and the technology remains commercially unproven at scale [3]. CEO transition from Hollub to Jackson introduces execution uncertainty during a period of commodity weakness. Payout ratio of 132% means the dividend currently exceeds earnings, though free cash flow covers it.
Key Catalysts
  1. STRATOS direct air capture plant achieving commercial-scale operations and generating carbon credit revenue, validating the $500M+ DAC investment thesis and differentiating OXY from E&P peers
  2. Oil price recovery above $80/barrel WTI would rapidly expand earnings given OXY's high operating leverage — forward P/E of 13.2x implies significant upside at normalized commodity prices
  3. New CEO Richard Jackson's operational focus on cost efficiency and technology application could drive margin expansion on the 1.45M BOE/day production base
Key Risks
  1. Oil price decline below $60/barrel WTI would severely compress margins and threaten the dividend — OXY's revenue is highly correlated with commodity prices, as demonstrated by the 27% YoY revenue decline from 2022 peaks
  2. DAC technology remains commercially unproven at scale with 'greenwashing' criticism from environmental groups — if carbon credits fail to generate meaningful revenue, the billions invested in 1PointFive become stranded assets
  3. CEO transition from Vicki Hollub (who orchestrated the CrownRock acquisition and DAC strategy) to Richard Jackson introduces strategic continuity risk during a period of commodity weakness and elevated debt

AI Resilience (25%)
AI Resilience LOW RISK
9
AI Risk Protection (8/9): Oil and gas exploration, production, and refining are physical-world operations that AI fundamentally cannot substitute — extracting hydrocarbons from geological formations, operating refineries, and managing pipeline infrastructure require massive capital assets and physical-world expertise [1]. AI may modestly improve seismic analysis, drilling optimization, and predictive maintenance, but these are efficiency tools that benefit incumbents like OXY rather than enabling new entrants. The energy transition to AI (data centers, compute infrastructure) actually increases near-term oil and gas demand as the power grid scales. No analysts have identified AI as a substitution risk for E&P companies. AI Opportunity (1/1): OXY's carbon capture technology stack and enhanced oil recovery operations use computational modeling and optimization that benefit from AI advancement. The company's DAC operations and reservoir management increasingly leverage data-intensive optimization that qualifies as AI-adjacent technology deployment [3].
