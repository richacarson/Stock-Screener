Company Profile
Financial Services ·Banks - Regional ·NASDAQ ·United States
Bank OZK is a regional bank headquartered in Little Rock, Arkansas, operating approximately 240 branches across the southern United States. The bank is nationally known for its Real Estate Specialties Group (RESG), which is a leading originator of large commercial real estate construction and development loans, and its Corporate and Institutional Banking (CIB) group spanning 42+ industry niches.

Investment Thesis
Bank OZK offers a compelling value-and-income combination at 7.4x forward P/E with a 3.83% dividend yield backed by 29 consecutive years of payments and a 10.0% 5yr dividend CAGR [4]. The bank reported Q1 2026 net income of $159.3M (EPS $1.44 beating expectations) with NIM of 4.20% — well above regional bank peers [1]. Revenue of $1.73B in 2025 with a 45.3% profit margin demonstrates the earnings power of the RESG CRE franchise, while the CIB expansion across 42 industry niches provides a diversification growth engine. CEO Gleason's 47-year tenure, OZK Labs' innovation investments, and the low D/E of 7.6% provide leadership stability and balance sheet strength unusual for a $5.2B market cap bank.
The bear case is CRE concentration risk. Bank OZK's $28.6B total CRE exposure represents 584% of equity — an extraordinary concentration that regulators and analysts have flagged repeatedly [3]. Charge-offs surged to $160M in 2025 (from $57.4M in 2024), with specific write-downs on a vacant Cambridge office ($72.4M), Santa Monica creative offices, and Chicago's Lincoln Yards project [3]. Revenue growth of just 1.4% YoY and a 2% EPS decline in Q1 2026 suggest the RESG growth engine is slowing. The bank's dependence on higher-cost funding (CDs) rather than cheap deposits compresses net interest margins as rate conditions shift. ROE of 11.8% is below the 15% target for a bank of this risk profile. The strategic pivot toward CIB diversification is rational but early-stage, and the bank must navigate CRE credit stress while simultaneously building new lending capabilities [1][3].
Key Catalysts
  1. CIB diversification across 42 industry niches — nearly two dozen new client relationships added in Q1 2026, providing revenue growth independent of CRE cycles [1]
  2. Dividend growth acceleration — 10.0% 5yr CAGR with a 28.2% payout ratio provides ample room for continued increases at the 3.83% current yield [4]
  3. NIM of 4.20% above peers with OZK Labs digital banking investments could drive operating efficiency gains that expand margins as the rate environment normalizes [1][2]
Key Risks
  1. CRE concentration risk — $28.6B exposure at 584% of equity with charge-offs tripling to $160M in 2025, and specific project write-downs in Cambridge, Santa Monica, and Chicago signal ongoing credit stress [3]
  2. Slowing growth — revenue up only 1.4% YoY and EPS declining 2% in Q1 2026, with RESG loan caps at $500M potentially constraining the bank's highest-returning business line [1][3]
  3. Higher-cost funding dependence on CDs rather than low-cost core deposits creates margin vulnerability in a rising-rate environment, compounding competitive pressure from fintechs [3]
