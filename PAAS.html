Company Profile

Pan American Silver is the world's largest primary silver producer, operating mines across Chile, Peru, Brazil, Mexico, Canada, Argentina, Bolivia, and Guatemala. The company also produces gold, zinc, lead, and copper as by-products, with 2025 attributable silver production of 22.8 million ounces and gold production of 742,200 ounces.

Investment Thesis
Pan American Silver is the world's largest primary silver producer delivering record financial results — $3.62B revenue (+44.7%), $980M net earnings ($2.56/share), and record $554M Q4 operating cash flow in 2025 [1]. The company is positioned for continued growth with 2026 silver production guided up 14% (25-27M oz), $1.3B in cash and investments, and a nearly debt-free balance sheet (D/E 12.5%) [1]. At a forward P/E of 10.5x with ROE of 16.7% and 27% profit margins, PAAS is attractively valued. Three consecutive dividend increases and a conservative 18% payout ratio signal growing shareholder returns, while La Colorada Skarn and Jacobina optimization provide multi-year growth optionality. The structural case for silver — solar panel demand, EV adoption, and industrial applications — supports favorable long-term pricing [1].
The primary risk is precious metals price dependency — PAAS's record 2025 results were driven by elevated silver and gold prices, and any meaningful decline would compress margins significantly [1]. 2026 silver segment AISC guidance of $15.75-18.25/oz is 67% higher than 2025's $9.51/oz, reflecting mine sequencing and cost inflation that could erode margins even at current prices. The stock has declined 29% from its $69.99 high, and production is weighted to H2 2026, creating a potentially weak first half. La Colorada Skarn development carries execution risk, and operations across 8 countries including Mexico, Argentina, and Bolivia carry geopolitical exposure [1].
