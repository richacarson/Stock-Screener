Company Profile
Healthcare ·Biotechnology ·NASDAQ ·United States
Palisade Bio is a clinical-stage biopharmaceutical company developing oral PDE4 inhibitor prodrugs engineered for targeted delivery to the terminal ileum and colon. Its lead candidate PALI-2108 achieved 100% clinical response in Phase 1b ulcerative colitis and is advancing into Phase 2 trials for both UC and fibrostenotic Crohn's disease, with IND submissions planned in 2026. Headquartered in Carlsbad, California.
14 Employees ·www.palisadebio.com

Investment Thesis
Palisade Bio offers high-risk/high-reward exposure to a differentiated IBD drug candidate with compelling Phase 1b clinical data. PALI-2108 achieved a 100% clinical response rate in Phase 1b UC and a 47.5% reduction in SES-CD in fibrostenotic Crohn's disease, validating the microbiota-activated PDE4 inhibitor mechanism [1]. The Phase 2 UC IND submission is planned for Q2 2026 with trial initiation in Q3 2026 and primary efficacy readout in H2 2027, followed by Phase 2 CD IND in H2 2026 targeting early 2028 readout [1][2]. The appointment of world-class IBD experts (Peyrin-Biroulet, Rubin, Verstockt) to the Clinical Advisory Board strengthens clinical development credibility. At $389M market cap with $132.6M cash ($0.80/share against $2.27 stock price), investors are paying approximately $256M for the pipeline — modest compared to the multi-billion dollar IBD market where drugs like Humira generated $21B at peak. The gut-targeted delivery mechanism could differentiate PALI-2108 from systemic PDE4 inhibitors with fewer side effects.
The bear case centers on clinical, regulatory, and financial risk inherent to a 14-person clinical-stage biotech. The Phase 1b data, while encouraging, involved small patient cohorts — Phase 2 and Phase 3 trials could fail to replicate the 100% UC response rate or 47.5% SES-CD reduction [1]. The company previously failed a Phase 2 trial with its prior lead asset, demonstrating the binary nature of the investment. R&D spending surged 540% in Q1 2026 ($6.4M vs $1.0M) with net losses of $9.6M quarterly, accelerating the cash burn against the $132.6M position [1]. While runway extends through major Phase 2 readouts, Phase 3 will require significantly more capital and likely dilutive financing. Competition in IBD is intense — AbbVie, J&J, Pfizer, and Eli Lilly all have established or advancing treatments with massive commercial infrastructure that a 14-person company cannot replicate. The failed shareholder quorum (28.34%) suggests limited institutional engagement, and CEO Finley's dual CEO/CFO role is a governance concern as the company enters later-stage development.
