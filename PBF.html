Company Profile
Energy ·Oil & Gas Refining & Marketing ·NYSE ·United States
PBF Energy operates petroleum refineries across the Northeast, Midwest, Gulf Coast, and West Coast of the United States. The company produces gasoline, ultra-low-sulfur diesel, heating oil, jet fuel, lubricants, petrochemicals, and asphalt through six refineries with approximately 1 million barrels per day of capacity. It also provides rail, truck, and marine terminaling services through its PBF Logistics subsidiary.

Investment Thesis
PBF Energy offers deep cyclical value at 12.4x forward P/E with analyst consensus expecting a return to profitability ($3.74 forward EPS) as refining crack spreads recover from 2025's trough [1]. Q4 2025 EPS of $0.49 beat consensus by 477%, signaling the worst may be over. The $350M annualized cost savings program ($230M achieved, $120M remaining) provides structural margin improvement. D/E of 53.5% is conservative for the sector, and the 2.42% dividend yield with a 15.4% payout ratio is well-covered even in a downturn. The company's six refineries with ~1M barrels/day capacity provide geographic diversification across the U.S. [1].
The bear case is substantial. PBF reported trailing EPS of -$1.39 with negative operating cash flow (-$78M) and negative free cash flow (-$470M) in 2025, burning cash in a weak crack spread environment [4]. Revenue has declined from $46.8B in 2022 to $29.3B in 2025 (-14.4% CAGR) as the post-COVID refining supercycle unwinds. Environmental liabilities are significant — $49.3M in penalties since 2000, 144 violations, and ongoing lawsuits against air quality regulators [3]. The Delaware City refinery has received 50+ violations in the past decade. Long-term structural risk from the energy transition and EV adoption will reduce gasoline demand over time. Gross margins of 0.78% in 2025 show how thin refining economics are in a downturn, and there is no guarantee crack spreads recover to prior peaks [1][4].
