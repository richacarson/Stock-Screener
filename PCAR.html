Investment Thesis
PACCAR offers a compelling cyclical recovery play at 16.5x forward earnings with an 87-year profitability streak and A+/A1 credit rating. The Aurora autonomous trucking partnership targeting Q2 2026 commercial launch could make PACCAR the first legacy OEM with an autonomous-ready platform at scale. PACCAR Parts' record $6.87 billion revenue provides earnings stability, and management expects margin recovery to 12.5-13% in Q1 2026 with 'very strong' order intake. The 2027 NOx emission regulations could drive significant pre-buy activity in 2026, boosting truck demand. Free cash flow of $2.84 billion and 29% payout ratio provide substantial financial flexibility.
2025 was materially impacted by the freight recession, with revenue declining 15.5% and EPS falling 35.9% including a $264.5 million European litigation charge. The cyclical nature of truck demand (2026 estimate range of 230,000-270,000 Class 8 units) creates ongoing earnings volatility. The Significant Level 3 Sustainalytics controversy rating (well above the 0.7 category average) suggests unresolved ESG concerns. Autonomous trucking timelines remain uncertain despite the Q2 2026 commercial launch target, and success depends on Aurora's technology maturation. South American truck market expectations of 100,000-110,000 units (down from 115,000) indicate continued regional weakness.
