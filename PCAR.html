Company Profile

PACCAR is one of the world's largest manufacturers of Class 8 trucks through its Kenworth, Peterbilt, and DAF brands, commanding 30% U.S./Canada market share. Investment Thesis
PACCAR offers a compelling combination of cyclical recovery potential, autonomous truck leadership, and deep value at a forward P/E of 16.5. December Class 8 orders surged 118% to 42,700 units, signaling the beginning of a freight recovery that management expects will drive 230,000-270,000 Class 8 retail sales in 2026 [1]. The Aurora autonomous driverless commercial launch in Q2 2026 could be transformative — PACCAR would be the first legacy OEM to offer autonomous-ready trucks at scale, creating a potential premium pricing opportunity [2]. The 87 consecutive years of profitability, A+/A1 credit rating, and generous capital return program ($1.40 special + $0.33 regular quarterly dividend) provide downside protection, while PACCAR Parts' record $1.74B quarterly revenue offers countercyclical stability [1][3]. PACCAR's North American manufacturing footprint minimizes tariff exposure under Section 232 regulations, providing a competitive pricing advantage [1].
The bear case centers on PACCAR's current cyclical trough — FY2025 revenue declined 13.7% with a 35.9% EPS decline, and the company faces a challenging transition from freight recession to recovery [1]. Profit margin of just 8.4% and ROE of 12.9% reflect below-target profitability at the trough, and the 2026 recovery may be slower than hoped if economic conditions deteriorate [1]. Autonomous trucking remains uncertain — CEO Feight warns 'drivers will be around for a long time' and regulatory approval for fully driverless operations faces significant political and safety hurdles [2]. The Amplify Cell Technologies battery factory is still under construction, and EV truck adoption faces headwinds from range limitations, charging infrastructure gaps, and higher upfront costs [1]. Mixed insider activity — EVPs sold $16.6M in shares over 90 days versus one director purchase of $175K — suggests insiders may see limited near-term upside despite the recovery narrative [3].
Key Catalysts
  1. Aurora autonomous driverless commercial launch in Q2 2026, positioning PACCAR as the first legacy OEM with autonomous-ready Class 8 trucks at scale [2]
  2. Class 8 order surge (118% in December) signaling freight recovery with 2026 retail sales estimated at 230,000-270,000 units vs. 233,000 in 2025 [1]

Key Risks
  1. Freight recession extending into 2026 if economic growth stalls, compressing truck demand below the 230,000-unit low end of industry estimates [1]
  2. Autonomous trucking regulatory delays or safety incidents could derail the Aurora commercial launch and associated revenue expectations [2]
  3. EV truck adoption slower than expected due to charging infrastructure gaps, range limitations, and fleet operators' reluctance to transition from proven diesel platforms [1]
