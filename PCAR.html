Investment Thesis
PACCAR is a premium truck manufacturer emerging from a cycle trough with 87 consecutive years of net income — among the longest profitability streaks in American industry. With 71% of profits now from parts and financial services, the company is structurally less cyclical than historical truck cycles would suggest. FY2025 generated $2.6B adjusted net income, $4.4B operating cash flow, and a record 55.5% return on invested capital. CEO Feight reports 'very strong' December-January order intake signaling cycle recovery, with margins improving from 12% to 12.5-13% in Q1 2026. Electric trucks won International Truck of the Year, R&D spending of $450-500M targets autonomous driving, and the CDP 'A' rating validates ESG commitment. The +56 Inspire score with zero negative attributions supports faith-aligned investing.
The bear case is cyclical and competitive. Revenue declined 13.7% YoY to $28.4B, and Q4 EPS of $1.06 was 36% below Q4 2024's $1.66. The $264.5M after-tax European civil litigation charge creates legal overhang. Class 8 truck industry retail sales guidance of 230,000-270,000 units for 2026 is a wide range reflecting demand uncertainty. EPA 2027 NOx regulations will add ~$10,000 per truck, potentially dampening demand in the transition year. D/E of 81.9% and ROE of 12.9% are both outside optimal ranges. The 1.2% dividend yield is low for income investors despite the 87-year payment history. Tesla Semi and autonomous trucking competitors represent long-term structural threats to traditional OEM positioning.

Key Catalysts
  1. Truck cycle recovery — 'very strong' December-January order intake signals demand inflection, with margins improving from 12% to 12.5-13% and 2026 Class 8 retail sales potentially reaching the upper end of the 230K-270K range
  2. PACCAR Parts aftermarket growth of 4-8% guided for 2026, capturing additional share of the $70B addressable market through AI-driven inventory management and expanding distribution network
  3. Electric truck commercialization — DAF XD/XF Electric International Truck of the Year winners and Kenworth/Peterbilt battery-electric models position PACCAR for the zero-emission fleet transition, with EPA 2027 regulations creating urgency
Key Risks
  1. Truck cycle false start — if freight conditions don't sustain recovery, the strong December-January orders could prove to be pre-EPA-2027 pull-forward demand rather than genuine cycle upturn, leaving revenue flat to down in H2 2026
  2. EPA 2027 compliance cost impact — the ~$10,000 per truck price increase from new NOx regulations could suppress demand in the transition year, particularly for small and mid-sized fleet operators with constrained capital budgets
  3. European litigation overhang — the $264.5M charge in Q4 2025 may not fully resolve European civil litigation, and additional charges could pressure earnings in future quarters while damaging brand reputation
