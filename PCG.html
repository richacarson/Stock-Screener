Investment Thesis
PG&E offers a compelling regulated utility growth story: 9% annual EPS growth guidance through 2030, 9% rate base growth, forward P/E of 9.6x, and PEG ratio of 0.65 — making it one of the cheapest growth utilities in the market [1]. The 3.6 GW of data center projects in final engineering provide a secular AI-driven demand catalyst that could accelerate rate base investment. Non-GAAP core EPS tightened to $1.64-1.66 for 2026 (10% growth), dividend doubled to $0.20 with a path to 20% payout by 2028, and $700M+ in cumulative O&M savings demonstrate operational discipline. Analyst targets (~$21) imply 21% upside from current price [1].
The bear case is dominated by wildfire liability: the recent 4.8% stock decline reflects rising concerns over increasing wildfire liabilities, and PG&E's catastrophic history (Camp Fire, 2019 bankruptcy) means any major fire season could reignite existential financial risk [1]. D/E of 187% and negative free cash flow of -$3.4B reflect the heavy capital investment needed for grid hardening and wildfire mitigation. California's regulatory environment creates political risk around rate cases and wildfire cost recovery. ROE of 8.2% is below the cost of capital for many investors, and the dividend yield of just 1.15% is unattractive for income seekers. The company's beta of 0.31 limits upside in bull markets [6].
