Investment Thesis
PG&E offers a compelling regulated utility growth story: Q1 2026 utility net income surged 37% YoY to $954M, 2026 EPS guidance was tightened to $1.64-$1.66 (10% growth, fifth consecutive year of double-digit growth), and 9%+ annual EPS growth is projected through 2030 with a $73B capital plan requiring zero new equity [1]. Forward P/E of 9.3x and PEG of 0.73 make it one of the cheapest growth utilities in the market. The 3.6 GW of data center projects in final engineering provide a secular AI-driven demand catalyst [1].
The bear case is dominated by wildfire liability: PG&E's catastrophic history — the Camp Fire (85 deaths), 2019 bankruptcy with $30B+ in liabilities, and criminal guilty plea — means any major fire season could reignite existential financial risk [3]. D/E of 188% reflects heavy capital requirements for grid hardening and wildfire mitigation. California's regulatory environment creates political risk around rate cases and wildfire cost recovery. Dividend yield of just 1.2% is unattractive for income seekers, and the 'Severe' Sustainalytics ESG Risk Rating constrains institutional demand [3].
