Company Profile
Industrials ·Pollution & Treatment Controls ·NASDAQ ·United States
PureCycle Technologies holds the exclusive global license for a patented solvent-driven purification recycling technology developed by Procter & Gamble that transforms waste polypropylene (No. 5 plastic) into ultra-pure recycled resin (PureFive). The company produced a record 22.4 million pounds of resin in 2025 and is expanding internationally with planned facilities in Thailand and Belgium.
174 Employees ·www.purecycle.com

Investment Thesis
PureCycle Technologies holds the exclusive global license for the only patented solvent-based polypropylene purification technology, addressing a massive unmet need in the $90B+ polypropylene market where less than 1% of PP waste is currently recycled [1][2]. The company produced a record 22.4M pounds of PureFive resin in 2025 with four consecutive quarters of sequential revenue growth, and rate tests at 12,500-14,000 lbs/hour suggest progress toward nameplate capacity [1]. International expansion to Thailand (groundbreaking H2 2026) and Belgium (permits H2 2026) could dramatically expand production capacity. The Inspire Insight score of +54 (ALIGNED) reflects the genuine environmental mission. At $5.19 per share ($938M market cap), the stock trades at a fraction of Cantor Fitzgerald's $14 price target, offering significant upside if the company successfully scales [1].
The bear case is substantial for a pre-profitability company burning cash with unproven scale economics. TTM revenue of only $8.36M with Q4 missing consensus by 57% ($2.7M vs. $6.37M expected) and -$0.42 EPS vs. -$0.25 expected demonstrates persistent execution challenges [1]. The company's -68.8% ROE, zero profit margin, and projected -$1.01 EPS for 2026 mean profitability remains distant. Hindenburg Research's 2021 report raised serious governance concerns about executive track records and triggered securities litigation [3]. The revelation that PureCycle's process generates hazardous waste including trichloroethylene undermines the clean-tech narrative [3]. Prior potential default on $250M in revenue bonds highlights financial fragility. The company must spend $39-45M in 2026 on expansion while generating minimal revenue, creating ongoing dilution and financing risk. Investors who bought five years ago are sitting on 40% losses [1].
