Company Profile
Technology ·Software - Application ·NYSE ·United States
PagerDuty operates a digital operations management platform that collects data and digital signals from software-enabled systems, leveraging AI and machine learning to correlate, process, predict, and remediate incidents. Products include Incident Management, AIOps, Automation, Customer Service Operations, and AI-powered generative and agentic AI capabilities for the PagerDuty Operations Cloud. The company serves 34,000+ customers across technology, telecom, retail, financial services, and other industries.

Investment Thesis
PagerDuty is a profitable SaaS company trading at a deeply discounted valuation — 4.5x forward P/E with 85% gross margins, 35% GAAP profit margins, and $115M in operating cash flow [1][4]. The company achieved its first full year of GAAP profitability in FY2026, expanded non-GAAP operating margins by 700 basis points to 24.6%, and grew ARR 5% to $499M [1]. At $6.24/share ($573M market cap), the market prices PagerDuty as if it is terminally impaired, yet the company generates $112M in free cash flow annually, has $470M cash against $413M debt, and serves 34,000+ customers including large enterprises dependent on its Operations Cloud for incident management [1][4]. The AI capabilities — 16 years of ML experience, AIOps, generative AI, and agentic AI — provide a foundation for platform expansion that the current valuation assigns zero credit.
The bear case centers on decelerating growth and competitive threats. Revenue grew only 5.4% in FY2026 and 2.7% in Q4, suggesting the company may be approaching a growth ceiling as larger platforms (ServiceNow, Datadog, Microsoft) bundle incident management into their broader suites [1]. The $499M ARR implies a $573M market cap values the business at roughly 1.1x ARR — distressed territory for SaaS. CEO Tejada's stated intention to replace employees with AI has triggered accelerating turnover and severely damaged employee morale (Glassdoor 3.6/5), creating organizational execution risk at a critical moment [2]. The 152.7% D/E ratio from convertible debt creates dilution risk if the stock recovers, and the absence of revenue growth re-acceleration makes it difficult to attract growth investors despite the cheap valuation. PagerDuty may be a classic value trap — cheap for a reason [2][4].
