Company Profile
Technology ·Software - Infrastructure ·NASDAQ ·United States
Palladyne AI Corp develops embodied artificial intelligence and collaborative autonomy software for defense and industrial markets. The company's Palladyne IQ platform enables robots to perceive and adapt to unstructured, dynamic environments in real time through AI-driven sensor fusion and edge computing. Formerly known as Sarcos Technology and Robotics Corporation (hardware exoskeletons), the company pivoted to pure software under CEO Ben Wolff in 2024. Headquartered in Salt Lake City, Utah, serving U.S. government, defense, and industrial customers.

Investment Thesis
Palladyne AI is a pure-play embodied AI software company at an inflection point, with Q1 2026 revenue surging 107% YoY to $3.5M and full-year guidance of $24-27M representing 357-415% growth over 2025 [1][2]. The $17M backlog (up from $13.5M at year-end 2025) with $7M in Q1 new awards provides strong near-term visibility, and the appointment of Lt. Gen. Sean Gainey (former Golden Dome and Army Space & Missile Defense commander) to the Defense Advisory Board signals growing credibility with the U.S. defense establishment [4]. The hardware-agnostic Palladyne IQ platform addresses the critical gap between robot hardware proliferation and autonomy software maturity — a market expanding rapidly as DoD and commercial customers deploy autonomous systems across air, ground, and maritime domains. CEO Wolff's significant personal share purchases ($375K+) and $43.7M cash position provide near-term stability.
The primary risk is execution and cash burn at scale. Palladyne AI burned approximately $8.5M per quarter in operating cash during Q1 2026 against just $3.5M in revenue, leaving approximately 5 quarters of runway from the $43.7M cash position without additional financing or revenue acceleration [1][2]. The company must execute on its $24-27M revenue guidance — heavily back-end weighted — to demonstrate that the Sarcos-to-Palladyne pivot was more than a financial restructuring. At $285M market cap on $7M TTM revenue (41x sales), the stock prices in significant growth that remains unproven at scale. Competition from well-funded defense primes (L3Harris, Northrop Grumman) and AI-native startups (Shield AI, Anduril) with deeper customer relationships and larger balance sheets could limit Palladyne's addressable market. The 2025 full-year revenue decline of 33% (from $7.8M to $5.2M) during the transition period raises questions about the durability of customer relationships through strategic pivots.
Key Catalysts
  1. Full-year 2026 revenue guidance of $24-27M with back-end loaded quarterly sequential increases — Q2-Q4 execution against this target is the critical proof point for the Sarcos-to-Palladyne transformation
  2. Defense Advisory Board appointment of Lt. Gen. Sean Gainey (Golden Dome, Army Space & Missile Defense Command) signals potential entry into high-value missile defense and space domain autonomy contracts
  3. Hardware-agnostic Palladyne IQ platform positioned to benefit from the DoD's push for autonomous multi-domain operations across air, ground, and maritime systems — a structural demand tailwind
Key Risks
  1. Cash burn of ~$8.5M per quarter against $43.7M cash position provides only 5 quarters of runway — dilutive equity raise is likely required if 2026 revenue guidance underperforms
  2. Price-to-sales ratio of 41x on $7M TTM revenue leaves no margin of safety — any execution miss on the back-end-loaded $24-27M guidance could trigger severe stock price decline
  3. Competition from well-capitalized defense primes (L3Harris, Northrop) and AI-native startups (Shield AI at $9.5B valuation, Anduril at $14B) with deeper customer relationships and larger R&D budgets
