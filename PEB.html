Company Profile
Real Estate ·REIT - Hotel & Motel ·NYSE ·United States
Pebblebrook Hotel Trust is a publicly traded REIT and the largest owner of urban and resort lifestyle hotels in the United States. The company owns 44 hotels totaling approximately 11,000 guest rooms across 13 urban and resort markets, with properties operated under brands including Margaritaville, LaPlaya, and independent boutique concepts through its Curator Hotel & Resort Collection.

Investment Thesis
Pebblebrook at $14 per share trades at a significant discount to estimated NAV for the largest US owner of urban and resort lifestyle hotels, with 2026 RevPAR growth guided at 2-4% and same-property EBITDA growth of 2.1-6% [1]. Adjusted FFO of $1.58/share in 2025 exceeded guidance, and the $65-75M redevelopment program nearing completion should unlock higher revenue and margins at transformed properties. AI-driven tech demand in urban markets like San Francisco provides a secular demand tailwind, while the Curator platform with 91 member properties creates a scalable revenue diversification opportunity [3].
The bear case is centered on persistent GAAP losses and balance sheet pressure. PEB reported a $62.2M net loss in 2025 including $48.9M in impairment charges, ROE is -2.3%, and forward EPS remains negative [1][2]. D/E of 97.7% limits financial flexibility in a capital-intensive hotel business. The token $0.01/quarter dividend provides no income return for a REIT. Hotel REITs face cyclical risk from economic slowdowns — leisure and business travel demand can decline sharply in recessions. Rising labor costs and insurance premiums in urban markets compress property-level margins. The company's 52-person trust structure relies entirely on third-party hotel operators, limiting direct control over guest experience and property performance.
Key Catalysts
  1. Completion of major redevelopment program ($65-75M invested in 2026) expected to unlock higher RevPAR and EBITDA margins at transformed properties through premium repositioning [1]
  2. AI and tech industry expansion driving transient and group demand in key urban markets (San Francisco, San Diego, Boston) where Pebblebrook concentrates its portfolio [1][3]
  3. Curator Hotel & Resort Collection growth to 91+ members creating a scalable platform for vendor agreements, technology access, and booking distribution that enhances the company's competitive moat
Key Risks
  1. Persistent GAAP net losses ($62.2M in 2025) and negative ROE (-2.3%) could trigger investor capitulation and further stock price decline if FFO-based metrics also deteriorate [1]
  2. Cyclical recession risk — urban hotel demand could decline sharply if the economy weakens, amplified by 97.7% D/E leverage that limits the company's ability to absorb revenue drops
  3. Rising labor costs, insurance premiums, and property taxes in urban markets (San Francisco, Los Angeles, Boston) could compress property-level margins faster than revenue growth can offset [1]
