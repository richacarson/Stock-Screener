Company Profile
Utilities ·Utilities - Regulated Electric ·NYSE ·United States
Public Service Enterprise Group operates through PSE&G (regulated electric and gas utility serving New Jersey) and PSEG Power (nuclear generation). The company transmits and distributes electricity and natural gas to residential, commercial, and industrial customers across 25,000 circuit miles, and operates nuclear plants generating 3,758 MW of carbon-free power. Founded in 1903 and headquartered in Newark, New Jersey.

Investment Thesis
PSEG is a well-positioned regulated utility benefiting from the convergence of clean energy mandates and AI-driven electricity demand. Full-year 2025 revenue of $12.2B grew 18.3% YoY with EPS of $4.22, and management guided 2026 non-GAAP EPS to $4.28-$4.40 (7% midpoint growth) [1]. The regulated capital spending plan of $22.5-$25.5B through 2030 supports a 6-7.5% rate-base CAGR, while the 3,758 MW nuclear fleet provides carbon-free power increasingly demanded by hyperscalers [2]. At 17.8x forward P/E with a 3.19% dividend yield and 15 consecutive annual increases, PSEG offers an attractive blend of income and growth for utility investors. The data center opportunity (100 MW Kenilworth facility scalable to 300 MW) positions PSEG at the intersection of AI infrastructure and clean energy [2].
The bear case centers on execution risk and capital intensity. D/E of 142.7% reflects the heavy capital investment required, and free cash flow was negative in 2025 at -$105M despite $3.3B operating cash flow — the capital program consumes substantial resources [4]. ROE of 12.8% trails the 15% target, constrained by the regulated return framework. The 59.7% payout ratio leaves limited room for dividend acceleration. Rate case outcomes in New Jersey are subject to regulatory risk, and the data center power opportunity faces competition from other utilities and potential for customer concentration risk. Nuclear operations carry inherent decommissioning liabilities and regulatory scrutiny. Revenue growth of 18.3% may moderate as rate case benefits normalize, and the 5.2% five-year dividend growth CAGR just trails 2x inflation targets [4].
Key Catalysts
  1. Data center power demand acceleration — 100 MW Kenilworth facility scalable to 300 MW by 2029, with hyperscaler contracts driving incremental revenue beyond traditional rate-base growth [2]
  2. Regulated capital spending plan of $22.5-$25.5B through 2030 supporting 6-7.5% rate-base CAGR, providing visible multi-year earnings growth trajectory [1]
  3. Nuclear fleet revaluation — 3,758 MW of carbon-free baseload power becoming increasingly valuable as AI data centers require 24/7 clean energy and nuclear receives bipartisan policy support [2]
Key Risks
  1. Regulatory risk in New Jersey — rate case outcomes determine allowed returns on $22.5-$25.5B capital program, and adverse regulatory decisions could compress ROE below already-modest 12.8% [4]
  2. Negative free cash flow from capital-intensive investment program — FCF was -$105M in 2025 despite $3.3B operating cash flow, creating dependence on debt markets to fund growth [4]
  3. Data center customer concentration risk — hyperscaler power contracts represent a new and potentially volatile revenue stream subject to technology shifts and customer renegotiation leverage [2]
