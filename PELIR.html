Investment Thesis
PELIR represents rights in a newly formed Arctic oil exploration company targeting Greenland's Jameson Land Basin, which has estimated potential of 13 billion barrels of recoverable oil across 2 million+ acres [2]. The company signed a Halliburton services agreement for two planned 2026 exploration wells and raised $70M to fund operations. If exploration succeeds, the upside is transformative given the scale of the resource estimate. The appointment of former BP executive Ashiq Merchant as CFO and former TD Ameritrade CEO Joe Moglia as board advisor adds credibility to the executive team [2].
The bear case is overwhelming. PELIR is a sub-$1 rights ticker for a pre-revenue SPAC-formed company with zero operating history, zero production, and zero revenue [1]. The $0.83 price and $544K daily dollar volume fail the IOWN liquidity floor, making the security untradeable for any institutional strategy. Arctic oil exploration carries extreme geological, environmental, regulatory, and geopolitical risk — Greenland imposed a drilling moratorium in 2021 (licenses are grandfathered but future regulatory changes could invalidate them) [2]. Environmental groups actively oppose Arctic drilling, and Environmental Impact Assessment approval is required from Greenlandic authorities. The $70M raised covers only initial exploration costs; commercial development would require billions in capital. The binary exploration outcome, SPAC sponsor dilution, and sub-penny rights structure make PELIR suitable only for pure speculation [1][2].
