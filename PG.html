The Procter & Gamble Company is a global consumer goods conglomerate operating across five segments: Beauty, Grooming, Health Care, Fabric & Home Care, and Baby/Feminine/Family Care. P&G owns iconic brands including Tide, Pampers, Gillette, Crest, and Olay, selling products in approximately 180 countries.

Investment Thesis
P&G represents the quintessential defensive quality compounder: 68 consecutive years of dividend increases, 31.6% ROE, near-20% profit margins, and a forward P/E of 19.4x that is reasonable for a consumer staples mega-cap [3]. The new CEO Jejurikar brings continuity with fresh energy, and the AI-driven restructuring targeting $1-1.6B in annual savings by 2026 provides a clear margin expansion pathway [2]. The $15B annual shareholder return program (dividends + buybacks) delivers compelling total return, and P&G's global brand portfolio with pricing power provides inflation protection. International expansion and premiumization in emerging markets offer a low-single-digit organic growth floor.
The bear case centers on P&G's anemic revenue growth — just 1.5% YoY and 1.7% CAGR — which limits total return potential in a low-inflation environment and makes the ~21x trailing P/E look expensive for a company with declining EPS (-5.4% YoY) [3]. The 7,000-job restructuring introduces execution risk and potential talent loss, while private label competition continues to gain share in key categories as consumer trade-down persists in a slowing economy. Currency headwinds from a strong dollar pressure international earnings (60%+ of sales), and the mature consumer staples sector offers limited upside optionality. At $333B market cap, the stock is priced for perfection with little margin of safety.
Key Catalysts
  1. AI-driven restructuring achieving $1-1.6B in annual savings by FY2026, expanding operating margins 100-200 basis points and offsetting commodity cost inflation [2]
  2. New CEO Jejurikar accelerating digital transformation and Supply Chain 3.0 initiatives, potentially unlocking incremental productivity gains and innovation speed
  3. Emerging market premiumization — P&G's expanding portfolio of premium-priced products in beauty and health care growing faster than the core business in developing markets
Key Risks
  1. Private label market share gains accelerating as consumer trade-down behavior persists, pressuring P&G's premium pricing strategy and requiring increased promotional spending
  2. Currency headwinds from a strong US dollar reducing international earnings (60%+ of revenue), potentially creating a 2-3% drag on reported revenue and EPS growth
  3. Restructuring execution risk — the 7,000-job reduction must be managed without disrupting operations, losing key talent, or damaging the promote-from-within culture that has been P&G's competitive advantage for decades [2]
