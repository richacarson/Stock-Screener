Company Profile
Technology ·Software - Infrastructure ·NASDAQ ·United States
Pagaya Technologies deploys proprietary AI and machine learning technology to evaluate consumer credit applications across personal loans, auto finance, credit cards, BNPL, and real estate. The platform integrates via API with lending partners (SoFi, LendingClub, Prosper, U.S. Bank, Ally, Klarna, Visa) to approve applicants who would otherwise be declined, using non-traditional creditworthiness indicators from a database of trillions in applications.

Investment Thesis
Pagaya trades at 3.5x forward P/E — an extreme discount for a profitable AI company growing revenue 20% YoY to $1.3B with GAAP net income of $81M and $371M adjusted EBITDA (28.5% margin) [1]. The company achieved four consecutive profitable quarters, turning around from a $402M net loss in 2024, while maintaining partnerships with SoFi, LendingClub, U.S. Bank, Ally, Klarna, and Visa [1][2]. 2026 guidance of $1.4-1.575B revenue and $410M+ EBITDA implies continued margin expansion. At $11.70 (74% below the 52-week high of $44.99), the stock prices in severe pessimism — if Pagaya sustains even modest growth and profitability, significant upside exists from multiple re-rating alone. Net cash positive ($1.18B cash vs. $858M debt) provides balance sheet safety [1][4].
The bear case is substantive and explains the extreme valuation discount. Iceberg Research (short-seller) alleges Pagaya used a managed fund to buy the riskiest ABS tranches, hiding losses from investors, with Israeli fund investors allegedly stonewalled when seeking redemptions — this governance risk could trigger regulatory action or lawsuits [3]. CTO Avital Pardo's prior company Gibui Holdings collapsed one year after his exit, raising leadership integrity questions [3]. Sustainalytics rates Pagaya's ESG risk management as 'Weak' [3]. The 19.8% revenue growth represents a sharp deceleration from 26% in 2025, and guidance of 8-21% growth for 2026 suggests further slowdown as the company pulls back from higher-risk lending segments [1]. Credit cycle risk is significant — a recession or rising delinquencies would impair Pagaya's ABS portfolio and network volume. The $965M market cap and high short interest signal sustained institutional skepticism about the business model's sustainability [3][4].
Key Catalysts
  1. Profitability trajectory — four consecutive profitable quarters with 2026 EBITDA guidance of $410M+ could trigger institutional re-rating of the stock from 3.5x forward P/E [1]
  2. Partner expansion to serve all major US banks as CEO Krubiner envisions, with existing partnerships (SoFi, U.S. Bank, Ally, Klarna, Visa) providing proof of concept at scale [2]
  3. Auto finance and credit card vertical growth could diversify revenue concentration and expand total addressable market beyond personal loans [2]
Key Risks
  1. Governance risk — Iceberg Research allegations about ABS tranche self-dealing, fund investor disputes, and CTO's prior company collapse create material regulatory and litigation exposure [3]
  2. Credit cycle deterioration — rising consumer delinquencies or a recession would impair Pagaya's 'second-chance lending' portfolio disproportionately, as these are borrowers traditional models rejected [1][3]
  3. Growth deceleration — revenue growth slowing from 26% (2025) to guided 8-21% (2026) as the company pulls back from higher-risk segments, potentially signaling the platform's addressable market is more limited than expected [1]
