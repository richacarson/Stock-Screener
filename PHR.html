Company Profile
Healthcare ·Health Information Services ·NYSE ·United States
Phreesia is a leading healthcare SaaS company providing patient intake, payments, and engagement solutions to healthcare organizations. The platform processes approximately 180 million patient visits and over $4.8 billion in patient payments annually across 4,658 average healthcare services clients (AHSCs). Revenue comes from three segments: subscription services (46%), network solutions (31%), and payment processing (23%). Founded in 2005 and headquartered in Raleigh, NC.

Investment Thesis
Phreesia is a 21-year co-founder-led healthcare SaaS platform that just reached a critical inflection point — FY2026 marked its first year of GAAP profitability ($2.3M) with $101.5M adjusted EBITDA (21% margin) and $54.4M free cash flow on $481M revenue [1]. At just 5.5x forward earnings and a $580M market cap, the stock trades at a massive discount to healthcare SaaS peers, with analysts targeting $19-35 (100-270% upside) [1]. FY2027 guidance of $545-559M revenue (14-16% growth including AccessOne) with $125-135M adjusted EBITDA (24-26% margin) implies accelerating profitability [3]. The platform processes 180M patient visits and $4.8B in payments annually across 4,658 clients, with both client count and revenue per client growing, creating a compounding revenue engine.
The bear case centers on valuation compression despite improving fundamentals. The stock has declined approximately 90% from its 2021 highs despite revenue nearly tripling, suggesting the market does not believe current margins are sustainable or that the total addressable market supports the growth trajectory [4]. GAAP profit margin of 0.48% is razor-thin — any execution stumble, competitive pressure, or macroeconomic slowdown in healthcare spending could push the company back to losses. The $160M AccessOne acquisition introduces integration risk and dilutes the already thin profit margin in the near term [3]. The healthcare IT market is increasingly competitive, with EHR vendors (Epic, Cerner/Oracle) bundling intake and payment functionality that could pressure Phreesia's standalone value proposition. Revenue growth is moderating from 27% CAGR (FY2019-2026) toward the mid-teens, and organic growth excluding AccessOne is only 6-8%, suggesting the core business is decelerating [1]. Debt-to-equity of 30.4% is manageable but leaves less flexibility for additional acquisitions [4].
Key Catalysts
  1. Margin expansion from 21% adjusted EBITDA margin (FY2026) to 24-26% (FY2027) as the platform scales and AccessOne integration delivers operational synergies [1][3]
  2. Revenue per AHSC growth of 8% YoY ($27,300) driven by cross-selling payments, network solutions, and patient financing across the 4,658-client base [1]
  3. Massive valuation gap — stock trades at 5.5x forward P/E vs. healthcare SaaS peers at 30-50x, with analyst targets of $19-35 implying 100-270% upside if profitability trajectory is sustained [1][4]
Key Risks
  1. EHR vendor bundling — Epic, Oracle Health (Cerner), and other EHR platforms increasingly offer integrated intake and payment functionality that could commoditize Phreesia's standalone value proposition [2]
  2. Decelerating organic revenue growth — excluding the AccessOne acquisition, organic growth is approximately 6-8% for FY2027, down from 27% CAGR over FY2019-2026, suggesting penetration challenges [1]
  3. Integration risk from the $160M AccessOne acquisition — patient financing is a new capability that requires different go-to-market, risk management, and regulatory compliance than the core intake and payments platform [3]
