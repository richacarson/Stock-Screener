Investment Thesis
Planet Labs hit a fundamental inflection in FY2026 — first full-year adjusted EBITDA profitability ($15.5M) on $307.7M revenue (+26% YoY), with Q4 revenue accelerating to $86.8M (+41% YoY). The company's $900M total backlog (+79% YoY) and FY2027 guidance of $415–$440M provide strong forward visibility [1]. The data-intelligence pivot — AI analytics layered on the world's largest daily Earth imagery dataset — addresses expanding intelligence demand across defense (50%+ growth), agriculture, climate, and financial services. As a Delaware Public Benefit Corporation with a structurally irreplaceable physical data moat, Planet is uniquely positioned at the intersection of space, AI, and sustainability.
The bear case centers on persistent GAAP losses (-$246.9M net loss in FY2026, worse than FY2025's -$123.2M) driven by stock-based compensation and satellite depreciation, plus elevated debt (D/E 245%) [1]. The stock has surged ~58% from $27 to $43, pricing in much of the growth acceleration. Competition from SAR providers (Capella Space, Umbra) offering all-weather imaging and high-resolution optical competitors (Maxar) could pressure government contract margins. GAAP profitability remains distant, and the company must sustain 30%+ revenue growth to justify current valuations.
Key Catalysts
  1. FY2027 revenue hitting $415M+ would confirm the data-intelligence business model is scaling and could drive re-rating toward software/data company multiples
  2. First full-year GAAP profitability would remove the final financial overhang and unlock institutional mandates requiring profitable holdings
  3. New multi-year government intelligence contracts (US DoD or allied nations) at $50M+ annual value would validate Planet as a strategic defense intelligence partner
Key Risks
  1. Government budget concentration: a significant portion of revenue comes from US and allied defense/intelligence contracts; budget sequestration or program cuts would impair near-term growth
  2. Persistent GAAP losses: net loss widened to -$247M in FY2026 from -$123M, driven by SBC and depreciation; inability to reach GAAP profitability could erode investor patience at current elevated valuations
  3. Satellite replenishment and competition: maintaining 200+ satellites requires continuous launches while SAR competitors (Capella, Umbra) offer all-weather imaging that optical systems cannot match
