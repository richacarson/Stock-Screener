Company Profile
Industrials ·Aerospace & Defense ·NYSE ·United States
Planet Labs PBC designs, constructs, and launches constellations of Earth-imaging satellites, operating the world's largest fleet of Earth observation satellites (~200 SuperDove satellites). The company delivers high-cadence geospatial data to customers through an online platform spanning defense & intelligence, civil government, and commercial sectors. Public benefit corporation since founding. Market cap ~$15.3B.

Investment Thesis
Planet Labs hit a fundamental inflection in FY2026 — record $307.7M revenue (26% YoY), first-ever adjusted EBITDA and free cash flow profitability, and backlog surging 79% to $900M [1]. Defense & intelligence revenue grew 50%+ YoY, positioning Planet as a critical geospatial intelligence supplier. FY2027 guidance of $415-440M (35% growth at midpoint) signals accelerating momentum. The structural moat — the world's largest Earth observation constellation providing daily global imaging — creates a data asset AI cannot replicate without physical space infrastructure, and the AI analytics layer built on this data creates a compounding flywheel.
The bear case centers on profitability and valuation. Despite adjusted EBITDA profitability, GAAP margin remains deeply negative at -80.2% due to heavy SBC and satellite depreciation, with D/E of 245.4% reflecting capital-intensive infrastructure investment [4]. At ~$15.3B market cap on $308M revenue (~50x sales), the stock requires sustained 35%+ growth and significant margin expansion to justify the premium. Commercial revenue declined in FY2026, suggesting competitive pressure from Maxar, Airbus, and emerging SAR providers like Capella Space could limit total addressable market in non-government segments [1]. Dependence on defense & intelligence spending creates policy risk if government budgets shift.
Key Catalysts
  1. FY2027 revenue execution at $415-440M guidance would confirm the data-intelligence business model is scaling, potentially driving re-rating toward software/data company multiples on improving margin profile
  2. Defense & intelligence revenue acceleration as classified programs expand and Planet deepens integration with national security customers requiring persistent global surveillance capabilities
  3. Pelican next-generation satellite constellation deployment improving resolution and hyperspectral capabilities, potentially unlocking higher-value commercial and government contract opportunities
Key Risks
  1. GAAP profitability remains deeply negative at -80.2% margin with heavy SBC dilution — the path to GAAP breakeven depends on operating leverage that has not yet materialized at scale
  2. Commercial segment revenue declined in FY2026 while defense grew 50%+, creating concentration risk and signaling that lower-resolution daily imaging faces competitive pressure from high-resolution and SAR providers
  3. Valuation at ~50x trailing revenue requires sustained 35%+ growth and significant margin expansion — any deceleration in defense spending or contract delays could trigger severe multiple compression from current $15.3B market cap
