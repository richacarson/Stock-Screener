Company Profile
Industrials ·Aerospace & Defense ·NYSE ·United States
Planet Labs PBC designs, builds, and operates the largest constellation of Earth-imaging satellites, capturing daily imagery of the entire planet at 3.5-meter resolution. The company's ~200 satellites provide geospatial data to agriculture, defense, forestry, finance, and government customers through its Earth Observation platform. Founded in 2010 as a Public Benefit Corporation, headquartered in San Francisco.
945 Employees ·www.planet.com

Investment Thesis
Planet Labs has reached an inflection point, delivering its first full fiscal year of non-GAAP profitability (adjusted EBITDA $15.5M) and positive free cash flow ($53M) while growing revenue 42% YoY to $336M TTM [1]. The defense and intelligence segment grew over 50% YoY, and a $900M+ backlog provides strong visibility into FY2027 guidance of $415-440M. With 200+ satellites capturing daily Earth imagery at scale, Planet possesses a proprietary data moat that becomes more valuable as AI/ML applications proliferate — no competitor can replicate the daily-cadence global dataset without matching the physical constellation. The AI-powered analytics platform, exemplified by the $12.8M NGA maritime analytics contract, demonstrates the evolution from raw imagery vendor to intelligence platform [1][2].
The primary concern is the disconnect between Planet's $10B market cap and its fundamentals — at $336M revenue with -111% GAAP profit margin, the stock trades at 30x TTM revenue with no path to GAAP profitability in the near term [4]. Forward P/E of 8,471x underscores that earnings remain negligible. The commercial segment declined modestly YoY while defense/intel carried growth, creating concentration risk in government contracts subject to budget cycles and political priorities. Net insider selling of $29M over 12 months raises questions about management conviction at current valuations. Capital-intensive satellite replacement cycles (constellations degrade over 3-5 years) create ongoing cash demands that could delay sustained free cash flow generation.
Key Catalysts
  1. FY2027 revenue guidance of $415-440M represents 35-43% growth, with $900M+ backlog providing execution visibility and potential for upward revision if defense contracts accelerate
  2. Pelican next-generation constellation achieving full deployment at 50cm resolution, enabling Planet to compete in the high-resolution market currently dominated by Maxar
  3. Expansion of AI-powered analytics platform from government use cases into commercial verticals (agriculture, insurance, climate) where daily-cadence data enables new applications
Key Risks
  1. GAAP profitability remains years away with -111% profit margin; $10B market cap at 30x TTM revenue leaves no margin of safety if growth decelerates
  2. Defense and intelligence revenue concentration (>50% of growth) exposes Planet to government budget cycles, contract re-competition risk, and shifting geopolitical priorities
  3. Capital-intensive satellite replacement requirements — constellation degradation over 3-5 years demands continuous capex that could consume free cash flow gains

AI Resilience (25%)
AI Resilience LOW RISK
8
AI Risk Protection (7/9): Planet Labs' core business is physically immune to AI disruption — designing, launching, and operating 200+ satellites in orbit requires hardware engineering, orbital mechanics, and space infrastructure that AI cannot replicate [2]. The satellite constellation itself is a proprietary data moat; no AI model can generate real-time, daily Earth imagery without physical satellites. AI actually increases demand for Planet's geospatial data as more AI applications require Earth observation training data for agriculture, climate, defense, and insurance models. The analytics layer faces some competitive pressure from AI companies processing publicly available satellite data, but Planet's proprietary daily-cadence dataset is a significant barrier [2]. AI Opportunity (1/1): Planet generates quantifiable AI revenue through its AI-powered Earth Observation platform, the $12.8M NGA maritime analytics contract, and on-satellite ML processing capabilities. AI is integral to the value proposition, not an add-on [1][2].
