Company Profile
Industrials ·Aerospace & Defense ·NYSE ·United States
Planet Labs PBC designs, builds, and launches the world's largest constellation of Earth-imaging satellites, providing daily global imagery at up to 50cm resolution through its SuperDove, SkySat, Pelican, and Tanager hyperspectral platforms. The company delivers geospatial data and analytics via APIs and browser-based tools to agriculture, defense, energy, forestry, finance, and government customers. Founded in 2010 by former NASA scientists, Planet is structured as a Public Benefit Corporation headquartered in San Francisco.

Investment Thesis
Planet Labs delivered a breakout FY2026 with revenue surging 26% to $307.7M, Q4 revenue of $86.8M beating estimates by 11%, and the company achieving adjusted EBITDA profitability ($15.5M) for the first time [1]. Backlog surged 79% to over $900M with remaining performance obligations up 106% to $852M, providing exceptional forward visibility. The Defense & Intelligence segment grew 50%+, validating Planet's pivot toward high-value government contracts. FY2027 guidance of $415-$440M implies 35-43% top-line growth. With 200+ satellites providing daily global imagery, the Pelican constellation delivering 50cm resolution, and the Tanager hyperspectral platform opening new spectral analysis markets, Planet is building an irreplaceable geospatial data franchise.
The bear case centers on persistent GAAP unprofitability — Planet lost $246.9M in FY2026 (including $161.4M in non-cash charges) with a -80.2% profit margin, and the path to sustainable GAAP profitability remains unclear despite the adjusted EBITDA milestone [1]. D/E of 245.4% reflects significant leverage, and the stock trades at an extreme valuation with no meaningful P/E ratio [4]. Competition from Maxar (Worldview Legion), BlackSky, and Airbus in high-resolution imagery is intensifying. Planet's 945-person workforce may face scaling challenges as revenue accelerates, and Glassdoor reviews flag inconsistent middle management and strategic follow-through concerns [2]. The company's 1,000%+ stock price increase from its 52-week low of $3.47 to $41.59 prices in substantial execution risk.
Key Catalysts
  1. FY2027 revenue guidance of $415-$440M (35-43% growth) supported by $900M+ backlog and 106% growth in remaining performance obligations, with potential for upside from new Pelican and Tanager contracts
  2. Defense & Intelligence segment momentum with 50%+ growth trajectory as Planet captures expanding government demand for daily persistent surveillance and geospatial AI analytics
  3. Path to GAAP profitability as gross margins expand with higher-margin Pelican and analytics revenue, potentially reaching breakeven by FY2028 as operating leverage improves on a larger revenue base
Key Risks

  2. Extreme valuation after 1,000%+ rally from 52-week lows with no P/E anchor — any miss against $415-$440M FY2027 guidance could trigger severe multiple compression
  3. Intensifying competition from Maxar's Worldview Legion constellation, BlackSky's real-time imaging, and government-built alternatives that could commoditize satellite imagery pricing over time

AI Resilience (25%)
AI Resilience MODERATE
7
AI Risk Protection (6/9): Planet's core business — designing, launching, and operating 200+ imaging satellites in orbit — involves physical-world space infrastructure that AI cannot replicate or substitute [1]. The company's proprietary daily global imagery dataset spanning 10+ years is a unique moat; no AI model can generate real satellite imagery of actual Earth conditions. Government and defense customers require verified, authoritative geospatial data with chain-of-custody, creating regulatory barriers. However, AI-generated synthetic imagery could theoretically reduce demand for lower-resolution monitoring in some commercial applications, and competitors with AI-enhanced analytics could erode Planet's value-added services layer. AI Opportunity (1/1): Planet actively generates revenue from AI-powered analytics, branding itself a 'Space and AI' company [2]. Its machine learning models process petabytes of imagery for automated change detection, crop health monitoring, and infrastructure analysis, with AI capabilities directly embedded in subscription products sold to defense, agriculture, and insurance customers.
