POET Technologies designs, develops, manufactures, and sells semiconductor products based on its patented POET Optical Interposer platform, which integrates electronic and photonic devices into a single chip. The company provides high-speed optical engines and modules for AI data centers, hyperscale networking, and chip-to-chip communication.

Investment Thesis
POET Technologies sits at the intersection of two powerful megatrends — AI infrastructure buildout and the shift from electrical to optical interconnects in data centers. The POET Optical Interposer enables wafer-level photonic integration that could dramatically reduce cost and improve performance of 800G/1.6T/3.2T optical engines [1]. Initial production orders of $5.6M from two customers validate commercial readiness, the 1.6T receiver developed with Semtech positions POET at the technology frontier, and $250M in institutional financing provides capital for the production ramp. If revenue scales from the current $5.6M base toward $50M+ in 2027, the $838M market cap could prove a bargain.
The bear case is that POET has been a pre-revenue technology company for over a decade, and the transition from engineering samples to volume production is the hardest execution challenge in semiconductors [1]. Revenue of $763K TTM is negligible, losses are $9.4M per quarter, and the $838M market cap prices in substantial future revenue that may never materialize. Competitors including Broadcom, Intel, and well-funded startups are pursuing similar photonic integration approaches with vastly greater manufacturing resources. The stock has declined 42% from its 52-week high of $9.41, and volume production ramp-ups in semiconductors frequently encounter yield, quality, and timeline problems [1].
Key Catalysts
  1. Revenue ramp from $5.6M initial orders through 2026 with additional production orders from existing and new customers validating commercial traction [1]
  2. 1.6T optical receiver qualification with Semtech and 3.2T engine development with Quantum Computing could position POET at the frontier of next-gen AI interconnects [1]
  3. Sivers partnership production readiness by end 2026 adds manufacturing capacity and validates the platform's scalability [1]
Key Risks
  1. Volume production ramp from engineering samples to high-yield manufacturing is the highest-risk transition in semiconductors — yield or quality issues could delay revenue significantly [1]
  2. Competition from Broadcom, Intel Silicon Photonics, and well-funded startups with established manufacturing infrastructure [1]
  3. Continued cash burn of ~$9.4M per quarter could require additional dilutive financing if the revenue ramp is slower than expected [1]
