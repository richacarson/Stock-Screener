Company Profile
Technology ·Semiconductors ·NASDAQ ·Canada
POET Technologies Inc. designs, develops, manufactures, and sells photonic integrated packaging solutions based on the POET Optical Interposer, a platform that integrates electronic and photonic devices onto a single chip using wafer-level semiconductor manufacturing. Products include optical engines, transceiver modules, and light source products serving AI data center, telecom, and sensing markets.

Investment Thesis
POET Technologies is pioneering wafer-level photonic integration with its Optical Interposer platform, positioning the company at the intersection of photonics and AI infrastructure demand. Q1 2026 revenue surged 194% YoY to $503K as the company transitions from R&D to commercial shipments [1]. Strategic partnerships with LITEON (hyperscale data centers), Lumilens ($500M collaboration, $50M purchase order), and Lessengers validate the technology platform. The company holds $429M in cash with minimal debt (D/E 1.6%), providing years of runway for commercial scaling [4]. The 1.6Tbps optical engine targets the highest-growth segment of AI interconnects, and POET's wafer-level manufacturing approach promises lower cost and power consumption than traditional discrete optical assemblies [2]. AI data center buildout creates a multi-year structural tailwind for optical interconnect demand.
The bear case is fundamental: POET has $1.4M in TTM revenue against a $2.1B market cap (1,500x revenue), is generating $12.3M quarterly losses, and has never demonstrated commercial-scale production or profitability [1][4]. The Optical Interposer technology, while award-winning, has not been validated at the manufacturing volumes required by hyperscale customers. POET competes against Broadcom, Lumentum, and Coherent — companies with billions in revenue, established customer relationships, and proven manufacturing capabilities. The $500M Lumilens collaboration and $50M purchase order are promising but Lumilens itself is an early-stage company, introducing counterparty risk. POET's 80-person workforce may lack the operational capability to scale manufacturing through the SPX and Globetronics partnerships. Forward EPS of -$0.17 means the company expects to remain unprofitable, and the $429M cash position (largely from equity raises) comes with shareholder dilution. If AI capex spending slows or larger competitors develop equivalent photonic integration platforms, POET's valuation premium could collapse rapidly.
Key Catalysts
  1. Lumilens $500M strategic collaboration and $50M purchase order for AI optical network products, with commercial shipments ramping through 2026-2027, could transform POET's revenue trajectory [1]
  2. 1.6Tbps optical engine sampling with hyperscale data center customers — design wins with Tier 1 hyperscalers would validate the Optical Interposer platform at commercial scale [2]
  3. Manufacturing scale-up through SPX acquisition and Globetronics partnership in Malaysia, providing wafer-level assembly capacity to convert partnerships into volume production [1][2]
Key Risks
  1. Extreme valuation at ~1,500x TTM revenue and negative forward earnings — any delay in commercial ramp or partnership execution could trigger severe stock decline from the $2.1B market cap [4]
  2. Unproven manufacturing scale — POET has never produced at the volumes required by hyperscale customers, and the transition from sample quantities to production volumes carries significant execution risk [1]
  3. Competition from established optical module companies (Broadcom, Lumentum, Coherent) with billions in revenue, proven supply chains, and deep customer relationships that could replicate or exceed POET's photonic integration approach [2]
