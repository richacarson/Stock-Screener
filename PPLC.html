Company Profile
Utilities ·Utilities - Regulated Electric ·NYSE ·United States
PPL Corporation provides electricity and natural gas to approximately 3.6 million customers in the United States through three regulated segments: Kentucky Regulated, Pennsylvania Regulated, and Rhode Island Regulated. The company engages in transmission, distribution, and generation of electricity from coal, gas, hydro, and solar sources. PPLC represents PPL Corporation Corporate Units (equity units) combining stock purchase contracts with senior notes, yielding 7.00% annually. Founded in 1920 and headquartered in Allentown, Pennsylvania.

Investment Thesis
PPL Corporation is a high-quality regulated utility positioned as a prime beneficiary of the AI-driven electricity demand surge. The company serves 3.6 million customers across Pennsylvania, Kentucky, and Rhode Island and is seeing 'massive data center requests' that it had not anticipated [3]. FY2025 EPS of $1.81 grew 7.1% with 2026 guidance of $1.90-$1.98, and management targets 6-8% annual EPS growth through 2029 driven by $23B in infrastructure investment at 10.3% annual rate base growth [1]. Forward P/E of 18.3x is reasonable for this growth profile. The 42-year dividend history with 2.95% current yield and 7.7% recent dividend growth CAGR provides income reliability. The PPLC corporate units (equity units) yield 7.0% including stock purchase contracts and senior notes, offering enhanced income with mandatory stock conversion by February 2029.
The bear case centers on utility-specific risks. D/E of 131.1% and $19.5B total debt create interest rate sensitivity — rising rates compress utility valuations and increase financing costs for the $23B capital plan [5]. ROE of 8.16% is below the 15% target, reflecting the capital-intensive, regulated return model where allowed ROEs are set by regulators, not market forces. The 2022 dividend cut from $1.66 to $0.875 (47% reduction following the UK utility sale) demonstrates that dividend reliability is not absolute despite the 42-year payment streak. Coal generation exposure (2,000 MW retiring by 2034) creates transitional execution risk and potential stranded asset charges. Regulatory proceedings across three states create uncertainty on allowed returns, capital recovery, and rate structure. The PPLC corporate units add complexity — mandatory stock purchase by 2029 means holders will own common shares at potentially unfavorable prices if the stock declines.
Key Catalysts
  1. AI-driven data center electricity demand creating unprecedented load growth in PPL's Pennsylvania and Kentucky territories, accelerating rate base investment and earnings growth [3]
  2. $23B infrastructure capital plan through 2029 at 10.3% annual rate base growth, providing long-term EPS visibility of 6-8% annually [1]
  3. Clean energy transition — 2,000 MW coal retirement by 2034 replaced by renewable generation, positioning PPL for favorable regulatory treatment and ESG-driven capital flows [4]
Key Risks
  1. Interest rate sensitivity — $19.5B debt and $23B forward capital plan create significant exposure to rising borrowing costs that compress utility valuations and increase financing expenses [5]
  2. Regulatory risk across three state jurisdictions — allowed ROE decisions, capital recovery mechanisms, and rate case outcomes directly determine earnings and could disappoint if regulators push back on rate increases [1]
  3. Data center demand execution risk — converting 'massive' interconnection requests into actual load growth requires successful grid buildout, permitting, and customer commitment that may take years to materialize [3]
