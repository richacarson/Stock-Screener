Company Profile
Basic Materials ·Other Precious Metals & Mining ·NASDAQ ·United States
Perpetua Resources Corp. is a development-stage mining company focused on the 100%-owned Stibnite Gold Project in Valley County, Idaho. The project contains gold, silver, and antimony deposits across approximately 11,526 hectares. Formerly known as Midas Gold Corp., the company rebranded in 2021 and commenced construction in October 2025 after raising over $800 million in equity. Antimony is a critical mineral for U.S. defense applications.

Investment Thesis
Perpetua Resources offers pure-play exposure to two compelling macro themes: gold at $2,300+/oz and U.S. critical minerals independence. The Stibnite Gold Project would be the only domestic source of mined antimony — a mineral where China controls 80%+ of global supply and has imposed export restrictions — earning White House Priority Project designation and potential U.S. Export-Import Bank financing [2][4]. With construction commenced, $800M+ in equity raised, and a debt-free balance sheet (D/E 0.03%), the company is well-capitalized for development through first production in 2028-2029, with projected output of 450,000+ oz gold and 35M+ lb antimony over the mine life [1][2].
The bear case is significant: Perpetua is a pre-revenue, single-asset development company burning $100M annually with negative ROE of -20.7% and no revenue generation expected until 2028-2029 at the earliest [7]. Multiple conservation groups and the Nez Perce Tribe have filed lawsuits challenging the Forest Service approval, citing threats to endangered salmon habitat, arsenic exposure from tailings, and inadequate air quality monitoring — any successful legal challenge could delay or derail the project [4][5]. CEO and officers have been net sellers of stock in April 2026, and US Antimony's CEO has publicly challenged whether Stibnite antimony is suitable for military-grade applications, undermining a core thesis pillar [6]. At $4.2B market cap with zero revenue, the stock prices in substantial execution success with no margin for permitting setbacks or construction cost overruns.
Key Catalysts
  1. U.S. Export-Import Bank final investment decision in spring 2026 providing project-level debt financing, de-risking the capital structure and validating the critical minerals thesis at the federal level
  2. Gold prices sustained above $2,300/oz improving project economics and NPV estimates, with antimony prices elevated due to continued Chinese export restrictions creating dual commodity tailwinds
  3. Successful dismissal of conservation group lawsuits challenging the Forest Service Record of Decision, removing the largest overhang on project timeline certainty
Key Risks
  1. Conservation group and Nez Perce Tribe lawsuits could secure an injunction halting construction, with legal challenges citing inadequate environmental review, threatened salmon habitat, and arsenic exposure risks from 465-foot tailings facility
  2. Single-asset concentration risk — Perpetua's entire $4.2B market cap depends on one mine in one location; any geological, permitting, or construction setback has no portfolio diversification to buffer
  3. Pre-revenue cash burn of ~$100M annually with first production 2-3 years away; additional equity raises would dilute shareholders, and gold price decline below $1,800/oz would undermine project economics
