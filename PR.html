Company Profile
Energy ·Oil & Gas E&P ·NYSE ·United States
Permian Resources Corp. is an independent oil and natural gas company and the second-largest pure-play Permian Basin E&P, operating approximately 480,000 net leasehold acres in the Delaware Basin. Created from the 2022 merger of Centennial Resource Development and Colgate Energy, the company achieved record production of 401,500 BOE/d in Q4 2025 and generated $1.6B in adjusted free cash flow in 2025.

Investment Thesis
Permian Resources combines the best attributes of a best-in-class oil producer: 0.9x leverage, investment-grade credit, 480,000 net Delaware Basin acres, $1.6B in FY2025 adjusted free cash flow, record Q4 2025 oil production of 188,600 BOE/d, and a 3.42% growing dividend with a 40%+ CAGR since 2022 inception[1]. With the analyst consensus target of $20.92 (15 analysts, Strong Buy consensus) implying ~12% upside from current prices and the company's proven cost leadership in the lowest-cost U.S. oil basin, PR offers a high-quality energy income and growth combination. The 2026 plan with $1.75-1.95B capex and 186-192 MBbl/d oil guidance targets further FCF growth.
The bear case centers on oil price risk: at $60/barrel or below, Permian Resources' free cash flow profile would deteriorate significantly, potentially stressing the dividend growth program and acquisitions strategy[1]. The 2026 capex of $1.75-1.95B exceeds the 2025 level and requires sustained $70+ WTI to generate meaningful free cash flow. Permian Basin competition for acreage and services has increased as Exxon, Chevron, and ConocoPhillips scale their Permian operations, potentially increasing D&C costs. Environmental regulatory pressure on methane emissions and flaring in New Mexico could increase operating costs.
Key Catalysts
  1. WTI oil price recovery to $80+/barrel driven by OPEC+ production discipline and strong U.S. economic activity, generating FCF per share well above the $1.94 achieved in 2025 and supporting accelerated dividend growth
  2. Q3 2026 production guidance achievement demonstrating that the 2025 acquisitions (250 new locations) are performing as modeled, building confidence in the 400-430 MBoe/d guidance
  3. Potential Permian Basin consolidation opportunity — the pending Exxon/Chevron dominance creates mid-tier E&P acquisition opportunities where Permian Resources' strong balance sheet (0.9x leverage) provides significant deal-making capacity
Key Risks
  1. WTI oil price decline below $60/barrel driven by OPEC+ production increase, weak Chinese demand, or U.S. economic slowdown, reducing FCF below the level required to sustain current capital return and dividend growth commitments
  2. New Mexico regulatory tightening on emissions, water disposal, or setback distances that increases compliance costs or forces curtailment of Delaware Basin operations which represent the majority of production
  3. D&C cost inflation from oilfield services tightening as Permian Basin activity ramps — after reaching $700/ft record lows in 2025, any reversal toward $800-900/ft would compress capital efficiency and reduce the well count economics
