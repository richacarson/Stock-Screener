Company Profile
Energy ·Oil & Gas E&P ·NYSE ·United States
Permian Resources Corporation is an independent oil and natural gas company focused on the development of crude oil and associated liquids-rich natural gas reserves in the Permian Basin. Led by co-CEOs James Walter and Will Hickey, both in their mid-30s, the company has grown rapidly through the 2023 Earthstone Energy merger and organic development. FY2025 revenue was $5.07B with record quarterly free cash flow per share in Q1 2026. Production guidance calls for 6% growth in 2026.

Investment Thesis
PR at 8.7x forward earnings with PEG of 1.2 offers deep value in a high-quality Permian Basin pure-play with uniquely youthful leadership. Q1 2026 delivered record quarterly free cash flow per share with production guidance calling for 6% growth in 2026, and FY2026 revenue is projected at $6.28B with $1.67 EPS [1]. D/E of 32.7% provides one of the cleanest balance sheets in E&P. The 3.3% dividend yield (initiated 2024) and aggressive share repurchase program return capital to shareholders while maintaining growth. Co-CEOs Walter and Hickey (mid-30s) represent decades of potential leadership continuity, and the data-driven management approach has delivered consistent operational outperformance and cost discipline in the most productive U.S. oil basin [2].
The bear case centers on commodity price exposure: revenue is entirely dependent on oil and natural gas prices, and the company's EPS declined 89% YoY in Q1 2026 as commodity prices normalized from 2022-2023 peaks [1]. Trailing P/E of 20.9x versus forward P/E of 8.7x highlights how volatile earnings are — a sustained oil price decline below $55/barrel WTI would compress margins and threaten the recently initiated dividend. The $3.7B debt load, while manageable at 32.7% D/E, was partially incurred for the Earthstone acquisition. Insider selling of $16.5M+ by directors and CFO in Q1 2026 is a negative signal, and the company's Sustainalytics ESG rating of 'Severe' may deter institutional ESG-mandated investors [3][4]. At 515 employees managing $5B+ operations, understaffing risk could impair execution during periods of rapid drilling activity.
Key Catalysts
  1. 6% production growth guidance for 2026 with record free cash flow per share demonstrating that PR can grow production while generating returns, validating the post-Earthstone integration thesis
  2. Oil price recovery above $75/barrel WTI would rapidly expand earnings given PR's low-cost Permian Basin operations — forward P/E of 8.7x implies significant upside at normalized commodity prices
  3. Potential further Permian Basin M&A at distressed valuations could replicate the Earthstone merger playbook, leveraging the co-CEOs' acquisition-integration track record
Key Risks
  1. Commodity price sensitivity: revenue is 100% correlated with oil and natural gas prices, and EPS declined 89% YoY as prices normalized — a sustained decline below $55/barrel WTI could force dividend cuts and capex reductions
  2. Insider selling of $16.5M+ by directors and CFO in Q1 2026, while potentially routine, sends a negative signal about near-term conviction at current valuation levels
  3. Sustainalytics 'Severe' ESG rating may exclude PR from ESG-mandated institutional portfolios and limit the investor base, creating a structural discount to peers with better ESG scores
