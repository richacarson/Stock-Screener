Company Profile

PROCEPT BioRobotics is a surgical robotics company that develops, manufactures, and sells the AquaBeam Robotic System and HYDROS Robotic System for image-guided, minimally invasive urologic surgery. The company focuses on treating benign prostatic hyperplasia (BPH) through its proprietary Aquablation therapy, targeting the estimated 30M+ men in the U.S. affected by BPH.

Investment Thesis
PROCEPT BioRobotics is a high-growth surgical robotics disruptor that grew FY2025 revenue 37% to $308M and guides for $410-430M in 2026 (+26-32%), driven by Aquablation therapy adoption across 400+ installed sites and international expansion of the AI-enabled HYDROS system [1]. Q4 2025 set a record with 65 new system placements, and the total addressable market for BPH surgical intervention is estimated at $10B+. The low D/E of 21.5% provides balance sheet flexibility, and TD Cowen maintains a Buy rating, viewing PROCEPT as a category-defining platform in urology [2]. The Inspire Impact Score of 41 (ALIGNED) reflects positive patient welfare and accessibility attributions.
The bear case is substantial. PROCEPT is unprofitable (-31% profit margin, -24.9% ROE) with no clear path to breakeven disclosed, and Spruce Point Capital's short report highlighted structural growth barriers, questioned AI capabilities, and flagged safety concerns [2]. At $24.24, the stock has declined significantly from highs, and the Q1 2026 guidance of just 14-19% growth represents sharp deceleration from the 43% Q3 2025 pace. Competition from Olympus and Boston Scientific in minimally invasive BPH treatments could compress PROCEPT's market opportunity. The September 2025 CEO transition creates execution risk during a critical commercialization phase. Single-product, single-indication companies carry binary risk — any safety signal or reimbursement challenge could be existential.
