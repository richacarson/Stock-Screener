Company Profile
Healthcare ·Drug Manufacturers - Specialty & Generic ·NYSE ·Ireland
Perrigo is a leading provider of over-the-counter consumer self-care products, offering store-brand alternatives to national brands across upper respiratory, pain relief, digestive health, infant formula, oral care, skin care, and women's health categories. The company operates through Consumer Self-Care Americas and Consumer Self-Care International segments, serving retail drug, supermarket, and mass merchandise chains globally. Founded in 1887 and headquartered in Dublin, Ireland.

Investment Thesis
Perrigo trades at a deeply discounted 4.5x forward P/E with a 10.7% dividend yield, reflecting substantial market skepticism about the company's turnaround trajectory [4]. The bull case rests on the company being the largest store-brand OTC healthcare provider in the Americas with $4.25B in revenue, a defensive end market (people buy OTC medicine regardless of economic conditions), and a restructuring program targeting operational efficiency gains in 2026-2028 [1]. The Opill OTC birth control launch represents a genuine category expansion, the innovation pipeline has reportedly tripled in value, and forward earnings of $2.25-$2.55 per share imply the stock trades at less than 5x recovery earnings [1]. If management executes on cost savings and stabilizes the top line, meaningful earnings recovery is achievable.
The bear case is formidable. Revenue has declined from $4.66B in 2023 to $4.25B in 2025, with FY2026 guidance calling for a further 1.5-5.5% decline [1]. Profit margins are deeply negative (-33.5%) due to impairment charges, plant under-absorption from lower volumes, and restructuring costs. The $3.85B debt load against $532M cash creates leverage risk with D/E at 131%. The dividend cut in 2025 and unsustainable 109.5% payout ratio on trailing earnings signal financial distress. Employee walkouts and leadership turnover (CSO replaced, supply chain leadership transitioned) indicate operational instability [2][3]. The infant formula business, which drove the HRA Pharma acquisition thesis, faces competitive headwinds and volume declines. Free cash flow of just $28M in 2025 leaves virtually no margin for error on debt service and capital allocation [4].
Key Catalysts
  1. Organizational restructuring under global category-led model targeting operational efficiency and cost savings beginning in 2026, with Disruptive Growth Team pursuing category expansion [2]
  2. Opill OTC birth control pill distribution expansion as the first FDA-approved OTC contraceptive, potentially driving new revenue growth in women's health [1]
  3. Recovery in forward earnings to $2.25-$2.55 EPS in FY2026, implying significant profit margin improvement from impairment-depressed 2025 levels [1]
Key Risks
  1. Revenue decline trajectory — sales dropped from $4.66B to $4.25B over two years with FY2026 guidance for further 1.5-5.5% decline, driven by infant formula volume losses and category headwinds [1]
  2. Leverage risk with $3.85B debt against $532M cash and free cash flow of only $28M in 2025, leaving minimal financial flexibility for debt service, dividends, and growth investment [4]
  3. Labor unrest and organizational instability — employee walkouts over compensation disputes, leadership turnover across scientific and supply chain functions, and ongoing restructuring creating execution risk [2][3]
