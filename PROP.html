Company Profile

Prairie Operating Co. is an independent energy company focused on the acquisition, development, and production of crude oil, natural gas, and natural gas liquids in the Denver-Julesburg Basin. The company transformed from a pre-revenue startup to a producing E&P through the $602.75M acquisition of Bayswater Exploration & Production assets, reaching approximately 28,000 net Boe/d production by year-end 2025.

Investment Thesis
Prairie Operating is a micro-cap E&P company that transformed from pre-revenue startup to $241.6M in annual revenue and 28,000 net Boe/d production through the $602.75M Bayswater acquisition in the prolific Denver-Julesburg Basin [1]. The stock trades at an extraordinary 1.35x forward P/E against 2026 guidance of $55-65M net income and $240-260M adjusted EBITDA, suggesting deep market skepticism despite strong operational execution [1][4]. Extensive hedging through 2029 provides commodity price protection, 70.4% gross margins demonstrate asset quality, and the DJ Basin acreage offers significant development runway. An ESG-forward approach (zero-flaring, green completions, enclosed combustors) differentiates Prairie from E&P peers and could attract ESG-aware capital [3]. At a $100M market cap with $155.5M in 2025 adjusted EBITDA, the equity appears deeply undervalued on operational metrics alone.
The bear case is severe and explains the extreme valuation discount. The simultaneous departure of CEO Kovalik and President Hanna in March 2026 creates a leadership vacuum at a critical juncture — the company has an interim CEO while conducting a permanent search, raising governance and execution risk [2]. Total debt of $371M against a $100M market cap and only $20K in cash represents extreme financial leverage that could become untenable if commodity prices decline or development costs overrun [4]. Free cash flow was negative $535M in FY2025, indicating massive capital consumption that requires continued access to debt or equity markets. The stock has fallen 73% from its 52-week high of $4.80, suggesting the market may be pricing in dilution, restructuring, or balance sheet distress. With only 59 employees, the company has minimal organizational depth to manage the transition. Net loss attributable to common stockholders was $60.9M in FY2025 despite $32M GAAP net income, implying significant preferred equity or minority interest dilution [1].
