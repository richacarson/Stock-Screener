Company Profile
Technology ·Semiconductors ·NASDAQ ·United States
Peraso Inc. is a fabless semiconductor company specializing in 60 GHz millimeter-wave (mmWave) wireless technology for fixed wireless access, tactical defense communications, and autonomous vehicle connectivity. The company also sells legacy bandwidth engine memory ICs, a product line approaching end-of-life.

Investment Thesis
Peraso's mmWave revenue grew 6x YoY to $9.4M in 2025, and the company halved its net loss to $4.8M while cutting operating expenses to $11.8M [1]. Design wins in defense (InTACT drone IFF systems), fixed wireless access (Tachyon, WeLink), and autonomous vehicles (Virewirx robotaxi) provide multiple growth vectors in the 60 GHz unlicensed spectrum. Gross margin of 58% is healthy for a fabless semiconductor. Management targets 50% revenue growth in 2026 to $12.7-15M, with analyst estimates projecting $6.3M quarterly revenue by Q4 2026. At $0.96/share with only $192K in debt and $2.9M cash, the stock offers asymmetric upside if the mmWave design wins convert to volume production [1].
The bear case is severe for Peraso. The company has active going-concern warnings and faces Nasdaq delisting risk after failing the $1.00 bid price requirement [3]. Q1 2026 revenue guided to just $1.2M due to customs delays, suggesting highly lumpy and unpredictable revenue [1]. The company needs approximately $6M quarterly revenue at 50% margins to break even — double its current run rate. Cash of $2.9M provides minimal runway. A hostile takeover bid from Mobix Labs was rejected, raising governance questions about whether the board is acting in shareholders' best interests [3]. The memory IC product line is approaching end-of-life, removing a revenue base before mmWave can fully compensate. Customer concentration risk is high with irregular order patterns from FWA customers. With a $9M market cap and no analyst coverage, this is an extremely high-risk micro-cap speculation.
Key Catalysts
  1. mmWave revenue trajectory — 6x growth in 2025 with design wins in defense, FWA, and autonomous vehicles suggesting continued momentum toward $12.7-15M in 2026 revenue [1]
  2. Defense sector expansion through InTACT partnership for next-generation drone Identification Friend or Foe systems, representing high-margin mission-critical applications [1]
  3. Break-even target at ~$6M quarterly revenue and 50% gross margin — achievable by H2 2026 if design wins convert, which would remove going-concern overhang [1]
Key Risks
  1. Going-concern warning and Nasdaq delisting risk — only $2.9M cash with ongoing losses; failure to maintain $1.00 bid price deadline of March 2026 could force reverse split or delisting [3]
  2. Revenue concentration and lumpiness — Q1 2026 guided to $1.2M (vs $2.9M Q4) due to a single delayed customs shipment, highlighting extreme order volatility from a small customer base [1]
  3. Hostile takeover and governance risk — Mobix Labs' unsolicited bid and Peraso board's refusal to engage raises questions about strategic direction and shareholder value maximization [3]
