Company Profile
Communication Services ·Entertainment ·NASDAQ ·United States
Paramount Skydance Corporation operates as a media and entertainment company worldwide, formed through the August 2025 merger of Paramount Global, National Amusements, and Skydance Media. It operates through Studios, Direct-to-Consumer (Paramount+, Pluto TV), and TV Media (CBS, Nickelodeon, MTV, BET) segments. The company was founded in 1914 and is headquartered in New York.

Investment Thesis
Paramount Skydance represents a high-risk turnaround play under CEO David Ellison, who has committed to a 20-year transformation of the 112-year-old entertainment company into a 'media and technology' platform. Q1 2026 revenue of $7.35B (+2% YoY) beat estimates, with Paramount+ growing subscribers to 79.6M and streaming revenue rising 11% to $2.4B [1]. Forward P/E of 11.4x and PEG of 1.31 offer attractive valuation for the turnaround thesis, and merger cost savings exceeding $3B are tracking ahead of plan [2]. Ellison's tech-forward vision, Oracle family connection for AI infrastructure, and $16.4B free cash flow generation (TTM) provide the tools for transformation. The company's iconic content library (CBS, Nickelodeon, MTV, Paramount Pictures) and franchise portfolio (Mission: Impossible, Star Trek, Transformers) are irreplaceable assets that benefit from global streaming distribution [1].
The bear case is substantial: PSKY carries $16.6B in debt against an $11.1B market cap, ROE is negative (-0.77%), GAAP profit margin is negative (-2.1%), and the TV Media segment declined 6% in Q1 as cord-cutting accelerates [1][4]. The entertainment industry faces structural AI disruption risk as AI-generated content threatens traditional content economics — Hollywood's 2023 strikes were explicitly about this existential threat. The merger integration introduces execution risk: reshuffled leadership, $3B+ in cost cuts, and cultural integration of Skydance's tech-forward approach with Paramount's legacy media operations. The DEI rollback drew employee backlash and may impact ESG-focused investor sentiment [3]. Revenue has been essentially flat for five years (0.4% 5yr CAGR), and Paramount+ remains unprofitable while competing against Netflix, Disney+, Amazon, and Apple with deeper pockets. The potential WBD acquisition would create additional integration complexity. At $9.94/share, PSKY is a binary bet on Ellison's ability to execute a transformation that Shari Redstone's leadership could not achieve.
