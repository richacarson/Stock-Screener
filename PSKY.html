Paramount Skydance Corporation is a global media and entertainment company formed in August 2025 through the merger of Skydance Media and Paramount Global. Operating across Studios, Direct-to-Consumer (Paramount+, Pluto TV), and TV Media (CBS, Nickelodeon, MTV, BET) segments, the company is pursuing a $110B acquisition of Warner Bros. Discovery to create the largest content empire in entertainment.

Investment Thesis
Paramount Skydance offers transformational upside if the $110B WBD acquisition closes, creating a combined media giant with $69B in revenue and an unmatched content library spanning HBO, Warner Bros., DC, CBS, Paramount Pictures, Nickelodeon, and MTV [1]. At a forward P/E of 9.4x and $8.88 share price, the stock is priced for the current struggling standalone business rather than the combined entity's potential. The $3B cost savings program (already $1.4B achieved) provides near-term earnings improvement, and Paramount+ reaching 79M subscribers with 17% revenue growth demonstrates streaming traction. The Oracle-powered tech transformation could unlock AI-driven efficiencies across the world's largest content operation [2].
The bear case is formidable: the WBD acquisition adds massive debt to an already leveraged balance sheet (117% D/E), creating financial fragility if integration falters or content spending disappoints. The company is currently losing money (-2.1% profit margin), the 666.7% payout ratio means the dividend is funded by debt rather than earnings, and the 3,000+ layoffs have gutted institutional knowledge [3]. Regulatory risk for the WBD deal remains — antitrust review of combining CBS, Paramount+, HBO, CNN, and Warner Bros. could result in mandated divestitures. The political compromises (Trump settlement, DEI elimination, Colbert non-renewal) create brand and talent risk, potentially driving creative talent to competitors. Linear TV revenue declined 5% YoY with no floor in sight, and streaming advertising fell 4%, suggesting structural headwinds persist.
Key Catalysts
  1. Warner Bros. Discovery acquisition closing in H2 2026, creating a $69B revenue media conglomerate with the world's largest combined content library and streaming platform [1]
  2. $3B cost savings program reaching full run-rate, with $2.4B+ achieved by end of 2026, potentially driving adjusted EBITDA toward $3.8B guidance [1]
  3. Paramount+ and HBO Max platform combination creating a competitive streaming offering rivaling Netflix and Disney+ in content breadth and subscriber scale
Key Risks
  1. WBD acquisition regulatory risk — antitrust review of combining CBS, Paramount+, HBO, CNN, and Warner Bros. could require significant divestitures or block the deal entirely, with a $7B termination fee payable if regulatory approval fails [1]
  2. Debt overload — adding WBD's leverage to Paramount's existing 117% D/E could create a combined entity with $50B+ in debt, limiting financial flexibility and increasing bankruptcy risk in a downturn
  3. Creative talent exodus — the DEI program elimination, Colbert non-renewal, and CBS News editorial changes may drive premium creative and journalistic talent to Netflix, Apple TV+, and Amazon, degrading content quality [3]
