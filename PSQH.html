Investment Thesis
PSQ Holdings offers speculative exposure to the values-aligned fintech niche through its PublicSquare platform, which grew revenue 81% YoY to $18.2M in 2025 and guides for $32M+ in 2026 (+76% growth) [1]. The company's pivot from marketplace to financial infrastructure — payments processing, BNPL, and credit for underserved industries — addresses a genuine market gap for conservative-aligned merchants. Q4 2025 revenue exceeded guidance by 10%+, and new CEO Dusty Wunderlich brings fintech-specific experience as the company focuses on 'measurable execution' [1]. The credit product expansion could drive meaningful revenue acceleration if the fintech infrastructure scales.
The bear case is severe. PSQH trades at $0.66 with a $34M market cap, has received a NYSE non-compliance notice for listing standards, and faces potential delisting [3]. The company burns cash at an alarming rate with a -201% profit margin and -123.6% ROE, generating an $18.2M net loss on $18.2M revenue [5]. The $7.5M registered direct offering in December 2025 diluted shareholders at distressed prices, and further dilution is likely given ongoing losses [3]. CEO turnover — founder Seifert departed in January 2026, two years after going public — raises governance concerns and strategic continuity risk. The fintech pivot is unproven, and PSQ competes against massively scaled incumbents (Stripe, Square, Affirm) with deeper technology and broader merchant bases. The ideological niche may limit total addressable market. Average daily liquidity of $566K makes the stock difficult to trade without significant price impact, and the 10-day average of $252K is even worse [5].
