Company Profile
Financial Services ·Asset Management ·NYSE ·United States
Pershing Square USA, Ltd. is a closed-end equity fund launched in April 2026 via a $5 billion IPO on the NYSE, managed by Pershing Square Capital Management, L.P. The fund invests in 12-15 high-quality, predominantly North American-listed, large-capitalization growth companies. Current holdings include Amazon, Microsoft, Uber, Meta, Brookfield, Restaurant Brands, Fannie Mae, and Freddie Mac. Led by Bill Ackman (CEO) and Ryan Israel (CIO).

Investment Thesis
PSUS offers access to Bill Ackman's 16% annualized track record (since 2004) through a permanent capital structure with no performance fee, at a ~20% discount to NAV ($39.79 vs. ~$50 NAV) [1]. The portfolio is concentrated in 12 high-quality large-cap names including Microsoft, Amazon, Meta, Uber, and Brookfield, with 85% of capital deployed. The forward P/E of 5.7x reflects attractive look-through valuations on the underlying holdings. Ackman and affiliates have invested $500+ million in personal capital, creating exceptional alignment [1]. If the NAV discount narrows from 20% to the 10-15% typical of mature closed-end funds, shareholders would capture 6-12% in additional return beyond portfolio performance.
The bear case centers on the persistent NAV discount, early performance disappointment, and key-person risk. PSUS has traded at a ~20% discount since its April 2026 IPO, and closed-end fund discounts often persist for years — there is no redemption mechanism to force convergence [1]. PSH (the parent fund) was down -4.7% YTD through May 2026, underperforming the S&P 500 during the same period. Ackman's concentrated, high-conviction style amplifies both gains and losses — the same conviction that generated 70%+ returns in 2019 produced significant losses in 2015-2016 (Valeant). The fund has no financial history as an independent entity, making traditional scoring metrics largely inapplicable. Key-person risk is extreme — the entire value proposition depends on Ackman's judgment, and the board has noted succession planning as a focus area [2]. The 0% profit margin and null revenue in yfinance data reflect the fund structure rather than operational performance, but this makes quantitative infrastructure scoring essentially impossible.
Key Catalysts
  1. NAV discount narrowing from ~20% to the 10-15% range typical of mature closed-end funds could generate 6-12% incremental return beyond portfolio performance [1]
  2. Pershing Square's concentrated portfolio in AI beneficiaries (Microsoft, Meta, Amazon) positions PSUS to benefit from continued AI infrastructure spending acceleration [2]
  3. Ackman's activist involvement in portfolio companies — particularly Fannie Mae/Freddie Mac recapitalization thesis — could unlock significant value if government policy shifts materialize
Key Risks
  1. Persistent NAV discount is structural for closed-end funds — PSUS has no redemption mechanism, and the discount could widen beyond 20% if performance disappoints or market sentiment sours [1]
  2. Key-person risk is extreme — Ackman's judgment drives all portfolio decisions, and his concentrated style has historically produced both spectacular gains and devastating losses (Valeant, JC Penney) [2]
  3. The fund has zero independent operating history (IPO April 2026), making all performance claims based on the parent fund PSH, which operates under different fee structures and capital constraints
