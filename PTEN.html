Company Profile
Energy ·Oil & Gas Drilling ·NASDAQ ·United States
Patterson-UTI Energy is a leading provider of drilling and completion services to oil and natural gas companies, operating 136 Tier-1 super-spec drilling rigs, approximately 2.7 million hydraulic horsepower in fracturing, and a global drilling products business. The 2023 merger with NexTier gave it nearly 20% of the North American drilling and completions market.

Investment Thesis
Patterson-UTI offers leveraged exposure to a potential natural gas drilling recovery driven by new LNG facility demand, with a dominant 20% North American market share, 136 Tier-1 super-spec rigs, and 2.7M HHP in completion services post-NexTier merger [1]. At $11.26, the stock trades at a steep discount to tangible book value, with D/E of only 39.7% and a healthy balance sheet that can weather the cycle. If LNG-driven natural gas drilling increases materialize in H2 2026, Patterson-UTI's integrated platform is positioned to capture disproportionate share of incremental demand.
The bear case is significant: the company is currently unprofitable with negative EPS, revenue declining 1% YoY, and a dividend payout ratio of 213% that appears unsustainable without an earnings recovery [4]. Legacy contracts are rolling off at lower rates, completion services adjusted gross profit is expected to decline sequentially, and the U.S. rig count has been range-bound for quarters. If oil prices decline further or LNG demand fails to materialize on schedule, Patterson-UTI could face a prolonged trough requiring dividend cuts and further cost restructuring.
Key Catalysts
  1. New LNG facility commissioning expected to drive incremental natural gas drilling demand in H2 2026 and beyond, with PTEN positioned as the largest integrated provider [1]
  2. International expansion with Saudi Arabia manufacturing center upgrade and North Africa natural gas opportunities creating multi-year growth runway outside the U.S. [1]
  3. Potential rig count recovery from current 94-rig average if oil prices stabilize above $70/bbl and natural gas demand accelerates
Key Risks
  1. Dividend sustainability risk with 213% payout ratio against negative EPS — a cut would likely pressure the stock further and signal management's lack of confidence in near-term recovery [4]
  2. Prolonged U.S. rig count stagnation as E&P companies prioritize capital discipline over production growth, limiting PTEN's utilization and pricing power [1]
  3. Oil price volatility below $60/bbl would accelerate E&P spending cuts, reducing demand for PTEN's drilling and completion services
