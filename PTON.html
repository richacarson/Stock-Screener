Investment Thesis
Peloton's bull case is a free cash flow turnaround story: the company generated $380M in free cash flow over the last 12 months, Q1 FY2026 adjusted EBITDA was $118M (above guidance), and the May 2025 $1.35B refinancing eliminated the near-term maturity wall[1]. With 2.7M+ highly loyal subscribers paying ~$44/month in subscription revenue, Peloton has a recurring revenue base that is sticky (97%+ gross retention) and high-margin (50.9% gross margin). At ~$8/share, the stock trades at a significant discount to the enterprise value implied by its free cash flow generation.
The bear case is structural: Peloton's subscriber count is declining (-164K YoY in Q1 FY2026) and hardware sales are contracting, meaning the high-margin subscription base is gradually eroding[1]. The $1.534B debt load with negative stockholders' equity is a structural vulnerability — any free cash flow shortfall reduces the already-thin buffer between solvency and distress. Short interest at 17.44% of outstanding shares reflects significant bearish conviction. Competing fitness content platforms (Apple Fitness+, Amazon Halo) are improving rapidly with AI personalization, and Peloton's hardware moat is weakening as competitors offer comparable connected fitness at lower price points.
Key Catalysts
  1. Subscriber base stabilization — reversing the 164K YoY subscriber decline in Q1 FY2026 would signal that the content-first strategy is working and that churn has been arrested
  2. New hardware product cycle at accessible price points — expanding the total addressable market beyond $1,500+ bikes could attract price-sensitive fitness consumers who have switched to competitors
  3. Strategic partnership or content licensing deal with a major gym chain or health system that monetizes Peloton's content library beyond hardware-owning subscribers, diversifying the revenue base
Key Risks
  1. Subscriber base continuing to decline below 2.5M as Apple Fitness+ and Lululemon Studio offer comparable content at lower cost, eroding the recurring revenue base that underpins the free cash flow thesis
  2. Debt refinancing risk — the $985M term loan and $549M convertible notes require ongoing debt service, and any revenue shortfall could trigger covenant violations or force distressed refinancing
  3. Product recall or safety incident similar to the 2021 treadmill recall that significantly damaged brand trust and accelerated subscriber churn at a company that cannot afford another reputational crisis
