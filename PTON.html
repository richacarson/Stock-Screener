Company Profile
Consumer Cyclical ·Leisure ·NASDAQ ·United States
Peloton Interactive, Inc. provides connected fitness products and subscription-based fitness content globally. Products include Peloton Bike, Bike+, Tread, Tread+, and Row. The company offers all-access memberships, rental programs, and the Peloton App. Sells through e-commerce, retail showrooms, and third-party retailers. Founded in 2012, headquartered in New York, New York.
2,605 Employees ·www.onepeloton.com

Investment Thesis
Peloton's turnaround story is showing early signs of traction under new CEO Peter Stern. Q3 FY2026 delivered $630.9M revenue (1% YoY growth, the first positive growth in years), beating guidance of $618.7M, with GAAP profitability achieved for the third time in four quarters [1]. Gross margins of 52% demonstrate the subscription model's underlying quality, and $398M in TTM free cash flow provides financial stability after years of cash burn. Subscription revenue grew 4% sequentially to $428M, and the company raised full-year guidance for both revenue ($2.42-2.44B) and free cash flow. The 2.66M paid connected fitness subscribers represent a valuable, recurring revenue base, and the newly created CTO role focused on AI-powered personalization could drive member engagement and reduce churn from the current 1.2% monthly rate [1][2].
The fundamental challenge is that Peloton's subscriber base is shrinking — paid connected fitness subscriptions declined 218,000 or 7.6% YoY — and no amount of cost optimization can offset a structurally declining revenue pool indefinitely [1]. The stock at $5.72 (down 38% from its 52-week high of $9.20) prices in continued skepticism about sustainable growth. Negative stockholders' equity (-$413.7M), $1.98B in total debt, and a trailing P/E of 95x on just $0.06 EPS reflect a business that is barely profitable on an absolute basis. Leadership instability remains acute: five CEO transitions, four CMOs in five years, and a pattern of zero insider buying against 62 insider sells over 12 months signals that even management lacks conviction [2][4]. The connected fitness category faces structural headwinds from free alternatives (YouTube, social media workouts), gym reopenings, and emerging AI fitness coaches that could erode the premium subscription value proposition.
Key Catalysts
  1. Sustained quarterly profitability and subscriber stabilization demonstrating that the turnaround under CEO Peter Stern is generating durable results beyond cost-cutting
  2. AI-powered personalization features under new CTO Francis Shanahan driving measurable improvements in member engagement, workout frequency, and churn reduction below 1.2% monthly
  3. Channel expansion through third-party retail partners and the Peloton App standalone subscription reaching new customer segments beyond the core premium connected fitness buyer
Key Risks
  1. Paid connected fitness subscriber base declining 7.6% YoY to 2.66M — if the subscriber trajectory does not inflect, revenue will eventually resume its decline despite pricing actions
  2. Negative stockholders' equity of -$413.7M and $1.98B in total debt create balance sheet fragility that limits strategic flexibility and increases vulnerability to any demand shock
  3. Zero insider buying and 62 insider sells over 12 months, including the Chief Commercial Officer selling all shares, signals leadership does not believe in the equity's upside potential
