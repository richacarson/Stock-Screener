Investment Thesis
Peloton is executing a turnaround under CEO Peter Stern, reaching positive net income ($26M in Q3 FY2026) for the first time in years while growing Adjusted EBITDA 41% YoY to $126M [1]. The subscription price increase in October 2025 is working — subscription revenue grew 2% YoY with better-than-expected churn, and AI-powered Personalized Plans reached nearly 500K members, improving engagement metrics [2]. At $5.17 per share with a $2.24B market cap, the stock is priced for continued skepticism, offering asymmetric upside if the margin expansion and AI-driven engagement improvements continue to compound.
The bear case centers on structural subscriber decline — paid connected fitness subscriptions fell 7.6% YoY to 2.66M, and the 3-year revenue CAGR of -11.4% reflects a business that has shrunk substantially from its pandemic peak of $3.58B [1]. With $1.98B in debt against negative equity of -$413.7M, the balance sheet remains stressed and limits strategic flexibility. The connected fitness category faces intensifying competition from free/low-cost fitness apps, AI-generated workout content, and gym reopenings, raising questions about whether Peloton's premium pricing model is sustainable. Four CMOs in five years and serial C-suite turnover create execution risk around the turnaround strategy [2].
Key Catalysts
  1. Continued margin expansion with Adjusted EBITDA growing 41% YoY to $126M in Q3 could establish a path to sustained free cash flow generation and debt reduction [1]
  2. AI-powered Personalized Plans reaching 500K members with data showing improved engagement could reduce churn and increase lifetime value per subscriber [2]
  3. B2B enterprise wellness partnerships and third-party retail expansion under Peter Stern's leadership could open new subscriber acquisition channels beyond direct-to-consumer [2]
Key Risks
  1. Connected fitness subscriber base declining 7.6% YoY to 2.66M — if the decline accelerates, subscription revenue will erode and the profitability turnaround reverses [1]
  2. Negative equity (-$413.7M) and $1.98B in debt create refinancing risk if the turnaround stalls, with limited balance sheet cushion for unexpected setbacks [5]
  3. Free AI-generated fitness content and low-cost competitors increasingly commoditize the workout content that underpins Peloton's premium subscription pricing [2]

AI Resilience (25%)
AI Resilience MODERATE
6
AI Risk Protection (5/9): Peloton's core business faces moderate AI exposure. The subscription fitness content model is defensible through its library of 10,000+ instructor-led classes and community engagement, but AI-generated fitness coaching (from Apple Fitness+, YouTube, and startups) creates substitution risk for the software/content layer [2]. The hardware component (bikes, treads, rowers) provides physical-world defensibility — AI cannot replace the physical equipment, but it can make the premium subscription less differentiated. Peloton's brand and instructor relationships provide meaningful moats, but the broader fitness content market is becoming more accessible through AI-powered alternatives. AI Opportunity (1/1): Peloton is actively deploying AI with Personalized Plans reaching ~500K members in Q3 FY2026, demonstrating tangible AI revenue enhancement through improved engagement and reduced churn [2]. The new CTO role focused on AI innovation signals serious investment in using AI as a competitive advantage.
