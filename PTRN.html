Company Profile
Technology ·Software - Application ·NASDAQ ·United States
Pattern Group is an ecommerce accelerator that combines proprietary AI-powered technology and on-demand expertise to help consumer brands operate across ecommerce marketplaces. The company acquires inventory from brand partners to sell to consumers across health & wellness, beauty, home, pet, sports, and consumer electronics categories. Formerly known as Covalent Group, it rebranded in November 2024 and is headquartered in Lehi, Utah.

Investment Thesis
Pattern Group is a high-growth ecommerce accelerator with remarkable revenue trajectory — $2.5B in 2025 (up 40% YoY) with management guiding to $3.12-$3.16B in 2026 (25-26% growth) [1]. The AI-powered platform leveraging 66 trillion data points provides a differentiated technology moat, and the pristine balance sheet ($289M cash, $31M debt) supports continued investment [2][4]. Non-Amazon revenue grew 60% in 2025, signaling successful diversification. At 23.5x forward P/E on $0.59 forward EPS, the stock offers growth at a reasonable valuation if the company can sustain its trajectory and expand margins. The $100M share repurchase program demonstrates management confidence [1].
The bear case centers on three critical risks. First, Amazon concentration at 93% of revenue creates existential platform dependency — any change to Amazon's marketplace policies, algorithms, or third-party seller economics could devastate Pattern's business model [1]. Second, profitability is razor-thin at 0.65% net margin on $2.5B revenue ($16.2M net income), and the company's inventory acquisition model carries working capital risk during demand downturns [4]. Third, insider selling is a red flag: CEO David Wright sold 9.4M shares for $122.6M and Director Daniel Gay sold 1.3M shares for $16.9M in September 2025, dwarfing the $3.9M in insider purchases — net insider selling exceeded $135M, suggesting those closest to the company are monetizing [4]. The ecommerce accelerator space has seen significant failures (Thrasio restructuring), and Pattern's margins leave no room for execution errors.
Key Catalysts
  1. Non-Amazon revenue diversification — the segment grew 94% in Q4 2025 and 60% for the full year, potentially reducing the existential Amazon concentration risk if the trend accelerates [1]
  2. 2026 revenue guidance of $3.12-$3.16B with $180-$182M adjusted EBITDA would demonstrate sustainable growth with improving unit economics as the AI platform scales [1]
  3. Margin expansion from AI-driven automation and platform operating leverage — the company's technology investments should yield higher margins as revenue scales beyond $3B [2]
Key Risks
  1. Amazon platform concentration at 93% of revenue creates existential dependency — policy changes, algorithm shifts, or fee increases could immediately compress already razor-thin 0.65% margins [1]
  2. Massive net insider selling ($135M+ in September 2025) by CEO and directors raises alignment concerns — the people with the most information about the business are cashing out at scale [4]
  3. Ecommerce accelerator business model risk — the inventory acquisition model requires substantial working capital and carries demand risk, as demonstrated by industry peer Thrasio's restructuring [1]
