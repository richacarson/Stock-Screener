Company Profile
Financial Services ·Capital Markets ·NASDAQ ·United States
Hyperliquid Strategies is a digital asset treasury company focused on accumulating HYPE, the native token of the Hyperliquid blockchain. The company provides investors exposure to HYPE through treasury management strategies including token accumulation, staking, and yield optimization. Incorporated in 2025 and led by former Barclays CEO Bob Diamond (Chairman) and David Schamis (CEO).

Investment Thesis
Hyperliquid Strategies offers leveraged exposure to the HYPE token through a publicly traded vehicle, holding 17.9M HYPE (1.86% of supply) acquired at $7.23 average cost with $562M in unrealized gains as of early 2026 [1]. The underlying Hyperliquid protocol processes ~$10B in daily trading volume with a 70% share of the decentralized perpetual futures market, and CEO Schamis argues HYPE is undervalued relative to its $600M+ annualized fee run rate [2]. The $1B S-1 filing and $30M buyback program signal management's conviction. For investors seeking HYPE exposure through a traditional brokerage account, PURR provides a regulated alternative to direct token ownership.
The bear case is overwhelming for a fundamentally-oriented framework like IOWN. PURR has no revenue, no earnings, no products, no employees, and no competitive moat — it is a single-asset holding vehicle with 100% correlation to HYPE token price [1]. The stock trades at a premium to NAV when crypto sentiment is positive and at a discount when negative, offering worse economics than direct token ownership due to management fees and corporate overhead. Cryptocurrency markets are inherently volatile, unregulated, and subject to black swan events (exchange hacks, protocol exploits, regulatory crackdowns). The $1B S-1 creates dilution risk. Seeking Alpha analysts have issued 'Sell' ratings noting the company is 'fading the crypto hype' with no fundamental business to fall back on [1]. For IOWN's Return on Intention framework, a company with no mission beyond token accumulation cannot score well across any dimension.
Key Catalysts
  1. HYPE token price appreciation — the company's value is entirely driven by HYPE price; protocol growth ($10B daily volume, 70% DEX perps market share) could drive token demand [2]
  2. $1B S-1 offering completion — successful capital raise would dramatically expand HYPE holdings and staking yield, though at the cost of shareholder dilution [2]
  3. Hyperliquid protocol expansion into commodities and equities trading could broaden the ecosystem and increase HYPE utility and demand [2]
Key Risks
  1. 100% concentration risk in a single cryptocurrency token — HYPE price collapse would destroy the company's entire value proposition with no alternative business model [1]
  2. Regulatory crackdown on decentralized finance or cryptocurrency treasury companies could force unwinding of positions or restrict operations [1]
  3. Structural dilution from $1B S-1 offering and potential premium-to-NAV erosion as crypto sentiment normalizes or competitors emerge [2]
