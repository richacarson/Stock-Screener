The Board's decision to hire Lores explicitly cited the need to compete more effectively in a rapidly evolving landscape.

The hire of Lores from HP suggests an attempt at a more disciplined, execution-focused approach, but the company has yet to demonstrate the existential flexibility needed to thrive against native digital competitors.

Investment Thesis
PayPal trades at a historically cheap 7.6x forward P/E with $33.2B revenue, 25.7% ROE, 15.8% profit margins, and $3.3B free cash flow — a quality business priced for permanent decline [1][6]. Venmo growing 20% to $1.7B demonstrates untapped monetization potential, and the new CEO Lores (March 2026 start) brings disciplined execution experience from HP where he delivered six consecutive quarters of revenue growth [2]. Analyst consensus target of $60.32 implies 45% upside from current levels, and if Lores can stabilize execution and articulate a clear AI commerce strategy, the multiple re-rating potential is significant.
The bear case is real: PayPal's 2026 guidance of flat transaction margin dollars and potential low single-digit EPS decline signals stagnation, not growth [1]. The 21.6% stock decline on Q4 earnings reflects the market's loss of confidence in management execution. Three CEO changes in three years is a red flag for organizational dysfunction. Apple Pay, Google Pay, and Stripe/Adyen are embedding payments deeper into their ecosystems, eroding PayPal's checkout relevance. Revenue growth of 3.7% for a company valued at $41B suggests the platform may be ex-growth in its core business, and Lores' HP background (hardware/printing) may not translate to fintech innovation [2].
