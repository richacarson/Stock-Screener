Investment Thesis
D-Wave is the quantum computing industry's first real revenue winner with 179% revenue growth, 82.6% gross margins, and Q1 2026 bookings of $32.8M+ already exceeding any prior full-year total [1]. The $884.5M cash position provides multi-year runway, and the new government business unit and Quantum Circuits acquisition expand the addressable market. At $15.73 (market cap ~$5.8B), D-Wave trades at a premium to current revenue but a reasonable multiple if the 1,500% pipeline increase converts to bookings. The company is first-to-market with commercial quantum computing at a time when enterprise demand is inflecting.
The bear case centers on the gap between hype and fundamentals: $24.6M annual revenue against a $5.8B market cap (236x revenue) with $84.5M in annual losses [1]. Quantum computing remains nascent, and competition from IBM, Google, and well-funded startups is intensifying. Revenue growth of 179% is impressive but from a tiny base, and conversion of the $32.8M in January bookings to recognized revenue is uncertain. The stock is 66% below its $46.75 52-week high. Quarterly OpEx is projected to increase 15% sequentially throughout 2026, accelerating losses before revenue can scale [1].
