Company Profile
Technology ·Computer Hardware ·NYSE ·United States
D-Wave Quantum Inc. develops and delivers quantum computing systems, software, and services. Founded in 1999, D-Wave is the only company selling commercial quantum annealing computers (Advantage and Advantage 2) and offers the Leap quantum cloud service, Ocean SDK, and hybrid quantum-classical solvers for enterprise optimization, logistics, materials simulation, and drug discovery applications.

Finite vs Infinite Game — Sinek (25%)
Mindset: FINITE Overall: 4 /10
D-Wave presents a mixed infinite game picture. On one hand, the company has persisted for 27 years in quantum computing — a field that most investors and analysts dismissed for decades — demonstrating remarkable long-term commitment. On the other hand, the company's competitive messaging tends toward 'we're the only real quantum computer company,' insider selling is persistent, and D-Wave has not demonstrated the existential flexibility or competitive humility that characterize true infinite game players [1][3][6].

Investment Thesis
D-Wave is the only publicly traded company delivering commercial quantum computers today, with FY2025 revenue surging 179% to $24.6M at 82.6% gross margins, and year-to-date 2026 bookings already exceeding $32.8M as of February [1]. The company occupies a unique position at the intersection of quantum computing and AI — its annealing technology solves optimization problems that classical approaches struggle with, making it complementary to the AI infrastructure buildout. With $884M in cash and minimal debt (D/E 5.1%), D-Wave has a multi-year runway to scale revenue toward profitability. The Inspire Impact Score of +50 (ALIGNED) and absence of negative attributions make QBTS attractive from a faith-alignment perspective [7].
The bear case is severe: at $8.4B market cap against $24.6M TTM revenue, QBTS trades at 340x trailing revenue for a company that has been unprofitable for 27 consecutive years (-$72M TTM operating cash flow) [7]. The quantum computing market remains speculative — no enterprise has demonstrated that quantum computing delivers ROI that justifies the investment at scale. Gate-model quantum competitors (IBM, Google, IonQ) are advancing rapidly and may render D-Wave's annealing approach obsolete if general-purpose quantum computing achieves practical advantage first. Consistent insider selling by the CEO ($983K in January 2026) and CFO ($189K in March) signals management may view the stock as overvalued [6]. OpEx is increasing ~15% per quarter in 2026, and if bookings fail to convert to revenue, the $884M cash position could deplete in 8-10 years at current burn rates [1][7].
Key Catalysts
  1. Year-to-date 2026 bookings of $32.8M+ already exceeding FY2025 total revenue of $24.6M, with H2 2026 expected to be stronger than H1, potentially driving a path toward profitability [1]
  2. Quantum-AI hybrid solutions gaining enterprise traction as organizations seek quantum advantages for optimization problems that classical AI cannot solve efficiently [2][3]
  3. Advantage2 processor scaling and new enterprise use cases in logistics, drug discovery, and portfolio optimization expanding the addressable market [1][3]
Key Risks
  1. Extreme valuation at 340x trailing revenue for a company with 27 years of cumulative losses — any deceleration in booking momentum could trigger severe multiple compression from the current $8.4B market cap [7]
  2. Gate-model quantum competitors (IBM, Google, IonQ) could achieve practical quantum advantage first, potentially rendering D-Wave's annealing approach a technological dead end [3]
  3. Persistent insider selling across CEO, CFO, and multiple directors despite stock appreciation suggests management views the current valuation as disconnected from near-term fundamentals [6]
