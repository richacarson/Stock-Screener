Company Profile
Technology ·Semiconductors ·NASDAQ ·United States
Qualcomm develops and commercializes foundational technologies for mobile, automotive, IoT, and edge AI computing. The company operates through QCT (chip design — Snapdragon platforms for smartphones, automotive, IoT, PCs, and data center) and QTL (patent licensing across 5G and wireless standards). Qualcomm's Snapdragon processors power the majority of premium Android smartphones and are the fastest-growing automotive chip platform, with annualized auto revenue exceeding $5B.

Investment Thesis
Qualcomm is a best-in-class semiconductor franchise delivering Infrastructure perfection (D/E 56%, ROE 36.4%, Forward P/E 18.5x, 22 consecutive years of dividend growth) while executing a transformative diversification into automotive, IoT, and data center markets [1][4]. Automotive revenue exceeded $5B annualized (record) in Q2 FY2026 with a $45B+ design win pipeline providing years of visibility, and the Alphawave IP acquisition positions QCOM for data center AI becoming a multibillion revenue stream by 2027 [1][2]. QTL licensing at ~$1.4B quarterly with 77%+ EBT margin provides a structural earnings floor that is entirely uncorrelated to QCT chip cycles. CEO Amon's vision of 2026 as 'the year of agents' positions Qualcomm's edge AI compute as essential infrastructure for the next computing paradigm, while the 2.0% dividend yield with 39% payout ratio leaves ample room for continued growth.
The bear case centers on near-term cyclical headwinds and customer concentration. Q2 FY2026 revenue of $10.6B declined 2.2% YoY with QCT down 4.2%, driven by Chinese customer weakness that management expects to bottom in Q3 FY2026 before sequential recovery [1]. Handsets still represent ~74% of QCT revenue, creating concentration risk if Apple successfully develops in-house modem chips or MediaTek gains premium share. Non-GAAP operating margin compressed to 31% from 34% YoY in Q2, and EPS declined 7% to $2.70 [1]. The stock has fallen 30% from its $259.92 52-week high to $181.92, reflecting uncertainty about the smartphone cycle and the pace of automotive/IoT diversification. While 5-year revenue CAGR of ~13.5% is strong, the TTM YoY growth of just 5.2% suggests the high-growth trajectory has decelerated, and the forward PEG (based on expected earnings growth) implies the market is not fully pricing in the diversification story [4].
Key Catalysts
  1. QCT Automotive revenue exceeding $5B annualized with $45B+ design win pipeline converting to production revenue as OEMs adopt Snapdragon Digital Chassis across more vehicle models globally [1]
  2. Data center solutions expected to become a multibillion revenue opportunity by 2027, with Alphawave IP acquisition providing connectivity IP to compete in AI inference infrastructure [1][2]
  3. Chinese handset customer recovery expected in Q4 FY2026, providing a revenue tailwind after Q3 trough, while edge AI proliferation across smartphones, PCs, and wearables drives upgraded ASPs [1][2]
Key Risks
  1. Chinese customer weakness drove Q2 FY2026 QCT revenue down 4.2% YoY — if recovery is slower than management's Q4 FY2026 timeline, revenue growth could remain subdued through FY2027
  2. Apple modem internalization — if Apple successfully develops in-house baseband modems, Qualcomm could lose its largest QCT customer (estimated 20-25% of chip revenue), though QTL licensing would continue
  3. Handset concentration at ~74% of QCT revenue creates vulnerability to smartphone market cyclicality and the gradual commoditization of mobile SoCs by MediaTek in the mid-range segment
