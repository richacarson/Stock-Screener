Investment Thesis
Qualcomm offers compelling value at 11.7x forward earnings for a company delivering record revenue of $12.3 billion in Q1 FY2026, record free cash flow of $12.8 billion annually, and 93% CEO approval. The diversification into automotive (35%+ growth), data center AI (AI200 launching 2026), and PC chips transforms QCOM from a smartphone-dependent business into a multi-market AI platform company. The 2.7% dividend yield with 20 consecutive years of growth provides income while investors benefit from the AI growth optionality. Handset leadership with Snapdragon provides a stable cash generation base funding the expansion.
Near-term headwinds include DRAM memory constraints temporarily reducing Q2 FY2026 handset revenues to ~$6 billion versus $7.8 billion in Q1. The 71% trailing payout ratio appears elevated on cyclically depressed earnings. Historical antitrust controversies and licensing disputes have drawn regulatory scrutiny that could resurface. Competition from MediaTek in mobile and NVIDIA in AI/data center is intense, and Qualcomm's data center AI entry is unproven against established incumbents. The semiconductor sector's cyclicality creates inherent earnings volatility, and Apple's in-house modem development could eventually reduce a major revenue stream.
