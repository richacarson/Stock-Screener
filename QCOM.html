Investment Thesis
QUALCOMM is the dominant AI edge computing platform company, with record Q1 FY2026 revenue of $12.3B driven by Snapdragon NPU leadership across smartphones (40+ AI devices in development), PCs (150 Snapdragon X designs), automotive ($1.1B revenue, $45B design pipeline, 35%+ Q2 growth guided), and new data center inference chips (AI200/AI250) [1]. At a forward P/E of just 11.7 with 21.5% ROE and $11.8B in cash, QUALCOMM offers deep value for a semiconductor company positioned at the center of the AI device revolution [1]. CEO Amon's vision that 'whoever has presence on the edge is going to win' is supported by 30 years of wireless technology leadership and $3.6B in quarterly shareholder returns ($2.65B buybacks + $949M dividends) [1][2]. The $45B automotive design pipeline provides multi-year revenue visibility as vehicles become AI-powered computing platforms.
The bear case centers on QUALCOMM's smartphone concentration risk — QCT handsets at $7.8B still represent 63% of QCT revenue, and Apple's in-house modem development threatens to reduce QUALCOMM's most profitable customer relationship [1]. Q2 FY2026 revenue guidance of $10.2-11.0B disappointed consensus of $11.1B, directly attributed to the global memory shortage impacting smartphone production [1]. Revenue growth of just 5% YoY and EPS decline of 1.8% reflect a mature mobile business that is growing slower than AI-diversified peers. The FTC antitrust case (reversed on appeal) and EU €242M predatory pricing fine highlight ongoing regulatory and litigation risk around QUALCOMM's licensing practices [3]. Profit margin of 12.0% and payout ratio of 71% suggest limited financial flexibility compared to peers, and persistent insider selling ($4.21M over 90 days with zero buys) signals management caution [3].

  1. Automotive revenue guided for 35%+ YoY growth in Q2 FY2026, with $45B design pipeline converting to production revenue as software-defined vehicles proliferate [1]

Key Risks
  1. Apple in-house modem development could eliminate QUALCOMM's largest single customer revenue stream, which contributes disproportionately to QCT handset profitability [1]
  2. Global memory shortage constraining smartphone production and driving Q2 FY2026 revenue guidance below consensus, with uncertain timeline for normalization [1]
  3. Antitrust and licensing risk from EU predatory pricing fine (€242M) and ongoing regulatory scrutiny of QUALCOMM's patent licensing practices across multiple jurisdictions [3]
