Company Profile
Financial Services ·Credit Services ·NASDAQ ·China
Qfin Holdings (formerly Qifu Technology / 360 Finance) operates an AI-driven credit-tech platform under the Qifu Jietiao brand in China, matching borrowers with financial institution partners for consumer and SME lending. The company provides credit assessment, fund matching, loan facilitation, and post-facilitation services. Qfin is dual-listed on NASDAQ and HKEX (3660) and uses its proprietary TRIDENT multimodal LLM for credit scoring, fraud detection, and customer service.

Investment Thesis
Qfin Holdings trades at a historically extreme valuation discount — 2.05x trailing P/E, 2.6x forward P/E, and 11.6% dividend yield — reflecting severe China and regulatory risk priced into the stock [4]. The underlying business is strong: RMB19.2B in 2025 revenue (up 11.9% YoY), RMB6.0B net income (31.2% profit margin), 24.7% ROE, and record operating cash flow of RMB11.1B [1]. The AI-native business model (TRIDENT MLLM powering credit scoring, fraud detection, and customer service) positions Qfin at the intersection of AI and fintech, with the capital-light facilitation model (49.3% of volume) reducing balance sheet risk [2]. Dividend growth of ~17% annually with a 23.9% payout ratio provides both income and growth optionality. The SEC's settlement of Gemini and dismissal of various China ADR concerns could signal easing regulatory pressure on Chinese listings.
The bear case is substantial and centers on existential regulatory and geopolitical risks. China's fintech lending crackdown now includes criminal prosecutions for exceeding the 24% interest rate cap, and Qfin has been summoned by regulators due to high volumes of customer complaints about fee practices [3]. Grizzly Research published a short report alleging 'fake financials, fraudulent backers, and an imploding business,' raising material fraud concerns typical of China Hustle allegations [3]. Parent company Qihoo 360 was sanctioned by the US Commerce Department (2020) and designated by the Pentagon as a Chinese military-linked concern (2022), creating ADR delisting risk [3]. Q4 2025 showed steep profit decline (-46.8% EPS) with elevated delinquency in legacy portfolios, and Q1 2026 guidance projects a further 51-53% YoY earnings decline [1]. Revenue declined 8.7% in the most recent period. The 2.05x P/E may be cheap, or it may accurately reflect the probability-weighted risk of regulatory destruction, fraud, or delisting.
Key Catalysts
  1. AI-driven SaaS platform for banks could open a new recurring revenue stream with higher-quality institutional customers, reducing regulatory exposure [2]
  2. Continued dividend growth (17% CAGR) with only 23.9% payout ratio from RMB11.1B operating cash flow provides shareholder return acceleration potential [1]
  3. Potential easing of US-China ADR regulatory tensions or Hong Kong dual-listing providing alternative trading venue protection against delisting risk [4]
Key Risks
  1. China fintech regulatory crackdown — criminal prosecutions for interest rate cap violations and regulatory summoning over customer complaints could fundamentally threaten the business model [3]
  2. ADR delisting risk — parent Qihoo 360's US sanctions and Pentagon military designation create existential risk for the NASDAQ listing [3]
  3. Earnings deterioration — Q4 2025 EPS fell 46.8% with Q1 2026 guidance projecting 51-53% YoY decline, suggesting the business is contracting under regulatory pressure [1]
