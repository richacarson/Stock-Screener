Company Profile
Healthcare ·Medical Devices ·NASDAQ ·United States
Quantum-Si is a life sciences company developing a proprietary single-molecule detection platform for next-generation protein sequencing (NGPS). The company offers Platinum and Platinum Pro instruments, library preparation kits, sequencing kits, and cloud-based analysis software for proteomic research, drug discovery, and diagnostics. Founded by Jonathan Rothberg in 2013, it is preparing to launch the next-generation Proteus platform in 2026.
145 Employees ·www.quantum-si.com

Investment Thesis
Quantum-Si is the pioneer in commercial next-generation protein sequencing, a potentially transformative technology for proteomics research, drug discovery, and diagnostics [2]. The Proteus platform — which completed 50+ prototype sequencing runs meeting or exceeding Platinum Pro performance — is priced at $425,000 and enters early access in summer 2026 [1][2]. With $230.5M cash providing runway to Q2 2028, the company has financial stability to execute the Proteus launch without immediate dilution risk [1]. At $167M market cap (below cash value), the market is pricing in near-zero probability of commercial success — any positive Proteus adoption data could trigger significant re-rating. The single-molecule protein sequencing platform could enable breakthroughs in precision medicine, drug target identification, and biomarker discovery that mass spectrometry cannot achieve. Founder Jonathan Rothberg's track record with Ion Torrent (acquired by Life Technologies for $725M) adds credibility to the vision [2].
The bear case is severe for a company trading below cash value with declining revenue. 2026 guidance of $1M revenue (down from $3.05M in 2024) means the company is moving backwards commercially, even if the decline is 'intentional' for Proteus positioning [1]. The 62.2% YoY revenue decline, -46.5% ROE, and zero profit margin reflect a business that has not proven product-market fit after 13 years and multiple product iterations. Proteus at $425,000 faces a challenging market environment — NIH funding uncertainty and tariffs are creating headwinds for life sciences capital equipment purchases [2]. The proteomics market itself is unproven at scale — protein sequencing may not follow the same adoption curve as DNA sequencing. Only 145 employees limits commercial reach, and the company must build sales and support infrastructure from near-zero. Five-year investors have lost 88% of their investment, suggesting the market has consistently overestimated the timeline and probability of commercial success [1]. Cash burn rate means the Q2 2028 runway leaves limited margin for Proteus launch delays.
Key Catalysts
  1. Proteus early access program launch in summer 2026 — first customer data and adoption metrics could validate the platform's commercial potential and trigger re-rating [1][2]
  2. Amino acid detection expansion to all 20 amino acids using AI and proprietary training data, significantly broadening the platform's research applications [2]
  3. Market cap ($167M) trading below cash position ($230.5M) — any positive commercial signal would close the discount and provide upside purely from sentiment shift [1]
Key Risks
  1. Proteus launch execution risk — the company has guided for only $1M in 2026 revenue, and any delays to early access would further erode investor confidence and cash runway [1]
  2. NIH funding uncertainty and tariff headwinds creating capital equipment spending freezes in life sciences — exactly the customer base Quantum-Si needs for $425,000 instrument sales [2]
  3. Unproven market — next-generation protein sequencing has not yet demonstrated the same market demand trajectory as genomic sequencing, and mass spectrometry incumbents continue to advance [2]
