Company Profile
Healthcare ·Medical Devices ·NASDAQ ·United States
Quantum-Si develops proprietary single-molecule detection technology for next-generation protein sequencing (NGPS). The company offers Platinum and Platinum Pro instruments, library preparation kits, sequencing kits, and cloud-based analysis software for proteomic research, drug discovery, and diagnostics. Founded by Jonathan Rothberg in 2013, the company is preparing to launch its next-generation Proteus platform by end of 2026, which has detected 17 of 20 amino acids in prototype testing.
145 Employees ·www.quantum-si.com

Investment Thesis
Quantum-Si is the pioneer in commercial single-molecule protein sequencing, a potentially transformative technology for proteomics research, drug discovery, and diagnostics [2]. The Proteus platform has detected 17 of 20 amino acids in prototype testing and targets commercial launch by end of 2026 at a $425,000 list price [1]. With $190.4M cash providing runway to 2028, the company has financial stability to execute the Proteus launch without immediate dilution. At $201M market cap — roughly equal to cash — the market assigns near-zero value to the technology and pipeline, meaning any positive Proteus adoption data could trigger significant re-rating. The NVIDIA collaboration for AI-accelerated data processing adds credibility, and founder Rothberg's track record with Ion Torrent (acquired for $725M) provides pedigree in life sciences platform development [2].
The bear case is formidable for a company that has consumed over $500M in capital with $1M in annual revenue. Q1 2026 revenue of $258K and full-year guidance of $1M (down from $3.05M in FY2024) means the company is moving backwards commercially, even if the decline is 'intentional' for the Proteus transition [1]. The -56% ROE, -5,600% profit margin, and $93M annual cash burn paint a picture of a company that has not proven product-market fit after 13 years. Proteus at $425,000 faces a challenging capital equipment environment with NIH funding uncertainty and tariff headwinds depressing life sciences instrument spending. The proteomics market itself is unproven at scale — protein sequencing may never follow the same adoption curve as DNA sequencing. Only 145 employees limits commercial reach, and the stock has declined 70%+ from its 52-week high of $3.10 to $0.92, with five-year investors having lost approximately 90% of their investment [3].
Key Catalysts
  1. Proteus commercial launch by end of 2026 — detection of 18+ amino acids and first customer installations could validate the platform and trigger re-rating from the current near-cash-value valuation
  2. Amino acid detection expansion to all 20 amino acids during 2026 using AI and NVIDIA collaboration, significantly broadening the platform's research applications and competitive moat
  3. Market cap ($201M) roughly equal to cash position ($190M) — any positive commercial signal would close the discount and provide upside from sentiment shift alone
Key Risks
  1. Proteus launch execution risk — any delay past end-of-2026 would further erode investor confidence and compress the cash runway that extends only to 2028 at $93M annual burn rate
  2. NIH funding uncertainty and tariff headwinds creating capital equipment spending freezes in life sciences — exactly the customer base needed for $425,000 instrument sales
  3. Unproven market — next-generation protein sequencing has not demonstrated market demand comparable to genomic sequencing, and mass spectrometry incumbents (Thermo Fisher, Bruker) continue advancing competitive approaches

AI Resilience (25%)
AI Resilience LOW RISK
8
AI Risk Protection (7/9): Quantum-Si operates at the intersection of semiconductor engineering, molecular biology, and precision instrumentation — developing physical hardware with proprietary chip-based single-molecule photon detection that AI cannot replicate or substitute [2]. The competitive moat is in the physical science of detecting individual protein molecules on a semiconductor chip, not in software. AI is an enabler — the NVIDIA collaboration and AI-powered analysis software strengthen rather than threaten the platform. Regulatory requirements for medical device instrumentation add additional protection. The primary AI-adjacent risk is computational protein prediction (AlphaFold-style models) potentially reducing some experimental sequencing demand, but single-molecule data provides unique information that computational models cannot generate. AI Opportunity (1/1): The company actively generates value from AI through its cloud-based analysis software (automated AI-driven data interpretation) and NVIDIA collaboration for accelerated protein sequencing data processing [2].
