Investment Thesis
Quantum Computing Inc. represents a highly speculative bet on the quantum-photonic computing thesis: that thin-film lithium niobate integrated circuits can deliver practical quantum advantage for AI optimization problems sooner and at lower cost than superconducting or trapped-ion alternatives. The Q4 2025 EPS beat (-$0.01 vs. -$0.04 expected) and 83% revenue growth (albeit from a tiny base) suggest the core technology is advancing. The government and defense market focus (DoD customers implied) provides contracted revenue potential [1].
The bear case is severe: $682K in annual revenue supporting a $1.57B market cap implies a Price/Sales ratio of ~2,300x — one of the most extreme valuations in the market. The company has 41 employees, negative ROE of -85%, losses accelerating in absolute dollar terms (-$18.67M in 2025), and insiders are selling aggressively. The path from photonic chip development to commercial quantum computing revenue at meaningful scale could take 5–10+ years, during which time dilutive capital raises would continue. Average analyst target of $17 implies significant upside, but the 'Strong Sell' signal from QuantumScape's comparable analysis of pre-revenue quantum companies should be heeded [1].
Key Catalysts
  1. First commercial EQC deployment at scale — a documented government or enterprise customer using the EQC for production workloads would validate the technology thesis and likely trigger significant re-rating
  2. Revenue milestone of $1M+ in a single quarter would signal the company is transitioning from prototype to product
  3. Strategic partnership or licensing deal with a hyperscaler (AWS, Microsoft Azure Quantum) would provide both capital and commercial validation
Key Risks
  1. Capital risk: with 41 employees and mounting losses, a dilutive equity raise could be required within 12–18 months, potentially at terms that significantly impair current shareholders
  2. Technology risk: superconducting (IBM, Google) and trapped-ion (IonQ) quantum architectures are better-funded; a breakthrough by either could make photonic approaches obsolete or irrelevant
  3. Insider selling: the 6,100% increase in insider selling activity in the past month is an unusually strong negative signal about management's conviction in near-term value creation
