Company Profile
Technology ·Computer Hardware ·NASDAQ ·United States
Quantum Computing Inc. is an integrated photonics company providing quantum machines to commercial and government markets. The company develops thin-film lithium niobate chips for optical devices, reservoir computing systems (Emucore, Neurawave), and quantum photonics platforms for telecommunications, finance, AI, defense, and environmental applications.

Investment Thesis
Quantum Computing Inc. is a speculative early-stage bet on quantum photonics hardware, with Q1 2026 revenue of $3.7M driven almost entirely by acquisitions (organic revenue was just $24K) and a $1.4B cash war chest from dilutive fundraising [1][7]. Under new CEO Dr. Yuping Huang (20+ years in quantum physics, $40M in government research funding), the company is pivoting toward industrial-scale manufacturing of lithium niobate photonics chips for AI, telecom, and defense applications.
This is a high-risk, high-reward speculation with no clear path to profitability — the company has 72 employees, loses money every quarter, has conducted massive share dilution ($750M placement), and faces a shareholder governance investigation [7][8]. The quantum computing sector remains largely pre-commercial, and QCI must prove its photonics approach can compete with better-funded rivals (IBM, Google, IonQ). Suitable only for investors with high risk tolerance willing to bet on quantum technology maturation over a 5-10 year horizon.
Key Catalysts
  1. Successful integration of Lumina Semiconductor and NuCrypt acquisitions driving organic revenue growth beyond the Q1 2026 organic figure of just $24K [1]
  2. Government contract wins leveraging Dr. Huang's $40M research portfolio and relationships with DARPA, NSF, DoD, and NASA for defense and sensing applications [4]
  3. Quantum photonics technology validation through commercial customer deployments in telecom, finance, or cybersecurity demonstrating real-world product-market fit
Key Risks
  1. Massive shareholder dilution: $750M private placement issued ~37M new shares, and the company is seeking approval for additional share issuance, creating ongoing dilution risk for existing holders [7]
  2. Pre-revenue fundamentals: organic revenue of just $24K in Q1 2026 with $4.1M net loss demonstrates no commercial traction beyond acquired businesses; company may never achieve profitability [1][3]
  3. Governance and insider selling concerns: shareholder investigation by Purcell & Lefkowitz into fiduciary duty breaches, combined with insider selling activity, raises questions about management alignment with shareholders [8]
