Company Profile
Technology ·Computer Hardware ·NASDAQ ·United States
Quantum Computing Inc. is an integrated photonics company providing quantum machines and thin-film lithium niobate foundry services. Products include thin-film lithium niobate chips for electro-optical modulators, the Entropy Quantum Computer (EQC) for optimization problems, Emucore reservoir computing machines, Neurawave photonic reservoir computers, quantum LiDAR, and quantum authentication products. Acquired Luminar Semiconductor in February 2026. Formerly Innovative Beverage Group Holdings. Founded 2001, based in Hoboken, New Jersey.
72 Employees ·www.quantumcomputinginc.com

Investment Thesis
QCi represents a speculative bet on quantum photonics commercialization. The bull case rests on the Luminar Semiconductor acquisition (February 2026) transforming QCi from an R&D company into an integrated photonics manufacturer with Fab 1 operational and Fab 2 planned for volume production [1][2]. Q1 2026 revenue of $3.7M (up from $39K) demonstrates the acquisition's immediate impact, and the 2026 revenue forecast of $22.8M implies continued ramp. The balance sheet is fortress-strong — $737.9M cash plus $782.5M investments against only $5.4M debt — providing years of runway at current burn rates [3]. Dr. Yuping Huang's DARPA/NSF/DoD research background (~$40M in government quantum funding) brings credibility to the technology roadmap.
The bear case is severe. Organic revenue (excluding Luminar) was only $24K in Q1 2026, meaning the core quantum computing business generates virtually zero commercial revenue after 25 years of existence [1]. At a $2.3B market cap on $4.3M TTM revenue, the stock trades at >500x sales — pricing in success that is far from assured. The company has undergone three CEO transitions in two years, a securities class action lawsuit, and delinquent SEC filings from officers, raising governance concerns [2][3]. The quantum computing industry remains pre-commercial with uncertain timelines — QCi is competing against massively better-funded players (IBM, Google, Microsoft, Amazon) with 72 employees. The previous identity as 'Innovative Beverage Group Holdings' underscores the speculative nature of the enterprise.
Key Catalysts
  1. Fab 1 scaling production of thin-film lithium niobate chips with meaningful order backlog, validating the integrated photonics manufacturing strategy and driving revenue toward the $22.8M 2026 forecast
  2. Government or defense contract awards leveraging Dr. Huang's DARPA/NSF/DoD relationships, providing high-visibility revenue and validating QCi's quantum technology for national security applications
  3. Successful demonstration of the Entropy Quantum Computer (EQC) solving real-world optimization problems for commercial customers, establishing product-market fit beyond foundry services
Key Risks
  1. Organic quantum computing revenue remains negligible ($24K in Q1 2026 ex-Luminar) after 25 years, suggesting the core technology may never achieve commercial scale despite the photonics pivot
  2. At >500x price-to-sales with a $2.3B market cap, the stock is priced for transformational success — any execution delay, failed product demonstration, or market sentiment shift could trigger severe multiple compression
  3. Governance instability including three CEO changes in two years, a securities class action lawsuit, and delinquent SEC filings from officers creates uncertainty about management execution capability
