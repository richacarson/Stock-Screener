Investment Thesis
uniQure is a gene therapy pioneer with the only FDA-approved AAV gene therapy for hemophilia B (HEMGENIX), demonstrating five-year durability data with ~90% bleeding rate reduction [1]. The company holds $622.5M cash providing runway into H2 2029, de-risking the AMT-130 (Huntington's) and AMT-191 (Fabry) pipeline development. Over 75 patients treated globally validates the real-world clinical and commercial pathway [1].
The bear case is significant: HEMGENIX revenue of just $16.1M in 2025 against a $199M net loss shows the approved product is not yet commercially successful at scale [1]. Gene therapy commercialization faces pricing pressure, payer resistance, and a small addressable hemophilia B patient population. D/E of 269.8% and deeply negative ROE indicate the company remains dependent on capital markets despite having an approved product. Pipeline risk is high — AMT-130 for Huntington's is in a highly competitive and historically difficult therapeutic area [1].
Key Catalysts
  1. AMT-130 Huntington's disease clinical data readouts could transform the company's pipeline value and addressable market [1]
  2. HEMGENIX commercial acceleration through expanded access in additional countries and growing physician adoption [1]
  3. Gene therapy regulatory and manufacturing advances reducing production costs and improving patient access [1]
Key Risks
  1. HEMGENIX commercial underperformance — $16.1M revenue suggests slow adoption despite FDA approval, risking cash burn acceleration [1]
  2. Pipeline failure risk — AMT-130 for Huntington's disease is in a historically difficult therapeutic area with high clinical failure rates [1]
  3. Capital markets dependence — despite $622.5M cash, continued losses of ~$200M/year may require additional dilutive financing [1]
