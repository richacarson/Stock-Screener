Company Profile
Technology ·Electronic Components ·NYSE ·United States
Ralliant Corporation is a precision instruments and engineered products company spun off from Fortive in 2025. It operates through two segments: Test and Measurement (Tektronix oscilloscopes, Keithley source measure units, EA Elektro-Automatik power supplies) and Sensors and Safety Systems (Qualitrol grid monitoring, Gems Sensors, Pacific Scientific energetic materials for defense and space).

Investment Thesis
Ralliant is a newly independent precision instrumentation company with strong brand franchises (Tektronix, Keithley, Qualitrol) trading at 17.2x forward earnings with $327M in annual free cash flow [1][3]. The company's three growth vectors — grid modernization, defense technologies, and electrification — align with secular megatrends, and AI hardware development is expanding demand for precision test equipment. Gross margins of 50.3% and a path to 18-20% adjusted EBITDA margins in 2026 reflect quality franchise economics. The $1.4B goodwill impairment has depressed GAAP metrics but is non-cash, and adjusted EPS guidance of $2.22-$2.42 implies the underlying business is profitable [1].
The bear case centers on Ralliant's lack of growth. Revenue has been essentially flat for three years at ~$2.1B, and 2026 guidance of 2-6% organic growth is modest for a company valued at 17x forward earnings [1]. The EA Elektro-Automatik acquisition appears to have been significantly overpaid given the $1.4B impairment, raising questions about capital allocation judgment. As a newly independent company, Ralliant lacks a track record of standalone execution, and the post-spin cost structure includes a 250bps headwind from higher operating expenses. The dividend yield of 0.43% is negligible, providing no income support for patient investors. Competition from Keysight Technologies (stronger AI integration, larger scale) and Rohde & Schwarz (private, long-term oriented) creates ongoing market share pressure in the core T&M segment [2][3].
Key Catalysts
  1. Tektronix 7 Series DPO and MP5000 modular test platform gaining traction in semiconductor and AI hardware validation markets, potentially accelerating T&M segment growth beyond the 2-6% guidance range [2]
  2. Grid modernization spending acceleration as utilities invest in monitoring infrastructure (Qualitrol), potentially driving Sensors segment growth above corporate average [2]
  3. Post-spin cost optimization and Ralliant Business System deployment could expand adjusted EBITDA margins beyond the 18-20% guidance, demonstrating standalone operational leverage [1]
Key Risks
  1. Persistent flat organic growth — three years of ~$2.1B revenue suggests the core business may be mature rather than a growth platform, making the 17x forward multiple hard to justify [1]
  2. Capital allocation risk — the EA Elektro-Automatik $1.4B impairment raises concerns about management's ability to deploy capital effectively, and future M&A could destroy value [1]
  3. Semiconductor cycle downturn could pressure T&M segment demand, as Tektronix and Keithley revenues are correlated with chip development and manufacturing capital spending [2]
