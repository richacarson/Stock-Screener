Company Profile
Industrials ·Specialty Business Services ·NYSE ·Canada
RB Global (formerly Ritchie Bros. Auctioneers) is the world's largest commercial asset marketplace operator, running live and online auctions, private sales, and insurance salvage auctions for heavy equipment, vehicles, and industrial assets. The 2023 merger with IAA created a combined platform spanning construction equipment (Ritchie Bros.) and insurance auto salvage (IAA), serving buyers and sellers across 15+ countries.

Investment Thesis
RB Global is the world's largest commercial asset marketplace with a dominant market position validated by 50+ years of operations and the transformative 2023 IAA merger [1]. FY2025 revenue of $4.59B (up 7.2%) with operating cash flow of $978M and FCF of $786M demonstrates strong cash generation. At PEG 0.87 and 22x forward P/E with forward EPS of $4.85 (vs trailing $2.04), the market is pricing significant earnings expansion as IAA integration synergies materialize [4]. 2026 guidance of 5-8% GTV growth and $1.47-1.53B adjusted EBIT shows continued execution. The buyer-seller network effects, 22-year dividend streak, and physical infrastructure create durable competitive advantages. The heavy equipment cycle is positioned for recovery driven by infrastructure spending and construction activity [1].
The bear case centers on integration execution and margin compression. Trailing P/E of 52.3x reflects depressed current earnings — if the forward EPS jump to $4.85 does not materialize, the stock is significantly overvalued [4]. D/E of 72.9% reflects the IAA acquisition debt load ($4.4B), and any earnings disappointment would increase leverage concerns. ROE of 7.3% is below the 15% target, weighed down by acquisition goodwill. Profit margin of 9.3% remains below potential, suggesting IAA integration synergies are still work-in-progress. Full-year construction equipment GTV declined 2% in 2025, raising questions about organic growth momentum [1]. The company faces cyclical risk — used equipment values and transaction volumes decline during economic downturns, though the insurance salvage business (IAA) provides countercyclical diversification. Management turnover (CEO change in 2023, leadership restructuring in 2025) creates organizational distraction during a critical integration period [2].
Key Catalysts
  1. IAA integration synergy realization — forward EPS of $4.85 vs trailing $2.04 implies 138% earnings growth, driven by cost synergies, cross-selling, and operational efficiency improvements [4]
  2. 2026 guidance of 5-8% GTV growth and $1.47-1.53B adjusted EBIT provides a clear execution path with potential for upside from infrastructure spending tailwinds [1]
  3. Construction equipment cycle recovery — after full-year GTV decline of 2% in 2025, Q4 rebound (GTV +9%) suggests improving fundamentals heading into 2026 [1]
Key Risks
  1. IAA integration execution — failure to achieve expected synergies would leave the stock trading at an unjustifiable 52x trailing P/E with $4.4B in acquisition debt [4]
  2. Cyclical exposure — economic downturn would compress used equipment values and auction volumes, pressuring both revenue and GTV in the core Ritchie Bros. business [1]
  3. ROE compression from acquisition goodwill — 7.3% ROE may not recover to peer levels, suggesting the IAA merger premium was too high relative to the value created [4]
