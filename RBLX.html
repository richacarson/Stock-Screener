Company Profile
Communication Services ·Electronic Gaming & Multimedia ·NYSE ·United States
Roblox Corporation operates an immersive platform for connection and communication globally. The platform enables users to explore millions of user-generated 3D experiences and allows developers to build, publish, and monetize games using Roblox Studio. With 132 million daily active users as of Q1 2026, Roblox is the largest user-generated content gaming platform in the world, increasingly positioning itself as a social media and advertising platform beyond traditional gaming.

Investment Thesis
Roblox is the dominant user-generated content gaming platform with 132 million daily active users (+35% YoY) and exceptional revenue growth of 39% YoY to $1.4B in Q1 2026 [1]. The platform's network effects — where more users attract more creators and more content — create a durable competitive moat that no competitor has replicated at scale. Roblox's AI strategy is a key differentiator: the Vector AI advertising platform is driving double-digit sequential ad revenue growth, while generative AI creation tools are expanding the developer base and lowering barriers to content creation [2]. Free cash flow of $1.2B demonstrates underlying economic value despite GAAP losses. With management guiding 20-25% revenue growth for 2026, the company is positioned to eventually achieve profitability as operating leverage improves [1]. The Inspire Insight score of -81 (MISALIGNED) reflects serious concerns around exploitation and sexually explicit content exposure, aligning with the six-state attorney general investigations [3].
The bear case centers on persistent unprofitability, extreme leverage, child safety risks, and a demanding valuation. After 22 years of operation, Roblox has never generated a GAAP profit — net losses totaled $1.07B in FY2025, with profit margins of -20.7% [4]. D/E of 435% reflects $1.79B in debt against negative equity, creating financial fragility if growth decelerates. Child safety controversies are the most material risk: six state attorneys general have launched investigations, and the Inspire Insight score of -81 with 'Exploitation' and 'Sexually Explicit' negative attributions reflects deep concern about the platform's impact on minors [3]. Management reduced 2026 bookings guidance to 8-12% (from 20%+) partly due to safety-driven changes that hurt user acquisition, demonstrating the real economic cost of addressing these issues [1]. The PEG ratio of 8.18 suggests the market prices in substantial growth that may not materialize if safety friction persists. At a $36.9B market cap with no path to near-term profitability, downside risk is significant.
