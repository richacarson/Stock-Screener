Company Profile
Communication Services ·Electronic Gaming & Multimedia ·NYSE ·United States
Roblox Corporation operates a global platform enabling users to create, share, and experience 3D immersive content. With 144 million daily active users (69% YoY growth), the platform serves as both a gaming and social experience primarily for children and young adults. The company monetizes through its virtual currency Robux and creator marketplace.

Investment Thesis
Roblox is building the dominant user-generated content platform with extraordinary growth metrics: 144M DAUs (+69% YoY), $4.89B revenue (+43% YoY), bookings growing 55%, and the 18+ cohort expanding 50%+ with 40% higher monetization [1]. The company's 2026 guidance of 22-26% bookings growth and $2.0-2.17B in adjusted EBITDA suggests accelerating monetization and path toward cash flow generation. International expansion is inflecting — APAC grew 96% YoY with Indonesia up 700% — providing a long runway as the platform penetrates developing markets with younger demographics. At $38B market cap on $4.89B revenue, the valuation is premium but potentially justified by the platform's network effects and growth trajectory.
The bear case is dominated by the child safety crisis: 132 consolidated federal lawsuits, suits from multiple state attorneys general, the LA County lawsuit, Brazil classifying Roblox as 18+ due to exploitative microtransactions, and bans on school computers in Argentina [3]. Settlement liability could reach hundreds of millions, and regulatory actions in the EU (Dutch DSA investigation), Philippines, and Latin America could restrict operations in key growth markets. Financially, the D/E of 480.7%, GAAP losses (-21.8% profit margin), and $1.13B annual stock-based compensation mean true profitability is distant despite strong bookings growth [1][2]. Morningstar's assessment that 'shares look far too rich' and the company's admission that margin expansion will be non-linear create downside risk at current valuations.
Key Catalysts
  1. Successful 18+ market expansion reaching 50%+ DAU growth with 40% higher ARPDAU, diversifying revenue away from the under-13 demographic and associated regulatory risk [1]
  2. Achieving 2026 adjusted EBITDA guidance of $2.0-2.17B, demonstrating operating leverage and a credible path toward GAAP profitability
  3. Resolution of child safety litigation through comprehensive settlements and regulatory compliance that remove the legal overhang and restore platform reputation
Key Risks
  1. Child safety litigation exposure — 132 federal MDL cases plus multiple state AG suits could result in billions in settlements, regulatory fines, and potentially platform-altering design mandates [3]
  2. Regulatory restrictions in key growth markets — Brazil's 18+ classification, Dutch DSA investigation, Philippine enforcement actions, and Argentine school bans could limit access to the youngest and fastest-growing user cohorts [3]
  3. Persistent GAAP unprofitability — $1.13B annual SBC, -21.8% profit margins, and D/E of 480.7% mean Roblox is economically losing money despite strong bookings growth, creating valuation risk if growth decelerates [1][2]
