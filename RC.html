Investment Thesis
Ready Capital trades at $1.55, an 82% discount to book value of $8.79/share, which could represent deep value if the underlying CRE loan portfolio proves to be worth more than the market implies [4]. The company has taken aggressive write-downs ($284M in Q4 2024, plus $150M additional reserves in Q4 2025) that may have over-reserved, and the $494M portfolio sale demonstrates willingness to crystallize losses and move forward [3]. If CRE credit conditions stabilize and the remaining performing portfolio generates positive returns, there is significant upside from current levels. The 2.58% dividend yield on a reduced $0.04/year basis provides minimal but continuing income.
The bear case is overwhelming. Ready Capital is in acute financial distress with negative revenue (-$24M in 2025), D/E of 361%, 27% of loans on nonaccrual, and a Q4 2025 GAAP loss of $1.46/share [3]. The stock has declined from $12+ to $1.55, destroying 87%+ of shareholder value. A securities fraud class action alleges management concealed the severity of CRE loan deterioration, creating legal liability and reputational damage [1]. The dividend has been cut 97% from $1.20/year to $0.04/year, effectively eliminating the income proposition. The external management structure through Waterfall Asset Management means management fees continue even as shareholder value evaporates. With $5.96B in debt against $215M in cash, refinancing risk is severe — a failed debt rollover could trigger bankruptcy. The 82% discount to book value reflects the market's belief that book value itself is overstated given the credit quality of the remaining portfolio.
Key Catalysts
  1. CRE credit cycle stabilization — if commercial real estate values bottom and nonperforming loans cure or are resolved at better-than-reserved levels, book value could recover from the current $8.79/share [3]
  2. Portfolio deleveraging completion — successful resolution of the $600M held-for-sale portfolio and remaining nonperforming assets would reduce balance sheet risk and rebuild investor confidence [3]
  3. Securities fraud lawsuit resolution — dismissal or favorable settlement of the class action would remove legal overhang and reduce the stock's risk premium [1]
Key Risks
  1. Continued CRE deterioration — 27% of loans on nonaccrual could worsen if commercial real estate values decline further, requiring additional write-downs beyond the $434M already taken [3]
  2. Liquidity and refinancing risk — $5.96B in total debt with $215M cash creates acute refinancing risk; failure to roll over maturing obligations could force distressed asset sales or bankruptcy [4]
  3. Securities fraud class action — ongoing litigation alleging concealment of CRE loan deterioration creates legal liability and management distraction, with potential for material damages [1]
