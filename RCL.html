Investment Thesis
Royal Caribbean is the best-operated cruise company in a structurally under-supplied vacation market, posting FY2025 revenue of $17.94B (up 8.8%) and guiding FY2026 to double-digit revenue growth, 14% adjusted EPS growth to $17.70-$18.10, and 6.7% capacity additions [1]. AI is a genuine competitive moat — 90% of RCL's 15M daily price points are AI-driven, and AI-powered food forecasting cut ship waste by 50%, both translating directly to yield and margin [2]. Icon-class ships (Icon, Star of the Seas) command premium pricing, and private destinations (Perfect Day CocoCay, Perfect Day Mexico) capture onboard spend that peers cannot match. At 15.6x forward EPS with 48.5% ROE and a reinstated dividend, RCL offers growth-at-a-reasonable-price with a strengthening balance sheet [4].
The bear case centers on macro and leverage. Debt-to-equity of 212% means RCL is still capital-structure sensitive to any demand shock — a recession or geopolitical event that curtails cruising would pressure free cash flow and could force another dividend pause [4]. Fuel price spikes, port access (especially new Mexican private destination dependence), and environmental regulation on shipping emissions are real industry risks. Cybersecurity of ship-board IT/OT systems is a growing exposure as AI deployment expands [2]. The 2026 guide assumes yields rise only 2.1-4.1% — any softer pricing would flow directly to EPS. Finally, the Inspire score is slightly negative (-1) due to onboard gambling operations.
Key Catalysts
  1. FY2026 execution on double-digit revenue growth and 14% adjusted EPS growth ($17.70-$18.10) with 6.7% capacity additions and 2.1-4.1% net yield expansion [1]
  2. Continued AI-driven yield management and waste reduction (90% of 15M daily price points AI-optimized; 50% food waste cut) that lifts operating margins structurally [2]
  3. Perfect Day Mexico private destination opening and Icon-class ship rollouts, driving onboard revenue per guest higher while lowering port fees [1]
Key Risks
  1. Debt-to-equity of 212% keeps RCL leveraged to demand; a recession or travel shock could force a dividend pause similar to 2020 [4]
  2. Fuel, environmental regulation, and Destination Net Zero 2050 capex commitments create multi-decade cost pressure in a high-carbon industry [3]
  3. Cybersecurity exposure on AI-heavy cruise ships — CEO Liberty himself has flagged data/privacy risks as the company manages 90%+ AI-driven pricing [2]
