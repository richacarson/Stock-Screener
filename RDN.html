Investment Thesis
Radian Group trades at a deeply discounted 6.6x forward P/E with a PEG ratio of 0.76, offering compelling value for a mortgage insurer generating 48.7% profit margins and 92.3% gross margins [5]. The transformative Inigo acquisition is expected to double annual revenues and deliver mid-teens returns on $1.7B deployed, diversifying Radian beyond cyclical mortgage insurance into specialty insurance and reinsurance [2]. Full-year 2025 earnings of $582.6M on $1.20B revenue demonstrate the profitability of the core business, while Q4 2025 EPS of $1.16 beat consensus by 5.45% [1]. The 2.87% dividend yield with only a 23.2% payout ratio and 5 consecutive years of growth provides income with substantial room for increases. At $4.85B market cap, Radian is attractively valued if the Inigo integration succeeds.
The bear case centers on Inigo integration risk and mortgage insurance cyclicality. The $1.7B Inigo acquisition carries significant execution risk — doubling revenues through an acquisition in specialty insurance, a market Radian has limited experience in, could strain management bandwidth and capital [2]. Mortgage insurance is inherently cyclical, tied to housing market health and interest rate dynamics; a housing downturn would stress loss reserves. Cultural concerns are notable: 4 rounds of layoffs in 3.5 years while reporting record profits suggest potential talent retention issues during a critical integration period [3]. The negative free cash flow of -$581M reflects the Inigo capital deployment but raises near-term liquidity questions. Rising interest rates could slow mortgage origination volumes, compressing new insurance written. The beta of 0.77 understates tail risk — mortgage insurers face asymmetric downside in housing stress scenarios similar to 2008 [5].
