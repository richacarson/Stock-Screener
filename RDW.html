Company Profile
Industrials ·Aerospace & Defense ·NYSE ·United States
Redwire Corporation provides critical space solutions and infrastructure for government and commercial customers in the United States and Europe, operating through Space and Defense Tech segments. Products include sensors, avionics, solar arrays (ELSA), camera systems, satellite platforms, in-space manufacturing, and autonomous UAS technology. Founded in 2010, headquartered in Jacksonville, Florida.

Investment Thesis
Redwire is positioned at the intersection of two secular tailwinds: Space Force budget expansion (from $28.8B to $39.9B in FY2026) and the $151B Golden Dome national defense initiative [1]. Q1 2026 delivered $97M revenue (+58% YoY) with gross margins jumping to 26.6% from 14.7% (+11.9pp), demonstrating the transition from development to production programs. Record backlog of $498.1M with a 1.92x Book-to-Bill ratio provides strong revenue visibility, and 2026 guidance of $450-500M (41.6% growth at midpoint) positions Redwire as one of the fastest-growing defense/space companies. The ALIGNED Inspire score (54) with zero negative attributions and 20 positive attributions reflects strong values alignment. At $11.56 with a $2.3B market cap, the stock offers significant upside if profitability improves as production programs scale.
The bear case centers on persistent and significant losses: Q1 2026 net loss of $76.5M (though $44M+ was non-recurring items including a $42.5M noncash Edge Autonomy vesting charge), full-year 2025 net loss of $226.6M, and negative free cash flow of $71.3M in Q1 [1][4]. Profit margin of -80.9% and ROE of -48.7% are deeply negative — even with the gross margin improvement, EBITDA breakeven remains aspirational rather than imminent. The high beta of 2.42 reflects extreme stock volatility, and the analyst target range has wide dispersion reflecting uncertainty about the profitability timeline. Sustainalytics rates Redwire's ESG management as 'Weak,' creating governance risk [3]. Defense programs face ongoing government shutdown risks, contract execution delays, and potential budget sequestration. Dilution risk is elevated given the company may need additional capital raises to fund operations while awaiting production-scale profitability.
Key Catalysts
  1. Transition from development to production programs driving gross margin expansion — Q1 2026 gross margin jumped 11.9pp to 26.6%, with continued improvement expected as SDA Tracking Layer and other programs enter production phase [1]
  2. Space Force FY2026 budget of $39.9B (up 39% from FY2025) with SHIELD, Golden Dome, and SDA contracts flowing to Redwire, providing a multi-year revenue tailwind for the $498M backlog [1]
  3. Edge Autonomy integration expanding the defense tech segment with autonomous UAS capabilities, CAESAR AI partnership at Stanford advancing autonomous space systems — positioning Redwire at the convergence of space and autonomous technology [2]
Key Risks
  1. Persistent net losses ($76.5M in Q1 2026, $226.6M in full-year 2025) and negative free cash flow with no clear GAAP profitability timeline, creating potential dilution risk from future capital raises [1][4]
  2. Government shutdown risk and defense budget uncertainty could delay contract execution and revenue recognition, as experienced in 2025 when government disruptions impacted quarterly results
  3. High beta (2.42) and $2.3B market cap create significant stock price volatility, with the stock having ranged from $4.87 to $22.25 in the past 52 weeks — a 357% spread reflecting extreme investor uncertainty [4]
