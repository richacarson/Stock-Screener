The Real Brokerage is a real estate technology company operating in the U.S. and Canada with 33,000+ agents. The company combines a cloud-based brokerage model with integrated mortgage, title, and financial technology services. Investment Thesis
The Real Brokerage is one of the fastest-growing real estate technology companies in North America, scaling from $382M revenue (2022) to nearly $2B in FY2025 (56% YoY growth) while growing agent count to 33,000+ (31% YoY) [1]. The company's cloud-native, zero-office model generates positive operating cash flow of $65.9M and adjusted EBITDA of $62.9M on a debt-free balance sheet, demonstrating unit economics that scale [1][5]. AI integration is a genuine competitive advantage: Leo handles all agent support calls, automated marketing creates content at scale, and the technology platform enables 435 employees to manage 33,000+ agents [2][3]. Management expects 2026 organic growth to meaningfully outpace the industry, and integrated mortgage and title services provide margin expansion runway [1].
The bear case centers on profitability, AI disruption risk, and market cyclicality. Despite nearly $2B in revenue, Real remains GAAP-unprofitable with -0.4% profit margin and -19.3% ROE, raising questions about whether agent acquisition economics can ever generate sustainable profits [5]. The NAR commission settlement and AI-powered consumer tools (Zillow, Redfin) threaten to compress brokerage commissions industry-wide, potentially undermining the revenue pool Real draws from. At $2.70 per share ($573M market cap), the stock trades at a significant discount to peers, potentially reflecting market skepticism about the path to profitability. Agent churn risk is real — independent contractors can leave at any time, and the low-cost model attracts price-sensitive agents who may switch to the next lowest-cost alternative. Housing market cyclicality adds macro risk: any downturn in transaction volumes would pressure revenue, and the company's near-breakeven economics leave no margin of safety [5].
Key Catalysts
  1. Path to GAAP profitability — operating cash flow is already positive at $65.9M, and incremental margin on 44%+ revenue growth should drive positive net income within 2026-2027 [1][5]
  2. AI-powered Leo concierge expansion from agent support to client-facing features, potentially reducing customer acquisition costs and differentiating Real's agent value proposition [3]
  3. Integrated mortgage and title services scaling to capture more transaction economics, increasing revenue per agent beyond commission splits [1]
Key Risks
  1. Commission compression risk — NAR settlement and AI-powered consumer tools could structurally reduce brokerage commission rates, shrinking the revenue pool Real depends on for growth [2]
  2. Agent retention uncertainty — 33,000+ independent contractor agents have no switching costs and could leave for competitors offering better economics or technology [1]
  3. Housing market cyclicality — any downturn in residential transaction volumes would pressure revenue, and the -0.4% profit margin leaves zero cushion for revenue declines [5]
