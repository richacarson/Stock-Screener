Company Profile
Energy ·Oil & Gas E&P ·NYSE American ·United States
Ring Energy is a small-cap independent oil and natural gas company focused on acquisition, exploration, development, and production of conventional assets in the Permian Basin. The company operates approximately 83,550 net developed acres and 12,684 net undeveloped acres across West Texas and New Mexico. Founded in 2004 and headquartered in The Woodlands, Texas.

Investment Thesis
Ring Energy offers deep value exposure to Permian Basin oil production at $1.40 per share with a forward P/E of 7.8x, backed by 153.3 MMBoe in proved reserves (up 14% YoY) and 25 consecutive quarters of cash flow positivity [1]. The company generated record adjusted free cash flow of $50.1M in 2025, paid down $40M in debt since the Lime Rock acquisition, and has 48% of 2026 oil production hedged at $65.21/bbl for downside protection [1]. Conservative D/E of 50.6%, a reaffirmed $585M borrowing base, and $166M total liquidity provide adequate balance sheet cushion. If oil prices recover above $70/bbl, Ring Energy's low-cost conventional operations could generate significant earnings leverage from the current depressed base.
The bear case is severe. Ring Energy reported a net loss of $34.7M in 2025 driven by $108.8M in ceiling test impairments, and revenue declined 20% YoY as realized oil prices fell roughly 18% [1]. The company's micro-cap status ($293M market cap), 111-person workforce, and $1.40 stock price create liquidity and delisting risks. Debt of $423M against $293M market cap means equity holders are significantly subordinated to creditors. The 2026 'flat production plan' offers no growth catalyst — it simply maintains output while hoping oil prices cooperate [1]. Ring Energy has no competitive moat: it operates conventional Permian Basin assets that larger, better-capitalized E&P companies could develop more efficiently. Negative free cash flow of -$47M on a GAAP basis and continued impairment risk if WTI stays below $60 could further erode the equity cushion. The stock is a leveraged bet on oil prices with no margin of safety.
Key Catalysts
  1. Oil price recovery above $70/bbl WTI would drive significant earnings leverage given 48% hedged production at $65.21 and low-cost conventional operations at $10.02/Boe LOE [1]
  2. Debt reduction trajectory — $40M paid down since Lime Rock closing with continued deleveraging targeted in 2026, reducing the 2.2x leverage ratio toward investment-grade levels [1]
  3. New zone discoveries and increased horizontal mix to 85% in 2026 drilling program could unlock higher-return development opportunities within existing acreage [1]
Key Risks
  1. Oil price decline below $55/bbl WTI would trigger additional ceiling test impairments, accelerate net losses, and potentially breach borrowing base covenants on the $420M credit facility [1]
  2. Micro-cap liquidity risk — $293M market cap, $1.40 share price, and $423M debt create significant equity subordination and potential delisting vulnerability if operational performance deteriorates [4]
  3. No growth catalyst — flat 2026 production guidance of ~20,150 Boe/d with a $115M maintenance capital program offers no upside absent commodity price recovery [1]
