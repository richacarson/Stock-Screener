Company Profile
Technology ·Software - Infrastructure ·NASDAQ ·United States
Remitly is a leading digital financial services provider for immigrants and their families, enabling cross-border remittances through its mobile app. The platform facilitates money transfers from 17 sending countries to over 170 receiving countries, processing $74.9B in annual send volume. Founded in 2011 and based in Seattle, the company went public in 2021.

Investment Thesis
Remitly offers a compelling growth-at-a-reasonable-price opportunity — 29% revenue growth to $1.635B, first full year of GAAP profitability ($67.9M), and $74.9B send volume (up 37%), all trading at just 10.8x forward earnings [1][4]. The 2026 guidance of $1.94-1.96B revenue and $340-360M adjusted EBITDA signals the business is scaling profitably. The regulatory moat (money transmission licenses across 17 sending countries, banking relationships in 170+ receiving countries) creates barriers that AI or crypto competitors cannot easily overcome. AI-powered fraud detection and virtual support have measurably improved unit economics. An investor's $21M stock purchase following earnings signals institutional conviction in the growth story [1]. With D/E of 22% and $325M operating cash flow, the balance sheet is clean and cash generative.
The bear case centers on margin sustainability, competitive pressure, and leadership transition risk. Profit margin of 4.2% is razor-thin and could turn negative if competitive pricing pressure from Wise, Western Union Digital, or crypto-native competitors compresses take rates [4]. The CEO transition from co-founder Matt Oppenheimer to Sebastian Gunningham in February 2026 introduces execution uncertainty at a critical scaling moment — new CEOs at high-growth companies frequently reset expectations. Revenue growth is decelerating from 29% (2025) to 19-20% (2026 guidance), and the market may re-rate the valuation multiple downward as the company transitions from hypergrowth to sustained growth. Glassdoor reviews citing frequent layoffs, micromanagement, and cultural challenges suggest organizational growing pains that could impair talent retention during the competitive AI talent market [2]. The 52x trailing P/E (vs 10.8x forward) reflects how dependent the valuation is on forward earnings projections materializing.
Key Catalysts
  1. Forward P/E of 10.8x for a 25%+ revenue growth company represents significant undervaluation — re-rating to fintech peer multiples of 20-25x forward would drive 80-130% upside [4]
  2. 2026 adjusted EBITDA guidance of $340-360M (18-19% margin) signals the business model is achieving operating leverage, with potential for significant margin expansion as the platform scales [1]
  3. Expansion beyond core remittance into broader financial services for immigrants creates a larger addressable market and higher lifetime customer value [1]
Key Risks
  1. CEO transition from co-founder Matt Oppenheimer to Sebastian Gunningham introduces execution uncertainty during a critical growth-to-profitability inflection, with potential for strategy reset or lowered guidance [1]
  2. Competitive pricing pressure from Wise (lower fees), Western Union Digital (brand trust), and crypto-native solutions could compress Remitly's take rate on the $74.9B send volume [2]
  3. Revenue growth deceleration from 29% to 19-20% guidance risks multiple compression if the market re-rates RELY as a mature fintech rather than a hypergrowth story [1]

AI Resilience LOW RISK

AI Risk Protection (6/9): Remitly's core business — cross-border money transfers — is protected by significant regulatory moats (money transmission licenses in 17 sending countries), banking relationships in 170+ receiving countries, and physical-world cash-out infrastructure that AI cannot replicate [2]. The concern that stablecoins or AI-powered fintech could drive remittance costs to zero is overblown — converting stablecoins to fiat still incurs overhead costs, and a small team cannot obtain the required licenses from every country needed to run a remittance business. However, AI does enhance competitors' ability to reduce transaction costs and improve fraud detection, creating competitive pressure on margins. Remitly's own AI deployment (fraud ML, virtual support assistant) positions it well to use AI as a defensive moat rather than being disrupted by it [2].
