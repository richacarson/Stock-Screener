Company Profile
Healthcare ·Biotechnology ·NASDAQ ·United States
Replimune Group, Inc. is a clinical-stage biotechnology company developing oncolytic immunotherapies to treat cancer. Its proprietary platform uses selectively replicating viruses to activate the immune system against tumors. Lead candidate RP1 is an HSV-1-based therapy for advanced melanoma, while RP2 and RP3 target hepatocellular carcinoma and biliary tract cancer. Founded in 2015 and headquartered in Woburn, Massachusetts.
479 Employees ·www.replimune.com

Investment Thesis
Replimune is a clinical-stage oncolytic immunotherapy company in severe distress following the FDA's second Complete Response Letter rejecting the RP1 BLA for advanced melanoma on April 10, 2026, which triggered a 75% stock decline to $2.42 [1][2]. The FDA determined that clinical data were 'insufficient to conclude substantial evidence of effectiveness,' and CEO Patel acknowledged that RP1 development is 'not viable' without accelerated approval, leading to layoffs and scaled-back U.S. manufacturing operations [2]. With $269M cash (as of December 2025) and quarterly burn of ~$70M, the company has runway only to early 2027 [1]. The remaining pipeline includes RP2 in Phase 2 for hepatocellular carcinoma (data expected late 2026) and biliary tract cancer, but these are early-stage programs that cannot substitute for the RP1 franchise value that has been destroyed.
The speculative bull case rests on RP2 pipeline success or a strategic transaction. The oncolytic immunotherapy platform retains scientific merit — engineering viruses to stimulate anti-tumor immunity is a differentiated approach, and RP2's combination with atezolizumab and bevacizumab in liver cancer addresses a large unmet need [1]. At $200M market cap with $269M in cash, the stock technically trades below cash value, though ongoing burn and potential restructuring costs diminish this margin of safety. A takeout by a larger oncology company seeking the viral vector platform is possible but speculative. The most likely scenarios are: (1) RP2 data readout in late 2026 provides a positive catalyst that revalues the platform, (2) the company pursues a strategic transaction or reverse merger, or (3) cash depletion forces a dilutive financing or wind-down by early 2027. The risk/reward is binary and unsuitable for conservative portfolios [3].
Key Catalysts
  1. RP2 Phase 2 data readout in hepatocellular carcinoma expected by end of 2026 — positive results could validate the oncolytic immunotherapy platform and restore investor confidence [1]
  2. Trading below cash value ($200M market cap vs $269M cash) creates potential floor and makes the company an acquisition target for larger oncology companies seeking viral vector technology [3]
  3. Potential strategic transaction, partnership, or reverse merger that leverages the proprietary oncolytic immunotherapy platform and remaining cash [1]
Key Risks
  1. Cash runway expiring in early 2027 with ~$70M quarterly burn — without RP2 success or a strategic transaction, dilutive financing or wind-down becomes likely [1]
  2. Second FDA rejection of lead candidate RP1 effectively kills the melanoma franchise and CEO acknowledged development is 'not viable' without accelerated approval [2]
  3. Layoffs and manufacturing scale-backs signal organizational contraction that could impair ability to execute remaining pipeline programs and retain key scientific talent [2]
