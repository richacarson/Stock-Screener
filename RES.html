Company Profile
Energy ·Oil & Gas Equipment & Services ·NYSE ·United States
RPC, Inc. provides specialized oilfield services and equipment (pressure pumping, coiled tubing, downhole tools, cementing, wireline, nitrogen, fishing services) primarily to independent and major oil and gas exploration and production companies in the U.S. The company was spun off from Rollins, Inc. in 1984 and remains majority-controlled by the Rollins family.

Investment Thesis
RPC is a small-cap, Rollins-family-controlled U.S. oilfield services provider that uses a conservative balance sheet and cycle-tested operational discipline to survive oil price volatility [2][3]. The April 2025 Pintail Completions acquisition expanded the wireline/completions platform and drove FY2025 revenue up 15% to $1.626B and Q4 adjusted EBITDA up 34% to $65.6M with margins improving 90 bps to 15.6% [1]. The $0.16 annualized dividend yields ~2.3% backed by historically low debt, and management remains candid about cyclical headwinds [1][7]. At ~$6.90/share and roughly 18x forward earnings on depressed numbers, RES is a patient cycle call for investors comfortable with small-cap oilfield exposure and family-controlled governance.
The bear case is structural and cyclical. U.S. land frac activity remains soft, Q4 2025 swung to a net loss of $3.1M, and management expects continued challenging conditions from oil price volatility, budget exhaustion, and pricing competition [1]. RPC lacks AI/digital differentiation versus Halliburton and Liberty Energy, which are deploying reservoir and completions optimization platforms. Rollins-family majority control limits board independence and the ability of minority holders to force capital allocation change [2]. A wealth manager fully exited the position in early April 2026, signaling institutional skepticism [5]. The Inspire score of +45 is favorable for faith-aligned investors but does not offset cyclical EPS risk. Without a sustained recovery in U.S. completion activity or a bolder strategic pivot, the stock is range-bound near the mid-single digits.
Key Catalysts
  1. Sustained U.S. frac spread recovery in 2026 driving pricing power and adjusted EBITDA margin expansion beyond Q4 2025's 15.6% [1]
  2. Further integration and synergy capture from the Pintail Completions acquisition supporting wireline and completions revenue [1]
  3. Continued dividend payment and potential special dividend given conservative balance sheet and Rollins-family capital-return culture [7]
Key Risks
  1. Oil price volatility and soft U.S. frac demand extending the cyclical trough — Q4 2025 already slipped to a net loss [1]
  2. Competitive pressure from Halliburton, Liberty Energy, and ProPetro, which have scale and digital/AI completions advantages RPC lacks [3]
  3. Rollins-family majority control limits minority shareholder influence over capital allocation and strategy [2]
