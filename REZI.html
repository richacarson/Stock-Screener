Company Profile
Industrials ·Industrial Distribution ·NYSE ·United States
Resideo Technologies, Inc. is a global provider of smart home and building technology solutions, spun off from Honeywell in 2018. The company operates in two segments: Products & Solutions (P&S), which develops thermostats, security and fire safety products, and connected home devices under the Honeywell Home brand; and ADI Global Distribution, a leading wholesale distributor of low-voltage security, fire, AV, and networking products. Headquartered in Scottsdale, Arizona, Resideo reported record FY2025 revenue of $7.47 billion and plans to spin off ADI as an independent public company in H2 2026.

Investment Thesis
Resideo is at an inflection point: record FY2025 revenue of $7.47B with adjusted EBITDA of $833M (up 20% YoY), stronger-than-expected 2026 guidance of $7.80-7.90B revenue and EPS of $3.00-3.20 (vs. $2.73 consensus), and the transformative ADI spin-off planned for H2 2026 that will unlock value by creating two focused public companies [1][3]. The $1.59B Honeywell payment eliminated a perpetual liability, the forward P/E of 13x is attractive relative to peers, and AI integration into the ProSeries platform positions Resideo for growth in the expanding smart home market.
The bear case centers on leverage and execution risk: D/E of 121% reflects the debt burden from the Honeywell payment and ADI acquisition, ROE and profit margin are GAAP-negative (though temporarily distorted), and the ADI spin-off introduces execution complexity and potential dissynergies [2]. Revenue growth of only 2% on a trailing basis (though guided higher) is modest for a company trading at growth multiples, and the ADI distribution business operates at thin 7.5% EBITDA margins that could face pricing pressure from larger competitors. If the spin-off stumbles or macro weakness depresses residential construction activity, the stock's recovery thesis could stall.
Key Catalysts
  1. ADI spin-off completion in H2 2026 expected to unlock sum-of-the-parts value — ADI alone generated $4.5B net revenue with 7.5% EBITDA margins, while P&S delivered $2.6B at 24.2% margins
  2. 2026 adjusted EBITDA guidance of $935M-985M represents 12-18% growth, supported by AI-enhanced product launches and cross-selling across the ProSeries security platform
  3. Elimination of the Honeywell indemnification through the $1.59B payment removes an overhang that depressed the stock and improves long-term free cash flow generation
Key Risks
  1. Elevated D/E ratio of 121% from the Honeywell payment and ADI acquisition limits financial flexibility and increases interest expense, requiring disciplined deleveraging through H2 2026
  2. ADI spin-off execution risk — separation of a $4.5B distribution business involves complex IT, supply chain, and organizational untangling that could distract management during a critical growth period
  3. Residential housing and construction market sensitivity — approximately 50% of Resideo's revenue is tied to home improvement and new construction activity that could slow in a rising rate environment
