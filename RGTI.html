Company Profile
Technology ·Computer Hardware ·NASDAQ ·United States
Rigetti Computing builds and operates quantum computers and superconducting quantum processors. The company provides quantum processing units (QPUs) and quantum computing as a service (QCaaS) through cloud platforms. Its product lineup includes the 108-qubit Cepheus-1-108Q modular system, the 84-qubit Ankaa-3, and the Novera 9-qubit QPU for on-premises research, alongside Rigetti Foundry Services for custom quantum chip fabrication.

Investment Thesis
Rigetti Computing is a pure-play quantum computing company with the 108-qubit Cepheus-1-108Q — one of the most powerful commercially available gate-based quantum computers — and a vertically integrated model spanning chip fabrication through cloud delivery. Q1 2026 revenue grew 193% YoY to $4.4 million with $569 million in cash and zero debt providing multi-year runway [1][5]. The company's Rigetti Foundry Services and Novera QPU product lines create revenue diversification across government, academic, and enterprise customers.
However, the investment case requires exceptional patience and conviction in quantum computing's commercial timeline. Revenue of $4.4 million quarterly supports a $6.8 billion market cap only on the basis of long-term optionality — the stock trades at over 380x annualized revenue. Management projects quantum advantage in 3-5 years and fault-tolerant computing in 7-9 years, meaning meaningful commercial returns are at least half a decade away [3]. The $26 million quarterly operating loss will deplete the $569 million cash position within roughly 5 years without additional capital raises, creating dilution risk. Competition from IBM, Google, and IonQ with far greater resources is intense. For IOWN's framework, the strong AI Resilience and innovation scores are offset by fundamentally pre-revenue infrastructure metrics.
Key Catalysts
  1. Achievement of quantum advantage within the 3-5 year timeline management projects would validate the technology and could drive a step-change in enterprise adoption and revenue [3]
  2. Expansion of Novera QPU deliveries and Foundry Services to government defense and national laboratory customers, where $4.4 million Q1 revenue showed 193% YoY growth momentum [1]
  3. Modular chiplet architecture enabling scaling beyond 108 qubits could establish Rigetti's approach as the industry standard for building larger quantum systems [1]
Key Risks
  1. Extreme valuation risk — $6.8 billion market cap on $4.4 million quarterly revenue (380x+ annualized) leaves no margin for execution delays or competitive setbacks
  2. Classical AI advances could narrow quantum computing's addressable market by solving optimization and simulation problems before quantum systems reach commercial viability [3]
  3. Cash burn of approximately $26 million per quarter will require additional capital raises within 5 years, creating dilution risk for existing shareholders at a time when quantum's commercial timeline remains uncertain [5]

AI Resilience (25%)
AI Resilience LOW RISK
7
AI Risk Protection (6/9): Quantum computing is structurally protected from AI disruption because it operates on fundamentally different computational principles — quantum mechanics rather than classical neural networks. AI cannot substitute for quantum computing's core value proposition in areas like molecular simulation, optimization, and cryptography. Rigetti's physical hardware (superconducting quantum processors) requires deep materials science and fabrication expertise that AI cannot replicate [2]. However, the timeline to commercial quantum advantage (3-5 years per management) creates risk that classical AI advances could solve some of the same problems quantum computing targets, potentially narrowing the addressable market [3]. AI Opportunity (1/1): Rigetti generates quantifiable revenue from quantum computing systems that are increasingly positioned as complementary to AI workloads, with researchers exploring quantum-classical hybrid algorithms for machine learning and optimization [1].
