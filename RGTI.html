Company Profile
Technology ·Computer Hardware ·NASDAQ ·United States
Rigetti Computing builds and operates quantum computers and superconducting quantum processors. The company designs and manufactures its chips in-house at Fab-1, the industry's first dedicated quantum device manufacturing facility. It offers the 108-qubit Cepheus-1-108Q system, Novera 9-qubit systems, and cloud-based quantum computing access through Rigetti Quantum Cloud Services and Amazon Braket.
162 Employees ·www.rigetti.com

Investment Thesis
Rigetti offers pure-play exposure to superconducting quantum computing with proprietary in-house fabrication at Fab-1, a concrete roadmap to 150+ qubits (end 2026) and 1,000+ qubits (end 2027), and $590M in cash providing multi-year runway through the pre-commercial phase [1]. The $8.4M C-DAC India order for a 108-qubit system and $5.7M in Novera system orders demonstrate growing international demand for quantum hardware. The 108-qubit Cepheus system is now generally available through Rigetti QCS and Amazon Braket, expanding commercial reach [1]. At $18.94 (market cap ~$6.3B), the stock offers significant quantum computing optionality if the technology achieves commercial viability in the 2028-2030 timeframe.
The bear case is stark: $7.1M annual revenue has declined three consecutive years (from $13.1M in 2022), and Q1 2026 actual revenue of $1.87M missed the $4.13M estimate, suggesting commercial execution is weakening even as quantum hype intensifies [1][4]. The $6.3B market cap at 888x trailing revenue requires enormous faith in a technology whose commercial timeline remains deeply uncertain. Competitors IBM (1,000+ qubits already), Google (Willow processor), and well-funded startups like PsiQuantum represent significant competitive threats from organizations with vastly greater resources. Rigetti's 162-person team faces a resource mismatch against tech giants investing billions in quantum computing. Quantum computing's practical commercial viability may be 5-10+ years away, and the company will need to continue raising capital to survive, creating dilution risk for existing shareholders.
Key Catalysts
  1. 150+ qubit system deployment with 99.7% gate fidelity by end of 2026 would validate Rigetti's superconducting technology roadmap and narrow the gap with IBM's qubit count
  2. Additional commercial system orders beyond C-DAC India, particularly from hyperscalers or government defense agencies, would demonstrate broadening demand and reduce revenue concentration risk
  3. Demonstration of practical quantum advantage on a real-world computational problem would fundamentally de-risk the entire quantum computing investment thesis
Key Risks
  1. 888x trailing revenue valuation creates extreme downside risk if quantum computing sentiment shifts or the Q1 2026 revenue miss of $1.87M vs $4.13M estimate signals continued commercial weakness
  2. Revenue declined 17.9% YoY and has fallen three consecutive years despite growing quantum computing hype, suggesting a disconnect between market narrative and actual customer demand
  3. IBM's 1,000+ qubit systems, Google's Willow processor, and well-funded competitors (PsiQuantum, IonQ) could render Rigetti's smaller-scale 108-qubit systems competitively irrelevant
