Company Profile
Consumer Cyclical ·Specialty Retail ·NYSE ·United States
RH operates as a luxury retailer and lifestyle brand in the home furnishings market across the United States, Canada, the United Kingdom, Germany, Belgium, and Spain. The company sells furniture, lighting, textiles, bathware, decor, and outdoor furnishings through immersive Design Galleries, hospitality venues (including guesthouses and restaurants), websites, and sourcebooks. Founded in 1980 and headquartered in Corte Madera, California.

Investment Thesis
RH is a uniquely positioned luxury lifestyle brand trading at 13.6x forward earnings with a PEG ratio of 0.67, reflecting deep market skepticism about its recovery trajectory [4]. Gary Friedman's transformation from mall-based Restoration Hardware to immersive Design Galleries with integrated hospitality is one of retail's most ambitious reinventions, creating a category with no direct competitors [1][2]. FY2026 revenue of $3.44B grew 3.7% as the housing recovery begins, with management guiding 4-8% growth in FY2027 accelerating to 10-12% in FY2028, targeting $5.4B-$5.8B by 2030 [1]. Gross margins of 44.1% confirm luxury pricing power, and the path from 14-16% EBITDA margin in 2026 to 25-28% by 2030 implies significant earnings upside. Free cash flow of $302M and projected $300-$400M in 2026 supports debt service. At $130 per share, the stock is pricing in almost none of the long-term margin expansion story [1].
The bear case is dominated by balance sheet risk and key-person dependence. D/E of 6,550% with $3.97B debt against $41M cash creates existential fragility — a sustained revenue decline could trigger a debt spiral [4]. Profit margin of 3.6% leaves almost no cushion for execution errors. Revenue has declined from a $3.59B peak in FY2023 and the 5-year CAGR is approximately -2%, raising questions about whether the housing recovery will materialize as guided [1]. The executive team's 'D-' Comparably rating and Friedman's acknowledged tendency to lash out at subordinates signal organizational dysfunction that could hinder the margin expansion story [2]. Friedman is irreplaceable — his departure would likely cause a severe repricing. International expansion (UK, Germany, Belgium, Spain) adds execution risk in unfamiliar luxury markets. Tariff impacts and supply chain disruption could further pressure margins in the near term [1].
Key Catalysts
  1. Revenue growth acceleration from 4-8% in FY2027 to 10-12% in FY2028 as housing market recovery strengthens and European galleries mature, supporting the path to $5.4B-$5.8B by 2030 [1]
  2. EBITDA margin expansion from 14-16% in 2026 toward 25-28% by 2030 would drive dramatic earnings growth — at 25% EBITDA margin on $5B+ revenue, RH would generate $1.25B+ EBITDA vs. current ~$500M [1]
  3. New gallery openings in European capitals and continued U.S. gallery refreshes driving brand elevation and higher revenue per square foot [1]
Key Risks
  1. Extreme leverage with D/E of 6,550% and $3.97B debt against $41M cash — a sustained revenue decline or rate increase could trigger covenant issues or force dilutive equity raises [4]
  2. Key-person risk: Gary Friedman IS the brand, vision, and strategy — his departure (voluntary or involuntary) would likely cause a 30%+ stock decline given the 'D-' executive team rating [2]
  3. Near-term revenue headwinds including Q1 FY2027 guidance of -2% to -4% revenue decline, tariff impacts on imported furnishings, and supply chain disruptions that could delay the recovery narrative [1]
