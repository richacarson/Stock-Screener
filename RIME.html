Company Profile
Technology ·Consumer Electronics ·NASDAQ ·United States
Algorhythm Holdings is an AI technology and consumer electronics holding company. Its core business is SemiCab, an AI-powered logistics platform that optimizes freight movement using AI/ML to enable fully loaded round trips and reduce empty trucking miles. Formerly The Singing Machine Company, the firm renamed in September 2024 following its acquisition of SemiCab in July 2024. Incorporated in 1982 and headquartered in Fort Lauderdale, Florida.

Investment Thesis
Algorhythm Holdings represents a speculative micro-cap AI logistics play with a compelling transformation narrative but severe financial risk. The SemiCab platform — using AI/ML to optimize freight routing and eliminate empty trucking miles — has attracted blue-chip enterprise customers including Procter & Gamble, Unilever, and Coca-Cola India [1][4]. ARR surpassed $12M in April 2026, growing more than 20% from $10M in December 2025, and Q1 FY2026 revenue hit a record $2.4M, up 71% sequentially and 1,851% YoY [1][2]. The radical pivot from a declining karaoke machine company to an AI logistics platform demonstrates extraordinary existential flexibility, and the Apex SaaS platform could open significant U.S. and European markets.
However, the financial profile is deeply distressed and presents existential risk. Gross margins are negative at -29.8%, with cost of sales exceeding revenue in Q1 2026 [1]. The net loss was $5.4M in Q1 alone, D/E stands at 372.4%, ROE is -400.8%, and management has flagged going concern risk [3][5]. The company relies on Streeterville Capital convertible note financing, which carries severe dilution risk for existing shareholders. The $9.6M market capitalization and sub-dollar stock price ($0.62) place RIME in penny stock territory with inherent liquidity risk — daily dollar volume of approximately $420,000 fails the $1M liquidity floor [5]. While the 1,851% YoY revenue growth is eye-catching, it comes from a near-zero base and has not yet translated into positive unit economics. The company must demonstrate a path to gross margin profitability and reduce its dependence on dilutive financing before the investment thesis becomes viable.
Key Catalysts
  1. Apex SaaS platform launch in U.S. and European markets could dramatically expand SemiCab's addressable market beyond India-focused operations, potentially accelerating ARR growth beyond the current $12M run rate [2][4]
  2. Continued ARR growth above $12M with 20%+ quarterly increases, particularly if gross margins inflect positive as the platform scales and fixed costs are absorbed across a larger revenue base [1][2]
  3. Additional blue-chip enterprise customer wins beyond P&G, Unilever, and Coca-Cola India would validate the AI logistics platform and could attract strategic partnership or acquisition interest from larger logistics technology players [1][4]
Key Risks

  2. Negative gross margins (-29.8%) indicate the company loses money on every dollar of revenue before any operating expenses, and there is no demonstrated path to unit economics profitability at current scale [1][5]
  3. Sub-$1M daily dollar volume ($420K) creates severe liquidity risk — institutional investors cannot build meaningful positions, and any selling pressure could cause outsized price declines in this $9.6M market cap stock [5]
