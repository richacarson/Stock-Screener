Company Profile

Riot Platforms is a Bitcoin mining and digital infrastructure company with 36.5 EH/s of deployed hash rate and approximately 2 gigawatts of power capacity across its Texas facilities. Investment Thesis
Riot holds 18,005 BTC ($1.6B at current prices) plus operational cash, against a market cap of approximately $1.8B — the Bitcoin treasury alone covers most of the market cap, meaning investors are getting the power infrastructure and AI data center business nearly for free [1]. Starboard's estimate that the data center business alone could be worth $23–53/share versus the current ~$9.50 price implies dramatic undervaluation if the AI pivot succeeds [2]. The AMD lease generating revenue in January 2026 validates commercial demand for Riot's power infrastructure at premium AI/HPC pricing.
The bear case centers on Bitcoin price volatility: mining cost of $49,645/BTC in 2025 means any sustained Bitcoin price below $60K destroys mining profitability and forces additional BTC liquidation, while $579M in convertible notes add pressure [1]. The data center pivot is real but early — the AMD lease is one customer, and scaling to fill 2 GW requires years of development, capital, and commercial execution. Starboard's activist pressure could force management into suboptimal short-term decisions (asset sales, buybacks) rather than patient infrastructure development. Bitcoin regulatory risk (potential exchange-traded product restrictions, mining energy regulations) remains an overhang.
Key Catalysts
  1. Additional AI/HPC data center lease signings: the AMD partnership is a proof-of-concept — each additional hyperscaler or AI company signing a power lease validates the 2 GW portfolio value and moves Riot toward Starboard's $23–53 data center valuation [2]
  2. Bitcoin price recovery above $100K: Riot holds 18,005 BTC — a $20K/BTC price increase adds $360M to the treasury value, creating significant balance sheet optionality [1]
  3. 2 GW power infrastructure monetization announcement: a comprehensive plan to develop or lease the remaining power capacity for data centers with specific capacity targets and lease rates would provide the earnings visibility that justifies a significant multiple re-rating
Key Risks
  1. Bitcoin price sustained below $60K: mining cost of $49,645/BTC in 2025 means a $55K Bitcoin price barely covers cash costs — additional BTC treasury sales would accelerate dilution of the primary asset backing the current market cap [1]
  2. Starboard activist pressure forcing premature asset sale: Riot's power infrastructure has maximum value as a long-term AI data center platform — a forced sale at current depressed valuations could destroy the long-term value that patient shareholders are waiting for [2]
  3. Data center development timeline: scaling from one AMD lease to filling 2 GW of data center capacity requires 3-5 years of permitting, construction, and commercial contracting — capital markets may not provide patient funding at current Bitcoin-linked volatility levels
