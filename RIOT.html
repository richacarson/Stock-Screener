Company Profile
Financial Services ·Capital Markets ·NASDAQ ·United States
Riot Platforms, Inc. is one of the largest publicly traded Bitcoin mining companies in the United States, operating through Bitcoin Mining and Engineering segments. The company operates large-scale data center facilities including the Corsicana site in Texas, and designs and manufactures power distribution equipment. Riot is evaluating conversion of its mining infrastructure for AI/HPC (high-performance computing) data center uses, with new board members and a Chief Data Center Officer appointed to lead this strategic pivot.

Investment Thesis
Riot Platforms delivered Q1 2026 revenue of $167M beating the $122M consensus estimate, with TTM revenue of $653M up 42% YoY driven by expanded hash rate capacity and higher Bitcoin prices [1]. The company's most compelling asset is its massive power infrastructure — including the Corsicana facility with over 1 GW of planned capacity — which could potentially be converted for AI/HPC data center use, creating an optionality play on AI infrastructure demand [3]. The appointment of Jonathan Gibbs as Chief Data Center Officer with $17B in infrastructure experience and board restructuring with Starboard Value and D.E. Shaw involvement signals serious institutional commitment to the AI pivot. At $10.4B market cap, investors are paying for both Bitcoin mining operations and AI/HPC optionality.
The bear case is severe. Riot posted a $500M net loss in Q1 2026 alone despite revenue beating estimates, with -132.8% profit margin and -32.5% ROE reflecting a fundamentally unprofitable business model at current Bitcoin prices [1][6]. Revenue is entirely dependent on Bitcoin price, which is beyond management's control and highly volatile. The AI/HPC conversion is aspirational — the company itself disclaims any assurance that its assets are suitable for conversion or that partnerships can be negotiated on favorable terms [3]. Environmental controversy is significant: Greenpeace has called Riot's Corsicana facility 'the most energy intensive Bitcoin mine in the U.S.,' and the company's dismissive response to New York Times reporting on emissions undermines credibility [5]. With no dividends, no profits, and a strategy in transition, Riot offers commodity Bitcoin exposure with high dilution risk and uncertain execution on the AI pivot.
Key Catalysts
  1. Successful AI/HPC data center conversion announcement at Corsicana with a named hyperscaler or cloud partner, potentially unlocking significant infrastructure value beyond Bitcoin mining
  2. Bitcoin price appreciation above $100K sustaining profitable mining economics and generating positive free cash flow from the core mining business
  3. Realization of activist-driven operational improvements from Starboard Value and D.E. Shaw board influence, potentially including asset sales, cost restructuring, or strategic transactions
Key Risks
  1. Persistent unprofitability with $500M Q1 2026 net loss — any Bitcoin price decline below mining breakeven levels would accelerate cash burn and potentially require dilutive capital raises
  2. AI/HPC conversion is aspirational with no executed deals — company explicitly disclaims assurance of suitability or favorable terms, and execution failure would eliminate the growth narrative [3]
  3. ESG and environmental opposition from Greenpeace, regulatory scrutiny of energy consumption, and community resistance in Texas create reputational and regulatory headwinds for facility expansion [5]
