Company Profile
Industrials ·Aerospace & Defense ·NASDAQ ·United States
Rocket Lab Corporation is an end-to-end space company providing launch services (Electron small rocket, Neutron medium-lift rocket in development), satellite manufacturing, and space systems components including solar panels, reaction wheels, star trackers, and separation systems. The company serves commercial, government, and defense customers globally.

Investment Thesis
Rocket Lab posted record Q1 2026 revenue of $200.3M (+63.5% YoY), surpassing all guidance metrics including revenue, margins, and adjusted EBITDA [1]. The $2.2B backlog (+108% YoY) provides multi-quarter revenue visibility, and the company sold more launches in Q1 than in all of 2025. Space Systems is scaling rapidly at $136.7M quarterly revenue (+57%), creating a diversified revenue base beyond launch alone. With $1.38B cash, minimal debt (D/E 6.1%), and Q2 guidance of $225-240M (another record), Rocket Lab is on a clear trajectory toward profitability as it scales. The Neutron medium-lift rocket represents a transformative growth catalyst that could unlock the large launch market [1][2][7].
The bear case is valuation versus fundamentals — at $61B market cap, RKLB trades at ~90x annualized Q1 revenue for a company with -26.9% profit margins and -$215M TTM free cash flow [7]. Neutron development carries significant execution risk with multiple timeline slips, and first launch revenue remains speculative. Heavy insider selling ($103M by CFO, $5.7M by COO, $1.3M by CEO in Q1 2026 alone) raises questions about management's view of current valuation [6]. The company competes in a market where SpaceX has demonstrated dominant pricing power, and any Neutron delays or failures could severely damage investor confidence. With no profitability path clearly defined, RKLB requires sustained faith in the growth narrative at a premium valuation [1][7].
Key Catalysts
  1. Q2 2026 guidance of $225-240M revenue with 33-35% GAAP gross margins, representing another sequential record and progress toward profitability inflection [1]
  2. Neutron medium-lift rocket first launch targeting 2026 — success would unlock a $10B+ addressable market for constellation deployment and defense missions [2]
  3. Motiv acquisition adding Mars-proven robotics capability, expanding Rocket Lab's addressable market into in-space servicing and assembly [1]
Key Risks
  1. Valuation at ~90x annualized revenue and 18,260x forward earnings assumes flawless execution — any Neutron delay, launch failure, or revenue miss could trigger severe multiple compression [7]
  2. Heavy insider selling ($110M+ in Q1 2026 across CEO, CFO, and COO) signals potential management concern about current valuation levels relative to fundamentals [6]
  3. Pre-profitability burn rate of -$215M TTM free cash flow creates dilution risk if Neutron development costs exceed projections and the company needs additional capital raises [7]

AI Resilience (25%)
AI Resilience LOW RISK
8
AI Risk Protection (7/9): Rocket Lab's core business — physically launching rockets and building satellites — is structurally immune to AI disruption. No AI model can replace the engineering, manufacturing, and physical infrastructure required to reach orbit [3]. The $2.2B backlog is for tangible hardware and launch services that require real-world execution. Government and defense contracts (a growing segment) add regulatory moats. However, RKLB faces indirect exposure if AI efficiency gains reduce the number of satellites needed for certain constellations, or if autonomous manufacturing advances enable new competitors [3]. AI Opportunity (1/1): Rocket Lab is positioned to benefit from AI-driven demand for space infrastructure — AI data centers need satellite communications, Earth observation AI models need satellite imagery, and defense AI systems require space-based assets.
