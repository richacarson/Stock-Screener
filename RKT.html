Company Profile
Financial Services ·Mortgage Finance ·NYSE ·United States
Rocket Companies is America's largest mortgage lender, operating Rocket Mortgage, Amrock (title insurance), Rocket Homes, and Rocket Money. Following its 2024 acquisition of Mr. Cooper Group for $9.4B, Rocket services approximately $1.4 trillion in mortgage loans. Q1 2026 revenue reached $2.94B with adjusted net income of $422M.

Investment Thesis
Rocket Companies is executing a transformative strategy to become the dominant end-to-end mortgage platform, combining the nation's largest origination channel with the largest servicing portfolio following the Mr. Cooper acquisition. Q1 2026 results validated this approach with $2.94B revenue (+127% YoY), $422M adjusted net income, and $738M adjusted EBITDA, driven by AI-powered efficiency gains and cross-selling across the integrated platform [1][2].
However, profitability remains structurally thin at 2.7% net margins, ROE is depressed at 1.7% during the integration phase, and the mortgage industry remains highly cyclical and rate-sensitive. The stock trades at an attractive 13.6x forward earnings, pricing in execution risk on the Mr. Cooper integration and continued rate uncertainty. A WATCH recommendation at 54 reflects strong top-line momentum and AI-driven efficiency gains offset by unproven profitability at scale, low ROE, and cyclical exposure [3].
Key Catalysts
  1. Full integration of Mr. Cooper's $1.4T servicing portfolio driving recurring revenue and cross-sell opportunities through 2026-2027 [1][2]
  2. AI-driven underwriting and client matching automation expanding margins — Q2 2026 guidance of $2.7-2.9B adjusted revenue signals continued momentum [1]
  3. Potential Fed rate cuts in H2 2026 reigniting refinance volumes and origination demand across the integrated platform [3]
Key Risks
  1. Mortgage market remains rate-sensitive — sustained high rates could compress origination volumes and delay the refinance wave thesis [3]
  2. Mr. Cooper integration execution risk with $9.4B price tag and cultural alignment challenges across combined workforce [2]
  3. Thin 2.7% profit margin and 1.7% ROE leave minimal buffer for operational missteps or rising credit losses in a weakening housing market [3]
