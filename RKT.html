Investment Thesis
Rocket is the largest U.S. mortgage lender at 11.9x forward earnings with Q1 2026 guidance 15.9% above consensus, a vertically integrated platform (Rocket Mortgage + Redfin + Mr. Cooper), and 97% net client retention [1]. The $130.4B origination volume and Compass strategic alliance create scale advantages that should compound as housing affordability improves with rates easing. Dan Gilbert's vision of a seamless home-buying experience is coming together through M&A integration.
The bear case centers on the cyclical nature of mortgage lending — Rocket's revenue is highly sensitive to interest rate movements, and the company trades at 8x P/S versus 1.9x for mortgage peers, implying execution risk is priced in [1]. GAAP profitability remains negative despite $7.1B revenue, and integrating three major acquisitions simultaneously (Redfin, Mr. Cooper, Compass partnership) creates operational complexity. D/E of 148% reflects $33.8B in warehouse debt. The stock is 44% below its $24.36 52-week high. Rising fintech competition and potential AI-driven underwriting commoditization could erode Rocket's digital-first advantage over time [1].
Key Catalysts
  1. Q1 2026 revenue guidance of ~$2.7B (15.9% above consensus) signals strong origination momentum continuing [1]
  2. Mr. Cooper integration creating servicing scale with $130.4B closed origination volume feeding the retention flywheel [1]
  3. Interest rate easing could drive a refinancing wave that disproportionately benefits the #1 digital mortgage lender [1]
Key Risks
  1. 8x P/S valuation versus 1.9x peer average creates significant downside risk if growth premium compresses [1]
  2. Simultaneous integration of Redfin, Mr. Cooper, and Compass partnership creates operational and cultural execution risk [1]
  3. Interest rate volatility directly impacts mortgage origination demand; rate increases could reverse recent volume gains [1]
