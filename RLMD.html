Investment Thesis
Relmada Therapeutics has rebuilt its pipeline following the REL-1017 depression drug failure with two promising programs: NDV-01 targeting non-muscle invasive bladder cancer (Phase 3 RESCUE program launching mid-2026 with FDA-aligned registrational pathways) and Sepranolone for Prader-Willi syndrome (Phase 2 initiating mid-2026) [1]. The $160M PIPE financing provides cash runway of $116.4M, and Jefferies initiated coverage with a Buy rating on the pipeline pivot [1]. Both programs address genuine unmet medical needs — BCG-unresponsive bladder cancer and Prader-Willi syndrome have limited approved therapies, creating potential for premium pricing and accelerated regulatory pathways. Net losses have narrowed from $157M (2022) to $57.4M (2025) as operating expenses rationalized [4].
The bear case is substantial for a pre-revenue biotech with a history of clinical failures. Relmada's original lead program REL-1017 failed in two Phase III trials (RELIANCE III in October 2022 and RELIANCE II futility in December 2024), destroying significant shareholder value and triggering a securities fraud investigation by Levi & Korsinsky [3]. The company has generated zero revenue in its 20+ year history, with a cumulative net loss exceeding $390M across 2022-2025 alone [4]. The new pipeline (NDV-01 and Sepranolone) is entirely in-licensed, creating dependency on partners Trigone and Asarina Pharma. With only 17 employees, execution capacity for two simultaneous registrational programs is questionable. The $756M market cap is pure clinical trial optionality — binary outcomes on Phase 2/3 results could drive the stock to zero or multiples of current price. Cash burn, while reduced, means additional dilutive financing is likely if trials extend beyond expected timelines.
Key Catalysts

  2. Sepranolone Phase 2 trial in Prader-Willi syndrome initiating mid-2026, targeting a rare disease with no approved treatments and potential orphan drug designation [1]

Key Risks
  1. Clinical trial failure risk — Relmada's track record includes two Phase III failures (REL-1017), and both new programs face binary outcomes with no commercial revenue to cushion negative results [3]
  2. Cash runway dependency — $116.4M cash with -$12.1M quarterly operating burn provides approximately 2+ years of runway, but Phase 3 oncology trials are expensive and may require additional dilutive financing [4]
  3. Securities fraud investigation by Levi & Korsinsky following REL-1017 failures creates governance and legal overhang that could distract management and impose settlement costs [3]
