Company Profile
Industrials ·Building Products & Equipment ·NASDAQ ·United States
Gibraltar Industries, Inc. manufactures and provides products and services for the residential, agtech, and infrastructure markets in the United States and internationally. The company operates through Residential (roof ventilation, mail systems, metal roofing, rain dispersion), Agtech (custom greenhouse solutions, controlled environment agriculture, structural canopies), and Infrastructure (expansion joints, structural bearings, bridge cable protection) segments. Founded in 1972 and headquartered in Buffalo, New York.

Investment Thesis
Gibraltar Industries offers an attractively valued industrial transformation story at 8.63x forward P/E and 0.71 PEG, trading near book value (1.38x P/B) following the transformational OmniMax acquisition and Renewables divestiture [1][4]. FY2025 continuing operations revenue grew 11% to $1.14B with adjusted EBITDA of $185.3M, and 2026 guidance of $1.76-1.83B in revenue (57% growth at midpoint including OmniMax's $570M contribution) with 17.6-17.8% adjusted EBITDA margins signals a step-change in scale [1]. The company's three focused segments — Residential building products, Agtech controlled environment agriculture, and Infrastructure bridge/highway products — are structurally immune to AI disruption. The Inspire Impact score of 60 (ALIGNED) with 15 positive ESG attributions reflects strong sustainability practices across the building products portfolio [3].
The bear case centers on integration risk, leverage, and organic growth uncertainty. The OmniMax acquisition approximately doubled Residential segment revenue but increased D/E to 156% with $1.37B in total debt against just $20.3M cash [4]. GAAP profit margin is -10.7% due to the $44.4M net loss from Renewables discontinuation, and while adjusted metrics look healthy, the gap between GAAP and adjusted results is wide. Organic revenue growth excluding OmniMax is guided at only ~5% for 2026, suggesting the legacy business is growing modestly [1]. The 3-year revenue CAGR of -6.5% reflects portfolio disruption from divestitures. ROE of 6.6% is well below the 15% target. Housing market cyclicality poses risk to the Residential segment, and the Agtech segment faces headwinds if controlled environment agriculture adoption slows. At 2,300 employees and $1.2B market cap, Gibraltar remains a small-cap industrial with limited analyst coverage and potential liquidity constraints during market stress.
