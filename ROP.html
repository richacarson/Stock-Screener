Company Profile
Technology ·Software - Application ·NASDAQ ·United States
Roper Technologies is a diversified technology company operating through three segments: Application Software, Network Software, and Technology Enabled Products. The company provides vertical software solutions for healthcare, insurance, food services, transportation, K-12 education, faith-based organizations, and financial services, along with water meters, medical devices, and RF technology. Roper follows an acquisition-driven model, deploying free cash flow into high-quality, asset-light vertical software businesses.
19,400 Employees ·www.ropertech.com

Investment Thesis
Roper Technologies is a best-in-class vertical software compounder delivering 11% revenue growth, 38.1% EBITDA margins, and $2.5B trailing free cash flow (19% 3-year CAGR) in Q1 2026 [1]. At 15.6x forward P/E with raised FY2026 DEPS guidance of $21.80-$22.05, the stock is attractively valued for a company with 69.4% gross margins and a proven acquisition-driven growth model [1]. The $3.8B remaining share repurchase authorization and robust M&A pipeline provide multiple levers for capital deployment. Roper's portfolio of deeply embedded vertical software businesses creates recurring revenue with high switching costs — the company's proprietary data and workflow integration in insurance, healthcare, education, and food services industries make displacement extremely difficult [2][3]. The AI strategy (hub-and-spoke model, dedicated AI leadership, AI-first M&A evaluation) positions Roper to enhance rather than be disrupted by AI [3].
The bear case focuses on acquisition integration risk and organic growth limitations. Organic revenue growth of 6% in Q1 2026 — while 5% came from acquisitions — reveals that Roper's impressive headline growth is significantly acquisition-dependent [1]. The $10.5B debt load (against $383M cash) reflects aggressive acquisition financing, and rising interest rates increase the cost of this strategy. ROE of 9.0% is suppressed by goodwill-heavy balance sheet from serial acquisitions, meaning returns on invested capital may be lower than headline margins suggest. EBITDA margins declined 70 basis points YoY in Q1 due to segment mix and higher input costs, and continued compression would pressure the investment thesis [1]. The diversified vertical software model means no single business drives outsized growth, creating a 'growth ceiling' around 10-12% absent larger acquisitions. AI disruption risk — while managed — exists across data analytics, financial software, and estimating businesses in the portfolio [3]. At $37.4B market cap, the stock requires continued M&A execution and organic growth to justify its premium.
