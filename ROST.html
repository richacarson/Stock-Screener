Investment Thesis
Ross Stores is the #2 U.S. off-price retailer executing best-in-class in an inflationary retail environment, posting 8% FY2025 revenue growth to $22.8B with 5% comps on top of 3% the year prior and 9% Q4 comps [1]. The off-price treasure-hunt model is structurally AI-resistant — consumers visit for in-person discovery of constantly rotating branded merchandise, shielding Ross from Amazon/Shein disruption that has gutted department stores [2]. New CEO James Conroy (Feb 2025) is layering AI-driven inventory analytics and self-checkout onto Barbara Rentler's merchandising foundation without disrupting the culture [3]. A fortress balance sheet (D/E 24.5%), ~40% ROE, 31 consecutive years of dividend growth, and FY2026 EPS guidance of $7.02-$7.36 (6-11% growth) support a durable compounder thesis [1][4].
The bear case centers on valuation and consumer cyclical risk. ROST trades at 34.8x TTM and 30.2x forward P/E — premium multiples that require continued best-in-class comp execution [4]. A consumer recession that forces Ross's core customer to cut apparel spending below essentials could compress the 12.3% operating margin. Shein/Temu continue to improve AI-powered personalization and logistics, potentially widening their value proposition vs. Ross for digitally native value-conscious shoppers [2]. Tariff risk on imported apparel (Ross sources heavily from Asia) could pressure merchandise margins if Trump-era tariffs expand. The Inspire Impact score of -49 with an LGBT Activism negative attribution may deter faith-based investors [CSV data]. Finally, Conroy is only in his second year — execution missteps in the first merchandising cycle under new leadership remain a risk [3].
Key Catalysts
  1. FY2026 EPS guidance of $7.02-$7.36 (6-11% growth) on 3-4% comps builds on 5% in FY2025, validating the treasure-hunt model's durability [1]
  2. New CEO Conroy's data analytics and self-checkout rollout could drive operating margin expansion above the 12.3% Q4 print [2][3]
  3. Continued consumer trade-down into off-price as inflation pressures middle-income budgets, supporting market share gains from department stores [1]
Key Risks
  1. Valuation at 30.2x forward P/E requires sustained best-in-class comp execution; any miss would compress the multiple [4]
  2. Shein/Temu AI-powered personalization and logistics could erode the off-price value proposition for digitally native shoppers [2]
  3. Tariff escalation on imported apparel could pressure merchandise margins given heavy Asian sourcing exposure [1]
