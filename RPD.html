Company Profile
Technology ·Software - Infrastructure ·NASDAQ ·United States
Rapid7, Inc. provides cybersecurity software and services including managed detection and response, vulnerability management, cloud security, and application security. The company's Command Platform integrates security data with AI, threat intelligence, and 25 years of expertise to help organizations reduce risk. Founded in 2000 and headquartered in Boston, Massachusetts, Rapid7 serves enterprises through both software subscriptions and managed security services.

Investment Thesis
Rapid7 is a cybersecurity platform company in transition, trading at an exceptionally low forward P/E of 3.6x and PEG of 0.27 after shares fell ~75% from 2024 highs. The company generated $860M in FY2025 revenue with strong free cash flow ($125-135M guided for 2026), but top-line growth has stalled at essentially 0%, and management guided for a 2% revenue decline in 2026 while declining to issue ARR guidance [1][2].
The deep value metrics (forward P/E 3.6x, guided FCF yield >30%) create a compelling margin of safety if the go-to-market restructuring succeeds, but the flat ARR, intense competition from CrowdStrike and Palo Alto Networks, and D/E of 626% from convertible debt create significant execution risk. Rapid7 scores well on infrastructure due to its extreme value metrics but mediocre on innovation and infinite game dimensions. This is a turnaround story requiring successful platform consolidation and sales execution to avoid becoming a cybersecurity also-ran.
Key Catalysts
  1. Go-to-market restructuring under new leadership could reignite growth if Command Platform consolidation drives better cross-sell and retention
  2. Forward P/E of 3.6x and FCF yield exceeding 30% create substantial margin of safety — any return to mid-single-digit growth would drive significant multiple expansion
  3. AI-powered cybersecurity demand is structurally growing as enterprises face increasingly sophisticated AI-driven threats requiring advanced detection and response
Key Risks
  1. Revenue guided to decline 2% in 2026 to $835-843M while management declined to issue ARR guidance, signaling deep uncertainty in the business trajectory
  2. D/E of 626% from convertible debt creates significant financial risk if the company cannot stabilize revenue to service obligations
  3. Intense competition from better-funded CrowdStrike, Palo Alto Networks, and SentinelOne threatens market share in managed detection and response

AI Resilience (25%)
AI Resilience MODERATE
6
AI Risk Protection (5/9): Rapid7 operates in a cybersecurity market where AI is both an enabler and a disruptor. On the positive side, the growing sophistication of AI-powered cyberattacks increases demand for security solutions — enterprises need AI-powered defenses against AI-powered threats [3]. On the negative side, AI-native security startups and hyperscaler-built security tools threaten Rapid7's managed services model. Large language models could automate security analysis tasks that currently require human analysts, compressing margins on Rapid7's managed detection and response services. The company's flat revenue growth and conservative 2026 guidance suggest it is already losing competitive ground, though not specifically to AI displacement [2]. AI Opportunity (1/1): Rapid7 generates revenue from AI-integrated products through its Command Platform, which uses AI for threat detection, automated remediation, and security analytics [3]. While not broken out separately, AI capabilities are embedded across the platform and contribute to the company's $860M ARR.
