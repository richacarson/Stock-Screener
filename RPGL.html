Republic Power Group Limited provides customized enterprise resource planning (ERP) software solutions, consulting and technical support services, and peripheral hardware to corporate clients and government agencies in Singapore and Malaysia. The company also develops AI-powered health tech products (RP Medbot) and participates in environmental tech initiatives. Incorporated in 2015, headquartered in Singapore, and a subsidiary of True Sage International Limited.

Investment Thesis
Republic Power Group is a micro-cap Singapore-based ERP software provider with $3.0M in annual revenue, 8 employees, and a $20M market cap [1][4]. The stock trades at a nominal 3.5x trailing P/E with 12% profit margins and 79% gross margins in FY2025, suggesting the core ERP consulting business is profitable when projects are active. The company is developing a SaaS subscription-based ERP product expected to launch in Q3 FY2026, which could provide more predictable recurring revenue, and its RP Medbot AI health screening device and Enviro Tech Innovation Hub involvement suggest nascent diversification [1][2]. Revenue surged from $0.7M (FY2024) to $3.0M (FY2025), demonstrating recovery from the shareholder-transition-driven trough.
The bear case is overwhelming for an institutional-quality investor. Republic Power fails the IOWN liquidity floor ($878K daily traded value vs. $1M minimum), received Nasdaq bid price deficiency and late filing non-compliance notices, and has only 8 employees serving a concentrated client base where 70% of revenue comes from a handful of customers [1][3][4]. Revenue volatility is extreme ($5M to $0.7M to $3M over three years), creating zero visibility into future earnings. The SaaS ERP product launch has been delayed due to technical bugs, and the company competes in a market dominated by Microsoft, SAP, and Oracle who are investing billions in AI-native ERP capabilities [1]. The subsidiary relationship with True Sage International Limited creates complex governance dynamics for minority shareholders. The stock has traded below $1.00, triggering Nasdaq delisting risk, and the company's micro-scale means any single client loss could be catastrophic to revenue [3].
