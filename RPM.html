Company Profile
Basic Materials ·Specialty Chemicals ·NYSE ·United States
RPM International is a multinational holding company that manufactures and markets specialty coatings, sealants, building materials, and related services. Operating through four segments — Construction Products Group (CPG), Performance Coatings Group (PCG), Specialty Products Group (SPG), and Consumer Group — the company's brands include Rust-Oleum, DAP, Zinsser, Tremco, and Carboline, serving industrial, commercial, and consumer markets worldwide.

Investment Thesis
RPM International delivered record Q3 FY2026 results with EPS of $0.57 (beating consensus of $0.35) and consolidated net sales growth of 8.9%, driven by strength across CPG, PCG, and acquisitions [1]. The MAP 2025 cost-savings initiative is delivering measurable results with gross margin expanding 110 bps, positioning the company for sustained margin improvement. At 17.3x forward earnings, RPM is reasonably valued for a Dividend King with 22.9% ROE, 51 consecutive years of dividend growth (7.7% 5-year CAGR), and leadership positions in specialty coatings niches that generate recurring maintenance and refurbishment demand [5]. The $7.7B revenue base across four diversified segments provides resilience, and the acquisition pipeline offers continued inorganic growth optionality.
The bear case centers on margin fragility and growth limitations. Profit margin of 8.6% remains thin for a specialty chemicals company, leaving little buffer if raw material costs spike or pricing power erodes [5]. Revenue growth of 3.2% 5-year CAGR reflects the mature, GDP-linked nature of coatings end markets — RPM cannot organically grow faster than construction and industrial maintenance spending. D/E of 92.1% reflects acquisition-driven leverage, and total debt of $2.9B requires refinancing in a higher-rate environment. Pension expense and inflationary cost pressures are expected to persist into FY2027 per management commentary [1]. The decentralized operating model, while culturally valuable, creates operational inefficiency that the MAP 2025 program is only partially addressing. Net insider selling in late 2025 and early 2026 suggests management may view the stock as fully valued near $100 [5].
Key Catalysts
  1. MAP 2025 cost-savings program driving sustained gross margin expansion — Q3 FY2026 showed 110 bps improvement, with further gains expected as restructuring benefits compound through FY2027 [1]
  2. Infrastructure spending tailwinds from U.S. infrastructure bill creating multi-year demand for RPM's construction products, waterproofing systems, and protective coatings [1]
  3. Bolt-on acquisition pipeline in specialty coatings niches at attractive multiples, leveraging RPM's decentralized integration model that preserves brand value and customer relationships [3]
Key Risks
  1. Raw material cost inflation — specialty chemical input costs (resins, solvents, pigments) remain volatile, and RPM's 8.6% profit margin provides limited buffer against cost spikes that outpace pricing actions [5]
  2. Construction cycle sensitivity — approximately 40% of revenue depends on construction and building maintenance activity, creating downturn risk if commercial/residential construction weakens [1]
  3. Acquisition integration risk — RPM's growth-through-acquisition model requires continuous deal flow at reasonable valuations, and leverage of 92.1% D/E constrains capacity for larger transformative deals [5]
