Investment Thesis
Range Resources delivered exceptional 2025 results with revenue of $3.12B (+28.9% YoY), earnings up 147.8%, and $1.3B operating cash flow driven by improving natural gas prices and operational efficiency [1]. At forward P/E of 10.03 with 22% profit margins and 15.9% ROE, the stock is attractively valued for an E&P with growing production. The 300 MMcf/d processing expansion mid-2026 targeting 2.5 Bcfe/d production and 2027 guidance of ~2.6 Bcfe/d show a clear growth pathway [1].
The bear case centers on commodity price dependence and limited diversification. Range Resources is a single-basin natural gas producer whose profitability is entirely dependent on Henry Hub and Appalachian gas pricing [1]. Natural gas prices are volatile and a sustained downturn would severely compress margins. The company's $650-700M capex program assumes constructive gas prices. Despite strong 2025 results, the stock's 0.9% dividend yield offers minimal downside protection. Long-term energy transition risks remain, though near-term AI-driven gas demand provides a tailwind [1].
Key Catalysts
  1. 300 MMcf/d processing expansion mid-2026 driving production toward 2.5 Bcfe/d, with 2027 guidance of ~2.6 Bcfe/d [1]
  2. AI data center power demand driving sustained natural gas consumption growth [1]
  3. Free cash flow generation of $650M+ enabling debt reduction and potential shareholder returns [1]
Key Risks
  1. Natural gas price volatility — single commodity exposure with no hedging detail makes earnings highly sensitive to price [1]
  2. Single-basin concentration — 100% Marcellus/Appalachian exposure creates geological and regulatory risk [1]
  3. Energy transition — long-term shift away from fossil fuels could impair asset values, though near-term AI demand provides support [1]
