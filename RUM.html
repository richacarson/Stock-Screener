Company Profile
Communication Services ·Internet Content & Information ·NASDAQ ·United States
Rumble Inc. operates a video-sharing platform and cloud services business positioned as a free-speech alternative to YouTube. The company is expanding into AI infrastructure through its $767M acquisition of Northern Data AG, gaining 22.4K NVIDIA GPUs, and offers Rumble Cloud, Rumble Shorts, and Rumble Wallet for creator monetization via cryptocurrency. Rumble reached 52 million monthly active users in Q4 2025.

Investment Thesis
Rumble is executing an ambitious transformation from a niche free-speech video platform into an integrated video, cloud, and AI infrastructure company. Q4 2025 revenue of $27.1M grew 9% sequentially, full-year 2025 surpassed $100M for the first time, and 2026 revenue is projected at $152.7M driven by the $100M Tether advertising commitment ($50M/yr over two years) and Rumble Cloud expansion [1]. The $767M Northern Data AG acquisition delivers 22.4K NVIDIA GPUs (20.4K H100s, 2K H200s), positioning Rumble as a meaningful AI/HPC infrastructure player with one of Europe's largest GPU fleets [2]. MAUs reached 52M in Q4 2025 (up 11% sequentially), and product innovation — Shorts, Wallet, Perplexity AI integration — broadens the platform's appeal. The near-zero debt balance sheet (D/E 0.7%) and $306M cash provide financial runway [5].
The bear case centers on chronic unprofitability and execution risk. Profit margin is -81.3% and ROE is -77.3%, reflecting a company burning cash faster than it generates revenue [5]. Q4 2025 EPS of -$0.13 missed analyst estimates of -$0.08, and the path to profitability remains unclear even with the Tether deal providing ~$50M in guaranteed annual ad revenue [1]. The Northern Data acquisition at $767M is enormous relative to Rumble's $1.4B market cap and introduces integration risk, capital requirements for GPU maintenance, and European regulatory complexity. Revenue growth YoY actually declined 10.5%, raising questions about organic platform growth momentum. Rumble's user base of 52M MAUs is heavily concentrated in conservative/free-speech demographics, limiting addressable market expansion. Advertiser hesitancy around controversial content remains a structural revenue constraint despite the Tether commitment [3][4].
Key Catalysts
  1. $100M Tether advertising commitment ($50M/yr) beginning Q1 2026 provides material, contracted revenue that could drive the company toward breakeven [1]
  2. Northern Data acquisition closing would add 22.4K NVIDIA GPUs and European AI/HPC cloud revenue, transforming Rumble's business model and revenue diversification [2]
  3. Rumble Cloud expansion and AI infrastructure services targeting customers seeking alternatives to AWS/Azure/Google Cloud for freedom-oriented or politically neutral hosting [2]
Key Risks
  1. Chronic unprofitability — profit margin of -81.3% and widening net losses require continued cash burn; $306M cash reserves may be insufficient to fund both operations and the $767M Northern Data acquisition without dilutive equity raises [1][5]
  2. Northern Data integration risk — a $767M acquisition of a European AI infrastructure company introduces operational complexity, GPU depreciation, energy cost exposure, and regulatory challenges that Rumble's small team has not previously managed [2]
  3. Advertiser concentration and platform risk — the $100M Tether deal represents a significant portion of projected 2026 revenue, and Rumble's association with controversial content limits the breadth of advertisers willing to spend on the platform [1][3]
