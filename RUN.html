Company Profile
Technology ·Solar ·NASDAQ ·United States
Sunrun Inc. designs, develops, installs, sells, owns, and maintains residential solar energy systems in the United States. The company offers battery storage alongside solar installations, with a record 73% storage attachment rate. Sunrun operates the largest fleet of residential solar energy systems in the US and was named to the Fortune 1000 in 2026 following 45% revenue growth. The company also operates distributed electricity power plants and offers virtual power plant services to utilities.

Sunrun is the largest residential solar energy company in the US, achieving 45% revenue growth to $2.96B in FY2025 and earning a spot on the Fortune 1000 in 2026 [1]. Q1 2026 revenue of $722M beat estimates by 9.8% with EPS of $0.62 crushing the $0.01 consensus — a 6,100% earnings surprise [1]. The company's record 73% battery storage attachment rate and 4.3 GWh of networked storage create a differentiated energy platform with virtual power plant capabilities that generate grid services revenue [1]. Forward P/E of 15.9x is attractive for a company growing revenue 43% YoY, and the transition to GAAP profitability (17.9% profit margin) marks a potential inflection point. AI is being deployed for customer acquisition optimization, system design, and grid services management under a dedicated Head of AI [2]. The clean energy transition provides a secular tailwind despite near-term policy uncertainty.
The bear case centers on extreme leverage, policy dependence, and cultural deterioration. D/E of 300% reflects $14.9B in debt against a $3.2B market cap — the entire business model depends on continued access to debt markets for solar system financing [4]. ROE is negative at -22.7%, and the FY2025 profit was driven largely by ITC recognition and fair value adjustments rather than sustainable operational earnings. Free cash flow was deeply negative at -$2.2B. The Inspire Insight score of -67 (MISALIGNED) reflects concerns around social activism positions [3]. Regulatory risk is the most material threat: changes to net metering policies, ITC phase-downs, or tariff adjustments on solar panels could fundamentally impair Sunrun's unit economics. Glassdoor reviews reveal cultural deterioration with multiple layoff rounds in 2025, and the ESG score of 42 falls below industry average despite the inherently green product [2][3]. The PEG ratio of 3.07 suggests the market is not pricing in significant earnings growth acceleration.

AI Resilience DEVELOPING
6
AI Risk Protection (5/9): Sunrun's core business — designing, installing, and maintaining rooftop solar systems — is inherently physical and cannot be replaced by AI [2]. Solar panels must be physically manufactured, transported, and installed on rooftops by trained crews. However, AI can improve customer acquisition (reducing the high cost of residential solar sales), optimize system design for maximum energy production, and enable predictive maintenance — all of which Sunrun is actively pursuing under its Head of AI [2]. The risk is that AI-powered competitors could use superior analytics to undercut Sunrun on pricing or system optimization, lowering barriers to entry in an already competitive market. AI Opportunity (1/1): Virtual power plant (VPP) technology is a genuine AI-enabled revenue stream — Sunrun's 4.3 GWh of networked battery storage can be aggregated and dispatched using AI optimization to provide grid services, earning revenue from utilities and grid operators [1]. As grid complexity increases with more renewable penetration, AI-optimized distributed energy resources become more valuable.
