Company Profile
Industrials ·Integrated Freight & Logistics ·NYSE ·United States
RXO is an asset-light third-party logistics (3PL) provider and the third-largest freight broker in North America after its 2024 acquisition of Coyote Logistics from UPS. Core businesses include truck brokerage, managed transportation, last-mile delivery, and freight forwarding, powered by the proprietary RXO Connect digital platform where 97% of brokerage loads are created or covered digitally.

Investment Thesis
RXO is a cycle-trough play on asset-light freight brokerage with a genuine tech moat in RXO Connect — 97% of brokerage loads are created or covered digitally, backed by AI/ML pricing and load matching [2]. The 2024 Coyote Logistics acquisition from UPS made RXO the third-largest freight broker in North America, and integration is ahead of schedule with synergies raised from $50M to $70M+ [1]. CEO Drew Wilkerson (SupplyTech Breakthrough CEO of the Year, Charlotte Most Admired CEO) has publicly argued that AI disruption fears in freight are 'overblown' because scale, relationships, and service matter more than pure tech, positioning RXO to benefit from AI rather than be displaced [2]. Inspire Impact Score of +42 with zero negative attributions reflects strong faith alignment on employee wellbeing, fair competition, hiring ethics, and GHG emissions.
The bear case is the freight cycle and profitability. Q1 2026 guidance calls for brokerage volumes DOWN 5-10% YoY, gross margin just 11-13%, and adjusted EBITDA of only $5-$12M — indicating continued financial pressure despite Coyote scale benefits [1]. RXO has negative trailing earnings and ROE, making traditional valuation metrics unusable [4]. The 70% YoY revenue growth in Q4 2025 was almost entirely Coyote acquisition-driven rather than organic. Freight recessions historically last 18-24 months, and this cycle has extended; a double-dip could pressure the balance sheet despite the reasonable 43% D/E [4]. Asset-light 3PLs are also exposed to any breakthrough in digital freight matching that could commoditize brokerage — even though Wilkerson argues otherwise. No dividend, concentrated customer base (top 5 = ~30% of revenue), and high operating leverage amplify cycle exposure.
Key Catalysts
  1. Freight cycle recovery through 2026-2027 combined with $70M+ Coyote synergies creating substantial operating leverage at the adjusted EBITDA line [1]
  2. RXO Connect platform continuing to push digital penetration beyond 97% and expand AI/ML capabilities in pricing and capacity, widening the gap vs sub-scale brokers [2]
  3. Coyote integration completion and margin normalization as one-time integration costs roll off, with management raising cash synergy guidance each quarter [1]
Key Risks
  1. Q1 2026 adjusted EBITDA guide of just $5-$12M with brokerage volumes down 5-10% YoY shows the freight cycle trough is grinding [1]
  2. Unprofitable trailing earnings (negative PE, negative ROE) and lack of organic revenue growth make RXO dependent on cycle recovery for valuation [4]
  3. Digital freight matching or AI-native logistics entrants could commoditize brokerage faster than Wilkerson expects, pressuring gross margins [2]
