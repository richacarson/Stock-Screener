Investment Thesis
Rackspace is repositioning as an AI-first multicloud integrator with specific catalysts: the Palantir partnership (deploying Foundry/AIP with plans to scale from 30 to 250+ trained engineers), RAISE AI Security Engine, and private cloud revenue growth of 6% guided for 2026 [1][2]. New CEO Gajen Kandiah brings proven AI/cloud transformation experience from Hitachi Digital and Cognizant (where he helped grow revenue from $368M to $16B). Non-GAAP operating profit is guided up 31% to $160-170M, showing margin improvement. At $1.69 per share with $248M free cash flow, the stock prices in significant distress that could ease if the AI pivot gains traction.
The bear case is severe: $2.76B debt against $397M liquidity and a $417M market cap means Rackspace is deeply overleveraged [1]. The company has been unprofitable for years with FY2026 EPS guided at -$0.20 to -$0.15, well below the -$0.03 consensus. Public cloud revenue is declining ~6% as a large government contract transitions out. Analysts rate the stock 'Reduce' with a consensus target of $1.70 — essentially flat. The AI pivot requires significant execution — scaling to 250 Palantir engineers and winning enterprise AI mandates against AWS, Azure, and Google — while carrying crushing debt that limits investment capacity. Three CEO changes in five years signals governance instability.
Key Catalysts
  1. Palantir partnership scaling from 30 to 250+ trained engineers within 12 months, targeting regulated and data-sovereign enterprise AI deployments
  2. Private cloud revenue growth of 6% in 2026, the first growth in years, driven by AI workload demand
  3. Non-GAAP operating profit guided up 31% to $160-170M, with potential for margin inflection as platform engineering scales
Key Risks
  1. $2.76B debt load against $417M market cap creates existential leverage risk if operating performance disappoints
  2. FY2026 EPS guidance of -$0.20 to -$0.15 well below consensus signals continued losses with no clear profitability timeline
  3. Competition from AWS, Azure, and GCP with embedded AI capabilities threatens Rackspace's managed services value proposition
