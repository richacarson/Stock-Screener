Investment Thesis
Rackspace showed encouraging signs in Q1 2026: $678M revenue (+2% YoY), $8.3M net income (vs. -$71.5M prior year), and public cloud revenue growing 7% YoY [1]. The AMD partnership for governed AI cloud infrastructure and the Palantir integration position Rackspace as a differentiated enterprise AI platform for regulated industries [2][3]. Full-year 2026 guidance of $2.6-$2.7B revenue and $305-$315M adjusted EBITDA suggests the turnaround is gaining traction. At $5.50/share ($1.37B market cap), the stock has tripled from its 2025 lows, but still trades at less than 0.5x revenue if profitability normalizes.
The bear case centers on $3.24B debt against -$1.22B stockholders' equity, creating extreme leverage risk [8]. Forward P/E of 89x on $0.06 forward EPS offers virtually no margin of safety if the turnaround stalls. Revenue has declined 14% from $3.12B (2022) to $2.69B (2025) over three years, and private cloud revenue fell 6% in Q1 2026 even as public cloud grew. Insiders are aggressively selling — CEO Maletira sold $2.4M in shares before departing, CFO Marino sold $460K in early 2026 [7]. Competition from hyperscalers embedding AI management tools directly into their platforms could erode Rackspace's managed services value proposition, and the company's $1.37B market cap sits precariously against $3.24B debt in a rising-rate environment.
Key Catalysts
  1. AMD governed AI cloud partnership moves from MOU to revenue-generating deployments in regulated industries by H2 2026
  2. Sustained quarterly profitability — Q1 2026's $8.3M net income was the first quarterly profit in years; consecutive profitable quarters would reset market perception
  3. Public cloud revenue growth accelerating beyond 7% YoY as enterprise AI workload demand drives managed multicloud adoption
Key Risks
  1. $3.24B debt against -$1.22B equity creates existential leverage risk — interest expense consumes a disproportionate share of operating income, and any refinancing at higher rates could be catastrophic
  2. Hyperscaler AI capabilities (AWS Bedrock, Azure AI Studio, Google Vertex AI) reduce enterprise demand for third-party managed AI infrastructure, commoditizing Rackspace's core offering
  3. Persistent insider selling by CEO and CFO signals lack of long-term conviction from the executives most informed about the company's trajectory
