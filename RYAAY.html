Existential Flexibility
7
Ryanair has evolved from a small Irish regional carrier to Europe's largest airline, pivoting from pure point-to-point to connecting flights, from basic transport to a digital travel platform generating substantial ancillary revenue [1]. The Gamechanger fleet transition and 215M passenger target for FY27 show continued strategic evolution. Willingness to slow growth when Boeing delivery delays constrain fleet expansion demonstrates discipline.

Investment Thesis
Ryanair is Europe's dominant low-cost carrier delivering exceptional profitability — H1 FY26 profit surged 42% to €2.54B on 13% fare recovery and 3% traffic growth to 119M passengers [1]. The full-year revenue forecast of €13.95B reflects the structural advantage of a 30%+ cost gap versus legacy carriers, now enhanced by the Boeing 737-8200 Gamechanger fleet delivering 16% fuel savings per seat [4]. At 10.9x forward P/E with a PEG of 0.83 and D/E of only 16.6%, Ryanair offers compelling value for a market leader with 26.4% ROE and a clear path to 215M passengers by FY27 [6]. The digital platform strategy — 90%+ direct bookings and growing ancillary revenue — creates a consumer flywheel that legacy airlines cannot replicate.
The bear case centers on cyclical risk and valuation compression during economic downturns. The -79% Q3 earnings decline (seasonal weakness in winter quarters) highlights the inherent volatility of airline economics [6]. Fare growth of 13% in H1 may not sustain as European consumer spending normalizes and Wizz Air expands aggressively. Boeing delivery delays constrain fleet growth and have forced Ryanair to moderate its 300M passenger ambition timeline. O'Leary's succession risk is material — he has led the company for 30+ years and no clear successor commands equivalent strategic authority. Environmental regulation tightening (EU ETS, potential kerosene taxes) could structurally increase costs for the industry's largest emitter by volume. The ASA ruling against Ryanair's 'lowest emissions' advertising claims underscores greenwashing risk [7]. Currency exposure (EUR revenues, USD aircraft costs) creates margin volatility.
Key Catalysts
  1. Boeing 737-8200 Gamechanger fleet rollout delivering 16% fuel savings per seat and 4% more capacity, with 30 aircraft delivered in FY25 and continued ramp [4]
  2. Full-year FY26 fare recovery expected to reverse prior year's 7% decline, driving meaningful profit growth on 3-4% traffic increases [1]
  3. Traffic growth trajectory toward 215M passengers in FY27 supported by European travel demand recovery and structural shift from legacy to low-cost carriers [2]
Key Risks
  1. Airline cyclicality — Q3 FY26 PAT of only €115M (pre-exceptional) demonstrates severe seasonal earnings volatility that could worsen in a European recession [1]
  2. Boeing delivery delays and supply chain constraints limiting fleet expansion below management's long-term 300M passenger target [4]
  3. CEO succession risk — Michael O'Leary has led Ryanair for 30+ years with no clearly identified successor of equivalent strategic caliber [5]
