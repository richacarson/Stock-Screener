Company Profile
Basic Materials ·Specialty Chemicals ·NYSE ·United States
Rayonier Advanced Materials manufactures and sells cellulose specialty products globally, serving applications including liquid crystal displays, impact-resistant plastics, pharmaceuticals, food thickeners, cigarette filters, and textiles. The company operates through five segments: Cellulose Specialties, Biomaterials, Cellulose Commodities, Paperboard, and High-Yield Pulp, with manufacturing facilities in the U.S., Canada, and France.

Investment Thesis
RYAM offers a deep-value turnaround opportunity at 7.9x forward earnings with an April 2026 strategic alternatives review that could unlock shareholder value through a sale or restructuring [1][2]. The company holds meaningful market positions in cellulose specialties — a niche product category with high switching costs for pharmaceutical, food, and LCD display customers — and the biomaterials pivot (bioethanol, prebiotics, lignosulfonates) provides optionality on the growing bio-based materials trend. Management's 2026 priorities of achieving positive free cash flow and 'substantially better' EBITDA through value-based pricing, cost cuts, and reduced capex could restore profitability if executed successfully [1]. At $627M market cap with $1.47B revenue, the stock is priced at 0.43x sales — a distressed valuation that offers asymmetric upside if the turnaround gains traction or a buyer emerges.
The bear case is severe and well-documented. RYAM has posted net losses every year from 2022-2025, with the 2025 loss of $422M representing a catastrophic deterioration [5]. D/E of 245.7% with $806M debt and only $75M cash creates genuine solvency risk — the Altman Z-Score of 1.21 places the company in the bankruptcy distress zone [2]. CEO turnover is alarming: Scott Sutton was appointed in January 2026 and has already resigned, with the board establishing an interim Office of CEO. Revenue has declined 15% from 2022 peak ($1.72B to $1.47B), and the Jesup facility fire creates additional operational uncertainty [2]. The strategic alternatives review may not result in a transaction, and dilutive equity raises or debt restructuring could destroy remaining shareholder value. Commodity pulp price volatility and declining demand for cigarette filters (a key cellulose specialties application) represent structural headwinds [1][5].
