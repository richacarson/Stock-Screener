Company Profile
Technology ·Software - Application ·NYSE American ·Singapore
Ryde Group Ltd. is a Singapore-based mobility technology company offering on-demand and scheduled ride-hailing, carpooling, and quick-commerce parcel delivery. It IPO'd in March 2024 and is a distant third player behind Grab and TADA in Singapore's ride-hailing market, with a recent pivot toward EV fleet expansion (RydeGreen) and AI integrations via Tencent Cloud.

Investment Thesis
Ryde is a sub-scale Singapore ride-hailing platform attempting a pivot toward sustainable, EV-focused, and AV-enabled mobility. TTM revenue of $10.32M is up 32% YoY and H1 2025 revenue of 5.75M SGD grew 9.86% [1]. The founder-led narrative under CEO Terence Zou (Harvard MBA) includes genuine social commitments — scholarships for driver partners' children, Singlife accident insurance for passengers and drivers — that differentiate from profit-only platforms [2][3]. Strategic pivots include RydeGreen (400 EVs in 6 months, targeting 1,200 by 2027), Tencent Cloud AI integration, and a MooVita autonomous shuttle pilot in Punggol [2][3]. For patient investors willing to bet on a niche differentiator in a duopoly market, there is optionality if the EV/AV transition plays out.
The bear case dominates. At $10.32M TTM revenue with -96% profit margins, Ryde burns cash rapidly and dilution risk is significant — the company already raised $12M via IPO and $4.5M in a follow-on in 2024 [2]. The Singapore market is a near-duopoly between Grab (dominant) and TADA, with Ryde a distant third with limited defensible share [3]. Forward P/E of -25.5 implies losses continue through 2026 [4]. The stock price has fallen from its 2024 IPO levels to $1.39 with a $174M market cap, and as of a February 2026 data point traded as low as $0.31, reflecting market skepticism about viability [1]. AV disruption within 5-10 years compresses the long-term value of any driver-based ride-hailing business, and Ryde lacks the capital to win the AV race vs. Grab and global AV players [2]. With -370% ROE and negative trajectory on book value, the equity is essentially a call option on successful pivot execution in an unforgiving market.
Key Catalysts
  1. RydeGreen EV fleet expansion to 400 EVs in 6 months and up to 1,200 EVs by 2027, enabling premium positioning vs. Grab's standard fleet [3]
  2. MooVita autonomous shuttle pilot in Punggol Q4 2025 — positioning on the right side of the AV transition [2]
  3. Tencent Cloud AI integration improving driver/rider unit economics and customer support efficiency [2]
Key Risks
  1. Duopoly structure — Grab's dominant Singapore platform economics and TADA's Korean chaebol backing make Ryde's path to profitability unclear [3]
  2. Cash burn — -96% profit margins on ~$10M revenue with prior reliance on equity raises ($16.5M combined in 2024) creates dilution risk [2][4]
  3. AV disruption long-term — Waymo, Tesla Robotaxi, and regional AV players could compress driver-based ride-hailing economics within 5-10 years [2]
