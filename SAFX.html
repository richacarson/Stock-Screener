Company Profile
Utilities ·Utilities - Renewable ·NASDAQ ·United States
XCF Global, Inc. manufactures and sells renewable fuels in the United States, developing and operating clean fuel production facilities to produce sustainable aviation fuel (SAF), renewable diesel, and renewable naphtha. Its flagship asset is New Rise Renewables Reno, a facility with 38-40 million gallon per year permitted nameplate capacity in Nevada. The company targets 2027 net revenue of $110-120M and EBITDA of $65-70M from 40-43 million gallons of production. CEO Chris Cooper joined November 2025 from Neste U.S., and the company is pursuing a three-party merger with DevvStream and Southern Energy Renewables.

Investment Thesis
XCF Global operates in a structurally attractive market — SAF demand is supported by ICAO CORSIA mandates, EU blending requirements, and U.S. energy security priorities — and targets $110-120M in 2027 net revenue and $65-70M EBITDA from 40-43M gallons of domestic SAF production at New Rise Renewables Reno [1]. The June 2026 facility restart, binding BGN INTL offtake agreement, Phillips 66 supply agreement, and the three-party merger with Southern Energy Renewables and DevvStream could collectively validate the business model and unlock a diversified renewable fuel platform [1][3]. Jet fuel prices at $4.18/gallon in April 2026 improve SAF economics, and CEO Cooper's tenure as President of Neste U.S. — the world's largest SAF producer — provides genuine operational credibility [2][4].
The risks are severe and immediate. D/E of 678.1% reflects a company in financial distress — $16.7M in payables were converted to equity in April 2026 to avoid covenant breach, the company entered a forbearance agreement on its ground lease through January 2027, and received a Nasdaq minimum bid price non-compliance notice requiring the stock to trade above $1.00 by June 8, 2026 [2][3]. Three C-suite departures in 12 months — CEO, CFO, and Chief Accounting Officer — raise serious execution credibility concerns for a 53-person company. Revenue and 5-year CAGR data are unavailable because the company has minimal operating history at scale. The TTM net loss of $127 million against $20.8M in TTM revenue reveals a cash-burning pre-scale operation. At $0.40/share and $119M market cap, the equity is a speculative option on successful facility restart — every financial metric in the IOWN framework fails the durability standard. SELL reflects present financial reality, not long-term market opportunity.
Key Catalysts
  1. Successful June 2026 restart of New Rise Renewables Reno and initial commercial deliveries under the BGN INTL offtake agreement — the single most critical near-term proof point; without this, 2027 revenue targets are unachievable
  2. Closing the three-party business combination with Southern Energy Renewables and DevvStream, diversifying the platform and potentially unlocking new capital sources and environmental credit revenue streams [1]
  3. Nasdaq compliance regained above $1.00/share by June 8, 2026 — preserving exchange listing would remove a near-term institutional investor access risk and stabilize the equity capital structure
Key Risks
  1. Facility restart failure at New Rise Renewables Reno — the plant already required a post-commissioning shutdown; any further technical failures in HEFA processing or feedstock procurement would exhaust remaining liquidity and force additional dilutive financing or insolvency proceedings [1]
  2. Nasdaq delisting on June 8, 2026 if the stock fails to close above $1.00 — delisting would eliminate institutional ownership eligibility, reduce liquidity, and trigger forced selling by index funds, compounding equity value impairment [2]
  3. Lender or landlord enforcement action — the forbearance agreement with the ground lease landlord expires January 2027, and any escalation from the lender dispute before the business combination closes could foreclose on New Rise Reno and effectively eliminate equity value [2][3]
