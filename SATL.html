Company Profile
Industrials ·Aerospace & Defense ·NASDAQ ·United States
Satellogic is a vertically integrated earth observation company that designs, manufactures, and operates a constellation of 19 sub-meter resolution satellites. The company provides high-resolution satellite imagery and data analytics for defense, intelligence, and commercial customers globally, with satellites produced for approximately $1.3 million each.

Investment Thesis
Satellogic represents a high-risk play on the rapidly growing earth observation market with a unique cost advantage — manufacturing satellites for ~$1.3M each versus $50M+ for competitors creates potential for profitable scale if revenue grows from its accelerating base ($17.7M in 2025, up 38% YoY, with Q4 surging 94%) [1]. The strategic pivot to U.S. defense/intelligence markets, $80M CEIIA contract, and AI-first Merlin constellation launching October 2026 provide specific catalysts. Net loss narrowed dramatically from $116.3M to $4.8M, showing a clear path toward profitability if revenue continues scaling. Cash of $94.4M plus the $35M January offering provides runway through the Merlin launch [1].
The bear case centers on Satellogic's persistent unprofitability — the $918M market cap is 52x TTM revenue for a company that has never been profitable [1]. D/E of 104.7% and negative ROE of -127.6% reflect the capital-intensive reality of space infrastructure. Revenue of $17.7M is growing but still tiny, and the $65.1M backlog may not convert at projected rates. Competition from Planet Labs (with 200+ satellites) and Maxar creates market share risk, and the defense pivot depends on winning competitive government contracts against established defense primes. The stock has already run significantly from its $1.26 low, and any Merlin launch delay would undermine the investment thesis [1].
Key Catalysts
  1. Merlin AI-first constellation first launch in October 2026 would validate next-generation technology and defense market positioning [1]
  2. Conversion of $65.1M backlog to revenue through 2026 should drive meaningful top-line growth toward analyst projections of $30.9M [1]

Key Risks
  1. Revenue of $17.7M against $918M market cap represents extreme valuation that requires sustained hypergrowth to justify [1]
  2. Merlin constellation launch delay or technical failure would undermine the AI-first technology thesis and defense market positioning [1]
