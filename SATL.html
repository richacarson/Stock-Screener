Company Profile
Industrials ·Aerospace & Defense ·NASDAQ ·United States
Satellogic Inc. is a vertically integrated earth observation company that designs, manufactures, and operates a constellation of 54+ sub-meter resolution satellites. The company provides constellation-as-a-service, space system solutions, AI-powered geospatial analytics, and satellite sales to government, defense, and commercial customers across Europe, Asia Pacific, North America, and South America. Founded in 2010 and headquartered in Davidson, North Carolina.

Investment Thesis
Satellogic offers pure-play exposure to the rapidly growing sovereign earth observation market with a uniquely vertically integrated model — the company designs, manufactures, and operates its own satellite constellation at ~$1.3M per satellite (an order of magnitude cheaper than competitors), then layers AI-powered analytics on top [1]. Q1 2026 revenue surged 80% YoY to $6.1 million, the company achieved positive operating cash flow for the first time in its public history, and the $64.8 million backlog provides near-term visibility [1]. The $80 million Portugal CEiiA contract and nearly $1 billion sovereign pipeline validate the constellation-as-a-service model for nations seeking domestic satellite intelligence capabilities. With $121.9 million in cash and operating losses narrowing 33% YoY, the path to profitability is becoming clearer. AI integration through the Aleph Observer platform positions the company to capture higher-margin analytics revenue rather than competing solely on raw imagery [1]. The Inspire Insight score of 0 reflects limited ESG coverage for this small-cap space company.
The bear case centers on Satellogic's pre-profitability status and extreme valuation. At $20.4M TTM revenue against an $894M market cap (~44x revenue), the stock is priced for substantial future growth that has not yet materialized [4]. The company lost $6.4M at the operating level in Q1 2026, and while losses are narrowing, the path to sustained profitability depends on converting the sovereign pipeline into booked contracts and scaling Data & Analytics margins. The Merlin next-generation constellation requires significant capex ($5.6M in Q1 alone) with revenue recognition not expected until H1 2027, creating interim cash burn risk despite the $121.9M cash position [1]. Competition from better-capitalized players like Planet Labs (800+ satellites) and Maxar (defense intelligence incumbent) could compress pricing and margins. The 154-employee workforce must execute across satellite manufacturing, launch operations, AI analytics development, and multi-continent sales simultaneously — a significant organizational challenge. Sovereign contract cycles are long and politically sensitive, creating revenue timing uncertainty.
