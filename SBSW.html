Company Profile
Basic Materials ·Other Precious Metals & Mining ·NYSE ·South Africa
Sibanye Stillwater is a multinational precious metals mining company producing gold, platinum group metals (palladium, platinum, rhodium), chrome, lithium, zinc, nickel, and silver. Operations span South Africa, the United States (Montana), Finland (Keliber lithium), and Australia (Century zinc), making it one of the world's most diversified mining companies.

Investment Thesis
Sibanye Stillwater's 2025 turnaround is dramatic — EBITDA surged 189% to R37.8B ($2.1B) as gold prices hit all-time highs ($5,595/oz in January 2026) and PGM operations returned to profitability [1]. At a forward P/E of 2.2x with R40B liquidity headroom and net debt/EBITDA of 0.59x, SBSW is among the cheapest mining stocks globally. The Keliber lithium project reaching mechanical completion positions the company for battery metals revenue in 2026, while 765MW contracted renewable energy will deliver R1B annual cost savings from 2028 [1]. The capital allocation framework — one-third dividends, one-third debt reduction, one-third growth — is disciplined, and the R3.7B dividend at the top of policy signals management confidence. SA PGM, SA gold, US PGM, and Century zinc all showed operational improvement, creating a diversified earnings base.
The bear case is anchored in Sibanye's severe ESG risk profile and commodity cyclicality. Mine safety remains a material liability — 18 deaths in 2021, ongoing fatalities at Driefontein and Kloof, a workforce trapped underground in May 2025, and the targeted assassination of a company lawyer during retrenchment proceedings [3]. Union conflict with NUM and Solidarity over safety culture and job cuts creates operational disruption risk. Revenue has declined from R172B (2021) to R130B (2025) on a 5-year basis as PGM prices fell from peaks. Negative ROE (-10.3%) and profit margin (-4.0%) despite the 'turnaround' show basic earnings remain a loss after impairments. South African political risk, power grid instability (Eskom), and currency volatility add macro risk. The CEO transition from Froneman to Stewart introduces leadership uncertainty. At $11.54, the stock is down 46% from its 52-week high of $21.29, reflecting the market's skepticism about sustainability of the recovery [4].
Key Catalysts
  1. Gold price momentum — spot gold at $5,595/oz provides extraordinary revenue amplification for SA gold operations, which generated R12.5B EBITDA in 2025 [1]
  2. Keliber lithium first production in 2026, opening a new battery metals revenue stream aligned with global EV and energy storage demand [1]
  3. Capital allocation framework delivering R3.7B dividend with net debt/EBITDA at 0.59x, creating a path to sustained shareholder returns if commodity prices hold [1]
Key Risks
  1. Mine safety and labor relations — 18 deaths in 2021, ongoing fatalities, union accusations of 'production at all costs' culture, and lawyer assassination create regulatory, legal, and operational risk [3]
  2. Commodity cyclicality — revenue declined 25% from 2021 to 2024 as PGM prices collapsed; current recovery depends on sustained gold prices above $5,000/oz and PGM stabilization [1]
  3. South African macro risk — Eskom load shedding, currency depreciation, political uncertainty, and Marikana community environmental complaints create a challenging operating environment [3]
