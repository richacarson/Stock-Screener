Company Profile
Basic Materials ·Other Precious Metals & Mining ·NYSE ·South Africa
Sibanye Stillwater Limited is a multinational mining and metals processing company headquartered in Weltevredenpark, South Africa. The company produces platinum group metals (PGMs including palladium, platinum, rhodium, iridium, and ruthenium), gold, chrome, lithium, zinc, nickel, silver, cobalt, and copper. Operations span South Africa (PGMs and gold), the United States (Stillwater PGM mine in Montana), Europe, and Australia. The company has diversified into battery metals through its Keliber lithium project in Finland. Founded in 2013 as a gold mining spinoff from Gold Fields, Sibanye Stillwater has grown through acquisitions into one of the world's largest PGM producers.

Investment Thesis
Sibanye Stillwater is a deeply discounted turnaround play, trading at 2.3x forward earnings with forward EPS of $3.70 against an $8.59 stock price — a 60% discount to its 52-week high of $21.29 [4]. Q1 2026 adjusted EBITDA surged 371% YoY to $1.2B driven by rising PGM and gold prices, with South African PGM EBITDA up 393% and gold EBITDA up 160% [1]. The Keliber lithium project in Finland completed construction in Q1 2026, positioning the company for revenue diversification into battery metals. The company's strategic transformation from a single-commodity gold miner into a diversified critical metals producer (PGMs, gold, lithium, recycling) creates optionality across multiple commodity cycles. At current prices, the stock offers exceptional leverage to PGM price recovery and the hydrogen economy thesis.
The bear case is substantial. Sibanye Stillwater has been unprofitable for two consecutive years with trailing profit margin of -4.0% and ROE of -10.3%, reflecting the devastating PGM price collapse in 2023-2024 that erased the earnings power built during the 2020-2022 PGM supercycle [4]. D/E of 99.4% represents meaningful leverage for a company with negative earnings, and the R43.9B ($2.5B) debt load requires consistent cash generation to service. The CEO transition from long-tenured Neal Froneman to Richard Stewart in September 2025 introduces execution uncertainty during a critical recovery period. South African mining operations face structural challenges including electricity supply instability (load shedding), labor costs, regulatory uncertainty, and deep-level mining safety risks. The Keliber lithium project, while strategically important, adds execution and ramp-up risk to an already complex operational portfolio. PGM demand faces long-term secular risk from battery electric vehicle adoption reducing catalytic converter demand.
Key Catalysts
  1. PGM price recovery sustaining Q1 2026 momentum, with palladium and rhodium prices improving from cyclical lows and gold at record highs, driving a potential return to profitability on a trailing basis in 2026

  1. PGM price decline from current recovery levels would reverse the Q1 2026 earnings improvement and extend the company's loss-making period, straining the $2.5B debt load and potentially requiring dividend suspension
  2. South African operational risks including electricity supply instability (Eskom load shedding), labor cost inflation, regulatory uncertainty, and deep-level mining safety hazards create persistent drag on margins and production reliability
  3. Battery electric vehicle adoption reducing long-term catalytic converter demand for palladium and platinum, creating secular headwinds for the company's largest revenue segment even as hydrogen fuel cells offer partial offset
