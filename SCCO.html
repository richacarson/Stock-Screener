Company Profile
Basic Materials ·Copper ·NYSE ·United States
Southern Copper Corporation is an integrated copper mining, smelting, and refining company operating in Peru, Mexico, Argentina, and Chile. It produces copper, molybdenum, zinc, silver, and gold from open-pit and underground mines. The company holds 164,805 hectares in Peru and 505,788 hectares in Mexico. A subsidiary of Americas Mining Corporation.

Investment Thesis
Southern Copper is a premier copper play benefiting from the structural copper supercycle driven by AI data center buildout, EV adoption, and grid electrification. Revenue surged 39% YoY to $13.42B in 2025 with 32.3% profit margins, 42.7% ROE, and $3.34B in free cash flow [1][4]. The company's D/E of 66.8% is conservative for a miner, and the 31-year dividend history with 2.12% yield provides income alongside growth exposure. At forward P/E of 30.5x, SCCO trades at a premium to mining peers but is positioned as the lowest-cost integrated copper producer with massive reserve bases across Peru and Mexico. The Tia Maria project (2027 start) adds 120,000 tonnes/year, and management projects $16B in 2026 revenue [1].
The bear case centers on commodity cyclicality and valuation. Forward P/E of 30.5x and PEG of 5.4x are expensive for a mining company, pricing in a perfect copper supercycle scenario. The sudden death of CEO Oscar Gonzalez Rocha in April 2026 creates leadership uncertainty at a critical moment in the Tia Maria development cycle [2]. SCCO is controlled by Americas Mining Corporation (Grupo Mexico), which raises minority shareholder governance concerns. Peru and Mexico both face political instability — mining nationalism, permitting delays, and community opposition have historically disrupted operations. Variable dividends (payout ratio 58.4%) mean income is not reliable in copper downturns — annual dividends ranged from $0.17 (2016) to $3.82 (2023) [4]. Over 25% of global copper supply is trapped by ESG roadblocks, and SCCO's Latin American operations face ongoing environmental scrutiny [3].
Key Catalysts
  1. Tia Maria copper project in Peru targeting 2027 commissioning, adding 120,000 tonnes/year of capacity to SCCO's production base [1]
  2. AI data center and EV infrastructure buildout driving projected global copper deficit through 2026+, supporting elevated copper prices [1]
  3. Management targeting $16B in 2026 revenue, representing ~19% YoY growth from 2025's $13.42B record [1]
Key Risks
  1. CEO succession risk following Oscar Gonzalez Rocha's sudden death in April 2026, with board yet to appoint a permanent successor during Tia Maria's critical development phase [2]
  2. Commodity price cyclicality — copper prices can decline 30-40% in recessions, compressing margins and triggering dividend cuts, as seen in 2015-2016 when annual dividends fell to $0.17 [4]
  3. Latin American political and permitting risk — Peru and Mexico face mining nationalism, community opposition, and regulatory uncertainty that could delay or cancel expansion projects [3]
