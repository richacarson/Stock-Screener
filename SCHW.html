Company Profile
Financial Services ·Capital Markets ·NYSE ·United States
The Charles Schwab Corporation is a savings and loan holding company providing wealth management, securities brokerage, banking, asset management, custody, and financial advisory services. With over $12 trillion in client assets and 46.5 million total client accounts, Schwab is the largest publicly traded U.S. brokerage firm, operating through Investor Services and Advisor Services segments.

Investment Thesis
Charles Schwab is a wide-moat franchise with $12T+ in client assets, 46.5 million accounts growing 6% YoY, and record Q1 2026 revenue of $6.5 billion (+16% YoY) with adjusted EPS of $1.43 (+38% YoY) [1]. Management raised 2026 revenue growth guidance to 14-15% — well above analyst expectations of 11.5% — driven by strong client engagement across trading, wealth, and lending solutions [1]. At 12.6x forward P/E with PEG of 1.09, 38% profit margin, and 19.1% ROE, Schwab offers compelling growth at a reasonable price. CEO Wurster's 'Schwab 2.0' strategy deploys 220+ AI use cases while targeting the Great Wealth Transfer to Gen Z and Millennials [2]. The completed TD Ameritrade integration provides a platform of unmatched scale, and the conservative 22.5% dividend payout ratio leaves substantial room for capital return increases as earnings continue accelerating.
The bear case centers on interest rate sensitivity and market correlation. D/E of 120.8% reflects banking leverage that amplifies any credit quality deterioration or interest rate compression, and net interest income — Schwab's largest revenue source — would compress if the Fed cuts rates significantly [4]. The 2023 banking stress episode, which saw client cash sorting and deposit flight, demonstrated Schwab's vulnerability to rate and confidence dynamics. Competition from Fidelity's zero-fee index funds and Robinhood's younger demographic penetration could limit Schwab's organic growth rate. If equity markets decline significantly, AUM-driven fee revenue would compress directly — a 20% market decline would immediately reduce the $12T+ asset base. Glassdoor reviews from technology employees citing management concerns and lack of direction in certain areas suggest post-integration cultural challenges that could slow the 'Schwab 2.0' AI transformation [2]. The Inspire Insight score of -49 (MISALIGNED) may concern faith-based investors despite 10 positive ESG attributions.
