Company Profile
Healthcare ·Health Information Services ·NASDAQ ·United States
Schrödinger, Inc. develops a physics-based computational platform that enables discovery of novel molecules for drug development and materials applications. Operating through Software and Drug Discovery segments, the company sells simulation software to all top 20 pharmaceutical companies and maintains a proprietary pipeline of clinical-stage drug candidates. Founded in 1990, the company combines physics-based modeling with AI to evaluate millions of molecular candidates, and is headquartered in New York, New York.

Investment Thesis
Schrödinger represents a high-conviction bet on the future of computational drug discovery, with the only platform adopted by all top 20 pharmaceutical companies and a proprietary clinical pipeline that could validate the technology's drug discovery potential. The software segment generates $200M+ in annual revenue with 10-15% ACV growth guidance, while the drug discovery segment contributed $56.4M in 2025 with partnerships including Eli Lilly [1]. The balance sheet is strong with $395M cash against $109M debt, providing multi-year runway to achieve the 2028 EBITDA profitability target. The 5-year revenue CAGR of 18.2% reflects genuine platform adoption momentum, and the Nimbus/Takeda validation ($6B deal) demonstrates that Schrödinger-enabled molecules can produce transformative value for pharma partners [2].
The bear case is substantial and centers on persistent unprofitability and clinical risk. Net margins of -40% with no projected profitability until 2028 mean the company is burning cash to fund drug discovery programs that may fail — clinical drug development has a ~90% failure rate, and SGR-1505 and SGR-3515 are early-stage programs [1]. The stock has declined dramatically from its 2021 peak of ~$100 to $11.74, destroying approximately 88% of shareholder value, reflecting the market's skepticism about the path to profitability [4]. Revenue growth turned negative at -1.2% YoY in 2025 despite the software-to-hosted transition, and drug discovery revenue is inherently lumpy and unpredictable. The competitive landscape is intensifying as AlphaFold, generative chemistry, and well-funded AI drug discovery startups (Recursion, Insilico, Isomorphic Labs) challenge Schrödinger's technological differentiation [2]. At $867M market cap with persistent losses, the company requires either a clinical breakthrough or sustained software acceleration to justify continued investment.
