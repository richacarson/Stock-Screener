Company Profile
Consumer Cyclical ·Packaging & Containers ·NYSE ·United States
Sealed Air Corporation provides packaging solutions through two segments: Food (Cryovac brand — barrier bags, pouches, films for meat, poultry, seafood, cheese) and Protective (Bubble Wrap, Autobag, Instapak for e-commerce and industrial). The company is being acquired by CD&R in an all-cash transaction at $42.15/share ($10.3B enterprise value), expected to close mid-2026. Founded in 1960, headquartered in Charlotte, North Carolina.

Investment Thesis
Sealed Air is being acquired by CD&R in an all-cash transaction at $42.15/share ($10.3B enterprise value), expected to close mid-2026 [1]. At the current stock price of $42.15, the shares are trading at the acquisition price, effectively making this a deal-closing arbitrage play with minimal upside. The underlying business generates ~$1.1B adjusted EBITDA at 19.8% margins on $5.36B revenue, with iconic brands (Cryovac, Bubble Wrap) that maintain pricing power in food safety and protective packaging [1]. Q4 2025 adjusted EBITDA margin improved 10bps to 19.8%, and forward P/E of 11.3x reflects the takeout price rather than growth expectations. ROE of 47.4% and profit margin of 9.4% demonstrate solid underlying profitability despite 3 years of revenue decline.
The primary risk is deal-break or regulatory delay. The CD&R acquisition was announced at $42.15/share with an expected mid-2026 close, but antitrust review of a $10.3B packaging industry consolidation could introduce delays or conditions [1]. If the deal breaks, Sealed Air faces significant downside — revenue has declined from $5.64B in 2022 to $5.36B in 2025, D/E of 331% reflects dangerous leverage from historical recapitalizations, and chronic CEO instability (3 CEOs in 3 years) suggests governance dysfunction [2][4]. The company walked back its 2025 sustainability pledge on recyclable packaging, creating reputational risk as ESG-conscious customers demand sustainable solutions [3]. The beef cattle cycle is at 50-year lows with 3-4% annual declines expected in 2025-2026, directly pressuring the Cryovac food packaging segment [1]. Without the CD&R buyout floor, the stock could trade significantly lower given the revenue decline trajectory and excessive leverage.

AI Risk Protection (6/9): Sealed Air's core business involves manufacturing physical packaging materials — barrier films, shrink wrap, foam systems, and automated packaging equipment — that AI cannot substitute.
