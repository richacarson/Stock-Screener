Company Profile
Financial Services ·Asset Management ·NASDAQ ·United States
SEI Investments Company is a publicly owned asset management holding company providing wealth management, retirement and investment solutions, asset management, asset administration, investment processing outsourcing solutions, financial services, and investment advisory services. The firm serves private banks, independent financial advisers, institutional investors, investment managers, and wealth management organizations globally, managing or administering approximately $1.3 trillion in assets.

Finite vs Infinite Game — Sinek (25%)

SEI demonstrates mixed infinite game characteristics. The company has a clear purpose in wealth management technology and a long operating history (founded 1968), with CEO Ryan Hicke bringing 27 years of internal experience. However, the transition from founder Al West to new leadership is still maturing, and the company's competitive language often focuses on market positioning rather than self-improvement. Strategic investments in AI and the India Global Capability Center show willingness to invest for the long term, but organizational culture reviews suggest room for improvement in trust and empowerment.

Investment Thesis
SEI Investments is a high-quality, asset-light financial services company with a near-zero debt load (D/E 1.1%), best-in-class profitability (31% profit margins, 29% ROE), and an attractive valuation at 12.4x forward earnings [1][6]. The company delivered record Q4 2025 results with $607.9M revenue (+9.1% YoY) and $1.38 EPS (+16% YoY), demonstrating strong organic growth driven by net new client wins and expanding AUM toward $1.3T [1]. Management's investments in SEIGPT and the TIFIN partnership position SEI to capture AI-driven efficiency gains across its wealth management platform.
The bear case centers on moderate revenue growth — the 5-year CAGR of ~5.6% lags true growth stocks, and FY2026 EPS estimates suggest potential deceleration from the strong FY2025 [1]. Fee compression across the asset management industry poses a structural headwind, and AI-powered robo-advisors could erode SEI's technology platform premium over time [7]. Additionally, the Glassdoor rating decline and organizational culture concerns under relatively new CEO Ryan Hicke warrant monitoring. The $20M in near-term severance and M&A integration costs may continue to pressure margins in the first half of 2026 [1].
Key Catalysts
  1. Successful deployment of SEIGPT across client-facing operations could drive measurable cost savings and strengthen competitive positioning in outsourced investment operations [2]
  2. Raymond James raised its price target to $117, reflecting analyst confidence in continued AUM growth and margin expansion toward 30%+ operating margins [8]
  3. Strategic M&A integration (reflected in Q4 2025 costs) may unlock new revenue streams and client segments, accelerating the path to double-digit earnings growth [1]
Key Risks
  1. Secular fee compression in asset management and OCIO services as passive investing and AI-powered alternatives reduce willingness to pay for active management outsourcing [7]
  2. CEO transition risk — Ryan Hicke took over from founder Al West in 2022 and is still establishing his strategic vision; declining Glassdoor scores suggest internal execution concerns [5]
  3. Slowing revenue growth trajectory — FY2025 revenue grew 8.1% but the 5-year CAGR of 5.6% suggests SEI may be a mid-single-digit grower, limiting upside in a growth-oriented portfolio [1]

AI Resilience (25%)
AI Resilience MODERATE
6
AI Risk Protection (5/9): SEI operates in asset management and investment operations outsourcing, a sector facing meaningful AI disruption as robo-advisors, AI-powered portfolio construction, and automated compliance tools commoditize parts of the value chain. However, SEI's moat lies in deeply embedded, mission-critical technology platforms that institutions rely on for back-office operations — switching costs are high and regulatory complexity provides a buffer against pure AI disintermediation [2][7]. Analyst commentary acknowledges AI as both a risk to fee compression and an enabler for operational efficiency. AI Opportunity (1/1): SEI is actively deploying SEIGPT internally for process automation, client service, and code development, and its $10M TIFIN investment positions it to monetize AI-driven wealth tools, constituting early-stage but real AI-related activity [2][3].
