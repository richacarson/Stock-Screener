Company Profile
Healthcare ·Biotechnology ·NYSE ·United States
Serina Therapeutics is a clinical-stage biotechnology company developing drugs to treat neurological diseases using its proprietary POZ Platform, which enables precise, controlled, and sustained drug delivery. The lead candidate SER-252 (POZ-apomorphine) is in Phase 1b clinical trials for advanced Parkinson's disease, and SER-214 (POZ-rotigotine) has completed Phase 1a for early Parkinson's and Restless Leg Syndrome.

Investment Thesis
Serina Therapeutics offers high-risk/high-reward exposure to a differentiated Parkinson's disease therapy through its proprietary POZ Platform. SER-252 (POZ-apomorphine) entered a registrational Phase 1b trial in February 2026 using the 505(b)(2) regulatory pathway, which leverages existing apomorphine safety data for a potentially faster path to approval [1][2]. The POZ Platform's scientific pedigree — founded by Dr. J. Milton Harris, the pioneer of PEGylation who sold Shearwater Polymers to Nektar — provides genuine polymer chemistry differentiation [3]. The $16M+ raised in March 2026 funds operations through the Cohort 1 safety review (Q3 2026), and the Pfizer POZ-lipid license validates the platform's broader applicability. One analyst has a $11 price target (5.7x upside), and 41% insider ownership aligns management with shareholders.
The bear case is severe: Serina has $3.1M cash (pre-March raise), negative book value (-$0.46/share), $19.4M net loss on $130K revenue, and faces NYSE delisting risk requiring a compliance plan [1][4]. The stock has fallen 75% from its 52-week high of $7.92 to $1.94, and short interest surged 1,080% to 17.9% of float — institutional investors are betting against survival [1]. The $30M private placement (if fully completed) provides only 12-18 months of runway at the $18M annual cash burn rate, virtually guaranteeing further dilutive raises. Clinical risk is binary: if the Phase 1b SER-252 trial fails or shows safety issues, the company has limited resources to pivot. The 505(b)(2) pathway still requires multi-year clinical development before revenue, and competing continuous dopaminergic stimulation approaches from AbbVie and others have larger clinical programs. At $24M market cap with negative equity, this is a speculative biotech where total capital loss is a realistic scenario.
