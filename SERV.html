Investment Thesis
Serve Robotics is a pure-play physical AI company at the forefront of autonomous sidewalk delivery, with 2,000+ deployed robots, 99.8% delivery completion, and rapid scaling across 20 cities [1][2]. The combination of Vayu Robotics' AI foundation models, NVIDIA-powered compute, and Diligent's hospital platform creates a multi-vertical robotics play with significant long-term optionality [3].
The bear case is real: $2.7M in 2025 revenue against $80M in operating cash burn, a -4,125% net margin, and community pushback in cities like Chicago where residents oppose sidewalk robots [5]. However, the $260M cash position provides runway through 2027+, the 2026 revenue guide of $26M (~10x growth) is credible given fleet scale, and first-mover advantage in physical AI delivery is difficult to replicate. This is a high-conviction, high-risk bet on the future of urban logistics.
Key Catalysts
  1. Achieving $26M 2026 revenue target driven by 2,000+ robot fleet across 20 cities and Diligent hospital contracts (~$7M recurring)
  2. Revenue diversification success with advertising, software/data, and enterprise verticals reducing delivery-fee dependency
  3. Regulatory expansion as cities formalize autonomous delivery frameworks and Serve's 99.8% completion rate builds trust
Key Risks
  1. Massive cash burn ($80M/year operating) with CapEx nearly matching projected 2026 revenue, creating dilution risk
  2. Community opposition in cities like Chicago where 83% of surveyed residents oppose sidewalk robots, threatening expansion
  3. Unit economics remain unproven at scale — gross margin of -482% indicates each delivery costs far more than revenue generated
