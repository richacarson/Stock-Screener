Company Profile
Industrials ·Specialty Industrial Machinery ·NASDAQ ·United States
Serve Robotics designs, develops, and operates autonomous sidewalk delivery robots for food delivery and other last-mile logistics applications in the United States. Spun off from Uber's Postmates robotics division in 2021, the company has deployed over 2,000 robots across 44 cities, partnering with Uber Eats, DoorDash, and 26 hospitals. Serve is expanding beyond food delivery into branding, software, data, and healthcare automation through its Physical AI platform and the acquisition of Diligent Robotics.

Investment Thesis
Serve Robotics is the U.S. leader in autonomous sidewalk delivery with 2,000+ robots across 44 cities, multi-platform integration with Uber Eats and DoorDash, and Q1 2026 revenue of $3.0M — up 578% YoY — with full-year guidance of $26M representing ~10x FY2025 revenue [1]. The company is transitioning from single-use delivery to a Physical AI platform through the Diligent Robotics acquisition (healthcare automation) and the Vayu Robotics acquisition (foundation model-based autonomy), with revenue diversifying into branding, software, and data — software services already contributed one-third of Q1 revenue with nearly half of total revenue now recurring [1]. DoorDash delivery volume is growing faster than other partners with merchant count up 6x since January. At $511M market cap with $187M cash and minimal debt, Serve offers significant optionality on the autonomous delivery thesis while the balance sheet provides ~2 years of runway [4].
The bear case is dominated by path-to-profitability uncertainty and execution risk at scale. Serve generated $5.2M TTM revenue against -$112M operating cash outflow — burning approximately $86M annually with no clear line of sight to breakeven [4]. The $511M market cap implies ~100x TTM revenue, requiring exceptional execution to justify. Community opposition is tangible: robots have blocked firetrucks, broken down on Miami Brightline train tracks (January 2026), and obstructed mobility scooter users, generating petitions and regulatory pushback that could slow deployment into new cities [3]. Management paused additional sidewalk deployments beyond 2,000 units in H1 2026 to focus on utilization, acknowledging that fleet scaling has outpaced demand activation. The autonomous delivery market itself remains unproven — Amazon discontinued Scout, and Seeking Alpha analysis notes the company 'utilizes less than half of its active fleet and is bleeding cash' [3]. At $6.01 per share with -$2.05 EPS, 1.88 beta, and a 68% decline from the 52-week high, SERV is a speculative bet on Physical AI whose unit economics have not been demonstrated at scale [4].
