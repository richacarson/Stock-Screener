Company Profile
Consumer Discretionary ·Restaurants ·NYSE ·United States
Sweetgreen, Inc. owns and operates restaurants serving fresh salads, warm bowls, and plates made from locally sourced, seasonal ingredients. The company operates approximately 230 locations across the United States and has developed the proprietary Infinite Kitchen automated assembly system. Founded in 2007 by Jonathan Neman, Nicolas Jammet, and Nathaniel Ru, headquartered in Los Angeles, California.

Investment Thesis
Sweetgreen has built a differentiated fast-casual brand around healthy, sustainable food with 230+ locations and a genuine mission-driven culture under co-founder CEO Jonathan Neman. The Infinite Kitchen automated assembly system provides a structural competitive advantage by boosting throughput 33% and reducing labor costs, creating a path to restaurant-level margins that peers cannot easily replicate [1][3]. The 5-year revenue CAGR of 18.9% demonstrates strong unit growth from $340M (FY2021) to $679M (FY2025), and the company guided to $1-6M adjusted EBITDA for FY2026, approaching profitability [1]. At $1.08B market cap, the stock trades at 1.6x revenue — reasonable for a restaurant brand with significant white-space expansion opportunity.
The bear case centers on a dramatic deceleration in same-store sales, which declined 12.8% in Q1 2026 (versus -3.1% prior year), driving a 2.9% revenue decline to $161.5M and restaurant-level profit margin compression from 17.9% to 10.0% [2]. FY2025 net loss widened 48% to $134M, and the stock has fallen 46% from its 52-week high of $16.70 to $9.10. Consumer spending weakness and premium pricing pressure in a recessionary environment could persist throughout FY2026. The company has never generated sustained positive GAAP net income since its 2021 IPO, and cumulative operating losses have consumed over $500M in cash ($472M at IPO down to $157M today) [5]. Forward P/E is unavailable because analysts do not project consistent profitability, and the trailing P/E of 64x on thin TTM earnings is unreliable as a valuation anchor.
Key Catalysts
  1. Infinite Kitchen expansion to additional locations driving restaurant-level margin improvement toward the 14.2-14.7% full-year guidance, potentially demonstrating the automation model's unit economics advantage
  2. Same-store sales stabilization in Q2-Q4 2026 as the company laps easier comparisons — FY2026 guidance of -4% to -2% implies significant improvement from Q1's -12.8% decline
  3. Achieving positive adjusted EBITDA for FY2026 ($1-6M guidance) would represent a critical inflection point toward sustained profitability and could re-rate the stock
Key Risks
  1. Same-store sales declined 12.8% in Q1 2026 and may continue deteriorating if consumer spending weakness persists, threatening the company's path to profitability and potentially forcing cost cuts that compromise the mission
  2. Cash burn has consumed $315M of the $472M IPO cash position without achieving sustained profitability — at current burn rates, the remaining $157M provides limited runway before additional capital raises would be needed
  3. Premium pricing strategy faces structural headwinds as inflation-weary consumers trade down to lower-cost fast-casual alternatives, compressing both traffic and average check size
