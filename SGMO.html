Company Profile
Healthcare ·Biotechnology ·OTC ·United States
Sangamo Therapeutics, Inc. is a clinical-stage genomic medicine company using proprietary zinc finger protein (ZFP) technology to develop gene therapies for serious neurological diseases. The company made medical history with the first intravenous in vivo genome-editing therapy. Sangamo filed for Chapter 11 bankruptcy on June 23, 2026, and is selling assets to Eli Lilly and Astellas Pharma. Stock has been delisted from NASDAQ to OTC markets.

Investment Thesis
Sangamo Therapeutics filed for Chapter 11 bankruptcy on June 23, 2026, and is selling assets to Eli Lilly and Astellas Pharma [3]. The stock has been delisted from NASDAQ to OTC markets, trading at $0.055 with a market cap of ~$30M. Q1 2026 revenue of $1.4M missed the $33.7M estimate by 96%, and the net loss was $31M [1]. The company's 30-year history produced foundational zinc finger nuclease IP and the first-ever in vivo genome-editing therapy, and the Fabry disease candidate ST-920 showed positive clinical data — hence acquirer interest from two major pharma companies. If asset sale proceeds exceed creditor claims, some equity recovery is theoretically possible.
The bear case is definitive: Sangamo is in bankruptcy with equity likely to be wiped out. The stock trades at $0.055 on OTC markets with daily liquidity under $600K, making it effectively untradeable [5]. Both Pfizer and Sanofi previously terminated major partnerships, validating concerns that the ZFP technology platform lacks standalone commercial viability. The Chapter 11 filing lists assets and liabilities both in the $100-500M range, and creditor priority in bankruptcy means equity holders are last in line [3]. Even if the underlying science retains value, that value will accrue to Lilly and Astellas, not SGMO shareholders. This is a clear SELL — the company as an independent entity is ending.
Key Catalysts
  1. Successful completion of asset sales to Eli Lilly and Astellas during Chapter 11 auction process, potentially recovering some value for equity holders if proceeds materially exceed creditor claims
  2. Positive regulatory developments for isaralgagene civaparvovec (Fabry disease) under acquirer stewardship could validate underlying ZFP technology value
  3. Potential competing bids during Chapter 11 auction could increase asset sale proceeds above stalking-horse agreements
Key Risks
  1. Chapter 11 creditor priority means equity holders are last in line — asset sale proceeds will likely be entirely consumed by debt and administrative claims, leaving zero recovery for shareholders
  2. NASDAQ delisting to OTC markets with stock at $0.055 and sub-$600K daily liquidity renders the stock effectively untradeable for any meaningful portfolio position
  3. 30-year failure to commercialize any product raises fundamental questions about ZFP technology viability — acquirers may shelve or deprioritize the acquired assets after completing due diligence
