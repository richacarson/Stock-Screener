Company Profile
Healthcare ·Biotechnology ·NASDAQ ·United States
Sangamo Therapeutics is a clinical-stage genomic medicine company using proprietary zinc finger protein (ZFP) technology to develop gene therapies, gene editing, and cell therapies. The company made medical history with the first intravenous in vivo genome-editing therapy. Lead programs target Fabry disease (ST-920, BLA submission planned), hemophilia A (regained from Pfizer), and kidney transplant rejection (TX200 CAR-Treg).

Investment Thesis
Sangamo Therapeutics holds potentially transformative science — its proprietary zinc finger protein platform enabled the first-ever in vivo genome editing therapy, and the ST-920 Fabry disease program showed positive STAR study results with a BLA submission planned for Q1 2026 [1][2]. If approved, ST-920 could be a one-time genetic cure for Fabry disease, fundamentally shifting the treatment paradigm from chronic enzyme replacement therapy. The hemophilia A program regained from Pfizer and TX200 CAR-Treg for kidney transplant provide additional pipeline optionality. At $0.31/share and ~$145M market cap, the stock prices in significant probability of failure, creating asymmetric upside if the Fabry program succeeds and a commercialization partner is secured [1].
The bear case is severe and immediate. Sangamo has disclosed going-concern risks, warning it may need to 'cease operations entirely or seek bankruptcy protection' [3]. Both Pfizer and Sanofi terminated major partnerships, eliminating collaboration revenue and validating concerns about the technology platform's commercial viability. Revenue collapsed 98.8% YoY to $32.9M, D/E is 407% (above the 300% distress threshold), and ROE is -480% [4]. The $0.31 stock price on 372M shares reflects extreme dilution risk — any capital raise will be massively dilutive. Even if ST-920 gains FDA approval, Sangamo lacks manufacturing infrastructure and commercial capabilities, requiring a partner that may demand unfavorable terms given Sangamo's desperate financial position. Sustainalytics rates ESG management as 'Weak,' and the company's survival as an independent entity is genuinely uncertain [3].
