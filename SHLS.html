Company Profile
Technology ·Solar ·NASDAQ ·United States
Shoals Technologies Group is the leading provider of American-made electrical balance of system (EBOS) solutions for solar, battery energy storage (BESS), and critical power infrastructure. The company designs, manufactures, and sells Big Lead Assembly (BLA) solutions, combiners, disconnects, and monitoring systems. Founded in 1996, Shoals has deployed its solutions on over 62 GW of solar systems globally.

Investment Thesis
Shoals Technologies is the dominant American EBOS provider for utility-scale solar, with 62+ GW deployed globally and a record $747.6M backlog providing exceptional visibility into 22% revenue growth in 2026 ($560-$600M guided) [1]. At 13.0x forward P/E and 0.62x PEG, the stock is deeply undervalued for a 20%+ growth company with multiple expansion catalysts: BESS backlog quadrupled to $67M, international revenue grew 13x to $13M, and the data center BLA product targets a massive new market in 2027 [1]. Shoals benefits from structural tailwinds — IRA incentives, AI-driven data center power demand, and domestic manufacturing preference — while its BLA technology provides a genuine competitive moat with 75% wire reduction and 18% fewer connections [2]. The stock has risen 92% from its 52-week low, but at $6.58 remains 85% below its all-time high, offering significant recovery potential.
The bear case centers on margin pressure and execution risk. Gross margins contracted to 31.6% in Q4 2025 from 37.6% a year earlier, driven by $3.7M in tariffs, facility consolidation costs, and increased labor expenses [1]. Q4 adjusted EPS of $0.10 missed the $0.14 consensus, and 2026 margins are guided only to the 'low-to-mid 30% range' with recovery not expected until 2027. Legal expenses from patent and shareholder litigation will remain elevated through 2026. The consolidated manufacturing facility introduces temporary inefficiencies that may persist longer than management expects. IRA policy risk is real — tax credits expire in 2029, and any acceleration of that timeline could damage the solar installation pipeline. Short interest at 8.4% of outstanding and beta of 1.77 reflect elevated volatility risk. The company has only $7.3M cash and $178.6M debt with negative free cash flow of -$9.9M in 2025 [1].
