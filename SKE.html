Company Profile
Basic Materials ·Gold & Silver Mining ·NYSE ·Canada
Skeena Resources is a Canadian mining development company focused on revitalizing the past-producing Eskay Creek gold-silver mine in the Golden Triangle region of British Columbia. The flagship Eskay Creek project consists of 51 mineral claims and eight mineral leases covering approximately 7,666 hectares, with initial production targeted for Q2 2027 at an expected rate of 450,000 gold-equivalent ounces annually in years 1-5.

Investment Thesis
Skeena Resources offers high-conviction exposure to gold at an attractive entry point — the Eskay Creek project is fully permitted, $750M in financing is secured, construction is underway, and initial production is targeted for Q2 2027 at 450,000 AuEq oz/year in the first five years [1]. The project is located in British Columbia's prolific Golden Triangle with strong metallurgical characteristics and infrastructure access. At a $3.6B market cap, the stock prices in significant production value but still offers upside if gold prices remain elevated above $2,000/oz. The consent-based agreement with the Tahltan Nation provides durable social license that de-risks one of the most common failure modes for mine development projects [3]. Cash of $151.6M and conservative D/E of 39.6% provide financial flexibility through the construction phase [5].
The bear case is substantial for a pre-revenue mining developer. Skeena burned $301.5M in free cash flow in the trailing twelve months with zero revenue, and any construction delays could require additional dilutive equity raises [5]. The company carries all the classic risks of mine development: cost overruns, permitting delays, geological surprises during construction, and commodity price exposure. Consensus EPS estimates of -$0.98 for FY2026 confirm continued losses through the construction phase [1]. Some Tahltan community members have raised environmental concerns about water contamination from the open-pit conversion, and the K'adesibe Collective's objections represent an unresolved social license risk that could surface during construction [4]. Net insider selling in April 2026 — with multiple officers exercising options and selling shares — suggests insiders may be locking in gains ahead of construction risk [5]. Single-asset concentration means the entire investment thesis depends on Eskay Creek performing as planned.
Key Catalysts
  1. Eskay Creek initial production targeted Q2 2027 — successful commissioning would transform Skeena from cash-burning developer to cash-generating producer with 450,000 AuEq oz/year [1]
  2. Gold price environment — sustained prices above $2,000/oz would significantly improve Eskay Creek project economics and accelerate payback on the $750M development investment [1]
  3. Exploration upside — AI-enhanced prospecting identified 80% of 2025 gold zone discoveries, with potential to extend mine life beyond the current reserve estimate through continued exploration [2]
Key Risks
  1. Construction execution risk — Eskay Creek conversion from historical underground mine to open-pit operation is complex, with cost overruns and timeline delays the most common failure mode for mine development projects [1]
  2. Community opposition — K'adesibe Collective and some Tahltan members have raised unresolved concerns about water contamination, representing ongoing social license risk despite the consent-based agreement [4]
  3. Pre-revenue cash burn — $301.5M annual free cash flow burn with $151.6M cash requires flawless execution or additional dilutive financing to reach Q2 2027 production [5]
