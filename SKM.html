Investment Thesis
SK Telecom is South Korea's dominant wireless carrier (30M subscribers) executing an ambitious 'AI Native' transformation under CEO Jung Jai-hun, with AIDC revenue growing 34.9% YoY to KRW 519.9B and plans to scale Korea's largest sovereign AI model from 519B to 1T parameters [1][2]. At a forward P/E of 16.8x and dividend yield of 3.7% with 27 consecutive years of payments, SKM offers an attractive valuation for a company with significant AI upside optionality. The PEG ratio of 0.77 suggests the market is undervaluing the company's forward earnings recovery as cybersecurity incident costs normalize. The partnership with SK hynix on manufacturing AI solutions and the national-scale ambition of the AI strategy create a differentiated growth narrative for a telecom stock [2]. D/E of 80% and $14.6B market cap provide a stable foundation for the AI investment cycle.
The bear case centers on the aftermath of the catastrophic April 2025 cybersecurity breach and execution risk on the AI pivot. The BPFDoor malware went undetected for nearly 3 years, compromising USIM data for 23-27 million subscribers and triggering a record $97.2M fine [4]. Net income collapsed 73% to KRW 375.1B in 2025, driving the trailing payout ratio to 149% — unsustainable without earnings recovery. Management acknowledges it will be 'difficult to return revenue to pre-incident levels' due to prolonged customer attrition [1]. The AI strategy requires massive capital investment in an uncertain competitive landscape where SKT faces global AI giants (OpenAI, Google, Meta) with vastly larger resources. Revenue has declined for three consecutive years on a CAGR basis (-0.4%), and profit margins of 2.4% leave minimal cushion for investment. Currency risk is inherent in the KRW-denominated business traded as a USD ADR [5].
Key Catalysts
  1. Earnings normalization as cybersecurity incident costs (USIM replacements, $97.2M fine) are one-time charges, with forward EPS of $2.26 representing significant recovery from trailing EPS of $0.68 [1][5]
  2. AIDC revenue scaling beyond KRW 520B with new Pangyo Data Center acquisition and AI model commercialization to enterprise customers, creating a high-growth revenue stream within the telecom structure [1]
  3. Sovereign AI model upgrade to 1T parameters with multimodal capabilities (voice, video) in H2 2026, potentially positioning SKT as the dominant AI platform in the Korean market [2]
Key Risks
  1. Prolonged subscriber attrition from the April 2025 cybersecurity breach — management has not provided a timeline for subscriber recovery, and competitive carriers may permanently capture defecting customers [1][4]
  2. Unsustainable dividend payout ratio of 149% trailing — if 2026 earnings do not recover to forward estimates, SKT may need to cut or freeze the dividend, removing a key investment thesis pillar [5]
  3. AI investment execution risk — building a competitive sovereign AI model requires sustained capital investment that may not generate returns for years, while global AI leaders have orders-of-magnitude more resources [2]
