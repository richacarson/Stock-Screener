Company Profile
Energy ·Oil & Gas Equipment & Services ·NYSE ·United States
SLB is the world's largest oilfield services company, operating through four divisions: Digital & Integration, Reservoir Performance, Well Construction, and Production Systems. The company rebranded from Schlumberger in 2022 to signal its evolution toward digital and energy technology services. FY2025 revenue was $35.71B with $4.1B in free cash flow. The ChampionX acquisition adds production chemistry capabilities, and the Digital & Integration segment generates $926M ARR at 32.7% EBITDA margins. CEO Olivier Le Peuch is driving a pivot toward AI-powered services, carbon capture, and geothermal, targeting $10B in new energy revenue by 2040.

Investment Thesis
SLB is the world's highest-quality energy technology company trading at 17.1x forward P/E with a 2.06% dividend yield and $4.1B in annual free cash flow [1]. The Digital & Integration segment — generating $926M ARR at 32.7% EBITDA margins — trades at an implicit discount to SaaS peers despite strong growth. The ChampionX acquisition adds $400M in annual pretax synergies by 2028 and is accretive to 2026 EPS [1]. The ALIGNED Inspire score of +51 with zero negative attributions makes SLB one of the most values-compatible energy holdings in the IOWN universe. Committed $4B+ shareholder returns in 2026 with room for continued dividend growth [4].
The primary risk is oilfield activity cyclicality: FY2025 revenue declined 1.6%, and Q1 2026 revenue excluding ChampionX declined 7% YoY as international spending moderated [1]. SLB's heavy international exposure (75%+ of revenue) introduces geopolitical risk across Latin America, Middle East, and Africa. The digital business — while growing 17% YoY — remains a small fraction of total revenue; the transformation to an energy technology company is real but will take 5-10 years to fully shift the margin profile. The trailing P/E of 25.2x and PEG of 1.97 suggest the market already prices significant transformation premium, limiting near-term upside if execution falters. Sustained oil prices below $55/bbl would materially reduce upstream spending and compress SLB earnings by 15-25%.
Key Catalysts
  1. ChampionX integration delivering $400M in annual synergies by 2028, with 2026 EPS accretion providing near-term positive earnings revisions [1]
  2. Digital & Integration ARR growing from $926M toward $1.5B — each incremental ARR dollar at 32.7% EBITDA margins is worth 5-7x revenue multiple versus traditional field services at 1-2x [1]
  3. New energy revenue exceeding $1.5B in 2026 (up from $1B+ in 2025) — carbon capture, geothermal, and data center solutions validating the energy technology pivot
Key Risks
  1. Oilfield services cyclicality: Q1 2026 core revenue declined 7% ex-ChampionX; sustained oil prices below $55/bbl would trigger 15-25% earnings compression as national oil companies and IOCs cut spending [1]
  2. International geopolitical risk: 75%+ of revenue from markets including Latin America, Middle East, Africa, and Russia — political instability, sanctions, or currency depreciation could materially impact results
  3. Transformation timeline: digital revenue remains a small fraction of $35.7B total — the technology pivot may take another 5-10 years to fully shift SLB's margin and growth profile [1]
