Company Profile
Energy ·Oil & Gas Equipment & Services ·NYSE ·United States
SLB N.V. (formerly Schlumberger Limited) is the world's largest oilfield services company, providing technology for the energy industry worldwide through four divisions: Digital & Integration, Reservoir Performance, Well Construction, and Production Systems. The company also develops carbon management, geothermal, and adjacent energy technology. Rebranded from Schlumberger to SLB in October 2025. Founded in 1926, headquartered in Houston, Texas.

Investment Thesis
SLB is the world's largest oilfield services company at $35.7B revenue with an unmatched global footprint, transitioning into a broader energy technology platform under CEO Olivier Le Peuch [1]. Q1 2026 revenue grew 3% to $8.72B, digital ARR surpassed $1B, and the company guided $36.9-37.7B for FY2026 with $4B+ shareholder returns planned (including $2.4B buybacks and a 3.5% dividend increase). D/E of 42.5% is conservative, Forward P/E of 16.7 is reasonable, and the 2.12% dividend yield provides income. The energy transition strategy — carbon capture, geothermal, hydrogen — positions SLB for long-term relevance as the only oilfield services company genuinely building beyond hydrocarbons.
The bear case centers on execution and valuation compression. EPS declined 24% in FY2025 to $2.38, and Q1 2026 net income fell 6% YoY despite revenue growth, reflecting margin pressure from Middle East disruptions in Qatar and Iraq [1]. International revenue declined 4% in Q1, concerning for a company that generates 74% of revenue outside North America. Profit margin of 9.3% is structurally low. The PEG ratio of 1.91 suggests the stock is not cheap relative to growth. Energy transition investments (carbon capture, geothermal) remain pre-revenue or early-revenue and may take years to contribute meaningfully — SLB Capturi and NewEnergy ventures still represent <1% of revenue. The 2020 dividend cut from $2.00 to $0.50 demonstrates that commodity cycle risk can rapidly destroy income quality, and consecutive years of dividend growth is only 4 years from the reduced base.
Key Catalysts
  1. Digital ARR surpassing $1B with DELFI and Lumi AI platforms gaining traction — digital services offer structurally higher margins than traditional oilfield services and could drive margin expansion above 10%
  2. Middle East and offshore deepwater spending recovery driving international revenue re-acceleration beyond the Q1 2026 dip, with $36.9-37.7B FY2026 revenue guidance implying 3-6% growth
  3. Energy transition portfolio (SLB Capturi carbon capture, geothermal, hydrogen) reaching commercial scale, potentially opening a new $5B+ addressable market by 2030 that diversifies away from hydrocarbon dependence
Key Risks
  1. International revenue declined 4% in Q1 2026 due to Middle East disruptions — geopolitical instability in Iraq, Qatar, and other key markets could create sustained headwinds for SLB's largest revenue base
  2. EPS declined 24% in FY2025 despite stable revenue, indicating margin erosion from pricing pressure and operational inefficiency that may worsen if commodity prices decline further
  3. Energy transition investments remain largely pre-commercial — if carbon capture, geothermal, and hydrogen fail to scale, SLB's rebranding narrative loses credibility while the core oilfield services business faces secular demand risk from peak fossil fuel scenarios

AI Resilience (25%)
AI Resilience LOW RISK
8
AI Risk Protection (7/9): SLB's core business involves physical-world operations — drilling wells, managing reservoirs, deploying subsea production systems, and manufacturing oilfield equipment — that AI structurally cannot displace [1]. These services require heavy equipment, specialized field crews, and execution in extreme environments (deepwater, high-pressure/high-temperature). The regulatory and safety requirements of global energy operations create additional defensive moats. AI enhances SLB's offerings (subsurface modeling, drilling optimization) but cannot replace the physical services. The only modest exposure is that AI-optimized operations could reduce the total number of service runs needed. AI Opportunity (1/1): SLB's digital annual recurring revenue surpassed $1B, driven by the DELFI and Lumi AI platforms providing cloud-based subsurface analytics and operational optimization [1]. This represents quantifiable, growing AI-driven revenue — though still under 3% of total revenue, it demonstrates a clear AI monetization pathway in energy technology.
