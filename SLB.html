Company Profile
Energy ·Oil & Gas Equipment & Services ·NYSE ·United States
SLB Limited (formerly Schlumberger) is the world's largest oilfield services company, providing technology, integrated project management, and information solutions to the oil and gas industry worldwide. The company operates through four divisions: Digital & Integration, Reservoir Performance, Well Construction, and Production Systems. Following the 2024 acquisition of ChampionX, SLB expanded its production chemicals and artificial lift capabilities. Under CEO Olivier Le Peuch since 2019, SLB has rebranded, embraced digital transformation, and committed to net-zero emissions by 2050 including Scope 3. Founded in 1926, headquartered in Houston, Texas.

Investment Thesis
SLB delivered Q1 2026 revenue of $8.72B (+3% YoY) with the Production Systems division growing 23% to $3.51B driven by the ChampionX acquisition, while North America revenue surged 26% to $2.17B [1]. As the world's largest oilfield services company, SLB is uniquely positioned to benefit from the structural increase in energy demand driven by AI data centers, electrification, and emerging market industrialization. At 17.0x forward P/E with a 2.1% dividend yield (3.5% increase announced for 2026) and $4B+ in planned shareholder returns, SLB offers attractive income characteristics. The Lumi AI platform and ChampionX integration provide technology differentiation, and D/E of 42.5% reflects conservative leverage. The 18.7% 5-year dividend growth CAGR demonstrates rapid dividend rebuilding post-2020 cut.
The primary risk is that global oilfield activity has plateaued. Total company revenue was essentially flat at $35.7B in 2025 vs. $36.3B in 2024, and international revenue declined 4% in Q1 2026 due to Middle East disruptions and customer budget discipline [1]. Net income fell 6% YoY in Q1 to $752M, and EPS declined 14% to $0.50, suggesting margin pressure even as revenue grew modestly. The 9.3% profit margin is structurally low for this capital-intensive model, limiting earnings leverage. The PEG ratio of 1.96 suggests the market is pricing in modest growth expectations. OPEC+ production decisions, oil price volatility, and the long-term energy transition create fundamental uncertainty about demand for traditional oilfield services. The 2020 dividend cut — though now well-recovered — demonstrates the inherent cyclical risk of this business model, and consecutive years paid of only 6 since the restart weighs on income quality metrics.
Key Catalysts
  1. ChampionX integration driving revenue synergies and margin expansion in the Production Systems division — Q1 2026 already showed 23% YoY growth in this segment
  2. AI data center proliferation driving structural electricity demand increase, supporting long-term oil and gas investment and SLB's addressable market
  3. Shareholder returns exceeding $4B in 2026 through dividends and $2.4B+ buybacks, representing ~5% of market cap and providing downside protection
Key Risks
  1. International revenue declined 4% in Q1 2026 due to Middle East disruptions and customer budget discipline — any sustained international downturn would disproportionately impact SLB given its ~75% international revenue mix
  2. Net income declined 6% and EPS declined 14% in Q1 2026 despite revenue growth — margin compression from ChampionX integration costs and competitive pricing could persist
  3. OPEC+ production decisions and oil price volatility create fundamental demand uncertainty — a prolonged low-price environment could trigger another activity downturn and potential dividend risk

AI Resilience (25%)
AI Resilience LOW RISK
8
AI Risk Protection (7/9): SLB's core business — providing physical equipment, chemicals, crews, and engineering services to drill and complete oil and gas wells — is structurally protected from AI disruption. Oilfield operations require heavy machinery, trained field engineers, specialized chemicals, and physical presence at wellsites that AI cannot substitute [1]. The deeply regulated safety environment, harsh operating conditions (deepwater, high-pressure/high-temperature), and complex logistics create barriers beyond AI's reach. Moreover, AI is a clear demand driver — as AI data centers proliferate, electricity demand increases, supporting demand for the hydrocarbons SLB helps extract. However, AI-driven automation could reduce per-well crew sizes over time. AI Opportunity (1/1): The Lumi data and AI platform generates meaningful revenue through SLB's Digital & Integration division, providing AI-powered well planning, drilling optimization, and production analytics to E&P operators [3]. The ChampionX acquisition adds chemical optimization capabilities that benefit from machine learning. SLB's digital division carries higher margins than traditional services, and AI-enhanced offerings command premium pricing.
