Company Profile
Energy ·Oil & Gas Equipment & Services ·NYSE ·United States
SLB (formerly Schlumberger) is the world's largest oilfield services company, providing technology-driven solutions for reservoir characterization, drilling, production, and processing to the oil and gas industry worldwide. The company operates in over 100 countries and has been pivoting toward a 'Digital-First' strategy under CEO Olivier Le Peuch, with digital annual recurring revenue surpassing $1 billion.

Investment Thesis
SLB is the technology leader in oilfield services, trading at a reasonable 16.6x forward earnings with a 2.1% dividend yield and 45-year dividend payment history. The company's Digital-First transformation has reached a critical milestone with $1B+ digital ARR, positioning SLB as an AI-enabled technology platform rather than a pure services provider. Q1 2026 revenue of $8.72B grew 3% despite significant Middle East disruptions.
The investment case hinges on whether SLB's digital transformation creates a durable competitive moat or merely delays commoditization. The bull case sees Lumi AI, Tela agentic systems, and autonomous drilling creating recurring technology revenue streams that command higher multiples than traditional services. The bear case notes declining international revenue (-4%), compressed net income (-6%), and the structural risk that E&P operators increasingly bring subsurface AI capabilities in-house. The $4B+ shareholder return commitment provides downside support while the digital transformation plays out.
Key Catalysts
  1. Digital ARR growth beyond $1B milestone, potentially reaching $1.5B+ as Lumi and Tela AI platforms gain operator adoption
  2. Middle East operations normalization in H2 2026 driving international revenue recovery from current -4% headwind
  3. Dividend restoration toward pre-2020 levels as FCF generation improves, supporting re-rating toward historical yield multiples
Key Risks
  1. AI democratization risk: E&P operators deploying hyperscaler AI tools in-house could reduce demand for SLB's high-margin digital interpretation services by 15-20%
  2. Middle East geopolitical disruptions continue to impact Well Construction and Reservoir Performance divisions, with 2-3 cents EPS quarterly headwind persisting
  3. Energy transition acceleration could structurally reduce global hydrocarbon investment, compressing the total addressable market for oilfield services over the next decade
