Investment Thesis
SLB is the technology leader in oilfield services with a differentiated digital platform (Lumi AI, $1B+ digital ARR) that positions it to capture value from the energy industry's digital transformation. The 2.13% dividend yield with 22% 5-year growth CAGR provides attractive and growing income, while the forward P/E of 16.6x reflects reasonable valuation for a company with secular technology tailwinds layered atop cyclical energy recovery potential.
Near-term headwinds are real: Q1 2026 net income declined 6% as Middle East geopolitical disruption forced operational demobilization in several countries, and international revenue fell 4%. The PEG of 1.92 suggests limited near-term earnings growth relative to valuation. However, North America surged 26% as US activity recovers, and the $36.9-37.7B revenue guidance for full-year 2026 implies acceleration from Q1 levels. SLB's technology moat, global scale across 100+ countries, and MSCI AA ESG rating position it as the quality name in oilfield services for long-term holders.
Key Catalysts
  1. Digital annual recurring revenue exceeding $1B and growing, with Lumi AI platform adoption driving margin-accretive technology revenue
  2. North America revenue surging 26% YoY as US drilling activity recovers and customers adopt SLB's digital solutions
  3. Dividend growth continuing at double-digit rates — 22% 5-year CAGR with 50.7% payout ratio still leaving room for increases
Key Risks
  1. Middle East geopolitical disruption already impacting Q1 results could worsen, threatening international revenue base that represents 74% of total
  2. Earnings growth turning negative (-5.6% YoY) with PEG of 1.92 suggests valuation may be stretched if recovery delays
  3. Oil price decline below $60/bbl would pressure customer capex globally, disproportionately affecting SLB's international-heavy revenue mix
