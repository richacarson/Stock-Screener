Company Profile
Energy ·Oil & Gas Equipment & Services ·NYSE ·United States
SLB N.V. (formerly Schlumberger Limited) is the world's largest oilfield services company, operating through four divisions: Digital & Integration, Reservoir Performance, Well Construction, and Production Systems. The company provides technology-driven solutions across the energy lifecycle including drilling, completion, production, carbon management, and digital platforms. Founded in 1926, headquartered in Houston, Texas, with 109,000 employees operating in over 100 countries.

Investment Thesis
SLB is the world's largest and most technologically diversified oilfield services company, with $35.9B in annual revenue, $3.0B in free cash flow, and a strategic vision under CEO Olivier Le Peuch that is transforming the company from traditional services into energy technology [1][3]. At 15.9x forward earnings, SLB trades at a reasonable premium to peers (HAL at 12.7x), justified by its superior international diversification, stronger balance sheet (42.5% D/E vs. HAL's 74.7%), and differentiated digital/AI platform revenue through DELFI and Lumi. The $7.8B ChampionX acquisition adds recurring production chemical revenue that diversifies beyond cyclical drilling activity. The 2.2% dividend yield with a 40+ year payment history and 4 consecutive years of post-2020 growth provides income support.
The bear case centers on weakening organic fundamentals. Excluding the ChampionX acquisition, Q1 2026 revenue declined 7% organically, with GAAP EPS dropping 14% YoY to $0.50 — Middle East disruptions and international project delays are compressing the core business [1]. The 50.7% payout ratio is elevated for a cyclical business and limits financial flexibility if earnings decline further. Profit margin of 9.3% remains below the 20% target, and ROE of 14.1% has not reached the 15% threshold despite significant scale advantages. The energy transition strategy — carbon capture, digital platforms — requires sustained investment but generates uncertain near-term returns. With $11.6B in total debt, a prolonged downturn in international E&P spending could pressure both the balance sheet and the dividend, as demonstrated by the 75% cut in 2020 [5].
Key Catalysts
  1. International E&P spending recovery in the Middle East as geopolitical disruptions ease, unlocking pent-up demand across SLB's largest revenue region
  2. DELFI and Lumi AI platform adoption accelerating digital services revenue growth, with the Digital & Integration division contributing an increasingly higher-margin revenue mix
  3. ChampionX integration delivering $400M+ in targeted synergies while diversifying SLB's revenue toward recurring production chemical streams less sensitive to drilling activity cycles
Key Risks
  1. Organic revenue declined 7% in Q1 2026 excluding ChampionX, and sustained international activity weakness could drive further earnings compression from the current $0.50 quarterly EPS
  2. Energy transition investments in SLB Capturi carbon capture and new energy businesses require sustained capital allocation with uncertain return timelines, potentially diluting near-term margins
  3. The 75% dividend cut in 2020 (from $2.00 to $0.50) demonstrates that the payout is not recession-proof — the current 50.7% payout ratio leaves limited buffer if oilfield services spending enters a prolonged downcycle

AI Resilience (25%)
AI Resilience LOW RISK
8
AI Risk Protection (7/9): SLB's core business — physical oilfield services including drilling, completions, subsea production, and wireline operations — requires heavy equipment and specialized crews operating in extreme environments that AI cannot substitute [1][3]. The company's operations involve managing physical forces thousands of feet underground and on the ocean floor, requiring hardware, engineering expertise, and on-site execution. AI enhances rather than threatens SLB's business by improving drilling efficiency, reservoir characterization, and production optimization through platforms like DELFI and Lumi. No analysts have identified AI substitution risk for oilfield services operations. The modest concern is that AI-driven drilling efficiency could reduce the total number of service hours per well. AI Opportunity (1/1): SLB generates meaningful revenue from AI and digital solutions through its Digital & Integration division, which contributed approximately $4B in 2025 revenue. The DELFI platform and Lumi AI engine are commercial products sold to E&P customers, with Rakesh Jaggi leading a dedicated division focused on digital and AI revenue growth [3].
