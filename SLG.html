Company Profile
Real Estate ·REIT - Office ·NYSE ·United States
SL Green Realty Corp. is Manhattan's largest office landlord, a fully integrated real estate investment trust (REIT) that acquires, manages, and repositions commercial office properties in New York City. The company holds interests in approximately 32 million square feet of wholly owned and joint-venture office space across midtown Manhattan.

Investment Thesis
SL Green offers deep value exposure to a Manhattan office recovery. Q1 2026 leasing volume of 930,000 sqft was the largest first quarter in the company's 28-year history, with 16% rent mark-to-market increases signaling genuine demand recovery for trophy Manhattan offices [1]. FFO guidance of $4.40-$4.70 per share implies a 9.5x FFO multiple at the current $42.47 share price — well below the 15-year average for office REITs — and management projects 10%+ same-store cash NOI growth in 2027 [1]. At a 6.3% dividend yield and with Manhattan's tightest office availability rate in years driving positive leasing spreads, SL Green provides attractive income with upside optionality if the post-COVID office recovery continues to gain momentum.
The bear case centers on structural headwinds to office demand and financial fragility. GAAP losses of $1.20 per share in Q1 2026 (missing estimates of -$0.76) reflect ongoing depreciation, impairment, and interest expense burdens on a highly leveraged balance sheet with 150% D/E [1][4]. The dividend has been cut 55% from its 2021 peak of $6.27 to $2.84 per share, and the GAAP payout ratio of 154% suggests the current dividend may not be sustainable without FFO support. Remote and hybrid work remain structural threats — even as trophy buildings see recovery, overall Manhattan office vacancy rates remain elevated compared to pre-COVID levels. AI-driven productivity improvements could further reduce office space demand per employee over the medium term. Interest rate sensitivity is acute: SL Green's leveraged model means higher-for-longer rates directly compress FFO and limit financial flexibility for development projects like 346 Madison [4]. The stock's decline from a $66.91 52-week high to $42.47 reflects market skepticism about the pace and sustainability of the office recovery.
