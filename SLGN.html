Company Profile
Consumer Cyclical ·Packaging & Containers ·NYSE ·United States
Silgan Holdings Inc. manufactures and sells rigid packaging solutions for consumer goods products globally. It operates through Dispensing and Specialty Closures, Metal Containers, and Custom Containers segments, serving fragrance, beauty, food, beverage, personal care, and household markets. The company operates over 124 manufacturing plants across North America, Europe, Asia, and South America.

Investment Thesis
Silgan Holdings offers a compelling value proposition at 9.66x forward P/E with a 2.13% dividend yield and 30.3% payout ratio, supported by $275M in free cash flow [1][4]. Q1 2026 revenue of $1.56B beat estimates with 6.4% YoY growth, driven by double-digit volume growth in pet food metal containers and continued fragrance/beauty dispensing strength [1]. Management raised full-year adjusted EPS guidance to $3.83 at midpoint, demonstrating confidence in execution. The company's essential consumer goods packaging (food, personal care, household products) provides defensive revenue characteristics. The Inspire Insight score of 42 with 9 positive ESG attributions and zero negative reflects strong sustainability practices [3]. At a PEG ratio of 0.79, Silgan appears undervalued relative to its growth trajectory, and the stock's low beta (0.74) provides portfolio stability.
The bear case centers on elevated leverage, thin margins, and limited growth potential. D/E of 200.8% with $4.66B in total debt reflects an acquisition-heavy capital structure that constrains financial flexibility and increases interest rate sensitivity [4]. Profit margin of just 4.3% leaves minimal room for error — input cost inflation, customer pricing pressure, or volume shortfalls could compress earnings significantly. Revenue growth of 6.5% YoY and 5-year CAGR of 5.1% suggest a mature, low-growth business with limited upside potential. CEO insider selling of $6.84M raises alignment concerns [2]. The packaging industry faces secular headwinds from sustainability regulation, plastic reduction mandates, and consumer preference shifts that could increase compliance costs. Earnings growth of -5.3% in the most recent period shows vulnerability, and the stock trades 4.8% below its 50-day SMA ($41.45), reflecting near-term market caution. Competition from Ball, Crown, and Berry Global in a commoditized industry limits pricing power.
