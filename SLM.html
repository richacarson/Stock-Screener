Company Profile
Financial Services ·Credit Services ·NASDAQ ·United States
SLM Corporation (Sallie Mae) originates and services private education loans to students and their families in the United States. The company also provides retail deposit accounts including high-yield savings, money market accounts, and certificates of deposit. Founded in 1972 and headquartered in Newark, Delaware.

Investment Thesis
Sallie Mae is the dominant U.S. private student lender trading at a deeply discounted 6.9x forward earnings with elite profitability — 45.1% profit margin, 32.3% ROE, and 15% payout ratio [1][3]. Q4 2025 revenue of $454M beat estimates with 16.4% YoY growth, and 2-year EPS CAGR of 20.2% demonstrates accelerating earnings power [1]. The $0.52 annual dividend (2.4% yield) is growing rapidly from a tiny base with a 15% payout ratio providing massive dividend growth runway. The company's technology modernization under CTO Steve Turner positions it to defend against fintech disruption while improving operational efficiency [2].
The bear case centers on regulatory and secular headwinds. Sallie Mae's history of predatory lending practices — including a $1.7B Navient settlement for loans with up to 92% expected default rates — creates ongoing reputational and regulatory risk [3]. The student loan market faces political pressure from federal forgiveness programs, income-driven repayment plans, and bipartisan skepticism of private student lending. D/E of 239% is structurally high for a lender but creates interest rate sensitivity. The stock has declined 37% from its 52-week high of $34.97 to $22.16, and 2026 EPS guidance of $2.75 at midpoint missed analyst estimates by 1% [1]. Revenue growth of 7.3% YoY is modest, and the 5-year CAGR of 2.9% suggests limited organic growth. Student enrollment trends and the rising cost of college could compress loan demand over time.
Key Catalysts
  1. Technology platform modernization enabling lower cost-to-serve and competitive fintech-grade digital lending experience [2]
  2. Dividend growth acceleration from 15% payout ratio — substantial room to increase the $0.52 annual dividend as EPS approaches $3.20 [1][3]
  3. Valuation re-rating from 6.9x forward P/E toward financial services peers at 10-15x as earnings consistency becomes apparent [3]
Key Risks
  1. Political and regulatory risk from federal student loan forgiveness programs and heightened CFPB scrutiny of private lending practices [3]
  2. Fintech disruption from SoFi, Earnest, and AI-driven lending platforms that could erode Sallie Mae's origination market share [2]
  3. Interest rate sensitivity on 239% D/E balance sheet — rising rates compress net interest margin while falling rates reduce loan yields [3]
