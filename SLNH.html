Company Profile
Technology ·Data Center Infrastructure ·NASDAQ ·United States
Soluna Holdings develops green data centers that convert surplus renewable energy into computing resources for cryptocurrency mining, AI training, and high-performance computing. The company operates modular data centers co-located with wind, solar, and hydroelectric power plants, with a 4.3GW development pipeline. Founded in 1961 as Mechanical Technology, rebranded in 2021, headquartered in Albany, New York.

Investment Thesis
Soluna Holdings occupies a compelling niche at the intersection of renewable energy and AI infrastructure — converting wasted renewable power into computing resources at a time when AI data center demand is growing 26-36% annually [2]. The 4.3GW development pipeline, advisory board featuring former Google and AWS/Meta executives, and $88.8M cash position provide runway for growth. The company doubled operating capacity in 2025, surpassed 1GW of renewable-powered computing, and the Helix AI initiative targets the rapidly growing AI training workload market with purpose-built facilities. At $79M market cap, significant upside exists if the company executes on even a fraction of its pipeline [1].
The bear case is severe: Soluna generated only $29.7M revenue in 2025 (down 21.8% YoY) with a staggering -$57M net loss, producing operating margins of -131% and profit margins of -180% [1][4]. The company has a history of aggressive shareholder dilution — $142M raised in 2025 alone — with short interest at 6.66% of float and stock borrowing costs of 26%, signaling significant skepticism. Revenue is heavily dependent on Bitcoin economics (hashprice), which the company cannot control. The sub-$1 stock price creates NASDAQ delisting risk. The pivot from crypto mining to AI infrastructure is unproven at scale, and competing against hyperscalers with $79M market cap is an extreme David vs. Goliath scenario. Forward estimates project continued -$32M losses in 2026 [1][3].
Key Catalysts
  1. Helix AI initiative deploying purpose-built GPU facilities for AI training workloads, potentially commanding higher margins than Bitcoin mining [2]
  2. 4.3GW renewable power pipeline deployment — even partial conversion to operating capacity would transform the revenue base [1]
  3. Rising AI compute demand (26-36% annual growth) increasing the strategic value of co-located renewable-powered data center infrastructure [2]
Key Risks
  1. Persistent cash burn — $57M net loss on $29.7M revenue with projected -$32M losses in 2026, requiring continued dilutive financing [1][3]
  2. Bitcoin hashprice dependency — crypto mining economics directly impact revenue, and the halving cycle creates structural headwinds [1]
  3. NASDAQ delisting risk from sub-$1 stock price combined with shareholder dilution history and 6.66% short interest [3][4]
