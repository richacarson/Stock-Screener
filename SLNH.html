Company Profile
Technology ·Data Center Infrastructure ·NASDAQ ·United States
Soluna Holdings, Inc. develops and operates behind-the-meter, renewable-powered data centers that co-locate with utility-scale wind and solar farms to monetize stranded or curtailed energy. The company provides computing capacity for AI training, Bitcoin mining, and other high-performance workloads. Key projects include Dorothy 1A, Dorothy 2, and Project Kati 1. Recently acquired the 150 MW Briscoe Wind Farm. Formerly Mechanical Technology, Inc., headquartered in Albany, New York.
52 Employees ·www.solunacomputing.com

Investment Thesis
Soluna Holdings occupies a distinctive niche at the intersection of renewable energy and AI computing infrastructure, co-locating data centers with stranded wind and solar power to monetize curtailed energy at below-market electricity costs [1][2]. Q1 2026 revenue grew 58% YoY to $9.4M — the fourth consecutive quarter of sequential growth — with data hosting revenue surging 178% to $6.7M (71% of total) as AI workload demand accelerates [1]. The $53M Briscoe Wind Farm acquisition is projected to add $20-24M in annualized revenue, and the $100M Generate Capital credit line provides growth capital for additional projects. At $199M market cap, the stock trades at 6x TTM revenue — modest for an AI infrastructure play with differentiated renewable positioning. If management can scale to profitability, the behind-the-meter model creates structural cost advantages that hyperscalers cannot easily replicate.
The bear case is substantial. Soluna has never generated positive net income — FY2025 net loss was $57M on $30M revenue, and the TTM loss is $67.5M [4][5]. The company required Nasdaq non-compliance resolution in May 2026 after the stock fell below $1.00, and shares have declined 75% from the 52-week high of $5.14 to $1.26 [3]. At 52 employees managing multiple data center projects and a newly acquired wind farm, execution risk is elevated. The renewable-powered data center market is attracting well-capitalized competitors (Amazon, Microsoft, Google) who are building their own renewable-powered facilities at vastly greater scale. Revenue has been volatile (80% growth in FY2024, -22% decline in FY2025), making forward projections unreliable. Beta of 5.12 signals extreme stock price volatility that compounds the operational uncertainty.
Key Catalysts
  1. Briscoe Wind Farm integration generating projected $20-24M annualized revenue and $6-11M Year-One adjusted EBITDA, potentially doubling the company's revenue base
  2. Helix initiative converting existing data center capacity to higher-margin AI training workloads, riding secular demand growth from AI model training and inference
  3. Sequential revenue growth continuing in Q2-Q4 2026 as Dorothy 2 and Kati 1 ramp to full capacity, potentially demonstrating a clear path to EBITDA breakeven
Key Risks
  1. Persistent cash burn ($67.5M TTM net loss on $33M revenue) with a 52-person team managing multiple large infrastructure projects creates severe execution and liquidity risk despite the current $86M cash position
  2. Stock price volatility (beta 5.12) and recent Nasdaq delisting scare undermine investor confidence and could restrict access to equity capital markets for future growth funding
  3. Hyperscaler competition — Amazon, Microsoft, and Google are building their own renewable-powered data centers at 100x Soluna's scale, potentially commoditizing the behind-the-meter advantage
