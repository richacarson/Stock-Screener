Company Profile
Healthcare ·Biotechnology ·NASDAQ ·United States
Soleno Therapeutics is a biopharmaceutical company focused on rare diseases. Its lead product VYKAT XR (diazoxide choline controlled-release) is the first FDA-approved treatment for hyperphagia in Prader-Willi syndrome (PWS), a rare genetic condition affecting an estimated 300,000-400,000 individuals worldwide. Note: Neurocrine Biosciences announced a definitive agreement to acquire Soleno for $53.00/share ($2.9B) in April 2026.

Investment Thesis
Soleno Therapeutics achieved a remarkable rare disease success story, launching the first-ever FDA-approved treatment for hyperphagia in Prader-Willi syndrome and generating $190.4M in revenue within nine months of commercial launch [1]. VYKAT XR's Q4 2025 revenue of $91.7M implies a $366M annualized run rate with only 12% market penetration, suggesting significant upside potential. The company reached profitability ($20.9M net income) and positive cash flow in its first year of sales, with $506.1M in cash providing a robust balance sheet [1]. CEO Bhatnagar's TIME Magazine recognition and the 98.6% gross margin validate the franchise quality. The Neurocrine acquisition at $53.00/share ($2.9B) represents a 34% premium and validates the platform's value [3].
The bear case centers on the securities fraud litigation and acquisition dynamics. In August 2025, Scorpion Capital published a short report alleging that Soleno's Phase 3 DESTINY PWS trial systematically downplayed evidence of safety concerns including excess fluid retention, elevated diabetes risk, and potential pulmonary edema [3]. A class action lawsuit followed, which could complicate the Neurocrine acquisition or result in liabilities. The $53.00/share acquisition price, while a 34% premium, may undervalue the long-term potential of a first-in-class orphan drug with only 12% market penetration and a $366M+ revenue run rate. Shareholders who believe in the platform's long-term value may view the sale as premature. If the acquisition closes, SLNO is no longer investable for new positions. If the deal fails (litigation, regulatory, or other reasons), the stock could face significant downside given the short seller allegations and ongoing legal proceedings [3].

AI Risk Protection (8/9): Pharmaceutical development and commercialization for rare diseases is highly immune to AI disruption.
