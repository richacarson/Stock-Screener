Company Profile
Financial Services ·Insurance Brokers ·NYSE ·United States
SelectQuote is a direct-to-consumer insurance distribution company specializing in Medicare Advantage, Medicare Supplement, life, and auto/home insurance policies. The company also operates SelectRx, a pharmacy services segment. SelectQuote connects consumers with insurance carriers through a technology-enabled platform staffed by licensed agents. Founded in 1985 and headquartered in Overland Park, Kansas.
4,269 Employees ·www.selectquote.com

Investment Thesis
SelectQuote offers deep value at 4.3x forward earnings with a $1.62B revenue base growing 12% YoY and SelectRx pharmacy growing 26% [1]. The company has demonstrated successful diversification from life insurance into Medicare Advantage distribution and pharmacy services, achieving a 26% three-year revenue CAGR. D/E of 70.4% and ROE of 16.4% reflect adequate financial health, and the recent $415M refinancing extended debt maturities [1]. The senior segment delivered near-record EBITDA margins of 39%, demonstrating operational efficiency. Operating cash flow is expected to swing positive to $25-35M in FY2026 from -$12M in FY2025, signaling improving free cash flow generation [1].
The bear case is dominated by legal and regulatory risk. The U.S. Department of Justice filed a False Claims Act complaint against SelectQuote for alleged misrepresentations in Medicare Advantage sales practices, triggering a 20% single-day stock decline and a securities class action lawsuit from Hagens Berman [3]. The stock has fallen over 40% in six months, reflecting the market's assessment of material litigation exposure. FY2026 guidance was lowered by $40M-$50M due to a national carrier's marketing budget cuts and PBM reimbursement changes, revealing dependency on carrier relationships [1]. Profit margin of 4.8% leaves minimal room for error in a business facing regulatory scrutiny over sales practices. The $436M debt load represents 2.7x the $160M market cap, creating leverage risk if revenue growth stalls or legal penalties materialize. At $0.91 per share, the stock is priced for distress — either the DOJ case resolves favorably and the stock re-rates significantly, or penalties and behavioral remediation permanently impair the business model [3][4].
Key Catalysts
  1. DOJ False Claims Act case resolution — a favorable outcome or settlement could remove the primary overhang and trigger significant stock re-rating from 4.3x forward earnings [3]
  2. SelectRx pharmacy segment growing 26% YoY with expanding contribution margins, diversifying revenue beyond commission-dependent insurance distribution [1]

Key Risks
  1. DOJ False Claims Act complaint alleging systemic misrepresentation in Medicare Advantage sales — potential penalties, behavioral remediation, and reputational damage could permanently impair the core business model [3]
  2. Securities class action lawsuit alleging inflated revenue and earnings disclosures during the class period, adding legal costs and management distraction [3]
  3. Carrier concentration risk — $40M FY2026 guidance reduction from a single national carrier's marketing budget cuts demonstrates dependency on a small number of insurance carrier relationships [1]
