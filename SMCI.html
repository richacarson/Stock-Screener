Investment Thesis
Supermicro is the cheapest pure-play AI infrastructure stock at 6.9x forward earnings with $40B+ FY2026 revenue guidance, 123% YoY growth, and $13B in Blackwell Ultra backlog [1]. The company's rack-scale liquid cooling expertise and speed-to-market positioning make it a critical supplier to hyperscale data center buildouts. If AI capex continues accelerating — with Microsoft, Google, and Meta each guiding $75B+ in annual spend — SMCI sits at the epicenter of the infrastructure boom at a fraction of peer valuations.
The bear case is severe and multifaceted: a co-founder was arrested in March 2026 for smuggling $2.5B in Nvidia chips to China, the stock has lost 67% from its 52-week high, gross margins have collapsed to 6.4%, and 63% customer concentration creates existential revenue risk [1][3]. The pattern of governance failures — 2006 Iran exports, 2020 SEC accounting charges, 2024 EY auditor resignation, 2026 smuggling indictment — suggests systemic compliance risk that could ultimately threaten the company's access to Nvidia GPUs and its listing status.
