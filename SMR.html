Company Profile
Industrials ·Specialty Industrial Machinery ·NYSE ·United States
NuScale Power is the first and only small modular reactor (SMR) technology company to receive U.S. NRC design approval, including the uprated 77 MWe NuScale Power Module. The company targets nuclear power for AI data centers, advanced manufacturing, and defense applications, with ENTRA1 as its exclusive global commercialization partner.

Investment Thesis
NuScale holds the only NRC-approved SMR design globally and is in discussions with a majority of tier-one hyperscalers seeking carbon-free baseload power for AI data centers [2]. ENTRA1's 6 GW TVA agreement, if executed, would represent transformative revenue. Analyst consensus price target of $21 (median) implies 83% upside, with bulls at $60. Zacks projects 2026 revenue of $91.1M (202% growth). $1.3B in liquidity provides multi-year runway. The secular tailwind of nuclear renaissance for AI power demand could catalyze significant re-rating.
The bear case is severe: NuScale has zero binding power purchase agreements, a securities fraud class action lawsuit over the $495M ENTRA1 payment, and $355.8M net losses on just $31.5M TTM revenue [1][3]. The ENTRA1 partner allegedly 'had never built, financed, or operated any significant project,' calling into question the company's primary commercialization strategy. Revenue declined 94.7% as licensing income dried up. The stock is down 63% in six months. Nuclear waste concerns, regulatory uncertainty, and the broader nuclear ESG debate create additional headwinds. Without a signed customer, NuScale's future remains entirely speculative.
Key Catalysts
  1. First binding power purchase agreement with a hyperscaler or utility would be the most significant de-risking event, proving commercial viability
  2. ENTRA1 TVA 6 GW collaborative agreement progression from non-binding to binding commitments with construction timelines
  3. Resolution of the securities fraud class action lawsuit removing legal uncertainty overhang from the stock
Key Risks
  1. Securities fraud class action lawsuit (April 20, 2026 lead plaintiff deadline) over $495M ENTRA1 payment creates significant legal and reputational risk
  2. Zero binding power purchase agreements — all revenue projections depend on future contract execution that is not guaranteed
  3. Nuclear waste management, water usage, and broader ESG concerns continue to limit institutional investor appetite for nuclear technology companies
