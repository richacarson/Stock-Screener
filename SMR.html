Company Profile
Industrials ·Specialty Industrial Machinery ·NYSE ·United States
NuScale Power Corporation develops small modular reactor (SMR) technology for nuclear energy. Its NuScale Power Module generates 77 MWe of electricity using light water reactor technology with passive safety systems. The company is the only SMR design to receive NRC design certification and is pursuing deployment through strategic partner ENTRA1 Energy, including a potential 6 GW program with the Tennessee Valley Authority.

Investment Thesis
NuScale Power holds the only NRC-certified small modular reactor design in the United States, positioning the company at the center of the nuclear renaissance driven by AI data center power demand. The ENTRA1 Energy/TVA partnership targeting up to 6 GW of SMR capacity represents the largest nuclear deployment program in U.S. history, with revenue projected to grow 49% annually over the next three years [3]. A $1.2 billion cash position provides substantial runway through commercialization [5].
The investment case requires patience and tolerance for pre-revenue risk. Q1 2026 revenue was only $0.6 million, and the company remains deeply unprofitable with -83% ROE [5]. The failed UAMPS project (canceled in 2023 after costs more than doubled to $9.3 billion) highlights execution risk in nuclear deployment [7]. NRC safety concerns about the steam generator design and boron depletion in passive cooling systems remain unresolved technical risks [8]. The stock has been highly volatile (beta 2.25) as market sentiment oscillates between nuclear enthusiasm and execution skepticism. For IOWN's framework, the exceptional AI Resilience score and strong innovation profile are offset by fundamentally weak infrastructure metrics, making this a high-conviction, high-risk holding.
Key Catalysts
  1. TVA Power Purchase Agreement expected by year-end 2026 would validate the commercial viability of NuScale SMRs and unlock the 6 GW pipeline [3]
  2. Growing hyperscaler interest in nuclear power for AI data centers — NuScale reports majority of tier-one hyperscalers have expressed interest in SMR-powered facilities [6]
  3. Revenue inflection expected as analyst estimates project $95.7 million for full-year 2026 and 49% annual growth for three years, driven by licensing and engineering services [3]
Key Risks
  1. Nuclear deployment execution risk — the UAMPS project's cost doubling to $9.3 billion and eventual cancellation in 2023 demonstrates how nuclear construction costs can spiral beyond projections [7]
  2. NRC safety concerns about passive emergency cooling systems and boron depletion could delay or prevent additional design approvals needed for commercial deployment [8]
  3. Competition from alternative SMR designs (GE Hitachi BWRX-300, TerraPower, X-energy) and from established large reactor builders could erode NuScale's first-mover advantage
