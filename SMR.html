Company Profile
Industrials ·Specialty Industrial Machinery ·NYSE ·United States
NuScale Power is the first and only small modular reactor (SMR) technology company to receive U.S. NRC design approval, including the uprated 77 MWe NuScale Power Module. The company targets nuclear power generation for AI data centers, advanced manufacturing, and defense applications, with ENTRA1 Energy as its exclusive global commercialization partner and a collaborative agreement with TVA for up to 6 GW of SMR capacity.

Investment Thesis
NuScale holds the only NRC-approved SMR design globally and occupies a unique position at the intersection of two secular megatrends: nuclear renaissance and AI data center power demand. The ENTRA1/TVA collaborative agreement for up to 6 GW of SMR capacity — potentially the largest nuclear deployment program in U.S. history — represents transformative revenue if definitized as expected by year-end 2026 [1]. With $1.2B in cash providing multi-year runway and management reporting discussions with a majority of tier-one hyperscalers, the company has both the resources and the market opportunity to capitalize on its first-mover regulatory advantage [1][2]. Analysts project 2026 revenue of ~$96M with significant acceleration into 2027.
The bear case is substantial: NuScale has zero binding power purchase agreements, Q1 2026 revenue of just $0.6M (down 95.5% YoY), and no reactor has ever been built using its technology [1]. The cancelled UAMPS project demonstrated that NRC approval alone does not guarantee commercial viability — costs escalated from $5.3B to $9.3B before the project was abandoned [3]. The securities fraud class action lawsuit over the $495M ENTRA1 payment creates legal and reputational overhang. NRC safety concerns about steam generator wear and the broader nuclear waste debate limit institutional investor appetite. At a $4.3B market cap on effectively zero commercial revenue, the stock prices in significant execution that remains entirely speculative, and the 3-year path to first power generation means investors face extended uncertainty with substantial dilution risk from the at-the-market offering program [4].
Key Catalysts
  1. TVA/ENTRA1 power purchase agreement definitization expected by year-end 2026 would be the most significant commercial de-risking event, converting the 6 GW framework into binding revenue commitments
  2. First binding contract with a tier-one hyperscaler for AI data center power would validate the nuclear-for-AI thesis and potentially catalyze additional agreements
  3. Resolution of the securities fraud class action lawsuit removing legal uncertainty overhang and restoring investor confidence in governance
Key Risks
  1. Zero binding commercial contracts — all revenue projections depend on future PPA execution, and the UAMPS cancellation precedent demonstrates that even advanced agreements can collapse
  2. Securities fraud class action lawsuit over $495M ENTRA1 payment creates significant legal liability and raises governance questions about the commercialization partnership
  3. Nuclear construction cost escalation risk and 3-5 year timeline to first power generation mean investors face extended cash burn with no guarantee of commercial success
