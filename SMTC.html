Company Profile
Technology ·Semiconductors ·NASDAQ ·United States
Semtech designs and sells analog and mixed-signal semiconductors, IoT systems, and cloud connectivity solutions. Key product lines include signal integrity chips for data center optical transceivers, LoRa wireless technology for IoT, and protection/sensing devices for infrastructure and industrial applications. The company crossed $1B in annual revenue in FY2026.

Investment Thesis
Semtech is a semiconductor growth story at an inflection point — record FY2026 revenue of $1.05B (up 15% YoY) was driven by a 58% surge in data center revenues and 34% LoRa IoT growth, positioning the company at the intersection of two secular trends: AI infrastructure and IoT connectivity [1]. The company's CopperEdge and FiberEdge signal integrity products are critical components in 800G/1.6T optical transceivers powering AI data center interconnects, with 'material design win progress' suggesting a strong design pipeline [1][2]. Adjusted EPS of $1.71 grew 94% YoY, and Q1 FY2027 guidance of $283M projects continued momentum. At $103 per share with a $9.6B market cap, the PEG of 1.21 suggests reasonable valuation relative to the growth trajectory [4].
The bear case focuses on profitability and execution risk. GAAP profit margin remains negative at -3.8% and ROE is -7.4% due to Sierra Wireless acquisition amortization and integration costs, meaning the company has not yet proven it can generate GAAP profits consistently [4]. Forward P/E of 35.5x requires sustained high growth to justify, and any slowdown in AI capex spending could disproportionately impact data center revenues that drove FY2026 results. D/E of 94.2% with $518M debt reflects the acquisition hangover that constrains balance sheet flexibility [4]. Three CEO changes in three years (Maheswaran, Pickle, Hou) signal governance instability, and the company faces intense competition from Broadcom, Marvell, and MaxLinear in the data center optical interconnect market. The 2.06 beta means SMTC amplifies market volatility, creating significant drawdown risk in risk-off environments [4].
