Company Profile
Technology ·Solar ·NASDAQ ·United States
SolarMax Technology is an integrated solar and renewable energy company offering residential/commercial solar installation, LED systems, and utility-scale EPC (Engineering, Procurement, Construction) services for solar farm and battery energy storage projects. The company has secured major utility-scale contracts including a $127.3M battery storage project in Texas and $158.3M in Puerto Rico EPC agreements. Founded in 2008 and headquartered in Riverside, California.

Investment Thesis
SolarMax has undergone a dramatic revenue transformation, surging from $23M (FY2024) to $91M (FY2025) driven by utility-scale EPC contracts including the $127.3M Pecos County battery storage project and $158.3M in Puerto Rico BESS agreements [1]. At a $36M market cap, the stock trades at just 0.4x trailing revenue with a potential $285M+ contract backlog that could deliver multi-year revenue visibility. The company is positioned at the intersection of two secular growth drivers — renewable energy infrastructure mandates and AI data center power demand — with its BESS systems marketed for grid support and AI/data center energy needs [2]. The IRA (Inflation Reduction Act) provides tax credit tailwinds for battery storage projects, and grid resilience needs in Puerto Rico and Texas create recurring EPC demand.
The bear case is severe. SolarMax operates with razor-thin 4.6% gross margins on utility-scale EPC contracts, meaning even minor cost overruns or delays could eliminate profitability entirely [4]. The company is unprofitable with a -6.95% profit margin and negative $6.3M net loss in FY2025 despite the revenue surge [1]. Daily trading liquidity of just $135K makes the stock effectively untradable for any institutional investor, and the 75% decline from 52-week highs signals deep market skepticism. With only 79 employees, SolarMax faces significant execution risk on $100M+ contracts that require managing large construction crews, supply chains, and regulatory approvals across multiple states. The company competes against well-capitalized giants (NextEra, Fluence) that can underprice projects to build volume. D/E and ROE data are unavailable, suggesting balance sheet opacity, and the $27M in total debt against an $8.5M cash position creates financial fragility [4]. Customer complaints about project timelines raise execution credibility concerns [3].
