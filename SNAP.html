Investment Thesis
Snap is showing improving financial execution with Q1 2026 revenue up 12% YoY to $1.529B, adjusted EBITDA more than doubling to $233M, and free cash flow of $286M (up 151%) [1]. The Snapchat+ subscription business surged 87% YoY to $285M in other revenue, reducing advertising dependence. Forward P/E of 8.4x is deeply discounted for a platform with 483M DAUs and 956M MAUs approaching 1 billion [4]. Project Phoenix AI camera integration and ML-driven ad optimization could accelerate growth, and Spiegel's statement that AI is 'probably the best thing that has ever happened to Snap' suggests the company views AI as an enabler [2]. At $6.08 (~$10B market cap), Snap trades at just 1.7x trailing revenue.
The bear case is severe: Snap remains unprofitable with a -$89M Q1 net loss, -6.7% profit margins, -18.6% ROE, and $3.6B in convertible debt driving 201.6% D/E [1][4]. The Perplexity AI partnership ending in Q1 removed a potential growth driver, and Middle East geopolitical headwinds are pressuring Q2 guidance [1]. Child safety controversies pose existential regulatory risk — the EU Commission's formal DSA investigation, Utah's lawsuit, and New Mexico's allegations of enabling sextortion and drug sales on the platform could result in costly compliance requirements or operational restrictions targeting Snap's core teen demographic [3]. North America eCPMs remain under pressure despite volume growth. Competition from TikTok, Instagram, and YouTube for Gen Z attention is intensifying, and Spiegel's own admission that 'there's no moat in software' raises fundamental questions about long-term defensibility [2].
Key Catalysts
  1. Project Phoenix AI camera integration in H2 2026 could differentiate Snapchat's creative tools and drive user engagement through generative AI-powered AR experiences
  2. Continued Snapchat+ subscription momentum beyond 87% YoY growth could establish a durable non-advertising revenue stream approaching $1B+ annually
  3. Path to GAAP profitability if Q1's $233M adjusted EBITDA trajectory continues improving — forward P/E of 8.4x would re-rate significantly on sustained positive earnings
Key Risks
  1. EU DSA investigation and multiple state lawsuits over child safety could result in regulatory restrictions, fines, or mandatory redesigns that constrain Snap's ability to serve its core teen demographic
  2. Structural unprofitability — 14 years post-founding, Snap has never achieved sustained GAAP profitability; -6.7% profit margin and -18.6% ROE demonstrate ongoing capital destruction
  3. AI-accelerated competitive threats — Spiegel's admission that 'there's no moat in software' and AI making features easier to copy undermines Snap's long-term defensibility against Meta and TikTok
