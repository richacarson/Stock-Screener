Company Profile
Industrials ·Airlines ·NASDAQ ·United States
Sun Country Airlines is a hybrid low-cost carrier based in Minneapolis operating scheduled passenger, charter, and cargo services across the U.S. and Latin America. The company operates a fleet of 70 Boeing 737-NG aircraft (47 passenger, 20 cargo, 3 leased). Sun Country has a significant Amazon Air cargo partnership. In January 2026, Allegiant Travel announced plans to acquire Sun Country for approximately $1.5 billion, pending regulatory approval.

Investment Thesis
Sun Country Airlines is a unique hybrid carrier trading at an attractive 8.9x forward P/E with projected earnings growth of 36.8% annually, driven by the Amazon Air cargo partnership expected to roughly double cargo revenue by 2027 [1]. The pending Allegiant Travel acquisition at approximately $1.5B provides a near-term price floor and potential premium [4]. Record 2025 revenue of $1.13B with strong Q1 2026 demand trends confirm the business is performing well. The hybrid model — combining leisure passenger, Amazon cargo, and charter operations across 70 737-NG aircraft — creates revenue diversification unusual for a sub-$1B market cap carrier [5]. Forward EPS of $1.84 versus trailing $0.96 implies significant margin expansion as the cargo ramp continues. The Inspire score of 32 (ALIGNED) with zero negative attributions supports faith-based ownership.
The bear case is dominated by acquisition uncertainty and airline industry risks. The Allegiant acquisition faces regulatory scrutiny from federal antitrust authorities, and if blocked, the stock could decline significantly from acquisition-premium levels [4]. The airline industry is notoriously cyclical with thin margins — Sun Country's 4.7% profit margin and 6.5% operating margin leave virtually no cushion for fuel price spikes, demand disruptions, or economic recession [5]. Earnings declined 37.9% in Q4 2025 despite 7.9% revenue growth, demonstrating the margin volatility inherent in airlines. D/E of 94.7% and beta of 1.59 reflect significant financial and market risk. The Amazon cargo partnership creates customer concentration — if Amazon shifts strategy or internalizes more air capacity, Sun Country's cargo revenue could be at risk. Free cash flow of just $25.1M provides limited buffer for fleet investments or demand shocks. If the acquisition closes, investors may face reinvestment risk as a premium carrier is absorbed into Allegiant's ultra-low-cost model.
Key Catalysts
  1. Pending Allegiant Travel acquisition at ~$1.5B provides a near-term price floor and potential premium above current levels, pending regulatory approval [4]
  2. Amazon Air cargo partnership ramping to roughly double cargo revenue by 2027, driving projected 36.8% annual earnings growth [1]
  3. Forward P/E of 8.9x with strong Q1 2026 demand trends and scheduled service growth later in 2026 suggest meaningful earnings expansion ahead [1]
Key Risks
  1. Allegiant acquisition regulatory risk — if federal regulators block the $1.5B deal, the stock could decline sharply from acquisition-premium levels [4]
  2. Thin margins and airline cyclicality — 4.7% profit margin and -37.9% earnings decline in Q4 2025 despite revenue growth demonstrate extreme operating leverage [5]
  3. Amazon customer concentration — the cargo partnership is a key growth driver, but dependence on a single customer for cargo operations creates concentration risk if Amazon shifts strategy [1]
