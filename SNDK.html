Company Profile
Technology ·Computer Hardware ·NASDAQ ·United States
Sandisk Corporation is a pure-play flash memory and SSD company spun off from Western Digital in late 2024. Through its joint venture with Kioxia, SanDisk designs, manufactures, and sells NAND flash storage solutions for consumer, enterprise, and data center applications, including high-capacity SSDs critical to AI training and inference infrastructure.

Investment Thesis
SanDisk at 10.7x forward earnings is a rare opportunity to own a direct AI infrastructure beneficiary at a deeply discounted valuation. The company's Q3 FY2026 revenue of $5.95B — up 251% YoY — and Q4 guidance of $7.75-8.25B demonstrate that AI data center buildout is translating into explosive, tangible revenue growth [1]. With forward EPS of $183 against a $1,959 stock price, the market is pricing in significant earnings deceleration that may not materialize given the multi-year AI infrastructure investment cycle. The near-zero D/E ratio of 1.5%, 39.3% ROE, and 34.2% profit margin reflect a business with exceptional financial quality [4]. The Kioxia JV provides manufacturing scale and technology leadership in NAND flash, a market where barriers to entry are measured in tens of billions of dollars.
The bear case centers on the inherent cyclicality of memory semiconductor markets. NAND flash has historically experienced brutal boom-bust cycles driven by capacity overbuild, and the current AI-driven supercycle could end abruptly if hyperscaler capital expenditure decelerates [4]. The trailing P/E of 66.9x reflects a business where current earnings are still catching up to the revenue surge, creating vulnerability if growth disappoints. SanDisk's reliance on the Kioxia JV introduces joint venture execution risk, and competition from Samsung and Micron in enterprise SSDs could compress margins as capacity comes online. As a newly independent company with limited operating history, SanDisk has not yet proven its ability to manage through a downturn as a standalone entity. The low Inspiration score (3/10) and Finite Game mindset (4/10) reflect a company that excels at financial execution but has not yet developed the cultural depth or purpose-driven leadership that sustains long-term outperformance.
Key Catalysts
  1. Q4 FY2026 revenue guidance of $7.75-8.25B with non-GAAP EPS of $30-33 would confirm continued acceleration and could trigger further earnings estimate revisions upward [1]
  2. Sustained hyperscaler AI capital expenditure through 2027-2028 extends the enterprise SSD demand cycle, with each new AI model generation requiring larger storage footprints for training data and inference caching
  3. Next-generation NAND technology transitions through the Kioxia JV could improve cost-per-bit economics and expand margins while competitors face capex headwinds [2]
Key Risks
  1. NAND flash markets are historically cyclical — a supply glut from industry capacity expansion or a pullback in hyperscaler AI spending could trigger sharp ASP declines and margin compression, as seen in the 2022-2023 downturn [4]
  2. The trailing P/E of 66.9x creates vulnerability to multiple compression if quarterly revenue growth decelerates from the current 251% YoY pace, even if absolute growth remains strong [1]
  3. Kioxia JV dependency introduces execution and governance risk — disagreements on capacity investment timing or technology roadmap could constrain SanDisk's ability to capitalize on demand surges [2]
