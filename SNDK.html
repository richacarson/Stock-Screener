Company Profile
Technology ·Semiconductors & Semiconductor Equipment ·NASDAQ ·United States
Sandisk Corporation is the only Western-based pure-play NAND flash memory manufacturer of scale, spun off from Western Digital in February 2025. The company designs, manufactures (via its Kioxia joint venture), and sells NAND-based SSDs, memory cards, and storage solutions for consumer, enterprise, and AI data center markets. Its BiCS8 218-layer 3D NAND technology and pivot toward AI infrastructure storage positions it as a beneficiary of the AI compute buildout.

Investment Thesis
SanDisk is the purest Western-listed way to invest in AI storage demand without NAND commodity volatility dilution from HDD exposure. Q2 FY2026 revenue grew 61% YoY to $3.03B with gross margins hitting 51.1%—a 20-point improvement from its final Western Digital years—while Q3 guidance calls for $4.4-4.8B revenue at 65-67% gross margins[2]. The AI data center buildout structurally increases NAND content per server, and hyperscaler customers are signing 1-5 year supply agreements providing unprecedented visibility[1]. With no HDD drag and a focused management team, SanDisk can compound earnings rapidly as BiCS10 technology ramps.
The bear case is that NAND is a cyclical commodity and SanDisk's current valuation (~$710/share, >35x forward earnings) prices in an extended supercycle that may be interrupted by Samsung or Micron supply discipline breaking down. Historical NAND cycles have compressed prices 50-70% from peak, and SanDisk has no pricing power in a glut. The company has limited operational history as an independent entity (<2 years), limited financial track record, and the spin-off structure means Western Digital retains a secondary stock offering overhang ($3.08B secondary in February 2026)[1]. At current prices, the margin of safety is thin.
Key Catalysts
  1. BiCS10 production ramp ahead of schedule potentially delivering 15%+ cost reduction per bit in H2 2026
  2. Hyperscaler long-term supply contracts extending visibility through 2028 reducing investor concern about NAND cycle downturn
  3. High-bandwidth flash (HBF) prototype delivery targeting inference workloads, enabling premium pricing above standard SSD
Key Risks
  1. NAND price correction triggered by Samsung supply ramp or demand softness in consumer electronics erasing current 65%+ gross margin gains
  2. Kioxia joint venture production disruption or renegotiation risk at the 2034 renewal window
  3. Stock trading at >35x forward earnings leaves no margin of safety if Q3-Q4 FY2026 guidance is missed or guided down
