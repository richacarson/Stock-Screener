Company Profile
Healthcare ·Biotechnology ·NASDAQ ·United States
Syndax Pharmaceuticals is a commercial-stage biopharmaceutical company developing therapies for cancer treatment. Its lead products are Revuforj (revumenib), a menin inhibitor for relapsed or refractory acute leukemia, and Niktimvo (axatilimab-csfr), a CSF-1 receptor blocking antibody for chronic graft-versus-host disease. Both products received FDA approval and launched commercially in 2024-2025.
298 Employees ·syndax.com

Investment Thesis
Syndax is in the early stages of a promising commercial ramp with Revuforj (revumenib) generating $124.8M in FY2025 net revenue and Q4 revenue of $44.2M representing 38% sequential growth [1]. Analyst consensus projects $376.6M in 2026 revenue, a 238% increase, as Revuforj gains traction in relapsed/refractory KMT2A-rearranged acute leukemia and Niktimvo generates collaboration revenue for chronic GVHD [1]. Both products address underserved patient populations with novel mechanisms of action — Revuforj as a first-in-class menin inhibitor and Niktimvo as a CSF-1 receptor blocker — creating defensible market positions. Management expects existing cash, product revenue, and collaboration income to fund operations to profitability, eliminating near-term dilution risk [1].
The bear case for Syndax centers on the fundamental challenges of an early-commercial biotech with deeply negative financials. Net loss was $285M in FY2025 on just $172M in revenue, with D/E of 534% and profit margin of -166% reflecting the company's pre-profitability investment phase [4]. The path to profitability requires sustained revenue acceleration while controlling the $400M guided 2026 expense base (excluding $50M stock comp) [1]. Competition in the menin inhibitor space is intensifying, with Kura Oncology's ziftomenib advancing and potentially offering comparable efficacy with a differentiated safety profile. Revuforj's current approval is limited to relapsed/refractory patients, a small addressable market — expansion into earlier-line settings is essential but uncertain. At $1.85B market cap with no near-term profitability, the stock is priced for execution, and any clinical setback in label expansion or competitive loss would severely impact valuation [4]. The negative forward P/E of -41x underscores that analysts do not expect GAAP profitability in 2026.
