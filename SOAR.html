Company Profile
Industrials ·Airports & Air Services ·NYSEAMERICAN ·United States
Volato Group, Inc. operates as a private aviation company offering fractional ownership, aircraft management, jet cards, deposit programs, and charter services in the United States. The company also provides Mission Control, a cloud-based SaaS platform for flight scheduling, CRM, and crew management, and the Vaunt SaaS subscription platform for aviation operators. Recently pivoting toward AI infrastructure and technology transactions through its Parslee AI platform. Founded in 2021, headquartered in Houston, Texas.
13 Employees ·www.flyvolato.com

Investment Thesis
Volato Group presents a speculative micro-cap opportunity with a P/E of just 2.77 on TTM earnings of $2.1M, and the Vaunt SaaS platform growing 221% YoY to $4.0M ARR as of May 2026 [1]. FY2025 revenue doubled to $78.6M with the company achieving its first-ever net profit ($0.85M), demonstrating that the business model can generate positive returns when revenue is sufficient [4]. The $2.2M strategic investment from institutional investors signals outside confidence in the company's AI infrastructure pivot, and the Parslee AI platform and Volato AI initiative could provide upside optionality in the growing AI services market.
The bear case is severe. At $5.78M market cap and $0.176 per share (down 96% from the 52-week high of $4.36), Volato is a penny stock with near-zero institutional ownership and extreme price volatility [1]. Revenue has been wildly inconsistent — $96.7M (FY2022) to $35.6M (FY2023) to $78.6M (FY2025), with TTM already declining to $54.1M [4]. Shareholders' equity is negative (-$0.45M), cash is a precarious $1.9M, and the company terminated its prior M2i Global strategic deal while evaluating vague AI-related letters of intent [1][3]. With only 13 employees managing an aviation business, execution capacity is severely limited. The ESG controversy around using aircraft resources for cryptocurrency mining raises questions about management credibility [3]. Average daily dollar volume of just $1.26M barely passes the IOWN liquidity floor, making meaningful position entry and exit nearly impossible.
Key Catalysts

  2. Successful closure of AI-related letters of intent could unlock a higher-value business model beyond traditional aviation services, re-rating the stock from penny-stock territory

Key Risks

  2. Revenue volatility is extreme ($96.7M to $35.6M to $78.6M over three years with TTM declining to $54.1M), making forward estimates unreliable and reflecting possible dependence on lumpy aircraft transactions
  3. Strategic uncertainty — termination of M2i Global deal and vague pivot toward AI infrastructure suggests management is searching for a viable path rather than executing a clear strategy
