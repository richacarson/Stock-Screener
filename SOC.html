Company Profile
Energy ·Oil & Gas Drilling ·NYSE ·United States
Sable Offshore Corp. is a Houston-based independent oil and gas company that acquired ExxonMobil's Santa Ynez Unit — three offshore platforms (Harmony, Heritage, Hondo) and associated pipeline infrastructure off the coast of Santa Barbara, California. The company is attempting to restart production shut down since 2015 following a major oil spill, with first commercial sales expected by April 2026 at 50,000 Bbls/d.

Investment Thesis
Sable Offshore represents a high-risk, high-reward binary bet on restarting the Santa Ynez Unit at 50,000 Bbls/d. If successful, the company would generate substantial cash flow from an asset acquired for $988M with significant remaining reserves, increasing California's domestic crude supply by 17% [1]. Oil flow resumed on March 14, 2026 under a Trump administration Defense Production Act order, with first commercial sales expected by April 1, 2026 and 540,000 barrels already in storage [1]. At a forward P/E of 8.5x, the stock prices in significant production earnings if regulatory and legal obstacles are resolved. CEO Flores's 40+ years of oil industry experience and the management team's deep operational expertise provide operational credibility for executing the restart [2].
The bear case is severe and multi-dimensional. Sable faces felony criminal charges from the Santa Barbara District Attorney, a record $18M fine from the California Coastal Commission for defying cease-and-desist orders, a county permit denial, and ongoing lawsuits from environmental groups [3]. The pipeline infrastructure is the same system that caused the 2015 Refugio oil spill — one of the worst in California history — and the State Fire Marshal has flagged that corrosion repairs do not meet restart standards [3]. The balance sheet carries $921.6M in short-term debt with only $97.7M cash and a going concern warning [1]. The $896.6M term loan requires refinancing, and oil prices near $60/barrel challenge project economics. California Governor Newsom has explicitly called the restart illegal and state regulators continue to fight the federal DPA order in court. Even with production flowing, sustained operations depend on surviving legal challenges that could shut operations at any time [3].
Key Catalysts
  1. First commercial sales by April 1, 2026 at 50,000 Bbls/d generating immediate cash flow from 540,000 barrels in storage and ongoing production [1]
  2. Successful refinancing of the $896.6M term loan, removing going concern risk and enabling commodity hedging and potential shareholder returns [1]
  3. Platform Hondo restart in June 2026 adding incremental production capacity beyond the initial Harmony and Heritage platforms [1]
Key Risks
  1. Criminal felony charges and $18M in regulatory fines — Santa Barbara DA has filed five felony counts for environmental violations, and the California Coastal Commission's record fine signals unresolved legal liability [3]
  2. Going concern risk from $921.6M short-term debt with only $97.7M cash — failure to refinance the $896.6M term loan could force asset forfeiture back to ExxonMobil [1]
  3. Judicial injunction risk — a Santa Barbara judge has signaled intent to rule against Sable's pipeline restart, and California state agencies continue to challenge the federal DPA order in court [3]
