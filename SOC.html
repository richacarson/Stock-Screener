Company Profile
Energy ·Oil & Gas Drilling ·NYSE ·United States
Sable Offshore Corp. is an oil and gas company focused on restarting production from the Santa Ynez Unit offshore California, which was shut down following a 2015 pipeline oil spill. The company acquired the Santa Ynez Unit assets in February 2024 and began restarting production in 2025-2026, leveraging a Trump administration emergency order to resume pipeline operations. The Santa Ynez Unit includes three offshore platforms (Hondo, Harmony, Heritage) and associated onshore processing facilities.

Investment Thesis
Sable Offshore is a high-risk, high-reward turnaround play on restarting the Santa Ynez Unit offshore California. The company has successfully resumed production in 2026 after securing a Trump administration emergency order, with 40 wells currently producing and plans to ramp to 74 wells with Platform Hondo coming online [1]. Management projects 2027 net production of 47.5-52.5 MBoe/d and EBITDA of $738M-$985M, which at the midpoint would represent extraordinary value creation from the $7.93 stock price — the forward P/E of 2.88 reflects this optionality [1]. CEO James Flores's 40+ years of industry experience and the management team's California operating expertise provide operational credibility [2]. The company is selling crude to Chevron and has raised $545M in capital to fund the restart.
The risks are extraordinary and multidimensional. The 2015 pipeline oil spill that shut down the Santa Ynez Unit (142,800 gallons of crude into the Pacific) led to criminal charges against the previous operator, and environmental groups warn the restarted pipeline has a 1-in-4 annual rupture probability [3]. California state regulators accuse Sable of 'cutting corners' in the restart, and a Santa Barbara judge tentatively ruled that the Trump administration's emergency order was insufficient to override state environmental authority [3]. If courts ultimately block the pipeline, Sable has no alternative path to market for its crude. D/E of 232% and $980M debt against near-zero revenue create severe financial risk. The stock has fallen from $13.05 (50-day SMA) to $7.93, reflecting market concern about legal and regulatory headwinds. This is a binary outcome bet — either production ramps to plan and the stock is dramatically undervalued, or legal challenges shut down the pipeline and equity value is at risk.
Key Catalysts
  1. Platform Hondo coming online by June 2026 and production ramping from 40 to 74 wells would validate the operational restart plan and move toward the 47.5-52.5 MBoe/d 2027 production target
  2. Favorable court rulings on California's legal challenges to the pipeline restart would remove the primary regulatory overhang and potentially re-rate the stock toward the EBITDA-implied valuation
  3. First meaningful quarterly revenue and earnings in Q2-Q3 2026 as Chevron crude sales ramp would demonstrate the economic viability of the Santa Ynez Unit restart
Key Risks
  1. California legal challenges could shut down pipeline operations — a Santa Barbara judge tentatively ruled against the Trump administration's emergency order, and environmental groups are pursuing multiple legal avenues to block production
  2. The 2015 pipeline that spilled 142,800 gallons of crude is the same infrastructure Sable is restarting — environmental groups cite a 1-in-4 annual rupture probability, and any new spill would likely end operations permanently
  3. D/E of 232% with $980M debt and near-zero current revenue creates existential financial risk if production ramp is delayed by legal or operational setbacks — the company may need additional capital at dilutive terms
