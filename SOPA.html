Company Profile
Communication Services ·Advertising Agencies ·NASDAQ ·Singapore
Society Pass is a Singapore-based holding company that acquires and operates fintech, e-commerce, and digital marketing platforms across Southeast Asia. The company operates food delivery (Handycart, Mangan), grocery (Pushkart), lifestyle e-commerce (Leflair), telecom (Gorilla), travel (NusaTrip), and digital marketing (Thoughtful Media) businesses. In late 2025, the company announced a strategic pivot to acquiring AI-driven software and data center companies.

Finite vs Infinite Game — Sinek (25%)
Mindset: FINITE Overall: 1 /10
Society Pass is a textbook finite player — the company has pivoted its strategic direction at least four times in six years (food delivery to e-commerce to loyalty platform to AI data center acquirer), has no enduring purpose beyond pursuing the next trend, and has faced repeated existential compliance crises. Serial dilutive offerings, a subsidiary under SEC inquiry, and a stock below $1.00 facing delisting all signal a company playing to survive the next quarter rather than playing to endure.
Just Cause
1
Society Pass has no Just Cause. The company has changed its strategic identity four times since 2018 — from Food Society to e-commerce loyalty platform to SEA digital ecosystem to AI data center acquirer — each pivot abandoning the previous narrative [2]. The stated vision of 'revolutionizing e-commerce in Southeast Asia' was effectively discarded in December 2025 when the company announced its pivot to AI-driven software and infrastructure acquisitions in SEA, Europe, and North America [2]. A company that reinvents its purpose annually does not have a cause — it has a slide deck.
Trusting Teams
2

Repeated Nasdaq compliance crises — equity rule violations in early 2025 and minimum bid price deficiency in March 2026 — create an environment of existential uncertainty that undermines organizational trust.

Investment Thesis
Society Pass trades at a $5.4 million market cap against an estimated $29 million in consolidated cash (including NusaTrip IPO proceeds), which superficially suggests deep undervaluation [1]. The NusaTrip subsidiary IPO'd on Nasdaq in August 2025 at a $70M+ market cap from a sub-$5 million acquisition cost, demonstrating potential value-creation capability. Ascendiant Capital maintains a BUY with an $18 price target, and Greenridge Global projects $18.2 million in 2026 revenue with $0.16 EPS profitability [1]. The company's December 2025 pivot to AI data center and telecom acquisitions positions it to capitalize on SEA infrastructure demand, with an $8.8 million investment in Sapience AI as the initial move [2].
The bear case is overwhelming and multi-dimensional. Society Pass is a $5.4 million market cap company trading at $0.42/share — 63% below its 50-day SMA of $1.13 — with 180 days to regain Nasdaq's $1.00 minimum bid price or face delisting [3]. The company has faced two separate Nasdaq compliance crises in 12 months (equity rule violation and minimum bid price deficiency). NusaTrip, the crown jewel subsidiary, had trading halted in late 2025 amid SEC document requests and Nasdaq inquiries — the resolution remains unknown [3]. Core revenue declined 17.6% YoY with a -146% net margin and -153% ROE, while the company diluted shareholders repeatedly ($3M offering at $0.55/share in February 2026) [1][3]. The strategic pivot to AI data center acquisitions — announced by a company with $7.2 million in revenue and no technology development capability — appears more promotional than substantive. The company has changed its strategic identity four times in six years without ever achieving profitability. Q3 2025 EPS of -$0.89 missed the -$0.07 estimate by over 1,100%, suggesting financial results are highly unpredictable [1]. The stock is uninvestable for institutional allocators due to micro-cap size, compliance risk, and SEC inquiry overhang.
