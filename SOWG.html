Investment Thesis
Sow Good's only investment case is the optionality embedded in its pivot from failed freeze-dried candy operations to graphite mining through the Nachu Graphite Project acquisition. Activist CEO David Lazar invested $6M in preferred stock and the company has implemented $5M+ in annualized cost savings through facility consolidation [1]. The graphite mining opportunity could benefit from AI infrastructure buildout driving demand for graphite in batteries and data centers. A 600-store private-label candy launch and Albertsons distribution deal provide some near-term revenue visibility from the legacy business under the Trea Grove distribution agreement [1]. The stock trades at a $36M market cap, providing potential asymmetric upside if the mining pivot succeeds.
The bear case is overwhelming. The company has disclosed 'substantial doubt about its ability to continue as a going concern,' with zero meaningful revenue, $6.8M in 2025 net losses, $1.5M in cash, and just 8 employees [1][4]. The freeze-dried candy business failed as major competitors (M&M's, Ferrara) entered the market, and manufacturing assets were sold for a mere $1.5M [1]. The pivot to graphite mining under an activist investor CEO represents a complete strategic reboot with no domain expertise, no operational track record, and no revenue visibility beyond the legacy Trea Grove distribution agreement expiring July 2026. Severe dilution from preferred stock offerings ($3M Series AA, potential $3M Series AAA with conversion rights) decimates existing shareholders. The stock has declined from $0.41 (50-day SMA) to $0.12, a 71% decline, reflecting market assessment that the company is functionally a shell entity [4]. At $0.12 per share with $36M market cap, this is a speculative micro-cap with existential risk.
