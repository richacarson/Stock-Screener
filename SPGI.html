Investment Thesis
S&P Global is a wide-moat compounder powering global capital markets with credit ratings, indices (S&P 500), Capital IQ data, and Platts commodity benchmarks [3]. 2025 delivered $15.336B revenue (+8%), GAAP EPS of $14.66 (+19%), and 47.3% adjusted operating margin, with 113% of FCF returned to shareholders via $5B+ in buybacks [1]. CEO Martina Cheung is accelerating GenAI integration via Capital IQ Pro, SparkAIR risk analytics, and the new Chief Technology & Transformation Officer role to defend and extend the data moat [3]. The 53-year dividend growth streak (Dividend King) reflects infinite-game stewardship. At 22.1x forward P/E and 0.91% yield, SPGI is fairly valued for 6-8% organic growth, 9-10% EPS growth, and one of the most defensible business models in financials [5].
The bear case starts with valuation: at 28.4x trailing P/E, SPGI prices in continued execution and tolerates little margin for error — the February 2026 stock plunge on 'in-line' 2026 guidance shows how high expectations are [4]. EU AI Act classifying credit scoring as high-risk could increase compliance costs, and global ESG backlash creates regulatory volatility around Sustainable1. Indices business is exposed to passive ETF outflows in market drawdowns, and Ratings revenue cyclicality tracks debt issuance volumes. AI also creates a long-tail competitive risk if open-source models commoditize basic analytics. Inspire score of -49 reflects LGBT activism attribution; on the positive side, SPGI scores well on business resilience, customer welfare, ethical labor, and 16 other ESG dimensions.
