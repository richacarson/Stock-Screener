Company Profile
Technology ·Solar ·NASDAQ ·United States
SunPower Inc. (formerly Complete Solaria) is a residential solar installer operating across 46 states through direct installation, new homes, and dealer channels. The company acquired SunPower Corporation's key assets out of bankruptcy in September 2024, rebranded as SunPower Inc. in October 2025, and targets $400M+ revenue in 2026 with a long-term vision of $1 billion in annual revenue.

Investment Thesis
SunPower Inc. represents a high-risk turnaround in residential solar, led by veteran CEO T.J. Rodgers who acquired SunPower's assets out of bankruptcy and achieved profitability within two quarters [1][2]. The company reported $300M in 2025 revenue (up 176% from 2024) and targets $400M+ in 2026, with a long-term vision of $1 billion [1]. At $0.98 per share and a $124M market cap, the forward P/E of 2.0 reflects extreme risk discount. The expansion from 22 to 46 states through acquisitions creates a national platform for residential solar installation, and the industry benefits from the Inflation Reduction Act's 30% Investment Tax Credit providing structural demand support [1]. Solar installation is AI-immune and serves the growing need for distributed generation as data center power demand surges.
The bear case is dominated by execution and solvency risk. SunPower Inc. is a sub-$1 penny stock with a $124M market cap, negative book value (price-to-book of -1.21), and $188M in total debt against only $9.6M cash [3]. Profit margin is -15.1% trailing, and while the company recently turned profitable, one quarter of marginal profit does not establish sustainability. The predecessor SunPower Corporation went bankrupt in August 2024 under the weight of $2 billion in debt, high interest rates, and California's NEM 3.0 policy change — risks that still threaten the residential solar industry [4]. Management tenure of 1.2 years and a 66% headcount reduction signal organizational fragility [2]. The acquisition-driven growth strategy requires capital the company may struggle to raise at sub-$1 share prices. Potential loss of the 30% Solar ITC under changing political leadership is an existential risk CEO Rodgers has explicitly flagged [4].
Key Catalysts
  1. Revenue growth toward $400M+ target in 2026 driven by national expansion to 46 states and continued acquisition integration [1]
  2. Sustained quarterly profitability proving the lean operating model works at scale, potentially re-rating the stock from distressed levels [1]
  3. AI data center buildout driving electricity demand growth, increasing homeowner interest in solar as utility rates rise [2]
Key Risks
  1. Penny stock with $124M market cap and negative book value — potential Nasdaq delisting risk if share price remains below $1 for extended period [3]
  2. Loss or reduction of the 30% Solar Investment Tax Credit under shifting federal energy policy would severely impact residential solar economics [4]
  3. Acquisition integration risk across three deals in 18 months with an inexperienced management team (1.2 years average tenure) [2]
