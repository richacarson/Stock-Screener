Investment Thesis
Sportradar is the dominant global sports technology integrator, delivering record 2025 revenue of $1.5B (+17%) with Adjusted EBITDA of $297M (+33%) and a 2026 guide of 23-25% constant-currency revenue growth to $1.56-1.58B and $390-400M EBITDA (+34-37%) [1][3]. The business is protected by multi-year exclusive rights with the NBA, NFL, NHL, MLB, ATP, and a newly renewed FIFA partnership, covering 900+ bookmakers and streaming 525,000+ matches (targeting 700K+ in 2026) [1][3]. A pristine balance sheet ($349M net cash, 6% D/E), 19.8% ROIC, and a newly hired Chief AI Officer (co-founder of Google Assistant) position the company for operating leverage and AI-driven product expansion, including the emerging prediction-markets opportunity [2][5].
The bear case centers on culture, valuation, and structural dependence on gambling. Glassdoor reviews flag a 'toxic' environment with 'talent drain among high performers' and accelerating attrition in AI/engineering [2], creating execution risk for the 2026 guide. Forward P/E of 36.6x and Forward PEG near 1.5 leave little cushion if growth decelerates [5]. The revenue base is concentrated in regulated sports betting, exposing Sportradar to jurisdictional crackdowns, tax increases (like the UK remote gaming duty), and long-dated rights-cost inflation as leagues extract more value at renewal. Net profit margin remains thin (~8%) and ROE of 10.5% is below IOWN's 15% target [5]. The Inspire negative attribution for 'Gambling' keeps the faith-alignment label MIXED.
Key Catalysts
  1. 2026 guide of 23-25% constant-currency revenue growth and 34-37% Adjusted EBITDA growth with 200-225 bps of margin expansion [1]
  2. Prediction markets expansion and AI-native products (alphaGo predictive tools, automated content, personalized streaming) under new CTO/CAIO Behshad Behzadi [2]
  3. FIFA rights renewal plus 700,000+ streamed events in 2026 extending the Sportradar moat across global sport [1][3]
Key Risks
  1. Culture and talent drain — Glassdoor and employee reports cite toxic leadership and accelerating departures of high performers, threatening execution on AI roadmap [2]
  2. Gambling regulation — new remote gaming duties (UK), US state-by-state taxation, and potential prediction-market crackdowns could compress bookmaker margins and Sportradar's take rate [4]
  3. Rights cost inflation — league renewals (NBA, NFL, FIFA) extract increasing economics, squeezing gross margin if pricing power with bookmakers doesn't keep pace [1]
