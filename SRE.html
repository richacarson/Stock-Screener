Company Profile

Investment Thesis
Sempra is a premier utility growth story positioned at the intersection of America's energy infrastructure buildout and AI-driven electricity demand. The $56B record five-year capital plan is the largest in company history, with Oncor expecting 30%+ base capital increases as Texas electricity demand is projected to nearly double by 2030 due to data centers [1][2]. Forward P/E of 17.7x is attractive for a utility guiding 7-9% long-term EPS growth, with 2026 EPS guidance of $4.80-$5.30 representing 12% YoY growth from the 2025 midpoint [1]. The 2.65% dividend yield with 14+ consecutive years of growth provides income stability, and the company's three-platform structure (California regulated, Texas regulated, Infrastructure) diversifies regulatory and commodity risk. Sempra serves more Americans' daily energy needs than any other U.S. company, creating an unmatched regulated utility moat [2].
The bear case centers on execution risk and financial strain from the massive capital program. The 93.8% payout ratio leaves virtually no earnings cushion, and ROE of just 5.2% raises questions about capital efficiency despite the growth narrative [4]. Revenue has been flat to declining (-0.2% YoY, negative 3-year CAGR) as commodity price normalization offset volume growth. The $35B in total debt against only $29M cash creates significant leverage risk if rate cases or capital deployment face delays. California wildfire liability remains a tail risk — SDG&E paid $379M for the 2007 wildfires and the Supreme Court upheld the prohibition on rate recovery [3]. Earnings growth of -48.2% in the most recent period contradicts the growth story, and trailing P/E of 35.6x reflects depressed near-term earnings. The Texas growth thesis depends on data center demand materializing as projected — any slowdown in AI infrastructure spending could compress Oncor's growth trajectory [1].
Key Catalysts
  1. AI and data center electricity demand driving Texas grid buildout — Oncor's 30%+ capital plan increase directly tied to projected doubling of Texas electricity demand by 2030 [1][2]
  2. 2026 EPS guidance of $4.80-$5.30 (12% YoY growth) with long-term EPS growth rate raised to 7-9%, supporting dividend growth acceleration [1]
  3. Sempra Infrastructure LNG export capacity expansion benefiting from global energy security demand and favorable U.S. natural gas pricing [1]
Key Risks
  1. California wildfire liability exposure — SDG&E operates in fire-prone Southern California where inverse condemnation doctrine can assign utility liability regardless of negligence [3]
  2. Elevated payout ratio of 93.8% leaves no margin for earnings misses; any shortfall in EPS could force dividend growth deceleration or suspension [4]
  3. Execution risk on $56B capital plan — regulatory delays, supply chain constraints, or slower-than-expected data center demand could impair returns on the largest capital program in company history [1]
