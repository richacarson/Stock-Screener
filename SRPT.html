Sarepta Therapeutics is a commercial-stage biotechnology company focused on precision genetic medicine for rare diseases, including RNA-targeted therapies (PMO platform for Duchenne muscular dystrophy) and gene therapy (ELEVIDYS, the first approved gene therapy for DMD). The company is pivoting toward its siRNA pipeline following ELEVIDYS safety and commercial setbacks.

Investment Thesis
Bull case: Sarepta is a mission-driven rare-disease leader trading near multi-year lows ($22.27 vs. 52-week high of $103.32) after a traumatic 2025 [4]. FY2025 revenue grew 16% to $2.2B and the company ended the year with ~$1.0B cash [1]. The 2026 'reset' guidance of $1.2-1.4B net product + $450-550M collaboration revenue is deliberately conservative as management rebuilds ELEVIDYS confidence through educational initiatives; positive clinical data in Q1 2026 drove a 20% rally [1]. The strategic pivot to the siRNA pipeline under new R&D President Louise Rodino-Klapac, combined with 500 layoffs and LGMD program halts, positions Sarepta as a more focused, cash-generative genetic medicine company heading into 2027.
Bear case: ELEVIDYS faces structural damage from two patient deaths, an FDA-requested voluntary shipment pause, and a boxed warning — commercial recovery depends on physician re-engagement that may take years [2]. The 2026 revenue guide of $1.2-1.4B represents a material contraction from 2025 combined PMO + ELEVIDYS levels, signaling 2026 will be the trough [1]. Operating cash flow of -$205M on top of -$713M net income burns through ~$1B cash quickly [4]. The CEO transition (Doug Ingram retiring), 500 layoffs, and near-total C-suite overhaul create organizational turbulence at the worst possible moment. Competitive threats from siRNA peers (Alnylam) and emerging DMD gene therapies could compress long-term market share.
Key Catalysts
  1. ELEVIDYS educational push and physician re-engagement could drive H2 2026 acceleration if safety confidence rebuilds [1]
  2. siRNA pipeline data catalysts under new R&D leadership (Rodino-Klapac) could validate the strategic pivot [2]
  3. Successful CEO succession after Doug Ingram's 2026 retirement would reduce leadership-transition overhang [2]
Key Risks
  1. ELEVIDYS commercial recovery may stall due to the boxed warning and two patient deaths, pressuring 2026-2027 revenue below even the conservative $1.2-1.4B guide [2]
  2. Operating cash flow of -$205M on $1.0B cash creates meaningful runway risk if 2026 reset revenue disappoints [4]
  3. CEO transition combined with 500 layoffs and C-suite overhaul creates execution risk during the most fragile commercial period [2]
