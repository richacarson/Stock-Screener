Company Profile
Basic Materials ·Specialty Chemicals ·NYSE ·South Africa
Sasol is an integrated energy and chemicals company headquartered in Sandton, South Africa. It operates coal-to-liquids and gas-to-liquids synfuels facilities at Secunda and Mozambique, and produces specialty chemicals globally. It is Africa's largest private-sector GHG emitter.

Investment Thesis
Sasol trades at an optically cheap 5.75x forward P/E with a $5.75B+ market cap on $13.9B TTM revenue, offering deep-value exposure to South African energy, Secunda synfuels, and global specialty chemicals. [1] CEO Simon Baloyi's April 2024 appointment (22-year internal tenure) brings operational continuity and a Capital Markets Day roadmap for Future Sasol decarbonization and energy system contribution. Secunda production improved 9% YoY in the most recent quarter on better coal quality.
Bear case dominates: H1 FY2026 EPS is guided at R0.10-R0.80 versus R7.22 prior year - an 89%-99% decline driven by oil prices and ZAR currency headwinds. [1] Adjusted EBITDA is guided at R19-23B vs R24B prior. ROE of 2.5% and profit margin of ~1% mean Sasol is barely earning its cost of capital. D/E of 70% sits at the IOWN target but remains high absolute leverage for a commodity-cyclical business. The 2050 net-zero pivot from a coal-based feedstock base is capital intensive and could strand legacy assets.
Key Catalysts
  1. Oil price recovery and ZAR stabilization lifting H2 FY2026 EPS back toward normalized ~R14 levels
  2. Secunda operational improvements continuing (+9% YoY production) and delivering unit cost improvements
  3. Future Sasol Capital Markets Day execution and Scope 1/2 GHG reduction milestones
Key Risks
  1. Further EPS downside from oil price decline or ZAR weakness - already guided down 89-99% YoY
  2. South African Eskom electricity, logistics (Transnet rail), and regulatory risk compressing margins
  3. Stranded-asset risk as 2050 net-zero pivot accelerates while legacy coal-to-liquids remains the revenue base
