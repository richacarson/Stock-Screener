Investment Thesis
SS&C Technologies offers compelling value at 9.9x forward P/E for a recurring-revenue software business generating $1.74B in operating cash flow (up 25.7% YoY) with cash conversion above 100%. The 2026 guidance projects adjusted EPS of $6.70-$7.02 (~12% growth) with EBITDA margin expansion targeting 40% by Q4, while AI-powered products like ARIA and Blue Prism position SS&C to capture growing enterprise automation demand. Founder-CEO Bill Stone's 40-year track record of disciplined acquisitions has built a 150+ product platform with high switching costs and 5.9% financial services recurring revenue growth.
The bear case centers on SS&C's elevated debt load ($7.5B gross, 2.8x net leverage) which limits financial flexibility in a higher-rate environment with ~$500M in annual interest expense. Profit margins of 12.7% trail pure-play software peers, and GAAP EPS of $3.15 significantly lags adjusted EPS ($6.70+ guided), highlighting acquisition-related amortization distortion. The 2025 whistleblower lawsuit raises governance concerns, while Morgan Stanley's reduced $86 target reflects persistent AI disruption skepticism. Integration risk from the $1.03B Calastone acquisition could create operational distraction during a critical AI pivot year.
