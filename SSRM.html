Company Profile
Basic Materials ·Gold ·NASDAQ ·United States
SSR Mining is a precious metals mining company with operations in the United States, Canada, Argentina, and formerly Turkey. The company operates the Marigold gold mine in Nevada, the Seabee gold operation in Saskatchewan, and the Puna silver-lead-zinc operation in Argentina. SSR Mining recently sold its 80% stake in the Copler mine in Turkey for $1.5B following a fatal heap leach pad failure in 2024.

Investment Thesis
SSR Mining is a compelling deep-value gold miner trading at 5.48x forward earnings versus peer multiples of 12-14x, offering potential 80-100% upside if the valuation gap closes [1]. The company generated $1.63B in FY2025 revenue (up 61.4% YoY) with 24.3% profit margins, holds minimal debt (D/E 9.2%), and sits on $1.5B in Copler sale proceeds that can fund acquisitions or buybacks [1][5]. Year-end mineral reserves of 11M gold-equivalent ounces (up 40%) provide strong reserve life. Analysts forecast EPS growth from $1.74 to $4.07 in 2026 (131% increase), and the $300M buyback authorization signals capital return commitment [1]. Gold prices above $2,000/oz provide a strong macro tailwind.
The bear case centers on the Copler disaster's lasting impact and execution risk. Nine workers were killed in the February 2024 heap leach pad collapse, exposing fundamental safety culture failures and resulting in a $1.5B forced asset sale [3]. Without Copler, SSR Mining is a smaller, less diversified miner with production guidance of just 450,000-535,000 gold-equivalent ounces for 2026 [1]. The deep discount to peers may reflect legitimate concerns about management credibility post-Copler. Glassdoor reviews (3.1/5) and only 42% positive business outlook suggest cultural issues that could impair talent retention and operational execution [2]. The company must successfully redeploy the Copler proceeds into value-accretive acquisitions — capital allocation in mining is notoriously difficult, and overpaying for assets during a gold bull market is a real risk.
Key Catalysts
  1. Value-accretive deployment of $1.5B Copler sale proceeds into new mining assets, potentially transforming SSR Mining's production profile and reserve base [1]
  2. Gold price strength above $2,000/oz driving analyst EPS estimates of $4.07 for 2026, a 131% increase over FY2025 [1]
  3. $300M share buyback program at 5.48x forward earnings providing significant per-share value accretion [1]
Key Risks
  1. Post-Copler credibility deficit — the fatal 2024 heap leach pad collapse killed nine workers and exposed safety culture failures, potentially impairing management credibility with investors and regulators [3]
  2. Capital redeployment risk — SSR Mining must find and execute value-accretive acquisitions with $1.5B in proceeds during a gold bull market where asset prices are elevated [1]
  3. Smaller post-divestiture production base of 450,000-535,000 gold-equivalent ounces leaves limited margin for operational setbacks at remaining Marigold, Seabee, or Puna mines [1]
