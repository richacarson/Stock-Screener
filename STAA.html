Company Profile
Healthcare ·Medical Instruments & Supplies ·NASDAQ ·United States
STAAR Surgical designs, develops, manufactures, and sells implantable collamer lenses (ICLs) for vision correction. Its flagship EVO ICL and EVO+ ICL products treat myopia and astigmatism as an alternative to LASIK. The company has completed over 2.5 million implants globally, with China as its largest market.
921 Employees ·www.staar.com

Investment Thesis
STAAR Surgical offers a compelling long-term thesis centered on the global myopia epidemic, which affects nearly half the world's population and is growing rapidly in Asia. The EVO+ ICL launch in China (its largest market) at higher ASPs, combined with FDA age-range expansion to 21-60 accessing 8M additional U.S. patients, creates a clear path to revenue recovery in 2026 [1][2]. The balance sheet is clean with D/E of just 11%, and the forward PEG of 0.76 suggests the market expects significant earnings growth. Broadwood Partners' aggressive insider buying — over $60M in six months — signals informed conviction in the turnaround [4].
The bear case is substantial. Full-year 2025 revenue collapsed 23.7% to $239.4M, the company is deeply unprofitable at -33.6% profit margin, and the new CEO (appointed February 2025) has not yet demonstrated execution capability [1][5]. China market concentration creates geopolitical and regulatory risk — the distributor inventory destocking that crushed 2025 revenue could recur. STAAR is a single-product company with 921 employees competing against well-funded refractive surgery alternatives (LASIK, PRK). The leadership instability — average management tenure of 1.7 years — raises execution risk during a critical turnaround period [3]. Forward P/E of 33x requires flawless execution on both revenue recovery and margin expansion to justify current valuation.
Key Catalysts
  1. EVO+ China launch ramp with higher ASPs following mid-2025 regulatory approval, supported by early customer demand exceeding expectations [2]
  2. FDA age-range expansion from 21-45 to 21-60 accessing approximately 8 million additional U.S. patients for EVO ICL [2]
  3. Cost restructuring driving return to profitability — management targets return to double-digit operating margins achieved historically [2]
Key Risks
  1. China market concentration risk — distributor inventory destocking drove 23.7% revenue decline in 2025, and geopolitical tensions could further disrupt STAAR's largest market [1]
  2. Single-product dependency on EVO ICL platform with no diversified revenue streams; competitive pressure from LASIK, PRK, and emerging refractive technologies [1]
  3. Leadership instability with average management tenure of 1.7 years and new CEO Stephen Farrell unproven at STAAR, creating execution risk during critical turnaround phase [3]
