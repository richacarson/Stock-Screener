Company Profile
Energy ·Oil & Gas Equipment & Services ·NASDAQ ·China
STAK Inc. is a Changzhou, China-based company specializing in the research, development, manufacturing, and sale of oilfield-specialized production and maintenance equipment including oil pumping trucks, well repair trucks, fracking trucks, and automation control modules. The company is expanding into new energy vehicles (NEVs) with four battery-electric models for oilfield logistics and construction. IPO'd on Nasdaq in February 2025.

Investment Thesis
STAK's H1 FY2026 showed meaningful improvement — revenue grew 13.4% to $19.2M, net income turned positive at $1.8M, operating cash flow swung positive to $0.7M, and cash improved to $1.9M [1]. The stock has recovered dramatically from its $0.29 low to $4.14, reflecting renewed investor interest. The NEV expansion into four battery-electric vehicle models targeting the industrial electric vehicle market (projected 46.6% CAGR) could be transformative for a company of this size, with growing IP protection through 79 patents and copyrights [3]. At roughly 3x trailing revenue, valuation is undemanding if the growth and profitability trajectory continues.
The bear case remains severe for this micro-cap. TTM profitability is still negative (-$5.9M net income, -22% margin) despite H1 improvement, and gross margins are compressing (27.2% vs 30.7% prior year) due to NEV development costs and promotional pricing [1]. The $79.5M market cap, Cayman Islands holding structure with 100% China operations, and 47 employees create significant execution, regulatory, and VIE risk. Average daily volume of 427K shares on a $4.14 stock produces only $1.8M in daily traded value — barely above the liquidity floor. The stock's 52-week range of $0.29-$9.50 reflects extreme volatility unsuitable for conservative portfolios. No analyst coverage, no dividend, and no institutional sponsorship leave the stock vulnerable to sharp drawdowns on any negative catalyst.
