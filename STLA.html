Company Profile
Consumer Discretionary ·Automobiles ·NYSE ·Netherlands
Stellantis N.V. is one of the world's largest automakers by volume, formed from the 2021 merger of Fiat Chrysler Automobiles and PSA Group. The company owns 14 automotive brands including Jeep, Ram, Dodge, Chrysler, Fiat, Alfa Romeo, Maserati, Peugeot, Citroën, and Opel/Vauxhall. Under new CEO Antonio Filosa, Stellantis is executing a 'profound reset' following a $22.3B net loss in FY2025, scaling back EV-only strategy and refocusing on Jeep and Ram in North America.

Investment Thesis
Stellantis trades at ~5x forward earnings—an extreme discount reflecting the magnitude of FY2025's $22.3B loss and ongoing uncertainty about Filosa's turnaround timeline. The catalysts for rerating are specific and measurable: the $500M slab contract improvement analogue in steel is replaced by the $6.5B cash restructuring savings over 4 years and the $13B U.S. production investment, which management says will grow U.S. finished-vehicle production by 50%[1]. If Filosa delivers his 2026 guided mid-single-digit revenue growth and positive IFCF improvement, the stock's discount to global auto peers (Ford, GM trade at 8-10x) could compress significantly.
The bear case is that Stellantis's $22.3B loss is symptomatic of structural competitive disadvantage in key markets—U.S. and Europe—that Filosa cannot reverse through operational discipline alone. Chinese OEMs (BYD, Geely) have cost structures 20-30% below Western automakers in EV production and are expanding aggressively into European markets. Meanwhile, the company's North American brands (Jeep, Ram, Dodge) face intensifying competition from Ford's truck lineup and the structural shift away from large ICE SUVs. With 14 brands, the portfolio may be too large to turnaround simultaneously on a $13B investment budget.
Key Catalysts
  1. Q1 2026 earnings (April 30) demonstrating sequential improvement in North American market share and gross margin, validating the 2026 turnaround execution
  2. Investor Day (May 21, Auburn Hills) detailing the full four-year $13B investment plan with specific brand strategies and financial targets
  3. POSCO strategic partnership announcement providing EV-grade steel at competitive pricing for the electrification of Ram pickup trucks
Key Risks
  1. Chinese automaker expansion into European markets (Stellantis's second-largest region) intensifying price competition and accelerating market share losses beyond current levels
  2. North American brand (Jeep, Ram) market share continuing to decline in 2026 despite Filosa's refocus, driven by competitive new Ford F-150 and GM truck launches
  3. €6.5B cash restructuring payments over 4 years straining cash flow while the company simultaneously needs to invest $13B in U.S. production—creating a potential capital allocation conflict
