Company Profile
Basic Materials ·Steel ·NASDAQ ·United States
Steel Dynamics is the third-largest carbon steel producer in the United States with over 13 million tons of annual steelmaking capacity. The company operates through four segments: Steel Operations, Metals Recycling, Steel Fabrication, and Aluminum Operations. Its electric arc furnace (EAF) mini-mill model produces steel from recycled scrap, serving construction, automotive, energy, and industrial end markets. Founded by CEO Mark Millett in 1993.

Investment Thesis
Steel Dynamics is delivering accelerating earnings in 2026 with Q1 EPS of $2.78 (up 93% YoY) on record steel shipments of 3.6M tons and $5.2B revenue (up 19%), followed by Q2 guidance of $3.51-$3.55 representing another 26% sequential improvement driven by metal margin expansion [1][2]. At 11.86x forward P/E with a fortress balance sheet (46% D/E, $1.45B operating cash flow), 22 consecutive years of dividends growing at ~15% 5yr CAGR, and the $2.7B aluminum flat-rolled operation ramping into a persistent domestic supply deficit enhanced by tariffs, STLD offers compelling cyclical value [7]. ROE has recovered to 15.25% and the fabrication backlog — driven by data center, warehouse, and healthcare construction — provides multi-quarter earnings visibility [2]. The stock has risen 73% over the past year but remains attractively valued at less than 12x forward earnings.
The bear case centers on steel's inherent cyclicality and margin vulnerability. Profit margin of 7.22% is thin, and STLD's earnings swing dramatically with steel prices — FY2025 net income fell 20% to $1.2B from $1.5B in FY2024 despite record shipments, as average selling prices compressed [1]. Trade policy risk is elevated: while current tariffs benefit domestic producers, policy reversals could flood the market with cheap imports and compress pricing power. The aluminum ramp carries execution risk — the Sinton facility had costly startup issues in 2022-2023, and the $16M Arizona-to-Mississippi relocation write-down signals planning challenges [2]. Net insider selling of $6.4M across senior management — including SVP Alvarez and Director Cornew selling at recent highs — signals those closest to the business may see limited near-term upside [6]. A construction downturn driven by higher interest rates could deflate the fabrication backlog that underpins the bull thesis.
Key Catalysts
  1. Q2 2026 EPS guidance of $3.51-$3.55 on continued metal margin expansion and strong steel demand, with H1 2026 annualized earnings trajectory exceeding $12/share versus trailing PE of 23.76x [2]
  2. Aluminum flat-rolled division ramping into a persistent domestic supply deficit enhanced by tariffs, providing significant through-cycle EBITDA diversification beyond cyclical steel operations [1]
  3. BIOEDGE and EDGE lower-embodied-carbon steel products capturing sustainability premium as construction and automotive customers face increasing scope 3 emissions scrutiny [1]
Key Risks
  1. Steel price cyclicality — profit margin of 7.22% is thin and earnings can swing 20%+ year-to-year with steel price cycles, as demonstrated by the FY2024-to-FY2025 earnings decline despite record volumes [1]
  2. Trade policy reversal risk — tariff rollbacks or new trade agreements could expose STLD to cheaper imported steel and aluminum, compressing domestic pricing power that drives current margin expansion [2]
  3. Net insider selling of $6.4M across senior management in the past 12 months despite strong earnings trajectory may signal limited near-term upside from current elevated levels [6]
