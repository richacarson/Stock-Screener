Investment Thesis
Steel Dynamics offers a compelling combination of cyclical upside and defensive characteristics at 10.4x forward P/E with a fortress balance sheet (49% D/E). Q1 2026 EPS guidance of $2.73-$2.77 represents a 90% sequential improvement driven by metal margin expansion and strong demand, with customer order backlog 35% higher than a year ago extending into Q3. The $2.7B aluminum flat rolled products mill is now producing qualified products, adding a new revenue stream that could contribute $3-4B annually at full capacity. Record 2025 steel shipments of 13.7M tons, conservative 25% payout ratio, and 12-year dividend growth streak provide income stability.
The bear case centers on steel's inherent cyclicality—2025 EPS of $7.99 was already down from $9.84 in 2024 and $20.92 at the 2022 peak, reflecting the brutal margin compression during steel price downturns. Profit margins of 6.5% are thin by industrial standards, and tariff-driven demand boosts may prove temporary if trade policies shift. The aluminum mill ramp carries execution risk and could pressure returns on invested capital during the commissioning phase. Multiple Clean Air Act EPA settlements ($475K in 2021, earlier violations) and a $4.6M antitrust settlement create governance overhang, though both were relatively modest in scale.
