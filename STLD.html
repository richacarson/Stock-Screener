Steel Dynamics, Inc. is the third-largest carbon steel producer in the United States with 13+ million tons of annual production capacity using electric arc furnace (EAF) technology. The company operates through Steel Operations, Metals Recycling (OmniSource), Steel Fabrication, and Aluminum Operations segments, representing a vertically integrated industrial metals platform.

Investment Thesis
Steel Dynamics offers a compelling combination of value (10.5x forward P/E), quality (A+ executive team, record 13.7M ton shipments), and growth catalysts (aluminum mill targeting $650-700M annual EBITDA at 90% utilization by end 2026). The vertically integrated steel-recycling-fabrication model provides structural advantages over pure-play steelmakers. The data center and infrastructure demand backdrop supports robust steel fabrication backlogs extending through H1 2026. The 13-year dividend growth streak at 15% CAGR with a 25% payout ratio provides substantial income growth potential, while $1.4 billion in operating cash flow supports continued capital returns.
Steel is inherently cyclical, and 2025 net income declined 19% despite record shipments as realized pricing fell. The negative free cash flow of -$109 million reflects the heavy capital investment cycle for aluminum and Sinton mills, requiring continued cash generation from core steel operations. The aluminum mill ramp carries execution risk, and the cyclical nature of aluminum demand introduces new volatility. Tariff policies provide near-term domestic pricing support but create long-term market distortion risk. The 49% debt-to-equity ratio has risen to fund expansion, and a severe economic downturn could pressure both steel and aluminum demand simultaneously.
