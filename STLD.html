Investment Thesis
Steel Dynamics is a best-in-class steel producer trading at an attractive forward P/E of 10.4x with strong Q1 2026 earnings guidance of $2.73-$2.77/share — a 50% sequential improvement from Q4 2025 [1]. Order backlog has grown 35%+ YoY extending into Q3 2026, driven by robust non-residential construction, energy, automotive, and industrial demand [1]. The aluminum mill in Columbus, Mississippi is now producing qualified products, opening a multi-billion-dollar addressable market [2]. With D/E of just 49%, a 25% payout ratio, 21 consecutive years of dividends, and a 14.0% 5-year dividend CAGR, STLD offers a compelling combination of cyclical upside and income growth [5]. The company's EAF-based circular manufacturing model provides structural cost and environmental advantages over blast furnace competitors.
The bear case centers on steel's inherent cyclicality — 2025 annual net income declined to $1.2B from $1.5B despite record revenue, as operating income fell 24% on margin compression [1]. Profit margin of just 6.5% underscores the thin-margin reality of steelmaking. The aluminum mill ramp adds execution risk in a new material category. EPA Clean Air Act violations at the Butler, Indiana plant ($475K penalty plus $3M in equipment upgrades) and Texas Water Code violations at Sinton highlight ongoing environmental regulatory risk [6]. Tariff policy uncertainty could disrupt steel pricing dynamics, and a construction downturn or economic slowdown would directly impact demand across STLD's key end markets.

  2. Environmental regulatory exposure including EPA Clean Air Act violations and Texas Water Code issues at key facilities [6]
  3. Aluminum mill execution risk as STLD enters an adjacent but unfamiliar material category competing against established aluminum producers [2]
