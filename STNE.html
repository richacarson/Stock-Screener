Investment Thesis
StoneCo offers compelling value as a profitable, growing Brazilian fintech trading at 5.9x forward earnings with a 20.8% ROE and 73.6% gross margins [1][6]. The company has successfully transitioned from a pure payments processor to a full financial services platform, with the credit portfolio expected to become a meaningful earnings driver in 2026 and management executing an aggressive buyback program signaling confidence in undervaluation [1]. Active payments clients grew 17% YoY to over 2M, and the 77.4% three-year EPS CAGR demonstrates exceptional operating leverage.
The bear case centers on growth deceleration and Brazil macro risk. Analysts expect forward two-year revenue growth of just 6.4% and EBITDA growth of 1.5%, a sharp deceleration from the 40%+ historical revenue CAGR that triggered a 19% stock decline after earnings [1]. The credit expansion strategy increases balance sheet risk — D/E of 159% will rise as the credit portfolio scales, and Brazilian interest rate volatility (Selic rate) directly impacts credit margins and provisioning [6]. CEO churn (three CEOs in four years) creates execution uncertainty during the critical financial services pivot, and competition from Nubank, PagSeguro, and traditional banks intensifies as the Brazilian digital payments market matures.
Key Catalysts
  1. Credit portfolio scaling in 2026 expected to become a meaningful P&L contributor, potentially reigniting revenue and earnings growth above the 6.4% forward consensus [1]
  2. Aggressive share buyback program at a double-digit yield provides downside support and signals management conviction in the stock's undervaluation at 5.9x forward P/E [1]
  3. ROE expansion from 16% to 24% over the past year demonstrates improving capital efficiency that could attract value investors as the financial services pivot matures [1]
Key Risks
  1. Revenue growth deceleration to mid-single digits from 40%+ historical CAGR could compress the multiple further if the credit expansion fails to reignite top-line momentum [1]
  2. Brazilian macroeconomic volatility — particularly Selic rate changes and BRL depreciation — directly impacts credit provisioning, net interest margins, and USD-denominated returns for foreign investors [6]
  3. Three CEO transitions in four years creates strategic continuity risk during the critical pivot from payments to full financial services, with new CEO Scherer untested in the top role [3]
