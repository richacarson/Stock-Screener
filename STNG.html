Company Profile

Scorpio Tankers Inc. is a leading international provider of marine transportation of refined petroleum products. The company owns, finances, and operates a modern fleet of product tankers including LR2, MR, and Handymax vessels, transporting gasoline, diesel, jet fuel, and other refined products globally across major trade routes.

Investment Thesis
Scorpio Tankers offers compelling value at 13.2x forward earnings with a fortress balance sheet (net cash $385M, D/E 19.4%) and a modern fleet generating 36.7% profit margins in a structurally tight product tanker market [1]. Q4 2025 revenue of $241.4M (+25.6% YoY) and Q1 2026 LR2 rates of $51K/day with Q2 projections jumping to $101K/day demonstrate sustained earnings power from refined product trade disruptions and low fleet growth [1].
The primary risk is tanker cycle mean-reversion — product tanker rates are highly cyclical and current elevated rates reflect geopolitical disruptions (Red Sea diversions, Russian sanctions) that could normalize. Forward P/E expansion from 11.8x trailing to 13.2x forward signals the market already prices some rate softening. The convertible notes create potential dilution of 1.3M+ shares. STNG rewards conviction in sustained tanker market tightness but carries significant downside if rates normalize toward historical averages.
Key Catalysts
  1. Q2 2026 LR2 TCE revenue projected at $101K/day, nearly doubling Q1's $51K/day, signaling continued market strength [1]
  2. Net cash position of $385M and low D/E (19.4%) provides flexibility for fleet growth, buybacks, or dividend increases during cycle peak [1]
  3. Structural tanker market tightness from low orderbook, aging global fleet, and Red Sea/geopolitical route disruptions extending trade distances
Key Risks
  1. Tanker rate cyclicality — mean reversion to $25-35K/day rates from current $50-100K+ levels would compress earnings 50%+
  2. Geopolitical resolution (Red Sea reopening, Russia sanctions easing) could reduce ton-mile demand and normalize vessel utilization
  3. Convertible notes ($375M) create potential dilution; share count could increase if conversion triggers are met [4]
