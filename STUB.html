Investment Thesis
StubHub is the dominant secondary ticketing marketplace with ~50% North American market share, $9.2B GMS, and 82% gross margins demonstrating powerful marketplace economics [1]. The stock trades at just 4.7x forward earnings and 1.2x trailing revenue despite guiding to $9.9B-$10.1B GMS and $400M-$420M adjusted EBITDA in 2026 (up ~80% YoY) [1]. Free cash flow of $675M in 2025 is remarkable for a $2.2B market cap company. Debt reduction of $900M in 2025 (net leverage from 6.7x to 4.5x) demonstrates financial discipline. Underlying GMS growth of 18% (ex-Eras Tour) shows the core marketplace is healthy, and new revenue streams from advertising and AI-driven direct issuance could provide upside as they scale in 2027+ [1].
The bear case revolves around AI disruption, culture dysfunction, and post-IPO overhang. StubHub's own SEC filing warns of 'Google Zero' risk — AI-driven search could erode the web traffic that drives ticket discovery, and agentic AI could bypass marketplace intermediation entirely [2]. Glassdoor's 2.5/5 rating with only 22% recommending the company and 'incredibly toxic' culture reviews suggest deep organizational challenges during a critical scaling period [2]. The IPO lock-up expired March 6, 2026, creating selling pressure from pre-IPO holders that may explain the stock's decline from $23.50 IPO price to $6.10 (74% below IPO). GAAP net loss of $1.9B in 2025 (including $1.4B SBC and $479M valuation allowance) signals massive dilution. Revenue growth of -15.8% headline (affected by Eras Tour comps) raises questions about sustainable growth rates. Regulatory risk around secondary ticketing practices (price gouging, bot-driven purchasing) remains an ongoing threat [1][3].
Key Catalysts
  1. 2026 adjusted EBITDA of $400M-$420M (up ~80% YoY) — execution on this guidance would demonstrate the marketplace's operating leverage and justify a re-rating from the current 4.7x forward P/E [1]
  2. Advertising and sponsored listings monetization — early Q4 2025 results showed 'good reaction from sellers'; scaling this high-margin revenue stream could significantly improve unit economics [1]
  3. International expansion acceleration (~15% of GMS today) into earlier-stage markets where secondary ticketing penetration is lower, providing a long runway for growth [1]
Key Risks
  1. AI-driven search disruption ('Google Zero') — StubHub's reliance on internet search engines for traffic is a self-acknowledged risk factor as AI assistants and agentic AI could disintermediate marketplace discovery [2]
  2. Post-IPO overhang — stock has fallen 74% from $23.50 IPO price to $6.10; lock-up expiration in March 2026 enables pre-IPO holder selling, and the $1.4B SBC charge signals massive equity dilution [1]
  3. Organizational dysfunction — 2.5/5 Glassdoor rating, 22% employee recommendation rate, and reviews describing 'incredibly toxic' culture create execution risk during a critical growth phase [2]
