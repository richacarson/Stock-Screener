Investment Thesis
Grupo Supervielle offers a leveraged bet on Argentina's economic normalization under the Milei government, with GDP expected to grow 3.2% in 2026 and inflation projected to decline to 22%. The forward P/E of 6.1x implies significant upside if the bank returns to profitability (management expects positive ROE in H2 2026). Loan growth of 25-30% guided for 2026, energy sector exposure through Vaca Muerta, and IOL's cross-selling opportunity (97% of 1.6M brokerage clients don't bank with Supervielle) provide multiple growth vectors.
The bear case is Argentina's chronic macroeconomic instability — the bank posted negative ROE in 2025, NPL ratios are rising to 5-6%, and NIM compression to 14-16% reflects central bank rate policy headwinds. No dividends are expected in 2026 due to 2025 losses. Currency devaluation risk could erode USD-denominated returns for ADR holders, and the trailing P/E of 37x on depressed earnings may be misleading. If monetary tightening persists or inflation re-accelerates, Supervielle could face extended periods of negative profitability. The Inspire score of 0 provides no faith alignment signal.
