Company Profile
Financial Services ·Banks - Regional ·NYSE ·Argentina
Grupo Supervielle is an Argentine financial services holding company operating the fourth-largest private domestically-owned bank in Argentina by assets. Founded in 1887, it provides banking, insurance, asset management, and digital financial services through a multi-brand, multi-channel platform across personal, business, and corporate segments.

Investment Thesis
Grupo Supervielle offers a high-risk, leveraged play on Argentina's economic recovery under President Milei's reform agenda. At 7.4x forward earnings, the stock prices in enormous macro risk but offers substantial upside if normalization proceeds — management targets 30-40% loan growth in 2026 and high single-digit to low double-digit ROE, a dramatic improvement from the current -4.66% [1]. Q4 2025 revenue of $189.8M slightly beat estimates, and CEO Patricio Supervielle expressed 'renewed optimism about Argentina's financial system,' citing advancing reforms, broadening international integration, and normalizing financial conditions [1]. The strengthened CET1 ratio provides capital flexibility for growth, Citigroup upgraded to Buy, and analyst price targets of $11-$15 imply 13-54% upside from the current $9.76 [4].
The bear case is substantial and centers on Argentina's persistent macroeconomic volatility combined with SUPV's currently negative profitability. ROE of -4.66% and profit margin of -6.66% reflect a business destroying value in real terms under IAS 29 hyperinflationary accounting [6]. Revenue declined 29.8% YoY in inflation-adjusted terms, and Q4 2025 EPS of -$0.006 missed consensus by -102.6% [1]. Argentina faces ongoing risks from inflation spikes around midterm elections, currency instability, and potential policy reversals that could derail the reform agenda. JPMorgan lowered its price target from $12 to $11 with a Neutral rating, signaling tempered expectations [1]. The stock's 52-week range of $4.54-$17.02 reflects extreme volatility, the $895M market cap limits institutional interest, and critical financial metrics (D/E, PEG, 5yr CAGR) are unavailable, making fundamental analysis exceptionally difficult [6].
