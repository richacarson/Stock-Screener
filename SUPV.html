Company Profile
Financial Services ·Banks - Regional ·NYSE ·Argentina
Grupo Supervielle is an Argentine financial services holding company operating the fourth-largest private domestically-owned bank in Argentina by assets. With origins dating to 1887, it provides banking, insurance, asset management, and digital financial services through a multi-brand, multi-channel platform.

Investment Thesis
Grupo Supervielle represents a high-risk, high-reward play on Argentina's economic normalization under the Milei government. At a forward P/E of just 6.1x, the stock prices in significant macro risk but offers 50.7% earnings growth and 26.6% revenue growth per annum if conditions normalize [1]. Management targets 30-40% loan growth in 2026 and high single-digit to low double-digit ROE, a dramatic improvement from the current -4.7% [1]. The 137-year banking franchise, 4th largest private domestic bank by assets, and proactive digital transformation with generative AI-powered channels position SUPV to capture disproportionate upside as Argentina stabilizes [2]. Analyst price targets of $11-$15 imply 35-85% upside from the current $8.11 price.
The bear case is substantial: Q4 2025 EPS of -$0.006 missed consensus by -102.6%, demonstrating the difficulty of operating in Argentina's hyperinflationary environment [1]. ROE of -4.7% and profit margins of -6.7% reflect a business currently destroying value in real terms. Revenue declined 29.8% YoY in inflation-adjusted terms. Argentina's macro risk remains acute — currency instability, regulatory uncertainty, and potential policy reversals under any future government could undermine the normalization thesis. JPMorgan cut its price target to $11 with a Neutral rating, and two analysts maintain Sell ratings [1]. The ADR structure adds liquidity risk, and no D/E or balance sheet data is available through standard screening tools.
