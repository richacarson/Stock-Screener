Company Profile
Financial Services ·Banks - Regional ·NYSE ·Argentina
Grupo Supervielle is an Argentine financial services holding company operating the fourth-largest private domestically-owned bank in Argentina by assets. Founded in 1887, it provides banking, insurance, asset management, and digital financial services through a multi-brand, multi-channel platform. Key subsidiaries include Banco Supervielle, invertirOnline (digital investment platform), and Supervielle Seguros (insurance). Headquartered in Buenos Aires.

Investment Thesis
Grupo Supervielle offers a high-risk, leveraged play on Argentina's economic recovery under President Milei's reform agenda. At 7.4x forward earnings, the stock prices in enormous macro risk but offers substantial upside if normalization proceeds — Q1 2026 revenue of $205.09M beat consensus and adjusted net income turned positive at ARS 6.7B when excluding extraordinary severance charges [1]. Management revised 2026 guidance to 20-25% loan growth and 10-15% deposit growth with NPL ratio stabilizing at 5-5.5%, reflecting cautious optimism about credit expansion [1]. The company's five consecutive years on BYMA's Sustainability Index and A+ ESG ranking in Latin America demonstrate institutional quality despite the challenging operating environment [5]. Analyst price targets of $11-$15 imply meaningful upside from $9.70.
The bear case is substantial and centers on Argentina's persistent macroeconomic volatility combined with SUPV's currently negative reported profitability. ROE of -7.75% and profit margin of -10.41% reflect a business destroying value in real terms under IAS 29 hyperinflationary accounting, despite underlying operational improvement [6]. Q1 2026 EPS of $0.0678 missed consensus by 50.8%, and the ARS 17B net loss — even though improved from Q4's ARS 21B loss — demonstrates the company is still navigating substantial restructuring costs [1]. Revised 2026 loan growth guidance of 20-25% (down from the earlier 30-40% target) signals more tempered expectations for Argentina's credit recovery. The stock's 52-week range of $4.54-$17.02 reflects extreme volatility, the $900M market cap limits institutional interest, and competition from MercadoPago and digital-native fintechs threatens deposit and payment market share in Argentina's rapidly digitizing financial landscape [1].
