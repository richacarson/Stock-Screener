Investment Thesis
Suzano is the world's largest eucalyptus pulp producer, delivering record 2025 sales of R$50B with R$21.7B EBITDA on 14.2M tonnes volume (+15% YoY) and declining cash costs to R$817/tonne [1]. At forward P/E of 6.09 with 35.2% ROE and 26.8% profit margin, the stock is deeply undervalued. No incremental market pulp capacity expected in 2026 creates a favorable supply-demand backdrop with potential for further price increases [1].
The bear case includes commodity price volatility, currency risk, and leverage. D/E of 231.5% is high and the company aims to reduce net debt to $11B [1]. Revenue in USD terms declined 7.5% due to BRL weakness, creating translation risk for ADR holders. Pulp prices are cyclical and a demand slowdown in China (major market) would pressure earnings. Maintenance downtimes in Q1-Q2 2026 may temporarily impact production. The dividend yield of 2.35% with no growth history provides modest income support [1].
Key Catalysts
  1. Tightening pulp supply-demand with no new market capacity in 2026, supporting price increases [1]
  2. Cash cost reductions continuing from R$817/tonne toward further efficiency gains through 2026 [1]
  3. Net debt reduction toward $11B target improving balance sheet and enabling increased shareholder returns [1]
Key Risks
  1. Pulp price volatility and China demand sensitivity — a slowdown in Chinese tissue and packaging demand would pressure earnings [1]
  2. Currency risk — BRL/USD volatility affects ADR holder returns and debt servicing costs [1]
  3. High leverage with D/E of 231.5% creates financial risk in a commodity downturn [1]
