Company Profile
Real Estate ·REIT - Hotel & Motel ·NASDAQ ·United States
Service Properties Trust is a REIT with approximately $10 billion invested in two asset categories: hotels and service-focused retail net lease properties. As of December 2025, SVC owned 94 hotels with over 21,000 guest rooms and 760 service-focused retail net lease properties with over 13.6 million square feet across the U.S., Puerto Rico, and Canada. Managed externally by The RMR Group. Founded 1995, headquartered in Newton, Massachusetts.

Investment Thesis
SVC is a deeply distressed hotel and net lease REIT trading at $1.53 (0.4x book value) with 2026 guidance of $500–520M Adjusted EBITDAre and Normalized FFO of $110–130M ($0.65–$0.77/share) [1]. The turnaround thesis rests on successful debt reduction through asset dispositions ($859M in hotel sales completed) and the equity raise ($542M to redeem $550M in 2027 notes), which collectively reduce near-term default risk. The net lease portfolio ($380–386M NOI guided) provides stable cash flows, and if hotel operations stabilize, the stock trades at a meaningful discount to estimated net asset value. CEO Bilotto's insider buying signals management conviction [2].
The bear case is overwhelming for the near term. D/E of 848% with $5.8B in debt maturities over five years creates ongoing refinancing risk at a time when hotel cash flows are declining [1]. The 479M share issuance at $1.20 (April 2026) diluted existing holders by nearly 75%, and further dilution may be necessary to address remaining maturities. Revenue declined 12.9% as hotel dispositions shrink the asset base, and negative profit margin of -11.1% with negative ROE of -27% show the business is destroying value [4]. The dividend was cut 95% from $0.80/year to $0.04/year, eliminating the income thesis that attracted many REIT investors. External management by RMR Group creates agency conflicts — RMR earns base management fees regardless of SVC's stock performance. Short interest has increased significantly, suggesting the market expects further deterioration [1]. The stock price of $1.53 risks Nasdaq delisting if it falls below $1.00 for sustained periods.
Key Catalysts
  1. Successful completion of hotel disposition program and debt redemption — reducing the $5.8B debt stack below 6x EBITDAre could restore financial flexibility [1]
  2. Hotel EBITDA stabilization at $124–144M guided for 2026 after renovation program completion across retained properties [1]
  3. Net lease portfolio providing $380–386M NOI as a stable income base that could anchor a restructured, smaller REIT focused on retail net lease [1]
Key Risks
  1. Debt maturity wall of $5.8B over five years with limited cash flow to service — further equity dilution or asset sales at distressed prices may be required to avoid default [1]
  2. Continued revenue decline as hotel dispositions shrink the asset base — the company sold 112 properties in 2025, reducing its hotel portfolio by over 40% [1]
  3. External management by RMR Group creates agency conflicts — base management fees are earned regardless of stock performance, reducing management incentive to prioritize equity holders [2]

AI Risk Protection (8/9): SVC's core business — owning and leasing physical hotel and retail properties — is structurally immune to AI disruption. Hotels require physical buildings, housekeeping, and in-person service that AI cannot replace [2]. Net lease retail properties generate contractual rental income regardless of technology disruption. AI may modestly improve property management efficiency (dynamic pricing, maintenance prediction) but cannot substitute for the physical assets. The hotel industry may face indirect pressure from AI-driven alternative lodging platforms, but SVC's branded hotel portfolio (Sonesta, Hyatt, Radisson) competes on physical presence.
