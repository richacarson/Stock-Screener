Investment Thesis
Savara is a binary clinical-stage biotech with a potential first-in-class therapy for autoimmune pulmonary alveolar proteinosis. Molgramostim achieved statistical significance on both primary and secondary endpoints in the pivotal IMPALA-2 trial, and the BLA is under FDA review with a PDUFA date of November 22, 2026 [1]. The aPAP market has no approved treatments, giving Savara orphan drug exclusivity and first-mover advantage in a rare disease with clear unmet need. Analyst consensus is 'Strong Buy' (1.3 rating) with the stock up 110% over the past 12 months as the market prices in approval probability [4]. At a $1.08B market cap, successful approval and commercialization could drive significant upside from orphan drug pricing.
The bear case is substantial. Savara is pre-revenue with a TTM net loss of $118.8M and depends entirely on a single FDA decision. The May 2025 refusal-to-file letter over chemistry, manufacturing, and controls deficiencies raised serious questions about manufacturing readiness, and a securities fraud class action was filed alleging the company failed to disclose BLA inadequacies [3]. Even if approved, commercialization of an inhaled biologic for ~5,000-10,000 U.S. patients requires significant sales infrastructure investment, and profitability may not arrive until 2028-2029. The extended PDUFA date (from August to November 2026) suggests ongoing FDA scrutiny. Book value of $0.80/share against a $5.28 price implies substantial premium for approval probability, creating downside risk of 60-80% if the FDA issues a Complete Response Letter [4].
Key Catalysts
  1. FDA PDUFA decision on MOLBREEVI BLA expected November 22, 2026 — approval would make molgramostim the first FDA-approved treatment for aPAP [1]
  2. New clinical data presentation at American Thoracic Society (ATS) 2026 conference could strengthen the approval narrative and demonstrate additional efficacy endpoints [1]
  3. Potential orphan drug exclusivity and premium pricing in a market with zero approved competitors, enabling rapid revenue ramp if approved
Key Risks
  1. Binary FDA risk — a Complete Response Letter would devastate the stock given the $1.08B market cap is built almost entirely on approval expectations, with potential 60-80% downside [3]
  2. Securities fraud class action lawsuit alleging failure to disclose BLA deficiencies prior to the May 2025 refusal-to-file letter creates legal liability and management distraction [3]
  3. Commercialization execution risk — building a specialty sales force for ~5,000-10,000 aPAP patients requires significant capital investment, with profitability potentially delayed until 2028-2029 even with approval
