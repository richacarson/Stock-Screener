Latham Group is the largest designer, manufacturer, and marketer of in-ground residential swimming pools in North America, Australia, and New Zealand. Products include fiberglass pools, packaged pools, automatic safety covers, all-season covers, and vinyl liners sold through a network of dealers and distributors.

Investment Thesis
Latham Group is the dominant in-ground pool manufacturer in North America executing a growth recovery after the 2022-2023 housing downturn. 2025 revenue grew 7.4% to $546M with adjusted EBITDA surging 24.4% to $99.8M (18.3% margin), and 2026 guidance of $580-610M (+9%) demonstrates sustained momentum driven by fiberglass market share gains and Sand States expansion [1]. The Freedom Pools acquisition adds $20M in revenue and international diversification. At $5.80/share with $678M market cap, the stock trades at 22x forward earnings on an earnings-recovery trajectory. The fiberglass pool category has significant runway (low-teens penetration vs concrete) providing a multi-year secular growth tailwind that is independent of new pool starts [1].
The bear case centers on housing cyclicality and profitability concerns. Trailing P/E of 64x and profit margin of only 2% highlight that current earnings are still depressed from the downturn cycle — any stall in the recovery would make the valuation appear expensive [1]. ROE of 2.8% is far below cost of capital, indicating the business has not yet earned its way back to adequate returns on the PE-originated debt load (D/E 78%). The 5-year revenue CAGR is approximately flat, meaning the company has not yet demonstrated it can grow sustainably above pre-IPO levels. U.S. pool starts remain near cyclical lows, and rising interest rates could further suppress new construction demand. Competition from local concrete builders and new fiberglass entrants could compress margins as the category grows. The stock has declined from its 2021 IPO price of $19, reflecting persistent market skepticism about the growth story [1].
Key Catalysts
  1. Fiberglass pool market share gains in Sand States (Arizona, Texas, Florida) where fiberglass penetration is lowest and conversion opportunity is greatest [1]
  2. Freedom Pools acquisition (February 2026) adding $20M revenue and $4M EBITDA with accretion before synergies, expanding addressable market internationally [1]
  3. Adjusted EBITDA margin expansion from 18.3% toward 20%+ as volume leverage and manufacturing efficiency gains flow through fixed-cost infrastructure [1]
Key Risks
  1. Housing cyclicality — U.S. pool starts remain near cyclical lows and rising interest rates could further suppress new construction demand, delaying revenue recovery [1]
  2. Weak profitability with 2% net margin and 2.8% ROE indicating the debt-heavy capital structure has not yet been de-leveraged sufficiently to generate adequate shareholder returns [1]
  3. New CEO transition risk — Sean Gadd joined January 2026 from James Hardie, creating execution uncertainty during a critical growth recovery period [2]
