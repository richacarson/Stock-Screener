Company Profile
Basic Materials ·Coking Coal ·NYSE ·United States
SunCoke Energy is the largest independent producer of high-quality metallurgical coke in the Americas, with over 45 years of production experience using proprietary heat recovery cokemaking technology. The company operates through two segments: Domestic Coke (serving blast furnace steelmakers) and Industrial Services (material handling, scrap processing, and logistics for steel and industrial customers). SunCoke acquired Phoenix Global in 2025 to expand industrial services.

Investment Thesis
SunCoke Energy offers a 7.3% dividend yield supported by the largest independent metallurgical coke operation in the Americas and a diversifying Industrial Services segment bolstered by the Phoenix Global acquisition [1]. Management guides $230-250M adjusted EBITDA for 2026, with Domestic Coke contributing $162-168M and Industrial Services (including a full year of Phoenix) contributing $90-100M [1]. The dividend has grown at a 14.9% 5-year CAGR ($0.24 to $0.48 annually), recently extended customer contracts provide revenue visibility, and the stock trades at just $6.56 with a $557M market cap, representing deep value if EBITDA targets are met [4].
The bear case centers on cyclicality and secular decline. SunCoke's core coke business is structurally tied to blast furnace steelmaking, which faces long-term decline as the steel industry transitions to electric arc furnaces (EAF) — EAF now represents over 70% of U.S. steel production [1]. Current TTM earnings are negative (-$0.52 EPS), with profit margin at -2.4% and ROE at -5.8%, driven partly by Q4 2025 operational headwinds including a turbine failure at Middletown and severe weather trimming ~$10M from Q1 2026 results [1]. Debt of $703.6M against $88.7M cash creates leverage risk (D/E 112%), particularly if steel demand weakens. Revenue has contracted from $2.06B (2023) to $1.84B (2025), and analysts forecast further 1.6% decline in 2026. The 64% payout ratio on negative earnings is unsustainable without a rapid return to profitability [4].
Key Catalysts
  1. Phoenix Global integration delivering full-year Industrial Services EBITDA of $90-100M and early synergies through 2026 [1]
  2. Return to profitability in 2026 with management's $230-250M adjusted EBITDA guidance, supported by recently extended long-term coke contracts [1]
  3. Dividend growth continuation — the stock yields 7.3% with a 14.9% 5-year dividend growth CAGR, attractive if earnings stabilize [4]
Key Risks
  1. Secular decline in blast furnace steelmaking as EAF adoption accelerates — SunCoke's core coke demand is structurally at risk from this industry transition [1]
  2. Operational headwinds — Middletown turbine failure and weather impacts are expected to trim ~$10M from Q1 2026, and similar disruptions could recur across aging facilities [1]
  3. Dividend sustainability — the 64% payout ratio is being paid from negative trailing earnings, making the dividend dependent on a rapid return to profitability in 2026 [4]
