Company Profile
Healthcare ·Biotechnology ·NASDAQ ·United States
60 Degrees Pharmaceuticals is a specialty pharmaceutical company focused on discovering, developing, and commercializing therapies for infectious diseases including malaria, babesiosis, and other tropical diseases. Its lead product ARAKODA (tafenoquine) is FDA-approved for malaria prevention, with a clinical pipeline targeting babesiosis, fungal pneumonias, and candidiasis.

Investment Thesis
60 Degrees Pharmaceuticals offers speculative upside driven by its FDA-approved ARAKODA franchise and promising babesiosis clinical data. Revenue surged 65% YoY to $1.0M in FY2025 as GoodRx and Runway Health partnerships expanded distribution to 70,000+ pharmacies [1]. All expanded access babesiosis patients were cured with tafenoquine treatment, and the interim analysis of the randomized hospital study is expected as early as September 2026 — a positive readout could unlock a significant new market for the drug [1]. Operating expenses decreased 16% to $8.4M while commercial activity expanded, and the near-zero debt ($153K) provides balance sheet flexibility. At a $4.3M market cap, the stock offers extreme asymmetric upside if babesiosis data reads out positively and ARAKODA commercial traction accelerates.
The bear case is existential for a company of this size. With only $1.0M in annual revenue against $8.4M in operating expenses, 60P burns approximately $7M annually and has only $2.75M in cash, implying roughly 4-5 months of runway without additional capital raises [1][4]. The company exhausted its $4M ATM facility, and further dilution is virtually guaranteed — shares outstanding have expanded significantly. The $4.3M market cap means a single bad clinical readout or capital raise could wipe out 50%+ of equity value. ARAKODA faces established, low-cost competition from generic doxycycline and atovaquone-proguanil for malaria prevention. The malaria prophylaxis market in the U.S. is small (primarily military and travelers), limiting the commercial ceiling. Babesiosis is a niche indication, and even a successful trial may not generate enough revenue to reach profitability. This is a binary biotech bet with high probability of continued dilution.
Key Catalysts
  1. Babesiosis randomized hospital study interim analysis expected as early as September 2026 — positive data could validate a new indication and significantly expand the addressable market [1]
  2. GoodRx and Runway Health partnerships expanding ARAKODA distribution to 70,000+ pharmacies and travel-focused telehealth, potentially accelerating revenue growth from the 65% YoY trajectory [1]
  3. FDA-approved tafenoquine platform with potential to address multiple infectious disease indications (fungal pneumonias, candidiasis, Zika/dengue) through pipeline expansion [1]
Key Risks
  1. Cash runway of approximately 4-5 months ($2.75M cash against ~$7M annual burn) with ATM facility exhausted — imminent dilutive capital raise required for survival [1][4]
  2. Binary clinical risk on babesiosis — a negative interim readout in September 2026 would eliminate the primary growth catalyst and likely trigger severe stock decline [1]
  3. Micro-cap concentration risk ($4.3M market cap) with negligible revenue ($1.0M), making the stock highly susceptible to single-event volatility and liquidity-driven price dislocations [4]
