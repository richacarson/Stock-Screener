Investment Thesis
Synchrony offers deep value at 6.3x forward earnings with a forward PEG of 0.50x for a company generating 21.3% ROE and 36% profit margins. The Walmart partnership (fastest-growing program ever) and Amazon renewal provide multi-year revenue visibility, while credit quality improvement (charge-offs down 90bps, delinquencies declining) signals the consumer credit cycle has turned favorably. The $2.1B buyback authorization and 1.8% dividend yield provide capital return support, and management guides for mid-to-high single-digit growth accelerating through 2026 driven by BNPL expansion across 6,200+ merchants.
The bear case centers on Synchrony's vulnerability to consumer credit deterioration: the company serves primarily subprime and near-prime borrowers, and a recession could spike charge-offs well above the current 5.16% rate. Revenue declined 4.3% in FY2025 after peaking at $16.1B in 2024, reflecting the complexity of the late fee regulatory environment. While the CFPB $8 cap was vacated, future administrations could reinstate similar rules, threatening the $2.7B late fee revenue stream. Synchrony's retention of elevated interest rates post-rule-vacatur creates reputational risk and potential political targeting. Competition from BNPL platforms could structurally erode revolving credit demand among younger consumers.
