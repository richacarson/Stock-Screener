Investment Thesis
Synchrony Financial offers deep value at 7.1x trailing earnings and 6.3x forward PE with a 21.3% ROE, well below consumer finance peers. The 2026 EPS guidance of $9.10-$9.50 implies continued earnings stability, while the Walmart OnePay partnership launching in fall 2026 and expanded Amazon BNPL integration provide concrete growth catalysts. The $2.5B buyback program and 20% dividend increase underscore management's confidence in capital generation, and the company's 94% Great Place to Work score suggests an operational culture that can sustain execution quality.
The bear case centers on declining revenue — full-year 2025 revenue fell 7.1% to $14.98B as loan receivables contracted 1% amid moderating consumer spending. Credit losses could rise in a recessionary scenario, threatening the $104B loan book's profitability. The 1.81% dividend yield falls short of the 2% threshold for income investors, the Inspire score of -49 limits faith-based appeal, and fintech/BNPL competitors continue to chip away at private label card market share. Additionally, the loss of the Walmart credit card program (now transitioning to OnePay) creates near-term revenue uncertainty.
