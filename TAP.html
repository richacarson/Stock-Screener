Company Profile
Consumer Defensive ·Beverages - Brewers ·NYSE ·United States
Molson Coors manufactures, markets, and distributes beer and malt beverages across the Americas, Europe, Middle East, Africa, and Asia Pacific. Its portfolio includes Coors Light, Miller Lite, Blue Moon, Peroni Nastro Azzurro, and Staropramen, spanning premium, above-premium, and economy segments. Founded in 1774, the company is headquartered in Golden, Colorado.

Investment Thesis
Molson Coors offers deep value at 8.7x forward P/E with a 4.4% dividend yield backed by 38+ consecutive years of payments and $1.78B in operating cash flow [1][4]. The #2 U.S. beer position with Coors Light and Miller Lite provides defensive brand equity, and the $450M cost-savings program should partially offset commodity inflation headwinds in 2026-2028. New CEO Rahul Goyal brings strategic continuity as former Chief Strategy Officer, and the company's above-premium portfolio expansion (Blue Moon, Peroni, Topo Chico Hard Seltzer) positions it for better mix over time [2]. At current levels, investors are paying less than 9x earnings for a stable, cash-generative beverage franchise with a growing dividend.
The bear case centers on deteriorating fundamentals and structural industry headwinds. Revenue declined 2.7% in 2025 to $11.14B, and 2026 guidance calls for flat revenue with underlying EPS declining 11-15% due to commodity inflation [1]. The $2.1B goodwill impairment in 2025 wiped out earnings (trailing EPS of -$10.75) and signals management's admission that prior acquisitions destroyed value. The U.S. beer market faces secular volume declines as younger consumers shift toward spirits, cannabis, and moderation trends. Molson Coors' economy brands (Keystone, Milwaukee's Best) face the steepest volume erosion. The debt load of $6.5B against $980M cash limits financial flexibility, and free cash flow of $700M constrains both debt reduction and dividend growth capacity. Three CEO changes in a decade raises questions about strategic continuity [2].
Key Catalysts
  1. $450M three-year cost savings program beginning in 2026, targeting operational efficiency to offset commodity inflation and protect margins [1]
  2. Above-premium portfolio expansion (Blue Moon, Peroni, Topo Chico Hard Seltzer, ZOA Energy) driving revenue mix improvement toward higher-margin brands [1]
  3. New CEO Rahul Goyal's organizational restructuring to enable 'bolder, quicker decision making' with direct commercial reporting lines [2]
Key Risks
  1. Commodity inflation headwinds driving 15-18% underlying earnings decline in 2026, with 2026 EPS guidance of $4.61-$4.82 materially below consensus of $5.42 [1]
  2. Secular U.S. beer volume decline as consumers shift toward spirits, cannabis, non-alcoholic alternatives, and moderation — particularly impacting economy brands [1]
  3. $6.5B debt load with $2.1B goodwill impairment in 2025 signaling overpayment for past acquisitions, limiting strategic flexibility and raising balance sheet risk [4]
