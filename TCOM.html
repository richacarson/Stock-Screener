Trip.com Group operates as a leading online travel agency through its Ctrip, Qunar, Trip.com, and Skyscanner brands, providing accommodation reservation, transportation ticketing, packaged tours, corporate travel management, and in-destination services across China and internationally. Founded in 1999, it serves over 400 million users globally.

Investment Thesis
Trip.com Group is a dominant travel platform delivering exceptional financial performance — FY2024 revenue of RMB 62.4B (up 17% YoY) with Q4 accelerating to 21% growth, profit margin of 53.3%, and ROE of 21.1% [1]. At 10.8x forward P/E with 20%+ revenue growth, the stock trades at a significant discount to global OTA peers like Booking Holdings (20x+), reflecting a persistent China discount despite accelerating international diversification. Pure international business is growing 60%+ YoY and management targets doubling inbound travelers to 40 million in 2026 [1]. The fortress balance sheet (18% D/E, RMB 105.8B liquidity) provides ample runway for buybacks, M&A, and the Tourism Innovation Fund. Entertainment-plus-travel (triple-digit growth) and corporate travel (RMB 2.8B, up 13%) diversify revenue streams beyond leisure booking [1].
The bear case centers on China macro risk and AI disruption. Geopolitical tensions, property market weakness, and consumer confidence headwinds could slow China's domestic travel recovery. The persistent valuation discount reflects real structural risk — regulatory uncertainty, VIE structure concerns, and potential US-China decoupling could compress multiples further regardless of operational performance. AI-powered travel agents from Google, OpenAI, and emerging startups threaten to disintermediate OTAs by enabling direct supplier booking through conversational interfaces. While Trip.com is investing in AI, its competitive moat rests on supplier relationships and inventory access that could erode as AI tools gain direct integration with airlines and hotels. Currency risk (RMB-denominated revenue, USD-listed ADRs) adds volatility [5].
Key Catalysts
  1. International OTA expansion targeting 60%+ revenue growth in pure international business and 40M inbound travelers by 2026, reducing China concentration risk [1]
  2. AI-powered travel discovery platform transformation from booking engine to intelligent trip planner, driving higher conversion and customer lifetime value [2]
  3. Entertainment-plus-travel segment posting triple-digit growth with 1,540 globally ticketed shows, creating a new high-margin revenue vertical [1]
Key Risks
  1. China macro headwinds — consumer confidence erosion, property market weakness, and regulatory uncertainty could slow domestic travel spending growth [5]
  2. AI disintermediation — conversational AI travel agents from Google and OpenAI could bypass OTAs by enabling direct supplier booking, threatening Trip.com's aggregator model [2]
  3. Geopolitical and structural risk — US-China tensions, VIE structure concerns, and potential ADR delisting risk contribute to persistent valuation discount [5]
