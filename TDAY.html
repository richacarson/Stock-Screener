Company Profile
Communication Services ·Publishing ·NYSE ·United States
USA TODAY Co., Inc. (formerly Gannett Co., Inc.) operates as a media and digital marketing solutions company. It publishes USA Today and over 200 local daily newspapers, operates digital media brands, and provides digital marketing solutions under the LocaliQ brand. The company is transitioning from legacy print toward digital revenue, which is projected to surpass 50% of total revenue by 2026.

Investment Thesis
USA TODAY Co. trades at a deeply discounted $1.07B market cap for a company generating $2.3B in annual revenue, reflecting the market's justified skepticism about legacy media's future. The $100M annualized cost reduction program is expected to drive adjusted EBITDA growth in 2025-2026, and digital revenue is approaching 50% of total revenue with digital advertising returning to growth (up 4% in Q2 2025) [1]. The LocaliQ platform provides a sticky SMB customer base with recurring revenue characteristics, and the PEG ratio of 0.94 suggests modest earnings improvement is priced in at current levels [4].
The bear case is overwhelming for a long-term holder. Revenue has declined every year from $2.95B (2022) to $2.30B (2025), a cumulative 22% contraction driven by secular print advertising declines that show no sign of abating [1]. D/E of 733% with $1.13B in debt against minimal equity creates existential refinancing risk. Profit margin of 0.08% leaves zero margin of safety — any revenue miss could push the company into losses. The company projects revenue 'flatness' as its best-case 2026 outcome, not growth [1]. AI-powered content generation and ad targeting platforms will continue to erode both the news and advertising businesses. With trailing EPS of $0.01, the stock trades on hope rather than fundamentals.
Key Catalysts
  1. Digital revenue crossing 50% of total revenue in 2026, potentially improving margin profile as print costs decline [1]
  2. Continued EBITDA margin expansion from the $100M cost reduction program, with management guiding to 'solid adjusted EBITDA growth' in 2026 [1]
  3. Potential strategic value of 200+ local media brands and the LocaliQ digital marketing platform to an acquirer
Key Risks
  1. Secular print advertising decline continues to accelerate as AI-powered ad platforms divert SMB budgets to Google, Meta, and emerging alternatives [1]
  2. Refinancing risk on $1.13B debt load with D/E of 733% and near-zero profitability — any credit market tightening could be existential [4]
  3. AI-generated local news content from Google, OpenAI, and Meta could directly substitute for USA TODAY Co.'s core local journalism product, eliminating the remaining structural moat [2]
