Investment Thesis
Teradata is a legacy data analytics company executing a credible cloud/AI pivot with improving economics. Cloud ARR grew 15% to $701M (46% of total ARR) with a 108% net expansion rate, and Enterprise AgentStack/Vector Store represent genuine AI products [1]. At 9.6x forward P/E with 2026 FCF guidance of $310-330M (12% yield on $2.6B market cap), the stock is deeply discounted relative to software peers [5]. Adjusted EPS guidance of $2.55-$2.65 represents 13% earnings growth, and net income has improved from $33M (2022) to $130M (2025) despite revenue declines [1]. The company's enterprise relationships in regulated industries (financial services, healthcare, government) provide sticky recurring revenue that cloud-native disruptors struggle to penetrate quickly.
The bear case is that Teradata is a shrinking legacy business attempting to pivot while being disrupted by better-funded competitors. Total revenue has declined from $1.83B (2023) to $1.66B (2025), and 2026 guidance projects further decline of 0-2% [1]. Snowflake, Databricks, Google BigQuery, and AWS Redshift offer more modern, cloud-native architectures that new workloads increasingly prefer. The PEG ratio of 6.0 reflects the market's skepticism about sustainable growth. D/E of 242% ($557M debt) limits financial flexibility for the aggressive investment needed to compete in AI analytics [5]. Employee reviews flagging 'constant layoffs' and 'killing off primary products that customers need in favor of joining the AI trend' suggest the transformation may be destabilizing the very enterprise relationships that sustain the business [3]. The cloud migration success (15% Cloud ARR growth) may simply represent existing customers moving workloads, not net new customer acquisition — creating a one-time boost that fades once migration completes.
Key Catalysts
  1. Cloud ARR trajectory toward majority of total business — currently at 46% and growing 15% YoY, with low double-digit growth targeted for 2026, creating a path to recurring revenue stabilization [1]
  2. Free cash flow generation of $310-330M in 2026 (12% yield) provides capital return optionality through buybacks and positions TDC as a deep value play if the turnaround gains traction [1]
  3. Enterprise AgentStack and Vector Store adoption by regulated industry customers could validate Teradata's AI strategy and differentiate from cloud-native competitors in enterprise security and governance [1]
Key Risks
  1. Total revenue decline continuing in 2026 (guided -2% to flat) as legacy on-premise erosion outpaces cloud growth, with no clear inflection point to revenue expansion [1]
  2. Cloud-native competitors (Snowflake, Databricks, Google BigQuery) with larger R&D budgets and modern architectures capturing new AI workloads, potentially making Teradata's hybrid approach obsolete [1]
  3. Organizational strain from ongoing layoffs and product strategy pivots — Glassdoor reviews citing leadership 'killing off primary products that customers need' suggest customer relationship risk during the transition [3]
