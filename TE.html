Company Profile
Technology ·Solar ·NYSE ·United States
T1 Energy (formerly FREYR Battery) is building an integrated U.S. solar and battery manufacturing supply chain, operating a 5 GW solar module plant (G1_Dallas) and planning an $850M, 5 GW solar cell plant (G2_Austin). The company generated $132.8M in Q2 2025 revenue from G1_Dallas sales and is targeting $375–450M in annual EBITDA run-rate at full G1 and G2 Phase I capacity. CEO Daniel Barcelo leads the company, which completed a transformative transaction in December 2024.

Investment Thesis
T1 Energy's G1_Dallas facility has demonstrated real manufacturing capability — surpassing 1 GW cumulative production in 2025 and ramping toward $375–450M in annual EBITDA at full 5 GW capacity [1]. The 45X domestic content tax credits provide a $0.07–0.12/watt subsidy advantage over Chinese imports, creating structural domestic competitiveness [2]. Analyst ratings average Strong Buy with $7.30 price targets implying 43% upside [3], and the Trump administration's solar manufacturing focus could accelerate procurement preferences. The $1.6B+ in U.S. solar demand commitments secured since the One Big Beautiful Bill passage validates demand.
The bear case is dominated by governance risk: active DoJ and SEC subpoenas (disclosed November 2025), CEO personal exploitation lawsuit, Culper Research's allegations of financial reporting irregularities, and RWE's contract cancellation for alleged misrepresentation collectively create an environment where the financial statements cannot be fully trusted [2]. Culper's claim that T1 is ineligible for 45X tax credits due to Trina Solar's FEOC status — if upheld by Treasury guidance — would eliminate the company's primary competitive advantage. The stock is down sharply from its 52-week high and faces continued regulatory scrutiny.
Key Catalysts
  1. G2_Austin Phase 1 construction start Q4 2025 with production launch Q4 2026 — adds 2.1 GW of domestic cell capacity that eliminates FEOC dependency and solidifies 45X tax credit eligibility [1]
  2. DoJ/SEC subpoena resolution without charges — clearing the CEO and company of the most serious allegations would restore institutional investor confidence and likely catalyze significant multiple expansion
  3. 45X tax credit Treasury guidance confirming eligibility — removes the Culper Research central thesis that T1 cannot claim the credits, validating the entire domestic manufacturing economics
Key Risks
  1. 45X tax credit ineligibility ruling: if Treasury confirms Trina Solar's FEOC status makes T1 ineligible, the company loses $100–200M in annual credit value and its competitive cost structure collapses [2]
  2. DoJ/SEC investigation resulting in charges against CEO Barcelo or the company — management disruption and potential financial penalties could trigger covenant defaults on construction financing [2]
  3. RWE litigation: largest independent offtake customer sued for alleged contract misrepresentation — loss could require $100M+ in damages and expose additional commercial contract irregularities
