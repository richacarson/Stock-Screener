Company Profile
Technology ·Electronic Components ·NYSE ·Ireland
TE Connectivity plc is a $63B global industrial technology leader that designs and manufactures connectivity and sensor solutions across two segments: Transportation Solutions and Industrial Solutions. The company provides connectors, sensors, antennas, relays, fiber optics, and cable assemblies serving automotive, aerospace, data centers, AI infrastructure, industrial machinery, medical devices, and energy markets. Founded in 1941 as Tyco Electronics, rebranded in 2011, headquartered in Ballybrit, Ireland with 90,000 employees globally.

Investment Thesis
TE Connectivity delivered exceptional Q2 FY2026 results with $4.74B revenue (up 15% YoY), record adjusted EPS of $2.73 (up 24% YoY), and record orders of $5.3B (up 25% YoY), demonstrating broad-based demand acceleration across transportation and industrial end markets [1]. The company sits at the intersection of three secular growth themes — AI data center buildout, electric vehicle adoption, and industrial automation — each requiring increasing volumes of high-reliability connectors, sensors, and power management solutions. With D/E of 43.8%, ROE of 22.7%, Forward P/E of 17.2x, a PEG of 1.0, and 17 consecutive years of dividend growth at an 8.4% CAGR, TE offers a rare combination of growth exposure and financial discipline. The Inspire Impact score of +58 with 14 positive attributions makes this one of the strongest faith-aligned industrial holdings available.
The primary concern is the cyclicality of TE's end markets and the sustainability of the current growth rate. Revenue 5-year CAGR of only 4.6% (dragged down by FY2023-2024 softness) reveals that TE's growth is uneven across cycles, and the current AI/EV-driven acceleration may prove temporary if capex spending normalizes [1][5]. Profit margin of 15.5% is below the 20% target for the Infrastructure score, reflecting the hardware manufacturing cost structure that limits margin expansion potential even as operating leverage improves. Q3 FY2026 guidance calls for 10% sales growth (decelerating from 15% in Q2 and 22% in Q1), which may indicate the growth rate is already peaking [1]. Geographic and customer concentration risks persist — automotive represents a large share of revenue and is sensitive to consumer demand cycles and EV adoption pace.
Key Catalysts
  1. AI data center infrastructure buildout driving record $5.3B quarterly orders — TE's high-speed optical and power connectors are essential components in every GPU rack and AI computing cluster being deployed globally
  2. Q3 FY2026 guidance of 10% sales growth and 17% EPS growth with improving operating margins, supported by a backlog that provides near-term revenue visibility into FY2027
  3. Dividend growth trajectory continuing at 8.4% CAGR with a conservative 29% payout ratio providing substantial room for continued increases through the current growth cycle
Key Risks
  1. Revenue growth decelerating from 22% in Q1 to 15% in Q2 to guided 10% in Q3 FY2026, suggesting the AI/EV-driven demand surge may be normalizing faster than the market expects
  2. Automotive end market representing a large share of revenue is sensitive to consumer demand cycles, EV adoption pace uncertainty, and potential tariff impacts on global supply chains
  3. Profit margin of 15.5% reflects hardware manufacturing cost structure that limits margin expansion potential relative to higher-margin technology peers, capping valuation multiple

AI Resilience (25%)
AI Resilience LOW RISK
9
AI Risk Protection (8/9): TE Connectivity's core business — manufacturing physical connectors, sensors, and cable assemblies for automotive, aerospace, data centers, and industrial applications — is structurally immune to AI disruption [1][2]. AI cannot replace physical hardware that connects processors, powers GPU racks, routes optical signals, or senses temperature and pressure in industrial environments. More importantly, AI is a massive demand driver for TE's business: every AI data center requires thousands of high-speed connectors, power distribution units, and cooling infrastructure components. Record Q2 FY2026 orders of $5.3B (up 25% YoY) are substantially driven by AI data center buildout [1]. The only exposure is potential AI-driven demand destruction in the automotive segment if autonomous vehicles reduce total vehicle miles or EV adoption slows. AI Opportunity (1/1): TE generates quantifiable revenue from AI-related connectivity products — high-speed optical connectors, power connectors for GPU rack infrastructure, and thermal management solutions for AI data centers. CEO Curtin has identified AI infrastructure as a primary growth catalyst, and the $5.3B record orders reflect significant AI-driven demand [1][2].
