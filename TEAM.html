Company Profile
Technology ·Software—Application ·NASDAQ ·United States
Atlassian Corporation provides enterprise collaboration and developer tools including Jira, Confluence, Trello, Bitbucket, and Jira Service Management. The company serves over 350,000 customers worldwide, including 75% of the Fortune 500. Founded in 2002 in Sydney, Australia by co-founders Mike Cannon-Brookes and Scott Farquhar, Atlassian is executing a cloud-first migration strategy and has launched Rovo, an AI agent platform now bundled for all enterprise customers. TTM revenue of $6.19B with 24.74% YoY growth.
13,813 Employees ·www.atlassian.com

Investment Thesis
Atlassian at 14.1x forward earnings with 25%+ revenue growth represents one of the most attractively valued high-growth enterprise SaaS companies in the market [5]. Cloud revenue crossed $1.132B per quarter (+29% YoY), and the Rovo AI platform has achieved remarkable traction — 14M+ assisted actions per month, 7x growth in agentic automation, adoption by 75% of Fortune 500 [1][2]. The non-GAAP PEG ratio of approximately 0.56 suggests significant undervaluation relative to growth. With 350,000+ customers, deep enterprise switching costs, and a 34% non-GAAP operating margin, Atlassian combines durable competitive moats with AI-driven engagement acceleration. The 'unleash the potential of every team' mission has remained consistent for 20+ years under founder-led management, and the decision to bundle Rovo free for enterprise users demonstrates conviction in long-term platform value. Net-zero emissions target by 2040, 100% renewable electricity since FY2020, and the Atlassian Foundation's 1% pledge model provide ESG credibility [3].
The bear case centers on GAAP profitability, competitive threats, and valuation sustainability. GAAP metrics are unflattering: ROE of -19.29%, profit margin of -3.50%, and P/E of -105.78 reflect significant stock-based compensation expense that dilutes shareholders [5]. D/E of 141% is elevated for a software company. While non-GAAP margins are strong at 34%, the gap between GAAP and non-GAAP profitability raises questions about the true economic cost of equity compensation. Microsoft's Azure DevOps, Teams, and Copilot integration create a formidable bundled competitor — enterprises already paying for Microsoft 365 face lower marginal cost to adopt Microsoft's collaboration tools [2]. AI-native project management tools like Linear, Notion AI, and GitHub Copilot Workspace could erode Atlassian's developer tools moat from below. The Data Center to Cloud migration, while strategically correct, creates revenue transition risk as some customers may not convert. FY2026 guidance of ~24% total revenue growth is strong but represents deceleration from Q3's 32% YoY pace, and maintaining growth rates above 20% becomes arithmetically harder as the $6B+ revenue base scales [1][4].
Key Catalysts
  1. Rovo AI platform monetization inflection — currently bundled free for enterprise users, future premium AI features and increased seat expansion driven by 14M+ monthly assisted actions and 7x agentic automation growth could unlock significant incremental revenue [2]
  2. Cloud migration completion driving margin expansion — as remaining Data Center customers convert to cloud subscriptions, operating leverage and recurring revenue predictability should improve, with non-GAAP operating margin already at 34% [1]
  3. FY2026 guidance reaffirmation of ~24% total revenue growth and ~26.5% cloud revenue growth, with Q3 FY2026 delivering 32% YoY revenue growth and cloud crossing $1.132B quarterly — demonstrating acceleration above guidance [1][4]
Key Risks
  1. GAAP profitability remains negative with -3.50% profit margin and -19.29% ROE, driven by significant stock-based compensation that dilutes existing shareholders — the persistent gap between GAAP and non-GAAP metrics masks the real economic cost of equity grants [5]
  2. Microsoft bundling threat — enterprises already paying for Microsoft 365 face minimal incremental cost to adopt Azure DevOps, Teams, and Copilot, potentially pressuring Atlassian's pricing power and new customer acquisition in large enterprise deals [2]
  3. Revenue growth deceleration risk as the $6B+ revenue base scales — FY2026 guidance of ~24% growth represents a deceleration from Q3's 32% pace, and maintaining 20%+ growth becomes arithmetically harder, which could compress the forward multiple if the market prices in slower growth [1][4]
