Company Profile
Basic Materials ·Other Industrial Metals & Mining ·NYSE ·Canada
Teck Resources is a leading Canadian mining company focused on copper and zinc production. After divesting its coal and oil sands businesses, Teck operates through Copper and Zinc segments, producing copper, zinc, and lead concentrates along with refined metals, precious metals, molybdenum, and fertilizers. The pending Anglo American merger would create a global critical minerals champion with 70%+ copper exposure.

Investment Thesis
Teck Resources is executing a transformative pivot from diversified mining to focused copper-zinc production, with the Anglo American merger (Canadian approval received December 2025) set to create a global critical minerals champion with 70%+ copper exposure and $800M in annual synergies [1]. QB2 in Chile is ramping to increase attributable copper production by ~75%, with TTM revenue already at $7.7B USD (up 29% YoY in CAD terms). Structural copper deficits projected from 2026 onward, driven by AI data center buildout and electrification, provide a secular commodity tailwind [1]. D/E of just 37.1% with $5B cash provides balance sheet flexibility, and at 18.8x forward P/E, the stock is reasonably valued for a copper miner with production growth visibility and merger-driven synergies [5]. Analyst EPS estimates surged 70% over the past 60 days, signaling growing confidence in operational momentum [1].
The bear case centers on execution risk and ESG legacy issues. The Anglo American merger introduces integration complexity — combining two global mining organizations with different cultures, geographies, and operational practices creates multi-year execution risk. ROE of just 4.0% is weak, reflecting the transition period costs and QB2 ramp-up expenses [5]. Teck's environmental legacy at Elk Valley — selenium contamination, millions in fines, and Indigenous opposition from the Ktunaxa Nation since 2012 — represents ongoing reputational and financial liability [3]. The coal spinoff was criticized as 'greenwashing' given the 90% cash flow royalty structure, and the sale to Glencore (with its own poor ESG record) raised further concerns [3]. Copper price volatility remains a fundamental risk — a cyclical downturn could compress margins and devalue the QB2 investment. The $9.6B debt load and negative free cash flow (-$254M) create vulnerability in a commodity downturn [5].
Key Catalysts
  1. Anglo American merger completion creating Anglo Teck — a global copper champion with 70%+ copper exposure, $800M in pre-tax synergies, and enhanced scale in critical minerals [1]
  2. QB2 copper mine reaching full production capacity, increasing Teck's attributable copper output by ~75% and driving revenue growth as structural copper deficits emerge in 2026 [1]
  3. Structural copper deficit driven by AI data center buildout, electrification, and EV adoption supporting sustained copper price appreciation above $4/lb [1]
Key Risks
  1. Anglo American merger integration complexity — combining global operations, cultures, and regulatory frameworks across multiple countries creates multi-year execution risk with $800M synergy targets at stake [1]
  2. Elk Valley selenium contamination legacy — ongoing fines, Indigenous opposition from the Ktunaxa Nation, and potential International Joint Commission referral creating financial and reputational liability [3]
  3. Copper price cyclicality — a global economic downturn or demand disappointment could compress margins on the copper-heavy portfolio, with ROE already at just 4% and free cash flow negative [5]

AI Resilience (25%)
AI Resilience LOW RISK

AI Risk Protection (8/9): Copper and zinc mining is structurally immune to AI disruption — AI cannot digitally replicate the extraction, processing, and refining of physical metals from the earth [2]. AI actually increases demand for Teck's primary product: copper is essential for data center electrical infrastructure, AI chip packaging, and the electrification of the energy grid needed to power AI workloads. Analysts project structural copper deficits starting in 2026 as AI-driven demand accelerates [1]. Teck's pivot to 70%+ copper exposure through the Anglo American merger positions it as a direct beneficiary of AI infrastructure buildout. Mining operations involve physical-world processes, heavy equipment, and geological expertise that no AI system can substitute. AI Opportunity (1/1): Teck generates indirect AI-linked revenue through copper sales to the AI supply chain and deploys AI internally for mining optimization and predictive maintenance [2].
