Company Profile
Basic Materials ·Copper ·NYSE ·Canada
Teck Resources Limited is a leading Canadian mining company focused on copper and zinc production, with principal operations in the Americas. Major assets include Quebrada Blanca (Chile), Highland Valley Copper (Canada), Antamina (Peru), and Trail zinc smelting operations (Canada). The company divested its steelmaking coal business in 2023 to become a pure-play critical minerals producer for the energy transition. Currently pursuing a merger of equals with Anglo American plc to create a global critical minerals champion. Headquartered in Vancouver, British Columbia.

Investment Thesis
Teck Resources is executing a compelling transformation from diversified miner to pure-play copper producer at precisely the right moment in the copper supercycle. Q1 2026 delivered record results — revenue surged to $3.94B (from $2.29B in Q1 2025), adjusted EBITDA more than doubled to $2.1B, and copper segment revenue jumped 92% to $2.9B on record QB production and copper prices of $5.78/lb [1]. The company ended Q1 in a net cash position of $488M with D/E of just 37%. The Anglo American merger of equals will create a top-5 global copper producer with over 70% copper exposure and a robust pipeline of growth projects [2]. Copper demand fundamentals are structurally positive — AI data centers, electric vehicles, and renewable energy all require significantly more copper than conventional alternatives.
The primary risk is commodity price cyclicality. Copper prices of $5.78/lb are near all-time highs, and any normalization could severely compress earnings — the TTM ROE of 3.06% reflects how quickly mining margins can evaporate in unfavorable price environments [4]. The 5-year revenue CAGR of -2.3% (distorted by coal divestiture) and trailing P/E of 27.80 suggest the market is pricing in continued elevated copper prices. The Anglo American merger introduces execution risk — integration of a company of similar scale with operations across multiple continents is inherently complex, and Teck shareholders will own only 37.6% of the combined entity [2]. ESG controversies around the legacy coal business and Elk Valley environmental issues persist despite the divestiture [3]. CEO Jonathan Price has been in role only since 2023, creating leadership uncertainty during a transformative period. Profit margin of 11.98% reflects the capital-intensive nature of mining and leaves limited earnings cushion against copper price declines.
Key Catalysts
  1. Anglo American merger of equals creating a top-5 global copper producer with 70%+ copper exposure and world-class growth project pipeline, expected to close in 2026-2027
  2. Quebrada Blanca Phase 2 production ramp to full capacity of 300K+ tonnes annually, with Q1 2026 already delivering record 70,300-tonne quarterly sales
  3. Structural copper demand growth from AI data center buildout (2-3x copper intensity), electric vehicle adoption, and renewable energy infrastructure supporting sustained above-trend pricing
Key Risks
  1. Copper price cyclicality — realized price of $5.78/lb is near all-time highs, and normalization to $3.50-4.00/lb would dramatically compress margins given the capital-intensive cost structure
  2. Anglo American merger execution risk — integrating a company of similar scale across multiple continents with 37.6% Teck shareholder ownership creates dilution, cultural integration, and operational challenges
  3. 5-year revenue CAGR of -2.3% and trailing P/E of 27.80 reflect a company priced for continued copper supercycle — any demand slowdown from China or delayed AI infrastructure buildout could trigger multiple compression
