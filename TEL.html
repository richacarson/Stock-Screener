Investment Thesis
TE Connectivity is the physical backbone of the AI revolution — every GPU, server rack, and data center requires TE's connectors and cable assemblies to function, creating a structural demand tailwind with record orders of $5.1B (+28% YoY) [1]. Q1 FY2026 delivered 22% revenue growth, 33% adjusted EPS growth, and 180bps margin expansion to 22.2% adjusted operating margin [1]. The AdrenaLINE 224G platform positions TE at the frontier of AI data center connectivity as speeds scale from 56Gbps to 224Gbps over the next 2-3 years [2]. With D/E of just 46%, a forward P/E of 15.8x, 19 consecutive years of dividends growing at 7.7% CAGR, and a 10% dividend increase just announced alongside a $3B buyback expansion, TE offers rare combination of AI-driven growth with dividend reliability [1]. The company also benefits from secular EV adoption and energy grid hardening trends that provide demand diversification beyond data centers.
The bear case centers on cyclicality — TE's transportation segment (automotive, commercial vehicles) is exposed to economic downturns, and GAAP profit margin of 11.4% reflects the manufacturing cost structure of a physical components business [1]. The $35.6M in regulatory penalties since 2000 and below-average MSCI ESG rating on environmental alignment create governance risk [3]. Revenue 5-year CAGR of 7.7% is slightly below the 10% target, and the Q2 FY2026 guidance of 6% organic growth signals potential deceleration from the 15% Q1 pace. Currency exposure from 150-country operations creates FX volatility. If AI data center buildout slows or shifts to alternative architectures requiring fewer physical connectors, the growth narrative would compress. The company's trailing P/E of 28x reflects elevated expectations relative to its industrial technology peer group.
