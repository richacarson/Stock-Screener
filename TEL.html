Investment Thesis
TE Connectivity is the essential physical layer for the AI infrastructure buildout, with Q1 FY2026 orders surging 28% to a record $5.1B and Industrial segment revenue up 24% driven by AI data center and energy grid demand. The conservative balance sheet (45.7% D/E), record 22.2% adjusted operating margins, and 15.8x forward P/E offer a compelling entry point for a company delivering double-digit revenue growth. The 12-year dividend growth streak, 7% five-year dividend CAGR, and $4.1B annual operating cash flow provide income durability, while factory automation recovery signals additional upside in H2 FY2026.
The bear case recognizes TE's exposure to cyclical industrial and automotive end markets that could reverse current momentum if macro conditions deteriorate. GAAP EPS of $6.16 significantly lags adjusted EPS due to intangible amortization (the company changed its non-GAAP methodology in FY2026 to exclude this), creating comparability challenges. Profit margins of 11.4% on a GAAP basis remain below technology peers, and the Inspire MISALIGNED score (-49) from LGBT Activism may concern faith-based investors. Tariff exposure across 130 countries of operations creates supply chain risk, and the company's AI revenue is indirect—through connector components rather than proprietary AI products.
