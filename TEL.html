Company Profile
Technology ·Electronic Components ·NYSE ·Ireland
TE Connectivity is a $17 billion global industrial technology leader in connectivity and sensor solutions. The company designs and manufactures connectors, sensors, and electronic components serving data centers, automotive (including EV), industrial, aerospace, and communications markets. With 10,000 engineers globally, TE produces the physical infrastructure — high-speed connectors, power distribution, liquid cooling solutions — that enables AI data center buildouts.

Investment Thesis
TE Connectivity is a premier play on the AI infrastructure buildout, delivering Q1 FY2026 revenue of $4.7B (up 22% YoY) with record orders of $5.1B (up 28%) as data center operators race to deploy high-speed connectors, liquid cooling, and power distribution for AI compute clusters [1]. Adjusted EPS of $2.72 beat estimates by $0.17 with operating margin expanding 180bps to 22.2%, demonstrating pricing power and operational leverage in a high-demand environment [1]. At 17.2x forward P/E with a fortress balance sheet (46% D/E), 14 consecutive years of dividend growth, a freshly announced 10% dividend increase and $3B buyback expansion, TEL offers compelling value for an AI-levered industrial compounder. CEO Curtin's focus on next-gen connectivity — 1.6T optical modules, liquid cooling for 600kW+ AI racks, and linear pluggable optics — positions TE at the physical infrastructure layer that every AI deployment requires [2].
The bear case centers on cyclicality and the sustainability of AI-driven demand. The 5-year revenue CAGR of 6.7% reveals that the current 22% growth is a spike from a cyclical trough (FY2023-2024 revenue declined), and a normalization in data center capex could rapidly compress growth and margins. Profit margin of 11.4% (GAAP) is thin for a technology company, and TE's electronic components face pricing pressure from Asian competitors in commoditized connector categories. Insider selling is persistent — $5.4M in sales in Q1 2026 alone with zero purchases over the past year, including EVP Jenkins netting $1.4M from a 22% position reduction [4]. The automotive segment (TE's largest historical end market) faces uncertainty from EV adoption pace and potential tariff disruption to global supply chains. If AI data center spending plateaus or shifts to optical-only architectures that reduce copper connector demand, TE's growth narrative would compress quickly. The $3B buyback signals capital returns over reinvestment.

AI Resilience (25%)
AI Resilience LOW RISK
8
AI Risk Protection (7/9): TE Connectivity manufactures the physical connectors, cables, power distribution, and cooling infrastructure that AI data centers require — AI cannot displace the need for physical interconnects, and in fact AI dramatically increases demand for TE's products [2]. As AI racks scale from 30kW today to 600kW by 2027 and 1MW by decade-end, the power delivery, thermal management, and high-speed connectivity requirements multiply. TE's record Q1 FY2026 orders of $5.1B (up 28%) are partly driven by AI data center buildouts [1]. The automotive and industrial segments similarly involve physical-world products (sensors, harnesses, connectors) that AI cannot substitute. However, TE does not have the same level of immune moat as pure-play physical infrastructure (e.g., steel, utilities) because some connector specification and sourcing decisions could be partially automated. AI Opportunity (1/1): TE generates meaningful and growing AI-related revenue through high-speed data center connectors, 800G/1.6T optical modules, liquid cooling solutions, and power distribution for AI compute clusters — this is the primary driver of the 22% revenue growth and record order backlog [1][2].
