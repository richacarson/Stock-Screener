Investment Thesis
Tempus AI is building the definitive AI platform for precision medicine, with 83% revenue growth in Q4 2025 to $367M, a $1.27B full-year run rate, and guidance for $1.59B in 2026 (~25% growth) with $65M adjusted EBITDA [1]. The business model is increasingly durable: diagnostics revenue grew 122% YoY driven by oncology and hereditary testing volume, data net revenue retention reached 126%, and contract bookings exceeded $1.1B [1]. The proprietary multimodal data library — spanning clinical, molecular, and imaging data — creates a compounding competitive moat that strengthens with each new patient record.
The bear case centers on governance and valuation risk. CEO Lefkofsky sold $127M in stock within 6 months of the IPO while the company remains deeply unprofitable (net loss of $245M in 2025, D/E of 270%) [6][7]. A securities fraud lawsuit alleges inflated contract values and misleading disclosures, and the Spruce Point short report triggered a 19% stock decline [6]. At $45/share and ~$8B market cap, the stock trades at 6.3x 2025 revenue despite GAAP losses — any deceleration in the 25% guided growth rate could compress the multiple significantly. Competition from Roche, Google Health, and Foundation Medicine intensifies as AI healthcare becomes mainstream [3].
Key Catalysts
  1. Achievement of $65M adjusted EBITDA target in 2026 would mark the company's first full year of positive adjusted profitability, validating the path to GAAP breakeven [1]
  2. Expansion of MRD (minimal residual disease) testing and FDA clearances for additional AI algorithms (Paige Predict, HRD-RNA) could accelerate diagnostics growth above the guided 25% [2]
  3. Tempus Loop drug target discovery platform could unlock new pharmaceutical partnerships and expand the data licensing revenue stream beyond current $1.1B backlog [2]
Key Risks
  1. Securities fraud lawsuit and Spruce Point short report allegations of inflated contract values and AI-washing could result in regulatory action, settlement costs, or sustained multiple compression [6]
  2. CEO insider selling of $127M with zero purchases signals potential misalignment between management and long-term shareholders, particularly for a company still burning $245M annually [6]
  3. Competition from Roche (Foundation Medicine), Google Health, and emerging AI diagnostics players could pressure pricing and market share as the precision medicine market matures [3]
