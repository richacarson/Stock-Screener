Company Profile
Healthcare ·Diagnostics & Research ·NASDAQ ·United States
Tempus AI is a healthcare technology company that has built one of the world's largest libraries of clinical and molecular data, using artificial intelligence to deliver precision medicine at scale. The company operates across two segments: Diagnostics (genomic profiling, molecular diagnostics, MRD testing) and Data & Applications (AI platform licensing to biopharma companies for drug discovery and development). Founded in 2015 by Eric Lefkofsky after a personal experience with cancer, Tempus IPO'd on NASDAQ in June 2024 at $37/share and reported TTM revenue of approximately $1.27B as of Q1 2026.

Investment Thesis
Tempus AI is the leading AI-native precision medicine platform, with Q1 2026 revenue of $348.1M (+36.1% YoY) across Diagnostics ($261.1M) and Data & Applications ($87.0M), powered by one of the world's largest clinical-molecular data libraries [1]. The company's proprietary data flywheel — where every test performed improves AI models, driving more adoption and generating more data — creates a compounding competitive moat that deepens over time. MRD testing volume grew 500% YoY in Q1 2026, representing a transformative new capability in cancer treatment monitoring, while the Merck collaboration validates the pharma AI platform at the highest tier of biopharma [1][3]. Founder-CEO Lefkofsky's deeply personal mission (founded after a cancer experience) and decade-long commitment to building the platform before IPO'ing signal the strongest possible alignment between leadership conviction and shareholder interests [2]. With adjusted EBITDA improving from deeply negative to near-breakeven (-$2.8M in Q1) and FY2026 guidance targeting approximately $65M positive adjusted EBITDA, the path toward profitability is emerging [1][3]. A perfect AI Resilience score of 10 reflects the reality that Tempus IS the AI disruption — you cannot be displaced by the technology you are building.
The bear case centers on persistent GAAP unprofitability and extreme valuation risk. At a market cap of approximately $10.25B against TTM revenue of $1.27B with -22.2% profit margin, D/E of 316.65%, and ROE of -89.48%, the stock is priced for flawless execution of a growth story that has not yet proven it can generate sustainable bottom-line earnings [4][5]. The forward P/E is not meaningful given negative GAAP earnings, and even on an adjusted basis, the company has only just begun to approach EBITDA breakeven. Revenue growth is decelerating — from 82% (2024 to 2025) to a guided 25% (2025 to 2026) — raising questions about whether the platform can sustain hypergrowth or is normalizing toward a more conventional trajectory [3]. Competition from well-capitalized rivals (Foundation Medicine/Roche, Guardant Health, Illumina) could pressure margins as genomic testing commoditizes. Data privacy risk is a structural governance concern given the company's handling of sensitive genomic and clinical data, and any breach or regulatory action could damage the platform's trust-based business model. Lefkofsky's track record at Groupon — which peaked spectacularly then declined — may give some investors pause about long-term execution in a very different industry.
