Terns Pharmaceuticals is a clinical-stage biopharmaceutical company developing small-molecule candidates in oncology and obesity, with its lead asset TERN-701 (BCR-ABL allosteric inhibitor) for chronic myeloid leukemia. Investment Thesis
Terns Pharmaceuticals is effectively a merger arbitrage security post-March 25, 2026, when Merck announced a $53/share tender offer valuing the company at approximately $6 billion, with close expected Q2 2026 [5]. The stock trades at $52.68 — essentially the deal price — meaning upside is limited to whether the deal closes on schedule versus downside to pre-deal levels if the tender fails [7]. The TERN-701 CML program provides the strategic rationale for Merck's interest, expanding Merck's hematology pipeline against Novartis's asciminib, and additional clinical assets in obesity and THR-beta add optionality. Cash balance is healthy with debt-to-equity of just 0.09, ensuring the business can fund operations through deal close [7].
The bear case focuses on deal risk and inherent biotech challenges. Merck reportedly lowered its offer price after reviewing updated trial data, and post-April 2026 Statnews analysis argues TERN-701 may be less competitive than initially believed — creating antitrust, due diligence, or price revision risk that could jeopardize the deal [4][6]. Kahn Swick & Foti is actively investigating the adequacy of price and process, indicating shareholder litigation pressure that could delay or reprice the transaction [6]. If the deal breaks, the stock likely reverts to pre-announcement levels near $35-40, implying 25-30% downside. As a pre-revenue biotech with no earnings, Forward P/E of -35.8x and ROE of -14.2% reflect that without the Merck bid, Terns remains a high-risk clinical-stage story with a non-AI discovery approach versus AI-first biotech competitors [7][8]. Recommendation WATCH reflects limited upside at deal price plus deal-break downside risk.
