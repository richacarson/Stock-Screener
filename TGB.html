Company Profile
Basic Materials ·Copper ·AMEX ·Canada
Taseko Mines is a copper mining company operating the Gibraltar mine in British Columbia and the newly commissioned Florence Copper in-situ recovery project in Arizona. The company also holds development-stage interests in the Yellowhead copper, New Prosperity gold-copper, and Aley niobium projects in British Columbia.

Investment Thesis
Taseko is a compelling copper growth story trading at just 7.6x forward P/E with two producing assets and transformative production growth ahead. Florence Copper — the first new US greenfield copper mine since 2008 — harvested first cathodes on March 2, 2026, targeting 30-35M lbs in 2026 ramping to 85M lbs at full capacity, which would make Taseko the third-largest US copper cathode producer [1]. Combined with Gibraltar's 110-115M lbs 2026 guidance (up from 98M lbs in 2025), total production could reach 140-150M lbs in 2026 and 195M+ lbs at full ramp [1]. Adjusted EBITDA of $230M in 2025 was achieved at lower copper prices; with prices ~25% higher in 2026, cash flows should expand significantly. The secular copper demand thesis — driven by AI data centers, electrification, and defense spending requiring a doubling of global copper supply — provides a powerful multi-year tailwind [2].
The bear case centers on execution risk and financial fragility during the Florence ramp-up. Despite $230M adjusted EBITDA, Taseko reported a net loss of $30M and negative free cash flow of -$290M in 2025, reflecting heavy construction spending [1]. D/E of 95.9% ($747M debt including the recent $400M bond refinancing) creates leverage risk if copper prices decline. Florence Copper's in-situ recovery technology at commercial scale is unproven — the company is targeting 30-35M lbs in 2026 but any wellfield performance issues could delay the ramp and extend cash burn [1]. The stock fell 6% on the Q4 earnings release despite strong results, suggesting the market is skeptical of near-term execution. Gibraltar's 2025 production of 98M lbs was below the prior year's 105M lbs, raising questions about mine-life sustainability. Environmental and community risks persist at Florence (aquifer contamination concerns) and New Prosperity (Indigenous consent requirements) [3].
Key Catalysts
  1. Florence Copper production ramp through 2026 toward 85M lbs annual capacity — successful scale-up would transform Taseko's production and cash flow profile and validate the in-situ recovery technology [1]
  2. Copper price strength driven by AI data center construction, electrification demand, and supply constraints — S&P Global projects a structural supply gap requiring global copper output to double [2]
  3. Gibraltar production improvement to 110-115M lbs in 2026 (up from 98M in 2025), combined with higher copper prices, should drive significant free cash flow generation [1]
Key Risks
  1. Florence Copper ramp-up execution risk — in-situ recovery at commercial scale is unproven, and wellfield performance issues could delay the production timeline and extend negative free cash flow [1]
  2. Copper price sensitivity — a sustained decline in copper prices below $3.50/lb would compress margins at both operations and make the debt load ($747M) increasingly burdensome [1]
  3. Environmental liability at Florence (aquifer contamination concerns from residents and the town council) and New Prosperity (consent-based development with the Tsilhqot'in Nation creates uncertainty on future development) [3]
