Company Profile
Healthcare ·Biotechnology ·NASDAQ ·United States
TG Therapeutics is a commercial-stage biopharmaceutical company focused on B-cell mediated diseases. Its lead product BRIUMVI (ublituximab-xiiy) is an anti-CD20 monoclonal antibody approved for relapsing forms of multiple sclerosis (RMS). The company's pipeline includes subcutaneous BRIUMVI and TG-1701 (BTK inhibitor).

Investment Thesis
TG Therapeutics is executing one of the most successful biotech commercial launches in recent history, with BRIUMVI generating $616.3M in 2025 revenue (92% YoY growth) and 2026 guidance raised to $875-$900M [1]. The financial profile is exceptional: 72.6% profit margin, 102.8% ROE, and forward P/E of just 13.9x — representing an unusual combination of hyper-growth and value-level multiples. The subcutaneous BRIUMVI pivotal data (expected year-end 2026/Q1 2027) could significantly expand the addressable market by improving patient convenience, and planned expansion into indications beyond MS opens multi-billion-dollar opportunities [1]. With $141.9M cash and a $750M credit facility, the company is well-capitalized for its growth trajectory [3][4].
The bear case centers on single-product concentration and organizational quality. BRIUMVI accounts for essentially 100% of revenue, creating binary risk if safety signals emerge, generic competition materializes, or market share shifts to next-generation MS therapies [1]. The company's Sustainalytics ESG Risk Rating flagged the highest controversy level in the last three years, and Glassdoor/Comparably data reveals a 'D'-rated executive team with toxic culture complaints — organizational dysfunction could impair commercial execution as the company scales from 399 employees [2][3]. Operating cash flow was negative $24.8M despite $616.3M in revenue, suggesting working capital challenges or revenue timing issues [4]. The $254.4M in debt and $750M credit facility add leverage risk if BRIUMVI growth stalls. Competition from Roche's Ocrevus subcutaneous formulation could blunt BRIUMVI's differentiation advantage.
Key Catalysts
  1. Subcutaneous BRIUMVI pivotal topline data expected year-end 2026/Q1 2027, which could significantly expand adoption by eliminating IV infusion requirements [1]
  2. 2026 revenue guidance of $875-$900M representing ~45% YoY growth on continued BRIUMVI commercial momentum in the US and ex-US markets [1]
  3. Registration-directed trials for BRIUMVI in indications beyond MS, potentially opening multi-billion-dollar B-cell disease markets [1]
Key Risks
  1. Single-product concentration — BRIUMVI generates ~100% of revenue, creating binary risk from safety signals, competitive displacement, or market share loss to next-generation MS therapies [1]
  2. Organizational culture concerns — Glassdoor/Comparably data reveals 'D'-rated executive team, toxic culture complaints, and high employee dissatisfaction that could impair execution as the company scales [2]
  3. Negative operating cash flow of $24.8M despite $616.3M revenue, with $254.4M debt and $750M credit facility adding leverage risk if growth decelerates [4]
