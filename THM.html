Investment Thesis
International Tower Hill Mines offers leveraged exposure to gold prices through the Livengood Gold Project, one of the largest undeveloped gold deposits in North America. The $115M equity financing closed in January 2026 — including Paulson & Co.'s $40M private placement — provides a multi-year cash runway for feasibility studies and permitting, with $50M earmarked for technical work and $35M for community engagement [1]. At a $696M market cap and $2.62/share, the stock is a pure gold optionality play that could deliver significant upside if Livengood proves economic in a sustained gold price environment above $2,000/oz. Paulson's continued backing signals institutional confidence in the project's geological merits.
The bear case is substantial and dominates the risk-reward profile. This is a pre-revenue company that has been in the exploration stage since 1978 — 48 years without generating a dollar of revenue or producing an ounce of gold [1][4]. The $115M raise is entirely dilutive to existing shareholders (33.7M new shares plus the $40M private placement). With 3 employees, negative ROE of -8.2%, and no production timeline, THM is a speculative bet on geology and gold prices rather than an investment in a functioning business. The company's survival depends on continued access to capital markets, and any sustained decline in gold prices or failure to demonstrate project economics could leave the equity worthless. Infrastructure scores 0/10 across all financial metrics — there is no financial foundation to evaluate.
Key Catalysts
  1. Feasibility study completion — the $50M allocated for technical studies from the 2026 equity raise could produce a positive feasibility assessment that de-risks the project and catalyzes a re-rating, particularly if gold prices remain above $2,000/oz [1]
  2. Permitting milestones — successful advancement through Alaska state and federal permitting processes, supported by the $35M community engagement allocation, would remove a key development overhang [1]

Key Risks
  1. Permanent capital destruction — 48 years of exploration with zero revenue raises the risk that the Livengood deposit may never reach economic production, in which case the equity is worthless regardless of gold prices [1][4]
  2. Dilution risk — the $115M equity financing is entirely dilutive, and additional capital raises will be required to fund any eventual mine construction, which could cost billions for a project of Livengood's scale
  3. Single-asset concentration — the company's entire value proposition depends on one deposit in one jurisdiction (Alaska), with no diversification to offset project-specific geological, regulatory, or political risks [3]
