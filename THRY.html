Company Profile
Technology ·Software - Application ·NASDAQ ·United States
Thryv Holdings provides digital marketing solutions and cloud-based SaaS tools to small-to-medium-sized businesses (SMBs) in the United States. The company operates in two segments: Thryv SaaS (CRM, scheduling, invoicing, payments, social media, AI website tools) and Thryv Marketing Services (legacy print and digital yellow pages). Formerly known as Dex Media Holdings.
2,702 Employees ·www.thryv.com

Investment Thesis
Thryv trades at a remarkably low forward P/E of 2.4x ($3.58 stock vs. $1.47 forward EPS) on $785M total revenue, representing a deep value opportunity if the SaaS transformation succeeds. SaaS revenue grew 5% in Q1 2026 to $116.7M (70% of total), ARPU increased 13% YoY to $378, and quality customers now represent 70% of SaaS revenue, suggesting improving unit economics [1]. The company exceeded Q1 2026 total revenue and EBITDA guidance, beat EPS estimates by $0.16, and raised full-year SaaS revenue guidance to $463-471M [1][2]. The planned AI-native unified platform launch in late 2026 could accelerate growth.
The bear case is significant: total revenue declined 7.5% YoY as the legacy Marketing Services segment shrank 27.5%, and the stock has collapsed from $25+ to $3.58, implying the market sees structural risk in the business [1]. SaaS growth of 5% is decelerating from 34% in 2025, raising questions about whether the platform can sustain momentum. Debt-to-equity of 119.8% adds financial risk, and profit margin of 0.04% means the company is barely profitable on a GAAP basis. The SMB SaaS market faces intense competition from HubSpot, GoDaddy, and emerging AI-native tools that could commoditize Thryv's features. Analyst downgrades citing 'shakier SaaS execution' and expected churn from smaller customers in H1 2026 during the platform transition add execution risk [3]. The $158M market cap suggests limited institutional interest and potential liquidity constraints.
Key Catalysts
  1. Late-2026 launch of the AI-native unified Thryv Platform, consolidating all products into a single growth engine that could re-accelerate SaaS growth and improve retention among quality customers
  2. Continued SaaS ARPU expansion (13% YoY to $378) and quality customer concentration (70% of SaaS revenue) could drive margin improvement and demonstrate the transition is working
  3. Potential rerating from extreme deep-value levels (2.4x forward P/E, 0.2x P/S) if Q2-Q3 2026 results confirm sustainable SaaS growth alongside controlled legacy decline
Key Risks
  1. SaaS growth deceleration from 34% (2025) to 5% (Q1 2026) could signal that the SMB market opportunity is saturating or that competitive pressure from HubSpot and AI-native tools is intensifying
  2. Legacy Marketing Services segment decline of 27.5% YoY may accelerate faster than SaaS growth can offset, creating a total revenue death spiral that depletes cash and forces capital structure actions
  3. Platform transition churn risk — migration of existing customers to the unified AI-native platform in late 2026 could trigger elevated cancellations, particularly among smaller and lower-ARPU customers
