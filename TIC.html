TIC Solutions, Inc. (formerly Acuren Corporation, rebranded October 2025) is a tech-enabled provider of mission-critical Testing, Inspection, Certification, and Compliance (TICC) and engineering services. Headquartered in Hollywood, Florida, the company serves industrials, energy, utilities, and public/commercial infrastructure across 250+ locations in North America. In August 2025 it closed the acquisition of NV5 Global to more than double its footprint.

Investment Thesis
TIC Solutions is a post-merger bet on a scaled North American Testing, Inspection, Certification and Compliance platform formed by combining Acuren with NV5 Global (closed August 2025) and rebranded October 2025 [3]. Management guides to 2026 revenue of $2.15-$2.25B (~50% YoY growth, NV5-driven) and Adjusted EBITDA of at least $330M, up from $234M in 2025 [1]. The underlying business is defensive — asset integrity work on pipelines, refineries, utilities and public infrastructure is mandatory, recurring and fundamentally AI-resistant. The stated values and Forbes Best Employer recognition in Canada point to a genuine people-first culture [3]. At a ~$1.58B market cap on $7.15, TIC trades near post-crash lows, offering optionality if the NV5 integration delivers its synergy case.
The risks are acute. 2025 closed with a $87.1M GAAP net loss, $1.6B of term-loan debt against a ~$1.58B equity market cap, and a stock that crashed on the Q4 2025 print over balance-sheet and execution fears [1][2][4]. The CEO succession announced alongside earnings — Ben Heraud replacing Tal Pizzey on March 31, 2026 — adds top-of-house disruption on top of an already complex NV5 integration [1][3]. A private placement of common stock and pre-funded warrant to an existing investor signals ongoing capital needs and dilution risk [1]. The Inspire Insight score is 0 (neutral/MIXED faith alignment) with no attributions in either direction, meaning no positive faith case but also no disqualifying activities. Valuation is essentially an EBITDA-multiple call (~5x 2026 guided EBITDA) on a company still proving it can execute — WATCH, not BUY, until the 2026 integration milestones and deleveraging show up in the cash-flow statement.
Key Catalysts
  1. Successful NV5 integration delivering 2026 revenue of $2.15-$2.25B and Adjusted EBITDA of at least $330M, validating the merger thesis [1]
  2. Organic margin expansion from 'tech-enabled' AI-assisted inspection workflows that can lift a mid-teens EBITDA margin into the high teens [3]
  3. Material deleveraging of the $1.6B term-loan stack over 2026-2027 from free cash flow conversion of the combined business [1][4]
Key Risks
  1. $1.6B term-loan debt against a ~$1.58B market cap creates extreme balance-sheet sensitivity if EBITDA misses guidance or rates stay elevated [4]
  2. CEO succession (Heraud replacing Pizzey March 31, 2026) concurrent with NV5 integration raises meaningful execution risk across the combined 11,000-employee organization [1]
  3. Additional private placements and pre-funded warrants to existing investors signal ongoing dilution risk if organic cash generation lags [1]
