Company Profile
Communication Services ·Telecom Services ·NASDAQ ·Luxembourg
Millicom International Cellular S.A. provides cable and mobile telecommunications services across Latin America under the Tigo brand. The company offers mobile data, voice, and financial services; broadband, fixed voice, and pay-TV; plus enterprise solutions including managed services, cloud, and security. It serves 46+ million customers across Colombia, Guatemala, Honduras, Paraguay, Bolivia, El Salvador, Nicaragua, and Panama.

Investment Thesis
Millicom is a well-positioned LatAm convergent telecom operator generating exceptional cash flow — $916M EFCF in FY2025 (exceeding the $750M target) on $5.82B revenue with 47.2% EBITDA margins [1]. The 2025 acquisitions of Telefonica's Colombia, Ecuador, and Uruguay operations expand the fiber-cable footprint to 14+ million homes and create a dominant fixed-mobile platform across 9 countries serving 46+ million customers. At 16.5x forward P/E with a PEG ratio of 1.08, the stock is reasonably valued for a company with 37.9% ROE and a reinstated $3.00/share dividend (3.65% yield) [5]. The 2026 EFCF target of $900M+ and leverage declining toward 2.5x provide visibility into continued cash return capacity.
The bear case centers on LatAm macro risk, leverage, and organic growth limitations. Organic service revenue grew only 2.8% in FY2025, with currency volatility across 9 LatAm countries creating reporting noise — D/E of 262.3% and net debt/EBITDA of 2.31x leave limited margin for error if macro conditions deteriorate [1][5]. Operating in countries like Honduras, Nicaragua, and Bolivia introduces political instability, currency devaluation, and regulatory risk that can destroy value quickly. The dividend was suspended for 5 years (2020-2024), demonstrating the fragility of shareholder returns in challenging environments. Integration risk on three simultaneous Telefonica acquisitions is significant, and the $6.65B 2026 revenue forecast implies sustained inorganic contribution in an environment where organic growth is low single digits. Millicom's 'Sexually Explicit' negative attribution from Inspire reflects content distribution risks inherent in telecom platforms [4].
Key Catalysts
  1. Successful integration of Telefonica's Colombia, Ecuador, and Uruguay operations driving revenue synergies and EBITDA margin expansion beyond the 47.2% FY2025 level [1]

Key Risks
  1. Latin American macroeconomic instability — currency devaluation, political risk, and inflation across 9 operating countries could compress reported earnings and cash flows [1]
  2. Integration execution risk on three simultaneous Telefonica acquisitions while maintaining $720M annual capex for fiber buildout [1]
  3. Low organic revenue growth of 2.0-2.8% suggests the underlying business may be ex-growth outside of M&A, limiting long-term value creation if acquisition opportunities diminish [1]

AI Risk Protection (6/9): Millicom's core business — physical telecom infrastructure (fiber-optic networks, cell towers, mobile spectrum) across Latin America — has strong structural protection against AI displacement.
