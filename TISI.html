Investment Thesis
Team Inc. offers a turnaround narrative with 8.3% Q1 2026 revenue growth, improving Adjusted EBITDA margins (3.6% in Q1 2026 vs. 2.7% prior year), and full-year 2026 guidance of $920-945M in revenue with $68-73M Adjusted EBITDA representing 16% growth [1]. The business provides essential, physical industrial services — inspection, heat treating, and mechanical repair — that are virtually immune to AI disruption and benefit from aging industrial infrastructure requiring ongoing maintenance. New CEO Gary Hill brings fresh strategic focus on aerospace and power generation markets. The Inspire Insight score of 49 with 14 positive ESG attributions and zero negative attributions reflects strong environmental and social alignment, particularly through the company's emissions detection services [3].
The bear case is dominated by a catastrophically overleveraged balance sheet: D/E of 2,318.9%, total debt of $306.5M against a stockholders' deficit of -$39.4M, and recurring net losses ($49.2M in 2025, $11.3M in Q1 2026) [1]. The stock is essentially uninvestable for IOWN due to extreme illiquidity — average daily volume of just 9,306 shares produces only ~$155K in daily dollar volume, far below the $1M minimum threshold. Free cash flow remains negative at -$20.6M for 2025 and -$11.5M in Q1 2026 alone. The $75M preferred stock issuance adds another layer of dilution risk. Revenue growth of ~4% at the midpoint of 2026 guidance barely outpaces inflation, and profit margins remain negative. Until the balance sheet is materially de-risked and liquidity improves by an order of magnitude, Team is a speculative turnaround play unsuitable for the IOWN framework.

AI Resilience (25%)
AI Resilience LOW RISK
8
AI Risk Protection (8/9): Team's core business — on-site industrial inspection, heat treating, leak repair, and mechanical services — is fundamentally physical work that AI cannot replace. Certified inspectors must physically access pipes, pressure vessels, and heat exchangers in refineries and petrochemical plants. The work requires NDE certifications, hands-on expertise, and presence in hazardous environments that no AI or robotic system can currently replicate at scale [2]. Regulatory requirements (ASME, API, NDE codes) mandate human-performed inspections for critical infrastructure. AI may enhance inspection accuracy through computer vision-assisted defect detection, but this augments rather than replaces Team's workforce. The physical, regulated, and safety-critical nature of these services provides near-total immunity to AI disruption. AI Opportunity (0/1): Team does not generate revenue from AI products. The ONEINSIGHT platform is a digital field management tool, not an AI product, and no AI-driven revenue has been reported [2].
