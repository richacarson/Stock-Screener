Investment Thesis
Tilray delivered record Q3 FY2026 net revenue of $207M with 11% organic growth, demonstrating the diversification strategy is generating momentum [1]. International cannabis revenue grew 73% YoY with 100% increase in flower sales volume, the BrewDog acquisition positions Tilray as a ~$1.2B annualized revenue company, and the Carlsberg partnership beginning in 2027 could further scale the beverage platform [1]. Adjusted EBITDA guidance of $62M-$72M for FY2026 represents 13-31% improvement, and the company holds $292M in cash with a net cash position. The stock's $715M market cap at 0.6x trailing revenue offers deep value if the profitability inflection materializes.
The bear case is severe. Tilray has destroyed enormous shareholder value — the stock is down over 95% from its 2021 highs, and GAAP profitability remains nonexistent with -252% profit margin and -84% ROE driven by massive goodwill impairments from serial acquisitions [4]. CEO Irwin Simon's $10.3M compensation while employees rate the company 2.9/5 on Glassdoor with only 28% recommending it as a workplace raises serious governance concerns [2]. The acquisition-driven strategy (BrewDog, SweetWater, Montauk) has not yet demonstrated ability to generate returns above cost of capital — adjusted EBITDA of $55M on $821M revenue in FY2025 represents just 6.7% margin. U.S. cannabis legalization, the perennial catalyst, remains politically stalled. Sustainalytics rates Tilray's ESG risk management as 'Weak,' and the company's cannabis operations create Inspire alignment issues [3]. Share dilution risk is elevated given ongoing losses and acquisition financing needs.
