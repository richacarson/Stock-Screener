Company Profile
Consumer Cyclical ·Residential Construction ·NYSE ·United States
Taylor Morrison designs, builds, and sells single-family and multifamily homes in the United States for entry-level, move-up, and resort lifestyle buyers under the Taylor Morrison and Esplanade brands. The company also develops master-planned communities, operates a Build-to-Rent business under the Yardly brand, and provides financial services, title insurance, and closing settlement services. Founded in 1936 and headquartered in Scottsdale, Arizona.

Investment Thesis
Taylor Morrison is a well-managed, fifth-largest U.S. homebuilder trading at just 0.93x book value and 9.4x forward earnings — a deep value opportunity if housing affordability improves [1]. CEO Sheryl Palmer has built a strong culture (96% Glassdoor CEO approval) and invested in digital tools that accelerate closings by 40% [2]. The company's conservative balance sheet (D/E 38.6%) and diversified buyer exposure (entry-level, move-up, resort lifestyle, Build-to-Rent) provide resilience. Guided for 11,000 closings in 2026 at $580K-$590K average price with 20.6% gross margins, suggesting steady execution despite cyclical headwinds [1]. The Inspire Impact Score of 56 (ALIGNED) reflects strong ESG practices across 13 positive attribution categories with zero negative flags [3].
The bear case centers on severe cyclical pressure — Q1 2026 revenue declined 26.8% YoY and EPS fell 51.2% as elevated mortgage rates (6.5%+) suppress demand [1]. Profit margin of 8.8% is thin for the industry, and ROE of 11.1% sits below the cost of equity during high-rate environments. With revenue essentially flat over 3 years ($8.2B in 2022 vs. $8.1B in 2025), organic growth has stalled. BofA downgraded TMHC to Neutral in early 2026, flagging EPS and delivery risks [2]. The company's geographic concentration in Sun Belt markets (Arizona, Florida, Texas) exposes it to overbuilding risk if migration trends reverse. At 2,800 employees managing $7.6B in TTM revenue, the company is highly leveraged to subcontractor availability and construction cost inflation.
Key Catalysts
  1. Mortgage rate relief below 6% would unlock significant pent-up demand, particularly for TMHC's entry-level and move-up buyer segments where affordability is the binding constraint [1]
  2. Build-to-Rent expansion under the Yardly brand provides counter-cyclical revenue as rental demand strengthens when homeownership becomes less affordable [1]
  3. AI-powered digital homebuying tools driving 40% faster closings and 23% higher customer spend, with continued rollout across all communities expected to improve conversion rates [2]
Key Risks
  1. Sustained elevated mortgage rates above 6.5% could further compress closings volume below the guided 11,000 units, with each 50bps rate increase estimated to reduce qualifying buyers by 5-7% [1]
  2. Sun Belt overbuilding risk — Arizona, Florida, and Texas markets face elevated new home inventory that could force pricing concessions and margin compression below 20% gross margin [1]
  3. BofA downgrade to Neutral with flagged EPS and delivery risks signals sell-side skepticism about the 2026 outlook; further downgrades could pressure the stock below book value [2]
