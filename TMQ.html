Company Profile
Basic Materials ·Other Industrial Metals & Mining ·NYSE American ·Canada
Trilogy Metals is an exploration-stage mining company focused on the Upper Kobuk mineral projects in Alaska's Ambler mining district, which include the Arctic polymetallic VMS deposit and the Bornite copper-cobalt deposit. The company operates through Ambler Metals, a 50/50 joint venture with South32, and has secured a 10% U.S. government equity stake tied to critical minerals strategy.

Investment Thesis
Trilogy Metals offers speculative exposure to one of the world's highest-grade undeveloped copper-zinc deposits at a time when U.S. critical mineral security has become a national priority. The October 2025 U.S. government investment of $35.6M for a 10% stake validates the strategic importance of the Ambler district [1]. With a $35M 2026 budget approved for geotechnical drilling and mine permitting preparation, the company is approaching a major inflection point as Ambler Metals initiates the Arctic Project permitting process [1]. Copper demand tailwinds from AI data center buildouts and electrification support the long-term commodity thesis.
The bear case is substantial: Trilogy has been exploring for 20+ years without generating a dollar of revenue, and the path to production requires billions in capital that will likely be highly dilutive to shareholders [1]. Auditors have flagged going concern risk, and the stock has fallen 68% from its 52-week high of $11.29 to $3.59 [2]. The 211-mile Ambler Access Road faces ongoing environmental and Indigenous opposition, and construction timelines remain uncertain despite federal permits [3]. The U.S. government's dual role as investor and regulator creates unprecedented governance conflicts that could delay permitting rather than accelerate it [3]. Billionaire John Paulson's exit from his decade-long position is a bearish signal from a sophisticated investor [2]. At $619M market cap with zero revenue, the valuation is entirely dependent on future mine development that remains years away.
