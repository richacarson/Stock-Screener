Company Profile
Communication Services ·Telecom Services ·NASDAQ ·United States
T-Mobile US is the largest wireless carrier in the United States by market capitalization, providing mobile communications services to over 125 million customers. The 'Un-carrier' disrupted the industry by eliminating contracts and hidden fees. Following the 2020 Sprint merger, T-Mobile holds the largest mid-band 5G spectrum portfolio in the U.S. and is expanding into broadband, enterprise, and AI-driven digital services.

Just Cause
10

It has survived three CEOs (Legere, Sievert, Gopalan), a transformative merger, and industry disruption while consistently shaping strategic decisions. The cause created a $240B company from a distant #4 carrier. Sinek would cite T-Mobile as an exemplar of purpose-driven competitive advantage.

Existential Flexibility

Transformative flexibility demonstrated (2/2 points). The company pivoted from pure wireless to broadband (targeting 18-19M broadband customers by 2030), enterprise services, and now AI-driven digital transformation under Gopalan. Courage to Lead
6
Some courageous decisions (1/2 points). The Sprint merger was a bold, multi-year bet that required massive capital commitment and integration discipline [1]. However, the repeated data breaches (8 since 2018, $350M settlement, $31.5M FCC fine) represent a failure of leadership courage in cybersecurity investment — the company knew about vulnerabilities for years and failed to act [3].

Investment Thesis
T-Mobile is the largest U.S. wireless carrier by market cap ($239B), delivering industry-leading customer growth (962K postpaid adds in Q4 2025) with 10% service revenue growth and a clear path to $18-18.7B adjusted free cash flow in 2026 [1]. The company's 5G network leadership — built on the largest mid-band spectrum portfolio post-Sprint merger — creates a durable competitive advantage as 5G use cases expand into broadband, enterprise, and IoT. At 15.2x forward earnings with ~7.5% FCF yield, TMUS offers compelling value for a business transitioning from wireless growth to multi-segment platform expansion targeting 18-19M broadband customers by 2030 [1]. CEO Gopalan's AI-driven digital transformation strategy projects nearly $3B incremental EBITDA by 2027, providing a clear margin expansion catalyst [2].
The bear case focuses on growth deceleration, leverage, and execution risk. Revenue 3yr CAGR of just 3.5% masks the Sprint merger distortions but raises questions about sustainable top-line growth as the wireless market matures. D/E of 209% reflects the Sprint merger financing that constrains financial flexibility. EPS declined 26.6% YoY in Q4 2025, though this includes one-time items [4]. The CEO transition from Sievert to Gopalan introduces strategic uncertainty — Sievert's $20.5M stock sale suggests reduced confidence [4]. Most critically, T-Mobile's pattern of data breaches (8 since 2018, impacting tens of millions of customers, resulting in $350M class action settlement and $31.5M FCC fine) represents a material governance failure and reputational risk [3]. The broadband expansion requires significant capex in fiber (T-Fiber) and fixed wireless, with execution risk against established cable incumbents. Dividend yield of 1.94% is below the 2% threshold for income investors.
Key Catalysts
  1. AI-driven EBITDA expansion — nearly $3B incremental contribution from digitalization and AI expected by end of 2027, transforming operational efficiency and customer experience [1][2]
  2. Broadband customer growth toward 18-19M target by 2030 (5G home internet + T-Fiber), creating a second major revenue pillar beyond wireless [1]
  3. Free cash flow acceleration to $18-18.7B in 2026 enabling continued dividend growth, buybacks, and deleveraging [1]
Key Risks
  1. Repeated cybersecurity failures — 8 data breaches since 2018, $350M settlement, $31.5M FCC fine, and ongoing AG lawsuits represent material governance and reputational risk [3]
  2. CEO transition risk — Gopalan taking over from Sievert in November 2025 introduces strategic and cultural uncertainty during a critical transformation period [2]
  3. Elevated leverage and growth deceleration — D/E of 209% with 3.5% revenue CAGR constrains financial flexibility if wireless market growth slows further [4]
