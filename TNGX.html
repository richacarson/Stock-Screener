Company Profile
Healthcare ·Biotechnology ·NASDAQ ·United States
Tango Therapeutics, Inc. is a precision oncology company focused on discovering and developing novel drug targets using CRISPR-based functional genomics. The company's pipeline includes TNG462 (vopimetostat), an MTA-cooperative PRMT5 inhibitor in Phase 1/2 for pancreatic and lung cancer; TNG456, a brain-penetrant PRMT5 inhibitor for glioblastoma; and TNG260, a CoREST inhibitor targeting immune evasion in solid tumors.
137 Employees ·www.tangotx.com

Investment Thesis
Tango Therapeutics is a high-risk, high-reward clinical-stage precision oncology play with first-mover positioning in MTA-cooperative PRMT5 inhibition targeting MTAP-deleted cancers, a genetically defined population representing ~15% of all tumors [1][2]. The lead candidate vopimetostat (TNG462) has shown promising Phase 1/2 data in pancreatic and lung cancer, and the $343.1 million cash position provides runway into 2028 without near-term dilution risk [1]. Collaboration revenue grew 48.3% to $62.4 million in FY2025, and the company beat Q4 2025 EPS estimates by 9.4%, suggesting improving operational discipline [1].
The bear case is significant: Tango remains deeply unprofitable with a -162.9% net margin, has zero approved products generating commercial revenue, and faces binary clinical trial risk that could erase a substantial portion of its $2.8 billion market capitalization on negative data [4]. The January 2026 CEO transition introduces execution uncertainty, revenue is entirely collaboration-dependent, and Sustainalytics rates ESG management as 'Weak' with the highest controversy designation [5]. The WATCH rating reflects strong underlying science and funded pipeline offset by a pre-commercial financial profile, finite game orientation, and the irreducible binary risk inherent in clinical-stage biotechs.
Key Catalysts
  1. Vopimetostat (TNG462) Phase 1/2 clinical data readouts in MTAP-deleted pancreatic and non-small cell lung cancer expected throughout 2026, representing potential de-risking events [1]
  2. TNG456 brain-penetrant PRMT5 inhibitor entering clinical development for glioblastoma, an indication with extremely limited treatment options and high unmet need [2]
  3. $343.1 million cash runway extending into 2028 provides financial stability to advance three clinical programs without dilutive financing pressure [1]
Key Risks
  1. Binary clinical trial risk: negative Phase 1/2 or pivotal data for vopimetostat could devastate the stock given the $2.8 billion market cap on zero commercial product revenue [1][4]
  2. CEO transition in January 2026 from founding CEO Dr. Barbara Weber to Dr. Malte Peters introduces execution and strategic continuity risk at a critical pipeline inflection point [3]
  3. Competitive threat from larger pharma companies and AI-powered drug discovery platforms accelerating rival PRMT5 inhibitor or MTAP-targeted programs in the same patient population [2]
