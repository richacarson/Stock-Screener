Company Profile
Healthcare ·Medical Devices ·NASDAQ ·United States
Tenon Medical is a medical device company developing and marketing implant systems for the treatment of severe lower back pain caused by sacroiliac (SI) joint disorders. The company offers the Catamaran SI-Joint Fusion System and SImmetry+ SI Joint Fusion System, which provide minimally invasive approaches to SI joint fusion using titanium implants. Founded in 2012 and headquartered in Los Gatos, California.
26 Employees ·www.tenonmed.com

Investment Thesis
Tenon Medical offers a differentiated approach to the growing SI joint fusion market through its Catamaran system's novel inferior-posterior approach, now FDA-cleared for expanded thoracolumbar fusion indication [1]. Revenue acceleration is striking — Q4 2025 surged 92% YoY to $1.5M, and the 190+ patent portfolio creates meaningful IP protection in a market dominated by SI-BONE [1][2]. At an $8.8M market cap, the stock is priced for failure, creating asymmetric upside if physician adoption continues accelerating and the company navigates its cash constraints. CEO Foster and CIO Geist bring credible MedTech track records with prior successful exits to Fortune 500 companies.
The bear case is existential: Tenon has only $3.8M cash remaining against $10.7M annual cash burn, implying approximately 4 months of runway without a capital raise [3]. Dilutive equity financing at $0.78/share and an $8.8M market cap would be severely destructive to existing shareholders. Revenue of $3.9M annually is immaterial — the company needs to grow 10-20x to reach profitability, which requires years of sustained commercialization spend. The SI joint fusion market is dominated by SI-BONE with ~$250M revenue and established surgeon relationships; Tenon's tiny sales force (26 total employees) cannot compete on reach. Daily liquidity of only $819K makes the stock essentially untradeable for institutional investors, and the stock price near $0.78 creates NASDAQ delisting risk below $1.00. The negative forward P/E (-1.25x) confirms no near-term profitability path [3].
Key Catalysts
  1. Q4 2025 revenue surge of 92% YoY to $1.5M signals accelerating Catamaran physician adoption that could sustain into 2026 if new surgeon training converts to recurring procedure volume [1]

  3. 190+ patent portfolio and differentiated inferior-posterior approach could attract acquisition interest from larger orthopedic companies (Stryker, Medtronic, SI-BONE) [2]
Key Risks

  3. Competitive disadvantage versus SI-BONE's established surgeon network and $250M revenue base; Tenon's 26-person team cannot match the sales reach needed for medical device adoption at scale

AI Resilience LOW RISK

AI Risk Protection (8/9): Tenon Medical manufactures physical titanium implants for spinal surgery — AI cannot replace a physical device that gets implanted into a patient's body to fuse the sacroiliac joint [3]. The surgical procedure requires a trained surgeon, an operating room, and a physical implant. The SI joint fusion market is regulated by the FDA with 510(k) clearance requirements that create substantial barriers. AI may eventually assist in surgical planning or robotic-assisted implant placement, but this would increase demand for precision implants like Catamaran rather than substitute them. CTO Ginn has publicly acknowledged that medical device companies must balance regulatory patience with technology adoption [2]. AI Opportunity (0/1): No AI-generated revenue. Tenon is a pure hardware medical device company with no AI products or services.
