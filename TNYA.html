Company Profile
Healthcare ·Biotechnology ·NASDAQ ·United States
Tenaya Therapeutics, Inc. is a clinical-stage biotechnology company focused on developing gene therapies and targeted treatments for genetic heart diseases. Lead programs include TN-201 for MYBPC3-associated hypertrophic cardiomyopathy (HCM) in Phase 1b/2 trials, TN-401 for PKP2-associated arrhythmogenic right ventricular cardiomyopathy (ARVC), and TN-301, a small molecule HDAC6 inhibitor for heart failure and Duchenne muscular dystrophy. Founded in 2016, headquartered in South San Francisco.

Investment Thesis
Tenaya occupies a unique niche in cardiac gene therapy, targeting genetic heart diseases (HCM, ARVC) with no curative options for patients. The TN-201 Phase 1b/2 MyPEAK-1 trial data expected in Q2 2026 represents a potentially transformative catalyst—if successful, it could establish the first gene therapy for hypertrophic cardiomyopathy, a disease affecting 1 in 500 people [1]. The March 2026 Alnylam collaboration ($10M upfront, $1.1B potential milestones) validates the platform and provides non-dilutive capital. With $80.9M cash, D/E of only 9.5%, and 8 analysts maintaining a 'Strong Buy' consensus with a $4.00 price target (405% upside), the risk/reward profile appeals to biotech investors willing to underwrite binary clinical outcomes [4].
The fundamental challenge is that TNYA is a pre-revenue company burning $19.3M per quarter with approximately 4 quarters of cash runway as of March 2026 [1]. An additional capital raise is inevitable, and at the current $0.79 stock price (down 66% from 52-week high of $2.35), dilution will be severe [4]. Daily trading volume has declined to ~656K shares, creating a liquidity-constrained position where the daily dollar volume ($518K) falls below IOWN's $1M minimum threshold. The entire investment thesis rests on binary clinical trial outcomes—TN-201 data from MyPEAK-1 will either validate or invalidate the platform. Gene therapy manufacturing scalability, payer reimbursement for expensive one-time therapies, and the long regulatory pathway to BLA approval all represent execution risks even in the success scenario.
Key Catalysts
  1. TN-201 Phase 1b/2 MyPEAK-1 trial data from both adult cohorts expected Q2 2026, representing a binary catalyst for the HCM gene therapy program with potential to establish a new therapeutic category
  2. Alnylam Pharmaceuticals collaboration advancing novel cardiovascular gene targets with up to $1.1B in potential milestones, providing non-dilutive funding and platform validation
  3. Advancement of TN-301 HDAC6 inhibitor into clinical development for HFpEF and DMD, diversifying the pipeline beyond gene therapy into a broader cardiac disease platform
Key Risks
  1. Cash runway of approximately 4 quarters ($80.9M at $19.3M/quarter burn) necessitates imminent capital raise at severely depressed share price ($0.79), threatening massive dilution to 217M shares outstanding
  2. Binary clinical risk—TN-201 MyPEAK-1 trial failure would invalidate the core gene therapy platform and likely render the company non-viable given limited runway and single-program dependency
