Company Profile
Healthcare ·Biotechnology ·NASDAQ ·United States
Tenaya Therapeutics is a clinical-stage biotechnology company developing potentially curative gene therapies for heart disease. Lead candidates include TN-201 (MYBPC3-associated hypertrophic cardiomyopathy), TN-401 (PKP2-associated ARVC), and TN-301 (HDAC6 inhibitor for heart failure). Founded in 2016, headquartered in South San Francisco.
70 Employees ·www.tenayatherapeutics.com

Investment Thesis
Tenaya Therapeutics occupies a first-in-class position in gene therapy for genetic cardiomyopathies — an area with zero approved therapies and significant unmet medical need. TN-201 has shown durable, dose-dependent protein restoration in HCM patients, and TN-401 demonstrated tolerability in ARVC without dose-limiting toxicities [1]. The Alnylam collaboration validates the platform with up to $1.1B in potential milestones plus an upfront payment. Multiple 2026 catalysts — Cohort 2 interim data in H1, one-year efficacy data in H2, and regulatory alignment on pivotal studies — could de-risk the investment thesis. Cash of $100.5M provides runway into H2 2027 [1].
The bear case is formidable: Tenaya is a pre-revenue biotech with zero product sales, burning approximately $80M annually with a $150M market cap. The 30-40% workforce reduction signals financial distress and raises questions about the ability to execute on a complex clinical pipeline with approximately 70 remaining employees [3]. Gene therapy development carries extreme binary risk — Phase 2/3 trial failures are common and would devastate the stock. The $55.8M December 2025 offering at $1.20/unit diluted shareholders, and additional raises will likely be necessary before any potential approval and revenue. The sub-$1 stock price creates NASDAQ delisting risk. Competitive gene therapy platforms from larger, better-capitalized companies (Rocket Pharmaceuticals, BioMarin) could leapfrog Tenaya's programs [4].

AI cannot substitute for biological therapies that deliver functional genes to heart muscle cells — this requires physical manufacturing, clinical trials, and deeply regulated FDA approval processes [2]. The barriers to entry are biological, regulatory, and manufacturing-based, not software-based. AI tools may eventually enhance drug discovery timelines, but this would be an enabler for Tenaya rather than a threat. No analyst has flagged AI as a disruption risk for cardiac gene therapy.
