Investment Thesis
Toll Brothers offers deep value at 9.48x trailing earnings with a fortress balance sheet ($3.5B liquidity, 14.2% net D/C ratio), 17% ROE, and the #1 position in US luxury homebuilding commanding $970K-$990K average selling prices. The structural housing shortage, aging housing stock, and favorable demographics for luxury buyers provide long-term tailwinds, while 8-10% community count growth in FY2026 positions the company to capture demand recovery. The Inspire score of +33 (Aligned) with 13 positive attributions and zero negatives makes this an attractive option for faith-based portfolios, and the $650M buyback program provides meaningful EPS accretion at current valuations.
The bear case is near-term demand softening — FY2026 delivery guidance of 10,300-10,700 homes is below FY2025's 11,292, and Q1 FY2026 revenue already declined 5% YoY to $1.90B. Adjusted gross margins are compressing from 27.1% in Q4 FY2025 to 26.0% guidance for full-year FY2026, and the stock sits 12% below its 50-day moving average. Rising mortgage rates and affordability constraints could further pressure the luxury segment, while independent ESG ratings from Censible flag very poor performance on pollution prevention, carbon footprint, water usage, and diversity — creating a gap between the positive Inspire score and environmental reality. Revenue growth has been essentially flat (2.2% 5-year CAGR) and the 0.77% dividend yield offers minimal income support.
