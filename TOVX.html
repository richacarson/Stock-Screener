Company Profile
Healthcare ·Biotechnology ·AMEX ·United States
Theriva Biologics, Inc. is a clinical-stage immuno-oncology company developing oncolytic viruses and microbiome-based therapeutics to treat cancer and related diseases. Its lead candidate, VCN-01, is an oncolytic adenovirus in Phase 2 trials for metastatic pancreatic cancer, with additional programs including SYN-004 (ribaxamase) for prevention of acute graft-versus-host disease and SYN-020 for gastrointestinal indications.

Investment Thesis
Theriva Biologics is a micro-cap clinical-stage biotech with a potentially meaningful oncolytic virus candidate (VCN-01) targeting pancreatic cancer, but the investment case is deeply challenged by financial fragility. Q1 2026 showed $0.3M in license revenue and a $2.0M net loss, with $14.4M cash providing runway only into Q1 2027 [1]. The company has FDA alignment on a pivotal Phase 3 trial design for VCN-01, and plans a dosing feasibility study in Spain for H2 2026, but execution requires capital the company does not currently have [1]. The Inspire Insight score of 28 (ALIGNED) reflects positive ethical attributes including Product Safety and Customer Welfare, consistent with its cancer-treatment mission [5].
The bear case is overwhelming for all but the most speculative investors. With a $11.5M market cap, 15 employees, negative ROE of -138.9%, and R&D spending that declined 88% YoY, Theriva lacks the resources to advance its pipeline without significant dilutive financing [1][4]. The company has changed its name and strategic focus multiple times, and the quorum issue delaying a warrant vote suggests weak institutional backing [3]. Forward P/E is meaningless at -1.26, and the stock trades at $0.25 — firmly in penny-stock territory. Even if VCN-01 shows promise in the Phase 3 trial, the company would need a well-funded partner or acquirer to bring it to market. The 88% R&D cut is particularly concerning, as it suggests the company may be prioritizing cash preservation over pipeline advancement.
