Company Profile
Consumer Cyclical ·Luxury Goods ·NYSE ·United States
Tapestry is a New York-based house of accessible luxury brands — Coach, Kate Spade, and Stuart Weitzman. After its $8.5B acquisition of Capri Holdings (Michael Kors / Versace / Jimmy Choo) was blocked by the FTC in October 2024 and terminated in November 2024, Tapestry pivoted to a $2B share buyback and focused on organic growth led by Coach.

Investment Thesis
Tapestry is a high-quality consumer brand operator with one of the best-executed brand turnarounds in retail. Coach grew 10% in fiscal Q2 2025 [1] driven by the Tabby/Brooklyn franchises and continued resonance with younger consumers. The failed Capri merger, while frustrating strategically, freed up $8.5B of dry powder that management redeployed into a $2B buyback at attractive valuations [1]. Management raised FY25 revenue guidance to $6.85B and EPS to $4.85-$4.90, signaling confidence in standalone execution. The AMPLIFY strategy launched in fall 2025 [2] and the AI-led retail operating system [3] support continued share gains.
The bear case centers on balance sheet leverage and brand concentration. Debt-to-equity of 715% is extreme — a function of the buyback and existing debt — and limits flexibility if a luxury downturn deepens. Coach generates the majority of profits, leaving Kate Spade and Stuart Weitzman to do most of the brand-rebuild heavy lifting; a stumble at Coach (which is highly fashion-cycle dependent) would compress earnings sharply. China demand remains volatile and structural. Forward P/E of 20.8 already prices in continued double-digit Coach growth, leaving little margin for execution missteps.
Key Catalysts
  1. Continued Coach double-digit revenue growth driven by Tabby franchise and AMPLIFY brand storytelling
  2. Kate Spade brand turnaround under new creative leadership returning to growth
  3. Continued share buybacks executing against the $2B post-Capri authorization
Key Risks
  1. Debt-to-equity of 715% leaves the balance sheet exposed to a luxury cycle downturn
  2. Coach concentration risk — fashion misstep at the flagship brand would crater consolidated earnings
  3. China consumer demand softness disproportionately affecting accessible luxury

AI Risk Protection (6/9): Luxury handbags are a physical-world brand-driven category where AI cannot substitute the product.
