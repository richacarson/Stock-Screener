Company Profile
Industrials ·Building Products & Equipment ·NYSE ·United States
Trex Company manufactures and sells composite decking, railing, and fencing products made from 95% recycled and reclaimed materials, including polyethylene plastic waste and reclaimed wood fiber. As the largest recycler of plastic bags in North America, the company has diverted over 5 billion pounds of recycled PE film since founding. Trex sells through wholesale distributors, retail lumber dealers, and big-box retailers including Home Depot and Lowe's.

Investment Thesis
Trex is the market share leader in composite decking with a sustainability-driven business model that converts 95% recycled materials into high-margin building products. The company posted 2025 revenue of $1.17B with 19.8% ROE, 39.2% gross margins, and a pristine balance sheet (D/E of 18%) [1][5]. New products drove 24% of 2025 sales (up from 18%), and railing is a double-digit growth engine. At a PEG of 1.0 with forward P/E of 23.4x, the valuation is reasonable for a quality compounder benefiting from the secular wood-to-composite conversion trend, which still has significant runway with composite at only ~25-30% of the total decking market. The CEO succession to COO Adam Zambanini provides leadership continuity [2]. An insider purchase of $504K in shares in March 2026 signals management conviction [4].
The bear case centers on growth deceleration and cyclical exposure. Revenue declined 3.9% YoY and 2026 guidance of $1.185-$1.230B implies only low single-digit growth, raising questions about whether the wood-to-composite conversion is slowing [1]. Earnings declined sharply (-89.4% EPS growth YoY, though partly driven by one-time items) and 2026 gross margins are expected to drop 250bps due to depreciation from the new Arkansas facility. The housing market is Trex's primary end market — higher mortgage rates and housing construction slowdowns directly impact deck building and remodeling activity. The stock trades at 24x trailing earnings for a company with negative revenue and earnings growth, creating valuation risk if growth doesn't reaccelerate. Competition from AZEK and Fiberon is intensifying, forcing Trex to increase marketing spend (18% of sales in 2026). Beta of 1.6 reflects significant cyclical risk, and the stock has dropped from a $68.78 52-week high to $42.77, a 38% decline [5].
Key Catalysts
  1. Secular wood-to-composite decking conversion — composite still represents only ~25-30% of the total decking market, providing a long growth runway as awareness and distribution expand [1]
  2. Double-digit railing growth projected for 2026, with railing and fencing adjacencies expanding the addressable market beyond core decking [1]
  3. New Arkansas manufacturing facility providing capacity for long-term demand growth, with near-term margin headwinds that should normalize as utilization ramps [1]
Key Risks
  1. Housing market cyclicality — higher mortgage rates and construction slowdowns directly reduce deck building and remodeling activity, Trex's primary demand driver [1]
  2. Margin compression from new Arkansas facility depreciation (250bps gross margin impact in 2026) and increased marketing spend (18% of sales), squeezing profitability during a growth slowdown [1]
  3. Intensifying competition from AZEK and Fiberon in the composite decking market, with Trex needing to increase brand investment to maintain market share leadership [1]
