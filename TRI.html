Investment Thesis
Thomson Reuters is a high-quality AI-transforming information-services compounder under CEO Steve Hasker, guiding to 2026 organic revenue growth of 7.5-8.0% with 100bps of EBITDA margin expansion from 39.2%, and free cash flow of ~$2.1B (up from $1.95B in 2025) [1]. The bull case centers on AI-enabled products already at 28% of annualized contract value (up from 24% in one quarter) and the company's $500M+ AI investment plus multi-billion-dollar acquisitions of agentic AI assistants, e-invoicing, and tax automation capabilities [1][2]. Proprietary, regulated content in Westlaw, Checkpoint, and Practical Law is exactly the kind of structured, citable data LLMs need but cannot legally replicate, creating a moat against AI disruption. The Woodbridge Company (Thomson family) ~67% controlling stake provides long-term patient capital, and the ~22-year dividend history with ~2.6% yield offers income for long-term holders [3].
The bear case starts with valuation — TRI trades around $150 at ~37x forward P/E and ~4.5x PEG, very rich even for a quality compounder [3]. AI remains a double-edged sword: while TR's proprietary content is defensible, AI-native legal-tech competitors like Harvey and Hebbia are targeting Westlaw's workflow layer, and CEO Hasker himself has publicly framed the 'expensive content retaining value' question as the central strategic challenge [2]. The Big 3 segment growth of ~9.5% is healthy but premium-priced, so any deceleration would compress multiples. Currency exposure (Canadian base, USD reporting) adds modest FX volatility.
