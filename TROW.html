Investment Thesis
T. Rowe Price is a 89-year-old active asset manager with a fortress balance sheet (D/E 3.9%, ROE 18.8%, profit margin 28.5%) trading at 9.96x forward earnings with a ~5.2% dividend yield and 39 consecutive years of dividend growth [4]. Q4 2025 revenue grew 6.4% YoY to $1.94B on higher average AUM, and full-year 2025 adjusted revenue grew 2.8% to $7.4B despite $56.9B of net outflows [1]. Strategic pivots are working: OHA raised a record $16B+ in 2025 (second consecutive record), and the first managed private equity fund closed in Q1 2026 [1]. For income investors, TROW is a Dividend Aristocrat with a covered payout (~54% ratio) and minimal debt, creating a high margin of safety.
The bear case centers on secular active-management headwinds. Net outflows totaled $56.9B in 2025 ($25.5B in Q4 alone) and continued with $13.7B more in Q1 2026, with equity mutual funds hemorrhaging ~$75B in redemptions [1]. The passive/ETF shift plus AI-driven portfolio tools threaten the high-fee active model that still dominates T. Rowe's revenue mix. March 2026 AUM of $1.71T is below the January peak of $1.80T, reflecting market volatility on top of outflows [1]. 2026 operating expenses are guided 3-6% higher versus a declining AUM base, compressing margins. The OHA and private-equity pivots are credible but remain a minority of revenue. HOLD: strong balance sheet, deep value (10x earnings), and a meaningful dividend offset persistent outflow pressure, but lack of clear revenue growth inflection prevents a BUY.
Key Catalysts
  1. OHA private credit platform continuing record fundraising pace ($16B+ in 2025) and private-equity launch scaling meaningfully in 2026 [1]
  2. Stabilization of equity outflows as retirement and target-date flows normalize; any quarter of net inflows would re-rate the multiple [1]
  3. Continued dividend aristocrat status (39 consecutive years of growth) supported by 28.5% margins and minimal debt, anchoring income investor demand [4]
Key Risks
  1. Continued passive/ETF and AI-driven portfolio tool share gains pressuring active mutual-fund fee revenue, which still dominates the mix [2]
  2. Ongoing net outflows ($13.7B in Q1 2026, $56.9B in 2025) compressing AUM base and operating leverage [1]
  3. Market volatility driving AUM declines (March 2026 $1.71T vs. January $1.80T) that directly compress revenue given ad-valorem fee model [1]
