Company Profile
Energy ·Oil & Gas Midstream ·NYSE ·Canada
TC Energy is a North American energy infrastructure company operating one of the largest natural gas pipeline networks (~93,700 km) across Canada, the U.S. and Mexico, plus Bruce Power nuclear generation and storage assets. Following the 2024 spin-off of its liquids pipelines business (South Bow), TRP is now a pure-play natural gas and power infrastructure company.

Investment Thesis
TC Energy is a post-spin-off natural gas and power infrastructure pure-play with 2026 EBITDA guidance of $11.6-$11.8B (6-8% growth) and a 5-7% EBITDA CAGR extended through 2028 [1]. The dividend was raised materially to C$3.51/share, reflecting visibility into $6B/year of net capex deployment through 2030 and the completion of Bruce Power Unit 3 MCR in 2026 [1]. AI-driven electricity demand is a structural tailwind for both natural gas transport and Bruce Power's clean baseload output. The business is highly AI-resilient (physical molecules and electrons cannot be disrupted by software) and offers a 5%+ yield with a fortress contracted asset base [1].
The bear case centers on leverage, execution, and Indigenous/ESG risk. D/E ~175% reflects the midstream/utility capital structure, limiting flexibility if rates stay high [1]. Glassdoor reviews describe a 'toxic culture' and 'old boys' club' where innovation is discouraged, suggesting the cultural debt needed to execute $6B/year of new capex is meaningful [2]. The company has a documented pattern of cost/schedule overruns on Coastal GasLink and Keystone XL, and sustained Indigenous activism (Wet'suwet'en) continues to challenge project permitting [3]. Dividend payout ratio ~75% leaves limited buffer if EBITDA growth disappoints. The Inspire Insight score of 0 (MIXED) reflects the lack of clear negative attributions but also the absence of Kingdom-aligned positives.
Key Catalysts
  1. Bruce Power Unit 3 MCR returning to service in 2026 and adding to the clean-baseload earnings base, alongside ~$4B of 2026 in-service capital [1]
  2. AI-driven power demand structurally lifting both natural gas pipeline utilization and nuclear generation economics across TRP's footprint [1]
  3. Continued 5-7% EBITDA growth through 2028 enabling mid-single-digit dividend growth from the new C$3.51/share base [1]
Key Risks
  1. Continued project cost/schedule overruns similar to Coastal GasLink, pressuring the $6B/year net capex cadence and credit metrics [3]
  2. Indigenous activism and environmental permitting risk on new natural gas pipelines, with academic literature characterizing TRP's prior response as 'strategic cosmetic change' [3]
  3. Interest rate sensitivity given ~175% D/E and 5%+ yield — a sustained rise in long rates compresses the equity valuation and dividend discount [1]
