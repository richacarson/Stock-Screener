Investment Thesis
TransUnion is accelerating in a regulated-oligopoly credit bureau market, delivering 13% Q4 2025 revenue growth to $1.17B and 9% full-year 2025 growth to $4.58B, with net income more than doubling to $455M as Neustar synergies emerge [1]. The OneTru platform embedding AI across Credit, Marketing, Fraud, and Consumer Solutions has become a genuine growth driver, and 2026 guidance of 8-9% organic constant-currency revenue growth plus 8-10% adjusted EPS growth to $4.63-$4.71 shows sustained momentum [1]. With a structural FCRA-regulated moat (only three national credit bureaus), proprietary data, and forward P/E near 17-18x, TRU offers growth-quality exposure at a reasonable multiple. AI is net-positive here — TransUnion's data IS the training ground AI models need.
The bear case is multifaceted. In September 2025, TransUnion disclosed a breach affecting 4.4M+ Americans (Social Security numbers, contact data), which led to multiple class-action lawsuits consolidated into MDL 3170 and ongoing regulatory investigation [4]. Debt/equity remains elevated post-Neustar and legal exposure could weigh on free cash flow. Glassdoor 3.6/5 (declining 3% YoY) and employee complaints of 'money-first' culture post-acquisition plus U.S. layoffs in favor of offshore Global Capability Centers indicate cultural strain [2]. CFPB regulatory pressure on credit reporting practices, the TransUnion LLC v. Ramirez Supreme Court precedent limiting class-action standing, and rising competitive pressure from fintech data providers all create risk.
