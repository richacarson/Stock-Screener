Company Profile
Basic Materials ·Gold ·NYSE ·Tanzania
TRX Gold Corporation (formerly Tanzanian Royalty Exploration) is a gold mining company advancing the Buckreef Gold Project in the Geita Region of Tanzania. The company operates an established open pit mine with a 2,000 tonnes per day processing plant and is expanding toward 3,500+ tpd capacity with a SAG/ball mill expansion. TRX Gold is a joint venture partner with STAMICO, the Tanzanian government mining entity, and sells gold domestically to the Bank of Tanzania.

Investment Thesis
TRX Gold is delivering explosive growth as Buckreef Gold production ramps and gold prices surge. Q2 FY2026 set records with 7,453 oz poured, $34.1M revenue, $21.1M gross profit (62% margin), and $20M adjusted EBITDA at a realized price of $4,655/oz [1]. Six-month revenue nearly tripled to $59.2M from $21.6M in the prior period, with gross profit expanding from $7.0M to $35.3M [1]. The balance sheet is clean with $26M cash, $12M+ undrawn revolver, and minimal debt (D/E 5%), while the 20,147 oz ROM stockpile represents ~$107.5M in fair value providing near-term revenue visibility [2]. At a Forward P/E of 6.0 and $270M market cap, the stock is deeply discounted relative to the production growth trajectory and the SAG/ball mill expansion to 3,500+ tpd.
The bear case reflects the concentration risks inherent in a single-asset junior miner in Tanzania. TTM net income is negative (-$7.9M) despite strong operating margins on gold sales, as exploration expenses, share-based compensation, and non-cash charges weigh on reported profitability [4]. The company is entirely dependent on gold prices — the record results are as much a function of gold at $4,655/oz as operational improvement, and any meaningful gold price correction would compress margins rapidly. Tanzania country risk is real, with the STAMICO partnership providing political cover but also creating profit-sharing obligations that limit upside. Daily liquidity of $1.77M is marginal for institutional investors. The pump-and-dump allegations from Bonitas Research, while involving prior management, created lasting reputational damage that may suppress the valuation multiple [5].
Key Catalysts
  1. SAG/ball mill expansion to 3,500+ tpd with updated PEA expected Q4 FY2026, which could more than double production capacity and establish TRX Gold as a mid-tier producer
  2. Continued gold price strength above $4,500/oz driving record margins — the company's all-in sustaining costs are well below current prices, creating significant operating leverage
  3. FY2026 production guidance of 25,000-30,000 oz on track, with Q3 record throughput of 1,833 tpd confirming operational momentum toward capacity expansion
Key Risks
  1. Single-asset concentration in Tanzania creates existential risk — any operational disruption, regulatory change, or geological challenge at Buckreef would impact the entire company
  2. Gold price dependency is extreme — current record margins require gold above $4,000/oz, and a correction to $2,500-3,000 would severely compress profitability
  3. Daily liquidity of $1.77M is marginal for institutional investors, limiting the stock's re-rating potential and creating vulnerability to sharp price moves on modest volume
