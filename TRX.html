Company Profile
Basic Materials ·Gold ·NYSE ·Tanzania
TRX Gold Corporation is a junior gold mining company focused on the exploration, development, and production of gold at its flagship Buckreef Gold Project in the Geita Region of Tanzania. The Tanzanian government (via STAMICO) holds a 45% non-dilutable equity interest in Buckreef Gold. The company is expanding processing capacity from current levels toward 62,000+ oz annual production with a new 3,000+ tpd sulphide processing circuit.

Investment Thesis
TRX Gold is a junior gold miner hitting its stride at the perfect time — record gold prices above $4,600/oz are supercharging margins at the Buckreef Gold Project, where Q1 FY2026 delivered 57% gross margin and 53% EBITDA margin on $25.1M revenue [1]. Q2 FY2026 production hit a record 7,453 oz (up 13% QoQ) at an average gold price of ~$4,682/oz, and cash surged to ~$25M with a clean capital structure (no warrants remaining) [1]. The expansion to 62,000+ oz annual production via a 3,000+ tpd sulphide processing circuit represents a 2x+ production increase that would generate substantial free cash flow at current gold prices. At a forward P/E of 6.95x and analyst price target of $1.50, TRX offers significant leverage to gold prices with improving operational execution.
The bear case centers on single-asset concentration and commodity-price dependence. All revenue comes from one mine in Tanzania — any operational disruption, regulatory change, or political instability could halt production entirely [2]. Tanzania has a history of aggressive mining regulation, including forcing government equity stakes and local processing requirements. Gold prices above $4,600/oz are at historic highs; any reversal would compress margins severely given TRX's operating leverage. Trailing profit margin remains negative at -6.4% and ROE is only 5.5%, reflecting the reinvestment phase [4]. The 45% STAMICO equity stake means TRX shareholders receive only 55% of Buckreef economics. With a $430M market cap and junior miner execution risks (construction delays, grade variability, permitting), TRX requires sustained gold price strength and flawless execution to deliver on the expansion promise.
Key Catalysts
  1. Sulphide processing circuit commissioning targeting 62,000+ oz annual production, representing a 2x+ increase from current 25,000-30,000 oz guidance [1]
  2. Record gold prices above $4,600/oz driving margin expansion — Q1 FY2026 EBITDA margin of 53% doubled YoY, and Q2 prices averaged $4,682/oz [1]
  3. Exploration spending of $3-5M in FY2026 aimed at expanding the Buckreef resource base, potentially extending mine life and supporting further production growth [1]
Key Risks
  1. Single-asset concentration in Tanzania — any operational disruption, regulatory change, or political instability at Buckreef halts 100% of revenue [2]
  2. Gold price reversal risk — margins are leveraged to historically elevated gold prices above $4,600/oz; a decline to $2,500/oz would severely compress profitability [1]
  3. Expansion execution risk — the 3,000+ tpd sulphide processing circuit requires significant capex ($15-20M guided) with construction, permitting, and grade variability risks that could delay the production ramp [1]
