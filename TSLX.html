Company Profile
Financial Services ·Asset Management ·NYSE ·United States
Sixth Street Specialty Lending is a business development company (BDC) providing senior secured loans (first-lien, second-lien, unitranche), unsecured loans, mezzanine debt, and equity investments to middle-market companies. Externally managed by Sixth Street Partners, the firm targets companies with $50M-$250M EBITDA across diverse sectors.

Investment Thesis
TSLX offers a compelling 9.3% dividend yield backed by Sixth Street Partners' $75B platform, providing access to high-quality middle-market direct lending deal flow [1]. The 2026 ROE target of 11-11.5% and adjusted NII guidance of $1.87-$1.95/share demonstrate continued income generation capability despite falling rates [1]. At 10.1x forward P/E (essentially price-to-NII), the stock trades at an attractive valuation relative to book value. The 12-year dividend track record since 2014 provides income stability, and the orderly CEO transition from Easterly to Stanley ensures continuity of the disciplined, investor-first strategy [2].
The bear case centers on declining NII as the Fed normalizes rates, with revenue already down 12.5% YoY and earnings declining 42.7% [4]. The elevated payout ratio of 113% signals that distributions may exceed sustainable NII in the near term, creating potential dividend cut risk if rates continue falling. BDC structural constraints limit growth optionality — TSLX cannot retain earnings to compound, must distribute income, and relies on external capital markets for growth. Credit risk in the middle-market portfolio could materialize if economic conditions deteriorate, particularly given D/E leverage of 108%. Competition from private credit funds with permanent capital (Apollo, Ares, Blue Owl) is intensifying, potentially compressing spreads on new originations [3].
Key Catalysts
  1. Stabilization of base rates could arrest NII decline and support current distribution levels, with TSLX positioned to benefit from any credit market dislocation [1]
  2. Sixth Street platform growth to $75B+ AUM provides expanding deal flow pipeline and co-investment opportunities for TSLX shareholders [2]
  3. Attractive 9.3% yield at 10.1x forward P/E offers meaningful income in a declining rate environment, with potential for price appreciation toward analyst target of $21.72 [1]
Key Risks
  1. Dividend sustainability concerns with 113% payout ratio — if NII continues declining with lower rates, base distribution cut becomes probable [4]
  2. Credit cycle risk in middle-market portfolio as economic deceleration could increase defaults among leveraged borrowers, particularly in cyclical sectors [1]
  3. Intensifying competition from permanent-capital private credit vehicles (Apollo, Ares, Blue Owl) compressing new deal spreads and reducing deployment opportunities [3]
