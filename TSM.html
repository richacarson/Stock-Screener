Investment Thesis
TSMC is the irreplaceable backbone of the AI revolution, with 35.9% revenue growth in 2025 to $122B, 59.9% gross margins, and 46.4% EPS growth driven by insatiable demand for advanced AI chips. The forward PE of 18.3x is reasonable for a company guiding to ~30% revenue growth in 2026 with expanding margins (63-65% gross margin guidance for Q1 2026), and the AI accelerator revenue CAGR target of mid-to-high 30s percent through 2029 provides a long growth runway. With 35% ROE, a conservative balance sheet (D/E 19.6%), and every major AI company as a customer, TSMC has the strongest competitive moat in technology.
The bear case revolves around concentration risk — Taiwan's geopolitical vulnerability to China remains an existential overhang that no amount of Arizona fabs can fully mitigate. The stock trades 5% below its 50-day moving average and 54% below its 52-week high, reflecting tariff uncertainty and cyclical fears. TSMC consumes 8% of Taiwan's electricity and faces growing environmental scrutiny over water usage (99,000 tonnes daily at its Southern Taiwan facilities) and slow renewable energy adoption (only 9% in 2021 vs. Intel's 80%). The 1.07% dividend yield won't attract income investors, and the effective tax rate rising to 17-18% in 2026 from overseas operations will compress net margins.
