Investment Thesis
TSMC is the most essential technology company in the world, manufacturing every major AI accelerator at advanced nodes no competitor can match. With 71% foundry market share, 2025 revenue of $122.4B (+36%), profit margins of 45.1%, and AI chip revenue growing at a 54-56% CAGR through 2029, the fundamental trajectory is extraordinary. At 18.3x forward earnings with a forward PEG of just 0.25x, the stock is remarkably cheap given the company's monopolistic position in AI infrastructure. The 28% dividend increase, $52-56B capex plan, and Q1 2026 guidance of 38% YoY revenue growth confirm sustained AI demand with no deceleration visible.
The primary risk is geopolitical: the bulk of TSMC's production remains in Taiwan, which faces increasing tension between the US and China. A Taiwan Strait crisis would be catastrophic for the global technology supply chain and TSMC's stock. The $100B US investment addresses this but creates near-term margin dilution as overseas fabs operate at lower efficiency. TSMC consumes 8% of Taiwan's electricity and 150,000 tonnes of water daily, creating environmental sustainability constraints that could become binding as fabs expand — projected to consume 10% of Taiwan's electricity by 2030. At 31.8x trailing earnings, the stock prices in significant growth that depends on the AI capex cycle continuing without a cyclical downturn.
