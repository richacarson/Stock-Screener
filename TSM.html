Investment Thesis
TSMC is arguably the most important company in the global technology ecosystem, manufacturing over 90% of advanced semiconductors with no viable alternative at scale [1][2]. The company delivered 36% revenue growth in 2025 to $122.5B and guides for ~30% USD growth in 2026, with AI accelerator revenue compounding at 54-56% CAGR through 2029 [1]. At a forward P/E of 18.3x, TSMC trades at a remarkable discount to its growth rate — a company growing at 30%+ with 45% profit margins and 35% ROE would command 30x+ in any other context. The geopolitical discount creates an extraordinary buying opportunity for investors willing to accept Taiwan risk [3]. With $52-56B in 2026 CapEx deepening the technology moat, 70% foundry market share, and D/E of just 20%, TSMC combines elite growth with fortress-like financial strength. The 10% dividend increase and 22 consecutive years of payment add income to the growth story.
The bear case is dominated by a single factor: Taiwan geopolitical risk. Polymarket estimates a 16% probability of a military clash between Taiwan and China, and any disruption to TSMC's Taiwan fabs would have a $490B annual spillover impact on global electronics [3]. The 'Silicon Shield' thesis is weakening as the U.S. deliberately diversifies production through CHIPS Act subsidies, potentially reducing Taiwan's strategic leverage [3]. Environmental concerns are mounting — TSMC is projected to consume 10% of Taiwan's electricity by 2030, with its Taichung fab driving 38% of local power consumption; medical experts blame power-hungry fabs for rising lung cancer rates [3]. The effective tax rate increase to 17-18% in 2026 will pressure earnings growth. Water consumption rose 70% from 2015-2019, and Taiwan faces periodic drought risk that could constrain production. Customer concentration risk exists with Apple and NVIDIA comprising significant revenue shares [1].
