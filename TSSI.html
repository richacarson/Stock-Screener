Company Profile

TSS, Inc. (Total Site Solutions) provides data center infrastructure services including AI rack integration, systems deployment, procurement, and facilities management. Based in Georgetown, Texas, the company builds, integrates, and deploys custom high-performance computing infrastructure for data centers, serving OEM equipment manufacturers and enterprise end users. TSS has emerged as a key enabler of AI infrastructure buildout.

Investment Thesis
TSS has emerged as a direct beneficiary of the AI infrastructure buildout, growing revenue from $30.6M in 2022 to $245.7M in FY2025 — a 100%+ 3-year CAGR [1]. The company's AI rack integration services are in high demand, with Q4 rack volumes nearly matching all of Q1-Q3 combined and plans to double volumes in 2026 [1]. At a forward PEG of 0.19, the market has not fully priced in the growth trajectory. ROE of 36.1% and D/E of 54.3% demonstrate efficient capital deployment and conservative leverage. The $20-22M Adjusted EBITDA guidance for 2026 implies continued rapid scaling, supported by the amended long-term AI rack integration agreement and new Georgetown facility capacity [1][2].
The bear case centers on margin risk and customer concentration. Profit margin of 6.2% is thin for a company trading at 46x forward earnings — any operational disruption, component cost spike, or pricing pressure could compress margins further [4]. Forward EPS of $0.34 vs trailing EPS of $0.56 implies the market expects near-term EPS decline even as revenue grows, suggesting margin headwinds from facility expansion costs. Component supply constraints and memory price volatility (flagged by management) could limit rack deliveries [1]. TSS likely has significant customer concentration risk given the 'long-term AI rack integration agreement' driving growth. The company's competitive moat is narrow — larger IT services companies (Dell, Celestica, Flex) could scale rack integration services, pressuring TSS's market position. At $455M market cap with 286 employees, TSS is a small company where a single contract loss could be material [4].
Key Catalysts
  1. Planned doubling of AI rack integration volumes in 2026, supported by amended long-term integration agreement and expanded Georgetown facility capacity [1]
  2. 2026 Adjusted EBITDA guidance of $20-22M represents continued operational scaling and margin improvement from higher volumes [1]
  3. Board addition of Vivek Mohindra (ex-Dell CSO) strengthens AI and go-to-market strategy, potentially opening new enterprise customer relationships [2]
Key Risks
  1. Customer concentration risk — growth appears driven by a single 'long-term AI rack integration agreement' whose loss or renegotiation would be material at current scale [1]
  2. Component supply constraints and memory price volatility could limit completed rack deliveries in 2026, as management explicitly cautioned [1]
  3. Competitive pressure from larger IT infrastructure companies (Dell, Celestica, Flex) that could enter or expand in AI rack integration at scale, pressuring TSS's margins and market position [4]
