Company Profile
Technology ·Software - Application ·NASDAQ ·United States
ServiceTitan provides an end-to-end cloud-based software platform for the trades industry, serving HVAC, plumbing, electrical, roofing, landscaping, pest control, and other home and commercial service contractors. The platform manages business workflows from advertising and scheduling to dispatching, invoicing, and payment processing, with integrated FinTech solutions and AI-powered features.

Investment Thesis
ServiceTitan is the dominant vertical SaaS platform for the $2T+ trades industry, posting FY2026 revenue of $961M (up 24%) with 70.5% gross margins, net dollar retention above 110%, and a clear path to profitability — FY2027 guidance calls for $1.11B-$1.12B revenue with $128M-$133M non-GAAP operating income [1]. The Atlas AI platform is a genuine differentiator, embedding intelligent scheduling, pricing, and demand forecasting directly into contractor workflows, driving expansion revenue and deepening platform lock-in [3]. At 39x forward P/E based on expected FY2027 profitability, the stock is reasonably valued for a 20%+ growth company approaching its earnings inflection with $429M cash and minimal debt [5]. The FinTech layer (payment processing, financing) on $82.1B GTV represents a massive monetization opportunity as take rates expand. Strategic partnerships and geographic expansion into new trades verticals provide long runway for sustained growth [1].
The bear case centers on profitability timing and valuation risk. GAAP net loss of -$160M and -16.6% profit margin mean the company is still burning cash despite strong revenue growth, and any slowdown in the SaaS ramp would call the profitability timeline into question [5]. The 39x forward P/E assumes the earnings inflection happens on schedule — execution risk is real given the aggressive AI investment and expansion spending. Glassdoor reviews citing a psychologically exhausting culture and sporadic layoffs suggest potential talent retention challenges that could constrain execution [3]. The trades industry is cyclical and sensitive to housing market dynamics — a construction downturn would slow new customer acquisition and contractor spending on software. Competition from well-funded alternatives (Housecall Pro backed by Thoma Bravo) and potential entry by horizontal platforms (Salesforce, HubSpot) into trades verticals could pressure market share. The stock trades below its 50-day SMA of $66.64, reflecting near-term momentum headwinds [5].
Key Catalysts
  1. FY2027 profitability inflection — non-GAAP operating income guidance of $128M-$133M on $1.11B-$1.12B revenue represents the transition from growth-stage investing to sustainable earnings power [1]
  2. Atlas AI platform deployment driving expansion revenue through intelligent scheduling, pricing optimization, and demand forecasting features that increase ARPU and reduce churn [3]
  3. FinTech monetization expansion on $82.1B gross transaction volume as payment processing and financing take rates increase across the contractor customer base [1]
Key Risks
  1. Persistent GAAP losses — net loss of $160M despite $961M revenue raises concerns about the timeline to true profitability if growth moderates [1][5]
  2. Culture and talent retention risk — Glassdoor 3.5/5.0 rating with complaints about exhausting work environment and layoffs could constrain execution during critical growth phase [3]
  3. Housing cycle sensitivity — the trades industry is tied to residential construction and maintenance spending, creating demand risk in a potential housing downturn [5]
