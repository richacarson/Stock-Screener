Company Profile
Communication Services ·Telecom Services ·NYSE ·Canada
TELUS Corporation is a leading Canadian telecommunications company providing a broad range of wireless and wireline services including internet, voice, television, and healthcare technology solutions. Operating through TELUS Technology Solutions (TTech) and TELUS Digital Experience (DLCX), the company serves approximately 19 million customer connections. TELUS also operates TELUS Health (digital healthcare) and TELUS Agriculture & Consumer Goods. Founded in 1990, headquartered in Vancouver, British Columbia.

Investment Thesis
TELUS offers the highest dividend yield in Canadian telecom at 9.73%, supported by 20 consecutive years of dividend growth and a fiber-to-the-home network covering 90%+ of its footprint [1][6]. Q1 2026 revenue was C$5.0B with adjusted EBITDA of C$1.84B, and management guided for 2-4% revenue and EBITDA growth with 10% FCF growth to C$2.45B in 2026. CEO Entwistle's 26-year tenure provides unmatched strategic continuity, and his $3.35M personal share purchase plus taking salary entirely in stock signals genuine conviction [5]. TELUS's $350M+ in AI benefits since 2023 and Canada's first sovereign AI factory demonstrate operational technology leadership that should drive margin expansion as capex declines 10%.
The critical concern is sustainability of the 9.73% dividend yield. The 146% payout ratio means TELUS is paying more in dividends than it earns, forcing the company to pause dividend growth in December 2025 [5][6]. D/E of 199% with $22.3B in debt against just $932M in cash leaves limited financial flexibility in a rising rate environment. Revenue growth of 0.81% YoY is essentially flat, and ROE of 3.74% and profit margin of 4.57% are both well below sector leaders. TELUS Digital faces direct AI substitution risk in its customer experience outsourcing business. The stock's 52-week decline from $16.74 to $10.79 (36% drawdown) reflects market skepticism about the dividend's sustainability and growth trajectory. Any dividend cut would trigger significant further downside given the income-investor shareholder base.
Key Catalysts
  1. Free cash flow growth of 10% to C$2.45B in 2026, driven by capex reduction of 10% as fiber buildout completes, potentially enabling dividend payout ratio improvement toward sustainable levels
  2. TELUS Health digital healthcare platform scaling as a growth driver beyond core telecom, with potential to capture share in the C$300B+ Canadian healthcare market
  3. Deleveraging from 3.3x net debt/EBITDA to 3.0x by year-end 2027, reducing balance sheet risk and potentially enabling resumption of dividend growth
Key Risks
  1. Dividend payout ratio of 146% is unsustainable — any further deterioration in earnings could force a dividend cut, which would devastate the income-investor shareholder base and compress the stock further
  2. D/E of 199% with $22.3B in debt creates significant interest rate exposure and limits financial flexibility for strategic investments or acquisitions
  3. TELUS Digital's customer experience outsourcing business faces direct substitution from AI chatbots and automated service tools, potentially reducing revenue in a high-growth segment

AI Resilience (25%)
AI Resilience MODERATE
7
AI Risk Protection (6/9): TELUS's core telecom infrastructure — wireless towers, fiber networks, spectrum licenses, and physical connectivity — is structurally protected from AI disruption. AI cannot substitute for the physical network that carries data, and AI adoption actually increases demand for bandwidth and connectivity [1]. However, TELUS Digital Experience (DLCX), the company's customer experience and business process outsourcing division, faces direct AI substitution risk as AI chatbots and automated customer service tools reduce demand for human-operated contact centers. TELUS Health's digital healthcare platform also faces competitive pressure from AI-native healthcare solutions. The core telecom business is protected but adjacent verticals create meaningful exposure. AI Opportunity (1/1): TELUS generates quantifiable AI revenue through TELUS Digital's AI-powered customer experience services and has delivered $350M+ in AI benefits internally since 2023, including Canada's first sovereign AI factory [2].
