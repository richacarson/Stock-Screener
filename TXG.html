Investment Thesis
10x Genomics is the category leader in single-cell and spatial biology, with a defensible installed base of Chromium, Visium, and Xenium instruments and a growing consumables flywheel — single-cell consumables volumes grew over 30% in Q4 2025 driven by new FLEX products [1]. The October 2025 Anthropic partnership embedding 10x analytics into Claude for Life Sciences positions the company as a foundational data layer for AI-driven biology [2]. Q4 2025 revenue of $166M beat guidance and full-year 2025 ex-litigation revenue of $599M demonstrated stabilization, while litigation settlements added $44M of upfront cash. Trading well below historical multiples after 2025's drawdown, TXG offers optionality on a recovery in academic/biopharma capex.
The bear case is structural: 2026 guidance of $600-625M implies just 0-4% growth and instrument revenue dropped 36% in Q4 2025, signaling that customers are deferring capital purchases as NIH and academic funding stays constrained [1][3]. The May 2025 layoffs (8% of workforce, $50M cost cut) reflect ongoing margin pressure, and the company remains unprofitable [3]. Microsoft's GigaTIME AI demonstration knocked the stock down 11% on legitimate fears that AI-from-image tools could compress demand for 10x's spatial proteomics consumables [2]. Glassdoor reviews flagging 'Do or Die Culture' and bottom-quartile executive ratings raise execution concerns just as the company needs to drive new product adoption [2][3].
Key Catalysts
  1. Adoption ramp of FLEX single-cell consumables — Q4 2025 volumes grew 30%+ and full-year volumes grew 22%, indicating a real transition to lower-cost workflows that could pull through more customer use cases [1]
  2. Anthropic / Claude for Life Sciences integration unlocking natural-language analysis of 10x data, potentially expanding the addressable user base beyond bioinformatics specialists [2]
  3. Recovery in NIH and academic research capex — a return to normalized federal funding would directly lift instrument revenue, which dropped 36% in Q4 2025 [1][3]
Key Risks
  1. Persistent U.S. academic and government funding pressure — 40-50% of revenue is NIH/academic exposed, and 2026 guidance assumes no recovery [3]
  2. AI substitution risk from tools like Microsoft GigaTIME that convert standard pathology images into spatial proteomics data, potentially compressing 10x consumables demand [2]
  3. Continued operating losses and execution risk — May 2025 layoffs, bottom-quartile leadership reviews, and 0-4% guided growth could push the company into another cost-cut cycle if 2026 disappoints [1][3]
