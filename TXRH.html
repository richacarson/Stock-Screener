Company Profile
Consumer Cyclical ·Restaurants ·NASDAQ ·United States
Texas Roadhouse, Inc. operates casual dining restaurants in the United States and internationally through three segments: Texas Roadhouse, Bubba's 33, and Jaggers. The company's restaurants offer hand-cut steaks, ribs, seafood, chicken, and made-from-scratch sides, with approximately 750 locations across all brands.

Investment Thesis
Texas Roadhouse is a best-in-class casual dining operator with a differentiated people-first culture, strong unit economics, and disciplined multi-brand expansion. Full-year 2025 revenue grew 9.4% to $5.878B driven by continued positive comp sales and store week growth, while the managing partner model creates structural competitive advantages in employee retention and guest satisfaction [1][2]. The planned 35 new restaurant openings in 2026 across three concepts and positive comp sales guidance suggest the growth runway remains substantial, supported by a fortress balance sheet with only 65.9% debt-to-equity [1][2].
The bear case centers on margin compression from 7% commodity inflation and 3-4% labor cost increases in 2026, which contributed to a 26.9% Q4 2025 earnings decline and 5.7% full-year EPS contraction despite revenue growth [1]. Profit margins at 6.9% are inherently thin for full-service dining, leaving minimal buffer against input cost spikes. The company's Growth sleeve valuation at 20.7x forward earnings assumes continued comp sales momentum, which could falter if consumer discretionary spending weakens in a potential recessionary environment. The Bubba's 33 and Jaggers concepts remain unproven at scale and could dilute management focus [1][2].

Key Risks

  3. Multi-brand execution risk as Bubba's 33 and Jaggers scale beyond proven Texas Roadhouse core [1]
