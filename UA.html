Company Profile
Consumer Cyclical ·Apparel Manufacturing ·NYSE ·United States
Under Armour develops, markets, and distributes performance apparel, footwear, and accessories globally. The company sells through wholesale channels (sporting goods chains, department stores, specialty retailers) and direct-to-consumer (Brand and Factory House stores, e-commerce) across North America, EMEA, Asia-Pacific, and Latin America under the UNDER ARMOUR, HOVR, and UA brands.

Finite vs Infinite Game — Sinek (25%)
Mindset: FINITE Overall: 4 /10
Under Armour under returning founder/CEO Kevin Plank is a company caught between finite and infinite game thinking. Plank's passion for the brand is genuine, but the strategic whiplash (Plank → Frisk → Plank), failed $710M data acquisition spree, and reactive restructuring reveal a leadership pattern of chasing trends rather than playing to endure. The company changes strategy every 2-3 years — exactly what Sinek warns against.

Investment Thesis
Under Armour is a deep-discount turnaround bet on founder/CEO Kevin Plank's ability to restore the brand. At $5.79/share and a $2.5B market cap (down from $20B+ peak), the stock prices in severe pessimism [4]. Plank says the 'most disruptive' restructuring phase is over, with the $255M restructuring driving operational simplification, cleaner inventories, and growing wholesale partner interest [1]. Gross margins of 47.3% (despite 275bps tariff headwind) show the underlying brand still commands pricing power. Forward P/E of 15.2x assumes profitability recovery in FY2027, and management anticipates North America revenue stabilization. The premiumization strategy, Curry Brand separation, and cost discipline could restore mid-single-digit operating margins if executed.
The bear case is overwhelming for conservative investors. Revenue has declined for two consecutive years (-9.4% FY2025, -4% expected FY2026), profit margin is -10.4%, ROE is -30.4%, and free cash flow is -$386M [4]. The S&P BB- downgrade, $1.69B debt against $465M cash, and negative operating income create genuine balance sheet risk [1]. The $710M fitness data acquisition failure destroyed shareholder value and credibility. Market share losses to On Running, Hoka, and Nike's resurgence suggest the performance apparel space has structurally moved beyond Under Armour's positioning [2]. CEO Plank's Glassdoor rating (C- on Comparably, bottom 30%) and the 'boy's club' culture criticisms raise governance concerns. ESG ratings are below peers — Good On You rates UA 'Not Good Enough,' and the company lacks SBTi-validated targets that competitors Nike, Adidas, and Puma all have [3]. Beta of 1.77 amplifies downside in market corrections. This is a high-risk speculative position unsuitable for income or defensive portfolios.
Key Catalysts
  1. FY2027 revenue stabilization in North America and APAC as the 'most disruptive' restructuring phase concludes and wholesale partners re-engage with the premiumized brand [1]
  2. Gross margin recovery as tariff headwinds moderate and premiumization (higher ASPs, reduced promotions, cleaner inventories) flows through to profitability [1]
  3. Curry Brand separation creating a standalone growth vehicle while simplifying the core Under Armour brand story and capital allocation [1]
Key Risks
  1. Continued brand erosion — On Running, Hoka, and Nike's resurgence are capturing performance-minded consumers, and Under Armour's years in discount channels may have permanently damaged brand perception [2]
  2. Balance sheet stress — $1.69B debt, $465M cash, negative free cash flow of -$386M, and S&P BB- rating create financing risk if the turnaround takes longer than planned [4]
  3. Leadership risk — CEO Plank's revolving-door tenure and D+/C- executive team ratings raise execution concerns; strategic whiplash (apps → wholesale → DTC → premiumization) erodes investor confidence [2]
