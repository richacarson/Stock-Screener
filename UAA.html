Company Profile
Consumer Cyclical ·Apparel Manufacturing ·NYSE ·United States
Under Armour designs, develops, markets, and distributes athletic and outdoor apparel, footwear, and accessories globally. The company is executing a multi-year brand repositioning under founder-CEO Kevin Plank, who returned in 2024 to lead a restructuring that involves exiting unprofitable channels, reducing inventory, and refocusing on the core performance athletic identity. Under Armour operates in North America, EMEA, Asia-Pacific, and Latin America.

Investment Thesis
Under Armour's bull case is a founder-led turnaround at trough valuation: Kevin Plank's return aims to restore the brand's performance athletic identity, and FY2027 guidance projects gross margin expansion of 220-270 bps with adjusted operating income of $140-160M [2]. International business showed resilience with EMEA +9% and Latin America +9% in FY2026, while the North American channel cleanup positions for higher-quality revenue in subsequent years [1]. At $5.12/share after a multi-year selloff from $25+, UAA trades near tangible book value with significant option value if the turnaround succeeds.
The bear case is formidable: FY2026 delivered a $496M net loss with revenue declining for the fifth consecutive year to $5.0B, and FY2027 guides to further decline [1][2]. ROE of -30% and D/E of 137% indicate serious financial stress. Nike and Lululemon continue to take share with superior brand execution and digital ecosystems, while UA's retraction of climate commitments creates ESG risk and competitive disadvantage [7]. Plank's micromanagement philosophy and the company's history of leadership instability raise execution concerns for a turnaround that requires 3-5 years of patient brand building [4]. The market is pricing in significant execution risk — appropriately so given the track record. WATCH until North American revenue stabilizes.
Key Catalysts
  1. North American revenue stabilization and return to growth in FY2027/2028, demonstrating that channel cleanup and brand repositioning are working after five years of decline [1][2]
  2. FY2027 gross margin expansion of 220-270 bps materializing, validating that exiting low-margin channels improves profitability even as revenue declines [2]
  3. Successful footwear product launch that captures meaningful market share — footwear is UA's weakest segment versus Nike and Adidas, and a breakthrough entry would expand the addressable market significantly [3]
Key Risks
  1. Revenue decline extending beyond FY2027, indicating the brand repositioning is failing to resonate with consumers and the turnaround timeline is longer than the balance sheet can support with D/E at 137% [1][2][6]
  2. Nike and Adidas accelerating AI-driven personalization and digital ecosystem advantages, systematically widening the competitive gap while UA's subscale digital platform cannot compete [5]
  3. Kevin Plank leadership instability or departure — the bull case requires founder-led brand restoration over 3-5 years; any disruption restarts the confidence-building clock, and his micromanagement style may limit talent retention [4][5]
