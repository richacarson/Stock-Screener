Investment Thesis
Under Armour's bull case is a founder-led turnaround with visible green shoots: Kevin Plank's return has stabilized the brand narrative, gross margin of 47.9% demonstrates underlying product pricing power, and EMEA was projected to grow at high single digits in FY2026[1]. The $500M buyback program signals management confidence, and the North American channel cleanup — exiting off-price and low-margin wholesale — positions the brand for higher-quality revenue in subsequent years. At ~$7/share after a multi-year selloff, UAA trades near balance sheet tangible book value with significant option value if the turnaround succeeds.
The bear case is that Under Armour's revenue decline (-9.4% in FY2025, -4% guided FY2026) shows no near-term reversal, while Nike and Lululemon continue to take share with superior brand execution and digital ecosystems[1]. The TTM debt load of $1.69B with negative ROE of -30% and Piotroski F-Score of 3/9 indicates fragile financial health. Any tariff escalation on Asian manufacturing (significant UA sourcing) would pressure gross margins that are the only current bright spot. The company has been in restructuring mode for five years — investors are right to demand evidence before rewarding the stock.
Key Catalysts
  1. North American revenue stabilization and return to growth in FY2027, demonstrating that the channel cleanup and brand repositioning are working and that Under Armour can grow alongside Nike and Adidas rather than losing share
  2. EMEA high single-digit growth materializing consistently for multiple quarters, validating that the international business can be a growth driver while North America recovers
  3. New footwear product launch that captures meaningful market share — footwear is Under Armour's weakest segment versus Nike and Adidas, and a successful footwear entry would significantly expand the brand's addressable market
Key Risks
  1. Nike and Adidas accelerating AI-driven personalization and digital ecosystem advantages that systematically erode Under Armour's performance positioning among tech-savvy athletes who prioritize data-driven training
  2. Tariff escalation on Asian-sourced manufacturing increasing COGS and compressing the 47.9% gross margin that is currently the primary financial bright spot in the turnaround story
  3. Kevin Plank departure or leadership instability — the bull case requires founder-led brand restoration over 3-5 years; any leadership disruption would restart the market's confidence-building clock

AI Resilience (25%)
AI Resilience MODERATE
6
Under Armour's core business — designing, manufacturing, and marketing athletic apparel — involves physical products and brand identity that AI cannot replace[1]. AI poses some threat to the design and marketing functions (generative AI for product design, AI-driven personalization at Nike and Adidas), but the brand itself and manufacturing relationships are defensible moats. The Connected Fitness platform (MapMyFitness) could benefit from AI-driven personalization and workout optimization, potentially creating AI-powered stickiness. AI is not an existential threat to athletic apparel, but competitors with superior data assets (Nike with 190M+ Nike app users) can use AI more effectively. Scores 6 on AI Risk Protection and 0 on AI Opportunity.
