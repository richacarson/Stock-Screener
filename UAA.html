Company Profile
Consumer Cyclical ·Apparel Manufacturing ·NYSE ·United States
Under Armour, Inc. designs, develops, markets, and distributes performance apparel, footwear, and accessories for men, women, and youth. The company offers compression, fitted, and loose-fit apparel, as well as running, training, and basketball footwear. It sells through wholesale channels and direct-to-consumer via owned stores and e-commerce.

Investment Thesis
Under Armour represents a turnaround opportunity at a depressed valuation of $5.15/share, with fiscal 2026 revenue of $4.97B reflecting a potentially bottoming cycle after two years of consecutive declines [1]. The company is investing in AI-powered personalization, premium brand repositioning, and executive leadership changes to stabilize the business. If Kevin Plank's restructuring succeeds in returning profit margins to the mid-single-digit range by FY2028, the stock could re-rate significantly from its current $2.2B market cap. The Inspire Insight score of 51 (ALIGNED) is among the strongest in the athletic apparel sector.
The bear case is substantial. Revenue has declined from $5.9B to $4.97B over three years with no evidence of stabilization — Q4 FY2026 missed estimates at -$0.03 EPS [1]. ROE of -30.0%, profit margins of -10.0%, and D/E of 137% paint a picture of a financially stressed company. Under Armour faces intensifying competition from On Running, Hoka, and Nike's DTC push in the premium athletic segment it is targeting. The C- employee leadership rating and history of CEO turnover raise questions about organizational capacity to execute a complex turnaround [2]. Good On You rates UA 'Not Good Enough' on environmental performance, and the company has a history of governance-related shareholder disputes [3].
Key Catalysts
  1. New executive leadership team announced May 2026 to 'accelerate transformation' could bring fresh strategic direction and operational discipline to stabilize declining revenue
  2. AI-driven personalization through the new head of AI and advanced analytics could differentiate UA's connected fitness ecosystem (MapMyRun, MyFitnessPal legacy) from competitors
  3. Brand elevation strategy — if the pivot to premium positioning succeeds, gross margin expansion from higher ASPs could restore profitability without requiring revenue growth
Key Risks
  1. Revenue decline has not bottomed — FY2026 showed another 3.8% decline after FY2025's 9.4% drop, and continued market share losses to On Running, Hoka, and Nike could extend the downturn
  2. Balance sheet stress with D/E of 137% and negative profit margins limits strategic flexibility and capacity to invest in the brand elevation and DTC transformation needed for turnaround
  3. Leadership instability — Kevin Plank's micromanagement style and C- employee ratings suggest cultural challenges that could undermine the organizational change required for a successful transformation
