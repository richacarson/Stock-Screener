Company Profile
Basic Materials ·Other Industrial Metals & Mining ·NYSE ·United States
United States Antimony Corporation produces and sells antimony, zeolite, and precious metals in the United States and Canada. It operates the only two antimony smelters in North America and positions itself as the first fully integrated antimony producer outside China. Products include antimony trioxide (flame retardants), antimony metal (batteries, ordnance), and antimony trisulfide (ammunition primers). Also produces zeolite for agricultural and industrial applications. Founded in 1968, headquartered in Dallas, Texas.

Investment Thesis
United States Antimony is a rare critical minerals play offering monopoly positioning as the only fully integrated antimony producer outside China and Russia. Revenue surged 163% to $39.3M in FY2025 driven by a 230% spike in antimony prices from Chinese export curbs, and the company guides for $125M in FY2026 revenue backed by a $354M contract backlog including a $248M DoD contract [1]. The company has virtually no debt (D/E of 0.17%), significant insider buying from CEO Evans ($900K+), and operates in a critical minerals category with bipartisan political support and direct national security relevance [4]. The Montana Stibnite Hill mine restart and $27M Department of War critical minerals award provide additional growth catalysts [2][3]. At the current $1.4B market cap, UAMY is priced for significant execution on the $125M revenue target.
The bear case centers on execution risk and valuation for what remains a 100-employee micro-cap mining operation attempting to scale revenue 3x in a single year. Forward P/E of 73.6 prices in flawless execution on contracts that have not yet been fully delivered [5]. The company remains unprofitable with -11.1% profit margin and -5.1% ROE, meaning profitability at scale is unproven. The CFO departure in May 2026 creates governance risk at a critical growth inflection point [2]. Antimony prices are elevated due to Chinese export restrictions that could be reversed through trade negotiations — any normalization of Chinese supply would collapse pricing and the revenue thesis. The company's 58-year history prior to 2024 was characterized by marginal operations and minimal revenue, raising questions about whether management can execute at the scale the backlog demands. Environmental compliance at the Montana mine site requires ongoing regulatory management that could delay production.
Key Catalysts
  1. FY2026 revenue tracking toward $125M guidance and first profitable quarter demonstrating operational scalability from $39.3M FY2025 base
  2. Department of Defense $248M contract deliveries beginning to generate meaningful revenue, validating the company's strategic supplier position for US national security
  3. Montana Stibnite Hill mine reaching full production capacity, providing vertically integrated domestic ore supply that eliminates reliance on imported feedstock
Key Risks
  1. Execution risk scaling from $39.3M to $125M revenue with only 100 employees — the company has never operated at this scale, and the CFO departure creates leadership gaps at a critical inflection point
  2. Antimony price collapse if Chinese export restrictions are reversed through trade negotiations, eliminating the 230% price premium that drove FY2025 revenue growth
  3. Forward P/E of 73.6 on a still-unprofitable company creates extreme valuation risk — any miss on revenue guidance or contract delivery could trigger severe multiple compression from the $1.4B market cap
