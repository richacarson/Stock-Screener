Company Profile
Basic Materials ·Other Industrial Metals & Mining ·NYSE ·United States
United States Antimony Corporation produces and sells antimony, zeolite, and precious metals in the United States and Canada. The company owns and operates the only two antimony smelters in North America, producing antimony trioxide for flame retardants, antimony metal for batteries and ordnance, and antimony trisulfide for ammunition primers.

Investment Thesis
USAC holds a unique strategic asset as the sole North American antimony smelter at a time when China's export ban has created critical supply chain urgency, securing a $245M DLA sole-source IDIQ contract and $354M in multi-year commercial contracts. Revenue surged 163% to $39.3M in FY2025 with guidance of $125M for FY2026, and the nearly debt-free balance sheet (D/E 0.15%) provides financial flexibility for the capacity buildout [1]. The company occupies a monopoly position in an increasingly strategic mineral essential for defense (ammunition), energy (batteries, solar panels), and technology (flame retardants), with government policy tailwinds from critical minerals legislation and tariff protection.
The bear case is significant: USAC has never been profitable at scale, posting net losses every year from 2023-2025 with negative ROE of -19.7% and profit margins of -41.4%, and Q1 2026 revenue of $6.8M represents only 5.4% of the $125M full-year guidance, requiring extraordinary back-half acceleration [1]. The company has only 100 employees attempting to scale from $39M to $125M in revenue — a 3.2x increase — which introduces severe operational and execution risk. The stock at $8.33 trades at 24.5x forward earnings on an EPS estimate of $0.34 that has never been achieved, and management's interim CFO situation raises governance concerns. If antimony prices normalize or China lifts export restrictions, the strategic premium evaporates [1][2].
Key Catalysts
  1. Execution on $125M FY2026 revenue guidance driven by $245M DLA sole-source contract and $354M in signed multi-year commercial antimony contracts
  2. Expansion of Montana mining operations and Thompson Falls smelter capacity to meet domestic antimony demand from defense and industrial customers
  3. Continued U.S. critical minerals policy support including tariff protection, stockpile replenishment programs, and potential additional government investment in domestic supply chains
Key Risks
  1. Severe execution risk in scaling from 100 employees and $39M revenue to $125M guidance — Q1 2026 revenue of $6.8M requires extraordinary back-half acceleration
  2. Chronic unprofitability with net losses in FY2023-2025 and negative ROE of -19.7% raises doubt about the company's ability to achieve sustained profitability even at higher revenue
  3. Geopolitical dependency — if China lifts antimony export restrictions or global supply normalizes, USAC's strategic premium and pricing power deteriorate rapidly
