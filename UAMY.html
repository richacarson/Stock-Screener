Company Profile
Basic Materials ·Other Industrial Metals & Mining ·NYSE ·United States
United States Antimony Corporation produces and sells antimony, zeolite, and precious metals in the United States and Canada. The company is the only primary antimony producer in the United States, operating the Thompson Falls smelter in Montana and the Madero smelter in Mexico. It holds a $245M Defense Logistics Agency contract and a $106.7M industrial supply contract, positioning it as a strategic critical mineral supplier.

Investment Thesis
UAMY holds a unique structural monopoly: the only U.S. permitted antimony processor with $351M in cumulative contracts ($245M DLA + $106.7M industrial) at a moment when China's export restrictions have made domestic supply strategically essential [3]. The company reiterated $125M full-year 2026 revenue guidance despite a weak Q1, with DLA contract deliveries and Montana ore processing expected to ramp in Q2-Q4 [1]. Virtually zero debt (D/E 0.15%) provides balance sheet flexibility, and the hydromet facility targeting 1,000 tons/month by 2028 represents next-generation production capacity.
The bear case is severe execution risk: Q1 revenue of $6.8M versus $15.1M consensus with an $11.3M net loss means the company must deliver ~$118M in the final three quarters of 2026 to hit guidance — a 17x acceleration that has never been achieved [1]. Gross margin collapsed to 16% from 34%, operating expenses surged from expansion investment, and zero DLA/Montana revenue materialized in Q1. At forward P/E of 61.5x on speculative $0.14 EPS, the stock prices in near-flawless execution that has not yet been demonstrated. Antimony price normalization if Chinese export controls ease would destroy the revenue thesis. A 100-person company attempting to deliver $125M in revenue faces extraordinary organizational scaling risk.
Key Catalysts
  1. DLA contract revenue commencing in Q2-Q3 2026, validating the $245M backlog and demonstrating the company can deliver government-spec antimony ingots at scale
  2. Thompson Falls smelter expansion completing and ramping production — required to achieve the 400% production increase underpinning $125M guidance
  3. Idaho hydrometallurgical facility permitting progress, de-risking the 1,000 tons/month 99.9% purity production target for 2028
Key Risks
  1. Massive execution gap: achieving $118M in Q2-Q4 after $6.8M Q1 requires operational ramp unprecedented for a 100-person company with no prior quarter above $10M
  2. Antimony price normalization risk if Chinese export controls are eased or global supply responds, collapsing the pricing premium driving revenue projections
  3. Organizational scaling risk: managing a Thompson Falls expansion, Stibnite Hill mine reopening, Mexico smelter restart, and Idaho hydromet development simultaneously with 100 employees
