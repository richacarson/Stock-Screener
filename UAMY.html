Company Profile
Basic Materials ·Other Industrial Metals & Mining ·NYSE ·United States
United States Antimony Corporation is the only significant antimony producer in the United States, vertically integrated across mining, milling, smelting, and sales. Produces antimony trioxide (flame retardants), antimony metal (batteries, ordnance), and antimony trisulfide (ammunition primers). Also operates a zeolite segment for soil amendment and water filtration. Designated as a critical minerals producer by the U.S. government. Founded in 1968, headquartered in Dallas, Texas, with a market cap of approximately $1.05B.

Investment Thesis
UAMY offers a unique critical minerals play as the only significant domestic antimony producer, backed by a $245M Pentagon contract and $27M DoD direct investment that validate the company's strategic importance [2]. Revenue grew 163% in 2025 to $39.3M, and management has reaffirmed ambitious $125M revenue guidance for 2026 as the Stibnite Hill mine reopens and the new 9-furnace smelter comes online [1][3]. The balance sheet is virtually debt-free (D/E 0.15%), and CEO Evans's $900K+ in personal open-market purchases signal strong insider conviction [4]. With China restricting antimony exports and the U.S. government prioritizing domestic critical mineral production, UAMY occupies a monopoly position in a market with powerful secular tailwinds.
The bear case is significant execution risk at scale. Q1 2026 revenue of $6.8M (down from $7.0M YoY) with gross margin compressing to 16% from 34% suggests the $125M annual target requires extraordinary acceleration — roughly $30M/quarter for the remaining three quarters versus $6.8M in Q1 [1]. The company is deeply unprofitable with -41.4% profit margin, -19.7% ROE, and -$62.3M in negative free cash flow, meaning the expansion is consuming cash faster than the business generates it [4]. With only 100 employees, the organizational capacity to scale 3x in revenue within a single year is unproven. The stock trades at $1.05B market cap on $39M TTM revenue (27x sales), implying the market has already priced in the $125M target and beyond. If antimony prices decline, China loosens export restrictions, or the Pentagon contracts underperform, the valuation has significant downside risk.
Key Catalysts
  1. Stibnite Hill mine production ramp and new 9-furnace smelter commissioning driving sequential revenue acceleration toward the $125M 2026 target
  2. Additional Pentagon or Department of Defense critical minerals contracts that expand UAMY's government revenue base beyond the existing $245M five-year agreement
  3. Antimony price appreciation driven by continued Chinese export restrictions and growing demand from defense, electronics flame retardants, and battery applications
Key Risks
  1. Q1 2026 revenue miss ($6.8M vs $7.0M YoY) and gross margin compression to 16% raise serious execution risk on the $125M full-year target — achieving it requires ~4.4x the Q1 run rate
  2. Deeply negative profitability (-41.4% margin, -$62.3M FCF) means the company is burning cash rapidly on expansion and may need additional capital raises that dilute shareholders at the $1.05B market cap
  3. China loosening antimony export restrictions or antimony prices declining would undermine the entire thesis — UAMY's monopoly value depends on geopolitical conditions that could shift unpredictably
