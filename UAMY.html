Investment Thesis
UAMY holds a unique structural advantage: it is the only U.S. primary antimony producer at a moment when China's export restrictions have driven antimony prices 400%+ to historic highs [1]. The $248M sole-source U.S. government contract, $354M total 2025 contracts, and 2026 guidance of $125M (3.2× revenue growth) reflect genuine demand that cannot be redirected to non-U.S. suppliers. With $91.3M in cash, virtually zero debt, and the Thompson Falls expansion completing in May 2026, UAMY has the balance sheet to execute on the growth plan.
The bear case is execution and price sustainability. The company remains unprofitable ($4.34M net loss in 2025 despite 163% revenue growth), and the Thompson Falls expansion delay to May 2026 (from earlier guidance) signals operational risk [1]. Antimony prices have already moderated from mid-2025 peaks — prices tripled but are no longer at maximum — and could continue normalizing if supply increases or demand weakens. At $8.16/share with $91.3M in cash and a $125M revenue guidance, the stock trades at a reasonable but not cheap premium to its asset value. The mining business inherently carries environmental, permitting, and operational risks.
Key Catalysts
  1. Thompson Falls smelter expansion completing in May 2026 — would double capacity and enable the $125M revenue guidance to be achieved
  2. Idaho hydrometallurgical facility permits and construction progress — would provide a second major production facility and reduce dependence on imported antimony feed
  3. Additional government contracts beyond the $248M sole-source award — expanding the government customer base would diversify revenue and improve pricing visibility
Key Risks
  1. Thompson Falls expansion already delayed once to May 2026; any further delay would push the $125M revenue guidance into 2027 and increase cash burn
  2. Antimony price normalization: Chinese export restrictions could be lifted or partially reversed, restoring supply and collapsing the price premium that drives UAMY's profitability
  3. Small company execution risk: at 150 employees managing a $91M capital base with ambitious expansion plans, UAMY has limited organizational depth for complex multi-site mining operations
