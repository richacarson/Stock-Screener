Company Profile
Industrials ·Aerospace & Defense ·NYSE American ·United States
AgEagle Aerial Systems, Inc. (doing business as EagleNXT) designs and delivers autonomous uncrewed aerial systems (UAS) and sensors for commercial and government applications. Products include senseFly eBee fixed-wing drones (the #1 most-used conventional fixed-wing drone in the US per FAA data), MicaSense multispectral cameras, and Ground Control cloud-based operating platform. Serves agriculture, military/defense, public safety, surveying/mapping, and utilities industries. Founded in 2010, headquartered in Wichita, Kansas.
59 Employees ·www.ageagle.com

Investment Thesis
AgEagle Aerial Systems offers speculative exposure to the growing defense and public safety drone market through its eBee platform — the #1 most-used conventional fixed-wing drone in the US per FAA data — and an integrated sensor+software ecosystem from the senseFly and MicaSense acquisitions [1][5]. The Q2 2025 quarter demonstrated the business model's potential, with drone sales surging 92% to $2.9M, gross margins reaching 55.7%, and net income turning positive at $5.78M [1]. CEO Irby's AI integration strategy for autonomous flight and ISR applications positions the company in a high-growth defense drone market benefiting from NDAA compliance requirements as the US military phases out Chinese-made drones [3]. At $0.82 per share with ~$48M market cap and $29.9M in cash (up 730% from capital raises), the stock offers high-risk optionality on defense contract wins and revenue inflection.
The bear case is multi-dimensional and severe. FY2025 revenue of $12.81M declined 4.3% YoY, and Q1 2026 collapsed to $1.40M (-61.6% YoY), suggesting the Q2 2025 turnaround was an anomaly rather than a trend [2][5]. The company remains deeply unprofitable with a -102.3% profit margin on $12.81M FY2025 revenue and a cumulative net loss of -$13.1M for the year. ROE is not meaningful due to equity swinging from -$5.7M to +$4.2M via dilutive financing. The 2021 Bonitas Research pump-and-dump allegations, while involving prior management, created lasting reputational damage and reflect governance weaknesses in the company's history [6]. With only 59 employees and near-complete executive team replacement in 18 months (new CEO, CFO, VP Sales, board members), organizational stability is fragile [4]. The competitive landscape is brutal — DJI dominates commercial drones, Skydio has substantial AI-powered military drone funding, and AeroVironment's systems are battle-proven — leaving limited room for a micro-cap company to win meaningful defense contracts. Liquidity passes the $1M floor by only $373K, creating execution risk if volume declines.
