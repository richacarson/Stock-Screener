Company Profile
Consumer Defensive ·Education & Training Services ·NASDAQ ·United States
Udemy operates a global online learning marketplace with over 250,000 courses across technology, business, and personal development. The platform serves 85 million learners and offers Udemy Business, a subscription-based enterprise solution. In December 2025, Coursera agreed to acquire Udemy for ~$930M in equity, creating a combined entity valued at $2.5B.

Investment Thesis
Udemy offers a compelling near-term valuation story driven by the pending Coursera acquisition at ~$930M in equity. FY2025 results showed improving profitability — Adjusted EBITDA margin expanded 700bps to 12% ($95.3M), operating cash flow reached $108.7M, and free cash flow hit $78.5M on $790M revenue [1]. The enterprise business (Udemy Business) grew subscription revenue 8% YoY to $566M, representing 72% of total revenue, with $13M net new ARR added in Q4 [1]. At $5.14/share with a forward P/E of 8.3x and $359M in cash, downside is limited by the acquisition floor. The combined Coursera-Udemy entity would serve 180M+ learners with both credentials and broad skills training, creating the largest online learning platform globally [3].
The bear case centers on execution risk around the merger and structural AI disruption. The Coursera deal remains subject to shareholder and regulatory approval, and integration of two historically unprofitable edtech platforms carries significant risk — combined losses could pressure the merged entity [3]. Revenue declined 3% YoY in Q4 2025 as the consumer segment continues to shrink, and without the merger, Udemy's standalone growth trajectory is decelerating [1]. AI disruption is the existential risk: free AI tutors from OpenAI, Google, and Khan Academy threaten to commoditize the core value proposition of pre-recorded video instruction. Two layoff rounds (30% cumulative workforce reduction) have damaged culture and institutional knowledge [5]. If the merger fails, Udemy faces the difficult reality of a sub-$1B market cap edtech platform competing against well-funded AI-native education tools.
Key Catalysts
  1. Coursera-Udemy merger expected to close mid-2026, creating a $2.5B combined online learning platform with 180M+ learners and combined credential + skills offerings [3]
  2. Enterprise subscription revenue growing 8% YoY with expanding EBITDA margins (700bps improvement to 12% in FY2025), demonstrating path to sustained profitability [1]
  3. AI skills training is among the highest-demand course categories on the platform, positioning Udemy to benefit from the corporate AI upskilling wave [2]
Key Risks
  1. AI disruption — free AI tutors from ChatGPT, Gemini, and Khanmigo threaten to commoditize pre-recorded video instruction, Udemy's core product [2]
  2. Merger execution risk — Coursera acquisition requires shareholder and regulatory approval; integration of two historically unprofitable platforms carries significant operational risk [3]
  3. Consumer segment decline — total revenue fell 3% YoY in Q4 2025 as individual learner demand shifts to free and AI-powered alternatives, pressuring the marketplace model [1]

AI Resilience (25%)
AI Resilience MODERATE
5
AI Risk Protection (4/9 — EXPOSED): Udemy's core business of aggregating and distributing human-created educational content faces meaningful AI disruption risk. AI tutors (ChatGPT, Google Gemini, Khan Academy's Khanmigo) can provide personalized, on-demand instruction that competes directly with pre-recorded video courses [2]. The marketplace model — connecting independent instructors with learners — could be disintermediated as AI generates increasingly high-quality educational content. However, Udemy Business enterprise subscriptions (72% of revenue) have higher switching costs and compliance/credential requirements that AI cannot easily replicate [1]. The pending Coursera merger adds accredited credentials as a defensive moat. AI Opportunity (1/1): Udemy generates AI-related revenue through AI skills training courses (among the highest-demand categories on the platform), AI transformation packages for enterprises, and the Udemy AI Assistant that enhances learner engagement [2].
