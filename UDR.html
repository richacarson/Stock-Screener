Company Profile
Real Estate ·REIT - Residential ·NYSE ·United States
UDR, Inc. is an S&P 500 multifamily REIT that owns or has an ownership position in 60,941 apartment homes across targeted U.S. markets. Founded in 1972, UDR has a 53+ year operating history and has delivered an average annual shareholder return of 10% under current CEO Tom Toomey. The company focuses on acquiring, developing, and managing apartment communities in high-barrier coastal and select Sunbelt markets.

Investment Thesis
UDR is a blue-chip multifamily REIT with a 53-year track record, 5.15% dividend yield, and S&P 500 membership. FY2025 FFO/share of $2.43 with 2026 guidance of $2.47-2.57 (1.6-5.8% growth) and a 1.2% dividend increase demonstrate steady cash generation from 60,941 apartment homes [1]. The company's 20% employee turnover (vs. 34% industry average) and AI-driven operational efficiencies — including 65% reduction in call management costs and 13% improvement in prospect conversion — create a durable competitive advantage in property management [2]. At $34.24/share with a 5.15% yield, UDR offers reliable income with potential for mid-single-digit total returns as same-store revenue growth of 0.25-2.25% and renewal rent increases of 5-6% drive NOI expansion [1].
The bear case centers on slow growth, high leverage, and macro uncertainty. Revenue grew only 1.5% YoY and the 3-year revenue CAGR of ~5% reflects a mature business in a supply-heavy apartment market [4]. D/E of 144.7% creates interest rate sensitivity — while $801M in 2026 debt repayments will deleverage, rising rates could pressure refinancing costs. Forward P/E of 62.3x on GAAP earnings is at extreme levels (though FFO-based valuation is more favorable). Sunbelt supply pressure from new apartment deliveries could constrain lease rate growth in key markets like Dallas, Nashville, and Denver [1]. Muted job growth expectations for 2026, consumer confidence at decade lows, and regulatory risk from potential rent regulation in coastal markets create demand headwinds. The planned ~$700M in asset sales could indicate management's view that current pricing is favorable for sellers, not buyers [1].
Key Catalysts
  1. Renewal rent growth of 5-6% in H1 2026 driving same-store revenue growth toward the upper end of 0.25-2.25% guidance range [1]
  2. Debt reduction of $801M in 2026 deleveraging the balance sheet and reducing interest rate sensitivity [1]
  3. AI-driven operational efficiency gains — ACE Virtual Leasing, predictive maintenance, and PropTech partnerships contributing incremental NOI above market-level growth [2]
Key Risks
  1. Sunbelt apartment supply wave — elevated new deliveries in key markets could pressure occupancy and rent growth below the low end of same-store revenue guidance [1]
  2. Interest rate sensitivity — D/E of 144.7% and $6B total debt create refinancing risk if rates remain elevated, compressing the spread between cap rates and borrowing costs [4]
  3. Consumer demand softening — decade-low consumer confidence, muted job growth expectations, and potential immigration restrictions could reduce renter demand in UDR's target markets [1]
