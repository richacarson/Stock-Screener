Uniti Group is a fiber-focused digital infrastructure REIT operating across 40 states with 125,000+ route miles and nearly 7 million strand miles of fiber. Following the August 2025 merger with Windstream, Uniti operates through three segments: Kinetic (consumer/enterprise broadband), Fiber Infrastructure (wholesale dark fiber and lit services), and Uniti Solutions (managed network services).

Investment Thesis
Uniti Group is a transformative fiber infrastructure play positioned at the intersection of two powerful secular trends: rural broadband expansion and AI-driven hyperscale data center demand. The August 2025 Windstream merger created a vertically integrated fiber operator with 125,000+ route miles across 40 states, projected to generate $3.6-3.65B revenue and $1.43-1.48B EBITDA in 2026 [1]. Kinetic Consumer Fiber is the growth engine — 24% Q4 revenue growth with record subscriber additions and a path to 2.3-2.35M fiber homes passed by year-end 2026 [1]. The $500M+ dark fiber contract for AI hubs validates Uniti's infrastructure as essential AI backbone [2]. Management projects fiber will exceed 50% of revenue by Q4 2026 and 75% by 2029, completing the transition from legacy copper-dependent REIT to pure-play fiber platform. At $11.06/share with a $2.6B market cap vs. $1.45B midpoint EBITDA, the stock trades at under 2x EBITDA — a fraction of fiber infrastructure peer valuations [1].
The bear case is dominated by one factor: extreme leverage. D/E of 2651% with $10.1B in debt against a $2.6B market cap creates a highly levered capital structure where modest operational setbacks could cause equity value destruction [3]. Forward EPS of -$0.71 means the company is expected to report GAAP losses in 2026 due to merger-related amortization, interest expense, and integration costs. The dividend was suspended in 2024 and there is no visibility on reinstatement. The 212.7% revenue growth is entirely merger-driven — pre-merger organic growth was approximately 3%, and Kinetic's legacy copper business is in secular decline, offsetting fiber growth [1]. The Windstream integration carries significant execution risk: combining two organizations with a decade of adversarial legal history, rationalizing overlapping networks, and migrating copper subscribers to fiber all require flawless execution. Interest expense on $10.1B of debt consumes the vast majority of EBITDA, leaving minimal free cash flow for equity holders ($-226M FCF in 2025). If interest rates remain elevated, refinancing risk could become existential [3].
Key Catalysts
  1. Fiber revenue crossover to 50%+ of total by Q4 2026, with 75% target by 2029, completing the transformation from legacy copper REIT to pure-play fiber infrastructure platform [1]
  2. AI infrastructure demand — $500M+ dark fiber contract for AI hubs and hyperscale data centers with 1,100-mile network expansion, plus growing pipeline of similar AI-driven wholesale opportunities [2]
  3. Kinetic consumer fiber acceleration — 24% revenue growth, record gross adds of 38,000/quarter, and 2.3-2.35M homes passed by year-end 2026 driving subscriber and ARPU growth [1]
Key Risks
  1. Extreme leverage — $10.1B debt (D/E 2651%) with negative free cash flow (-$226M) and forward GAAP losses (EPS -$0.71); rising interest rates or inability to refinance could be existential [3]
  2. Windstream integration execution risk — merging organizations with adversarial legal history, rationalizing networks, and managing copper-to-fiber migration across 1M+ subscribers requires flawless execution [1]
  3. Legacy copper decline — Kinetic's copper broadband base is in secular decline as subscribers churn to fiber or competitors; the speed of copper revenue erosion could outpace fiber revenue ramp [1]
