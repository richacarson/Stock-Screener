Investment Thesis
Union Pacific is an essential American infrastructure company with natural monopoly characteristics — one of two western Class I railroads with 32,000+ miles of track serving 23 states [1]. Record 2025 operations with $11.98 EPS (+8%), 29.1% profit margins, and 40.4% ROE demonstrate operational excellence. Forward P/E of 18.13 and 2.24% dividend yield (growing at 8% 5yr CAGR with 45.4% payout ratio) make it attractively valued. Mid single-digit EPS growth guidance for 2026 provides steady compound returns [1].
The bear case includes flat revenue growth (-0.6% YoY) suggesting volume headwinds, high D/E of 177.7%, and economic sensitivity. Railroad volumes correlate with industrial production and consumer spending [1]. Rail inflation above 4% in 2026 could pressure customers to seek trucking alternatives for shorter hauls. The stock's premium valuation assumes continued margin expansion which has a natural ceiling. Regulatory risk from potential new railroad safety requirements could increase costs [1].
