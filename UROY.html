Company Profile
Energy ·Uranium ·NASDAQ ·Canada
Uranium Royalty Corp. is the first and only publicly listed pure-play uranium royalty company. The company acquires and manages a geographically diversified portfolio of uranium royalties and interests, invests in companies with uranium exposure, and engages in the purchase and sale of physical uranium. Incorporated in 2017 and headquartered in Vancouver, Canada.
14 Employees ·www.uraniumroyalty.com

Investment Thesis
Uranium Royalty Corp. offers unique exposure to the nuclear energy renaissance as the only publicly listed pure-play uranium royalty company. The company holds $138.8M in cash and uranium assets with virtually zero debt (D/E of 0.04%), providing a clean balance sheet to fund royalty acquisitions and physical uranium purchases [4]. CEO Scott Melbye's 40+ years of nuclear industry experience and active deal sourcing position the company to capitalize on the growing pipeline of uranium projects driven by AI data center power demand and the global shift toward nuclear energy [2]. The royalty model provides operating leverage — rising uranium prices and production volumes flow directly to revenue without the cost inflation risk borne by miners. Interim results through January 2026 showed higher cash, assets, and uranium sales profits [1].
The bear case reflects the company's early-stage nature and valuation challenges. Revenue is extremely lumpy — quarterly figures ranged from $0 to $33.2M in the past year, making financial analysis unreliable [4]. Forward P/E of 1,013x and trailing P/E of 121x reflect minimal current earnings, and the company generated just $0.03 in trailing EPS. Analysts forecast negative revenue growth of -9.7% for UROY, underperforming the uranium industry's average forecast growth of 42% [1]. ROE of 1.3% and profit margin of 8% demonstrate the royalty portfolio is not yet generating meaningful returns on the capital deployed. With only 14 employees, the company is essentially a small investment vehicle rather than a scalable operating business, and the $533M market cap may overvalue the current royalty portfolio relative to the actual cash flows it generates. Uranium prices are cyclical and subject to geopolitical disruption, regulatory changes in nuclear policy, and competition from alternative energy sources including advanced solar and energy storage [4].
Key Catalysts
  1. Nuclear energy renaissance driven by AI data center power demand — hyperscalers contracting for nuclear power directly increases the value of uranium royalty streams and physical uranium inventory [2]
  2. New royalty acquisitions — CEO Melbye actively sourcing deals from miners and developers, with the clean balance sheet ($138.8M assets, near-zero debt) providing capacity for portfolio expansion [2]

Key Risks
  1. Revenue lumpiness and earnings uncertainty — quarterly revenue ranged from $0 to $33.2M, making financial modeling unreliable, with forward EPS of just $0.004 and trailing P/E of 121x [4]
  2. Uranium price cyclicality — the royalty model amplifies both upside and downside of uranium prices, and a nuclear energy policy reversal or uranium price collapse would severely impair the business [1]
  3. Early-stage concentration risk — 14 employees, limited royalty portfolio diversification, and dependence on a small number of uranium projects for revenue generation [2]
