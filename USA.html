Liberty All-Star Equity Fund is a closed-end equity mutual fund that employs a multi-manager approach, allocating assets equally among five independent investment management firms with distinct value and growth styles. Managed by ALPS Advisors, the fund targets approximately 10% annual distributions based on NAV.

Investment Thesis
Liberty All-Star Equity Fund offers a ~10% distribution yield through a multi-manager closed-end fund structure that has operated since 1986. The fund provides diversified large-cap equity exposure through five sub-advisors spanning value and growth styles, with top holdings in mega-cap technology names (NVIDIA, Microsoft, Alphabet).
The fund currently trades at a ~9% discount to NAV, which could narrow if market sentiment improves or activist investors push for structural changes. However, the persistent discount reflects the market's assessment that a 1%+ expense ratio plus sub-advisory fees is excessive for equity exposure available through near-zero-cost index ETFs. The distribution policy is NAV-linked (not earnings-linked), meaning payouts can include return of capital in weak markets, eroding NAV over time. Suitable primarily for income-focused investors who value monthly distributions over total return optimization.
Key Catalysts
  1. NAV discount narrowing from current 9% toward par if equity markets rally and closed-end fund sentiment improves
  2. Strong 2026 equity market performance would increase NAV-based distributions automatically under the 10% policy
  3. Potential sub-advisor changes could inject fresh alpha generation into underperforming style buckets
Key Risks
  1. Persistent NAV discount may widen further as low-cost ETF alternatives continue gaining market share
  2. Distribution includes return of capital in weak years, gradually eroding underlying NAV per share
  3. Earnings declined 34% YoY in 2025 despite revenue growth, indicating investment losses in the portfolio
