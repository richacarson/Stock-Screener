The company develops the USBC deposit token, a blockchain-based tokenized deposit embedded with digital identity, and maintains a Bitcoin treasury strategy. Investment Thesis
USBC, Inc. is a speculative micro-cap ($145M market cap) that underwent a complete corporate transformation in August 2025 when Greg Kidd's Goldeneye 1995 LLC acquired a controlling interest for $125M, renaming Know Labs to USBC and pivoting from health monitoring to digital financial services [1][2]. The company is developing the USBC deposit token — a blockchain-based tokenized deposit with digital identity — and has adopted a Bitcoin treasury strategy. Greg Kidd's credentials as former Ripple Chief Risk Officer provide credibility in the digital assets space, and the company's low D/E of 0.35% suggests manageable debt [5]. The stock has declined 93% from its 52-week high of $5.36 to $0.37.
The bear case is overwhelming for a fundamental investor. USBC generates zero revenue — down from $4.4M when it was Know Labs in FY2022 — and lost $22.1M in FY2025 with only 11 employees [5]. The company has no commercial product, no customers, and no revenue visibility in its new fintech direction. The liquidity floor test fails ($426K daily dollar volume vs. $1M minimum), making the stock unsuitable for institutional investment. R&D spending collapsed 71% from $6.1M to $1.8M, suggesting the company is not investing meaningfully in technology development [5]. The Bitcoin treasury strategy introduces cryptocurrency volatility into an already speculative equity. Share dilution is extreme — diluted shares outstanding grew from ~1M (FY2022) to 63M (FY2025). The $125M acquisition price appears disconnected from any fundamental value the company generates. This is a speculative vehicle, not an operating business.
Key Catalysts
  1. Launch of the USBC deposit token as a commercial product, generating first revenue from the new fintech strategy and validating the $125M acquisition thesis [2]
  2. Bitcoin treasury appreciation — if Bitcoin prices rise, USBC's treasury reserves could increase in value and fund operations [2]
  3. Greg Kidd's industry connections from Ripple could accelerate partnerships with established financial institutions for token distribution [1]
Key Risks
  1. Zero revenue with $22.1M annual losses and only 11 employees — the company is a pre-revenue speculative vehicle with no proven business model in its new fintech direction [5]
  2. Liquidity floor failure at $426K daily dollar volume — the stock is too illiquid for institutional investment and subject to volatile price swings on minimal trading activity [5]
  3. Extreme dilution risk — shares outstanding grew from ~1M to 63M in three years, and continued cash burn will likely require additional equity issuance, further diluting shareholders [5]
