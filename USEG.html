Company Profile
Energy ·Oil & Gas E&P ·NASDAQ ·United States
U.S. Energy Corp. is an independent energy company transitioning from conventional oil and gas operations to an integrated industrial gas, energy, and carbon management platform. The company owns and operates the Big Sky Carbon Hub and Cut Bank oil field in Montana, targeting helium sales, carbon management, and CO2-enhanced oil recovery.

Investment Thesis
U.S. Energy Corp. is a speculative micro-cap undergoing a risky transformation from declining conventional oil and gas operations to an integrated helium, carbon management, and CO2-EOR platform. The company controls 1.3 BCF of certified helium and 444 BCF of CO2 resources in Montana, but first helium sales are not expected until early 2027 and commercial viability remains unproven [1].
The bear case is dominant: FY2025 revenue collapsed 65% to $6.8M, the net loss was $14.4M, profit margins are deeply negative at -211%, and the March 2026 dilutive offering at $1.00/share signals ongoing capital needs [1][3]. At a $50M market cap with $575K in cash pre-offering, this is a survival-mode company where the risk of permanent capital loss is significant. Only suitable for highly speculative investors willing to bet on unproven helium and CCUS commercialization.
