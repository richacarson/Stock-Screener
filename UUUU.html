Company Profile
Energy ·Uranium ·NYSE American ·United States
Energy Fuels Inc. is the largest U.S. uranium producer and a growing rare earth element (REE) processor, operating the White Mesa Mill in Utah — the only conventional uranium mill in the United States. The company produces uranium concentrate, vanadium as a co-product, and is developing a fully integrated REE supply chain. Operates through Uranium, REE, and HMS segments. Market cap ~$4.9B.

Investment Thesis
Energy Fuels holds a structurally irreplaceable position in the U.S. critical minerals supply chain — operating the only conventional uranium mill in America while building a fully integrated REE processing platform with $3.7B estimated NPV across six projects [1][3]. Q1 2026 revenue surged 112% YoY to $35.8M driven by uranium concentrate sales, with net loss narrowing significantly from $26.3M to $11.0M. AI data center power buildout is accelerating nuclear energy demand, creating a secular tailwind for uranium producers. The company's $927M+ working capital provides substantial runway to fund the dual uranium/REE growth strategy. New CEO Ross Bhappu brings 35 years of mining and private equity experience to scale the business.
The bear case centers on commodity price risk and execution uncertainty. Trailing twelve-month revenue declined 15.6% to $65.9M despite the Q1 2026 rebound, and the company still operates at a GAAP net loss with -82.7% profit margin [4]. The CEO transition from Chalmers to Bhappu in April 2026 comes at a critical scaling moment for both uranium production and REE processing, introducing execution risk [2]. The REE business remains pre-commercial in terms of meaningful revenue, and the $430M capital estimate for Lone Tree Plant Phase 1 represents significant capex. At $4.9B market cap on $66M trailing revenue (~74x sales), the stock prices in substantial execution on both uranium and REE strategies. Environmental regulatory risks for uranium mining operations and political opposition in the Southwest could delay permitting.
