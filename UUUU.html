Company Profile
Energy ·Uranium ·AMEX ·United States
Energy Fuels Inc. is the largest uranium producer in the United States, operating the White Mesa Mill — the only conventional uranium mill and monazite-to-separated-rare-earth-oxide processing facility in the country. The company also produces vanadium, rare earth elements, and heavy mineral sands (ilmenite, rutile, zircon). Founded in 1987, headquartered in Lakewood, Colorado.

Investment Thesis
Energy Fuels is uniquely positioned as the largest US uranium producer at a time when nuclear energy demand is surging — AI data centers require massive baseload electricity, the DOE is promoting nuclear power, and uranium prices have risen from $30/lb to $70+ [1]. Q1 2026 delivered $35.7M revenue (112% YoY growth) from 510,000 lbs U3O8 at $70.04/lb, with full-year guidance of 1.5-2.0M lbs sold. The White Mesa Mill's monopoly as the only conventional uranium mill in the US and only licensed REE separation facility creates structural competitive advantages that cannot be replicated quickly. Diversification into rare earth elements and heavy mineral sands reduces single-commodity risk while leveraging existing infrastructure. With $911M cash vs $677M debt and the ASM acquisition expanding capacity, Energy Fuels has the financial flexibility to execute its multi-commodity strategy [1][4].
The investment carries significant commodity risk. Uranium prices are cyclical and have historically experienced multi-year downturns that render mining operations unprofitable — the 2016-2020 period saw prices below $25/lb and industry-wide shutdowns [1]. GAAP profitability remains deeply negative at -82.7% margin despite strong revenue growth, and Zacks estimates a -$0.14 EPS loss for 2026. Net insider selling of $8.36M against only $40.6K in purchases signals management concern about near-term valuation. The CEO transition from Chalmers to Bhappu introduces execution risk during a period of simultaneous uranium production ramp-up, rare earth processing expansion, and heavy mineral sands integration. Revenue is lumpy — uranium delivery schedules can shift quarter-to-quarter, and spot market conditions determine whether inventory is held or sold [3].
Key Catalysts
  1. AI data center electricity demand driving sustained uranium price increases above $70/lb, with DOE nuclear energy policy providing additional demand visibility for US-sourced uranium
  2. Rare earth element separation at White Mesa Mill achieving commercial-scale production, creating a domestic alternative to Chinese REE dominance and capturing government strategic material premiums
  3. Full-year 2026 uranium production of 2.0-2.5M lbs at sustained prices above $70/lb generating first positive GAAP earnings and validating the multi-commodity business model
Key Risks
  1. Uranium price cyclicality — a decline below $50/lb would render most US mining operations uneconomic and compress the $70.04/lb average selling price that drove Q1 2026 revenue
  2. CEO transition from Chalmers (50-year uranium veteran) to Bhappu creates execution risk across simultaneous uranium ramp-up, rare earth expansion, ASM integration, and HMS operations
  3. Net insider selling of $8.36M vs $40.6K in purchases over the past 12 months signals potential management concern about near-term valuation or commodity price outlook
