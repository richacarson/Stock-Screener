Company Profile
Energy ·Uranium / Critical Minerals ·NYSE American ·United States
Energy Fuels is the largest U.S. uranium producer and a growing rare earth element (REE) processor, operating the White Mesa Mill in Utah — the only conventional uranium mill in the United States. In 2025, the company mined 1.72 million pounds of U3O8, sold 650,000 pounds for $48.2M in uranium revenue, and exceeded guidance on all metrics. The company is building a fully integrated REE supply chain and acquired an Australian rare-earth producer for $299M in January 2026.

Investment Thesis
Energy Fuels holds a structurally irreplaceable position in the U.S. critical minerals supply chain — operating the only conventional uranium mill in America while building a fully integrated REE processing platform with $3.7B in estimated NPV across six projects [1]. The company exceeded 2025 uranium production guidance, mining 1.72M lbs of U3O8, and expects COGS to drop to $30-40/lb in Q1 2026 from higher levels, improving margins as production scales. AI data center power demand is accelerating nuclear energy adoption, creating a secular tailwind for uranium. The $299M Australian rare-earth acquisition provides international diversification and vertical REE integration [2].
The bear case centers on commodity price risk: uranium spot prices fell 13.8% in 2025, and the company posted a -$85.6M net loss on only $65.9M in total revenue [1]. Annual revenue actually declined 15.6% YoY despite production growth, reflecting the commodity pricing headwind. The $700M convertible notes (due 2031) add balance sheet leverage, and the $430M+ estimated REE capex requirements could necessitate additional dilutive financing. The Pinyon Plain mine near the Grand Canyon faces environmental scrutiny and permitting risk. The REE business remains pre-commercial in meaningful revenue terms, and China's dominance in rare earth processing creates competitive pricing pressure.
Key Catalysts
  1. Uranium spot price recovery toward $90-100/lb (currently ~$65/lb) would transform profitability given 2026 production cost targets of $23-30/lb, driving significant margin expansion
  2. REE commercial-scale production at White Mesa achieving first meaningful rare earth revenue would validate the critical minerals platform thesis and attract defense/government offtake agreements
  3. AI data center nuclear buildout announcements (e.g., new reactor contracts with major tech companies) would drive long-term uranium contract prices higher and increase Energy Fuels' contracted volume
Key Risks
  1. Uranium spot price remaining depressed below $70/lb, extending the net loss period and testing liquidity as operational cash burn continues through the production ramp
  2. REE capital requirements exceeding the $430M+ Lone Tree Plant estimate, requiring additional equity raises that dilute shareholders before the business reaches profitability
  3. Environmental and permitting delays at Pinyon Plain mine or White Mesa Mill due to political opposition or regulatory review, constraining production growth
