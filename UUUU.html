Company Profile
Energy ·Uranium ·NYSE American ·United States
Energy Fuels Inc. is the largest U.S. uranium producer and a growing rare earth element processor, operating the White Mesa Mill in Utah — the only conventional uranium mill in the United States. The company also produces vanadium, heavy mineral sands, and is developing a fully integrated domestic REE supply chain, positioning itself as a critical minerals company serving the U.S. energy security and clean energy transition.

Energy Fuels has executed a meaningful strategic pivot from pure-play uranium to critical minerals company, adding REE separation capabilities at White Mesa Mill with Phase 2 designed to boost both light and heavy REE production capacity [1]. Investment Thesis
Energy Fuels holds a structurally irreplaceable position in the U.S. critical minerals supply chain — operating the only conventional uranium mill in America while building a fully integrated REE processing platform [2]. Q1 2026 showed revenue more than doubling YoY to $35.8M with positive operating cash flow of $8.3M, while the $956.6M working capital war chest funds continued expansion [1]. Uranium demand is accelerating due to AI data center power requirements and nuclear energy revival, with Energy Fuels uniquely positioned as the dominant domestic producer.
The bear case centers on persistent GAAP losses (-83% profit margin) despite revenue growth, commodity price sensitivity (uranium spot prices remain volatile), and the REE business still being pre-commercial at meaningful scale. The $700M convertible notes due 2031 add balance sheet risk if the uranium/REE thesis takes longer than expected. Environmental opposition to White Mesa Mill operations and permitting delays for new mines represent ongoing regulatory headwinds. The WATCH recommendation reflects the company's unique strategic position offset by unproven profitability and commodity dependence [5].
Key Catalysts
  1. Phase 2 REE separation circuit achieving commercial-level heavy REE recovery, creating the first fully domestic U.S. rare earth supply chain and potential government offtake agreements [1]
  2. AI data center power buildout accelerating nuclear energy demand, driving long-term uranium contract prices higher and enabling profitable production at current cost structures [3]
  3. Donald Project JV in Australia advancing to construction, providing long-term monazite feedstock to White Mesa with exceptional light and heavy REE concentrations [1]
Key Risks
  1. Uranium and REE commodity prices remaining volatile or declining, extending the period of GAAP losses despite revenue growth [5]
  2. REE capital requirements exceeding estimates — Phase 2 development and potential Lone Tree Plant could require additional equity raises diluting shareholders
  3. Environmental and permitting challenges at White Mesa Mill, where community opposition to waste disposal and uranium processing operations creates regulatory risk [4]
