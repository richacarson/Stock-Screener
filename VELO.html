Company Profile

Velo3D produces metal additive 3D printing systems (Sapphire family) for aerospace, defense, space, and energy applications. The company offers end-to-end solutions including Flow print preparation software, Assure quality control, and Intelligent Fusion manufacturing processes. Operates as a subsidiary of Arrayed Additive Inc. following a 2024 restructuring.

Investment Thesis
Velo3D is a high-risk turnaround story in metal additive manufacturing, now 95% owned by Arrayed Additive following a 2024 debt-for-equity restructuring. Revenue grew 12% in 2025 to $46M with 3D printer sales up 54%, and 2026 guidance of $60-$70M suggests accelerating momentum [1]. Backlog grew to $31M as defense and aerospace customers move from testing to production adoption. New CEO Arun Jeldi targets positive EBITDA in H2 2026 and gross margins above 30% in the second half, which would represent a significant inflection from current -16.1% gross margins. The defense sector's growing reliance on additive manufacturing for rapid production of complex metal parts provides a secular tailwind [1].
The bear case is overwhelming for most investors. Gross margins are -16.1% including a $7M obsolete inventory write-down, the company burns $27.3M in annual operating cash flow on $46M revenue, and profit margin is -155% [4]. The 95% ownership transfer to Arrayed Additive effectively diluted prior shareholders to 5% equity, and the restructured capital table raises governance questions for minority holders. Revenue of $46M is 42% below the $78.7M 2022 peak, suggesting the turnaround has not yet recovered lost ground [1]. Cash of $39M barely exceeds debt of $40.2M, leaving minimal buffer if the turnaround stalls. Capital expenditure guidance of $40-$50M for 2026 exceeds the company's trailing revenue, requiring significant additional financing. The metal AM market remains competitive with larger, better-capitalized players (EOS, GE Additive, Nikon/SLM Solutions) [4].
Key Catalysts
  1. H2 2026 positive EBITDA target representing first-ever sustained profitability, with gross margins expected to exceed 30% in the second half [1]
  2. 2026 revenue guidance of $60-$70M (30-52% growth) driven by defense and aerospace production adoption and $31M backlog [1]
  3. Defense sector production scaling — customers moving from additive manufacturing testing to multi-system production deployments [1]
Key Risks
  1. Negative gross margins (-16.1%) and profit margin (-155%) with only $39M cash against $40.2M debt — the company may need additional financing if the H2 2026 EBITDA target is not met [4]
  2. 95% ownership dilution to Arrayed Additive from debt-for-equity restructuring — minority shareholders have limited governance influence and face continued dilution risk [2]
  3. Revenue 42% below the 2022 peak of $78.7M despite turnaround efforts — the addressable defense/aerospace additive manufacturing market may not be large enough to support the company's capital requirements [1][4]
