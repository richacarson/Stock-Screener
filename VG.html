Company Profile
Energy ·Oil & Gas Midstream ·NYSE ·United States
Venture Global is a U.S. LNG (liquefied natural gas) company operating two facilities on the Louisiana Gulf Coast: Calcasieu Pass (now operational) and Plaquemines LNG (commissioning). The company generated $13.8B in revenue in 2025 (+177% YoY) and Consolidated Adjusted EBITDA of $6.3B (+198% YoY), driven by record LNG cargo exports. The company completed its NYSE IPO in early 2025 and is constructing CP2 Phase I with first production targeted for late 2027.
800 Employees ·www.ventureglobal.com

Investment Thesis
Venture Global has executed one of the most rapid LNG scale-ups in U.S. history, growing revenue 177% to $13.8B in 2025 while exporting a record 380 cargos [1]. The 2026 EBITDA guidance of $5.2–5.8B at a market cap of roughly $30B (8x forward earnings) appears attractive, and the long-term contracted capacity of ~9.75 MTPA from recent deals provides multi-year visibility [1]. Plaquemines Phase I commercial operation in Q4 2026 and Phase II in mid-2027 represent a clear production ramp that could double EBITDA by 2028. European and Asian LNG demand remains structurally elevated as countries diversify away from Russian supply.
The BP litigation is the primary existential risk — Venture Global disputes with its foundation buyers could result in billion-dollar damage awards that impair liquidity and destroy the long-term commercial relationships required to fill CP2 and future capacity [2]. The 'Venture Global Discount' is visible in the stock's underperformance versus peers: Morgan Stanley initiated with Underweight, JPMorgan downgraded, reflecting analyst skepticism about contractual integrity [2]. The 11% projected EBITDA decline in 2026 versus 2025 signals the revenue cycle has peaked near term, and $175–185B in 2026 capex for ongoing construction creates continuous balance sheet pressure.
Key Catalysts
  1. Plaquemines Phase I commercial operation declaration in Q4 2026 — triggers contracted revenue from long-term SPAs and removes the commissioning/startup uncertainty that has driven the 'VG Discount' [1]
  2. BP quantum hearing resolution: a settlement or favorable ruling eliminates the largest contingent liability and would likely catalyze a significant stock re-rating from the current depressed level [2]
  3. New long-term SPA signings: total contracted capacity growing beyond 70% of 2026 production provides earnings visibility and validates the commercial strategy for CP2 [1]
Key Risks
  1. BP and foundation buyer litigation: losing the quantum hearing could require $2–5B in damages, potentially triggering covenant violations on construction debt and requiring dilutive equity raises [2]
  2. Plaquemines execution risk: Phase I COD target of Q4 2026 is critical for triggering contracted SPA revenue — any delay extends the commissioning/spot-only revenue period and maintains discount valuation
  3. LNG price compression: European TTF natural gas prices have moderated and new LNG supply from Qatar, Australia, and Mozambique is coming online — sustained TTF below $8/MMBtu would compress VG's spot market margins materially [1]
