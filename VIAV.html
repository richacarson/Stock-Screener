Company Profile
Technology ·Communication Equipment ·NASDAQ ·United States
Viavi Solutions provides network test, monitoring, and assurance solutions for telecommunications, cloud, AI data centers, aerospace, and defense. Its NSE segment offers testing and AIOps tools for wireless, wireline, and cloud networks, while the OSP segment leverages optical coating technologies for anti-counterfeiting, 3D sensing, and industrial markets. Formerly JDS Uniphase, the company was founded in 1923 and is headquartered in Chandler, Arizona.

Investment Thesis
Viavi Solutions is a network test-and-measurement leader riding the AI data center infrastructure wave — Q2 FY2026 revenue of $369.3M grew 36.4% YoY with NSE segment surging 46%, driven by hyperscaler demand for AI data center testing [1]. Non-GAAP operating margin expanded 440 bps to 19.3%, and the company guided Q3 revenue of $386-400M (vs. $353.5M consensus), signaling sustained momentum. The 1.6T TestCenter D2 Appliance and AI-driven 6G digital twin position Viavi for multi-year demand cycles as networks upgrade to support AI workloads [2]. The Spirent acquisition adds ~$200M annual revenue and deepens competitive positioning. At 33x forward P/E on expected EPS of $1.01, the stock is reasonably valued for a company inflecting from loss to profitability with 36%+ revenue growth [4].
The bear case centers on cyclicality, execution risk, and near-term losses. Viavi's trailing EPS of -$0.19 and negative ROE (-5.5%) reflect a company still recovering from the telecom spending downturn [4]. Only 42% of Glassdoor employees have a positive business outlook, suggesting internal skepticism about sustainability of the recovery [2]. Revenue concentration risk around hyperscaler AI capex means any slowdown in data center spending would disproportionately impact the NSE segment's 46% growth rate. The Spirent integration adds execution risk and near-term restructuring charges on top of existing profitability challenges. D/E of 86.4% with $670M debt creates refinancing risk. Wireless test weakness and delayed service provider spending remain headwinds outside the AI data center vertical. Geopolitical risks including tariffs could disrupt supply chains for this global operation. The stock's 112% gain over the past 12 months means much of the AI tailwind may already be priced in [1].
