Company Profile
Technology ·Communication Equipment ·NASDAQ ·United States
Viavi Solutions Inc. provides network test, monitoring, and assurance solutions to telecommunications, cloud, enterprise, government, aerospace, and defense customers worldwide. The company operates through three segments: Network Enablement (NE), Service Enablement (SE), and Optical Security and Performance Products (OSP). Formerly JDSU, the company was renamed in 2015 following a corporate split. Market cap approximately $10.5B.

Investment Thesis
VIAV Solutions is experiencing a powerful revenue recovery driven by AI data center infrastructure demand, with Q3 FY2026 revenue surging 42.8% YoY to $406.8M and non-GAAP operating margin expanding to 21.0% [1]. The company developed the industry's first high-density OSFP test platform for 1.6T Ethernet AI infrastructure, positioning it at the center of the multi-year data center buildout cycle. Q4 FY2026 guidance of $427-$437M revenue signals continued acceleration, and B.Riley upgraded to Buy citing AI and data center growth as key factors [2]. The Network Enablement segment's $216M quarterly revenue from data center demand provides clear visibility into the AI-driven growth cycle. Non-GAAP EPS grew 80% YoY to $0.27 in Q3, demonstrating significant operating leverage.
Despite the revenue recovery, VIAV carries meaningful financial risk: TTM net profit margin remains negative at -4.09% and ROE is -7.0%, indicating the company has not yet achieved sustainable GAAP profitability [4]. Debt-to-equity of 129% and a forward P/E of 40.8x price in considerable optimism relative to the company's still-negative GAAP earnings. The 5-year revenue CAGR of only 2.3% reveals that the current growth surge follows years of stagnation, raising questions about sustainability once the AI infrastructure buildout normalizes. The Optical Security and Performance Products segment faces different demand dynamics than the AI-driven network test business, creating mixed portfolio exposure. CEO Khaykin's recent $704K stock sale signals caution from inside the company [2]. The stock's 92x trailing P/E (on negative GAAP earnings) leaves minimal margin of safety if AI data center capex cycles peak.
Key Catalysts
  1. Continued AI data center buildout driving demand for VIAV's first-to-market 1.6T OSFP test platform, with Q4 guidance of $427-$437M suggesting sustained momentum into FY2027
  2. Non-GAAP operating margin expansion from 15.7% in Q1 to 21.0% in Q3 FY2026 demonstrates significant operating leverage that could drive the transition to sustained GAAP profitability
  3. Aerospace and defense demand growth provides a second growth vector beyond AI data centers, diversifying revenue drivers and reducing dependence on a single end market
Key Risks
  1. Forward P/E of 40.8x prices in substantial growth that may not materialize if AI data center capex cycles moderate — any demand deceleration could trigger significant multiple compression
  2. GAAP profitability remains negative (-4.09% net margin, -7.0% ROE) despite the revenue surge — the company must demonstrate sustainable bottom-line improvement to justify its valuation
  3. CEO insider selling of $704K in VIAV stock and 5-year revenue CAGR of only 2.3% raise questions about whether leadership views the current AI-driven growth as a temporary cycle rather than a permanent step-change
