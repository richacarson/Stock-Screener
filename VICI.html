Company Profile
Real Estate ·REIT - Diversified ·NYSE ·United States
VICI Properties is an S&P 500 experiential real estate investment trust that owns one of the largest portfolios of market-leading gaming, hospitality, wellness, entertainment, and leisure destinations. The company's properties include Caesars Palace, MGM Grand, and other iconic Las Vegas assets under very long-term triple-net leases with built-in annual escalators.

Investment Thesis
VICI Properties offers a compelling income thesis with a 6.56% dividend yield growing at ~8% annually, backed by ~30-year triple-net leases with CPI-linked escalators from creditworthy gaming tenants (Caesars, MGM, Hard Rock) [1]. At 9.3x forward P/E with 69.3% profit margins, the stock is attractively valued for a REIT with S&P 500 inclusion and $29B market cap. The Golden Entertainment acquisition ($87M annual rent from $1.16B investment) demonstrates continued accretive deal-making, and the planned non-gaming diversification could earn a re-rating from income investors who currently avoid gaming exposure. AFFO per share grew 5.3% in recent quarters with a clear path to ~4% annual growth.
The bear case includes the 2026 AFFO guidance of $2.42-$2.45/share falling below the $2.66 consensus, creating near-term earnings disappointment [1]. VICI faces $1.75B in debt maturities in 2026 that must be refinanced in an uncertain rate environment, and the $17.1B total debt load amplifies refinancing cost sensitivity. Questions around Caesars' regional casino portfolio and softer Las Vegas leisure trends weigh on the valuation multiple. Gaming-sector concentration means VICI's fate is tied to a narrow industry, and the Inspire Insight MISALIGNED rating (-65 for Gambling and Sexually Explicit content) limits the shareholder base for faith-aligned investors. The shift toward non-traditional investments introduces new risks as VICI expands beyond its core gaming real estate competency.
Key Catalysts
  1. Golden Entertainment acquisition generating $87M annual rent from a $1.16B investment, providing immediate AFFO accretion [1]
  2. Built-in CPI-linked annual rent escalators across the ~30-year lease portfolio providing organic AFFO growth regardless of deal activity [1]
  3. Planned diversification into non-gaming experiential assets could attract income investors currently avoiding gaming exposure, supporting valuation re-rating
Key Risks
  1. $1.75B in 2026 debt maturities requiring refinancing — higher rates would directly pressure AFFO and dividend coverage ratios [1]
  2. Tenant concentration risk with Caesars and MGM representing the majority of rent revenue — any single tenant's financial distress could materially impact VICI
  3. Gaming industry slowdown risk from softer consumer spending or Las Vegas leisure trend weakness would pressure tenant rental coverage ratios
