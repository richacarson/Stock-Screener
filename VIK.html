Investment Thesis
Viking Holdings is the category-defining leader in premium destination cruising, delivering strong operational results. FY2025 revenue of $6.5B grew 21.9% on 12% capacity and 7.4% net yield growth, with adjusted EBITDA of $1.9B (up 38.8%) and adjusted net income of $1.2B (up 43.9%) [1]. 2026 is already 86% booked with $6B of advance deposits up 13% YoY as of February 2026, giving exceptional revenue visibility [1]. Founder-CEO Torstein Hagen has led consistently for 28 years, and the float-out of the Viking Libra (first hydrogen-powered cruise ship, 6 MW fuel-cell propulsion, November 2026 delivery) demonstrates category-defining innovation [2]. ROIC of 45.8% and 17.66% profit margin on conservative 1.1x net leverage — one of the strongest financial profiles in the cruise industry [3].
The bear case centers on cyclical risk and valuation. Cruise demand is consumer-discretionary and sensitive to recession risk, and Viking's premium-aged-demographic customer base (typically 55+) is exposed to retirement-portfolio drawdowns in a market correction [1]. Forward P/E of 22.3x prices in continued high teens revenue growth, and as capacity growth moderates (9% for 2026) that multiple will compress unless yields keep expanding [3]. Balance sheet D/E of 524% reflects industry-normal shipbuilding capital structure, but rising rates would pressure incremental newbuild economics. The hydrogen-powered ship is a technology-transition bet that carries execution risk and may not deliver commercial premiums commensurate with the capital outlay.
Key Catalysts

  3. Hydrogen propulsion execution risk — first-of-kind technology could face commissioning or operating issues that impair the Libra's premium positioning [2]
