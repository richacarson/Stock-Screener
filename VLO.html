Investment Thesis
Valero offers a compelling income and value proposition at 16.8x forward P/E with a 2.0% dividend yield (recently increased 6%) backed by $4.1B+ in annual operating cash flow and a conservative 44% D/E ratio. Q4 2025 refining margins surged to $6.05/barrel (from $1.60 a year ago), and 2026 analyst estimates of $12.29 EPS would represent 44% growth from 2025's $7.57. The Diamond Green Diesel venture (1.2B gallons renewable diesel + 235M gallons SAF) positions Valero for the aviation decarbonization megatrend, while the 25-year dividend history and aggressive buybacks ($931M in Q4 alone) demonstrate shareholder commitment.
The bear case centers on refining's inherent cyclicality and thin margins—GAAP profit margin of 2.0% means small changes in crack spreads create massive earnings swings (EPS ranged from $7.57 to $29.04 over the last three years). Revenue declined 5.5% YoY and is down 30% from the 2022 peak, reflecting the normalization of post-COVID refining super-margins. The 'High' Sustainalytics ESG risk rating (30) and Level 2 controversy level create institutional investor headwinds, while the long-term demand trajectory for refined petroleum faces structural decline as EV adoption accelerates. ROE of 8.3% significantly trails the 15% target, suggesting suboptimal capital efficiency in the current refining environment.
