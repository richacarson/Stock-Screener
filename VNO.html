Company Profile
Real Estate ·REIT - Office ·NYSE ·United States
Vornado Realty Trust is a fully integrated REIT with a 26 million square-foot portfolio of premier New York City office, retail, and multifamily assets, anchored by the $2.5 billion PENN District redevelopment. The company also owns flagship properties in Chicago (THE MART) and San Francisco (555 California Street). 100% of in-service office buildings are LEED certified.

However, the company's financial engineering (dividend cuts, leverage management) and concentration risk reflect finite-game vulnerabilities.

Investment Thesis
Vornado's bull case hinges on the PENN District inflection point — with PENN 2 at 78% occupancy (targeting low-90s) and an incremental cash yield of 11.6%, $200M+ of currently unrecognized rent should translate into substantial earnings growth starting in 2027 [1]. The 36-year dividend payment history, premier NYC portfolio with 100% LEED certification, and significant insider buying ($5.6M by Director Tisch in Q1 2026) signal long-term conviction from those closest to the assets. NOI grew 1.5% in 2025 to $1.11B, and the company expects 2026 comparable FFO to be roughly flat at $2.32/share before the 2027 earnings inflection [1].
The bear case is formidable. Forward P/E of 101x reflects near-zero GAAP earnings power today, with FFO a more relevant metric for REITs but one that shows no growth in 2026 [4]. Revenue declined 8.1% YoY, and the 5yr revenue CAGR is essentially flat at 0.2%. The dividend was cut from $4.59/share in 2019 to $0.74/share today — a 84% reduction that destroyed income investor confidence [4]. Office REIT fundamentals remain challenged by remote and hybrid work adoption, particularly outside the premium segment. Debt-to-equity of 116.7% with $7.9B total debt against only $855M cash creates balance sheet risk if interest rates remain elevated. The PENN District thesis requires continued Manhattan office demand recovery, which faces structural headwinds from work-from-home trends [1][4].
Key Catalysts
  1. PENN District lease-up approaching 2027 inflection point — $200M+ of currently unrecognized rent expected to convert into substantial earnings growth as PENN 2 moves from 78% to low-90s occupancy [1]
  2. Meta establishing NYC retail flagship and Newmark engaged as exclusive leasing agent for PENN District retail renaissance, signaling major tenant demand [1]
  3. Significant insider buying by Director Daniel Tisch ($5.6M in Q1 2026) at $25-28/share suggesting informed confidence in near-term catalysts [4]
Key Risks
  1. Structural office demand decline from remote and hybrid work trends that could permanently impair occupancy rates and rental income even in premium NYC locations [1]
  2. Elevated leverage at 116.7% D/E with $7.9B total debt and limited liquidity ($855M cash), creating refinancing risk in a higher-for-longer interest rate environment [4]
  3. Dividend destruction — 84% cut from $4.59/share (2019) to $0.74/share today may permanently impair the stock's income investor base and limit valuation recovery [4]
