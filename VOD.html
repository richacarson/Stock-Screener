Company Profile
Communication Services ·Telecom Services ·NASDAQ ·United Kingdom
Vodafone Group is a global telecommunications company providing mobile and fixed-line services across Europe, Turkey, and Africa. It operates IoT platforms, M-PESA mobile payments (Africa's largest), cloud and edge computing solutions, and enterprise connectivity services. Following the Three UK merger, Vodafone serves over 300 million mobile customers worldwide.

Investment Thesis
Vodafone is a turnaround story gaining momentum under CEO Della Valle. Q3 FY2026 revenue grew 6.5% to EUR 10.5B, H1 revenue rose 7.3% to EUR 19.6B, and the company guided to the upper end of EBITDAaL (EUR 11.3-11.6B) and FCF (EUR 2.4-2.6B) ranges [1]. The Three UK merger adds scale in a key market, Africa service revenue grew 13.5% organically, and digital services now represent 35% of revenue [1][2]. At 17.1x forward P/E with a 3.5% dividend yield and newly progressive dividend policy, Vodafone offers reasonable value if the operational turnaround delivers sustained margin expansion. The stock has rallied 61% from its 52-week low of $8.05, suggesting improving sentiment [4].
The bear case centers on Vodafone's history of value destruction and structural industry headwinds. The stock has declined from $75 to $15 over two decades, and trailing net income remains negative (-$4.4B) despite revenue growth [4]. Operating profit fell 9.2% in H1 as Three UK integration costs and higher D&A offset revenue gains. Total debt of EUR 53.9B against EUR 13.5B cash creates significant leverage. The European telecom market faces structurally low returns on capital as regulators suppress pricing power while demanding massive 5G capex. The payout ratio exceeds 100%, and Vodafone's dividend history includes a 40% cut in 2019, eroding investor trust [4]. Competition from Deutsche Telekom (which vastly outperformed via T-Mobile US) highlights Vodafone's strategic missteps. Analyst consensus price target of $11.89 is below the current $15.02, suggesting the stock may have run ahead of fundamentals [4].
Key Catalysts
  1. Three UK integration synergies expected to deliver GBP 700M+ in cost savings, with UK revenue already up 27.9% from consolidation [1]
  2. Africa growth acceleration — Vodacom organic service revenue grew 13.5% in Q3, and M-PESA continues expanding financial services across the continent [1]
  3. FY2026 guidance at upper end of EBITDAaL range (EUR 11.3-11.6B) with progressive dividend policy signaling management confidence [1]
Key Risks
  1. Three UK integration execution risk — GBP 700M+ synergy targets require successful network consolidation, brand integration, and employee rationalization [1]
  2. European telecom structural headwinds — regulatory pricing pressure, massive 5G capex requirements, and OTT cannibalization constrain returns on capital [2]
  3. Analyst consensus price target of $11.89 is 21% below current price of $15.02, suggesting potential overvaluation after the recent rally [4]
