Company Profile
Industrials ·Consulting Services ·NASDAQ ·United States
Verisk Analytics, Inc. provides data analytics and technology solutions to the insurance industry in the United States and internationally. The company offers underwriting solutions, catastrophe modeling, claims analytics, anti-fraud detection, life insurance solutions, and specialty business management services. Founded in 1971 and headquartered in Jersey City, New Jersey, Verisk serves as the insurance industry's essential data backbone.

Investment Thesis
Verisk Analytics is a high-quality, near-monopoly data analytics franchise embedded in the US property/casualty insurance industry's core workflows, trading at 20.7x forward P/E on FY2026 guidance of $3.19-$3.24B revenue and $7.45-$7.75 adjusted EPS [1]. The bull case centers on Verisk's irreplaceable proprietary data moat — 50+ years of forms, rules, loss costs, and claims databases that insurers cannot replicate — creating exceptional pricing power reflected in 29.6% profit margins and $1.0B in free cash flow [1][4]. Organic revenue growth of 6.6% in 2025 is driven by subscription pricing increases, expanded analytics adoption, and AI-powered underwriting and claims products [1][2]. The 2022 Wood Mackenzie divestiture for $3.1B sharpened the pure-play insurance focus, and CEO Shavel's AI innovation agenda positions Verisk to capture incremental value as insurers automate underwriting and claims workflows [2]. Great Place to Work certification and strong Glassdoor ratings support talent retention in a competitive analytics labor market [2].
The bear case centers on valuation and growth limitations. At 27.5x trailing P/E and 20.7x forward, Verisk is priced as a premium compounder, but organic revenue growth of 6.6% and mid-single-digit 5-year CAGR suggest limited upside acceleration [1][4]. The D/E ratio of 1,582% — driven by aggressive buybacks that have nearly eliminated equity — creates balance sheet fragility if growth slows or interest rates remain elevated, despite the strong cash flow generation [4]. The terminated AccuLynx acquisition represents a failed growth-by-acquisition attempt that cost opportunity but also revealed FTC scrutiny of Verisk's market dominance [1]. While AI enhances Verisk's analytics, it also enables insurtechs and alternative data providers to build competing solutions that could erode Verisk's data exclusivity over time. Revenue growth guidance for 2026 implies only 4-6% growth, below the historical trajectory, partly due to the Verisk Marketing Solutions divestiture [1]. The insurance industry's inherent conservatism limits Verisk's ability to accelerate growth through new product adoption, making this a steady compounder rather than a high-growth opportunity.

AI Resilience (25%)
AI Resilience MODERATE
7
AI Risk Protection (6/9): Verisk's core moat — 50+ years of proprietary insurance industry data including forms, rules, loss costs, and claims databases — is structurally protected from AI disruption because AI tools require the very data Verisk aggregates. Insurers cannot replicate this data independently, and switching costs are extremely high as underwriting and claims systems are built on Verisk's data infrastructure [1]. However, AI creates a double-edged risk: while it increases demand for Verisk's data and analytics, it also enables new entrants and insurtech competitors to build competing analytics layers on top of alternative data sources. Large language models could eventually reduce insurers' dependence on Verisk's traditional forms and rules by automating policy language generation. CMS and regulatory trends toward open data sharing could also erode data exclusivity over time. AI Opportunity (1/1): Verisk generates meaningful AI-related revenue through AI-powered underwriting automation in Life Solutions FAST, fraud detection algorithms, and machine learning-enhanced catastrophe models that are embedded in customer workflows and priced into subscription fees [2].
