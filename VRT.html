Investment Thesis
Vertiv is the premier infrastructure pick-and-shovel play on the AI data center buildout, with a record $15B backlog, 252% organic order growth, and 2026 guidance calling for $13.25-$13.75B in revenue (27-29% organic growth) and adjusted EPS of $5.97-$6.07 (42-45% growth) [1]. The company's dominant position in power and cooling infrastructure makes it an essential supplier to hyperscalers spending up to $700B in combined 2026 capex. Adjusted free cash flow is guided to $2.1-$2.3B in 2026, supporting further capacity expansion.
The primary risk is cyclicality and valuation. At 31.5x forward earnings, Vertiv is priced for continued hypergrowth that depends on sustained hyperscaler capex. Any moderation in AI infrastructure spending — whether from economic slowdown, technology shifts, or capex fatigue — could trigger a sharp multiple contraction. The 82% D/E ratio adds leverage risk, and the company's reliance on a few massive customers (Microsoft, Amazon, Google, Meta) creates concentration risk. The stock has already surged 191% in the past year, raising the question of how much growth is already priced in [1].
Key Catalysts
  1. Hyperscaler capex of up to $700B in 2026 directly drives demand for Vertiv's power and cooling solutions, with the $15B backlog providing 12+ months of revenue visibility [1]
  2. Liquid cooling adoption for AI workloads is accelerating as GPU power density increases, and Vertiv's Intel/NVIDIA partnerships position it as the category leader [2]
  3. Adjusted operating margin expansion to 22-23% in 2026 guidance (from ~20% in 2025) as scale benefits flow through and higher-margin service revenue grows [1]
Key Risks
  1. Hyperscaler capex concentration risk — if Microsoft, Amazon, Google, or Meta reduce or delay data center spending, Vertiv's order pipeline could decelerate sharply from 252% growth [1]
  2. At 31.5x forward P/E after a 191% stock surge, any earnings miss or guidance reduction could trigger 20-30% multiple compression as growth expectations recalibrate
  3. Supply chain bottlenecks in transformers, switchgear, and specialty components could constrain Vertiv's ability to convert its $15B backlog into revenue on schedule
