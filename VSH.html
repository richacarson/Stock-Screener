Company Profile
Technology ·Semiconductors ·NYSE ·United States
Vishay Intertechnology manufactures discrete semiconductors and passive electronic components used in automotive, industrial, computing, consumer, telecommunications, military, aerospace, and medical applications. Products include MOSFETs, diodes, optoelectronic components, resistors, inductors, and capacitors. Founded in 1962 and headquartered in Malvern, Pennsylvania.

Investment Thesis
Vishay Intertechnology is a cyclical semiconductor recovery play trading at $23.76 with revenue inflecting upward — Q4 2025 revenue of $790.6M was up 7.5% YoY and beat analyst estimates by 1.2% [1]. The Vishay 3.0 transformation is expanding capacity in high-growth areas including SiC power semiconductors for AI servers and EVs, with the company supplying over 80% of the bill of materials for power applications in AI infrastructure [2]. Forward P/E of 23.6x reflects expectations of a sharp earnings recovery to $1.01 EPS from -$0.07 trailing, supported by analyst consensus of 11.3% revenue growth in Q1 2026 [1]. D/E of 51.4% provides a clean balance sheet to fund capacity expansion, and the company has maintained its $0.40 annual dividend through two consecutive loss years.
The bear case is significant: Vishay posted net losses in both 2024 and 2025, with profit margin at -0.3% and ROE at -0.4% [3]. Revenue of $3.07B in 2025 remains 12% below the 2022 peak of $3.50B, yielding a negative 3-year CAGR. The forward P/E of 23.6x prices in a substantial earnings recovery that has not yet materialized — if the semiconductor cycle stalls or AI demand disappoints, the multiple will compress significantly. Competition from ON Semiconductor and Infineon with superior SiC solutions threatens Vishay's AI growth narrative [2]. The bottom-5% executive team rating on Comparably (40/100) raises cultural concerns [2]. The $0.40 annual dividend (1.68% yield) has been flat since 2022 and is being paid from negative earnings, creating sustainability risk. Tariff uncertainty on semiconductor components adds additional margin pressure.

AI Resilience (25%)
AI Resilience MODERATE
7
AI Risk Protection (6/9): Vishay's core business — manufacturing discrete semiconductors and passive components — is protected by physical manufacturing moats, proprietary processes, and the essential nature of these components in every electronic device [1]. AI cannot replace resistors, capacitors, or MOSFETs. However, Vishay faces indirect exposure through customer concentration risk as AI shifts computing architectures. Competitors like ON Semiconductor and Infineon with superior SiC solutions could capture more AI-adjacent share [2]. The AI boom could prove cyclical, and Vishay's commodity-oriented product mix (standard resistors, capacitors) faces ongoing pricing pressure from Asian competitors regardless of AI trends. AI Opportunity (1/1): Vishay generates meaningful AI-adjacent revenue — the company supplies over 80% of the bill of materials for power applications in AI servers and showcased AI-specific solutions at PCIM Asia 2025 [2]. AI server demand for power semiconductors, inductors, and capacitors directly benefits Vishay's product portfolio.
