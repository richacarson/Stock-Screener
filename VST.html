Company Profile
Utilities ·Utilities - Independent Power Producers ·NYSE ·United States
Vistra Corp. is an integrated retail electricity and power generation company operating approximately 44,000 MW of generation capacity across natural gas, nuclear, coal, solar, and battery storage facilities. The company serves approximately 5 million retail customers and owns the second-largest competitive nuclear fleet in the United States, including the Comanche Peak Nuclear Plant in Texas and PJM nuclear facilities acquired through Energy Harbor.

Investment Thesis
Vistra is uniquely positioned as an AI infrastructure play within the utility sector, combining the second-largest competitive nuclear fleet in the U.S. (~44,000 MW total capacity) with a 5-million-customer retail electricity business. The company's 2026 EBITDA guidance of $6.8-7.6B represents 15-29% growth over 2025's $5.9B, driven by 20-year nuclear PPAs with Meta (2,600+ MW) and Amazon that lock in premium pricing for decades [1]. At 13.5x forward P/E with forward EPS of $11.27 and adjusted FCFbG projected to exceed $12.5/share in 2026, VST offers compelling value for a regulated asset base with exceptional demand visibility [1]. The structural increase in U.S. electricity demand (record levels in 2025, up 2.5% YoY) driven by data center buildouts provides a secular tailwind that differentiates Vistra from traditional utilities.
The bear case centers on leverage, environmental liabilities, and regulatory risk. D/E of 399.6% is extremely elevated, reflecting acquisition-driven growth that leaves little margin for error if power prices decline or interest rates remain high. Profit margin of only 5.3% and trailing P/E of 69.5x (depressed by one-time items) indicate that current profitability is thin despite the growth narrative. The Martin Lake coal plant remains the nation's top sulfur dioxide and mercury polluter, creating ongoing regulatory and reputational risk that could result in forced early retirement and asset write-downs [4]. EPS declined 52.3% YoY in 2025 due to integration costs and accounting items, though forward estimates suggest recovery. Regulatory risk in ERCOT (Texas deregulated market) and PJM is significant — changes to capacity market rules or emissions standards could materially impact economics. The nuclear fleet carries inherent operational risk; an unplanned outage at Comanche Peak or PJM facilities during a PPA period would trigger financial penalties and reputational damage.
Key Catalysts
  1. Full-year realization of 20-year Meta and Amazon nuclear PPAs in 2026, driving EBITDA toward the $6.8-7.6B guidance range with premium contracted pricing [1]
  2. Nuclear uprate opportunities and additional hyperscaler PPA signings that could add incremental high-margin revenue beyond current guidance [1]
  3. New 860 MW natural gas capacity in West Texas and potential additional gas plant acquisitions to serve growing ERCOT data center load [1]
Key Risks
  1. Elevated leverage at 399.6% D/E leaves limited financial flexibility if power prices decline or integration costs exceed expectations [5]
  2. Environmental liability from Martin Lake and remaining coal assets — the nation's top sulfur dioxide and mercury polluter faces forced retirement risk from tightening EPA regulations [4]
  3. Nuclear operational risk — an extended unplanned outage at Comanche Peak or PJM facilities during contracted PPA periods would create material financial and reputational damage [1]
