Company Profile
Healthcare ·Biotechnology ·NASDAQ ·United States
Verastem Oncology is a commercial-stage biopharmaceutical company focused on developing and commercializing novel therapies targeting RAS/MAPK pathway-driven cancers. Its FDA-approved AVMAPKI FAKZYNJA CO-PACK (avutometinib + defactinib) is the first treatment specifically approved for KRAS-mutated recurrent low-grade serous ovarian cancer. The pipeline includes programs in pancreatic cancer, NSCLC, and a next-generation oral KRAS inhibitor (VS-7375). Founded in 2010, headquartered in Needham, Massachusetts.

Investment Thesis
Verastem is a newly commercial-stage oncology company with a first-in-class, FDA-approved combination therapy for KRAS-mutated recurrent LGSOC — a condition with no prior approved treatment [1]. The CO-PACK launch generated $30.9M in 8 months with nearly 300 prescribers by February 2026 and accelerating Q4 revenue of $17.5M, suggesting a $70M+ annualized run rate trajectory [1]. Gross margins of 85.1% on product revenue are outstanding [4]. The pipeline is deep and differentiated — RAMP 205 showed 83% ORR in pancreatic cancer (10/12 patients), RAMP 301 confirmatory data expected mid-2027, and VS-7375 oral KRAS inhibitor has IND clearance with Fast Track designation [1]. At $573M market cap with $205M cash and net cash of $118M, the stock trades at ~6x annualized Q4 revenue for a company with 85% gross margins, multiple pipeline catalysts, and partnerships with Amgen, Chugai, and Eli Lilly collaboration potential.
The bear case centers on commercial execution risk and cash burn. Verastem's $82.3M annual FCF outflow means the company must scale revenue rapidly to reach profitability before cash runs low [4]. D/E of 152.4% adds financial risk if additional capital is needed. The LGSOC market is a niche indication — estimates vary, but the addressable patient population for KRAS-mutated recurrent LGSOC is relatively small, potentially limiting peak revenue to $200-300M annually. RAMP 301 confirmatory trial results are not expected until mid-2027, and failure would undermine the commercial franchise and potentially trigger regulatory risk [1]. The 83% ORR in pancreatic cancer (N=12) is encouraging but based on a tiny sample — pancreatic cancer has a long history of destroying promising early data. Competition in the RAS pathway is intensifying with programs from Amgen, Mirati/BMS, Revolution Medicines, and others with substantially greater resources [1]. The stock has declined from ~$10 in November 2025 to $6.52, reflecting market skepticism about the pace of the commercial ramp and cash runway concerns [3][4].
Key Catalysts

  3. RAS pathway competitive intensification — Amgen, Revolution Medicines, Mirati/BMS, and others have well-funded KRAS-targeted programs that could compress Verastem's differentiation window [1]

AI Risk Protection (6/9): Pharmaceutical commercialization and clinical development are deeply regulated, physical-world processes that AI cannot replace — FDA trials require human patients, regulatory submissions require specific data packages, and physician prescribing decisions involve clinical judgment [1]. Verastem's first-in-class mechanism (dual RAF/MEK + FAK inhibition) is protected by patents and regulatory exclusivity. The commercial infrastructure (specialty pharmacy, patient support programs, KOL relationships) creates switching costs. However, AI-driven drug discovery at larger pharma companies could accelerate the development of competing RAS pathway therapies, potentially compressing Verastem's competitive window. Verastem's VS-7375 oral KRAS program competes in a space where Amgen (sotorasib), Mirati/BMS, and others are well-funded [1]. AI Opportunity (1/1): While Verastem does not explicitly use AI in drug discovery, its computational approach to RAS/MAPK pathway combination therapies and the platform strategy of testing multiple combinations (RAMP trials) represent a data-intensive approach that benefits from computational analysis. The partnership with GenFleet Therapeutics for VS-7375 brings additional discovery capabilities [1].
