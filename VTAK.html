Investment Thesis
Catheter Precision is a micro-cap ($2.6M market cap) medical device company undergoing a radical transformation through its acquisition of Fly Flyte, an AI-enabled regional air mobility operator using Cirrus Vision Jets [2]. The bull case rests on the Flyte opportunity: $160K in bookings from a single-aircraft soft rollout in Florida, plans for five jets by Memorial Day 2026, and up to $88M in institutional financing commitments to fund expansion [2][4]. Analyst forecasts project $28M in 2026 revenue (up from $819K in 2025) if the Flyte business scales as planned, which would represent a transformational revenue inflection [1]. The medical device segment (VIVO cardiac mapping, LockeT) provides a healthcare technology floor, and D/E of 57.8% is manageable [5].
The bear case is severe. VTAK has a $2.6M market cap with $17.2M in annual losses and only $88K cash on hand — the company is entirely dependent on the $88M financing commitment, which will likely result in massive shareholder dilution [1][4]. Revenue of $819K after 19 years demonstrates the medical device business has failed to achieve commercial viability. The pivot from cardiac devices to private jet charter has no strategic coherence and signals desperation. The $28M revenue forecast assumes Flyte scales rapidly in a competitive private aviation market with razor-thin margins and high capital intensity (aircraft acquisition, maintenance, fuel, insurance) [2]. Forward P/E is negative, ROE is -192%, and the stock trades at $0.95 — this is a speculative micro-cap with significant risk of further dilution or delisting [5].
Key Catalysts
  1. Fly Flyte fleet expansion to five Cirrus Vision Jets by Memorial Day 2026 with potential to generate $28M in annual revenue if booking momentum from Florida soft launch sustains [2]
  2. Execution on $88M institutional financing commitments, providing multi-year runway for Flyte fleet acquisition and route expansion [4]
  3. Potential monetization of VIVO cardiac mapping technology through licensing or partnership with a larger EP company as a non-core asset [3]
Key Risks
  1. Extreme dilution risk — $88M in financing commitments against a $2.6M market cap will massively dilute existing shareholders unless the stock price appreciates dramatically before capital draws [4]
  2. Execution risk on Flyte scaling — private aviation is capital-intensive with tight margins, and VTAK has zero operational experience in aviation prior to the March 2026 acquisition [2]
  3. Going concern risk — $88K cash, $17.2M annual losses, and $3.8M debt create existential dependency on continued financing access; any disruption to capital commitments could force restructuring [1][5]
