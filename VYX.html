Company Profile
Technology ·Information Technology Services ·NYSE ·United States
NCR Voyix provides digital commerce solutions for the retail and restaurant industries, offering point-of-sale hardware and software, self-checkout kiosks, payment processing, and the Voyix Commerce Platform. Formerly NCR Corporation (founded 1884), the company split from NCR Atleos in 2023 and is transitioning to a cloud-native, SaaS-focused business model while moving hardware to an ODM model with Ennoconn.

Investment Thesis
NCR Voyix represents a deep-value turnaround bet trading at 6.6x forward earnings with FY2026 adjusted EPS guidance of $0.93-0.96 for a stock at $6.73 [1][5]. The company is executing a fundamental business model shift from hardware-centric POS provider to cloud-native SaaS platform, with the ODM hardware transition to Ennoconn removing capital-intensive manufacturing operations. Q4 2025 revenue of $720M beat estimates by $30M, and the company beat Q4 EPS consensus by $0.02, suggesting the underlying business is stabilizing [1]. The Voyix Commerce Platform, serving enterprise retailers and restaurants across 35 countries with 13,500 employees, creates significant switching cost protection in its installed base. If the SaaS transition succeeds, margin expansion from ~2.3% to software-like margins would create substantial upside from the current $935M market cap [5].
The bear case is severe and multifaceted. Revenue has declined from $3.17B (2022) to a guided $2.2-2.3B for FY2026 — a 28% erosion in four years [1][5]. Net debt of $1.15B ($1.38B debt, $231M cash) exceeds the company's entire market cap of $935M, creating significant leverage risk if the turnaround stalls [5]. Free cash flow is negative at -$112.9M, meaning the company is burning cash while servicing heavy debt [5]. Employee morale is critically low — Glassdoor reviews describe multiple layoff rounds, pay cuts, and disconnected leadership [2]. The stock has fallen 54% from its 52-week high of $14.67, reflecting market skepticism. Competition from Toast, Square, and Shopify in restaurant/retail POS is intensifying with AI-powered features that NCR Voyix lacks. The shared $150M environmental liability with NCR Atleos adds an unusual contingent liability [3]. Management guidance for FY2026 projects pro forma revenue flat to down 2%, suggesting organic growth remains elusive even after the ODM adjustment [1].
