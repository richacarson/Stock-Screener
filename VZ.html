Investment Thesis
Verizon offers a 5.66% dividend yield backed by $21.5B+ in 2026 free cash flow, with 40+ years of consecutive payments and 20 years of consecutive growth [1]. At 9.5x forward earnings, the stock is deeply undervalued relative to its FCF generation capacity. CEO Schulman's turnaround plan targeting 750K-1M net postpaid adds in 2026 (2-3x 2025 levels) and 150-200 bps of operating leverage suggests margin expansion ahead. The stock is near its 52-week high of $51.67, reflecting early investor recognition of the turnaround.
The bear case centers on $184.8B in total debt (D/E 175%) and structural low-growth dynamics in U.S. wireless, where subscriber penetration is near saturation [1]. Revenue growth of 2% and EPS decline of 53% YoY in the latest quarter demonstrate the earnings pressure from the current competitive and restructuring environment. Wireless service revenue is guided flat for 2026. Dividend growth of 2% annually barely keeps pace with inflation, and the 50% payout ratio limits flexibility. The 13,000 job cuts signal that efficiency gains, not innovation, will drive near-term results [1].
Key Catalysts
  1. 750K-1M postpaid phone net adds targeted in 2026 (2-3x 2025 levels) would signal successful competitive turnaround [1]
  2. Free cash flow of $21.5B+ in 2026 growing 7%+ provides increasing dividend coverage and debt reduction capacity [1]
  3. Capital spending reduction to $16-16.5B from $17B frees cash for deleveraging the $184.8B debt load [1]
Key Risks
  1. $184.8B debt load (D/E 175%) creates interest expense burden and limits strategic flexibility in a rising rate environment [3]
  2. Wireless service revenue guided flat for 2026 suggests volume gains may be offset by pricing competition with T-Mobile [1]
  3. 53% EPS decline in latest quarter despite revenue growth raises questions about the quality of the turnaround [1]
