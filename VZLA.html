Basic Materials ·Other Industrial Metals & Mining ·NYSE ·Canada
Vizsla Silver Corp. (formerly Vizsla Gold Corp.) is a mineral exploration and development company focused on advancing its 100%-owned Panuco silver-gold project in southern Sinaloa, Mexico. The Panuco-Copala project covers 7,190 hectares and contains a high-grade silver-gold deposit with a completed feasibility study showing an after-tax NPV(5%) of US$1.8B and 111% IRR. The company is pre-revenue and fully funded with US$450M+ in cash for construction, targeting first production in H2 2027.

Investment Thesis
Vizsla Silver offers a compelling development-stage precious metals investment anchored by the Panuco silver-gold project's exceptional economics — an after-tax NPV(5%) of US$1.8B, 111% IRR, and 7-month payback at $35.50/oz silver and $3,100/oz gold [4]. The company is fully funded with US$450M+ in cash (approximately 2x the initial capex requirement), eliminating the dilution risk that typically plagues junior miners. The feasibility study confirms annual production of 17.4 Moz AgEq over a 9.4-year mine life from one of the highest-grade undeveloped silver-gold deposits globally, with 60,000m of 2026 drilling potentially expanding the resource further [4]. At $1.2B market cap against $1.8B NPV, the stock trades at a meaningful discount to its feasibility-stage value.
The fundamental risk is that Vizsla has never built or operated a mine — the transition from exploration company to mining producer is the most dangerous phase for any junior miner, with execution risk in construction, permitting, metallurgical processing, and operational ramp-up [1][4]. The MIA environmental permit targeted for mid-2026 is a binary event that could delay the entire timeline if not approved. Mexico's mining jurisdiction faces political risks including regulatory changes, community opposition, and security concerns in Sinaloa. With zero revenue, negative ROE (-38.8%), and an Infrastructure score penalized by the mechanical scoring framework's inability to value pre-revenue assets, the financial metrics paint a misleadingly weak picture. Silver price sensitivity is extreme — the NPV was calculated at $35.50/oz, and any sustained decline below $25/oz would materially compress project economics. The stock's $3.52 price represents significant optionality on successful mine construction but also material downside if execution falters.
Key Catalysts
  1. MIA environmental permit approval targeted for mid-2026, unlocking the construction decision and de-risking the path to first production in H2 2027
  2. 60,000m diamond drilling program in 2026 targeting resource expansion at Copala, Christiano, and Tajitos deposits — a materially larger resource could extend mine life and improve NPV beyond the current $1.8B
  3. Rising silver prices driven by solar panel demand, monetary debasement hedging, and industrial AI applications — silver above $40/oz would significantly improve project economics beyond the $35.50/oz feasibility base case
Key Risks
  1. Execution risk — Vizsla has never built or operated a mine, and the transition from explorer to producer requires construction management, metallurgical optimization, and operational capabilities the 79-person team has not yet demonstrated
  2. Permitting risk — the MIA environmental permit targeted for mid-2026 is a binary event in a Mexican jurisdiction with increasing regulatory scrutiny of mining operations, and any delay cascades through the entire production timeline
  3. Single-asset concentration — 100% of the company's value depends on the Panuco project in Sinaloa, Mexico, with no diversification against project-specific, jurisdictional, or commodity price risks

AI Risk Protection (8/9): Mining is a physical-world operation that AI structurally cannot replace. Extracting silver and gold from underground veins in Sinaloa, Mexico requires drilling, blasting, ore processing, and tailings management — activities that demand heavy equipment, skilled labor, and regulatory permits that exist entirely outside the digital realm [1]. The precious metals themselves serve as inflation hedges and monetary assets whose value is driven by macroeconomic factors, not technology substitution. If anything, AI-driven productivity gains increase global economic activity and electricity demand, supporting gold's role as a store of value and silver's industrial applications in electronics and solar panels. The only AI-adjacent risk is AI-optimized exploration by competitors, but Vizsla's 100% ownership of the Panuco district eliminates competitive exploration risk.
