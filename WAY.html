Investment Thesis
Bull case: Waystar is a category-leading healthcare payments AI platform processing 1-in-3 U.S. hospital discharges with 7.5B annual transactions, Q4 2025 revenue up 24% YoY to $303.5M (38% subscription growth), and FY2025 revenue of $1.1B with adjusted EBITDA margin of 42% [1][2]. AltitudeAI has prevented $15.5B in denials for customers in under a year with 95% time savings — quantified AI ROI that few healthcare software peers can match [3][4]. 2026 guidance of $1.274-$1.294B revenue and $530-$540M adjusted EBITDA implies continued 16-18% growth with margin expansion. The $1.25B Iodine acquisition is integrating ahead of plan, immediately accretive to gross margin and EBITDA, with revenue/EPS accretion expected in 2027 [2]. Founder-CEO Matt Hawkins (Utah Business CEO of the Year 2026) has guided the company through two PE ownership cycles and a 2024 IPO. Forward P/E of 24.8 is reasonable for a profitable healthcare AI SaaS with 17%+ growth.
Bear case: 90-day share price return of -31.5% and 30-day return of -12.5% reflect market skepticism on organic growth durability [6]. The reported 17% growth normalizes to ~10% organic when adjusting for the Iodine acquisition, rapid customer implementations, and time-to-revenue benefits — a material disclosure that raises questions about core franchise momentum [1]. Private-equity sponsor overhang from EQT, CPPIB, Bain, and Francisco Partners creates secondary offering risk that will continue pressuring shares. Competitive intensity from Change Healthcare (Optum/UnitedHealth), Availity, and Experian Health is rising as AI commoditizes some claim-processing workflows. GAAP ROE of just 2.81% reflects the large equity base and amortization headwind from acquisition intangibles [6]. Integration risk from the $1.25B Iodine deal remains — ahead-of-plan commentary is early. Rising rates or a healthcare IT multiple compression could pressure the forward P/E of 24.8x.
Key Catalysts
  1. Iodine Software integration synergies and 2027 accretion to revenue/EPS as guided — extending AltitudeAI into clinical intelligence [2]
  2. AltitudeAI monetization expansion beyond denials into broader revenue cycle automation, with $15.5B in prevented denials already demonstrated [3][4]
  3. Organic growth reacceleration — moving from ~10% normalized organic growth closer to reported 17% as Iodine and new products fully annualize [1]
Key Risks
  1. Private-equity sponsor (EQT, CPPIB, Bain, Francisco Partners) secondary-offering overhang creating continued share-price pressure [6]
  2. Organic growth deceleration — reported 17% FY2025 growth normalizes to ~10% organic when adjusting for Iodine acquisition and implementation benefits [1]
  3. Competitive intensity from Change Healthcare (Optum), Availity, and Experian Health as AI commoditizes portions of the claim-processing stack [3]
