Investment Thesis
Workday is a high-quality enterprise software franchise trading at a significant discount — forward P/E of 10.4x with 14.5% subscription growth, 75.7% gross margins, and $3.01B FCF [1]. The 97% gross revenue retention rate and 10,000+ enterprise customer base (60% Fortune 500) create durable recurring revenue that AI disruption cannot easily displace due to deep data integration and switching costs [1]. AI monetization is accelerating with $100M+ in new AI ACV in Q4 (100%+ YoY growth) and 75%+ AI attach rates on new sales through Workday Illuminate [1]. Co-founder Bhusri's return as CEO specifically to lead the AI transformation provides visionary product leadership at a critical inflection point [2]. At $34B market cap, Workday trades at roughly 11x FCF — historically cheap for a dominant enterprise software platform with predictable revenue.
The bear case is the most serious facing any software company: AI disruption risk. Morningstar downgraded Workday's moat from wide to narrow, and the stock has declined 53% from its 52-week high of $276 to $129.92, reflecting genuine market concern that LLMs could enable AI-native competitors to build enterprise HR and finance platforms without legacy constraints [2]. FY2027 guidance of 12-13% subscription growth represents continued deceleration from 14.5% in FY2026, suggesting organic growth is slowing even as AI investment ramps [1]. The successive layoffs (1,750 in Feb 2025, 400 in Feb 2026) contradict Workday's people-first brand and signal margin pressure. Glassdoor reviews noting cultural erosion and Oracle-style management creep threaten the company's talent advantage [2]. Seat-based pricing models face structural pressure as AI automates tasks, potentially shrinking the addressable market for traditional HCM/finance software [2]. The CEO transition from Eschenbach to Bhusri introduces execution risk during a period requiring focused strategic clarity.
