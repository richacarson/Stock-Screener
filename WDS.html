Investment Thesis
Woodside Energy offers a solid dividend yield (4.76%) backed by long-life LNG assets and conservative leverage (D/E 34%), with near-term catalysts from Scarborough first cargo in Q4 2026. The company's AI-enhanced operations and Q1 2026 revenue beat ($3.26B vs expectations) demonstrate operational competence.
However, significant ESG headwinds—shareholder climate plan rejection, Indigenous rights controversies, and Glass Lewis alerts—create governance risk that could constrain institutional ownership. Declining revenue (-11% YoY), weak ROE (7.2%), and heavy capex for growth projects pressure near-term returns. The stock is fairly valued but lacks catalysts for re-rating until Scarborough delivers and ESG concerns are addressed.
