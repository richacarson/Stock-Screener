Company Profile
Healthcare ·Health Information Services ·NYSE ·United States
Weave Communications, Inc. operates an AI-powered patient communications, engagement, and payments platform for small and medium-sized healthcare businesses. The company offers AI receptionists, unified phone systems, text messaging, review management, online scheduling, digital forms, insurance verification, payment processing, and practice analytics for dental, optometry, veterinary, and medical practices. Founded in 2008 and headquartered in Lehi, Utah.
904 Employees ·www.getweave.com

Investment Thesis
Weave Communications is an AI-powered healthcare communications platform executing a compelling growth trajectory — $239M FY2025 revenue grew 17% YoY with Q4 gross margins hitting a record 73.3% and operating income turning positive at $2.3M [1]. FY2026 guidance of $273-$276M (14-16% growth) with $8-$12M non-GAAP operating income represents a profitability inflection point for this six-time Great Place to Work [1][2]. The TrueLark AI receptionist acquisition accelerates Weave's transition to autonomous front-office automation, with AI revenue expected to ramp in H2 2026 via the omnichannel AI Receptionist product [2]. Payments processing growing at 2x total revenue provides a high-margin expansion vector. At $5.33 per share and $419M market cap (~1.5x FY2026 revenue), Weave trades at a significant discount to healthcare SaaS peers, pricing in execution risk while offering asymmetric upside if AI product adoption accelerates and profitability improves [4]. D/E of 63% and $45M positive free cash flow provide financial stability.
The bear case centers on execution risk and profitability uncertainty. Weave remains GAAP unprofitable with -11.7% net margins and -37.6% ROE, reflecting a business model that has not yet proven sustainable unit economics at scale [4]. The TrueLark acquisition introduces integration risk — merging an AI startup into Weave's existing platform requires seamless technical alignment, and management acknowledged AI revenue contribution is back-end loaded into 2026, meaning near-term expectations may disappoint [2]. Forward P/E of 25.7x on thin forward EPS of $0.21 means any profitability setback would cause significant multiple compression. Competition from larger players (Dentrix/Henry Schein, Patterson Dental) and AI-native entrants could compress Weave's pricing power in the SMB healthcare segment. At only 904 employees and $419M market cap, Weave lacks the scale and resources to compete with well-funded competitors if AI commoditizes healthcare communications tools. Revenue growth is decelerating from 17% to guided 14-16%, and the path from $8-$12M non-GAAP operating income to sustainable GAAP profitability remains uncertain [1][4].

AI Risk Protection (4/9): Weave's core business — healthcare practice communications and engagement software — faces meaningful AI disruption risk from both directions.
