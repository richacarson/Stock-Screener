WEC Energy Group is a Midwestern regulated utility holding company (We Energies, Wisconsin Public Service, Peoples Gas, North Shore Gas, Michigan Gas Utilities, Minnesota Energy Resources) serving 4.7M electric and gas customers. The company is executing a $37.5B capital plan tied to hyperscale data center demand growth from Microsoft, Vantage Data Centers (Oracle/OpenAI), and other I-94 corridor customers.

Investment Thesis
WEC Energy Group is a high-quality Midwestern regulated utility benefiting from a uniquely powerful hyperscale data center load-growth catalyst that is funding a $37.5B capital plan and reaffirmed 7–8% long-term EPS CAGR [1][2]. FY2025 delivered adjusted EPS of $5.27 (up $0.39) and Q4 revenue of $2.54B (+11.3% YoY), and management guides 2026 EPS to $5.51–$5.61 [1]. Microsoft's I-94 corridor complex (+500MW recently added) plus Vantage Data Centers' $15B Oracle/OpenAI buildout provide multi-decade visible load growth, and the board raised the dividend 6.7% in January 2026 — the 23rd consecutive annual increase — now yielding ~3.3% [1][4]. Inspire Insight assigns +41 with 16 positive attributions including GHG Emissions and Environmental Risk Mitigation, making WEC one of the more faith-aligned utilities in the universe [CSV].
The bear case centers on execution risk and environmental strategy tension. Debt-to-equity of 159% and a $37.5B capex program require sustained regulatory capital recovery and access to debt markets at reasonable rates — a rising-rate environment could compress returns and force equity issuance [1][5]. Sierra Club and allied coalitions are publicly opposing WEC's plan to build 1,300MW of new gas plants and convert 2,000MW of coal to gas, a 3,000MW gas footprint that may persist past the 2030 80% carbon reduction deadline, creating regulatory and reputational risk [3]. Reported YoY earnings growth of -32.5% per yfinance reflects one-time items and a mismatch with adjusted EPS; investors must monitor rate cases and equity issuances closely. Forward P/E of 19.4x is fair for a 7–8% EPS grower with a durable dividend, but upside requires data center execution to match management guidance.
Key Catalysts
  1. Microsoft I-94 corridor data center buildout adding 500MW+ to load forecast, with 15 additional data center buildings cleared and energy already flowing to the first phase [1][2]
  2. Vantage Data Centers' $15B Oracle/OpenAI-anchored 670-acre first phase breaking ground in December 2025 and targeting 2028 completion, anchoring rate-base growth through decade [2]
  3. 23rd consecutive dividend increase (6.7% in 2026) with 7–8% long-term EPS CAGR guidance support a durable total-return profile at ~3.3% yield [1][4]
Key Risks
  1. Regulatory and environmental risk — Sierra Club coalition opposition to 3,000MW of new/converted gas plants threatens rate-case outcomes and long-term carbon commitments [3]
  2. Capex financing risk — a $37.5B multi-year capex plan against 159% debt-to-equity requires consistent debt market access; rising rates or credit spreads could force dilutive equity issuance [1][5]
  3. Data center execution risk — if Microsoft or Vantage delay expansion or reduce planned capacity, forecast load growth and the associated rate-base investment thesis would deteriorate [1][2]
