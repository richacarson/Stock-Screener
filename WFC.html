Investment Thesis
Wells Fargo is the most compelling major U.S. bank turnaround story — the March 2026 formal enforcement termination marks the definitive end of the 2016 scandal era, and Scharf declared 'the world is our oyster now' [3][4]. At 9.3x forward earnings with loans exceeding $1T, NII guided at ~$50B, and ROTCE targeting 17-18% (vs. 12% today), the stock trades at a 30%+ discount to JPMorgan despite similar underlying franchise quality [1][2]. The 27% payout ratio provides substantial room for dividend increases as earnings grow toward the ROTCE target.
The bear case centers on whether the 7-year suppression created permanent structural damage — large corporate and wealth clients who left during consent order restrictions may not return regardless of regulatory cleanup. The Q1 revenue miss ($21.4B vs. $21.8B expected) suggests growth acceleration is not yet materializing despite asset cap removal [1]. Consumer banking faces persistent fintech competition in segments where WFC's brand was most damaged, and if Fed rate cuts exceed the 2-3 assumed in guidance, NII compression could prevent the ROTCE improvement thesis from playing out. Sustainable equity funds have largely divested WFC due to scandal history [5].
Key Catalysts
  1. ROTCE improvement from 12% toward 17-18% target as freed balance sheet enables loan growth and resolved consent orders reduce compliance costs
  2. Corporate and investment banking wallet share gains with institutional clients returning after asset cap removal — loans already exceeded $1T
  3. AI-driven efficiency delivering expense savings within $55.7B guidance while 30-35% engineering productivity gains scale across the enterprise
Key Risks
  1. Revenue growth failing to accelerate despite regulatory freedom — Q1 2026 revenue missed expectations at $21.4B vs. $21.8B consensus
  2. Credit quality deterioration as loan growth extends into riskier segments during potential economic softening
  3. Federal Reserve rate cuts exceeding the 2-3 assumed in NII guidance, compressing the ~$50B NII projection and delaying ROTCE improvement
