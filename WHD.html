Company Profile
Energy ·Oil & Gas Equipment & Services ·NYSE ·United States
Cactus designs, manufactures, sells, and rents engineered pressure control and spoolable pipe technologies for onshore unconventional oil and gas wells. The company operates through Pressure Control (wellheads under the Cactus Wellhead brand) and Spoolable Technologies (FlexSteel pipes for production and gathering pipelines) segments, serving customers in the U.S., Australia, Canada, and the Middle East.

Investment Thesis
Cactus is executing a transformative growth strategy through its January 2026 acquisition of 65% of Baker Hughes' Surface Pressure Control business, which provides a $16.6 billion backlog and is expected to drive 35.5% revenue growth in Q1 2026 [1]. The company's balance sheet is pristine (D/E 2.6%, $123.6M cash vs $37.7M debt) with strong operating cash flow of $258.4M, and the forward P/E of 16.0x is reasonable for a company entering a major growth phase [4]. Q4 2025 results beat estimates with revenue of $261M and adjusted EPS of $0.65 vs $0.58 expected, demonstrating operational execution [1]. The dual-segment model (Pressure Control + Spoolable Technologies) provides diversification within oilfield services.
The bear case centers on oil price cyclicality and integration risk. Cactus's revenue is tied to U.S. onshore drilling activity, which remains sensitive to WTI crude prices — a sustained downturn below $60/bbl would compress both equipment sales and rental activity. The Baker Hughes JV integration introduces execution risk: managing a 65/35 joint venture with a major competitor requires navigating complex operational and cultural dynamics. Revenue declined 4% YoY in 2025 (pre-acquisition), and the company's free cash flow turned negative (-$194.7M) due to acquisition-related capital deployment [4]. The dividend yield of 1.0% offers minimal income support, and the stock trades near its 52-week high ($59.25), limiting near-term upside if integration hiccups emerge.
