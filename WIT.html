Company Profile
Technology ·Information Technology Services ·NYSE ·India
Wipro Limited is a global IT consulting and business process services company headquartered in Bengaluru, India. Operating through IT Services and IT Products segments, Wipro serves enterprises across banking, healthcare, manufacturing, and technology sectors with digital strategy, cloud infrastructure, AI-powered solutions, and systems integration services.

Excellence Evaluation — Think Like an Owner (50%)
Innovation DEVELOPING
5
Wipro has invested significantly in AI capabilities, with CEO Srini Pallia declaring at Davos 2026 that 'AI is driving a new IT services boom' and CTO forecasting agentic AI to drive autonomous enterprises by 2026 [1]. The company launched a new operating model combining advisory, AI, and enterprise transformation services in early 2026 [2]. However, Morningstar assigns Wipro a 'no moat' rating, noting the company lacks differentiated IP and competes primarily on labor cost arbitrage rather than genuine technological innovation [3].
Inspiration DEVELOPING
5
Wipro benefits from the legacy of founder Azim Premji, whose philanthropic foundation has donated over $21B to education and social causes, giving the company a unique inspirational heritage among IT services peers. CEO Srini Pallia has maintained Wipro's values-driven culture, and the company participates actively in the World Economic Forum [1]. However, with 230,000 employees in a commoditized IT services market, individual employee engagement and mission connection inevitably dilute at scale.

Investment Thesis
Wipro offers an attractive 9.2% dividend yield at a reasonable forward P/E of 14.5x with minimal debt (D/E 22%), making it a compelling income play in the IT services sector. CEO Pallia's AI-first strategy pivot and the company's new consulting-led operating model position Wipro to capture enterprise AI transformation spending, with the CTO projecting agentic AI will drive autonomous enterprises by 2026 [1][2]. Revenue growth has returned at 5.5% YoY, and the company's $10.7B revenue base provides scale advantages in competing for large enterprise deals.
The bear case centers on AI disruption risk — Morningstar's 'no moat' rating and assessment that Wipro is 'vulnerable to AI's potential disruption' reflect the existential threat that AI code generation, automated testing, and enterprise AI agents could structurally reduce demand for traditional IT services labor [3]. The 86.9% payout ratio leaves minimal margin for error or reinvestment, earnings declined 7.2% YoY, and revenue CAGR of just 4% over three years significantly lags industry leaders TCS and Infosys. The ADR stock price of $2.09 reflects the market's skepticism about Wipro's ability to navigate the AI transition while maintaining margins.
Key Catalysts
  1. Successful scaling of AI consulting practice and enterprise transformation deals, with Wipro targeting 20%+ of new deal revenue from AI-powered services by FY2027 [2]
  2. IT services spending recovery driven by enterprise AI adoption — Wipro CEO notes AI is 'driving a new IT services boom' at Davos 2026 [1]
  3. Potential margin expansion from automation of delivery processes, reducing headcount dependency while maintaining revenue per engagement
Key Risks
  1. AI-driven structural disruption to the IT services labor model — generative AI could reduce demand for 30-40% of traditional IT services tasks within 3-5 years, directly threatening Wipro's 230,000-person workforce model [3]
  2. High payout ratio of 86.9% leaves no buffer if earnings deteriorate, creating dividend cut risk that would remove the stock's primary investment thesis
  3. Persistent underperformance versus TCS and Infosys in growth, margins, and deal wins could lead to market share erosion in a consolidating industry
