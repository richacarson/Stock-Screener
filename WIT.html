Company Profile
Technology ·Information Technology Services ·NYSE ·India
Wipro Limited is a global IT consulting and business process services company headquartered in Bengaluru, India. Operating through IT Services and IT Products segments, Wipro serves enterprises across banking, healthcare, manufacturing, and technology sectors with digital strategy, cloud infrastructure, AI-powered solutions, and systems integration services. IT Services revenue reached $10.48B in FY2026. Founded in 1945, trades as ADRs on NYSE.
240,000 Employees ·www.wipro.com

Investment Thesis
Wipro offers an attractive 8.6% dividend yield at a reasonable 14.2x forward P/E with minimal debt (D/E 22.9%) and a net cash balance sheet, making it one of the most accessible income plays in global IT services [4]. FY2026 marked a return to growth with 7.7% YoY revenue expansion to $10.48B and operating margin expansion to 17.2% [1]. CEO Pallia's AI-first strategy, including the Anthropic partnership and Applied AI Center of Excellence, positions Wipro to capture enterprise AI transformation spending as clients modernize legacy systems [2]. The 150B INR share buyback demonstrates shareholder-friendly capital allocation, and the stock's $2.24 ADR price provides a low-cost entry point.
The bear case centers on existential AI disruption risk. Morningstar's 'no moat' rating and explicit assessment that Wipro is 'vulnerable to AI's potential disruption' reflect the structural threat that generative AI, automated testing, and agentic AI platforms could reduce demand for 30-40% of traditional IT services tasks within 3-5 years, directly threatening the 240,000-person workforce model [1]. The 87.9% payout ratio (including buybacks) leaves no reinvestment buffer if earnings deteriorate. Q1 FY2027 guidance of -2% to 0% sequential growth signals near-term softness after the FY2026 recovery. Wipro has persistently underperformed TCS and Infosys in growth, margins, and deal wins, and the current AI pivot mirrors peer strategies rather than establishing differentiated positioning. The stock's discount to peers may be structurally warranted rather than a buying opportunity.
Key Catalysts
  1. Successful scaling of AI consulting practice through Anthropic partnership and Applied AI CoE, with AI-powered deals potentially driving 20%+ of new bookings by FY2028
  2. Enterprise IT services spending recovery as AI adoption drives modernization demand — CEO Pallia projects AI is 'driving a new IT services boom' [2]
  3. Margin expansion from delivery automation reducing headcount dependency while maintaining revenue per engagement, potentially lifting operating margin from 17.2% toward 19-20%
Key Risks
  1. AI-driven structural disruption to the IT services labor model — generative AI and agentic platforms could reduce demand for 30-40% of traditional IT services tasks, threatening the billable-hours revenue model
  2. High payout ratio of 87.9% (including buybacks) leaves no buffer if earnings deteriorate, creating dividend cut risk that would undermine the primary investment thesis
  3. Persistent competitive gap versus TCS and Infosys in growth, margins, and large deal wins could widen as AI transformation spending concentrates with market leaders

AI Resilience (25%)
AI Resilience MODERATE
4
AI Risk Protection (3/9): Wipro's core business of IT services delivery — application development, maintenance, testing, and infrastructure management — faces direct substitution risk from AI-powered code generation, automated testing, and agentic AI platforms that enable enterprises to accomplish more with fewer services workers [1]. Morningstar explicitly rates Wipro as 'vulnerable to AI's potential disruption,' noting the company lacks a competitive moat and competes primarily on labor cost arbitrage. While Wipro is investing heavily in AI consulting capabilities, the fundamental threat is structural: AI reduces the billable-hours-per-project that drives IT services revenue. The 240,000-person workforce is both the asset and the liability. AI Opportunity (1/1): Wipro generates meaningful AI-related revenue through its consulting-led AI transformation practice, Applied AI Center of Excellence with Anthropic, and enterprise AI implementation services, though specific AI revenue figures are not separately disclosed [2].
