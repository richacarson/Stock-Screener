Company Profile

Waste Management is North America's largest environmental services company, providing collection, transfer, recycling, resource recovery, and disposal services to residential, commercial, industrial, and municipal customers. Investment Thesis
WM is the dominant North American waste management operator generating $25.2B in revenue with improving operational efficiency — operating expenses fell below 60% of revenue for the first time in company history, driving EBITDA margin to 31.3% in Q4 2025 [1]. Free cash flow grew 27% to $2.94B with ~30% growth guided for 2026, supported by core pricing power (5.4-5.8% price increases), volume recovery, and $235M-$255M sustainability EBITDA contribution [1]. The Stericycle acquisition adds a high-margin medical waste platform with significant cross-sell opportunities. At 24.1x forward earnings with mid-single-digit revenue growth accelerating to high-single-digit EPS growth, WM offers defensive compounding in an essential-services business with multi-decade landfill asset lives [4].
The bear case centers on elevated leverage and valuation. D/E of 239% from the Stericycle acquisition constrains financial flexibility and increases interest expense in a higher-rate environment [4]. Profit margin of 10.7% is below sector leaders, and revenue growth of 7.1% YoY is modest for a stock trading at 33.6x trailing earnings [4]. The 1.52% dividend yield is below the 2% threshold for income investors despite 21 consecutive years of growth. Recycling commodity price volatility creates earnings lumpiness, and EPR legislation spreading across states could impose new regulatory costs. WM's workforce reduction plan (5,000 positions by 2026) through automation creates execution risk if safety or service quality suffers during the transition [2].
Key Catalysts
  1. Free cash flow guided to grow ~30% in 2026 to approximately $3.8B, enabling accelerated debt paydown and shareholder returns post-Stericycle integration [1]
  2. Sustainability segment EBITDA of $235M-$255M in 2026 from renewable energy and recycling, scaling toward a material earnings contributor [1]
  3. Core pricing power of 5.4-5.8% with volume recovery of 0.2-0.6% driving organic revenue growth above inflation [1]
Key Risks
  1. Elevated leverage at 239% D/E from $23.9B total debt constrains financial flexibility and increases refinancing risk if rates remain elevated [4]
  2. Recycling commodity price volatility and potential wildfire-related volume headwinds (50bps guided impact) create near-term earnings variability [1]
  3. Workforce automation transition — eliminating 5,000 positions while maintaining service quality and safety standards — creates operational execution risk [2]
