Investment Thesis
The only bull case for Meiwu is asset value: as of year-end 2024, the company held $43.4M cash against a $5M market cap, implying the stock trades at roughly 10 cents on the dollar of cash value [5]. Revenue rebounded from $158K (FY2024) to $7.1M TTM, suggesting some operational recovery. The AI skincare initiative, if successfully executed, could provide a differentiated product in China's large functional skincare market [3]. The low D/E of 0.08% means the balance sheet carries virtually no debt risk.
The bear case is overwhelming. Meiwu has received a Nasdaq delisting determination, implemented a 1:20 reverse stock split, and reported a $18.2M net loss on $7.1M revenue in its 2025 20-F filing [2][6]. Cash declined from $43.4M to $17.9M in a single year due to $41M in investing cash outflows, raising serious questions about cash deployment and governance [2]. The BVI holding company structure with Chinese operating subsidiaries means US shareholders face VIE risk, capital repatriation uncertainty, PRC regulatory intervention, and HFCAA compliance risk [2]. Revenue history is wildly volatile — $12.3M (2021), $11M (2022-2023), $158K (2024), $7.1M (TTM) — with no evidence of a sustainable business model in any segment. Market cap of $5M and daily dollar volume of $2.8M make this stock essentially uninvestable for institutional capital.
