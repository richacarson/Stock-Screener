Investment Thesis
Petco is a turnaround story executing on margin recovery and deleveraging under Joel Anderson, the ex-Five Below CEO who grew that chain from 361 to 1,600+ stores and $500M to $3.5B revenue [2]. FY2025 marked a return to profitability ($9.1M net income vs -$101.8M loss in 2024), Adjusted EBITDA rose 21.3% to $408M, free cash flow surged 276% to $187M, and net debt/EBITDA fell to 3.0x from 4.2x — real deleveraging and cost discipline [1]. Anderson has publicly deployed named AI initiatives (predictive delivery, digital twin store monitoring, GenAI customer tools, RFID+AR associate tools), and 2026 Adjusted EBITDA guidance of $415-430M continues the improvement trajectory [2][1]. Forward P/E of 11.63 on a sustainable turnaround would re-rate meaningfully.
The bear case is that Petco is structurally disadvantaged in a Chewy/Amazon/Walmart-dominated pet market. FY2025 net sales declined 2.5% to $6.0B, Q4 comparable sales fell 1.6%, and 2026 guidance of flat to 1.5% growth with 15-20 net store closures confirms the top-line is not yet growing [1]. Profit margin of 0.15% and ROE of 0.80% are anemic, and D/E of 247.9% with $1.6B debt against $1.2B equity leaves little cushion if the consumer slows [4]. Three CEOs in four years and a Comparably 'D' executive-team rating signal cultural instability at odds with the 'pet-first culture' messaging [2]. The 2024 $1.75M misleading-eco-claims fine on biodegradable waste bags is a concrete governance failure [3].
