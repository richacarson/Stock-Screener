Company Profile
Healthcare ·Health Information Services ·NASDAQ ·United States
SCWorx Corp. develops and markets healthcare information technology solutions and associated services that improve healthcare processes and information flow within hospitals and other healthcare facilities. Software modules include electronic medical record management, charge description master management, contract management, RFP automation, rebate management, big data analytics, and data integration and warehousing. Based in Middleton, Massachusetts with only 9 full-time employees. Facing Nasdaq delisting after trading was suspended April 14, 2026.

Investment Thesis
SCWorx is a micro-cap healthcare IT company with $2.88M in trailing revenue, 9 employees, and a $288K market cap that is facing Nasdaq delisting after failing the $1.00 minimum bid price requirement — trading was suspended April 14, 2026 [1][4]. Q1 2026 showed marginal improvement with revenue of $745K (up from $720K YoY) and net loss narrowing to $174K from $476K, but the company has posted cumulative net losses exceeding $35M against total equity of $7.2M (inflated by $5.8M in goodwill). Cash of $1.34M at Q1 2026 end provides limited runway.
The fundamental problem is scale: SCWorx competes against Epic, Oracle Health, and other healthcare IT giants with 9 employees and negligible market share in healthcare data management. Revenue has declined from $4.0M in 2022 to $2.9M in 2025, and the company has not demonstrated any product innovation or market traction that would reverse this trajectory [1][2]. The Nasdaq delisting and sub-penny stock price create severe liquidity risk — daily dollar volume of ~$88K makes the stock essentially untradeable for institutional investors. Even if the company successfully appeals delisting, the underlying business does not generate sufficient revenue or margin to sustain operations without continued dilutive equity raises.
Key Catalysts
  1. Successful Nasdaq delisting appeal that restores trading and maintains public company status, potentially stabilizing the stock price above $1.00
  2. New CTO Anders Ohlsson delivers a differentiated AI-powered healthcare data product that opens a new revenue stream and reverses the multi-year revenue decline
  3. Strategic acquisition or partnership with a larger healthcare IT company that provides distribution scale for SCWorx's data management modules
Key Risks
  1. Nasdaq delisting proceeds as scheduled, moving shares to OTC markets and eliminating remaining institutional investor interest, effectively making the equity worthless
  2. Cash runway of $1.34M with quarterly burn rates suggests the company may need additional dilutive capital raises within 6-12 months, further destroying per-share value
  3. AI-powered data management tools from Epic, Oracle Health, and emerging startups render SCWorx's legacy data normalization software obsolete, accelerating revenue decline
