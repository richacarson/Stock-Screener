SCWorx Corp. provides cloud-based data management, normalization, and interoperability solutions for healthcare providers in the United States, primarily supporting hospital Workday ERP implementations with item master file repair, charge description master management, and data governance services. The company has 7 employees, $2.78M in trailing twelve-month revenue, and a market cap of $3M. It faces Nasdaq delisting for falling below the $1.00 minimum bid price requirement, with a compliance deadline of April 6, 2026.

Investment Thesis
SCWorx's only viable bull case is acquisition: the company has a working SaaS platform, documented hospital customer relationships with long-term contracts, and a niche in supporting Workday implementations — a growing enterprise market. A larger healthcare IT firm could acquire SCWorx for its customer base and technology at a premium to the $3M market cap [1].
The bear case is overwhelming: WORX fails IOWN's basic liquidity threshold by approximately 97% ($27K avg daily value vs. $1M minimum); the stock has lost 89.6% of its value in 52 weeks; Nasdaq delisting by April 6, 2026, is likely given the $0.1373 stock price; an Altman Z-Score of -5.8 signals extreme financial distress; and the company has 7 employees generating $2.78M in revenue against $3.89M in net losses. This is a company in terminal decline absent an acquisition or capital infusion [1].
