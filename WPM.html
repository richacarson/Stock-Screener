Company Profile
Basic Materials ·Other Precious Metals & Mining ·NYSE ·Canada
Wheaton Precious Metals is the world's largest pure-play precious metals streaming company, providing upfront capital to mining partners in exchange for the right to purchase a percentage of future gold, silver, palladium, and cobalt production at a fixed low price. The streaming model offers leveraged commodity exposure without operational mining risk. Haytham Hodaly succeeded co-founder Randy Smallwood as CEO effective March 31, 2026; Smallwood became Chair [3].

Investment Thesis
Wheaton Precious Metals is the world's largest pure-play precious metals streaming company, generating capital-light, high-margin cash flow from contracted gold/silver/palladium/cobalt streams across world-class mines [4]. Q3 2025 revenue of $476M (+55%) and net earnings of $367M (+138%) reflect the operating leverage of streams to rising commodity prices, and Q4 2025 EPS of $1.22 beat consensus by 18.6% [1]. 2025 production exceeded guidance and 2026 production is set to grow 11.3%, with 40% production growth by 2029 already contracted from existing streams [1][4]. The new CEO Haytham Hodaly (March 31, 2026) inherits a debt-free, high-quality portfolio from Randy Smallwood, who remains as Chair after a planned, orderly succession [3]. At $144.45 with 28.9x forward P/E, WPM is fairly valued for the streaming model's leverage to gold/silver prices and contracted production growth.
The bear case starts with valuation: at 44.7x trailing P/E and 28.9x forward, WPM is priced for sustained gold/silver price strength and execution on the 40% production runway [5]. A reversal in precious metals prices would compress earnings sharply given the streaming model's operating leverage. The new CEO transition (Hodaly succeeding Smallwood) introduces execution risk, though the deep co-founder bench should mitigate it. Mining-partner risk remains — environmental/operational issues at any major partner mine (Salobo, Antamina, Penasquito) could disrupt deliveries. The Inspire score of 0 (MIXED) reflects neutral attributions; some faith investors may have concerns about exposure to mining-partner ESG controversies even if WPM itself is not the operator. Dividend yield of 0.48% is modest for income investors.
