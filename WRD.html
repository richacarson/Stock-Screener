Investment Thesis
WeRide is the most globally diversified autonomous driving company in the world — the only AV firm with permits in eight countries, deployed across 40+ cities in 12 nations, and dual-listed on both NASDAQ and HKEX. FY2025 revenue surged 90% to RMB 685M with robotaxi revenue up 210%, Q4 revenue beat expectations at RMB 314M (up 123% YoY), and net losses narrowed 34% [1]. The Uber partnership (15-city, 5-year rollout, 1,200 vehicles in Middle East by 2027) provides a capital-efficient commercialization path, and the 2,600-vehicle fleet target by end-2026 sets the stage for the company's $1B annual ride-hailing revenue target [1]. At $2.7B market cap with RMB 7.1B ($1B) cash, the enterprise value implies a modest premium for the leading international autonomous driving platform. Singapore public passenger service launching April 2026 provides near-term catalyst [1][2].
The bear case centers on persistent unprofitability and geopolitical risk. WeRide lost RMB 1.65B on RMB 685M revenue in FY2025, with -242% profit margins and no clear timeline to breakeven — CEO Han admits profitability timing is 'difficult to predict' [1][3]. The $1B cash war chest provides ~2 years of runway at current burn, but additional fundraising may be needed at dilutive terms. U.S.-China geopolitical tensions directly threaten WeRide — President Biden's proposed ban on Chinese autonomous driving software above Level 3 could lock WeRide out of the U.S. market, and similar restrictions could emerge in Europe [3]. Competition from Waymo ($200B+ Alphabet parent), Tesla (FSD + massive fleet data), and Baidu Apollo creates winner-take-most dynamics where WeRide's $2.7B market cap cannot match rivals' R&D spending. The 30% gross margin, while improving from 6.5% in Q3 2024, must expand significantly to cover operating expenses at scale [1][4].
