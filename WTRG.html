Company Profile
Utilities ·Utilities - Regulated Water ·NYSE ·United States
Essential Utilities is one of the largest publicly traded water, wastewater, and natural gas providers in the U.S., serving approximately 5.5 million people across 9 states under the Aqua and Peoples brands. The company invests heavily in regulated infrastructure, has paid dividends for 80 consecutive years, and announced an all-stock merger of equals with American Water (NYSE: AWK) in 2025 to create the leading U.S. regulated water and wastewater utility.

Investment Thesis
Essential Utilities is a high-quality regulated water and natural gas utility with one of the longest dividend track records in the U.S. — 80 consecutive years of payments, 35 increases in 34 years, and a 5.25% raise in July 2025 [1][3]. Q4 2025 EPS of $0.47 beat consensus by 17.5% and full-year revenue of ~$2.5B grew 18.6% YoY, demonstrating that rate cases and tuck-in M&A continue to translate into earnings power. The 2025 announcement of an all-stock merger of equals with American Water (AWK) would create the leading U.S. regulated water/wastewater utility, unlocking scale economies and meaningfully expanding rate-base growth opportunities [3]. With $1.7B of 2026 capex, $450M+ committed to PFAS remediation, and a 5-7% long-term EPS CAGR target through 2027, Essential offers regulated, inflation-hedged growth at a 3.2% dividend yield [3].
The bear case centers on rate-case risk, leverage, and merger execution. ROE of 9.4% is below the 15% target (typical for capital-intensive utilities) but signals limited ability to organically fund the $1.7B capex without continued debt and equity issuance — D/E is already 121.6%. Rising interest rates raise the cost of utility capex programs and pressure dividend coverage; the 2025 EPS decline of ~30% YoY illustrates rate-lag and weather sensitivity. The American Water merger is subject to regulatory approval across multiple state utility commissions, with closing risk and integration complexity that could delay synergy realization [3]. PFAS regulation creates material capex obligations that may not be fully recoverable in rates if state commissions push back. Forward P/E of 16.3 is fair but not cheap for a 5-7% EPS grower in a rising-rate environment.
Key Catalysts
  1. American Water (AWK) merger close — creates the leading U.S. regulated water/wastewater utility with scale economies and expanded rate-base growth runway [3]
  2. Continued tuck-in water/wastewater M&A — three additional signed deals expected to close in 2026 add to the 12,700 customers acquired in 2025 [1]
  3. $1.7B 2026 capex program plus PFAS remediation drives regulated rate-base growth and supports the 5-7% long-term EPS CAGR target through 2027 [3]
Key Risks
  1. Regulatory approval risk on the American Water merger — multi-state commission reviews could delay close, force divestitures, or limit synergy realization [3]
  2. Rate-case lag and rising-rate environment — EPS fell ~30% YoY in 2025 reflecting weather and rate-case timing; continued cost-of-capital pressure could compress earnings
  3. PFAS regulatory and capex risk — $450M+ commitment is a floor, not a ceiling; if rate recovery is delayed by state commissions, dividend coverage could tighten [3]
