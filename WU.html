Investment Thesis
Western Union at 4.7x forward earnings with a 10.5% dividend yield represents deep contrarian value if the Intermex acquisition and digital transformation execute successfully [1]. Branded digital grew 7% with transactions up 13%, digital account payouts surged 30%, and consumer services revenue grew 26% — showing the growth businesses within WU are performing. The USDPT stablecoin and stable card could position WU at the intersection of crypto and remittances. Adjusted FCF conversion above 100% for three consecutive years demonstrates cash generation capability despite revenue decline.
Western Union is widely viewed as a value trap. The 10.5% dividend has been frozen at $0.235/quarter since 2021, core CMT revenue declined 7% in Q4, and organic 2026 revenue is expected 'closer to flattish' [1]. The Intermex acquisition adds $650M in revenue but increases leverage to 2.1x and carries integration risk. Digital-native competitors Wise and Remitly offer 50-70% lower fees with superior user experience, structurally disadvantaging WU's agent network. The U.S. remittance tax risk and immigration policy uncertainty add regulatory headwinds. The 324% D/E ratio limits financial flexibility, and five years of dividend stagnation suggests management does not expect meaningful earnings recovery. The guidance caveat assuming 'no material changes' in macro or immigration policy is an unusually broad disclaimer.
Key Catalysts
  1. Intermex acquisition closing in Q2 2026 adds $650M revenue and $30M cost synergies, potentially driving the 6-9% adjusted revenue growth guidance [1]
  2. USDPT stablecoin launch and stable card deployment in 12+ countries in 2H 2026 could attract crypto-native remittance users and reduce transfer costs [1]
  3. Travel money business approaching $150M revenue in 2026 (from near zero a few years ago) via Eurochange demonstrates successful new business creation
Key Risks
  1. Core Consumer Money Transfer declining 7% YoY reflects structural market share loss to digital competitors (Wise, Remitly) that may accelerate [1]
  2. Proposed U.S. remittance tax could reduce transaction volumes — CEO's claim of 'no material impact' through six weeks is insufficient evidence for a full-year assessment
  3. The frozen $0.235 quarterly dividend since 2021 may face cut risk if Intermex integration costs or organic revenue deterioration compress FCF below the ~$500M annual dividend burden [1]
