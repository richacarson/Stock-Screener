Investment Thesis
TeraWulf has secured $12.8B+ in long-term HPC/AI contracts with hyperscalers (Google Cloud, AWS), operates a 2.8 GW platform across five sites, and Morgan Stanley initiated coverage with an Overweight rating and $37 price target (145% upside from $15.10) [1]. The company's access to clean, cost-effective power positions it as attractive infrastructure for AI data centers. Revenue growth of 132% is projected for 2026 as HPC capacity comes online, and valuation multiples could expand to 21-22x 2026 revenues as the market reclassifies TeraWulf from a bitcoin miner to an AI infrastructure company.
The bear case centers on extreme financial leverage: $5.2B in debt (D/E 3,699%) with $661.4M net loss in 2025 and negative operating cash flow [1]. Q4 revenue of $35.8M missed consensus by 19% as mining revenue declined. The company has zero track record operating AI data centers, creating execution risk against established competitors like Equinix. Bitcoin at ~$60K versus ~$87K mining cost means the legacy business is cash-flow negative during the transition. The stock is 18% below its $18.51 52-week high, and the capital-intensive HPC buildout requires additional financing that could further leverage the balance sheet [1].
