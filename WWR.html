Company Profile
Basic Materials ·Other Industrial Metals & Mining ·NYSE American ·United States
Westwater Resources is a pre-revenue energy technology company developing battery-grade natural graphite materials in the United States. It holds interests in the Coosa Graphite project in Alabama and operates the Kellyton Graphite Processing Plant qualification line, capable of producing over one metric ton per day of coated spherical purified graphite for customer sampling.

Investment Thesis
Westwater Resources is positioned in a strategically important niche — domestic U.S. graphite processing for EV batteries and energy storage — supported by IRA incentives and growing urgency to de-risk supply chains from China [1]. The Kellyton qualification line is operational, producing CSPG samples for customer testing, and the company holds approximately $48.6M in cash with minimal debt of $6.3M [3]. The Coosa Graphite deposit in Alabama provides a domestic feedstock source, and Phase I targets 7,500 MT/year capacity with expansion potential [1].
However, the investment case is almost entirely speculative. Westwater is pre-revenue with a $83M market cap, negative operating cash flow of -$9.9M annually, and needs $200M+ in financing to build Phase I of Kellyton — financing that has not been secured [1]. The stock has declined 82% from its 52-week high of $3.75 to $0.67, reflecting deep market skepticism about financing prospects and execution [3]. With only 20 employees, the organization lacks the scale to execute a major construction project without significant outside support. The graphite market is dominated by Chinese processors operating at much lower cost, and customer qualification timelines remain uncertain. The stock fails IOWN's liquidity floor with daily dollar volume under $1M [3].
Key Catalysts
  1. Securing $200M+ project financing for Kellyton Phase I construction, which would transform the company from development-stage to construction-stage [1]
  2. Customer qualification completion with major battery or EV manufacturers, providing demand visibility and potential offtake agreements [2]
  3. Expansion of IRA critical mineral incentives or tariffs on Chinese graphite imports, increasing the competitive advantage of domestic U.S. supply [1]
Key Risks
  1. Financing risk — the company needs over $200M that has not been secured, and equity raises at $0.67/share would be massively dilutive to existing shareholders [1]
  2. Liquidity risk — daily dollar volume under $1M makes the stock difficult to trade in size, trapping institutional investors [3]
  3. Execution risk — transitioning from a 1 MT/day qualification line to a 7,500 MT/year commercial plant is an enormous operational leap for a 20-person company [2]
