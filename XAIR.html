Investment Thesis
Beyond Air is a commercial-stage micro-cap medical device company with a differentiated nitric oxide delivery platform (LungFit PH) that generates NO from ambient air, eliminating the need for costly compressed gas cylinders [1]. Revenue is accelerating — Q3 FY2026 revenue of $2.2M was up 105% YoY, with management guiding $8-10M for full-year FY2026 [1][2]. Customer retention exceeds 90% with more than half of customers on multiyear agreements, providing revenue visibility [1]. The business is effectively immune to AI disruption given the physical, FDA-regulated nature of medical device manufacturing and NICU deployment. Massive insider buying (8.5M shares purchased vs. 370K sold) signals management conviction, and the company's pro forma cash of $22.3M funds operations into 2027 [1][5].
However, the investment case is severely undermined by fundamental financial weakness. At a $6.3M market cap and $0.48 stock price, Beyond Air fails the liquidity floor test — daily dollar volume of ~$152K makes it nearly impossible for institutional investors to participate, and the Infrastructure score is overridden to 0 [5]. The company is deeply unprofitable with a -462.58% profit margin, burning $32M annually against just $6.92M in revenue. Cash runway extends only to 2027, creating dilution risk if the company needs to raise capital at these depressed levels. The CEO transition — Steve Lisi resigned in 2026 and was replaced by former CCO Robert Goodman — introduces leadership uncertainty at a critical commercial inflection point [3]. Sustainalytics rates ESG management as 'Weak' [4]. Revenue growth, while impressive in percentage terms, is from a negligible base, and the path to profitability remains unclear. This is a speculative micro-cap with binary risk — the technology may be promising, but the financial profile does not meet IOWN screening standards.

AI Resilience (25%)
AI Resilience LOW RISK
8
AI Risk Protection (8/9): Beyond Air's LungFit platform is effectively immune to AI disruption. The company manufactures physical medical devices — nitric oxide generators and delivery systems — used in neonatal intensive care units for treating hypoxic respiratory failure [1]. FDA-regulated medical devices require extensive clinical validation, manufacturing quality controls, and regulatory clearance that create formidable barriers AI cannot circumvent. The physical nature of the product (hardware + consumables), the clinical setting (NICUs requiring hands-on medical intervention), and the regulatory framework (FDA Class II/III device clearance) make this business fundamentally resistant to AI substitution. The only minor vulnerability is that AI could eventually optimize competitive device designs or accelerate rival companies' R&D cycles. AI Opportunity (0/1): Beyond Air has not deployed AI in its operations, manufacturing, or product development in any revenue-generating capacity. No AI-enhanced features in the LungFit platform have been announced [5].
