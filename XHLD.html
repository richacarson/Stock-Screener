Company Profile
Communication Services ·Broadcasting ·NASDAQ ·United States
TEN Holdings provides event planning, production, and broadcasting services in the United States, organizing virtual, hybrid, self-service, and physical events including conferences, product launches, trainings, and shareholder meetings. The company's platforms (Xyvid Pro and TEN Pro) are internet-based broadcast platforms with interactive engagement tools. Formerly known as The Events Network, Inc., the company was founded in 2011 and is headquartered in Langhorne, Pennsylvania.
25 Employees ·www.tenholdingsinc.com

Investment Thesis
TEN Holdings represents a speculative micro-cap bet on the virtual events SaaS pivot. Analyst projections forecast revenue growing from $3.1M to ~$24.6M in 2026 if the Ten Events Pro platform and Webinar.net partnership gain traction, which would represent a transformative revenue acceleration [1]. The forward P/E of 0.87x on analyst estimates of $1.65 EPS implies explosive earnings potential if the company executes. The V-Cube AI partnership adds conversational AI capabilities, and SOC 2 certification positions the platform for enterprise clients with compliance requirements [2]. At a $5.7M market cap, even modest commercial success could drive significant share price appreciation.
The bear case is overwhelming for all but the most speculative investors. TEN Holdings generated only $3.1M in revenue in 2025 (down 35% over three years), operates at a loss with -$10.1M in operating cash flow, and has only $1.6M in cash against $5.1M in debt [1][4]. The company has 25 employees competing against Zoom ($4.5B+ revenue), Microsoft Teams, and dozens of well-funded virtual event platforms. Revenue has declined every year since 2022, and the analyst projections of $24.6M revenue in 2026 require an 8x revenue increase that has no historical precedent at this company. The $5.7M market cap is smaller than most VC seed rounds, and the stock trades at $1.43 with penny-stock volatility. Dilution risk is severe if the company needs capital to fund operations, and the cash burn rate suggests additional financing will be required within 12 months [4].
