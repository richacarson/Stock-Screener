Company Profile

Xometry operates an AI-powered online manufacturing marketplace connecting buyers with suppliers of custom-manufactured parts. The platform uses machine learning for instant quoting across CNC machining, injection molding, 3D printing, and sheet forming, serving aerospace, automotive, medical, and defense industries. Also operates Thomasnet industrial sourcing platform.

Investment Thesis
Xometry is the largest AI-powered on-demand manufacturing marketplace, delivering 29.5% YoY revenue growth to $686.6M in 2025 while achieving its first full year of positive adjusted EBITDA ($18.5M vs. -$9.7M prior year) [1]. The company guided at least 21% revenue growth for 2026, targeting the $1B revenue milestone, with incremental EBITDA margins of at least 20% signaling operating leverage as the marketplace scales [1]. Q1 2026 guidance of $187-189M (24-25% YoY growth) exceeded consensus, and the AI Instant Quoting Engine creates a data flywheel that strengthens with each transaction. Enterprise adoption is expanding and the total addressable market for custom manufacturing exceeds $2 trillion globally.
The bear case centers on valuation and profitability risk. At 45.6x forward P/E, the stock prices in significant earnings growth that depends on sustained 20%+ revenue growth AND margin expansion simultaneously. GAAP net income remains negative (-9% profit margin) despite revenue scale, and the -20.8% ROE reflects ongoing cash burn [4]. The 2026 leadership transition (CEO stepping to Executive Chairman) creates execution uncertainty during a critical growth phase. Glassdoor reviews (46% recommend, 3.0/5 culture) suggest employee satisfaction challenges that could hinder scaling. Competition from Protolabs, Amazon Business, and enterprise procurement platforms could compress marketplace take rates. D/E of 122.7% from convertible debt adds financial risk if growth decelerates.
Key Catalysts
  1. Path to GAAP profitability in 2026 as EBITDA margins expand beyond 20% incrementally, with potential to reach $1B revenue milestone [1]
  2. Enterprise adoption acceleration — large manufacturers increasingly using Xometry for procurement automation, driving higher-value recurring transactions [1]
  3. AI Instant Quoting Engine data flywheel — each transaction improves pricing accuracy, creating compounding competitive advantage versus manual quoting competitors [2]
Key Risks
  1. Valuation risk at 45.6x forward P/E requires sustained 20%+ growth and margin expansion; any deceleration could trigger significant multiple compression [4]
  2. Leadership transition risk — CEO Randy Altschuler moving to Executive Chairman in 2026 during critical scaling phase creates execution uncertainty [1]
  3. Platform competition — Amazon Business, large ERP vendors (SAP Ariba), and Protolabs could erode marketplace take rates through pricing pressure or enterprise bundling
