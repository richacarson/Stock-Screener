Company Profile
Industrials ·Electrical Equipment & Parts ·NASDAQ ·United States
Expion360 Inc. designs, assembles, and sells lithium iron phosphate (LiFePO4) batteries and accessories for recreational vehicles, marine, golf, home energy storage, and industrial applications. Founded in 2016 and headquartered in Redmond, Oregon, the company operates with just 22 employees and generates approximately $9.6M in annual revenue from its 12V and 48V battery products, battery monitors, chargers, inverters, and e360 Home Energy Storage solutions.

Investment Thesis
Expion360 is a micro-cap ($7.4M market cap) lithium iron phosphate battery manufacturer that grew revenue 72% in 2025 to $9.6M and narrowed its net loss from $13.5M to $6.2M, with new CEO Joseph Hammer driving expansion into home energy storage and industrial markets through the HESS line and DASGen partnership [1][2]. Three next-generation battery models are planned for H2 2026, and the LiFePO4 chemistry offers structural advantages (safety, longevity, no thermal runaway risk) in a rapidly growing energy storage market.
The bear case is overwhelming for institutional investors: the stock trades at $0.68 with a $7.4M market cap and daily dollar volume of just $169K, making it essentially uninvestable at scale [3]. The company has only 22 employees, burns $6.1M in annual operating cash flow against $3.0M in cash — implying approximately 6 months of runway without additional financing, which will likely require highly dilutive equity issuance. Profit margin of -64.6% and ROE of -137.7% demonstrate the company is far from profitability. Expion360 competes against massive players (BYD, CATL, Battle Born) with virtually unlimited capital and manufacturing scale advantages. The NASDAQ listing is at risk given the sub-$1.00 share price, and delisting would further restrict liquidity.
Key Catalysts
  1. Three next-generation battery models with commercial availability in H2 2026 could accelerate revenue growth if demand materializes in industrial and construction markets
  2. DASGen Hybrid Energy Storage partnership with Dealer Accessory Supply represents potential entry into the larger industrial energy storage market
  3. Home energy storage (HESS) product line targets the residential solar storage market, which is experiencing secular tailwinds from utility rate increases and grid reliability concerns
Key Risks
  1. Cash runway of approximately 6 months at current burn rate ($3.0M cash, -$6.1M annual operating cash flow) creates imminent dilution or insolvency risk
  2. Sub-$1.00 share price puts NASDAQ listing at risk — delisting would severely restrict trading liquidity and institutional investor access
  3. Micro-cap scale disadvantage versus BYD, CATL, and Battle Born Batteries, which have manufacturing cost advantages, broader distribution, and stronger brand recognition in the LiFePO4 market
