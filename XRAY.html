Company Profile
Healthcare ·Medical Instruments & Supplies ·NASDAQ ·United States
Dentsply Sirona, Inc. is the world's largest manufacturer of professional dental products and technologies. The company designs, manufactures, and markets dental consumables, dental equipment (including CAD/CAM systems, imaging equipment, and treatment centers), and dental technology solutions for dentists, dental laboratories, and dental specialists worldwide. Formed through the 2016 merger of DENTSPLY International and Sirona Dental Systems, headquartered in Charlotte, North Carolina.

Investment Thesis
Dentsply Sirona trades at a forward P/E of just 7.40 on adjusted EPS guidance of $1.40-$1.50, representing deep value if the turnaround succeeds [1][5]. The company remains the world's largest professional dental products manufacturer with global scale, and analysts project a $13.40 price target (24% upside) [5]. Q1 2026 showed sequential improvement with a narrowing net loss ($10M vs. $628M full-year 2025), and the $300M cost reduction program should drive margin recovery [1]. The dental market benefits from aging demographics, emerging market growth, and increasing digital adoption, providing a favorable secular backdrop.
The bear case is substantial and multidimensional. Revenue has declined from $3.79B (2024) to $3.68B (2025) with 2026 operational growth guided at -3% to -1%, indicating continued market share losses [1]. Gross margins compressed 450bps YoY to 48.5% in Q1 2026, and adjusted EBITDA margins fell from 19.0% to 14.7% [1]. The $628M net loss in 2025 renders the 2.96% dividend yield unsustainable without a rapid earnings recovery. Organizational instability — three CEOs in eight years, an SEC investigation, employee trust at 39%, and massive turnover — makes execution of any turnaround strategy extremely difficult [3][4]. The company's failed Byte acquisition and recurring leadership crises suggest deep structural governance problems that no single CEO can fix quickly.
Key Catalysts
  1. $300M cost reduction program reaching full run-rate could restore adjusted EBITDA margins toward historical 20%+ levels, driving significant EPS recovery from the $1.40-$1.50 guidance range
  2. Stabilization under new leadership with potential for improved organizational trust and reduced employee turnover if the current management team can deliver consistent execution
  3. Forward P/E of 7.40 creates substantial re-rating potential if the company demonstrates two consecutive quarters of positive organic revenue growth
Key Risks
  1. Revenue decline continuing at -1% to -3% operational growth signals ongoing market share losses to competitors, with no pipeline of new products to reverse the trajectory
  2. Gross margin compression from 53.0% to 48.5% in Q1 2026 suggests pricing pressure and cost inflation that the $300M restructuring may not fully offset, especially with tariff headwinds
  3. Three CEOs in eight years and only 39% employee trust in leadership create existential governance risk — the next leadership transition could further destabilize the turnaround
