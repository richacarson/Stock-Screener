Investment Thesis
Block at 12.6x forward earnings on $3.66 adjusted EPS (54% growth) represents compelling value for a fintech platform with 59M Cash App users and accelerating Square commerce volumes [1]. The AI-driven workforce reduction from 10,000+ to under 6,000 employees creates a structurally more profitable business with 2026 adjusted operating income guided to $3.2B (54% growth) and 20% margins. Cash App's 33% gross profit growth and 9.3M primary banking actives (22% growth) validate the consumer finance platform. The $5B buyback authorization and $9.2B liquidity provide capital flexibility.
Significant risks remain. GAAP Q4 EPS of $0.19 missed the $0.65 estimate, and the 5.4% GAAP profit margin reveals that stock-based compensation remains a massive dilution headwind [1]. The 40%+ workforce reduction creates execution risk as institutional knowledge is lost and remaining employees face increased workload. Revenue growth of only 3.6% YoY raises questions about top-line momentum, with the company increasingly reliant on margin expansion rather than growth. Bitcoin revenue concentration (significant but low-margin) distorts revenue metrics. The GAAP/non-GAAP earnings gap and aggressive adjustments make true profitability difficult to assess. Competition from PayPal, Stripe, and Apple Pay continues to intensify.
Key Catalysts
  1. 2026 gross profit guided to $12.2B (18% growth) with adjusted operating income of $3.2B (54% growth) demonstrates margin expansion story that could attract value-oriented investors [1]
  2. Cash App's 9.3M primary banking actives growing 22% could accelerate lending, investing, and direct deposit penetration, driving higher ARPU [1]
  3. AI-driven engineering productivity gains (40%+ increase in code shipped per engineer) could sustain margin expansion beyond current guidance if applied across all functions
Key Risks
  1. The 4,000+ employee layoff (40%+ of workforce) creates execution risk through knowledge loss, morale impact, and potential service quality degradation across Square and Cash App [1]
  2. GAAP EPS missing non-GAAP by ~70% ($0.19 vs. $0.65) reveals that stock-based compensation is diluting shareholders even as adjusted earnings grow rapidly
  3. Revenue growth of only 3.6% means the margin expansion story must sustain through economic cycles — any top-line deceleration or recession would pressure the adjusted earnings growth narrative [1]
