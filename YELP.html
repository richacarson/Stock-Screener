Company Profile
Communication Services ·Internet Content & Information ·NYSE ·United States
Yelp Inc. operates a platform connecting consumers with local businesses across categories including restaurants, home services, health, beauty, and auto. The company provides advertising products (cost-per-click, multi-location ads), Yelp Guest Manager (restaurant SaaS), Yelp Guaranteed, data licensing through Yelp Fusion, and content trust tools. Founded in 2004 and headquartered in San Francisco, Yelp operates as a remote-first company.

Investment Thesis
Yelp is a profitable local business platform trading at a deep value of 7.2x forward earnings with a pristine balance sheet (3.5% D/E), reflecting the market's severe skepticism about the company's AI disruption risk [4]. The bull case centers on Yelp's 280M+ review database as an irreplaceable data asset that AI models need — evidenced by the OpenAI partnership — and the deliberate AI investment cycle that sacrifices 2026 EBITDA ($310-330M vs $369M in 2025) to 're-conceive the Yelp experience' [1][2]. Record FY2025 revenue of $1.46B, services advertising growth of 8% to $948M, and 17% growth in other revenue (transactions, SaaS, data licensing) demonstrate diversification beyond core search advertising [1]. The 90.3% gross margin and 20% ROE provide a high-quality earnings base, and at $1.6B market cap (just 1.1x revenue), any AI transformation success would drive significant multiple expansion.
The bear case is that AI represents an existential threat to Yelp's core business model. Google AI Overviews and ChatGPT can synthesize local business recommendations without directing consumers to Yelp, potentially collapsing the traffic and advertising model that generates the majority of revenue [2]. Revenue has stalled at -0.5% growth with 2026 guidance of $1.455-1.475B implying continued flatness, while EBITDA declines 11-16% — this is not a growth story [1]. The company's 21-year history of failing to escape Google's shadow in local search raises fundamental questions about management's ability to execute the AI pivot. Yelp's advertising revenue model depends on driving traffic to business listing pages, but AI assistants may permanently reroute this discovery channel. The stock has declined from $41.22 to $28.74 (a 30% drop from 52-week high), and the Comparably C-rated executive team faces the challenge of transforming an aging platform while competitors spend orders of magnitude more on AI [2][4].
Key Catalysts
  1. AI transformation investment — deliberate $60M+ EBITDA compression in 2026 could position Yelp for reacceleration if AI-powered products drive user engagement and advertiser ROI [1]
  2. Non-advertising revenue diversification growing 17% YoY — transactions, Yelp Guest Manager SaaS, and data licensing reduce dependence on advertising model vulnerable to AI disruption [1]
  3. Deep value at 7.2x forward P/E with 3.5% D/E and 90% gross margin — any stabilization in revenue growth or AI transformation progress could drive significant multiple expansion from depressed levels [4]
Key Risks
  1. Existential AI disruption risk — Google AI Overviews, ChatGPT, and Perplexity can synthesize local business recommendations without directing traffic to Yelp, threatening the core advertising model [2]
  2. Revenue stagnation — flat guidance of $1.455-1.475B for 2026 with declining EBITDA suggests the business model is losing competitive relevance despite record gross margins [1]
  3. Google dependency — Yelp has spent two decades fighting Google's dominance in local search without successfully differentiating, and AI further tilts the competitive landscape toward Google's integrated ecosystem [2]
