Company Profile
Consumer Cyclical ·Leisure ·NYSE ·United States
YETI Holdings designs, markets, and distributes premium outdoor lifestyle products including hard and soft coolers, drinkware (Rambler, Yonder), bags, cargo solutions, outdoor furniture, and apparel. Founded in 2006 and headquartered in Austin, Texas, YETI sells through direct-to-consumer channels (websites, retail stores) and wholesale partners across the US, Canada, Australia, Europe, and Japan.

Investment Thesis
YETI is a premium outdoor lifestyle brand trading at a compelling 12.3x forward P/E — a deep discount to consumer brand peers — with 23.8% ROE, 57.4% gross margins, and strong insider buying (526K net shares purchased in six months) [1][4]. Full-year 2026 guidance projects 6-8% revenue growth with adjusted EPS of $2.77-$2.83 (12-14% growth), driven by balanced Drinkware and Coolers & Equipment contributions, high teens to 20% international growth, and over $200M in free cash flow [1]. The international opportunity (25% growth in FY2025) provides a long growth runway as YETI expands across Europe, Japan, and other markets. The brand's premium positioning, product durability ethos, and circular economy programs (Rambler Buy Back, YETI RESCUES) support pricing power in a competitive landscape [3].
The bear case centers on decelerating domestic growth, consumer cyclicality, and competitive intensification. Revenue growth of 6.8% YoY and 5.4% 3-year CAGR suggest YETI is maturing as a domestic brand — the $1.87B revenue base limits the growth multiple premium. Net profit margin of only 8.9% (despite 57% gross margins) reflects heavy SGA spending on international expansion and retail stores that may not deliver proportional returns [4]. The premium consumer goods category is inherently cyclical — YETI's discretionary products face demand pressure in economic downturns. Competition from Stanley, Hydro Flask, and RTIC continues to intensify, and Stanley's viral social media success demonstrated that brand momentum in drinkware can shift rapidly. Tariff exposure on products manufactured primarily in China and Southeast Asia creates margin risk that could compress the 56-57% gross margin target [1].
Key Catalysts
  1. International expansion driving high teens to 20% growth with significant runway remaining in Europe and Japan, while providing geographic diversification [1]
  2. Forward P/E of 12.3x represents potential multiple expansion as market recognizes YETI's brand durability, 23.8% ROE, and $200M+ annual free cash flow generation [4]
  3. Significant insider buying (526K net shares in 6 months) signals management conviction in the company's undervaluation and growth trajectory [4]
Key Risks
  1. Consumer cyclicality — YETI's premium-priced discretionary products face meaningful demand risk in economic downturns when consumers trade down [1]
  2. Stanley's viral social media success demonstrated that brand momentum in insulated drinkware can shift rapidly, threatening YETI's premium positioning and market share [2]
  3. Tariff exposure on products manufactured in China and Southeast Asia could compress the 56-57% target gross margin and force price increases that risk demand destruction [1]

YETI's retail stores and wholesale distribution involve physical product experiences that AI cannot displace.
