Investment Thesis
Yext achieved a meaningful profitability inflection in FY2026, swinging from a $27.9M net loss to $37.9M net income, with Adjusted EBITDA expanding to $107.3M (24% margin) and free cash flow of $53.3M [1]. At a forward P/E of 4.8x and ARR of $444.3M, the stock appears deeply undervalued on a fundamental basis — the market is pricing in severe revenue decline. The Hearsay acquisition successfully added $25.6M in financial services revenue and the $140M tender offer signals management confidence in the stock's value. ROE of 24.2% demonstrates the platform's capital efficiency [5].
The bear case centers on Yext's existential exposure to AI-powered search disruption. As Google AI Overviews, ChatGPT search, and Perplexity increasingly synthesize business information directly, the value of Yext's listing management platform may erode structurally [3]. Organic revenue declined approximately 1% in FY2026, indicating the core business is already shrinking before AI search has fully matured. The stock has fallen 34% from its 50-day SMA ($5.06 to $3.34), reflecting market anxiety about this secular risk. With only 1,120 employees, limited R&D scale, and a $412M market cap, Yext may lack the resources to reinvent itself as an AI-native platform before its core business erodes. The extremely low forward P/E could be a value trap rather than an opportunity if revenue declines accelerate.
