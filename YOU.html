Company Profile
Technology ·Software - Application ·NYSE ·United States
Clear Secure, Inc. operates the CLEAR secure identity platform, providing biometric identity verification primarily in airports across the United States. Products include CLEAR Plus (aviation subscription service), CLEAR ID (free digital ID), TSA PreCheck enrollment, Sora ID (B2B KYC), and CLEAR Concierge. The company has 38 million total members and 7.6 million active CLEAR+ subscribers as of Q4 2025.

Investment Thesis
Clear Secure is a high-growth identity platform with a founder-CEO, exceptional financial profile, and expanding TAM. Revenue of $901M grew 16.7% in 2025 with Q4 bookings accelerating to 25.4% growth, and the company guided to $440M+ FCF in 2026 — roughly 28% above 2025 [1]. At 19.5x forward P/E, the stock is reasonably valued for a business with 27% 3-year revenue CAGR, 64.4% gross margins, expanding EBITDA margins (33.2% in Q4), and a $700M net cash position [4]. Total members of 38M (up 31.5%) and active CLEAR+ subscribers of 7.6M (up 6.0%) demonstrate the membership flywheel. The expansion into Sora ID (B2B KYC), TSA PreCheck enrollment, and partnerships with Uber and Greenhouse opens multiple growth vectors beyond the core aviation product. In an era of AI-powered deepfakes, biometric identity verification becomes more — not less — essential [2].
The bear case focuses on regulatory risk, competitive threats, and customer concentration. Biometric data collection faces increasing scrutiny — senators have raised privacy concerns about CLEAR's facial recognition technology and the risks of a single private company managing biometric data [3]. A biometric data breach would be catastrophic since, unlike passwords, 'a person cannot change their biometric traits' [3]. The TSA could revoke CLEAR's airport access or introduce competing government-run biometric verification. CLEAR Plus subscriber growth decelerated to 6.0% YoY despite total member growth of 31.5%, suggesting the free-to-paid conversion funnel may be plateauing [1]. Trailing P/E of 48.9x reflects a stock priced for perfection — EPS declined 71.4% YoY in 2025 due to non-recurring items, but any sustained growth miss could trigger a sharp derating. CEO Seidman-Becker sold 174,324 shares at $48-49 in March 2026 — a $8.5M sale that, while modest relative to her total holdings, warrants monitoring [4].
Key Catalysts
  1. Sora ID and B2B identity verification expansion into hiring (Greenhouse partnership), rideshare (Uber), and healthcare verticals, diversifying revenue beyond aviation [1][2]
  2. CLEAR guided to $440M+ FCF in 2026, roughly 28% above 2025, with margin expansion as CLEAR Plus scales and B2B products contribute higher-margin revenue [1]
  3. AI-driven deepfake proliferation increasing enterprise and consumer demand for trusted biometric identity verification — CLEAR positioned as 'identity layer of the internet' [2]
Key Risks
  1. Regulatory and privacy risk — biometric data collection faces senatorial scrutiny, potential state-level biometric privacy laws (like Illinois BIPA), and catastrophic breach risk since biometric data cannot be changed [3]
  2. TSA competitive risk — the government could expand its own biometric verification program, renegotiate CLEAR's airport access agreements, or impose pricing restrictions on a service that leverages public airport infrastructure [3]
  3. CLEAR Plus subscriber growth deceleration to 6.0% YoY despite 31.5% total member growth, suggesting the free-to-paid conversion rate may be declining and the premium subscription TAM could be limited [1]

AI Resilience (25%)
AI Resilience MODERATE
7
AI Risk Protection (6/9): CLEAR's core biometric identity verification business has meaningful protection from AI disruption. Physical airport security verification requires in-person biometric scanning — AI cannot replace the physical act of a traveler proving their identity at an airport checkpoint [1]. The company's proprietary biometric database of 38 million members represents a moat that AI alternatives would need years to replicate. Switching costs are high for enrolled members. However, AI does create some exposure: the rise of deepfakes and synthetic identities makes biometric verification both more valuable (increasing demand) and more challenging (requiring constant technology advancement). Government-controlled alternatives like TSA PreCheck could adopt AI-powered verification that bypasses CLEAR entirely [3]. The B2B identity platform (Sora ID) faces competition from AI-native KYC/identity startups. AI Opportunity (1/1): CLEAR has deployed an AI agent built on Sierra for customer service across web and mobile [2]. The company actively uses AI for customer journey optimization, and CEO Seidman-Becker positions CLEAR as essential infrastructure in 'an age of AI, deepfakes and bad actors' — making AI a demand driver for identity verification [2].
