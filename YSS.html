Company Profile
Industrials ·Aerospace & Defense ·NYSE ·United States
York Space Systems is a space and defense prime providing mission-critical solutions for national security, government, and commercial customers. The company designs, produces, integrates, and operates spacecraft including S-CLASS, LX-CLASS, and M-CLASS platforms, along with proprietary software for payload integration and constellation management. Founded in 2012, York IPO'd in January 2026 raising $629M.

Investment Thesis
York Space Systems is a high-growth space prime that posted 52% revenue growth to $386M in FY2025 and guided 2026 revenue of $545-595M (41-54% growth), with >70% backed by funded backlog [1]. The January 2026 IPO raised $629M, providing substantial liquidity to scale spacecraft manufacturing with 107 satellites in backlog through 2027. D/E of 17.3% and $163M cash provide a clean balance sheet. Management targets profitability by Q3 2026 with 35% contribution margins on new business, and Q4 adjusted EBITDA loss narrowed to -$1.4M, signaling an inflection point [1]. The space defense market benefits from secular tailwinds as the U.S. government increases spending on space-based national security infrastructure.
The bear case centers on execution risk and valuation. York remains unprofitable with FY2025 net loss per share of -$0.89, negative operating cash flow of -$121M, and forward P/E of 71x — pricing in perfection on profitability trajectory [1][5]. Gross margins of 19.5% are thin for a company valued at ~$4.9B, and the 35% contribution margin target on new business has not been demonstrated at scale. Glassdoor reviews rating culture 2.1/5 suggest serious talent retention risk that could impair execution as the company scales from 710 employees [2]. Government contract concentration creates lumpy revenue risk, and defense spending priorities could shift under changing administrations. The company has only 3 years of public revenue history, making long-term projections speculative [1].
