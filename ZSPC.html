Company Profile
Technology ·Computer Hardware ·NASDAQ ·United States
zSpace provides augmented and virtual reality educational technology solutions for K-12 schools, career and technical education markets. The company's autostereoscopic 3D laptop products (Inspire, Imagine) deliver glasses-free AR/VR learning experiences, complemented by the zKnow AI-powered assistant and Career Coach AI. Founded in 2006, headquartered in San Jose, California, with approximately 3,500 school district customers.

Investment Thesis
zSpace operates genuinely innovative autostereoscopic AR/VR technology deployed in 3,500+ school districts, and the AI-powered zKnow assistant and Career Coach have won multiple industry awards [2]. The company's 20-year track record of pursuing educational transformation through immersive technology demonstrates unusual mission commitment. The pivot to 53% software/services revenue improves gross margins and could provide a sustainable recurring revenue base if the company survives its current financial crisis [1]. With market cap under $1M and share price at $0.24, any recovery would offer asymmetric upside.
The overwhelming bear case: zSpace faces existential financial risk with a going concern warning from both management and auditors [1]. Cash fell to $1.0M at year-end from $4.9M, operations consumed $18M in cash during 2025, net loss widened to $25.4M on just $27.9M revenue (-91% margin), and the company required a $3M emergency investment to continue operating [1]. Revenue declined 43% in Q4 and 27% for full-year 2025, driven by expiration of pandemic-era ESSER school funding and Department of Education grant freezes. Five material weaknesses in internal controls with three unresolved signal fundamental governance failures [3]. A class action lawsuit alleges the December 2024 IPO registration statement was materially misleading. The stock trades at $0.24 with under $1M market cap and faces potential Nasdaq delisting. This company may not survive the next 12 months.
Key Catalysts

  2. Recovery of K-12 education funding as ESSER replacement programs or restored Department of Education grants revive school technology budgets [1]
  3. Software/services mix reaching 53% of revenue provides higher-margin recurring revenue that could reach profitability at lower revenue levels if hardware costs are minimized [1]
Key Risks
